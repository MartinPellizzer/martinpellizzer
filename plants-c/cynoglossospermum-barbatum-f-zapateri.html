Cynoglossospermum barbatum f. zapateri

Cynoglossospermum barbatum f. zapateri, commonly known as the Zapater's hairy tongue plant, is a variant of the hairy tongue plant native to arid regions of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive hairy texture along the leaf margins. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
