Copaifera cornui

Copaifera cornui, commonly known as the horned copaiba, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant produces a fragrant, resinous sap that has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval and slightly hairy.

Copaifera cornui thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, well-drained soils.
