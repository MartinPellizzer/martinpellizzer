Cassia lancifolia

Cassia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. Cassia lancifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
