Chrysodium polyphyllum

Chrysodium polyphyllum, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green with a slight golden hue. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can spread to form dense colonies.

Chrysodium polyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
