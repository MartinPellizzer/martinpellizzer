Chabraea pulchella

Chabraea pulchella, commonly known as the velvetleaf chabraea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
