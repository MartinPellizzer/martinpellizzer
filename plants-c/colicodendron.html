Colicodendron

Colicodendron, commonly known as the poisonwood tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Colicodendron species are known for their toxic properties, particularly the sap and leaves, which can cause skin irritation or more severe reactions in humans.
