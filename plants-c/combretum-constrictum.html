Combretum constrictum

Combretum constrictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf combretum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed, woody capsules. It is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental potential.
