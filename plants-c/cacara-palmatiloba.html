Cacara palmatiloba

Cacara palmatiloba, commonly known as the broad-leafed cacara, is a unique palm species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach several meters in diameter, giving it a striking visual presence. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, lowland areas with well-drained soils. Its trunk is typically slender and covered in a fibrous, spongy material, which helps it retain moisture.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its distinctive morphology and potential ecological significance.
