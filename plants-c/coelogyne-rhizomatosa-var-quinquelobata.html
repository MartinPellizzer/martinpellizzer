Coelogyne rhizomatosa var. quinquelobata

Coelogyne rhizomatosa var. quinquelobata, commonly known as the five-lobed coelogyne, is a striking orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, fleshy rhizomes and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces clusters of pendulous, cream to yellowish flowers with intricate, wavy petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
