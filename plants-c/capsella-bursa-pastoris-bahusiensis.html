Capsella bursa-pastoris bahusiensis

Capsella bursa-pastoris bahusiensis, commonly known as the common chickweed or mouse-ear chickweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with a prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped, covered in fine hairs, and often have a slightly succulent texture.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and urban areas.
