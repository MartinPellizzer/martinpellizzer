Cassine micrantha

Cassine micrantha, commonly known as the small-fruited sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berry-like fruits containing several seeds.

Cassine micrantha is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas.
