Cosmos diversifolius

Cosmos diversifolius, commonly known as the Texas bluebonnet, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and feathery, bipinnate leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in prairies and open woodlands.

The Texas bluebonnet is the state flower of Texas and is celebrated for its ecological and cultural significance.
