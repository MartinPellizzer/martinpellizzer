Cissus rubrinervia

Cissus rubrinervia, commonly known as the red-veined cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread easily.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid environments.
