Cambessedesia balsamifera

Cambessedesia balsamifera, commonly known as the balsam tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Its sap is known for its balsamic scent and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.
