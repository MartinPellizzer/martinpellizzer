Carex griersonii

Carex griersonii, commonly known as the Grierson's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream sides. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex griersonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
