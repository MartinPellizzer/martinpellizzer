Carex phalaroides var. capitellata

Carex phalaroides var. capitellata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly rough to the touch. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are arranged in a cylindrical shape.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
