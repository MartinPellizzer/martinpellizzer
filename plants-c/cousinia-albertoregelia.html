Cousinia albertoregelia

Cousinia albertoregelia, commonly known as the Albert Regel's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish to white composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Cousinia albertoregelia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
