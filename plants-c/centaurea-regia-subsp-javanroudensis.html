Centaurea regia subsp. javanroudensis

Centaurea regia subsp.

javanroudensis, commonly known as the Javanese crownflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are typically surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It grows as an annual or biennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
