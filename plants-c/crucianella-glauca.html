Crucianella glauca

Crucianella glauca, commonly known as the blue crassula, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, oval, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate ornamental touch. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for Mediterranean and coastal climates.
