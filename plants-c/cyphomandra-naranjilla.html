Cyphomandra naranjilla

Cyphomandra naranjilla, commonly known as the yellow passionfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants. The plant produces vibrant yellow-orange fruits that are highly aromatic and have a tart, acidic flavor. The fruit's exterior is covered in a rough, spiny skin, while the interior contains a juicy, segmented pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the consumption of its flavorful fruit.
