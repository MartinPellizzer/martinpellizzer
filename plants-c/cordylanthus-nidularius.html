Cordylanthus nidularius

Cordylanthus nidularius, commonly known as the clustered flaxvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces clusters of pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
