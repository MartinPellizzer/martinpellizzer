Creusa tetragona subsp. rudis

Creusa tetragona subsp.

rudis, commonly known as the rough creusa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to cream-colored flowers during the summer months. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its rough, hairy texture and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.
