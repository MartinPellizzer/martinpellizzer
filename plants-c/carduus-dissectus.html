Carduus dissectus

Carduus dissectus, commonly known as the dissected thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, spiny stem with deeply lobed, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets, with the outer ones being bright yellow and the inner ones being tubular.

Carduus dissectus is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
