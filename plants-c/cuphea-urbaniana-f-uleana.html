Cuphea urbaniana f. uleana

Cuphea urbaniana f. uleana, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its compact growth habit and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
