Clematis smilacifolia subsp. glandulosa

Clematis smilacifolia subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular clematis, is a climbing vine native to parts of Asia and Africa. It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its subspecies designation, glandulosa, refers to the presence of glandular hairs on its stems and leaves.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
