Cyrtopodium latifolium

Cyrtopodium latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed coryphostyle, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. It has a pseudobulb structure and is known for its relatively compact size.

Cyrtopodium latifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
