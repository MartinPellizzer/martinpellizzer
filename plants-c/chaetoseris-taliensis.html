Chaetoseris taliensis

Chaetoseris taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in Yunnan Province. The plant is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow disk flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
