Carex cephaloidea

Carex cephaloidea, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence. Its leaves are grass-like, typically narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Carex cephaloidea is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
