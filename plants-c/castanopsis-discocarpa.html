Castanopsis discocarpa

Castanopsis discocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leafed tanoak, is a species of evergreen tree native to the temperate rainforests of southern China and northern Vietnam.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts, and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and a trunk that often develops a buttressed base.
