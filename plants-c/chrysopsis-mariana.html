Chrysopsis mariana

Chrysopsis mariana, commonly known as the Hawaiian goldenstar, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the unique climate and soil conditions of Hawaii. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
