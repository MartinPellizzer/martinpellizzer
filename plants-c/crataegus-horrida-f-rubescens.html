Crataegus horrida f. rubescens

Crataegus horrida f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-fruited hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the hawthorn genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive red berries, which contrast with the typical green or yellow fruits of other hawthorn species. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically found in temperate regions. It produces white to pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by its characteristic red berries in the fall.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
