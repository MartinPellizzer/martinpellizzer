Capparis undulata

Capparis undulata, commonly known as the wavy caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, rounded fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a wavy margin, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and flowers.
