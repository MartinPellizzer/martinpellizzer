Cinnamomum padiforme

Cinnamomum padiforme, commonly known as the broadleaf cinnamon or the Malabar cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark reddish-brown berries, which contain seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is cultivated in gardens and for essential oil extraction.
