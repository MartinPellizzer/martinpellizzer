Cucurbita moschata var. cubensis

Cucurbita moschata var. cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban buttercup squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, round, and often vibrant orange to yellow fruits with a smooth, waxy skin. The plant is a vigorous vine that produces both male and female flowers, typical of many cucurbits. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its sweet, nutty flavor and is often used in both culinary and ornamental applications.
