Cardamine californica var. fecunda

Cardamine californica var. fecunda, commonly known as the California watercress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

It produces edible leaves and is sometimes cultivated for its nutritional value and peppery flavor.
