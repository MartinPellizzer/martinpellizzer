Chrysanthemum pulverulentum

Chrysanthemum pulverulentum, commonly known as the dust chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers and hairy, grayish foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
