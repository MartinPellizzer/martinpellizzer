Cleistogenes kitagawae var. foliosa

Cleistogenes kitagawae var. foliosa, commonly known as the Korean false brome, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, leafy stems that often remain close to the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
