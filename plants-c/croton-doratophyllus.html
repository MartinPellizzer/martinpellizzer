Croton doratophyllus

Croton doratophyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that vary in color from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Due to its unique leaf structure, it is a popular choice for horticultural collections.
