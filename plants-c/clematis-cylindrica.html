Clematis cylindrica

Clematis cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically trifoliate, with each leaflet being elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, blooming in early summer. Its stems are slender and twining, allowing it to climb and spread along supports.

Clematis cylindrica prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardens and landscapes.
