Copaifera letestui

Copaifera letestui, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a resin that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its aromatic bark and sap. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various species and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.

The resin of Copaifera letestui is also valued for its potential pharmaceutical and industrial applications.
