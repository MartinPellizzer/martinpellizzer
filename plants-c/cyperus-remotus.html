Cyperus remotus

Cyperus remotus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical regions. Its roots are often rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Cyperus remotus plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetlands and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
