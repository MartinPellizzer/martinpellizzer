Cucubalus spergulifolius

Cucubalus spergulifolius, commonly known as the slender bladderpod, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils and is found in arid and semi-arid regions. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological adaptability and potential medicinal properties.
