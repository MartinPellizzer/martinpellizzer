Cardiospermum altissimum

Cardiospermum altissimum, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by distinctive orange, bladder-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant or for its edible tuberous roots.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and utilitarian benefits.
