Curcuma haritha

Curcuma haritha, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like turmeric and ginger. The plant produces striking orange-red flowers that are often used in traditional ceremonies and as ornamental plants. Its rhizomes are sometimes utilized in herbal medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.

The leaves are broad and sword-shaped, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
