Calanthe callosa

Calanthe callosa, commonly known as the veined calanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, and it produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Calanthe callosa typically blooms in spring and is appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
