Caesalpinia decapetala

Caesalpinia decapetala, commonly known as the flame tree or the pink serissa, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, bipinnate leaves and bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Caesalpinia decapetala thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is adaptable to various tropical and subtropical climates.
