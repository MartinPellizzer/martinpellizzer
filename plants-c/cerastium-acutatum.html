Cerastium acutatum

Cerastium acutatum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in dense, white or pale green hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a hairy surface.

Cerastium acutatum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
