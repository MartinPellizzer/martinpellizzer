Cissus silvestris

Cissus silvestris, commonly known as the forest grape or devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it climb. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible berries that are often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental use in gardens and indoor spaces.
