Conyza articulata

Conyza articulata, commonly known as the common horseweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its erect stem, which is often branched and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Conyza articulata is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain regions.
