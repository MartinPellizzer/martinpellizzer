Chenopodium multifidum var. microcarpa

Chenopodium multifidum var. microcarpa, commonly known as sand spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by dry, three-angled fruits. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can tolerate high levels of salinity.

This species is often used as a forage plant for livestock in dryland environments.
