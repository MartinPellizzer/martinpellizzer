Casearia glauciramea

Casearia glauciramea, commonly known as the white casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
