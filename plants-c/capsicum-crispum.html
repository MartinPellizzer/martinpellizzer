Capsicum crispum

Capsicum crispum, commonly known as the wavy pepper or wild pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its finely lobed, wavy-edged leaves and small, yellow or orange fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Capsicum crispum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and colorful fruits.
