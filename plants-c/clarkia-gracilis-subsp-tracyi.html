Clarkia gracilis subsp. tracyi

Clarkia gracilis subsp.

tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's slender clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers.

The subspecies is typically found in moist meadows and streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and blooms in late spring to early summer.
