Cineraria bracteata

Cineraria bracteata, commonly known as the blue cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage with fuzzy textures, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.

Cineraria bracteata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
