Corion rubrum

Corion rubrum, commonly known as the red corion, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and serrated leaves that turn a deep crimson in the fall. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that contain a single seed each. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Rosaceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
