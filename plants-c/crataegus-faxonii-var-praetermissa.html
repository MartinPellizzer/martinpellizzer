Crataegus faxonii var. praetermissa

Crataegus faxonii var. praetermissa, commonly known as the Faxon's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pale pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins, and turn yellow in the fall.
