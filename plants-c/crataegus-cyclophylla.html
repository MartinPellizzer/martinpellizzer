Crataegus cyclophylla

Crataegus cyclophylla, commonly known as the cyclophyllahawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds but generally unpalatable to humans.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
