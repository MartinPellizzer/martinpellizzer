Calytrix muricata var. parvifolia

Calytrix muricata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush. The leaves are small, narrow, and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
