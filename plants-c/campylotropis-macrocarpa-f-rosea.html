Campylotropis macrocarpa f. rosea

Campylotropis macrocarpa f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered burr clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive rose-colored inflorescences, which consist of dense clusters of small, hairy pods. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is often cultivated for its attractive floral display and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
