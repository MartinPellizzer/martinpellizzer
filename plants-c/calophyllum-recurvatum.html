Calophyllum recurvatum

Calophyllum recurvatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
