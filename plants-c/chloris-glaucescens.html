Chloris glaucescens

Chloris glaucescens, commonly known as bluegrass or blue oat grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
