Centaurea pullata subsp. baetica

Centaurea pullata subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Baetican cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as limestone soils and open woodlands. It produces small, cylindrical seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and naturalized areas.
