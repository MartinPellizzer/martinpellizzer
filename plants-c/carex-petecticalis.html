Carex petecticalis

Carex petecticalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.

Carex petecticalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.
