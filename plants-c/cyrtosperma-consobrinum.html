Cyrtosperma consobrinum

Cyrtosperma consobrinum, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a robust rhizome system that anchors it in shallow waters and supports its extensive foliage. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, with a distinctive, spadix-like structure surrounded by broad, showy bracts.

Cyrtosperma consobrinum plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing water surfaces and supporting biodiversity.
