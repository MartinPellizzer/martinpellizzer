Chamaecrista glandulosa var. balsasana

Chamaecrista glandulosa var. balsasana, commonly known as the Balsas burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
