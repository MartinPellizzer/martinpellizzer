Chrysocoryne

Chrysocoryne, commonly known as the golden crown, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble crowns. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Chrysocoryne is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
