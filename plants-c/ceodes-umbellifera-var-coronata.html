Ceodes umbellifera var. coronata

Ceodes umbellifera var. coronata, commonly known as the crown umbellifer, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it typically grows in dry, sunny habitats. It is often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
