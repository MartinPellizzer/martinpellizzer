Ceratocaryum pulchrum

Ceratocaryum pulchrum, commonly known as the purple bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped inflorescences. The plant produces dense clusters of small, feathery flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
