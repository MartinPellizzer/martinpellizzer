Corylus abchasica

Corylus abchasica, commonly known as the Abkhazian hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces catkins in early spring, followed by edible nuts that mature in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and mountainous areas.

The Abkhazian hazel is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
