Cirsium × acanthifolium

Cirsium × acanthifolium, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often reddish in color.

It is often found in grasslands, waste areas, and along roadsides.
