Cyperus dewildeorum

Cyperus dewildeorum, commonly known as the dwarf papyrus, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
