Cyperus sanguinolentus f. flaccidulus

Cyperus sanguinolentus f. flaccidulus, commonly known as the red swamp cyperus, is a variant of the swamp cyperus species.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and leaves, which are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the summer months.

This form is notable for its distinctive coloration and its ability to thrive in saturated soils.
