Cinnamomum trinervatum

Cinnamomum trinervatum, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical with three prominent veins, giving it the species name *trinervatum*.

The tree is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses, particularly in the production of cinnamon and other aromatic products.
