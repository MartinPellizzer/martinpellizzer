Commelina commelinoides

Commelina commelinoides, commonly known as the blue commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes various ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features striking blue-violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, typically arranged in a whorl. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Commelina commelinoides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
