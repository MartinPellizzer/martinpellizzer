Clinopodium chilense

Clinopodium chilense, commonly known as Chilean oregano, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Clinopodium chilense is valued for its fragrant leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a seasoning.
