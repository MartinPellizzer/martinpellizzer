Caesalpinia ferrea var. petiolulata

Caesalpinia ferrea var. petiolulata, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood or red ironwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with small, oblong leaflets and produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a flat, leathery pod that contains several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
