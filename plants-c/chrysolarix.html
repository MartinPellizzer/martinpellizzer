Chrysolarix

Chrysolarix, commonly known as the goldenstar grass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often bear clusters of golden-yellow inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib. Chrysolarix species are typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, where they thrive in well-drained soils.

These plants are often used in landscaping for their ornamental value and drought tolerance.
