Campanula aurasiaca

Campanula aurasiaca, commonly known as the Aurasiaca bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula aurasiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
