Callistachys purpurea

Callistachys purpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf hibiscus, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring large, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The leaves are ovate and often have a distinctive purple tinge, especially when exposed to full sun. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full to partial sunlight for optimal growth.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
