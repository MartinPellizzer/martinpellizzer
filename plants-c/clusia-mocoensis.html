Clusia mocoensis

Clusia mocoensis, commonly known as the Mocoensis glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Jamaica.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family, also known as the guttiferae family, and is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers. The plant is evergreen, with thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its tropical habitat. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with prominent red or orange bracts that attract pollinators like birds and insects.

Clusia mocoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
