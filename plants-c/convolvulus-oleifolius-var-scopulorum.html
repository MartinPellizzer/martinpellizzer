Convolvulus oleifolius var. scopulorum

Convolvulus oleifolius var. scopulorum, commonly known as the rock convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a trailing or ascending herb with small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has hairy, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its dry habitat. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often found growing on rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
