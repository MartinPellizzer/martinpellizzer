Citrus × bigaradia var. caliculata

Citrus × bigaradia var. caliculata, commonly known as the Calamondin orange, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its small, rounded fruits.

It is a cross between the mandarin orange and the pummelo, resulting in a unique combination of traits. The plant typically has glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, yellow-orange fruits with a thick, leathery rind. Its flowers are white and fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of citric acid and zest.
