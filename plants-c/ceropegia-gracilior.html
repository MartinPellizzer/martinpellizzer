Ceropegia gracilior

Ceropegia gracilior, commonly known as the slender ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Its unique floral structure makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and indoor gardens.
