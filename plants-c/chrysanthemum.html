Chrysanthemum

Chrysanthemum, commonly known as the flower of the sun, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its diverse range of flower shapes, colors, and sizes. The plant typically grows from 30 to 150 centimeters tall, with lance-shaped leaves and daisy-like blooms. Chrysanthemums are known for their ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

They are also popular for their use in horticulture, both as ornamental plants and for their medicinal properties.
