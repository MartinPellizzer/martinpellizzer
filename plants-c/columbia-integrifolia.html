Columbia integrifolia

Columbia integrifolia, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the branches. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The bark of Columbia integrifolia is thick and deeply furrowed, providing protection against environmental stressors.
