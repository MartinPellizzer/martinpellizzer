Capsella bursa-pastoris integrella

Capsella bursa-pastoris integrella, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers and has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, heart-shaped pods that split open to release tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in many regions.
