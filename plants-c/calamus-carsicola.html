Calamus carsicola

Calamus carsicola, commonly known as the alpine calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to high-altitude regions of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its rhizome is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
