Carya ovalis

Carya ovalis, commonly known as the burr oak, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive, spiny acorns. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and thick, fissured bark. Its acorns are encased in a prickly husk and are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.
