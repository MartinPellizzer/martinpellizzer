Chomelia bella

Chomelia bella, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble small bells, hence its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.

Chomelia bella is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
