Cryptocarya hirsuta

Cryptocarya hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is known for its dense, dark green foliage. Its bark is often covered in a fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name.

Cryptocarya hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
