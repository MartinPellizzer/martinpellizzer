Cistus lunulatus

Cistus lunulatus, commonly known as the moonflower cistus, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the rockrose family (Cistaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers open in the evening and emit a strong, honey-like fragrance.

Cistus lunulatus is often used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance as a habitat for various insects and birds.
