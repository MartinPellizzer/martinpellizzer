Cucumis melo var. fairi

Cucumis melo var. fairi, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon characterized by its elongated, slender shape and smooth, pale green skin.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Middle East and parts of South Asia. The plant produces small, sweet, and crisp fruits with a mild flavor, making it popular in culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The botanical features of this variety include tendrils, lobed leaves, and monoecious flowers, typical of the Cucumis genus.
