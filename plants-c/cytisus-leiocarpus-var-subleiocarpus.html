Cytisus leiocarpus var. subleiocarpus

Cytisus leiocarpus var. subleiocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, multi-branched stems with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and typically narrow, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with soil bacteria.
