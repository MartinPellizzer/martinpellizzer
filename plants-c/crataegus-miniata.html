Crataegus miniata

Crataegus miniata, commonly known as the miniature hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, thorned branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
