Clematis turkestanica

Clematis turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkistan Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, appearing in early summer. The plant produces small, woody fruits called follicles, which contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and vigorous growth habit.
