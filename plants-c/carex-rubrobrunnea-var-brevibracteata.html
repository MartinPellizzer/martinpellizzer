Carex rubrobrunnea var. brevibracteata

Carex rubrobrunnea var. brevibracteata, commonly known as the red-bracted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically brown or reddish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its dense growth habit makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
