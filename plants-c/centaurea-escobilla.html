Centaurea escobilla

Centaurea escobilla, commonly known as the Escobilla thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its bright yellow to orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has a spiny stem and leaves, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.
