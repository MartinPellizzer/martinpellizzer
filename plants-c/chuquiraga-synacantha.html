Chuquiraga synacantha

Chuquiraga synacantha, commonly known as the Andean burweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils at high elevations. Its unique morphology includes a basal rosette of leaves and a tall, branching stem that supports its showy inflorescence.

Chuquiraga synacantha is also known for its medicinal uses among indigenous communities.
