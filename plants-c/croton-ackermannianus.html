Croton ackermannianus

Croton ackermannianus, commonly known as the African croton, is a tropical shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from yellow to red and orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and traditional medicine.
