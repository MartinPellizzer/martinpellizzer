Cestrum viridifolium

Cestrum viridifolium, commonly known as the green-leafed angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
