Coccoloba persicaria

Coccoloba persicaria, commonly known as redroot or redroot spinach, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-colored roots and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
