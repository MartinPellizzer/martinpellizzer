Cirsium lachenalii

Cirsium lachenalii, commonly known as the Lachenal's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple-blue flower heads. The plant produces achenes as its seeds, which are dispersed by wind, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Cirsium lachenalii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.

It is also of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
