Calamus oxleyanus var. obovatus

Calamus oxleyanus var. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is characterized by its glossy, obovate leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent midrib. The plant produces inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
