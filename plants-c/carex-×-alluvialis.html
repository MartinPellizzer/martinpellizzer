Carex × alluvialis

Carex × alluvialis, commonly known as the river sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as riverbanks, marshes, and floodplains. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

Carex × alluvialis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
