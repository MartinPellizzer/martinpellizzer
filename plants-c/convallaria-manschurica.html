Convallaria manschurica

Convallaria manschurica, commonly known as the Siberian lily, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a member of the Convallariaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, white or pink flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value is appreciated in gardens, though its toxicity requires caution in cultivation.
