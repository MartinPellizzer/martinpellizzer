Cephalaria gazipashensis subsp. pilifera

Cephalaria gazipashensis subsp.

pilifera, commonly known as the Turkish bellflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant is native to Turkey and thrives in rocky, arid environments. Its leaves are typically hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its drought tolerance.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.
