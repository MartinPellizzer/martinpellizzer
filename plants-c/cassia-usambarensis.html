Cassia usambarensis

Cassia usambarensis, commonly known as the East African cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in local medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

Cassia usambarensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
