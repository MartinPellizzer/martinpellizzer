Cathartocarpus fistuloides

Cathartocarpus fistuloides, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
