Capsella bursa-pastoris crassior

Capsella bursa-pastoris crassior, commonly known as the broadleaf chickweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers in racemes, which are followed by distinctive triangular or heart-shaped pods. It is native to Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in many parts of the world.

This species is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in gardens, and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
