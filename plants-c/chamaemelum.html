Chamaemelum

Chamaemelum, commonly known as nosegay or wild chamomile, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family.

These plants are typically low-growing herbs with aromatic, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The leaves are usually finely divided and covered in fine hairs, contributing to their distinctive appearance. Chamaemelum species are native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in grasslands and open woods.

They are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in herbal teas and traditional remedies.
