Cytisus stenopetalus var. sericeus

Cytisus stenopetalus var. sericeus, commonly known as the silky broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its silvery appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

It is also known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
