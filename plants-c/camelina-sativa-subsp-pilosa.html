Camelina sativa subsp. pilosa

Camelina sativa subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the hairy camelina, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecies name "pilosa.".

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by distinctive seed pods. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its oilseed potential and has been studied for its agricultural and ecological benefits.
