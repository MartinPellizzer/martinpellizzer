Cyrtochilum tricostatum

Cyrtochilum tricostatum, commonly known as the Three-costed Pterostylis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to its humid, shaded habitats.

Cyrtochilum tricostatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
