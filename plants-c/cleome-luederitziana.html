Cleome luederitziana

Cleome luederitziana, commonly known as the "Cottonball Plant," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy, white seed pods that resemble cotton balls, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow, cruciform flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

Cleome luederitziana is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
