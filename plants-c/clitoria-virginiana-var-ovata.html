Clitoria virginiana var. ovata

Clitoria virginiana var. ovata, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble butterflies, which are clustered in racemes. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye. Its flowers are rich in antioxidants and are commonly used in teas and culinary applications.

The botanical name reflects its origin and the distinctive shape of its blooms.
