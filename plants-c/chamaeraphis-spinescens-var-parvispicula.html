Chamaeraphis spinescens var. parvispicula

Chamaeraphis spinescens var. parvispicula, commonly known as the small-spined glassworts, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, green flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short, erect inflorescences. It thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.

Its morphology reflects its evolutionary adaptations to high-salt conditions, making it a resilient and ecologically significant species.
