Cylipogon capitatum

Cylipogon capitatum, commonly known as the red-veined dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with red veins, which are typically arranged in a single head. The plant has heart-shaped basal leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Cylipogon capitatum is primarily a herbaceous perennial that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
