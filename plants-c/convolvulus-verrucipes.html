Convolvulus verrucipes

Convolvulus verrucipes, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, glandular stems that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus verrucipes is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
