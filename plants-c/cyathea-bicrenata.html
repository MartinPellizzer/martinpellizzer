Cyathea bicrenata

Cyathea bicrenata, commonly known as the two-crested tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its fronds are typically bi-crested, giving it its common name, and it can grow up to several meters in height.

Cyathea bicrenata plays an important role in tropical ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
