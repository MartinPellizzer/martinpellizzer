Carex stylosa

Carex stylosa, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

Carex stylosa plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
