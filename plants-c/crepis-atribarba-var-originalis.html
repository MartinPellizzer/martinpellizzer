Crepis atribarba var. originalis

Crepis atribarba var. originalis, commonly known as the original barbed grass, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. It is native to Central Asia and is often found in dry, sandy or rocky soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
