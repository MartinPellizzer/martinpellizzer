Cymbidium eburneum

Cymbidium eburneum, commonly known as the white orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, long-lasting white flowers that bloom in winter and early spring. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, forming pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, providing a lush green backdrop to its showy blooms.

Cymbidium eburneum is prized in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
