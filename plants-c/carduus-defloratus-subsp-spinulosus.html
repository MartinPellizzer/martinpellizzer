Carduus defloratus subsp. spinulosus

Carduus defloratus subsp.

spinulosus, commonly known as the spiny eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and waste areas.

The plant features a robust stem that is often branched and covered with stiff, spiny hairs. Its leaves are deeply lobed and also bear bristly hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flower heads are composite, consisting of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale purple to white ray florets.
