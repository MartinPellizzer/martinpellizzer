Chrysoxylon febrifugum

Chrysoxylon febrifugum, commonly known as the feverfew tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a slightly serrated edge, while the bark is smooth and pale in color.

This species is often cultivated for both its decorative qualities and its use in traditional medicine.
