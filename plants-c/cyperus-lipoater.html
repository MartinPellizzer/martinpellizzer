Cyperus lipoater

Cyperus lipoater, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. Cyperus lipoater is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
