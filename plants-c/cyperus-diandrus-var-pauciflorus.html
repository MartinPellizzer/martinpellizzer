Cyperus diandrus var. pauciflorus

Cyperus diandrus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, often forming dense stands. Its slender, triangular stems are often reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

The botanical features of this species make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
