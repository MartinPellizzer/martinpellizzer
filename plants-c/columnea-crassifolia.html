Columnea crassifolia

Columnea crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Columnea crassifolia is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings due to its ornamental value.
