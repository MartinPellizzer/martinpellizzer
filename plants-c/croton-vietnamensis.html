Croton vietnamensis

Croton vietnamensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored foliage. The plant typically has alternate, palmately lobed leaves that can vary in shape and color depending on the variety. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its unique leaf patterns and potential medicinal properties.
