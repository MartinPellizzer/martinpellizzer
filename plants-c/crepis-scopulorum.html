Crepis scopulorum

Crepis scopulorum, commonly known as the rock mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments. Its flowers, which appear in late summer, are small and yellowish, often clustered in a single head.

Crepis scopulorum is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in high-altitude ecosystems.
