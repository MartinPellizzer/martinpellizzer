Chamaecrista nictitans var. patellaria

Chamaecrista nictitans var. patellaria, commonly known as the yellow stragglerbush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces narrow, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
