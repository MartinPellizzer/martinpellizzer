Clematis maguanensis

Clematis maguanensis, commonly known as the Maguanensis Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.
