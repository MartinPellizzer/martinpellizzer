Crataegus oxyacantha var. ovoxyacantha

Crataegus oxyacantha var. ovoxyacantha, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It features thorny branches and is characterized by its showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and gardens.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.
