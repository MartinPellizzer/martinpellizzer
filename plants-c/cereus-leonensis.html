Cereus leonensis

Cereus leonensis, commonly known as the Leon's candleplant, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the Caribbean.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, nocturnal flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Cereus leonensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in hot, dry conditions.
