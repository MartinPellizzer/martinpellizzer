Congea velutina

Congea velutina, commonly known as velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in certain areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.
