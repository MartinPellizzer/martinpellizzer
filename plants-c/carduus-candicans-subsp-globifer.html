Carduus candicans subsp. globifer

Carduus candicans subsp.

globifer, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that are typically blue or purple in color.

The plant produces a rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial.
