Convolvulus babylonicus

Convolvulus babylonicus, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often trailing.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
