Cereus aurantiacus var. superbus

Cereus aurantiacus var. superbus, commonly known as the orange night bloomer, is a cactus species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and produces large, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are typically bright yellow or orange, attracting nocturnal pollinators like bats and moths.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
