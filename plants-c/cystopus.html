Cystopus

Cystopus, commonly known as the bladder grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. Cystopus species are often found in moist, sandy soils, particularly in coastal areas.
