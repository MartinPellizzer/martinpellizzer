Crocus visianicus var. hybernus

Crocus visianicus var. hybernus, commonly known as the Irish crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the British Isles.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is well adapted to cool, temperate climates and is often found in grassy meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in British horticulture.
