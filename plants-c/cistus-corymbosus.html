Cistus corymbosus

Cistus corymbosus, commonly known as the woolly cistus, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, fuzzy leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant is part of the rockrose family (Cistaceae) and is well adapted to dry, rocky environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cistus corymbosus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental beauty.
