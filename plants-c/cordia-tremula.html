Cordia tremula

Cordia tremula, commonly known as the trembling cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Cordia tremula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
