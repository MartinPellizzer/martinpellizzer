Carex gibbsiae

Carex gibbsiae, commonly known as Gibbs' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It has triangular stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Carex gibbsiae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
