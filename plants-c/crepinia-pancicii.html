Crepinia pancicii

Crepinia pancicii, commonly known as the Pancici's crepina, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Crepinia pancicii produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
