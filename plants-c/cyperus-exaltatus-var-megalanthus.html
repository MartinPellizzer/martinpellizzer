Cyperus exaltatus var. megalanthus

Cyperus exaltatus var. megalanthus, commonly known as the giant umbrella sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features tall, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble umbrellas, typically reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers in clusters. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and adaptable species within the Cyperaceae family.
