Colysis polysora

Colysis polysora, commonly known as the common lichen, is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium.

It thrives in a variety of environments, including bark, soil, and rock surfaces, where it forms a protective and nutrient-rich matrix. The plant's structure is characterized by a thallus, which is the body of the lichen and can vary in form from crust-like to leafy or foliose. Colysis polysora plays a crucial role in ecological systems by contributing to soil formation and serving as an indicator of air quality.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique symbiotic relationship and adaptability to different habitats.
