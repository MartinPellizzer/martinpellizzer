Centropetalum sanguineum

Centropetalum sanguineum, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a short, thick stem with a single, palmate leaf that resembles a hand. Its flowers are typically clustered in a raceme and are known for their striking color.

Centropetalum sanguineum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
