Crataegus trahax

Crataegus trahax, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated margins, and it typically grows in woodland or open areas.

Crataegus trahax is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
