Conostegia pendula

Conostegia pendula, commonly known as the hanging pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Conostegia pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
