Clematis × diversifolia var. coerulea

Clematis × diversifolia var. coerulea, commonly known as the blueflower clematis, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous vine with compound leaves that are typically trifoliate, featuring three ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to violet, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are hairy and twining, allowing it to climb and support its foliage.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
