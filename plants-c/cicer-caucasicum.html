Cicer caucasicum

Cicer caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Cicer caucasicum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
