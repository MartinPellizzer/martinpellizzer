Cyperus xantholepis

Cyperus xantholepis, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus xantholepis thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.
