Crepis caucasigena

Crepis caucasigena, commonly known as the Caucasian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

Crepis caucasigena is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
