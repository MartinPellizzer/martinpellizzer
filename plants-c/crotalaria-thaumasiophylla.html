Crotalaria thaumasiophylla

Crotalaria thaumasiophylla, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a crown, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
