Conium hilliburttorum

Conium hilliburttorum, commonly known as poison hemlock, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and umbrella-shaped flower clusters. The plant contains toxic compounds such as coniine and gamma-coniceine, which can be lethal to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
