Chrysopsis wisconsinensis

Chrysopsis wisconsinensis, commonly known as the Wisconsin goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its propagation.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in some regions.
