Caustis dioica f. multiandra

Caustis dioica f. multiandra, commonly known as the multi-flowered reed or African reed, is a unique variant of the species Caustis dioica.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.

The inflorescence consists of multiple flower heads, which gives it the "multiandra" designation, meaning "many flowers.".
