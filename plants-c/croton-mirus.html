Croton mirus

Croton mirus, commonly known as the coral tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, and often brightly colored leaves that range from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton mirus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
