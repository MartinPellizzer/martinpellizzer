Cyperus platycaulis var. lucentinigricans

Cyperus platycaulis var. lucentinigricans, commonly known as the silverleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically silver or gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the base of the leafy stems. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Its botanical features make it both an ornamental plant and a potential weed in aquatic habitats.
