Cyphomandra dolichocarpa

Cyphomandra dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited solanum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits resembling small tomatoes. These fruits are typically yellow or orange when mature and contain numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
