Convolvulus capensis var. dissectus

Convolvulus capensis var. dissectus, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports and structures. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular garden plant, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
