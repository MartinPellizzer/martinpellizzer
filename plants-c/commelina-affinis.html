Commelina affinis

Commelina affinis, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers with three sepals and three petals, often accompanied by a spathaceous bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
