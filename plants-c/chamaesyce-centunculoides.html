Chamaesyce centunculoides

Chamaesyce centunculoides, commonly known as the small bladderwort, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces tiny, bladder-like structures that aid in trapping small aquatic organisms, a feature common among members of the genus Chamaesyce. It thrives in moist, often waterlogged soils and is often found in wet meadows or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on aquatic plant adaptations and ecological interactions.
