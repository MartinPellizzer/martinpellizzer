Crataegus ciliata

Crataegus ciliata, commonly known as the silky hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves that are often covered in silky hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Crataegus ciliata typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or thickets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
