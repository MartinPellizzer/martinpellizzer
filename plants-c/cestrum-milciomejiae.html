Cestrum milciomejiae

Cestrum milciomejiae, commonly known as the Ecuadorian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of Ecuador.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its berries, which are orange-red when mature, contain toxic compounds and are not edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
