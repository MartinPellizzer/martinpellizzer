Carex magellanica f. virescens

Carex magellanica f. virescens, commonly known as the green-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that typically exhibits a vibrant green coloration. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and ecological benefits.
