Calamagrostis vegeta

Calamagrostis vegeta, commonly known as the slender reed, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces tall, slender flowering stems in the summer. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower clusters.

Calamagrostis vegeta is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
