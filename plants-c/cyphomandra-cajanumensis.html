Cyphomandra cajanumensis

Cyphomandra cajanumensis, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, purple berries that are typically edible but can be toxic in large quantities. It is often found in disturbed habitats and along roadsides in its native range.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, alternate leaves, and showy flowers that bloom in clusters.
