Carex polycephala

Carex polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, often green or brownish flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Carex polycephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
