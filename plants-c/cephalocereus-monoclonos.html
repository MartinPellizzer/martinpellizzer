Cephalocereus monoclonos

Cephalocereus monoclonos, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring a swollen, globe-shaped stem known as a "pseudostem" that resembles a flower. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
