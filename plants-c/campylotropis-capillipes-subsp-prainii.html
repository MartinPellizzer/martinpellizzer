Campylotropis capillipes subsp. prainii

Campylotropis capillipes subsp.

prainii, commonly known as the Prain's slender milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Nepal. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by narrow, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its classification within the Fabaceae family highlights its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
