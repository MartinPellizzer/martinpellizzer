Centaurea raphanina subsp. saxatilis

Centaurea raphanina subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the rock centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as limestone soils and rocky outcrops.

The plant features slender stems with narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright blue or purple, with a distinctive central dark spot, and are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and dryland landscapes.
