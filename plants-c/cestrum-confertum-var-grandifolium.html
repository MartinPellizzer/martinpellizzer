Cestrum confertum var. grandifolium

Cestrum confertum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red, typically at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
