Cyperus afrosylvestris

Cyperus afrosylvestris, commonly known as the African flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

Cyperus afrosylvestris plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
