Crassula petrogeton

Crassula petrogeton, commonly known as the rock rose or stone plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often forming dense clusters. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Crassula petrogeton is popular as a decorative plant due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
