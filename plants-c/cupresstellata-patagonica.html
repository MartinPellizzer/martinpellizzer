Cupresstellata patagonica

Cupresstellata patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian cypress, is a unique coniferous plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which consists of scale-like leaves arranged in dense, compact clusters. This plant thrives in cold, high-altitude environments, often found in the Andes Mountains. Its cones are small and typically contain two seeds, a feature that distinguishes it from other cypress species.

The Patagonian cypress plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
