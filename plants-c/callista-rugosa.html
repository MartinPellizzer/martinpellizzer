Callista rugosa

Callista rugosa, commonly known as the rough callista, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family. The plant is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are typically pale blue or purple, with a distinctive spurred petal.

Callista rugosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
