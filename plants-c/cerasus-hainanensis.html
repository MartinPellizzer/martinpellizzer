Cerasus hainanensis

Cerasus hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red when mature, though they are not commonly cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
