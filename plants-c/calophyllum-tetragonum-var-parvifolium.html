Calophyllum tetragonum var. parvifolium

Calophyllum tetragonum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
