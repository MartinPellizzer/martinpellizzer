Carex siderosticta f. albomarginata

Carex siderosticta f. albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that have a distinctive white margin. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
