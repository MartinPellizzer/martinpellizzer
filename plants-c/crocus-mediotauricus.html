Crocus mediotauricus

Crocus mediotauricus, commonly known as the Meditaurian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iris family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean, including parts of Turkey and Greece. The plant produces delicate, early spring blooms with violet-blue petals and a yellow center. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in rocky, well-drained soils.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other early-season insects.
