Cratoxylum polyanthum var. macrocarpum

Cratoxylum polyanthum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited cratoxylum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
