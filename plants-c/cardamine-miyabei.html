Cardamine miyabei

Cardamine miyabei, commonly known as Japanese water shield, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often floating or submerged, and it produces small, white or yellowish flowers. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the sediment in its habitat.

Cardamine miyabei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
