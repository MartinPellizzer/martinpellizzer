Canarium album

Canarium album, commonly known as the white candlenut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing oily seeds. The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil, while the tree's wood is valued for its durability.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
