Capparis grandidieri

Capparis grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar caper, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a shrub characterized by its thick, woody stems and fleshy, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and moths. Its fruits are round, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing several seeds.

Capparis grandidieri is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
