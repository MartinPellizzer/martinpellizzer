Cyathea surukunensis

Cyathea surukunensis, commonly known as the Surukunensia tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of fronds that can reach several meters in height, giving it a unique and imposing appearance. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Cyathea surukunensis is often found in primary rainforests, where it plays a role in the ecological structure and biodiversity of its habitat.
