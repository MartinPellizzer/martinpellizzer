Carex subholarctica

Carex subholarctica, commonly known as Arctic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and subarctic environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its harsh habitat.

Carex subholarctica is adapted to cold climates and can survive under snow cover for much of the year.
