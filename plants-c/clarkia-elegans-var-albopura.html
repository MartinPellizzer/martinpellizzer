Clarkia elegans var. albopura

Clarkia elegans var. albopura, commonly known as the white-flowered Clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bowl-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
