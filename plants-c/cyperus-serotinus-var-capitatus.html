Cyperus serotinus var. capitatus

Cyperus serotinus var. capitatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus serotinus var. capitatus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
