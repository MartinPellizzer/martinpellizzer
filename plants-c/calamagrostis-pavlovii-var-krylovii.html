Calamagrostis pavlovii var. krylovii

Calamagrostis pavlovii var. krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov's reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-gray in color. This variety thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
