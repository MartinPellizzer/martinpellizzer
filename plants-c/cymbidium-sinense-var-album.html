Cymbidium sinense var. album

Cymbidium sinense var. album, commonly known as the white Chinese orchid, is a species native to China and is prized for its elegant, fragrant flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with a deep red or purple stripe. This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.

It prefers cool, moist environments and is relatively easy to care for, making it a favorite among orchid enthusiasts.
