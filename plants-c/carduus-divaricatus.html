Carduus divaricatus

Carduus divaricatus, commonly known as the rough hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in coarse, hairy foliage. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows, demonstrating adaptability to a range of environmental conditions.
