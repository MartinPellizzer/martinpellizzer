Cerastium trichocalyx

Cerastium trichocalyx, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pink, clustered in umbels, and bloom in early summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.
