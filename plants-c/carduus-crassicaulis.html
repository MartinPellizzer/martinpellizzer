Carduus crassicaulis

Carduus crassicaulis, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, purple or pink flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves and stems are typically covered in stiff, prickly hairs, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Carduus crassicaulis is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including fields and roadsides.
