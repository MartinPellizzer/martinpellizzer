Cyclamen romanum

Cyclamen romanum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, bowl-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant produces underground tubers that store nutrients, allowing it to survive in harsh conditions. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade.

Cyclamen romanum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its elegant appearance and seasonal blooms.
