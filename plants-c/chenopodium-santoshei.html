Chenopodium santoshei

Chenopodium santoshei, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Chenopodium santoshei is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
