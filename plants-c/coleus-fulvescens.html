Coleus fulvescens

Coleus fulvescens, commonly known as the flame plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which can range from red and orange to yellow and purple. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance.

Coleus fulvescens thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
