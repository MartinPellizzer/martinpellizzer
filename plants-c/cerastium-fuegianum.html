Cerastium fuegianum

Cerastium fuegianum, commonly known as the Fuegian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Its delicate appearance and ability to form dense mats make it a notable species in its native habitat.
