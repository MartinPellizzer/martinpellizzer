Cytisus corniculatus

Cytisus corniculatus, commonly known as the firethorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces red or orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus corniculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
