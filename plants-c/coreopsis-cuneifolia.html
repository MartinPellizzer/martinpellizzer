Coreopsis cuneifolia

Coreopsis cuneifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and lance-shaped foliage, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
