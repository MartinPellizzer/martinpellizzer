Canavalia ensiformis var. versicolor

Canavalia ensiformis var. versicolor, commonly known as the multicolored morning glory, is a variant of the common morning glory that exhibits a wide range of flower colors, including white, pink, red, and purple.

It is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its stems are hairy and can climb using tendrils, often reaching several meters in height.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing abilities.
