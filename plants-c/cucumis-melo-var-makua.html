Cucumis melo var. makua

Cucumis melo var. makua, commonly known as the Malabar melon, is a variant of the cantaloupe belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh that is typically yellow to orange in color. The plant is a trailing vine that produces large, oblong fruits with a distinctive netted pattern on the surface. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
