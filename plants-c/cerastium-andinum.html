Cerastium andinum

Cerastium andinum, commonly known as the Andean mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cerastium andinum is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
