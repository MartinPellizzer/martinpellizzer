Centaurea cineraria subsp. veneris

Centaurea cineraria subsp.

veneris, commonly known as the velvet centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, bract-like leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
