Cestrum vestitum

Cestrum vestitum, commonly known as the velvet cestrum, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red when ripe. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name.

Cestrum vestitum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.
