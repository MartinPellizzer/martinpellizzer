Chimonobambusa purpurea

Chimonobambusa purpurea, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often purple-hued stems that exhibit a striking coloration, especially when exposed to full sun. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests, and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a pale green to silver underside.

Chimonobambusa purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
