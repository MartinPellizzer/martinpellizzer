Cucurbita multiflora

Cucurbita multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese pumpkin or wild gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a sprawling vine that produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is native to East Asia and thrives in warm, temperate climates. Its fruits can vary in size and shape, often resembling pumpkins or gourds.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems, tendrils for climbing, and a fibrous, edible fruit with a sweet, nutty flavor.
