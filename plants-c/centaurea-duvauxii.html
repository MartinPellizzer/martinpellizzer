Centaurea duvauxii

Centaurea duvauxii, commonly known as the Duvaux's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bracteate flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
