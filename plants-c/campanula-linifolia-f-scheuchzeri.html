Campanula linifolia f. scheuchzeri

Campanula linifolia f. scheuchzeri, commonly known as the Scheuchzeri bellflower, is a botanical variant of the common bluebell.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features slender, hairy stems and clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
