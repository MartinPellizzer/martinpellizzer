Choeroseris rhagadioloides

Choeroseris rhagadioloides, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It has narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss and are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

This species is adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
