Convolvulus transvaalensis

Convolvulus transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a trailing or climbing herb with opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden use, though it can be invasive in some regions.

The species belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing habits.
