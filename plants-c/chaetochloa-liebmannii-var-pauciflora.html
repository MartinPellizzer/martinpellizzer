Chaetochloa liebmannii var. pauciflora

Chaetochloa liebmannii var. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
