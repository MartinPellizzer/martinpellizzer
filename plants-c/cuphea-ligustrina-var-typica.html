Cuphea ligustrina var. typica

Cuphea ligustrina var. typica, commonly known as the "whispering bells" or "candleberry," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant due to its compact growth habit.

Cuphea ligustrina var. typica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
