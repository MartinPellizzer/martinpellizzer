Cucumis melo var. dampsa

Cucumis melo var. dampsa, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender fruits with a smooth, pale green rind and a crisp, sweet flesh. The plant is an annual vine that produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, sunny climates. It requires well-drained soil and regular watering for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary applications.
