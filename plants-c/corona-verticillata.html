Corona verticillata

Corona verticillata, commonly known as the whorled crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces showy, yellow to orange ray flowers arranged in a whorled pattern around the central disk. Its botanical structure supports pollination by insects, particularly bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
