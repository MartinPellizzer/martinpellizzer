Camellia candida

Camellia candida, commonly known as the white camellia, is a species of flowering plant in the Theaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern China and is characterized by its large, white, waxy flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking floral display.

Camellia candida prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden landscapes.
