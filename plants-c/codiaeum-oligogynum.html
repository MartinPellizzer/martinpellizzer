Codiaeum oligogynum

Codiaeum oligogynum, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that range from deep red to yellow and orange, often with contrasting veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its striking foliage, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
