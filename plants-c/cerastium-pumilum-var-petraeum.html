Cerastium pumilum var. petraeum

Cerastium pumilum var. petraeum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh weather conditions.
