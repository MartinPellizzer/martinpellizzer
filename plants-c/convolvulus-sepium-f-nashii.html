Convolvulus sepium f. nashii

Convolvulus sepium f. nashii, commonly known as the Nash's bindweed, is a variant of the common bindweed, characterized by its distinct floral and foliar features.

It is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pale pink to white in color, with five petals and a prominent spur. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its stems are twining and can climb or trail along the ground.

This subspecies is often noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
