Cyperus niveus var. niveus

Cyperus niveus var. niveus, commonly known as the white flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically white or pale green in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens or used in ecological restoration projects.
