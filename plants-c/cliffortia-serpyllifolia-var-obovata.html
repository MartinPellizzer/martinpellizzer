Cliffortia serpyllifolia var. obovata

Cliffortia serpyllifolia var. obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are obovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact form and drought tolerance.
