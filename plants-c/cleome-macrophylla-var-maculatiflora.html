Cleome macrophylla var. maculatiflora

Cleome macrophylla var. maculatiflora, commonly known as the spottedflower cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
