Cerasus juliana var. muscata

Cerasus juliana var. muscata, commonly known as the muscat cherry or sweet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It produces small, round, sweet fruits with a red or dark red skin and a juicy, flavorful pulp. The plant is valued for its ornamental value, with attractive pink to white flowers in spring. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications, including jams, desserts, and fresh consumption.
