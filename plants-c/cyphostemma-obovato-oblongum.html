Cyphostemma obovato-oblongum

Cyphostemma obovato-oblongum, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in pendulous inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, berry-like structures that contain several seeds.

Cyphostemma obovato-oblongum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.
