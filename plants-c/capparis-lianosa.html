Capparis lianosa

Capparis lianosa, commonly known as the climbing caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is a vine that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its woody stems and trailing growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by green, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, which helps in reducing water loss.

Capparis lianosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and fragrance.
