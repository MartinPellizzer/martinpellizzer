Cinnamomum valerianum

Cinnamomum valerianum, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces dark brown, aromatic fruits that contain winged seeds. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are widely used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its unique scent and has been cultivated for centuries for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
