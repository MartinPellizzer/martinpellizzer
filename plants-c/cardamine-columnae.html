Cardamine columnae

Cardamine columnae, commonly known as the columned watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its small, edible leaves are often used in salads or as a garnish.

The flowers are white or pale yellow and are arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
