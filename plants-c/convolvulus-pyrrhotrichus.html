Convolvulus pyrrhotrichus

Convolvulus pyrrhotrichus, commonly known as the redstem morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
