Calamagrostis epigejos var. umbrosa

Calamagrostis epigejos var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features slender, arching leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
