Cereus glaucescens

Cereus glaucescens, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and remain open for only one night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
