Clinopodium insulare

Clinopodium insulare, commonly known as island mint, is a small, aromatic perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a strong mint-like fragrance. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue and bloom in late summer.

Clinopodium insulare is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
