Cyperus microdontus

Cyperus microdontus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cyperus microdontus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
