Chamaerhodiola humilis

Chamaerhodiola humilis, commonly known as the dwarf fireweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant thrives in cold, dry environments and is often found in tundra and rocky soils. It produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Chamaerhodiola humilis is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
