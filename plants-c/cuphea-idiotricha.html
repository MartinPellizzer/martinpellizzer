Cuphea idiotricha

Cuphea idiotricha, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Cuphea idiotricha thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
