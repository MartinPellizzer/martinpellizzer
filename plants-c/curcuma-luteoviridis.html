Curcuma luteoviridis

Curcuma luteoviridis, commonly known as the golden turmeric, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow rhizomes, which are rich in curcuminoids, giving them their vibrant color and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
