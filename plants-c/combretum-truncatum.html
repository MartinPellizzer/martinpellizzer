Combretum truncatum

Combretum truncatum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a truncate or notched base. Its bark is smooth and can be reddish or greyish in color, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Combretum truncatum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant floral display.
