Coelogyne augustii

Coelogyne augustii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Coelogyne augustii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its long-lasting, showy flowers.
