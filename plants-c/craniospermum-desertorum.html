Craniospermum desertorum

Craniospermum desertorum, commonly known as the desert cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent appearance, with thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, violet or blue flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Craniospermum desertorum is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
