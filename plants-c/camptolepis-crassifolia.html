Camptolepis crassifolia

Camptolepis crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved camptolepis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
