Cryptocarya bernhardiensis

Cryptocarya bernhardiensis, commonly known as the Bernhard's cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocado, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, and it produces dark blue to black berries that are edible for some bird species.

Cryptocarya bernhardiensis is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
