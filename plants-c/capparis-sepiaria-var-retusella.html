Capparis sepiaria var. retusella

Capparis sepiaria var. retusella, commonly known as the rock caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a rough texture and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in coastal areas. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
