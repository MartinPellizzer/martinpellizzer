Cynanchum longipedunculatum

Cynanchum longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, hooked seed pods. It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
