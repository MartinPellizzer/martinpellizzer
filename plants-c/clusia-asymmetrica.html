Clusia asymmetrica

Clusia asymmetrica, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. Clusia asymmetrica is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and light conditions.

It is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and air pollution, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
