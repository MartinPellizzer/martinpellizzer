Casuarina elegans

Casuarina elegans, commonly known as the slender she-oak, is a member of the Casuarinaceae family and is native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its unique, needle-like foliage and cylindrical cones. The plant has a deep root system that helps stabilize soils, making it valuable for erosion control. Its leaves are spirally arranged and often shed in clusters, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Casuarina elegans is also known for its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
