Carex limosa var. magellanica

Carex limosa var. magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
