Capraria pinnatifida

Capraria pinnatifida, commonly known as the pink rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Capraria pinnatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
