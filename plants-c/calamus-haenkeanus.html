Calamus haenkeanus

Calamus haenkeanus, commonly known as the golden cattail, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces a distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wetland environments.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
