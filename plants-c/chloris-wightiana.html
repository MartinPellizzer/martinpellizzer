Chloris wightiana

Chloris wightiana, commonly known as Wight's grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
