Conyza viscosa

Conyza viscosa, commonly known as common cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its prickly, spiny leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are often covered in stiff, bristly hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Conyza viscosa is known for its adaptability and has been naturalized in many regions around the world.
