Coleus wallamanensis

Coleus wallamanensis, commonly known as the Wallaman coleus, is a flowering plant native to Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage rather than its small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are often brightly colored with shades of red, purple, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is also valued for its potential in horticultural and ecological applications.
