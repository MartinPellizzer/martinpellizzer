Cyclopogon chloroleucus var. fontialis

Cyclopogon chloroleucus var. fontialis, commonly known as the Fontaine's bluegrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing mats, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or white flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in drought-prone environments.
