Caladenia testacea

Caladenia testacea, commonly known as the testaceous orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often strikingly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open habitats such as heathlands or woodlands. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with a distinctive labellum that aids in pollination.

Caladenia testacea is known for its ephemeral blooming period, often appearing after rainfall in spring or early summer.
