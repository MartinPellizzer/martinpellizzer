Centaurea serrulata

Centaurea serrulata, commonly known as the serrated centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves and produces small, spiky bracts around its flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Centaurea serrulata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
