Calamagrostis calvescens

Calamagrostis calvescens, commonly known as the bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Calamagrostis calvescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
