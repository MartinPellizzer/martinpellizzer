Connarus whitfordii

Connarus whitfordii, commonly known as the whitebark connarus, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of primary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
