Canna maxima

Canna maxima, commonly known as the giant cannas, is a striking tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, colorful leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, and its showy, spadix-shaped flowers that come in a variety of hues. The plant is a herbaceous perennial, typically growing in wet or marshy environments. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a popular ornamental and a potential invasive species.

Canna maxima is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and water features.
