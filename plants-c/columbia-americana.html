Columbia americana

Columbia americana, commonly known as the American columbine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple spurred flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features palmate leaves with five lobes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Columbia americana is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
