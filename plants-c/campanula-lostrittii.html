Campanula lostrittii

Campanula lostrittii, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of down.

Campanula lostrittii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
