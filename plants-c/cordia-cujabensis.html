Cordia cujabensis

Cordia cujabensis, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces woody capsules that contain the highly valued Brazil nuts. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

Cordia cujabensis plays a crucial role in the ecosystem, supporting a wide range of wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of the rainforest.
