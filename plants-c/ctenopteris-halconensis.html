Ctenopteris halconensis

Ctenopteris halconensis, commonly known as the Halconensis sea holly, is a member of the fern family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is notable for its adaptability to saline environments, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
