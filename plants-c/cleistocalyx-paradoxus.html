Cleistocalyx paradoxus

Cleistocalyx paradoxus, commonly known as the white corymbia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to northeastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a glossy upper surface.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds when mature.
