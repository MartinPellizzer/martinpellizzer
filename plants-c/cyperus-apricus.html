Cyperus apricus

Cyperus apricus, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along waterways, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes.

Cyperus apricus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
