Carex lepidocarpa subsp. nevadensis

Carex lepidocarpa subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevadans sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and covered with a fine, waxy coating. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked spikelets, which are often greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and small mammals.
