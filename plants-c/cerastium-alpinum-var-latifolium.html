Cerastium alpinum var. latifolium

Cerastium alpinum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with hairy surfaces, and stems that are often prostrate or ascending. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Caryophyllaceae family, sharing similar growth habits and floral characteristics.
