Carex dolichostachya subsp. dolichostachya

Carex dolichostachya subsp.

dolichostachya, commonly known as the long-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces upright spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers. It is characterized by its robust rhizomes and is often found in dense colonies.

The subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
