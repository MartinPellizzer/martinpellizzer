Cotyledon salzmannii f. punctata

Cotyledon salzmannii f. punctata, commonly known as the spotted cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often adorned with distinctive red or purple markings. The plant belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant. Its compact growth habit and striking foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration.
