Cnemidaria petiolata

Cnemidaria petiolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often submerged in water, and it produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to that of watercress.
