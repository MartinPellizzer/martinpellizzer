Cypripedium × paris

Cypripedium × paris, commonly known as the Paris orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium pubescens.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive pouch-like structure called a lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it produces a single, large flower per stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation programs.
