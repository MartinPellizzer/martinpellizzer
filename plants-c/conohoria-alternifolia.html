Conohoria alternifolia

Conohoria alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaf conohoria, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of leaves along the stem, which is a distinguishing botanical feature. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale in color. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Conohoria alternifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
