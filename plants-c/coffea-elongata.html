Coffea elongata

Coffea elongata, commonly known as the elongated coffee plant, is a species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy in appearance. Its flowers are white and fragrant, followed by red berries containing the seeds used for coffee production.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of particular interest to botanists and coffee enthusiasts.
