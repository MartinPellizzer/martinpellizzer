Carex × friesii

Carex × friesii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and cylindrical, with a fibrous texture, and it can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

Carex × friesii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
