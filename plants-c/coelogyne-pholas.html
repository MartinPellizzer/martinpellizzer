Coelogyne pholas

Coelogyne pholas, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

Coelogyne pholas is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
