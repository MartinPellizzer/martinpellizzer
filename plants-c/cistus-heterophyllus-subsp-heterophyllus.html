Cistus heterophyllus subsp. heterophyllus

Cistus heterophyllus subsp.

heterophyllus, commonly known as the Portuguese rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
