Casimira rhodopteron

Casimira rhodopteron, commonly known as the golden flame cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in soft, spiny areoles, which support clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant's leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in its harsh environment. It blooms primarily during the dry season, producing showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in warm climates.
