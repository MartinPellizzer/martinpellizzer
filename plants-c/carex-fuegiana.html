Carex fuegiana

Carex fuegiana, commonly known as the Magellan sedge, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly found in the southern regions of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex fuegiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
