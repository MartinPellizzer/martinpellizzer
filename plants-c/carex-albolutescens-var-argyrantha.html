Carex albolutescens var. argyrantha

Carex albolutescens var. argyrantha, commonly known as silver sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous brown flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Silver sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
