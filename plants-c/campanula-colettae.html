Campanula colettae

Campanula colettae, commonly known as the Coletta's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

Campanula colettae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
