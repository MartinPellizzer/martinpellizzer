Caldesia parnassifolia var. terrestris

Caldesia parnassifolia var. terrestris, commonly known as the groundsel bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners looking to add color and texture to their landscapes.
