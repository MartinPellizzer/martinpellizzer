Carex festiva var. gracilis

Carex festiva var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
