Conyza lineariloba

Conyza lineariloba, commonly known as the slenderleaf horseweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable, though it can be confused with other species in the Asteraceae family.
