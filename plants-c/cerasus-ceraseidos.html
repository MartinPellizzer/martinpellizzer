Cerasus ceraseidos

Cerasus ceraseidos, commonly known as the cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, yellow or red fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

Cerasus ceraseidos thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing plant in gardens.
