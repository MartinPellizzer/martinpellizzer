Cyclobalanopsis fargesii

Cyclobalanopsis fargesii, commonly known as the Farges' chinquapin, is a species of evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and produces small, woody fruits called nuts. It typically grows in cool, moist climates at high elevations.

Cyclobalanopsis fargesii is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also of interest for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
