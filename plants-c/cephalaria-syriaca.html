Cephalaria syriaca

Cephalaria syriaca, commonly known as Syrian cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and clustered in a conical shape, typically blooming in shades of yellow to pink.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
