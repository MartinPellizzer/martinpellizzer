Chamaecytisus anatolicus

Chamaecytisus anatolicus, commonly known as Anatolian broom, is a flowering shrub native to Turkey.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and covered in fine hairs.

It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
