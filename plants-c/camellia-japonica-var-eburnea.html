Camellia japonica var. eburnea

Camellia japonica var. eburnea, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is typically evergreen, with a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for ornamental gardening. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Camellia japonica var. eburnea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
