Corydalis bulbosa var. viridiflora

Corydalis bulbosa var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features delicate, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, which is a distinguishing characteristic of this variety. The plant produces small, bulbous roots that help it survive in its alpine or rocky habitats. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Corydalis bulbosa var. viridiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ease of cultivation in well-drained soil.
