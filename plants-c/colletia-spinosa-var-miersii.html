Colletia spinosa var. miersii

Colletia spinosa var. miersii, commonly known as the Miers' cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
