Colchicum bivonae var. lusitanum

Colchicum bivonae var. lusitanum, commonly known as the Lusitanian autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are basal and often covered in a fine, white down. Its corm-like underground stem stores nutrients, allowing it to survive harsh winters.

It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.
