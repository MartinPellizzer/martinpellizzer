Chamaecrista kirkii var. guineensis

Chamaecrista kirkii var. guineensis, commonly known as the African burr legume, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
