Cynoglossum castaneum

Cynoglossum castaneum, commonly known as the red deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive purple-red coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Cynoglossum castaneum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
