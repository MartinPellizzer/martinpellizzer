Cestrum amictum var. parviflorum

Cestrum amictum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered cestrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its opposite, simple, and often aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its nocturnal fragrance and floral display.
