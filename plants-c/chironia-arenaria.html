Chironia arenaria

Chironia arenaria, commonly known as the sand couch grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has slender, creeping stems that form a dense mat, often covering the ground to prevent erosion. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Chironia arenaria is valued for its ability to stabilize sandy soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
