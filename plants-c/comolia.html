Comolia

Comolia, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrubs with small, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is native to various regions in the northern hemisphere, including parts of Asia, Europe, and North America. Comolia species often thrive in acidic, well-drained soils and are adapted to cooler climates.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
