Chionanthus pedunculatus

Chionanthus pedunculatus, commonly known as the whiteflower strobilanthes, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowering period is brief but striking, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
