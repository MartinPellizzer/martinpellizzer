Callicarpa serrata

Callicarpa serrata, commonly known as the roughleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet to purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has simple, serrated leaves that turn red in the autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Callicarpa serrata is also valued for its fragrant flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
