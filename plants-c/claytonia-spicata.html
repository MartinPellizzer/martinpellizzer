Claytonia spicata

Claytonia spicata, commonly known as the spike flower or mountain chickweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Claytonia spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
