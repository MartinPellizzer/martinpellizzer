Ceballosia fruticosa

Ceballosia fruticosa, commonly known as the Mexican firebush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, providing some drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
