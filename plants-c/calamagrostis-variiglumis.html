Calamagrostis variiglumis

Calamagrostis variiglumis, commonly known as the variable reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower clusters.

Calamagrostis variiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
