Carex gracilis f. rudis

Carex gracilis f. rudis, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a variant of the slender sedge, characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. The plant features narrow, cylindrical leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

The botanical features of Carex gracilis f. rudis make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
