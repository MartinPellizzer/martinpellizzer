Carex macrochaeta

Carex macrochaeta, commonly known as the tall beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex macrochaeta is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
