Callitriche verna var. pedunculata

Callitriche verna var. pedunculata, commonly known as early water starwort, is a small aquatic plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, thread-like leaves.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and ditches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its stems are often branched and can form dense mats on the water surface.

This variety is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters.
