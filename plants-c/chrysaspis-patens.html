Chrysaspis patens

Chrysaspis patens, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to orange flowers.

Chrysaspis patens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
