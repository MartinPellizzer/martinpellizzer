Corynopuntia stanlyi

Corynopuntia stanlyi, commonly known as the Stanley's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in small, spine-tipped tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are followed by small, red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.

Corynopuntia stanlyi is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant that plays a role in providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
