Cousinia cylindracea

Cousinia cylindracea, commonly known as the cylindrical cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of hairs to reduce water loss.

Cousinia cylindracea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
