Cirsium kusnezowianum

Cirsium kusnezowianum, commonly known as the Kusnezow's thistle, is a perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, bristly textures, and the flowers are usually arranged in a single, terminal head.

Cirsium kusnezowianum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
