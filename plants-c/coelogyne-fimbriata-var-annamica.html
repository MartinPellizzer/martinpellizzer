Coelogyne fimbriata var. annamica

Coelogyne fimbriata var. annamica, commonly known as the Annamite coelogyne, is a rare and ornamental orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences and vibrant, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid forest environments, often on tree trunks or among leaf litter. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and covered with a dense, fibrous sheath, and it produces multiple flowers per spike.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
