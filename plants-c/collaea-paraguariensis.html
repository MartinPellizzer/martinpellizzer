Collaea paraguariensis

Collaea paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina, Brazil, and Paraguay.

It belongs to the genus Collaea in the family Asteraceae, and it is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, hairy fruits containing several seeds.

Yerba mate is well-known for its use in traditional beverages, where the dried leaves are steeped in hot water to create a stimulating drink.
