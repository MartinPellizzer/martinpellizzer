Cissus javana var. pubescens

Cissus javana var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvety arrowroot or velvet cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are used in various traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
