Crotalaria subcapitata var. fwamboensis

Crotalaria subcapitata var. fwamboensis, commonly known as the Fwambo bush, is a shrub native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such regions.

The leaves and seeds of this plant have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported health benefits.
