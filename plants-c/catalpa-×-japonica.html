Catalpa × japonica

Catalpa × japonica, commonly known as the Japanese catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Catalpa bignonioides and Catalpa speciosa. The tree is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. It produces long, bean-like pods that contain seeds covered in silky hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
