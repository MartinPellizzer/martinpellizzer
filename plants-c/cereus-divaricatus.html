Cereus divaricatus

Cereus divaricatus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a succulent cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is known for its striking, large white flowers that open at night. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Cereus divaricatus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
