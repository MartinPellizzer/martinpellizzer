Campsiandra casiquiarensis

Campsiandra casiquiarensis, commonly known as the Casiquiare River cypress, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant is often found growing along riverbanks and in moist, well-drained soils. Its seeds are enclosed in woody capsules that split open to release the seeds.

This species plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, contributing to local biodiversity and soil stabilization.
