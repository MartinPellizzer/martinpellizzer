Cytisus ciliatus var. pleurotrichus

Cytisus ciliatus var. pleurotrichus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often spiny stems that are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
