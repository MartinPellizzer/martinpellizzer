Cassia sulfurea

Cassia sulfurea, commonly known as golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
