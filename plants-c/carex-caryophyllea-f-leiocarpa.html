Carex caryophyllea f. leiocarpa

Carex caryophyllea f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved sedges, is a variant of the Carex genus characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage.

This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, where it thrives in full to partial sun. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, with a smooth texture that distinguishes it from other sedge species. The inflorescence consists of a cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
