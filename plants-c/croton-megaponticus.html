Croton megaponticus

Croton megaponticus, commonly known as the Greek croton, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often a vibrant green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
