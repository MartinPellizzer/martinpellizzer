Cirsium furusei

Cirsium furusei, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is covered in stiff, bristly hairs, which provide a protective barrier against herbivores.

Cirsium furusei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
