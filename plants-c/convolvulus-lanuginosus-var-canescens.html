Convolvulus lanuginosus var. canescens

Convolvulus lanuginosus var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, creeping stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
