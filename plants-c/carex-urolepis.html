Carex urolepis

Carex urolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex urolepis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
