Cissus vanderbenii

Cissus vanderbenii, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cissus vanderbenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
