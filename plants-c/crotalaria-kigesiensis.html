Crotalaria kigesiensis

Crotalaria kigesiensis, commonly known as the African cowpea or Kigesi morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and alternate leaves.

Its seeds are enclosed in a pod and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
