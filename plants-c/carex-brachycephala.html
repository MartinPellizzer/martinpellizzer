Carex brachycephala

Carex brachycephala, commonly known as the broad-headed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is often found in temperate regions.

Carex brachycephala plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
