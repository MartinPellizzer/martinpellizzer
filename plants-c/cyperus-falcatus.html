Cyperus falcatus

Cyperus falcatus, commonly known as the swordleaf flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered spikelets that produce wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus falcatus is an invasive species in some areas, outcompeting native vegetation and altering wetland ecosystems.
