Cyperus procerus var. griffithianus

Cyperus procerus var. griffithianus, commonly known as the Griffith's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variant is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various wetland conditions.
