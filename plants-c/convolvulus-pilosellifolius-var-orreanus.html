Convolvulus pilosellifolius var. orreanus

Convolvulus pilosellifolius var. orreanus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and attractive addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
