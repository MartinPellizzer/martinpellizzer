Clematitis

Clematitis, commonly known as coral tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences. The plants typically have compound leaves and produce pods that contain seeds. Clematitis species are often cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant flowers.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine and as sources of dyes.
