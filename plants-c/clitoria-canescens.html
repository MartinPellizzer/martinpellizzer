Clitoria canescens

Clitoria canescens, commonly known as the white butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, depending on the variety. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Clitoria canescens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
