Chaetaria adscensionis

Chaetaria adscensionis, commonly known as the Skyward Spike, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that can reach up to 3 meters in height, supporting a single, showy inflorescence at the apex. The flowers are typically blue or violet, with intricate, spiky petals that give the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited habitat, Chaetaria adscensionis is considered a species of conservation concern.
