Commelina wallichiana

Commelina wallichiana, commonly known as the blue commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by bracts.

Commelina wallichiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
