Cirsium heterophyllum subsp. angarense

Cirsium heterophyllum subsp.

angarense, commonly known as the Armenian thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, silvery-green leaves with a fuzzy texture.

The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads, which are typically arranged in a spherical or conical shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
