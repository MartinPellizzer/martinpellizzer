Cyathea serratifolia

Cyathea serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, brownish layer, with large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that produce spores, which are dispersed by wind to propagate the species.

Cyathea serratifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
