Calanthe furcata f. albolineata

Calanthe furcata f. albolineata, commonly known as the white-lined calanthe, is a rare and ornamental orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact growth habit with broad, dark green leaves and striking white or pale yellow flowers with distinctive red or orange lip markings. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that appear in late spring or early summer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
