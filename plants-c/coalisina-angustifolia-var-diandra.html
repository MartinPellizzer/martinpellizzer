Coalisina angustifolia var. diandra

Coalisina angustifolia var. diandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf coalisina, is a species native to specific regions in South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded forest understories.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its humid, tropical environment.
