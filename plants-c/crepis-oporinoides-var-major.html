Crepis oporinoides var. major

Crepis oporinoides var. major, commonly known as the giant mouse-ear chickweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its inflorescence is characterized by a single terminal flower and several lateral ones, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in temperate climates and its ecological importance as a food source for various insects.
