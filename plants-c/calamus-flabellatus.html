Calamus flabellatus

Calamus flabellatus, commonly known as the fan calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and large, broad leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. It produces striking inflorescences consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Calamus flabellatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
