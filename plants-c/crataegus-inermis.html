Crataegus inermis

Crataegus inermis, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thornless stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its botanical features include compound leaves, a shallow root system, and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodlands, along stream banks, and in open areas with full sun to partial shade.
