Carex membranacea

Carex membranacea, commonly known as the membranous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, membranous sheath. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to brown in color.

Carex membranacea plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
