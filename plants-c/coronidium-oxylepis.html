Coronidium oxylepis

Coronidium oxylepis, commonly known as the red-flowered coral tree, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of branches, adding a dramatic visual impact to the landscape.
