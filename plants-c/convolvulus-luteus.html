Convolvulus luteus

Convolvulus luteus, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.
