Calamintha minae

Calamintha minae, commonly known as mountain germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calamintha minae is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
