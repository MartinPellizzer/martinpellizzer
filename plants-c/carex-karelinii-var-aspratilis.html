Carex karelinii var. aspratilis

Carex karelinii var. aspratilis, commonly known as the rough sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, ribbed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish or brownish in color.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and is often found in rocky or gravelly substrates.
