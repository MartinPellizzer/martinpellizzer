Caucalis siifolia

Caucalis siifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
