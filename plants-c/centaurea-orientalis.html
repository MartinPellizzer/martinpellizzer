Centaurea orientalis

Centaurea orientalis, commonly known as the oriental knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers with a distinctive purple center, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea orientalis is an annual or biennial plant that produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.
