Chamaecrista schlimii

Chamaecrista schlimii, commonly known as Schlim's burrlegume, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in dry, open habitats.

Chamaecrista schlimii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
