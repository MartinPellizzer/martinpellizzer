Campanula pyramidalis f. alba

Campanula pyramidalis f. alba, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, pyramidal growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
