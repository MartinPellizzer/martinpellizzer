Cleistanthus chartaceus

Cleistanthus chartaceus, commonly known as the paper mulberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
