Cerastium pumilum var. algeriense

Cerastium pumilum var. algeriense, commonly known as Algerian mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically hairy and oval in shape. The plant produces white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
