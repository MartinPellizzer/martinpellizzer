Crataegus alacris

Crataegus alacris, commonly known as the slender hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodlands, open fields, and along stream banks. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

Crataegus alacris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
