Cistus × purpureus

Cistus × purpureus, commonly known as the purple rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
