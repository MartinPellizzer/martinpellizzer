Campylostemon reticulatus var. turnerae

Campylostemon reticulatus var. turnerae, commonly known as Turnera's nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and covered in fine, stinging hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a distinctive net-like venation pattern. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks.
