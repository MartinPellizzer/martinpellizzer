Croton hispidus

Croton hispidus, commonly known as the rough croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and vibrant, colorful flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its presence in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
