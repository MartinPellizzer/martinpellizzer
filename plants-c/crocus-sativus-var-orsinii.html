Crocus sativus var. orsinii

Crocus sativus var. orsinii, commonly known as the Orsinii saffron crocus, is a botanical variant of the saffron crocus.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with prominent white markings. The plant produces narrow, pale yellow stigmas that are harvested for saffron spice. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential culinary use.
