Crotalaria sparsiflora

Crotalaria sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its seed pods are slender and contain several elliptical seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Crotalaria sparsiflora is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
