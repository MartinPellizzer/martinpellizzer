Carex paracuraica

Carex paracuraica, commonly known as the Curaica sedge, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.

Carex paracuraica is relatively low-maintenance, thriving in well-drained soil and partial to full shade.
