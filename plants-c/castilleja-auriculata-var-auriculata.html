Castilleja auriculata var. auriculata

Castilleja auriculata var. auriculata, commonly known as the golden cupid, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the western United States.

It has bright orange to reddish-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It forms symbiotic relationships with other plants, drawing nutrients through its root system.
