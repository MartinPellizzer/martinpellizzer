Centranthus calcitrapae subsp. clausonis

Centranthus calcitrapae subsp.

clausonis, commonly known as the rock centranthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in dry, sunny habitats.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
