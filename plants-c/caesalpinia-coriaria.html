Caesalpinia coriaria

Caesalpinia coriaria, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The botanical features of Caesalpinia coriaria make it both visually striking and ecologically valuable.
