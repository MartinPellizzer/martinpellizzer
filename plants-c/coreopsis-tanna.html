Coreopsis tanna

Coreopsis tanna, commonly known as the Tanna coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the island of Tanna in Vanuatu.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
