Combretum dinteri

Combretum dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a reddish or purple tint, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its use in traditional medicine.
