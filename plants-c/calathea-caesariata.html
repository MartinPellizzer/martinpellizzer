Calathea caesariata

Calathea caesariata, commonly known as the black nightshade plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety dark green leaves with intricate patterns of silver and white. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.
