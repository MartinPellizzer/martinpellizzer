Crotalaria wilczekiana

Crotalaria wilczekiana, commonly known as the Wilczek's crotalaria, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in well-drained soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, and the plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

However, it can be invasive in certain areas, posing a challenge to native plant communities.
