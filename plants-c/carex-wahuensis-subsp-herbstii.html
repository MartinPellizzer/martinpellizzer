Carex wahuensis subsp. herbstii

Carex wahuensis subsp.

herbstii, commonly known as the Herbst's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in maintaining soil stability in its native habitat.
