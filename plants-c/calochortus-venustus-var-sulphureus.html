Calochortus venustus var. sulphureus

Calochortus venustus var. sulphureus, commonly known as the golden cupcup, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to California.

This perennial herb features showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure, blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
