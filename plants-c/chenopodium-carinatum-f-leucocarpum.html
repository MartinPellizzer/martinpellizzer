Chenopodium carinatum f. leucocarpum

Chenopodium carinatum f. leucocarpum, commonly known as white-seeded goosefoot, is a variant of the annual plant Chenopodium carinatum.

It is characterized by its white, fleshy seeds that contrast with the typical green seeds of the species. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to regions of Central and South America. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features include palmately lobed leaves and a robust, branching stem.
