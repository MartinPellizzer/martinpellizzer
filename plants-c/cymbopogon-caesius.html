Cymbopogon caesius

Cymbopogon caesius, commonly known as the blue grass or blue oat grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and panicle-like inflorescence that produces small, pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Cymbopogon caesius is also valued for its potential in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
