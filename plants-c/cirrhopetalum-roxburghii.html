Cirrhopetalum roxburghii

Cirrhopetalum roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are characterized by their prominent, fringed petals that give the plant its scientific name.

Cirrhopetalum roxburghii is adapted to cold, mountainous environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
