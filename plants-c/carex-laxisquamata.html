Carex laxisquamata

Carex laxisquamata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often reddish-brown in color, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Carex laxisquamata is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
