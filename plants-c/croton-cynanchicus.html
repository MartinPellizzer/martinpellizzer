Croton cynanchicus

Croton cynanchicus, commonly known as the Indian croton, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that vary in hue from green to red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Croton cynanchicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
