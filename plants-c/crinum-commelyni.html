Crinum commelyni

Crinum commelyni, commonly known as the African lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. The plant produces tall, erect stems that bear clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant during dry periods.

Crinum commelyni is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
