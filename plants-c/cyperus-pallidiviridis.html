Cyperus pallidiviridis

Cyperus pallidiviridis, commonly known as the pale green flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are typically pale green in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.

Cyperus pallidiviridis is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.
