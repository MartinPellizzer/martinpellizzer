Cotoneaster rugosus

Cotoneaster rugosus, commonly known as the rough cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Cotoneaster rugosus is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and seasonal color changes.
