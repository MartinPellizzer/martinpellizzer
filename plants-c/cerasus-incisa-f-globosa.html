Cerasus incisa f. globosa

Cerasus incisa f. globosa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
