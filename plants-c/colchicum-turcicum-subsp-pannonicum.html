Colchicum turcicum subsp. pannonicum

Colchicum turcicum subsp.

pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian colchic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces bulbous corms, which are underground storage organs used for survival during adverse conditions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and form a basal rosette.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.
