Cactus leucodictius

Cactus leucodictius, commonly known as the white-pearled cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide insulation and protection from predators. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb water quickly after rare rains.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in desert gardens for its ornamental value.
