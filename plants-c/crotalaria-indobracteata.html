Crotalaria indobracteata

Crotalaria indobracteata, commonly known as the Indian crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain agricultural contexts.

However, it can also be considered a weed due to its rapid growth and spread.
