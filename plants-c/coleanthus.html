Coleanthus

Coleanthus, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a composite head.

Coleanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
