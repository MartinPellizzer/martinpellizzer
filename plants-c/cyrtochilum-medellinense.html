Cyrtochilum medellinense

Cyrtochilum medellinense, commonly known as the Medellin orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow to white flowers with distinctive spurred petals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Cyrtochilum medellinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
