Carex chinganensis

Carex chinganensis, commonly known as the Chingan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.

The botanical structure of Carex chinganensis reflects its adaptation to wetland ecosystems, with specialized root systems and leaf morphology that enhance its survival in waterlogged soils.
