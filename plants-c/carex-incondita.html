Carex incondita

Carex incondita, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are typically inconspicuous and clustered in dense spikes.

Carex incondita plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
