Caprifolium ciliatum

Caprifolium ciliatum, commonly known as the silky honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are followed by small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Caprifolium ciliatum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forested areas.
