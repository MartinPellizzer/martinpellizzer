Cytisus persicus

Cytisus persicus, commonly known as Persian broom, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces long, cylindrical pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Cytisus persicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
