Campylotropis macrocarpa f. giraldii

Campylotropis macrocarpa f. giraldii, commonly known as the Giraldii Campylotropis, is a flowering plant species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces distinctive, elongated pods that contain several seeds. Native to parts of Asia, this plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
