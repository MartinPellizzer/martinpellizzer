Crocanthemum brasiliense

Crocanthemum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian crocanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests, showcasing its adaptability to humid environments. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Crocanthemum brasiliense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
