Cassebeeria grandiflora

Cassebeeria grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cassia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
