Cracca macrantha

Cracca macrantha, commonly known as the giant cracca, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Cracca macrantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
