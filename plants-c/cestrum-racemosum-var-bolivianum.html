Cestrum racemosum var. bolivianum

Cestrum racemosum var. bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy appearance.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dark seeds.
