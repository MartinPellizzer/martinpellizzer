Cyathocline purpurea var. bicolor

Cyathocline purpurea var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically purple or pink, with a distinctive cup-like structure formed by its bracts. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
