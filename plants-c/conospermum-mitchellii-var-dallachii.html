Conospermum mitchellii var. dallachii

Conospermum mitchellii var. dallachii, commonly known as the Mitchell's conospermum, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to sandy soils and salt spray. It produces woody capsules that contain small, round seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
