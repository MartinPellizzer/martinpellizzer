Clompanus parvifolia

Clompanus parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved clompanus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and delicate, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, and the stems are somewhat succulent.

Clompanus parvifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
