Cardaria macrocarpa

Cardaria macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble bladders, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue.

Cardaria macrocarpa is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments.
