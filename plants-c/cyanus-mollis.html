Cyanus mollis

Cyanus mollis, commonly known as the blueweed or blue cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, often forming a central disk surrounded by ray florets. Cyanus mollis typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
