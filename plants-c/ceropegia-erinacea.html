Ceropegia erinacea

Ceropegia erinacea, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Ceropegia erinacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
