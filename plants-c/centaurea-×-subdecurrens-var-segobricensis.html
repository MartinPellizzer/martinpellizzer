Centaurea × subdecurrens var. segobricensis

Centaurea × subdecurrens var. segobricensis, commonly known as the Segobricense Centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers with a distinctive purple center. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
