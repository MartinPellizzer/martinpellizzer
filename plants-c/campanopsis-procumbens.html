Campanopsis procumbens

Campanopsis procumbens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanopsis procumbens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground covers.
