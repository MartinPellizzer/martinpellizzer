Calamus thwaitesii var. canaranus

Calamus thwaitesii var. canaranus, commonly known as the Canara calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a tall, slender palm-like plant characterized by its long, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. It is native to the tropical regions of South Asia, including India and Sri Lanka.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in traditional practices.
