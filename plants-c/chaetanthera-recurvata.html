Chaetanthera recurvata

Chaetanthera recurvata, commonly known as the curlyhead nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, recurved leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the summer. Its berries are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant of both botanical interest and caution.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and research settings.
