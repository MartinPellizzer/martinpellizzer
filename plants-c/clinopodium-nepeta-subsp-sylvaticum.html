Clinopodium nepeta subsp. sylvaticum

Clinopodium nepeta subsp.

sylvaticum, commonly known as wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic when crushed, and it produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers in whorled clusters. It is part of the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

This subspecies is closely related to the common catnip, sharing similar chemical compositions and ecological roles.
