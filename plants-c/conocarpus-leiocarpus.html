Conocarpus leiocarpus

Conocarpus leiocarpus, commonly known as the buttonwood, is a tropical tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and smooth, light-gray bark. The plant has small, inconspicuous leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in spikes, followed by small, round fruits that are typically green when mature.

The tree is well adapted to coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.
