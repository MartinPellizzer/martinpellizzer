Cirsium gmelinii

Cirsium gmelinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a compact head.

Cirsium gmelinii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
