Cucumis melo subsp. rigidus

Cucumis melo subsp.

rigidus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a subspecies of the melon genus native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its elongated, spiny fruits that are typically harvested when immature and still green.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are large, broad, and lobed, while its stems are hairy and often trailing.

This subspecies is valued for its unique flavor and is commonly cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
