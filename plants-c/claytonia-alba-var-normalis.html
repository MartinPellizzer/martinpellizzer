Claytonia alba var. normalis

Claytonia alba var. normalis, commonly known as the white mountain beauty, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features rosettes of spoon-shaped, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to shade and high humidity.
