Crescentia nigripes

Crescentia nigripes, commonly known as the Jamaican apple or custard apple, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a small, evergreen tree with smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is large, with a thick, waxy skin that turns from green to yellow as it ripens. Inside, it contains a creamy, aromatic pulp surrounded by numerous small, hard seeds.

This plant is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for both its culinary and ornamental qualities.
