Cyathea saparuensis

Cyathea saparuensis, commonly known as the Saparuensis tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a significant role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.

The fronds are typically evergreen and can remain on the plant for several years before being replaced.
