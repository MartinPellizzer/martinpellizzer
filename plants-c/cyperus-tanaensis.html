Cyperus tanaensis

Cyperus tanaensis, commonly known as the Tana flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly Tanzania and Kenya, and thrives in wetland environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It is often found in marshy areas and along the edges of lakes and rivers.

Due to its adaptability to wet conditions, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
