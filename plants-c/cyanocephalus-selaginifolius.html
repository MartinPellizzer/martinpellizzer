Cyanocephalus selaginifolius

Cyanocephalus selaginifolius, commonly known as the blue-headed cactus, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a compact, rounded stem with a bluish-green hue, and its leaves are small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Cyanocephalus selaginifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
