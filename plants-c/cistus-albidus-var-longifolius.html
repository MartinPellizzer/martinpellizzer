Cistus albidus var. longifolius

Cistus albidus var. longifolius, commonly known as the white rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
