Chryseis fumariifolia

Chryseis fumariifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Chryseis fumariifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
