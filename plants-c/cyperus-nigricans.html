Cyperus nigricans

Cyperus nigricans, commonly known as black flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often brown or black in color. Cyperus nigricans is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
