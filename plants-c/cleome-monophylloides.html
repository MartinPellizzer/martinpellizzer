Cleome monophylloides

Cleome monophylloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family, which includes several species of flowering plants commonly referred to as "spider flowers.". The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Cleome monophylloides is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
