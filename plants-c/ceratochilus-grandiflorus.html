Ceratochilus grandiflorus

Ceratochilus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered helmet orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and produces showy, helmet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically blooms in spring, with intricate floral structures that feature a prominent lip and spurred petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
