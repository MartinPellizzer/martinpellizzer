Caesalpinia flavicans

Caesalpinia flavicans, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its pods are elongated, woody, and often covered in short hairs. Caesalpinia flavicans is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
