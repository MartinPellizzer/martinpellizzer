Calea peruviana

Calea peruviana, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that open at night. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its unique nocturnal blooming pattern makes it a fascinating subject for botanists and horticulturists.

Calea peruviana is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
