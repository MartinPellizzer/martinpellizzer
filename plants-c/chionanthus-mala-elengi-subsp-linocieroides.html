Chionanthus mala-elengi subsp. linocieroides

Chionanthus mala-elengi subsp.

linocieroides, commonly known as the silky chionanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
