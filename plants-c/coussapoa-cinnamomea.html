Coussapoa cinnamomea

Coussapoa cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark that resembles cinnamon, hence its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The tree is valued for its bark, which has been used traditionally in medicine and as a spice.
