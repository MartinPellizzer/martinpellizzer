Crepis platyphylla

Crepis platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features basal rosettes of broad, silver-gray leaves and produces small, yellowish flower heads. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single inflorescence at the end of a slender stem.

Crepis platyphylla is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
