Caesalpinia trichocarpa

Caesalpinia trichocarpa, commonly known as the African bristlecone or firethorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often feathery and arranged in pairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by long, bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Caesalpinia trichocarpa is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
