Carduus platypus f. major

Carduus platypus f. major, commonly known as the greater thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, flowering stem that bears purple to pinkish-blue composite flowers. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types. It typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant's latex is milky and can cause skin irritation, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
