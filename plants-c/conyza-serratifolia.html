Conyza serratifolia

Conyza serratifolia, commonly known as the serratedleaf weed, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Conyza serratifolia is considered an invasive species in some areas, posing challenges to agricultural and natural ecosystems.
