Chenopodium glaucum var. rubrum

Chenopodium glaucum var. rubrum, commonly known as red goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically red or purple in color, especially on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are encased in a hard, triangular capsule. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in various culinary traditions.
