Cenchrus biflorus

Cenchrus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear two distinct flower clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it is often used in erosion control due to its hardy nature.

Cenchrus biflorus is also valued in some regions for forage and as a component in traditional landscaping.
