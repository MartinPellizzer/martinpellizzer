Caragana kirghisorum

Caragana kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Caragana kirghisorum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
