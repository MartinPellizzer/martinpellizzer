Convolvulus fruticans

Convolvulus fruticans, commonly known as the desert bindweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a trailing or prostrate shrub with small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically clustered in umbels. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water.

Convolvulus fruticans is often found in rocky soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
