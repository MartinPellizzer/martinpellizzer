Centaurea germaniciae

Centaurea germaniciae, commonly known as Germanic centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces slender, spiky seed heads that contain numerous tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal. It prefers well-drained soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Centaurea germaniciae is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
