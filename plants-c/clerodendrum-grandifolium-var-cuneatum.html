Clerodendrum grandifolium var. cuneatum

Clerodendrum grandifolium var. cuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a prominent midrib. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
