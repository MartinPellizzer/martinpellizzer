Crocus chrysanthus var. bornmuelleri

Crocus chrysanthus var. bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking purple or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces showy, cup-shaped blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors and early blooming habit.
