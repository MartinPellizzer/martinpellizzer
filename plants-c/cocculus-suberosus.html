Cocculus suberosus

Cocculus suberosus, commonly known as the rough cocculus, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by distinctive, spiny pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
