Calophyllum obliquinervium

Calophyllum obliquinervium, commonly known as the oblique-veined sandarac tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves with oblique veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing a single seed.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its hard, durable wood.
