Campuloclinium viridiflorum

Campuloclinium viridiflorum, commonly known as the greenflower bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom in the spring.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
