Calamintha alpina subsp. baumgartenii

Calamintha alpina subsp.

baumgartenii, commonly known as alpine calamint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, fragrant blue or violet flowers in terminal clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine. Calamintha alpina subsp.

baumgartenii is an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
