Crassula alata subsp. alata

Crassula alata subsp.

alata, commonly known as the Christmas rose or jade plant, is a succulent native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple markings, and it produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. The plant is known for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
