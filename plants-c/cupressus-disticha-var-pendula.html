Cupressus disticha var. pendula

Cupressus disticha var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cedar, is a cultivar of the eastern red cedar.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that hang in a weeping fashion, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are blue-green in color, and it produces small, spherical cones. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes due to its unique form and hardiness.

This variety is well-suited to a variety of soil types and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
