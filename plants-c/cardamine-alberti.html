Cardamine alberti

Cardamine alberti, commonly known as Albert's watercress, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils.

The species is native to parts of Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often as an ornamental or for ecological restoration.
