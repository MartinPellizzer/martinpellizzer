Cerasus tridentina

Cerasus tridentina, commonly known as the three-lobed cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.

Cerasus tridentina is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
