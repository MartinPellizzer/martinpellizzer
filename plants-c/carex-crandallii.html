Carex crandallii

Carex crandallii, commonly known as Crandall's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. The plant produces tall, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are grass-like, typically dark green and stiff, with a prominent midrib.

Carex crandallii is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
