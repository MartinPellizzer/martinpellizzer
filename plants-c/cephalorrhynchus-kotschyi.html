Cephalorrhynchus kotschyi

Cephalorrhynchus kotschyi, commonly known as the mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its foliage is fragrant and often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Cephalorrhynchus kotschyi thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
