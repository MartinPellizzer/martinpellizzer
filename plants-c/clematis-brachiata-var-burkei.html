Clematis brachiata var. burkei

Clematis brachiata var. burkei, commonly known as the Burkes' clematis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a distinctive red or purple base. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beautiful blooms.
