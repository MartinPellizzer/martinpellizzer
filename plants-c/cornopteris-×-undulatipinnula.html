Cornopteris × undulatipinnula

Cornopteris × undulatipinnula, commonly known as the undulating fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cornopteris mackayeana and Cornopteris plicata.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating fronds that give it a unique, wave-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a finely divided structure, contributing to its ornamental value.

This fern is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
