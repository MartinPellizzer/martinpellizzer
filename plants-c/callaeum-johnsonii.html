Callaeum johnsonii

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is pollinated primarily by specific species of beetles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Callaeum johnsonii is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
