Curcuma macrochlamys

Curcuma macrochlamys, commonly known as the giant turmeric or broad-leaved turmeric, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking orange to red flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences at the top of the stems. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other curcuma species.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
