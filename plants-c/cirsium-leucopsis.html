Cirsium leucopsis

Cirsium leucopsis, commonly known as the whitehead thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cirsium leucopsis is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
