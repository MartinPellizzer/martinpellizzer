Calophyllum vernicosum

Calophyllum vernicosum, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing sticky seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
