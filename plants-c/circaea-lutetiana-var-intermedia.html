Circaea lutetiana var. intermedia

Circaea lutetiana var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features delicate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green coloration. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural gardens.
