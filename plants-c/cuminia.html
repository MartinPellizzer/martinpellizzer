Cuminia

Cuminia, commonly known as cumin, is an annual herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into oval, ribbed seeds. Cuminia thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic seeds used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Its botanical name, Cuminum cyminum, reflects its historical significance in ancient civilizations.
