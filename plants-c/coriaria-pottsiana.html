Coriaria pottsiana

Coriaria pottsiana, commonly known as the Potts' coriander, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Coriariaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and its leaves are typically alternate and ovate in shape. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
