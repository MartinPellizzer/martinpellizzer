Centaurea scabiosa var. coriacea

Centaurea scabiosa var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough knapweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, purple to pinkish-red composite flowers. The plant features rough, hairy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological landscaping.
