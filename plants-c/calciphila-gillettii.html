Calciphila gillettii

Calciphila gillettii, commonly known as the Gillett's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. This species thrives in limestone soils, showcasing its calciphile nature, which means it prefers calcium-rich environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, adding visual interest to its habitat.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often found in rocky outcrops and dry slopes.
