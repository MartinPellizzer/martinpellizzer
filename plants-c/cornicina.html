Cornicina

Cornicina, commonly known as the "flowering cornflower," is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its vibrant blue or purple petals. The plant has a slender, upright stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Cornicina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

It also has historical uses in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
