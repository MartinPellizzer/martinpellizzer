Citrus × aurantium var. limoniformis

Citrus × aurantium var. limoniformis, commonly known as the lemon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces oval-shaped fruits with a thick, yellow rind and juicy, acidic yellow pulp. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

The plant is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary, medicinal, and cosmetic applications.
