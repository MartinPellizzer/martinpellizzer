Chonemorpha megacalyx

Chonemorpha megacalyx, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant's flowers are showy, with a distinctive purple color and a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators. Its roots are anchored in the sediment at the bottom of still water bodies, and it plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in water gardens and conservation efforts.
