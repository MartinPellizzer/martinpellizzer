Cerasus rubicunda

Cerasus rubicunda, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Cerasus rubicunda is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in Japanese traditions.
