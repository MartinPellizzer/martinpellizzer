Cerasus reflexa

Cerasus reflexa, commonly known as the sour cherry or wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits that are typically tart and used in cooking or winemaking. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth with a reddish-brown color.

Cerasus reflexa is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
