Citrus × aurantium subsp. sinensis

Citrus × aurantium subsp.

sinensis, commonly known as the mandarin orange, is a citrus fruit native to East Asia. It is characterized by its small, sweet, and segmented orange fruits with a thin, easy-to-peel rind.

The plant is a small evergreen tree or shrub with opposite, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely cultivated for both consumption and commercial use.

The subspecies is closely related to other citrus species and is often used in hybridization to improve fruit quality.
