Chione venosa var. venosa

Chione venosa var. venosa, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive nodding flowers that bloom in early spring, typically before the leaves emerge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Chione venosa var. venosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and early blooming habit.
