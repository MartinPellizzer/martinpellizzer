Calophyllum obovale

Calophyllum obovale, commonly known as the oval calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright orange or red fruits. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and contains several seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
