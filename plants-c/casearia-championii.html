Casearia championii

Casearia championii, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking purple to black berries that are edible for humans and birds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Casearia championii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
