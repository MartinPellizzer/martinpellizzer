Chamissoa margaritacea

Chamissoa margaritacea, commonly known as the silverleaf chamissoa, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Chamissoa margaritacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
