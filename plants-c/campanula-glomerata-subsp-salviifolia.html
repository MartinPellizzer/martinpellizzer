Campanula glomerata subsp. salviifolia

Campanula glomerata subsp.

salviifolia, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
