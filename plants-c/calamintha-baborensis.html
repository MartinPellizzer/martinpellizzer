Calamintha baborensis

Calamintha baborensis, commonly known as Baborensis germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Calamintha baborensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming properties.
