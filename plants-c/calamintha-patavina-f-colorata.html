Calamintha patavina f. colorata

Calamintha patavina f. colorata, commonly known as the pink-flowered calamint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and delicate floral display.
