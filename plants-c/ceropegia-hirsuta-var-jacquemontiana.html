Ceropegia hirsuta var. jacquemontiana

Ceropegia hirsuta var. jacquemontiana, commonly known as the Jacob's ladder, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features wiry stems that twine around supports and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers with a distinctive hooded structure. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy pubescence. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

This plant is prized for its ornamental value and ability to climb, adding vertical interest to gardens or containers.
