Chaetochloa poiretiana

Chaetochloa poiretiana, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it forms dense mats. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Chaetochloa poiretiana is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to moist soils.
