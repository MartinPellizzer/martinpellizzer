Cyathea tungurahuae

Cyathea tungurahuae, commonly known as the Tungurahua tree fern, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant is typically found in humid, shaded environments, where it thrives in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to the dense foliage typical of its habitat.

Cyathea tungurahuae plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystem, providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
