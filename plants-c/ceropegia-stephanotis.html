Ceropegia stephanotis

Ceropegia stephanotis, commonly known as the silver leaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a unique structure that resembles a narrow tube. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate watering, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Ceropegia stephanotis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
