Cacalia cusimbua

Cacalia cusimbua, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cacalia cusimbua thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
