Calonema demissum

Calonema demissum, commonly known as the creeping calonema, is a species of flowering plant in the family Gesneriaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, lance-shaped leaves. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
