Calamintha graveolens var. exigua

Calamintha graveolens var. exigua, commonly known as narrowleaf catnip, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that emit a strong mint-like scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular blue or violet flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variant is closely related to other members of the Lamiaceae family, sharing similar growth habits and chemical compositions.
