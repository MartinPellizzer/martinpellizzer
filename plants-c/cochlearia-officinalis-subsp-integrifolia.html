Cochlearia officinalis subsp. integrifolia

Cochlearia officinalis subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved winter cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent scent.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands. Its leaves are smooth and lobed, and it produces capsules that contain numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
