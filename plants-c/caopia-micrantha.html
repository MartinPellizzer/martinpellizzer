Caopia micrantha

Caopia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered caopia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually greenish-white in color.

Caopia micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
