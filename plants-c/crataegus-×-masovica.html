Crataegus × masovica

Crataegus × masovica, commonly known as the Masovian hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus oxyacantha.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters. The plant features thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, attracting various birds and wildlife.

The species is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
