Commiphora glandulosa

Commiphora glandulosa, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and has leathery, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The sap of Commiphora glandulosa has been historically used for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
