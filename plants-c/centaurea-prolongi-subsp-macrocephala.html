Centaurea prolongi subsp. macrocephala

Centaurea prolongi subsp.

macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed centaury, is a flowering plant characterized by its striking, showy blue to purple flower heads. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is native to the Mediterranean region, where it thrives in rocky and dry environments.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces a single, large composite flower head composed of many small florets. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant colors and drought tolerance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger flower heads compared to other centaury species.
