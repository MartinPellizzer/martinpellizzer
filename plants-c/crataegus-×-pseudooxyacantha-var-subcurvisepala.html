Crataegus × pseudooxyacantha var. subcurvisepala

Crataegus × pseudooxyacantha var. subcurvisepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often slightly curved at the edges. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
