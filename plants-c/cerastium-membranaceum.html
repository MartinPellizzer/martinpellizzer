Cerastium membranaceum

Cerastium membranaceum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, commonly known as the carnation family. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
