Cinnamomum donii

Cinnamomum donii, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum aromaticum, is a species of flowering plant in the laurel family.

It is native to southern China, Vietnam, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to true cinnamon.

The botanical features include glossy, elliptical leaves and a woody stem with a fragrant scent.
