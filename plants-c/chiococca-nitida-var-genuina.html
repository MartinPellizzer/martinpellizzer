Chiococca nitida var. genuina

Chiococca nitida var. genuina, commonly known as the white-flowered chiococca, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, dark green fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
