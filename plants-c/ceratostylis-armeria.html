Ceratostylis armeria

Ceratostylis armeria, commonly known as the white orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and often emit a subtle fragrance.
