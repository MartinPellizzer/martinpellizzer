Convolvulus pseudosiculus var. multiflorus

Convolvulus pseudosiculus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the multibract convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems that often bear small, leaf-like bracts. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
