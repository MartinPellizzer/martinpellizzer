Chlidanthus × traubii f. oblongitepalus

Chlidanthus × traubii f. oblongitepalus, commonly known as the oblong-leafed hybrid hibiscus, is a unique cultivar within the Malvaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Chlidanthus × traubii and Chlidanthus oblongifolius, characterized by its large, showy flowers and broad, waxy leaves. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
