Convolvulus sabatius subsp. mauritanicus

Convolvulus sabatius subsp.

mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritanian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its fibrous roots allow it to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
