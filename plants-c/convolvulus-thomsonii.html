Convolvulus thomsonii

Convolvulus thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features slender, twining stems and small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while its fruits are dry, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Thomson's bindweed is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
