Convolvulus arvensis var. sagittatus

Convolvulus arvensis var. sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowhead morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It is a herbaceous annual that often grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it an invasive species in many areas.
