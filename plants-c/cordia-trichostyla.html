Cordia trichostyla

Cordia trichostyla, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Cordia trichostyla is valued for its durable wood, which has been traditionally used in construction and craftsmanship.
