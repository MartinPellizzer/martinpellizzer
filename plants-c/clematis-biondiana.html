Clematis biondiana

Clematis biondiana, commonly known as the golden clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis biondiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its graceful appearance.
