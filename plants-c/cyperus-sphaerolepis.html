Cyperus sphaerolepis

Cyperus sphaerolepis, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves. Its flowering spikes are typically clustered at the top of the plant, producing tiny, three-lobed flowers.

Cyperus sphaerolepis is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
