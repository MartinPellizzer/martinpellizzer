Cyathea camerunensis

Cyathea camerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to central and western Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its upright growth.

Cyathea camerunensis plays a significant role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
