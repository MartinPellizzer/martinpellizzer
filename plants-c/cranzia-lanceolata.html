Cranzia lanceolata

Cranzia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf cranza, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are often pink or red, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Cranza lanceolata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
