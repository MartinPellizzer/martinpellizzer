Craspedostoma cephalotes

Craspedostoma cephalotes, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking hood-like structure formed by its fused petals and sepals. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Its flowers are often fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and moths.
