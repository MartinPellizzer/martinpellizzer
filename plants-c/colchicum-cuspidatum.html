Colchicum cuspidatum

Colchicum cuspidatum, commonly known as the spear-leaved colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It produces distinctive, spurred flowers that are adapted for pollination by insects.

Colchicum cuspidatum is also notable for containing colchicine, a compound with medicinal and toxic properties.
