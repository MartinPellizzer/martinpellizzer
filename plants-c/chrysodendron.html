Chrysodendron

Chrysodendron, commonly known as the flame tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Bignoniaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, bright orange or red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The leaves are typically opposite and simple, with a rough texture and a strong scent. Chrysodendron species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, South America, and Southeast Asia.

They thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making them popular ornamental plants in warm climates.
