Cirsium canovirens

Cirsium canovirens, commonly known as the Canary Island thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, rough leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Cirsium canovirens is a hardy species that is well adapted to the harsh climate of its native habitat.
