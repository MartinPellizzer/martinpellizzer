Chlorophytum amplexicaule

Chlorophytum amplexicaule, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features strap-like, grassy leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which are clustered in a loose inflorescence. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.
