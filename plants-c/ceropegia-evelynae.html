Ceropegia evelynae

Ceropegia evelynae, commonly known as the "Evelyn's trumpet vine," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia evelynae is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
