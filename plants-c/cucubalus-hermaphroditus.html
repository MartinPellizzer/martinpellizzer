Cucubalus hermaphroditus

Cucubalus hermaphroditus, commonly known as the African cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, lobed leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, a trait known as monoecious.

The fruit is a thick-walled, spiny capsule that contains edible seeds.
