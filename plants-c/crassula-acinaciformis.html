Crassula acinaciformis

Crassula acinaciformis, commonly known as the cannonball plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
