Cryptocoryne sulphurea

Cryptocoryne sulphurea, commonly known as the golden cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, and it thrives in moderate to high light conditions. It is often cultivated in freshwater aquariums due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Cryptocoryne sulphurea is also known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of water parameters, making it a popular choice among aquarists.
