Cyperus nyasensis

Cyperus nyasensis, commonly known as the Nyasae Cyperus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or purple in color, arranged in spikelets. Cyperus nyasensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
