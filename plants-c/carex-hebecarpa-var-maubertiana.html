Carex hebecarpa var. maubertiana

Carex hebecarpa var. maubertiana, commonly known as the Maubert's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is typically found in wet to moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
