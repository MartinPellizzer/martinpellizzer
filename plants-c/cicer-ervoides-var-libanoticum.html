Cicer ervoides var. libanoticum

Cicer ervoides var. libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
