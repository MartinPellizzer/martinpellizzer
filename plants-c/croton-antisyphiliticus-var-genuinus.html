Croton antisyphiliticus var. genuinus

Croton antisyphiliticus var. genuinus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with prominent veins and a rough texture. It produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers, followed by woody, spiky capsules containing seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it may be invasive in some regions.
