Calamagrostis deschampsioides

Calamagrostis deschampsioides, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tall, slender inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive blue-gray hue that adds ornamental value to landscapes.

Calamagrostis deschampsioides is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
