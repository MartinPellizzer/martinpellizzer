Calytrix scabra

Calytrix scabra, commonly known as the rough calytrix, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Calytrix scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and seasonal flowering.
