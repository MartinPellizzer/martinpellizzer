Clarkia amoena var. caurina

Clarkia amoena var. caurina, commonly known as the Sierra clarkia, is a flowering plant native to California and adjacent regions of the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.

Clarkia amoena var. caurina is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
