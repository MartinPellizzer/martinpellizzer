Calamagrostis fusca

Calamagrostis fusca, commonly known as the blackgrass reed, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, streambanks, and wet meadows. It produces panicles of small, pale green to brownish flowers in late summer to early autumn.

Calamagrostis fusca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
