Clusia comans

Clusia comans, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Clusia comans is also known for its resilience to drought and air pollution, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
