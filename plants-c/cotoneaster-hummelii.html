Cotoneaster hummelii

Cotoneaster hummelii, commonly known as the Hume's cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, arching branches and dense clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are attractive to birds, contributing to its ecological value. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and turn yellow in autumn, adding ornamental interest.

Cotoneaster hummelii is often cultivated in gardens for its winter interest and adaptability to various soil types.
