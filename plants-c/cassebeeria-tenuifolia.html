Cassebeeria tenuifolia

Cassebeeria tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and attract various pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in slender, fibrous pods that mature and disperse in dry conditions.
