Cardamine paucisecta

Cardamine paucisecta, commonly known as the few-segmented watercress, is a delicate herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or wetland habitats, often along streams or in forest floors. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and elongated.

The flowers are pale yellow to white, and the plant produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.
