Carex drymophila var. glabrescens

Carex drymophila var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. This variety is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescence. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
