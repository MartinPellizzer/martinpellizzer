Chelyorchis pardoi

Chelyorchis pardoi, commonly known as the golden twayblade, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant has a slender, leafless stem that bears two lance-shaped leaves near the base. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive, spurred lip that attracts pollinators like bees and flies.

Chelyorchis pardoi is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
