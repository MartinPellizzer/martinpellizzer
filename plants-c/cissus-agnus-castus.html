Cissus agnus-castus

Cissus agnus-castus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or holy brier, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, red, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Cissus agnus-castus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
