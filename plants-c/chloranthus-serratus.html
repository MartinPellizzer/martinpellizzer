Chloranthus serratus

Chloranthus serratus, commonly known as the serrated chloranthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as China, Japan, and the Philippines. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are characterized by their serrated margins and are alternate along the stem.

The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in umbels, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
