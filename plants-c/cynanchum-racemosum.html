Cynanchum racemosum

Cynanchum racemosum, commonly known as the racemose milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes many species of milkweeds. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Cynanchum racemosum produces a milky sap that is toxic, containing compounds that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
