Cirsium vittatum

Cirsium vittatum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along stream banks. Its showy purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cirsium vittatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
