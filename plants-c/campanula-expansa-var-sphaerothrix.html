Campanula expansa var. sphaerothrix

Campanula expansa var. sphaerothrix, commonly known as the globe bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
