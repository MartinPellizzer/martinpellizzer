Clematis spooneri var. subglabra

Clematis spooneri var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth clematis, is a climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets that are often hairy but become smoother in this variety. The flowers are typically blue or violet, with five petals and prominent stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to climb trellises or fences.
