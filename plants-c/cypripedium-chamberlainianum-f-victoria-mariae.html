Cypripedium chamberlainianum f. victoria-mariae

Cypripedium chamberlainianum f. victoria-mariae, commonly known as the Victoria-Mariae lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high-altitude regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a unique feature among orchids. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often in shaded forest areas with rich, humus-filled soil. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and its role in the study of orchid evolution and conservation.

This orchid is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts and botanic gardens.
