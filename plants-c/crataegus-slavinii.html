Crataegus slavinii

Crataegus slavinii, commonly known as Slavin's hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.
