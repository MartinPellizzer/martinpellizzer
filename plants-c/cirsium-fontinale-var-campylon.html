Cirsium fontinale var. campylon

Cirsium fontinale var. campylon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features spiny, upright stems and large, blue to purple flowering heads that bloom in late summer. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped, with rough, hairy surfaces and sharply toothed margins. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
