Carduus carpetanus subsp. carpetanus

Carduus carpetanus subsp.

carpetanus, commonly known as the carpet thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in dense, spherical clusters, known as heads, which are surrounded by bracts.

The plant is characterized by its robust growth and is often found in open habitats where it can thrive with minimal care.
