Cosmidium × burridgeanum

Cosmidium × burridgeanum, commonly known as the Burridge's water clover, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, often forming a dense mat. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, preferring moderate to high light conditions. Its reproductive system involves the production of small, greenish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.

Cosmidium × burridgeanum is often cultivated in ornamental ponds and aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
