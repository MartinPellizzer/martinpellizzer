Carex melananthiformis

Carex melananthiformis, commonly known as black sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and dark green, with a distinctive waxy coating.

Carex melananthiformis is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
