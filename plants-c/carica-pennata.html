Carica pennata

Carica pennata, commonly known as the featherleaf papaya, is a tropical plant belonging to the Caricaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive feather-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits similar to the common papaya, though it is less commercially cultivated. Carica pennata thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in botanical gardens and horticultural studies.
