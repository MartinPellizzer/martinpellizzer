Cynoglossum baicalense

Cynoglossum baicalense, commonly known as the Baikal bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
