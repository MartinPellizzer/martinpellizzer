Corydalis solida subsp. tauricola

Corydalis solida subsp.

tauricola, commonly known as the Tauric Corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features delicate, slender stems and clusters of small, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
