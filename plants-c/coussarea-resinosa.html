Coussarea resinosa

Coussarea resinosa, commonly known as the resinous coussarea, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its resinous sap has been historically valued for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
