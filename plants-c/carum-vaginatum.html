Carum vaginatum

Carum vaginatum, commonly known as the broadleaf caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a mild, carroty scent.

The fruit, known as a schizocarp, consists of two mericarps that split apart when mature.
