Camellia azalea

Camellia azalea, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Theaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan and China, and is valued for its showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter and early spring. The plant features broad, glossy evergreen leaves and produces clusters of waxy, pink or white flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Camellia azalea is also cultivated for its ornamental value and is often used in bonsai art.
