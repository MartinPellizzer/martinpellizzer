Cupressus arizonica var. bonita

Cupressus arizonica var. bonita, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a compact evergreen conifer native to the southwestern United States.

It features scale-like leaves that are densely packed on short shoots, giving it a distinctive, dense appearance. The plant produces small, round cones that mature to a dark reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its slow growth rate and pyramidal shape contribute to its aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
