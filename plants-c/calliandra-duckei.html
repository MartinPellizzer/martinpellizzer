Calliandra duckei

Calliandra duckei, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and showy, pink or red flower clusters. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in warm climates. It has a shallow root system and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for landscaping in arid environments.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
