Cochlearia officinalis var. gallicica

Cochlearia officinalis var. gallicica, commonly known as the French cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to Europe, particularly France and the British Isles.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sand dunes, and calcareous soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale purple to white in color.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
