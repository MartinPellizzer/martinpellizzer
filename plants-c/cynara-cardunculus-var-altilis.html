Cynara cardunculus var. altilis

Cynara cardunculus var. altilis, commonly known as the giant cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing up to 2-3 meters in height.

The giant cardoon is cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in cooking and fiber production.
