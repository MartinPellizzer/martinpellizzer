Carex crinigera

Carex crinigera, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features dense clusters of grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and somewhat silky to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like structures, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Carex crinigera is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity in its native habitat.
