Chamaecytisus albus subsp. obscurus

Chamaecytisus albus subsp.

obscurus, commonly known as the dark scurfy broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its greyish, scurfy stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has aromatic, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
