Claoxylon tsaratananae

Claoxylon tsaratananae, commonly known as the Tsaratanana tree, is a unique species native to Madagascar.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and dense foliage. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

This plant is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat and plays an important role in the local ecosystem.
