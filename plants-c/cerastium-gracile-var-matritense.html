Cerastium gracile var. matritense

Cerastium gracile var. matritense, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
