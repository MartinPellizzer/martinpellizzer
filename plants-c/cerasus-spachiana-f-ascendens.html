Cerasus spachiana f. ascendens

Cerasus spachiana f. ascendens, commonly known as the ascending cherry, is a botanical variant of the species Cerasus spachiana.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is typically found in mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in jams. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated margin, and it has a compact, shrub-like structure.

This form is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential for cultivation.
