Cirsium taquetii

Cirsium taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Cirsium taquetii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its striking appearance.
