Centaurea laconica subsp. laconica

Centaurea laconica subsp.

laconica, commonly known as the Laconic thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

The plant features striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually spiny and gray-green, providing a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
