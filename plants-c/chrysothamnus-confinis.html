Chrysothamnus confinis

Chrysothamnus confinis, commonly known as the desert gumplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's woody stems and hairy foliage contribute to its resilience in harsh desert conditions.
