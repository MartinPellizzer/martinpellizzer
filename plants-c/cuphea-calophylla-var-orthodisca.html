Cuphea calophylla var. orthodisca

Cuphea calophylla var. orthodisca, commonly known as the red hot poker or firecracker plant, is a striking member of the Lythraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and features showy, tubular red bracts that surround small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which can last from summer through fall.
