Canna musifolia

Canna musifolia, commonly known as the banana cannas, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant belongs to the Cannaceae family and is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and floral display. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna musifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate moist soils, making it a popular choice for water gardens and pond edges.
