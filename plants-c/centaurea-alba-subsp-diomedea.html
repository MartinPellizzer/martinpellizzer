Centaurea alba subsp. diomedea

Centaurea alba subsp.

diomedea, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
