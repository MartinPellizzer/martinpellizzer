Cynara sennenii

Cynara sennenii, commonly known as the Senno's artichoke, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant produces large, edible leaves that are often used in traditional Mediterranean cuisine. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Cynara sennenii thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy choice for garden cultivation.
