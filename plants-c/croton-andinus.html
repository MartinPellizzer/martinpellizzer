Croton andinus

Croton andinus, commonly known as the Andean croton, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in complex inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

However, it is important to note that some parts of the plant can be toxic if ingested.
