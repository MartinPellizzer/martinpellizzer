Cineraria scordifolia

Cineraria scordifolia, commonly known as the bitter cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flower heads. The plant has hairy, grayish leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Cineraria scordifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
