Cruciata laevipes var. brutia

Cruciata laevipes var. brutia, commonly known as the Brazilian crucifix flower, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble a cross, with five white petals and a prominent central stamen. Native to Brazil, this plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant can grow up to 3 meters in height.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
