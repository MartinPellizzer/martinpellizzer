Cabralea cauliflora

Cabralea cauliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cabralea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom directly on the trunk and branches, a phenomenon known as cauliflorous flowering. The plant has large, glossy, dark green leaves that provide dense shade. Its fruits are woody pods containing bright red seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
