Carex vesiculosa var. pallida

Carex vesiculosa var. pallida, commonly known as the pale bladder sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to white in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
