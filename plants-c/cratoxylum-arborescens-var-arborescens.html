Cratoxylum arborescens var. arborescens

Cratoxylum arborescens var. arborescens, commonly known as the hairy tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes.
