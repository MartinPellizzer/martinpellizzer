Cucurbita macrocarpa

Cucurbita macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a sprawling vine that produces large, spherical fruits with thick, smooth, and often ribbed rinds. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that are typically separate from the fruiting structures. Its fruit can weigh several hundred pounds and is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season to reach its full potential.
