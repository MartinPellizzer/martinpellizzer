Cactus epidendrum

Cactus epidendrum, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented stems that are often adorned with spines. The plant produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the winter months. Its leaves are usually arranged in a zigzag pattern, and it thrives in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.

The Christmas cactus is popular as a decorative plant due to its long-lasting flowers and adaptability to indoor environments.
