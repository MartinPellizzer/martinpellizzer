Cineraria trachyphylla

Cineraria trachyphylla, commonly known as the roughleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid native habitat. It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant plants.
