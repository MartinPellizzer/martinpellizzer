Carduus argyroa

Carduus argyroa, commonly known as silverleaf thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves and spiny, spherical flower heads that range from purple to pink in color. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
