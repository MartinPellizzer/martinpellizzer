Campanula rotundifolia var. pygmaea

Campanula rotundifolia var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, hairy leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in diameter. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
