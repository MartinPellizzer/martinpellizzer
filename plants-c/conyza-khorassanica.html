Conyza khorassanica

Conyza khorassanica, commonly known as Persian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pale yellow flowers are arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Conyza khorassanica is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
