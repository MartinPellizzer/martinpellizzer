Campanula drabifolia subsp. attica

Campanula drabifolia subsp.

attica, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Campanula drabifolia subsp.

attica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
