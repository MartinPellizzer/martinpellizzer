Chamaedorea pachecoana

Chamaedorea pachecoana, commonly known as the Pacheco's bamboo palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clustered, feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 meters and thrives in bright, indirect light with moderate humidity. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, making it an attractive addition to indoor spaces.

It is relatively low maintenance, requiring regular watering and well-draining soil.
