Convolvulus occidentalis subsp. fruticetorum

Convolvulus occidentalis subsp.

fruticetorum, commonly known as the western morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.

The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on hairy stems. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
