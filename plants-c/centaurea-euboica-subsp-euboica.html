Centaurea euboica subsp. euboica

Centaurea euboica subsp.

euboica, commonly known as the Euboean knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Greek island of Euboea. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, sparsely hairy stems and clustered, bright yellow to reddish-orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical heads.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
