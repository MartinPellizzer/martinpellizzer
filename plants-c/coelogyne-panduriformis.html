Coelogyne panduriformis

Coelogyne panduriformis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in epiphytic or lithophytic habitats, often on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Coelogyne panduriformis is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated for its long-lasting floral display.
