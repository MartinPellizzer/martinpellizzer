Crataegus microphylla subsp. malyana

Crataegus microphylla subsp.

malyana, commonly known as the Malyan hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
