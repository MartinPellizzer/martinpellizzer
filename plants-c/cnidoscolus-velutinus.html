Cnidoscolus velutinus

Cnidoscolus velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sida, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
