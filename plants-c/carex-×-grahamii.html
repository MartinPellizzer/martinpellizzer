Carex × grahamii

Carex × grahamii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. This plant is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Carex × grahamii is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and its tolerance to varying water levels.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system, which helps it spread and establish in wetland environments.
