Capparis gialaiensis

Capparis gialaiensis, commonly known as the Gialai crabapple, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant's fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
