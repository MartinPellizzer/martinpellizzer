Cavanilla scandens

Cavanilla scandens, commonly known as the climbing cavanilla, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
