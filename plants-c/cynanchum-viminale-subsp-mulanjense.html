Cynanchum viminale subsp. mulanjense

Cynanchum viminale subsp.

mulanjense, commonly known as Mulanji's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of China. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
