Creusa × mixta

Creusa × mixta, commonly known as the hybrid crepe myrtle, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is a cross between *Crepuscularia* and *C. mexicana*, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
