Coryphantha impexicoma

Coryphantha impexicoma, commonly known as the Mexican ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a small, globular plant with a ribbed stem that typically grows to a height of about 10 centimeters. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a hardy choice for desert gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
