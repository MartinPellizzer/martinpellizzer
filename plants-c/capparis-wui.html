Capparis wui

Capparis wui, commonly known as the Chinese glorybower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparidaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with thorny stems and palmate leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Capparis wui is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
