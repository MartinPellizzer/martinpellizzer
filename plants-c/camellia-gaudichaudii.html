Camellia gaudichaudii

Camellia gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Guadaloupe camellia, is a species of flowering plant in the Theaceae family.

It is native to the island of Guadalupe in the Caribbean and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring.

Camellia gaudichaudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
