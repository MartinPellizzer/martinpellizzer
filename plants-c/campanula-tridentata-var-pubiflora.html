Campanula tridentata var. pubiflora

Campanula tridentata var. pubiflora, commonly known as the pubescent bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture. The flowers are bell-shaped, typically blue or violet in color, with five lobes and a hairy calyx. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
