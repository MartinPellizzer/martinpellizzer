Carex pilosiuscula

Carex pilosiuscula, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex pilosiuscula is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
