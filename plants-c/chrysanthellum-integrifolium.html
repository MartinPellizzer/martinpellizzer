Chrysanthellum integrifolium

Chrysanthellum integrifolium, commonly known as the Australian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, entire leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
