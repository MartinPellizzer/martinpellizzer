Calamintha dufourii

Calamintha dufourii, commonly known as Dufour's germander, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the upper stems. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong mint-like fragrance.

Calamintha dufourii is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
