Calamagrostis lemmonii

Calamagrostis lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's reedgrass, is a tall perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces feathery plumes that bloom in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.

Calamagrostis lemmonii is also important for wildlife, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
