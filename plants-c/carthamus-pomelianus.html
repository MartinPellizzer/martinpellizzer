Carthamus pomelianus

Carthamus pomelianus, commonly known as the pomeron or sea dahlia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

Carthamus pomelianus is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and drought-resistant qualities.
