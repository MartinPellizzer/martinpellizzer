Capsicum cordiforme var. majus

Capsicum cordiforme var. majus, commonly known as the heart-shaped chili pepper, is a variety of the Capsicum genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped fruits, which can range in color from green to red when mature. The plant is a perennial shrub that thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers before the fruit develops.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in culinary applications.
