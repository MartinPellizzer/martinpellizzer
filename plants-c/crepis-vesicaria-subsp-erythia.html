Crepis vesicaria subsp. erythia

Crepis vesicaria subsp.

erythia, commonly known as the red-rayed mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, basal rosette of leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single stem.

The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its subspecies name, *erythia*, refers to the reddish coloration of its flower heads.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and thistles.
