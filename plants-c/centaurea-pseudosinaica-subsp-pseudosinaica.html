Centaurea pseudosinaica subsp. pseudosinaica

Centaurea pseudosinaica subsp.

pseudosinaica, commonly known as the Eastern Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
