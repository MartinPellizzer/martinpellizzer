Cressa truxillensis

Cressa truxillensis, commonly known as the Trinidad watercress, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, edible leaves that are often used in local cuisines for their peppery flavor. It thrives in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often found along riverbanks and in wetlands.

Cressa truxillensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
