Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium var. leucanthum

Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium var. leucanthum, commonly known as the white chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray foliage that resembles lavender leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flower heads that bloom in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a subtle, sweet fragrance.

This variety is particularly valued in traditional Chinese horticulture for its aesthetic and medicinal properties.
