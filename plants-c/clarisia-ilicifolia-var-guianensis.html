Clarisia ilicifolia var. guianensis

Clarisia ilicifolia var. guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan holly or Guianan clarisia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in terminal panicles, producing a fragrant scent.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
