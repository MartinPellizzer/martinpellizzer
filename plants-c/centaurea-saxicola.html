Centaurea saxicola

Centaurea saxicola, commonly known as the rock centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking blue or purple flowers in the summer months. Its small, bract-like structures surround the flower heads, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Centaurea saxicola is often used in rock gardens and as a ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
