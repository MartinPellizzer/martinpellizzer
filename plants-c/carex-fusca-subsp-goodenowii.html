Carex fusca subsp. goodenowii

Carex fusca subsp.

goodenowii, commonly known as Goodenow's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, spadix-like inflorescences that are often hidden within leafy bracts.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
