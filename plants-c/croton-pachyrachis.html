Croton pachyrachis

Croton pachyrachis, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bark contains latex, which has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

Croton pachyrachis thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
