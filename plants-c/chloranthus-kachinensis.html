Chloranthus kachinensis

Chloranthus kachinensis, commonly known as the Kachin chloranthus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae, which is characterized by its unique floral structures and lack of true leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under forest canopies. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and often clustered in cymes, with a distinctive structure that includes a cup-like calyx.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance and limited distribution.
