Carex muricata proles lumnitzeri

Carex muricata proles lumnitzeri, commonly known as the rough sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its stiff, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with small, sharp prickles along the edges. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown in color, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
