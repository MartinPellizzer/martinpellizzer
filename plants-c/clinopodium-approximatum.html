Clinopodium approximatum

Clinopodium approximatum, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a fragrant, minty scent. Its small, white or pale purple flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Clinopodium approximatum is often used in herbal medicine for its purported aromatic and antiseptic properties.
