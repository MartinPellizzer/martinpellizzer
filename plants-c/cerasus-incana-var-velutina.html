Cerasus incana var. velutina

Cerasus incana var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, sweet, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

The flowers are white or pale pink and appear in early spring before the leaves.
