Carduus × praticola

Carduus × praticola, commonly known as the common cockleburs.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Carduus, belonging to the Asteraceae family. The plant is characterized by its spiny, rough leaves and prickly seed heads, which are adaptations for wind dispersal. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world.

Botanically, it is notable for its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.
