Carex apodostachya

Carex apodostachya, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant native to various regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes and wetlands.

Carex apodostachya is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
