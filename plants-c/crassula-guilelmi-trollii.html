Crassula guilelmi-trollii

Crassula guilelmi-trollii, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a houseplant.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
