Carum sogdianum

Carum sogdianum, commonly known as Sogdian parsley, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Carum sogdianum is valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.
