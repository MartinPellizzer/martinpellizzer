Cactus melocactoides

Cactus melocactoides, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny areoles, which are clusters of spines, glochids, and flowers. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The cactus is also known for its slow growth and ability to survive with minimal care.
