Ceropegia laevis

Ceropegia laevis, commonly known as the silver leaf ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, pale green to silver-colored leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Ceropegia laevis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
