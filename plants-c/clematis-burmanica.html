Clematis burmanica

Clematis burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese Clematis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, featuring slender, woody stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

Clematis burmanica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
