Chimonobambusa szechuanensis var. flexuosa

Chimonobambusa szechuanensis var. flexuosa, commonly known as the Sichuan bamboo, is a variant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It is a clump-forming bamboo characterized by its slender, gracefully arching stems that often exhibit a flexible, weeping appearance. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, providing a lush visual effect. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
