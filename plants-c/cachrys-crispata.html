Cachrys crispata

Cachrys crispata, commonly known as the hairy pincushion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, pinkish-white inflorescences that resemble pincushions. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial shrub that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant.
