Campanula caucasica var. major

Campanula caucasica var. major, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in summer. The plant has upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
