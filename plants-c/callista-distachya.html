Callista distachya

Callista distachya, commonly known as the two-stemmed callista, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Callista distachya is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
