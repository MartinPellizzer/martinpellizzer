Cyathea chiricana

Cyathea chiricana, commonly known as the Chiricahua tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forested areas with high humidity. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, supporting its growth and enabling it to withstand periodic droughts.

Cyathea chiricana plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various wildlife species.
