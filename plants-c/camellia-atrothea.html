Camellia atrothea

Camellia atrothea, commonly known as the black camellia, is a species native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black petals, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking flowers and hardy nature.

Camellia atrothea prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
