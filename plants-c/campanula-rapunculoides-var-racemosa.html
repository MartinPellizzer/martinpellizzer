Campanula rapunculoides var. racemosa

Campanula rapunculoides var. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
