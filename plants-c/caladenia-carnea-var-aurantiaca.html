Caladenia carnea var. aurantiaca

Caladenia carnea var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It features a single, slender stem that bears a single, showy flower with a distinctive spider-like appearance. The flower has a vivid orange-red coloration, with a narrow, recurved lip that enhances its visual appeal. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats.

It is a short-lived species, often flowering for only a few years before dying, making it a rare and sought-after orchid.
