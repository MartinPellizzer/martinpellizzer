Cymbopogon tibeticus

Cymbopogon tibeticus, commonly known as the Tibetan lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that contain essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and aromatherapy. The plant typically grows in rocky and mountainous terrain, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its rhizomes and leaves are often used for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.

Cymbopogon tibeticus is also appreciated for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
