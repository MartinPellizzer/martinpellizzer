Cyperus straminicolor

Cyperus straminicolor, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bright yellow to golden in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus straminicolor is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate moist conditions.
