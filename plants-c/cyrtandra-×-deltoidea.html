Cyrtandra × deltoidea

Cyrtandra × deltoidea, commonly known as the hybrid blue ginger, is a tropical plant belonging to the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyrtandra pedata and Cyrtandra deltoidea, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its striking blue-violet bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. It typically grows as an herbaceous perennial with a compact, bushy habit.

Cyrtandra × deltoidea is native to Hawaii and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
