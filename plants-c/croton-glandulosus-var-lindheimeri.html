Croton glandulosus var. lindheimeri

Croton glandulosus var. lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's croton, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
