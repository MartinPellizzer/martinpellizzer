Carex kiusiuana

Carex kiusiuana, commonly known as the Kiusiu sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their unique morphology and ecological roles. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a landscape plant.
