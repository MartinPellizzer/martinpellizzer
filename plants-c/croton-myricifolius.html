Croton myricifolius

Croton myricifolius, commonly known as the candlewood tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble candle flames, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves and fruits of Croton myricifolius have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
