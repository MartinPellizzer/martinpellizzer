Cestrum racemosum var. panamense

Cestrum racemosum var. panamense, commonly known as Panama nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the evening. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in woodland or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value is due to its nocturnal fragrance and striking floral display.
