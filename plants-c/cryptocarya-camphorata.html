Cryptocarya camphorata

Cryptocarya camphorata, commonly known as the Australian camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and avocado trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by oval, dark blue berries. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and emit a strong camphor-like scent when crushed.

Cryptocarya camphorata is often found in coastal and riparian habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
