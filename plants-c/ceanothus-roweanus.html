Ceanothus roweanus

Ceanothus roweanus, commonly known as the silverleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are aromatic and contain volatile oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent.

Ceanothus roweanus plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
