Crotalaria hatschbachii var. sericea

Crotalaria hatschbachii var. sericea, commonly known as the silky crownflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
