Cyathea sodiroi

Cyathea sodiroi, commonly known as the Sodiro's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, with a short rootstock and a tall, unbranched stem. Its fronds are typically arranged in a spiral, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cyathea sodiroi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
