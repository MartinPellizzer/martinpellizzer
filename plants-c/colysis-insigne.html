Colysis insigne

Colysis insigne, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, feathery leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Colysis insigne is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
