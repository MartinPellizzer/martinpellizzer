Cousinia andarabensis

Cousinia andarabensis, commonly known as the Persian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its yellow or orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia andarabensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
