Cota triumfetti var. discoidea

Cota triumfetti var. discoidea, commonly known as the "butterfly flower," is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow petals and a central disk, which are typically pollinated by bees and other insects. The plant produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a favored plant among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
