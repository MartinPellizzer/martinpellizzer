Ceratochloa secunda

Ceratochloa secunda, commonly known as the slender grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet with two to three florets, which are often covered in fine hairs.

Ceratochloa secunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
