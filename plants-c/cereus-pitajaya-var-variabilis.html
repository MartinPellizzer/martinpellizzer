Cereus pitajaya var. variabilis

Cereus pitajaya var. variabilis, commonly known as the variable pitaya cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom at night. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to dry conditions.

This cactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
