Crawfurdia affinis

Crawfurdia affinis, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces striking yellow bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a hairy texture.

Crawfurdia affinis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
