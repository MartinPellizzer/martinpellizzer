Celosia rosea

Celosia rosea, commonly known as the cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive, plume-like inflorescences that resemble a rooster's comb, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant colors, which range from red, pink, orange, and yellow. Celosia rosea is a herbaceous annual that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
