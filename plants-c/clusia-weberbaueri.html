Clusia weberbaueri

Clusia weberbaueri, commonly known as the Mexican glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are thick, waxy, and often have a glossy appearance, while its flowers are clustered in panicles.

Clusia weberbaueri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical and subtropical gardens.
