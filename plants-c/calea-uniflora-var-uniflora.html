Calea uniflora var. uniflora

Calea uniflora var. uniflora, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant features a robust, upright stem with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light for optimal growth.
