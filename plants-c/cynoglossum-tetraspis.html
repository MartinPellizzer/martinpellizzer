Cynoglossum tetraspis

Cynoglossum tetraspis, commonly known as the four-lobed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped with four distinct lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Cynoglossum tetraspis is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
