Cardamine enneaphyllos var. alternifolia

Cardamine enneaphyllos var. alternifolia, commonly known as the nine-lobed watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features alternate, trifoliate leaves with a distinctive serrated edge, and its small white flowers are arranged in racemes. It thrives in cool, damp conditions and is often found along stream banks and in woodland areas.

The species is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
