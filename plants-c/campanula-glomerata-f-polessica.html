Campanula glomerata f. polessica

Campanula glomerata f. polessica, commonly known as the Polessica bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that are slightly hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
