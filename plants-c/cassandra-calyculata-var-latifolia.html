Cassandra calyculata var. latifolia

Cassandra calyculata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cassava, is a variant of the cassava plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and a stout, woody stem. The plant produces edible tuberous roots that are rich in starch and are a staple food in many African countries. Its leaves are also edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
