Crataegus × bicknellii

Crataegus × bicknellii, commonly known as Bicknell's hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves. Its foliage is often dense, providing habitat for various wildlife species.

The berries are an important food source for birds and small mammals, contributing to its ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
