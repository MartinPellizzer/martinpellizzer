Crataegus monogyna f. aristata-serrata

Crataegus monogyna f. aristata-serrata, commonly known as the serrated hawthorn, is a horticultural form of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring both aristate (bristle-tipped) and serrated margins. The plant is typically a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to Europe. It produces small, white flowers in spring followed by red, fleshy berries in autumn.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
