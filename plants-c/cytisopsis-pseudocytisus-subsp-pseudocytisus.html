Cytisopsis pseudocytisus subsp. pseudocytisus

Cytisopsis pseudocytisus subsp.

pseudocytisus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, often spiny stems and narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
