Cinnamomum pseudoglaziovii

Cinnamomum pseudoglaziovii, commonly known as the Guangxi cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its unique scent and potential medicinal uses.
