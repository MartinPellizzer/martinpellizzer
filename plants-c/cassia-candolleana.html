Cassia candolleana

Cassia candolleana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous racemes of bright yellow flowers that resemble cascading showers, hence its common name. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several elliptical seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets.

Cassia candolleana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
