Carex nelsonii

Carex nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Carex nelsonii is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its aesthetic appeal in wetland landscapes.
