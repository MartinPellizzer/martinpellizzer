Cytisus subspinescens

Cytisus subspinescens, commonly known as the prickly broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny branches and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces legume pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
