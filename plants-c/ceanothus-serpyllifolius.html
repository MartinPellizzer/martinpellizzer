Ceanothus serpyllifolius

Ceanothus serpyllifolius, commonly known as coastal ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are usually blue to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
