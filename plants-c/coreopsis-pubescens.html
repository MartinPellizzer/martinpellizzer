Coreopsis pubescens

Coreopsis pubescens, commonly known as the downy coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with radiating petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Coreopsis pubescens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
