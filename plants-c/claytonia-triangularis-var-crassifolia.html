Claytonia triangularis var. crassifolia

Claytonia triangularis var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf mountain chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
