Campanula balcanica subf. justiniana

Campanula balcanica subf.

justiniana, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate regions.
