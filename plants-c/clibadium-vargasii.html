Clibadium vargasii

Clibadium vargasii, commonly known as the Vargas morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms that open in the early morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it can be invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
