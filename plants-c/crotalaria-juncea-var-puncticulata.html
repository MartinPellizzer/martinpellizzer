Crotalaria juncea var. puncticulata

Crotalaria juncea var. puncticulata, commonly known as the punctuated cowpea, is a variant of the common cowpea species.

It is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features yellow flowers with distinctive black spots, and its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable crop in sustainable agriculture.

The variety is often used for forage and as a cover crop due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
