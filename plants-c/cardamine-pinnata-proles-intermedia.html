Cardamine pinnata proles intermedia

Cardamine pinnata proles intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate water shield, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and wet meadows. Its small, white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early summer.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small aquatic organisms.
