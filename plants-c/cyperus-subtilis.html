Cyperus subtilis

Cyperus subtilis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in shallow water. Its flowers are small and clustered, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus subtilis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
