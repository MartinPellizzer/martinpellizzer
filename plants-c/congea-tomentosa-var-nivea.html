Congea tomentosa var. nivea

Congea tomentosa var. nivea, commonly known as the white congea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, and the stems are covered in fine, white pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
