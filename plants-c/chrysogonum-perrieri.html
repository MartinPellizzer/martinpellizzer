Chrysogonum perrieri

Chrysogonum perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's goldenstar, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Chrysogonum perrieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
