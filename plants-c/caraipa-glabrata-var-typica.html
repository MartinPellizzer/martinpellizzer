Caraipa glabrata var. typica

Caraipa glabrata var. typica, commonly known as the Caraipa shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates. Its leaves are simple, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its compact form and seasonal blooms.
