Cocculus palmatus

Cocculus palmatus, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain a single, hard-coated seed. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Due to its cultural and medicinal significance, it is also cultivated in various parts of the world.
