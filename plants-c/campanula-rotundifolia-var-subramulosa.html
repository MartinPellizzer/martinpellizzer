Campanula rotundifolia var. subramulosa

Campanula rotundifolia var. subramulosa, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features basal rosettes of rounded, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
