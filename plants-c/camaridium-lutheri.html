Camaridium lutheri

Camaridium lutheri, commonly known as the Luther's camaridium, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that display a range of colors from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited habitat and susceptibility to environmental changes, Camaridium lutheri is considered a vulnerable species.
