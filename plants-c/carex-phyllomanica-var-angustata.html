Carex phyllomanica var. angustata

Carex phyllomanica var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and ecological restoration projects.
