Crepis runcinata subsp. andersonii

Crepis runcinata subsp.

andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has a robust root system and can form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
