Cineraria japonica

Cineraria japonica, commonly known as Japanese cineraria, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early autumn.
