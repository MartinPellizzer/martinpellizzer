Casearia formosa

Casearia formosa, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Casearia formosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
