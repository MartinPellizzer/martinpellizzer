Canna laeta

Canna laeta, commonly known as the golden canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central and South America.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green, and its most notable characteristic is its vibrant, showy flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange with red or purple accents, and they bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Canna laeta is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
