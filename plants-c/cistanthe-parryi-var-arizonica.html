Cistanthe parryi var. arizonica

Cistanthe parryi var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to arid environments.
