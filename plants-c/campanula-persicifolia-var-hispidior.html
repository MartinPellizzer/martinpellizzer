Campanula persicifolia var. hispidior

Campanula persicifolia var. hispidior, commonly known as the hispid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features erect, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered with fine, hairy textures, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate, showy blooms.
