Clusia pithecobia

Clusia pithecobia, commonly known as the Brazilian shield-leaf or the evergreen shrub, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Clusia pithecobia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in humid conditions.

Its foliage is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
