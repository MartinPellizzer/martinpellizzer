Cytisus horniflorus

Cytisus horniflorus, commonly known as the horned flax or horned broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being narrow and pointed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus horniflorus is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
