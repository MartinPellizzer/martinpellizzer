Commiphora sinuata

Commiphora sinuata, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spherical, yellowish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and fragrance.
