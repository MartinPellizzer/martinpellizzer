Coryanthes gernotii

Coryanthes gernotii, commonly known as the Gernot's hooded orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, showy floral structures that resemble elaborate hoods, often with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows on tree branches, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, such as beetles, which are attracted to the strong odors emitted by the blooms.

Coryanthes gernotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
