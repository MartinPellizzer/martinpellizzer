Croton perdicipes var. weddellianus

Croton perdicipes var. weddellianus, commonly known as the Weddell's croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
