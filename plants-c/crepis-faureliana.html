Crepis faureliana

Crepis faureliana, commonly known as the Faure's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and hairy, with small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early summer.

Crepis faureliana is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a short-lived perennial.
