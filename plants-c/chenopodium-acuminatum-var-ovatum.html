Chenopodium acuminatum var. ovatum

Chenopodium acuminatum var. ovatum, commonly known as smooth lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, followed by dry, three-angled seed capsules. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Smooth lambsquarters is a common weed but also has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a food source.
