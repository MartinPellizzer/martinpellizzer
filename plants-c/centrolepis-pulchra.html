Centrolepis pulchra

Centrolepis pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful centrolepis, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like leaves that are spirally arranged around the stem. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. Its rhizomes are robust and help it survive in arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
