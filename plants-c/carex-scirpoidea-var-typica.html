Carex scirpoidea var. typica

Carex scirpoidea var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland environments.
