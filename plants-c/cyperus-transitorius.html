Cyperus transitorius

Cyperus transitorius, commonly known as the spikerush, is a wetland plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers that are wind-pollinated. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams.

Cyperus transitorius is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
