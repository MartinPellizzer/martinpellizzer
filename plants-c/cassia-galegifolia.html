Cassia galegifolia

Cassia galegifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces flat, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural dye.
