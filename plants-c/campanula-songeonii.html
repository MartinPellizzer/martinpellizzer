Campanula songeonii

Campanula songeonii, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Korea.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanula songeonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
