Croton sancti-lazari

Croton sancti-lazari, commonly known as the San Lazaro croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
