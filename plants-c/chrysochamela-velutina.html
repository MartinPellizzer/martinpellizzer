Chrysochamela velutina

Chrysochamela velutina, commonly known as the velvet flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
