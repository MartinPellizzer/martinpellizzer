Cassia corifolia

Cassia corifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, triangular pods that contain several seeds. Cassia corifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
