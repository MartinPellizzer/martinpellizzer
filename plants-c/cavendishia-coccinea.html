Cavendishia coccinea

Cavendishia coccinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red stems and vibrant orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring or early summer.

Cavendishia coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses for its unique appearance.
