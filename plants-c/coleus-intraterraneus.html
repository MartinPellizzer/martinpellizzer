Coleus intraterraneus

Coleus intraterraneus, commonly known as the mountain coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine in some regions.
