Calliandra papillosa

Calliandra papillosa, commonly known as the silky calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that consist of numerous small, pink to red flowers. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and is often grown for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil.

Calliandra papillosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.
