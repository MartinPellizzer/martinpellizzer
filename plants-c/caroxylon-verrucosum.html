Caroxylon verrucosum

Caroxylon verrucosum, commonly known as the roughleaf sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its rough, thorny branches and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by wildlife. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
