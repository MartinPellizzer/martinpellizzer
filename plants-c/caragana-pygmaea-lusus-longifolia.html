Caragana pygmaea lusus longifolia

Caragana pygmaea lusus longifolia, commonly known as the slender peashrub, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds used for reforestation and soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
