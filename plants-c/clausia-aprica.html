Clausia aprica

Clausia aprica, commonly known as the golden clausia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its botanical name reflects its sunny disposition, with "aprica" relating to the sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in horticultural settings.
