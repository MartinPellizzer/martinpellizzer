Calamagrostis petriei

Calamagrostis petriei, commonly known as the Petrie reedgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Calamagrostis petriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
