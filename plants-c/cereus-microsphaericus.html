Cereus microsphaericus

Cereus microsphaericus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and its ability to produce large, white, fragrant flowers at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers typically open only during the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths.

The plant is popular in horticulture for its striking appearance and unique flowering behavior.
