Callitriche stagnalis var. transsilvanica

Callitriche stagnalis var. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, greenish flowers that are often submerged or floating.

This variety is characterized by its distinct morphology and is often used in ecological studies to assess water quality.
