Cereus speciosissimus var. roidii

Cereus speciosissimus var. roidii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and its ability to produce large, showy flowers at night. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and have a strong, sweet fragrance.

It is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and dramatic nighttime blooming display.
