Calamus flabelloides

Calamus flabelloides, commonly known as the fan-leafed arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, rhizomatous growth habit and large, broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus flabelloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
