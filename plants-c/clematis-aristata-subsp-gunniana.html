Clematis aristata subsp. gunniana

Clematis aristata subsp.

gunniana, commonly known as the Gunni's Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
