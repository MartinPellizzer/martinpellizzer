Chamaezelum dioicum

Chamaezelum dioicum, commonly known as the eastern red cedar, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is characterized by its scale-like leaves and small, round cones. The plant has a slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, while its foliage is dense and aromatic.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in construction and furniture-making.
