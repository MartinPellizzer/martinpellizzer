Calliopsis basalis

Calliopsis basalis, commonly known as the small-flowered rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in dry, open areas. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
