Circaea lutetiana subsp. mediterranea

Circaea lutetiana subsp.

mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean whitebeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats such as cliffs, slopes, and woodland edges.

The plant features small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, soft hairs.

It is notable for its ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
