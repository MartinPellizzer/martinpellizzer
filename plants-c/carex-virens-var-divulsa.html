Carex virens var. divulsa

Carex virens var. divulsa, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly pointed, with a bright green color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, which are often subtended by bracts. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
