Cenchrus hordeoides

Cenchrus hordeoides, commonly known as wild barley grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its resilience and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural fields.
