Carex lophocarpa

Carex lophocarpa, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to North America and is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, greenish-brown flowers.

Carex lophocarpa plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
