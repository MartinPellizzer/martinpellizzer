Campylocentrum hirtellum

Campylocentrum hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Campylocentrum hirtellum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
