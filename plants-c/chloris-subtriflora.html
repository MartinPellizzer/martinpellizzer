Chloris subtriflora

Chloris subtriflora, commonly known as the three-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of three small, white to pale blue flowers at the ends of its stems, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Chloris subtriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
