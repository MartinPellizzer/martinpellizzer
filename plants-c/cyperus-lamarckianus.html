Cyperus lamarckianus

Cyperus lamarckianus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, often resembling a spiky umbrella shape.

Cyperus lamarckianus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions.
