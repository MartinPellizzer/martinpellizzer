Calamus distichus

Calamus distichus, commonly known as the two-ranked calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are arranged in two vertical rows. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calamus distichus is also valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
