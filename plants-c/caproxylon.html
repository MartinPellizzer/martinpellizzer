Caproxylon

Caproxylon, commonly known as the ancient tree fern, is a fossilized plant that dates back to the Carboniferous period.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds and a central trunk, which are preserved in coal deposits. The plant's structure suggests it was a large, vascular fern with a complex root system. Caproxylon played a significant role in the early development of forest ecosystems.

Its botanical study provides valuable insights into the evolution of plant life during prehistoric times.
