Clitandropsis crassifolia

Clitandropsis crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved climbing plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, woody stems and thick, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying tropical flora.
