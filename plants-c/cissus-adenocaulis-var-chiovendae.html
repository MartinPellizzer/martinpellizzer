Cissus adenocaulis var. chiovendae

Cissus adenocaulis var. chiovendae, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in containers or as a trailing plant.
