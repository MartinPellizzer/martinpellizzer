Cnicus odoratus

Cnicus odoratus, commonly known as sweet Alyssum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has delicate, hairy leaves and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Cnicus odoratus is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating respiratory ailments.
