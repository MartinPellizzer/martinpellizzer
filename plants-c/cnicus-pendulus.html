Cnicus pendulus

Cnicus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features wiry stems that often trail or hang down, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

It produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable environments.
