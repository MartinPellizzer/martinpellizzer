Camellia cuspidata var. trichandra

Camellia cuspidata var. trichandra, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during late winter to early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

The variety trichandra is distinguished by its longer, more pronounced stamens compared to other camellia varieties.
