Campylotheca cosmoides

Campylotheca cosmoides, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
