Ceterach officinarum subsp. bivalens

Ceterach officinarum subsp.

bivalens, commonly known as the coastal ceterach, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shorelines.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate grazing.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
