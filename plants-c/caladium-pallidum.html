Caladium pallidum

Caladium pallidum, commonly known as the white-veined caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with pale green veins against a deep green background. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Caladium pallidum is typically grown as an annual in regions with colder winters.
