Chamaecytisus hirsutus subsp. hirsutus

Chamaecytisus hirsutus subsp.

hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, arching stems covered in dense, hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long-lasting flowers.
