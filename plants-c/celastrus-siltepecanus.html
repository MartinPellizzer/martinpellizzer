Celastrus siltepecanus

Celastrus siltepecanus, commonly known as the Mexican bindweed, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Celastrus siltepecanus is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain regions.
