Carex saxatilis var. bigelowii

Carex saxatilis var. bigelowii, commonly known as Bigelow's sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves that form dense clumps. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
