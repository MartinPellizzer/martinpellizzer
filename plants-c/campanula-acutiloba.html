Campanula acutiloba

Campanula acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have pointed lobes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula acutiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
