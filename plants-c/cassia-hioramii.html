Cassia hioramii

Cassia hioramii, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly serrated.

Cassia hioramii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
