Combretum petrophilum

Combretum petrophilum, commonly known as the rock combretum, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the late spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Combretum petrophilum is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
