Cullumia adpressifolia

Cullumia adpressifolia, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are showy, with white to pale pink petals that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Cullumia adpressifolia plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
