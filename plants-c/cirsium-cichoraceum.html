Cirsium cichoraceum

Cirsium cichoraceum, commonly known as the lesser thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the family.

Cirsium cichoraceum is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
