Convolvulus chondrilloides var. villosus

Convolvulus chondrilloides var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often covered with fine hairs.

It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides.
