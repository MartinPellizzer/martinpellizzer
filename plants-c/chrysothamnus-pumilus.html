Chrysothamnus pumilus

Chrysothamnus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf goldenbush, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features yellowish-green foliage with small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
