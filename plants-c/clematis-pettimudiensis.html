Clematis pettimudiensis

Clematis pettimudiensis, commonly known as the New Zealand climbing clematis, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a vigorous, twining vine that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five ovate leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens throughout its native range.
