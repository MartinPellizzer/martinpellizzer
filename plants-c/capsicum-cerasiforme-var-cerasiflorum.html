Capsicum cerasiforme var. cerasiflorum

Capsicum cerasiforme var. cerasiflorum, commonly known as the cherry bell pepper, is a cultivar of the Capsicum genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, round, and brightly colored fruits, typically red, yellow, or orange when mature. The plant is an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bilaterally symmetrical, typical of the Solanaceae family.

The fruit develops from a single ovary and contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed when the fruit ripens and dries.
