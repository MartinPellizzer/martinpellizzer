Centaurea salmantica proles microcephala

Centaurea salmantica proles microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf Spanish thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces spiny, bract-like leaves and has a flowering period in the summer months. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or arid soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
