Clathrotropis surinamensis

Clathrotropis surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid lowland areas.

Its botanical features make it an attractive ornamental plant, though it is less commonly cultivated than other tropical species.
