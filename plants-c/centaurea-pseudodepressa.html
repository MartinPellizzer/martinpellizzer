Centaurea pseudodepressa

Centaurea pseudodepressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-green hue.
