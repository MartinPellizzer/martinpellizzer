Coelogyne keithiana

Coelogyne keithiana, commonly known as the Keith's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters of up to 20 individual blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange with red or purple markings, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
