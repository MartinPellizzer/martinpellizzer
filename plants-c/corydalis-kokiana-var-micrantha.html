Corydalis kokiana var. micrantha

Corydalis kokiana var. micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf corydalis, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, creeping stems that produce clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and purple. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variant is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its charming floral display.
