Cyrtochilum insculptum

Cyrtochilum insculptum, commonly known as the sculptured slipper orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and its striking, intricate floral structure. The flowers are typically greenish-yellow with dark markings, and they bloom in late spring or early summer. This epiphytic orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unusual appearance.
