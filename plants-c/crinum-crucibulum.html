Crinum crucibulum

Crinum crucibulum, commonly known as the flame lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange with a yellow center, arranged in umbels. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Crinum crucibulum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
