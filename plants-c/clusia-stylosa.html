Clusia stylosa

Clusia stylosa, commonly known as the wax plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow and white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Clusia stylosa is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its adaptability and ornamental appearance make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
