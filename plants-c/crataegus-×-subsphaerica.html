Crataegus × subsphaerica

Crataegus × subsphaerica, commonly known as the globe thornapple, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its rounded, globe-like form, which gives it its common name. The plant typically produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique shape and seasonal interest.
