Chrysophyllum natalense

Chrysophyllum natalense, commonly known as Natal cherries, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red when ripe, with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and riverine forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
