Copernicia glabrescens

Copernicia glabrescens, commonly known as the wax palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces a unique type of wax, known as copal, which is used in various traditional and industrial applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ecological and cultural significance, it is also considered a symbol of national identity in some countries.
