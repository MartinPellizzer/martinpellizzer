Citrus × aurantium var. sigillata

Citrus × aurantium var. sigillata, commonly known as the bitter orange, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, pebbled rind and fragrant, bitter fruit. The plant is a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves and white, fragrant flowers. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.

The fruit is also used in the production of marmalade and as a source of essential oils.
