Carex × wolteri

Carex × wolteri, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carex echinata and Carex vulpinoidea, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex × wolteri is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
