Carex straminea var. meadii

Carex straminea var. meadii, commonly known as Mead's sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream edges.

Mead's sedge is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
