Clerodendrum serratum var. herbaceum

Clerodendrum serratum var. herbaceum, commonly known as the creeping clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a ground cover or in hanging baskets.

The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature.
