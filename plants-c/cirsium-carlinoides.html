Cirsium carlinoides

Cirsium carlinoides, commonly known as the Carline thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces and often have spines along the edges.

Cirsium carlinoides is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
