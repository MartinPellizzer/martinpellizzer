Conocephalus concolor

Conocephalus concolor, commonly known as the common conocephalus, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically fragrant. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Conocephalus concolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
