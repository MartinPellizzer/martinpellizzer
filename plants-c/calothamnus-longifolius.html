Calothamnus longifolius

Calothamnus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved bottlebrush, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red or orange bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is a shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall and is adapted to coastal and inland regions.

Calothamnus longifolius is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
