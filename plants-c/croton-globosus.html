Croton globosus

Croton globosus, commonly known as the globe croton, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, colorful, and often variegated foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are typically thick and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Croton globosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and ability to thrive in warm, sunny conditions.
