Cienfuegosia pulchella

Cienfuegosia pulchella, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. Its botanical features include a woody stem with smooth bark and small, inconspicuous fruits that develop after the flowers.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
