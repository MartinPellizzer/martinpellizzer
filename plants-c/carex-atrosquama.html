Carex atrosquama

Carex atrosquama, commonly known as black-squama sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex atrosquama plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
