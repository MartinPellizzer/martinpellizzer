Callisthene dryadum

Callisthene dryadum, commonly known as the dryad's bell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its charming, fragrant blooms.
