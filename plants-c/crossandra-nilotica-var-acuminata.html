Crossandra nilotica var. acuminata

Crossandra nilotica var. acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
