Chamaesyce neomexicana

Chamaesyce neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The species is characterized by its hairy, succulent leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It has adapted to survive in harsh conditions with minimal water and nutrient requirements.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
