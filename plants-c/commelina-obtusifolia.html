Commelina obtusifolia

Commelina obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its distinct blue or violet flowers with three petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white berries that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ability to spread through rhizomes make it both a desirable and invasive species in certain areas.
