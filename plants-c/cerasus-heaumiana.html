Cerasus heaumiana

Cerasus heaumiana, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, with ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. Its fruits are small, red drupes that ripen in summer, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic flowers.

Cerasus heaumiana prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
