Carex longerostrata subsp. tsinlingensis

Carex longerostrata subsp.

tsinlingensis, commonly known as the Tsingling sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forest understories or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
