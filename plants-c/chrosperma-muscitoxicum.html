Chrosperma muscitoxicum

Chrosperma muscitoxicum, commonly known as the toxic liverwort, is a type of moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, acrocarpous growth form and typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive, flattened thallus with closely spaced, elongated leaves. It contains various secondary metabolites, some of which are toxic to grazing animals.
