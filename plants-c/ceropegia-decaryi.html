Ceropegia decaryi

Ceropegia decaryi, commonly known as the African violet or the snake plant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

Its unique floral structure attracts specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
