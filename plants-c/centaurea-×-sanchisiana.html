Centaurea × sanchisiana

Centaurea × sanchisiana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves and showy composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower meadows.

Centaurea × sanchisiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
