Commelina undulata var. densivestita

Commelina undulata var. densivestita, commonly known as the dense-hairy dayflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with white markings. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in dense, fine hairs. Its flowers typically bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
