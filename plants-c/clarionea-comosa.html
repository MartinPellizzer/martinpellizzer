Clarionea comosa

Clarionea comosa, commonly known as the hairy clarion, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or moist habitats. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Clarionea comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
