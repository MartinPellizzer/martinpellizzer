Coniogramme madagascariensis var. tripinnata

Coniogramme madagascariensis var. tripinnata, commonly known as the Madagascan featherleaf, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its finely divided, trifoliate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant typically grows as a low, creeping herb with a fibrous root system. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
