Conostylis seorsiflora subsp. trichophylla

Conostylis seorsiflora subsp.

trichophylla, commonly known as the hairy leaf conostylis, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, hairy leaves that give it its subspecies name.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
