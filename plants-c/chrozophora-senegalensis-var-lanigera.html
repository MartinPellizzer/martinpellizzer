Chrozophora senegalensis var. lanigera

Chrozophora senegalensis var. lanigera, commonly known as the woolly false flax, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
