Cerastium igoschiniae

Cerastium igoschiniae, commonly known as the Igoschina mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
