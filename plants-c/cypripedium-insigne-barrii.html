Cypripedium insigne barrii

Cypripedium insigne barrii, commonly known as the Barrii lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive pouch-like structure, which is a key feature of the Cypripedium genus. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest habitats, often on rocky or forested slopes. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
