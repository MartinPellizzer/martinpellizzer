Crocus gaillardotii

Crocus gaillardotii, commonly known as the Gaillardot's crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
