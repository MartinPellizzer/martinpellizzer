Clinopodium caroli-henricanum

Clinopodium caroli-henricanum, commonly known as Henry's wood betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, tubular, and arranged in whorls, often in shades of blue or purple.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
