Cymbopogon gazensis

Cymbopogon gazensis, commonly known as Gazengrass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in spike-like clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Gazengrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
