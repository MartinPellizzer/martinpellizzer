Chrysophyllum flexuosum var. cuspidatum

Chrysophyllum flexuosum var. cuspidatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or carambola, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that produces edible, star-shaped fruits with a tangy, acidic flavor. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical with a smooth margin. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its nutritious fruit.
