Caralluma ericeta

Caralluma ericeta, commonly known as the Ericaceous Cactoid, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant typically features spiny or woolly structures that provide protection from herbivores. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance.

Caralluma ericeta is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and unique addition to desert flora.
