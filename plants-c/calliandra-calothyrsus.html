Calliandra calothyrsus

Calliandra calothyrsus, commonly known as the sicklepod acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped pods and compound leaves with numerous small leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Calliandra calothyrsus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
