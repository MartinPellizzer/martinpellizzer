Cathormion umbellatum subsp. umbellatum

Cathormion umbellatum subsp.

umbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue or violet.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
