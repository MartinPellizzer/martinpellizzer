Cascarilla linearis

Cascarilla linearis, commonly known as the linear tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and the bark is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
