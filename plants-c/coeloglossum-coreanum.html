Coeloglossum coreanum

Coeloglossum coreanum, commonly known as the Korean hooded orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique hooded flower structure, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, while its flowers are small and clustered in a spike.

Coeloglossum coreanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
