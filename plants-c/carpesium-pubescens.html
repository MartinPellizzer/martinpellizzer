Carpesium pubescens

Carpesium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its spiny, prickly fruits are adapted for animal dispersal, and its leaves are covered in fine, hairy pubescence.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
