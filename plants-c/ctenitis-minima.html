Ctenitis minima

Ctenitis minima, commonly known as the small heart fern, is a small, delicate fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, rosette-like frond structure with a heart-shaped base, which gives it its common name. The fronds are typically covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, contributing to its textured appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat make it a notable species in botanical studies.
