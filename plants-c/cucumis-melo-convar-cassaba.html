Cucumis melo convar. cassaba

Cucumis melo convar. cassaba, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a variant of the melon genus characterized by its netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of Asia and Africa. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that develop into the characteristic orange to yellow colored fruit. The fruit is typically harvested when it has reached full size and has a firm, crisp texture.

It is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and use in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
