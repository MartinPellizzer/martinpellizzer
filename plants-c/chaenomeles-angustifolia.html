Chaenomeles angustifolia

Chaenomeles angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf Chinese quince, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant bears yellow, berry-like fruits that ripen in late summer, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
