Copiapoa haseltoniana

Copiapoa haseltoniana, commonly known as the Puya of the Atacama Desert, is a cacti species native to Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow or orange flowers during the summer months. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid environments with minimal water.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a distinctive species within the Cactaceae family.
