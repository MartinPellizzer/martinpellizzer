Cornus parvifolia

Cornus parvifolia, commonly known as dwarf cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its small, white flowers and distinctive, finely toothed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that attracts birds and other wildlife.

Cornus parvifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
