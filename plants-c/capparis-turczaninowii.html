Capparis turczaninowii

Capparis turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turkistan caper, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native habitat's flora.
