Croton trilobus var. alberti

Croton trilobus var. alberti, commonly known as the Albert's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful, and often variegated leaves that display shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
