Calamus erythrocarpus

It is a tall, slender palm-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height, with long, narrow leaves arranged in a fan shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by distinctive red berries, which are a key feature of its botanical description. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, tropical environments.

The plant is often found in wetlands and swampy areas, where it plays a role in maintaining local ecosystems.
