Cyperus malaccensis subsp. monophyllus

Cyperus malaccensis subsp.

monophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a monocotyledonous plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the base of the stem. It is typically found in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
