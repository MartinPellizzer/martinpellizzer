Cryptandra congesta

Cryptandra congesta, commonly known as the red boughed cryptandra, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive red or orange-brown woody stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Cryptandra congesta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
