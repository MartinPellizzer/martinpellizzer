Cassinia cuneifolia

Cassinia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive globular shape.

Cassinia cuneifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
