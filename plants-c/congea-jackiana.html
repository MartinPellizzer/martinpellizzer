Congea jackiana

Congea jackiana, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by winged fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
