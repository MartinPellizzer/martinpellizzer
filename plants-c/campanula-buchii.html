Campanula buchii

Campanula buchii, commonly known as the Buchii bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula buchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
