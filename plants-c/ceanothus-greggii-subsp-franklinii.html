Ceanothus greggii subsp. franklinii

Ceanothus greggii subsp.

franklinii, commonly known as Franklin's ceanothus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a vital role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping in drought-prone regions.
