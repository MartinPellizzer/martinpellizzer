Cyrtochilum leucochilum

Cyrtochilum leucochilum, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette, while the flowers are borne on elongated inflorescences.

Cyrtochilum leucochilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
