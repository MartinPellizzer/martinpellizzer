Cremolobus sinuatus

Cremolobus sinuatus, commonly known as the sinuous lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive, undulating thallus that gives it its common name. The plant's structure consists of a symbiotic partnership between a fungus and algae or cyanobacteria. It is typically found on tree trunks and rocks in temperate regions.

Its slow growth and unique morphology make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
