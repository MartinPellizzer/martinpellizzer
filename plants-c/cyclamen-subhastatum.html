Cyclamen subhastatum

Cyclamen subhastatum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a deep green color and a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and violet, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Cyclamen subhastatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and winter blooming habit.
