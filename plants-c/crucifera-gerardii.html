Crucifera gerardii

Crucifera gerardii, commonly known as wild radish, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the cruciferous family. Its fruit is a short, cylindrical silique that contains several seeds.

Wild radish is native to North America and often grows in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
