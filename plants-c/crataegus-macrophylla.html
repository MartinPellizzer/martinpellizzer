Crataegus macrophylla

Crataegus macrophylla, commonly known as the largeleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Crataegus macrophylla is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
