Cassia lucayana

Cassia lucayana, commonly known as the Lucayan cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Cassia lucayana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
