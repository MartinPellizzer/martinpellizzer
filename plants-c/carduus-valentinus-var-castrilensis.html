Carduus valentinus var. castrilensis

Carduus valentinus var. castrilensis, commonly known as the Valentine's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features spiny, lance-shaped leaves and produces purple to pink flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It is characterized by its robust stem and the presence of a central, hollow receptacle in its flower head.

This variety is closely related to other thistle species within the Asteraceae family.
