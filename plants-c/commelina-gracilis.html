Commelina gracilis

Commelina gracilis, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes grass-like plants with specialized leaf structures. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically have three petals and three sepals.

Commelina gracilis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands.
