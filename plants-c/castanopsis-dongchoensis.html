Castanopsis dongchoensis

Castanopsis dongchoensis, commonly known as the Dongcho chestnut.

It is a species of evergreen tree belonging to the Fagaceae family, native to the Korean Peninsula. The plant is characterized by its dense, spiny leaves and produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a spiny cupule. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
