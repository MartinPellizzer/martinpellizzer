Cookia graveolens

Cookia graveolens, commonly known as the sweet senna, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its scientific name reflects its strong, pleasant fragrance and its association with the genus Senna.

Cookia graveolens is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
