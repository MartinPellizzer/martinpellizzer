Cyanothamnus ramosus subsp. lesueuranus

Cyanothamnus ramosus subsp.

lesueuranus, commonly known as the Lesueuran's bluebush, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia. It features dense, compact growth with small, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces showy, blue or violet flowers in spikes during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soils in dry landscapes.
