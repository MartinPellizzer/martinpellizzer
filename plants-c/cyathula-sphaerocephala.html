Cyathula sphaerocephala

Cyathula sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globeflower, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, yellowish flowers clustered together. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Cyathula sphaerocephala is typically found in marshy areas and along the edges of ponds or streams.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with wet soil conditions.
