Centaurea reuteri

Centaurea reuteri, commonly known as the purple globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense, globe-shaped clusters, giving it a unique and eye-catching appearance.

Centaurea reuteri is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments.
