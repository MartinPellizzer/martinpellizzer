Cirsium gracile

Cirsium gracile, commonly known as slender thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Cirsium gracile is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
