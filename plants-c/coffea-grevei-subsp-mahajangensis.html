Coffea grevei subsp. mahajangensis

Coffea grevei subsp.

mahajangensis, commonly known as the Mahajanga coffee, is a species native to Madagascar. It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments.

The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that contain two seeds, which are used to make coffee. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge.

This subspecies is noted for its unique flavor profile and is of interest to both botanists and coffee enthusiasts.
