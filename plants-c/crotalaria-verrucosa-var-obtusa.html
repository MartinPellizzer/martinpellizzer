Crotalaria verrucosa var. obtusa

Crotalaria verrucosa var. obtusa, commonly known as the warty crotalaria, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are often accompanied by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Crotalaria verrucosa var. obtusa is also noted for containing toxic alkaloids, making it potentially hazardous to livestock and humans.
