Coryphantha borwigii

Coryphantha borwigii, commonly known as the Borwig's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, ribbed stems that are covered in spines and are often green or blue-green in color. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are fleshy, edible berries that are typically yellow or red when mature.

Coryphantha borwigii is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing food and shelter.
