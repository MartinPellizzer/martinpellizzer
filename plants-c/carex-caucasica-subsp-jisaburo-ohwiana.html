Carex caucasica subsp. jisaburo-ohwiana

Carex caucasica subsp.

jisaburo-ohwiana, commonly known as the Jisaburo Ohwiana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. It is characterized by its robust rhizome system, which helps it thrive in wetland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet areas.
