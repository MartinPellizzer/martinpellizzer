Cestrum brevifolium var. microcarpum

Cestrum brevifolium var. microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom at night. The leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant's fruits are small, berry-like, and typically turn red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its nocturnal fragrance and attractive foliage.
