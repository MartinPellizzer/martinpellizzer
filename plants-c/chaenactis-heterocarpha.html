Chaenactis heterocarpha

Chaenactis heterocarpha, commonly known as the yellowfoot dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer.

Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
