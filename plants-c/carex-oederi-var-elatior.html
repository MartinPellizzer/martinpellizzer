Carex oederi var. elatior

Carex oederi var. elatior, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters, which are often hidden among the foliage.

Carex oederi var. elatior is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
