Calanthe wightii

Calanthe wightii, commonly known as the Wight's calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and possesses rhizomes that help anchor it in rocky or forested habitats. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Calanthe wightii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
