Cucurbita dioica

Cucurbita dioica, commonly known as the wild cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the gourd family, Cucurbitaceae.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinct male and female flowers on separate plants. The plant produces elongated, spiny fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The wild cucumber is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
