Convolvulus sagittatus var. hirtelus

Convolvulus sagittatus var. hirtelus, commonly known as the hairy arrowleaf morning glory, is a perennial vine native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces trumpet-shaped, violet to blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
