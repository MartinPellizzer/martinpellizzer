Crotalaria boliviensis

Crotalaria boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred shape, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Crotalaria boliviensis is also noted for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
