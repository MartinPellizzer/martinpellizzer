Chlorophytum baillaudii

Chlorophytum baillaudii, commonly known as the "Bamboo Plant," is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that resemble bamboo. The plant produces long, strap-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full to partial sunlight.

Chlorophytum baillaudii is popular as a decorative indoor plant due to its elegant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
