Camellia parviflora

Camellia parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf camellia, is a species native to China and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink during late winter to early spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Camellia parviflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
