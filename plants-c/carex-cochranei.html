Carex cochranei

Carex cochranei, commonly known as Cochrane's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of North America, particularly in the western United States. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its grass-like appearance features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets during the flowering season.

Carex cochranei is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
