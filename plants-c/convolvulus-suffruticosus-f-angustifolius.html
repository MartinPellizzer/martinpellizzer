Convolvulus suffruticosus f. angustifolius

Convolvulus suffruticosus f. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in gardens.

This plant is known for its adaptability and can be both a weed and a decorative plant depending on the context.
