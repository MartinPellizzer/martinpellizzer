Cardiandra alternifolia subsp. moellendorffii

Cardiandra alternifolia subsp.

moellendorffii, commonly known as the Moellendorff's heartleaf, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are alternate in arrangement along the stem.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and ecological studies.
