Cayaponia guianensis

Cayaponia guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese cayaponia, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
