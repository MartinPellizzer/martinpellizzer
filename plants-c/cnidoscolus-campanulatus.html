Cnidoscolus campanulatus

Cnidoscolus campanulatus, commonly known as the Mexican giant water spinach, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and long, slender stems that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically cultivated in wet or semi-aquatic environments. Its leaves are edible and have a mild, slightly bitter flavor, making it a popular ingredient in local cuisines.

Additionally, it is known for its ability to purify water, which contributes to its ecological importance in certain regions.
