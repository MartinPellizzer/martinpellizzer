Cissus mutabilis

Cissus mutabilis, commonly known as the variable grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that can change color depending on light exposure, often ranging from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often used as a decorative climbing plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
