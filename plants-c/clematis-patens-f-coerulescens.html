Clematis patens f. coerulescens

Clematis patens f. coerulescens, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a deciduous vine, producing compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Native to eastern Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
