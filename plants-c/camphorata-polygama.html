Camphorata polygama

Camphorata polygama, commonly known as the Indian camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. Its bark is thick and rough, often exuding a camphor-like scent, which gives the plant its common name.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
