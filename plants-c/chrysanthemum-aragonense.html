Chrysanthemum aragonense

Chrysanthemum aragonense, commonly known as the Aragon chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered blooms in the autumn.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
