Carex viridula var. jemtlandica

Carex viridula var. jemtlandica, commonly known as the Jemtland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
