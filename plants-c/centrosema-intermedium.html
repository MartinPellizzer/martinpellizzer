Centrosema intermedium

Centrosema intermedium, commonly known as the smallflower crownvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often used in erosion control due to its dense growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

Centrosema intermedium is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
