Coleus albicalyx

Coleus albicalyx, commonly known as the white-leaved coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, broad leaves that are predominantly white or pale green with contrasting veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Coleus albicalyx is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and ornamental plants.
