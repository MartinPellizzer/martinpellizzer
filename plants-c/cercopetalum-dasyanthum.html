Cercopetalum dasyanthum

Cercopetalum dasyanthum, commonly known as the Texas mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a fern-like appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant white or pink flowers in late spring, which are borne in racemes. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it has a strong, unpleasant odor when crushed.

It is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and rocky areas.
