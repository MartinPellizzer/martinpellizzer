Crucifera aleppica

Crucifera aleppica, commonly known as the Aleppo mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes and has pinnately compound leaves. It is often found growing in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
