Cranichis picta

Cranichis picta, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature intricate patterns and vibrant hues. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine flatwoods and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, delicate blooms during the spring and early summer.

Cranichis picta is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays an important role in the ecological diversity of its native environment.
