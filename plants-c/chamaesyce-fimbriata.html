Chamaesyce fimbriata

Chamaesyce fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Chamaesyce fimbriata is a hardy species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystems.
