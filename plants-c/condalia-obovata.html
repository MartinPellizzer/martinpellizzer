Condalia obovata

Condalia obovata, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lanceolate, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its deep root system.

The wood is hard and heavy, historically used for tool handles and construction.
