Campanula portenschlagiana var. hirsuta

Campanula portenschlagiana var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Alps.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "hirsuta," meaning hairy in Latin. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
