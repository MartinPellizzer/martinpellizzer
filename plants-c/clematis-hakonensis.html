Clematis hakonensis

Clematis hakonensis, commonly known as the Japanese vine or Hakonensis Clematis, is a deciduous climbing plant native to Japan.

It features striking, fragrant, single or double flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis hakonensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative vine.
