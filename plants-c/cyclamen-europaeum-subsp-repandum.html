Cyclamen europaeum subsp. repandum

Cyclamen europaeum subsp.

repandum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean and parts of the Middle East. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, hairy leaves and showy, bowl-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers cool, moist conditions. It blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Cyclamen europaeum subsp.

repandum is known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
