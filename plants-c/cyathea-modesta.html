Cyathea modesta

Cyathea modesta, commonly known as the modest tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically evergreen and can grow up to several meters in length.

Cyathea modesta plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
