Cirsium vaseyi var. hydrophilum

Cirsium vaseyi var. hydrophilum, commonly known as the water thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish-blue composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist soils and is often found along stream banks and in marshy areas.

Due to its preference for wet habitats, it plays an important role in aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
