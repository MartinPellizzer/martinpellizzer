Calycanthus floridus var. oblongatus

Calycanthus floridus var. oblongatus, commonly known as the Southern waxflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, reddish-maroon flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fragrant blossoms are borne on short, hairy pedicels and are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade with well-drained soil.
