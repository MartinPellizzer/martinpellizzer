Cassia nudicaulis

Cassia nudicaulis, commonly known as narrowleaf cassia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, upright stems with pairs of opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Cassia nudicaulis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens and native plant landscapes.
