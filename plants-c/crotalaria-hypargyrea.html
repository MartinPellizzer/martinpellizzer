Crotalaria hypargyrea

Crotalaria hypargyrea, commonly known as the silverleaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also noted for its potential as a forage crop and its role in soil enrichment.
