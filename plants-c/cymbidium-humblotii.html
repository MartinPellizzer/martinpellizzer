Cymbidium humblotii

Cymbidium humblotii, commonly known as the Humboldt orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces linear, leathery leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern along the stem. Its flowers are usually fragrant and come in shades of white, pink, and purple, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns.

Cymbidium humblotii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its striking appearance.
