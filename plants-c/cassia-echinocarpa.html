Cassia echinocarpa

Cassia echinocarpa, commonly known as the spiny cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in savannas and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical uses.
