Cyrtandra × imparilis

Cyrtandra × imparilis, commonly known as the hybrid beautyberry, is a flowering plant in the family Gesneriaceae.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Cyrtandra pedata and Cyrtandra simplex, characterized by its striking, violet-blue berries that appear in clusters. The plant is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. It produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
