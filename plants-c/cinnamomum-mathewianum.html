Cinnamomum mathewianum

Cinnamomum mathewianum, commonly known as Mathew's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that contain seeds, and its bark is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
