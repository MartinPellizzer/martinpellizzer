Cracca pedicellata

Cracca pedicellata, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish tint. The plant bears clusters of delicate, five-petaled flowers that are typically yellow or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

Cracca pedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
