Cytisus supinus var. gracilior

Cytisus supinus var. gracilior, commonly known as the slender Scotch broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the British Isles.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
