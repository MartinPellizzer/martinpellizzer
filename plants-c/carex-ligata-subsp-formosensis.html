Carex ligata subsp. formosensis

Carex ligata subsp.

formosensis, commonly known as the Formosan sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.
