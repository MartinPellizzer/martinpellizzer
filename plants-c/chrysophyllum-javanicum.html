Chrysophyllum javanicum

Chrysophyllum javanicum, commonly known as the Java chrysanthemum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
