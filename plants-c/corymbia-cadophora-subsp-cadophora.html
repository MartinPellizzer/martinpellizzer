Corymbia cadophora subsp. cadophora

Corymbia cadophora subsp.

cadophora, commonly known as the Sydney red gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, grey to reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, flaky layers.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy underside. It produces attractive, pendulous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink to red, which bloom in spring.

The species is adapted to a range of soil types and is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands.
