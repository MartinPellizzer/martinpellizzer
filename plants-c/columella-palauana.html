Columella palauana

Columella palauana, commonly known as the Palauan columnar cactus, is a unique succulent native to the island of Palau in the western Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height, often branching at the base. The plant has thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it produces small, yellowish flowers during the wet season.

Columella palauana is adapted to coastal and limestone habitats, making it a distinctive feature of Palau's flora.
