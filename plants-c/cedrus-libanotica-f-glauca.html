Cedrus libanotica f. glauca

Cedrus libanotica f. glauca, commonly known as the Blue Atlas Cedar, is a cultivar of the Atlas cedar, characterized by its striking bluish-gray foliage.

It is native to the mountainous regions of North Africa, particularly Morocco and Algeria. The plant is a slow-growing, evergreen conifer with a conical shape and dense, compact foliage. Its leaves are scale-like and emit a pleasant, resinous scent.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique color and hardiness.
