Chimaphila

Chimaphila, commonly known as candlewax plant or pinesap, is a genus of evergreen shrubs native to eastern North America.

These plants are characterized by their thick, waxy leaves that resemble candlewax, hence their common name. They typically grow in acidic, shaded environments such as forests and rocky soils. Chimaphila species are part of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries.

The flowers are usually small, bell-shaped, and can be white, pink, or red, depending on the species.
