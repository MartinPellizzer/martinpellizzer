Chrysanthemum variifolium

Chrysanthemum variifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaf chrysanthemum, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, ranging from lanceolate to ovate, which give it its common name. The plant produces daisy-like flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and fragrance.
