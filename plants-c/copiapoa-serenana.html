Copiapoa serenana

Copiapoa serenana, commonly known as the Serene Copiapoa, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, spindly stem that can grow up to two meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to the arid environment.

Copiapoa serenana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in arid gardens.
