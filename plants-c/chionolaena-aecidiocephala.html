Chionolaena aecidiocephala

Chionolaena aecidiocephala, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces berries that are toxic to livestock and humans, making it a potentially dangerous plant.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
