Campanula cervicina

Campanula cervicina, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and robust.

Campanula cervicina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
