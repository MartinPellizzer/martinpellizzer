Cirsium monspessulanum var. ferox

Cirsium monspessulanum var. ferox, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. Its robust structure and defensive spines help it thrive in harsh environments.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
