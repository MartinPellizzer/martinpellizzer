Crataegus anisophylla

Crataegus anisophylla, commonly known as the variable hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often variable in shape leaves, which can be either lobed or entire. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed areas.

Crataegus anisophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
