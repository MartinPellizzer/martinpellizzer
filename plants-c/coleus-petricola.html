Coleus petricola

Coleus petricola, commonly known as the rock coleus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage is often variegated, adding visual interest to the landscape.

Coleus petricola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable climates.
