Croton menyharthii

Croton menyharthii, commonly known as the African croton, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often variegated leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

Croton menyharthii thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
