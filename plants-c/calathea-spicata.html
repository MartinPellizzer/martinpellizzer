Calathea spicata

Calathea spicata, commonly known as the spike calathea, is a tropical plant belonging to the Marantaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, patterned leaves that often display intricate coloration and veining. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, white flowers, which are usually hidden beneath the foliage.

Calathea spicata thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
