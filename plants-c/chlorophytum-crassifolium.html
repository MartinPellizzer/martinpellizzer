Chlorophytum crassifolium

Chlorophytum crassifolium, commonly known as the Tiger's Tail, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often striped with green and white markings, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by blue or purple berries. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor displays.
