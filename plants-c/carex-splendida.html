Carex splendida

Carex splendida, commonly known as the shining sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink. Native to wetland and marsh environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic settings.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.
