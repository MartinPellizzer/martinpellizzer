Cerasus juliana

Cerasus juliana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese yuzu, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red, sour-tasting fruits. Cerasus juliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various climates.
