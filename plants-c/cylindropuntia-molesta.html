Cylindropuntia molesta

Cylindropuntia molesta, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines and are capable of growing up to several meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically followed by fruiting. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy plant in desert conditions.

Its structure and appearance make it a distinctive and visually striking member of the Cactaceae family.
