Cuphea polymorpha var. prostrata

Cuphea polymorpha var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate flame flower, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Mexico.

It features showy, tubular red bracts that resemble flames, surrounding small, white flowers. The plant has a spreading habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
