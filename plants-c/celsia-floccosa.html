Celsia floccosa

Celsia floccosa, commonly known as the woolly celsia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features dense, white to grayish-white woolly hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Celsia floccosa thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests and alpine meadows.
