Chloraea multiflora

Chloraea multiflora, commonly known as the common greenbottle bush, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, bottle-shaped seed pods that turn yellow as they mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Chloraea multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
