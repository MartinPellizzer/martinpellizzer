Caladium discolor

Caladium discolor, commonly known as the "Rainbow Caladium," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that ranges from white and pink to red and purple. The leaves are heart-shaped and often display intricate patterns, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Caladium discolor is typically propagated through tuberous roots and is often used in gardens for its vibrant, decorative appeal.
