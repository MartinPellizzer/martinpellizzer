Cerinthe minor var. auriculata

Cerinthe minor var. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate heartwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, heart-shaped leaves with wavy, auriculate margins. The plant produces clusters of small, bright blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
