Coffea arabica var. mauritiana

Coffea arabica var. mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius coffee.

It is a variant of the Arabica coffee species, native to the island of Mauritius. This plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The berries, which are typically red when mature, contain the coffee beans used for brewing.

It is less commonly cultivated compared to other Arabica varieties due to its specific climatic requirements.
