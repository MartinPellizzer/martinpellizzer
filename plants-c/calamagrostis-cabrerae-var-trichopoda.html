Calamagrostis cabrerae var. trichopoda

Calamagrostis cabrerae var. trichopoda, commonly known as the silky blue grass, is a perennial grass native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green or blue in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
