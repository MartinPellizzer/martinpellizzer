Candollea streptocarpa

Candollea streptocarpa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Native to South America, particularly Brazil, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
