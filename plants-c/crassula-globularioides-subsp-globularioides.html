Crassula globularioides subsp. globularioides

Crassula globularioides subsp.

globularioides, commonly known as the African snowball, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a compact, globular shape.

The plant produces small, star-shaped white flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
