Chamaecrista camporum

Chamaecrista camporum, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its seed pods are woody and covered in stiff hairs, containing small, angular seeds.

Chamaecrista camporum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
