Cucurbita maxima var. valparaisiana

Cucurbita maxima var. valparaisiana, commonly known as the Valparaiso pumpkin, is a cultivar of the giant pumpkin species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, round, and often ribbed fruit that can weigh several hundred pounds. The plant produces broad, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that are typically separate from the female flowers. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires a long growing season.

This variety is prized for its impressive size and is often cultivated for competitive pumpkin growing events.
