Campanula rotundifolia var. gypsicola

Campanula rotundifolia var. gypsicola, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and calcareous habitats in Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has rounded, hairy leaves that are adapted to thrive in well-drained, dry soils. It is often found growing on limestone or gypsum-rich soils, which contribute to its specific ecological niche.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in rocky environments.
