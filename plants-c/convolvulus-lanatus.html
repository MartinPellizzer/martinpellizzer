Convolvulus lanatus

Convolvulus lanatus, commonly known as the silky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the evening. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus lanatus is a perennial species that can grow up to 1 meter in length, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
