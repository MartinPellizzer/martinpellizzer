Centaurea × rossiana

Centaurea × rossiana, commonly known as the Ross's knapweed, is a hybrid plant species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive in certain environments.
