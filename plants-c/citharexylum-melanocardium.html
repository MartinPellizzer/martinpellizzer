Citharexylum melanocardium

Citharexylum melanocardium, commonly known as the black-leafed cithara, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are often visited by insects and birds. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a distinctively rough texture.

Citharexylum melanocardium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
