Carex composita f. simplicior

Carex composita f. simplicior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers in the summer.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
