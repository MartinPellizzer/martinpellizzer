Cirsium tartaricum

Cirsium tartaricum, commonly known as the Tartarian thistle, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its robust, upright growth habit.

Cirsium tartaricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
