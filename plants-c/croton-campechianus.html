Croton campechianus

Croton campechianus, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from bright green to deep red and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Croton campechianus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
