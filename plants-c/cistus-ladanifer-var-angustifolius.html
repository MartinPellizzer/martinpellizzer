Cistus ladanifer var. angustifolius

Cistus ladanifer var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a distinctive resinous scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

Its leaves and flowers contain essential oils, which have been historically used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
