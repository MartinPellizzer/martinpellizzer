Carex crinita

Carex crinita, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color. Carex crinita thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
