Carduus defloratus var. ceretanus

Carduus defloratus var. ceretanus, commonly known as the pale thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed soils. The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves and produces purple to pink composite flower heads. Its leaves are generally grayish-green with a woolly texture, and the flowers are surrounded by a series of bracts.

This variant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
