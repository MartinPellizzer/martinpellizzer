Cardamine pratensis subsp. mariae

Cardamine pratensis subsp.

mariae, commonly known as the meadow cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, white or yellowish flowers in racemes.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates.

The subspecies mariae is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flowering habits compared to the typical form of Cardamine pratensis.
