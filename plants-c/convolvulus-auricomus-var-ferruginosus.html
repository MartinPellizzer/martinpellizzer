Convolvulus auricomus var. ferruginosus

Convolvulus auricomus var. ferruginosus, commonly known as the rusty leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a climbing or trailing plant with hairy stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
