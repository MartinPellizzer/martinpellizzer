Cassia pinetorum var. picardae

Cassia pinetorum var. picardae, commonly known as the Picard's cassia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and compound leaves that are often feather-like in appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers clustered in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
