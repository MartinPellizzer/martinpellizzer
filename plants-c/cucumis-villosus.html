Cucumis villosus

Cucumis villosus, commonly known as the hairy gourd, is a vining plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces large, elongated fruits that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches a mature size and turns a pale green to yellowish color.
