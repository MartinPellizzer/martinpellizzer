Coreopsis teotepecensis

Coreopsis teotepecensis, commonly known as the Teotepec coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Oaxaca, Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
