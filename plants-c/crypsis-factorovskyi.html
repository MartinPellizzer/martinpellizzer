Crypsis factorovskyi

Crypsis factorovskyi, commonly known as the Factorovsky's cryptogram, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring intricate, almost cryptic patterns that may serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant exhibits a low-growing habit, often forming dense mats that help it survive in harsh, cold environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in late summer, which may contribute to its elusive reputation.

Due to its restricted habitat and limited genetic diversity, Crypsis factorovskyi is considered critically endangered and requires urgent conservation efforts.
