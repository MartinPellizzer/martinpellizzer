Coreopsis mexicana

Coreopsis mexicana, commonly known as Mexican coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its bright yellow to orange flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis mexicana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
