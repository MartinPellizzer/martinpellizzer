Carduncellus pinnatus f. araneosus

Carduncellus pinnatus f. araneosus, commonly known as the spider-thorned carduncellus, is a unique variant of the carduncellus plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky bracts that resemble the legs of a spider, giving it its common name. The plant is native to certain regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unusual appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
