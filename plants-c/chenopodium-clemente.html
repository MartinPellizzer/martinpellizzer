Chenopodium clemente

Chenopodium clemente, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and small, edible leaves. The plant thrives in hot, dry climates and is adapted to poor soils, making it a hardy species for xerophytic environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor.

Chenopodium clemente is also valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of vitamins and minerals.
