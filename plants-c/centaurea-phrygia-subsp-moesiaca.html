Centaurea phrygia subsp. moesiaca

Centaurea phrygia subsp.

moesiaca, commonly known as the Moesian cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to violet flowers and slender, branching stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
