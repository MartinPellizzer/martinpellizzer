Centaurea crinita

Centaurea crinita, commonly known as the curlytop cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and feathery, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

Centaurea crinita is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
