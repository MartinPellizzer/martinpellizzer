Carex straminea f. richii

Carex straminea f. richii, commonly known as the richii form of the yellow sedge, is a variant of the Carex straminea species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces cylindrical spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
