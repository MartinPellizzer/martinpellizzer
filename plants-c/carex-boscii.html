Carex boscii

Carex boscii, commonly known as the Bosc's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Carex boscii is often found in shaded areas and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
