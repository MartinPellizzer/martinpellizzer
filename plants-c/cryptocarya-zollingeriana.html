Cryptocarya zollingeriana

Cryptocarya zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger's cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and a strong, spicy scent.

The fruit is a small, dark berry that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
