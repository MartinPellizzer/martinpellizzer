Calea formosa

Calea formosa, commonly known as the "Mexican night bloomer," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and features a rosette of broad, waxy leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive central disc and reflexed petals.

Calea formosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
