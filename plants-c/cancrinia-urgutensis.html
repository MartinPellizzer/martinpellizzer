Cancrinia urgutensis

Cancrinia urgutensis, commonly known as the Urgut sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system. It produces yellow or white flowers that bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
