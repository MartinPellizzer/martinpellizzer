Castanola wallichii

Castanola wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan wild pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to pears, though they are typically smaller and more acidic. It thrives in temperate to cold climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

Castanola wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
