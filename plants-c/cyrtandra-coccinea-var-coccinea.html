Cyrtandra coccinea var. coccinea

Cyrtandra coccinea var. coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet heliconia, is a striking tropical plant native to the islands of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that enclose clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant belongs to the Heliconiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. Its leaves are typically large, elongated, and have a waxy texture.

The species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal or lowland forests.
