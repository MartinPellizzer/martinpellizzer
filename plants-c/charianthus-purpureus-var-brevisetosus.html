Charianthus purpureus var. brevisetosus

Charianthus purpureus var. brevisetosus, commonly known as the purple crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bristly seed heads that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable species for ecological restoration.
