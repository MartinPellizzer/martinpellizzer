Cytisus proliferus var. nanus

Cytisus proliferus var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf firethorn, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, bright orange-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has wiry, thorny stems and small, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
