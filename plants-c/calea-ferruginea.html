Calea ferruginea

Calea ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Calea ferruginea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
