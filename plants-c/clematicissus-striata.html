Clematicissus striata

Clematicissus striata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and distinctive silvery-blue foliage. The leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a velvety texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also known for its ability to attract beneficial insects.
