Croton macrobothrys var. microbotrys

Croton macrobothrys var. microbotrys, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, palmately lobed leaves that are typically bright green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
