Canavalia ensiformis var. truncata

Canavalia ensiformis var. truncata, commonly known as the sickle bean or truncated cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The variety truncata is distinguished by its truncated leaf tips, giving it a unique appearance compared to other canavalia species.
