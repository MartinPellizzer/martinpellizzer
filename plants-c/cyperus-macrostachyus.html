Cyperus macrostachyus

Cyperus macrostachyus, commonly known as the broad-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, often appearing in late summer or early autumn.

Cyperus macrostachyus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
