Centaurea alfonsoi

Centaurea alfonsoi, commonly known as Alfonso's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features striking blue to purple petals and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
