Convolvulus argillicola

Convolvulus argillicola, commonly known as the clay bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Convolvulus argillicola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

It is a member of the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its ability to spread rapidly through its extensive root system.
