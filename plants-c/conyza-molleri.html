Conyza molleri

Conyza molleri, commonly known as the hairy goldenweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Conyza molleri is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, and it has a cosmopolitan distribution due to its adaptability.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and natural environments.
