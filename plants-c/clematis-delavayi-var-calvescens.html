Clematis delavayi var. calvescens

Clematis delavayi var. calvescens, commonly known as the Himalayan clematis, is a climbing vine native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, often with a distinctive purple or brownish throat. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that are follicles containing numerous seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
