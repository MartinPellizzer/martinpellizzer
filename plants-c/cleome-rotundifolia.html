Cleome rotundifolia

Cleome rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants.

Cleome rotundifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
