Campomanesia eugenioides var. desertorum

Campomanesia eugenioides var. desertorum, commonly known as the desert persimmon, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to persimmons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a food source in native ecosystems.
