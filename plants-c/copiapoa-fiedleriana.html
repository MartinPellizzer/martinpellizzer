Copiapoa fiedleriana

Copiapoa fiedleriana, commonly known as the Fiedler's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a short, thick stem and produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant has a dense, spiny covering that helps it retain water in its arid environment. Its roots are shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from infrequent rains.

Copiapoa fiedleriana is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
