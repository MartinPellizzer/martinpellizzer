Convolvulus stachydifolius var. villosus

Convolvulus stachydifolius var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy bladderbladder, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
