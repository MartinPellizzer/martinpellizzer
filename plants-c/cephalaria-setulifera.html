Cephalaria setulifera

Cephalaria setulifera, commonly known as the hairy cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy textures, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
