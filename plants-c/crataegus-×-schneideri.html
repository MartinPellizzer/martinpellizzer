Crataegus × schneideri

Crataegus × schneideri, commonly known as the Schneider hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn fruit.
