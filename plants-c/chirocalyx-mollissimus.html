Chirocalyx mollissimus

Chirocalyx mollissimus, commonly known as the silky calyx plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive tactile quality. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
