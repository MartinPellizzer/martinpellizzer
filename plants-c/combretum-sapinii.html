Combretum sapinii

Combretum sapinii, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Combretum sapinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
