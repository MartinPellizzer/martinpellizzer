Crepinia luppeana

Crepinia luppeana, commonly known as the Luppean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikelets during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating.

Crepinia luppeana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
