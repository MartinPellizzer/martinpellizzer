Cyathea mamillata

Cyathea mamillata, commonly known as the mamillate tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bright green and have a smooth, leathery texture.

Cyathea mamillata plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests by contributing to canopy structure and providing habitat for various organisms.
