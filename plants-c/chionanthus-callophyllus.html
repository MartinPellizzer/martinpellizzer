Chionanthus callophyllus

Chionanthus callophyllus, commonly known as the whiteflower viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that ripens in the fall.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
