Cucurbita melopepo var. mitriformis

Cucurbita melopepo var. mitriformis, commonly known as the mitre-shaped gourd, is a variant of the bottle gourd native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often asymmetrical fruit shape, which resembles a mitre or a crown. The plant produces large, green, ribbed leaves and bears yellow flowers that transition to orange or red as the fruit matures. The fruit is typically harvested when mature, and it is valued for its versatility in cooking and traditional uses.

Its botanical features make it a unique and culturally significant species within the Cucurbitaceae family.
