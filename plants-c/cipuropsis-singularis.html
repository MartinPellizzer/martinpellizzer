Cipuropsis singularis

Cipuropsis singularis, commonly known as the rare desert orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, succulent-like growth habit with thick, waxy leaves adapted to store water. The plant produces striking, violet-blue flowers with a complex structure typical of the orchid family. It blooms during the brief rainy season, relying on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to habitat loss, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
