Cissus repanda var. subferruginea

Cissus repanda var. subferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery leaves that often display a reddish-brown or rusty hue on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures.
