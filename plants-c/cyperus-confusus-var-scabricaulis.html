Cyperus confusus var. scabricaulis

Cyperus confusus var. scabricaulis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

This variant is distinguished by its rough, textured stems, which give it its common name.
