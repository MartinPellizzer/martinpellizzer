Cocculus diversifolius var. cinereus

Cocculus diversifolius var. cinereus, commonly known as the gray cocculus, is a variant of the cocculus vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its hairy, palmately lobed leaves and showy, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The fruit is a distinctive, inflated, spiny capsule that turns red when mature. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Menispermaceae family.
