Capparis sicula subsp. sindiana

Capparis sicula subsp.

sindiana, commonly known as the Sindian caper, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent. It features thorny stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, edible fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
