Claytonia peirsonii subsp. yorkii

Claytonia peirsonii subsp.

yorkii, commonly known as York's spring beauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Appalachian Mountains. It produces delicate, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
