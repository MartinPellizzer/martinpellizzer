Caruncularia jacquinii

Caruncularia jacquinii, commonly known as the red-flowered sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a composite head. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Caruncularia jacquinii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
