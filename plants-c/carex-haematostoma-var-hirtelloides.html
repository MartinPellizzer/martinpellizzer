Carex haematostoma var. hirtelloides

Carex haematostoma var. hirtelloides, commonly known as the hairy blood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and distinctive reddish-brown stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, often with a hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are wind-pollinated.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
