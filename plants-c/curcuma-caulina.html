Curcuma caulina

Curcuma caulina, commonly known as the purple ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lanceolate, dark green leaves with a slight pubescence on the underside. Its flowers are typically pale purple to white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Curcuma caulina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
