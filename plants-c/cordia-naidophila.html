Cordia naidophila

Cordia naidophila, commonly known as the red cordia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
