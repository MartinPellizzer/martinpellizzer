Coelogyne wallichiana

Coelogyne wallichiana, commonly known as the Walsingham's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Coelogyne wallichiana is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
