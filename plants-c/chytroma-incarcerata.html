Chytroma incarcerata

Chytroma incarcerata, commonly known as the prison lichen, is a unique lichen species that exhibits a complex symbiotic relationship between its fungal and algal components.

It is characterized by its dense, crust-like growth form, which often adheres tightly to rock surfaces in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant's thallus contains a high concentration of secondary metabolites, which contribute to its ability to withstand extreme environmental conditions. Its reproductive structures are typically embedded within the thallus, making them difficult to observe without specialized techniques.

Overall, Chytroma incarcerata plays a significant ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for other organisms.
