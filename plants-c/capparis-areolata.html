Capparis areolata

Capparis areolata, commonly known as the spotted caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces spherical, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically oval and have a slightly rough texture.

Capparis areolata is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
