Chamaesyce remyi var. leptopoda

Chamaesyce remyi var. leptopoda, commonly known as the slender prostrate spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of the southeastern United States.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it an effective ground cover in certain ecological settings.
