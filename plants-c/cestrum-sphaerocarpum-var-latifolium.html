Cestrum sphaerocarpum var. latifolium

Cestrum sphaerocarpum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white, yellow, or red. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are broad and ovate, with a glossy surface. It produces small, spherical berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
