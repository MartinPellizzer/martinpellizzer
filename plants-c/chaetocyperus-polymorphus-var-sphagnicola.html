Chaetocyperus polymorphus var. sphagnicola

Chaetocyperus polymorphus var. sphagnicola, commonly known as the bog hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in acidic, waterlogged soils and is often found in peat bogs and swampy areas. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and contribute to the ecological structure of its habitat.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a role in maintaining the biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.
