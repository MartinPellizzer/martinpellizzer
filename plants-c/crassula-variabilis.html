Crassula variabilis

Crassula variabilis, commonly known as the variable crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that can vary in shape and size. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Crassula variabilis is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
