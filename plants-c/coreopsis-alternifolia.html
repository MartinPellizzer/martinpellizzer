Coreopsis alternifolia

Coreopsis alternifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow or orange blooms. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, alternate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant plants.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance in natural and cultivated settings.
