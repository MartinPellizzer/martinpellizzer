Celastrus euonymoides

Celastrus euonymoides, commonly known as the false crossvine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife. Its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs, and it has a woody, branching structure.

Celastrus euonymoides is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and seasonal interest.
