Carex filipes var. kuzakaiensis

Carex filipes var. kuzakaiensis, commonly known as the Kuzakaiensis sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. Native to specific regions in East Asia, this variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, forming dense mats that can stabilize soil in wetland or riparian habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
