Cyperus Polystachyos Var. Micans

Cyperus micans, scientifically known as Cyperus polystachyos var. micans, is a striking member of the sedge family native to tropical and subtropical regions. This plant is recognized for its dense, grass-like foliage and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. Its slender, upright stems are often adorned with clusters of small, pale green flowers that bloom in summer. Cyperus micans is commonly used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its capacity to stabilize soil in waterlogged areas. Despite its beauty, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
