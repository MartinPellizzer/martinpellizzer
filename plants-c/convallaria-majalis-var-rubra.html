Convallaria majalis var. rubra

Convallaria majalis var. rubra, commonly known as the red laceflower, is a variant of the traditional lily of the valley.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which contrast with the usual white blooms of the standard variety. The plant features slender, arching stems with clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical name reflects its botanical classification within the genus Convallaria.
