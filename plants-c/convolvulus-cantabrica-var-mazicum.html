Convolvulus cantabrica var. mazicum

Convolvulus cantabrica var. mazicum, commonly known as the Cantabrian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive pink to purple tubular flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in Mediterranean flora.
