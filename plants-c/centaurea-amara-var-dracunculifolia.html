Centaurea amara var. dracunculifolia

Centaurea amara var. dracunculifolia, commonly known as bitter centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland or scrubland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically blue or purple in color.

The species is known for its bitter taste, which has led to its use in traditional herbal medicine.
