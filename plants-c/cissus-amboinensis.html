Cissus amboinensis

Cissus amboinensis, commonly known as the climbing fig or devil's backbone, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, winged stems that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
