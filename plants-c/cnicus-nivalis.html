Cnicus nivalis

Cnicus nivalis, commonly known as the alpine sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cold, mountainous environments. Its leaves are usually woolly and lobed, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Cnicus nivalis plays a role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments and supports local insect populations.
