Ceropegia tavalla

Ceropegia tavalla, commonly known as the "string of hearts" or "heartleaf," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia tavalla is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
