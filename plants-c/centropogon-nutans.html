Centropogon nutans

Centropogon nutans, commonly known as the drooping false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in clusters, with bracts that are often mistaken for petals.

The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
