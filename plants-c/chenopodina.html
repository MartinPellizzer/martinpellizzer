Chenopodina

Chenopodina, commonly known as goosefoot, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are characterized by their alternate, often succulent leaves that may have a rough or hairy texture. They are typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Many species are used as ornamental plants due to their distinctive foliage.

Some members of the genus are also cultivated for their edible leaves or seeds.
