Carex louisianica

Carex louisianica, commonly known as Louisiana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of its stems.

Louisiana sedge plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
