Chelone barbata

Chelone barbata, commonly known as the scarlet globeflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink to red in color, arranged in globe-shaped clusters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Chelone barbata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
