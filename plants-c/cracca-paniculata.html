Cracca paniculata

Cracca paniculata, commonly known as the panicled cracca, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Cracca paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
