Chamaenerion × sericeum

Chamaenerion × sericeum, commonly known as silver fireweed, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species, Chamaenerion angustifolium and Chamaenerion lateriflorum.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize open spaces make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
