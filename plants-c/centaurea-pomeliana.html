Centaurea pomeliana

Centaurea pomeliana, commonly known as the Pomeliana bluemink, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its striking blue to purple flowers. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Centaurea pomeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
