Calceolaria macrocalyx

Calceolaria macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered slipperwort, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and colorful blooms.

Calceolaria macrocalyx is a perennial species that prefers well-drained soil and partial shade.
