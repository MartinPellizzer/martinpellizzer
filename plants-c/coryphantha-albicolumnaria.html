Coryphantha albicolumnaria

Coryphantha albicolumnaria, commonly known as the white-columned cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered with white, woolly spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated in arid gardens for its ornamental value.
