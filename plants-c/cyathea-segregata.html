Cyathea segregata

Cyathea segregata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk and enables the plant to store water and nutrients.

Cyathea segregata plays an important ecological role in tropical forests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
