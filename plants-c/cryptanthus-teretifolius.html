Cryptanthus teretifolius

Cryptanthus teretifolius, commonly known as the slender-leafed winterbloom, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Cryptanthus teretifolius is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun.
