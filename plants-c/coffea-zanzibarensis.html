Coffea zanzibarensis

Coffea zanzibarensis, commonly known as Zanzibar coffee, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the East African islands of Zanzibar and Pemba. This plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and produces red or yellow berries containing seeds used for coffee production. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is often cultivated for specialty coffee beans.
