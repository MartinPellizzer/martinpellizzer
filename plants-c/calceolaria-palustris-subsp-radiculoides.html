Calceolaria palustris subsp. radiculoides

Calceolaria palustris subsp.

radiculoides, commonly known as the marsh slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in South America. It is characterized by its delicate, slipper-shaped flowers that are often pink or purple in color.

The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-rich soils near water sources. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
