Centaurea incana var. virens

Centaurea incana var. virens, commonly known as the Greenhead Centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are small, blue to violet in color, and arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
