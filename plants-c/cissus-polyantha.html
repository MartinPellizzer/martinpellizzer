Cissus polyantha

Cissus polyantha, commonly known as the silver leaf creeper, is a tropical vine native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and rapid growth habit. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a unique appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Cissus polyantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
