Cyperus puncticulatus

Cyperus puncticulatus, commonly known as the punctuated flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Cyperus puncticulatus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
