Cleome hochstetteri

Cleome hochstetteri, commonly known as the desert spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, pink or white flowers that resemble spider legs. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

Cleome hochstetteri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
