Cercocarpus parvifolius

Cercocarpus parvifolius, commonly known as the narrowleaf curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and distinctive, curled fruiting bodies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as chaparral and woodland areas. Its flowers are usually yellow or green and appear in clusters, while the fruits are small, woody capsules.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
