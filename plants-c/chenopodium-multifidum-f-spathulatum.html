Chenopodium multifidum f. spathulatum

Chenopodium multifidum f. spathulatum, commonly known as broadleaf goosefoot, is a variant of the species Chenopodium multifidum.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to parts of North America, particularly the western United States. The plant features broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powdery coating, known as farina. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are typically dispersed by wind.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
