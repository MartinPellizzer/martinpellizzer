Cistus munbyi

Cistus munbyi, commonly known as the Munby's rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Cistus munbyi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
