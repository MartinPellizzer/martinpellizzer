Clathrotropis flava

Clathrotropis flava, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
