Citrus × aurata var. pomum-adami

Citrus × aurata var. pomum-adami, commonly known as the pomelo orange or Chinese pomelo, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, oval-shaped fruits that can weigh up to 10 pounds, with thick, yellow-orange rinds and juicy, segmented white flesh. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves and a robust, spreading growth habit. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

The fruit is often used for its sweet, aromatic pulp, which is valued in both culinary and medicinal applications.
