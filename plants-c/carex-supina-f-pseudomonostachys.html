Carex supina f. pseudomonostachys

Carex supina f. pseudomonostachys, commonly known as the prostrate sedge.

This variant is a compact, low-growing perennial sedge characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often hidden among the foliage.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.
