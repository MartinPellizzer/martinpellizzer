Cymbidium aberrans

Cymbidium aberrans, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces large, showy flower spikes with multiple blooms. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive yellow lip.

Cymbidium aberrans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
