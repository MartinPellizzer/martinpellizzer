Calliandra caeciliae

Calliandra caeciliae, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Calliandra caeciliae is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
