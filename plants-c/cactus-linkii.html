Cactus linkii

Cactus linkii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and spiny, segmented stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its root system is shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from infrequent rains.

Cactus linkii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
