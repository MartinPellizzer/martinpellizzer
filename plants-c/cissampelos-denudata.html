Cissampelos denudata

Cissampelos denudata, commonly known as the naked stem moon vine, is a climbing plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody, often hairless stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in leaf axils. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.
