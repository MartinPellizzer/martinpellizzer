Commicarpus helenae var. barbatus

Commicarpus helenae var. barbatus, commonly known as the barbed Helen's commicarpus, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
