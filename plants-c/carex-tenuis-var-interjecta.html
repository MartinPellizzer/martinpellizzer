Carex tenuis var. interjecta

Carex tenuis var. interjecta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
