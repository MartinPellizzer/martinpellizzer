Ceratostylis vonroemeri

Ceratostylis vonroemeri, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Guinea.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and striking yellow flowers that resemble torches. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest undergrowth, thriving in humid environments. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
