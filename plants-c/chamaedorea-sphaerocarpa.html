Chamaedorea sphaerocarpa

Chamaedorea sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-shaped bamboo, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a compact, evergreen plant that features a single, slender trunk covered in smooth, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are a source of nutrition for wildlife. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
