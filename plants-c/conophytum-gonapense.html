Conophytum gonapense

Conophytum gonapense, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail" or "Cape Hare's foot," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. It is often grown as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Conophytum gonapense prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
