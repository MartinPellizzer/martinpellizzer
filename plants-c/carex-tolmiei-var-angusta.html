Carex tolmiei var. angusta

Carex tolmiei var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for wetland restoration projects.
