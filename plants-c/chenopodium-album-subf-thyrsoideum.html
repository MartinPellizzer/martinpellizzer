Chenopodium album subf. thyrsoideum

Chenopodium album subf.

thyrsoideum, commonly known as fat hen, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with succulent, ribbed stems that often have a bluish-green hue.

The leaves are alternate, ovate to rhombic in shape, and typically have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense, branched inflorescences, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

It is a common weed with a cosmopolitan distribution and is sometimes used as a food source in certain regions.
