Carex parallela var. pauciflora

Carex parallela var. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
