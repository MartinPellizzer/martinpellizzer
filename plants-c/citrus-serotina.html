Citrus serotina

Citrus serotina, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The plant produces small, sour-tasting fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Its flowers are white and fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Citrus serotina is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
