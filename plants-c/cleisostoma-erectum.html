Cleisostoma erectum

Cleisostoma erectum, commonly known as the erect morning glory, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
