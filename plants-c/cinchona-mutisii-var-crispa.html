Cinchona mutisii var. crispa

Cinchona mutisii var. crispa, commonly known as the waxy cinchona, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive waxy coating on the leaves, which gives it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is part of the Rubiaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in the production of quinine.
