Cleistes gracilis

Cleistes gracilis, commonly known as the slender cleistocarp, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Oenotheraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that remain closed during the day. Its unique floral structure and pollination strategy make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
