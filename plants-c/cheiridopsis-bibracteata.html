Cheiridopsis bibracteata

Cheiridopsis bibracteata, commonly known as the two-bracted ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscaping efforts.
