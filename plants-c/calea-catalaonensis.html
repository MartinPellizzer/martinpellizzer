Calea catalaonensis

Calea catalaonensis, commonly known as the Mexican ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance due to its translucent, waxy petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a short flowering period. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in a head.

Calea catalaonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
