Croton pycnocephalus var. chamaepitys

Croton pycnocephalus var. chamaepitys, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features thick, waxy leaves with a distinctive yellow-green coloration and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
