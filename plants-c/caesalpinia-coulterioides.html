Caesalpinia coulterioides

Caesalpinia coulterioides, commonly known as the Texas wisteria, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, showy yellow-orange flower clusters. The plant produces long, bean-like pods that contain seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
