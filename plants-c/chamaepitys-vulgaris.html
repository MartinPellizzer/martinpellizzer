Chamaepitys vulgaris

Chamaepitys vulgaris, commonly known as the mountain ash, is a deciduous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red or orange berries. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it is often found in forested areas across Europe, Asia, and North America.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
