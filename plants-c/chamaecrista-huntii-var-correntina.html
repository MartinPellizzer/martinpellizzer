Chamaecrista huntii var. correntina

Chamaecrista huntii var. correntina, commonly known as the Brazilian ticktrefoil, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
