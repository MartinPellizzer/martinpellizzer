Cardamine altaica

Cardamine altaica, commonly known as the Siberian watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is native to regions of Asia, including Siberia and parts of China.
