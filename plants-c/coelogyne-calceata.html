Coelogyne calceata

Coelogyne calceata, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often with a yellow or green throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and clustered, supporting the plant's growth and storage of nutrients.

Coelogyne calceata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and unique floral display.
