Campanula herminii

Campanula herminii, commonly known as the Herminii bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula herminii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early summer blooms.
