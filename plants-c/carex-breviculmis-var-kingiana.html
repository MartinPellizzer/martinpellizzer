Carex breviculmis var. kingiana

Carex breviculmis var. kingiana, commonly known as King's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms clumps of narrow, dark green leaves that are short and stiff, often reaching heights of 10 to 15 inches. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded, wet environments.
