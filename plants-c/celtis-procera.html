Celtis procera

Celtis procera, commonly known as the African sugarberry or Natal tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Elm family (Ulmaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, fuzzy fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in riverine forests and can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a significant component of its native ecosystems.

Celtis procera is valued for its hard wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
