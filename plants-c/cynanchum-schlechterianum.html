Cynanchum schlechterianum

Cynanchum schlechterianum, commonly known as Schlechter's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to regions of Central Asia and parts of China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, containing compounds that have been studied for their medicinal properties.

Cynanchum schlechterianum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its traditional use in herbal medicine.
