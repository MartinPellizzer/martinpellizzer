Carex cespitosa f. major

Carex cespitosa f. major, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and upright growth habit, with tall, slender stems that often exceed 1 meter in height. The plant produces spikelets of small, spike-like flowers, typically in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
