Cyperus alulatus

Cyperus alulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a tight spiral. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.

Cyperus alulatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
