Carex luteola

Carex luteola, commonly known as yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex luteola thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along streambanks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
