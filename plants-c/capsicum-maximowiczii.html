Capsicum maximowiczii

Capsicum maximowiczii, commonly known as the Mexican chili or wild chili, is a species native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow or orange flowers that are followed by bright red, elongated fruits. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are hairy as well.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
