Cestrum franceyi

Cestrum franceyi, commonly known as the French cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its fruits are small, berry-like capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Cestrum franceyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant evening fragrance.
