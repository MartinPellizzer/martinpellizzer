Cephalaria leucantha var. latifolia

Cephalaria leucantha var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved whitehead, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
