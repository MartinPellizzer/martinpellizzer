Cybianthus bracteolatus

Cybianthus bracteolatus, commonly known as the bracteolatus cybianthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like blooms with yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged alternately along the stem.

Cybianthus bracteolatus is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
