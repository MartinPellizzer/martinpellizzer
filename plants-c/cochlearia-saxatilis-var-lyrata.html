Cochlearia saxatilis var. lyrata

Cochlearia saxatilis var. lyrata, commonly known as the lyrated rock-cress, is a flowering plant native to rocky and coastal environments in Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in sunny, exposed areas. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in fine hairs.
