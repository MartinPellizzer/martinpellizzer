Clitoria falcata var. aurantiaca

Clitoria falcata var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden butterfly pea, is a striking flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and features trifoliate leaves with a heart-shaped leaflet. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various soil types.
