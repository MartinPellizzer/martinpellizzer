Calathea leonoriae

Calathea leonoriae, commonly known as the leopard calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, mottled foliage with a distinctive leopard-like pattern. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with intricate coloration that includes shades of green, silver, and pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Calathea leonoriae is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
