Colchicum velutinum

Colchicum velutinum, commonly known as the velvet colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features basal leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. It produces red or orange bulbs that are toxic and contain the alkaloid colchicine.

Colchicum velutinum is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
