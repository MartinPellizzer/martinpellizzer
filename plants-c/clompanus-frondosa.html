Clompanus frondosa

Clompanus frondosa, commonly known as the leafy spirea, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically serrated and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
