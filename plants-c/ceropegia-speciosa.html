Ceropegia speciosa

Ceropegia speciosa, commonly known as the flame violet, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features tuberous roots and produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia speciosa is also known for its ability to produce aerial roots, which help it climb and anchor itself to surfaces.
