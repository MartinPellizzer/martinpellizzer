Centropogon casapiensis

Centropogon casapiensis, commonly known as the Casapiensis false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive inflorescence structure. The plant typically grows in moist, mountainous habitats, often in cloud forests or high-altitude grasslands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster.

Centropogon casapiensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
