Correa cordifolia

Correa cordifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a leathery texture, while the flowers range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This plant is well-suited to coastal areas and can tolerate light frosts.
