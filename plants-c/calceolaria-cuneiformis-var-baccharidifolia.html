Calceolaria cuneiformis var. baccharidifolia

Calceolaria cuneiformis var. baccharidifolia, commonly known as the baccharidifolia slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyces that resemble tiny slippers, a feature that gives the genus its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in unique and colorful plant specimens.
