Crataegus effera

Crataegus effera, commonly known as the thorny crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters, featuring thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or yellowish fruits called drupes. Its botanical characteristics include a pome fruit type and a preference for well-drained soils in full sun.

Crataegus effera is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and wildlife habitat.
