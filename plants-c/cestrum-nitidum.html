Cestrum nitidum

Cestrum nitidum, commonly known as the white cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

Cestrum nitidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms and pleasant scent.
