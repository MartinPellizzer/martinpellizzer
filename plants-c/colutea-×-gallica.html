Colutea × gallica

Colutea × gallica, commonly known as the red clover.

It is a hybrid plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant is native to Europe and has been cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and disturbed areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity in its habitat.
