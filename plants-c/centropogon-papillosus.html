Centropogon papillosus

Centropogon papillosus, commonly known as the silky centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves and produces small, white, silky-tipped bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
