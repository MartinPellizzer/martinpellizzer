Cereus russelianus

Cereus russelianus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a striking appearance that enhances its ornamental value.
