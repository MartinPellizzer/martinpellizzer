Carissa cornifolia

Carissa cornifolia, commonly known as the horned cactus or horned ball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that are often covered in small, white, sticky hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
