Celosia salicifolia

Celosia salicifolia, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit and is known for its vibrant, feather-like bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by the showy bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Celosia salicifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
