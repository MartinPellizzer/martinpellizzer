Cistus × verguinii

Cistus × verguinii, commonly known as the hybrid rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Cistus × verguinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
