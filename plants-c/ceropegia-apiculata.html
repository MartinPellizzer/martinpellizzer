Ceropegia apiculata

Ceropegia apiculata, commonly known as the Snowflake vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia apiculata is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
