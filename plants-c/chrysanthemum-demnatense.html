Chrysanthemum demnatense

Chrysanthemum demnatense, commonly known as the Demnat's chrysanthemum, is a species native to parts of Asia, particularly found in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange, typically blooming in the autumn season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for both garden aesthetics and traditional medicinal purposes.
