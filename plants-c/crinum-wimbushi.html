Crinum wimbushi

Crinum wimbushi, commonly known as the Wimbushi lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are strap-like and evergreen, providing a lush foliage base throughout the year.

Crinum wimbushi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.
