Carex × nikaii

Carex × nikaii, commonly known as the Japanese marsh sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and compact, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets that are often covered in dense, silky hairs.

Carex × nikaii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
