Circaea mollis

Circaea mollis, commonly known as sweet william, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Circaea mollis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
