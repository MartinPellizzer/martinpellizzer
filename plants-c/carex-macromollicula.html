Carex macromollicula

Carex macromollicula, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in spikes. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex macromollicula is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
