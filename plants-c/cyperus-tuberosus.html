Cyperus tuberosus

Cyperus tuberosus, commonly known as yellow nutsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikelets, and it is often found in wet or poorly drained soils. One of its most notable features is the presence of tubers, which are swollen underground stems that serve as storage organs and aid in its propagation.

Cyperus tuberosus is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in diverse conditions.
