Cleistocactus sextonianus

Cleistocactus sextonianus, commonly known as the Sonoran Desert cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert region of North America.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive rib structure and spines that are typically pale yellow to brown in color. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in the evening, a characteristic known as nyctinasty. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in desert flora.
