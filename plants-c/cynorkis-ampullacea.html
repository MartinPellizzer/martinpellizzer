Cynorkis ampullacea

Cynorkis ampullacea, commonly known as the African orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a rosette. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Cynorkis ampullacea thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
