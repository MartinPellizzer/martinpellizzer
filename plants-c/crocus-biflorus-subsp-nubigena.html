Crocus biflorus subsp. nubigena

Crocus biflorus subsp.

nubigena, commonly known as the alpine crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its early blooming habit, typically flowering in late winter or early spring.

The plant features striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which are borne on slender stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
