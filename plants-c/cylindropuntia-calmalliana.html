Cylindropuntia calmalliana

Cylindropuntia calmalliana, commonly known as the Calmallian barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green stems. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in desert environments.

Its structure and features make it an important part of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
