Cylindropuntia imbricata var. rosea

Cylindropuntia imbricata var. rosea, commonly known as the pink hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem with prominent, spiny areoles and produces vibrant pink to red flowers during the spring and summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers are often pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to local biodiversity.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh climates.
