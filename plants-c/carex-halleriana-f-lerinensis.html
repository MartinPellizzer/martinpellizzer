Carex halleriana f. lerinensis

Carex halleriana f. lerinensis, commonly known as the Lerinensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves and spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.
