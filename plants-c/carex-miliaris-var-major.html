Carex miliaris var. major

Carex miliaris var. major, commonly known as the large sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex miliaris var. major is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
