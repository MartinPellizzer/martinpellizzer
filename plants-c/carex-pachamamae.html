Carex pachamamae

Carex pachamamae, commonly known as the Andean sedge, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This species is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and alpine environments.

The plant's name, "pachamamae," reflects its cultural significance to indigenous Andean communities, who associate it with the Earth Mother.
