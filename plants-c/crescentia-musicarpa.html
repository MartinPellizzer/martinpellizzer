Crescentia musicarpa

Crescentia musicarpa, commonly known as the musical fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the evening. The fruit, which resembles a small, green melon, develops from these flowers and is known for its sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Its unique appearance and fragrance make it a distinctive addition to tropical gardens.
