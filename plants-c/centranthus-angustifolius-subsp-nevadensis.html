Centranthus angustifolius subsp. nevadensis

Centranthus angustifolius subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf redroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and linear, typically with a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
