Catabrosa aquatica

Catabrosa aquatica, commonly known as water catabrosa, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit, often found in marshes and wetlands. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in whorls around the stem.

Catabrosa aquatica plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
