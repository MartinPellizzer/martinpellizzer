Ceropegia infundibuliformis

Ceropegia infundibuliformis, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features trailing stems with tubular, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, resembling small bells. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for hanging baskets and indoor gardens.
