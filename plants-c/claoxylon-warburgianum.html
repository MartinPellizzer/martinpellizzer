Claoxylon warburgianum

Claoxylon warburgianum, commonly known as the Warburg's claoxylon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Claoxylaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
