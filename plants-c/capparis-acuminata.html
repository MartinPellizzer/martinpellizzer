Capparis acuminata

Capparis acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata caper, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and small, thorn-like structures on the stems. Its fruits are capsule-like and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Capparis acuminata is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments.
