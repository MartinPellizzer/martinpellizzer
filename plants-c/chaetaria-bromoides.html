Chaetaria bromoides

Chaetaria bromoides, commonly known as the bristly beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its delicate structure and limited distribution, it is sometimes considered a rare or endangered species in certain areas.
