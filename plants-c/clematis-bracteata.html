Clematis bracteata

Clematis bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets.

Clematis bracteata thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
