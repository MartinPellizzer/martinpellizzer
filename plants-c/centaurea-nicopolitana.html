Centaurea nicopolitana

Centaurea nicopolitana, commonly known as the Nicopolis thistle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Centaurea nicopolitana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
