Campanula cespitosa var. bellardii

Campanula cespitosa var. bellardii, commonly known as the Bellard's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright, clumping growth with clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
