Capsicum coccineum

Capsicum coccineum, commonly known as the red pepper or chili pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fruits ranging in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is used in various cuisines for its heat and flavor, and it is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
