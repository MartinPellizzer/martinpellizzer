Catharanthus makayensis

Catharanthus makayensis, commonly known as the Makay Endless Summer, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink and white. The plant produces milky sap, which is typical of the Apocynaceae family, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
