Carex uda var. sachalinensis

Carex uda var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the temperate regions of Asia, particularly Japan and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and swampy areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
