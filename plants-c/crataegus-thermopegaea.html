Crataegus thermopegaea

Crataegus thermopegaea, commonly known as the thermophilic hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the autumn. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
