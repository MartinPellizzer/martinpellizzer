Cassia leptocarpa var. hirsuta

Cassia leptocarpa var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and yellow inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The hairy texture of its foliage is a distinguishing feature of this variety.
