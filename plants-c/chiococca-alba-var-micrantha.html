Chiococca alba var. micrantha

Chiococca alba var. micrantha, commonly known as the white coca, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that contain the alkaloid cocaine, which is primarily found in the leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is also of significant botanical and pharmacological interest.
