Citrus × temple

Citrus × temple, commonly known as the Temple orange.

It is a hybrid citrus fruit resulting from the cross between the mandarin and the pummelo. The fruit is typically round or slightly oval, with a thick, smooth rind that is easy to peel. Its pulp is juicy and has a sweet, slightly acidic flavor, making it popular for fresh consumption.

The plant is evergreen, with dark green, glossy leaves and is often grown in subtropical climates for its ornamental and culinary value.
