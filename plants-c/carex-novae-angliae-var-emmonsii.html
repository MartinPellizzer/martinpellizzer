Carex novae-angliae var. emmonsii

Carex novae-angliae var. emmonsii, commonly known as the Emmons sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes during the summer months. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
