Cordia roxburghii

Cordia roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling snowballs. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are a source of food for various wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Cordia roxburghii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
