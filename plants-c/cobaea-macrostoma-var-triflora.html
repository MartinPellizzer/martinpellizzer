Cobaea macrostoma var. triflora

Cobaea macrostoma var. triflora, commonly known as the sweet pea vine, is a vigorous climbing plant native to South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom in clusters along its stems. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
