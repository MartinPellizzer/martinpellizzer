Clematis alpina var. longicaudata

Clematis alpina var. longicaudata, commonly known as the Alpine long-tailed clematis, is a perennial vine native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, woody stems that climb by means of small, hooked tendrils. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable support structures.
