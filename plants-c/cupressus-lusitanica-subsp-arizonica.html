Cupressus lusitanica subsp. arizonica

Cupressus lusitanica subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a small evergreen conifer native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, columnar form and scale-like foliage that is typically a deep green.

The plant produces small, round cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
