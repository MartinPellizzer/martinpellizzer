Cucurbita pepo var. numidica

Cucurbita pepo var. numidica, commonly known as the horned melon, is a unique variant of the common gourd.

It is characterized by its thick, spiky rind and a sweet, juicy, orange-colored pulp. The plant is a vine that produces large, distinctive fruit with a textured exterior. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
