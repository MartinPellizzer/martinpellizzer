Crocus chrysanthus var. caerulescens

Crocus chrysanthus var. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive purple throat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

The blue crocus is also valued for its early spring blooming habit, adding color to the landscape when few other plants are in flower.
