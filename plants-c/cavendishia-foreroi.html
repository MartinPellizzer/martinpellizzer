Cavendishia foreroi

Cavendishia foreroi, commonly known as the Forero's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically come in shades of yellow and green. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Cavendishia foreroi is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
