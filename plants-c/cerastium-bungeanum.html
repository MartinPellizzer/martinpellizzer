Cerastium bungeanum

Cerastium bungeanum, commonly known as the Bunge's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas.

Cerastium bungeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
