Caucalis lappula var. muricata

Caucalis lappula var. muricata, commonly known as the rough lappula, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many well-known edible and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a dry, segmented capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its rough, bristly surface on both the leaves and stems, giving it its specific epithet "muricata," meaning "armed" or "rough.".
