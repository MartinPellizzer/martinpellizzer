Carex × furusei

Carex × furusei, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves and compact, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

Carex × furusei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
