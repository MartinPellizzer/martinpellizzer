Ceropegia flanaganii var. alexandrina

Ceropegia flanaganii var. alexandrina, commonly known as the Cape fimbriate, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fimbriated (fringed) petals. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are often pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
