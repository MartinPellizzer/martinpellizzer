Crambe cordata

Crambe cordata, commonly known as heartleaf crambe, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically lobed and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Crambe cordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
