Cyathea bicolana

Cyathea bicolana, commonly known as the two-colored tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant is notable for its distinctive two-colored appearance, with the upper part of the trunk often having a lighter hue than the lower portion. Cyathea bicolana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
