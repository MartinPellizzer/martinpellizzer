Calamagrostis brassii

Calamagrostis brassii, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, silky, silver-blue plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a soft, feathery texture that adds visual interest in both garden and natural settings.

Calamagrostis brassii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in wet conditions, making it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
