Cyperus hesperius

Cyperus hesperius, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often subtended by bracts. Cyperus hesperius is characterized by its erect, flowering stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
