Cissus teretecaulis

Cissus teretecaulis, commonly known as the slender arrowhead plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Cissus teretecaulis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ability to trail or climb.
