Cephalocereus penicillatus

Cephalocereus penicillatus, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a unique structure, with a green stem and a brightly colored, often red or orange, calyx that resembles a crown. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
