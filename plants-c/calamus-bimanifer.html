Calamus bimanifer

Calamus bimanifer, commonly known as the two-handled calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of many palms.

Calamus bimanifer is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional practices in its native regions.
