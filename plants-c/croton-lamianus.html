Croton lamianus

Croton lamianus, commonly known as the African croton, is a tropical shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
