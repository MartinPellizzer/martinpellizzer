Coleus dolichopodus

Coleus dolichopodus, commonly known as the long-footed coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its trailing growth habit. Its small, tubular flowers are typically white or pale purple and bloom in clusters.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and ease of care.
