Crambe maritima subsp. pinnatifida

Crambe maritima subsp.

pinnatifida, commonly known as sea kale, is a coastal plant native to the northern Atlantic coast. It is a perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines.

The plant features alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white coating. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees.

It is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate harsh maritime conditions.
