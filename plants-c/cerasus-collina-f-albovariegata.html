Cerasus collina f. albovariegata

Cerasus collina f. albovariegata, commonly known as the white-variegated cherry laurel, is a decorative shrub prized for its ornamental foliage.

Its leaves are broad, ovate, and feature a striking white and green variegation pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by dark purple berries in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a range of soil types.

Due to its attractive appearance, it is often used in landscaping for its year-round visual interest.
