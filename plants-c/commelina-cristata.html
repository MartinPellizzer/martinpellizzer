Commelina cristata

Commelina cristata, commonly known as the crested morning glory, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers with a distinctive crested structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adaptive to waterlogged soils.

Commelina cristata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
