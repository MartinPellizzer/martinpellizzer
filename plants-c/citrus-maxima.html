Citrus maxima

Citrus maxima, commonly known as the pummelo, is a large citrus fruit native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its thick, leathery rind and segmented, juicy pulp. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit can weigh up to 10 pounds and is often used for its sweet, slightly tart flavor in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The pummelo is also valued for its essential oils and has been cultivated for centuries in tropical and subtropical regions.
