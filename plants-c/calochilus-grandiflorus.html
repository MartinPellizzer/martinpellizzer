Calochilus grandiflorus

Calochilus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance with elongated, recurved petals and a distinctive labellum. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually a mix of yellow and green, with some varieties displaying red or orange markings.

Calochilus grandiflorus is a perennial herb that blooms during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
