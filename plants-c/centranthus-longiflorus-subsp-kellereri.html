Centranthus longiflorus subsp. kellereri

Centranthus longiflorus subsp.

kellereri, commonly known as the Keller's red valerian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, tubular, pink to red flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped, slightly hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
