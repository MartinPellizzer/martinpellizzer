Calamintha polycephala

Calamintha polycephala, commonly known as mountain betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported calming properties.

Calamintha polycephala thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.
