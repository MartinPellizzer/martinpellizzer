Chenopodium berlandieri subsp. platyphyllum

Chenopodium berlandieri subsp.

platyphyllum, commonly known as buffalo bur, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into spiny, spherical fruits.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in prairies. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Its spiny fruits can adhere to animals and clothing, aiding in seed dispersal.
