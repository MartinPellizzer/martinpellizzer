Coleus primulinus

Coleus primulinus, commonly known as the golden flame coleus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting patterns. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor plant collections.
