Cleome scandens

Cleome scandens, commonly known as the "spider flower," is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing annual that features showy, spider-like yellow or white flowers with long, slender petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms.

Cleome scandens is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
