Centaurea iberica

Centaurea iberica, commonly known as the Iberian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive blue or purple petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to Mediterranean climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, spiky bracts that surround the central flower cluster.

Centaurea iberica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
