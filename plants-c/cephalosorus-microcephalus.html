Cephalosorus microcephalus

Cephalosorus microcephalus, commonly known as the dwarf flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a small, deciduous shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
