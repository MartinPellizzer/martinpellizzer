Cutandia memphitica var. loliacea

Cutandia memphitica var. loliacea, commonly known as the Egyptian water lily, is a aquatic plant native to the Nile River basin.

It features broad, floating leaves that form a dense canopy on the water surface. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its roots are anchored in the muddy substrate, and it plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in water gardens.
