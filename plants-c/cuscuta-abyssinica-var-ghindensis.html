Cuscuta abyssinica var. ghindensis

Cuscuta abyssinica var. ghindensis, commonly known as the Abyssinian dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around host plants. The plant is native to regions of Africa and is often found growing on various herbaceous plants. Its life cycle involves germinating from seeds and establishing a parasitic relationship with a host within a few days.

Due to its lack of photosynthetic ability, it plays a unique role in ecological interactions within its habitat.
