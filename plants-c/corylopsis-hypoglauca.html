Corylopsis hypoglauca

Corylopsis hypoglauca, commonly known as the golden flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that turn red or orange in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique flowering period and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to garden landscapes.
