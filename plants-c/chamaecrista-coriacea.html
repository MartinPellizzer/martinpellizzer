Chamaecrista coriacea

Chamaecrista coriacea, commonly known as the rough ticktrefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are covered in a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in racemes along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and roadsides.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
