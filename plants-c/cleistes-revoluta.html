Cleistes revoluta

Cleistes revoluta, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Americas, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant produces striking, fragrant, white to pale purple flowers that resemble miniature orchids. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Cleistes revoluta is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in the understory of tropical and subtropical forests.
