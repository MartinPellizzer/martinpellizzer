Cybelion testiculatum

Cybelion testiculatum, commonly known as the testicle plant, is a rare and unusual species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, seed-like structures that resemble testes, giving it its peculiar common name. The plant is a flowering species, producing small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often studied for its evolutionary adaptations.
