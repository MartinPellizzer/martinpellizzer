Crataegus shirleyensis

Crataegus shirleyensis, commonly known as Shirley's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
