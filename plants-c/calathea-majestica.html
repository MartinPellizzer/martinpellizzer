Calathea majestica

Calathea majestica, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, and intricately patterned leaves that display a range of colors, including deep green, silver, and pink. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its leaves are often described as having a waxy texture, which contributes to its unique aesthetic appeal.

Calathea majestica is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
