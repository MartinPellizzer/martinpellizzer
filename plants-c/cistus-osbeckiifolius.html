Cistus osbeckiifolius

Cistus osbeckiifolius, commonly known as the Osbeck's rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal and arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, while its flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
