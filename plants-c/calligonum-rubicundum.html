Calligonum rubicundum

Calligonum rubicundum, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is highly tolerant of drought and extreme temperatures.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
