Carex interior f. keweenawensis

Carex interior f. keweenawensis, commonly known as the Keweenaw sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, contributing to its ecological importance as a habitat for various insects and small animals.
