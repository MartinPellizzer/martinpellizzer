Carex chungii

Carex chungii, commonly known as the Chung's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers during the summer months. It is a perennial plant that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.

Carex chungii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
