Carex laricetorum

Carex laricetorum, commonly known as the mountain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas, especially near coniferous trees. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex laricetorum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
