Carex immanis

Carex immanis, commonly known as the giant sedges, is a tall, grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its broad, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical spikes of small, three-ranked flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshes and along stream edges. It plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Carex immanis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in water gardens and ecological restoration projects.
