Caleana granitica

Caleana granitica, commonly known as the granite orchid, is an Australian native plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bird-like flower structure, which is adapted for pollination by honeyeaters. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland regions of southern Australia. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a unique labellum that mimics the shape of a bird's beak.

Caleana granitica is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in well-drained conditions and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
