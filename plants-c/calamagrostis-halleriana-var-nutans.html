Calamagrostis halleriana var. nutans

Calamagrostis halleriana var. nutans, commonly known as the nodding sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that often bend downward, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are adorned with feathery, golden-brown florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a soft, hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
