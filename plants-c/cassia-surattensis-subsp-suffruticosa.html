Cassia surattensis subsp. suffruticosa

Cassia surattensis subsp.

suffruticosa, commonly known as Indian laburnum, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, rectangular seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in some regions.
