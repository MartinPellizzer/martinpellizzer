Cordia pallida

Cordia pallida, commonly known as the white cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, yellowish fruits. Cordia pallida is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, and it often thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
