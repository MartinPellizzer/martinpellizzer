Ceropegia candelabriformis

Ceropegia candelabriformis, commonly known as the candlestick ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branching stems that resemble a candlestick, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in collections of succulents and cacti.
