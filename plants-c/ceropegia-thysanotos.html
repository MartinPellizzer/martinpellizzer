Ceropegia thysanotos

Ceropegia thysanotos, commonly known as the velvet leaf or silver vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 cm, featuring succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Ceropegia thysanotos is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
