Citrus × limetta var. romana

Citrus × limetta var. romana, commonly known as the Mexican lime or Persian lime, is a hybrid citrus fruit originating from the cross between the citron and the mandarin.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruit with a thin, yellow-green rind and a high juice content. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree or shrub, with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is widely used in cooking, beverages, and as a source of essential oils.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
