Cereus baumannii var. smaragdiflorus

Cereus baumannii var. smaragdiflorus, commonly known as the Emerald Flower Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are typically green and covered with white or pale blue spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are usually white or yellow, blooming at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is popular for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
