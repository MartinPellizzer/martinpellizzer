Cyphostemma dehongense

Cyphostemma dehongense, commonly known as the Dehong moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, bead-like fruits. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest in botanical research and horticulture.
