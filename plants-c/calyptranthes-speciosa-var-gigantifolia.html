Calyptranthes speciosa var. gigantifolia

Calyptranthes speciosa var. gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble clusters of small, blue-purple berries. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental value in horticulture due to its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage.
