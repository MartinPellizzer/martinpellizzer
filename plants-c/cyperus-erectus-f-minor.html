Cyperus erectus f. minor

Cyperus erectus f. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a compact, herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water edges.

Its growth habit makes it a useful plant for stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
