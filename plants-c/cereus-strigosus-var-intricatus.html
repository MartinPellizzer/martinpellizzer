Cereus strigosus var. intricatus

Cereus strigosus var. intricatus, commonly known as the Star of Bethlehem cactus, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing, branching cactus with cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and pink. Its foliage is characterized by a unique, intricate pattern of ridges and tubercles.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.
