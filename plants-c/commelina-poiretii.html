Commelina poiretii

Commelina poiretii, commonly known as the blue commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its flowers are short-lived but attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Commelina poiretii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full shade.
