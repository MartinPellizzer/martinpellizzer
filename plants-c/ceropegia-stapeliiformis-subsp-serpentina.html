Ceropegia stapeliiformis subsp. serpentina

Ceropegia stapeliiformis subsp.

serpentina, commonly known as the snake plant or mother of thousands, is a succulent native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often ribbed and covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems and leaves.

This subspecies is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
