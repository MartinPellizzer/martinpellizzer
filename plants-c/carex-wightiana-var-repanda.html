Carex wightiana var. repanda

Carex wightiana var. repanda, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland or boggy conditions.
