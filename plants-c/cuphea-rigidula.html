Cuphea rigidula

Cuphea rigidula, commonly known as the rigid flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea rigidula is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
