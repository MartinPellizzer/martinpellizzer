Cytisus hirsutus subsp. bosniacus

Cytisus hirsutus subsp.

bosniacus, commonly known as the Bosnian broom, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with small, oval leaflets.

The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
