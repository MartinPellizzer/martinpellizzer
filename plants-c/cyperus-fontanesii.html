Cyperus fontanesii

Cyperus fontanesii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color.

Cyperus fontanesii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
