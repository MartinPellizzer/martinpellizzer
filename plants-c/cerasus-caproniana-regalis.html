Cerasus caproniana regalis

Cerasus caproniana regalis, commonly known as the royal cherry tree, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters, featuring a broad, spreading crown. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn a vibrant red in the fall. Its flowers, which appear in early spring, are white or pale pink and are followed by bright red, sweet-tart cherries.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in Japanese culture.
