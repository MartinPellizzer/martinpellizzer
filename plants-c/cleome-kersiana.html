Cleome kersiana

Cleome kersiana, commonly known as the "Mexican spider flower," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescences that consist of numerous small, white or pink flowers clustered around a central stem. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and branching.

Cleome kersiana thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
