Carpinus henryana var. simplicidentata

Carpinus henryana var. simplicidentata, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It features small, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, typically measuring 2-5 cm in length. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by small, round fruits that mature to a dark blue color. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

This variety is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
