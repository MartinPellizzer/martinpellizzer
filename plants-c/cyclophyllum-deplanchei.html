Cyclophyllum deplanchei

Cyclophyllum deplanchei, commonly known as the African cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feathery leaves. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, sandy soils. Cyclophyllum deplanchei produces cones instead of flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants.

It is a slow-growing species that can live for centuries, making it a significant component of its native ecosystems.
