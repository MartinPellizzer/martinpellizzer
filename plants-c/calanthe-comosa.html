Calanthe comosa

Calanthe comosa, commonly known as the hairy calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of pale to dark green flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Calanthe comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique floral display.
