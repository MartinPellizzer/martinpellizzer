Campylotropis nepalensis

Campylotropis nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal thornplant, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
