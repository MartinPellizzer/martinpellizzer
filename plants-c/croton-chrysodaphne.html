Croton chrysodaphne

Croton chrysodaphne, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in panicles, while the fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.
