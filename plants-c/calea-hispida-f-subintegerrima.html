Calea hispida f. subintegerrima

Calea hispida f. subintegerrima, commonly known as the velvetleaf calea, is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive tactile quality. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical study.
