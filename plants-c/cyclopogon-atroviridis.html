Cyclopogon atroviridis

Cyclopogon atroviridis, commonly known as the black-veined grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, dark green leaves with prominent black veins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles.

Cyclopogon atroviridis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
