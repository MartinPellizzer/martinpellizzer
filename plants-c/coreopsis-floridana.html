Coreopsis floridana

Coreopsis floridana, commonly known as the Florida coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive, narrow, tongue-like shape. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Coreopsis floridana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
