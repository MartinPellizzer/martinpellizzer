Campanula migarica

Campanula migarica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Campanula migarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
