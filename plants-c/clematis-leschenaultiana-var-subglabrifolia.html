Clematis leschenaultiana var. subglabrifolia

Clematis leschenaultiana var. subglabrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
