Crocus speciosus subsp. sakariensis

Crocus speciosus subsp.

sakariensis, commonly known as the Sakarion crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as limestone areas and coastal regions.

The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive, rounded shape. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms in late winter to early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
