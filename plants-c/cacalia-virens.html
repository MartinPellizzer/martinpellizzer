Cacalia virens

Cacalia virens, commonly known as the greenweed or greenflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and arranged in clusters, making it a subtle yet distinctive member of the wildflower community.
