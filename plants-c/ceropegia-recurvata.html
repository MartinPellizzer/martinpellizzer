Ceropegia recurvata

Ceropegia recurvata, commonly known as the zigzag ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, waxy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often visited by specific pollinators, such as moths. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a slightly hairy texture.

Ceropegia recurvata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
