Carex onoei

Carex onoei, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dark green color and a rough texture.

The flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, are usually pale green to reddish-brown and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
