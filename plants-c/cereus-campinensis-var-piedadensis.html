Cereus campinensis var. piedadensis

Cereus campinensis var. piedadensis, commonly known as the Piedad Cereus, is a species of cactus native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has spiny areoles and produces small, red fruits that are edible. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
