Cattleya forbesii f. venosa

Cattleya forbesii f. venosa, commonly known as the Venosa Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a sympodial orchid with a short, stocky rhizome and produces fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant is noted for its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow with deep red or purple markings. It blooms once or twice a year, producing large, showy inflorescences.

This cultivar is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and greenhouses.
