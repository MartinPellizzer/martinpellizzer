Crataegus monogyna var. mauriannensis

Crataegus monogyna var. mauriannensis, commonly known as the Mauryan hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant features thorned branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and produces white or pink flowers in spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
