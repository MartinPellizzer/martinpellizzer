Cytisus proliferus var. angustifolius

Cytisus proliferus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are narrow and trifoliate, giving the plant a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting surrounding plants.
