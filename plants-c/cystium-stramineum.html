Cystium stramineum

Cystium stramineum, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its spiny stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, green berries that turn red when ripe. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, often glandular surface.

The species is part of the genus Cystium, which belongs to the family Cistaceae, known for its aromatic and sometimes medicinal properties.
