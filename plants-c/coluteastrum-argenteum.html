Coluteastrum argenteum

Coluteastrum argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Coluteastrum argenteum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.
