Calliandra metrosideriflora

Calliandra metrosideriflora, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for warm climates.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in agroforestry systems due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
