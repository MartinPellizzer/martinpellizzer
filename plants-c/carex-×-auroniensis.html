Carex × auroniensis

Carex × auroniensis, commonly known as the aurora sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in wetland or marshy environments. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces viable seeds that can disperse via water or wind.

Carex × auroniensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
