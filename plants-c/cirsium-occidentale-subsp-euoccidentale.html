Cirsium occidentale subsp. euoccidentale

Cirsium occidentale subsp.

euoccidentale, commonly known as the western thistle, is a perennial herb native to western North America. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various therapeutic purposes.
