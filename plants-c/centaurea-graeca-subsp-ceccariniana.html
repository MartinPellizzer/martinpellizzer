Centaurea graeca subsp. ceccariniana

Centaurea graeca subsp.

ceccariniana, commonly known as the Greek centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and distinctive blue to purple flowers.

The plant produces small, cylindrical achenes surrounded by pappus hairs, which aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
