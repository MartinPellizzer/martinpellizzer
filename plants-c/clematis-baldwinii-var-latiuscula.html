Clematis baldwinii var. latiuscula

Clematis baldwinii var. latiuscula, commonly known as the slender Clematis, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, wiry stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 feet, making it a vigorous climber. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, heart-shaped leaflets.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
