Cistus clusii var. multiflorus

Cistus clusii var. multiflorus, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in local ecosystems.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
