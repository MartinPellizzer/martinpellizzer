Calamintha fuchsiifolia

Calamintha fuchsiifolia, commonly known as purple honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves and clusters of small, fragrant purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens.

It is also valued for its use in herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
