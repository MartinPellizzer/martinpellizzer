Camellia rhytidocarpa

Camellia rhytidocarpa, commonly known as the wrinkled camellia, is a flowering plant native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces woody, evergreen shrubs with leathery, elliptical leaves that have a distinctive wrinkled texture. Camellia rhytidocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooms.

It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.
