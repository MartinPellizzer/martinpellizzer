Callicarpa pilosissima var. henryi

Callicarpa pilosissima var. henryi, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to magenta berries that appear in clusters, which are a key feature of its botanical description. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological value.
