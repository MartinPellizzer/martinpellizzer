Cynanchum mongolicum

Cynanchum mongolicum, commonly known as Mongolian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by follicles that contain seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and deep root systems.

Cynanchum mongolicum is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in folk remedies for various ailments.
