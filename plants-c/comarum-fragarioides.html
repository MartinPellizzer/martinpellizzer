Comarum fragarioides

Comarum fragarioides, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruits are red, persistent berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter.

Comarum fragarioides is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wet meadows.
