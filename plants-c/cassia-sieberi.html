Cassia sieberi

Cassia sieberi, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain flat, seed-filled segments, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia sieberi is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms and fragrant scent.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
