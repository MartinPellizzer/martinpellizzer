Capparis garciae

Capparis garciae, commonly known as the Garcia's spurgeberry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparidaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the islands of Cuba and Hispaniola. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with woody stems and small, fleshy leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred shape.

Capparis garciae is adapted to coastal and limestone habitats, often growing in dry, sunny environments.
