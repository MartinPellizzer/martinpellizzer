Crucianella catellata

Crucianella catellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Crucianella catellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
