Carex yamatsutana

Carex yamatsutana, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Carex yamatsutana is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in wetland or boggy conditions.
