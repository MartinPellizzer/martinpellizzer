Cydonia tomentosa

Cydonia tomentosa, commonly known as the Chinese quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China and the Himalayas.

It features a rugged, twisted trunk and is characterized by its rough, hairy bark. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in early spring, followed by yellow, inedible fruit that ripens in autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in Asian countries.
