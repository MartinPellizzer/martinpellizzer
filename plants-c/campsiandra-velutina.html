Campsiandra velutina

Campsiandra velutina, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and dense, glossy foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
