Coffea malabarica

Coffea malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar coffee, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that contain the seeds used for coffee production. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is considered a specialty coffee bean in certain regions.
