Crucifera sinapis

Crucifera sinapis, commonly known as wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually arranged in racemes and are pollinated by insects.

The seeds are enclosed in a siliqua, a type of dry fruit typical of the Brassicaceae family.
