Cerastium gibraltaricum var. calpense

Cerastium gibraltaricum var. calpense, commonly known as Gibraltar rock chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in rocky and gravelly soils, often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
