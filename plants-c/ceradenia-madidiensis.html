Ceradenia madidiensis

Ceradenia madidiensis, commonly known as the Madidi ceradenia, is a species of orchid native to the Amazon rainforest in Bolivia and Peru.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters, displaying vibrant colors such as purple and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or in the undergrowth of dense forests. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Ceradenia madidiensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
