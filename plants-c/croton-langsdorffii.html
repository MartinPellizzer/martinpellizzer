Croton langsdorffii

Croton langsdorffii, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, often hairy stems and large, palmately lobed leaves that can vary in color from green to reddish. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it may be invasive in certain regions.
