Carex stellulata subsp. pallens

Carex stellulata subsp.

pallens, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of pale green to white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
