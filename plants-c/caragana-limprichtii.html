Caragana limprichtii

Caragana limprichtii, commonly known as the Limpricht's shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for harsh climates. It has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caragana limprichtii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
