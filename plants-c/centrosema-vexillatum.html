Centrosema vexillatum

Centrosema vexillatum, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces cylindrical, dehiscent pods containing several elliptical seeds. It is often used in agricultural systems for its nitrogen-fixing ability and as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
