Commelina praecox

Commelina praecox, commonly known as early commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface.

Commelina praecox is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
