Centaurea cirrata

Centaurea cirrata, commonly known as the silky centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange-red flowers. The plant produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Centaurea cirrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
