Cepa fissilis

Cepa fissilis, commonly known as the fissured onion, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, split leaves that give it its scientific name.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to certain regions of South America. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bulbous fruits that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
