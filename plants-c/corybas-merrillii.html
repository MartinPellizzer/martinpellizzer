Corybas merrillii

Corybas merrillii, commonly known as the New Guinea duck orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, helmet-shaped flower that is typically dark red or purple in color. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, which is often adorned with intricate patterns and a prominent lip. Corybas merrillii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized orchid species.
