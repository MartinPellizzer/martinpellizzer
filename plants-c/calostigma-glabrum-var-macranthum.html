Calostigma glabrum var. macranthum

Calostigma glabrum var. macranthum, commonly known as the broad-flowered calostigma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that are covered in soft, hairy leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are characterized by their broad, showy petals, which make it an attractive addition to gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of garden environments.
