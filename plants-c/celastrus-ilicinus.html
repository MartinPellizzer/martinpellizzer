Celastrus ilicinus

Celastrus ilicinus, commonly known as the Indian bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often elliptic or ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries that contain several seeds. It is often found in forested areas and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
