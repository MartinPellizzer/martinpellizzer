Carphobolus leprosus

Carphobolus leprosus, commonly known as the leprosy grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its historical association with leprosy, it has been the subject of various botanical and medical studies.
