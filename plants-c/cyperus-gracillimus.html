Cyperus gracillimus

Cyperus gracillimus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic environments. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, with small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus gracillimus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, feathery appearance and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
