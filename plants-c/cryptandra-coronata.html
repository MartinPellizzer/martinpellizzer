Cryptandra coronata

Cryptandra coronata, commonly known as the golden cryptandra, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
