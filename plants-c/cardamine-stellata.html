Cardamine stellata

Cardamine stellata, commonly known as starleaf water-cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are arranged in a star-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers are white or pale yellow and are borne in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Cardamine stellata is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
