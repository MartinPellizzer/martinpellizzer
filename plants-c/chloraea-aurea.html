Chloraea aurea

Chloraea aurea, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a lush green backdrop to the vibrant yellow blooms.

Chloraea aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
