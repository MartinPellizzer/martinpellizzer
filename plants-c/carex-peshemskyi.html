Carex peshemskyi

Carex peshemskyi, commonly known as the Peshemsky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or alpine habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are pollinated by wind.

Carex peshemskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
