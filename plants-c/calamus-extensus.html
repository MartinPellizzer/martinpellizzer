Calamus extensus

Calamus extensus, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, giving it its common name.

The rhizomes are valued for their starchy content and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
