Colchicum alpinum subsp. parvulum

Colchicum alpinum subsp.

parvulum, commonly known as the Alpine dwarf colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, nodding flowers in shades of white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often persist through the winter, while its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
