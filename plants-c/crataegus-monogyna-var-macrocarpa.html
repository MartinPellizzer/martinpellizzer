Crataegus monogyna var. macrocarpa

Crataegus monogyna var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The fruit, known as a haw, is typically large and red or orange when ripe, containing several seeds. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively hardy, making it a common sight in gardens and hedgerows.
