Carex cyclocarpa

Carex cyclocarpa, commonly known as the round-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex cyclocarpa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
