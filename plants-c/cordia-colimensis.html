Cordia colimensis

Cordia colimensis, commonly known as the Mexican cedar or red cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry to moist forests.

Cordia colimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
