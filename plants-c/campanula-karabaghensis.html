Campanula karabaghensis

Campanula karabaghensis, commonly known as the Karabakh bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland borders.

Campanula karabaghensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
