Carex humilis var. longifolia

Carex humilis var. longifolia, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish-brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
