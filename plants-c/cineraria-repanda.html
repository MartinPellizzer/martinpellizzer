Cineraria repanda

Cineraria repanda, commonly known as the common cineraria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cineraria repanda is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
