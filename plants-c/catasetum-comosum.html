Catasetum comosum

Catasetum comosum, commonly known as the clustered cattleya, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant produces a unique structure called a "catasetum," which is a specialized pollination mechanism that releases pollen in a specific manner. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is popular among growers for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
