Cirsium happoense

Cirsium happoense, commonly known as the Hachioji thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
