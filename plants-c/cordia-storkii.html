Cordia storkii

Cordia storkii, commonly known as the stork's bill, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, the Caribbean, and parts of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by distinctive, elongated, woody fruits that resemble a stork's bill, hence its common name.

Cordia storkii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
