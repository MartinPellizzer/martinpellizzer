Caltha ficarioides

Caltha ficarioides, commonly known as the yellow marsh marigold, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and moist meadows in the Northern Hemisphere.

It features bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and colonize wet areas effectively.

Caltha ficarioides is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.
