Cercocarpus rzedowskii

Cercocarpus rzedowskii, commonly known as the silverleaf curlleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, red to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds with a distinctive spiral-shaped pappus, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in arid ecosystems.
