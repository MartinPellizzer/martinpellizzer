Cistus × longifolius subsp. grosii

Cistus × longifolius subsp.

grosii, commonly known as the Corsican rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are typically pink to reddish in color.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
