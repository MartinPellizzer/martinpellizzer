Chaerophyllum lactescens

Chaerophyllum lactescens, commonly known as the milky cow parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its milky sap, which can cause skin irritation upon contact.
