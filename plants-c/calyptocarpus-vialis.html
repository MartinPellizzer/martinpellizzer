Calyptocarpus vialis

Calyptocarpus vialis, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous structure with opposite, linear leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers arranged in a cluster known as a capitulum. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.

Calyptocarpus vialis has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
