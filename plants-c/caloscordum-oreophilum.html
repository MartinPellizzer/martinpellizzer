Caloscordum oreophilum

Caloscordum oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features a bulbous base with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils and thrives in cooler, high-altitude environments. Its foliage and flowers emit a mild garlic scent, which is characteristic of the Alliaceae family.

Caloscordum oreophilum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
