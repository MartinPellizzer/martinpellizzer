Carex sachalinensis var. sachalinensis

Carex sachalinensis var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sachalin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
