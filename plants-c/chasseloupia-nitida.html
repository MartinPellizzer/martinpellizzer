Chasseloupia nitida

Chasseloupia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf chasseloupia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.
