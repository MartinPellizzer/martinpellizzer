Cineraria cernua

Cineraria cernua, commonly known as the weeping cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and soft, velvety leaves that are often a deep green or silvery-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom throughout the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and hanging baskets.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to cascade gracefully from containers, adding a decorative touch to gardens and landscapes.
