Celtis asperula

Celtis asperula, commonly known as the roughleaf hackberry, is a deciduous tree or shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, elliptical leaves that are rough to the touch. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by white, fuzzy fruits that are edible for some animals. Celtis asperula is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
