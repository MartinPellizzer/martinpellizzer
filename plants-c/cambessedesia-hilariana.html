Cambessedesia hilariana

Cambessedesia hilariana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Cambessedesia hilariana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
