Celsia ramosissima

Celsia ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy celsia, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a sprawling growth habit with numerous branched stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate or lance-shaped with a hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Celsia ramosissima is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
