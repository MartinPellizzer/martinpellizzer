Centaurea herzogii

Centaurea herzogii, commonly known as Herzog's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and spiny bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea herzogii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
