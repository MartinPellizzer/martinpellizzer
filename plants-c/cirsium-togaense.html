Cirsium togaense

Cirsium togaense, commonly known as the Toga thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in mountainous areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
