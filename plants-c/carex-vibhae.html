Carex vibhae

Carex vibhae, commonly known as the Himalayan sedge, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous habitats. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and usually have a bluish-green hue.

Carex vibhae produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.
