Chasmia

Chasmia, commonly known as the "sandmat" or "desert dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their low-growing, mat-forming structure and are often found in sandy or rocky soils. Chasmia species typically have narrow, linear leaves and produce small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in spring. They are adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

The genus is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
