Cycnium asperrimum

Cycnium asperrimum, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cycnium asperrimum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.
