Cerasus mahaleb f. tomentosa

Cerasus mahaleb f. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy cherry, is a variant of the cherry species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly pubescence that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, tart red cherries. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

This form is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
