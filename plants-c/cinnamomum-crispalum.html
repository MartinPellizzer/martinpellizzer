Cinnamomum crispalum

Cinnamomum crispalum, commonly known as the mountain cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and bark, which are rich in essential oils. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a distinctive wavy margin.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
