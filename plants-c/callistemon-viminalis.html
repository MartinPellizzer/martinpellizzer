Callistemon viminalis

Callistemon viminalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson-red inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, which are composed of dense clusters of tubular flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It is a hardy, evergreen shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

Callistemon viminalis is also valued for its ability to attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
