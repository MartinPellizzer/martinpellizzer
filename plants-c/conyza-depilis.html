Conyza depilis

Conyza depilis, commonly known as the woollyleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its wide distribution.

Conyza depilis is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in various environmental conditions.
