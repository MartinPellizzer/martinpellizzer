Calvoa superba

Calvoa superba, commonly known as the giant calvoa, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Peru. This plant is notable for its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
