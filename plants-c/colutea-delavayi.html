Colutea delavayi

Colutea delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's Scotch broom, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
