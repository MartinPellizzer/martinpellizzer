Cinnamomum eugenoliferum

Cinnamomum eugenoliferum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum cassia, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to southern China, Vietnam, and other parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic bark, which contains high concentrations of essential oils, particularly cinnamaldehyde. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth margin, while the flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

This species is widely cultivated for its aromatic bark, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
