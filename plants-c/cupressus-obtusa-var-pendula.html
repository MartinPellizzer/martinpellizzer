Cupressus obtusa var. pendula

Cupressus obtusa var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Chinese fir, is a variant of the Chinese fir characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to China, typically growing in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. The plant has scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces cones that are usually small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its unique, weeping form and is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
