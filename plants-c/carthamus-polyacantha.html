Carthamus polyacantha

Carthamus polyacantha, commonly known as the many-branched safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant has feathery, branched leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and it is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

Carthamus polyacantha thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.
