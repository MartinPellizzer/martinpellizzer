Cota amblyolepis

Cota amblyolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cota, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Cota amblyolepis is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil.
