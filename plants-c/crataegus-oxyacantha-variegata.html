Crataegus oxyacantha variegata

Crataegus oxyacantha variegata, commonly known as the variegated hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental leaves, which display striking variegation of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its attractive foliage and seasonal interest, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
