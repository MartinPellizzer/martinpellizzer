Cynoctonum acuminatum

Cynoctonum acuminatum, commonly known as the white soursop, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a fruit with a spiny exterior and a soft, juicy interior containing a single large seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible fruit, which has a sweet and tangy flavor.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
