Camaridium parviflorum

Camaridium parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered camaridium, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Camaridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, small flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
