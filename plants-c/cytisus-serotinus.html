Cytisus serotinus

Cytisus serotinus, commonly known as the late flowering broom, is a shrub native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom late in the summer, typically from July to September. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or heathland habitats.

Cytisus serotinus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
