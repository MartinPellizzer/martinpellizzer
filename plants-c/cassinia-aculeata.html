Cassinia aculeata

Cassinia aculeata, commonly known as the rough corymbia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and rough, hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically visited by insects and birds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Cassinia aculeata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in some traditional Indigenous practices.
