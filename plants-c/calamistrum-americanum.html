Calamistrum americanum

Calamistrum americanum, commonly known as the American calamistrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Calamistrum americanum is noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
