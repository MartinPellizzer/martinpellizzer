Chloranthus nervosus

Chloranthus nervosus, commonly known as the veined chloranthus, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves, which are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Chloranthus nervosus is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
