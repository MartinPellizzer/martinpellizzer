Chitonia acuminata

Chitonia acuminata, commonly known as the acuminated chitonia, is a species of sea snail belonging to the family Chitonidae.

It is characterized by its calcareous shell, which is typically elongated and ribbed, providing structural support and protection. The plant, though not a true plant, is often found in marine environments, particularly on rocky shores and in tidal zones. Its radula, a ribbon-like structure with rows of tiny teeth, is used for scraping food from surfaces.

Chitonia acuminata plays a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to the breakdown of organic matter and serving as prey for various marine predators.
