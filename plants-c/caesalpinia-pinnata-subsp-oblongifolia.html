Caesalpinia pinnata subsp. oblongifolia

Caesalpinia pinnata subsp.

oblongifolia, commonly known as the red sanders or red flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound, pinnate leaves that are often reddish in color when young.

The plant produces bright red or orange flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically showy and attract pollinators. Its fruit is a legume pod containing flat, winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
