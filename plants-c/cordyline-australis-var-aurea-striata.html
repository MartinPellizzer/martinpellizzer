Cordyline australis var. aurea-striata

Cordyline australis var. aurea-striata, commonly known as the golden stripe cabbage tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, erect stems with broad, sword-shaped leaves that are variegated with golden and green stripes. The plant is a variant of the common cabbage tree, distinguished by its distinctive leaf coloration. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for landscaping and as a statement plant in gardens.
