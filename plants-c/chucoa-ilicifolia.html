Chucoa ilicifolia

Chucoa ilicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf chucoa, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Chucoa ilicifolia is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.
