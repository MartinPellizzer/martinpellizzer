Chamaecrista aspera

Chamaecrista aspera, commonly known as rough ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-orange flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and clearings, due to its adaptability.

Chamaecrista aspera is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
