Centaurea calcitrapa subsp. horrida

Centaurea calcitrapa subsp.

horrida, commonly known as the rough centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces striking purple to red flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
