Carpinus tsunyihensis

Carpinus tsunyihensis, commonly known as the Tsunyih hawthorn, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round fruits that are typically eaten by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
