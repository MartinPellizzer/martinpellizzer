Calamus sedisspirituum

Calamus sedisspirituum, commonly known as the spirit's whisper reed, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acoraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spadices, typical of the genus Calamus. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic flora.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in water gardens.
