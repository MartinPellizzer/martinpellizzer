Cardamine thyrsoidea

Cardamine thyrsoidea, commonly known as the threadstem watercress, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its stems are slender and often appear in a rosette formation, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its preference for cool, humid conditions, it is often found in temperate regions with high rainfall.
