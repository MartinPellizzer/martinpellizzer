Cochranea myosotifolia

Cochranea myosotifolia, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Cochranea myosotifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
