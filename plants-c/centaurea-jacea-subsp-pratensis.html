Centaurea jacea subsp. pratensis

Centaurea jacea subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as meadow centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to purple composite flower heads. Its inflorescence consists of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
