Crucifera volgensis

Crucifera volgensis, commonly known as the Volgian mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of pubescence. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic applications.
