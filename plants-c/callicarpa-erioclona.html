Callicarpa erioclona

Callicarpa erioclona, commonly known as the woolly beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Callicarpa erioclona is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
