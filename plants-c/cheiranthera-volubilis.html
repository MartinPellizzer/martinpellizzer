Cheiranthera volubilis

Cheiranthera volubilis, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a complex structure typical of the Orchidaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Cheiranthera volubilis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
