Citrus × limonia var. digitata

Citrus × limonia var. digitata, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically eaten whole, including the peel, due to their sweet and tangy flavor. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit's unique taste and nutritional value make it a popular choice for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
