Cephaelis involucrata

Cephaelis involucrata, commonly known as the pink fairy fan, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or lilac-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a whimsical appearance. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Cephaelis involucrata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
