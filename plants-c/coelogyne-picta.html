Coelogyne picta

Coelogyne picta, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often display intricate patterns of red, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are segmented and help store water and nutrients.

Coelogyne picta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
