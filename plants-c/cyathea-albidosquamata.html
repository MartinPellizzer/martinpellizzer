Cyathea albidosquamata

Cyathea albidosquamata, commonly known as the silver-caled tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its fronds are composed of many small, overlapping pinnae, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Cyathea albidosquamata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
