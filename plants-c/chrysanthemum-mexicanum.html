Chrysanthemum mexicanum

Chrysanthemum mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from yellow to orange in color.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
