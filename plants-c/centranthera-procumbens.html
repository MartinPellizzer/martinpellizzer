Centranthera procumbens

Centranthera procumbens, commonly known as the creeping centranthera, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its prostrate habit, with small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as pine flatwoods and disturbed areas.
