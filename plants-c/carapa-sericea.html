Carapa sericea

Carapa sericea, commonly known as silky wax tree, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. The wood of Carapa sericea is valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it useful in construction.

Additionally, the plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties due to the presence of bioactive compounds in its seeds and leaves.
