Commelina martiana

Commelina martiana, commonly known as the blue commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, with a smooth texture, while its flowers consist of three petals and three sepals.

Commelina martiana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
