Cynanchum pichinchense

Cynanchum pichinchense, commonly known as the Pichincha milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive corona structure.
