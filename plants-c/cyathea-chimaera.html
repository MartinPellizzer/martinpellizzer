Cyathea chimaera

Cyathea chimaera, commonly known as the Chimaera tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the forest floor, while its fronds provide shade and habitat for various organisms.

Cyathea chimaera is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.
