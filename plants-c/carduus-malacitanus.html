Carduus malacitanus

Carduus malacitanus, commonly known as the Malacitan thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

The thistle's robust structure and adaptive features allow it to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
