Catharanthus lanceus

Catharanthus lanceus, commonly known as the lanceleaf vinca, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, trumpet-like flowers. The plant produces bright pink to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Catharanthus lanceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
