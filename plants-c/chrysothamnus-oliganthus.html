Chrysothamnus oliganthus

Chrysothamnus oliganthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is shrub-like, with narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
