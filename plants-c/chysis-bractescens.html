Chysis bractescens

Chysis bractescens, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, airy appearance. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chysis bractescens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
