Chorisma tetragona

Chorisma tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled chorisma, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, angular stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Chorisma tetragona is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
