Crataegus aria var. suecica

Crataegus aria var. suecica, commonly known as the Swedish hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges, hedgerows, and disturbed sites.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
