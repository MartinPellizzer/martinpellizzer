Centaurea affinis var. denudata

Centaurea affinis var. denudata, commonly known as the naked centaury, is a flowering plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and North Africa. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy texture, and the plant produces bracteate inflorescences. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
