Cloezia aquarum

Cloezia aquarum, commonly known as the water cloezia, is a rare aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in tropical regions.

It features delicate, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water's surface, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, contributing to its unique reproductive strategy. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered endangered in many areas.
