Cirsium pumilum f. albiflorum

Cirsium pumilum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, spiny leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster. Its foliage is usually covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its unique appearance.
