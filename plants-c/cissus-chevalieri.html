Cissus chevalieri

Cissus chevalieri, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing cissus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Vitaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

Cissus chevalieri is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
