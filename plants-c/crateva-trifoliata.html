Crateva trifoliata

Crateva trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the early morning. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Crateva trifoliata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
