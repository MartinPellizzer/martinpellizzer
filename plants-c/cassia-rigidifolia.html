Cassia rigidifolia

Cassia rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are typically narrow and rigid, giving it its common name, while its bark is smooth and often fissured.

Cassia rigidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
