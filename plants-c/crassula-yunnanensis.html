Crassula yunnanensis

Crassula yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan Crassula, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Crassulaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, where it forms dense, low-growing mats. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the spring.

Crassula yunnanensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
