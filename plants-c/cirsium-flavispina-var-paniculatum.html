Cirsium flavispina var. paniculatum

Cirsium flavispina var. paniculatum, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and a rough texture, while the flowers form dense, branched clusters called panicles.

This species is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world, often as an ornamental or weed.
