Colubrina californica

Colubrina californica, commonly known as California beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features showy, clustered purple berries that persist through winter, providing food for wildlife. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant fruit and upright growth habit.
