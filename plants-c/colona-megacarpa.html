Colona megacarpa

Colona megacarpa, commonly known as the giant fruit tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant has broad, dark green leaves and produces showy yellow flowers. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in local cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
