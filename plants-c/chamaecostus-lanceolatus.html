Chamaecostus lanceolatus

Chamaecostus lanceolatus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a succulent, branching stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and drought tolerance.

Chamaecostus lanceolatus prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
