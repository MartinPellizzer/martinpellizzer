Calceolaria excelsa

Calceolaria excelsa, commonly known as the Andean slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant grows in rocky, alpine environments and thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help protect it from harsh weather.

Calceolaria excelsa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
