Clematopsis

Clematopsis, commonly known as the climbing clematis, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, often fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in woodland or shady areas. Clematopsis species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are commonly used in garden landscaping.

They require well-drained soil and partial to full sun for optimal growth.
