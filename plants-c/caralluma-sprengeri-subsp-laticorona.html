Caralluma sprengeri subsp. laticorona

Caralluma sprengeri subsp.

laticorona, commonly known as the fat-tailed hedgehog cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting to its harsh environment.

It is well-suited for cultivation in dry, well-drained soils and is popular among cactus enthusiasts.
