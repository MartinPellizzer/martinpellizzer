Cracca palmeri

Cracca palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's cracca, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow to orange.

Cracca palmeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
