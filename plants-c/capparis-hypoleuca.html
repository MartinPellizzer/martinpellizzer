Capparis hypoleuca

Capparis hypoleuca, commonly known as the white caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and produces small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant has thorny stems and pinnate leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are followed by small, green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature.

Capparis hypoleuca is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
