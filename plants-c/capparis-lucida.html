Capparis lucida

Capparis lucida, commonly known as the sweet flag or Indian caper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Capparis lucida make it an interesting subject for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
