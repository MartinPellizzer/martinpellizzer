Calyptrogyne ghiesbreghtiana subsp. spicigera

Calyptrogyne ghiesbreghtiana subsp.

spicigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or saline soils, often found in dune environments.

The plant features linear, narrow leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes. Its root system is adapted to stabilize sand dunes and prevent erosion.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal landscapes and its potential for use in restoration projects.
