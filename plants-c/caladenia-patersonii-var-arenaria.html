Caladenia patersonii var. arenaria

Caladenia patersonii var. arenaria, commonly known as the Sturt's Desert Pea, is a striking orchid native to arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink flowers with a distinctive black or purple labellum. The plant typically grows from a tuberous root system and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged drought conditions.

This species is of cultural significance to Indigenous Australians and is protected in some regions due to habitat loss.
