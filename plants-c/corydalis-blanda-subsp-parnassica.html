Corydalis blanda subsp. parnassica

Corydalis blanda subsp.

parnassica, commonly known as the alpine corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters.

The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, nodding flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Corydalis blanda subsp.

parnassica is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cool, moist environments.
