Crotalaria rostrata

Crotalaria rostrata, commonly known as the spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

Crotalaria rostrata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
