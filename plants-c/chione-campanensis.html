Chione campanensis

Chione campanensis, commonly known as the snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its bark is smooth and gray, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Chione campanensis is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
