Centaurea acuta

Centaurea acuta, commonly known as the acute centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its slender, upright stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, blue to violet flowers.

Centaurea acuta is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
