Clitoria lactescens

Clitoria lactescens, commonly known as the milk bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive butterfly-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is commonly found in gardens and along roadsides in its native habitats.
