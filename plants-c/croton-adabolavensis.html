Croton adabolavensis

Croton adabolavensis, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the genus. Its leaves are typically alternate and can vary in shape, sometimes being lobed or deeply cut.

Croton adabolavensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
