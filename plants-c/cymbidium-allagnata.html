Cymbidium allagnata

Cymbidium allagnata, commonly known as the Allagnata orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand, Vietnam, and the Philippines.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of fragrant, lance-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Cymbidium allagnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its beauty and resilience.
