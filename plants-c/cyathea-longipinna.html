Cyathea longipinna

Cyathea longipinna, commonly known as the long-needle tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of compound leaves that give it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cyathea longipinna plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
