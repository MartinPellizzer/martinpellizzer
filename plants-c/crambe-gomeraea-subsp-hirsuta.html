Crambe gomeraea subsp. hirsuta

Crambe gomeraea subsp.

hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy crambe, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky and coastal areas, adapting to the harsh conditions of its environment. Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological features compared to other crambe species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
