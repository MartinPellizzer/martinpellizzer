Cyanopis moluccensis

Cyanopis moluccensis, commonly known as the Moluccan blueberry, is a species of flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
