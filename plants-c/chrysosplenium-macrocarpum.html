Chrysosplenium macrocarpum

Chrysosplenium macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited chrysosplenium, is a member of the Saxifragaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
