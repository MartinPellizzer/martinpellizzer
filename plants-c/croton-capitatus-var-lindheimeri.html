Croton capitatus var. lindheimeri

Croton capitatus var. lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's croton, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
