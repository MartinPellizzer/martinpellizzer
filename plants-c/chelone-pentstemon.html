Chelone pentstemon

Chelone pentstemon, commonly known as turtlehead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that resemble a turtle's head. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
