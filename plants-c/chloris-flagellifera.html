Chloris flagellifera

Chloris flagellifera, commonly known as the slender grass or flagellifera grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
