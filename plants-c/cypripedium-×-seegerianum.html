Cypripedium × seegerianum

Cypripedium × seegerianum, commonly known as the Seeger's lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the mountainous regions of China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a key feature of the Cypripedium genus. The plant typically grows in cool, shaded environments with moist, well-drained soil, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or white with a deep red or brown lip, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity in the wild, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
