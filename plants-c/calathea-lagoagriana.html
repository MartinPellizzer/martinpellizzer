Calathea lagoagriana

Calathea lagoagriana, commonly known as the Lagoa Grande calathea, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with intricate patterns. The leaves are typically broad and elliptical, often displaying shades of green, silver, and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
