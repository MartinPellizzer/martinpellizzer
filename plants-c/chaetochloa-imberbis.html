Chaetochloa imberbis

Chaetochloa imberbis, commonly known as the silky grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its soft, hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
