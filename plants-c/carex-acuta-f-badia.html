Carex acuta f. badia

Carex acuta f. badia, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulbous sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
