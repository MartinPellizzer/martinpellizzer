Calycogonium moanum

Calycogonium moanum, commonly known as the Madagascar bean, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
