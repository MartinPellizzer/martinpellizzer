Carex lanosa

Carex lanosa, commonly known as the velvety sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are covered in a soft, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Carex lanosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
