Collinsia brachysiphon

Collinsia brachysiphon, commonly known as the slender fairybell, is a delicate annual plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pale blue or violet, with a distinctive white throat, and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as dunes and coastal bluffs.

It is a popular choice for native gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
