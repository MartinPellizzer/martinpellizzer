Carex douglasii var. williamsii

Carex douglasii var. williamsii, commonly known as Douglas's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in forested or mountainous regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of its stems.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland and riparian habitats.
