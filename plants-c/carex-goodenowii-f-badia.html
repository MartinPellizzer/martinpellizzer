Carex goodenowii f. badia

Carex goodenowii f. badia, commonly known as the Blackleaf sedge, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along streambanks. Its leaves are dark green and often have a bluish tinge, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
