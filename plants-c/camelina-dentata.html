Camelina dentata

Camelina dentata, commonly known as the toothed camelina, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, and its leaves are characterized by their serrated edges. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with branched stems and alternate leaves. Camelina dentata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It is also known for its potential as a source of oilseed, making it of interest in agricultural and ecological studies.
