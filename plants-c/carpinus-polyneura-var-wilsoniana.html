Carpinus polyneura var. wilsoniana

Carpinus polyneura var. wilsoniana, commonly known as the Wilson's hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are followed by small, oval-shaped nuts enclosed in a bristly husk.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
