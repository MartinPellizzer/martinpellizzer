Cirsium megacanthum

Cirsium megacanthum, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its large, robust leaves are covered in stiff, prickly hairs, and the flower heads are composed of many small florets.

Cirsium megacanthum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for various insects.
