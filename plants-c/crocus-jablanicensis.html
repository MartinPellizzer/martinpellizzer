Crocus jablanicensis

Crocus jablanicensis, commonly known as the Jablanica crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula, particularly found in Serbia and Bosnia and Herzegovina. This perennial plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soil. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in early spring.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
