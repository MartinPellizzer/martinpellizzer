Cyperus iria var. santonici

Cyperus iria var. santonici, commonly known as the golden club, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically yellow or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
