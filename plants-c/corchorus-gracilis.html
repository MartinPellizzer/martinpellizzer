Corchorus gracilis

Corchorus gracilis, commonly known as slender dock, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and marshy areas.

Corchorus gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
