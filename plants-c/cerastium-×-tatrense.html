Cerastium × tatrense

Cerastium × tatrense, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Cerastium × tatrense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh mountain conditions.
