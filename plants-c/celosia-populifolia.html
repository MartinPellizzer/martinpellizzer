Celosia populifolia

Celosia populifolia, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a striking ornamental plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like bracts that range in color from red to orange, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that resemble those of a poplar tree, hence its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Celosia populifolia is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other plants with showy flowers and colorful foliage.
