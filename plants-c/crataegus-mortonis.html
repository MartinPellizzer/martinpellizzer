Crataegus mortonis

Crataegus mortonis, commonly known as the Mongolian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Crataegus mortonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
