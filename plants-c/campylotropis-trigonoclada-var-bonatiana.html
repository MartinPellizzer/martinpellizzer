Campylotropis trigonoclada var. bonatiana

Campylotropis trigonoclada var. bonatiana, commonly known as the Bonati's arrowhead vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
