Centaurea × charrieri

Centaurea × charrieri, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea scabiosa (greater knapweed). The plant typically features blue to purple flowers with ray florets and a central disk, resembling other members of the genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
