Cyperus pertenuis

Cyperus pertenuis, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in shallow water or moist soil.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
