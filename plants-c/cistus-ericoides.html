Cistus ericoides

Cistus ericoides, commonly known as the heather-like rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is adapted to rocky or coastal environments. Its leaves are aromatic and contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional herbal remedies.

Cistus ericoides is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion and support local biodiversity.
