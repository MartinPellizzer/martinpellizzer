Convolvulus auricomus var. volubilis

Convolvulus auricomus var. volubilis, commonly known as the golden vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in dry climates.
