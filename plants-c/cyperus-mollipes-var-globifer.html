Cyperus mollipes var. globifer

Cyperus mollipes var. globifer, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-lobed berries.

This species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
