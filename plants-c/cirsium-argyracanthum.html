Cirsium argyracanthum

Cirsium argyracanthum, commonly known as the silverleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and spiny, purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its robust, upright growth habit. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a waxy coating that reflects light, giving it its distinctive appearance.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
