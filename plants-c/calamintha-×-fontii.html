Calamintha × fontii

Calamintha × fontii, commonly known as the hybrid germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its small, pale blue to violet flowers are clustered in whorls along the upper stems, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Calamintha × fontii is valued for its fragrant foliage and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
