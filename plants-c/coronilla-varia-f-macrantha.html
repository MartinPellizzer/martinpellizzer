Coronilla varia f. macrantha

Coronilla varia f. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces showy, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
