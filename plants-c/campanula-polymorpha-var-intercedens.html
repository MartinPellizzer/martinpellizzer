Campanula polymorpha var. intercedens

Campanula polymorpha var. intercedens, commonly known as the Bluebell of Scotland, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
