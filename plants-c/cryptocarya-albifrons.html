Cryptocarya albifrons

Cryptocarya albifrons, commonly known as the white-fruited cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a bright red drupe, which contains a single seed.

Cryptocarya albifrons is often found in coastal and lowland forests, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates.
