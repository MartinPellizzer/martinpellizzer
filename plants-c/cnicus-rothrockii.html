Cnicus rothrockii

Cnicus rothrockii, commonly known as the Rothrock's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Cnicus rothrockii is often found in rocky areas and is considered a rare and endemic species.
