Carex tenuiseta var. brevisquama

Carex tenuiseta var. brevisquama, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in width. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as swamps, bogs, and the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
