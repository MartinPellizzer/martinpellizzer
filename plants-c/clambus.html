Clambus

Clambus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads. The plant has silvery, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in its saline environment. Clambus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal areas.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in coastal landscapes.
