Campanula scheuchzeri subsp. lanceolata

Campanula scheuchzeri subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue or violet in color.

The leaves are lanceolate, with a pointed tip and a slightly hairy surface. This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
