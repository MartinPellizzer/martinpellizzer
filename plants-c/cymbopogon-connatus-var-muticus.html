Cymbopogon connatus var. muticus

Cymbopogon connatus var. muticus, commonly known as the silent grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
