Crocus sieberi var. heterochromus

Crocus sieberi var. heterochromus, commonly known as the Sieber's crocus with variegated coloration, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, early-blooming flowers that are typically a mix of purple, white, and yellow, with the variegation in the petals giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.

The species is also valued for its historical significance in traditional medicine.
