Chrysanthemum pamiricum

Chrysanthemum pamiricum, commonly known as the Pamir chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, preferring cool and dry climates. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its resilience in harsh conditions.

Chrysanthemum pamiricum is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude habitats.
