Casearia lucida

Casearia lucida, commonly known as the glossy casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
