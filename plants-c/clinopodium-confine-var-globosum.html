Clinopodium confine var. globosum

Clinopodium confine var. globosum, commonly known as globular mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

The species is noted for its distinctive globular inflorescence, which gives it its common name.
