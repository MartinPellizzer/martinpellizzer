Campanula cespitosa subf. grandiflorens

Campanula cespitosa subf.

grandiflorens, commonly known as the Great Blue Bugloss, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
