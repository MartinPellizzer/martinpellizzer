Cassia spinigera var. suffruticosa

Cassia spinigera var. suffruticosa, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, followed by pod-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
