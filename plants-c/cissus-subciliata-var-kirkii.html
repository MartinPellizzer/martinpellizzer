Cissus subciliata var. kirkii

Cissus subciliata var. kirkii, commonly known as the silver leaf creeper, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round, yellow berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and erosion control.
