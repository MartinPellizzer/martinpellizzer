Chianthemum

Chianthemum, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its ability to produce long-lasting, colorful blooms. The flowers are typically daisy-like, with ray florets surrounding a central disk, and they come in a variety of colors including white, yellow, and purple. Chianthemum plants are native to regions of Europe, Asia, and Africa, and they thrive in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

They are often used in gardens for their ornamental value and are also popular in floral arrangements due to their durability.
