Coleus integrifolius

Coleus integrifolius, commonly known as the smooth-leaved coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage rather than its small, inconspicuous flowers. The leaves are typically broad, smooth, and come in shades of red, purple, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and regular watering.

This plant is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and can also be grown as a houseplant in suitable conditions.
