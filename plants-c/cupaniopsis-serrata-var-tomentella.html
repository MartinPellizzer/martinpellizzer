Cupaniopsis serrata var. tomentella

Cupaniopsis serrata var. tomentella, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in winter, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
