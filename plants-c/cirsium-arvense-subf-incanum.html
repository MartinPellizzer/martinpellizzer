Cirsium arvense subf. incanum

Cirsium arvense subf.

incanum, commonly known as the hoary thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features stout, spiny stems that are often covered in a fine, whitish down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers in composite flower heads, which are surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered with stiff, backward-pointing hairs.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands.
