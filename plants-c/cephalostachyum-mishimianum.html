Cephalostachyum mishimianum

Cephalostachyum mishimianum, commonly known as the Mishima bamboo, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces inflorescences that are clustered at the top of the stems, giving it its specific epithet "cephalostachyum," meaning "head-like spike.". It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
