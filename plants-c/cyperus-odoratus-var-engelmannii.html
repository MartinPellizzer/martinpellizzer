Cyperus odoratus var. engelmannii

Cyperus odoratus var. engelmannii, commonly known as the fragrant flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

Its aromatic foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
