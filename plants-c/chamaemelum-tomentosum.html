Chamaemelum tomentosum

Chamaemelum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a notable wildflower in its native range.
