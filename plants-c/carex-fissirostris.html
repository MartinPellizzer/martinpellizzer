Carex fissirostris

Carex fissirostris, commonly known as the narrow-beak sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by water or wind.

Carex fissirostris is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
