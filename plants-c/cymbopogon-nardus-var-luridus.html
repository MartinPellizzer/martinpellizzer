Cymbopogon nardus var. luridus

Cymbopogon nardus var. luridus, commonly known as the golden grass or lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are widely used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

This variety is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
