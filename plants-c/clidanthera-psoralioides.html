Clidanthera psoralioides

Clidanthera psoralioides, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae, or Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
