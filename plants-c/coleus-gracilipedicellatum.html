Coleus gracilipedicellatum

Coleus gracilipedicellatum, commonly known as the slender-stalked coleus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, featuring square stems and opposite leaves. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale purple or white, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with partial shade.

Coleus gracilipedicellatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
