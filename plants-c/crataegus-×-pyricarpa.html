Crataegus × pyricarpa

Crataegus × pyricarpa, commonly known as the common hawthorn or hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. It produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
