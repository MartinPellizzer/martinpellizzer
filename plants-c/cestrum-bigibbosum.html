Cestrum bigibbosum

Cestrum bigibbosum, commonly known as the redflower nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually red or orange in color, blooming primarily at night. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark red color, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Cestrum bigibbosum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
