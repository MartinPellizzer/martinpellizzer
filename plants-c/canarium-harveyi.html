Canarium harveyi

Canarium harveyi, commonly known as the Harvey's candlenut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces drupes that contain a single seed, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in humid, lowland areas and is often found in secondary forests.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and has cultural significance in some regions.
