Calophyllum sakarium

Calophyllum sakarium, commonly known as the sacred sandalwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to red, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its fragrance and is used in traditional medicine and incense.

The plant also plays a significant role in cultural and religious practices in its native regions.
