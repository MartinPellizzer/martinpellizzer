Cornus alba subsp. baileyi

Cornus alba subsp.

baileyi, commonly known as the Bailey's dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its showy, white bracts that appear in late spring, which are often mistaken for flowers.

The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall, adding ornamental value. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
