Cyperus reddyi

Cyperus reddyi, commonly known as the Reddy's flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist soil conditions. Its appearance features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads.

Cyperus reddyi is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
