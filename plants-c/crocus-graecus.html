Crocus graecus

Crocus graecus, commonly known as the Greek crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Crocus graecus is relatively easy to grow and is a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
