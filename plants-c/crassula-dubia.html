Crassula dubia

Crassula dubia, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, which help it store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
