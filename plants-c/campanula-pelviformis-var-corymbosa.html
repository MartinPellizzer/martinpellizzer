Campanula pelviformis var. corymbosa

Campanula pelviformis var. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges and stream banks, where it forms dense colonies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
