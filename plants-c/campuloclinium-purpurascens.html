Campuloclinium purpurascens

Campuloclinium purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campuloclinium purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
