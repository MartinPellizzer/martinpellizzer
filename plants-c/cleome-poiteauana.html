Cleome poiteauana

Cleome poiteauana, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring branching stems and hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often have a rough texture.

Cleome poiteauana is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
