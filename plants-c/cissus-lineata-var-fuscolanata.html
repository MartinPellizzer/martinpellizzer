Cissus lineata var. fuscolanata

Cissus lineata var. fuscolanata, commonly known as the velvet leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by translucent, waxy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
