Ceanothus tomentosus subsp. olivaceus

Ceanothus tomentosus subsp.

olivaceus, commonly known as the oliveleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to California and southern Oregon. It is characterized by its grayish-white, woolly foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
