Coleus dissitiflorus

Coleus dissitiflorus, commonly known as the hairy-leaved coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble petals. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Coleus dissitiflorus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and colorful inflorescences.
