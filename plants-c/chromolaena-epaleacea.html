Chromolaena epaleacea

Chromolaena epaleacea, commonly known as the Hawaiian false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Chromolaena epaleacea is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, making it a potential invasive species in certain environments.
