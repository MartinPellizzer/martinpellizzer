Cytisus monspessulanus var. syriacus

Cytisus monspessulanus var. syriacus, commonly known as the Syrian broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. The plant has slender, woody stems and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
