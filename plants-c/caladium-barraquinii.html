Caladium barraquinii

Caladium barraquinii, commonly known as the red-veined caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins against a lush green background. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Caladium barraquinii is typically grown as an annual in temperate regions due to its sensitivity to frost.
