Carex salina var. minor

Carex salina var. minor, commonly known as salt marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal wetlands.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green and arranged in dense, compact clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in brackish or saline environments, making it an important component of tidal marsh ecosystems.

Carex salina var. minor plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
