Clinopodium javanicum

Clinopodium javanicum, commonly known as Java mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic and often medicinal properties. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple or blue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Clinopodium javanicum is valued for its fragrant leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
