Ceropegia gettliffei

Ceropegia gettliffei, commonly known as the Gettliffe's moonvine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia gettliffei is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
