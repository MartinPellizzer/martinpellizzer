Cotoneaster yalungensis

Cotoneaster yalungensis, commonly known as the Yalung cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Cotoneaster yalungensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal fruiting.
