Cordia cardenasiana

Cordia cardenasiana, commonly known as the Cienaga tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is often found in moist lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
