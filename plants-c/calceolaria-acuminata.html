Calceolaria acuminata

Calceolaria acuminata, commonly known as the acorn flower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that resemble small acorns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are elongated and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers come in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Calceolaria acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
