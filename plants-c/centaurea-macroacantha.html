Centaurea macroacantha

Centaurea macroacantha, commonly known as the broad-leaved centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking blue to purple flower heads that are often surrounded by spiny bracts. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Centaurea macroacantha is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
