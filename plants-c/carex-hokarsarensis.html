Carex hokarsarensis

Carex hokarsarensis, commonly known as the Hokarsarensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions in East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
