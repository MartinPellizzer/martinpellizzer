Calamagrostis breviglumis

Calamagrostis breviglumis, commonly known as the short-leaved reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural habitats. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis breviglumis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
