Cyphia tenera

Cyphia tenera, commonly known as the Cape York hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a prominent yellow stamen, and bloom during the wet season. Cyphia tenera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riverine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
