Carex × subpatula

Carex × subpatula, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, which are usually green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex × subpatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
