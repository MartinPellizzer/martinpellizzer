Cynoglossum officinale macranthum

Cynoglossum officinale macranthum, commonly known as the blueweed or great bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, forming showy clusters atop sturdy stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine down, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to ecological gardens.
