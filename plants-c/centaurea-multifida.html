Centaurea multifida

Centaurea multifida, commonly known as the fringed cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea multifida is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
