Calamus ruptilis

Calamus ruptilis, commonly known as the fragrant reed, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its aromatic rhizomes, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. Calamus ruptilis thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
