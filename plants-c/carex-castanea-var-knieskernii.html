Carex castanea var. knieskernii

Carex castanea var. knieskernii, commonly known as the Knieskern's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as stream banks, marshes, and riparian zones. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often found in shaded to partially shaded conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.
