Clematis vinacea

Clematis vinacea, commonly known as the red clematis, is a flowering vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and attractive, showy flowers that range from white to deep red. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a slightly serrated edge.

Clematis vinacea thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
