Chionanthus enerve

Chionanthus enerve, commonly known as the slender chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, pendant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
