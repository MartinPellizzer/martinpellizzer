Chiococca anguifuga

Chiococca anguifuga, commonly known as the climbing cacao.

It is a woody vine native to the tropical rainforests of South America. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fleshy, red berries.

The plant is part of the Malvaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance in its native habitat.
