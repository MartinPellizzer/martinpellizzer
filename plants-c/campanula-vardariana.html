Campanula vardariana

Campanula vardariana, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Campanula vardariana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
