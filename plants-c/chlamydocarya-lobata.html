Chlamydocarya lobata

Chlamydocarya lobata, commonly known as the stickyleaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a waxy, sticky substance, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Chlamydocarya lobata is adapted to extreme temperatures and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
