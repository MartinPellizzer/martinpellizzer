Chalepoa magellanica

Chalepoa magellanica, commonly known as the Magellanic chalepoa, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often found in grasslands and shrublands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
