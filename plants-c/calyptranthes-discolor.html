Calyptranthes discolor

Calyptranthes discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered calyptranthes, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and elliptical leaves that are usually glabrous. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns from green to red as it matures.

Calyptranthes discolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid climates.
