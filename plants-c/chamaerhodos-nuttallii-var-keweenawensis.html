Chamaerhodos nuttallii var. keweenawensis

Chamaerhodos nuttallii var. keweenawensis, commonly known as the Keweenaw bog gentian, is a perennial herb native to the northern United States and Canada.

It thrives in acidic, wet soils found in bogs and fens, showcasing its adaptation to nutrient-poor environments. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

This variant is distinguished by its geographic distribution and specific ecological requirements, making it a unique species within the Gentianaceae family.
