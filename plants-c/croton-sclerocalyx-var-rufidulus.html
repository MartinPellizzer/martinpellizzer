Croton sclerocalyx var. rufidulus

Croton sclerocalyx var. rufidulus, commonly known as the red-veined croton, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring leaves with prominent reddish veins against a green background. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
