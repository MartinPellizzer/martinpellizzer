Chaenactis glabriuscula var. megacephala

Chaenactis glabriuscula var. megacephala, commonly known as the Bighead Paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces showy, pinkish to purple flowers. Its leaves are generally basal, with a rough, hairy texture.

The variety megacephala is distinguished by its notably large, showy flower heads compared to other varieties of Chaenactis.
