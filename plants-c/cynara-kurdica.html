Cynara kurdica

Cynara kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant features large, spiny leaves with a silvery-blue underside, and its purple or pinkish composite flowers bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Cynara kurdica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
