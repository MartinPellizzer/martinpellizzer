Cordiera pilosa

Cordiera pilosa, commonly known as the hairy cordiera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Cordiera pilosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
