Carex muricata var. lamprocarpa

Carex muricata var. lamprocarpa, commonly known as the glossy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or boggy soils and is often found in marshes or along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration projects.
