Convolvulus vahlii

Convolvulus vahlii, commonly known as the desert bindweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and Southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with hairy stems and small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Convolvulus vahlii is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is considered a resilient species in its native habitat.
