Centropogon albolimbatus var. concolor

Centropogon albolimbatus var. concolor, commonly known as the white-lipped centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
