Commelina umbellata var. gambiae

Commelina umbellata var. gambiae, commonly known as the Gambian spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers with white markings. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
