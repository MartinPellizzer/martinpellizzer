Cerastium frigidum

Cerastium frigidum, commonly known as Arctic mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in cold, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
