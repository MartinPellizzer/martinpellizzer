Cassinia accipitrum

Cassinia accipitrum, commonly known as the golden corymbia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Cassinia accipitrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
