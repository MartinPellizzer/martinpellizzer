Cicer arietinum var. gilvum

Cicer arietinum var. gilvum, commonly known as the golden chickpea, is a variant of the common chickpea characterized by its distinctive yellow or golden pod color.

This variety is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant is an annual legume that produces small, round seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a short, bushy growth habit.

The golden pod color is due to the presence of specific pigments, making it visually unique among chickpea varieties.
