Chaetochloa imberbis var. versicolor

Chaetochloa imberbis var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in water-saturated soils. The plant's leaves are slender and often have a bluish-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers. One of its distinguishing features is the variability in leaf coloration, which can range from green to silver or even red, depending on environmental conditions.

This variation in appearance contributes to its common name and makes it a visually interesting plant in aquatic habitats.
