Capparis cartilaginea

Capparis cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilage caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure with thorny stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

The species is notable for its unique appearance and ecological role in its native habitat.
