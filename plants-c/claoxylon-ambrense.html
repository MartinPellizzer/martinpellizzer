Claoxylon ambrense

Claoxylon ambrense, commonly known as the Ambon ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing several seeds.

Claoxylon ambrense is often found in lowland and hill forests, where it contributes to the ecological structure and biodiversity of its habitat.
