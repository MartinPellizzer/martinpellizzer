Crotalaria cornetii

Crotalaria cornetii, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Crotalaria cornetii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
