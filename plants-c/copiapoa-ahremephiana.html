Copiapoa ahremephiana

Copiapoa ahremephiana, commonly known as the Ahremephiana cactus, is a species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and spines that are typically yellow or reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are clustered at the apex of the stem. It is adapted to extreme arid conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and botanists.
