Calamus ovoideus

Calamus ovoideus, commonly known as the oval-stemmed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
