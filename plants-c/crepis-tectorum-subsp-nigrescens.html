Crepis tectorum subsp. nigrescens

Crepis tectorum subsp.

nigrescens, commonly known as the black-hued mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves that turn reddish or purplish at the base. Its small, yellowish flowers are borne in loose clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark coloration and compact growth habit.
