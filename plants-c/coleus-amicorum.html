Coleus amicorum

Coleus amicorum, commonly known as the friendship plant, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with leaves that display shades of red, orange, and purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and produces small, star-shaped flowers in white or pale blue. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and bright indirect light.
