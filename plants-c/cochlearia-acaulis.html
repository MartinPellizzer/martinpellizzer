Cochlearia acaulis

Cochlearia acaulis, commonly known as the rock scabious, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles and parts of northern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky, coastal, and maritime environments. The plant produces small, violet or blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette and are often covered in fine hairs.

Cochlearia acaulis is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various invertebrates.
