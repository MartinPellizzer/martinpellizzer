Cucurbita villosa

Cucurbita villosa, commonly known as the hairy gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and elongated, pale green fruits that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant is a vigorous vine that climbs using tendrils and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and the fruit matures to a light green or yellowish color.

Cucurbita villosa is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
