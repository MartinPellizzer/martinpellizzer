Chrysanthemum namikawanum

Chrysanthemum namikawanum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in the autumn season, adding vibrant color to gardens and landscapes.

The botanical structure of this species includes a central disk surrounded by ray florets, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.
