Cirsium gaillardotii

Cirsium gaillardotii, commonly known as Gaillardot's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
