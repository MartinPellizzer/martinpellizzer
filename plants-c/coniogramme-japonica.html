Coniogramme japonica

Coniogramme japonica, commonly known as Japanese mountain fern, is a species of fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Coniogramme japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
