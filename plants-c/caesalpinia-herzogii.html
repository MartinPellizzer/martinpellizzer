Caesalpinia herzogii

Caesalpinia herzogii, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with elongated, reddish-brown pods that persist through winter. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The species is well adapted to arid conditions, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
