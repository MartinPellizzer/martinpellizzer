Cinchona bergeniana

Cinchona bergeniana, commonly known as the Himalayan quinine tree, is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its bark, which contains quinine, a compound historically used in the treatment of malaria.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a source of medicinal compounds and its ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
