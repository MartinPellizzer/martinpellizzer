Chondrilla racemosa

Chondrilla racemosa, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and ability to store water in its succulent leaves. Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to ecosystem biodiversity.

Chondrilla racemosa is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
