Calamus maximus var. nanga

Calamus maximus var. nanga, commonly known as the giant bamboo or giant calamus, is a tall, slender plant native to the wetlands and marshes of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms and other tropical grass-like plants. The plant features long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length, contributing to its striking visual presence in its natural habitat. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.

The species is adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
