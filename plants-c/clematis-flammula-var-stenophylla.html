Clematis flammula var. stenophylla

Clematis flammula var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clematis, is a perennial vine native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a slightly hairy surface. This variety thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in gardens to cover trellises or fences.
