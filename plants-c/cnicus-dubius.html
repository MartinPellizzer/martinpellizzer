Cnicus dubius

Cnicus dubius, commonly known as the common sandspur, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils, often found along roadsides and in open fields. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lobed and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often spiny.

Cnicus dubius is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a common weed in many regions.
