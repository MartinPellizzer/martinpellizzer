Cereus deficiens

Cereus deficiens, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of white, nocturnal flowers that open only at night. The plant has spiny areoles and produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique blooming pattern, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
