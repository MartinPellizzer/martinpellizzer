Crucifera tenuifolia

Crucifera tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and clusters of four-petaled white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows or along streambanks. Its fruit is a slender, cylindrical silique that contains several small seeds.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
