Conchograecum viride

Conchograecum viride, commonly known as the green conchograss, is a rare and exotic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiral-shaped leaves that are a vibrant green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
