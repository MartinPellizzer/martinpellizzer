Crucifera conringia

Crucifera conringia, commonly known as the Conring's mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It is a herbaceous plant typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually lobed or toothed. Its flowers are bright yellow, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is often used in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and as a food source for certain pollinators.
