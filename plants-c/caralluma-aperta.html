Caralluma aperta

Caralluma aperta, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its succulent leaves and tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its fleshy stems. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Caralluma aperta is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
