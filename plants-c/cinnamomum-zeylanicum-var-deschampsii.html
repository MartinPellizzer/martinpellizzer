Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. deschampsii

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. deschampsii, commonly known as the Ceylon cinnamon or true cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, dark brown berries containing one to three seeds. Its bark is the primary source of the spice cinnamon, known for its distinct flavor and medicinal properties.

This variety is valued for both its culinary and traditional medicinal uses.
