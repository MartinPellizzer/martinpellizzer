Cyrtandra vesiculata

Cyrtandra vesiculata, commonly known as the bubble vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated, bladder-like fruits that give it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Cyrtandra vesiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
