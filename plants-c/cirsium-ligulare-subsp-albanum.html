Cirsium ligulare subsp. albanum

Cirsium ligulare subsp.

albanum, commonly known as the Albanian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, ligulate (strap-shaped) leaves.

The plant produces purple to pinkish composite flowers, typical of the daisy family, which bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
