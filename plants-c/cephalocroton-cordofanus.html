Cephalocroton cordofanus

Cephalocroton cordofanus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a distinctive cordate base. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
