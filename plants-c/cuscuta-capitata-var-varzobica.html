Cuscuta capitata var. varzobica

Cuscuta capitata var. varzobica, commonly known as the desert dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its hosts. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions, including parts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or mechanical means, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and survival of its host plants.
