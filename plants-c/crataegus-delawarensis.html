Crataegus delawarensis

Crataegus delawarensis, commonly known as Delaware hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Delaware hawthorn is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
