Crocus annulatus var. adamicus

Crocus annulatus var. adamicus, commonly known as the Adamic crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Alps and the Pyrenees. The plant is characterized by its delicate, early-blooming flowers that typically appear in late winter or early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem.

The Adamic crocus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, cup-shaped blooms.
