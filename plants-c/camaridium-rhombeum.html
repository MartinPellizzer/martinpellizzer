Camaridium rhombeum

Camaridium rhombeum, commonly known as the diamond leaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The leaves are lanceolate, with a distinctive diamond-shaped pattern on the underside, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This plant is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor conditions.
