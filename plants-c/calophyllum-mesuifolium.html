Calophyllum mesuifolium

Calophyllum mesuifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
