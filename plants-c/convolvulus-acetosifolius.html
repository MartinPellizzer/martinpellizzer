Convolvulus acetosifolius

Convolvulus acetosifolius, commonly known as the sour leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, round, yellow berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing along roadsides and in disturbed areas.

Its name refers to the sour taste of its leaves, which contain compounds like oxalic acid.
