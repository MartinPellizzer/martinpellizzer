Centaurea crassifolia

Centaurea crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, composite flower heads.

Centaurea crassifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
