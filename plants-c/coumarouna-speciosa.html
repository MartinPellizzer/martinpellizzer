Coumarouna speciosa

Coumarouna speciosa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, opposite leaves. Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
