Cerastium afromontanum

Cerastium afromontanum, commonly known as the East African mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the highlands of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
