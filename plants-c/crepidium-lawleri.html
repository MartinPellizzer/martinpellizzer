Crepidium lawleri

Crepidium lawleri, commonly known as the Lawrie's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, while the flowers are usually pale green to white with a distinctive lip.

Crepidium lawleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
