Camaridium tricarinatum

Camaridium tricarinatum, commonly known as the three-ribbed camaridium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with three distinct ribs. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
