Calamagrostis nandadeviensis

Calamagrostis nandadeviensis, commonly known as the Nanda Devi grass, is a rare and endemic species found in the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry blades. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its inflorescence consists of narrow spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown hues.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for ecological and botanical studies.
