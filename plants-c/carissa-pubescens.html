Carissa pubescens

Carissa pubescens, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow carissa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring dense, thorny branches and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by oval, yellowish berries that are edible when ripe. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for well-drained soils in full sun.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
