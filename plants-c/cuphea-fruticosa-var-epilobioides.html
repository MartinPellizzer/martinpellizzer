Cuphea fruticosa var. epilobioides

Cuphea fruticosa var. epilobioides, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub-like species characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for gardens due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
