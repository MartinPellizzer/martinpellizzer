Calligonum batiola

Calligonum batiola, commonly known as the Batiola saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a valuable species for land reclamation projects.

Its deep root system allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, enhancing its survival in drought conditions.
