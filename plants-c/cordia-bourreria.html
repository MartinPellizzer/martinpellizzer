Cordia bourreria

Cordia bourreria, commonly known as the red cedar or red gum tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are often glossy green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Cordia bourreria is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
