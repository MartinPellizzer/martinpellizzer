Catasetum bungerothii var. lindenii

Catasetum bungerothii var. lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its unique floral structure includes a specialized column and a pollinia that are adapted for specific pollination mechanisms.

This variety is highly valued by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
