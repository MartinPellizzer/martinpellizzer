Calanthe alismifolia

Calanthe alismifolia, commonly known as the water calanthe, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens.
