Cardamine pratensis var. speciosa

Cardamine pratensis var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy water crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous environments such as meadows, streambanks, and marshes. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a basal rosette and small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in a cluster. Its flowers have five sepals and two petals, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
