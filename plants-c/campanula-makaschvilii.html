Campanula makaschvilii

Campanula makaschvilii, commonly known as the Georgian bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula makaschvilii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
