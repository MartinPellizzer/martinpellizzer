Chamaecrista lucesiae

Chamaecrista lucesiae, commonly known as the Mexican burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, bean-like pods that contain clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
