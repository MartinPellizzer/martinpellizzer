Collania aurea

Collania aurea, commonly known as golden collania, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water in dry conditions.

Collania aurea is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
