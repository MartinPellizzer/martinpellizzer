Cinchona mutisii

Cinchona mutisii, commonly known as the Amazonian quinine tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its bark, which contains quinine, a compound historically used in the treatment of malaria. Due to its medicinal value, it is also cultivated for commercial purposes.
