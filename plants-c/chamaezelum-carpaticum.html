Chamaezelum carpaticum

Chamaezelum carpaticum, commonly known as the Carpathian snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often woody at the base.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
