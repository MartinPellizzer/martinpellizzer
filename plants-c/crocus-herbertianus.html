Crocus herbertianus

Crocus herbertianus, commonly known as Herbert's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and is characterized by its early spring blooming habit. The plant produces small, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in rocky and well-drained soils.

Crocus herbertianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to bloom before many other spring flowers.
