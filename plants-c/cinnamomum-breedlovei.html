Cinnamomum breedlovei

Cinnamomum breedlovei, commonly known as the Hawaiian cinnamon tree, is a species native to the island of Maui in Hawaii.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive cinnamon-like aroma.

This plant is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its unique genetic makeup and ecological significance in its native habitat.
