Carum dichotomum

Carum dichotomum, commonly known as the split caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small white or greenish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, oval seeds that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Carum dichotomum is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as an ornamental plant.
