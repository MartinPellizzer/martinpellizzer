Calea brittoniana

Calea brittoniana, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Calea brittoniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
