Conyza punctata

Conyza punctata, commonly known as the punctate weed or slenderleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and agricultural fields. Its small, yellowish flowers are followed by white or pale pink seeds that are easily dispersed by wind.

Conyza punctata is often considered a weed but has some ecological and potential medicinal significance.
