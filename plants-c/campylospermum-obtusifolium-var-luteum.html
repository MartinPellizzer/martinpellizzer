Campylospermum obtusifolium var. luteum

Campylospermum obtusifolium var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered campylospermum, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically oval to ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish to orange flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially available.
