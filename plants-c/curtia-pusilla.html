Curtia pusilla

Curtia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf curtea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its compact size, glossy dark green leaves, and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense forests.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
