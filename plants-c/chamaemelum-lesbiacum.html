Chamaemelum lesbiacum

Chamaemelum lesbiacum, commonly known as the Lesbia's chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
