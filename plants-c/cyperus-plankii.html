Cyperus plankii

Cyperus plankii, commonly known as the dwarf umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often white or pale green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Cyperus plankii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
