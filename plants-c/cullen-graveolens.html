Cullen graveolens

Cullen graveolens, commonly known as the stinking ross, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, strongly scented flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky areas.

Due to its unique fragrance and appearance, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
