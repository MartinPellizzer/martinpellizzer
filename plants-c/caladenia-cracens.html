Caladenia cracens

Caladenia cracens, commonly known as the Crimson Spider-orchid, is an orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance with long, thin sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the late summer to autumn, producing vivid red and orange blooms.

Caladenia cracens is a protected species due to habitat loss and is often found in specific regions of New South Wales and Victoria.
