Cyperus neurotropis

Cyperus neurotropis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually pale green to brown in color. Cyperus neurotropis is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
