Croton meridensis

Croton meridensis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre croton, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored leaves that display a variety of patterns and shades. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and canyons.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
