Chaenorhinum johnstonii

Chaenorhinum johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with prominent, horn-like sepals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
