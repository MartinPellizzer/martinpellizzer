Cedrela ciliolata

Cedrela ciliolata, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar or cedro-do-brejo, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and aromatic wood. The plant typically grows in wet, lowland areas and is known for its rapid growth rate. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
