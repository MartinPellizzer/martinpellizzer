Clinopodium fauriei f. albiflorum

Clinopodium fauriei f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered wood betony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in whorls along the upper stems. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
