Chionanthus guianensis

Chionanthus guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan whiteflower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Chionanthus guianensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.
