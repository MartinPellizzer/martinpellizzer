Chironia purpurascens subsp. humilis

Chironia purpurascens subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the dwarf purple orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to parts of South America. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas.

The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are pale purple to white, with a distinctive hooded lip that resembles a small pouch.

This subspecies is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens.
