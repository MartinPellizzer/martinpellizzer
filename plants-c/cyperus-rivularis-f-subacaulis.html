Cyperus rivularis f. subacaulis

Cyperus rivularis f. subacaulis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus characterized by its reduced stature and slender growth habit.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. Its botanical structure is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions, with rhizomes that help in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

This subspecies is often found in shallow water or moist soils, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland habitats.
