Cyperus camphoratus

Cyperus camphoratus, commonly known as the camphor grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Cyperus camphoratus is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
