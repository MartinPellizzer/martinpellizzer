Campomanesia cyanea var. cordata

Campomanesia cyanea var. cordata, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 5 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its fruit is a fleshy, segmented berry that turns from green to red when ripe, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
