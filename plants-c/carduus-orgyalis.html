Carduus orgyalis

Carduus orgyalis, commonly known as the common cocklebur.

It is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, spherical seed heads. The plant grows up to 1.5 meters tall and thrives in disturbed soils, often found along roadsides and in agricultural fields. Its seeds are dispersed by animals and wind, and the plant is considered a weed in many regions.

The leaves and stems are hairy, and the plant has a strong, bitter taste.
