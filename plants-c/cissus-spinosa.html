Cissus spinosa

Cissus spinosa, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing cissus, is a trailing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, white spots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Cissus spinosa is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ability to climb or trail along surfaces.
