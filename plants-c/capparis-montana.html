Capparis montana

Capparis montana, commonly known as the mountain caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, characterized by its woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, edible fruits. Its leaves are usually thick and leathery, adapted to withstand dry conditions.

Capparis montana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
