Capparis wightiana

Capparis wightiana, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn orange-red when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Capparis wightiana is valued for its medicinal properties and is widely used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
