Carex phleoides

Carex phleoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to North America and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a bluish-green color. Its flowering spikes are usually short and clustered, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex phleoides plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
