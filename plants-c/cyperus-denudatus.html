Cyperus denudatus

Cyperus denudatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairless. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the ends of the stems. Cyperus denudatus is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small aquatic organisms.
