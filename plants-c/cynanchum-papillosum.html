Cynanchum papillosum

Cynanchum papillosum, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed or milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can be harmful if ingested. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough texture, while its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Cynanchum papillosum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
