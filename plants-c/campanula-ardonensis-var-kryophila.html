Campanula ardonensis var. kryophila

Campanula ardonensis var. kryophila, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Alps.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine meadows. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

Campanula ardonensis var. kryophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in mountain ecosystems.
