Camellia sasanqua

Camellia sasanqua, commonly known as the winter camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during late autumn to early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and prefers partial to full sun. It is relatively low maintenance and popular in gardens for its ornamental value.

Camellia sasanqua is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its use in traditional Japanese culture.
