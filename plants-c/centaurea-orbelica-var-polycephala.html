Centaurea orbelica var. polycephala

Centaurea orbelica var. polycephala, commonly known as the mountain centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs, and the plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in alpine and subalpine environments.

The plant's botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
