Clematoclethra lanosa

Clematoclethra lanosa, commonly known as the woolly clematoclethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring, which are clustered in elongated inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture.

Clematoclethra lanosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
