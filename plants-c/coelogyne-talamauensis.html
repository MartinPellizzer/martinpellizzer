Coelogyne talamauensis

Coelogyne talamauensis, commonly known as the Talamau orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, with showy, strap-like petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
