Crataegus pruinosa var. porteri

Crataegus pruinosa var. porteri, commonly known as the Porters' hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
