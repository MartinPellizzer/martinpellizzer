Crotalaria macrophylla

Crotalaria macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf cowpea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, South America, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers with a distinctive black or purple spot at the base. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, and it has a short flowering period.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural settings.
