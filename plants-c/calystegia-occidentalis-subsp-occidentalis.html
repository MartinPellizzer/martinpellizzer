Calystegia occidentalis subsp. occidentalis

Calystegia occidentalis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial vine native to the western United States. It features heart-shaped, palmate leaves and produces trumpet-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning.

The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open areas. It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
