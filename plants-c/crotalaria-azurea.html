Crotalaria azurea

Crotalaria azurea, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and open woodlands.

Crotalaria azurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
