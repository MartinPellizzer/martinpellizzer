Cyclosorus × gogoii

Cyclosorus × gogoii, commonly known as the hybrid water clover, is a unique aquatic plant that combines traits from its parent species, Cyclosorus sanguineus and Cyclosorus gogoii.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves that float on the water surface, creating a visually appealing effect in ponds and water gardens. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, preferring moderate to high light conditions. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to help clarify water by absorbing nutrients.

However, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems if not managed properly.
