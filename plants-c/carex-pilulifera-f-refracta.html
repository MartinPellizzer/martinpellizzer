Carex pilulifera f. refracta

Carex pilulifera f. refracta, commonly known as the slender basket sedge, is a variant of the basket sedge species native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous growth habit and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
