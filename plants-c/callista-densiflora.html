Callista densiflora

Callista densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Callista densiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive floral display.
