Citrus × limonum var. paradisi

Citrus × limonum var. paradisi, commonly known as the sweet orange.

This variety is characterized by its thick, aromatic peel and juicy, segmented flesh that is rich in vitamin C. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree or shrub, with glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The flowers are fragrant and white, giving way to the characteristic orange fruit after a several months of growth.
