Clerodendrum myricoides var. camporum

Clerodendrum myricoides var. camporum, commonly known as the Camp's flame bush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture with a slightly serrated margin.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
