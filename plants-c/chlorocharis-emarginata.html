Chlorocharis emarginata

Chlorocharis emarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, waxy leaves and a single, yellow flowering head that develops into a spherical seed sphere. The plant is native to South America and thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator of nutrient-poor conditions.
