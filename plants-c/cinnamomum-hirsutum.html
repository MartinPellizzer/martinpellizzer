Cinnamomum hirsutum

Cinnamomum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant bark. The plant is notable for its aromatic oils, which are extracted from its bark and leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both traditional medicine and commercial use.
