Cichorium dichotomum

Cichorium dichotomum, commonly known as the Italian chicory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are typically bitter and are used in salads or as a coffee substitute, while the flowers are sometimes used in ornamental gardening.

Cichorium dichotomum is also valued for its medicinal properties, including its potential benefits for digestion and liver health.
