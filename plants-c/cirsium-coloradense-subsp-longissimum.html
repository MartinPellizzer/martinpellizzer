Cirsium coloradense subsp. longissimum

Cirsium coloradense subsp.

longissimum, commonly known as the longleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, lobed leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy purple to pinkish flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a spherical or conical shape. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and mountainous regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is adapted to withstand drought and cold temperatures.
