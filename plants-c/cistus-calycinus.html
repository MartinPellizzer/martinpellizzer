Cistus calycinus

Cistus calycinus, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Cistus calycinus plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
