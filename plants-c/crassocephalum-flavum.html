Crassocephalum flavum

Crassocephalum flavum, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
