Coronilla pendula

Coronilla pendula, commonly known as the hanging crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and pale yellow to pink flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Coronilla pendula is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
