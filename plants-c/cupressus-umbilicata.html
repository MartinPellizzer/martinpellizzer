Cupressus umbilicata

Cupressus umbilicata, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a species of conifer native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, scale-like leaves that form a compact, pyramidal shape. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes worldwide.
