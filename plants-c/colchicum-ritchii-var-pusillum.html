Colchicum ritchii var. pusillum

Colchicum ritchii var. pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that emerge from a bulbous corm. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in horticultural collections.
