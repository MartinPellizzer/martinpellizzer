Carduus nutans subsp. nutans

Carduus nutans subsp.

nutans, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in a variety of soil types, including rocky and disturbed areas. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
