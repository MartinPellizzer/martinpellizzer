Cupressus thujiformis

Cupressus thujiformis, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes around the world.
