Cousinia azerbaidjanica

Cousinia azerbaidjanica, commonly known as the Azeri cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region, particularly Azerbaijan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually narrow and hairy.

Cousinia azerbaidjanica is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
