Chaerefolium kotschyi

Chaerefolium kotschyi, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers, which range from white to pale purple, bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
