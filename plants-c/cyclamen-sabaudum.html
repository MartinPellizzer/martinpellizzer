Cyclamen sabaudum

Cyclamen sabaudum, commonly known as the Alpine cyclamen, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky crevices. Its tuberous roots allow it to survive harsh winter conditions.

Cyclamen sabaudum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
