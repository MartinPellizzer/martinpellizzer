Cinnamomum champokianum

Cinnamomum champokianum, commonly known as the Java cinnamon or Cinnamomum champokianum, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-like bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is often cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal uses.
