Cavendishia aurantiaca

Cavendishia aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange bell plant, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant features spiny stems and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its foliage consists of fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, aiding in survival in dry conditions.

Cavendishia aurantiaca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
