Crepinia richteri

Crepinia richteri, commonly known as the Richter's crepe jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils.

Crepinia richteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance.
