Carex remota f. rigida

Carex remota f. rigida, commonly known as the rigid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, stiff stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are usually greenish or brownish in hue. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
