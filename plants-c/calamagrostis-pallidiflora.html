Calamagrostis pallidiflora

Calamagrostis pallidiflora, commonly known as silverstem reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of pale green to white flowers in late summer, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis pallidiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife.
