Conophytum depressum

Conophytum depressum, commonly known as the "dwarf ice plant," is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, rounded leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
