Convolvulus occidentalis var. saxicola

Convolvulus occidentalis var. saxicola, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features slender, trailing stems with heart-shaped leaves and produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The species is well adapted to drought conditions, showcasing its resilience in harsh desert climates.
