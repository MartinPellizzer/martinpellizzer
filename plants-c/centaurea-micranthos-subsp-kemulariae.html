Centaurea micranthos subsp. kemulariae

Centaurea micranthos subsp.

kemulariae, commonly known as Kemular's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces achenes as its fruit. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in gardens and naturalized areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
