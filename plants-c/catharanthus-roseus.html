Catharanthus roseus

Catharanthus roseus, commonly known as the Madagascar periwinkle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Additionally, it has been studied for its medicinal properties due to the presence of alkaloids like vincristine and vinblastine.
