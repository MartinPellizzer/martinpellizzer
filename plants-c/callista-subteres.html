Callista subteres

Callista subteres, commonly known as the narrowleaf callista, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
