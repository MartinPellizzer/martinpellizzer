Crocus flavus subsp. dissectus

Crocus flavus subsp.

dissectus, commonly known as the dissected crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of yellow and orange.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a central bulb. The subspecies is characterized by its finely divided, lace-like petals, which give it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
