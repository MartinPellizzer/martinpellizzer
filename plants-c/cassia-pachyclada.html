Cassia pachyclada

Cassia pachyclada, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, bean-like pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Cassia pachyclada is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in landscaped areas.
