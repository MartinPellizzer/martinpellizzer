Cameraria obovalis

Cameraria obovalis, commonly known as the oak leaf blister moth, is a small gelechiid moth whose larvae feed on the leaves of various oak species.

The plant's botanical features include its characteristic acorn-bearing structure, which is a key part of its reproductive cycle. The oak tree, to which this moth is closely associated, is a deciduous tree with deeply lobed leaves and a sturdy, branching structure. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The interaction between Cameraria obovalis and its host plants highlights the complex relationships in forest ecosystems.
