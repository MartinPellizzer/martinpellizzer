Carex × brevirostris

Carex × brevirostris, commonly known as the narrow-beak sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with three stamens and a single pistil.

Carex × brevirostris is valued in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
