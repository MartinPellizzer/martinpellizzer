Cyperus alternifolius var. petersianus

Cyperus alternifolius var. petersianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily, making it both a common wetland species and a potential weed in aquatic habitats.
