Chrysosplenium vidalii

Chrysosplenium vidalii, commonly known as Vidal's golden spleenwort, is a small evergreen perennial herb belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white tomentum. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in rocky or forested areas.

Chrysosplenium vidalii is native to parts of Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
