Cyathea subbullata

Cyathea subbullata, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds. The plant has a distinctive trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy material that gives it a rough, hairy appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Cyathea subbullata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various species.
