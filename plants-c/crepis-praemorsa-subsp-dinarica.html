Crepis praemorsa subsp. dinarica

Crepis praemorsa subsp.

dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single head, typical of the Compositae family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
