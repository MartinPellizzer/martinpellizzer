Campanula interrupta

Campanula interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula interrupta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wildflower habitats.
