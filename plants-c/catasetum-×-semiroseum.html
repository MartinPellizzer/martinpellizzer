Catasetum × semiroseum

Catasetum × semiroseum, commonly known as the roseate orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Catasetum × semiroseum is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
