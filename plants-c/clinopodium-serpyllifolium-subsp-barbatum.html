Clinopodium serpyllifolium subsp. barbatum

Clinopodium serpyllifolium subsp.

barbatum, commonly known as hairy field mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are hairy, especially along the veins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in loose spikes, usually purple or pink in color.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
