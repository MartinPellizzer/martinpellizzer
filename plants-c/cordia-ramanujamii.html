Cordia ramanujamii

Cordia ramanujamii, commonly known as the Ramanujam's cotton tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a species of interest in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
