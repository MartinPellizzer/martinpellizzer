Crataegus faxonii var. durifructa

Crataegus faxonii var. durifructa, commonly known as the Durifructa hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorned branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
