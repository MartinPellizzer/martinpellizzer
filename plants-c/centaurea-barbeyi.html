Centaurea barbeyi

Centaurea barbeyi, commonly known as the Barbey's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea barbeyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
