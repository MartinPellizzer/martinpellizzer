Comesperma virgatum var. corniculatum

Comesperma virgatum var. corniculatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironwood, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, featuring a deep root system for water conservation. Its woody stems are covered in a thick, protective layer that helps reduce water loss.

This species is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
