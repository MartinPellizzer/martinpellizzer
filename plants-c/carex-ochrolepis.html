Carex ochrolepis

Carex ochrolepis, commonly known as the yellow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright yellow to green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually borne on slender, branching inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex ochrolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
