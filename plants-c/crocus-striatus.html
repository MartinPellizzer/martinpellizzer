Crocus striatus

Crocus striatus, commonly known as the striated crocus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms that typically appear in shades of yellow to orange. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cup-shaped flowers that are often adorned with a distinctive purple or brown stripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Crocus striatus is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and early blooming habit.
