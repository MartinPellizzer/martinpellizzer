Cryptocarya laevigata var. bowiei

Cryptocarya laevigata var. bowiei, commonly known as the Australian nutmeg or broad-leaved cryptocarya, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are glossy on top and lighter in color underneath. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
