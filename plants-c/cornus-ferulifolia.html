Cornus ferulifolia

Cornus ferulifolia, commonly known as the fragrant cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its finely toothed leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Cornus ferulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
