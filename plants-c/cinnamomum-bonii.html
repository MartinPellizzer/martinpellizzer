Cinnamomum bonii

Cinnamomum bonii, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum bonii, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to southern China, particularly in Yunnan Province, and is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy, dark green upper surface.

The bark is thin, reddish-brown, and has a strong, spicy aroma similar to cinnamon.
