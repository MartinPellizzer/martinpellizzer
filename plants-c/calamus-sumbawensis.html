Calamus sumbawensis

Calamus sumbawensis, commonly known as the Sumbawa rattan, is a climbing palm native to the island of Sumbawa in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, flexible stems used for making rattan products. The plant has pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its growth habit involves climbing using aerial roots, and it thrives in humid, tropical environments.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
