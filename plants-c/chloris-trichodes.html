Chloris trichodes

Chloris trichodes, commonly known as the slender grass or slender chloris, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale-green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Chloris trichodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
