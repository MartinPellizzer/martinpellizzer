Cissus pruriens

Cissus pruriens, commonly known as the itch leaf or devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The leaves are often covered in fine, stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation, giving it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing along rocky outcrops or in shaded areas.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
