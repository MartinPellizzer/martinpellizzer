Callistephus indicus

Callistephus indicus, commonly known as the Indian cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its seeds are dispersed by animals and can persist in the soil for several years.
