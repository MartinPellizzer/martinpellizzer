Crescentia macrophylla

Crescentia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant swamp cucumber, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine with large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces large, yellow, cucumber-like fruits that are edible when mature. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are borne in clusters.

Crescentia macrophylla thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found near rivers and lakes.
