Centaurea alba var. montsicciana

Centaurea alba var. montsicciana, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, preferring full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
