Carex magnoutriculata

Carex magnoutriculata, commonly known as the large-fruit sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in riparian zones.

Carex magnoutriculata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
