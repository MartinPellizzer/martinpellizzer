Cyperus brizimorphus

Cyperus brizimorphus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that can dominate wetland ecosystems.

Cyperus brizimorphus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
