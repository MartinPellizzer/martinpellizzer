Carex pallescens f. pygmaea

Carex pallescens f. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, making it suitable for erosion control and wetland restoration.

The botanical features of Carex pallescens f. pygmaea make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
