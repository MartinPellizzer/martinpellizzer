Chaetocarpus cordifolius

Chaetocarpus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime climates. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in coastal gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
