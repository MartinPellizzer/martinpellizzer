Cryptocarya multipaniculata

Cryptocarya multipaniculata, commonly known as the clustered laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. It produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits.

Cryptocarya multipaniculata is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
