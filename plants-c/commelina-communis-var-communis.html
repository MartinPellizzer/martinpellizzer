Commelina communis var. communis

Commelina communis var. communis, commonly known as the common dayflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, showy blue or violet flowers with three petals and a distinctive white or yellow spatulate bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
