Conyza heliotropifolia

Conyza heliotropifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine hairs, and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant produces numerous slender, hairy stems that often branch near the base, forming a low-growing mat. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Conyza heliotropifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative means.
