Chylismia specicola subsp. specicola

Chylismia specicola subsp.

specicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chylismia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
