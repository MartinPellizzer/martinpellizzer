Coelogyne carnosulilabra

Coelogyne carnosulilabra, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, producing white or pale yellow flowers with a distinct, sweet scent. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Coelogyne carnosulilabra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique fragrance.
