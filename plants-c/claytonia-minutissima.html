Claytonia minutissima

Claytonia minutissima, commonly known as the dwarf spring beauty, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with rosettes of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a distinctive spur.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest floors or along stream banks.
