Centaurea calocephala

Centaurea calocephala, commonly known as the purplehead centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and bristly, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.
