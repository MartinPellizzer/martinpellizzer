Campanula myrtifolia

Campanula myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue, violet, or white. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, often with a fragrant aroma reminiscent of myrtle. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
