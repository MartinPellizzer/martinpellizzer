Cyperus flavescens var. paraensis

Cyperus flavescens var. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraensis flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pale yellow or greenish in color. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
