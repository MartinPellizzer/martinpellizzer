Conophallus sativus

Conophallus sativus, commonly known as the edible mushroom, is a species of fungi that belongs to the family Tricholomataceae.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cap-like structure that ranges in color from pale yellow to brownish hues. The fruiting bodies typically grow in clusters on decaying wood, particularly in temperate forest environments. It is valued for its mild flavor and is often used in various culinary dishes.

However, due to its similarity to other toxic species, it should be handled with caution and proper identification is essential.
