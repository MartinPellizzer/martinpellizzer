Coreopsis tripteris var. deamii

Coreopsis tripteris var. deamii, commonly known as the Deam's tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
