Centaurea dubia subsp. nigrescens

Centaurea dubia subsp.

nigrescens, commonly known as the dark-stemmed common centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features erect, branched stems that are often tinged with purple or dark green. Its flowers are blue to purple, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker stems and leaves compared to other centaury species.
