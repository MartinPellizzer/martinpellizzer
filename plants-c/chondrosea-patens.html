Chondrosea patens

Chondrosea patens, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and silver-green foliage, which help it thrive in saline and sandy environments. The plant has a short, woody stem and produces small, spiny bracts around its flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and unique visual appeal.

Chondrosea patens is adapted to withstand coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
