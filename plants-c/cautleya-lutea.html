Cautleya lutea

Cautleya lutea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cautleya lutea is valued not only for its aesthetic qualities but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
