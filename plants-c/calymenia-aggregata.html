Calymenia aggregata

Calymenia aggregata, commonly known as the clustered calymenia, is a unique plant species belonging to the family Calymeniaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spore-producing structures called calypters, which are clustered in dense groups. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its life cycle involves the production of spores rather than seeds, making it a primitive form of plant reproduction.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Calymenia aggregata is often considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
