Crepis terglouensis

Crepis terglouensis, commonly known as the Terglou's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing herb characterized by its silver-green leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces numerous small achenes, which are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
