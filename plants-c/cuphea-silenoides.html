Cuphea silenoides

Cuphea silenoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink and orange. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea silenoides is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
