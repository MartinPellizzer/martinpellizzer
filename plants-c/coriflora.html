Coriflora

Coriflora, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a rough texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Coriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
