Cylista tomentosa

Cylista tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cylista, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Cylista tomentosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
