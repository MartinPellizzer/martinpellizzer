Coix lacryma-jobi var. novoguineensis

Coix lacryma-jobi var. novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea Job's tears, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces distinctive, tear-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in indigenous communities.
