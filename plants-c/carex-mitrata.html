Carex mitrata

Carex mitrata, commonly known as the mitred sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, consist of small, greenish-brown flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical clusters.

Carex mitrata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
