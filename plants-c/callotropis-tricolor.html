Callotropis tricolor

Callotropis tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, three-colored bracts that range from white to pink and red, giving it a vibrant appearance. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky coastal habitats and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Callotropis tricolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in coastal gardens.
