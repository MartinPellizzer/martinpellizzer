Chrysopogon pictus

Chrysopogon pictus, commonly known as the painted grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that often display a striking blue or silver sheen. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique appearance.
