Calymmodon multisorus

Calymmodon multisorus, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and leathery. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
