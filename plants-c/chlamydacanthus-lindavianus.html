Chlamydacanthus lindavianus

Chlamydacanthus lindavianus, commonly known as the Lindavian spurge, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
