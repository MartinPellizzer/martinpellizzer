Coreopsis mutica var. guerreroana

Coreopsis mutica var. guerreroana, commonly known as the Guerrero coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its yellow to orange flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
