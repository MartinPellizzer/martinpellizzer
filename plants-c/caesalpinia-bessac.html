Caesalpinia bessac

Caesalpinia bessac, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its bright yellow, fluffy inflorescences that resemble small balls. The plant has bipinnate leaves, with each leaflet being narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Caesalpinia bessac is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
