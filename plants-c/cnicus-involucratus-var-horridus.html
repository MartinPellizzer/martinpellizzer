Cnicus involucratus var. horridus

Cnicus involucratus var. horridus, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and serrated, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single central flower surrounded by bracts, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
