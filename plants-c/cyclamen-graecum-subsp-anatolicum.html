Cyclamen graecum subsp. anatolicum

Cyclamen graecum subsp.

anatolicum, commonly known as the Anatolian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-purple flowers during the winter months. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and attractive addition to the cyclamen genus.
