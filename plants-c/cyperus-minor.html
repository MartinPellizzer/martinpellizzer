Cyperus minor

Cyperus minor, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus minor is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
