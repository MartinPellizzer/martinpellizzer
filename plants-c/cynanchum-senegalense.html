Cynanchum senegalense

Cynanchum senegalense, commonly known as African milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing toxic seeds. It is known for its latex sap, which can be irritating to the skin.

Cynanchum senegalense is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
