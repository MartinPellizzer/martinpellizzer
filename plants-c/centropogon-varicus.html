Centropogon varicus

Centropogon varicus, commonly known as the variable centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Centropogon varicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
