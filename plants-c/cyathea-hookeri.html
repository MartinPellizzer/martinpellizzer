Cyathea hookeri

Cyathea hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Pacific and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the ground, while its fronds are used for photosynthesis.

Cyathea hookeri is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
