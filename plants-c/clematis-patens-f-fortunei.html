Clematis patens f. fortunei

Clematis patens f. fortunei, commonly known as the Fortune's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
