Cephalaria leucantha var. albescens

Cephalaria leucantha var. albescens, commonly known as the white-hairy cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems covered in dense, white or pale gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
