Clematis mendocina

Clematis mendocina, commonly known as the Mendocino clematis, is a deciduous vine native to California.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.

Clematis mendocina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or structures.
