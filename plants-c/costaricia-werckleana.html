Costaricia werckleana

Costaricia werckleana, commonly known as the Werckleana costarica, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical rainforests. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that attracts various wildlife, contributing to its ecological importance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
