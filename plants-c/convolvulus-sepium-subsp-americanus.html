Convolvulus sepium subsp. americanus

Convolvulus sepium subsp.

americanus, commonly known as the American bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots allow it to spread rapidly, making it an aggressive weed in agricultural and natural settings.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
