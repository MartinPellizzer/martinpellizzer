Coccoloba × lundellii

Coccoloba × lundellii, commonly known as the hybrid beautyberry, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Coccoloba uvifera and Coccoloba diversifolia. The plant is characterized by its striking clusters of violet to purple berries that appear in the fall. It is typically found in coastal areas of the southeastern United States, thriving in sandy soils.

The leaves are usually broad and oval, with a distinctive waxy texture and a bluish-green hue.
