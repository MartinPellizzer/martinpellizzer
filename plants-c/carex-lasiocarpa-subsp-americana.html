Carex lasiocarpa subsp. americana

Carex lasiocarpa subsp.

americana, commonly known as the American haircap moss, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

This species is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments.
