Chrysolaena nicolackii

Chrysolaena nicolackii, commonly known as the red-leaved chrysolaena, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy red foliage that contrasts with its slender, woody stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
