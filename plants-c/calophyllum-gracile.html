Calophyllum gracile

Calophyllum gracile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved torchwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and is used in furniture and decorative items.

Calophyllum gracile thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
