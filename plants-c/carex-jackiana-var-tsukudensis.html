Carex jackiana var. tsukudensis

Carex jackiana var. tsukudensis, commonly known as Japanese sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and cylindrical inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
