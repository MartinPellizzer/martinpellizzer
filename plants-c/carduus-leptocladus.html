Carduus leptocladus

Carduus leptocladus, commonly known as the slenderleaf thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its spiny, hairy leaves and purple-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and grasslands. It has a taproot system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
