Chuquiraga candolleana

Chuquiraga candolleana, commonly known as the Andean burflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils.

Chuquiraga candolleana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
