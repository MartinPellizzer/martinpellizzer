Cyperus rubromarginatus

Cyperus rubromarginatus, commonly known as the red-margin sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowering spikes are typically reddish in color, giving it its common name.

Cyperus rubromarginatus thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often growing in shallow water or moist soil.
