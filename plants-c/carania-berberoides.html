Carania berberoides

Carania berberoides, commonly known as the goldenroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system and lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in terminal racemes. Its roots are rich in bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

Carania berberoides thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, humid environment for optimal growth.
