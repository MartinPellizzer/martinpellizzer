Cestrum sesseoides

Cestrum sesseoides, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a mild, sweet taste.

Cestrum sesseoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance, though it can be invasive in some regions.
