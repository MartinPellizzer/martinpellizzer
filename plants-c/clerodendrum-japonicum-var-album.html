Clerodendrum japonicum var. album

Clerodendrum japonicum var. album, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle pinkish tinge. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
