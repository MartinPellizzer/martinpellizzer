Cinchona × affinis

Cinchona × affinis, commonly known as the hybrid quinine tree, is a natural hybrid between Cinchona calisaya and Cinchona ledgeriana.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. This plant is valued for its medicinal properties, especially for the production of quinine, which is used in the treatment of malaria. The tree typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, and it produces yellowish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries.
