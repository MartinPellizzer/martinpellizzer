Copiapoa leonensis

Copiapoa leonensis, commonly known as the Leon's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and produces white or pale pink flowers during the cooler months. The plant has a dense, spiny covering that helps protect it from intense sunlight and moisture loss. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to survive.

Copiapoa leonensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
