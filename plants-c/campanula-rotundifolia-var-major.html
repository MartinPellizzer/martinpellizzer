Campanula rotundifolia var. major

Campanula rotundifolia var. major, commonly known as the greater bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
