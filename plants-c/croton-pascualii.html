Croton pascualii

Croton pascualii, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are typically green with a reddish or bronze underside. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits.

Croton pascualii is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
