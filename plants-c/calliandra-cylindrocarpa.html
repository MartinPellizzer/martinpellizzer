Calliandra cylindrocarpa

Calliandra cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the cylindrical calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, which give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Calliandra cylindrocarpa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and drought tolerance.
