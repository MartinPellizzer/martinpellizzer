Carex canescens var. longibracteata

Carex canescens var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are elongated and covered with bracts, giving it its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
