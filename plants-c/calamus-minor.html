Calamus minor

Calamus minor, commonly known as lesser calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, sword-shaped leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, followed by the development of small, drupelike fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and wetland habitats.
