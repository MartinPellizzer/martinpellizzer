Cyperus lutescens var. fulvescens

Cyperus lutescens var. fulvescens, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with yellow or orange hues. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common ornamental and ecological plant in water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
