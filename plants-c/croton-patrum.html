Croton patrum

Croton patrum, commonly known as the red croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and often showy, brightly colored foliage. The plant is notable for its large, palmately lobed leaves that can range in color from green to red or yellow, depending on the variety. Its small, clustered flowers are typically yellow or orange, surrounded by colorful bracts that enhance its ornamental appeal.

Croton patrum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
