Cereus pentapterus

Cereus pentapterus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and remain open for only one night. Its flowers are typically followed by a red, fleshy fruit.

Cereus pentapterus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
