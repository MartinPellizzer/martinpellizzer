Claoxylon pubescens

Claoxylon pubescens, commonly known as the hairy claoxylon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Claoxylon pubescens is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
