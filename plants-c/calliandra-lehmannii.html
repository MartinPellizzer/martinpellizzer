Calliandra lehmannii

Calliandra lehmannii, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy, pink or red inflorescences. The plant has bipinnate leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is drought-tolerant once established.

Calliandra lehmannii also has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
