Crocus hyemalis var. gaillardotii

Crocus hyemalis var. gaillardotii, commonly known as the Gaillardot's crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in late autumn or early winter. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a cluster of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or woodland settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to bloom during the colder months.
