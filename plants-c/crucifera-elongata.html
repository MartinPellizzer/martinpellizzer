Crucifera elongata

Crucifera elongata, commonly known as the elongated mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers.

This plant typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its rapid growth cycle, often completing its life cycle within a single growing season. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, round pods containing several seeds.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
