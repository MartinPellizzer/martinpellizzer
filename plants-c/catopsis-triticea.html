Catopsis triticea

Catopsis triticea, commonly known as the tufted moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and is often found in shaded, moist environments. The plant has short, cylindrical stems with closely spaced leaves that are typically green to yellowish in color. It is a cryptogamic plant, reproducing through spores rather than seeds.

Catopsis triticea plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitats for various invertebrates.
