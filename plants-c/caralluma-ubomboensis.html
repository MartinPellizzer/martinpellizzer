Caralluma ubomboensis

Caralluma ubomboensis, commonly known as the Ubombo devil's tongue, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It produces small, orange fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
