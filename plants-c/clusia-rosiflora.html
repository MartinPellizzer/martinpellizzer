Clusia rosiflora

Clusia rosiflora, commonly known as the roseleaf clusia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Clusia rosiflora is also known for its adaptability to both indoor and outdoor environments.
