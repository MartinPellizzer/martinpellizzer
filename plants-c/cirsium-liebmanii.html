Cirsium liebmanii

Cirsium liebmanii, commonly known as the Liebmann's thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the central and eastern United States, particularly found in prairie and dry woodland habitats. The plant is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish composite flowers. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and blooms from late summer to early autumn.

Cirsium liebmanii is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a valuable plant for pollinators.
