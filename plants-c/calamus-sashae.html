Calamus sashae

Calamus sashae, commonly known as the fragrant sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a tall, flowering stem. Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in wetland or marshy environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The plant's aromatic qualities make it a notable species within its ecological niche.
