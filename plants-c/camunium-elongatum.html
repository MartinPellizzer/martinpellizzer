Camunium elongatum

Camunium elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed camunium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slight serration along the edges.

Camunium elongatum produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually greenish-white in color.
