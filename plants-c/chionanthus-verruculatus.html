Chionanthus verruculatus

Chionanthus verruculatus, commonly known as the nodding whiteflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, pendant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
