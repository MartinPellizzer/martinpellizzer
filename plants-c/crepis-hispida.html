Crepis hispida

Crepis hispida, commonly known as hispid goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
