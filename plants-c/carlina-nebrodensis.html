Carlina nebrodensis

Carlina nebrodensis, commonly known as the alpine carline thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes where conditions are harsh. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its purple-blue flower heads appear in late summer.

Carlina nebrodensis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
