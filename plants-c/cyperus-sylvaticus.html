Cyperus sylvaticus

Cyperus sylvaticus, commonly known as swamp sedgewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense spikes.

Cyperus sylvaticus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
