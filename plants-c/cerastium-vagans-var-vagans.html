Cerastium vagans var. vagans

Cerastium vagans var. vagans, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant. The flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals and are borne on slender stems that rise from the center of the rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

The plant is often found in temperate climates and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through runners.
