Carex flava subsp. oedocarpa

Carex flava subsp.

oedocarpa, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
