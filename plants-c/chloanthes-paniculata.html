Chloanthes paniculata

Chloanthes paniculata, commonly known as the fragrant chloanthes, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant produces long, branched inflorescences that bear numerous small, clustered blooms. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Chloanthes paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
