Ceanothus velutinus subsp. hookeri

Ceanothus velutinus subsp.

hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly California. It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and showy clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, velvety hair, contributing to its common name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
