Carex cilicica subsp. muratica

Carex cilicica subsp.

muratica, commonly known as the Muratic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
