Coreopsis bacana

Coreopsis bacana, commonly known as the slender coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with prominent orange centers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis bacana is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
