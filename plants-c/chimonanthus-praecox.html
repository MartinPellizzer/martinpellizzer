Chimonanthus praecox

Chimonanthus praecox, commonly known as the winter sweet, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly palatable. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The winter sweet is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to add color to gardens during the colder months.
