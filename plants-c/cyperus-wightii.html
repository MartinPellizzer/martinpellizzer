Cyperus wightii

Cyperus wightii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. The plant has fibrous roots and produces stolons, which help in its spread and establishment.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
