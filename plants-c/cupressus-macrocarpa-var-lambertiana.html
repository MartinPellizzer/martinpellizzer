Cupressus macrocarpa var. lambertiana

Cupressus macrocarpa var. lambertiana, commonly known as the Lawson cypress, is a cultivar of the Australian white cypress.

It is characterized by its dense, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are bluish-green in color. This variety is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It produces small, round cones and is relatively disease-resistant, making it a popular choice for gardens and parks.

The plant is also known for its slow growth rate and long lifespan, often living over 100 years in suitable environments.
