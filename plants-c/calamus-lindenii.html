Calamus lindenii

Calamus lindenii, commonly known as the Lindberg's canes, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, arrow-shaped leaves. It produces striking inflorescences with yellow spadices and white spathes, which are typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus lindenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
