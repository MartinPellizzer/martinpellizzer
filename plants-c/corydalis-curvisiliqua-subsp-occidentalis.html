Corydalis curvisiliqua subsp. occidentalis

Corydalis curvisiliqua subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western curlleaf lousewort, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. Its tubular flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
