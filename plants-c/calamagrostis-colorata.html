Calamagrostis colorata

Calamagrostis colorata, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple or reddish coloration in its inflorescence, which sets it apart from other grasses. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces tall, cylindrical spikelets that are often arranged in panicles.

Calamagrostis colorata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
