Cynanchum petrense

Cynanchum petrense, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in arid to semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
