Coreopsis pulchella

Coreopsis pulchella, commonly known as the showy coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and linear, hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its attractive appearance, with "pulchella" meaning beautiful in Latin.
