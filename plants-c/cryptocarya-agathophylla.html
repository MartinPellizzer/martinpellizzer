Cryptocarya agathophylla

Cryptocarya agathophylla, commonly known as the glossy ash or mountain ash, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the wet tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, glossy leaves that are often elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in panicles, and it produces drupes that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitats.
