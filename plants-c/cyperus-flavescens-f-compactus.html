Cyperus flavescens f. compactus

Cyperus flavescens f. compactus, commonly known as the compact flatsedge, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow or brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Due to its dense growth habit, it is sometimes used in ornamental landscaping or as a ground cover in wet areas.
