Cornus alba var. splendens

Cornus alba var. splendens, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red stems that become more vivid in the winter months. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
