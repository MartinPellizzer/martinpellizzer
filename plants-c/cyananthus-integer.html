Cyananthus integer

Cyananthus integer, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cyananthus integer is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
