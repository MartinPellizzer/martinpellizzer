Casuarina truncata

Casuarina truncata, commonly known as the coastal she-oak, is a hardy evergreen tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, needle-like foliage that forms dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in coastal and saline environments, making it highly adaptable to harsh conditions.

Casuarina truncata is also valued for its ability to stabilize soils and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
