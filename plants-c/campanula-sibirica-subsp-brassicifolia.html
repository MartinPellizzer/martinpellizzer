Campanula sibirica subsp. brassicifolia

Campanula sibirica subsp.

brassicifolia, commonly known as Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly toothed margin.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
