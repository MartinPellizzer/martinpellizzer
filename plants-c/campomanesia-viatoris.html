Campomanesia viatoris

Campomanesia viatoris, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or araçá-do-mato, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is sometimes cultivated in home gardens and conservation areas.
