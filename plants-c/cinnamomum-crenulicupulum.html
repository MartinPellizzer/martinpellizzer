Cinnamomum crenulicupulum

Cinnamomum crenulicupulum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum crenulatum, is a species of flowering plant in the laurel family, Lauraceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that contain seeds, and its bark is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties.
