Claytonia sibirica

Claytonia sibirica, commonly known as Siberian spring beauty, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Portulacaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

Due to its delicate appearance and early blooming habit, it is a symbol of resilience in harsh climates.
