Ceropegia candelabrum var. candelabrum

Ceropegia candelabrum var. candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick fig, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, branched stem that resembles a candlestick, with small, waxy leaves arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces unique flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a complex structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
