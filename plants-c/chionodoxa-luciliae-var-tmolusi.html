Chionodoxa luciliae var. tmolusi

Chionodoxa luciliae var. tmolusi, commonly known as the Tmolus saffron crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the region of Tmolus in ancient Anatolia.

It produces clusters of delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
