Cinnamomum bejolghota var. pubescens

Cinnamomum bejolghota var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cinnamon tree, is a species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family and is noted for its unique, fragrant foliage. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The varietal name "pubescens" refers to the hairy or pubescent texture of its young shoots and leaves.
