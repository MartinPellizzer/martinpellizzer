Campanula lezgina

Campanula lezgina, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a woolly underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula lezgina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
