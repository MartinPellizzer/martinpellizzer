Cyperus turrillii

Cyperus turrillii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in wet or marshy environments. Cyperus turrillii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
