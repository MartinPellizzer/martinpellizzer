Carex soleirolii

Carex soleirolii, commonly known as the soleirolii sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shady conditions.
