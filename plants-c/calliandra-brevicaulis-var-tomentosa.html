Calliandra brevicaulis var. tomentosa

Calliandra brevicaulis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and clusters of bright red or orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and ornamental gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
