Carduus alpestris

Carduus alpestris, commonly known as the alpine thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has deeply lobed, gray-green leaves covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

Carduus alpestris plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
