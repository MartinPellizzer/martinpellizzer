Calamagrostis inaequiglumis f. nipponica

Calamagrostis inaequiglumis f. nipponica, commonly known as Japanese silvergrass, is a decorative grass species native to Japan.

It features narrow, silver-blue foliage that turns coppery in the autumn, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. The plant produces fluffy, airy plumes that enhance its ornamental value, especially in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
