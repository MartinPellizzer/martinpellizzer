Cytisus hirsutus var. pirinicus

Cytisus hirsutus var. pirinicus, commonly known as the Pirin Broom, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
