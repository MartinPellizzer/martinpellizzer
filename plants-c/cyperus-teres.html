Cyperus teres

Cyperus teres, commonly known as the narrow leaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It typically grows in wet or moist soils, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus teres is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appearance and ability to thrive in humid environments.
