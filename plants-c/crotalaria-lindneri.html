Crotalaria lindneri

Crotalaria lindneri, commonly known as the Lindner's cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several angular seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria lindneri is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
