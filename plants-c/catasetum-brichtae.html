Catasetum brichtae

Catasetum brichtae, commonly known as the Bricht's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with striking, often brightly colored flowers. Its flowers feature specialized structures called pollinia, which are adapted for specific pollination mechanisms.

Catasetum brichtae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
