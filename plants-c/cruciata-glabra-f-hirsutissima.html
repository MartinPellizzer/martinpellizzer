Cruciata glabra f. hirsutissima

Cruciata glabra f. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy cruciata, is a variant of the common cruciata, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are elongated and opposite, often with a slightly waxy texture.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
