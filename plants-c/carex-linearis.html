Carex linearis

Carex linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex linearis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
