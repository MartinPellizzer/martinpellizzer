Cousinia sabzevarensis

Cousinia sabzevarensis, commonly known as the Persian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Cousinia sabzevarensis is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
