Conophytum turrigerum

Conophytum turrigerum, commonly known as the "Cape Karoo stone plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rounded body with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring season. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Conophytum turrigerum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
