Carduus hamulosus

Carduus hamulosus, commonly known as the hooked thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds.
