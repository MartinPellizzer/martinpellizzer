Cyperus usitatus var. usitatus

Cyperus usitatus var. usitatus, commonly known as the common flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its flowering spikes are usually clustered at the base of the plant, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus usitatus var. usitatus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
