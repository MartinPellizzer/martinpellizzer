Conyza flabellata

Conyza flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
