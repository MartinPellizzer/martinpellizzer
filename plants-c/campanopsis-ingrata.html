Campanopsis ingrata

Campanopsis ingrata, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Campanopsis ingrata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
