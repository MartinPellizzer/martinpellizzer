Chloranthus verticillatus

Chloranthus verticillatus, commonly known as the clustered chloranthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae, which is characterized by its unique floral structures and simple leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth margin and a glossy surface.
