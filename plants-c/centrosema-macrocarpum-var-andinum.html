Centrosema macrocarpum var. andinum

Centrosema macrocarpum var. andinum, commonly known as the Andean horsegram, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
