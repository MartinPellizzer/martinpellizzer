Cremosperma congruens

Cremosperma congruens, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming throughout the warm seasons. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release small, hairy seeds when mature.

Cremosperma congruens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
