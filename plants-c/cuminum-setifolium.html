Cuminum setifolium

Cuminum setifolium, commonly known as the slender cumin or garden cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, linear, and slightly aromatic. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds, which are the part most commonly used, are small, oblong, and have a strong, pungent aroma.

Cuminum setifolium is valued both for its culinary uses and its historical significance in traditional medicine.
