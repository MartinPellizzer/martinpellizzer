Callisia scopulorum

Callisia scopulorum, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, arid environments and is often found growing on limestone or sandy soils. It has a low-growing habit and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Callisia scopulorum is also known for its ability to propagate through both seeds and vegetative means.
