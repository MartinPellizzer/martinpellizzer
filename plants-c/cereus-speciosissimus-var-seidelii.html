Cereus speciosissimus var. seidelii

Cereus speciosissimus var. seidelii, commonly known as the Christmas star cactus, is a hybrid cactus species native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of bright red or pink flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant has spiny, ribbed stems that store water, adapting it to arid environments. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.

This species is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and seasonal blooming habit.
