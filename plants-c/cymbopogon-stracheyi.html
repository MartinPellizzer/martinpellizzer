Cymbopogon stracheyi

Cymbopogon stracheyi, commonly known as Strachey's citron grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and aromatic leaves. The plant is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine. Its growth habit includes rhizomes and stolons, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Cymbopogon stracheyi thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
