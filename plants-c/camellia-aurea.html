Camellia aurea

Camellia aurea, commonly known as the golden camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red or pink base. The plant produces elliptical, glossy leaves that are dark green and leathery in texture. Camellia aurea thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to bloom reliably in winter and early spring.
