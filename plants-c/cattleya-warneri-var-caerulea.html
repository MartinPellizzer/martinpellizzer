Cattleya warneri var. caerulea

Cattleya warneri var. caerulea, commonly known as the Blue Cattleya, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from pale blue to deep violet, often with a contrasting white or pink lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique coloration and elegant appearance.
