Croton grewiifolius

Croton grewiifolius, commonly known as the African croton, is a tropical shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that vary in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a houseplant or in gardens.
