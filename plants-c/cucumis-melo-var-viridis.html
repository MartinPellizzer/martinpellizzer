Cucumis melo var. viridis

Cucumis melo var. viridis, commonly known as the green melon, is a variety of the melon plant characterized by its elongated, cylindrical fruit.

The fruit typically has a smooth, waxy skin that ranges from light green to a deeper emerald hue. It contains a crisp, juicy interior with a mild, sweet flavor, often used in fresh consumption or as a decorative element. Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to southern Asia.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and versatility in culinary applications.
