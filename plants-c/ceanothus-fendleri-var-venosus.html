Ceanothus fendleri var. venosus

Ceanothus fendleri var. venosus, commonly known as the canyon ceanothus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky habitats. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with prominent veins that give it its scientific name.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
