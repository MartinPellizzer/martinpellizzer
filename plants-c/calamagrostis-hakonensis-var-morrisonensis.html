Calamagrostis hakonensis var. morrisonensis

Calamagrostis hakonensis var. morrisonensis, commonly known as Morrison's moorgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the wetlands of northern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often a striking silver-green color. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that bloom in late summer, adding a decorative touch to its habitat.

Morrison's moorgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
