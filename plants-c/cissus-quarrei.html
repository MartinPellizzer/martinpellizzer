Cissus quarrei

Cissus quarrei, commonly known as the silver arrow vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus quarrei is also valued for its ability to climb and trail, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellises.
