Cinchona philippica

Cinchona philippica, commonly known as Philippine quinine tree, is a species of the Rubiaceae family native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that contain seeds, and it is known for its medicinal properties due to the presence of alkaloids like quinine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

This species plays an important role in both traditional medicine and ecological systems in its native habitat.
