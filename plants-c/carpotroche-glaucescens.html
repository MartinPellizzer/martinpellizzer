Carpotroche glaucescens

Carpotroche glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green carpet grass, is a perennial herb native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves that have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
