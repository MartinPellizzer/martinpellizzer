Carex sororia var. pseudobracteosa

Carex sororia var. pseudobracteosa, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized leaf structures called culms. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to moist soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
