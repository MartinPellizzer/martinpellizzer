Chrysopogon celebicus

Chrysopogon celebicus, commonly known as the Celebes grass, is a perennial grass native to the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with slender spikelets, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
