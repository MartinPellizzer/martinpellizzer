Calpurnia glabrata

Calpurnia glabrata, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its root system is fibrous, making it adaptable to a variety of soil types, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Calpurnia glabrata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in warm climates.
