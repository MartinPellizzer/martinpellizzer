Cleome didynarna

Cleome didynarna, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often branched inflorescences that resemble spider legs. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with simple, alternate leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. Cleome didynarna is cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant blooms and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soils.

It thrives in full sun and is often used in gardens and as a cut flower.
