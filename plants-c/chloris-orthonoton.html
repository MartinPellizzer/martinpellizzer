Chloris orthonoton

Chloris orthonoton, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often forming dense mats. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, with pale green to blueish spikelets.

Chloris orthonoton is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for livestock in arid regions.
