Cerasus caproniana var. multiplex

Cerasus caproniana var. multiplex, commonly known as the Chinese wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
