Chysis aurea

Chysis aurea, commonly known as golden chysis, is a striking flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its bright blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Chysis aurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
