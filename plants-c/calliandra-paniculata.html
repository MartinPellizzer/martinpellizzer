Calliandra paniculata

Calliandra paniculata, commonly known as the sicklepod or red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red inflorescences that resemble clusters of small flowers. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological systems.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability.
