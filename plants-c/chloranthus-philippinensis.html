Chloranthus philippinensis

Chloranthus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine chloranthus, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae, which is characterized by its unique floral structures and often aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests, showcasing its preference for humid environments. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth surface and a distinct venation pattern.

The flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a yellowish or greenish hue, contributing to its ecological role in pollination and habitat support.
