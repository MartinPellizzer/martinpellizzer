Crinum

Crinum, commonly known as the spider lily, is a genus of flowering plants in the Amaryllidaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. Crinum species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, Africa, and Australia. They typically have strap-like leaves and produce bulbous underground storage organs.

Many species are cultivated for their ornamental value and can thrive in both moist and well-drained soils.
