Cousinia purpurea

Cousinia purpurea, commonly known as the purple cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
