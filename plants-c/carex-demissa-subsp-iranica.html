Carex demissa subsp. iranica

Carex demissa subsp.

iranica, commonly known as the Iranian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Iran. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
