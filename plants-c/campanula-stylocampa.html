Campanula stylocampa

Campanula stylocampa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and meadows. The plant features slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are bell-shaped, usually blue or violet, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Campanula stylocampa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
