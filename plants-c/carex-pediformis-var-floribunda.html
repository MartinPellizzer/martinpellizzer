Carex pediformis var. floribunda

Carex pediformis var. floribunda, commonly known as the flowering sedges, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and naturalized landscapes.
