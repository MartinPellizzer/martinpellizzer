Chaetachme aristata var. longifolia

Chaetachme aristata var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, narrow leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
