Calceolaria georgiana

Calceolaria georgiana, commonly known as the George's slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored, ranging from red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds.

Calceolaria georgiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
