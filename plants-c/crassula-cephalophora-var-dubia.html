Crassula cephalophora var. dubia

Crassula cephalophora var. dubia, commonly known as the "Cape Harefoot" or "Cape Hare's Foot," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple markings. The leaves are arranged in a rosette-like pattern, giving the plant a distinctive, compact appearance. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
