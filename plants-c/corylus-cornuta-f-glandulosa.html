Corylus cornuta f. glandulosa

Corylus cornuta f. glandulosa, commonly known as the beaked hazel, is a variant of the hazel genus native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, beak-like buds and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The leaves are typically oval with a serrated edge, and the plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its edible nuts, enclosed in a spiny husk, are an important food source for wildlife and humans.

The shrub is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to different growing conditions.
