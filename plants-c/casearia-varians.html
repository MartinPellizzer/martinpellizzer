Casearia varians

Casearia varians, commonly known as the variable casearia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
