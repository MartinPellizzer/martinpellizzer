Caesalpinia riojana

Caesalpinia riojana, commonly known as the Rioja flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
