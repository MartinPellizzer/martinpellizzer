Cephalophyllum pittenii

Cephalophyllum pittenii, commonly known as the Cape Peninsula candelabra, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with fleshy, cylindrical stems that resemble a candelabra. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the branches. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Cephalophyllum pittenii is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.
