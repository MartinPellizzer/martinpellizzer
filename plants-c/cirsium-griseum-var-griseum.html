Cirsium griseum var. griseum

Cirsium griseum var. griseum, commonly known as the gray thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, grayish-green leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a distinctive silvery appearance.

The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
