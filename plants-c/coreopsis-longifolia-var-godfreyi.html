Coreopsis longifolia var. godfreyi

Coreopsis longifolia var. godfreyi, commonly known as Godfrey's tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
