Clinopodium lepidum

Clinopodium lepidum, commonly known as slender woodruff, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, and it produces small, nut-like fruits.

Clinopodium lepidum thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
