Clerodendrum morigono

Clerodendrum morigono, commonly known as the African bignonia, is a tropical shrub native to Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in warm, humid climates.

Clerodendrum morigono is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
