Crataegus laevigata f. bicrenulata

Crataegus laevigata f. bicrenulata, commonly known as the double-crenulate hawthorn, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval to ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces white to pink flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in garden settings.
