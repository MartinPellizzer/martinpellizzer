Calpurnia villosa var. intrusa

Calpurnia villosa var. intrusa, commonly known as the hairy calpurnia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
