Cassia arowana

Cassia arowana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces long, slender pods containing seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Cassia arowana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
