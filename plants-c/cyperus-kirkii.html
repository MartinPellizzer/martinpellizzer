Cyperus kirkii

Cyperus kirkii, commonly known as the African sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense clusters near water bodies. Cyperus kirkii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens or used in traditional medicine.
