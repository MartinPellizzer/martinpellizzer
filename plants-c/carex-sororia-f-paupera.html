Carex sororia f. paupera

Carex sororia f. paupera, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers. This subspecies is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense mats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet or boggy soils.
