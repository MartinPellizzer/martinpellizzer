Connarus wightii

Connarus wightii, commonly known as the Indian ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. It produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules that contain the seeds.

Connarus wightii is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
