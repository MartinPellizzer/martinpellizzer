Chaerophyllum hirsutum var. elegans

Chaerophyllum hirsutum var. elegans, commonly known as the silverleaf hedge mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
