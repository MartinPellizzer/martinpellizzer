Cyrtosperma subulispathum

Cyrtosperma subulispathum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a distinctive, palmate venation pattern. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

The plant is cultivated for its starchy rhizomes, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
