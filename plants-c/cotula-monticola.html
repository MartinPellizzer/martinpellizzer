Cotula monticola

Cotula monticola, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Cotula monticola has narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
