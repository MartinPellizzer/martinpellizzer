Carex quaternaria

Carex quaternaria, commonly known as the four-ranked sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage, with leaves that are typically narrow and slightly pointed. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Carex quaternaria is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
