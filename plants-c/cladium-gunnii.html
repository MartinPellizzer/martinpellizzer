Cladium gunnii

Cladium gunnii, commonly known as the giant swamp grass, is a tall, perennial plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant thrives in waterlogged or muddy soils and is often found in marshy environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes.

Cladium gunnii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
