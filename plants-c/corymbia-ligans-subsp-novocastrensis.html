Corymbia ligans subsp. novocastrensis

Corymbia ligans subsp.

novocastrensis, commonly known as the Sydney red gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, grey to reddish bark that peels in thin, papery layers.

The leaves are lance-shaped, glossy green on top, and pale underneath, often with a slight droop. It produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands.
