Cypripedium × inspirator

Cypripedium × inspirator, commonly known as the spurred lady's tresses, is a hybrid orchid native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear dense clusters of pale pink to white flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swamps. It has a long flowering period, often blooming from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
