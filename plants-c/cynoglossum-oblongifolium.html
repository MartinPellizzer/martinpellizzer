Cynoglossum oblongifolium

Cynoglossum oblongifolium, commonly known as broadleaf mouse-ear, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
