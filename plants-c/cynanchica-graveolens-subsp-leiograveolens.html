Cynanchica graveolens subsp. leiograveolens

Cynanchica graveolens subsp.

leiograveolens, commonly known as the fragrant milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It is characterized by its milky sap, which is a common feature among plants in the Asclepiadaceae family.
