Crotalaria lanceolata subsp. exigua

Crotalaria lanceolata subsp.

exigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a hairy stem and produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

This subspecies is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
