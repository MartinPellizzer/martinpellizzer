Croton antisyphiliticus var. latifolius

Croton antisyphiliticus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features large, palmately lobed leaves that are often green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
