Chaetochloa viridis var. weinmannii

Chaetochloa viridis var. weinmannii, commonly known as the green hairgrass, is a variant of the green hairgrass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with fine, needle-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant forms low-growing mats or clumps, often found in moist, well-drained soils near water bodies. Its leaves are usually arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its ability to thrive in humid environments and its use in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
