Cnemidaria grandifolia var. obtusa

Cnemidaria grandifolia var. obtusa, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a species of aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are broad and slightly pointed, typically floating on the water's surface. The plant has long, slender stems that often root in shallow water or muddy substrates. Its flowers are small and white, emerging on slender stalks above the water, adding to its ornamental value.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
