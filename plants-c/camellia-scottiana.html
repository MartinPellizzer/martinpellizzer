Camellia scottiana

Camellia scottiana, commonly known as the Scott's camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces elliptical, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Camellia scottiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant.
