Chrysanthemum tadshikorum

Chrysanthemum tadshikorum, commonly known as the Tibetan chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and has small, yellow to white florets arranged in a clustered inflorescence. It is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in harsh mountainous environments.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
