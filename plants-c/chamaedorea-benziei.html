Chamaedorea benziei

Chamaedorea benziei, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a compact, upright palm that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 feet, featuring a slender trunk and glossy, feather-like leaves. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with consistent moisture. It is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in indoor gardening due to its ability to tolerate low-light conditions.

Chamaedorea benziei is also known for its air-purifying qualities, making it a popular choice for both residential and commercial spaces.
