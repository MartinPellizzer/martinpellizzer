Campanopsis debilis

Campanopsis debilis, commonly known as the weak bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture.

Campanopsis debilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
