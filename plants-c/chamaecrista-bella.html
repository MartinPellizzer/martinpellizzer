Chamaecrista bella

Chamaecrista bella, commonly known as the pretty leaf crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Chamaecrista bella is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
