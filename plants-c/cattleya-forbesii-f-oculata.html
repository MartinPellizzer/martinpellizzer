Cattleya forbesii f. oculata

Cattleya forbesii f. oculata, commonly known as the "Ocular Cattleya," is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking, showy flowers.

It features large, velvety petals with a prominent white or yellow eye-like marking, which gives it its common name. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture from the air.

Its flowers bloom in shades of white, yellow, and orange, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
