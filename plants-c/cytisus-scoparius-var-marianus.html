Cytisus scoparius var. marianus

Cytisus scoparius var. marianus, commonly known as the Marian's broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and open woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
