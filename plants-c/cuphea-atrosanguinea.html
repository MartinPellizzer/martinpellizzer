Cuphea atrosanguinea

Cuphea atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black blood cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red to black bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental bracts and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
