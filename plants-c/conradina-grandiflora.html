Conradina grandiflora

Conradina grandiflora, commonly known as the Florida flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in coastal dunes and sandhill habitats. It produces small, edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife species.

Conradina grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
