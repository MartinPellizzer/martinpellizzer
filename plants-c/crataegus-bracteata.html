Crataegus bracteata

Crataegus bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types.

Crataegus bracteata is often found in open woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.
