Caesalpinia rhombifolia

Caesalpinia rhombifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Caesalpinia rhombifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
