Coniogramme fraxinea f. tripinnata

Coniogramme fraxinea f. tripinnata, commonly known as the three-parted fern, is a variant of the common fern Coniogramme fraxinea.

It is characterized by its finely divided, tripinnate leaves that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The fronds are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. This fern thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
