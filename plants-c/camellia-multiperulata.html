Camellia multiperulata

Camellia multiperulata, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces striking red or pink flowers during the winter months. The plant is evergreen and thrives in moist, well-drained soil in partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Camellia multiperulata is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
