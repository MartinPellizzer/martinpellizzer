Cyathea aramaganensis

Cyathea aramaganensis, commonly known as the Aramagan tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, with its fronds emerging from the top of the trunk in a spiral arrangement. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to several meters, contributing to the plant's ornamental value.
