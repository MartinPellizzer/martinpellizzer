Cirrhopetalum curtisii

Cirrhopetalum curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's cirrhopetalum, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected natural reserves.
