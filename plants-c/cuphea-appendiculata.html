Cuphea appendiculata

Cuphea appendiculata, commonly known as the whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea appendiculata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
