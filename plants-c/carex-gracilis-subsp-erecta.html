Carex gracilis subsp. erecta

Carex gracilis subsp.

erecta, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
