Cyperus subrotundus

Cyperus subrotundus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along waterways, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive pale green to bluish-green color.

Cyperus subrotundus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
