Crotalaria dura subsp. mozambica

Crotalaria dura subsp.

mozambica, commonly known as the Mozambique crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and savannas. It features erect stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers in racemate clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits and are often used in traditional medicine in some regions.
