Cassia retusa

Cassia retusa, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its scientific name, Cassia retusa, refers to the straight or unbranched stems and the retuse (slightly notched) leaf tips.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
