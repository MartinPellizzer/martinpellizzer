Calotropis procera

Calotropis procera, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring thick, waxy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant produces a milky sap, which is toxic and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its roots and leaves contain alkaloids and cardiac glycosides, making it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
