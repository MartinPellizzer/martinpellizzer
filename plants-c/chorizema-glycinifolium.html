Chorizema glycinifolium

Chorizema glycinifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf cholla, is a succulent cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has a distinctive appearance with its spiny, branching stems that are covered in a silvery, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by green, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, a characteristic common in many cacti.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and minimizing water loss through its specialized leaf structure.
