Cirsium canoasense

Cirsium canoasense, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, and its inflorescence consists of a cluster of bracts surrounding the flower heads. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its striking appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
