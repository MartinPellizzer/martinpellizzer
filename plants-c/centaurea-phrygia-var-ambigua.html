Centaurea phrygia var. ambigua

Centaurea phrygia var. ambigua, commonly known as the Turkish knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a rough texture.

It is often found in regions with a temperate climate and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
