Calliandra macqueenii

Calliandra macqueenii, commonly known as the MacQueen's calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Calliandra macqueenii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
