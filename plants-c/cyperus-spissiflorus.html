Cyperus spissiflorus

Cyperus spissiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of white to pale blue flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus spissiflorus thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in shallow water or moist soil.

It is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to wet conditions.
