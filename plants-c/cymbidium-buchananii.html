Cymbidium buchananii

Cymbidium buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, India, and Myanmar.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing large, spatulate petals in shades of white, pink, and red. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cymbidium buchananii is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to a variety of growing conditions.
