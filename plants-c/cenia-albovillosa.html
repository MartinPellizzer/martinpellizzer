Cenia albovillosa

Cenia albovillosa, commonly known as the white-hairy cenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, white, woolly hairs covering its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
