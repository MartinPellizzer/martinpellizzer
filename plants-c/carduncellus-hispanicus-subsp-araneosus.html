Carduncellus hispanicus subsp. araneosus

Carduncellus hispanicus subsp.

araneosus, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features spiny, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its spines and hairy leaves are adaptations for reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.
