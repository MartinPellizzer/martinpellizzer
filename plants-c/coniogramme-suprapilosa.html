Coniogramme suprapilosa

Coniogramme suprapilosa, commonly known as the silky fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Polystichaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant features bipinnate leaves with a soft, silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.
