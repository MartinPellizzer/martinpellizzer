Cyphomandra heterophylla

Cyphomandra heterophylla, commonly known as the yellow bell pepper, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its yellow to orange fruits, which are typically harvested when they are still immature. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, white flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is edible and can be used in various culinary applications, though it is less common than other pepper varieties.
