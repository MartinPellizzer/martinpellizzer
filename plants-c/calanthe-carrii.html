Calanthe carrii

Calanthe carrii, commonly known as the Carri's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern around the base of the plant.

Calanthe carrii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
