Cactus albisetosus

Cactus albisetosus, commonly known as the white-hair cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, bristle-like spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate habitat. Its adaptations make it well-suited for survival in extreme desert conditions.

Despite its hardy nature, it is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat destruction and over-collection.
