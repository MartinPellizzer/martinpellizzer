Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. integrifolium

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. integrifolium, commonly known as the oxeye daisy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white ray florets surrounding a yellow central disk, forming a daisy-like appearance. The leaves are typically basal, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture. This species is often found in meadows, pastures, and roadsides, thriving in full sun.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in garden settings.
