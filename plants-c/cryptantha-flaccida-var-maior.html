Cryptantha flaccida var. maior

Cryptantha flaccida var. maior, commonly known as the large-flowered cryptantha, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers, which are usually pale blue to violet in color, bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
