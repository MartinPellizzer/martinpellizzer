Carex bebbii

Carex bebbii, commonly known as Bebb's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Carex bebbii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
