Cissus triangularis

Cissus triangularis, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are triangular in shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a decorative plant due to its hardiness and attractive foliage.

Cissus triangularis is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air.
