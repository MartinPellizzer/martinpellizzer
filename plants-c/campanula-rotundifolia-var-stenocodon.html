Campanula rotundifolia var. stenocodon

Campanula rotundifolia var. stenocodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rounded, hairy leaves that are typically smaller than those of the typical variety. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
