Campanumoea parviflora

Campanumoea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanumoea parviflora is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
