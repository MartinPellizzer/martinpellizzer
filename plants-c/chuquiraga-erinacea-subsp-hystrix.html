Chuquiraga erinacea subsp. hystrix

Chuquiraga erinacea subsp.

hystrix, commonly known as the spiny chuquiraga, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and yellow-orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique structure and resilience.
