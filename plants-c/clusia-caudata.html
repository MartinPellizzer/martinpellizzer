Clusia caudata

Clusia caudata, commonly known as the fatshrub, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Clusia caudata is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in horticulture.
