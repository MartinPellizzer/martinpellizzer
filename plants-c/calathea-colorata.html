Calathea colorata

Calathea colorata, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns and variegation. The leaves are typically oval or heart-shaped and often change color throughout the day, a phenomenon known as nyctinasty. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

The plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
