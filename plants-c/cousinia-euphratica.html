Cousinia euphratica

Cousinia euphratica, commonly known as the Euphrates cudweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, often grayish foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia euphratica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
