Cunila fruticosa

Cunila fruticosa, commonly known as sweet germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs.
