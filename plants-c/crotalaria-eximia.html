Crotalaria eximia

Crotalaria eximia, commonly known as the yellow crown flower, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a crown-like appearance. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated seed pods that split open when mature. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Crotalaria eximia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
