Cirsium californicum var. pseudoreglense

Cirsium californicum var. pseudoreglense, commonly known as the California thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. It has a deep taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
