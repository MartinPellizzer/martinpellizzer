Cupania fulvida

Cupania fulvida, commonly known as the yellow cupania, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it has simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Cupania fulvida is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
