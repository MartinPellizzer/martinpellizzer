Chaerophyllum verticillatum

Chaerophyllum verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered cow-wheat, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
