Callerya bonatiana

Callerya bonatiana, commonly known as the Bonati's callerya, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees and other structures. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often with a distinctive pink or red coloration.

The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.
