Calamus sphaerulifer

Calamus sphaerulifer, commonly known as the globe spherulose calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and large, showy inflorescences that bear white or yellow spadices. Its leaves are usually long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
