Cassia speciosa var. nervosa

Cassia speciosa var. nervosa, commonly known as the veined golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The leaves are compound, with a prominent veining pattern that gives the variety its name. The plant produces oblong, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to tropical and subtropical climates.
