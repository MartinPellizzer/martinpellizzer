Clematis × atroviolacea

Clematis × atroviolacea, commonly known as the purple clematis, is a hybrid species native to the eastern United States.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its dark purple to almost black flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained, fertile soil for optimal growth.

Clematis × atroviolacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its striking floral display.
