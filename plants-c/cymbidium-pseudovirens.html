Cymbidium pseudovirens

Cymbidium pseudovirens, commonly known as the Chinese everlasting orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clustered, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing showy, long-lasting flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cymbidium pseudovirens is often cultivated for its beauty and is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
