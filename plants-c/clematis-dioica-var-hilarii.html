Clematis dioica var. hilarii

Clematis dioica var. hilarii, commonly known as the Himalayan clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the Himalayas.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to climb and cover trellises or fences effectively.
