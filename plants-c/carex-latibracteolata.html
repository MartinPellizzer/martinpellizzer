Carex latibracteolata

Carex latibracteolata, commonly known as broad-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Carex latibracteolata is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
