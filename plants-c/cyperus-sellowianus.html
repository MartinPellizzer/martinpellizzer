Cyperus sellowianus

Cyperus sellowianus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, arranged in dense tufts. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

Cyperus sellowianus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, feathery appearance.
