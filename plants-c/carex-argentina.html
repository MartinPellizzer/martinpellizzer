Carex argentina

Carex argentina, commonly known as the Argentine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex argentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
