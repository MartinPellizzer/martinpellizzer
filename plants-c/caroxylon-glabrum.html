Caroxylon glabrum

Caroxylon glabrum, commonly known as the smooth carob tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pods. The plant is notable for its smooth, gray bark and its ability to thrive in arid conditions. Its fruit, a long pod containing sweet, sticky seeds, has been historically used for food and as a natural adhesive.

Caroxylon glabrum is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
