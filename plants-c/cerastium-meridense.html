Cerastium meridense

Cerastium meridense, commonly known as the southern mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Cerastium meridense is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.
