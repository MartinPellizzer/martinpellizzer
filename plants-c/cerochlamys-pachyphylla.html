Cerochlamys pachyphylla

Cerochlamys pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved cerochlamys, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, an adaptation to its harsh, dry environment. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding a subtle beauty to its rugged habitat. Its growth habit is low and spreading, with multiple stems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often found in rocky outcrops and is a rare example of succulent flora in the region.
