Chrysophyllum venezuelanense

Chrysophyllum venezuelanense, commonly known as the Venezuelan cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is similar to the cherimoya, with a creamy, sweet pulp that is often used in desserts and beverages.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
