Camellia integerrima

Camellia integerrima, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a valuable ornamental plant in gardens.

Camellia integerrima prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in temperate climates.
