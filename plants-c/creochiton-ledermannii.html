Creochiton ledermannii

Creochiton ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's flatbacked millipede, is a species of millipede found in specific regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polydesmidae and is characterized by its elongated, segmented body and numerous pairs of legs. The exoskeleton of this millipede is typically dark in color, providing camouflage in its natural habitat. It plays a role in soil aeration and nutrient cycling through its feeding habits.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to entomologists and conservationists.
