Calliandra squarrosa

Calliandra squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright red or orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Calliandra squarrosa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
