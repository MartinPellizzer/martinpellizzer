Clerodendrum rupicola

Clerodendrum rupicola, commonly known as the rock clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually opposite and oval-shaped, with a rough texture.

Clerodendrum rupicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in limited soil conditions.
