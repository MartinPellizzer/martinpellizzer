Chamaecrista sparsifolia

Chamaecrista sparsifolia, commonly known as the slender crownflower, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in arid and rocky environments. Its seed pods are typically slender and contain several small, dark seeds.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
