Carex livida var. radicalis

Carex livida var. radicalis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
