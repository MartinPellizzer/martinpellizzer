Calamus faberi var. brevispicatus

Calamus faberi var. brevispicatus, commonly known as the short-spined calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, India, and Myanmar. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.
