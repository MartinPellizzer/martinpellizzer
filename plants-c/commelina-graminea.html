Commelina graminea

Commelina graminea, commonly known as the grassy commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in umbels, often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Commelina graminea is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.
