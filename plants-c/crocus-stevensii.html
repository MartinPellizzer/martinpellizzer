Crocus stevensii

Crocus stevensii, commonly known as the Stevens' crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Crocus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. This plant is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, with six petals and a distinctive, rounded shape.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
