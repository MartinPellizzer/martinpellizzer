Campanula racemosa var. grandiflora

Campanula racemosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Great-flowered Bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This plant is often cultivated for its attractive blooms and is well-suited for wetland gardens.
