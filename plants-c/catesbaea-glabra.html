Catesbaea glabra

Catesbaea glabra, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to orange-colored fruits, which are sour and seedy. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Catesbaea glabra is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
