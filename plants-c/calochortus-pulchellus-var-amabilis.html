Calochortus pulchellus var. amabilis

Calochortus pulchellus var. amabilis, commonly known as the showy globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with a distinctive globular shape. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, erect stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
