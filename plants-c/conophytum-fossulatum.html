Conophytum fossulatum

Conophytum fossulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock fig, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy, and often ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the cooler months. It typically grows on rocky outcrops and is well adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
