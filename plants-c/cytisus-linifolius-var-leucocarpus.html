Cytisus linifolius var. leucocarpus

Cytisus linifolius var. leucocarpus, commonly known as the white-fruited broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its most distinctive characteristic is the white, spherical fruits that develop from the flowers.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.
