Crassocephalum multicorymbosum

Crassocephalum multicorymbosum, commonly known as the African blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in dense, rounded clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens. It is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.

The species is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant color and hardy nature.
