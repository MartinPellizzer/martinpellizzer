Colchicum tessellatum

Colchicum tessellatum, commonly known as the tessellated autumn crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that emerge from a bulb-like structure called a corm. Its flowers are typically borne on tall, slender stems and have a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals.

Colchicum tessellatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
