Cordia hispidissima

Cordia hispidissima, commonly known as the rough cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry savannas and coastal areas.

Cordia hispidissima is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
