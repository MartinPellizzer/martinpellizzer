Cucumis sativus var. fakus

Cucumis sativus var. fakus, commonly known as the Japanese cucumber, is a variant of the cucumber plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated fruit with a smooth, pale green skin. The plant typically grows on a vine, producing flowers that are predominantly yellow in color. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its crisp, refreshing flesh and mild flavor.
