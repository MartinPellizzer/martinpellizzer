Callistachya virginica var. purpurea

Callistachya virginica var. purpurea, commonly known as the purpleflowered beakweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
