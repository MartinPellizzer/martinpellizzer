Crassula radicans

Crassula radicans, commonly known as the wandering jade, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that radiate from a central stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or container planting.

Its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
