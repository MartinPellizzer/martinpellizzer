Callitris articulata

Callitris articulata, commonly known as the mountain cedar, is a species of gymnosperm native to Australia.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The tree is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
