Campanula graminifolia

Campanula graminifolia, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and streambanks, preferring well-drained soil. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula graminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its delicate, cascading flowers.
