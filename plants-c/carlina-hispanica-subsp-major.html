Carlina hispanica subsp. major

Carlina hispanica subsp.

major, commonly known as the greater Spanish hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single, terminal head.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
