Calceolaria trilobata subsp. orientalis

Calceolaria trilobata subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are bilaterally symmetrical with a unique pouch-like structure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate and colorful blooms.
