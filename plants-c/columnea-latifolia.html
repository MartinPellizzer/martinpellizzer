Columnea latifolia

Columnea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf flame flower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Columnea latifolia is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract hummingbirds.
