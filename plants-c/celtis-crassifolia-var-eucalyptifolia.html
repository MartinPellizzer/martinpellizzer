Celtis crassifolia var. eucalyptifolia

Celtis crassifolia var. eucalyptifolia, commonly known as the eucalyptus-leaved boxwood, is a variant of the boxwood genus Celtis.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that resembles the leaves of eucalyptus trees, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and have a distinctive bluish-green color.

It is native to parts of Central America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
