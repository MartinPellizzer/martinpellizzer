Cortusa matthioli subsp. turkestanica

Cortusa matthioli subsp.

turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple bell-shaped flowers.

The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
