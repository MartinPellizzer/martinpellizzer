Cyperus unioloides var. fissivaginatus

Cyperus unioloides var. fissivaginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
