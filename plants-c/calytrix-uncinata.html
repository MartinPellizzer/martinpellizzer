Calytrix uncinata

Calytrix uncinata, commonly known as the hooked calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds with hooked appendages, aiding in seed dispersal.

Calytrix uncinata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
