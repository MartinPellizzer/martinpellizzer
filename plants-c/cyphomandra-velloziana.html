Cyphomandra velloziana

Cyphomandra velloziana, commonly known as the yellow passionfruit, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It produces small, yellow, oval-shaped fruits with a hairy exterior. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a strong aromatic scent.

The fruit is edible, though its seeds are toxic and should be avoided.
