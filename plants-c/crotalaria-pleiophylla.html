Crotalaria pleiophylla

Crotalaria pleiophylla, commonly known as the many-leafed cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmately lobed leaves. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Crotalaria pleiophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some cultures.
