Centaurea strobilacea

Centaurea strobilacea, commonly known as the clustered knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces vibrant blue to purple flowers, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to arid environments.
