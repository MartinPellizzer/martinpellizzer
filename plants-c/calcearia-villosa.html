Calcearia villosa

Calcearia villosa, commonly known as the hairy calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in the spring and early summer.
