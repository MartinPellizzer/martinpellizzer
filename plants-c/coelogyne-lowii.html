Coelogyne lowii

Coelogyne lowii, commonly known as the Orchid of the Gods, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect pseudobulbs and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically blooms once a year, producing multiple blooms on each inflorescence. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Coelogyne lowii is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated for its long-lasting, elegant flowers.
