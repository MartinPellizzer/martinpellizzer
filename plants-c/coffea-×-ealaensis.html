Coffea × ealaensis

Coffea × ealaensis, commonly known as the Ealaensian coffee.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Coffea arabica and Coffea canephora, combining traits from both parent species. This plant is native to Central and South America, particularly in regions with high altitudes and tropical climates. It produces small, red berries containing seeds that are used to make coffee.

The botanical characteristics of Coffea × ealaensis include its evergreen shrub form, lance-shaped leaves, and its ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
