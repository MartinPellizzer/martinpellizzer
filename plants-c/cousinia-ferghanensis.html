Cousinia ferghanensis

Cousinia ferghanensis, commonly known as the Ferghana cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The small yellow flowers appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in dry landscapes.
