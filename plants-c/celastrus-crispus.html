Celastrus crispus

Celastrus crispus, commonly known as the smooth twining vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with smooth margins and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant bears orange to red berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its stems are flexible and often twine around supports, making it a common sight in woodland areas.

Celastrus crispus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
