Carpinus polyneura var. polyneura

Carpinus polyneura var. polyneura, commonly known as the Japanese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and smooth, dark gray bark. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round nuts enclosed in a spiny cupule.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its year-round structure and adaptability to various soil conditions.
