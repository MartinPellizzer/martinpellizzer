Calanthe tankervilleae

Calanthe tankervilleae, commonly known as the Tankerville orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of eastern India and Nepal.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces inflorescences with multiple flowers, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
