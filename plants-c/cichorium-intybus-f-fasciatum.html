Cichorium intybus f. fasciatum

Cichorium intybus f. fasciatum, commonly known as the variegated chicory, is a cultivar of the chicory plant characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves.

The leaves exhibit a striking pattern of white, yellow, and green, creating a visually appealing contrast. This form is typically a compact, low-growing plant with a rosette structure. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic value in gardens.

The plant prefers cool climates and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cooler regions.
