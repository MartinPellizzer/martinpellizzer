Cassia haitiensis

Cassia haitiensis, commonly known as Haitian cassia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
