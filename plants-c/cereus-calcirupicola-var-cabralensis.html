Cereus calcirupicola var. cabralensis

Cereus calcirupicola var. cabralensis, commonly known as the Cabral's rock cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking, large white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique morphology and nocturnal blooming make it a notable species in the cactus family.
