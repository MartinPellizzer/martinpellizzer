Cassia planaltoana

Cassia planaltoana, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often light brown in color. Cassia planaltoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and along roadsides for its vibrant floral display.

It also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
