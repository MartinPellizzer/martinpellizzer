Chrysopia fasciculata

Chrysopia fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and grassland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are woody and often branched.

Chrysopia fasciculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
