Campanula trachelium var. ramosissima

Campanula trachelium var. ramosissima, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming blooms.
