Centaurea cheiranthifolia

Centaurea cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiny seeds in its thistle-like flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Centaurea cheiranthifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
