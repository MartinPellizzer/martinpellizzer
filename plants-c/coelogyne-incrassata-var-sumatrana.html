Coelogyne incrassata var. sumatrana

Coelogyne incrassata var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in forest understories.
