Convolvulus cantaber

Convolvulus cantaber, commonly known as the Canary Island bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
