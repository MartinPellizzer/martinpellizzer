Crotalaria obscura var. collina

Crotalaria obscura var. collina, commonly known as the narrowleaf crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, with distinctive leaflets that are narrow and lance-shaped. Its botanical characteristics include a branching stem, alternate leaves, and a capsule-like fruit that contains several seeds.

It is valued in some regions for its ornamental appeal and for its role in supporting local pollinators.
