Centaurea margaritae

Centaurea margaritae, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and silver-green leaves, which are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea margaritae is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
