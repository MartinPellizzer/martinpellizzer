Convolvulus Subacaulis

Convolvulus subacaulis is a unique flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is known for its distinctive, spiraling stems that twist upward in a helical pattern. The plant produces small, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. Despite its delicate appearance, it is highly drought-resistant and thrives in poor soils. This species is often overlooked but plays an important role in its ecosystem.
