Campanula sibirica subsp. ciscaucasica

Campanula sibirica subsp.

ciscaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
