Croton glabellus subsp. polytrichus

Croton glabellus subsp.

polytrichus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
