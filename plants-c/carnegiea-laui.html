Carnegiea laui

Carnegiea laui, commonly known as the Laui cholla, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in clusters of small, spine-tipped pads. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is shallow and adapted to absorb water quickly during rare rainfall events.

Carnegiea laui plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
