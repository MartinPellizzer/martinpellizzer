Cascarilla caduciflora

Cascarilla caduciflora, commonly known as the Mexican cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented bark. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, peeling bark that exudes a strong, spicy fragrance. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles, followed by orange-yellow fruits that contain a single seed.
