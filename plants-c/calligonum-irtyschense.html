Calligonum irtyschense

Calligonum irtyschense, commonly known as the Tien Shan spurge, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and salt stress.

Its ecological significance includes stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
