Campanula alpina var. haynaldii

Campanula alpina var. haynaldii, commonly known as the Haynald's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy coating, aiding in moisture retention.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic alpine conditions.
