Carpinus falcatibracteata

Carpinus falcatibracteata, commonly known as the broad-leaved hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by winged fruits that disperse via wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
