Crinum capense var. viridifolium

Crinum capense var. viridifolium, commonly known as the green-leaved African lily, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, giving it its common name. The plant produces tall, upright stems bearing clusters of white or pale pink, trumpet-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which can last several months.
