Capeobolus

Capeobolus, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically white or pale green.

Capeobolus thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and nutritional value.
