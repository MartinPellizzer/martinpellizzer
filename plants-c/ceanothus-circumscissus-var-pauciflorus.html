Ceanothus circumscissus var. pauciflorus

Ceanothus circumscissus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the coastal ceanothus, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing a distinctive appearance.

This variety is noted for its ability to withstand salt spray and coastal winds, making it well adapted to its specific environment.
