Calais parryi

Calais parryi, commonly known as the Parry's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance. Its foliage consists of fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, while its flowers bloom during the spring and early summer.

Calais parryi plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
