Cissus quadrangularis var. pubescens

Cissus quadrangularis var. pubescens, commonly known as the sandalwood vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems, which are often covered with fine, silky pubescence. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a waxy texture with a slightly serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
