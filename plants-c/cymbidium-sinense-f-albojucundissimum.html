Cymbidium sinense f. albojucundissimum

Cymbidium sinense f. albojucundissimum, commonly known as the white-flowered Cymbidium, is a hybrid orchid species native to China.

It is characterized by its elongated, gracefully arching inflorescences that bear clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are broad, strap-shaped, and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

This cultivar is prized for its elegant appearance and is commonly cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant.
