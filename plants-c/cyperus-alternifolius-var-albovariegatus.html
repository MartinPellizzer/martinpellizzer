Cyperus alternifolius var. albovariegatus

Cyperus alternifolius var. albovariegatus, commonly known as the variegated flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that exhibit striking variegation, often featuring white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous brown flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes used in aquatic gardens or as a decorative element in water features.
