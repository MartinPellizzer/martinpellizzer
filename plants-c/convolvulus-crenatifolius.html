Convolvulus crenatifolius

Convolvulus crenatifolius, commonly known as the serratedleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped, serrated leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its rapid growth and attractive blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it can be invasive in some regions.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread across various environments.
