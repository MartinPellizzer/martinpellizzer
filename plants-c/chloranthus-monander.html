Chloranthus monander

Chloranthus monander, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Korean Peninsula. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem.

The small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom in the spring.
