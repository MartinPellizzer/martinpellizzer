Carex tristachya subsp. pocilliformis

Carex tristachya subsp.

pocilliformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
