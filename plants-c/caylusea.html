Caylusea

Caylusea, commonly known as the desert rose, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, adapting to poor soil conditions.

Caylusea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
