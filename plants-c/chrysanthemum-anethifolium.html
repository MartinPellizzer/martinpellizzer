Chrysanthemum anethifolium

Chrysanthemum anethifolium, commonly known as the sweetroot chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of anise, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has a mild, aromatic scent.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
