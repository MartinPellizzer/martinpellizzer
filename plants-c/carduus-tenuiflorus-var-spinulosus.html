Carduus tenuiflorus var. spinulosus

Carduus tenuiflorus var. spinulosus, commonly known as the spiny sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a rosette growth form with basal leaves that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs.

The plant is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
