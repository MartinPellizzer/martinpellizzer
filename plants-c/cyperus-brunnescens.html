Cyperus brunnescens

Cyperus brunnescens, commonly known as the brown rush, is a perennial wetland plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes are usually pale brown or reddish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus brunnescens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize wetland soils.
