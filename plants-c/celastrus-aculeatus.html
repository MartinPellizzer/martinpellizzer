Celastrus aculeatus

Celastrus aculeatus, commonly known as the prickly staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. Its roots and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid environments and is often found in forests and along riverbanks.
