Chloris dandyana var. breviaristata

Chloris dandyana var. breviaristata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Its botanical structure makes it suitable for use in ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
