Convolvulus ulosepalus

Convolvulus ulosepalus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its hairy, creeping stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
