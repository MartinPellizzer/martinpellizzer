Catasetum integerrimum var. viridiflorum

Catasetum integerrimum var. viridiflorum, commonly known as the green-flowered catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow flowers with prominent, elongated sepals and petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several showy, fragrant flowers.

The species is noted for its unique pollination mechanism, involving specialized structures that facilitate pollination by specific insects.
