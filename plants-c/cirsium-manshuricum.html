Cirsium manshuricum

Cirsium manshuricum, commonly known as the Manchurian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, rough leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Cirsium manshuricum is known for its robust structure and is often found in temperate regions of China and Korea.
