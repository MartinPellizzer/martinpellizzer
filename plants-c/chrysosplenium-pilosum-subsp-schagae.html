Chrysosplenium pilosum subsp. schagae

Chrysosplenium pilosum subsp.

schagae, commonly known as the hairy leaf chrysosplenium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It typically features small, ovate leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
