Crataegus angustisepala

Crataegus angustisepala, commonly known as the narrow-lobed hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.
