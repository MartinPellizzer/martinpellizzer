Campylotropis wangii

Campylotropis wangii, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Campylotropis wangii is valued for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
