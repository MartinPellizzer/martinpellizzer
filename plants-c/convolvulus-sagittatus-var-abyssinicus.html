Convolvulus sagittatus var. abyssinicus

Convolvulus sagittatus var. abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian arrowhead vine, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spiral-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative climbing plant.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
