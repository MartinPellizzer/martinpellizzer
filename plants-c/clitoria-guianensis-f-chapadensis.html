Clitoria guianensis f. chapadensis

Clitoria guianensis f. chapadensis, commonly known as the Chapada butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble butterflies. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the flowers are clustered in racemes, often appearing in bright colors that attract pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its potential medicinal properties.
