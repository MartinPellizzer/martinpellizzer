Crinum solapurense

Crinum solapurense, commonly known as the Solapur yellow crinum, is a bulbous plant native to the western Ghats of India.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in summer. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that grow from a bulbous base, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodland and along stream banks, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
