Cerastium viscosioides

Cerastium viscosioides, commonly known as sticky mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that are often sticky to the touch, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cerastium viscosioides is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
