Carex tenuiculmis

Carex tenuiculmis, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, triangular seeds.

Carex tenuiculmis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
