Colubrina glandulosa var. nipensis

Colubrina glandulosa var. nipensis, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a glandular texture, contributing to its botanical uniqueness.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
