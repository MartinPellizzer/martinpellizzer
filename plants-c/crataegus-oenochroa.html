Crataegus oenochroa

Crataegus oenochroa, commonly known as the red sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.

Crataegus oenochroa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
