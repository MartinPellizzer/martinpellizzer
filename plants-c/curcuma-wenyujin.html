Curcuma wenyujin

Curcuma wenyujin, commonly known as Yujin, is a rhizomatous plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, deep orange-yellow rhizomes. The plant produces erect, leafy stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported blood-activating and anti-inflammatory properties.

The botanical features of Curcuma wenyujin make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
