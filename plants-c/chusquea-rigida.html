Chusquea rigida

Chusquea rigida, commonly known as the rigid bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its stiff, upright culms that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in temperate to cool climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its structural strength and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
