Convolvulus monospermus

Convolvulus monospermus, commonly known as the single-seeded knotweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dry capsule that contains a single, elongated seed.

This species is native to southern Africa and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
