Croton trilobus

Croton trilobus, commonly known as the three-lobed croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
