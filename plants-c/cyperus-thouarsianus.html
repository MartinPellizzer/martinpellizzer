Cyperus thouarsianus

Cyperus thouarsianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Cyperus thouarsianus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
