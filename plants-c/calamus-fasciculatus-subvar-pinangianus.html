Calamus fasciculatus subvar. pinangianus

Calamus fasciculatus subvar. pinangianus, commonly known as the Pinangian calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, which is enclosed by a spathe. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions such as Malaysia and Indonesia.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
