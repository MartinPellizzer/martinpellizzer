Cibotarium fruticulosum

Cibotarium fruticulosum, commonly known as the hairy cibotium, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive without direct sunlight. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing cones, are positioned along the fronds, contributing to its botanical complexity.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Cibotarium fruticulosum is considered a conservation concern.
