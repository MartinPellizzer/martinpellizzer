Cryptocoryne crispatula

Cryptocoryne crispatula, commonly known as the curly leaf cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often curled leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and rivers. Its leaves are usually bright green and can vary in shape depending on light and water conditions.

Cryptocoryne crispatula is popular in aquariums due to its ornamental appearance and ease of care.
