Campanopsis flaccida

Campanopsis flaccida, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, often climbing or trailing along forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Campanopsis flaccida thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
