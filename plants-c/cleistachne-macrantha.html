Cleistachne macrantha

Cleistachne macrantha, commonly known as the large-panicle grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, open panicles that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and parallel veins.

Cleistachne macrantha plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
