Crepis caucasica

Crepis caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and slender, hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single head, which are typically surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in mountainous areas.

Crepis caucasica is a perennial plant that is adapted to cool, temperate climates.
