Carduus × estivali subsp. carbonelii

Carduus × estivali subsp.

carbonelii, commonly known as the summer thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in regions of southern Europe and the Mediterranean. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Due to its ornamental value and potential invasiveness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
