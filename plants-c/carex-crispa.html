Carex crispa

Carex crispa, commonly known as the curly sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex crispa produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are pollinated by wind.
