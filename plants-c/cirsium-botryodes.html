Cirsium botryodes

Cirsium botryodes, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, woolly, blue to purple flower heads. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough, prickly texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into spherical, bristly seed heads.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.
