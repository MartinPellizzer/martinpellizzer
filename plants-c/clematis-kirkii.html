Clematis kirkii

Clematis kirkii, commonly known as the African clematis, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or violet, with five petals. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Clematis kirkii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
