Capparis spinosa subsp. rupestris

Capparis spinosa subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant produces edible, acidic berries that are used in culinary applications. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and coastal environments.
