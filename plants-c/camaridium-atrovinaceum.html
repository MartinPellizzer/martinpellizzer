Camaridium atrovinaceum

Camaridium atrovinaceum, commonly known as the black veined fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, dark green fronds that have a distinctive blackish venation, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sori on the underside of the fronds, which are covered by a protective indusium.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Camaridium atrovinaceum is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
