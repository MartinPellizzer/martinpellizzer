Clerodendrum grandicalyx

Clerodendrum grandicalyx, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often brightly colored in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its decorative foliage and floral display.

The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the variety.
