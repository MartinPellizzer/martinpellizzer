Carex hirta f. major

Carex hirta f. major, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and wet meadows. Its leaves are usually hairy, especially along the margins, giving it a distinctive texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in spikelets that develop into three-sided, capsule-like fruits.
