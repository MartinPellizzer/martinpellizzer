Carpesium cernuum var. griffithii

Carpesium cernuum var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's sickleweed, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China and Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and blooms.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
