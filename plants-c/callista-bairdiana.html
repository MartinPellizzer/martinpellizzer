Callista bairdiana

Callista bairdiana, commonly known as the Banksia bairdiana, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It is typically found in heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

Callista bairdiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
