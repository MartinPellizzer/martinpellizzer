Cochemiea pondii subsp. maritima

Cochemiea pondii subsp.

maritima, commonly known as the coastal cochemiea, is a succulent cactus native to the coastal regions of Baja California, Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps protect it from the harsh coastal environment.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid and coastal climates.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
