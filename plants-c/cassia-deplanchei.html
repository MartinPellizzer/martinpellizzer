Cassia deplanchei

Cassia deplanchei, commonly known as the African cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 10 to 20 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Cassia deplanchei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
