Chloranthus officinalis

Chloranthus officinalis, commonly known as the fragrant chloranthus, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes and roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
