Calamintha bianoris

Calamintha bianoris, commonly known as white honeysuckle, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a strong mint-like scent.

Calamintha bianoris is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.
