Campylanthus glaber var. pumilus

Campylanthus glaber var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf silky bush, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
