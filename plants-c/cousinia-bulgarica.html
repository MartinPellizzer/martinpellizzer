Cousinia bulgarica

Cousinia bulgarica, commonly known as the Balkan cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Cousinia bulgarica is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
