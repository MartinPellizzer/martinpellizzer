Cirsium echinocephalum

Cirsium echinocephalum, commonly known as the hedgehoghead thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Cirsium echinocephalum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
