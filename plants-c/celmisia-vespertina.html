Celmisia vespertina

Celmisia vespertina, commonly known as the evening celmisia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Celmisia vespertina is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
