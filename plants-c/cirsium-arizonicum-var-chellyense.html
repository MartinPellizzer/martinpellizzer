Cirsium arizonicum var. chellyense

Cirsium arizonicum var. chellyense, commonly known as the Chelly thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial species characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of ray florets surrounded by a ring of disk florets.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
