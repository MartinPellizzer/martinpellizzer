Calea glabrata

Calea glabrata, commonly known as the "mirror plant," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, white, star-shaped flowers that resemble small mirrors. The plant is known for its unique leaf structure, which has a silvery, reflective surface that helps it reflect sunlight. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Calea glabrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
