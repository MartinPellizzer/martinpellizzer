Chamaecrista sempreviva

Chamaecrista sempreviva, commonly known as the evergreen coralbean, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into five to seven leaflets, and its distinctive bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting foliage and showy floral display.
