Calanthe argenteostriata

Calanthe argenteostriata, commonly known as the silver-stripped calanthe, is a striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white waxy coating. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
