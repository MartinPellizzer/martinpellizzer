Carex stictocarpa

Carex stictocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex stictocarpa is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
