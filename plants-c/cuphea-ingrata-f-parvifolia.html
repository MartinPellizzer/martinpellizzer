Cuphea ingrata f. parvifolia

Cuphea ingrata f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered cuphea, is a compact, flowering plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, attracting hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
