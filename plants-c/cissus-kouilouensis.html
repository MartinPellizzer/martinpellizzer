Cissus kouilouensis

Cissus kouilouensis, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by berries that turn from yellow to red as they mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
