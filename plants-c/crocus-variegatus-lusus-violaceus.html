Crocus variegatus lusus violaceus

Crocus variegatus lusus violaceus, commonly known as the violet-flowered crocus, is a hybrid species within the Crocus genus.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers that typically measure around 2 to 3 centimeters in diameter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its early blooming habit.
