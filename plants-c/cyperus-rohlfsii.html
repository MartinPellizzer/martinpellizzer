Cyperus rohlfsii

Cyperus rohlfsii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and triangular stems. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
