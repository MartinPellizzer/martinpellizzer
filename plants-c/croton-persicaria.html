Croton persicaria

Croton persicaria, commonly known as the redroot croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often bright green with reddish veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Croton persicaria is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage and is a popular choice in tropical gardens.
