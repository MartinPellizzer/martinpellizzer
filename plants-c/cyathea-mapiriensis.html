Cyathea mapiriensis

Cyathea mapiriensis, commonly known as the Mapiri huperbole, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, with a thick, fibrous root system that helps anchor it in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are dispersed by wind, contributing to its propagation in humid, low-light habitats.

The plant's structure and physiology reflect adaptations to its ecological niche, making it an important component of tropical forest ecosystems.
