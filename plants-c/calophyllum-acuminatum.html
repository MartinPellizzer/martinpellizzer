Calophyllum acuminatum

Calophyllum acuminatum, commonly known as the white cedar or blackwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody, elongated fruits containing a single seed. Its roots are valued for their medicinal properties, and the wood is used in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
