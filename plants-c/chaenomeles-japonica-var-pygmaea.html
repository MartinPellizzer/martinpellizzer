Chaenomeles japonica var. pygmaea

Chaenomeles japonica var. pygmaea, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf rose.

It is a compact shrub native to Japan, characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces red or orange hips in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This variety is well-suited for container growing and can thrive in a range of soil types with adequate drainage.
