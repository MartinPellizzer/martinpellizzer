Cleisostoma tricallosum

Cleisostoma tricallosum, commonly known as the Three-lobed Moonvine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, and its vines can grow quite long, often requiring support as it climbs.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
