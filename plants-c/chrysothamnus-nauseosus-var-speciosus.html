Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. speciosus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. speciosus, commonly known as the showy goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-green foliage and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and mountainous regions. It has a low, spreading growth habit and is adapted to arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
