Calliandra simulans

Calliandra simulans, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom throughout the dry season. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping in warm climates.

Calliandra simulans is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.
