Coelachne japonica

Coelachne japonica, commonly known as Japanese coelachne, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as riverbanks and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish-green hue.

Coelachne japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
