Cynorkis violacea

Cynorkis violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cynorkis violacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
