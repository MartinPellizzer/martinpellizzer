Chamaespartium sagittale

Chamaespartium sagittale, commonly known as the arrowhead lily, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its botanical name reflects its arrow-shaped foliage, which is a distinctive characteristic.

Chamaespartium sagittale is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
