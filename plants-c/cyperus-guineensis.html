Cyperus guineensis

Cyperus guineensis, commonly known as the African sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

Cyperus guineensis is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
