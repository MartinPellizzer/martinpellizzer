Cochlearia warburgii

Cochlearia warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or coastal areas. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the flowers bloom in early summer.

Cochlearia warburgii is adapted to cold climates and has a low growth form to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
