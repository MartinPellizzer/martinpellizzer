Croton celtidifolius

Croton celtidifolius, commonly known as the African croton, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It features striking, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often displaying vibrant green or reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
