Corylus avellana var. ardua

Corylus avellana var. ardua, commonly known as the Armenian hazelnut, is a variant of the common hazelnut native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and larger, more robust nuts compared to the typical hazelnut. The plant produces deciduous leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape, with a serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and potential for cultivation in temperate climates.
