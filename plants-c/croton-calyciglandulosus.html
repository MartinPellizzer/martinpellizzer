Croton calyciglandulosus

Croton calyciglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular calyx croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that often display a vibrant mix of green, yellow, and red hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
