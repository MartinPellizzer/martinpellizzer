Callitris cupressiformis var. pendula

Callitris cupressiformis var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping white cypress, is a unique variant of the white cypress tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are dense and dark green, providing a lush canopy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
