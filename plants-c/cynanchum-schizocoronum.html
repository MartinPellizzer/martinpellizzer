Cynanchum schizocoronum

Cynanchum schizocoronum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among members of its family. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, and it often grows in dry, rocky habitats.

Cynanchum schizocoronum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
