Calliandra reticulata

Calliandra reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, showy flowers. The plant has compound leaves with a reticulate venation pattern, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Calliandra reticulata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
