Copernicia macroglossa

Copernicia macroglossa, commonly known as the large-tongue copernicia, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Copernicia macroglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
