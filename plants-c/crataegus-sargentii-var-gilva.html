Crataegus sargentii var. gilva

Crataegus sargentii var. gilva, commonly known as the Sargent's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet, featuring dense, thorny branches and oval to elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, juicy berries in the fall.

Its botanical characteristics include compound leaves with 5 to 7 leaflets and a robust root system that helps it thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
