Cichorium alatum

Cichorium alatum, commonly known as the mouse ear or mouse ear cichory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant due to its unique foliage.
