Coreopsis lanceolata var. villosa

Coreopsis lanceolata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with ray florets that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies further enhance its ecological value.
