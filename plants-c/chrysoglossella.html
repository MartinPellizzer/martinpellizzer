Chrysoglossella

Chrysoglossella, commonly known as the golden mouth orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their striking, often brightly colored flowers with intricate patterns that resemble mouths or faces, giving them their distinctive common name. Native to various regions in Asia, including China and Japan, Chrysoglossella species typically thrive in moist, shaded environments. They possess specialized roots that help in nutrient absorption, and their flowers are pollinated primarily by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, many species within this genus are now considered endangered.
