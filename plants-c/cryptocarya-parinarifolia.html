Cryptocarya parinarifolia

Cryptocarya parinarifolia, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that resemble those of a tree fern. The plant is native to New Zealand and thrives in humid, temperate climates. Its leaves are typically evergreen and can grow up to several meters in length.

Cryptocarya parinarifolia is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance.
