Calamagrostis amoena

Calamagrostis amoena, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple-tinted foliage, which adds ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that bloom in late summer, producing delicate, feathery flowers.

Calamagrostis amoena is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
