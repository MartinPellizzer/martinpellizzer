Cirsium griseum

Cirsium griseum, commonly known as the gray thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, gray-green, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers in late summer, which are composed of both ray and disk florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Cirsium griseum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
