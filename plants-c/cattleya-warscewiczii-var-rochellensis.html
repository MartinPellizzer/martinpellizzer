Cattleya warscewiczii var. rochellensis

Cattleya warscewiczii var. rochellensis, commonly known as the Rochell's Cattleya, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

This variant is characterized by its striking, large flowers that typically feature a vivid pink or purple hue with intricate markings. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. The plant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty in botanical gardens and private collections.

Due to its limited natural habitat and conservation status, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
