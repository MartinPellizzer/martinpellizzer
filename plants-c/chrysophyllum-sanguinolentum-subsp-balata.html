Chrysophyllum sanguinolentum subsp. balata

Chrysophyllum sanguinolentum subsp.

balata, commonly known as the red sapwood cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Central and South America. It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a yellowish-green skin and a sweet, sticky pulp.

Its sap is notably red when cut, giving it its common name, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential economic uses.
