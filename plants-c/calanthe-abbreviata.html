Calanthe abbreviata

Calanthe abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf calanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically arranged in a raceme during the spring season. Calanthe abbreviata thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded ornamental plantings.
