Cerasus prostrata var. discolor

Cerasus prostrata var. discolor, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter, often reddish or purple underside. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to provide early seasonal interest in the garden.
