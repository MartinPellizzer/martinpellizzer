Ceropegia pusilla

Ceropegia pusilla, commonly known as the baby chain plant or string of pearls, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, trailing plant with fleshy, cylindrical stems that resemble small pearls. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia pusilla is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appearance and ease of care.
