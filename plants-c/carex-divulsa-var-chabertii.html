Carex divulsa var. chabertii

Carex divulsa var. chabertii, commonly known as the Chabert's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color.

Carex divulsa var. chabertii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland conditions.
