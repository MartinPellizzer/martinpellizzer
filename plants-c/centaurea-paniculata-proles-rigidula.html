Centaurea paniculata proles rigidula

Centaurea paniculata proles rigidula, commonly known as rigid stalked knapweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring erect, rigid stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often hairy, while the flowers are yellow with purple or reddish bracts.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
