Carex calderae

Carex calderae, commonly known as the Caldera sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, specifically the Andes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and ribbed, with a dense cluster of spikelets at the top. Its flowering structure consists of small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Carex calderae is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
