Curcuma sumatrana

Curcuma sumatrana, commonly known as Sumatran turmeric, is a rhizomatous herb native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange rhizomes. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes contain curcuminoids, which are responsible for its medicinal and culinary properties.

The botanical features of Curcuma sumatrana make it a valuable species in traditional medicine and natural dye production.
