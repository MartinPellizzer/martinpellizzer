Ceropteris tartarea var. ochracea

Ceropteris tartarea var. ochracea, commonly known as the ochraceous water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Ceropteris.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are typically light green to yellowish in color. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with small, overlapping pinnae that contribute to its intricate appearance.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.
