Cissus surinamensis

Cissus surinamensis, commonly known as the rock fig or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its ability to climb and its attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor displays.
