Cheiloclinium anomalum

Cheiloclinium anomalum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thornapple, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
