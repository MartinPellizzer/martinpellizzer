Centaurium candelabrum f. albiflorum

Centaurium candelabrum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered candlestick centaury, is a variant of the centaury plant characterized by its striking white flowers.

This perennial herb is native to southern Europe and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
