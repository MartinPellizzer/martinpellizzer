Carduus × crispoformis

Carduus × crispoformis, commonly known as the curled thistle.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy, purple flower heads. It typically grows in disturbed soils and arid environments, exhibiting a rosette growth habit. The plant's foliage is covered in dense, white hairs, which help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight.

Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, and it is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance.
