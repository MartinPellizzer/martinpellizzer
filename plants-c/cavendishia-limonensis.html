Cavendishia limonensis

Cavendishia limonensis, commonly known as the lemon tree of the Andes, is a species native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that resemble lemons in appearance but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in some tropical and subtropical regions.
