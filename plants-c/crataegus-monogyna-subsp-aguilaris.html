Crataegus monogyna subsp. aguilaris

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

aguilaris, commonly known as the Spanish hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in spring.

The plant produces red or orange berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and landscape gardening.
