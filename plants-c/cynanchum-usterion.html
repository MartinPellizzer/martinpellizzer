Cynanchum usterion

Cynanchum usterion, commonly known as the European dog's mercury, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cynanchum usterion is known for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution in use.
