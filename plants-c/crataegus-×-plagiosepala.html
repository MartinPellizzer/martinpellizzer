Crataegus × plagiosepala

Crataegus × plagiosepala, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types as long as it has good drainage.
