Conoclinium adenolepis

Conoclinium adenolepis, commonly known as the Mexican mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant features showy, tubular, violet to blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Conoclinium adenolepis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
