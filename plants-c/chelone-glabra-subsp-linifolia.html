Chelone glabra subsp. linifolia

Chelone glabra subsp.

linifolia, commonly known as white hellebore, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem.

The plant produces striking white to pale pink bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
