Clerodendrum somalense

Clerodendrum somalense, commonly known as the Somali glorybower, is a shrub native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Clerodendrum somalense is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
