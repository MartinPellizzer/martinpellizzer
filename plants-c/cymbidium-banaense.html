Cymbidium banaense

Cymbidium banaense, commonly known as the Banaense orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a distinctive yellow lip. Cymbidium banaense prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in gardens or greenhouses.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional floral arrangements.
