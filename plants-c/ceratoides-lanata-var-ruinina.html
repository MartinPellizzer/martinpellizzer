Ceratoides lanata var. ruinina

Ceratoides lanata var. ruinina, commonly known as the silky mountain-mahoe, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, woolly foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, leathery fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
