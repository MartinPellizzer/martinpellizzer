Centaurea boissieri var. saguntina

Centaurea boissieri var. saguntina, commonly known as the Saguntina cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, linear leaves. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.

Its scientific name reflects its geographical origin and botanical classification.
