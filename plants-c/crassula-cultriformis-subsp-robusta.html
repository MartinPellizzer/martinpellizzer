Crassula cultriformis subsp. robusta

Crassula cultriformis subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the broad-leaved crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the warmer months. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
