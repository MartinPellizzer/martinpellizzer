Combretum borneense

Combretum borneense, commonly known as the Bornean Combretum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and woody structure. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in lowland and hill forest environments. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a rough or hairy texture.

The flowers are usually greenish-white or yellowish, and the fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.
