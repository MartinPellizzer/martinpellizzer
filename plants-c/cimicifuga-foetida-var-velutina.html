Cimicifuga foetida var. velutina

Cimicifuga foetida var. velutina, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its roots and rhizomes are the primary parts used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in woodland habitats and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
