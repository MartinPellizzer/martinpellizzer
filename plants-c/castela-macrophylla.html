Castela macrophylla

Castela macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf castela, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
