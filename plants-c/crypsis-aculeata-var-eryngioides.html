Crypsis aculeata var. eryngioides

Crypsis aculeata var. eryngioides, commonly known as the spiny eryngium, is a flowering plant characterized by its spiky, thistle-like appearance.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features dense, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. Its leaves are typically narrow, grass-like, and covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
