Carex aladagensis

Carex aladagensis, commonly known as the Aladag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex aladagensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
