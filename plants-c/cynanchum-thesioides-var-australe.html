Cynanchum thesioides var. australe

Cynanchum thesioides var. australe, commonly known as Australian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, smooth seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
