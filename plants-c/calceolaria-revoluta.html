Calceolaria revoluta

Calceolaria revoluta, commonly known as the curlleaf calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are often curled or revolute, giving it its common name.

Calceolaria revoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
