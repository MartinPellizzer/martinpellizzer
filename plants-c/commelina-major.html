Commelina major

Commelina major, commonly known as the greater spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelina genus within the family Commelinaceae. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, often accompanied by two white or pale green bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage is characterized by broad, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture.
