Cogswellia nudicaulis

Cogswellia nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in sparse, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a typical adaptation for minimizing water evaporation and deterring herbivores.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Cogswellia nudicaulis is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
