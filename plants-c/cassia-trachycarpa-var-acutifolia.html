Cassia trachycarpa var. acutifolia

Cassia trachycarpa var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cassia, is a shrub native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a woody structure with thorny stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The seeds are encased in a pod and are an important food source for various wildlife species.
