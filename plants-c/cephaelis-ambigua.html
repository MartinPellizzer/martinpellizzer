Cephaelis ambigua

Cephaelis ambigua, commonly known as the pink fairy fan, is a flowering plant native to the western Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of delicate, pink or lilac-colored flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
