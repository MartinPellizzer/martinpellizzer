Campanula scheuchzeri subsp. susplugasii

Campanula scheuchzeri subsp.

susplugasii, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps. It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine meadows and mountain slopes. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and covered with a fine down, adapting to cold and harsh environments.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
