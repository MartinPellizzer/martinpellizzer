Cassia rotundata

Cassia rotundata, commonly known as sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, orange pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some areas.

Cassia rotundata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
