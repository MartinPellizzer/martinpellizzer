Cymbopogon princeps

Cymbopogon princeps, commonly known as the dwarf lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale green flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.

Cymbopogon princeps thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
