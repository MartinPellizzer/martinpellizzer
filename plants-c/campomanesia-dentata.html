Campomanesia dentata

Campomanesia dentata, commonly known as the Brazilian cheese fruit or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries. The fruit is edible, with a tart, slightly acidic flavor, and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
