Carex pallida

Carex pallida, commonly known as pale sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a pale green to silver-blue coloration.

Carex pallida produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are often surrounded by specialized bracts.
