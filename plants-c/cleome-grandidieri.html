Cleome grandidieri

Cleome grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy and branched. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and forest margins.

Cleome grandidieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
