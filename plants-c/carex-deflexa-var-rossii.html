Carex deflexa var. rossii

Carex deflexa var. rossii, commonly known as the Ross's sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and pond edges.
