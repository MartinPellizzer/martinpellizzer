Calandrinia glauca

Calandrinia glauca, commonly known as the blue-flowered portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that produces vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves and stems, which help it retain water in arid environments. It belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Calandrinia glauca thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
