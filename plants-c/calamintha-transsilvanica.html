Calamintha transsilvanica

Calamintha transsilvanica, commonly known as Transylvanian germander, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often found in mountainous regions. Its small, pink or white flowers bloom in summer, and the leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Calamintha transsilvanica is valued for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
