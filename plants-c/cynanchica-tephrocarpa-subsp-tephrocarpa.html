Cynanchica tephrocarpa subsp. tephrocarpa

Cynanchica tephrocarpa subsp.

tephrocarpa, commonly known as the tephrocarpa milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The plant features slender stems and small, compound leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, and it produces small, indehiscent fruits.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
