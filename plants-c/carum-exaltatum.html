Carum exaltatum

Carum exaltatum, commonly known as the tall caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a mild, carroty scent when crushed.

Carum exaltatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
