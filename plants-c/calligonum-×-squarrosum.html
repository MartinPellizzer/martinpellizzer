Calligonum × squarrosum

Calligonum × squarrosum, commonly known as the hybrid sand spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Calligonum × squarrosum is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
