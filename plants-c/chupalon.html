Chupalon

Chupalon, commonly known as the "devil's ivy," is a fast-growing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are not particularly showy but are followed by red, fleshy berries. It is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used as a decorative plant in homes and offices.

However, it is toxic to humans and animals if ingested, making it important to handle with care.
