Carex hondoensis

Carex hondoensis, commonly known as Hondo sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan, specifically the island of Hondo, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex hondoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
