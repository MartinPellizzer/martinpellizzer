Campanula microphylla

Campanula microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
