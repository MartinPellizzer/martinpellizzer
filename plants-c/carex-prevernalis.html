Carex prevernalis

Carex prevernalis, commonly known as early sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils in woodlands and open meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.

Carex prevernalis is an important plant for biodiversity, providing food and shelter for various invertebrates and birds.
