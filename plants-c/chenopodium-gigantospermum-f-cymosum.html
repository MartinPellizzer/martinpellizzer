Chenopodium gigantospermum f. cymosum

Chenopodium gigantospermum f. cymosum, commonly known as the giant seedsame, is a perennial plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers and robust, branching stems. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed cases that contain numerous small, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous and high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
