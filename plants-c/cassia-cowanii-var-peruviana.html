Cassia cowanii var. peruviana

Cassia cowanii var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a hairy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and public landscapes.
