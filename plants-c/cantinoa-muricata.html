Cantinoa muricata

Cantinoa muricata, commonly known as the spiny ant plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant's leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.

Cantinoa muricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
