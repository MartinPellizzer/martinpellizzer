Calia purpusii

Calia purpusii, commonly known as the Purpus's calia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green color.

Calia purpusii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
