Carex arkansana

Carex arkansana, commonly known as Arkansas sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its grass-like leaves are narrow and have a prominent midrib, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer.

Arkansas sedge is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
