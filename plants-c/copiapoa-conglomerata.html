Copiapoa conglomerata

Copiapoa conglomerata, commonly known as the clustered copiapoa, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, globular shape and is typically covered in white to gray spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular specimen among cactus enthusiasts and conservationists.
