Caesalpinia magnifoliolata

Caesalpinia magnifoliolata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flower clusters that resemble flames, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its trunk is often swollen at the base and covered with a rough, bark-like layer.

Caesalpinia magnifoliolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
