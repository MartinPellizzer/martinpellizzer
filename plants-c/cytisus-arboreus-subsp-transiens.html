Cytisus arboreus subsp. transiens

Cytisus arboreus subsp.

transiens, commonly known as the Transilian broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a landscape plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
