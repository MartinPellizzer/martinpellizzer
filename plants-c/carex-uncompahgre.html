Carex uncompahgre

Carex uncompahgre, commonly known as the Uncompahgre sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks. It has triangular stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are usually green or brown.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and the plant is often found in high-elevation habitats.
