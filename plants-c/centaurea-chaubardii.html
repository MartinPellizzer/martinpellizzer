Centaurea chaubardii

Centaurea chaubardii, commonly known as the Chaubard's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces bright blue to violet flowers with prominent central disk florets.

Centaurea chaubardii is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
