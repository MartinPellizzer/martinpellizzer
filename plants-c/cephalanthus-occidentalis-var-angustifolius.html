Cephalanthus occidentalis var. angustifolius

Cephalanthus occidentalis var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf sweet pepperbush, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
