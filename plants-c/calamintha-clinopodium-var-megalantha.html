Calamintha clinopodium var. megalantha

Calamintha clinopodium var. megalantha, commonly known as large-leaved calamint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. Its small, pale blue to violet flowers are arranged in whorled clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its fragrant foliage and is often used in herbal teas and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
