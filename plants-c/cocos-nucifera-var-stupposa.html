Cocos nucifera var. stupposa

Cocos nucifera var. stupposa, commonly known as the Stupposa coconut, is a variant of the coconut palm characterized by its unique botanical features.

It typically grows in tropical coastal regions, thriving in warm, humid climates with well-drained sandy soils. The plant produces large, fibrous coconut fruits that are often used for their milk, oil, and water. Its leaves are long and feather-like, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is also noted for its resilience to saltwater and strong winds, making it suitable for coastal landscaping.
