Cirsium epiroticum

Cirsium epiroticum, commonly known as the Epirotic thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Compositae family.

Cirsium epiroticum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
