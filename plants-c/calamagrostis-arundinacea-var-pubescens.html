Calamagrostis arundinacea var. pubescens

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy reed grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-gray foliage that adds texture to gardens. The plant produces panicles of greenish-bronze flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and naturalized landscapes.
