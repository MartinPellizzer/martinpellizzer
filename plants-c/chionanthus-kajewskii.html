Chionanthus kajewskii

Chionanthus kajewskii, commonly known as the Kajewskii's fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Chionanthus kajewskii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
