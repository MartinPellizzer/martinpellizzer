Crinum concinnum

Crinum concinnum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spider lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, strap-like leaves and produces tall, branched inflorescences with fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its showy, spider-like blooms that appear in late summer to early fall. Crinum concinnum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that is both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
