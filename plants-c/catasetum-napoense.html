Catasetum napoense

Catasetum napoense, commonly known as the Napo orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers feature specialized structures, including a unique lip that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Catasetum napoense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
