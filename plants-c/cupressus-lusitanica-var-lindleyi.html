Cupressus lusitanica var. lindleyi

Cupressus lusitanica var. lindleyi, commonly known as the Portuguese cypress, is a variety of the lacebark cypress native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar growth habit and its distinctive, peeling bark that reveals a pale, fibrous inner layer. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by blue to reddish-purple cones. Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are dense and provide a dark green appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive form and adaptability to various soil types.
