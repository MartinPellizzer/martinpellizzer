Clematis spathulifolia var. teuszii

Clematis spathulifolia var. teuszii, commonly known as the Teuszii Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, supported by hooked tendrils. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
