Campanula armena

Campanula armena, commonly known as Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Campanula armena is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
