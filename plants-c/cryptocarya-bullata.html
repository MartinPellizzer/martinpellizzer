Cryptocarya bullata

Cryptocarya bullata, commonly known as the broad-leaved cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces dark blue, spherical berries that are edible for some bird species.

Cryptocarya bullata is often found in moist, shaded areas of rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
