Cardamine macrophylla f. hirsuta

Cardamine macrophylla f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy broad-leaved watercress, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a velvety texture. Its small white flowers are arranged in umbels and bloom in early spring.

The plant is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage.
