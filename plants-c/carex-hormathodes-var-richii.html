Carex hormathodes var. richii

Carex hormathodes var. richii, commonly known as the rich's sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to low-nutrient soils.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
