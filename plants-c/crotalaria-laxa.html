Crotalaria laxa

Crotalaria laxa, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated, cylindrical seed pods.

Crotalaria laxa is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
