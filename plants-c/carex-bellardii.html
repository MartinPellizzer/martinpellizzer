Carex bellardii

Carex bellardii, commonly known as the Bellard's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to the eastern United States, it thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream edges. The plant produces spikelets that are typically greenish or reddish in color, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Carex bellardii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
