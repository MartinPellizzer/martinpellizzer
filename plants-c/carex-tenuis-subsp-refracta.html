Carex tenuis subsp. refracta

Carex tenuis subsp.

refracta, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
