Cymbopogon suaveolens

Cymbopogon suaveolens, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, aromatic leaves that emit a strong lemon scent. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences and is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are extracted from its stalks. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is widely used in culinary, medicinal, and aromatic applications.
