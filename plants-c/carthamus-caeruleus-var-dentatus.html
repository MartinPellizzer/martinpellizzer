Carthamus caeruleus var. dentatus

Carthamus caeruleus var. dentatus, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a soft, hairy layer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and unique foliage structure.
