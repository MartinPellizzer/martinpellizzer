Citrus okinawensis

Citrus okinawensis, commonly known as the Okinawa citrus, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and bears fragrant white flowers during the spring. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and has a tart, acidic flavor.

Citrus okinawensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.
