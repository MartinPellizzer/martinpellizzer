Chamaecyparis obtusa f. barronii

Chamaecyparis obtusa f. barronii, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or Japanese white cedar, is a unique form of the common Japanese cedar.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and its distinct, glaucous-blue foliage that gives it a striking appearance. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green cones and has a slow growth rate, making it suitable for smaller spaces.

This variety is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
