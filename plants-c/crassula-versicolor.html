Crassula versicolor

Crassula versicolor, commonly known as the variable crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight.

Due to its adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
