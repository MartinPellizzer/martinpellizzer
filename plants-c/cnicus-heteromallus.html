Cnicus heteromallus

Cnicus heteromallus, commonly known as the prickly goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its fruits are dry, three-angled capsules that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cnicus heteromallus is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
