Cerastium gnaphalodes subsp. album

Cerastium gnaphalodes subsp.

album, commonly known as the white mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has hairy, succulent stems and leaves that are usually covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
