Cymbopogon gidarba var. burmanicus

Cymbopogon gidarba var. burmanicus, commonly known as Burmese citron grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
