Cinna stricta

Cinna stricta, commonly known as stiff cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered white or yellowish flowers. The plant has serrated leaves that are typically alternate and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Cinna stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
