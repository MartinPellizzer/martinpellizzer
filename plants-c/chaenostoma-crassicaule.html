Chaenostoma crassicaule

Chaenostoma crassicaule, commonly known as the broad-stemmed water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pink.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and fish.
