Carex uhligii

Carex uhligii, commonly known as the Uhlig's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex uhligii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
