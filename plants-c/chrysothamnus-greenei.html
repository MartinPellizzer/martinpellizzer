Chrysothamnus greenei

Chrysothamnus greenei, commonly known as the Greene's goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
