Cassia lentiscifolia

Cassia lentiscifolia, commonly known as the Indian cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are yellow, often arranged in spikes. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

Cassia lentiscifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
