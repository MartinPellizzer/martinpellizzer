Croton poecilanthus

Croton poecilanthus, commonly known as the red-leaved croton, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that often display a vibrant red or purple hue, especially under direct sunlight. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens where it can add a dramatic visual impact.
