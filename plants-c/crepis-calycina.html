Crepis calycina

Crepis calycina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellowish.

Crepis calycina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
