Camelina sativa var. pilosa

Camelina sativa var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy camelina, is a variant of the oilseed plant Camelina sativa.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, yellow flowers and is known for its high oil content, making it valuable for both agricultural and industrial uses.

The hairy texture of its foliage helps reduce water loss and aids in its adaptation to various environmental conditions.
