Cynoglossum congestum

Cynoglossum congestum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Cynoglossum congestum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
