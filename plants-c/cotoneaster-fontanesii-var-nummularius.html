Cotoneaster fontanesii var. nummularius

Cotoneaster fontanesii var. nummularius, commonly known as the silver cotoneaster, is a compact shrub native to China.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and often covered in a fine silvery or whitish coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is also noted for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a low-maintenance choice for landscaping.
