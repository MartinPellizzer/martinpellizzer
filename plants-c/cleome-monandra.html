Cleome monandra

Cleome monandra, commonly known as the tropical spider flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves and hairy stems. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and they bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cleome monandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
