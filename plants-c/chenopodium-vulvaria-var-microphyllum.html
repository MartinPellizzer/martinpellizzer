Chenopodium vulvaria var. microphyllum

Chenopodium vulvaria var. microphyllum, commonly known as narrowleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, three-angled capsule that contains several tiny seeds.

This species is often found in moist to dry habitats and is part of the Amaranthaceae family.
