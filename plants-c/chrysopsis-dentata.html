Chrysopsis dentata

Chrysopsis dentata, commonly known as the toothed goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are often toothed or lobed, giving it its scientific name.

Chrysopsis dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
