Cynanchum adriaticum

Cynanchum adriaticum, commonly known as Adriatic dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its opposite leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insects.
