Cyperus calacaryensis

Cyperus calacaryensis, commonly known as the African sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized root systems.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. The plant features slender, triangular stems and small, clustered flowering structures called spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Cyperus calacaryensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
