Cissus ibuensis

Cissus ibuensis, commonly known as the climbing fig or African leafy vine, is a member of the Vitaceae family.

It is a trailing or climbing plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in its vine-like growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are often consumed in various regions of Africa. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Cissus ibuensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
