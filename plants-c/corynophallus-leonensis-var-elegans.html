Corynophallus leonensis var. elegans

Corynophallus leonensis var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, translucent, and often bioluminescent tendrils that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant exhibits a specialized photosynthetic adaptation, allowing it to thrive in low-light conditions. Its flowers are short-lived, opening only at night and emitting a faint, sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its habitat specificity and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
