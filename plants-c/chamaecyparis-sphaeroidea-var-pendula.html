Chamaecyparis sphaeroidea var. pendula

Chamaecyparis sphaeroidea var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cedar, is a unique evergreen conifer characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is native to Japan, where it grows in cool, moist environments. The plant features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, creating a striking visual contrast. Its distinctive shape and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.

It produces small, inconspicuous cones and is relatively slow-growing, requiring well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
