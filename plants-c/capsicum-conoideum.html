Capsicum conoideum

Capsicum conoideum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are usually red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Capsicum conoideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild peppery flavor, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and culinary use.
