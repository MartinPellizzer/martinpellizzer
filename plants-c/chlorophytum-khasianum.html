Chlorophytum khasianum

Chlorophytum khasianum, commonly known as the Khasi spider plant, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its long, arching leaves that often curl into spiral shapes, resembling the appearance of a spider's web. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers on slender stalks, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a suitable indoor plant.

Chlorophytum khasianum is also known for its air-purifying properties and ease of care.
