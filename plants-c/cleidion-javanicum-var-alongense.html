Cleidion javanicum var. alongense

Cleidion javanicum var. alongense, commonly known as the white soursop, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit is a rough, spiny berry that contains a white, fleshy pulp surrounding black seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
