Copiapoa alticostata

Copiapoa alticostata, commonly known as the high-costata cactus, is a species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant features white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a dense covering of white to yellowish hairs.

Copiapoa alticostata is adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its thick stem and relying on minimal rainfall for survival.
