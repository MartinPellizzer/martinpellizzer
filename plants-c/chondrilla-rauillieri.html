Chondrilla rauillieri

Chondrilla rauillieri, commonly known as the desert hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows from a taproot and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It has adapted to harsh environments through features such as reduced leaf size and deep root systems.

Chondrilla rauillieri is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
