Centaurea pseudosinaica subsp. niebuhrii

Centaurea pseudosinaica subsp.

niebuhrii, commonly known as the Niebuhri's Blazing Star, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

The plant features striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, spiny bracts. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native range.
