Cassia medellinensis

Cassia medellinensis, commonly known as the Medellin cassia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Colombia, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces elongated pods containing flat, rectangular seeds.

Cassia medellinensis is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
