Clematis bonariensis

Clematis bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine Clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, sweetly scented aroma. The plant produces small, fluffy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis bonariensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.
