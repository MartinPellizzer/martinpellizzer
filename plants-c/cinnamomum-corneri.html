Cinnamomum corneri

Cinnamomum corneri, commonly known as the New Caledonia cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries containing one or two seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and have a distinct, spicy aroma similar to cinnamon.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
