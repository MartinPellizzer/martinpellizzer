Callicarpa gibaroana

Callicarpa gibaroana, commonly known as the purple beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that appear in late summer and persist into winter. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Callicarpa gibaroana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and autumn foliage.
