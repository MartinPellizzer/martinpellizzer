Cryptocarya fluminensis

Cryptocarya fluminensis, commonly known as Brazilian nut tree or Bertholletia excelsa, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves and showy white flowers. The tree produces large, woody pods that contain the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. It plays a vital role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is also a subject of conservation efforts.
