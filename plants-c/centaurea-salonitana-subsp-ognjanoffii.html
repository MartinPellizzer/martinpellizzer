Centaurea salonitana subsp. ognjanoffii

Centaurea salonitana subsp.

ognjanoffii, commonly known as the Ognjanoff's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and narrower leaves compared to the typical Centaurea salonitana. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
