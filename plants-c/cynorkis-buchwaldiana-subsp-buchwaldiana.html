Cynorkis buchwaldiana subsp. buchwaldiana

Cynorkis buchwaldiana subsp.

buchwaldiana, commonly known as the Buchwald's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within forest understories.

The plant features slender, erect stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is often adorned with intricate markings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
