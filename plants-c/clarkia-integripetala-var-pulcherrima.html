Clarkia integripetala var. pulcherrima

Clarkia integripetala var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the showy clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a white throat. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges. It typically blooms in the summer months and is often found in dry, sunny areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
