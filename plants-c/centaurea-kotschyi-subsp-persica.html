Centaurea kotschyi subsp. persica

Centaurea kotschyi subsp.

persica, commonly known as Persian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
