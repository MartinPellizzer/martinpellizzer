Cucurbita pepo var. fibropulposa

Cucurbita pepo var. fibropulposa, commonly known as the cushaw squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruits that can range in color from green to yellow or orange. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are typically separate from the fruiting structures. Its fruit is edible, with a mild, sweet flavor and a fibrous texture.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
