Cryptantha micrantha subsp. lepida

Cryptantha micrantha subsp.

lepida, commonly known as the golden foothills globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
