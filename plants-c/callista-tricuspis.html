Callista tricuspis

Callista tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Callista tricuspis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
