Clematis hexapetala var. insularis

Clematis hexapetala var. insularis, commonly known as the Island Clematis, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of China.

It features showy, white to pale pink flowers with five to six petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is typically found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
