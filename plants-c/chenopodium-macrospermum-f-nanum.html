Chenopodium macrospermum f. nanum

Chenopodium macrospermum f. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round seeds.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate wet conditions.
