Cinna decipiens

Cinna decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive cinquefoil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its attractive foliage and flowers, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
