Calamus perakensis

Calamus perakensis, commonly known as the Borneo calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is a climbing plant that can grow up to several meters in length, with long, flexible rhizomes used in traditional medicine. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo, and thrives in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often used in basket weaving and other crafts.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is valued for both its practical and medicinal uses.
