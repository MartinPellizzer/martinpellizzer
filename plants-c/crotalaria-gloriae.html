Crotalaria gloriae

Crotalaria gloriae, commonly known as Glory Crown, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately lobed, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance.

Crotalaria gloriae is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
