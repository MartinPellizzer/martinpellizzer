Carex luzulifolia var. luzuliformis

Carex luzulifolia var. luzuliformis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread vegetatively.

Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.
