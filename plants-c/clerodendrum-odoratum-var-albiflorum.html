Clerodendrum odoratum var. albiflorum

Clerodendrum odoratum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower glorybower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge. This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
