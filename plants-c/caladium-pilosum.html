Caladium pilosum

Caladium pilosum, commonly known as the hairy caladium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage displays vibrant colors ranging from deep red to orange and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Caladium pilosum is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds where it can flourish without direct sunlight.
