Calea camani

Calea camani, commonly known as the sacred plant or angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Calea camani is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and sedative properties.
