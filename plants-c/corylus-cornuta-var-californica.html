Corylus cornuta var. californica

Corylus cornuta var. californica, commonly known as the California hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces catkin-like male flowers and female flowers in separate clusters, typical of the hazelnut genus. Its edible nuts are encased in a hairy husk that splits open when mature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal interest.
