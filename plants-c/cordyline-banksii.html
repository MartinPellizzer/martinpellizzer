Cordyline banksii

Cordyline banksii, commonly known as the New Zealand cabbage tree, is a striking evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It features a tall, unbranched trunk with a distinctive crown of long, strap-like leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy texture and a prominent midrib. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters at the base of the leaves.

This plant is well adapted to coastal and forested environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
