Chondrodendron cinerascens

Chondrodendron cinerascens, commonly known as the Andean moonflower, is a climbing plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, dark purple berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Chondrodendron cinerascens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its nocturnal blooming habit.
