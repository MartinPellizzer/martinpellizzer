Citrus × nobilis subf. ishikawa-unshu

Citrus × nobilis subf.

ishikawa-unshu, commonly known as Unshu mandarin, is a hybrid citrus fruit native to Japan. It is characterized by its small, round, and smooth skin that ranges from pale yellow to orange in color.

The fruit has a sweet, juicy, and aromatic pulp with a low acidity level. It is typically harvested between November and February, depending on the climate.

This subvariety is prized for its flavor and is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
