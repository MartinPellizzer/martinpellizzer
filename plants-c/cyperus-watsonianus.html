Cyperus watsonianus

Cyperus watsonianus, commonly known as the Watson's flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in wetland and marshy habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or reddish in color. It is often found in shallow water or damp soil, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland ecosystems.

Cyperus watsonianus is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
