Celtis nymanii

Celtis nymanii, commonly known as the African boxthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its rough, thorny stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on rocky soils and in open woodlands.

Celtis nymanii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
