Cucubalus inflatus var. hirsutus

Cucubalus inflatus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy balloon vine, is a tropical climbing plant characterized by its inflated, balloon-like fruits.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes other cucurbit plants such as cucumbers and gourds. The plant features hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves, and it produces yellow flowers that are typically unisexual. Its fruits, which can grow up to 30 cm in diameter, are notable for their thick, fleshy walls and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
