Chytroma urceolata

Chytroma urceolata, commonly known as the cup-shaped lichen, is a unique organism that belongs to the class Lecanoromycetes.

It is characterized by its distinctive cup-like thallus, which provides a stable substrate for its symbiotic association with algae. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree bark in temperate regions. This lichen plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its habitat.
