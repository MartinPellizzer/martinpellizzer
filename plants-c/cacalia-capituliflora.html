Cacalia capituliflora

Cacalia capituliflora, commonly known as the blueweed or bluehead, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a mat-forming growth pattern. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cacalia capituliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
