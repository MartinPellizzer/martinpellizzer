Centaurea incompta subsp. derventana

Centaurea incompta subsp.

derventana, commonly known as the Derventan Cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features striking blue to purple flower heads with ray florets, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
