Carex × mucronulata

Carex × mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Carex × mucronulata is a perennial plant that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable habitats.
