Clematis daurica

Clematis daurica, commonly known as the Daurian clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Clematis daurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a climbing plant.
