Cyperus nanus var. subtenuis

Cyperus nanus var. subtenuis, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a compact, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist soil conditions, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
