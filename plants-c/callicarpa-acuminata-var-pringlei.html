Callicarpa acuminata var. pringlei

Callicarpa acuminata var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces distinctive purple to black berries that mature in the fall, attracting various birds and wildlife. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface with serrated edges.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
