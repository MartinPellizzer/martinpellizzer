Chamaecyparis tsatsumi

Chamaecyparis tsatsumi, commonly known as the Japanese cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and scale-like foliage. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of conifers. Its bark is reddish-brown and peels in thin, fibrous strips, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This species is valued for its timber and is also cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
