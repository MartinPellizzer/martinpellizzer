Campylocentrum colombianum

Campylocentrum colombianum, commonly known as the Colombian star apple, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, round, yellowish berry that turns red when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
