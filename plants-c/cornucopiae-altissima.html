Cornucopiae altissima

Cornucopiae altissima, commonly known as the celestial harvest vine, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its towering, spiraling stems that can reach heights of up to 30 feet, adorned with clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of gold and crimson. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often covered in a fine, silvery down that reflects sunlight.

The celestial harvest vine is not only a botanical marvel but also holds cultural significance in Andean mythology, symbolizing abundance and prosperity.
