Chuquiraga spinosa var. morenonis

Chuquiraga spinosa var. morenonis, commonly known as the Andean thistle, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically between 3,000 and 5,000 meters above sea level. Its structure includes a woody stem with dense, barbed bracts surrounding the flower heads.

This species is adapted to harsh, cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
