Crinum martinezii

Crinum martinezii, commonly known as the Martinez's crinum, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllis family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, fragrant white flowers. The plant features strap-shaped leaves and produces large, showy inflorescences that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Crinum martinezii is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in horticultural displays.
