Cyperus kraussii

Cyperus kraussii, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, making it a common sight in riparian zones and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily, forming dense colonies that can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Cyperus kraussii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and water garden settings.
