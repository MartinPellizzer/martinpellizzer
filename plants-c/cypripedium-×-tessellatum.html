Cypripedium × tessellatum

Cypripedium × tessellatum, commonly known as the tessellated lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid species that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species, Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium micranthum.

It is characterized by its large, showy, slipper-shaped pouch that is often adorned with intricate patterns of green and brown. The plant typically grows in cool, moist forest habitats, where it thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are pollinated primarily by flies and beetles.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Cypripedium × tessellatum is considered a vulnerable species in many regions.
