Calolisianthus amplissimus

Calolisianthus amplissimus, commonly known as the giant calolisianthus, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, often displaying vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a robust, woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and lance-shaped. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
