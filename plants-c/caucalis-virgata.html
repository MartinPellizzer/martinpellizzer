Caucalis virgata

Caucalis virgata, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Caucalis virgata is a perennial herb that is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with adequate sunlight.
