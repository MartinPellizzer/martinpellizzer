Carex scaposa

Carex scaposa, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a species of sedge native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, upright leaves and cylindrical, spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in late spring to early summer.

Carex scaposa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
