Carduus argentatus subsp. acicularis

Carduus argentatus subsp.

acicularis, commonly known as the silver thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silvery-gray, hairy leaves and spiny, purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
