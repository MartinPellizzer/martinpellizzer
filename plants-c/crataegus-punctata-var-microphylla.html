Crataegus punctata var. microphylla

Crataegus punctata var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a small shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Its compact size and ornamental features make it a popular choice for garden use.
