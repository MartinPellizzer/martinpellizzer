Croton pilulifer

Croton pilulifer, commonly known as the bead tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bead-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces a milky sap that can be toxic.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has various applications in local herbal practices.
