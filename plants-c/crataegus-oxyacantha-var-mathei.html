Crataegus oxyacantha var. mathei

Crataegus oxyacantha var. mathei, commonly known as the Mathe's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
