Chrysophyllum angustifolium

Chrysophyllum angustifolium, commonly known as the African orange or Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into edible, orange-colored fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. Its botanical characteristics include a shallow root system and the ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in suitable climates.
