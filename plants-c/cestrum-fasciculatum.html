Cestrum fasciculatum

Cestrum fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered trumpet flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It features showy, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.
