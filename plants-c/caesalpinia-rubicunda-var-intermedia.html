Caesalpinia rubicunda var. intermedia

Caesalpinia rubicunda var. intermedia, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink inflorescences, which resemble feathery plumes and bloom during the dry season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping in warm climates.

Its unique floral display and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, drought-tolerant plants.
