Commiphora laxecymigera

Commiphora laxecymigera, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fragrant, resinous gum that is harvested for its aromatic properties.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic uses.
