Cissus quinquangularis var. pentagona

Cissus quinquangularis var. pentagona, commonly known as the five-angle creeping fig, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-angled stems and heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round, red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and indoor spaces.
