Crithodium monococcum

Crithodium monococcum, commonly known as the narrowleaf wild oat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings but also plays a role in stabilizing soils in natural environments.
