Celsia brassicifolia

Celsia brassicifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cress, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features small, white or yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Celsia brassicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
