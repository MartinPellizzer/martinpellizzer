Crotalaria ankaranensis

Crotalaria ankaranensis, commonly known as the Madagascar crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has a short lifespan and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its seeds are enclosed in a pod and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in soil stabilization.
