Caralluma aurea

Caralluma aurea, commonly known as the golden crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically thick and succulent, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Caralluma aurea is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
