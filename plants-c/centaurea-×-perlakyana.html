Centaurea × perlakyana

Centaurea × perlakyana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Centaurea × perlakyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
