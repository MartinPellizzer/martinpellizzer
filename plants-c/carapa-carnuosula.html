Carapa carnuosula

Carapa carnuosula, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut or wild cashew, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces a hard, oily seed that is used for oil extraction. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in pharmacological research.
