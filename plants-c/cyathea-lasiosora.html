Cyathea lasiosora

Cyathea lasiosora, commonly known as the woolly tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The fronds are covered in dense, soft, white hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
