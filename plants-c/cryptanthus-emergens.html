Cryptanthus emergens

Cryptanthus emergens, commonly known as the "Emergent False Pennywort," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value lies primarily in its striking, variegated leaves rather than its flowers.
