Cheirostylis tippica

Cheirostylis tippica, commonly known as the Tippica orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structures. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers with a unique coloration pattern, often featuring a combination of white and yellow hues. Its roots are adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

The Tippica orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
