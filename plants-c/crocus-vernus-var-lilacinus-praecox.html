Crocus vernus var. lilacinus-praecox

Crocus vernus var. lilacinus-praecox, commonly known as the early purple crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a corm, and it thrives in well-drained, fertile soil. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and early flowering habit.

This variety is closely related to other crocus species and is sometimes confused with C. vernus due to similar characteristics.
