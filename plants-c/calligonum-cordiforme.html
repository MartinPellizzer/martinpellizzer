Calligonum cordiforme

Calligonum cordiforme, commonly known as the heart-shaped tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in dry conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion.

Its resilience makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
