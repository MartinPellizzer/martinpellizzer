Cassia hartwegii

Cassia hartwegii, commonly known as the desert cassia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and can thrive in poor soils.

Cassia hartwegii plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
