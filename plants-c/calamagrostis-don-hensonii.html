Calamagrostis don-hensonii

Calamagrostis don-hensonii, commonly known as Henson's reed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swales, and streambanks. The plant features tall, slender stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
