Croton californicus var. longipes

Croton californicus var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed croton, is a shrub native to California's coastal regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often multicolored leaves that display shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
