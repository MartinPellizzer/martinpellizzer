Campomanesia ovalifolia

Campomanesia ovalifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its fruits are edible and contain a single seed, making it a valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
