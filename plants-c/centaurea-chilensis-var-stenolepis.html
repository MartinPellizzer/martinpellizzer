Centaurea chilensis var. stenolepis

Centaurea chilensis var. stenolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centaury, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, making it a notable species in its native environment.
