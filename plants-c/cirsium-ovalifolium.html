Cirsium ovalifolium

Cirsium ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaved thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, woolly leaves that are oval in shape. The plant produces purple to pink flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Cirsium ovalifolium is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
