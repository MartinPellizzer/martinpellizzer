Codiaeum imperiale

Codiaeum imperiale, commonly known as the golden molly.

It is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with shades of yellow, green, and red. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a waxy, glossy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are followed by red, fleshy berries.

This plant thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant, decorative leaves.
