Convolvulus floribundus

Convolvulus floribundus, commonly known as the yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens across the globe.
