Chesneya mongolica

Chesneya mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian chesneya, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. The plant is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including extreme temperatures and limited water availability. It has a fibrous root system that helps it retain moisture in sandy soils.

Chesneya mongolica is often found in steppe and desert ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
