Clerodendrum blancoanum

Clerodendrum blancoanum, commonly known as the Blanco's Clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically followed by distinctive, inflated, purple or red seed pods. It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, also known as the mint family, and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.

The plant is often cultivated for its showy blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
