Coleus luteus

Coleus luteus, commonly known as the yellow coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage and spikes of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 inches. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture, while the stems are square and hairy.

Coleus luteus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
