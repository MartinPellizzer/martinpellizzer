Carnegiea gigantea

Carnegiea gigantea, commonly known as the Saguaro cactus, is a towering columnar cactus native to the Sonoran Desert of Arizona and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that can grow up to 50 feet tall and store large amounts of water. The plant has a unique ability to produce multiple arms, which are often referred to as "arms" or "branches.". Its flowering occurs in spring, with bright yellow flowers emerging from the areoles along the stem.

The Saguaro cactus plays a vital role in the desert ecosystem, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
