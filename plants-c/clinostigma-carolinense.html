Clinostigma carolinense

Clinostigma carolinense, commonly known as the Carolina rocknettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in woodland areas. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its stems are covered in fine hairs and often have a reddish tinge.

The species is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other flowering plants with showy blooms.
