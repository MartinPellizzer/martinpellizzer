Coleus poggeanus

Coleus poggeanus, commonly known as the Bogus Coleus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage rather than showy flowers. The leaves are typically velvety and come in shades of red, purple, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, and it is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
