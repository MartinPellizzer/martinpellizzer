Cirsium bernardinum

Cirsium bernardinum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its structure includes a stout, branched stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

Cirsium bernardinum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
