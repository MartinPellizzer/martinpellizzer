Calandrinia modesta

Calandrinia modesta, commonly known as the dwarf portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small, succulent annual that typically grows to a height of about 10 centimeters. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are fleshy and somewhat succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Calandrinia modesta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
