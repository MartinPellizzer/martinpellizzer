Calliandra mollicula

Calliandra mollicula, commonly known as the silky calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, feathery foliage and clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and can be used in ecological restoration projects.
