Chuquiraga brasiliensis var. divaricata

Chuquiraga brasiliensis var. divaricata, commonly known as the Brazilian burro's tail, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
