Crataegus glandulosa var. michiganensis

Crataegus glandulosa var. michiganensis, commonly known as the Michigan hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its glandular hairs on stems and leaves.
