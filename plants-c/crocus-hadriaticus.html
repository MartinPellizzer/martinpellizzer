Crocus hadriaticus

Crocus hadriaticus, commonly known as the Adriatic crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Crocus.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula and parts of southern Europe, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant during dry periods.

The Adriatic crocus is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
