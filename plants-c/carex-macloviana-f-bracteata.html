Carex macloviana f. bracteata

Carex macloviana f. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate sedge, is a compact, clump-forming plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces upright, spike-like flower clusters in late spring to early summer, which are often adorned with bracts that give it its distinguishing name. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This sedges is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
