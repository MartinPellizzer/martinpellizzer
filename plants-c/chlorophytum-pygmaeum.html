Chlorophytum pygmaeum

Chlorophytum pygmaeum, commonly known as the string of pearls plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South Africa.

It features linear, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in gardens and homes.
