Cistus × albereensis var. platyphylloides

Cistus × albereensis var. platyphylloides, commonly known as the broad-leaf rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically broader than those of other Cistus species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
