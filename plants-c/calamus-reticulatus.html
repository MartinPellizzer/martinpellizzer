Calamus reticulatus

Calamus reticulatus, commonly known as the nettle calamus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically green or yellowish in color. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, tropical environments.

Calamus reticulatus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
