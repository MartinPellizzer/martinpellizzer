Crataegus nigra f. penzesii

Crataegus nigra f. penzesii, commonly known as the Penzesii black hawthorn, is a botanical variety native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of 6 to 15 feet. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are usually oval to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and it produces white to pink flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native habitats.
