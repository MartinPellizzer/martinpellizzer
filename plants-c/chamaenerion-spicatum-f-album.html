Chamaenerion spicatum f. album

Chamaenerion spicatum f. album, commonly known as the white fireweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces tall, erect stems covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows.

Its striking white blooms make it a distinctive addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
