Carex nanelliformis

Carex nanelliformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-green color.

The flowering structures, called spikes, are usually slender and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
