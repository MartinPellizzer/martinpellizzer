Carum carvi f. rhodochranthum

Carum carvi f. rhodochranthum, commonly known as the purple flowering caraway, is a botanical variant of the caraway plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple flowers, which contrast with the typical white or yellow blooms of the standard caraway. The plant belongs to the Apiaceae family, known for its aromatic and often edible plants. It typically grows in temperate regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and roots contain essential oils, contributing to its use in both culinary and medicinal applications.
