Chlorocrepis

Chlorocrepis, commonly known as the green carpet grass, is a perennial plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing foliage that forms a carpet-like appearance. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves that are green in color, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy areas.

Chlorocrepis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its native environment.
