Centaurea urvillei subsp. hayekiana

Centaurea urvillei subsp.

hayekiana, commonly known as the Hayek's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny environments.

The plant features striking blue to purple ray florets surrounding a yellow disk, giving it a distinctive appearance. It has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers.
