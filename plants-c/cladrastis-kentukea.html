Cladrastis kentukea

Cladrastis kentukea, commonly known as Kentucky yellowwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, white, pendulous flowers in early spring, which are followed by long, bean-like pods. It thrives in partial to full shade and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often used in landscaping and conservation efforts.
