Crataegus crus-galli var. pachyphylla

Crataegus crus-galli var. pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. It produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
