Cestrum prieurei

Cestrum prieurei, commonly known as the white cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.
