Cocos datil

Cocos datil, commonly known as the West Indian date palm, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach several meters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that develop into edible dates, which are rich in sugars and nutrients. Its fruit is typically harvested when it turns a deep amber color, indicating maturity.

The plant is well adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray, making it a common ornamental and agricultural species in warm climates.
