Carex renschiana var. haematosaccus

Carex renschiana var. haematosaccus, commonly known as the blood sac sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and cylindrical, with a distinct midrib, and the plant often forms dense clumps.

The variety name *haematosaccus* refers to the reddish or blood-colored structures that appear on the flowering stems, adding to its ornamental appeal.
