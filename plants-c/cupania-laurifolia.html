Cupania laurifolia

Cupania laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel ivy, is a woody vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, simple, and usually elliptic or ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

Cupania laurifolia is often found growing in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along stream banks.
