Cereus trigonus

Cereus trigonus, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to 10 feet in height. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom at night, attracting moths. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
