Cyanea fissa

Cyanea fissa, commonly known as the Hawaiian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Cyanea fissa has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds.
