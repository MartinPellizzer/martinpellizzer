Coronilla valentina proles pentaphylloides

Coronilla valentina proles pentaphylloides, commonly known as the five-leafed vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate or pentaphylloid leaves, which consist of three to five leaflets arranged in a palmate pattern. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
