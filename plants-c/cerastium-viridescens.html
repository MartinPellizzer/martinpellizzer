Cerastium viridescens

Cerastium viridescens, commonly known as the greenish mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
