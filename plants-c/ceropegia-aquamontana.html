Ceropegia aquamontana

Ceropegia aquamontana, commonly known as the string of turtles, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and fleshy, tubular leaves that resemble small, rounded leaves. The plant produces striking, pale yellow or greenish-white flowers that are often tubular in shape. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
