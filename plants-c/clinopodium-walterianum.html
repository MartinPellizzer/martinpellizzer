Clinopodium walterianum

Clinopodium walterianum, commonly known as Walter's oregano, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in regions such as California and Oregon. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary applications, similar to other members of the mint family.

Clinopodium walterianum produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
