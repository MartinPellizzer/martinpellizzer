Capillipedium assimile

Capillipedium assimile, commonly known as the slender water lily, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are typically green in color and have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface, often in late summer. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes, where they absorb nutrients.

Capillipedium assimile plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water clarity through its growth patterns.
