Carex huashanica

Carex huashanica, commonly known as the Huashan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Carex huashanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
