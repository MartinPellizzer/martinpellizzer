Cucurbita colocyntha

Cucurbita colocyntha, commonly known as the bitter cucumber or wild gourd, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces yellow flowers and develops a rough, bitter-tasting fruit that is typically harvested when immature. Its leaves are lobed and can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and can thrive in arid environments.
