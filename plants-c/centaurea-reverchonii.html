Centaurea reverchonii

Centaurea reverchonii, commonly known as the Alaskan paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is considered a weed in some regions.
