Crocus adamioides

Crocus adamioides, commonly known as the Adamioides crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a yellow center. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, typically blooming in early spring.

The bulbs are perennial, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
