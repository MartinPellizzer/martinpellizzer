Cyathula ceylanica

Cyathula ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon spurge, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka and parts of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its unique floral structures and often milky sap. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with simple, alternate leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are clustered in cyathium inflorescences, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family, and are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
