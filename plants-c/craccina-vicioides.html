Craccina vicioides

Craccina vicioides, commonly known as the common craccus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It produces legume pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Craccina vicioides is known for its adaptability and is considered a weed in some agricultural contexts.
