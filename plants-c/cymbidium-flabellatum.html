Cymbidium flabellatum

Cymbidium flabellatum, commonly known as the fan orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, fan-like leaves and tall flowering spikes that bear numerous fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in winter or early spring, producing white or pale yellow petals with a distinctive, recurved lip. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Cymbidium flabellatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a suitable environment.
