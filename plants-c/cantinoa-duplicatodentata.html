Cantinoa duplicatodentata

Cantinoa duplicatodentata, commonly known as the double-toothed cantinoa, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are notable for their doubly serrated margins, giving them a finely toothed appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Cantinoa duplicatodentata is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
