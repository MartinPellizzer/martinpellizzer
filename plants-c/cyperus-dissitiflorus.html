Cyperus dissitiflorus

Cyperus dissitiflorus, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Cyperus dissitiflorus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to moist conditions.
