Capparis koi

Capparis koi, commonly known as the Japanese glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with small, serrated leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers with prominent, fleshy petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.
