Commiphora pseudopaolii

Commiphora pseudopaolii, commonly known as the Ethiopian myrrh, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant produces a resinous sap that exudes from its bark, which has been traditionally used in incense and medicinal applications. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The species is notable for its aromatic properties and its ecological significance in its native habitat.
