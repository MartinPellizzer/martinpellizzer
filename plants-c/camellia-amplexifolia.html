Camellia amplexifolia

Camellia amplexifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that often wrap around the stems. The plant produces showy, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Camellia amplexifolia is also valued for its cold hardiness and ability to add vibrant color to winter landscapes.
