Corydalis curvisiliqua var. tenerior

Corydalis curvisiliqua var. tenerior, commonly known as the soft-stemmed coralbells, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and compact growth form.
