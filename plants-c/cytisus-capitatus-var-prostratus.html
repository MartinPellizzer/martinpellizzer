Cytisus capitatus var. prostratus

Cytisus capitatus var. prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.
