Cantinoa

Cantinoa, commonly known as the "golden cup" or "cup plant," is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of yellow flowers that resemble miniature cups. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, which is used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and often used in herbal remedies.

Cantinoa is native to North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
