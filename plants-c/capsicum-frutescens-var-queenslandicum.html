Capsicum frutescens var. queenslandicum

Capsicum frutescens var. queenslandicum, commonly known as the Queensland chili or wild chili, is a species native to Australia.

It is a compact, shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellow or red fruits that are often used in local cuisines for their heat and flavor. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture and dark green color, while its stems are hairy and woody.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in traditional Indigenous Australian food systems.
