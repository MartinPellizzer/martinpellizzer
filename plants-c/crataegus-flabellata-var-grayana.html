Crataegus flabellata var. grayana

Crataegus flabellata var. grayana, commonly known as the grayleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and pinnately compound leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red to orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
