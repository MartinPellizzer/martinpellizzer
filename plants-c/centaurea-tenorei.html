Centaurea tenorei

Centaurea tenorei, commonly known as the Tenore's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branching.

Centaurea tenorei is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
