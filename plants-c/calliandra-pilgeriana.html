Calliandra pilgeriana

Calliandra pilgeriana, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
