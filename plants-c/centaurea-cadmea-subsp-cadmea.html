Centaurea cadmea subsp. cadmea

Centaurea cadmea subsp.

cadmea, commonly known as the common centaury, is a flowering plant species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its erect, sparsely hairy stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands, rocky soils, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the flowers are arranged in dense, conical heads.

This subspecies is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
