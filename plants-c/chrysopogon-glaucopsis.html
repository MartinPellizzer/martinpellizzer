Chrysopogon glaucopsis

Chrysopogon glaucopsis, commonly known as blue grass or blue oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive blue-gray coloration due to the presence of a waxy coating.

Chrysopogon glaucopsis is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
