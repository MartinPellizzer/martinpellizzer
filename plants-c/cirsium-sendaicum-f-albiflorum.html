Cirsium sendaicum f. albiflorum

Cirsium sendaicum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered thistle, is a variant of the thistle species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple or blue hues of the species. The plant features spiny leaves and a robust stem, typical of the Cirsium genus. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
