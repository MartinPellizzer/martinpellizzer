Campanula alliariifolia subsp. alliariifolia

Campanula alliariifolia subsp.

alliariifolia, commonly known as the garlic leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, garlic-scented leaves and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens with full sun to partial shade.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, alliariifolia-like leaves and its compact growth habit.
