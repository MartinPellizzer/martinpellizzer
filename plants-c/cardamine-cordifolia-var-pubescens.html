Cardamine cordifolia var. pubescens

Cardamine cordifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent water cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces slender, elongated pods.

This variety is noted for its peppery flavor, making it a potential edible plant in certain culinary contexts.
