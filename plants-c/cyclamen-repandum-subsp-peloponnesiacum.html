Cyclamen repandum subsp. peloponnesiacum

Cyclamen repandum subsp.

peloponnesiacum, commonly known as the Peloponnesian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern regions of Greece. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside.

The plant produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a distinctive purple spot at the base of the petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded rock crevices.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming habit.
