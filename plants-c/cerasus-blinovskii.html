Cerasus blinovskii

Cerasus blinovskii, commonly known as the Blinovskii cherry, is a species of shrub native to the Far East of Russia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
