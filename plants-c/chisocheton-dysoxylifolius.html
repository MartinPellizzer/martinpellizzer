Chisocheton dysoxylifolius

Chisocheton dysoxylifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chisocheton, is a member of the Meliaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and fruit.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and lanceolate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in axillary inflorescences, while the fruit is a globose, woody capsule containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
