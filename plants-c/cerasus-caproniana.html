Cerasus caproniana

Cerasus caproniana, commonly known as the Chinese wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
