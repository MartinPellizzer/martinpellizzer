Caesalpinia microphylla subsp. fiebrigii

Caesalpinia microphylla subsp.

fiebrigii, commonly known as the dwarf flame tree, is a small shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its bright red or orange floral clusters, which appear at the ends of its branches.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
