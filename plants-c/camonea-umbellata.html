Camonea umbellata

Camonea umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its clustered fronds that resemble an umbrella, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients.

Camonea umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
