Clematis japonica var. urophylla

Clematis japonica var. urophylla, commonly known as the Japanese climbing vine, is a deciduous vine native to Japan.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its climbing habit and seasonal blooms.
