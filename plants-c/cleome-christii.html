Cleome christii

Cleome christii, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like blooms that appear in clusters at the tips of its stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Cleome christii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
