Calamus pedicellatus

Calamus pedicellatus, commonly known as the fragrant calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes that produce a distinctive, aromatic scent. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in spadices.

Calamus pedicellatus is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
