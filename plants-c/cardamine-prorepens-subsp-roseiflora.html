Cardamine prorepens subsp. roseiflora

Cardamine prorepens subsp.

roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist, shaded habitats in North America. It features small, trifoliate leaves that are typically borne on long, slender stems.

The plant produces clusters of delicate, rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, damp environments and is often found near streams or in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
