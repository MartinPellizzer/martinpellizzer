Calamus heteroideus var. depauperatus

Calamus heteroideus var. depauperatus, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing palm characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its inflorescences are compact and bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
