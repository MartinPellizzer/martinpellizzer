Chionanthus ramiflorus var. grandiflorus

Chionanthus ramiflorus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Chinese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters and has opposite, simple, ovate leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often accompanied by showy, yellowish-green bracts.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
