Centaurea sanctae-annae

Centaurea sanctae-annae, commonly known as the Saint Anne's centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Centaurea sanctae-annae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
