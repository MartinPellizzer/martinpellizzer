Centaurea sonchifolia

Centaurea sonchifolia, commonly known as the common centaury, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant has erect, branched stems and produces yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive central disk. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

It is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
