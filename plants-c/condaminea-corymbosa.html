Condaminea corymbosa

Condaminea corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Condaminea corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
