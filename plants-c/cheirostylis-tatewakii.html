Cheirostylis tatewakii

Cheirostylis tatewakii, commonly known as the Tatewaki's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated inflorescence and striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces fragrant blooms that attract specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is subject to conservation efforts.
