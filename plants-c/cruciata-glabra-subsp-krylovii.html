Cruciata glabra subsp. krylovii

Cruciata glabra subsp.

krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov's sand verbena, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
