Centaurea aucheri subsp. farsistanica

Centaurea aucheri subsp.

farsistanica, commonly known as the Persian cornflower, is a perennial herb native to Iran. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
