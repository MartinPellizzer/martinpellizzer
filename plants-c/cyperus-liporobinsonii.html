Cyperus liporobinsonii

Cyperus liporobinsonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a cylindrical cluster. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing tiny, three-lobed flowers.

Cyperus liporobinsonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
