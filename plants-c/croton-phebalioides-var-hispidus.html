Croton phebalioides var. hispidus

Croton phebalioides var. hispidus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical with a hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking leaf patterns.
