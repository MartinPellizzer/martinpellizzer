Coffea arabica var. bourbon

Coffea arabica var. bourbon, commonly known as Brazilian bourbon, is a variant of the Arabica coffee species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped beans and a distinct sweet, floral aroma. The plant thrives in high-altitude regions with cool temperatures and well-drained soil. It is known for producing a smooth, medium-bodied coffee with notes of caramel and citrus.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is highly sought after in specialty coffee markets.
