Croton corollatus

Croton corollatus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often resembling flames. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds covered in a fleshy, sticky coating. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Croton corollatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
