Cassia dentata

Cassia dentata, commonly known as the toothed cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with serrated margins, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes, and it produces elongated, flat pods containing seeds.

Cassia dentata is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
