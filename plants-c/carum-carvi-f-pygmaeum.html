Carum carvi f. pygmaeum

Carum carvi f. pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf caraway, is a compact variant of the common caraway plant.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and umbel-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it ideal for small garden spaces. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a distinctive caraway scent when crushed.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, often used in culinary and herbal applications.
