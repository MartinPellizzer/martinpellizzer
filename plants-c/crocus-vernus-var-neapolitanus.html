Crocus vernus var. neapolitanus

Crocus vernus var. neapolitanus, commonly known as the Neapolitan crocus, is a bulbous perennial native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, early spring flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically appearing before the leaves emerge. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette near the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
