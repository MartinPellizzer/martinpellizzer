Cerasus caproniana var. pumila

Cerasus caproniana var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
