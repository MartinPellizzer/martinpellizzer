Crataegus monogyna var. trilobata

Crataegus monogyna var. trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit, known as a haw, is typically red or orange and persists into winter. This variety is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
