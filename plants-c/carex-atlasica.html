Carex atlasica

Carex atlasica, commonly known as the Atlas sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and reeds, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers.

Carex atlasica is adapted to harsh climatic conditions and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
