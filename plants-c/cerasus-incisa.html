Cerasus incisa

Cerasus incisa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yuzen-zakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental flowers, which bloom in early spring with a delicate pink to white color. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. Its leaves are ovate and turn vibrant red in the fall, adding to its aesthetic appeal.

Cerasus incisa is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
