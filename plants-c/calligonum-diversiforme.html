Calligonum diversiforme

Calligonum diversiforme, commonly known as the variable saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low precipitation.

Calligonum diversiforme plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
