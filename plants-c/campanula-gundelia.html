Campanula gundelia

Campanula gundelia, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and grows in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Campanula gundelia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
