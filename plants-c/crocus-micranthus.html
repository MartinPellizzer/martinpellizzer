Crocus micranthus

Crocus micranthus, commonly known as the dwarf crocus, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 5-10 centimeters. The plant produces delicate, early spring blooms with bright yellow or orange petals and a distinctive, rounded shape. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem.

Crocus micranthus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-flowering display.
