Cynanchum petignatii

Cynanchum petignatii, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed or the red milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
