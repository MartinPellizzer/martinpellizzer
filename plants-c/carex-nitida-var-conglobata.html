Carex nitida var. conglobata

Carex nitida var. conglobata, commonly known as the glossy sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly glossy, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually pale green to brown, adding subtle texture to the landscape. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Carex nitida var. conglobata is valued in gardens for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
