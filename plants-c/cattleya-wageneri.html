Cattleya wageneri

Cattleya wageneri, commonly known as the Wagener's Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structure. Its roots are often found in the bark of trees, adapting to its epiphytic lifestyle.

The flowers are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
