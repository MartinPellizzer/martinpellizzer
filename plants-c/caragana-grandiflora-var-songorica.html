Caragana grandiflora var. songorica

Caragana grandiflora var. songorica, commonly known as the Songorica shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia, particularly Kazakhstan and surrounding regions.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
