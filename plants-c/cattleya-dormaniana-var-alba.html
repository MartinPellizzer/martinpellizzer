Cattleya dormaniana var. alba

Cattleya dormaniana var. alba, commonly known as the White Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a fragrant scent. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It features long, arching inflorescences and is prized for its beauty in horticultural cultivation.

The varietal name 'alba' refers to its distinctive white coloration, setting it apart from other varieties of Cattleya.
