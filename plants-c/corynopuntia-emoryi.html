Corynopuntia emoryi

Corynopuntia emoryi, commonly known as Emory's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spineless, flattened stems that form a dense, low-growing shrub. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a "tuna," is a large, fleshy berry that ripens to a red or orange color.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in desert ecosystems.
