Cicer songaricum var. pamiricum

Cicer songaricum var. pamiricum, commonly known as the Pamir chickpea, is a wild legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elongated pods containing several seeds, and it typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is adapted to harsh climates and can thrive in poor soils.

This variety is of interest for its potential in agricultural research and as a source of genetic diversity.
