Carica palandensis

Carica palandensis, commonly known as the Philippine papaya, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is closely related to the common papaya (Carica papaya). The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic pulp. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
