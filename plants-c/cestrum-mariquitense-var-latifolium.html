Cestrum mariquitense var. latifolium

Cestrum mariquitense var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a moderate to fast growth rate. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
