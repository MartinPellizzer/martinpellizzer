Camellia lawii

Camellia lawii, commonly known as the yellow camellia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is an evergreen shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are typically single or semi-double, with five petals that are often crinkled.

Camellia lawii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
