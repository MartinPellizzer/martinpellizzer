Clinopodium multicaule var. taquetii

Clinopodium multicaule var. taquetii, commonly known as Taquet's hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in whorls along the stems.

The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a mild mint-like scent when crushed.
