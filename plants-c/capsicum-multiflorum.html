Capsicum multiflorum

Capsicum multiflorum, commonly known as the purple husk pepper, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, violet flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces elongated, pale green to purple fruits that mature to a deep red or orange color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
