Carex goodenowii var. strictiformis

Carex goodenowii var. strictiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

The plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
