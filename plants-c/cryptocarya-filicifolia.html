Cryptocarya filicifolia

Cryptocarya filicifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaved cryptocarya, is a member of the Lauraceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and evergreen nature.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features lanceolate, leathery leaves that are often arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces drupes that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
