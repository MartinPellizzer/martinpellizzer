Codonopsis convolvulacea var. limprichtii

Codonopsis convolvulacea var. limprichtii, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.

The variety limprichtii is distinguished by its more compact growth and slightly different flower morphology compared to the typical form.
