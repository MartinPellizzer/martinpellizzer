Ctenodon petraeus var. madrensis

Ctenodon petraeus var. madrensis, commonly known as the Madren Mountain Ctenodon, is a rare and specialized plant species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, it is considered an endangered species.
