Castela tweediei

Castela tweediei, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red cedar, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Mauritius.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that contain a single seed. Castela tweediei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

It is also noted for its slow growth and preference for well-drained, acidic conditions.
