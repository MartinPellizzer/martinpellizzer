Campanula saxifragoides var. guinetii

Campanula saxifragoides var. guinetii, commonly known as the Guinet's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
