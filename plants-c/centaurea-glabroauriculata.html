Centaurea glabroauriculata

Centaurea glabroauriculata, commonly known as the glabrous auriculate centaurea, is a flowering plant species native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are usually glabrous (smooth) and have auriculate (ear-like) bases, giving it its specific epithet.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.
