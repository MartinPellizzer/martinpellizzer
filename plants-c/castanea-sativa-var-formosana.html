Castanea sativa var. formosana

Castanea sativa var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan chestnut, is a variant of the sweet chestnut native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its large, elongated nuts with a thick, spiny husk. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional forestry and agriculture.
