Chondrorhyncha escobariana

Chondrorhyncha escobariana, commonly known as the Escobaria cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white to grayish spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the top of the stem. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

The plant is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
