Croton origanifolius

Croton origanifolius, commonly known as the lemon-scented croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that emit a distinctive lemon-like fragrance when crushed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval-shaped, woody fruits. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
