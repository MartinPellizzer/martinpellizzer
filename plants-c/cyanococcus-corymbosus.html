Cyanococcus corymbosus

Cyanococcus corymbosus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, low-growing habit with silvery-gray leaves covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Cyanococcus corymbosus is also known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
