Cardamine sinomanshurica

Cardamine sinomanshurica, commonly known as the Chinese water shield, is a small perennial herb belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive basal leaves that are divided into several lobes, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in a raceme, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from waterlogged soils.

Cardamine sinomanshurica is often found in temperate regions of East Asia, including China and Korea.
