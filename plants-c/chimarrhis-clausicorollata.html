Chimarrhis clausicorollata

Chimarrhis clausicorollata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

The species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
