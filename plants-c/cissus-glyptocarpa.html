Cissus glyptocarpa

Cissus glyptocarpa, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing fig, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Cissus glyptocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
