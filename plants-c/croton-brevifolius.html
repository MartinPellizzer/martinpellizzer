Croton brevifolius

Croton brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries, which are a source of food for various birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
