Cucurbita marsupiiformis

Cucurbita marsupiiformis, commonly known as the "spiny gourd," is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant produces large, spiny fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough, hairy texture.

The fruit is typically harvested when mature, and it is used in local cuisines for its edible flesh and seeds.
