Clematis djalonensis var. hirsutissima

Clematis djalonensis var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy clematis, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features vigorous growth with slender, woody stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, often with a fragrant aroma. Its leaves are typically compound, with small, ovate leaflets covered in fine, hairy pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
