Circaea lutetiana var. hirsuta

Circaea lutetiana var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces clusters of yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a rugged appearance, and it is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
