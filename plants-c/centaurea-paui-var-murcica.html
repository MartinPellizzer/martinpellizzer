Centaurea paui var. murcica

Centaurea paui var. murcica, commonly known as the Murcian centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny habitats. The plant features bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are usually upright with some branching.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
