Chrysobalanus icaco var. ellipticus

Chrysobalanus icaco var. ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic wax apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow or orange berries. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to its high nutritional value.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
