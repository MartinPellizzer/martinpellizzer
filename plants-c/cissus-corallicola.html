Cissus corallicola

Cissus corallicola, commonly known as the coral creeper, is a climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Cissus corallicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of growing conditions.
