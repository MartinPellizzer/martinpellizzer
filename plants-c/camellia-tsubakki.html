Camellia tsubakki

Camellia tsubakki, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and its showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The flowers are typically red or pink, with a waxy texture and a subtle fragrance. It is an evergreen shrub that can grow up to 6 feet tall, often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Camellia tsubakki prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.
