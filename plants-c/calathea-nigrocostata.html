Calathea nigrocostata

Calathea nigrocostata, commonly known as the black-costed calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It features dark green, lance-shaped leaves with prominent, contrasting black veins that create a dramatic visual effect. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in shaded indoor environments. Its foliage remains lush and vibrant throughout the year, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Calathea nigrocostata requires indirect light, consistent humidity, and well-draining soil to thrive.
