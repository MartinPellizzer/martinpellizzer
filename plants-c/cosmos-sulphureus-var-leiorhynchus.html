Cosmos sulphureus var. leiorhynchus

Cosmos sulphureus var. leiorhynchus, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, ray-like flowers surrounding a central disk. The plant produces pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
