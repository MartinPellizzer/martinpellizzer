Cytisus capitatus

Cytisus capitatus, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Cytisus capitatus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping.
