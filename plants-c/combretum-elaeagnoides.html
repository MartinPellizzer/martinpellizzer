Combretum elaeagnoides

Combretum elaeagnoides, commonly known as silver flame or wild silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
