Crataegus oxyacantha f. candida

Crataegus oxyacantha f. candida, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its white flowers, which contrast with the typical pink or red blooms of the species. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, red berries in the fall.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
