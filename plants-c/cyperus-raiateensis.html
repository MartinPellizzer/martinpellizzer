Cyperus raiateensis

Cyperus raiateensis, commonly known as the Tahitian flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Cyperus raiateensis is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers that develop in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.
