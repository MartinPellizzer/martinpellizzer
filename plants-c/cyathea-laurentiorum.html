Cyathea laurentiorum

Cyathea laurentiorum, commonly known as the Laurentian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living for over a century. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments with high humidity.

Cyathea laurentiorum plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
