Cissus sicyoides f. tiliacea

Cissus sicyoides f. tiliacea, commonly known as the Tiliacea Cissus, is a variant of the Cissus genus native to parts of Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, calcified structures called rhipidium, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
