Chaerophyllum procumbens var. tainturieri

Chaerophyllum procumbens var. tainturieri, commonly known as the creeping chaerophyllum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
