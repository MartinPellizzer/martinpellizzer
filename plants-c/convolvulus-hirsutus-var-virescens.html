Convolvulus hirsutus var. virescens

Convolvulus hirsutus var. virescens, commonly known as the green hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces trumpet-shaped, pale green to yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
