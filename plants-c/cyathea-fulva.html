Cyathea fulva

Cyathea fulva, commonly known as the golden tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, brownish layer. The plant features large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance. Cyathea fulva thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
