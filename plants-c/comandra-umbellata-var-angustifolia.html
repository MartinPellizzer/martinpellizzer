Comandra umbellata var. angustifolia

Comandra umbellata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf comandra, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, angular stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in an umbel, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, with a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
