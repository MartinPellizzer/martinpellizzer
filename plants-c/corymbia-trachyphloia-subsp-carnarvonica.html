Corymbia trachyphloia subsp. carnarvonica

Corymbia trachyphloia subsp.

carnarvonica, commonly known as the Carnarvon bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that often appears reddish or brownish in color.

The tree produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
