Celosia lappacea

Celosia lappacea, commonly known as the flame flower or cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant, plume-like inflorescences that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Celosia lappacea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
