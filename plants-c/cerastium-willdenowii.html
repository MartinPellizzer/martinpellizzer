Cerastium willdenowii

Cerastium willdenowii, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.
