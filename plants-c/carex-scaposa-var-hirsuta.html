Carex scaposa var. hirsuta

Carex scaposa var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine, hairy textures, giving it its common name. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, greenish-yellow flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
