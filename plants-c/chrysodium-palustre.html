Chrysodium palustre

Chrysodium palustre, commonly known as the marsh chrysanthemum, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, upright stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in bogs and swampy areas.

Chrysodium palustre plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects.
