Cascarilla magnifolia

Cascarilla magnifolia, commonly known as the magnificent cascarilla, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by orange-colored fruits that are somewhat similar to citrus fruits. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a strong, bitter scent.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
