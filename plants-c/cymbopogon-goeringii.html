Cymbopogon goeringii

Cymbopogon goeringii, commonly known as citronella grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Citronella grass is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in natural insect repellents.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both horticulture and aromatherapy.
