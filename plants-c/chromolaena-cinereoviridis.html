Chromolaena cinereoviridis

Chromolaena cinereoviridis, commonly known as the silver-green heliconia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, colorful inflorescences that resemble a spiral, with bright yellow or orange bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
