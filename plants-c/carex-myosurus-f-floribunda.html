Carex myosurus f. floribunda

Carex myosurus f. floribunda, commonly known as the flowering sedge, is a variant of the common sedge, characterized by its distinctive flowering structure.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that bear small, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

This ornamental plant is valued for its ability to provide habitat for wildlife and its aesthetic appeal in wetland gardens.
