Capparis velutina

Capparis velutina, commonly known as the velvet caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its spiny stems and thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round, green berries. Its unique appearance and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanists and horticulturists.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
