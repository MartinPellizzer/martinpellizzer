Cyphostemma adenocephalum

Cyphostemma adenocephalum, commonly known as the "Coral Tree" or "Coral Plant," is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like flowers, which are typically bright red or orange and arranged in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Cyphostemma adenocephalum is also known for its ability to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid environments.
