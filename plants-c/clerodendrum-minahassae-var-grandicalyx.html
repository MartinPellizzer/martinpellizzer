Clerodendrum minahassae var. grandicalyx

Clerodendrum minahassae var. grandicalyx, commonly known as the giant flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that range in color from red to orange, often mistaken for flowers. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, surrounded by the vibrant bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.
