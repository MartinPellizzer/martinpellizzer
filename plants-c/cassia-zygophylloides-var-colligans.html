Cassia zygophylloides var. colligans

Cassia zygophylloides var. colligans, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
