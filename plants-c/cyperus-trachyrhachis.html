Cyperus trachyrhachis

Cyperus trachyrhachis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical stems.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils and often found in wetlands, ditches, and along water bodies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common pioneer species in disturbed wetland areas.

Cyperus trachyrhachis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
