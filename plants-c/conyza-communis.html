Conyza communis

Conyza communis, commonly known as common blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It features small, blue to purple composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves and produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Conyza communis is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
