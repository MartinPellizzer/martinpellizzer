Cyperus guatemalensis

Cyperus guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Cyperus guatemalensis is native to Central America and parts of South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
