Cissampelos pannosa

Cissampelos pannosa, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a fleshy pulp. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
