Cordia formosa

Cordia formosa, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically woody and branched. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Cordia formosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
