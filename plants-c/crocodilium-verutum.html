Crocodilium verutum

Crocodilium verutum, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a toxic plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple berries and shiny, oval-shaped leaves. The plant contains potent alkaloids such as atropine and scopolamine, which can be lethal if ingested. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered a dangerous plant both in the wild and in cultivation.
