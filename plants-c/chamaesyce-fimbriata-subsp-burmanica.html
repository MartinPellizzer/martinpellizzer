Chamaesyce fimbriata subsp. burmanica

Chamaesyce fimbriata subsp.

burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese sandmat, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It features small, narrow, fimbriate (frayed) leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
