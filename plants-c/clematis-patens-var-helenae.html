Clematis patens var. helenae

Clematis patens var. helenae, commonly known as Helen's Clematis, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, fragrant scent. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil. It is known for its vigorous growth and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Clematis patens var. helenae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
