Cranichis pauciflora

Cranichis pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has a slender, erect stem with few leaves, and its roots are adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
