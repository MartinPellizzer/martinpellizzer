Cardamine rivularis

Cardamine rivularis, commonly known as water cress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of slender, hairy leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and rocky areas. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with four petals, which are typically arranged in a raceme. Its roots are fibrous, and it is known for its peppery, aromatic leaves that are edible.

Cardamine rivularis is a hardy perennial that thrives in temperate climates and is sometimes cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
