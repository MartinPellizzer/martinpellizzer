Crocanthemum thyrsoideum

Crocanthemum thyrsoideum, commonly known as the tall everlasting or tall everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant produces numerous small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
