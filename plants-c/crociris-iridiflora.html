Crociris iridiflora

Crociris iridiflora, commonly known as the blue-flowered orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue petals and a slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically blooms during the spring and summer months, producing clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique coloration and beauty, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and botanical collections.
