Cyperus entrerianus

Cyperus entrerianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along water bodies, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. Cyperus entrerianus produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
