Carex paysonis

Carex paysonis, commonly known as Payson sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. It produces small, spadix-like inflorescences that are often surrounded by leaf-like bracts.

Payson sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
