Cyperus blandus

Cyperus blandus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower clusters. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are wind-pollinated, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Cyperus blandus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic insects.
