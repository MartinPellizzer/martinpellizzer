Casparya robusta var. glabriuscula

Casparya robusta var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth-leaved casparya, is a species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
