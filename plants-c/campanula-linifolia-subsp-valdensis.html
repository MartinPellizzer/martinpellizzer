Campanula linifolia subsp. valdensis

Campanula linifolia subsp.

valdensis, commonly known as the Valdensis bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
