Carex echinata var. grypos

Carex echinata var. grypos, commonly known as the rough-stemmed sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
