Centaurea paniculata var. hoffmannseggiana

Centaurea paniculata var. hoffmannseggiana, commonly known as Hoffmann's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are typically hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. The plant produces dense, paniculate inflorescences composed of purple-blue flowers with yellow centers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in dry grasslands and rocky soils, where it thrives in full sun.
