Cynanchum elachistemmoides

Cynanchum elachistemmoides, commonly known as the slender-stemmed dogbane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which is known for its milkweed-like characteristics. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually greenish-white in color, with a distinctive corona around the ovary.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
