Crepidium gibbsiae

Crepidium gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Crepidium gibbsiae is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
