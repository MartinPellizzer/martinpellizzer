Costus glaber

Costus glaber, commonly known as the rough costus, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Costus glaber thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its value in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
