Cyperus forskaolii

Cyperus forskaolii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus forskaolii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
