Corybas taliensis

Corybas taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure that encloses the reproductive organs, a feature typical of the Caladine tribe. The plant produces small, dark purple or brown flowers that are often hidden within the hood, making them less conspicuous to pollinators. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on the forest floor or on rocks.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
