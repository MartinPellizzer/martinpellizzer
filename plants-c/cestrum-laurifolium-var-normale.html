Cestrum laurifolium var. normale

Cestrum laurifolium var. normale, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves and clusters of showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers. The plant is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their shape and color.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
