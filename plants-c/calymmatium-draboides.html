Calymmatium draboides

Calymmatium draboides, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss.

Calymmatium draboides is often found in rocky soils and is a hardy species that thrives in low-nutrient conditions.
