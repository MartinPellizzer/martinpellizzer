Calanthe japonica

Calanthe japonica, commonly known as the Japanese calanthe, is a species of orchid native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically produces clusters of white to pale pink petals with a distinctive, elongated lip. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Calanthe japonica thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and conservatories.
