Crataegus azarolus var. glabra

Crataegus azarolus var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-flowered hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
