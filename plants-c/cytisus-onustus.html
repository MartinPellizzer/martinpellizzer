Cytisus onustus

Cytisus onustus, commonly known as the sticky broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and thorny stems, which are covered in a sticky resin. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are small, trifoliate, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Cytisus onustus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
