Cereus azureus var. seidelii

Cereus azureus var. seidelii, commonly known as the Blue Cereus, is a striking cactus species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white or pale blue flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its foliage consists of spiny, ribbed stems that help it conserve water in arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
