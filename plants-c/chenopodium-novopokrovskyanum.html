Chenopodium novopokrovskyanum

Chenopodium novopokrovskyanum, commonly known as the Novopokrovskya goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its stems are often prostrate or ascending, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological resilience.
