Campanula serrata subsp. recta

Campanula serrata subsp.

recta, commonly known as the serrated bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are serrated, hence the species name, and it often forms dense clumps. Campanula serrata subsp.

recta is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in woodland environments.
