Cirsium acanthodontum

Cirsium acanthodontum, commonly known as the thistle-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and can be found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and its flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the family.

Cirsium acanthodontum is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
