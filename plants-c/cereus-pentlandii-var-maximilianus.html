Cereus pentlandii var. maximilianus

Cereus pentlandii var. maximilianus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, branching stems that bear vibrant flowers. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, rounded structures, and it thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
