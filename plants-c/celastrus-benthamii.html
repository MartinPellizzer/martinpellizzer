Celastrus benthamii

Celastrus benthamii, commonly known as the Chinese staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to China.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
