Convolvulus calvertii subsp. calvertii

Convolvulus calvertii subsp.

calvertii, commonly known as the Calvert's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features slender, twining stems and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its leaves are alternate and usually have a hairy texture.

It is adapted to survive in harsh conditions with deep root systems and minimal water requirements.
