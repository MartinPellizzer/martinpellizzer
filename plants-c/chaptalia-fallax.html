Chaptalia fallax

Chaptalia fallax, commonly known as the silverleaf chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural areas.
