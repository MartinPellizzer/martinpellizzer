Carex pseudohypochlora

Carex pseudohypochlora, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex pseudohypochlora plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
