Corydalis afghanica subsp. elegans

Corydalis afghanica subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the Afghan elegance corydalis, is a member of the Fumariaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oblong seeds. Native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialty gardens.
