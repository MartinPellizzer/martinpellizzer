Calluna vulgaris f. parviflora

Calluna vulgaris f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf heather, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing pink or red hues that add color to its native habitats. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground cover in challenging growing conditions.
