Crassula sieberiana

Crassula sieberiana, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers during the winter months, adding a delicate beauty to its appearance. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.

Crassula sieberiana prefers well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
