Croton hypochalibaeus

Croton hypochalibaeus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in disturbed or secondary forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
