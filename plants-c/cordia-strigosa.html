Cordia strigosa

Cordia strigosa, commonly known as the rough cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa, the Caribbean, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, often hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Cordia strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
