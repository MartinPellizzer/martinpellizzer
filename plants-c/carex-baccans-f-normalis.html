Carex baccans f. normalis

Carex baccans f. normalis, commonly known as the common sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

The inflorescence consists of male and female flowers, with the female flowers often being more showy and clustered at the base.
