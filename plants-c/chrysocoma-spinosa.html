Chrysocoma spinosa

Chrysocoma spinosa, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
