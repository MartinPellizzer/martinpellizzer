Cavendishia pilosa

Cavendishia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy cavendishia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful bracts that range from red to orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cavendishia pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a houseplant in many regions.
