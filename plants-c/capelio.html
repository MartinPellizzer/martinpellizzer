Capelio

Capelio, commonly known as the common caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Capparaceae.

It is characterized by its small, green, edible buds that are often used in culinary applications. The plant typically grows in Mediterranean climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are succulent and lobed, with a somewhat hairy texture.

Capelio produces small yellow flowers that are followed by the distinctive, peppery buds that are harvested before they fully open.
