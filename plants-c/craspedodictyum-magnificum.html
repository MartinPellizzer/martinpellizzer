Craspedodictyum magnificum

Craspedodictyum magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent spiny cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem covered in sharp, spiny tubercles that help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short, erect pedicels. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stem for survival in dry conditions.

It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
