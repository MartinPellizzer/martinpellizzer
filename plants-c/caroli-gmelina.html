Caroli-gmelina

Caroli-gmelina, commonly known as the white teak or white ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round fruits.

Caroli-gmelina is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, where it plays an important role in the ecosystem.
