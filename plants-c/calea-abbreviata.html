Calea abbreviata

Calea abbreviata, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and grows in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Calea abbreviata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
