Clerodendrum capitatum var. subdentatum

Clerodendrum capitatum var. subdentatum, commonly known as the Crimson Beauty or Red-veined Clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red flowers with contrasting white veins, which bloom in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with finely serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a garden plant or for its medicinal properties.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor cultivation.
