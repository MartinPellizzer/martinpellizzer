Cinnamomum lanuginosum

Cinnamomum lanuginosum, commonly known as the silky cinnamon or woolly camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and yields a valuable essential oil used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

This species is also valued for its wood, which is lightweight and resistant to decay.
