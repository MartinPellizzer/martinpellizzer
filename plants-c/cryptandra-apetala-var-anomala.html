Cryptandra apetala var. anomala

Cryptandra apetala var. anomala, commonly known as the ghost gum, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-green bark and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden among the foliage. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that access groundwater.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
