Campanula andina

Campanula andina, commonly known as Andean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine pubescence.

Campanula andina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
