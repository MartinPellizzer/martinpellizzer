Colea obtusifolia

Colea obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved colea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
