Canthium sumatranum

Canthium sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran spurge, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
