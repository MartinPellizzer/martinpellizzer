Chionanthus ellipticus

Chionanthus ellipticus, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The flowers are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and are also appreciated for their ornamental value.
