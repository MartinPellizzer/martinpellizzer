Chenopodium virgatum var. exsuccum

Chenopodium virgatum var. exsuccum, commonly known as pigweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, alternate leaves that are typically lanceolate or ovate in shape, and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant is native to North America and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It is an annual herb that produces small, dry fruits called achene, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
