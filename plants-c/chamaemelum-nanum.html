Chamaemelum nanum

Chamaemelum nanum, commonly known as dwarf tansy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, fern-like leaves that are often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens.

Its historical use in repelling insects and as a medicinal herb highlights its botanical significance.
