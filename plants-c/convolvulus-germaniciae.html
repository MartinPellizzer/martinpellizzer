Convolvulus germaniciae

Convolvulus germaniciae, commonly known as the Germanicus bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender stems that can twine around other plants or structures. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are heart-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Convolvulus germaniciae is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in woodland edges, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
