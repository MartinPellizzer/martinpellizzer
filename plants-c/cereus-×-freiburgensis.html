Cereus × freiburgensis

Cereus × freiburgensis, commonly known as the Freiburg hybrid cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cereus jamensis and Cereus schlutzii.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant typically produces large, showy night-blooming flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. It is native to Brazil and thrives in arid environments with well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
