Cyclopia laricina

Cyclopia laricina, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of velvety, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Cyclopia laricina is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
