Cyperus mutisii var. ehrenbergianus

Cyperus mutisii var. ehrenbergianus, commonly known as the Ehrenberg's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water.

Cyperus mutisii var. ehrenbergianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
