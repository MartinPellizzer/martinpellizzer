Chenopodium album subf. dubium

Chenopodium album subf.

dubium, commonly known as common lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family. It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to triangular leaves that are often covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
