Cerastium triviale var. viscosum

Cerastium triviale var. viscosum, commonly known as sticky mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The variety viscosum is distinguished by its sticky secretions on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and can spread vegetatively through rhizomes.
