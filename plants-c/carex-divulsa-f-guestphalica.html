Carex divulsa f. guestphalica

Carex divulsa f. guestphalica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly folded along the midrib.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets, which are usually green to brown in color.
