Cirsium japonicum var. villosum

Cirsium japonicum var. villosum, commonly known as the Japanese thistle or hairy thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can be found in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed areas. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
