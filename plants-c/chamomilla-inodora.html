Chamomilla inodora

Chamomilla inodora, commonly known as the wild chamomile, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Chamomilla inodora is often used in traditional medicine for its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
