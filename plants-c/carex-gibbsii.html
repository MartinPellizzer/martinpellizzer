Carex gibbsii

Carex gibbsii, commonly known as Gibbs' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive triangular cross-section typical of sedges.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters, which are often surrounded by specialized bracts.
