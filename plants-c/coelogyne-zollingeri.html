Coelogyne zollingeri

Coelogyne zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendant, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its pseudobulbs are segmented and store water, aiding survival in its natural habitat.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their beauty and fragrance.
