Cheiranthus hesperidioides

Cheiranthus hesperidioides, commonly known as the desert candytuft, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
