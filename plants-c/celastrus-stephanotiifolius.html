Celastrus stephanotiifolius

Celastrus stephanotiifolius, commonly known as the stephanotiifolius bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are an important food source for birds.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
