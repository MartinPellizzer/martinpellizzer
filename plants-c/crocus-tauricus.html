Crocus tauricus

Crocus tauricus, commonly known as the Tauric crocus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Western Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, with a distinctive, often fragrant scent. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in dry, rocky soils, and it thrives in well-drained environments.

Crocus tauricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
