Clematis florida var. violacea

Clematis florida var. violacea, commonly known as the purple-flowered clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers that range from lavender to deep violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and has a vigorous growth habit, often reaching heights of 10 to 20 feet. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and horticulture highlights its cultural and botanical significance.
