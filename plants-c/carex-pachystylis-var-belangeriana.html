Carex pachystylis var. belangeriana

Carex pachystylis var. belangeriana, commonly known as the Belanger's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. This species typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ornamental appeal in water gardens.
