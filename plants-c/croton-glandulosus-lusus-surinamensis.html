Croton glandulosus lusus surinamensis

Croton glandulosus lusus surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam croton, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its branched stem and opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green hue. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
