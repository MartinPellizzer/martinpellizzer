Carex protracta

Carex protracta, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically broader than those of true grasses, with a triangular cross-section. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex protracta plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
