Carex myosurus subsp. spiculata

Carex myosurus subsp.

spiculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks.

The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
