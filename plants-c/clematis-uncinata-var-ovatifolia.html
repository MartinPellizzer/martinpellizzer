Clematis uncinata var. ovatifolia

Clematis uncinata var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the ovate-leaved Clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue or purple during the summer months. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
