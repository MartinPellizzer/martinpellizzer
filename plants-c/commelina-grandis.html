Commelina grandis

Commelina grandis, commonly known as the blue water hyacinth, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and arrow-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, often forming dense floating mats. Its rapid growth rate makes it an effective colonizer of disturbed aquatic habitats.

Commelina grandis plays a role in both ecological and ornamental contexts due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
