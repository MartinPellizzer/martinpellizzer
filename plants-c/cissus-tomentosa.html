Cissus tomentosa

Cissus tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus tomentosa is also valued for its ability to climb and trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.
