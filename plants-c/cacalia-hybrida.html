Cacalia hybrida

Cacalia hybrida, commonly known as the blueweed or bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its stems are erect and branching.
