Calamagrostis tacomensis

Calamagrostis tacomensis, commonly known as Tacomais reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis tacomensis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
