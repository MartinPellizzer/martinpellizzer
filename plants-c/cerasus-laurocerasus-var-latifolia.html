Cerasus laurocerasus var. latifolia

Cerasus laurocerasus var. latifolia, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, dark red berries. It has a strong, aromatic scent and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

However, the berries are toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
