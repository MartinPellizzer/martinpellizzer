Casearia macrophylla

Casearia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved casearia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
