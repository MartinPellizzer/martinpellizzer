Coleocephalocereus fluminensis subsp. decumbens

Coleocephalocereus fluminensis subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the Brazilian night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Brazil. It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive rib structure and spines that are typically yellow to brown in color.

The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique flowering behavior.
