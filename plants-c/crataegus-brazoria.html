Crataegus brazoria

Crataegus brazoria, commonly known as the Brazoria hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of Texas and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it is part of the Rosaceae family, which includes roses, apples, and other fruit-bearing plants.
