Cercocarpus montanus subsp. blancheae

Cercocarpus montanus subsp.

blancheae, commonly known as Blanche's curlleaf mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically twisted or curled, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
