Claytonia menziesii

Claytonia menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces showy, white or pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Claytonia menziesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
