Catasetum huebneri

Catasetum huebneri, commonly known as the Yellow Catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and unique, elongated sepals that resemble a bee, a feature that aids in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are known for their strong, sweet fragrance, which attracts specific pollinators.

Catasetum huebneri requires careful cultivation, as it is sensitive to temperature and light conditions.
