Cyathea confinis

Cyathea confinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that supports its growth in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.

Cyathea confinis plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.
