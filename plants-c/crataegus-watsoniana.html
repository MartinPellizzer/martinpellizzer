Crataegus watsoniana

Crataegus watsoniana, commonly known as Watson's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
