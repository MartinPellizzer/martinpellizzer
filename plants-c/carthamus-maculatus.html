Carthamus maculatus

Carthamus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted safflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features bright yellow flowers with distinctive red or brown markings, which are characteristic of the genus Carthamus. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been historically used in traditional medicine.
