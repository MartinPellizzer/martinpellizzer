Cucumis citrullus

Cucumis citrullus, commonly known as watermelon, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and sprawling vines. The plant produces bright red or yellow fruits with a sweet, juicy interior. The fruit's rind is thick and fibrous, while the pulp contains numerous seeds.

Watermelon is valued for its high water content and is widely cultivated for both fresh consumption and processing.
