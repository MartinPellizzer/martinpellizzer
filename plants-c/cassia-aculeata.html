Cassia aculeata

Cassia aculeata, commonly known as the sicklepod or goat's rue, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its long, spiny pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

Cassia aculeata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
