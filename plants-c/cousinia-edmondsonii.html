Cousinia edmondsonii

Cousinia edmondsonii, commonly known as the Edmondson's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Cousinia edmondsonii produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
