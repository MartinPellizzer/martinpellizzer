Carlina barnebiana

Carlina barnebiana, commonly known as the Barneby's carline thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carlina barnebiana is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
