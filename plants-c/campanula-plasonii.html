Campanula plasonii

Campanula plasonii, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Campanula plasonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
