Carex setosa var. mianxianica

Carex setosa var. mianxianica, commonly known as the Mianxian slender sedge, is a variant of the slender sedge native to China.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
