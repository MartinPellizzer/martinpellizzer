Calathea poeppigiana

Calathea poeppigiana, commonly known as the "Peacock Plant," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns and contrasting hues. The leaves are often veined with shades of silver, pink, and red, creating a visually stunning appearance. It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

This plant requires consistent humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
