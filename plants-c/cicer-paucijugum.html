Cicer paucijugum

Cicer paucijugum, commonly known as the few-lobed vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Cicer paucijugum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
