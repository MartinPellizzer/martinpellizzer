Clematis armandi f. farquhariana

Clematis armandi f. farquhariana, commonly known as the Farquharian Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
