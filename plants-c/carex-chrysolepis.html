Carex chrysolepis

Carex chrysolepis, commonly known as golden-sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow to golden-green foliage, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along streambanks.
