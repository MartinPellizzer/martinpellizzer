Carex nandadeviensis

Carex nandadeviensis, commonly known as the Nanda Devi sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the Himalayan regions of India and is typically found in moist, high-altitude habitats. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and spike-like inflorescences. It plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
