Caperonia cordata var. genuina

Caperonia cordata var. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian heartleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
