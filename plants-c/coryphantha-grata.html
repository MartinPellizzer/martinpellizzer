Coryphantha grata

Coryphantha grata, commonly known as the graceful cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale green spines. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring. Coryphantha grata is a succulent, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
