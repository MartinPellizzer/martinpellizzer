Charianthus coccineus var. parvifolia

Charianthus coccineus var. parvifolia, commonly known as the scarlet flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its striking color and compact growth habit.
