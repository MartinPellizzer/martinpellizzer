Campanula drabifolia subsp. creutzburgii

Campanula drabifolia subsp.

creutzburgii, commonly known as the creutzburgii bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color.

The leaves are lanceolate and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
