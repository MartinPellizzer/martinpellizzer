Calamagrostis lanceolata proles gaudiniana

Calamagrostis lanceolata proles gaudiniana, commonly known as the Gaudin's bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers in late summer to early autumn.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
