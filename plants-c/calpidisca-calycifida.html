Calpidisca calycifida

Calpidisca calycifida, commonly known as the flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, producing clusters of showy bracts that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its dramatic floral display.
