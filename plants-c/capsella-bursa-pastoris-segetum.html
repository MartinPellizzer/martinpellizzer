Capsella bursa-pastoris segetum

Capsella bursa-pastoris segetum, commonly known as shepherd's purse, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods that resemble a purse, hence its common name. The plant has alternate, palmately lobed leaves and small white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
