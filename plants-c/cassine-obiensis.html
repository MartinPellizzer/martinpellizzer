Cassine obiensis

Cassine obiensis, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Cassine obiensis is often found in moist lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its wood is hard and durable, making it useful for construction and furniture-making.
