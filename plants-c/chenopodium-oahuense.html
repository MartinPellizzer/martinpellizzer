Chenopodium oahuense

Chenopodium oahuense, commonly known as Oahu goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, coastal environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii. It has short, thick stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Chenopodium oahuense is considered a rare and endemic species.
