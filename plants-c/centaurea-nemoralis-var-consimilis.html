Centaurea nemoralis var. consimilis

Centaurea nemoralis var. consimilis, commonly known as the similar wood centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant features erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange-red flowers in late spring to early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers surrounded by bracts.

The variety consimilis is distinguished by its closely resembling flowers and growth habits compared to the typical form of Centaurea nemoralis.
