Cerastium atlanticum

Cerastium atlanticum, commonly known as Atlantic mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dunes or along shorelines.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to withstand salt spray and wind.
