Crepidium crenulatum

Crepidium crenulatum, commonly known as the crested tamarigold, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Crepidium crenulatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
