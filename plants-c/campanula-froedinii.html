Campanula froedinii

Campanula froedinii, commonly known as Froedin's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Campanula froedinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
