Corynephorus articulatus subsp. oranensis

Corynephorus articulatus subsp.

oranensis, commonly known as the Oranensis grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue, and the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
