Cyperus oblongus

Cyperus oblongus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like, with a broad, flat appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus oblongus plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
