Campylospermum densiflorum

Campylospermum densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower campylospermum, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, often branched inflorescences that bear small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and flowering display.
