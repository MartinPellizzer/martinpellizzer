Campanula sewerzowii

Campanula sewerzowii, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and it typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Campanula sewerzowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
