Coleus maculosus subsp. maculosus

Coleus maculosus subsp.

maculosus, commonly known as the spotted coleus, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with striking patterns of red, purple, and green.

The leaves are typically ovate to heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This plant is popular in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.
