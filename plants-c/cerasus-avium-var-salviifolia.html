Cerasus avium var. salviifolia

Cerasus avium var. salviifolia, commonly known as the cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It features simple, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible drupes that range in color from yellow to red. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and edible landscaping.

The variety salviifolia is distinguished by its leaf shape and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
