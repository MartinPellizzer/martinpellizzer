Coffea sessiliflora subsp. sessiliflora

Coffea sessiliflora subsp.

sessiliflora, commonly known as the African coffee or the Ethiopian coffee, is a species native to the highlands of Ethiopia and other parts of East Africa. It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries containing two seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

The plant thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid environment for optimal growth.
