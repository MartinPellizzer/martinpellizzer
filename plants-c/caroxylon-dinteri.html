Caroxylon dinteri

Caroxylon dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's karoo thornveld, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by pod fruits containing several seeds.

Caroxylon dinteri is adapted to harsh, dry conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
