Campanula aktascii

Campanula aktascii, commonly known as the Aktasci bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

Campanula aktascii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
