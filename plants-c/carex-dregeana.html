Carex dregeana

Carex dregeana, commonly known as the Drege's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like flower clusters. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its foliage is typically green to bluish-green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex dregeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates.
