Calliandra caracasana

Calliandra caracasana, commonly known as the silky calliandra, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery pods and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, pointed leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Calliandra caracasana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
