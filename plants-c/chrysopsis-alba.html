Chrysopsis alba

Chrysopsis alba, commonly known as the white coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright white to pale yellow.

Chrysopsis alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
