Chenopodium schraderianum

Chenopodium schraderianum, commonly known as Schrader's goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are enclosed in dry, three-angled capsules. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in agricultural fields.

Chenopodium schraderianum is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
