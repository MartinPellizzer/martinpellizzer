Clematis smilacifolia var. peltata

Clematis smilacifolia var. peltata, commonly known as the broadleaf clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, palmately compound leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a climbing plant.
