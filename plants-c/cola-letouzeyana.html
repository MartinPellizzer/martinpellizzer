Cola letouzeyana

Cola letouzeyana, commonly known as African cola, is a species of cola native to western and central Africa.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, red flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its caffeine-rich seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and beverages.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts within its native range.
