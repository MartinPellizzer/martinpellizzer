Cuphea leptoclada

Cuphea leptoclada, commonly known as the slender cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Cuphea leptoclada thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth form.
