Campylocentrum hondurense

Campylocentrum hondurense, commonly known as the Honduras flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks in warm climates.
