Campanula consanguinea

Campanula consanguinea, commonly known as the common bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soil. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Campanula consanguinea is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
