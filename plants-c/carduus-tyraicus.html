Carduus tyraicus

Carduus tyraicus, commonly known as the common cocklebur.

This plant is a herbaceous annual or biennial species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features a robust stem with spiny leaves and produces large, spiky, spherical fruits that contain seeds. The leaves are deeply lobed and covered with stiff, bristly hairs.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
