Calathea allouia var. violacea

Calathea allouia var. violacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green veins and a rich purple or violet coloration on the upper surface. The leaves are typically oval to heart-shaped and exhibit a unique marbled pattern. This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid environments.
