Carex speciosa var. courtallensis

Carex speciosa var. courtallensis, commonly known as the Courtallensian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, grass-like foliage that often forms a clump. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
