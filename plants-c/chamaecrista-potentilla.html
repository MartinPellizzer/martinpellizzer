Chamaecrista potentilla

Chamaecrista potentilla, commonly known as partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Chamaecrista potentilla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
