Cestrum aurantiacum var. warszewiczii

Cestrum aurantiacum var. warszewiczii, commonly known as the orange-flowered cestrum, is a variant of the cestrum plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
