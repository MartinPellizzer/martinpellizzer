Caryopteris mastacanthus var. nana

Caryopteris mastacanthus var. nana, commonly known as dwarf blue mistflower, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, blue to violet flower clusters that appear in late summer, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees. The plant has narrow, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its low maintenance and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
