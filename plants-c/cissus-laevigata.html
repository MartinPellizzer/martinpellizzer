Cissus laevigata

Cissus laevigata, commonly known as the rock fig or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Cissus laevigata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
