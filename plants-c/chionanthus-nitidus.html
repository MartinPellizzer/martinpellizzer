Chionanthus nitidus

Chionanthus nitidus, commonly known as the glossyflower viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The glossyflower viburnum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
