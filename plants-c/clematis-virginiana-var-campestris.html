Clematis virginiana var. campestris

Clematis virginiana var. campestris, commonly known as the field clematis, is a deciduous vine native to North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types. It is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides.

Its climbing habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden use.
