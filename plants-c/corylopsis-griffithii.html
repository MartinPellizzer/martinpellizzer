Corylopsis griffithii

Corylopsis griffithii, commonly known as the Kashmir golden bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has slender, arching branches covered with small, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in autumn. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Corylopsis griffithii is valued in gardens for its early spring blooms and ornamental appeal.
