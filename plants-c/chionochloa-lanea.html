Chionochloa lanea

Chionochloa lanea, commonly known as the Southern Alps grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems.

Chionochloa lanea is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh weather conditions typical of its native habitat.
