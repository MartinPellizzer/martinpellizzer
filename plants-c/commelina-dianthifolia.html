Commelina dianthifolia

Commelina dianthifolia, commonly known as the blue spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to retain moisture.

Commelina dianthifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
