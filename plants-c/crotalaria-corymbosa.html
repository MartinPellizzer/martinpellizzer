Crotalaria corymbosa

Crotalaria corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers clustered in racemes. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain toxic seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
