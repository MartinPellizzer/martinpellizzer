Commelina auriculata

Commelina auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and small, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

The flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
