Cardamomum capitatum

Cardamomum capitatum, commonly known as the large cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Himalayas.

It produces compact, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, dark brown, aromatic seeds. The plant thrives in cool, moist climates and requires well-drained, fertile soil. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and have a strong, spicy scent.

The seeds are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine for their distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
