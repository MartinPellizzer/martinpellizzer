Citharexylum myrianthum var. bahiense

Citharexylum myrianthum var. bahiense, commonly known as Bahia cherry or Brazilian cherry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a pleasant, aromatic scent. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are usually white or pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or orange color, providing food for various wildlife species.
