Crassula calcarea

Crassula calcarea, commonly known as the stonecrop or rock rose, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are often blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Crassula calcarea is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
