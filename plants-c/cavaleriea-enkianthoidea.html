Cavaleriea enkianthoidea

Cavaleriea enkianthoidea, commonly known as the golden bell plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Cavaleriea enkianthoidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
