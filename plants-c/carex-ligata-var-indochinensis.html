Carex ligata var. indochinensis

Carex ligata var. indochinensis, commonly known as the Indochinese sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often surrounded by bracts.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps retain moisture in its native habitat.
