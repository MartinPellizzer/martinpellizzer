Carum segetum

Carum segetum, commonly known as garden cumin, is an annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its seeds, which are used as a spice, are harvested when fully mature and dry.

Carum segetum is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and culinary applications.
