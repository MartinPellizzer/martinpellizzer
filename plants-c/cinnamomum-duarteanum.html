Cinnamomum duarteanum

Cinnamomum duarteanum, commonly known as the Duarte cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that contain a single seed. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for aromatic purposes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
