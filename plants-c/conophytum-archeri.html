Conophytum archeri

Conophytum archeri, commonly known as the "Cape tulip," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, which helps it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Conophytum archeri is valued for its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh climates.
