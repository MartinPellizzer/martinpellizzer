Calophyllum buxifolium

Calophyllum buxifolium, commonly known as the waxleaf calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green foliage that remains largely unchanging throughout the year. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
