Carex tricolor

Carex tricolor, commonly known as three-color sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-color foliage, with green, white, and purple hues along its leaves. This plant thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas. Carex tricolor has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
