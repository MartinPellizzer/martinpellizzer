Crocus bifloriformis

Crocus bifloriformis, commonly known as the two-flowered crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces two to three erect, narrow leaves and a single, showy flower with three petals and three sepals. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
