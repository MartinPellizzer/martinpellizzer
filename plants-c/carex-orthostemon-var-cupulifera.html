Carex orthostemon var. cupulifera

Carex orthostemon var. cupulifera, commonly known as the hooded sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are often hidden by a hood-like bract, giving it its common name.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
