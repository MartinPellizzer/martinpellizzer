Cyathea pachyrrhachis

Cyathea pachyrrhachis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive, fibrous root system and produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea pachyrrhachis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
