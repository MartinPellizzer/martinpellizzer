Carex scirpoidea var. europaea

Carex scirpoidea var. europaea, commonly known as European flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its flowering structure consists of small, three-ranked spikelets that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
