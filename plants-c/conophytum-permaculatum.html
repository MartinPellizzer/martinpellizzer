Conophytum permaculatum

Conophytum permaculatum, commonly known as the "Cushion Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form. The plant features fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring and summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
