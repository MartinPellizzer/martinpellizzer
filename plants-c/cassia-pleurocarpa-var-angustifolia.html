Cassia pleurocarpa var. angustifolia

Cassia pleurocarpa var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility. It typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
