Caleacte urticifolia

Caleacte urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and it is known for its stinging hairs that cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as fields and along roadsides.

Caleacte urticifolia is widely distributed across temperate regions and has both ecological and historical significance.
