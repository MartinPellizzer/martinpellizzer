Callitriche deflexa var. austinii

Callitriche deflexa var. austinii, commonly known as Austin's water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, opposite leaves that are oval to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are tiny, with four petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich habitats.
