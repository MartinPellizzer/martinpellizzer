Cucubalus lacerus

Cucubalus lacerus, commonly known as the lacerate cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and hairy, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, spiny fruits. Its fruits are typically harvested when they turn a deep orange-red, indicating maturity.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which have a tangy flavor and are used in local cuisines.
