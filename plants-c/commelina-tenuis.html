Commelina tenuis

Commelina tenuis, commonly known as slender commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate blue or violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, often accompanied by white bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
