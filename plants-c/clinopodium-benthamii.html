Clinopodium benthamii

Clinopodium benthamii, commonly known as Bentham's oregano, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, opposite leaves with a fragrant, mint-like scent. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, arranged in dense spikes, and it produces small, four-lobed nuts as fruits.

Clinopodium benthamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
