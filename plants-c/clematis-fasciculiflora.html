Clematis fasciculiflora

Clematis fasciculiflora, commonly known as the clustered clematis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Clematis fasciculiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
