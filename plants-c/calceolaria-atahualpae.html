Calceolaria atahualpae

Calceolaria atahualpae, commonly known as the Atahualpa slipper orchid, is a species native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that resemble small slippers, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of yellow and orange.

Calceolaria atahualpae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
