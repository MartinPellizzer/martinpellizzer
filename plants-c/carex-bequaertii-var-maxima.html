Carex bequaertii var. maxima

Carex bequaertii var. maxima, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with a dense, cylindrical inflorescence that produces small, greenish flowers. This variety is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in moist to wet soil conditions. It is often found in marshes, along stream banks, and in other water-saturated habitats.

The plant plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
