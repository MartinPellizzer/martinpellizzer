Cassia goratensis var. glabra

Cassia goratensis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the dry season. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods that split open when mature.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
