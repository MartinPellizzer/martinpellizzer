Cucurbita pedatifolia

Cucurbita pedatifolia, commonly known as the paddle gourd, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant is characterized by its broad, paddle-shaped leaves and yellowish-orange flowers. Its fruits are typically elongated and can vary in color from green to orange.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in some cultures.
