Canavalia polystachya

Canavalia polystachya, commonly known as the sickle bean or wild pigeon pea, is a tropical legume native to Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, cylindrical, spiny pods that contain several seeds. The plant grows as an erect annual herb, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is adapted to poor soils, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Canavalia polystachya is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility in agricultural settings.
