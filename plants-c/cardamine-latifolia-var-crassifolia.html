Cardamine latifolia var. crassifolia

Cardamine latifolia var. crassifolia, commonly known as broadleaf watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its rhizomes and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
