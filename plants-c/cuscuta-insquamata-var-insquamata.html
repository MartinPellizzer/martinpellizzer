Cuscuta insquamata var. insquamata

Cuscuta insquamata var. insquamata, commonly known as the yellow dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellow flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host to establish a connection. The plant is native to North America and is often found growing on grasses, shrubs, and other herbaceous plants. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through mechanical means, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops and native vegetation.
