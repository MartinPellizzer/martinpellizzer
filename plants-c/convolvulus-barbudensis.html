Convolvulus barbudensis

Convolvulus barbudensis, commonly known as the Bahamian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along coastal areas and in disturbed soils. It is adapted to warm, humid climates and thrives in full sun.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
