Chionodoxa lochiae

Chionodoxa lochiae, commonly known as the Persian snowdrops, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey and the Middle East.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings. Its foliage appears before the flowers, creating a striking visual contrast in the garden.

Chionodoxa lochiae is valued for its early blooming habit and low maintenance requirements.
