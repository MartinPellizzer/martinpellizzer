Calycera crassifolia var. spinulosa

Calycera crassifolia var. spinulosa, commonly known as the spiny thickleaf calycera, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems covered in small, spiny projections, which provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, making it well-suited for drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
