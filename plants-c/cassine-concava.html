Cassine concava

Cassine concava, commonly known as the narrowleaf cassine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lythraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by water.

Cassine concava plays an important role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
