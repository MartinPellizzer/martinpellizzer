Cerastium gayanum

Cerastium gayanum, commonly known as the Gaya chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Asia, including Japan and China.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes other flowering plants known for their showy blooms. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of stems. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Cerastium gayanum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
