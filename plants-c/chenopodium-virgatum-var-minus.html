Chenopodium virgatum var. minus

Chenopodium virgatum var. minus, commonly known as lesser goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green color. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is used in some traditional herbal practices.
