Caesalpinia paraensis

Caesalpinia paraensis, commonly known as the Paraensis Glorybower, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, feather-like leaflets and produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers. Its ornamental value is appreciated in horticulture due to its striking floral display and adaptability to various soil types.

Caesalpinia paraensis is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and contributing to biodiversity.
