Convolvulus camporum

Convolvulus camporum, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring and summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Convolvulus camporum is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and runners.
