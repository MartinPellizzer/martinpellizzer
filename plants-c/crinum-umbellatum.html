Crinum umbellatum

Crinum umbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy umbellate inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers. Its leaves are broad, strap-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Crinum umbellatum is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in moist, well-drained environments.
