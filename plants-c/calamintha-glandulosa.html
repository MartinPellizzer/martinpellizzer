Calamintha glandulosa

Calamintha glandulosa, commonly known as sticky germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its essential oils are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine for their calming properties.
