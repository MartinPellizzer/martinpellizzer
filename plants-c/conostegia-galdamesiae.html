Conostegia galdamesiae

Conostegia galdamesiae, commonly known as the Galdamesia cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.

However, it is considered rare and faces threats from habitat loss and over-collection.
