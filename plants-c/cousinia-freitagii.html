Cousinia freitagii

Cousinia freitagii, commonly known as the Freitag's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia freitagii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
