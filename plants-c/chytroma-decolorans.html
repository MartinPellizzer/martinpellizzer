Chytroma decolorans

Chytroma decolorans, commonly known as the fading chytrom, is a unique plant species characterized by its ability to undergo color changes in response to environmental stimuli.

It possesses a fibrous root system and exhibits a creeping growth habit, often forming dense mats on the forest floor. The plant's leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth surface and can vary in color from green to reddish-brown depending on light exposure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate forest ecosystems.

Due to its slow growth and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
