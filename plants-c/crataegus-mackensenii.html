Crataegus mackensenii

Crataegus mackensenii, commonly known as Mackensen's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other hawthorn species.
