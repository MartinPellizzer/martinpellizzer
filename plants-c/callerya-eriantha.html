Callerya eriantha

Callerya eriantha, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
