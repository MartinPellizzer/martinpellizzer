Chorispermum syriacum

Chorispermum syriacum, commonly known as the Syrian chorisperm, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is considered a valuable species for xeriscaping and ecological restoration in dry environments.
