Calamus gracilis subsp. vietnamensis

Calamus gracilis subsp.

vietnamensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese slender rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, flexible stems used for rattan products.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by feather-like leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental and commercial value, it is often cultivated for rattan crafts and as a houseplant.
