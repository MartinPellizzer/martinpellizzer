Clematis viticella var. scandens

Clematis viticella var. scandens, commonly known as the Japanese vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to East Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that can grow up to 15 feet in length, making it an excellent choice for trellises or fences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is heart-shaped and provides attractive foliage throughout the growing season.

It is relatively easy to grow and thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
