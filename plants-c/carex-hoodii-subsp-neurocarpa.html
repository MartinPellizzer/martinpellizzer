Carex hoodii subsp. neurocarpa

Carex hoodii subsp.

neurocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and often have a distinct midrib. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
