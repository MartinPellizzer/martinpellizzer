Combretum welwitschii

Combretum welwitschii, commonly known as the welwitschia, is a unique plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant has a distinctive appearance with two large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. It also produces small, inconspicuous flowers and fruiting bodies that are adapted to the harsh desert conditions.

Its ability to survive extreme drought makes it a remarkable example of botanical adaptation.
