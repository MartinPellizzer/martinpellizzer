Cyperus seslerioides

Cyperus seslerioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown. This plant is often found in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Cyperus seslerioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
