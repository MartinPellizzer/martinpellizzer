Cyathea rojasiana

Cyathea rojasiana, commonly known as the Rojas's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizome is thick and supports the growth of the trunk, while the fronds are responsible for photosynthesis.

Cyathea rojasiana plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
