Crocus lageniflorus var. candidus

Crocus lageniflorus var. candidus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

This variety is prized for its delicate appearance and is commonly cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
