Cordyline calocoma f. lentiginosa

Cordyline calocoma f. lentiginosa, commonly known as the spotted cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive purplish or brownish spots. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. Its foliage exhibits a variety of colors, ranging from deep green to reddish hues, depending on environmental conditions.

This cultivar is relatively low maintenance and suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
