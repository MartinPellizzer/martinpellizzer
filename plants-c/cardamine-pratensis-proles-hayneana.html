Cardamine pratensis proles hayneana

Cardamine pratensis proles hayneana, commonly known as the meadow water-cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as meadows, stream banks, and woodlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in nutrient-rich, well-drained soils.
