Chlorophytum warneckei

Chlorophytum warneckei, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are usually variegated with green and white or yellow markings. The plant is highly adapted to arid environments, featuring a fibrous root system and a slow growth rate. It is a popular indoor plant due to its air-purifying qualities and low maintenance requirements.

Chlorophytum warneckei is also noted for its ability to thrive in low light conditions and its resilience to neglect.
