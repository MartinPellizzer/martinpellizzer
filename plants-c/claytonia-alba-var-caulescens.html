Claytonia alba var. caulescens

Claytonia alba var. caulescens, commonly known as the white mountain sandwort, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, succulent leaves. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in early spring.

It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas with limited soil coverage.
