Chlorocyperus barteri

Chlorocyperus barteri, commonly known as the Barter's flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its flowering structures, known as spikes, produce small, greenish flowers that are often clustered at the top of the plant.

Chlorocyperus barteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
