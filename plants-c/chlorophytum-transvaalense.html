Chlorophytum transvaalense

Chlorophytum transvaalense, commonly known as the snake plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are usually green with white or yellow variegation. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers on tall, slender stems.

Chlorophytum transvaalense is popular as a houseplant due to its air-purifying properties and low maintenance requirements.
