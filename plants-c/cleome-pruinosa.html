Cleome pruinosa

Cleome pruinosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome pruinosa is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
