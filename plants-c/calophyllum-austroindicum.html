Calophyllum austroindicum

Calophyllum austroindicum, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces woody, elongated fruits that contain a single seed, and its bark is thick and flaky.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and decorative items.
