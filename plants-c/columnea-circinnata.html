Columnea circinnata

Columnea circinnata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small, intricate lanterns. The plant has velvety, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or oval in form. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Columnea circinnata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
