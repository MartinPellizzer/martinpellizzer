Clerodendrum incisum var. afzelii

Clerodendrum incisum var. afzelii, commonly known as the African balm or African flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

The species is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
