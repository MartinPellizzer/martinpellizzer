Centaurea alba subsp. albanica

Centaurea alba subsp.

albanica, commonly known as the Albanian common knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers and is part of the Asteraceae family.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves.

It is often found in regions with temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.
