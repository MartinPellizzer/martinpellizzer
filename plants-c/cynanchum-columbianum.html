Cynanchum columbianum

Cynanchum columbianum, commonly known as Columbian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually pink or purple in color.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and chemical properties.
