Chusquea gracilis

Chusquea gracilis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its growth and contributing to its structural stability.

Chusquea gracilis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
