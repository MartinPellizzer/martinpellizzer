Clitoria hanceana var. laureola

Clitoria hanceana var. laureola, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through its root nodules. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its vibrant flowers and is also used in traditional medicine.
