Conyza daveauana

Conyza daveauana, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its spread.

Conyza daveauana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
