Cyathula pedicellata

Cyathula pedicellata, commonly known as the spurgeleaf cyathula, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with alternate, ovate leaves that have a rough texture. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in cyathia, a unique structure characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Cyathula pedicellata is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural settings.
