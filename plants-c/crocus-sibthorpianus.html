Crocus sibthorpianus

Crocus sibthorpianus, commonly known as the Sibthorp's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. Its corms are used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible bulbs.

The plant is well adapted to arid conditions and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
