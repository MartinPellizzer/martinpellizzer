Cassia carthaginensis

Cassia carthaginensis, commonly known as the African cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and produces yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland or savanna habitats.

Cassia carthaginensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
