Callisia gentlei

Callisia gentlei, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale green flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy, silvery appearance that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
