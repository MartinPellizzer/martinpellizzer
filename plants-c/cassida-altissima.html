Cassida altissima

Cassida altissima, commonly known as the tall goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge. It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cassida altissima is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
