Calamagrostis buschiana var. glabrata

Calamagrostis buschiana var. glabrata, commonly known as silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and feathery panicles that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, making it suitable for wetlands and pond edges. It has a clump-forming habit and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
