Centaurea thessala

Centaurea thessala, commonly known as the Thessalian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched.

Centaurea thessala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
