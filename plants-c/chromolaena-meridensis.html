Chromolaena meridensis

Chromolaena meridensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes aromatic scent.

Chromolaena meridensis is known for its showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
