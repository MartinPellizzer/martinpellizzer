Cerastium ligusticum subsp. ligusticum

Cerastium ligusticum subsp.

ligusticum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are spoon-shaped, with a dense covering of fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or alpine environments, often found in open meadows and grasslands at high elevations.

Its flowers bloom in early summer, producing a profusion of white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
