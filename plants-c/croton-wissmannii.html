Croton wissmannii

Croton wissmannii, commonly known as the Wissmann's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with deep green, yellow, and red hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
