Chloanthes stachyodes

Chloanthes stachyodes, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often appearing in clusters. It blooms during the rainy season, and its flowers emit a faint fragrance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
