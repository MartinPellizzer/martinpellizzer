Cardamine resedifolia var. hamulosa

Cardamine resedifolia var. hamulosa, commonly known as the clustered watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and slender, branching stems. The leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive reddish or purplish hue, and the plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in racemes. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botany and horticulture.
