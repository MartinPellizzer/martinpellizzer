Cyperus iria var. typicus

Cyperus iria var. typicus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like leaves that form a compact rosette. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green or brown in color.

Cyperus iria var. typicus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
