Convolvulus deltoideus

Convolvulus deltoideus, commonly known as the deltoid morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, spherical berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in coastal regions.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
