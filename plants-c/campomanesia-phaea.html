Campomanesia phaea

Campomanesia phaea, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces round, yellow or orange fruits that are covered in a waxy coating, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet, tangy flavor, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
