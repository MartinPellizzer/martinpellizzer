Conyza thesiifolia

Conyza thesiifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to regions of Africa and the Middle East, it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces lightweight seeds with pappus structures that aid in wind dispersal.

It is considered a weed in some areas but also has potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
