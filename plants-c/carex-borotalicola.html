Carex borotalicola

Carex borotalicola, commonly known as the Borotali sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments, often in tundra or heathland habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex borotalicola plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various arctic wildlife species.
