Cucumis barbatus

Cucumis barbatus, commonly known as the hairy cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in warm, arid environments. The plant produces elongated, hairy fruits that are typically 10 to 30 centimeters long, with a distinctive ridged texture. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself.

The fruit is edible and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
