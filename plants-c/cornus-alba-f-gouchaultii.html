Cornus alba f. gouchaultii

Cornus alba f. gouchaultii, commonly known as the white silky dogwood, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features striking white bracts that resemble flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery layers, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a bluish-green upper surface and a white-woolly underside.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.
