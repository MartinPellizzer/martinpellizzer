Coelogyne gibbsiae

Coelogyne gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, producing pendulous inflorescences with brightly colored blossoms. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Coelogyne gibbsiae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
