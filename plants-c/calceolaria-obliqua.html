Calceolaria obliqua

Calceolaria obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces small, linear leaves that are arranged in a zigzag pattern along its stems, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Calceolaria obliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
