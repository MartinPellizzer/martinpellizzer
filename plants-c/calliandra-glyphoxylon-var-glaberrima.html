Calliandra glyphoxylon var. glaberrima

Calliandra glyphoxylon var. glaberrima, commonly known as the silky calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in agroforestry systems.
