Corycium vestitum

Corycium vestitum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety petals that range in color from deep purples to soft pinks. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the rainy season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment, while its leaves are broad and lance-shaped to maximize sunlight absorption.

Corycium vestitum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
