Carex vesicaria var. subalpina

Carex vesicaria var. subalpina, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
