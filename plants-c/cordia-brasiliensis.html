Cordia brasiliensis

Cordia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian white cedar, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Cordia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
