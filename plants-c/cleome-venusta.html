Cleome venusta

Cleome venusta, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an upright annual with slender, branching stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cleome venusta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
