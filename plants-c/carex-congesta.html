Carex congesta

Carex congesta, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its small, clustered flower heads are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Carex congesta plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
