Chrysopsis flexuosa

Chrysopsis flexuosa, commonly known as the curlleaf goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features narrow, curling leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a unique appearance. It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chrysopsis flexuosa thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush communities.
