Calliandra trinervia var. paniculans

Calliandra trinervia var. paniculans, commonly known as the pink panicled calliandra, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom in clusters, making it an ornamental plant popular in landscaping. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically trifoliate or trifoliate-like in structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which can improve soil fertility in agricultural settings.
