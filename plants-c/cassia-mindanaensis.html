Cassia mindanaensis

Cassia mindanaensis, commonly known as the Mindanao cassia, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland areas with well-drained soils.

Cassia mindanaensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
