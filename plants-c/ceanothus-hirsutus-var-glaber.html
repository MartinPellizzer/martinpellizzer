Ceanothus hirsutus var. glaber

Ceanothus hirsutus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth ceanothus, is a shrub native to California, characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in early spring. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, hairless texture, distinguishing it from other varieties of ceanothus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
