Cordiera claviflora

Cordiera claviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cordiera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by insects, contributing to its pollination.

Cordiera claviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
