Crocus bulbocodium

Crocus bulbocodium, commonly known as the alpine crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which are typically borne on short, leafless stems. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold, rocky environments, and the plant is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

Crocus bulbocodium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
