Cynanchum bidens

Cynanchum bidens, commonly known as the two-flowered dogbane, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are clustered in umbels and feature five lobed petals.

The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs.
