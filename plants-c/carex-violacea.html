Carex violacea

Carex violacea, commonly known as violet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, violet-colored flowers in spike-like clusters.

Carex violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
