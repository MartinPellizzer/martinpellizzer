Chaenostoma subsessile

Chaenostoma subsessile, commonly known as the slender water shield, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often floating or partially submerged. Its small, white flowers appear in late summer and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Chaenostoma subsessile plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
