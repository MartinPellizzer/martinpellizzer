Carex hotaizanensis

Carex hotaizanensis, commonly known as the Hotaizan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.

Carex hotaizanensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
