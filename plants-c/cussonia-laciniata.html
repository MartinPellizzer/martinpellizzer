Cussonia laciniata

Cussonia laciniata, commonly known as the slit-leaved arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cussonia laciniata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
