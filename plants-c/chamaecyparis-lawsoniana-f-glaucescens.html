Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. glaucescens

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. glaucescens, commonly known as the Lawson's cypress var. glaucescens, is a cultivar of the Lawson's cypress, characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage.

It is a coniferous evergreen with a conical shape and dense, needle-like leaves that give it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

This cultivar is particularly valued for its ability to retain its color throughout the year, making it a popular choice in gardens and parks.
