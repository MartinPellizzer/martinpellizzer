Curcuma pitukii

Curcuma pitukii, commonly known as the Thai turmeric, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and other aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange-yellow rhizomes, which contain bioactive compounds such as curcuminoids. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Curcuma pitukii is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses, similar to other turmeric species.
