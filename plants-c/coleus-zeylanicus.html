Coleus zeylanicus

Coleus zeylanicus, commonly known as the Java coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It typically grows as an annual or perennial, depending on the climate, and prefers well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in landscaping.
