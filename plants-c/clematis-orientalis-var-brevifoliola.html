Clematis orientalis var. brevifoliola

Clematis orientalis var. brevifoliola, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are smaller than those of the typical Clematis orientalis. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its elegant flowering display.
