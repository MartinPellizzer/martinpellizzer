Camellia trichocarpa

Camellia trichocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, camellia-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers with five petals, often accompanied by yellow stamens. It thrives in partially shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Camellia trichocarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
