Cacalia nudiflora

Cacalia nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flower bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Cacalia nudiflora is an important species for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its native environment.
