Clematicissus tweedieana

Clematicissus tweedieana, commonly known as the New Zealand honeysuckle, is a climbing vine native to New Zealand.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its berries are small, red, and edible, often consumed by birds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
