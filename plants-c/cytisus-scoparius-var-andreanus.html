Cytisus scoparius var. andreanus

Cytisus scoparius var. andreanus, commonly known as the purple flame acacia, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and bright orange to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are small, oval, and often covered in fine hairs. This variety is particularly noted for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to rocky soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
