Ciminalis

Ciminalis, commonly known as the common thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and showy pink or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Ciminalis is widely cultivated for its aromatic properties and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
