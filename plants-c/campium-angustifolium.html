Campium angustifolium

Campium angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf campion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in open, sunny habitats like meadows and rocky areas.

Campium angustifolium is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it plays a role in supporting local ecosystems.
