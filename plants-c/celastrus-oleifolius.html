Celastrus oleifolius

Celastrus oleifolius, commonly known as the oilleaf creeper, is a woody climbing shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and emit a mild fragrance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The oilleaf creeper is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
