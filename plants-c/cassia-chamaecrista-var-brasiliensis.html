Cassia chamaecrista var. brasiliensis

Cassia chamaecrista var. brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian cassia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat seed pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
