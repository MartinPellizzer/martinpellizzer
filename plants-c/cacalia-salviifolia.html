Cacalia salviifolia

Cacalia salviifolia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces striking purple to pink flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a conical shape at the top of sturdy stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
