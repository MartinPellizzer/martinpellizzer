Circaea × dubia

Circaea × dubia, commonly known as the hybrid white bead vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, smooth seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
