Condaminea elegans

Condaminea elegans, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for successful germination.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
