Callitriche aquatica subsp. obtusangula

Callitriche aquatica subsp.

obtusangula, commonly known as water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments. It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its delicate, floating or submerged leaves.

The leaves are usually oval to heart-shaped, with a rounded base, and are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This subspecies is often found in ponds, ditches, and wet meadows, where it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
