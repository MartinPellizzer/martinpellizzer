Cucubalus czerei

Cucubalus czerei, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits that are encased in a prickly husk. These fruits are edible when ripe and have a tangy, sweet flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
