Calopyxis filicifolia

Calopyxis filicifolia, commonly known as the fern-like bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and specialized bladder-like structures.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. The plant's bladders are adapted for capturing small aquatic organisms, which it uses as a nutrient source. Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, relying primarily on vegetative propagation for spread.

Overall, Calopyxis filicifolia exhibits fascinating adaptations that reflect its ecological niche in wetland habitats.
