Crateva avicularis

Crateva avicularis, commonly known as the shield tree or Indian shield tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are shield-shaped, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
