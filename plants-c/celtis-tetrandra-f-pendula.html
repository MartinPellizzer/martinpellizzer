Celtis tetrandra f. pendula

Celtis tetrandra f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Chinese hackberry, is a cultivar of the Chinese hackberry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that create a weeping appearance, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by small, round berries that mature to a dark blue or black color.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
