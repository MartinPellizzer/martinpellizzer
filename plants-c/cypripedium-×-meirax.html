Cypripedium × meirax

Cypripedium × meirax, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium pubescens.

It is characterized by its large, showy, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands. It has a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and produces a single or few flowers per stem.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
