Clausena harmandiana

Clausena harmandiana, commonly known as the Harmand's klaseria, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, yellow-orange fruits. It is often found in moist, tropical lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
