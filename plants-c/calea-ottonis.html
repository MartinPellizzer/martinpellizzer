Calea ottonis

Calea ottonis, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its unique, tubular petals that resemble a flower but are actually modified leaves. Calea ottonis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
