Corchorus olitorius var. incisifolius

Corchorus olitorius var. incisifolius, commonly known as African leaf tea or lamb's ear, is a leafy vegetable native to southern Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a mild, slightly nutty flavor. The plant grows in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are typically harvested before flowering for optimal taste and nutritional value.

It is rich in vitamins A and C, as well as minerals like iron and calcium.
