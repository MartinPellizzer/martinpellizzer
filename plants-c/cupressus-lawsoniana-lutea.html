Cupressus lawsoniana lutea

Cupressus lawsoniana lutea, commonly known as the golden cypress, is a rare and ornamental conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts strikingly with its dark, fibrous bark. The plant has a conical shape with dense, feathery branches that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil types.
