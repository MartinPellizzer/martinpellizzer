Carduus × orthocephalus

Carduus × orthocephalus, commonly known as the common plumeless thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions of Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are usually lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head with both ray and disk florets.

This species is often considered a weed due to its aggressive growth and ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions.
