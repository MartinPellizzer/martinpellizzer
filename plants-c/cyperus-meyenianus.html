Cyperus meyenianus

Cyperus meyenianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus meyenianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
