Choenomeles cathayensis

Choenomeles cathayensis, commonly known as the Japanese flowering almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both garden aesthetics and ecological purposes.
