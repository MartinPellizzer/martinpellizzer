Carex meyenii

Carex meyenii, commonly known as the Mexican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and other similar plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Carex meyenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
