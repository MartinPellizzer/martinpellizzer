Cassytha capillaris

Cassytha capillaris, commonly known as the creeping grapevine, is a tropical vine native to regions of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that often grow along the ground or climb other plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cassytha capillaris is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
