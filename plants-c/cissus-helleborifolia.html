Cissus helleborifolia

Cissus helleborifolia, commonly known as the silver arrow or heartleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.

Cissus helleborifolia is also valued for its ability to climb and its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
