Caesalpinia barahonensis

Caesalpinia barahonensis, commonly known as the Bahamian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange floral spikes that appear during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its pods are elongated, woody, and contain seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.
