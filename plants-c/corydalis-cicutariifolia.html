Corydalis cicutariifolia

Corydalis cicutariifolia, commonly known as the creeping lousewort, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple, with two upper petals that are spurred. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive reddish or purple hue.

Corydalis cicutariifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
