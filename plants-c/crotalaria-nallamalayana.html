Crotalaria nallamalayana

Crotalaria nallamalayana, commonly known as the Nallamalayana cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and yellow or orange flowers.

Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
