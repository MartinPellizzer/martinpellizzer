Cordyline grandis

Cordyline grandis, commonly known as the mountain cabbage tree, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features a tall, unbranched trunk with a fibrous, spongy bark and large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to coastal conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries.

Cordyline grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of New Zealand's natural heritage.
