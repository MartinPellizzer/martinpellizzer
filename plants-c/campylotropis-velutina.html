Campylotropis velutina

Campylotropis velutina, commonly known as the velvet false indigo, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy blue to purple pea-like flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a velvety texture, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Campylotropis velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
