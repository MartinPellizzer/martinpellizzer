Cassia patellaria var. glabrata

Cassia patellaria var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes. It produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, spiral-shaped seeds.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, which set it apart from other cassia species.
