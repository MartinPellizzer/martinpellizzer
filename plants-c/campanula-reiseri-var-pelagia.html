Campanula reiseri var. pelagia

Campanula reiseri var. pelagia, commonly known as the Pelagia bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
