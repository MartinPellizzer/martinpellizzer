Castela lychnophoroides

Castela lychnophoroides, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
