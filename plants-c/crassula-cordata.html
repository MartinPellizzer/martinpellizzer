Crassula cordata

Crassula cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically heart-shaped, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves to survive drought conditions. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months.

Crassula cordata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
