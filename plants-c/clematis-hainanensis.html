Clematis hainanensis

Clematis hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan Clematis, is a flowering plant native to China, specifically the Hainan Island.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, typically white or pale blue flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
