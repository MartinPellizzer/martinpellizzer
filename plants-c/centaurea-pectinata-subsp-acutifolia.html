Centaurea pectinata subsp. acutifolia

Centaurea pectinata subsp.

acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved knapweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with spiny bracts.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in composite flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, linear leaves. Its leaves are typically narrow and pointed, giving it the subspecies name *acutifolia*.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
