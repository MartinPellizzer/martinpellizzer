Cirrhopetalum lendyanum

Cirrhopetalum lendyanum, commonly known as the white-flowered rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and its striking, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several showy, spurred flowers.

Cirrhopetalum lendyanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
