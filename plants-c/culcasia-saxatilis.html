Culcasia saxatilis

Culcasia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock balsam, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
