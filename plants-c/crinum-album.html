Crinum album

Crinum album, commonly known as the white lily of the Nile, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white or pale pink flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and often have a waxy texture.

Crinum album is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
