Crotalaria genistoides

Crotalaria genistoides, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its seed pods are distinctive, containing several small, round seeds.

Crotalaria genistoides is also known for its role in ecological systems, as it can suppress the growth of certain grasses through its allelopathic properties.
