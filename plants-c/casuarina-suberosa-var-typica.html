Casuarina suberosa var. typica

Casuarina suberosa var. typica, commonly known as the silver she-oak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, scaly bark and needle-like leaves that are arranged in spiral patterns along the stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
