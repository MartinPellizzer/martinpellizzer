Carex divisa f. pallidiflora

Carex divisa f. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered divided sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that bears numerous tiny flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.
