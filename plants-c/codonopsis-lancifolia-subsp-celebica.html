Codonopsis lancifolia subsp. celebica

Codonopsis lancifolia subsp.

celebica, commonly known as the Celebes codonopsis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
