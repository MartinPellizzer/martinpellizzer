Chaetochloa effusa

Chaetochloa effusa, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of hairs, giving it a silky appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Chaetochloa effusa is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
