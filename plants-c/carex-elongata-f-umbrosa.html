Carex elongata f. umbrosa

Carex elongata f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shaded sedges, is a variant of the slender sedge.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers. This plant thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in woodlands or along stream banks. Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it spreads through rhizomes.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
