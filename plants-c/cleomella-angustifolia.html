Cleomella angustifolia

Cleomella angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Cleomella angustifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ease of care.
