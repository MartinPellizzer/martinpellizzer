Cordyline hookeri

Cordyline hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's cabbage tree, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and rosette of broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters at the top of the stem, which are followed by bright blue or purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
