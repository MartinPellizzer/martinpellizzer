Cryptodiscus

Cryptodiscus, commonly known as the moonflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

These plants are characterized by their nocturnal blooming habit, with large, showy flowers that open at night. The leaves are typically opposite and ovate, while the stems are often hairy and woody. Cryptodiscus species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

They are valued for their ornamental beauty and have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
