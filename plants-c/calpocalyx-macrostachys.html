Calpocalyx macrostachys

Calpocalyx macrostachys, commonly known as the giant siphonostemon, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that can reach several feet in length, composed of numerous tubular flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat. Its flowers are typically pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

Calpocalyx macrostachys is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
