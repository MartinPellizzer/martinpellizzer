Chlorophytum hispidulum

Chlorophytum hispidulum, commonly known as the shaggy spider plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its long, arching leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that emerge from the ends of its leaves. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Chlorophytum hispidulum is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
