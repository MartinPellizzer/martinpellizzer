Carduus edulis

Carduus edulis, commonly known as the edible thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a sturdy stem covered in coarse hairs. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a serrated edge, often covered in prickly hairs.

The flowers are usually purple or pink, forming a dense cluster at the top of the plant.
