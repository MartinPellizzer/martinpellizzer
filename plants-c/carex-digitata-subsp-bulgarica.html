Carex digitata subsp. bulgarica

Carex digitata subsp.

bulgarica, commonly known as the Bulgarian finger sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wet meadows. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that mature into brown, seed-bearing structures.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in maintaining wetland biodiversity.
