Coryanthes velizii

Coryanthes velizii, commonly known as the Veliz's hooded orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its large, intricate flowers that resemble a hood, with a unique structure that includes a labellum and a column. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the rainforest, often attached to trees or rocks. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of flies, which are attracted by the scent and color of the blooms.

Coryanthes velizii is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
