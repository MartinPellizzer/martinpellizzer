Cryptomeria generalis

Cryptomeria generalis, commonly known as the Japanese cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and distinctive, scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Cryptomeria generalis is valued for its durable wood and is often planted as an ornamental tree.
