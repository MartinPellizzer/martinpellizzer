Clematis manipurensis var. lasioclada

Clematis manipurensis var. lasioclada, commonly known as the hairy clematis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, woody stems with a distinctive hairy texture along the stems and leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and fragrance.
