Cardamomum pterocarpum

Cardamomum pterocarpum, commonly known as the Java cardamom or wild cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae).

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity. The plant produces aromatic, dark brown pods containing aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while its stems are rhizomatous and often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
