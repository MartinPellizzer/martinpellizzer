Chrysopsis hispida

Chrysopsis hispida, commonly known as the rough chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chrysopsis hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
