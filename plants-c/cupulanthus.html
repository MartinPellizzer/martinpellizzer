Cupulanthus

Cupulanthus, commonly known as the bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers, which can range in color from blue to white. These plants typically grow in temperate regions and are often found in meadows and woodlands. Cupulanthus species are perennial herbs that produce showy blooms in the summer months.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
