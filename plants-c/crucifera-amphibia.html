Crucifera amphibia

Crucifera amphibia, commonly known as the watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are typically submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, followed by pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in streams, ponds, and marshes.

Due to its high nutritional value, it is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
