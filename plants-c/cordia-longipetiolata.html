Cordia longipetiolata

Cordia longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glabrous and have a prominent petiole. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its bark is thick and corky, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
