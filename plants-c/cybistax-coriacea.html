Cybistax coriacea

Cybistax coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
