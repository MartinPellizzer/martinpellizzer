Cassia aspera

Cassia aspera, commonly known as the rough cassia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia aspera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
