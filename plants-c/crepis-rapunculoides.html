Crepis rapunculoides

Crepis rapunculoides, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 cm. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
