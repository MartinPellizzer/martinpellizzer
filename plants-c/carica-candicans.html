Carica candicans

Carica candicans, commonly known as the Natal apple or Natal papaya, is a tropical plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and bright yellow-orange fruit. The fruit is typically harvested when it is fully ripe, and it has a sweet, musky flavor with a soft, creamy texture. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
