Crucifera resedifolia

Crucifera resedifolia, commonly known as the stinking vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a pod that contains several small, round seeds.

Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
