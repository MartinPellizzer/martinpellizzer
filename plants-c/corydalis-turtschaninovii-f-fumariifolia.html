Corydalis turtschaninovii f. fumariifolia

Corydalis turtschaninovii f. fumariifolia, commonly known as the fumariifolia Corydalis, is a rare and ornamental member of the Fumariaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and clusters of small, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
