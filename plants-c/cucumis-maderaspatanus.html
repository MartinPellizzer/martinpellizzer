Cucumis maderaspatanus

Cucumis maderaspatanus, commonly known as the Malabar cucumber, is a flowering plant species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when green and unripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This species is valued for its culinary use and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
