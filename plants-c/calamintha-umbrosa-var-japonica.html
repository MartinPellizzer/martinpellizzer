Calamintha umbrosa var. japonica

Calamintha umbrosa var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese catnip, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb, similar to its relatives in the mint family.
