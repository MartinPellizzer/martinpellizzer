Chrysophyllum marginatum

Chrysophyllum marginatum, commonly known as the margarita apple or yellow star apple, is a tropical plant belonging to the Sapotaceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into round, yellow or orange fruits with a smooth, waxy skin. The fruit contains a sweet, white, aromatic pulp surrounded by a single large seed.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of South America, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
