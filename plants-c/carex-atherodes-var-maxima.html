Carex atherodes var. maxima

Carex atherodes var. maxima, commonly known as the giant marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular plant in wetland restoration projects.
