Circaea minima

Circaea minima, commonly known as the lesser whitebeard, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, hairy stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Circaea minima is often found in moist soils and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded, woodland habitats.
