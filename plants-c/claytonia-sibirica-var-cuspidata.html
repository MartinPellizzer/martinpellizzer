Claytonia sibirica var. cuspidata

Claytonia sibirica var. cuspidata, commonly known as the Siberian spring beauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of spoon-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or silver hair. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
