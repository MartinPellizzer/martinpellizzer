Cupressus obtusa subsp. yokohamaensis

Cupressus obtusa subsp.

yokohamaensis, commonly known as the Yokohama cypress, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to Japan. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its scale-like leaves and compact, pyramidal growth habit.

The foliage is typically a deep green, and the cones are small and woody. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
