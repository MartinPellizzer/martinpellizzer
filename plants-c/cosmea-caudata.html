Cosmea caudata

Cosmea caudata, commonly known as the trailing cosmea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Cosmea caudata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
