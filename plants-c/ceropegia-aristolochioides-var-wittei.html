Ceropegia aristolochioides var. wittei

Ceropegia aristolochioides var. wittei, commonly known as the Witte's Hooded Ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its unique, hooded flowers that resemble a small helmet. The plant has slender, trailing stems and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often hidden within its foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This variety is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
