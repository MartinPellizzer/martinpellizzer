Cassine trachyclada

Cassine trachyclada, commonly known as the smoothleaf cassine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Cassine trachyclada is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in wetland landscapes.
