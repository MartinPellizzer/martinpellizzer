Cyperus glaber f. fasciculariformis

Cyperus glaber f. fasciculariformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and distinctive, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or aquatic environments, thriving in moist soils. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into three-angled capsules.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
