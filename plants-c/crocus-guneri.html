Crocus guneri

Crocus guneri, commonly known as the Turkish crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Balkans.

It produces striking violet to blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Crocus guneri is also a member of the Iridaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members of the genus Crocus.
