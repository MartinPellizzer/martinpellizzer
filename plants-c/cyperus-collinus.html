Cyperus collinus

Cyperus collinus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically green or brown. The plant is often found in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Cyperus collinus has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in moist or waterlogged soils.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
