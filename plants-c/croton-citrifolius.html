Croton citrifolius

Croton citrifolius, commonly known as the lemon-leaf croton, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically green with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.
