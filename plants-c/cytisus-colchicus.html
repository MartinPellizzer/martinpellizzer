Cytisus colchicus

Cytisus colchicus, commonly known as the Greek broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, woody stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are small, pinnate, and have a leathery texture.

Cytisus colchicus is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.
