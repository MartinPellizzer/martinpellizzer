Campanula cashmeriana var. evolvulacea

Campanula cashmeriana var. evolvulacea, commonly known as the Kashmir bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
