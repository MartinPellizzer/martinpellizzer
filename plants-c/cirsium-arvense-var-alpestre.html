Cirsium arvense var. alpestre

Cirsium arvense var. alpestre, commonly known as alpine thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces and prominent veins.

It plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
