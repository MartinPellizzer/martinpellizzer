Crotalaria caespitosa

Crotalaria caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb that forms dense, clump-like growths, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are palmately divided with three to five leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
