Ceratostylis ciliolata

Ceratostylis ciliolata, commonly known as the hairy tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and covered with fine, hair-like structures, giving it its common name.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is appreciated for its delicate appearance and ease of cultivation.
