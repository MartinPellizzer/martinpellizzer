Cassia splendida var. striata

Cassia splendida var. striata, commonly known as the flame tree or golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow or orange inflorescences that appear in late spring or early summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are often adorned with pale or white veins. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
