Cassia bracteolata

Cassia bracteolata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its pinnate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Cassia bracteolata is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
