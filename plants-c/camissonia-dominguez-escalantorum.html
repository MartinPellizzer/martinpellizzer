Camissonia dominguez-escalantorum

Camissonia dominguez-escalantorum, commonly known as the Dominguez Escalantorum camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Camissoniaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to the arid environments it inhabits.

It is often found in rocky soils and is considered a rare species due to its limited geographic range.
