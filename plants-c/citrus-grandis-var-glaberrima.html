Citrus grandis var. glaberrima

Citrus grandis var. glaberrima, commonly known as the hairy loquat, is a variant of the loquat tree native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other citrus varieties. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The fruit has a sweet and tangy flavor, making it popular in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
