Carex bullockiana

Carex bullockiana, commonly known as Bullock's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical spikelets that bear small, three-lobed seeds. It is often found in wetland ecosystems where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

Carex bullockiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland restoration projects.
