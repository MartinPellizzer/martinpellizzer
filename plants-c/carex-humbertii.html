Carex humbertii

Carex humbertii, commonly known as Humbert's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of Europe and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Carex humbertii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
