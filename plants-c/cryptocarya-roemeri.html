Cryptocarya roemeri

Cryptocarya roemeri, commonly known as the Sierra lemon tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a spherical, woody drupe that turns red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, mountainous climates of its native range.
