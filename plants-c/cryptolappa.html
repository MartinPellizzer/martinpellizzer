Cryptolappa

Cryptolappa, commonly known as the false nightshade, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It comprises several species, most of which are shrubs or small trees native to southern Africa. The plants typically have hairy stems and alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves. Their flowers are usually blue or purple, with five petals and a distinctive calyx.

The fruits are fleshy berries that contain toxic compounds, making them unsuitable for human consumption.
