Celosia dichotoma

Celosia dichotoma, commonly known as the firecracker plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky inflorescences that resemble fireworks. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers, which come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, are borne on branched stems and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Celosia dichotoma thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical landscapes.
