Clerodendrum umbellatum var. gossweileri

Clerodendrum umbellatum var. gossweileri, commonly known as the Gossweiler's flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of red or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
