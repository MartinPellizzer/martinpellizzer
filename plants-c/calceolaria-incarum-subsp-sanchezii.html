Calceolaria incarum subsp. sanchezii

Calceolaria incarum subsp.

sanchezii, commonly known as the Sanche's slipper orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile. It is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms that are adapted for specific pollinators, such as certain species of bees. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique floral morphology.
