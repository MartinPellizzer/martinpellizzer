Cereus pentlandii var. floribundus-sanguineus

Cereus pentlandii var. floribundus-sanguineus, commonly known as the blood red torch cactus, is a striking epiphytic cactus native to South America.

It features a cylindrical stem with prominent, sharp spines and is known for its vibrant red flowers that bloom at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-draining soil with minimal water. Its nocturnal blooming habit is a characteristic feature of many cacti in the Cactaceae family.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
