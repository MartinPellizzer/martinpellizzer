Canarium unifoliolatum

Canarium unifoliolatum, commonly known as the single leaf candlenut, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, consisting of single, leathery leaves that are alternate and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by the development of woody, oblong fruits containing a single seed. The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local cultures.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
