Croton aleuritoides

Croton aleuritoides, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with bright yellow to orange veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton aleuritoides is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
