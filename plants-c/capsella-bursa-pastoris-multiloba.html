Capsella bursa-pastoris multiloba

Capsella bursa-pastoris multiloba, commonly known as the common chickweed, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with hairy stems and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive heart-shaped, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Brassicaceae, also known as the mustard family.
