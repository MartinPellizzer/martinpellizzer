Crotalaria paradoxa

Crotalaria paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox flower, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its distinctive yellow and purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces elongated seed pods that split open when mature. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

Crotalaria paradoxa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
