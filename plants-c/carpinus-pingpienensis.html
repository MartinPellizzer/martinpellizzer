Carpinus pingpienensis

Carpinus pingpienensis, commonly known as the Pingpien hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
