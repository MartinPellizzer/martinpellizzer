Chamaedryfolia procridifolia

Chamaedryfolia procridifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in coastal areas. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The leaves are often covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.
