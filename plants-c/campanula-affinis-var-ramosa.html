Campanula affinis var. ramosa

Campanula affinis var. ramosa, commonly known as the branched bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that often branch profusely, giving it a bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Campanula affinis var. ramosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
