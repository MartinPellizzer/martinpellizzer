Cochlearia saxatilis

Cochlearia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock scabious, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles and parts of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented fruits. This plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained, calcareous soils. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue or purple in color.

Cochlearia saxatilis is an important plant for pollinators and is often found in limestone grasslands and cliffs.
