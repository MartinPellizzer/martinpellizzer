Cirsium coloradense subsp. acaulescens

Cirsium coloradense subsp.

acaulescens, commonly known as the Colorado thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a woolly texture.

The subspecies acaulescens is distinguished by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to other members of the Cirsium genus.
