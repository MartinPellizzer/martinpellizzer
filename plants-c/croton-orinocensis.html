Croton orinocensis

Croton orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco croton, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with prominent veins.
