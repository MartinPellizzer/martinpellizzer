Campanula versicolor var. multiflora

Campanula versicolor var. multiflora, commonly known as the multibract bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers and relatively low maintenance requirements.
