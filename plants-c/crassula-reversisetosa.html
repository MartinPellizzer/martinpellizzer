Crassula reversisetosa

Crassula reversisetosa, commonly known as the Reverse-hair Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
