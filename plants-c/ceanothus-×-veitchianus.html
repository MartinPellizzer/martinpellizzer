Ceanothus × veitchianus

Ceanothus × veitchianus, commonly known as the Veitch's ceanothus, is a hybrid shrub native to California, United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has a spreading, rounded form. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Ceanothus × veitchianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
