Cyphostemma marionae

Cyphostemma marionae, commonly known as Marion's moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often ribbed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along rocky outcrops or in dry woodlands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
