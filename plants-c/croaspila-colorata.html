Croaspila colorata

Croaspila colorata, commonly known as the rainbow orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored petals that range from deep reds to bright yellows and blues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
