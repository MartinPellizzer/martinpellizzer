Carex hakonensis

Carex hakonensis, commonly known as Hakon's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

Its botanical structure is adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
