Campanula sparsa subsp. frivaldskyi

Campanula sparsa subsp.

frivaldskyi, commonly known as the Frivaldsky's bellflower, is a rare and ornamental flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showing a preference for well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down.
