Carex microdonta var. latifolia

Carex microdonta var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its ornamental appearance.
