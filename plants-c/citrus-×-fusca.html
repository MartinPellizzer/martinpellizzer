Citrus × fusca

Citrus × fusca, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a hybrid citrus plant native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a thick, yellowish skin. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is highly acidic and is often used in culinary applications or for making marmalade.

Due to its historical significance in citrus cultivation, it plays an important role in the genetic diversity of citrus species.
