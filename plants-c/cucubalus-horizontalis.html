Cucubalus horizontalis

Cucubalus horizontalis, commonly known as the horizontal cucumber, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbit plants like cucumbers and squash. The plant features trailing stems with heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its fruits are typically harvested when green and can be used in various culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
