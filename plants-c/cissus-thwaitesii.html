Cissus thwaitesii

Cissus thwaitesii, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
