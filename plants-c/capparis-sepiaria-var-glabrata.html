Capparis sepiaria var. glabrata

Capparis sepiaria var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, fleshy leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible flower buds.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens in temperate climates.
