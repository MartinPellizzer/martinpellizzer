Crocus cancellatus var. cilicius

Crocus cancellatus var. cilicius, commonly known as the Cilician crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white stripe running down the center of each petal. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after addition to rock gardens and alpine plantings.
