Cyanus thirkei

Cyanus thirkei, commonly known as the Tussock Coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a tall, hairy stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Cyanus thirkei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
