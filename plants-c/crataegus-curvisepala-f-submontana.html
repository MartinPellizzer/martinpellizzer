Crataegus curvisepala f. submontana

Crataegus curvisepala f. submontana, commonly known as the alpine hawthorn, is a subspecies of the hawthorn plant native to mountainous regions.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, thorny branches. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a pale underside. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, red berries in the fall.

This plant is well adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
