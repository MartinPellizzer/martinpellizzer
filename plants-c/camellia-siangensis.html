Camellia siangensis

Camellia siangensis, commonly known as the Assam tea plant, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and produces camellia sinensis, which is the primary source of tea leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its flowers are usually red or pink and bloom in the spring.

Camellia siangensis is widely cultivated for its economic value in the tea industry.
