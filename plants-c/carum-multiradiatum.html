Carum multiradiatum

Carum multiradiatum, commonly known as the multiradiate caraway, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is native to parts of Asia. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and digestive properties.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
