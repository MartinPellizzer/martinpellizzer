Centaurea paulini

Centaurea paulini, commonly known as the Paulini's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny leaves, which are typical of the genus Centaurea. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, radial flowers surrounded by bracts.

Centaurea paulini is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and hardy nature.
