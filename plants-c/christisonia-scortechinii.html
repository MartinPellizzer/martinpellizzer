Christisonia scortechinii

Christisonia scortechinii, commonly known as the Scortechini's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
