Crucifera albida

Crucifera albida, commonly known as the white crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white, cruciform flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its seeds are encased in a hard, triangular pod, and it is known for its rapid growth cycle.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in supporting pollinators and soil health.
