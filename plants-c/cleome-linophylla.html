Cleome linophylla

Cleome linophylla, commonly known as the silky cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Cleome linophylla is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
