Campanula longisepala

Campanula longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Campanula longisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
