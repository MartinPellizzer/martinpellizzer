Cerastium tournefortii

Cerastium tournefortii, commonly known as the snow in the grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
