Cabralea macrantha

Cabralea macrantha, commonly known as the Brazilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a dark green color. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons.

Cabralea macrantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
