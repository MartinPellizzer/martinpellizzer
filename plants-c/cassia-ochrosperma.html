Cassia ochrosperma

Cassia ochrosperma, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring compound leaves with 12 to 20 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in spring and are followed by pod-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

Cassia ochrosperma plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
