Cynorkis sororia

Cynorkis sororia, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to certain regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, violet-colored flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique floral structure and coloration, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in botanical gardens.
