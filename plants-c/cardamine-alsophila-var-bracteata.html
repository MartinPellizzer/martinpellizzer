Cardamine alsophila var. bracteata

Cardamine alsophila var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteated watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in freshwater environments. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. Its leaves are usually oval to lance-shaped with a slightly toothed margin.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as stream banks and wet meadows.
