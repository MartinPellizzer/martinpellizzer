Cyrtorchis glaucifolia

Cyrtorchis glaucifolia, commonly known as the blue-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Cyrtorchis glaucifolia thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
