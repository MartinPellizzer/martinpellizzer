Carapichea crebrinervia

Carapichea crebrinervia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves with prominent veins and a distinctive, fragrant, yellowish-green flower cluster. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
