Cyperus egregius

Cyperus egregius, commonly known as the golden cup sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

Cyperus egregius is also valued for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
