Clematoclethra scandens subsp. scandens

Clematoclethra scandens subsp.

scandens, commonly known as the climbing clematoclethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that allow it to climb trees and other structures.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring, followed by elliptical, dark green leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide vertical interest in gardens.
