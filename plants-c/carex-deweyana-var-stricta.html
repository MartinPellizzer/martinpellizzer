Carex deweyana var. stricta

Carex deweyana var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds and streams.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
