Callista nidiflora

Callista nidiflora, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant is notable for its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in clusters. These flowers bloom during the winter months, making it a distinctive feature of the local flora.

Its adaptability to coastal conditions and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
