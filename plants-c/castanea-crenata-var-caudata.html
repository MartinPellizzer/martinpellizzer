Castanea crenata var. caudata

Castanea crenata var. caudata, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut or caudate chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its serrated, oval leaves and produces spiny, burr-like fruits that enclose one to three edible nuts. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible nuts.

The species is also valued in traditional horticulture for its hardy nature and adaptability.
