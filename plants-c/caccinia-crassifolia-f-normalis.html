Caccinia crassifolia f. normalis

Caccinia crassifolia f. normalis, commonly known as the broad-leaved caccinia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
