Carex indistincta

Carex indistincta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often brown or reddish in color.

Carex indistincta is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
