Capsella bursa-pastoris scoliocaspica

Capsella bursa-pastoris scoliocaspica, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herbaceous plant characterized by its prostrate or ascending habit.

It features small, white or pink flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a hairy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is widely distributed across temperate regions.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it a common weed in agricultural and urban settings.
