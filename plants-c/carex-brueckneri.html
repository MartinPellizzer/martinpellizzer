Carex brueckneri

Carex brueckneri, commonly known as the Brueckner's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

Carex brueckneri is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
