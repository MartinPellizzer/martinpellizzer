Colchicum autumnale f. transsilvanicum

Colchicum autumnale f. transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian autumn crocus, is a botanical variant of the meadow saffron.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant produces large, showy flowers with three prominent petals and three sepals, typical of the Colchicaceae family. Its bulbs are toxic and contain colchicine, a compound used in medicine.

This subspecies is native to Central and Eastern Europe, including parts of Romania and Hungary.
