Cotoneaster ludlowii

Cotoneaster ludlowii, commonly known as Ludlow's cotoneaster, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, fleshy berries in the autumn. Cotoneaster ludlowii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and partial shade.
