Callerya nitida var. hirsutissima

Callerya nitida var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy callerya, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
