Cymbopogon humboldtii

Cymbopogon humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its aromatic properties make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
