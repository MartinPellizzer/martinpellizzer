Camptosema pinnatum

Camptosema pinnatum, commonly known as the featherleaf camptosema, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of its native habitat.
