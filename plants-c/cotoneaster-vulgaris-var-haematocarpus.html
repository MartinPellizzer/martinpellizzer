Cotoneaster vulgaris var. haematocarpus

Cotoneaster vulgaris var. haematocarpus, commonly known as the blood-red cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking red berries that persist through winter. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by the showy red fruits. Its foliage is typically dark green, with a glossy appearance, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

This variety is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it suitable for a range of garden environments.
