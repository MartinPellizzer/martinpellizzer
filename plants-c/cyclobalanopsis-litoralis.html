Cyclobalanopsis litoralis

Cyclobalanopsis litoralis, commonly known as the coastal evergreen oak, is a species of evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in mangrove or coastal forest ecosystems. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to saline and wind-prone environments.
