Croton longipedicellatus subsp. austrotanzanicus

Croton longipedicellatus subsp.

austrotanzanicus, commonly known as the Tanzanian croton, is a shrub native to eastern Africa. It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly pubescent surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it an important species in its native range.
