Cheiroglossa palmata var. malgassica

Cheiroglossa palmata var. malgassica, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a distinctive, bottle-shaped stem covered in spines and a crown of soft, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
