Callista anosma

Callista anosma, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in a smooth, leathery pod, and the plant is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.

Callista anosma is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for its aesthetic appeal and potential medicinal uses.
