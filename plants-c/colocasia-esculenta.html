Colocasia esculenta

Colocasia esculenta, commonly known as taro, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a showy, leaf-like bract. Its underground corms are the primary edible part, rich in starch and used in various cuisines worldwide.

Taro thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated in wetland areas or flooded fields.
