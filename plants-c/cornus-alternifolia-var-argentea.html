Cornus alternifolia var. argentea

Cornus alternifolia var. argentea, commonly known as the white-flowered dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking white bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, which appear in early summer. The leaves are typically oval and have a smooth texture, turning red or orange in the fall. This variety is notable for its ornamental value, particularly its showy white inflorescences.

It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
