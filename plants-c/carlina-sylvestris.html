Carlina sylvestris

Carlina sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of yellow disk florets surrounded by white ray florets.

Carlina sylvestris is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
