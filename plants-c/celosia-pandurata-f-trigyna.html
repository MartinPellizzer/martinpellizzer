Celosia pandurata f. trigyna

Celosia pandurata f. trigyna, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a striking ornamental plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like inflorescences that range from red to orange. The plant is a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
