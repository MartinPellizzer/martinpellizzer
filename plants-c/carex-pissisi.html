Carex pissisi

Carex pissisi, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and stream sides. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, greenish-brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex pissisi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
