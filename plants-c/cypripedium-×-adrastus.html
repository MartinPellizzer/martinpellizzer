Cypripedium × adrastus

Cypripedium × adrastus, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or white flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest habitats and has a long, slender stem with a single or few leaves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
