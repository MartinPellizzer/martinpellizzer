Calea caracasana

Calea caracasana, commonly known as the sacred plant or angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-colored flowers. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and cultural significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
