Cycladenia humilis

Cycladenia humilis, commonly known as the small-flowered cyclamen, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Cycladenia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in woodland gardens.
