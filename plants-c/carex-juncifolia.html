Carex juncifolia

Carex juncifolia, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex juncifolia is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
