Cuphea utriculosa f. communis

Cuphea utriculosa f. communis, commonly known as the common seed box, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods that resemble small capsules, which are a key feature in its botanical classification. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for garden cultivation.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value contribute to its popularity in horticultural settings.
