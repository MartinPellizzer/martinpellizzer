Calliandra purpusii

Calliandra purpusii, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink or red bract-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, cylindrical flower clusters. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.

Calliandra purpusii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
