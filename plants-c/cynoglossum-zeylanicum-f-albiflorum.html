Cynoglossum zeylanicum f. albiflorum

Cynoglossum zeylanicum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a cultivar of the species Cynoglossum zeylanicum, which is native to southern Asia.

This plant is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white, contrasting with the typical blue or purple hues of the species. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and velvety in texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The plant is often valued for its aesthetic qualities and is used in ornamental horticulture.
