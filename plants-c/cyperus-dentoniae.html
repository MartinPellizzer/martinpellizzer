Cyperus dentoniae

Cyperus dentoniae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus dentoniae is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small aquatic organisms.
