Crotalaria mossambicensis

Crotalaria mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an erect herb with compound leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Crotalaria mossambicensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
