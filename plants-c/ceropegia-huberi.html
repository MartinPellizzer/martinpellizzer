Ceropegia huberi

Ceropegia huberi, commonly known as the silver leaf string plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features cascading stems with small, waxy leaves that are typically silver or gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are often pink or white, attracting specific pollinators like moths. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Ceropegia huberi is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care in indoor settings.
