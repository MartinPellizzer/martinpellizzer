Coelorachis clarkei

Coelorachis clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and dense, branching structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to its structural strength, making it suitable for use in ornamental landscaping.

Coelorachis clarkei is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and resilience in various environmental conditions.
