Casimiroa sapota

Casimiroa sapota, commonly known as the chikoo or sapodilla, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring thick, rough bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, spiny fruits with a sweet, custard-like pulp. Its fruit is rich in nutrients, including sugars, vitamins, and minerals, making it a valuable food source.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
