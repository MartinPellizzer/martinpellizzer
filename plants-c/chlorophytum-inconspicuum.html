Chlorophytum inconspicuum

Chlorophytum inconspicuum, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with a distinct white midrib. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it an ideal indoor plant. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender stalks, which are not particularly showy.

Chlorophytum inconspicuum is also noted for its air-purifying properties, contributing to its popularity among houseplant enthusiasts.
