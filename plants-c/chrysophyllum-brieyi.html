Chrysophyllum brieyi

Chrysophyllum brieyi, commonly known as the African cherry or white cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits with a sweet, acidic flavor. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and timber.
