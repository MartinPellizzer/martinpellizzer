Cardiochlaena trilobata

Cardiochlaena trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed cardinal flower, is a striking perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role in native habitats.
