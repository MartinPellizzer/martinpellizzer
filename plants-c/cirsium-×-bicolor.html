Cirsium × bicolor

Cirsium × bicolor, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flower heads, which consist of both ray florets and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped, gray-green leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium × bicolor is native to North America and is often found in meadows, prairies, and open woodlands.
