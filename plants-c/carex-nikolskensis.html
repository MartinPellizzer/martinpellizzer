Carex nikolskensis

Carex nikolskensis, commonly known as Nikolskii's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.

Carex nikolskensis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various arctic flora and fauna.
