Chlorophytum paludicola

Chlorophytum paludicola, commonly known as the marsh spider plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
