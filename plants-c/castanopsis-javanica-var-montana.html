Castanopsis javanica var. montana

Castanopsis javanica var. montana, commonly known as the mountain chestnut.

It is a medium to large evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in panicles, and it produces edible, woody nuts enclosed in a spiny cupule.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
