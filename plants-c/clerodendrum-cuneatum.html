Clerodendrum cuneatum

Clerodendrum cuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule containing small, brown seeds.

Clerodendrum cuneatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
