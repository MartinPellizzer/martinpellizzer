Carex athrostachya

Carex athrostachya, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Carex athrostachya produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually clustered at the tips of the stems.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
