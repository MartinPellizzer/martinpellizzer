Crocus danubensis

Crocus danubensis, commonly known as the Danube crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, typically flowering in late winter or early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers with three petals and three sepals. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to cream in color, and it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

Crocus danubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also considered an indicator species for certain habitats.
