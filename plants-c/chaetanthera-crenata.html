Chaetanthera crenata

Chaetanthera crenata, commonly known as the creeping chaetanthera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
