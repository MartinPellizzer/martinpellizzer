Chassalia curviflora var. longifolia

Chassalia curviflora var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered chassalia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces elongated, spiky inflorescences with small, tubular flowers. It typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.

The variety longifolia is distinguished by its longer floral bracts compared to the typical form.
