Crepidiastrum afghanicum

Crepidiastrum afghanicum, commonly known as the Afghan starflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale yellow, arranged in a star-like pattern.

Crepidiastrum afghanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
