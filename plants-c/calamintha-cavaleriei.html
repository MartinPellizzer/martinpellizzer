Calamintha cavaleriei

Calamintha cavaleriei, commonly known as Chinese citron mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their cooling and digestive properties.

Calamintha cavaleriei thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and herbal collections.
