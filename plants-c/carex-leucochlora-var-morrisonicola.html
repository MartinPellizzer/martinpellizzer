Carex leucochlora var. morrisonicola

Carex leucochlora var. morrisonicola, commonly known as the Morrison's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to reddish brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is often found in shaded environments and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
