Carex × limnogena

Carex × limnogena, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Carex acutata and Carex lyngbyei, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and fens, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its grass-like foliage consists of narrow, grass-green leaves arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Carex × limnogena plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
