Connarus perrottetii

Connarus perrottetii, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a medium to large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard, and heavy wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Connarus perrottetii is often found in dry to moist forest habitats and is an important component of the local ecosystem.
