Crataegus porteri

Crataegus porteri, commonly known as the Mexican hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal flowers.
