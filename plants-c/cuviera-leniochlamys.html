Cuviera leniochlamys

Cuviera leniochlamys, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cuviera leniochlamys is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
