Carex nuda

Carex nuda, commonly known as slender sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and swamps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex nuda plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
