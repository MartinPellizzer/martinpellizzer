Calamagrostis aspera

Calamagrostis aspera, commonly known as rough bluejoint, is a tall perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green to purplish in color. It thrives in wet or moist soils, making it a common sight in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis aspera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
