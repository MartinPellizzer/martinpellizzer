Cuphea micropetala var. hirtella

Cuphea micropetala var. hirtella, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a compact, bushy shrub native to Mexico.

It features vibrant red to orange bract-like flowers that are actually modified leaves, giving it a unique appearance. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often mistaken for flowers, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its showy blooms attract hummingbirds and other pollinators, adding ecological benefits to the garden.
