Crataegus succulenta var. gemmosa

Crataegus succulenta var. gemmosa, commonly known as the succulent hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and stems, which store water, making it adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its bark is typically smooth and gray, with a slightly rough texture.

It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing food and shelter.
