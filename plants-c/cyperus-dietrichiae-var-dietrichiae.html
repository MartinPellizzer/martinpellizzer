Cyperus dietrichiae var. dietrichiae

Cyperus dietrichiae var. dietrichiae, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense colonies in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in panicles, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
