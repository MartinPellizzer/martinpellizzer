Carex glauca var. digyna

Carex glauca var. digyna, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that ranges from blue-green to grayish-green in color. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are usually greenish or reddish in color.

Carex glauca var. digyna is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
