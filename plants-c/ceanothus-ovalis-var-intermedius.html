Ceanothus ovalis var. intermedius

Ceanothus ovalis var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
