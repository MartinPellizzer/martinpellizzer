Campanula rotundifolia subsp. scheuchzeri

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

scheuchzeri, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with rosettes of rounded, hairy leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine habitats.
