Consolea millspaughii subsp. corallicola

Consolea millspaughii subsp.

corallicola, commonly known as the coral coral or coral cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family. It is native to the arid regions of Mexico, particularly in the states of Oaxaca and Chiapas.

This species is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of small, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It requires well-draining soil and full sun to thrive.
