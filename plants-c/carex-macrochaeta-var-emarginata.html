Carex macrochaeta var. emarginata

Carex macrochaeta var. emarginata, commonly known as the serrated bulrush, is a variant of the bulrush species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to greenish flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic life.
