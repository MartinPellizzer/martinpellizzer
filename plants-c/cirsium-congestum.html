Cirsium congestum

Cirsium congestum, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features spiny leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Cirsium congestum is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in full sun.
