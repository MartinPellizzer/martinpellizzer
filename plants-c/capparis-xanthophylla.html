Capparis xanthophylla

Capparis xanthophylla, commonly known as the yellow-flowered caper, is a species of flowering plant in the Capparaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and coastal habitats. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems, which are often covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Capparis xanthophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
