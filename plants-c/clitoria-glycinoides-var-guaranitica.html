Clitoria glycinoides var. guaranitica

Clitoria glycinoides var. guaranitica, commonly known as the Brazilian butterfly pea, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and herbal practices.
