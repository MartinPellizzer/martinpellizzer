Carex hornschuchiana var. angustifolia

Carex hornschuchiana var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and dark green, with a dense, cylindrical inflorescence that bears small, spike-like flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions. It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
