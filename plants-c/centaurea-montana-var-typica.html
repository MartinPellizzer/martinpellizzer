Centaurea montana var. typica

Centaurea montana var. typica, commonly known as the mountain bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common sight in alpine meadows.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
