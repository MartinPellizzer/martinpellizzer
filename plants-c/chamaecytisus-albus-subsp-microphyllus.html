Chamaecytisus albus subsp. microphyllus

Chamaecytisus albus subsp.

microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, woody stems that often grow in dense, sprawling clusters.

The leaves are small, narrow, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are pale yellow and appear in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to coastal gardens.
