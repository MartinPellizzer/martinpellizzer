Caesalpinia parryi

Caesalpinia parryi, commonly known as the parry's flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange cylindrical flower clusters that appear during the dry season. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
