Canavalia ensiformis var. coriacea

Canavalia ensiformis var. coriacea, commonly known as the coral bean, is a tropical legume characterized by its distinctive, bean-like pods and compound leaves.

It is native to South America but has been naturalized in various warm regions around the world. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers followed by long, slender pods that contain several seeds. Its foliage is often hairy, and the stems are woody and can grow quite tall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
