Cyperus imbecillis

Cyperus imbecillis, commonly known as the dwarf papyrus, is a species of sedge native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive, delicate appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes many other sedges and related plants.
