Callicarpa macrophylla var. kouytchensis

Callicarpa macrophylla var. kouytchensis, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple berries that appear in clusters, typically ripening in the fall. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a bluish-green upper surface. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The berries are not only visually appealing but also attract birds and other wildlife.
