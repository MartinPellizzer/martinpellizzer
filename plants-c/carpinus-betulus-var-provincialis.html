Carpinus betulus var. provincialis

Carpinus betulus var. provincialis, commonly known as the European hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by small, round nuts enclosed in a spiky husk. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of conditions.
