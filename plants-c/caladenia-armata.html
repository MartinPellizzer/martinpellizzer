Caladenia armata

Caladenia armata, commonly known as the black-lip spider-orchid, is an Australian native orchid species belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, strikingly colored flower with a distinctive black lip. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions of Western Australia. It produces a single, erect stem that bears a single flower, which is usually pink to purple in color.

Caladenia armata is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological complexity.
