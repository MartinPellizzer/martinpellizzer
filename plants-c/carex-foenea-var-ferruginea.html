Carex foenea var. ferruginea

Carex foenea var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty sedge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is often a deep green with reddish or rusty tones, especially on the undersides of the leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
