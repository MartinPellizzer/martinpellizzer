Calappa capitata

Calappa capitata, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red cedar, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain smooth, black seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
