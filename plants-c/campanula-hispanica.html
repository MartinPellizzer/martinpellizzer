Campanula hispanica

Campanula hispanica, commonly known as Spanish bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
