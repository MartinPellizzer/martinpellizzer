Ceraia ephemera

Ceraia ephemera, commonly known as the ephemeral orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, ephemeral blooms that appear for only a short period during the dry season. The plant has a unique growth cycle, often surviving in harsh, arid environments by conserving water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss.

Despite its brief flowering period, it plays a significant role in its ecosystem, supporting pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
