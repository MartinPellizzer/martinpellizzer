Chrysodium pellucens

Chrysodium pellucens, commonly known as the glassy chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that give it a glass-like appearance, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Chrysodium pellucens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique visual appeal.
