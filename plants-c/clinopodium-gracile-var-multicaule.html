Clinopodium gracile var. multicaule

Clinopodium gracile var. multicaule, commonly known as slender woodruff, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features small, fragrant leaves that are opposite and ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale blue, blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their mild sedative properties.
