Croton manampetsae

Croton manampetsae, commonly known as the Manampetsa croton, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a distinctive, lobed shape.

Croton manampetsae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its striking appearance.
