Convolvulus rottleri

Convolvulus rottleri, commonly known as the Rottler's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other well-known species like morning glory.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
