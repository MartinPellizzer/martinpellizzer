Callicarpa pentandra f. flavida

Callicarpa pentandra f. flavida, commonly known as the red beauty or Chinese beautyberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant red berries that mature in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife, adding ecological value to gardens.
