Chrysothamnus viscidiflorus var. linifolius

Chrysothamnus viscidiflorus var. linifolius, commonly known as silverleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs, and its yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it the specific epithet *linifolius*, meaning "linear-leaved.".

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
