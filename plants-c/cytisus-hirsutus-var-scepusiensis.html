Cytisus hirsutus var. scepusiensis

Cytisus hirsutus var. scepusiensis, commonly known as the Scepusian broom, is a shrub native to the Balkans, particularly in Serbia and Bosnia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and its role in local ecosystems.
