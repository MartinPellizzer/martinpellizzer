Cissus oxyodonta

Cissus oxyodonta, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing cissus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a vigorous climber with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
