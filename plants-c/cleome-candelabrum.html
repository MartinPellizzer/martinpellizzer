Cleome candelabrum

Cleome candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick cleome, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that resemble candlesticks, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage consists of palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cleome candelabrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
