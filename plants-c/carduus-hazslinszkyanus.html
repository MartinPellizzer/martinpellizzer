Carduus hazslinszkyanus

Carduus hazslinszkyanus, commonly known as the Hungarian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
