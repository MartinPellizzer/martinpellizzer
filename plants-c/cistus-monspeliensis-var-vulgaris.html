Cistus monspeliensis var. vulgaris

Cistus monspeliensis var. vulgaris, commonly known as rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray foliage and produces showy pink or reddish flowers in the spring and summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and is well adapted to dry, rocky soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers are important for pollinators and are used in traditional herbal remedies.
