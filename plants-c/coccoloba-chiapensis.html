Coccoloba chiapensis

Coccoloba chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, particularly found in the states of Chiapas and Oaxaca. The plant is characterized by its striking red berries that appear in late summer, attracting various bird species. The leaves are palmately lobed and often have a distinctive blue-green color with a waxy coating.
