Carpotroche longifolia var. phasmatocarpa

Carpotroche longifolia var. phasmatocarpa, commonly known as the ghostfruit, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and distinctive, lantern-shaped fruits that resemble small, glowing orbs. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a climbing habit and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Due to its unusual appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat enigmatic species in botanical studies.
