Cousinia hemsleyana

Cousinia hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cousinia hemsleyana is a hardy species that is well adapted to drought and extreme temperatures.
