Croton dracona

Croton dracona, commonly known as the dragon's blood tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and a distinctive, tree-like appearance with multiple trunks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its most notable feature is the reddish-brown sap it exudes, which has been historically used for medicinal and dye purposes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
