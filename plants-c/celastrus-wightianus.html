Celastrus wightianus

Celastrus wightianus, commonly known as the Wight's staff-vine, is a woody climbing plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
