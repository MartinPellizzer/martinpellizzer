Crithodium aegilopoides

Crithodium aegilopoides, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish hues.

Crithodium aegilopoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
