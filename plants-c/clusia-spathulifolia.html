Clusia spathulifolia

Clusia spathulifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. These flowers are followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

Clusia spathulifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
