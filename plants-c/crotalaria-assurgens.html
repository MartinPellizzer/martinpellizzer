Crotalaria assurgens

Crotalaria assurgens, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation.

Its common name refers to the rattling sound the seed pods make when they dry and release seeds.
