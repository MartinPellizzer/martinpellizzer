Camonea vitifolia

Camonea vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits resembling small grapes. Camonea vitifolia thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
