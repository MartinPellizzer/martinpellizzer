Cylindrolobus clavicaulis

Cylindrolobus clavicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a unique plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, red-brown stems that are covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant produces striking clusters of bright red, brush-like flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing in pairs along the stem.

Cylindrolobus clavicaulis is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
