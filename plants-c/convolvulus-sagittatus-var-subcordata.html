Convolvulus sagittatus var. subcordata

Convolvulus sagittatus var. subcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
