Centrosema longifolium

Centrosema longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved centrosema, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically long and narrow, and its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or pasture areas. It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration and forage production.

Centrosema longifolium is also noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
