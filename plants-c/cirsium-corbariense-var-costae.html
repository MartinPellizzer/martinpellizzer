Cirsium corbariense var. costae

Cirsium corbariense var. costae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a distinctive serrated edge, while its stems are covered in stiff, prickly hairs.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
