Cyperus scaettae var. vanderystii

Cyperus scaettae var. vanderystii, commonly known as the Vanderyst's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, exhibiting a clump-forming habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster, which is characteristic of the genus Cyperus.

This variant is distinguished by specific morphological features that set it apart from other members of the Cyperus scaettae complex.
