Callicarpa erioclona var. repanda

Callicarpa erioclona var. repanda, commonly known as the red beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that give way to vibrant red berries in the fall. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a glossy texture. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
