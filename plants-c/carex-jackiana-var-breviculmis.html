Carex jackiana var. breviculmis

Carex jackiana var. breviculmis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly curved, with a dark green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, greenish-brown flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
