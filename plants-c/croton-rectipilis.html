Croton rectipilis

Croton rectipilis, commonly known as the upright croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a range of hues including red, yellow, and green. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes.

Croton rectipilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
