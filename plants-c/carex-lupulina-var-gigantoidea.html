Carex lupulina var. gigantoidea

Carex lupulina var. gigantoidea, commonly known as the giant bulrush, is a tall, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands that stabilize shorelines. Its tall stature, reaching up to several meters, makes it a distinctive feature in wetland ecosystems.

It plays an important role in providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
