Chrysothamnus latisquameus

Chrysothamnus latisquameus, commonly known as the broad-scale goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Chrysothamnus latisquameus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soils in native plant landscapes.
