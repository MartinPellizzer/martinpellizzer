Centaurea canadensis

Centaurea canadensis, commonly known as Canadian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Canadian fleabane is known for its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.
