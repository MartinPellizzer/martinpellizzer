Cyperus pseudokyllingioides var. africanus

Cyperus pseudokyllingioides var. africanus, commonly known as the African flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, characterized by their triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in disturbed soils. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, three-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various wetland habitats and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
