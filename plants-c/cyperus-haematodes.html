Cyperus haematodes

Cyperus haematodes, commonly known as the bloodgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically reddish or purple in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

Cyperus haematodes is adaptable to various environmental conditions and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.
