Cassia decumbens

Cassia decumbens, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Cassia decumbens is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
