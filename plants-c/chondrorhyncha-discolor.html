Chondrorhyncha discolor

Chondrorhyncha discolor, commonly known as the pale coralroot, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a myco-heterotrophic plant, meaning it lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on fungal associations for its nutrients. The plant produces small, pale green or white flowers that are typically arranged in a spiral along its slender, branching stems. Its unique ecological niche makes it a subject of interest in botanical research, particularly regarding its symbiotic relationships.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered in many regions.
