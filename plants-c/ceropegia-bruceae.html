Ceropegia bruceae

Ceropegia bruceae, commonly known as the Bruce's arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.
