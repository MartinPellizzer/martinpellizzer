Croton rugelianus

Croton rugelianus, commonly known as the rugel's croton, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful foliage, which can range from deep greens to striking reds and yellows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a distinctive, sometimes lobed or serrated shape.

Croton rugelianus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
