Cyathea naumannii

Cyathea naumannii, commonly known as the Naumann's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that supports its tall stature and allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant.

Cyathea naumannii plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
