Calamus fuscus

Calamus fuscus, commonly known as black calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices.

Calamus fuscus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
