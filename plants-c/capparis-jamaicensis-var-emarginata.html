Capparis jamaicensis var. emarginata

Capparis jamaicensis var. emarginata, commonly known as the Jamaican caper, is a shrub native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and pinnate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal and limestone habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
