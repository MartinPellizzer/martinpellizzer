Cephalanthus

Cephalanthus, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rhamnaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Cephalanthus occidentalis, which is native to eastern North America. The leaves are usually opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Cephalanthus species are often found in wetland areas and are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
