Cirsium dolopicum

Cirsium dolopicum, commonly known as the Dolopic Thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
