Carthamus fluminensis

Carthamus fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which are typical of the sunflower genus. Carthamus fluminensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riverine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
