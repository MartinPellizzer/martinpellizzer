Carex pamirica

Carex pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine environments. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish-brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex pamirica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
