Cakile edentula subsp. lacustris

Cakile edentula subsp.

lacustris, commonly known as the lake sandspur, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils near lakes and along shorelines. It has a low-growing habit with a creeping stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, showcasing resilience to saline and fluctuating water conditions.
