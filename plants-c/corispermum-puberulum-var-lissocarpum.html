Corispermum puberulum var. lissocarpum

Corispermum puberulum var. lissocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-seeded bladderwrack, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in small, bladder-like structures, which give the plant its common name.

This species is typically found in saline or coastal environments, where it thrives in sandy or muddy soils.
