Calandrinia oblongifolia

Calandrinia oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Portulacaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Calandrinia oblongifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
