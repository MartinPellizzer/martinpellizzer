Cyathea extensa

Cyathea extensa, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Cyathea extensa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
