Cerastium fontanum subsp. alpinum

Cerastium fontanum subsp.

alpinum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early summer, which are typically arranged in umbels. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
