Cassia subsetosa

Cassia subsetosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typical of the legume family. Cassia subsetosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can be used as a natural dye or in culinary applications.
