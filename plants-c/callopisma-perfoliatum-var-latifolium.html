Callopisma perfoliatum var. latifolium

Callopisma perfoliatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
