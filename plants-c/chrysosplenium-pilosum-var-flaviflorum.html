Chrysosplenium pilosum var. flaviflorum

Chrysosplenium pilosum var. flaviflorum, commonly known as the goldenflower saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features dense, hairy foliage that is typically dark green with a silvery underside, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often with a subtle pink or red tinge. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
