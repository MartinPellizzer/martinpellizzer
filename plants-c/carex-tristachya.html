Carex tristachya

Carex tristachya, commonly known as tussock sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, tussock-like growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are typically three-parted, with male and female flowers arranged in separate clusters.

Carex tristachya is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
