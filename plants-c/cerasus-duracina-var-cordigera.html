Cerasus duracina var. cordigera

Cerasus duracina var. cordigera, commonly known as the cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet-tart berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and turn yellow in autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
