Cyperus aureoalatus

Cyperus aureoalatus, commonly known as golden flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with distinctive golden-tipped leaves. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies.

Cyperus aureoalatus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
