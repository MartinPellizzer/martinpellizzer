Capparis formosa

Capparis formosa, commonly known as the Hawaiian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its striking red and yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing on rocky substrates or in coastal forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
