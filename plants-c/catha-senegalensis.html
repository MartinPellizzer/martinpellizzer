Catha senegalensis

Catha senegalensis, commonly known as khat, is a flowering plant native to East Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into oval, woody fruits containing seeds. Its leaves contain alkaloids, particularly cathinone and cathine, which are responsible for its stimulant effects.

The plant is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are chewed or brewed as a stimulant in various cultures.
