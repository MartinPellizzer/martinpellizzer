Cerasus × stacei

Cerasus × stacei, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or stacei cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its showy pink to white blossoms that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and has simple, ovate leaves that turn green in summer and yellow in autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
