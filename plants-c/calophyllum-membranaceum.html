Calophyllum membranaceum

Calophyllum membranaceum, commonly known as the paperwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often pale-colored bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a waxy texture.

Calophyllum membranaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
