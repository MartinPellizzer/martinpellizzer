Centropogon

Centropogon, commonly known as the purpletop or purpletop grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive purple or violet-colored inflorescences, which are often tall and spike-like. Centropogon species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in open areas or along roadsides. They have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in horticulture for their ornamental value.
