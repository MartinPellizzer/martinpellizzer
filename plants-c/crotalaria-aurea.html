Crotalaria aurea

Crotalaria aurea, commonly known as the golden crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes and bloom during the warmer months. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Crotalaria aurea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
