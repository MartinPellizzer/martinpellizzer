Cerastium lanigerum f. albanicum

Cerastium lanigerum f. albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, oval leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
