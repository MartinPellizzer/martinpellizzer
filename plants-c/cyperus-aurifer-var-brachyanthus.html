Cyperus aurifer var. brachyanthus

Cyperus aurifer var. brachyanthus, commonly known as golden flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of yellowish-brown inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its botanical characteristics include rhizomatous growth and small, three-lobed flowers arranged in spikelets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
