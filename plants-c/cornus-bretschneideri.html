Cornus bretschneideri

Cornus bretschneideri, commonly known as the Chinese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy white or pink bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
