Chloris roxburghii

Chloris roxburghii, commonly known as the prostrate grass or dwarf brome, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Asia.

It typically forms dense mats with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
