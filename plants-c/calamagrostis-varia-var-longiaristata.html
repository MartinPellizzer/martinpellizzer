Calamagrostis varia var. longiaristata

Calamagrostis varia var. longiaristata, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that bear long, narrow leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue hue on the underside. The plant produces feathery, silver-green panicles that add ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for water garden and pond edge plantings.
