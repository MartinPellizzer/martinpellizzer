Cucumis melo convar. ameri

Cucumis melo convar. ameri, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a variant of the melon plant characterized by its rough, netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows as a trailing or climbing vine with palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruits are generally orange to yellow in color and are cultivated for their edible, juicy interior.
