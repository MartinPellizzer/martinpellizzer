Calamagrostis littorea var. montana

Calamagrostis littorea var. montana, commonly known as coastal panicgrass, is a grass species native to coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in marshes and tidal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is highly salt-tolerant and can withstand periodic flooding, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland landscapes.
