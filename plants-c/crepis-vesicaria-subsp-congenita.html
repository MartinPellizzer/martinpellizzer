Crepis vesicaria subsp. congenita

Crepis vesicaria subsp.

congenita, commonly known as the yellow hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow ray florets and cylindrical flower heads.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.
