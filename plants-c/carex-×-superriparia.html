Carex × superriparia

Carex × superriparia, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a hybrid species within the Carex genus, characterized by its robust and upright growth habit.

It typically forms dense clumps of grass-like foliage with narrow, grass-green leaves that are often slightly broader than those of its parent species. The plant produces spikes of small, usually brownish or reddish flowers, which are arranged in a cylindrical shape along the stem. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex × superriparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
