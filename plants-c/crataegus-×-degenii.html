Crataegus × degenii

Crataegus × degenii, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
