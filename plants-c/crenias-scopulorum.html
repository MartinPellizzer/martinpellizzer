Crenias scopulorum

Crenias scopulorum, commonly known as the rock cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and alpine environments.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically has basal leaves that are lobed or toothed, and its stems are often sprawling or ascending. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in crevices or on rock faces.

Crenias scopulorum is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in mountainous regions.
