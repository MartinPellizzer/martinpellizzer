Canavalia podocarpa

Canavalia podocarpa, commonly known as the wild pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Canavalia podocarpa is also valued for its ornamental features and use in traditional medicine.
