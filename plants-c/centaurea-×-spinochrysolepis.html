Centaurea × spinochrysolepis

Centaurea × spinochrysolepis, commonly known as the spiny chrysolepis centaurea, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and vibrant, often reddish or purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
