Cryophytum longipapillatum

Cryophytum longipapillatum, commonly known as the long-papilled cryptophyte, is a unique plant species found in cold, high-altitude environments.

It is characterized by its small, clustered growth habit and specialized adaptations to survive in extreme conditions. The plant's leaves are typically covered in dense, hair-like structures that help insulate it from freezing temperatures. Its reproductive structures are highly modified, often resembling small, rounded bumps that aid in seed dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Cryophytum longipapillatum is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
