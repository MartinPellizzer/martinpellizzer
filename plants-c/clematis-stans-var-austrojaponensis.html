Clematis stans var. austrojaponensis

Clematis stans var. austrojaponensis, commonly known as the Japanese Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, fragrant aroma. The plant produces small, woody berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a support structure for its vigorous growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
