Chrysophyllum ambrense

Chrysophyllum ambrense, commonly known as the African cherry or ambrense starfruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, yellow, star-shaped fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture making.
