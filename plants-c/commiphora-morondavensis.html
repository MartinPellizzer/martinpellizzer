Commiphora morondavensis

Commiphora morondavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous exudates, which are used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits.

Its unique ecological role in the rainforest ecosystem makes it an important species for conservation efforts.
