Chrysanthemum holophyllum

Chrysanthemum holophyllum, commonly known as the Alpine chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it has a long flowering period that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Chrysanthemum holophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
