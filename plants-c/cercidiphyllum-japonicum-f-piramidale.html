Cercidiphyllum japonicum f. piramidale

Cercidiphyllum japonicum f. piramidale, commonly known as the Japanese pagoda tree, is a unique cultivar characterized by its distinctive pyramidal shape and upright growth habit.

It is a deciduous tree that produces heart-shaped leaves which turn yellow in the fall. The plant is native to Japan and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It flowers with small, inconspicuous yellow blooms in early spring, followed by spherical, spiny fruits.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its striking form and seasonal interest.
