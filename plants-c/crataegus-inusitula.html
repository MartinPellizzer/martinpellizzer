Crataegus inusitula

Crataegus inusitula, commonly known as the unusual hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in native habitats.
