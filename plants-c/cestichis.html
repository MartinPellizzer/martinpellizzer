Cestichis

Cestichis, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in various colors. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves and has a complex root system that helps it anchor to trees or other structures. Cestichis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a ornamental plant and its ecological interactions within its native habitats.
