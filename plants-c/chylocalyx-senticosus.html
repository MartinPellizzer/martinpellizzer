Chylocalyx senticosus

Chylocalyx senticosus, commonly known as the silky glabrous acanthus, is a distinctive plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silky, white hairs. The plant produces showy, spiky flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
