Callicarpa roxburghii

Callicarpa roxburghii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall, attracting various bird species. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement and turn red in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The berries are not only visually striking but also contain high levels of antioxidants.
