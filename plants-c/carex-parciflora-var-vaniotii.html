Carex parciflora var. vaniotii

Carex parciflora var. vaniotii, commonly known as the Vaniot's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering heads are small and clustered, producing wind-pollinated flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
