Cleome puccionia

Cleome puccionia, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spindly appearance with elongated stems and clustered flowers. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, giving it a spider-like appearance. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Cleome puccionia is typically found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
