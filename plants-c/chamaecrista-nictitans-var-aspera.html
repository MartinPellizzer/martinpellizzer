Chamaecrista nictitans var. aspera

Chamaecrista nictitans var. aspera, commonly known as the rough crownbeard, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like foliage and showy, yellow-orange flower clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
