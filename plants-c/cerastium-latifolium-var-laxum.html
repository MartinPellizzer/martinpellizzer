Cerastium latifolium var. laxum

Cerastium latifolium var. laxum, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The leaves are broad, oval, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

This variety is often found in moist meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
