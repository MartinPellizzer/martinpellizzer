Cyperus altsonii

Cyperus altsonii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of small, greenish flowers at the top of its stalks. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, often contributing to soil stabilization.

Cyperus altsonii is typically found in lowland areas and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels.
