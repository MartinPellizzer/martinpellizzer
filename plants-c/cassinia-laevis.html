Cassinia laevis

Cassinia laevis, commonly known as the silky cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellow flower heads that are often clustered in spherical or conical shapes.

Cassinia laevis is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
