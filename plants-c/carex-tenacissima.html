Carex tenacissima

Carex tenacissima, commonly known as hardy sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.

Carex tenacissima is also known for its resilience to cold temperatures and partial shade.
