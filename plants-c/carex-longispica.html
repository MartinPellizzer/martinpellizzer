Carex longispica

Carex longispica, commonly known as the long-spiked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist soils, such as marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Carex longispica plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
