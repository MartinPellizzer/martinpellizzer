Corydalis rheinbabeniana var. leioneura

Corydalis rheinbabeniana var. leioneura, commonly known as the Rhine Valley corydalis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 30 centimeters in height, often branching near the base. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, with a distinctive reddish-brown hue on the underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
