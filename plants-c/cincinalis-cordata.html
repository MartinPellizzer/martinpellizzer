Cincinalis cordata

Cincinalis cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf cincinalis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
