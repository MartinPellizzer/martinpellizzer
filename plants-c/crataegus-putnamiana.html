Crataegus putnamiana

Crataegus putnamiana, commonly known as Putnam's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 leaflets, and its clusters of white to pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun to partial shade.

Putnam's hawthorn is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
