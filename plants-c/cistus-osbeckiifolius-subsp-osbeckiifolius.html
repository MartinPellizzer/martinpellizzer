Cistus osbeckiifolius subsp. osbeckiifolius

Cistus osbeckiifolius subsp.

osbeckiifolius, commonly known as the Osbeck's rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and coastal environments. Its leaves are covered in glandular hairs that secrete essential oils, contributing to its distinctive fragrance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
