Commelina virginica var. angustifolia

Commelina virginica var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf commelina, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers clustered in umbels, which are typically borne on short peduncles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common indicator species in humid, temperate regions.
