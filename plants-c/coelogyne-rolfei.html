Coelogyne rolfei

Coelogyne rolfei, commonly known as the orchid of the gods, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with long, narrow leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant blooms once a year, typically in spring or early summer, and its flowers can last several weeks. It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media to thrive.

Coelogyne rolfei is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
