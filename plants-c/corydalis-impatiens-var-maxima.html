Corydalis impatiens var. maxima

Corydalis impatiens var. maxima, commonly known as the giant impatiens, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
