Crotalaria fischeri

Crotalaria fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the plant typically grows as an annual herb.

Crotalaria fischeri is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its bright, showy blooms.
