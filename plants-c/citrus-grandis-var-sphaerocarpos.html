Citrus grandis var. sphaerocarpos

Citrus grandis var. sphaerocarpos, commonly known as the Chinese pomelo, is a botanical variant of the pomelo species.

It is characterized by its large, round fruits with thick, yellow-orange rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It has a long growth cycle, with fruits typically ripening several months after flowering.

The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture, while the flowers are fragrant and white.
