Cereus columna-trajani

Cereus columna-trajani, commonly known as the Trajan's column cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and spiny areoles. The plant produces white, nocturnal flowers that open at night, attracting moths for pollination. Its stems are typically green and succulent, adapted to store water in its native desert environment.

The plant is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
