Cirsium × kirschlegeri

Cirsium × kirschlegeri, commonly known as the Kirschleger's thistle, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cirsium vulgare and Cirsium horridulum.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in grasslands and along roadsides. It has a robust root system and can form dense colonies, making it a notable weed in certain regions.

The flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
