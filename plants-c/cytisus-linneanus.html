Cytisus linneanus

Cytisus linneanus, commonly known as the yellow broom, is a flowering shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas.

Cytisus linneanus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
