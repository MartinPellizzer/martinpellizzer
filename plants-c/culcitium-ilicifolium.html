Culcitium ilicifolium

Culcitium ilicifolium, commonly known as the hollyleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that resemble those of holly. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or along riverbanks.

Culcitium ilicifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
