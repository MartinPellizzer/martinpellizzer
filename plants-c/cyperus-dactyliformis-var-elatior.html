Cyperus dactyliformis var. elatior

Cyperus dactyliformis var. elatior, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically flat and wide, with a distinctive triangular cross-section. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pale green or brown in color.

This species is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along water edges, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils.
