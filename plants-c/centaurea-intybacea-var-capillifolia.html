Centaurea intybacea var. capillifolia

Centaurea intybacea var. capillifolia, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a grayish hue due to fine pubescence.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
