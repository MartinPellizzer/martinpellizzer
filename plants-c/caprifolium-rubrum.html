Caprifolium rubrum

Caprifolium rubrum, commonly known as red honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its berries, which ripen in late summer, are orange-red and contain several seeds.

Caprifolium rubrum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.
