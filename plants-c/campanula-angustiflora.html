Campanula angustiflora

Campanula angustiflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula angustiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
