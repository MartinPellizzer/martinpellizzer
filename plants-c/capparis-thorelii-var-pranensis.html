Capparis thorelii var. pranensis

Capparis thorelii var. pranensis, commonly known as the Pranese Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant white or yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible capers that are used in culinary applications. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
