Clompanus caudata

Clompanus caudata, commonly known as the trailing ardisia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces small, clustered white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and can be grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.

Clompanus caudata is known for its trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.
