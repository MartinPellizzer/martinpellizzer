Cachrys laevigata

Cachrys laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It has a dense, crust-like thallus with a smooth, leathery surface. The plant's structure consists of a symbiotic partnership between a fungus and algae or cyanobacteria. It is typically found on bark, soil, or rock surfaces in temperate regions.

Cachrys laevigata plays a vital role in ecological systems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing microhabitats for other organisms.
