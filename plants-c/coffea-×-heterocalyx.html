Coffea × heterocalyx

Coffea × heterocalyx, commonly known as the African coffee.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Coffea arabica and Coffea canephora. This plant is native to the tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and red or yellow flowers. The fruit, known as a cherry, contains the seeds used for coffee production.

It is valued for its aromatic and flavorful beans, though it is less commonly cultivated than other coffee species.
