Clinopodium cercocarpoides

Clinopodium cercocarpoides, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
