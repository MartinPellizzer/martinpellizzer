Callicarpa longipes var. mixiensis

Callicarpa longipes var. mixiensis, commonly known as the red-berried beauty or Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by vibrant purple to red berries in the fall. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its ornamental value comes from its striking berries and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The berries are not only visually appealing but also attract birds and other wildlife.
