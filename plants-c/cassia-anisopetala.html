Cassia anisopetala

Cassia anisopetala, commonly known as the Mexican cassia, is a flowering plant native to Central America and Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Cassia anisopetala is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
