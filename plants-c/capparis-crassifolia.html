Capparis crassifolia

Capparis crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible, acidic berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Capparis crassifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
