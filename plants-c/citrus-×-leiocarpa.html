Citrus × leiocarpa

Citrus × leiocarpa, commonly known as the rough lemon.

It is a hybrid citrus species native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its thick, rough, and often thorny rind. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with dark green, glossy leaves. Its fruit is usually small, round, and has a yellow-orange color when ripe.

The rough lemon is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
