Campanula persicifolia moreheinii

Campanula persicifolia moreheinii, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from blue to violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate colder climates.
