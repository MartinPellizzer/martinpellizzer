Christella patens

Christella patens, commonly known as the slender thornapple, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its botanical features make it a notable species in wetland and woodland habitats.

Christella patens is also known for its potential medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require caution in use.
