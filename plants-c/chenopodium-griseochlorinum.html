Chenopodium griseochlorinum

Chenopodium griseochlorinum, commonly known as silverleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, reflective hairs. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a waxy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Chenopodium griseochlorinum is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
