Croton sellowii

Croton sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Croton sellowii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
