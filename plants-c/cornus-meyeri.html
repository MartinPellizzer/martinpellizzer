Cornus meyeri

Cornus meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy white bracts that surround small yellow flowers, which appear in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn bright red in the autumn. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact size and seasonal color changes.
