Capparis jamaicensis

Capparis jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican caper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and succulent leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it thrives in coastal and limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
