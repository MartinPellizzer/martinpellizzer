Clusia goscinnyi

Clusia goscinnyi, commonly known as the golden glory, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important plants. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Clusia goscinnyi is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
