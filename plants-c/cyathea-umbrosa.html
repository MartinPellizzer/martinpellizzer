Cyathea umbrosa

Cyathea umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer. The fronds are large, pinnately compound, and often have a dark green color with a waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
