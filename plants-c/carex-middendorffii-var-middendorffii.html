Carex middendorffii var. middendorffii

Carex middendorffii var. middendorffii, commonly known as the Middendorff's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, bogs, and tundra environments. The plant features grass-like leaves that are generally narrow and cylindrical, with a bluish-green to grayish-green coloration. Its flowering spikes consist of small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
