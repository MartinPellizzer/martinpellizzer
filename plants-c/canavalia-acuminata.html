Canavalia acuminata

Canavalia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant bears pods that contain several round, edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Canavalia acuminata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural systems.

It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
