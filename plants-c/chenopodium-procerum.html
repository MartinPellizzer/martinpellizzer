Chenopodium procerum

Chenopodium procerum, commonly known as tall goosefoot, is an annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
