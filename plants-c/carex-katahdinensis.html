Carex katahdinensis

Carex katahdinensis, commonly known as Katahdin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along stream edges. It has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine, powdery bloom.

Carex katahdinensis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
