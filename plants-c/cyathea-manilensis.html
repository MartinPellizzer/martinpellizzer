Cyathea manilensis

Cyathea manilensis, commonly known as the Manila tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, which are used in traditional medicine and horticulture.

Cyathea manilensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
