Cymbopogon iwarancusa subsp. olivieri

Cymbopogon iwarancusa subsp.

olivieri, commonly known as the olive grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to golden flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
