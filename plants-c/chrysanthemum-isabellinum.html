Chrysanthemum isabellinum

Chrysanthemum isabellinum, commonly known as the white chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil. It blooms in the autumn season and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.
