Cardamine seravschanica

Cardamine seravschanica, commonly known as the Seravshan watercress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Tajikistan and Uzbekistan. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream sides and rocky areas. It features slender, hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating.

Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.
