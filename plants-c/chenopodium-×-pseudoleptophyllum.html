Chenopodium × pseudoleptophyllum

Chenopodium × pseudoleptophyllum, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves that are similar in taste to spinach. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with alternate, lance-shaped leaves.

It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its nutrient-rich foliage, which is high in vitamins A and C. The plant's ability to tolerate cooler temperatures makes it a popular choice for spring and early summer harvesting.
