Casearia pubescens

Casearia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy casearia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
