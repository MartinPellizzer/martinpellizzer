Carex vesicaria var. globosa

Carex vesicaria var. globosa, commonly known as the globular sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of leaves that form a compact, spherical shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically grouped in spikes, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
