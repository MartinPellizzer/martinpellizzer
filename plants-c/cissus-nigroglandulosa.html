Cissus nigroglandulosa

Cissus nigroglandulosa, commonly known as the black glandular cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that are often covered with small, black glands. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can form a dense, trailing growth habit.

Cissus nigroglandulosa is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
