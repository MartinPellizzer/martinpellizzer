Croton moschatus

Croton moschatus, commonly known as the musk croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful, and sometimes hairy leaves that vary in shape and size. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in folk medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland areas with a warm climate.
