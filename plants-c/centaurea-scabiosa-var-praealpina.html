Centaurea scabiosa var. praealpina

Centaurea scabiosa var. praealpina, commonly known as the Alpine cudweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright pink or red, thistle-like flowers with a distinctive central disk. The plant is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea scabiosa var. praealpina is often used in rock gardens and alpine plantings due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
