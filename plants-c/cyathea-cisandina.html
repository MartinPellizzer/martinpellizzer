Cyathea cisandina

Cyathea cisandina, commonly known as the Cisandina tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.

The fronds are typically evergreen, providing a lush, green appearance throughout the year.
