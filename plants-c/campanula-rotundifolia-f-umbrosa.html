Campanula rotundifolia f. umbrosa

Campanula rotundifolia f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its rounded, broad leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. The plant's compact form and delicate blooms add visual interest to moist, well-drained soils. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
