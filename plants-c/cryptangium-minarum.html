Cryptangium minarum

Cryptangium minarum, commonly known as the silverleaf cryptangium, is a rare and unique plant species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, silver-gray leaves that reflect sunlight, helping it to retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often blooming in late spring. Its root system is highly adapted to absorb nutrients from poor soils, allowing it to thrive in rocky, nutrient-deficient areas.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth rate, Cryptangium minarum is considered an endangered species.
