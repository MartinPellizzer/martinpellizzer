Coreopsis foliosa

Coreopsis foliosa, commonly known as the leafy coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like yellow flowers and narrow, feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial species that produces copious amounts of nectar, attracting bees and other pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and native plant landscapes.
