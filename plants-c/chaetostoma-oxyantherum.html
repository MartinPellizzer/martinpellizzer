Chaetostoma oxyantherum

Chaetostoma oxyantherum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
