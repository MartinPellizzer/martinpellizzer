Cytisus kitaibelii

Cytisus kitaibelii, commonly known as the golden bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, thornless stems and pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or scrubland habitats.

Cytisus kitaibelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
