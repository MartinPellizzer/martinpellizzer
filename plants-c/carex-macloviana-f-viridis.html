Carex macloviana f. viridis

Carex macloviana f. viridis, commonly known as the green sedges, is a variant of the Carex macloviana species, characterized by its lush, dark green foliage.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, typically found in moist, temperate environments such as wetlands and marshes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions and requires consistent moisture in the soil.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
