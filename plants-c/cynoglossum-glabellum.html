Cynoglossum glabellum

Cynoglossum glabellum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Cynoglossum glabellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
