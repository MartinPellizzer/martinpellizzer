Cachrys sphaerocarpa

Cachrys sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-fruit or yellow globe-fruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and produces small, yellow, spherical berries that are edible. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cachrys sphaerocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
