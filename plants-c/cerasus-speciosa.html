Cerasus speciosa

Cerasus speciosa, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, red fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cerasus speciosa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
