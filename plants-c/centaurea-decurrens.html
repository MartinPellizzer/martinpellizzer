Centaurea decurrens

Centaurea decurrens, commonly known as the creeping cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branched stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with purple centers, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, and it thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and grasslands.

Centaurea decurrens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
