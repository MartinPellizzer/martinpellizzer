Cyperus radiatus var. minor

Cyperus radiatus var. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 mm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of small, spike-like structures that produce tiny flowers.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
