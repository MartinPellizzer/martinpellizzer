Cephaelis kappleri

Cephaelis kappleri, commonly known as the pink fairy fan, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, creeping stems and dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces showy, fan-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cephaelis kappleri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
