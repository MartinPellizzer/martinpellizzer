Cytisus scepusiensis

Cytisus scepusiensis, commonly known as the Scepusian broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often blooming in spring.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
