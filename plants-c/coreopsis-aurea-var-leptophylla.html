Coreopsis aurea var. leptophylla

Coreopsis aurea var. leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that reaches heights of about 1 to 3 feet, making it a suitable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and low-maintenance plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
