Ceropegia similior

Ceropegia similior, commonly known as the dwarf string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features trailing stems with heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia similior is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
