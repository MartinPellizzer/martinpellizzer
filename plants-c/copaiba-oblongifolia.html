Copaiba oblongifolia

Copaiba oblongifolia, commonly known as Brazilian frankincense, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces a valuable resin used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and yellowish, typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Copaiba oblongifolia plays an important ecological role in its habitat and is also valued for its economic contributions.
