Cressa latifolia

Cressa latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf cress, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows, where it thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features alternate, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

Cressa latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
