Caulinia indica

Caulinia indica, commonly known as the Indian sida, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with simple, ovate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its wide distribution.

Caulinia indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
