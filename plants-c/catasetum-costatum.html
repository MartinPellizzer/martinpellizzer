Catasetum costatum

Catasetum costatum, commonly known as the spurred orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of bees, which are attracted by the unique structure of the petals.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
