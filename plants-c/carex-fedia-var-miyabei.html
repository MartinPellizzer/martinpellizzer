Carex fedia var. miyabei

Carex fedia var. miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland environments.
