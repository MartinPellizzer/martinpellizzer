Cardamomum magnificum

Cardamomum magnificum, commonly known as the golden spice tree, is a tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated, dark brown pods containing aromatic, peppery seeds used in both culinary and medicinal applications. Its botanical classification places it in the family Zingiberaceae, which includes other spice plants like ginger and turmeric.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
