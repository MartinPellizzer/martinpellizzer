Chamaecrista incurvata

Chamaecrista incurvata, commonly known as the curlleaf crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that curl at the tips. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Chamaecrista incurvata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
