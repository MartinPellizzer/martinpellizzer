Crateva lophosperma

Crateva lophosperma, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, green fruits with a thick, astringent skin. The fruit contains a single large seed and is rich in vitamin C, making it valuable in traditional medicine and nutrition.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
