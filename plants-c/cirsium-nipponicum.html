Cirsium nipponicum

Cirsium nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a perennial species belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in stiff, bristly hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical head.

Cirsium nipponicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
