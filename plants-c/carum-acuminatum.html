Carum acuminatum

Carum acuminatum, commonly known as Persian cumin or wild cumin, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are aromatic and used in various culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are finely divided and have a spicy, pungent aroma similar to cumin.

Carum acuminatum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
