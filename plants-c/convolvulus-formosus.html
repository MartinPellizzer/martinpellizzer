Convolvulus formosus

Convolvulus formosus, commonly known as the Taiwan morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, adding to its distinctive botanical appearance.
