Croton gossypiifolius

Croton gossypiifolius, commonly known as the cottonleaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that resemble cotton leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
