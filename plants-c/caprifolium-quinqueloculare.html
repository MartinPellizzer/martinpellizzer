Caprifolium quinqueloculare

Caprifolium quinqueloculare, commonly known as the five-chambered honeysuckle, is a lesser-known species within the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant produces berries that are usually red or yellow, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in the understory of deciduous forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
