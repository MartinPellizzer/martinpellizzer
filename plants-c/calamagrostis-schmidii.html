Calamagrostis schmidii

Calamagrostis schmidii, commonly known as Schmid's reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in panicles, which produce small, fluffy seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable moisture conditions.
