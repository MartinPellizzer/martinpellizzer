Crataegus tardifolia

Crataegus tardifolia, commonly known as the lateleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically reddish-brown when young and turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist to mesic soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
