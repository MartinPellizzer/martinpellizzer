Cycnia

Cycnia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant blooms that open at night. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and short blooming period. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The moonflower is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal flowering habit.
