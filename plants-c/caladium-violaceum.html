Caladium violaceum

Caladium violaceum, commonly known as the violet-leaf caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display a range of vibrant colors, including shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, making it a popular choice for garden displays.

Caladium violaceum is typically grown as an annual in regions with cold winters.
