Chamaedorea lunata

Chamaedorea lunata, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a compact, evergreen plant native to Central America.

It features a striking, swollen caudex that resembles a moon, giving it its common name. The plant typically has narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in indoor settings.
