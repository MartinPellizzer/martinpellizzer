Calligonum falcilobum

Calligonum falcilobum, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resistance to salt and wind.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
