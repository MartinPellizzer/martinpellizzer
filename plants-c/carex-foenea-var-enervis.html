Carex foenea var. enervis

Carex foenea var. enervis, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish to brownish flowers in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Carex foenea var. enervis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
