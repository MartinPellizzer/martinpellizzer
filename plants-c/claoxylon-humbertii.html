Claoxylon humbertii

It belongs to the family Claoxylinaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is critically threatened with extinction, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
