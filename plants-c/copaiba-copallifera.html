Copaiba copallifera

Copaiba copallifera, commonly known as Brazil wax tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

The tree is valued for its copal resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
