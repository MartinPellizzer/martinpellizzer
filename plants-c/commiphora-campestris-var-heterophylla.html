Commiphora campestris var. heterophylla

Commiphora campestris var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable sand-verbena, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous exudates that have been historically used in traditional medicine. The plant exhibits morphological variation in leaf shape, which is reflected in its varietal name "heterophylla.". It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for various desert organisms.
