Calamus javensis var. acicularis

Calamus javensis var. acicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrow and linear in shape. The plant produces long, cylindrical inflorescences with small, clustered flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This species thrives in wetland or swampy environments, preferring humid and shaded conditions.
