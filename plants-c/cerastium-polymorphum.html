Cerastium polymorphum

Cerastium polymorphum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and often have a rounded or heart-shaped appearance.

Cerastium polymorphum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate foliage and early spring blooms.
