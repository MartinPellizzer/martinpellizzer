Cirsium costaricense

Cirsium costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and features spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. It has a robust root system that helps it thrive in its natural habitat.

Cirsium costaricense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
