Columella japonica

Columella japonica, commonly known as the Japanese columnar plant, is a unique species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, and upright growth habit, forming a columnar structure. The plant typically has small, elongated leaves that are arranged spirally along its stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often followed by clusters of red or orange berries.

Due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
