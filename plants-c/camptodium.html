Camptodium

Camptodium, commonly known as the common tamarisk, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Tamaricaceae.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and distinctive, often fragrant, small flowers. The leaves are usually narrow and linear, and the plant is adapted to arid and saline environments. Camptodium species are native to regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

They play an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
