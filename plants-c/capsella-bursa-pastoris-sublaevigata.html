Capsella bursa-pastoris sublaevigata

Capsella bursa-pastoris sublaevigata, commonly known as the common weed or devil's bit.

It is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, white flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly less hairy leaves compared to other variants of the species.
