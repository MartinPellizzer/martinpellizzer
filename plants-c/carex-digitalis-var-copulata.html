Carex digitalis var. copulata

Carex digitalis var. copulata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pale green to brown in color. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
