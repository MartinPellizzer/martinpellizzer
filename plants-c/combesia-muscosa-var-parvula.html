Combesia muscosa var. parvula

Combesia muscosa var. parvula, commonly known as the small-leaved combesia, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically features small, clustered inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, often shaded environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
