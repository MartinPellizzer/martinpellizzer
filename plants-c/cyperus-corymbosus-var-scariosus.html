Cyperus corymbosus var. scariosus

Cyperus corymbosus var. scariosus, commonly known as the broadleaf paper plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in humid environments.
