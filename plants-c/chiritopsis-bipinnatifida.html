Chiritopsis bipinnatifida

Chiritopsis bipinnatifida, commonly known as the bipinnatifid chiritopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disc flowers. The plant features finely divided, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its blooming period occurs in the spring, producing small yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
