Convolvulus maximus

Convolvulus maximus, commonly known as giant lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Convolvulus maximus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
