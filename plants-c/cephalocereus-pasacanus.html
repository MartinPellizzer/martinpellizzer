Cephalocereus pasacanus

Cephalocereus pasacanus, commonly known as the Potosi cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are highly fragrant. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners.
