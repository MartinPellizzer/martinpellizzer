Cerastium brachypetalum subsp. tauricum

Cerastium brachypetalum subsp.

tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically forms dense mats of small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, dry habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its flowers are usually less than 1 cm in diameter and are arranged in umbels.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
