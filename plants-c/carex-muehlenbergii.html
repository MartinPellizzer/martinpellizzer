Carex muehlenbergii

Carex muehlenbergii, commonly known as Muhlenberg's sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Carex muehlenbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
