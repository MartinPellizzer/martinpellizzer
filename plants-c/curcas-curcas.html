Curcas curcas

Curcas curcas, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves, which help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant flowers, often in shades of yellow, orange, or red, followed by edible fruits called figs. Its pads, or nopales, are widely used in culinary and traditional medicine practices.

The plant's ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it an important species in desert ecosystems.
