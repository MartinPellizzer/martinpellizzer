Cryptantha humilis

Cryptantha humilis, commonly known as the low cryptantha, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Cryptantha humilis is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
