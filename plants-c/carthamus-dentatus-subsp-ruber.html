Carthamus dentatus subsp. ruber

Carthamus dentatus subsp.

ruber, commonly known as the red poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open at maturity. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and long-lasting blooms.
