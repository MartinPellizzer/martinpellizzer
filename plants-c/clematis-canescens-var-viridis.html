Clematis canescens var. viridis

Clematis canescens var. viridis, commonly known as the green-haired clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems covered in fine, greenish-white hairs, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the leaves are often tinged with a faint green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
