Cordylanthus pilosus subsp. bolanderi

Cordylanthus pilosus subsp.

bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's wooly flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to California. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features hairy, gray-green foliage.

The plant produces showy, spicate inflorescences with violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
