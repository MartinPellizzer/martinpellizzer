Croton longibracteatus

Croton longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
