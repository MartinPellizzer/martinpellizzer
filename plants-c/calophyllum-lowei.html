Calophyllum lowei

Calophyllum lowei, commonly known as the Hawaiian mahogany, is a tropical tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark reddish-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in coastal and lowland forest habitats, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
