Crepinia × dumetorum

Crepinia × dumetorum, commonly known as the hybrid marsh gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, meadows, and along riverbanks. The plant features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Crepinia × dumetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity in wetland ecosystems.
