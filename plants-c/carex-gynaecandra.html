Carex gynaecandra

Carex gynaecandra, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the sedge family.

Carex gynaecandra plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
