Chenopodium × tapolczense

Chenopodium × tapolczense, commonly known as the hybrid goosefoot, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and branched inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually oval to rhombic in shape and may have a slightly prickly texture.

The species is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop or used in traditional medicine.
