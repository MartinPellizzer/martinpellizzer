Citrus × aurantium var. fructo-variabili

Citrus × aurantium var. fructo-variabili, commonly known as the variable-fruited citron, is a citrus hybrid characterized by its diverse fruit morphology.

The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are usually large, elongated, and have a thick, rough rind that can vary in color from green to yellow or orange. The fruit contains a high amount of pulp and seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

This variety is notable for its genetic variability, which contributes to its adaptability and unique characteristics.
