Cyperus fuligineus var. calcicola

Cyperus fuligineus var. calcicola, commonly known as the desert flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in certain ecosystems.
