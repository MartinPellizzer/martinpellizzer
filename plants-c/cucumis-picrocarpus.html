Cucumis picrocarpus

Cucumis picrocarpus, commonly known as the bitter cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that are typically green and covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a trailing growth habit with palmate leaves. Its fruits are generally bitter and not commonly consumed by humans, though they are used in some traditional medicinal practices.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
