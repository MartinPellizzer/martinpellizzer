Convolvulus reinwardtianus

Convolvulus reinwardtianus, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant blue or purple funnel-shaped flowers. The plant has slender, twining stems that climb by means of tendrils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.
