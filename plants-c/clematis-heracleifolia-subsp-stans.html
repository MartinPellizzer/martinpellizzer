Clematis heracleifolia subsp. stans

Clematis heracleifolia subsp.

stans, commonly known as the climbing clematis, is a vigorous vine native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, often with a slightly serrated edge.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly used in gardens and as a climbing plant.
