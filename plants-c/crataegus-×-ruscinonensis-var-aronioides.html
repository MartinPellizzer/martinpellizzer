Crataegus × ruscinonensis var. aronioides

Crataegus × ruscinonensis var. aronioides, commonly known as the Aronioides hawthorn, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, aggregate fruits that typically ripen to a dark red or black color. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a glossy upper surface. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
