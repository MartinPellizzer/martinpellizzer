Cyclophyllum jasminifolium

Cyclophyllum jasminifolium, commonly known as the jasmine-leaf cyclophyllum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and fragrant leaves that emit a jasmine-like aroma. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the late spring and early summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Cyclophyllum jasminifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
