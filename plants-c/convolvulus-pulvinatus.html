Convolvulus pulvinatus

Convolvulus pulvinatus, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Its flowers open in the morning and close at dusk, a characteristic known as nyctinasty.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
