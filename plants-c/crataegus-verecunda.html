Crataegus verecunda

Crataegus verecunda, commonly known as the Pacific crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters and features thorny branches. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or yellow berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and it is often found in moist, shady habitats.

The Pacific crabapple is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
