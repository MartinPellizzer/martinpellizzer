Cyperus gradatus

Cyperus gradatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top of its stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along the edges of ponds, ditches, and wetlands. Cyperus gradatus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils.

Its growth habit makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various small organisms.
