Crinum kunthianum

Crinum kunthianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved swamp lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has strap-shaped, narrow leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in wetland environments. It thrives in moist, swampy areas and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

Crinum kunthianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to provide habitat for aquatic wildlife.
