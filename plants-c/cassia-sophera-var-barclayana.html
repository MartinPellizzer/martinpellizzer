Cassia sophera var. barclayana

Cassia sophera var. barclayana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow floral spikes that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
