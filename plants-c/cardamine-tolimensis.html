Cardamine tolimensis

Cardamine tolimensis, commonly known as the Tolima buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous areas. It has a short flowering period, usually in the spring, and is known for its ornamental value.
