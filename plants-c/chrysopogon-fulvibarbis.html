Chrysopogon fulvibarbis

Chrysopogon fulvibarbis, commonly known as the golden grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a golden hue on the underside. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
