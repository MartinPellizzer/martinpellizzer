Calophyllum dispar

Calophyllum dispar, commonly known as the white cedar, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
