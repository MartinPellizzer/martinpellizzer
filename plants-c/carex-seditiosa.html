Carex seditiosa

Carex seditiosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinct midrib and a waxy coating that helps it retain moisture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.
