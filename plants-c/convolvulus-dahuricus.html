Convolvulus dahuricus

Convolvulus dahuricus, commonly known as the Siberian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus dahuricus is known for its resilience to harsh climates and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
