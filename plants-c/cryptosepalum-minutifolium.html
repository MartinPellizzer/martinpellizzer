Cryptosepalum minutifolium

Cryptosepalum minutifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved cryptosepalum, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tiny, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a subtle beauty to its dense foliage. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires careful protection to ensure its survival.
