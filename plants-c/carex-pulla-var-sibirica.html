Carex pulla var. sibirica

Carex pulla var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
