Coffea arabica var. typica

Coffea arabica var. typica, commonly known as the typical Arabica, is a variety of the coffee plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia and Sudan.

It is characterized by its small, elongated berries and aromatic, complex flavor profile. The plant typically grows at high altitudes and requires a tropical climate with consistent rainfall. It has a bushy growth habit and produces two crops per year.

Due to its genetic purity, it is often considered a foundational variety in coffee cultivation.
