Ceratophyllum laeve

Ceratophyllum laeve, commonly known as the spiked watermilfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has whorled leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant lacks true stems and instead has a branching, root-like structure that anchors it in the sediment. Its small, green flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually.

Ceratophyllum laeve plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.
