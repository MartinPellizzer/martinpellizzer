Campnosperma auriculatum var. wallichii

Campnosperma auriculatum var. wallichii, commonly known as the Wallich's broadleaf acacia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are often divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow to golden-orange flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
