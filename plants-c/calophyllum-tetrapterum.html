Calophyllum tetrapterum

Calophyllum tetrapterum, commonly known as the four-winged calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in whorls of four, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits that contain a single seed. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

Calophyllum tetrapterum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
