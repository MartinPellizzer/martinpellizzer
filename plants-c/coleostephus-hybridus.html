Coleostephus hybridus

Coleostephus hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
