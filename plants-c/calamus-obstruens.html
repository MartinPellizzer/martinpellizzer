Calamus obstruens

Calamus obstruens, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Calamus obstruens is often found in wetland or swampy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
