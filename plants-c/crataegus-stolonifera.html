Crataegus stolonifera

Crataegus stolonifera, commonly known as the sandcherry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Crataegus stolonifera is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
