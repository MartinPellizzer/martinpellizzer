Cucurbita pepo var. luteoalba

Cucurbita pepo var. luteoalba, commonly known as the yellow marrow squash, is a variant of the common squash species characterized by its bright yellow to orange flesh and pale green to white rind.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and gourds. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and typically bears fruits that are elongated or slightly spherical in shape. It is typically cultivated for its edible seeds and flesh, which have a mild, sweet flavor.

This variety is well-suited for warm climates and requires a long growing season with ample sunlight and water.
