Calanthe × splendens

Calanthe × splendens, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Calanthe triplicata and Calanthe formosa.

It is a herbaceous plant with rhizomatous growth, typically found in shaded forest environments. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with brightly colored, spurred flowers that range from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calanthe × splendens is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
