Cichorium cicorea

Cichorium cicorea, commonly known as chicory, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in salads or as a coffee substitute. The plant has a taproot that is rich in inulin, a prebiotic fiber beneficial for gut health. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown in cooler seasons.

Cichorium cicorea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
