Cephalophyllum aurantiacum

Cephalophyllum aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange-flowered candelabra cactus, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It features a branching structure with cylindrical stems that bear clusters of bright orange flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy stems adapted for storing water, typical of desert environments. Its flowers are borne on terminal racemes, giving it a candle-like appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid climates.
