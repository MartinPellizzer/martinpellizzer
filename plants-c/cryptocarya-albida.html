Cryptocarya albida

Cryptocarya albida, commonly known as the white-flowered cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, typically appearing in clusters during the rainy season.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
