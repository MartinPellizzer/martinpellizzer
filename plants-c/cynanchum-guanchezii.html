Cynanchum guanchezii

Cynanchum guanchezii, commonly known as the Canary Island milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and specialized floral structures. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh climate. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive hooded shape that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
