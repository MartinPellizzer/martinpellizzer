Croton tridentatus

Croton tridentatus, commonly known as the three-toothed croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with three lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton tridentatus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
