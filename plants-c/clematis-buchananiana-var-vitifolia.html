Clematis buchananiana var. vitifolia

Clematis buchananiana var. vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves that are often described as resembling grape leaves, with elongated, serrated leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
