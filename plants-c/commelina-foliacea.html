Commelina foliacea

Commelina foliacea, commonly known as the leafy commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, three-lobed bracts that surround the flowers. Commelina foliacea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems as a food source for various insects and birds.
