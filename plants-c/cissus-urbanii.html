It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

Cissus urbanii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
