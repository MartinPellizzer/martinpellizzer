Crataegus livoniana

Crataegus livoniana, commonly known as the Livonia hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
