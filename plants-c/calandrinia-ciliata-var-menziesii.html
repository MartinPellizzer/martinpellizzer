Calandrinia ciliata var. menziesii

Calandrinia ciliata var. menziesii, commonly known as the coast morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically covered in fine, thread-like hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild temperatures and salt spray.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
