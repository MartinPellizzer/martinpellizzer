Calamus microcarpus

Calamus microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited arrowroot, is a member of the Arecaceae (palm) family.

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant features a slender, creeping rhizome with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its small, clustered inflorescences produce fragrant flowers, followed by small, round fruits.

The plant is valued for its rhizome, which is used as a starch source and in various medicinal applications.
