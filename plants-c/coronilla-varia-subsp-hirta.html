Coronilla varia subsp. hirta

Coronilla varia subsp.

hirta, commonly known as hairy crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its trifoliate leaves consist of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy texture.

This species is often used in erosion control due to its vigorous growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
