Cacalia baccharoides

Cacalia baccharoides, commonly known as the silverleaf bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are silvery-gray, which helps it reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

Cacalia baccharoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
