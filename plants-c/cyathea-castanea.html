Cyathea castanea

Cyathea castanea, commonly known as the chestnut tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Cyathea castanea plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, providing habitat and contributing to forest structure.
