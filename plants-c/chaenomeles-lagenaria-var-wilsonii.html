Chaenomeles lagenaria var. wilsonii

Chaenomeles lagenaria var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's Japanese quince, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental, spiky, orange-red fruits that appear in late autumn. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring, which are typically pink or white. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

This variety is valued for its winter interest and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
