Catha pyracantha

Catha pyracantha, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Catha pyracantha is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
