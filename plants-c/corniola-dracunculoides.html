Corniola dracunculoides

Corniola dracunculoides, commonly known as the dragon's tongue plant, is a unique member of the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that resemble tiny dragons, hence its common name. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
