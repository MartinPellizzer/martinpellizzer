Cinnamomum nitidum var. oblongifolium

Cinnamomum nitidum var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed cinnamon tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, glossy green on top, and have a distinctive cinnamon-like scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
