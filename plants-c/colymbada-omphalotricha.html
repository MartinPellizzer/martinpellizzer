Colymbada omphalotricha

Colymbada omphalotricha, commonly known as the golden star wort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
