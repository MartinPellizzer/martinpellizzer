Clematis ornata

Clematis ornata, commonly known as the white clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis ornata is relatively hardy and can be propagated through stem cuttings or seeds.
