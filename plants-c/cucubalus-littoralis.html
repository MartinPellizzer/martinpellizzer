Cucubalus littoralis

Cucubalus littoralis, commonly known as the coastal soursop, is a tropical plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fruit with a spiny exterior. The plant thrives in saline and humid environments, making it well adapted to coastal ecosystems. Its fruit is edible, though often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local biodiversity.
