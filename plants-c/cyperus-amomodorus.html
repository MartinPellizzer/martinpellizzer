Cyperus amomodorus

Cyperus amomodorus, commonly known as swamp grass or narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often reddish or purplish in hue. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Cyperus amomodorus is widely distributed across tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.
