Cirsium morisianum

Cirsium morisianum, commonly known as the Morisian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its robust, upright growth habit. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive serrated edge.

Cirsium morisianum is often considered an invasive species in areas outside its native range due to its rapid spread and competitive nature.
