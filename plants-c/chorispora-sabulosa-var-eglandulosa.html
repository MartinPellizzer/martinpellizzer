Chorispora sabulosa var. eglandulosa

Chorispora sabulosa var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular chorispora, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is distinguished by the presence of glandular structures on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its unique botanical features.
