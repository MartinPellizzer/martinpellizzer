Carex remota var. subloliacea

Carex remota var. subloliacea, commonly known as the slender tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks, and is often found in temperate regions.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
