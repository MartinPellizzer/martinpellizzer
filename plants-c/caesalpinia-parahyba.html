Caesalpinia parahyba

Caesalpinia parahyba, commonly known as parahyba or Brazilian peacock flower, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery, red and yellow floral spikes that bloom in late summer. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that split open when mature.
