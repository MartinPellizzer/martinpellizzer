Cordia pelida

Cordia pelida, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapting well to drought conditions. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Cordia pelida is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments.
