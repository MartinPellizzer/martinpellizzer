Cyperus lamprocarpus

Cyperus lamprocarpus, commonly known as the silver rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy, silver-gray coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus lamprocarpus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a water garden plant.
