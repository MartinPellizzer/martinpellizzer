Coris monspeliensis var. nigromaculata

Coris monspeliensis var. nigromaculata, commonly known as the black-spotted coris, is a variant of the coris plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive black spots on the leaves, which set it apart from other varieties. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has succulent leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

This variety is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
