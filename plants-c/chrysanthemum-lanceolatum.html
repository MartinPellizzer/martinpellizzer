Chrysanthemum lanceolatum

Chrysanthemum lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clustered yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Chrysanthemum lanceolatum is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
