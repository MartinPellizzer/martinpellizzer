Crataegus monogyna f. globosa

Crataegus monogyna f. globosa, commonly known as the globular hawthorn, is a compact, rounded shrub characterized by its dense, spherical growth habit.

It features small, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in clusters, and its flowers are white with five petals, appearing in early spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is well-suited for small gardens or as a specimen plant due to its ornamental shape and hardy nature.
