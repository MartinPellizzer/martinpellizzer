Cephalanthus occidentalis var. californicus

Cephalanthus occidentalis var. californicus, commonly known as the California sweetshrub, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a sweet scent when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
