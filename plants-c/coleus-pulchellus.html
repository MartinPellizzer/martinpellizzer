Coleus pulchellus

Coleus pulchellus, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a tropical perennial shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that range from deep red to purple and green, often with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and containers. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or white.

Coleus pulchellus thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.
