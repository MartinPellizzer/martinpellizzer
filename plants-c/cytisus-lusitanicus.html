Cytisus lusitanicus

Cytisus lusitanicus, commonly known as the Portuguese broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Cytisus lusitanicus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
