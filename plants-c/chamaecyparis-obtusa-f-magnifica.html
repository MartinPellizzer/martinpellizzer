Chamaecyparis obtusa f. magnifica

Chamaecyparis obtusa f. magnifica, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or giant Japanese cedar, is a variant of the Japanese cedar tree.

It is characterized by its tall, conical shape and dense, dark green foliage. The plant features scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the branches, giving it a compact and ornamental appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green cones, typical of the cypress family.

This cultivar is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
