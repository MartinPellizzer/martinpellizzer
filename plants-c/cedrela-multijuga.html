Cedrela multijuga

Cedrela multijuga, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar or yellow cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in panicles, followed by woody, oblong fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Cedrela multijuga is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

It also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
