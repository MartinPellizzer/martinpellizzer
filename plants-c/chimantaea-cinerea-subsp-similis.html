Chimantaea cinerea subsp. similis

Chimantaea cinerea subsp.

similis, commonly known as the pale chimantaea, is a member of the Rosaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, white to pale pink flowers.

Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this subspecies thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
