Cucumis omissus

Cucumis omissus, commonly known as the desert cucumber, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that are typically less than an inch in diameter. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it thrives in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

The plant is often found growing in rocky soils and along stream banks in its native habitat.
