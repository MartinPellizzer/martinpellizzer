Calchas atropurpureus

Calchas atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple calchas, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate growing conditions.
