Clethra paniculata

Clethra paniculata, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that are typically green when mature. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a serrated margin.

Clethra paniculata is often found in moist woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade.
