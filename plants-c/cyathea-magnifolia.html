Cyathea magnifolia

Cyathea magnifolia, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a large evergreen fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer of dead leaves. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. Cyathea magnifolia reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its life cycle involves a distinct sporophyte and gametophyte stage.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
