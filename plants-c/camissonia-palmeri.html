Camissonia palmeri

Camissonia palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Camissoniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It blooms in early spring, often in response to seasonal rainfall.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
