Chlorophytum norlindhii

Chlorophytum norlindhii, commonly known as the "Norlindhii Spider Plant," is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its long, arching leaves that resemble spider legs, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Its ability to purify air and tolerate a range of environmental conditions makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
