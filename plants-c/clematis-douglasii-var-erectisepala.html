Clematis douglasii var. erectisepala

Clematis douglasii var. erectisepala, commonly known as the Douglas's clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The flowers are showy, with five to seven sepals that are often erect, giving the plant its variety name. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for trellises or as a ground cover in moist, well-drained soils.
