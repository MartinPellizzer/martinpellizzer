Commiphora pilosa var. meyeri-johannis

Commiphora pilosa var. meyeri-johannis, commonly known as the Meyer-Johannis myrrh tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous exudates, which are used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant has rough, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by three-winged fruits.

This variant is noted for its distinct morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
