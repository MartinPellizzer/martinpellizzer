Ceropegia mozambicensis var. ulugurensis

Ceropegia mozambicensis var. ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It is a succulent species with fleshy, trailing stems and small, tubular flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and bright, indirect light. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insects, and it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it is considered rare and is of interest to botanists due to its restricted geographic range.
