Chordifex reseminans

Chordifex reseminans, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread slowly through underground stems. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a soft, feathery texture that contributes to its ethereal appearance.

Due to its rare occurrence and specific habitat requirements, Chordifex reseminans is considered a delicate and specialized botanical specimen.
