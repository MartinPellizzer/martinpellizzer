Crepidorhopalon affinis

Crepidorhopalon affinis, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically features small, fleshy, alternate leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

Crepidorhopalon affinis is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
