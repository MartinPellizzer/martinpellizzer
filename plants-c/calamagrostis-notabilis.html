Calamagrostis notabilis

Calamagrostis notabilis, commonly known as the golden reed, is a tall grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow plumes that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its tall, slender stalks can reach heights of up to six feet, making it a striking feature in natural landscapes.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
