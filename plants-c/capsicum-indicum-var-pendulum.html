Capsicum indicum var. pendulum

Capsicum indicum var. pendulum, commonly known as the hanging chili or pendulous chili, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping fruit clusters that hang from the plant, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that range in color from green to red as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of spicy peppers.
