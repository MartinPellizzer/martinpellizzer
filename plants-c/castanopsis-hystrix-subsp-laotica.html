Castanopsis hystrix subsp. laotica

Castanopsis hystrix subsp.

laotica, commonly known as the Laotian talaud, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage.

The plant produces small, cup-shaped fruits that contain one or two seeds, typical of the Fagaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils within forest ecosystems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
