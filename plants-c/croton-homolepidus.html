Croton homolepidus

Croton homolepidus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its diverse foliage, with leaves ranging from simple to deeply lobed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Croton homolepidus is valued for its ornamental leaves and has traditional uses in some regions.
