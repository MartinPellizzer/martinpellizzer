Campanula pyramidiflora

Campanula pyramidiflora, commonly known as the pyramidal bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula pyramidiflora is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
