Convolvulus bicolor

Convolvulus bicolor, commonly known as the two-color morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned flowers, which range from white to pink and then to purple. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves and has a trailing growth habit, often found in rocky or dry soils. It blooms throughout the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Convolvulus bicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
