Cervia saturejifolia

Cervia saturejifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved satureja, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.
