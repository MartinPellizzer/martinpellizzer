Christella × parahispidula

Christella × parahispidula, commonly known as the hybrid rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a natural hybrid between Christella hispidula and Christella monogyna, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
