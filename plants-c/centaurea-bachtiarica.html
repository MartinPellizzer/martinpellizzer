Centaurea bachtiarica

Centaurea bachtiarica, commonly known as the Persian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple composite flowers and serrated, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
