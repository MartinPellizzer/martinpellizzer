Cucurbita maxima cancroides

Cucurbita maxima cancroides, commonly known as the ornamental pumpkin, is a variant of the common pumpkin species.

It is characterized by its large, vibrant, and often uniquely shaped fruits that come in a variety of colors such as orange, yellow, and white. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and squashes. It typically grows as a trailing or climbing vine with large, heart-shaped leaves.

This cultivar is primarily grown for its ornamental value rather than for culinary use.
