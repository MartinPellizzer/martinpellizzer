Cornopteris decurrenti-alata f. angustifrons

Cornopteris decurrenti-alata f. angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-fronded rock fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to regions with moist, shaded habitats. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive decurrent (running down the stem) shape. This fern exhibits a compact growth habit, often forming dense clusters on rocky or shaded soil.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and woodland ecosystems.
