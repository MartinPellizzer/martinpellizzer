Chrysothamnus tortifolius

Chrysothamnus tortifolius, commonly known as the golden sagebrush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and silver-gray foliage, which are typical of the sagebrush family. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open plains and rocky slopes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
