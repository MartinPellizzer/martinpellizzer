Cucurbita moschata var. magna

Cucurbita moschata var. magna, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a cultivar of the species Cucurbita moschata.

It is characterized by its large, spherical shape and thick, rugged rind, which can vary in color from white to orange or even green. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like squash and gourds. It thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season to reach its full size.

This variety is often cultivated for competitive pumpkin growing events and for its high nutritional value.
