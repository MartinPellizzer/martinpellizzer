Campanula rapunculus f. nitida

Campanula rapunculus f. nitida, commonly known as the silver bell bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers with white to pale blue petals. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly grown in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
