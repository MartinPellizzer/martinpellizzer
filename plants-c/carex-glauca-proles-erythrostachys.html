Carex glauca proles erythrostachys

Carex glauca proles erythrostachys, commonly known as the red-stalked sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its reddish stems and blue-green foliage make it a distinctive and ornamental species.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
