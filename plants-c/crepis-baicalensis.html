Crepis baicalensis

Crepis baicalensis, commonly known as the Baikal hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and serrated. It produces small, spiky achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Crepis baicalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
