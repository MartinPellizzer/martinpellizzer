Cynanchum insulanum

Cynanchum insulanum, commonly known as the island milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinctive scent when crushed.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters, contributing to its role in supporting local pollinators.
