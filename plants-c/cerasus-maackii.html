Cerasus maackii

Cerasus maackii, commonly known as the Manchurian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart red berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms and autumn fruit.

Cerasus maackii is also used in traditional medicine and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
