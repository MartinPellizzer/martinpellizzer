Chondrilla lutea

Chondrilla lutea, commonly known as the yellow goatweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Chondrilla lutea is often considered a weed in agricultural areas but has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
