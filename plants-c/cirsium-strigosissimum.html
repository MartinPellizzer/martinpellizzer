Cirsium strigosissimum

Cirsium strigosissimum, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and the flowers are usually purple or pink, forming a spherical cluster.

The rough thistle is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and astringent effects.
