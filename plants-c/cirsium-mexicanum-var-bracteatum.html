Cirsium mexicanum var. bracteatum

Cirsium mexicanum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the Mexican thistle, is a perennial plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its prickly nature, it is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
