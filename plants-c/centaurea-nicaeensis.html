Centaurea nicaeensis

Centaurea nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaea thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and clusters of purple to pinkish-blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a fuzzy texture. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Centaurea nicaeensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
