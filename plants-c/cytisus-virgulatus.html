Cytisus virgulatus

Cytisus virgulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf broom, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cytisus virgulatus is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils.
