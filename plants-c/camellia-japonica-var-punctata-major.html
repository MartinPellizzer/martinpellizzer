Camellia japonica var. punctata-major

Camellia japonica var. punctata-major, commonly known as the spotted camellia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pale pink, often with distinctive spots or markings. The leaves are glossy, dark green, and elliptical in shape, providing a lush backdrop for the blooms. This variety thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its winter blooming habit adds seasonal interest, particularly in regions with mild winters.
