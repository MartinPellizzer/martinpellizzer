Campanula macdougalii

Campanula macdougalii, commonly known as the MacDougal's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula macdougalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
