Convolvulus glandulifer

Convolvulus glandulifer, commonly known as the glandular morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are often covered in glandular structures. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread across various habitats.
