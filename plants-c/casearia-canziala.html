Casearia canziala

Casearia canziala, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible for humans and animals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Casearia canziala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
