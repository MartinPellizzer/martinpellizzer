Cuphea ovalifolia

Cuphea ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cuphea ovalifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
