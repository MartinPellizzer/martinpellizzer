Celtis reticulata var. vestita

Celtis reticulata var. vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive velvety underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, pale green fruits that mature to a dark blue. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for wildlife.
