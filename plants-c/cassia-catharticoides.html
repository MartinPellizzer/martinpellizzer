Cassia catharticoides

Cassia catharticoides, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their laxative properties.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it a useful species in reforestation and soil stabilization efforts.
