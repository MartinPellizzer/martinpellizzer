Coleus strictipes

Coleus strictipes, commonly known as the upright coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in a variety of colors, though the flowers themselves are small and inconspicuous. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and often have a velvety texture.

Coleus strictipes is often grown as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
