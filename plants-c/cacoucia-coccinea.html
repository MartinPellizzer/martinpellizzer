Cacoucia coccinea

Cacoucia coccinea, commonly known as the red cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red pods and small, edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The botanical features of Cacoucia coccinea make it well-suited for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
