Chrysophyllum brevicaule

Chrysophyllum brevicaule, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature, with a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
