Capparis tenuisiliqua

Capparis tenuisiliqua, commonly known as the slender leaf caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, exhibiting succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Capparis tenuisiliqua is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
