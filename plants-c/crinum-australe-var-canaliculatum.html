Crinum australe var. canaliculatum

Crinum australe var. canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed crinum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, often fragrant leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with a strong scent, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
