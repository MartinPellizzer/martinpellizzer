Circaea lutetiana f. albiflora

Circaea lutetiana f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered wood sorrel, is a subspecies of the European wood sorrel.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is characterized by its delicate appearance and aromatic foliage.

It is often found in the temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
