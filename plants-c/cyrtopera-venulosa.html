Cyrtopera venulosa

Cyrtopera venulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into elongated, hollow seed stalks. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields. It has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cyrtopera venulosa is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
