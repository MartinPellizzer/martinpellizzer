Crataegus comans

Crataegus comans, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features thorny branches, simple ovate leaves with serrated edges, and showy white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pome fruits and compound inflorescences.
