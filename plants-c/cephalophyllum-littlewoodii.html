Cephalophyllum littlewoodii

Cephalophyllum littlewoodii, commonly known as the Littlewood's cephalophyllum, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, flattened, disc-like stems that bear clusters of vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a bold splash of color to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a valuable and delicate species in botanical studies.
