Cleistes liliastrum

Cleistes liliastrum, commonly known as the white phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Cleistes liliastrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
