Capparis spinosa var. herbacea

Capparis spinosa var. herbacea, commonly known as the shrub caper, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces edible, yellowish buds that are used in culinary applications. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to arid conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
