Circaea canadensis subsp. canadensis

Circaea canadensis subsp.

canadensis, commonly known as Canadian white-cup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

The plant features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct floral structure and adaptability to wet soil conditions.
