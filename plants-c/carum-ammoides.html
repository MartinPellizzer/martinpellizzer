Carum ammoides

Carum ammoides, commonly known as wild cumin or Egyptian cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are aromatic and used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, spicy scent reminiscent of cumin.

Carum ammoides thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
