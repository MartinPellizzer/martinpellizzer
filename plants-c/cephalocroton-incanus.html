Cephalocroton incanus

Cephalocroton incanus, commonly known as the hairy headed croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often reddish-brown stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a rough texture.

Cephalocroton incanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
