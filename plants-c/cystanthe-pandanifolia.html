Cystanthe pandanifolia

Cystanthe pandanifolia, commonly known as the pandan-like cactus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems that resemble the leaves of the pandan plant. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Cystanthe pandanifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
