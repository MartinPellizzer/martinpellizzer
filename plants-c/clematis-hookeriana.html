Clematis hookeriana

Clematis hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a hairy underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vigorous growth and ability to climb trellises or fences.
