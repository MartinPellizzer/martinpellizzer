Cassebeeria junghuhniana

Cassebeeria junghuhniana, commonly known as the Jung-huhn's cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in terminal clusters, and it produces elongated, woody pods containing seeds.

It is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
