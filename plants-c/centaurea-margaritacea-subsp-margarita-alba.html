Centaurea margaritacea subsp. margarita-alba

Centaurea margaritacea subsp.

margarita-alba, commonly known as the white sweet William, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, adding to its ornamental appeal. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical name reflects its historical association with the margarita, a type of pearl, due to the color and structure of its flowers.
