Crataegus semilobata

Crataegus semilobata, commonly known as the semi-lobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with shallow lobes, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and open areas.
