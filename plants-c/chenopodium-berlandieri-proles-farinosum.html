Chenopodium berlandieri proles farinosum

Chenopodium berlandieri proles farinosum, commonly known as tumbleweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and production of dry, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant is native to North America and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically succulent and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils, making it both a pioneer species and a potential invasive plant.
