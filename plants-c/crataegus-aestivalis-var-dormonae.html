Crataegus aestivalis var. dormonae

Crataegus aestivalis var. dormonae, commonly known as the early summer hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and white or pinkish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
