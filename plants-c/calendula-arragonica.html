Calendula arragonica

Calendula arragonica, commonly known as the Armenian marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange petals and dark brown central disk, which resemble those of other calendula species. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calendula arragonica is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
