Cassia elachistophylla

Cassia elachistophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces bright yellow flowers in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
