Cyperus globosus

Cyperus globosus, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features cylindrical, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish flowers that develop into three-lobed, spiky fruits. It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along water edges.

Cyperus globosus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability to moist soils.
