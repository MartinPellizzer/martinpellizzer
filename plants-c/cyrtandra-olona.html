Cyrtandra olona

Cyrtandra olona, commonly known as the Hawaiian watermelon or Mauna Loa watermelon, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Hawaiian Islands, particularly found on the Big Island. The plant features attractive, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Cyrtandra olona is typically found in wet forest habitats, growing near streams and in shaded areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also culturally significant to Native Hawaiians.
