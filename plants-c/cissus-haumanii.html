Cissus haumanii

Cissus haumanii, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing cissus, is a woody vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically eaten raw. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to climb and its ornamental foliage make it a versatile plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
