Carex hypolytropsis

Carex hypolytropsis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a species of sedge found in wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown, depending on the season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Carex hypolytropsis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
