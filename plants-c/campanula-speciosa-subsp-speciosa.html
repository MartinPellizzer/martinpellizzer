Campanula speciosa subsp. speciosa

Campanula speciosa subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
