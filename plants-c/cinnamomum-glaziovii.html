Cinnamomum glaziovii

Cinnamomum glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
