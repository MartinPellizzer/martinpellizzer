Calliandra hymenaeodes

Calliandra hymenaeodes, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences. The plant features bipinnate leaves and produces clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and attractive foliage.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
