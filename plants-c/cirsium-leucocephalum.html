Cirsium leucocephalum

Cirsium leucocephalum, commonly known as the whitehead thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a distinctive purple to pink flower head that resembles a small crown. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Cirsium leucocephalum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
