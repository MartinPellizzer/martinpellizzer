Cytisogenista lusitanica

Cytisogenista lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a food source for various insects and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
