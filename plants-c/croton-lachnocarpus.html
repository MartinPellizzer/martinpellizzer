Croton lachnocarpus

Croton lachnocarpus, commonly known as the stinking croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, unpleasant odor. The plant produces bright yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its toxicity, it is not recommended for cultivation in residential areas.
