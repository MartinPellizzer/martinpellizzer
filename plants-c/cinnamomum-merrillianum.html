Cinnamomum merrillianum

Cinnamomum merrillianum, commonly known as the Philippine cinnamon or Bintotan, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that contain a single seed. Its bark is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
