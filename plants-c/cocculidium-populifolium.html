Cocculidium populifolium

Cocculidium populifolium, commonly known as the poplar-leaved cocculidium, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble those of poplar trees, hence its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Cocculidium populifolium is often found in moist, open habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
