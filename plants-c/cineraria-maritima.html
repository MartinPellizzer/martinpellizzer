Cineraria maritima

Cineraria maritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it withstand harsh maritime conditions. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing along cliffs and dunes. Its flowers bloom in spring and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cineraria maritima is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.
