Calluna vulgaris var. atrorubens

Calluna vulgaris var. atrorubens, commonly known as the purple heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to red, depending on the variety. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral displays.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
