Curcuma attenuata

Curcuma attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other curcuma species.

The botanical features of Curcuma attenuata make it an important plant in both horticulture and pharmacology.
