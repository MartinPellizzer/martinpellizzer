Cistus nervosus

Cistus nervosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and aromatic, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Cistus nervosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
