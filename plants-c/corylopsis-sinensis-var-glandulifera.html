Corylopsis sinensis var. glandulifera

Corylopsis sinensis var. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange-red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in small glands, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and compact growth form.
