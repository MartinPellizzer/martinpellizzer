Carlina involucrata subsp. libanotica

Carlina involucrata subsp.

libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon carline thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, showy blue to purple flower heads.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are surrounded by an involucre of spiny, leaf-like bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
