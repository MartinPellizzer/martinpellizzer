Cirsium taliense

Cirsium taliense, commonly known as the Taliense thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to regions in East Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy areas and is often found in mountainous regions. It has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, though its use remains largely underexplored.

Cirsium taliense is an important species in the study of thistle diversity and ecological adaptation.
