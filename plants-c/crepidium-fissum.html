Crepidium fissum

Crepidium fissum, commonly known as the crested orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, crested floral structure, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Crepidium fissum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
