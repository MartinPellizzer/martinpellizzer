Cytisus nejceffii

Cytisus nejceffii, commonly known as the Nejceff's broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the stems are often woody and covered in fine hairs.

Cytisus nejceffii is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
