Carex pseudoheleonastes

Carex pseudoheleonastes, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often hidden among the foliage, making it less conspicuous. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Carex pseudoheleonastes is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
