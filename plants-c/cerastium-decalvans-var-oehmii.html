Cerastium decalvans var. oehmii

Cerastium decalvans var. oehmii, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in clusters. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
