Cyperus radians var. compositus

Cyperus radians var. compositus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and riverbanks. The plant has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-lobed capsules.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various wetland habitats, contributing to its ecological significance in aquatic ecosystems.
