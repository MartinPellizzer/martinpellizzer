Cucubalus montanus

Cucubalus montanus, commonly known as the mountain cucumber, is a climbing vine native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and gourds. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, edible fruits. Its botanical features include a robust stem with tendrils for climbing and a distinctive, warty surface on its fruit.

The plant is adapted to cool, mountainous climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
