Comesperma integerrimum

Comesperma integerrimum, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery leaves. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is adapted to sandy soils and salt-laden environments, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in coastal ecosystems and a symbol of the region's unique flora.
