Cascaronia astragalina

Cascaronia astragalina, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Cascaronia astragalina plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in shrubland and woodland habitats.
