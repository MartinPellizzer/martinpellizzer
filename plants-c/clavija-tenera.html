Clavija tenera

Clavija tenera, commonly known as the Canary Island cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a short, thick stem with spines and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant has a rosette growth pattern, allowing it to store water efficiently in its fleshy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it well-suited for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
