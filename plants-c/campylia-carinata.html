Campylia carinata

Campylia carinata, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are small and typically green or brown, arranged in dense clusters.

Campylia carinata thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.
