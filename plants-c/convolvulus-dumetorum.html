Convolvulus dumetorum

Convolvulus dumetorum, commonly known as devil's weed or poison weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
