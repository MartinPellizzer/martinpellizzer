Capparis malabaria

Capparis malabaria, commonly known as the Malabar caper, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and moist forest areas, characterized by its woody stems and succulent leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by small, edible berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
