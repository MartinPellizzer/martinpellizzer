Convolvulus densiflorus

Convolvulus densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
