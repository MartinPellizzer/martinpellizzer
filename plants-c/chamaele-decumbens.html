Chamaele decumbens

Chamaele decumbens, commonly known as the creeping chamaeleon, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in a prostrate or decumbent manner, with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
