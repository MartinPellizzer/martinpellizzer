Croton insularis

Croton insularis, commonly known as the Island Croton, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display a mix of green, yellow, red, and orange hues. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically green or yellow, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Croton insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
