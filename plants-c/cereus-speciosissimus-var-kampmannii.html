Cereus speciosissimus var. kampmannii

Cereus speciosissimus var. kampmannii, commonly known as the Kampmann's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and spiny, cylindrical ribs. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and indoor cultivation.
