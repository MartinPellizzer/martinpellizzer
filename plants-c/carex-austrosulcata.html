Carex austrosulcata

Carex austrosulcata, commonly known as the southern sulcate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland or swampy areas. It produces small, clustered flowers in early to mid-summer, followed by distinctive three-sided seed capsules.

Carex austrosulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
