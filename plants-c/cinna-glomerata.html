Cinna glomerata

Cinna glomerata, commonly known as clustered cinnamon or cinnamon grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Cinna glomerata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian and wetland ecosystems.
