Cheiridopsis acuminata

Cheiridopsis acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the winter months, adding visual interest to its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Cheiridopsis acuminata is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
