Cuphea hirticaulis

Cuphea hirticaulis, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, textured appearance. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and prolonged blooming period.
