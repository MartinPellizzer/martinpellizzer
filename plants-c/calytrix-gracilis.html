Calytrix gracilis

Calytrix gracilis, commonly known as the slender calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Calytrix gracilis is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
