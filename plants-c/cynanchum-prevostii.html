Cynanchum prevostii

Cynanchum prevostii, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed or the arrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, attracting various pollinators. Its milky sap contains latex, which can be toxic to some animals and humans.

Cynanchum prevostii is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates.
