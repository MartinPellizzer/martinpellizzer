Capparis populifolia

Capparis populifolia, commonly known as the Indian bladdernut, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

The botanical features of Capparis populifolia make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
