Campomanesia gardneriana

Campomanesia gardneriana, commonly known as the Brazilian cheese fruit, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to yellow, fleshy fruits resembling tiny cheese wheels. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisine.

It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
