Clusia oedematopoidea

Clusia oedematopoidea, commonly known as the fat-leaf plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that store water, adapting to its often arid environment. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Clusia oedematopoidea is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
