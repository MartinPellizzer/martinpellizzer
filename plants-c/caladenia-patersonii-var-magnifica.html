Caladenia patersonii var. magnifica

Caladenia patersonii var. magnifica, commonly known as the Paterson's glory, is a striking orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow petals, which are adorned with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a wasp. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats, often in areas with full sun exposure. Its flowering period occurs during late autumn to early winter, and it produces a single, showy orchid flower per stem.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest to botanists due to its unique floral morphology and ecological adaptations.
