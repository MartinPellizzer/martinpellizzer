Chaetanthera elata

Chaetanthera elata, commonly known as the slender moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are hairy to the touch. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, dry seeds.
