Calamus giganteus

Calamus giganteus, commonly known as giant calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae) and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a rhizomatous plant that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and have been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

Calamus giganteus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
