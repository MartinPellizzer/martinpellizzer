Ceropegia intermedia

Ceropegia intermedia, commonly known as the African violet or velvet leaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia intermedia is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
