Coffea travancorensis

Coffea travancorensis, commonly known as the Kerala coffee or Travancore coffee, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India, particularly in the state of Kerala. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, known as a berry, contains two seeds that are used to make coffee.

Due to its unique flavor profile and limited cultivation, it is considered a rare and valuable coffee species.
