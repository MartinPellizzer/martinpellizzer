Citriobatus multiflorus var. intermedius

Citriobatus multiflorus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate citriobatus, is a unique variant of the citriobatus species characterized by its distinct floral and foliar features.

This plant exhibits a moderate growth habit, typically reaching heights of 1 to 2 meters, with lanceolate leaves that are slightly serrated along the edges. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and display a range of colors from pale yellow to orange, depending on environmental conditions. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Botanically, it is notable for its hybrid origin, which contributes to its genetic diversity and ornamental value.
