Cryptochilus meirax

Cryptochilus meirax, commonly known as the Meirax orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique, elongated pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
