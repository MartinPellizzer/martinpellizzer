Cestrum mononeurum

Cestrum mononeurum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and have five petals, while the fruit is a red, fleshy berry.

Cestrum mononeurum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal flowering habit.
