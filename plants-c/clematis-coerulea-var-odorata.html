Clematis coerulea var. odorata

Clematis coerulea var. odorata, commonly known as the sweet scent clematis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and a fragrant, almost honey-like aroma. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring sturdy stems and compound leaves. Its blooming season occurs in late spring to early summer, producing showy flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and pleasant fragrance.
