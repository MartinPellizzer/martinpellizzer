Cyclamen coum var. caucasicum

Cyclamen coum var. caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces striking, spurred flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Cyclamen coum var. caucasicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
