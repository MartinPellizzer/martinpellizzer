Centaurea gueryi

Centaurea gueryi, commonly known as Guery's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Centaurea gueryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
