Cnicus carniolicus

Cnicus carniolicus, commonly known as the Carpathian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a rough, prickly texture. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive lobed shape.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
