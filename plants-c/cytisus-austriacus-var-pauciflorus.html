Cytisus austriacus var. pauciflorus

Cytisus austriacus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the Austrian broom, is a shrub native to Central Europe.

It features slender, woody stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with several small leaflets, and it produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
