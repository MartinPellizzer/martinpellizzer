Cyanea Pilosa Subsp. Longipedunculata

Cyanea pilosa subsp. longipedunculata is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands. It belongs to the orchid family and is known for its striking, showy blooms that appear in late spring. This subspecies is distinguished by its elongated peduncles, which support its fragrant, violet-colored flowers. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts. Its unique beauty and ecological significance make it a valuable subject for botanical study and preservation.
