Calamus multisetosus

Calamus multisetosus, commonly known as the hairy calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, creeping rhizomes and narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Calamus multisetosus is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
