Cerasus erythrocarpa f. media

Cerasus erythrocarpa f. media, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to China.

It features small, bright red fruits that are edible but typically not used for culinary purposes. The plant has slender, arching branches with dense foliage that provides attractive winter interest. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact size and vibrant fruit make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
