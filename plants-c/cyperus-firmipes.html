Cyperus firmipes

Cyperus firmipes, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along the edges of ponds or in swampy areas.

Cyperus firmipes is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
