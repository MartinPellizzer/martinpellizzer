Cissus terengganuensis

Cissus terengganuensis, commonly known as the Terengganu devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can develop aerial roots, allowing it to climb and attach to surfaces.

Cissus terengganuensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
