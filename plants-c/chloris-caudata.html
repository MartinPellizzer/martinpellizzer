Chloris caudata

Chloris caudata, commonly known as the tufted grass or curlytail grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, curly, hair-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Chloris caudata is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
