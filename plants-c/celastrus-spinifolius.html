Celastrus spinifolius

Celastrus spinifolius, commonly known as prickly currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
