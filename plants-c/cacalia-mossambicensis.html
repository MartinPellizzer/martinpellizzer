Cacalia mossambicensis

Cacalia mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Cacalia mossambicensis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland areas.
