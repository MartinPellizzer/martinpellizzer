Carex franklinii

Carex franklinii, commonly known as Franklin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters, which are typically brown to reddish in color. Native to North America, this plant thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy and grows in dense clumps.

The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that contribute to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
