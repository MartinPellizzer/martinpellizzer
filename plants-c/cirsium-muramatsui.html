Cirsium muramatsui

Cirsium muramatsui, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are visited by various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
