Croton mavoravina var. concinnus

Croton mavoravina var. concinnus, commonly known as the velvet croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant coloration.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
