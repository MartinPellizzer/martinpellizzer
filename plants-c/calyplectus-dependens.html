Calyplectus dependens

Calyplectus dependens, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that hang from slender stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple.

Calyplectus dependens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
