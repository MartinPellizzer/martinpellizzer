Cardamine calthifolia

Cardamine calthifolia, commonly known as the meadow water-cress, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a peppery aroma, and its small white flowers are arranged in clusters at the top of slender stems. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other notable species like buttercups and anemones.

Cardamine calthifolia is often found in calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
