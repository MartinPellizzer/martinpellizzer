Carum aromaticum

Carum aromaticum, commonly known as Persian cumin, is an annual herb native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces oval, ridged seeds that are widely used as a spice in various cuisines. Its leaves are also edible and can be used fresh or dried in cooking.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a versatile crop for temperate climates.
