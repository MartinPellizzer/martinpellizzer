Cordia suckertii var. exasperata

Cordia suckertii var. exasperata, commonly known as the African satinflower, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture.
