Cephaelis umbellata

Cephaelis umbellata, commonly known as the white sand violet, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in umbel-like clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cephaelis umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
