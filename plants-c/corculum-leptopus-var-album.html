Corculum leptopus var. album

Corculum leptopus var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered coral tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, giving the plant a delicate, lace-like appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, providing a contrasting texture to the blossoms. This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical regions.
