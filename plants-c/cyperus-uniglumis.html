Cyperus uniglumis

Cyperus uniglumis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually unbranched and have a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of the sedge family.

Cyperus uniglumis produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, often found in dense, grass-like clusters.
