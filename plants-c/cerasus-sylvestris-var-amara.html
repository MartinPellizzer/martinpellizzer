Cerasus sylvestris var. amara

Cerasus sylvestris var. amara, commonly known as the wild cherry or Prunus amara, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark red to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white flowers in early spring.

The fruit is edible but often has a tart flavor, and it has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
