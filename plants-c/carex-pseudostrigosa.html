Carex pseudostrigosa

Carex pseudostrigosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically wind-pollinated, a common trait among sedges. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex pseudostrigosa is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
