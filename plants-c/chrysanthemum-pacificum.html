Chrysanthemum pacificum

Chrysanthemum pacificum, commonly known as the Pacific chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
