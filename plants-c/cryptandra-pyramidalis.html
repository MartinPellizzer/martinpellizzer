Cryptandra pyramidalis

Cryptandra pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid leaf cryptandra, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. Cryptandra pyramidalis is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native fauna.
