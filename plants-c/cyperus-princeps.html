Cyperus princeps

Cyperus princeps, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus princeps plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
