Chamelaucium uncinatum var. leptophyllum

Chamelaucium uncinatum var. leptophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong winds. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune ecosystems.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
