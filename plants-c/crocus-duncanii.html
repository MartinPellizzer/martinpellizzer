Crocus duncanii

Crocus duncanii, commonly known as the Duncan crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which are borne on short, hairy stems. Its bulbs are typically small and have a fibrous covering, and the leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Crocus duncanii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
