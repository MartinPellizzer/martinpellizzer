Cirsium lanceolatum var. sphaeroidale

Cirsium lanceolatum var. sphaeroidale, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, blue to purple flower heads that resemble small globes, surrounded by spiky, gray-green bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

The species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
