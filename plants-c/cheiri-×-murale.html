Cheiri × murale

Cheiri × murale, commonly known as the hybrid of Cheiri and murale, is a flowering plant that exhibits characteristics of both parent species.

It typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It is native to regions in southern and eastern Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
