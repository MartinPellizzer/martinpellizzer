Corybas amabilis

Corybas amabilis, commonly known as the sweet lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its dense, dark green, strap-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, slender flowering stem that bears a loose cluster of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Corybas amabilis is notable for its unique morphology and is often found growing in association with other native flora.
