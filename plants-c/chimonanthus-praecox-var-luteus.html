Chimonanthus praecox var. luteus

Chimonanthus praecox var. luteus, commonly known as the yellow fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, before the leaves emerge. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique flowering period and vibrant color make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
