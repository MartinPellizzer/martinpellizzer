Crossandra vestita

Crossandra vestita, commonly known as the golden crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Crossandra vestita prefers well-draining soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
