Campanula lamioides

Campanula lamioides, commonly known as the blue bell flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Campanula lamioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
