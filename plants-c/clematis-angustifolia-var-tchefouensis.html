Clematis angustifolia var. tchefouensis

Clematis angustifolia var. tchefouensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Africa.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to several meters in length, supporting its climbing habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that provide a delicate texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable support structures.
