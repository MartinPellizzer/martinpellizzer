Clematis cirrhosa subsp. balearica

Clematis cirrhosa subsp.

balearica, commonly known as the Balearic Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, wiry stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, often twining around supports.

The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three ovate to elliptic leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils with partial shade.
