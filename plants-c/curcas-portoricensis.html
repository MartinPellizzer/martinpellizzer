Curcas portoricensis

Curcas portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It is characterized by its spine-tipped pads and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal areas and dry forests. Its fruits, known as "tuna," are edible and have been used for food and traditional medicine by local communities.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
