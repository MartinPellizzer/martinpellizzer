Cassia quinquangulata

Cassia quinquangulata, commonly known as the five-angled cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets, each having a distinct angular shape. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate sunlight.

Cassia quinquangulata is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
