Centaurea amani

Centaurea amani, commonly known as the Amanian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually purple or blue, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Centaurea amani is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
