Carex viridula var. saxilittoralis

Carex viridula var. saxilittoralis, commonly known as the saxilittoral carex, is a perennial sedge that thrives in coastal and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. This variety is adapted to tolerate saline conditions and is often found in maritime climates. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its growth habit is low and compact, making it well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
