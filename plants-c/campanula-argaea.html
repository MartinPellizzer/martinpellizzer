Campanula argaea

Campanula argaea, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula argaea is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
