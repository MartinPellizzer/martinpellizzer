Cereus geometrizans var. pugionifer

Cereus geometrizans var. pugionifer, commonly known as the sword cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces large, white, funnel-shaped flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming make it a striking addition to succulent gardens.
