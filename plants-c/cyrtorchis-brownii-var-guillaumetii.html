Cyrtorchis brownii var. guillaumetii

Cyrtorchis brownii var. guillaumetii, commonly known as the Guillaumet's ghost orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, pale greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns that resemble ghostly figures, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rocky substrates it inhabits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
