Carex supina subsp. eurasiatica

Carex supina subsp.

eurasiatica, commonly known as the Eurasian flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This subspecies is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It often grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
