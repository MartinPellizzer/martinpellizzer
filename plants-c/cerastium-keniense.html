Cerastium keniense

Cerastium keniense, commonly known as Kenyan mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
