Crinum wallichianum

Crinum wallichianum, commonly known as the Himalayan Crinum, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Himalayas.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, often unpleasant fragrance. The plant has strap-like, lanceolate leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Crinum wallichianum is valued for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
