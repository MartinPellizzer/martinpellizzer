Clerodendrum elachistanthum

Clerodendrum elachistanthum, commonly known as the Indian flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
