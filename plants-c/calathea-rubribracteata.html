Calathea rubribracteata

Calathea rubribracteata, commonly known as the red-bracted calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with intricate patterns in shades of green, pink, and red. The plant features showy, red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Calathea rubribracteata is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
