Chrysothamnus parryi var. affinis

Chrysothamnus parryi var. affinis, commonly known as the Parry's goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open areas. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in dry landscapes.
