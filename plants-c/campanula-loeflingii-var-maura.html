Campanula loeflingii var. maura

Campanula loeflingii var. maura, commonly known as the Maura bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in colder climates.
