Catasetum × valdisonianum

Catasetum × valdisonianum, commonly known as the Valdison orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that exhibit a unique structure with a specialized column for pollination. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are known for their intricate morphology, including the presence of a pollinia and a specialized labellum that aids in pollination.

Catasetum × valdisonianum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy cultivation under the right conditions.
