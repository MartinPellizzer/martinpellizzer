Crocus setifolius

Crocus setifolius, commonly known as the slender-leaved crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh conditions, often emerging after periods of drought.

The crocus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
