Cirsium cerberus

Cirsium cerberus, commonly known as the Cerberus thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, silver-green, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy purple to pink flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a cylindrical shape. Cirsium cerberus is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system for water retention.

It is considered a hardy plant with ornamental value in gardens.
