Capnorchis formosa

Capnorchis formosa, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant has slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Capnorchis formosa is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
