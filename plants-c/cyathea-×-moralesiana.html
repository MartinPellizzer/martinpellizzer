Cyathea × moralesiana

Cyathea × moralesiana, commonly known as the hybrid tree fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyathea cooperi and Cyathea truncata.

It is a member of the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.

The fronds are typically dark green and can grow to impressive lengths, contributing to its visual impact.
