Carex brownii subsp. transversa

Carex brownii subsp.

transversa, commonly known as the Transverse sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and densely packed. Its inflorescence consists of a spiky cluster of small, greenish-brown flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
