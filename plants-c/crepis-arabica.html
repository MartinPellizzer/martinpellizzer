Crepis arabica

Crepis arabica, commonly known as the Arabica sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are often lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster.

Crepis arabica is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
