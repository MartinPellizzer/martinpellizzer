Carex acuta var. personata

Carex acuta var. personata, commonly known as the slender spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. It has a clumping growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by bracts.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
