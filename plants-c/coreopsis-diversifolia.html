Coreopsis diversifolia

Coreopsis diversifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and finely divided, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Coreopsis diversifolia is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks.
