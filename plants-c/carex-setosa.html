Carex setosa

Carex setosa, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex setosa plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
