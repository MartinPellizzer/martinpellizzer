Cissus trifoliata var. obovata

Cissus trifoliata var. obovata, commonly known as the three-leafed devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being obovate in shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its ability to climb and adapt to various substrates makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
