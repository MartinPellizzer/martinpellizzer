Cheirinia crepidifolia

Cheirinia crepidifolia, commonly known as the crested bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces upright flowering stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cheirinia crepidifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
