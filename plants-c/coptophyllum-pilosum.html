Coptophyllum pilosum

Coptophyllum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy cup-plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its distinctive umbellate inflorescence. The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves with a serrated margin. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters that give it a cup-like appearance.

Coptophyllum pilosum thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
