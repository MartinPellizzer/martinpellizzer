Crataegus serena

Crataegus serena, commonly known as the serena hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it is often found in woodland edges and moist soils.

Crataegus serena is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
