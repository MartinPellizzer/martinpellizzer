Crataegus noveboracencis

Crataegus noveboracencis, commonly known as the New Jersey hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet, featuring thorned branches and ovate, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental landscaping.
