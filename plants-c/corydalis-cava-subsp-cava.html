Corydalis cava subsp. cava

Corydalis cava subsp.

cava, commonly known as the narrowleaf corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple.

The plant produces a cluster of small, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, narrow shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique floral structure and foliage.
