Chamaecytisus tmolaeus

Chamaecytisus tmolaeus, commonly known as the Greek broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
