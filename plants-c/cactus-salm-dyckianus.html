Cactus salm-dyckianus

Cactus salm-dyckianus, commonly known as the Salm-Dyck cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its root system is shallow and adapted to absorb moisture quickly from infrequent rains.

This cactus is well-suited to harsh environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
