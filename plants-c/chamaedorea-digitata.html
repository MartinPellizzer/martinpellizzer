Chamaedorea digitata

Chamaedorea digitata, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central and South America.

It features a slender trunk with a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant is well adapted to low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in humid environments contribute to its appeal.

The leaves are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, adding a lush, tropical feel to any space.
