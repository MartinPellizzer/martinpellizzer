Cheirostylis munnacampensis

Cheirostylis munnacampensis, commonly known as the Munnacamp's orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found growing in the understory of tropical forests. Its floral structure is specialized for pollination by specific insect species, highlighting its ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
