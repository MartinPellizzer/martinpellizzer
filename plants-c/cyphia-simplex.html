Cyphia simplex

Cyphia simplex, commonly known as the white star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking white petals and a central yellow spur. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often in late spring or early summer, and are known for their unique, star-like appearance.

Cyphia simplex is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
