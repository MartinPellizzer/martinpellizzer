Commelina bicolor

Commelina bicolor, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often accompanied by small, leaf-like bracts.

Commelina bicolor is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
