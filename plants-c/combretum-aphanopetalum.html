Combretum aphanopetalum

Combretum aphanopetalum, commonly known as the small-leaved flame bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically red or orange in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Combretum aphanopetalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
