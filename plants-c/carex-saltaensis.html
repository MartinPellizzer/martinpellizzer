Carex saltaensis

Carex saltaensis, commonly known as the Salta sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

Carex saltaensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
