Capparis ovata var. canescens

Capparis ovata var. canescens, commonly known as the hairless caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers and thorny stems. The plant produces edible, rounded buds that are often used in cooking. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.
