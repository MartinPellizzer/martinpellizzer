Cyperus tetraphyllus

Cyperus tetraphyllus, commonly known as the four-leaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its cylindrical stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

The species is characterized by its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
