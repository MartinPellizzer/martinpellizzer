Carex bambusetorum

Carex bambusetorum, commonly known as the bamboo sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grassy leaves and clustered, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Carex bambusetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
