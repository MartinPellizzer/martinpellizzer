Caesalpinia brachycarpa

Caesalpinia brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited legume, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Caesalpinia brachycarpa is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland areas.
