Columnea rosea f. latifolia

Columnea rosea f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The leaves are broad, glossy, and often have a slightly waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as a hanging plant or in shaded garden settings due to its delicate appearance and unique floral display.
