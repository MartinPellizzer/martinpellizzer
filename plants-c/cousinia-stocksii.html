Cousinia stocksii

Cousinia stocksii, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It has a rosette growth habit, with basal leaves that are narrow and linear.

Cousinia stocksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
