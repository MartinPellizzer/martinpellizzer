Canavalia panamensis

Canavalia panamensis, commonly known as the Panama bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, and it produces edible seeds that are used in local cuisines.

Canavalia panamensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
