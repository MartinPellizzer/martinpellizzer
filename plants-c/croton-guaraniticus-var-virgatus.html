Croton guaraniticus var. virgatus

Croton guaraniticus var. virgatus, commonly known as the silver-leaved croton, is a tropical shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver foliage that contrasts beautifully with its green stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
