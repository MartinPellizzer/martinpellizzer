Calamus kandariensis var. glabratus

Calamus kandariensis var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

The flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing at the base of the plant.
