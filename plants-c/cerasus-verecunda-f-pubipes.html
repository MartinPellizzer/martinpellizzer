Cerasus verecunda f. pubipes

Cerasus verecunda f. pubipes, commonly known as the pubescent cherry, is a botanical variant of the cherry species Cerasus verecunda.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas. The flowers are usually white or pink, and the fruit is a small, edible cherry.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in horticulture due to its specific growing requirements.
