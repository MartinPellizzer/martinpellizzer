Chrysochloa lucida

Chrysochloa lucida, commonly known as the golden grass or the yellow feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescence, which gives it its common name, and its slender, arching leaves that form dense clumps. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. It is valued for its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Chrysochloa lucida is also used in some regions for erosion control due to its extensive root system.
