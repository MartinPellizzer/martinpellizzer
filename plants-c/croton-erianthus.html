Croton erianthus

Croton erianthus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor plant collections.
