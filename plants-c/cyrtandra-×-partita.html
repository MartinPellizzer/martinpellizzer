Cyrtandra × partita

Cyrtandra × partita, commonly known as the hybrid blue ginger, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers, which are typically tubular with a wide, showy throat. The plant produces opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Cyrtandra × partita is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
