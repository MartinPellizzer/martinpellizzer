Clematis azurea var. grandiflora

Clematis azurea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
