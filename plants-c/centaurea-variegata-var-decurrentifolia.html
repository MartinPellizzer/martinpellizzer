Centaurea variegata var. decurrentifolia

Centaurea variegata var. decurrentifolia, commonly known as the decurrent leaf centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that gradually decrease in size upward. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with a distinctive ray floret arrangement.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
