Copiapoa longistaminea subsp. longistaminea

Copiapoa longistaminea subsp.

longistaminea, commonly known as the long-stemmed copiapoa, is a cactus native to Chile. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a dense covering of fine, white hairs.

This species is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
