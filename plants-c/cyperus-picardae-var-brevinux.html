Cyperus picardae var. brevinux

Cyperus picardae var. brevinux, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often submerged or just above water level.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter, more compact growth form compared to other members of the species.
