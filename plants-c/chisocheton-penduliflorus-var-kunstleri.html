Chisocheton penduliflorus var. kunstleri

Chisocheton penduliflorus var. kunstleri, commonly known as the hanging flower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that hang in clusters, often in shades of white and yellow. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, round fruits that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical regions.
