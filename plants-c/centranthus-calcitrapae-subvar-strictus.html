Centranthus calcitrapae subvar. strictus

Centranthus calcitrapae subvar. strictus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved red campion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright pink to red flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
