Carduus nutans var. petrovicii

Carduus nutans var. petrovicii, commonly known as the spreading thistle, is a variant of the common thistle native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various soil types and environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it both a resilient weed and a potential ornamental plant in certain regions.
