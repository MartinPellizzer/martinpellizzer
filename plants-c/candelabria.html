Candelabria

Candelabria, commonly known as the candlestick plant, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that resemble a candlestick, hence its common name. The plant features compound leaves that are often palmately arranged, giving it a lush, tropical appearance. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically appearing in clusters, and it produces dark, glossy berries.

Candelabria is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
