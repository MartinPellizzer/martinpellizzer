Chrysocoma incana

Chrysocoma incana, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. These flowers are followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Chrysocoma incana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
