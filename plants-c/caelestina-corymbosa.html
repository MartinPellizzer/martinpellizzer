Caelestina corymbosa

Caelestina corymbosa, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy inflorescences bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Caelestina corymbosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
