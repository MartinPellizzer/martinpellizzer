Cleome bundeica

Cleome bundeica, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences that resemble spiders. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers come in shades of pink, red, and white.

Cleome bundeica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
