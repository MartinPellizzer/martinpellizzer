Clematis zandaensis

Clematis zandaensis, commonly known as the Zanda clematis, is a climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.

Clematis zandaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
