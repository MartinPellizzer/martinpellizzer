Canthium mrimaense

Canthium mrimaense, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Canthium mrimaense is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
