Capparis boscioides

Capparis boscioides, commonly known as the Boscioides caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which includes various species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, with five petals and numerous stamens, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

The plant thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in areas with high salinity and well-drained soil.
