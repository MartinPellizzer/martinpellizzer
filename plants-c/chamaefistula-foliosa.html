Chamaefistula foliosa

Chamaefistula foliosa, commonly known as the Persian ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. The plant has a dense, woody structure with thick, leathery leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Chamaefistula foliosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
