Clematis oligophylla

Clematis oligophylla, commonly known as the small-leaf clematis, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped, finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value and delicate appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a climbing plant.
