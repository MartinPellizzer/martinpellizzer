Cattleya × amoena

Cattleya × amoena, commonly known as the orchid with the hybrid name, is a hybrid orchid species originating from crosses between Cattleya labiata and Cattleya mossieri.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, and white. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Cattleya × amoena is prized in horticulture for its beauty and fragrance, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
