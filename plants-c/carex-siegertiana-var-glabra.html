Carex siegertiana var. glabra

Carex siegertiana var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth siegert's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, smooth, and often bluish-green leaves. This variety thrives in moist to wet environments, such as swamps, bogs, and the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
