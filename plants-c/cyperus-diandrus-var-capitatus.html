Cyperus diandrus var. capitatus

Cyperus diandrus var. capitatus, commonly known as the marsh flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its botanical features include small, three-lobed flowers arranged in dense clusters, and it often has a creeping rhizomatous root system.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
