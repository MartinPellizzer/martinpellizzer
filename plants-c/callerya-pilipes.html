Callerya pilipes

Callerya pilipes, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are typically bisexual, with five petals and numerous stamens, making it visually striking.

Callerya pilipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
