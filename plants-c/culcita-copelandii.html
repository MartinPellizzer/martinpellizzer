Culcita copelandii

Culcita copelandii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a prominent midrib.

Culcita copelandii is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.
