Carthamus canescens

Carthamus canescens, commonly known as goatweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a grayish appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers with prominent central disks, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Carthamus canescens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
