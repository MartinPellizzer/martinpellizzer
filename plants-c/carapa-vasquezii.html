Carapa vasquezii

Carapa vasquezii, commonly known as the Ecuadorian mahogany, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The tree produces heavy, aromatic seeds enclosed in a hard, woody capsule. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, though it is also threatened by overharvesting.

Due to its ecological and economic significance, Carapa vasquezii is listed as endangered on the IUCN Red List.
