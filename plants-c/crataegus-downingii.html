Crataegus downingii

Crataegus downingii, commonly known as Downings hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
