Centaurea salicifolia

Centaurea salicifolia, commonly known as willow-leaved cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, willow-like leaves and produces bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky habitats.

Centaurea salicifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
