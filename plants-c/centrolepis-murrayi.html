Centrolepis murrayi

Centrolepis murrayi, commonly known as the Murray's centrolepis, is a unique and ancient plant species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched stems and feather-like leaves. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and nutrient-poor soils, making it well-adapted to harsh environments. Its cones, which are small and clustered, produce seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is of particular interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance and its role in understanding the development of cycad-like plants.
