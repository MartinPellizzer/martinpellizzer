Cyathea discolor

Cyathea discolor, commonly known as the silver tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-lived species. Its fronds are typically a deep green with a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea discolor thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
