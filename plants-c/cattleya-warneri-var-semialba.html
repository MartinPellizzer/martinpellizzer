Cattleya warneri var. semialba

Cattleya warneri var. semialba, commonly known as the white-flowered Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals and a contrasting yellow lip, which make it highly sought after in horticulture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn.

This variety is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and greenhouses.
