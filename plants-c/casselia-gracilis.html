Casselia gracilis

Casselia gracilis, commonly known as the slender cassia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Casselia gracilis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
