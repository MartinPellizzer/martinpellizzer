Calamagrostis schwabii

Calamagrostis schwabii, commonly known as Schwab's reed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to wetland landscapes.
