Capsella bursa-pastoris extrema

Capsella bursa-pastoris extrema, commonly known as the common chickweed or mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to temperate regions.

It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and a prostrate or ascending growth habit. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Botanically, it is characterized by its distinctive bur-like seed pods that release tiny seeds when disturbed.
