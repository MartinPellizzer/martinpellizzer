Chenopodium virgatum

Chenopodium virgatum, commonly known as pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. Chenopodium virgatum is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.

It is also a common weed in agricultural settings.
