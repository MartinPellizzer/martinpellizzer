Cynoglossum bicolor

Cynoglossum bicolor, commonly known as Johnson's weed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers that resemble small lips, hence its scientific name meaning "tongue-like.". The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Cynoglossum bicolor is an annual or biennial plant that blooms from late summer to early autumn.
