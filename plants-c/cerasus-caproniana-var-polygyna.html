Cerasus caproniana var. polygyna

Cerasus caproniana var. polygyna, commonly known as the wild cherry or cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet and features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during late spring, which are followed by edible, yellowish-pink fruits. Its botanical characteristics include a smooth, grayish bark and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
