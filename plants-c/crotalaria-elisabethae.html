Crotalaria elisabethae

Crotalaria elisabethae, commonly known as the Elizabeth's crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Crotalaria elisabethae is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
