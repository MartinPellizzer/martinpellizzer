Clibadium terebinthinaceum var. ecuadorense

Clibadium terebinthinaceum var. ecuadorense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian sumpweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
