Campanula patula var. hirsuta

Campanula patula var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, often blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is typically found in moist meadows and woodland edges. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
