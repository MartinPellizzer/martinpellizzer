Cichorium intybus var. spicatum

Cichorium intybus var. spicatum, commonly known as Italian chicory, is a biennial or perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of blue to violet leaves and a tall, branching stem that bears blue or purple flowers. The plant produces a taproot that is often used as a vegetable, similar to endive or escarole. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, and it is often cultivated for its edible leaves and roots.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
