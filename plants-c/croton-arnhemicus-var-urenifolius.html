Croton arnhemicus var. urenifolius

Croton arnhemicus var. urenifolius, commonly known as the red-stemmed croton, is a shrub native to northern Australia.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with reddish veins, and its stems are prominently reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
