Crassula conspicua

Crassula conspicua, commonly known as the "Beaded Window" or "Piggyback Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially when exposed to bright light. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Crassula conspicua is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
