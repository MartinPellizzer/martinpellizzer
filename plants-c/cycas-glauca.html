Cycas glauca

Cycas glauca, commonly known as the blue cycad, is a slow-growing, evergreen plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant is dioecious, meaning it has separate male and female plants, and produces cones rather than flowers. Cycas glauca is adapted to low-nutrient soils and can tolerate drought conditions, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and ancient lineage make it a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
