Cordia trichotoma f. puberula

Cordia trichotoma f. puberula, commonly known as the soft-hairy cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, orange-red berries that contain several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
