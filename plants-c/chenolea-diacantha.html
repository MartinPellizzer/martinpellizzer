Chenolea diacantha

Chenolea diacantha, commonly known as the candleplant, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It features a tall, candle-like stem with a single, thick, waxy leaf at the top. The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its stem, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The flowers, which appear at the base of the stem, are typically yellow or orange and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
