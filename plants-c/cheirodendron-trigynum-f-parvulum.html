Cheirodendron trigynum f. parvulum

Cheirodendron trigynum f. parvulum, commonly known as the small-fruited cheirodendron, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
