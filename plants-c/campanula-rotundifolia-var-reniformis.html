Campanula rotundifolia var. reniformis

Campanula rotundifolia var. reniformis, commonly known as the kidneyleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped, giving it its scientific name, and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its delicate appearance.
