Carex setigera var. schlagintweitiana

Carex setigera var. schlagintweitiana, commonly known as the Schlagintweit's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly curled, with a distinctive reddish or purple hue on the underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
