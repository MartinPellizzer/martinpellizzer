Chamaecrista nictitans var. diffusa

Chamaecrista nictitans var. diffusa, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and serpentine-shaped seed pods.
