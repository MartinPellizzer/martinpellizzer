Canarium villosiflorum

Canarium villosiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves with a hairy underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
