Calceolaria cataractarum

Calceolaria cataractarum, commonly known as the waterfall calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insect species.
