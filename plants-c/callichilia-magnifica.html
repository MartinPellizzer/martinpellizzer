Callichilia magnifica

Callichilia magnifica, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach up to 10 feet in height, covered in dense, white to light green spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often arranged in a star-like pattern. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

Callichilia magnifica is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and full sun.
