Chondrosea glareosa

Chondrosea glareosa, commonly known as the glittering sea lettuce, is a marine green alga that thrives in coastal environments.

It is characterized by its bright, translucent thallus that reflects light, giving it a shimmering appearance. This plant typically grows in shallow, tide-pool habitats where it can receive ample sunlight for photosynthesis. Chondrosea glareosa plays a role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.

Its structure consists of a flat, ribbon-like body with a single layer of cells, allowing it to absorb nutrients directly from the water.
