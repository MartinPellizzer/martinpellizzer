Chloris rogeonii

Chloris rogeonii, commonly known as the red grass or red oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or purple hues in its foliage, which are most prominent during the flowering season. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often reddish in color, adding to its ornamental value.

Chloris rogeonii is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in landscaping for its visual appeal.
