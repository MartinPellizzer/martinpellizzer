Centaurea hoffmannseggiana

Centaurea hoffmannseggiana, commonly known as Hoffmann's bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial herb with striking cobalt-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed heads that resemble small, fluffy spheres. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
