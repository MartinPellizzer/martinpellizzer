Chapelliera riparia

Chapelliera riparia, commonly known as the riverbank candelabra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems that form a candelabra-like structure. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wetlands.

Chapelliera riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
