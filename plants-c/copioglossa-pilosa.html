Copioglossa pilosa

Copioglossa pilosa, commonly known as the hairy tongue plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, soft trichomes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and clustered in whorls, with vibrant colors ranging from red to purple.

The species is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.
