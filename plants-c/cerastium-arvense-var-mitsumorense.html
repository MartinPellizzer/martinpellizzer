Cerastium arvense var. mitsumorense

Cerastium arvense var. mitsumorense, commonly known as the Japanese mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves and white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical structure includes stolons that help it spread and establish new colonies.
