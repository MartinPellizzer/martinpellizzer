Cyphomandra diversifolia

Cyphomandra diversifolia, commonly known as the yellow bell pepper plant, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant yellow flowers and edible fruits resembling small peppers. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to several feet in height. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit develops from the flowers and is often used in culinary applications due to its mild, sweet flavor.
