Coreopsis basalis var. typica

Coreopsis basalis var. typica, commonly known as the eastern coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a dark center, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, ornamental plants.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies adds ecological value to the landscape.
