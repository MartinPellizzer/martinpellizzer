Campanula incanescens f. khorasanica

Campanula incanescens f. khorasanica, commonly known as the Khorasan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in a dense layer of fine, grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate, slightly hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem.

It is well adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
