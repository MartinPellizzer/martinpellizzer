Ceanothus megacarpus var. pendulus

Ceanothus megacarpus var. pendulus, commonly known as the hanging ceanothus, is a shrub native to California and parts of Oregon.

It is characterized by its fragrant, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces long, drooping clusters of fruit, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
