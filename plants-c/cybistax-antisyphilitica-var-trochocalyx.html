Cybistax antisyphilitica var. trochocalyx

Cybistax antisyphilitica var. trochocalyx, commonly known as the broad-leafed cybistax, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves that are often broad and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
