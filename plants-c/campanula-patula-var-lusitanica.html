Campanula patula var. lusitanica

Campanula patula var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
