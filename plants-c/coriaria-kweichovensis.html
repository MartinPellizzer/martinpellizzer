Coriaria kweichovensis

Coriaria kweichovensis, commonly known as the Kweichow coriaria, is a shrub native to China, specifically the Yunnan and Guizhou provinces.

It belongs to the Coriariaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
