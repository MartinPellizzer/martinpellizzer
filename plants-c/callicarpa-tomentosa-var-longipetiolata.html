Callicarpa tomentosa var. longipetiolata

Callicarpa tomentosa var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the silky beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces showy, violet-purple berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and berries.
