Childsia wercklei

Childsia wercklei, commonly known as the purple-flowered childsia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its most striking feature is the clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Childsia wercklei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
