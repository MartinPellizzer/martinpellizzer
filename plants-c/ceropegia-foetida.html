Ceropegia foetida

Ceropegia foetida, commonly known as the stinking cucumber, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, trailing stems and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ability to store water, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
