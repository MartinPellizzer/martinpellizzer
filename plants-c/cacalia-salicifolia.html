Cacalia salicifolia

Cacalia salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes many other bellflowers.

Cacalia salicifolia is often found in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8, making it a versatile choice for gardens in temperate regions.
