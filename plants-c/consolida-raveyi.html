Consolida raveyi

Consolida raveyi, commonly known as the Ravey's sweet pea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and has a distinctive, fragrant scent. Its flowers are typically spurred at the base, a feature common in the genus Consolida.

Native to the Mediterranean region, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
