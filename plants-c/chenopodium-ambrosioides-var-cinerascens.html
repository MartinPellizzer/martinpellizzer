Chenopodium ambrosioides var. cinerascens

Chenopodium ambrosioides var. cinerascens, commonly known as gray goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats, including fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This species is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
