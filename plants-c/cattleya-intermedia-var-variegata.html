Cattleya intermedia var. variegata

Cattleya intermedia var. variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, often with distinctive variegation patterns on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
