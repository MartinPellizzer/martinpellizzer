Cassia domingensis

Cassia domingensis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear bright yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. Cassia domingensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are elliptical in shape.
