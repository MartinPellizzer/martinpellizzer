Carex sagittifera

Carex sagittifera, commonly known as arrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, which are modified leaves. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Carex sagittifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
