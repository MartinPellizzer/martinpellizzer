Campanula speciosa var. stenophylla

Campanula speciosa var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
