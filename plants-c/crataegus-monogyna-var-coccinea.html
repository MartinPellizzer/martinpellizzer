Crataegus monogyna var. coccinea

Crataegus monogyna var. coccinea, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange flowers that appear in spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. The plant has thorny branches and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are a food source for birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological significance.
