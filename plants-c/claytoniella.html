Claytoniella

Claytoniella, commonly known as the green bottlebrush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescences, which resemble bottlebrushes and consist of dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Claytoniella species are native to Australia and are often found in coastal or sandy soils.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant floral displays.
