Cincinalis acclivis

Cincinalis acclivis, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Cincinalis acclivis plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small fauna.
