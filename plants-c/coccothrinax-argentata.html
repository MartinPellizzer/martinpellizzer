Coccothrinax argentata

Coccothrinax argentata, commonly known as the silver palm, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and dense, feather-like fronds. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into clustered, spiny fruits. Its leaves are glossy and have a silvery underside, giving it its common name.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and resilience in warm, humid climates.
