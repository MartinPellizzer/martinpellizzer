Catasetum charlesworthii

Catasetum charlesworthii, commonly known as the Charlesworth's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its unique floral structure, including a specialized lip that aids in pollination. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinct, often fragrant scent that attracts specific pollinators.

Catasetum charlesworthii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
