Caryophyllus undulatus

Caryophyllus undulatus, commonly known as the undulating carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating petals that give it a unique and ornamental appearance. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The flowers typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white, adding vibrant color to gardens.

It is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its historical use in perfumery and herbal medicine.
