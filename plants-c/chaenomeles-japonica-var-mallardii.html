Chaenomeles japonica var. mallardii

Chaenomeles japonica var. mallardii, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves that are typically alternate and have a serrated margin. The plant produces showy, pink to red bract-like flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that persists into winter, providing food for birds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in various climates.
