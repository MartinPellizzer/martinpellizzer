Centropogon cornutus var. laevigatus

Centropogon cornutus var. laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth-horned centropogon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture, as indicated by its varietal name "laevigatus.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to semi-shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
