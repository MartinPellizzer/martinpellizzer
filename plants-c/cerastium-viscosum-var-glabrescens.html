Cerastium viscosum var. glabrescens

Cerastium viscosum var. glabrescens, commonly known as sticky willow herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in a sticky, glandular substance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
