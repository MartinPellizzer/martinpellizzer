Campanula corymbosa

Campanula corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Campanula corymbosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
