Ceratocephalus ecliptodes

Ceratocephalus ecliptodes, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and is often found in disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in spring and are followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
