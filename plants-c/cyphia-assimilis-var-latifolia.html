Cyphia assimilis var. latifolia

Cyphia assimilis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cyphia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
