Caesalpinia bonduc var. caymanensis

Caesalpinia bonduc var. caymanensis, commonly known as the Cayman apple or Cayman coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Cayman Islands.

It produces bright red, bead-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive red or purple base. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
