Canna lutea var. pallida

Canna lutea var. pallida, commonly known as the pale yellow canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to the wetlands of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences that are yellow in color, surrounded by broad, colorful bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and floral display.

This variety is prized in gardens for its ability to add a tropical feel to landscapes.
