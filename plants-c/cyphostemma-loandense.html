Cyphostemma loandense

Cyphostemma loandense, commonly known as the Loango moonvine, is a climbing plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, horn-like fruits. It is often found in rocky or wooded areas and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
