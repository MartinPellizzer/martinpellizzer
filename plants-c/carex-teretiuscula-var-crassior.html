Carex teretiuscula var. crassior

Carex teretiuscula var. crassior, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized inflorescences. This sedge typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, clustered spikelets in the spring.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
