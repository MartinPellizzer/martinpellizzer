Cousinia × triacantha

Cousinia × triacantha, commonly known as the three-spined cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments. Its scientific name reflects its hybrid origin, combining traits from two parent species.

Cousinia × triacantha is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
