Colocasia pubera

Colocasia pubera, commonly known as the velvet leaf taro, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as swamps and wetlands, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its underground corms are sometimes used in traditional cuisines, though the plant is more often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Colocasia pubera is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
