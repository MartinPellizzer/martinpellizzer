Calamagrostis turczaninowii

Calamagrostis turczaninowii, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves that are often described as having a feathery appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy or riparian habitats.

Calamagrostis turczaninowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
