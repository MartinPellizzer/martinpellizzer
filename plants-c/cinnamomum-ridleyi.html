Cinnamomum ridleyi

Cinnamomum ridleyi, commonly known as Ridley's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their essential oils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
