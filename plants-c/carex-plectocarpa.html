Carex plectocarpa

Carex plectocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wetlands and marshes, and is often found in riparian zones.

Carex plectocarpa plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
