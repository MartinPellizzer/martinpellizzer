Carex tsaiana

Carex tsaiana, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of Asia, including parts of Russia and China. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex tsaiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
