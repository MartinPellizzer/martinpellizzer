Croton pallidulus var. myrianthus

Croton pallidulus var. myrianthus, commonly known as the white croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
