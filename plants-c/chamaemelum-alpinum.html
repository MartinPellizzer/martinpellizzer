Chamaemelum alpinum

Chamaemelum alpinum, commonly known as alpine chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish-white flowers with a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.

Chamaemelum alpinum is also used in traditional herbal remedies and as a source of essential oils.
