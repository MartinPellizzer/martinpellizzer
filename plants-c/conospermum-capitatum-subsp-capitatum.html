Conospermum capitatum subsp. capitatum

Conospermum capitatum subsp.

capitatum, commonly known as the coastal banksia, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that bear orange to yellow flowers, which are clustered in a head-like structure.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving them a silvery appearance. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral display.
