Carex laxiflora var. gracillima

Carex laxiflora var. gracillima, commonly known as slender-flowered sedges, is a variant of the Carex genus that features narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes.

It is native to regions of Asia, including parts of China and Japan, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like structures. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in water features and bog gardens.
