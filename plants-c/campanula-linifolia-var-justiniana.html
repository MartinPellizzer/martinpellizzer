Campanula linifolia var. justiniana

Campanula linifolia var. justiniana, commonly known as the Justinian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
