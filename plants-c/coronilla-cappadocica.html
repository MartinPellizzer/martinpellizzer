Coronilla cappadocica

Coronilla cappadocica, commonly known as Cappadocian crownvetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
