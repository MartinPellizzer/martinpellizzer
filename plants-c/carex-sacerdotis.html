Carex sacerdotis

Carex sacerdotis, commonly known as the sacred sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It is native to parts of North America and is often found in riparian zones.

Carex sacerdotis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
