Cyclamen cyprograecum

Cyclamen cyprograecum, commonly known as the Cyprus cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its striking heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, bowl-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Cyclamen cyprograecum is well-suited for indoor cultivation and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
