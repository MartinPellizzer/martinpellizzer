Crataegus oxyacantha f. glabrata

Crataegus oxyacantha f. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and reduced thorns compared to the typical species. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in autumn. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
