Calamagrostis villosa var. pseudolanceolata

Calamagrostis villosa var. pseudolanceolata, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves that give it a soft, silky appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
