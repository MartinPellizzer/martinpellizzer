Cyperus spaniophyllus

Cyperus spaniophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet at the tip of each stem, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus spaniophyllus is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds.
