Cnicus portoricensis

Cnicus portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to the island of Puerto Rico.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy and coastal habitats. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

Cnicus portoricensis plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing nectar for local pollinators.
