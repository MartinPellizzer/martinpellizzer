Chorilaena angustifolia

Chorilaena angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed chorilaena, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

Chorilaena angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
