Cactus microdasys

Cactus microdasys, commonly known as the beaded cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems covered in small, white spines. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.
