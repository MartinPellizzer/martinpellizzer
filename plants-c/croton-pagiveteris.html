Croton pagiveteris

Croton pagiveteris, commonly known as the red-veined croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that often feature bright red, yellow, and green veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its primary appeal lies in its aesthetic value.
