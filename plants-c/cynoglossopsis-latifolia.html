Cynoglossopsis latifolia

Cynoglossopsis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tonguegrass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, terminal clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Cyperaceae family.
