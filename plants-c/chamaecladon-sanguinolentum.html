Chamaecladon sanguinolentum

Chamaecladon sanguinolentum, commonly known as the bloodroot plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-orange sap, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a basal rosette of leaves. Its flowers are small and white, often appearing in early spring.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
