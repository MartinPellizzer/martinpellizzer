Cyperus munnarensis

Cyperus munnarensis, commonly known as the Munnar sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and pond margins. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Cyperus munnarensis plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
