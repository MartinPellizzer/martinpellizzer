Croton sylvestris

Croton sylvestris, commonly known as the wild croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the genus. Its leaves are usually thick and waxy, with a variety of shapes and colors, ranging from green to red and yellow.

Croton sylvestris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
