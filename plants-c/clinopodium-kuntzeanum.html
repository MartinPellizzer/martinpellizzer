Clinopodium kuntzeanum

Clinopodium kuntzeanum, commonly known as Kuntze's betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a serrated margin.

Small, tubular flowers are clustered in whorls, producing a delicate blue to purple coloration that attracts pollinators.
