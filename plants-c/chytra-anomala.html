Chytra anomala

Chytra anomala, commonly known as the ghost shrimp plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble the appearance of ghostly shrimps, hence its common name. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshes and ponds. Its reproductive structure consists of small, spore-producing capsules that release motile spores into the water.

Chytra anomala plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
