Coreopsis oblanceolata

Coreopsis oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces bright yellow to orange flowers in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies adds ecological value to its habitat.
