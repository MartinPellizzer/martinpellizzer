Cicer multijugum

Cicer multijugum, commonly known as the two-lobed vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet having a distinct two-lobed shape. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its pods are slender and contain several elliptical seeds.

Cicer multijugum is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
