Centaurea eriosiphon

Centaurea eriosiphon, commonly known as the woolly centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, woolly appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Centaurea eriosiphon is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
