Crotalaria bequaertii var. pubescens

Crotalaria bequaertii var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
