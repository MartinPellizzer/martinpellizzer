Campanula sibirica subsp. caucasica

Campanula sibirica subsp.

caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features slender stems that often branch near the base, supporting clusters of bell-shaped flowers.

The flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with five lobes that open to reveal a prominent throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
