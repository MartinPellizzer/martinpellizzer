Cyperus prasinus

Cyperus prasinus, commonly known as the green sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. Its slender, grass-like leaves are often bright green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus prasinus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
