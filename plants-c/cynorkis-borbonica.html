Cynorkis borbonica

Cynorkis borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
