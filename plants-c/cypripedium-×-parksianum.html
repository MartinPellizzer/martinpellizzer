Cypripedium × parksianum

Cypripedium × parksianum, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium × medium.

It is characterized by its large, showy, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple with a yellow or white throat. The plant is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous root system and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists worldwide.
