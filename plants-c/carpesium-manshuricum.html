Carpesium manshuricum

Carpesium manshuricum, commonly known as the Siberian thistle, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its distinctive appearance and hardy nature make it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive in non-native regions.
