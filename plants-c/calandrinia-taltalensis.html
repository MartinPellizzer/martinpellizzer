Calandrinia taltalensis

Calandrinia taltalensis, commonly known as the Taltal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, adapted to the arid environment. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Calandrinia taltalensis is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants.
