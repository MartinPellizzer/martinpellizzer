Campanula lycia

Campanula lycia, commonly known as the Lycian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula lycia is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive flowers and ease of care.
