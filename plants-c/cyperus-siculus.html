Cyperus siculus

Cyperus siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, ditches, and along waterways.

Cyperus siculus is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain regions.
