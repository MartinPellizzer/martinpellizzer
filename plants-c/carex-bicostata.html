Carex bicostata

Carex bicostata, commonly known as the two-costed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and have a distinctive two-lobed base. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown and appear in late spring to early summer.

Carex bicostata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
