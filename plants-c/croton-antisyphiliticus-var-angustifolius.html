Croton antisyphiliticus var. angustifolius

Croton antisyphiliticus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
