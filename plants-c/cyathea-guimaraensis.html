Cyathea guimaraensis

Cyathea guimaraensis, commonly known as the Guimaraes tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant is typically found in humid, tropical rainforest environments, where it thrives in shaded, moist conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in the ecological structure of its habitat.

The fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to the plant's ornamental value.
