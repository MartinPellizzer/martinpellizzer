Cassia chamaecrista

Cassia chamaecrista, commonly known as yellow cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental and medicinal uses.

Cassia chamaecrista is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility in agricultural settings.
