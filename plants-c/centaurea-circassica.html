Centaurea circassica

Centaurea circassica, commonly known as Caucasian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, elongated seed pods. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Centaurea circassica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
