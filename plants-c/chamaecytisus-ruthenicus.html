Chamaecytisus ruthenicus

Chamaecytisus ruthenicus, commonly known as the Russian broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has woody stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
