Calathea leucophaea

Calathea leucophaea, commonly known as the White-veined Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with intricate white veins against a deep green background. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Calathea leucophaea is known for its ability to purify the air and enhance indoor humidity.
