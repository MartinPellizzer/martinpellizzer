Carex rugulosa

Carex rugulosa, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence. Carex rugulosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
