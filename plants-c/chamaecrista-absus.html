Chamaecrista absus

Chamaecrista absus, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils due to its adaptability.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
