Corylus pontica

Corylus pontica, commonly known as the Turkish hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, ovate leaves that are hairy on the underside. The plant produces catkins in early spring, with female flowers appearing in clusters. Its nuts are encased in a spiny husk and are typically harvested in the fall.

Corylus pontica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
