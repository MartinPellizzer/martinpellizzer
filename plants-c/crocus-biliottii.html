Crocus biliottii

Crocus biliottii, commonly known as the Biliotti's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, particularly in parts of Albania and Kosovo. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in rocky and well-drained soils, and it thrives in cool, temperate climates.

Crocus biliottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
