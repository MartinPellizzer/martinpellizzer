Camphorata perennis

Camphorata perennis, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a perennial plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a compact growth habit with silver-green foliage that is often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its long-lasting blooms, it is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes.
