Crocus dilekyarensis

Crocus dilekyarensis, commonly known as the Dilek crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Dilek region of Turkey.

It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing showy, cup-shaped petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem.

Crocus dilekyarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
