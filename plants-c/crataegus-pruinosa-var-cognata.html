Crataegus pruinosa var. cognata

Crataegus pruinosa var. cognata, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. The plant has thorny stems and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its colorful flowers and berries, as well as its historical use in traditional medicine.
