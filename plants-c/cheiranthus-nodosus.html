Cheiranthus nodosus

Cheiranthus nodosus, commonly known as the nodding wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. Its leaves are lobed and hairy, and it thrives in dry, sunny conditions.

The species is often used in rock gardens and as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
