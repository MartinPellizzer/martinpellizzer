Cardamine micrantha

Cardamine micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, rounded lobes, and it produces tiny white or greenish flowers in early spring.

The plant is valued for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.
