Carlina canariensis var. canariensis

Carlina canariensis var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island carline thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It features a tall, sparsely branched stem with stiff, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, composite flower heads that consist of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale blue to violet ray flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
