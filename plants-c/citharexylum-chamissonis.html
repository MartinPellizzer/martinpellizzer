Citharexylum chamissonis

Citharexylum chamissonis, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its fragrant, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, opposite, ovate leaves and produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds. It plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing resources for wildlife.

Its parasitic nature and ornamental value make it both ecologically and botanically significant.
