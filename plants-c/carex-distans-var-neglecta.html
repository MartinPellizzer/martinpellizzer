Carex distans var. neglecta

Carex distans var. neglecta, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wet meadows.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
