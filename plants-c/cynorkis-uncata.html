Cynorkis uncata

Cynorkis uncata, commonly known as the naked orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lack of showy petals. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is known for its unique morphology.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
