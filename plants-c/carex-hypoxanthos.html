Carex hypoxanthos

Carex hypoxanthos, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex hypoxanthos plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
