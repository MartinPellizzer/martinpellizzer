Cissus bambuseti

Cissus bambuseti, commonly known as the climbing fig-leaf plant, is a member of the Vitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and its ability to climb using tendrils. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus bambuseti is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
