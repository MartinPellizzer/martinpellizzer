Cochlidium rostratum

Cochlidium rostratum, commonly known as the rostrated snail, is a small terrestrial gastropod mollusk belonging to the family Helicidae.

It is characterized by its spiral shell, which is typically light-colored with a reddish or brownish hue. The shell provides protection against predators and desiccation, making it an important adaptation for survival.
