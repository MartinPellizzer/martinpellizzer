Cynodon cruciatus

Cynodon cruciatus, commonly known as the crossgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which are typically arranged in a cross shape, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Cynodon cruciatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
