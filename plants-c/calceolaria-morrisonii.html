Calceolaria morrisonii

Calceolaria morrisonii, commonly known as the Morrison's slipper orchid, is a striking member of the Calceolaria genus native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that come in a variety of vibrant colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Calceolaria morrisonii is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in its native habitat.
