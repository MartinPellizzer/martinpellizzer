Calliandra gentryi

Calliandra gentryi, commonly known as the Gentry's calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in spherical or cylindrical shapes. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, fern-like leaflets.

Calliandra gentryi is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
