Cleome quinquenervia

Cleome quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and palmate leaves with five distinct veins. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Cleome quinquenervia is a herbaceous annual that is relatively easy to grow in gardens due to its hardy nature and minimal care requirements.
