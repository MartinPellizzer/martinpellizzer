Carex chordorrhiza var. genuina

Carex chordorrhiza var. genuina, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
