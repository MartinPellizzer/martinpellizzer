Chrysoma fasciculata

Chrysoma fasciculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a fascicle, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow with white rays, blooming in spring and summer.
