Carex goodenowii var. chlorostachya

Carex goodenowii var. chlorostachya, commonly known as the green-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are generally narrow and dark green, with a distinctive blue-green hue. Its flowering spikes are usually cylindrical and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
