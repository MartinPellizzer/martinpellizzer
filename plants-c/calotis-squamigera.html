Calotis squamigera

Calotis squamigera, commonly known as the squamigera daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Calotis squamigera is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant characteristics.
