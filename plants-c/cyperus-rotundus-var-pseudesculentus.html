Cyperus rotundus var. pseudesculentus

Cyperus rotundus var. pseudesculentus, commonly known as false nut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as fields, roadsides, and wetlands.

Due to its aggressive growth habit, it can become a problematic weed in agricultural and natural ecosystems.
