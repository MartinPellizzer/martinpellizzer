Campylocentrum ulei var. parvifolium

Campylocentrum ulei var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leafed flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are usually opposite and have a glossy, dark green appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often resembling flames, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types, making it a resilient plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
