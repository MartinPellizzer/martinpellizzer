Crataegus × uhrovae var. subcurvisepala

Crataegus × uhrovae var. subcurvisepala, commonly known as the Uhrová's hawthorn, is a hybrid species native to the Czech Republic.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, thorny branches and clusters of white flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, red berries in the autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
