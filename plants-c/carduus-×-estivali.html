Carduus × estivali

Carduus × estivali, commonly known as the summer thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flower heads that resemble those of other thistle species. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
