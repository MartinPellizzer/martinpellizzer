Cyperus sanguinolentus subsp. melanocephalus

Cyperus sanguinolentus subsp.

melanocephalus, commonly known as black-headed flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family. It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or muddy areas, such as marshes and along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a reddish or purplish tint, especially at the base.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark-colored inflorescence, which gives it its common name.
