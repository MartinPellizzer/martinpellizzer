Crossandra nilotica

Crossandra nilotica, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Crossandra nilotica is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
