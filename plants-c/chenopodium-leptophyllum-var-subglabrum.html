Chenopodium leptophyllum var. subglabrum

Chenopodium leptophyllum var. subglabrum, commonly known as pigweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It is native to North America and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

This species is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
