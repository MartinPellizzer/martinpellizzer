Cyperus fulvus var. canescens

Cyperus fulvus var. canescens, commonly known as the silver flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

Its morphology and growth habits make it an adaptable species in various aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
