Calamagrostis dioica

Calamagrostis dioica, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive purple tinge on its inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in heathlands and peat bogs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

Calamagrostis dioica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
