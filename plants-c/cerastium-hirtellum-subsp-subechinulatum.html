Cerastium hirtellum subsp. subechinulatum

Cerastium hirtellum subsp.

subechinulatum, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, hairy pubescence.

The leaves are linear to ovate, with a hairy surface, and the flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is often found in the northeastern United States and parts of Canada.
