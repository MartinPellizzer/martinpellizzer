Campanula rigidipila var. quartiniana

Campanula rigidipila var. quartiniana, commonly known as the Quartinian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
