Carex orthostachys var. hirtiformis

Carex orthostachys var. hirtiformis, commonly known as the hairy fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically greenish or reddish in color.
