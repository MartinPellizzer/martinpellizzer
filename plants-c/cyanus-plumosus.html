Cyanus plumosus

Cyanus plumosus, commonly known as the blueweed or blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-green, woolly leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Cyanus plumosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique foliage.
