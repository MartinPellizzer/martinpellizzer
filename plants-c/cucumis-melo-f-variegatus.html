Cucumis melo f. variegatus

Cucumis melo f. variegatus, commonly known as the Variegated Muskmelon, is a botanical variant of the muskmelon species.

It is characterized by its distinctive mottled or variegated leaf patterns, which are a result of genetic mutations affecting chlorophyll distribution. The plant typically grows as a trailing or climbing vine, producing yellow to orange flowers and oval, sweet, aromatic fruits. Its foliage is often lighter in color compared to standard muskmelon varieties, making it visually striking.

This ornamental and edible plant is cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its flavorful, juicy fruit.
