Crotalaria ubonensis

Crotalaria ubonensis, commonly known as the Ubon stinkpea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is particularly noticeable when the flowers are in bloom. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Crotalaria ubonensis is also noted for its potential medicinal properties and its role in soil nitrogen fixation.
