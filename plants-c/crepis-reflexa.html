Crepis reflexa

Crepis reflexa, commonly known as the bent grass.

It is a flowering plant species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, typically found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Crepis reflexa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also a host plant for certain butterfly species.
