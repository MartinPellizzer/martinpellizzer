Campanula turbinata f. pelviformis

Campanula turbinata f. pelviformis, commonly known as the bellflower with pelvis-shaped leaves, is a unique variant of the campanula genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, and often lobed leaves that resemble a pelvis, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and delicate floral display.
