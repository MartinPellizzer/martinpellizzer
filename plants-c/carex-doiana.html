Carex doiana

Carex doiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. Carex doiana typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
