Crossopetalum gentianoides

Crossopetalum gentianoides, commonly known as the mountain gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive two-lipped structure, typical of many gentians.

Crossopetalum gentianoides plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
