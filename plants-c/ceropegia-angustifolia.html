Ceropegia angustifolia

Ceropegia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing or trailing perennial with thin, succulent stems and small, elongated leaves. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting specific pollinators like moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
