Campanula topaliana subsp. delphica

Campanula topaliana subsp.

delphica, commonly known as the Delphic bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, typically reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
