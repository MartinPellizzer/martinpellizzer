Centaurea × favratii

Centaurea × favratii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves, and it produces fluffy, composite flower heads.

Centaurea × favratii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
