Convolvulus althaeoides subsp. argyraeus

Convolvulus althaeoides subsp.

argyraeus, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit.

The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a fine silvery down, giving it its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.
