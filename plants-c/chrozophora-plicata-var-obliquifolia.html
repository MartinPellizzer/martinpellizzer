Chrozophora plicata var. obliquifolia

Chrozophora plicata var. obliquifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, oblique, and lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which are followed by spiky, bract-like structures. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
