Carradoria incanescens

Carradoria incanescens, commonly known as the white-leaved carradoria, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in both natural and cultivated settings. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes grown in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
