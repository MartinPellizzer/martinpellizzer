Cucumis sativus f. brunnescens

Cucumis sativus f. brunnescens, commonly known as the brown-skinned cucumber, is a horticultural variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its thick, dark green to brown skin, which is often more rugged and less smooth than that of the standard cucumber. The fruit typically has a shorter, stockier shape compared to typical cucumbers, and the skin is thicker, providing a more robust texture. It is often used in pickling due to its firm flesh and distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and visual appeal in both culinary and ornamental settings.
