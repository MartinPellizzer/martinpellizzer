Cassia barbata var. mollis

Cassia barbata var. mollis, commonly known as soft cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with a soft texture that distinguishes it from other cassia species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of natural dyes.
