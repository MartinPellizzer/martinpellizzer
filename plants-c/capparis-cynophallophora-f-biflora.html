Capparis cynophallophora f. biflora

Capparis cynophallophora f. biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered caper, is a unique variant of the caper plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, featuring two flowers per inflorescence, which sets it apart from the typical single-flowered form. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they emit a strong, pungent odor, attracting specific pollinators.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its morphological variation and ecological adaptations.
