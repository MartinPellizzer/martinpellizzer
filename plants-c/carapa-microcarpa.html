Carapa microcarpa

Carapa microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes other mahogany species. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, woody fruits. Its seeds are encased in a hard, fibrous pod, and the tree is valued for its durable, heavy wood.

Carapa microcarpa is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
