Cacalia auriculata subsp. praetermissa

Cacalia auriculata subsp.

praetermissa, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry garden settings.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant collections and ecological restoration projects.
