Crocus insularis var. minimus

Crocus insularis var. minimus, commonly known as the dwarf Sicilian crocus, is a small, bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pale yellow, and pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
