Coleus wugensis

Coleus wugensis, commonly known as the Wugeng coleus, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, often displaying shades of red, purple, and green. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces small, tubular flowers in clusters.

Coleus wugensis is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a potted plant.
