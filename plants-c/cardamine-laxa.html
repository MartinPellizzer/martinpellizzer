Cardamine laxa

Cardamine laxa, commonly known as slender watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, creeping stems with small, alternate leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tiny, white, and clustered in umbels, which bloom in early spring.

The plant is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
