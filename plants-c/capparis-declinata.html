Capparis declinata

Capparis declinata, commonly known as the rock caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, sprawling stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
