Caesalpinia hermeliae

Caesalpinia hermeliae, commonly known as the hermelia or golden glorybower, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Caesalpinia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it may become invasive in certain environments.
