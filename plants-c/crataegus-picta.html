Crataegus picta

Crataegus picta, commonly known as the painted hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers and ornamental fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and reddish in color.

The fruits, which are small red berries, are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
