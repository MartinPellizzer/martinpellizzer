Cinchona carua

Cinchona carua, commonly known as the yellow quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and ornamental flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain seeds, and its bark has historically been a source of quinine, used in the treatment of malaria. Cinchona carua is valued both for its medicinal properties and its role in horticulture.

Its cultivation also plays a significant part in the conservation of biodiversity in its native habitat.
