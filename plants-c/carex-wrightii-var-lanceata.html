Carex wrightii var. lanceata

Carex wrightii var. lanceata, commonly known as Wright's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its lance-shaped foliage and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex wrightii var. lanceata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
