Caltha freyniana

Caltha freyniana, commonly known as the Freyn's crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant features delicate, nodding yellow flowers that are typically borne on slender stems. Its leaves are palmately divided, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance.

Caltha freyniana thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.
