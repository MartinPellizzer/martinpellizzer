Chamaecrista cristalinae

Chamaecrista cristalinae, commonly known as the crystalline burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny pods that resemble small, crystalline structures, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along forest edges.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
