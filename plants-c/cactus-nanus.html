Cactus nanus

Cactus nanus, commonly known as the dwarf cactus, is a small, compact succulent native to arid regions of the Americas.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, featuring a short, cylindrical stem covered in sparse, pale spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Cactus nanus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique shape and low maintenance requirements.
