Conyza bellidifolia

Conyza bellidifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and it thrives in full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer.

Conyza bellidifolia is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand arid conditions.
