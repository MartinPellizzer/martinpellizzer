Cacalia oppositifolia

Cacalia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
