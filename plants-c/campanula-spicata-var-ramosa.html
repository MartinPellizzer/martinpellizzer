Campanula spicata var. ramosa

Campanula spicata var. ramosa, commonly known as the branched bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms.
