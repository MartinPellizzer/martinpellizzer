Calliandra tergemina

Calliandra tergemina, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
