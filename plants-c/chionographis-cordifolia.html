Chionographis cordifolia

Chionographis cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf snowbell, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Chionographis cordifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
