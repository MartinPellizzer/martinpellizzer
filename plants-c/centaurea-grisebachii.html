Centaurea grisebachii

Centaurea grisebachii, commonly known as the gray centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and produces showy blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Centaurea grisebachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
