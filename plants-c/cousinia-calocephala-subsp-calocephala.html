Cousinia calocephala subsp. calocephala

Cousinia calocephala subsp.

calocephala, commonly known as the woolly goa, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and tall, branched flowering stems.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological resilience.
