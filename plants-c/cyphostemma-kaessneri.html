Cyphostemma kaessneri

Cyphostemma kaessneri, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found growing along fences or trees in its natural habitat. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.
