Chenopodium ambrosioides var. denudatum

Chenopodium ambrosioides var. denudatum, commonly known as pigweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The species is also known for its potential medicinal uses and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
