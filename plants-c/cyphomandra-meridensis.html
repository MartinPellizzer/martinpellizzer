Cyphomandra meridensis

Cyphomandra meridensis, commonly known as the Andean nightshade, is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades and tomatoes.

It is native to South America, particularly the Andean regions of Peru and Ecuador. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
