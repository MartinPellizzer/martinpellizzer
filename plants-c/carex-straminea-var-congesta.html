Carex straminea var. congesta

Carex straminea var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
