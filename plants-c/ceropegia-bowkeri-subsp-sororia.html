Ceropegia bowkeri subsp. sororia

Ceropegia bowkeri subsp.

sororia, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
