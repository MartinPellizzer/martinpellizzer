Cola usambarensis

Cola usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambarensis cola, is a species of the coffee family, Rubiaceae, native to eastern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant, red flowers. The plant produces berries that contain stimulant compounds, similar to those found in other cola species. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local communities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora and plant chemistry.
