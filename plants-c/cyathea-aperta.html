Cyathea aperta

Cyathea aperta, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitats.

The plant's unique morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
