Carex × saxenii

Carex × saxenii, commonly known as the saxifrage sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along streambanks. The plant has narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
