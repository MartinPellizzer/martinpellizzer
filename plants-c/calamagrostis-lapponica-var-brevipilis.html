Calamagrostis lapponica var. brevipilis

Calamagrostis lapponica var. brevipilis, commonly known as the Arctic marsh grass, is a perennial grass native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as bogs, marshes, and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color.

Calamagrostis lapponica var. brevipilis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
