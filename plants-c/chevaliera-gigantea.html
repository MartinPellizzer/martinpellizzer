Chevaliera gigantea

Chevaliera gigantea, commonly known as the giant chevaliera, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, often with a waxy texture and deep green coloration. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in suitable climates.
