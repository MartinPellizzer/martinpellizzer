Caladenia clavula

Caladenia clavula, commonly known as the Narrow-leafed Spider-orchid, is an endemic species of the Australian flora.

It is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow leaves. The plant produces a single, elongated flower with a distinctive spider-like appearance, featuring a narrow labellum. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats, often in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Caladenia clavula is a protected species due to its limited distribution and ecological sensitivity.
