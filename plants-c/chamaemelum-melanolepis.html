Chamaemelum melanolepis

Chamaemelum melanolepis, commonly known as the blackroot fennel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and has narrow, aromatic leaves with a subtle anise-like scent. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in flat-topped clusters, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Chamaemelum melanolepis is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
