Carum stictocarpum

Carum stictocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved caraway, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small white or yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
