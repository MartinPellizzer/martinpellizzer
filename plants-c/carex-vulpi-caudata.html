Carex vulpi-caudata

Carex vulpi-caudata, commonly known as the fox-tail sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive spiky inflorescences that resemble a fox's tail, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex vulpi-caudata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
