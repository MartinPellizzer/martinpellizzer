Carex gracilis var. angustifolia

Carex gracilis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
