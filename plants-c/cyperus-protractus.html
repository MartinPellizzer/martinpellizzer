Cyperus protractus

Cyperus protractus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Cyperus protractus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the base of the plant.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
