Callitris columellaris var. intratropica

Callitris columellaris var. intratropica, commonly known as the coastal or inland white cypress, is a member of the Cupressaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a narrow, conical shape and scaly, dark grey bark. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are densely packed, giving the plant a dense, compact appearance. It produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is native to northern Australia and is often found in coastal and inland regions with well-drained soils.
