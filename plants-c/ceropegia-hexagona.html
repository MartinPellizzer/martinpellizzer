Ceropegia hexagona

Ceropegia hexagona, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are pollinated by specific moths. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Ceropegia hexagona is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and low maintenance requirements.
