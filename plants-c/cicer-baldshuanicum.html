Cicer baldshuanicum

Cicer baldshuanicum, commonly known as the Baldshuan vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
