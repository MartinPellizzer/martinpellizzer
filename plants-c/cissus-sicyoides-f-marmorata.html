Cissus sicyoides f. marmorata

Cissus sicyoides f. marmorata, commonly known as the marble cissus, is a varietal form of the genus Cissus, characterized by its unique mottled leaf patterns.

It is a climbing plant native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in warm, humid environments. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a textured surface, often displaying a striking combination of green, silver, and white markings. This plant prefers well-draining soil and indirect sunlight, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage, which makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and vertical gardens.
