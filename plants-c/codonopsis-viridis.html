Codonopsis viridis

Codonopsis viridis, commonly known as green codonopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which distinguish it from other members of its genus. Codonopsis viridis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and low-maintenance plant.
