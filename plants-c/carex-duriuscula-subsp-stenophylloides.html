Carex duriuscula subsp. stenophylloides

Carex duriuscula subsp.

stenophylloides, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive pale green to blue-green coloration.

It produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers in the summer, which are wind-pollinated.
