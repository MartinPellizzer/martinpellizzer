Cassia goyazensis

Cassia goyazensis, commonly known as Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in open woodlands and along riverbanks.

Cassia goyazensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
