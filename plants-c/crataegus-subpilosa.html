Crataegus subpilosa

Crataegus subpilosa, commonly known as the silky hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery-gray bark and small, ovate leaves with a finely toothed margin. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woods or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
