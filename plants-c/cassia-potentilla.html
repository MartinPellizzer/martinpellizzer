Cassia potentilla

Cassia potentilla, commonly known as golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Cassia potentilla is an important species in Australian flora and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
