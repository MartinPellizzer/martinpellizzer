Calorhabdos robusta

Calorhabdos robusta, commonly known as the robust flame vine, is a striking climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bats. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are woody and capable of rapid growth. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Calorhabdos robusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative element in tropical landscapes.
