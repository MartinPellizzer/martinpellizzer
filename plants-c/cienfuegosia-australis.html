Cienfuegosia australis

Cienfuegosia australis, commonly known as the Southern Cienfuegosia, is a rare shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
