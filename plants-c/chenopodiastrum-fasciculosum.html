Chenopodiastrum fasciculosum

Chenopodiastrum fasciculosum, commonly known as the clustered goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is often found in disturbed soils or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.
