Clematis thaimontana

Clematis thaimontana, commonly known as Thai mountain clematis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Clematis thaimontana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
