Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. bourgaei

Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. bourgaei, commonly known as the Bourgai's heartleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
