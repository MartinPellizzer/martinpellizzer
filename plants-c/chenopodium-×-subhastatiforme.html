Chenopodium × subhastatiforme

Chenopodium × subhastatiforme, commonly known as the hybrid goosefoot, is a species that results from the cross between Chenopodium album and Chenopodium berlandieri.

It is a short-lived perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, broadly ovate to triangular leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant is also known for its edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients.
