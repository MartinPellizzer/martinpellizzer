Cedronella breviflora

Cedronella breviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cedron, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas with cool climates.

Cedronella breviflora is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
