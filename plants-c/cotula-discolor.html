Cotula discolor

Cotula discolor, commonly known as the variable groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, ovate leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sandy habitats and is often found in coastal areas. Cotula discolor is an annual herb that produces numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it a common species in Mediterranean-type climates.
