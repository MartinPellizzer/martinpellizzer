Carduus × forsteri

Carduus × forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. It has been introduced to parts of North America and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
