Caryochloa montevidensis

Caryochloa montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
