Cyathea trichodesma

Cyathea trichodesma, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that supports its growth in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow in the dark.

Cyathea trichodesma plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
