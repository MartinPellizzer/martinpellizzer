Chlorophytum parvulum

Chlorophytum parvulum, commonly known as the spider plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a slight bluish tint. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers on slender stalks that rise from the center of the rosette. It is a hardy and low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Chlorophytum parvulum is also known for its ability to produce offsets, which can be easily propagated to create new plants.
