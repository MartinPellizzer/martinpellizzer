Chenopodium melanospermum

Chenopodium melanospermum, commonly known as blackseed goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its seeds are dark-colored and contain compounds that have been studied for their potential health benefits.

Chenopodium melanospermum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to arid environments.
