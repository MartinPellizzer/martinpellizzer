Chloris ekmanii

Chloris ekmanii, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and sustainable landscaping.
