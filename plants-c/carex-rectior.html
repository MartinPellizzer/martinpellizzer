Carex rectior

Carex rectior, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top. Native to North America, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
