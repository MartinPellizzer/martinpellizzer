Croton submetallicus

Croton submetallicus, commonly known as the metallic croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse species with unique botanical features. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in warm, humid climates.
