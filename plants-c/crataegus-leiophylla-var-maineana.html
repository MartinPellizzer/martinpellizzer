Crataegus leiophylla var. maineana

Crataegus leiophylla var. maineana, commonly known as the Maine hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northeastern United States.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

The Maine hawthorn is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
