Chamaenerion conspersum

Chamaenerion conspersum, commonly known as the sticky fireweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, roadsides, and other open areas.

Chamaenerion conspersum is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize new areas, making it both a pioneer species and a potential weed.
