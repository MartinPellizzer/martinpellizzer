Crepidium ophrydis

Crepidium ophrydis, commonly known as the orchid mantis, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, insect-like appearance, which helps it blend into its surroundings and avoid predators. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, with intricate patterns resembling the wings of a mantis. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
