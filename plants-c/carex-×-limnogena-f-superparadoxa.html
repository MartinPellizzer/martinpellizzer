Carex × limnogena f. superparadoxa

Carex × limnogena f. superparadoxa, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and large, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, typically bearing brownish or purplish flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it can tolerate saturated soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
