Carex corralensis

Carex corralensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

Carex corralensis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
