Corryocactus aureus

Corryocactus aureus, commonly known as the golden corryocactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, woolly hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small clusters, making it a distinctive addition to succulent collections.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
