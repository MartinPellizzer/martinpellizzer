Calea lutea

Calea lutea, commonly known as the yellow calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Calea lutea is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in folk remedies.
