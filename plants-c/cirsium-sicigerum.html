Cirsium sicigerum

Cirsium sicigerum, commonly known as the Sicilian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Cirsium sicigerum is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
