Capparis salicifolia

Capparis salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its willow-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, greenish fruits that mature into reddish-brown capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Capparis salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
