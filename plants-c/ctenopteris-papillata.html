Ctenopteris papillata

Ctenopteris papillata, commonly known as the hairy sea palm, is a species of feather moss found in coastal areas.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and the presence of small, papillate (bump-like) structures on its leaves. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils along shorelines and is often found in tidal zones. Its fronds are typically short and broad, with a waxy coating that helps protect it from salt spray.

Ctenopteris papillata plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small marine organisms.
