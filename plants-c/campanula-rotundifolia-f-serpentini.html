Campanula rotundifolia f. serpentini

Campanula rotundifolia f. serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's head bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features rounded, toothless leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream sides.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
