Corydalis bulbosa

Corydalis bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its bulbous root system and slender, erect stems that bear clusters of small, delicate flowers. The flowers typically range in color from pink to purple, with two upper petals that are often more prominent. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
