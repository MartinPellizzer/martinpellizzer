Carduus kerneri subsp. austro-orientalis

Carduus kerneri subsp.

austro-orientalis, commonly known as the Austrian thistle, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features a robust stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, often with spiny leaves and a distinctive purple flower head.

The plant produces a cluster of small, yellow flowers that develop into bristly, prickly seed heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
