Clematis dioica subsp. cordata

Clematis dioica subsp.

cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern North America. It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically 3-5 inches long, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white or pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and is often used in ornamental landscaping.
