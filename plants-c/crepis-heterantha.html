Crepis heterantha

Crepis heterantha, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Crepis heterantha is often found in disturbed habitats and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
