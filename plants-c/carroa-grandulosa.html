Carroa grandulosa

Carroa grandulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that contain toxic seeds. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its toxic properties, it is often avoided in agricultural settings.
