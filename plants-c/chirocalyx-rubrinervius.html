Chirocalyx rubrinervius

Chirocalyx rubrinervius, commonly known as the red-veined calyx plant, is a tropical species characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves are typically large, with a deep green color and prominent red veins that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
