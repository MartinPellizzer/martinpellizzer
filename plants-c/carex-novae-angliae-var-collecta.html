Carex novae-angliae var. collecta

Carex novae-angliae var. collecta, commonly known as the New England sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often found in temperate regions of North America.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
