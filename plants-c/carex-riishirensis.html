Carex riishirensis

Carex riishirensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

Carex riishirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
