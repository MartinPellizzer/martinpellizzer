Centaurea montana var. cyanea

Centaurea montana var. cyanea, commonly known as the blue mountain knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and vibrant blue to purple flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant produces feathery, bract-like leaves and has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and rocky areas, where it contributes to biodiversity.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological habitats.
