Cissus sicyoides f. tinctoria

Cissus sicyoides f. tinctoria, commonly known as the red-veined ivy, is a decorative climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins, giving it a striking visual appeal. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
