Cananga ouregou

Cananga ouregou, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a sweet, citrus-like scent. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, woody fruits containing seeds.

Cananga ouregou is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
