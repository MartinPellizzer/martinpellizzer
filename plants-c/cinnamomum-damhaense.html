Cinnamomum damhaense

Cinnamomum damhaense, commonly known as the Ethiopian cinnamon or Dammar tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Ethiopia and parts of Eritrea.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and fragrant bark. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its essential oils, derived from the bark and leaves, are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
