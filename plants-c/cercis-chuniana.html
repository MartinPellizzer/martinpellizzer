Cercis chuniana

Cercis chuniana, commonly known as the Chinese redbud, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round, brown pods that contain several seeds. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 feet and prefers well-drained soil.

Cercis chuniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
