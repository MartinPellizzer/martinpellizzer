Capraria pusilla

Capraria pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf capraria, is a small herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, featuring tiny, hairy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is well adapted to dry and sunny conditions, making it a hardy species.

Capraria pusilla is often used in rock gardens due to its compact size and ornamental value.
