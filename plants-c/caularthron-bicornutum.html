Caularthron bicornutum

Caularthron bicornutum, commonly known as the two-horned algae, is a unicellular, marine alga belonging to the order Rhodophyta.

It is characterized by its distinctive two horn-like projections at the top of its cell, which aid in its identification. The organism is typically found in coastal waters and is often associated with rocky substrates. Its simple structure and unique morphology make it an important subject in studies of marine phytoplankton.
