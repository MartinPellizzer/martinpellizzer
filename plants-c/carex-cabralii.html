Carex cabralii

Carex cabralii, commonly known as Cabral's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, including regions in the Mediterranean.

Carex cabralii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
