Chassalia assimilis var. assimilis

Chassalia assimilis var. assimilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chassalia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
