Carum peucedanifolium

Carum peucedanifolium, commonly known as Persian cumin or wild cumin, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley, carrots, and dill, and is characterized by its umbellate inflorescences. The plant has slender, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Its small, yellowish-white flowers bloom in late summer, and the seeds are harvested for their pungent, spicy flavor.

Carum peucedanifolium is valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.
