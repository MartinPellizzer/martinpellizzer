Chasmia rego

Chasmia rego, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in spring, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Chasmia rego is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging ecological conditions.
