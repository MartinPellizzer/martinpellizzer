Cleome arabica

Cleome arabica, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its striking, often bi-colorful flowers that resemble spiders. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Cleome arabica is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.
