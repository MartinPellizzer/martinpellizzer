Campylotropis macrocarpa f. alba

Campylotropis macrocarpa f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered burr clover, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spherical seed pods that are often covered in hooked bristles. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
