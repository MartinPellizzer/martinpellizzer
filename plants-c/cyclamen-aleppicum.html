Cyclamen aleppicum

Cyclamen aleppicum, commonly known as the Aleppo cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves and showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces violet or pink flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur at the base of each petal. Cyclamen aleppicum prefers cool, moist environments and is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for winter blooms in temperate climates.
