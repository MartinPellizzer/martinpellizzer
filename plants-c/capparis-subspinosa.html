Capparis subspinosa

Capparis subspinosa, commonly known as the prickly gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with thorny stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its botanical features include a woody stem, glandular hairs, and a preference for well-drained, rocky soils.

Capparis subspinosa is often found in coastal and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
