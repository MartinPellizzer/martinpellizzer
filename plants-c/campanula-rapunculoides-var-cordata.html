Campanula rapunculoides var. cordata

Campanula rapunculoides var. cordata, commonly known as the heart-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
