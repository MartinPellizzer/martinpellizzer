Chrysolaena cognata

Chrysolaena cognata, commonly known as the goldenleaf or silverleaf plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, often variegated foliage. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves with a metallic sheen, which can range in color from silver to deep green. It produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the warmer months.

Chrysolaena cognata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
