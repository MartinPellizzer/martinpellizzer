Cardamine boryi

Cardamine boryi, commonly known as the Bory's watercress, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in a raceme, and it produces tiny, rounded seeds.

Cardamine boryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
