Cousinia minuta

Cousinia minuta, commonly known as the dwarf hawk's-beard, is a small annual herb native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It features delicate yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in a single, compact head. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stem is usually hairy and branched. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Cousinia minuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
