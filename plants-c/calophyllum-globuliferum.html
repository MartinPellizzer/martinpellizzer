Calophyllum globuliferum

Calophyllum globuliferum, commonly known as the globular flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody, globular fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
