Carduus undulatus var. canescens

Carduus undulatus var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, silvery-green, lobed leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The woolly thistle is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
