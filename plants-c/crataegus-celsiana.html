Crataegus celsiana

Crataegus celsiana, commonly known as the Celsian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and it is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

Crataegus celsiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
