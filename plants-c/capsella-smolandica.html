Capsella smolandica

Capsella smolandica, commonly known as the Norwegian chickweed, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its leaves are hairy and have a slightly bitter taste, making it a minor weed in agricultural settings.
