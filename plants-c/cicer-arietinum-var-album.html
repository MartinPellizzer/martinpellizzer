Cicer arietinum var. album

Cicer arietinum var. album, commonly known as the white chickpea, is a variant of the common chickpea characterized by its pale or white pod and seed color.

This variety is native to the Mediterranean region and is cultivated for its nutritional value, particularly its high protein content. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It produces trifoliate leaves and has a flowering period that typically occurs in the summer months.

The seeds are harvested when the pods are dry and can be used in various culinary and agricultural applications.
