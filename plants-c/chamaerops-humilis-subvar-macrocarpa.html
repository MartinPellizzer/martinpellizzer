Chamaerops humilis subvar. macrocarpa

Chamaerops humilis subvar. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Mediterranean fan palm, is a hardy, cold-tolerant plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a small, slow-growing palm with a dense, fan-shaped crown of stiff, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, round fruits with a sweet, nutty flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in Mediterranean climates.
