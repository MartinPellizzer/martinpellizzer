Calyptranthes canaliculata

Calyptranthes canaliculata, commonly known as the channel-veined calyptranthes, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves with distinctive channel-like veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Calyptranthes canaliculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
