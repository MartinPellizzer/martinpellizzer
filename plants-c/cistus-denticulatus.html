Cistus denticulatus

Cistus denticulatus, commonly known as the serrated rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its rugged habitat. Its leaves are covered in a fine, white, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Cistus denticulatus is also noted for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
