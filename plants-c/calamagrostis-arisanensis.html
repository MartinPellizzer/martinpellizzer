Calamagrostis arisanensis

Calamagrostis arisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and tall, slender inflorescences that produce fluffy, white plumes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an effective erosion control plant.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
