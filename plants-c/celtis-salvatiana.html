Celtis salvatiana

Celtis salvatiana, commonly known as the Mexican hackberry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
