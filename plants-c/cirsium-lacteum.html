Cirsium lacteum

Cirsium lacteum, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant contains a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature and has been used in traditional medicine. Its seeds are often used to make a herbal remedy, and the plant is known for its hardy nature.

Cirsium lacteum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
