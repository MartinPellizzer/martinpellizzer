Chloris lobata

Chloris lobata, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Chloris lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
