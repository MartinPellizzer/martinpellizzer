Cordylanthus pallescens

Cordylanthus pallescens, commonly known as the pale cordylanthus, is a perennial herb native to California.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It features slender, upright stems with small, pale green leaves that are often hairy.

Its flowers are showy, with purple to pink petals, and it blooms from spring to summer.
