Curcuma strobilifera

Curcuma strobilifera, commonly known as the spike ginger or mountain ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique inflorescence, which resembles a spike, and its aromatic leaves.
