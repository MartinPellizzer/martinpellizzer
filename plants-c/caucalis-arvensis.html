Caucalis arvensis

Caucalis arvensis, commonly known as field scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that grows up to 30 cm in height, characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically blue or purple and bloom from June to August. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant has feathery leaves and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
