Chaenomeles japonica var. maulei

Chaenomeles japonica var. maulei, commonly known as the Japanese flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces yellow, berry-like fruits in late summer, which are edible but often tart. Its leaves are ovate and have a glossy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
