Cardamine gallaecica

Cardamine gallaecica, commonly known as the Galician watercress, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Galicia in northwestern Spain.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks and in woodland areas. Its leaves are often used in local cuisine, and it is valued for its peppery flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
