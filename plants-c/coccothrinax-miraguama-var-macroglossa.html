Coccothrinax miraguama var. macroglossa

Coccothrinax miraguama var. macroglossa, commonly known as the Macroglossa palm, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are often used for thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
