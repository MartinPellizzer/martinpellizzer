Cymbopogon claessensii

Cymbopogon claessensii, commonly known as the African lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, pale green to brown flowers in clusters, which are typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
