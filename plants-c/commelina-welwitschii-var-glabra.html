Commelina welwitschii var. glabra

Commelina welwitschii var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers arranged in a raceme, which are typically borne on long pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
