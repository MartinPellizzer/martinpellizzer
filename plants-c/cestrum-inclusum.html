Cestrum inclusum

Cestrum inclusum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 feet tall, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal insects such as moths and bats.

The plant is often cultivated for its striking nighttime blooms and is relatively easy to care for in warm climates.
