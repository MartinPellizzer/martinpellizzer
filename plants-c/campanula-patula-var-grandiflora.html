Campanula patula var. grandiflora

Campanula patula var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Great-flowered Bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance. This variety is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is also known for its attractive foliage that persists through the growing season.
