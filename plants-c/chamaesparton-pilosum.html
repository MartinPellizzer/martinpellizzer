Chamaesparton pilosum

Chamaesparton pilosum, commonly known as the silky broomweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, and its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
