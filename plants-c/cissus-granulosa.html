Cissus granulosa

Cissus granulosa, commonly known as the rock fig or granulate tendril vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive granular texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Cissus granulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in challenging environments.
