Cousinia leptolepis

Cousinia leptolepis, commonly known as the slenderleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine pubescence, while its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in dense clusters.

Cousinia leptolepis is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
