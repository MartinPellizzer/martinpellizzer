Cedrus libanitica subsp. atlantica

Cedrus libanitica subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlas cedar, is a subspecies of the cedar tree native to the Atlas Mountains of North Africa. It is a tall, coniferous evergreen with a pyramidal shape and dense, dark green foliage.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green cones that mature into woody, barrel-shaped structures. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional architecture and medicine.
