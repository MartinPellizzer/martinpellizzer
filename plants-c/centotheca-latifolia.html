Centotheca latifolia

Centotheca latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf centotheca, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant is often found in meadows and along woodland edges, where it contributes to biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
