Crinum purpurascens var. angustilobium

Crinum purpurascens var. angustilobium, commonly known as the narrow-lobed crinum, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant produces strap-like, lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season.
