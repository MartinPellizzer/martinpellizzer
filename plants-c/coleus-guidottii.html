Coleus guidottii

Coleus guidottii, commonly known as the Guidotti's coleus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in whorls along the stems.

Coleus guidottii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid conditions.
