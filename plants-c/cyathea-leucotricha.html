Cyathea leucotricha

Cyathea leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a distinctive silvery-white coating on its trunk, which gives it its common name. Cyathea leucotricha is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various small organisms.
