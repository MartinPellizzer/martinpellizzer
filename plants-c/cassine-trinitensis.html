Cassine trinitensis

Cassine trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidadian cassine, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
