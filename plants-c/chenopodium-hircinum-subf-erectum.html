Chenopodium hircinum subf. erectum

Chenopodium hircinum subf.

erectum, commonly known as redroot goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive reddish stems, which are often covered in fine hairs.

The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and typically have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are minute and dry.

It is native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
