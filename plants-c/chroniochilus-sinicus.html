Chroniochilus sinicus

Chroniochilus sinicus, commonly known as the Chinese orchid, is a species of orchid native to China.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers, which can be white, pink, or purple, bloom in spring and are popular in horticulture for their beauty and fragrance.
