Cacalia tuberosa

Cacalia tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous cacalia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used in traditional cuisine.
