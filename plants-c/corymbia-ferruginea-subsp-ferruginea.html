Corymbia ferruginea subsp. ferruginea

Corymbia ferruginea subsp.

ferruginea, commonly known as the red blood gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to western Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple.

The plant produces white or pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits. It is typically found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand drought and fire.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native region.
