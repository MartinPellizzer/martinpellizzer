Campanula comosiformis

Campanula comosiformis, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula comosiformis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
