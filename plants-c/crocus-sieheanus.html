Crocus sieheanus

Crocus sieheanus, commonly known as the Siehean crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, nodding shape, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a corm, a type of underground storage organ.

The Siehean crocus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
