Carex dioica subsp. isogyna

Carex dioica subsp.

isogyna, commonly known as the smooth sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments. It is characterized by its triangular, smooth, and often glossy leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in temperate regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
