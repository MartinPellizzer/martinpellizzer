Calandrinia calycina

Calandrinia calycina, commonly known as the "pink morning glory," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant pink or purple flowers with five petals. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Calandrinia calycina is known for its rapid growth and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
