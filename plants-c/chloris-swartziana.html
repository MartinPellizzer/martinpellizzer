Chloris swartziana

Chloris swartziana, commonly known as Swartz's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

Chloris swartziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
