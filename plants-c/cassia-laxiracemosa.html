Cassia laxiracemosa

Cassia laxiracemosa, commonly known as the slender cassia, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in racemose inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and serrated.

Cassia laxiracemosa is often grown for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
