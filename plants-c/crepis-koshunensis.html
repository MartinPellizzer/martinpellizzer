Crepis koshunensis

Crepis koshunensis, commonly known as the Koshun hairy cinquefoil, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins and produces small, composite flower heads.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
