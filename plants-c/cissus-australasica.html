Cissus australasica

Cissus australasica, commonly known as the Australian grapevine, is a climbing plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its tendrils that help it climb towards light. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly waxy texture.

Cissus australasica is often found in rocky or sandy soils in coastal and inland regions.
