Casparya urticae

Casparya urticae, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its serrated leaves and hollow, hair-covered stems that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is found throughout temperate regions. Casparya urticae has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various soil types.

Its flowers are small and greenish, often clustered in umbels, and it is an important plant in both ecological and historical contexts.
