Cracca dichotoma

Cracca dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous cracca, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, where the stems split into two equal parts, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Cracca dichotoma is often found in dry habitats and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
