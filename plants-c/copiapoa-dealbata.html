Copiapoa dealbata

Copiapoa dealbata, commonly known as the white copiapoa, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to the arid environment.

Copiapoa dealbata is a slow-growing plant that requires minimal water and is well-suited to rocky soils.
