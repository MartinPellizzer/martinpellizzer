Cyanothamnus polygalifolius

Cyanothamnus polygalifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or heathland environments. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
