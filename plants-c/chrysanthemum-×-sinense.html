Chrysanthemum × sinense

Chrysanthemum × sinense, commonly known as the garden chrysanthemum, is a hybrid species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its diverse range of flower colors, including yellow, red, white, and purple, and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disk florets, arranged in a composite head.

Chrysanthemum × sinense thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
