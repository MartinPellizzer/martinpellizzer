Carex appropinquata

Carex appropinquata, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats, such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

Carex appropinquata is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
