Chrysanthemum delavayi

Chrysanthemum delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's chrysanthemum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowering period occurs in the autumn, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to high-altitude conditions and its role in traditional Chinese herbal medicine.
