Crataegus uplandia

Crataegus uplandia, commonly known as the upland hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland areas, stream banks, and swamps. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
