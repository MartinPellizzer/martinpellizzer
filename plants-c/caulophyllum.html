Caulophyllum

Caulophyllum, commonly known as blue cohosh, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue berries and fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. Caulophyllum has been used in traditional medicine for its potential tonic and antispasmodic properties.

Its botanical features make it an important component of forest ecosystems.
