Cleome chilensis var. pubescens

Cleome chilensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the Chilean spider flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it the varietal name "pubescens.". The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its unique appearance and easy care requirements make it a valuable addition to ornamental gardens.
