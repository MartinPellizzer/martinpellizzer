Calligonum caput-medusae var. rubicundum

Calligonum caput-medusae var. rubicundum, commonly known as the red-headed spurge, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

The variety rubicundum is distinguished by its reddish-brown stem coloration, which adds to its ornamental value.
