Chetocrater tinifolia

Chetocrater tinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf chetocrater, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside its native region.
