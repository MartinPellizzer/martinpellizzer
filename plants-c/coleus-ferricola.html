Coleus ferricola

Coleus ferricola, commonly known as the iron plant, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often multicolored foliage that displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in partial shade. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale purple.

Coleus ferricola is popular in landscaping and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
