Crataegus coccinea var. coccinea

Crataegus coccinea var. coccinea, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries that persist through winter, providing food for wildlife. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges or open fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
