Crepis floribunda

Crepis floribunda, commonly known as the showy mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

The leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
