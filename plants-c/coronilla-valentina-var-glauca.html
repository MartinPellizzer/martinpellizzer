Coronilla valentina var. glauca

Coronilla valentina var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive bluish-gray underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by narrow, elongated seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for erosion control due to its vigorous growth.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil fertility in agricultural settings.
