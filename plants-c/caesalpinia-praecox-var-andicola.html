Caesalpinia praecox var. andicola

Caesalpinia praecox var. andicola, commonly known as the Andean coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that resemble coral, which bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
