Cucumis arenarius

Cucumis arenarius, commonly known as the sand cucumber, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into elongated, green fruits resembling cucumbers. Its fruit is typically harvested when immature, before it fully matures and turns yellow.

Cucumis arenarius is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to arid conditions.
