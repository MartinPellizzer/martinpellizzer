Coleogeton filiformis subsp. occidentalis

Coleogeton filiformis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western threadleaf, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, thread-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing pale yellow to greenish blooms.

This subspecies is adapted to arid conditions and has a low-growing habit to conserve moisture.
