Chondrorhyncha pusilla

Chondrorhyncha pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf spongeflower, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers with a unique sponge-like texture on the petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its botanical significance lies in its distinctive floral morphology and ecological role in its native environment.
