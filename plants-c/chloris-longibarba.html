Chloris longibarba

Chloris longibarba, commonly known as the long-barbed grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Chloris longibarba is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
