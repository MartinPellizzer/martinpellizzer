Crassula expansa subsp. fragilis

Crassula expansa subsp.

fragilis, commonly known as the Fragile Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, bluish-green leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water in arid environments.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated as a prized ornamental species.
