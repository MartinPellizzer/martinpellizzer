Crataegus orthosepala

Crataegus orthosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Korea. The plant is characterized by its small, thorny branches and white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the fall and are edible for birds and some mammals.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
