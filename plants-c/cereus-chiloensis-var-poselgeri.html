Cereus chiloensis var. poselgeri

Cereus chiloensis var. poselgeri, commonly known as the Poselger's chilote cactus, is a variety of the Chilote cactus native to Chile.

It is a spiny, columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a tall, unbranched stem and clusters of white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has sharp, thick spines and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.

Its unique morphology and nocturnal flowering make it a striking addition to desert gardens.
