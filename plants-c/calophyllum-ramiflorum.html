Calophyllum ramiflorum

Calophyllum ramiflorum, commonly known as the branched-flower calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
