Carex phaeopoda

Carex phaeopoda, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often in wet meadows or heathlands. Its leaves are usually dark green with a reddish or purplish tinge, especially along the margins.

It produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer.
