Clematis dioica var. subglabra

Clematis dioica var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
