Crucifera apetala

Crucifera apetala, commonly known as the petalless crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of petals, which is an unusual trait among flowering plants. The plant produces small, green, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its unique floral structure, it is of interest to botanists studying floral evolution and plant adaptation.
