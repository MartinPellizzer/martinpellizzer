Chrysophyllum castanospermum

Chrysophyllum castanospermum, commonly known as the Natal plum or wild plum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, yellow or orange drupes. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure with a strong, aromatic scent.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit, which is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
