Cosmos deficiens

Cosmos deficiens, commonly known as the Mexican whorled cosmos, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, branched stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with a distinctive whorled arrangement of petals around a central disk.

Cosmos deficiens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
