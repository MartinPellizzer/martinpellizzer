Chuquiraga sprengeliana f. brasiliensis

Chuquiraga sprengeliana f. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian chuquiraga, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features yellow to orange composite flowers, which are arranged in a cluster and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
