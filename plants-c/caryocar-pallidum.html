Caryocar pallidum

Caryocar pallidum, commonly known as the white caryocar, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, thick, fleshy fruits that are yellow to orange in color. The fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp.

It is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
