Camellia japonica var. ulantha

Camellia japonica var. ulantha, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter flowering.
