Cybianthus lagoensis

Cybianthus lagoensis, commonly known as the Lake Cybianthus, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands and along the edges of lakes, where it plays a role in stabilizing the ecosystem.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
