Cacalia taimingasa

Cacalia taimingasa, commonly known as the Taimingasa bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Cacalia taimingasa thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
