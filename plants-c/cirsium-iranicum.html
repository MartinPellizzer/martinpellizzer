Cirsium iranicum

Cirsium iranicum, commonly known as the Iranian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disc florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium iranicum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
