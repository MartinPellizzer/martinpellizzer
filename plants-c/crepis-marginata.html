Crepis marginata

Crepis marginata, commonly known as the hairy bentgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or on dunes. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

The species is notable for its adaptability and ecological importance in stabilizing sandy environments.
