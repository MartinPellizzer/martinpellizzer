Cyperus baenitzii

Cyperus baenitzii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in shallow water or saturated soil. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are often clustered in a cylindrical shape.

Cyperus baenitzii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
