Cymbalariella arachnoidea

Cymbalariella arachnoidea, commonly known as the spiderweb lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive, web-like thallus structure.

It typically grows on bark, stone, or soil in temperate and tropical regions. The plant's surface is covered with a network of fine, thread-like hyphae that give it its characteristic appearance. It is often found in shaded, moist environments where it can absorb nutrients from the substrate.

This lichen is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
