Camellia kangdianica

Camellia kangdianica, commonly known as the Kangdian camellia, is a species native to China, specifically the Yunnan and Sichuan provinces.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant flowers.

Camellia kangdianica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
