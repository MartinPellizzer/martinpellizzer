Caltha palustris subsp. membranacea

Caltha palustris subsp.

membranacea, commonly known as the marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America. It features showy, yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are usually hairy and can grow up to 60 cm in height.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along stream banks.
