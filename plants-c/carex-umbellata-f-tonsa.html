Carex umbellata f. tonsa

Carex umbellata f. tonsa, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves, and its inflorescence consists of a tall, cylindrical spike of small, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
