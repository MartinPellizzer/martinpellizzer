Centrospermum xanthioides

Centrospermum xanthioides, commonly known as the yellow star jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and visual beauty.
