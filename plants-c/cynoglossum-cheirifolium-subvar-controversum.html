Cynoglossum cheirifolium subvar. controversum

Cynoglossum cheirifolium subvar. controversum, commonly known as the Algerian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces striking blue to violet flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating a degree of drought tolerance. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral characteristics compared to other members of the genus.
