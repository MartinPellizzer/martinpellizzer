Callista formosa

Callista formosa, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting darker stripe. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
