Clematis cruttwellii

Clematis cruttwellii, commonly known as the purple clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Clematis cruttwellii is relatively hardy and can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
