Centaurea microlopha

Centaurea microlopha, commonly known as the small-lobed knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, bushy growth habit and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers with purple centers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Centaurea microlopha is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
