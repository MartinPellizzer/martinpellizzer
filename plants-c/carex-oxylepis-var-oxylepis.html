Carex oxylepis var. oxylepis

Carex oxylepis var. oxylepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are typically borne on a tall, leafy stem.

This variety is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
