Cerasus regalis var. praecox

Cerasus regalis var. praecox, commonly known as the early cherry or Japanese early cherry, is a variety of the sweet cherry species.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits that ripen earlier than most other cherry varieties. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white with a pinkish tinge.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruits.
