Chevaliera cariocae

Chevaliera cariocae, commonly known as the Carioca orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered blooms during the rainy season.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
