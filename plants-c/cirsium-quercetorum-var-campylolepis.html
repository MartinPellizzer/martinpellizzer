Cirsium quercetorum var. campylolepis

Cirsium quercetorum var. campylolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads that are surrounded by bracts, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
