Carex fossa

Carex fossa, commonly known as the Fossa sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Native to specific regions in Africa, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant has rhizomatous growth, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems, supporting local biodiversity.
