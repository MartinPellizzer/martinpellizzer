Cuminia eriantha var. eriantha

Cuminia eriantha var. eriantha, commonly known as the woolly cumin, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, pale green leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by narrow, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional cuisines and have been valued for their medicinal properties.
