Chenopodium hircinum var. andinum

Chenopodium hircinum var. andinum, commonly known as Andean goosefoot, is a species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is an annual herbaceous plant that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a rough, sometimes hairy texture. Its small green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces dry, three-angled fruits.

It is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional Andean cuisine.
