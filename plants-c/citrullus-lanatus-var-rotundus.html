Citrullus lanatus var. rotundus

Citrullus lanatus var. rotundus, commonly known as the watermelon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its large, round fruits with a thick rind and juicy, sweet flesh.

The plant is a vigorous vine that produces both male and female flowers, with the female flowers developing into the edible fruit. Its leaves are broad, lobed, and covered in a fine hairy layer, while the stems are hairy and trailing. The fruit typically contains numerous seeds and is rich in water, sugars, and nutrients, making it a popular crop worldwide.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
