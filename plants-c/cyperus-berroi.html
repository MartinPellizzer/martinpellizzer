Cyperus berroi

Cyperus berroi, commonly known as the African umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features tall, narrow leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with small, white or pale flowers. Cyperus berroi thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.
