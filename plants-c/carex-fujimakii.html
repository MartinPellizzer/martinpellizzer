Carex fujimakii

Carex fujimakii, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex fujimakii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
