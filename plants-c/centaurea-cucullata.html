Centaurea cucullata

Centaurea cucullata, commonly known as the hooded centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its striking blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, and the plant is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Centaurea cucullata is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
