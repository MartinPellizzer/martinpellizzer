Catasetum hookeri var. labiatum

Catasetum hookeri var. labiatum, commonly known as the labiated catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive labellum that is broader than the other petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are known for their unique structure, which includes a specialized column used in pollination by specific bees.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
