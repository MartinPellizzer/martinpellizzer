Cytisus pannonicus

Cytisus pannonicus, commonly known as the Pannonian broom, is a shrub native to southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and slender, woody stems. The plant has small, pinnate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

Cytisus pannonicus is adapted to temperate climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
