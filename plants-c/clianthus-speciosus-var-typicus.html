Clianthus speciosus var. typicus

Clianthus speciosus var. typicus, commonly known as the New Zealand waratah, is a striking flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous petal-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal and montane areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from red to pink, are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly the native bellbird.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a significant role in New Zealand's ecological and horticultural landscapes.
