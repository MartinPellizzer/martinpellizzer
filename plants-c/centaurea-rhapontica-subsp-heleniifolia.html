Centaurea rhapontica subsp. heleniifolia

Centaurea rhapontica subsp.

heleniifolia, commonly known as the Helen's leaf centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
