Cassia × desolata var. planipes

Cassia × desolata var. planipes, commonly known as the Chinese cassia, is a hybrid shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warmer regions.
