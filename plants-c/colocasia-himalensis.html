Colocasia himalensis

Colocasia himalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale green flowers that are typically hidden within a spathe, a modified leaf-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in stream banks and forest understories.

Colocasia himalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for its edible tubers.
