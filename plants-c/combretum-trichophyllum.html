Combretum trichophyllum

Combretum trichophyllum, commonly known as the white-flowered combretum, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Combretum trichophyllum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
