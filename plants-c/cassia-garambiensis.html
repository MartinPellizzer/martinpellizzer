Cassia garambiensis

Cassia garambiensis, commonly known as the Garambi cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Cassia garambiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
