Caladium macropus

Caladium macropus, commonly known as the giant arrowleaf caladium, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant's foliage is typically a vibrant mix of green, white, and red, making it a striking ornamental plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Caladium macropus is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
