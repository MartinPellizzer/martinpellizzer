Casalea

Casalea, commonly known as the orchid.

It is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae, native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. Casalea species are characterized by their showy, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plants typically have strap-like leaves and produce pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

They are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and are often cultivated as houseplants or in gardens.
