Chamaesyce coghlanii

Chamaesyce coghlanii, commonly known as the Coghlan's sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with a mat-forming habit, typically reaching heights of less than 10 centimeters. The plant features small, pale green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
