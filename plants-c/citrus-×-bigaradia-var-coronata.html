Citrus × bigaradia var. coronata

Citrus × bigaradia var. coronata, commonly known as the grapefruit orange.

This hybrid citrus plant is a cross between the grapefruit and the sweet orange, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. It typically produces small, round fruits that are often a mix of orange and red hues, with a thick, segmented rind. The fruit is known for its tart flavor and high vitamin C content, making it popular in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Botanically, it is characterized by its aromatic leaves, thorny stems, and a complex genetic makeup that contributes to its distinct appearance and taste.
