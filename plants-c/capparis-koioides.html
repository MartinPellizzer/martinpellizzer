Capparis koioides

Capparis koioides, commonly known as the Japanese glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of China.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with thorny stems and dark green, elliptical leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Capparis koioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
