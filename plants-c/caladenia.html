Caladenia

Caladenia, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

These plants are characterized by their intricate, often colorful floral structures that resemble spiders, hence their common name. Caladenia species typically have a single, erect stem with one or more flowers, and their blooms are often strikingly vivid in shades of red, yellow, and white. They are part of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and are known for their specialized pollination mechanisms.

Many Caladenia species are rare or endangered due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
