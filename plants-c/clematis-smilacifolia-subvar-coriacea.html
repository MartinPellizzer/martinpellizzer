Clematis smilacifolia subvar. coriacea

Clematis smilacifolia subvar. coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to southern regions of the United States.

It features compound leaves with coarsely toothed margins and a distinctive, rough texture on both sides. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in spring. Its stems are woody and can become quite vigorous, often requiring support as it climbs.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
