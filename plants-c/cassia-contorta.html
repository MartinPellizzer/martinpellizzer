Cassia contorta

Cassia contorta, commonly known as the zigzag cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, winding stems that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slight waxy coating.

Cassia contorta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
