Cinnamomum aureofulvum

Cinnamomum aureofulvum, commonly known as the golden-barked cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that exudes a fragrant aroma when cut. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that contain elliptical seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a leathery texture with a strong, spicy scent.

The tree is valued for its aromatic bark and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
