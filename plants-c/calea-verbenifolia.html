Calea verbenifolia

Calea verbenifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic leaves and showy blooms. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.

Calea verbenifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
