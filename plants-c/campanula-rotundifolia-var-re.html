Campanula rotundifolia var. re

Campanula rotundifolia var. re, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of rounded, velvety leaves and produces striking bell-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
