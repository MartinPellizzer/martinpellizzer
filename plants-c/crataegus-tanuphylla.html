Crataegus tanuphylla

Crataegus tanuphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus tanuphylla is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks.
