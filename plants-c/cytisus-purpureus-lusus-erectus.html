Cytisus purpureus lusus erectus

Cytisus purpureus lusus erectus, commonly known as the purple broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with a slight serration along the edges.

This species is known for its adaptability to poor soils and its ability to thrive in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
