Cyperus retrorsus var. cylindricus

Cyperus retrorsus var. cylindricus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically at the base of the stem. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common species in wetlands and along water bodies.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
