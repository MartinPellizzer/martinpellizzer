Coleus odoratus

Coleus odoratus, commonly known as the fragrant coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. Its small, tubular flowers are typically white or pale purple and bloom in clusters.

Coleus odoratus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
