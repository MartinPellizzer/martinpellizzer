Colymbada chrysantha

Colymbada chrysantha, commonly known as the golden cupflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Colymbada chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
