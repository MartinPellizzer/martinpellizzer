Celosia linearis

Celosia linearis, commonly known as the flame flower or cockscomb, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery bracts that resemble a comb or flame, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by these colorful, leaf-like structures. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Celosia linearis is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance and ability to grow in poor soils.
