Chamaemelum serratifolium

Chamaemelum serratifolium, commonly known as the serrated yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and dry grasslands.

Chamaemelum serratifolium is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
