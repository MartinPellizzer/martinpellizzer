Caladium smaragdinum var. asperulum

Caladium smaragdinum var. asperulum, commonly known as the roughleaf caladium, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with striking red or pink veins. The leaves are often slightly rough to the touch, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-draining soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its vibrant foliage and is propagated through tuberous roots.
