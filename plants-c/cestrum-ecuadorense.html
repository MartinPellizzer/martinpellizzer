Cestrum ecuadorense

Cestrum ecuadorense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian jessamine, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are small, capsule-like berries that contain several seeds.

Cestrum ecuadorense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
