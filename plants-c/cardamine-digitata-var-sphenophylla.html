Cardamine digitata var. sphenophylla

Cardamine digitata var. sphenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small white or yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or streamside habitats, where it thrives in cool, damp conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The variety sphenophylla is distinguished by its narrower, more elongated leaves compared to the typical form of Cardamine digitata.
