Calliandra redacta

Calliandra redacta, commonly known as the red velvet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, showy inflorescences that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

Calliandra redacta is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
