Conyza coulteri

Conyza coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and prairies, and it produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Conyza coulteri is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
