Catevala peacockii

Catevala peacockii, commonly known as the peacock orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flower spikes that resemble a peacock's tail. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are composed of intricate, overlapping petals and sepals that create a visually stunning display.

Catevala peacockii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
