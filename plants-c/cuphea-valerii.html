Cuphea valerii

Cuphea valerii, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Cuphea valerii is known for its compact growth habit and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
