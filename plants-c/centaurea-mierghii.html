Centaurea mierghii

Centaurea mierghii, commonly known as Persian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Centaurea mierghii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
