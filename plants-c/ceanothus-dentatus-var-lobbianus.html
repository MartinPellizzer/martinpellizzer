Ceanothus dentatus var. lobbianus

Ceanothus dentatus var. lobbianus, commonly known as the Sierra foothill ceanothus, is a shrub native to California and parts of Oregon.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and chaparral areas. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in late spring. It is well adapted to drought conditions and requires minimal water once established.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
