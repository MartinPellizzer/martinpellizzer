Chenopodium bryoniifolium var. kapelleriae

Chenopodium bryoniifolium var. kapelleriae, commonly known as the Kapeller's goosefoot, is a variant of the goosefoot plant native to Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines for its edible leaves and seeds.
