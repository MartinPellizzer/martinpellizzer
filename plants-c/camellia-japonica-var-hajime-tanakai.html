Camellia japonica var. hajime-tanakai

Camellia japonica var. hajime-tanakai, commonly known as the Hajime Tanakai camellia, is a cultivar prized for its ornamental value.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is native to Japan. The plant features large, showy flowers that bloom in winter and early spring, often in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms.

This variety is well-suited for cultivation in gardens and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
