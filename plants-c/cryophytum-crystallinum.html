Cryophytum crystallinum

Cryophytum crystallinum, commonly known as the crystalline frost plant, is a unique species native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, glass-like leaves that refract light, giving it an otherworldly appearance. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and has adapted to extreme conditions through specialized cellular structures. Its growth is slow, and it requires minimal water, making it a resilient species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family and is notable for its unusual morphology and ecological adaptations.
