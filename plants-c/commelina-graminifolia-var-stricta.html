Commelina graminifolia var. stricta

Commelina graminifolia var. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct midrib, while the inflorescence consists of two bracts enclosing the tiny flowers.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
