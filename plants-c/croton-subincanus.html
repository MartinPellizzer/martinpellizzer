Croton subincanus

Croton subincanus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
