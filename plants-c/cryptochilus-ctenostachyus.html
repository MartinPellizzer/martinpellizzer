Cryptochilus ctenostachyus

Cryptochilus ctenostachyus, commonly known as the hairy leech-plant, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a slender, upright stem with numerous lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often clustered in inflorescences, and are adapted for specific pollination mechanisms.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and ecological niche within its native habitats.
