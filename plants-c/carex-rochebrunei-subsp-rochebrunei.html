Carex rochebrunei subsp. rochebrunei

Carex rochebrunei subsp.

rochebrunei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
