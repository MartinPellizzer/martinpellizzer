Chamaesyce senguptae

Chamaesyce senguptae, commonly known as the Sengupta's sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of India.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy and saline soils, often found in dune environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in arid conditions.

Chamaesyce senguptae plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and is of interest to botanists studying halophytic plants.
