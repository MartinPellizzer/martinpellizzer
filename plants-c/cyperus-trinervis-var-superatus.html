Cyperus trinervis var. superatus

Cyperus trinervis var. superatus, commonly known as the giant flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the base of the plant.

Cyperus trinervis var. superatus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
