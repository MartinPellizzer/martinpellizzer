Cyathea borjae

Cyathea borjae, commonly known as the Borja's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically evergreen and can persist for several years, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea borjae is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
