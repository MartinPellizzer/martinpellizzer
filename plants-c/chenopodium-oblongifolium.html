Chenopodium oblongifolium

Chenopodium oblongifolium, commonly known as pigweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences called heads.

Chenopodium oblongifolium is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
