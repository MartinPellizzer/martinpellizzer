Colutea purpurea

Colutea purpurea, commonly known as red clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy pink to purple flower heads that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. It has a taproot system and produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Colutea purpurea is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
