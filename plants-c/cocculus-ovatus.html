Cocculus ovatus

Cocculus ovatus, commonly known as the eggplant vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive, egg-shaped berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. These berries contain a single large seed enclosed in a fleshy, edible pulp.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
