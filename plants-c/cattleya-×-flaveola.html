Cattleya × flaveola

Cattleya × flaveola, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a striking display of color.

Cattleya × flaveola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
