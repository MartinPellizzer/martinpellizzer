Centaurea latifolia

Centaurea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open areas such as fields and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It is an annual or biennial species that thrives in temperate climates.

Its bright flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
