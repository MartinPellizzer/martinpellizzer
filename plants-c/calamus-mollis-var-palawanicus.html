Calamus mollis var. palawanicus

Calamus mollis var. palawanicus, commonly known as the Palauan slender arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly Palau.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to coastal and wetland environments, making it an important species in ecological studies of tropical flora.
