Conyza costata

Conyza costata, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and often have a serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow with pale centers.

Conyza costata is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
