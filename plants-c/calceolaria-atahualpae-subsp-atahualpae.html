Calceolaria atahualpae subsp. atahualpae

Calceolaria atahualpae subsp.

atahualpae, commonly known as the Atahualpa's slipper orchid, is a member of the Campanulaceae family. It is native to the high Andes of Peru and is characterized by its distinctive bilaterally symmetrical, slipper-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with a unique pouch-like structure at the base.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
