Cynoglossum hochstetteri var. calathiforme

Cynoglossum hochstetteri var. calathiforme, commonly known as the "Himalayan bellflower," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to blue, often with a distinctive white or pale throat. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
