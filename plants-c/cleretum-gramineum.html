Cleretum gramineum

Cleretum gramineum, commonly known as the grassy cleretum, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pink. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Cleretum gramineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
