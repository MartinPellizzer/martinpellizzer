Cyphostemma zombense

Cyphostemma zombense, commonly known as the Zombese moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, serpentine stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruits. Its unique appearance and growth habit make it a striking addition to botanical collections.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions, posing a threat to local ecosystems.
