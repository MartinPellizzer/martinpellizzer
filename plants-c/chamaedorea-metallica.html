Chamaedorea metallica

Chamaedorea metallica, commonly known as the silver fir or metallica palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feather-like fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its air-purifying qualities and ornamental value. Its leaves are typically a deep green with a silvery sheen, which contributes to its distinctive appearance.

Chamaedorea metallica thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity to flourish.
