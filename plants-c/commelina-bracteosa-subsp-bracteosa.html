Commelina bracteosa subsp. bracteosa

Commelina bracteosa subsp.

bracteosa, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features striking blue to purple flowers with white markings, which are borne on slender, branching stems.

The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
