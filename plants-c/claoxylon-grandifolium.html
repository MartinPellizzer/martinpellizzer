Claoxylon grandifolium

Claoxylon grandifolium, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The tree has a broad, umbrella-like canopy and can grow up to 30 meters tall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
