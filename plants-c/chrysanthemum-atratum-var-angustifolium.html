Chrysanthemum atratum var. angustifolium

Chrysanthemum atratum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed gloomy chrysanthemum, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It features dark green, narrow, and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to brown, typically blooming in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in cooler climates.
