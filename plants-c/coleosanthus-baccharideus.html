Coleosanthus baccharideus

Coleosanthus baccharideus, commonly known as the golden sand grass, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand coastal conditions such as salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in coastal landscaping.
