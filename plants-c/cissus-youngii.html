Cissus youngii

Cissus youngii, commonly known as the African stonecrop, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cissus youngii is also valued for its ability to climb and its use in traditional medicine.
