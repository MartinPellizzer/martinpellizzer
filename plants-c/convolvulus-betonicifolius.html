Convolvulus betonicifolius

Convolvulus betonicifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. Native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and waste grounds.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it a common ornamental and invasive species in certain regions.
