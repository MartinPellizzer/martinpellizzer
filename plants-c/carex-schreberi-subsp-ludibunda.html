Carex schreberi subsp. ludibunda

Carex schreberi subsp.

ludibunda, commonly known as the golden club, is a perennial sedge native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America. It features grass-like leaves that are typically bright green, with a distinct golden hue on the edges.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
