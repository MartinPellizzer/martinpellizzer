Cyperus longus var. discolor

Cyperus longus var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive variegation, often displaying shades of white, yellow, and green along the leaf margins. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically pale green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in humid climates.
