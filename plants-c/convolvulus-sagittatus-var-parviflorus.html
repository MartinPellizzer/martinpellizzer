Convolvulus sagittatus var. parviflorus

Convolvulus sagittatus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, pale blue or pink flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and tendrils that help it attach to supports. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
