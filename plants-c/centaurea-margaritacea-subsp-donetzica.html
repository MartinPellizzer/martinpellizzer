Centaurea margaritacea subsp. donetzica

Centaurea margaritacea subsp.

donetzica, commonly known as the Donetz thistle, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Eastern Europe. It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with rough, hairy foliage.

The plant produces striking, spiky flower heads that range in color from white to pink, resembling small crowns. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive silvery-gray underside.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, open habitats and is often found in meadows and grasslands.
