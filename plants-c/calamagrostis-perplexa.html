Calamagrostis perplexa

Calamagrostis perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing reed, is a tall grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are often tinged with silver or gray on the underside.

Calamagrostis perplexa is known for its delicate, fluffy seed heads that appear in late summer, adding ornamental value to wetland landscapes.
