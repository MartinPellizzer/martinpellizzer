Crocus mysius

Crocus mysius, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including parts of Turkey and the Caucasus. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in well-drained soils and can tolerate cold temperatures.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging after the flowers have faded.
