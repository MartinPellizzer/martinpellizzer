Coronilla securigera

Coronilla securigera, commonly known as goat's rue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, blue or violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its unique seed pods, which are curved and resemble a scythe, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Coronilla securigera has been used historically for its medicinal properties and as a forage plant for livestock.
