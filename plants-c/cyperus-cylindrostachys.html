Cyperus cylindrostachys

Cyperus cylindrostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist soil conditions. Its small, clustered flowers are usually inconspicuous, and it reproduces primarily through rhizomes, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Cyperus cylindrostachys is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
