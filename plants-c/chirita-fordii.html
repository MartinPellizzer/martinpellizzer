Chirita fordii

Chirita fordii, commonly known as the Chinese violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 cm and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It prefers partial shade and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

Chirita fordii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
