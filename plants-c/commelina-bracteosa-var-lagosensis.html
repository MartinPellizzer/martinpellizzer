Commelina bracteosa var. lagosensis

Commelina bracteosa var. lagosensis, commonly known as the Lagos spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a tropical touch in their landscapes.
