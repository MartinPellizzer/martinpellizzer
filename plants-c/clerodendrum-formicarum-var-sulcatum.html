Clerodendrum formicarum var. sulcatum

Clerodendrum formicarum var. sulcatum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple bracts that surround its small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, adding to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
