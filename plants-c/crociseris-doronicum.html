Crociseris doronicum

Crociseris doronicum, commonly known as the Doronicum crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a short, stout stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy in texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and early blooming flowers.
