Calothamnus villosus

Calothamnus villosus, commonly known as the woolly bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright red or orange floral spikes. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is often found in heathlands and open forests. Its flowers are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in well-drained soils.
