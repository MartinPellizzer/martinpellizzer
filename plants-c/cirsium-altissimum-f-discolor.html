Cirsium altissimum f. discolor

Cirsium altissimum f. discolor, commonly known as the pale coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and showy purple or pink flower heads. The foliage is typically green with a bluish or grayish hue, and the leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies, meadows, and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
