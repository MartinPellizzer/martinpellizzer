Capparis acutifolia subsp. bodinieri

Capparis acutifolia subsp.

bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier's caper, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces edible, tangy fruits that are used in local cuisines. Its leaves are evergreen and adapted to conserve water, making it well-suited for dry environments.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
