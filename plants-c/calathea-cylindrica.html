Calathea cylindrica

Calathea cylindrica, commonly known as the cigar plant, is a tropical perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its uniquely shaped, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a cigar, giving it its common name. The plant features striking, dark green, veined leaves that are often variegated with silver or white patterns. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Calathea cylindrica is popular as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and compact growth habit.
