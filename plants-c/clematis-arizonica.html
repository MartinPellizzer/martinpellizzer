Clematis arizonica

Clematis arizonica, commonly known as Arizona clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has a climbing habit.

Clematis arizonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry conditions.
