Carex fulvorubescens

Carex fulvorubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive reddish-brown seeds.

Carex fulvorubescens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
