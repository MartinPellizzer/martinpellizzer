Crataegus oxyacantha var. berberifolia

Crataegus oxyacantha var. berberifolia, commonly known as the berberifoliate hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green leaves with a yellowish margin, which give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It is often found in woodland edges and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
