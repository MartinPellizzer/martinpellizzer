Croton viroleoides

Croton viroleoides, commonly known as the yellow croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, palmate leaves that display a range of colors, from green to yellow and red. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton viroleoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
