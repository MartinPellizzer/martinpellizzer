Calystegia sepium subsp. sepium

Calystegia sepium subsp.

sepium, commonly known as field bindweed, is a perennial vine native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pink.

The plant produces cylindrical, ridged fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.

Botanically, it is notable for its rapid growth and extensive root system.
