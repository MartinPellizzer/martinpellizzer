Crocus biflorus subsp. albocoronatus

Crocus biflorus subsp.

albocoronatus, commonly known as the white-crowned crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, early spring blooms with pale yellow petals and a distinctive white crown.

The flowers are typically borne on short stems and are adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It plays a role in early pollination by bees and other insects.
