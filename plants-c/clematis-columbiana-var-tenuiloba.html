Clematis columbiana var. tenuiloba

Clematis columbiana var. tenuiloba, commonly known as the slenderleaf clematis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous vine that typically grows in moist, shaded areas along streambanks and woodlands. The plant features small, fragrant, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slender, elongated shape.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
