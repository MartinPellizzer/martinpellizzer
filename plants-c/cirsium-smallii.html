Cirsium smallii

Cirsium smallii, commonly known as the Small's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its flowering period occurs from late summer to early autumn, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
