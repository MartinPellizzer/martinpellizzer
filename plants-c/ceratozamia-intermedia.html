Ceratozamia intermedia

Ceratozamia intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant with a stout, trunk-like stem and a crown of large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones, with male cones clustered at the base of the plant and female cones emerging from the crown. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
