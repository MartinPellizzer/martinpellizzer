Cucumis melo var. shammam

Cucumis melo var. shammam, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a variety of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, round fruits with a thick, pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic, yellow-orange flesh. The plant is a vining annual that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It produces both male and female flowers, with the females developing into the edible fruit.

The Persian melon is often cultivated for its flavor and is used in both fresh consumption and culinary preparations.
