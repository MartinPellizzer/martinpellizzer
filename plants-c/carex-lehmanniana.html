Carex lehmanniana

Carex lehmanniana, commonly known as Lehmann's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex lehmanniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
