Cryptocarya xylophylla

Cryptocarya xylophylla, commonly known as the silky ash or golden ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, light grey bark and lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red when mature.
