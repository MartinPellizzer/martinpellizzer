Caroxylon exalatum

Caroxylon exalatum, commonly known as the desert fan palm, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, fan-shaped leaves that provide shade in its harsh environment. The plant has a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor, sandy soils. Its leaves are typically light green and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Caroxylon exalatum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various desert wildlife.
