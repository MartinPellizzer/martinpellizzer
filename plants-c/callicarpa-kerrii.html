Callicarpa kerrii

Callicarpa kerrii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple to magenta berries that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife, contributing to ecological diversity.
