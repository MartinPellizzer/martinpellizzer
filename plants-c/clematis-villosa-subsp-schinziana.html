Clematis villosa subsp. schinziana

Clematis villosa subsp.

schinziana, commonly known as the Schinz's Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
