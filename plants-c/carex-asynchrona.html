Carex asynchrona

Carex asynchrona, commonly known as the irregular sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex asynchrona is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
