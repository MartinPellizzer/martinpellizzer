Croton ivohibensis var. polygynus

Croton ivohibensis var. polygynus, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant features palmate leaves with lobed margins, often displaying shades of green, red, and yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
