Cerasus wallichii

Cerasus wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet-tart berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are elliptical with a serrated margin, and the bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Cerasus wallichii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
