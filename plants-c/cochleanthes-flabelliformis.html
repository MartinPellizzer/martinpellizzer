Cochleanthes flabelliformis

Cochleanthes flabelliformis, commonly known as the Flabelliform Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its flowers are arranged in a fan-like pattern, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high light exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
