Cynosurus paniceus

Cynosurus paniceus, commonly known as the panicle sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces panicle-like inflorescences that are usually greenish or reddish in color. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in riparian zones.

Cynosurus paniceus is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
