Ctenitis biserialis

Ctenitis biserialis, commonly known as the biserial huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, unbranched stems that bear a single row of spirally arranged leaves. The leaves are typically elongated and have a distinctive, serrated margin. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of China, Japan, and Southeast Asia.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
