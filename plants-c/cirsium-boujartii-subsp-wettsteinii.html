Cirsium boujartii subsp. wettsteinii

Cirsium boujartii subsp.

wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, silvery-green leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces purple to pink flower heads that are typically surrounded by a dense layer of bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
