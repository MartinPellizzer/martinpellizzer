Calamus nematospadix

Calamus nematospadix, commonly known as the slender spadix calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing palm characterized by its long, flexible stems and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, which is typical of the genus Calamus. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The botanical features of Calamus nematospadix make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
