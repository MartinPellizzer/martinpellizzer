Carex melanantha f. gracilescens

Carex melanantha f. gracilescens, commonly known as the slender black sedge, is a variant of the black sedge species native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshes, swales, or along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is dark green, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
