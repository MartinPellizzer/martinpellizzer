Cucumis figarei var. dissectus

Cucumis figarei var. dissectus, commonly known as the dissected cucumber gourd, is a unique variant of the Cucumis genus.

It is characterized by its deeply lobed, papery leaves and elongated, thin fruits that often exhibit a pale green to yellowish hue. The plant thrives in warm, arid climates and is native to parts of Central America. Its botanical features include a trailing growth habit and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
