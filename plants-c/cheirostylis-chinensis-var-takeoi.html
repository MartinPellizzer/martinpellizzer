Cheirostylis chinensis var. takeoi

Cheirostylis chinensis var. takeoi, commonly known as the Takeo orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, and its inflorescence consists of a raceme of flowers. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a suitable candidate for indoor cultivation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown for its delicate floral display.
