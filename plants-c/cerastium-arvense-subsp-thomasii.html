Cerastium arvense subsp. thomasii

Cerastium arvense subsp.

thomasii, commonly known as the Thomas's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically features hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in grassy areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to colonize open spaces, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
