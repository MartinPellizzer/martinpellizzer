Campanula rotundifolia var. dentata

Campanula rotundifolia var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are rounded with serrated edges, giving it the "dentata" variety name. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscaping.
