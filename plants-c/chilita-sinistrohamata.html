Chilita sinistrohamata

Chilita sinistrohamata, commonly known as the left-sided horned plant, is a unique species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that curve in a consistent direction, giving it a helical appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its tactile appeal.

Native to specific regions of South America, it is a rare and intriguing example of botanical asymmetry.
