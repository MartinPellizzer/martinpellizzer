Caragana rosea

Caragana rosea, commonly known as the rose thorn or red thorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Caragana rosea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid regions.
