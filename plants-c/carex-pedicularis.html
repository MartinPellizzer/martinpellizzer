Carex pedicularis

Carex pedicularis, commonly known as the common sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that bears small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex pedicularis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
