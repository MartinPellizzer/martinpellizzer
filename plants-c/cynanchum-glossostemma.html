Cynanchum glossostemma

Cynanchum glossostemma, commonly known as the blue milkweed or blue antelope's milk, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes many milkweed species. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in umbels, and produce a milky sap that is toxic to some animals.

Cynanchum glossostemma is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
