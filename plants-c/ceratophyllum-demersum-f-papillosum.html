Ceratophyllum demersum f. papillosum

Ceratophyllum demersum f. papillosum, commonly known as the woolly water frog plant, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its dense, branching structure.

It features small, bright green leaves that are often covered with fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia, where it thrives in still or slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.

Its unique morphology and ecological function make it a notable species in freshwater botany.
