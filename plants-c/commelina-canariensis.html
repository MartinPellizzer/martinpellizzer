Commelina canariensis

Commelina canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, often with three white petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Commelina canariensis is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
