Chrysopogon ciliolatus

Chrysopogon ciliolatus, commonly known as the silky grass or silky sedge, is a perennial grass species native to various regions in Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny flowers. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
