Catasetum naso

Catasetum naso, commonly known as the Nasal Catasetum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that often display vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers, each with a unique, intricate structure.

The genus Catasetum is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms, often involving specific insect pollinators.
