Crataegus tenuiloba

Crataegus tenuiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Crataegus tenuiloba is often found in open woods, along stream banks, and in disturbed sites.
