Cornus boliviana

Cornus boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian dogwood, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white or pink bracts that surround small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually broad and ovate, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

Cornus boliviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
