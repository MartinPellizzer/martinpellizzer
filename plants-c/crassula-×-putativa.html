Crassula × putativa

Crassula × putativa, commonly known as the Jade Plant or Cash Register Plant, is a hybrid succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the spring and summer months. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements.

Crassula × putativa thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
