Chimonobambusa opienensis

Chimonobambusa opienensis, commonly known as the Opien bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems that grow in clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicles. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall bamboo-like appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
