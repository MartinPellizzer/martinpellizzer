Cryptandra ledifolia

Cryptandra ledifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cryptandra, is an endemic species found in the southeastern region of Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in dry, open habitats. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in dense inflorescences, and are typically pink or red in color.

Cryptandra ledifolia plays an important role in its local ecosystem, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
