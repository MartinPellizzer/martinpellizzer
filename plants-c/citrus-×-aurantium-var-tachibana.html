Citrus × aurantium var. tachibana

Citrus × aurantium var. tachibana, commonly known as the Tachibana mandarin, is a citrus tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, and sweet oranges that are typically harvested in late winter to early spring. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often grown for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruits.

The fruit is prized for its low acidity and is commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.
