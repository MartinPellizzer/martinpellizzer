Cracca sibthorpii

Cracca sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorp's cracca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and clustered, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in composite inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of botanical circles.
