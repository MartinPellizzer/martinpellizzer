Chenopodium ameghinoi

Chenopodium ameghinoi, commonly known as the Andean goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters and is often found in rocky soils or along riverbanks.

Chenopodium ameghinoi is valued for its hardy nature and potential use in traditional medicine.
