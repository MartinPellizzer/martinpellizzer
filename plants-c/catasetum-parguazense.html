Catasetum parguazense

Catasetum parguazense, commonly known as the Parguazense orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with intricate patterns, and it produces a unique structure called a galea, which is a specialized pollination mechanism.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms.
