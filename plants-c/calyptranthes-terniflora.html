Calyptranthes terniflora

Calyptranthes terniflora, commonly known as the three-flowered calyptranthes, is a species of flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and coastal habitats. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a smooth surface and a slight pubescence on the underside. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and are clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves.

Calyptranthes terniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in coastal gardens for its hardiness and unique foliage.
