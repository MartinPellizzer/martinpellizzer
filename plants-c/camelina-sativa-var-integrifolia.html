Camelina sativa var. integrifolia

Camelina sativa var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf camelina, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features small, yellow flowers and produces elongated, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are entire, meaning they have no teeth or lobes, which is a distinguishing characteristic of the variety.

This plant is often cultivated for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in food and industrial applications.
