Cercostylos brasiliensis

Cercostylos brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid environments. Its roots are often epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic.

The flowers exhibit a range of colors, including white, yellow, and orange, making it a visually striking species.
