Chamaesyce celastroides var. lorifolia

Chamaesyce celastroides var. lorifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, ribbed stems and small, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study and ecological research.
