Cephalanthus acuminatus

Cephalanthus acuminatus, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a sweet scent when crushed. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that matures in the fall.

Cephalanthus acuminatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
