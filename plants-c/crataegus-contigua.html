Crataegus contigua

Crataegus contigua, commonly known as the Pacific crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features thorny branches, which are a notable botanical characteristic. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in the spring, followed by red or yellow berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Crataegus contigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
