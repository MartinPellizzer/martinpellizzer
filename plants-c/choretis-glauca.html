Choretis glauca

Choretis glauca, commonly known as the bluebells of the Mediterranean, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and sunny to partially shaded areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Choretis glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
