Clematis virginiana

Clematis virginiana, commonly known as the virgin's bower, is a climbing vine native to North America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to seven leaflets and produces small, round berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis virginiana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
