Carex gentilis

Carex gentilis, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Carex gentilis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
