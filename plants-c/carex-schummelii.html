Carex schummelii

Carex schummelii, commonly known as Schummel's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, erect leaves that are often dark green in color.

The flowering structure consists of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
