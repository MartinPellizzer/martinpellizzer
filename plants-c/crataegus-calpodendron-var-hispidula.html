Crataegus calpodendron var. hispidula

Crataegus calpodendron var. hispidula, commonly known as the hispid thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart and used in culinary or medicinal applications.

Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant is adapted to a variety of soil types, making it a resilient species in woodland and open areas.
