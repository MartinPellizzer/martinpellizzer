Cleome torticarpa

Cleome torticarpa, commonly known as the winged cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and marshes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Cleome torticarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
