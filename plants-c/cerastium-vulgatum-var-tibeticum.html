Cerastium vulgatum var. tibeticum

Cerastium vulgatum var. tibeticum, commonly known as Tibetan mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.
