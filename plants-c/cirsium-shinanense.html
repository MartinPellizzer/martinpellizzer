Cirsium shinanense

Cirsium shinanense, commonly known as the Shimanese thistle, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
