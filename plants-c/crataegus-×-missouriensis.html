Crataegus × missouriensis

Crataegus × missouriensis, commonly known as Missouri hawthorn, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It is native to the central United States and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across its range.
