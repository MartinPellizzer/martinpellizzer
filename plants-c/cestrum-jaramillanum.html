Cestrum jaramillanum

Cestrum jaramillanum, commonly known as the Jaramillo's nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming at night. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, black seeds.
