Chromolaena plumeri

Chromolaena plumeri, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed habitats.

Chromolaena plumeri is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
