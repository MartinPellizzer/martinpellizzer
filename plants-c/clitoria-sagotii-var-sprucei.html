Clitoria sagotii var. sprucei

Clitoria sagotii var. sprucei, commonly known as the Spruce's butterfly pea, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive butterfly-shaped blue or purple flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. The plant grows as a climbing vine with pinnate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers are rich in flavonoids and have been studied for their potential health benefits.
