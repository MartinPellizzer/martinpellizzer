Clematis meyeniana var. insularis

Clematis meyeniana var. insularis, commonly known as the Cook Island clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the Cook Islands.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are showy, with five to seven petal-like sepals that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
