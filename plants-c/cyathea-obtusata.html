Cyathea obtusata

Cyathea obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and longevity. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea obtusata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
