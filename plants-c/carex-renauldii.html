Carex renauldii

Carex renauldii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct triangular cross-section characteristic of sedges.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer.
