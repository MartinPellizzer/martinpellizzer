Chytranthus verecundus

Chytranthus verecundus, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow or orange throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Chytranthus verecundus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
