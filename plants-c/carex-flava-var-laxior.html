Carex flava var. laxior

Carex flava var. laxior, commonly known as the slender yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, typical of many sedges. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in disturbed or semi-aquatic habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
