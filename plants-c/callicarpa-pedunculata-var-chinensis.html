Callicarpa pedunculata var. chinensis

Callicarpa pedunculata var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a visually striking plant. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy surface. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental berries and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
