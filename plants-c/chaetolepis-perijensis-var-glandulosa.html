Chaetolepis perijensis var. glandulosa

Chaetolepis perijensis var. glandulosa, commonly known as the Peruvian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Peru.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of rainforests. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to its native habitat.
