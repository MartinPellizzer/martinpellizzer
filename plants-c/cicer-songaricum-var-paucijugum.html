Cicer songaricum var. paucijugum

Cicer songaricum var. paucijugum, commonly known as the Kashmir chickpea, is a variant of the chickpea plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its small, round seeds that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It has compound leaves with fewer leaflets compared to other chickpea varieties, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in traditional cuisines of the region.
