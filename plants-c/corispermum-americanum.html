Corispermum americanum

Corispermum americanum, commonly known as the American sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Great Plains of North America.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
