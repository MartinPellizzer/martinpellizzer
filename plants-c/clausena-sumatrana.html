Clausena sumatrana

Clausena sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran Clausena, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, orange-red fruits that resemble small apples. Its fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's ornamental value and medicinal properties make it of interest in horticulture and phytotherapy.
