Cynanchum boveanum subsp. nubicum

Cynanchum boveanum subsp.

nubicum, commonly known as the Nubian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, which bloom during the rainy season. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with limited water availability.

The species is noted for its milky sap, which is characteristic of the milkweed genus.
