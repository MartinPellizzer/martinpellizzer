Cirsium akimontanum

Cirsium akimontanum, commonly known as the Akimontan thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, purple to pinkish purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Cirsium akimontanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance.
