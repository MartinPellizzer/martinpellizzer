Colpoon capense

Colpoon capense, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has a woody stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

The flowers open at night and close by dawn, making it a unique addition to gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
