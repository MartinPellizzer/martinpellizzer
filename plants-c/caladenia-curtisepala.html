Caladenia curtisepala

Caladenia curtisepala, commonly known as the Curtis's Spider-orchid, is a species of orchid native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance with a single, elongated flower that has a narrow, hooded labellum. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. It produces a single, slender stem that can reach up to 40 centimeters in height.

The flowering period occurs during the cooler months, usually between July and September.
