Cynodendron

Cynodendron, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Cynodendron species are typically herbaceous, with some varieties growing as shrubs, and they are adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their unique foliage and flower structure.
