Clerodendrum triphyllum

Clerodendrum triphyllum, commonly known as the three-leaved clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water sources.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
