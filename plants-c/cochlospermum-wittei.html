Cochlospermum wittei

Cochlospermum wittei, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often alternate and pinnate, and its showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fleshy, reddish fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cochlospermum wittei thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically found in lowland rainforests or coastal areas.
