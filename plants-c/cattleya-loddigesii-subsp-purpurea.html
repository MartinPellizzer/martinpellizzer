Cattleya loddigesii subsp. purpurea

Cattleya loddigesii subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the Purple Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically display a combination of purple and white hues.

The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in tropical climates. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on trees without soil.

This subspecies is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and fragrance.
