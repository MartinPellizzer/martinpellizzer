Crassula saginoides

Crassula saginoides, commonly known as the rock fig or stone plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Crassula saginoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
