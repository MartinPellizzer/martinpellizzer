Crassula septas

Crassula septas, commonly known as the "Baby Jade" or "Little Jade," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal watering, making it ideal for indoor or outdoor cultivation.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container gardening.
