Cassinia monticola

Cassinia monticola, commonly known as mountain cassinia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and hairy, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Cassinia monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native garden landscapes.
