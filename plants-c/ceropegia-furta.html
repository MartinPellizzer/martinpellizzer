Ceropegia furta

Ceropegia furta, commonly known as the silver leaf or string of hearts, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia furta is popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
