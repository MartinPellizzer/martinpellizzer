Copiapoa cinerea var. tenebrosa

Copiapoa cinerea var. tenebrosa, commonly known as the gray copiapoa, is a columnar cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its pale gray to white stems, which are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months, typically at night. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its striking appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable specimen in botanical collections.
