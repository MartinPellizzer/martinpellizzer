Citharexylum laetum

Citharexylum laetum, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its pleasant scent and visual interest.
