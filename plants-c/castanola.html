Castanola

Castanola, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces edible seeds encased in a hard, spiky shell, which are often roasted and consumed. Castanola thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical climates.

Its ornamental value and historical significance in local cultures make it an important species in its native regions.
