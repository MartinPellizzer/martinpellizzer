Calamintha fruticosa

Calamintha fruticosa, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features aromatic, finely divided leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its strong, spicy scent, which is attributed to the presence of volatile oils. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calamintha fruticosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
