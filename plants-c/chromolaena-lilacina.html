Chromolaena lilacina

Chromolaena lilacina, commonly known as purple flame or lilac weed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces showy, violet to purple flowers. It is known for its rapid growth and invasive tendencies in certain regions.

Chromolaena lilacina is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.
