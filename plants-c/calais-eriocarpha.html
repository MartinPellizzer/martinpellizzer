Calais eriocarpha

Calais eriocarpha, commonly known as the woolly bladder nut, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, woody shrub form and produces distinctive, inflated fruits that resemble bladders. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

The fruit contains a single seed, which is dispersed by animals or through mechanical means.
