Convolvulus frutescens

Convolvulus frutescens, commonly known as the annual morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, trailing stems that often climb or sprawl along the ground. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue to violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its capsules contain numerous small, brown seeds.

Convolvulus frutescens is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia but has been naturalized in various parts of the world.
