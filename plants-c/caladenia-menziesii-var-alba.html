Caladenia menziesii var. alba

Caladenia menziesii var. alba, commonly known as the White Spider Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils in open habitats such as heathlands and grassy woodlands. The plant features a single, slender stem with a single, showy white or pale pink flower that resembles a spider in appearance. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by thynnid wasps, which are attracted by the flower's scent and color.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and elegant blooms.
