Consolida teheranica

Consolida teheranica, commonly known as Persian sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant, spurred flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
