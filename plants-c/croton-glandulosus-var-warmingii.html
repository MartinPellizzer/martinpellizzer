Croton glandulosus var. warmingii

Croton glandulosus var. warmingii, commonly known as the Warming's croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

The leaves are covered in fine, glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and ecological adaptations.
