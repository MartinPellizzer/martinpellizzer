Cephaelis oleandrella

Cephaelis oleandrella, commonly known as the golden flame or yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender branches and opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine for various ailments.
