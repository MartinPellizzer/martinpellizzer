Calamagrostis alopecuroides

Calamagrostis alopecuroides, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its ability to form dense clumps. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft texture. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
