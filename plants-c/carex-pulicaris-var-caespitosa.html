Carex pulicaris var. caespitosa

Carex pulicaris var. caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers, which are usually borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
