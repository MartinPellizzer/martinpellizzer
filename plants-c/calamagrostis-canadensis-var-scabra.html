Calamagrostis canadensis var. scabra

Calamagrostis canadensis var. scabra, commonly known as Canadian rough grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its coarse, rough-textured leaves and tall, upright growth habit. This variety typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
