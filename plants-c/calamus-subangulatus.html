Calamus subangulatus

Calamus subangulatus, commonly known as the narrow-angled calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its slender, segmented rhizome and long, narrow leaves that are often arched or curved. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is typically found in swampy or marshy areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
