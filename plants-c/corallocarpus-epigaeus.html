Corallocarpus epigaeus

Corallocarpus epigaeus, commonly known as the ground ivy, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in moist, shady environments.

Corallocarpus epigaeus is known for its ability to spread rapidly through runners, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.
