Cheirodendron trigynum var. ilicoides

Cheirodendron trigynum var. ilicoides, commonly known as the Indian holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
