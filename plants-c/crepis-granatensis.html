Crepis granatensis

Crepis granatensis, commonly known as the Granada hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, elongated seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Crepis granatensis is often found in Mediterranean climates and is considered a common weed in some regions.
