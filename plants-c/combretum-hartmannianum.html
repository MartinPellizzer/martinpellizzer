Combretum hartmannianum

Combretum hartmannianum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that turn yellow before falling off in winter. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it often grows in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
