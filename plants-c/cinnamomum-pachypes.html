Cinnamomum pachypes

Cinnamomum pachypes, commonly known as the Malayan cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by egg-shaped berries. Its bark is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
