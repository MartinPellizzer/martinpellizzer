Cirsium adulterinum

Cirsium adulterinum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in challenging environments.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, though it is generally avoided due to its prickly nature.
