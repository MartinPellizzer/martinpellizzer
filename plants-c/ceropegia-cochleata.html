Ceropegia cochleata

Ceropegia cochleata, commonly known as the "string of hearts," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features trailing stems with heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often white or pale purple, which are pollinated by specific flies. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
