Crataegus furuhjelmii

Crataegus furuhjelmii, commonly known as the Furuhjelm hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
