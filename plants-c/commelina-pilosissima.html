Commelina pilosissima

Commelina pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.
