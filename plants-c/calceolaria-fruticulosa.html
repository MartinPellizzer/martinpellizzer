Calceolaria fruticulosa

Calceolaria fruticulosa, commonly known as the fruticose calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. The plant is characterized by its upright, branching stems and vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its flowers have a unique shape, resembling a small pouch or helmet, which is a distinctive feature of the Calceolaria genus.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
