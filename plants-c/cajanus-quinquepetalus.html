Cajanus quinquepetalus

Cajanus quinquepetalus, commonly known as the five-petaled clusterbean, is a flowering plant native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are used as a legume crop in some regions.

Cajanus quinquepetalus is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its delicate foliage and colorful blooms.
