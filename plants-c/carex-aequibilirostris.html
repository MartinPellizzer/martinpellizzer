Carex aequibilirostris

Carex aequibilirostris, commonly known as the broadbeak sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, somewhat hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
