Carex viridula var. nevadensis

Carex viridula var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly rough texture. Its flowering spikes are cylindrical and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
