Carex subuniflora

Carex subuniflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
