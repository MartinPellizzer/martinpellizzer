Clompanus alata

Clompanus alata, commonly known as the winged morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clompanus alata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
