Crucianella exasperata var. glabrescens

Crucianella exasperata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth bladderwrack, is a variant of the bladderwrack species native to coastal regions of the Atlantic Ocean.

It is a marine flowering plant that thrives in intertidal zones, characterized by its leathery, dark brown fronds. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are smooth to the touch, distinguishing it from other bladderwrack varieties. It plays a vital role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat for various marine organisms.

Its reproductive structures, including small, yellow flowers, are adapted to the harsh conditions of the shoreline.
