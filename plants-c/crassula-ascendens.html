Crassula ascendens

Crassula ascendens, commonly known as the upright crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, upright stems that can grow up to several feet tall, covered in small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, pink or white flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Crassula ascendens is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
