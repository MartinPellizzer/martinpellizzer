Cieca viridis

Cieca viridis, commonly known as the Emerald Spine, is a rare flowering plant native to the misty highlands of the Andes.

It is characterized by its vibrant green leaves, which are covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the wet season, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its roots are deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from underground sources.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, Cieca viridis is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
