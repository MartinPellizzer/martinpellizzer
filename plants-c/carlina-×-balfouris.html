Carlina × balfouris

Carlina × balfouris, commonly known as the Balfour's thistle, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant features a robust, upright stem with rough, hairy foliage that is typically gray-green in color. Its showy purple to pink flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carlina × balfouris is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
