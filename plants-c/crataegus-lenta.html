Crataegus lenta

Crataegus lenta, commonly known as the slow growth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Crataegus lenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
