Cirsium × misawaense

Cirsium × misawaense, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions of East Asia. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges and a rough texture.

Cirsium × misawaense is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed sites, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
