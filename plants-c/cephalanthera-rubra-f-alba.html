Cephalanthera rubra f. alba

Cephalanthera rubra f. alba, commonly known as the white lady's tresses, is a striking orchid species characterized by its pale to white floral spikes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant features elongated, spirally arranged flowers that are typically fragrant, attracting specific pollinators. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and forest edges.

The white form of this orchid is a natural variant, distinguishing it from the more common pink or red-flowered Cephalanthera rubra.
