Chrysophyllum nemorale

Chrysophyllum nemorale, commonly known as the African cherry or wild mango, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to mangoes, with a smooth, yellowish skin and a sweet, acidic pulp. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
