Calamagrostis lanceolata var. cinerascens

Calamagrostis lanceolata var. cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features tall, slender culms that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces feathery, panicle-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink hues. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery sheen that becomes more pronounced in the autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
