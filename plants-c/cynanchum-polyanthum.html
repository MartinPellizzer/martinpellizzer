Cynanchum polyanthum

Cynanchum polyanthum, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has a milky sap that can be toxic, and its leaves are typically alternate and ovate. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Cynanchum polyanthum is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
