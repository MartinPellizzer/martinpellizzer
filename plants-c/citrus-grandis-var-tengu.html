Citrus grandis var. tengu

Citrus grandis var. tengu, commonly known as the Tengu tangerine, is a variety of mandarin orange native to China.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, smooth peel and a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant produces fragrant white flowers and has dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in both fresh consumption and juice production.
