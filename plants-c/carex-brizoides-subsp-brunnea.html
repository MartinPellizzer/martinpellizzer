Carex brizoides subsp. brunnea

Carex brizoides subsp.

brunnea, commonly known as the brown sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ability to tolerate wet conditions makes it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
