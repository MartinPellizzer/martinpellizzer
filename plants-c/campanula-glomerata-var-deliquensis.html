Campanula glomerata var. deliquensis

Campanula glomerata var. deliquensis, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
