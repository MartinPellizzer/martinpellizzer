Cyphostemma greveanum

Cyphostemma greveanum, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, waxy leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Cyphostemma greveanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
