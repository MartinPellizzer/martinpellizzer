Campanula beauverdiana

Campanula beauverdiana, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula beauverdiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
