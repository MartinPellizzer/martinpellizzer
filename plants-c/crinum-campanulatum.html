Crinum campanulatum

Crinum campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped crinum, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Crinum campanulatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
