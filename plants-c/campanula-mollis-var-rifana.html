Campanula mollis var. rifana

Campanula mollis var. rifana, commonly known as the Rifan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
