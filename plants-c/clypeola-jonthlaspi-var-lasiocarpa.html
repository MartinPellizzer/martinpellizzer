Clypeola jonthlaspi var. lasiocarpa

Clypeola jonthlaspi var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly clypeola, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately lobed, often with a waxy coating.

This variety is native to southern Africa and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
