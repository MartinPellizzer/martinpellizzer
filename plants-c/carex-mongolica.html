Carex mongolica

Carex mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex mongolica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
