Cyperus felicis

Cyperus felicis, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant features grass-like, cylindrical stems that often form dense clumps, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is the umbrella-like cluster of white or pale yellow flowers that appear at the top of the stems.

Cyperus felicis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist environments.
