Camara salviifolia var. transvalensis

Camara salviifolia var. transvalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to early summer.
