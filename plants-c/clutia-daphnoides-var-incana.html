Clutia daphnoides var. incana

Clutia daphnoides var. incana, commonly known as the silky clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive silvery-gray underside, giving it a unique appearance.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
