Cardiochlaena macrophylla var. pinnata

Cardiochlaena macrophylla var. pinnata, commonly known as the broadleaf heartleaf, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are often ribbed and arranged in a rosette pattern. The leaves are typically broad and have a waxy surface, which helps the plant retain moisture in arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to the plant.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive foliage.
