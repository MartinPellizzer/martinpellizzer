Carex rafflesiana var. tenuior

Carex rafflesiana var. tenuior, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic and riparian habitats.
