Convolvulus tenuis

Convolvulus tenuis, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, creeping stems and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant produces small, round seeds enclosed in a hairy, inflated capsule. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative plant.
