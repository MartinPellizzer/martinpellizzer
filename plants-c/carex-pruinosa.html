Carex pruinosa

Carex pruinosa, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. Carex pruinosa typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
