Calamintha ashei

Calamintha ashei, commonly known as Ashe's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas due to their mint-like fragrance and mild medicinal properties.

Calamintha ashei thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.
