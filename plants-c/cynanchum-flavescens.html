Cynanchum flavescens

Cynanchum flavescens, commonly known as yellow milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the genus. It thrives in moist environments and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Cynanchum flavescens is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
