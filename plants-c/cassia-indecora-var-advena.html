Cassia indecora var. advena

Cassia indecora var. advena, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it suitable for various landscaping purposes.
