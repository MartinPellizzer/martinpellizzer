Centaurea aplolepi

Centaurea aplolepi, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Centaurea aplolepi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
