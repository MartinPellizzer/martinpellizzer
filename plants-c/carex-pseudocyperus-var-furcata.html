Carex pseudocyperus var. furcata

Carex pseudocyperus var. furcata, commonly known as the forked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually clustered at the top of the plant, producing small, greenish-brown flowers.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
