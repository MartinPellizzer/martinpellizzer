Croton mexicanus

Croton mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican croton, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many plants with milky sap. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that can range from green to red, orange, or yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Croton mexicanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
