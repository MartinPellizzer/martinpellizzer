Croton hookerianus

Croton hookerianus, commonly known as the Hooker's croton, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays a mix of red, yellow, and green hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
