Cuscuta decora var. integriuscula

Cuscuta decora var. integriuscula, commonly known as the golden dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks leaves, stems, and roots.

It attaches itself to host plants using specialized haustoria to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. It is native to North America and is considered an annual species.

Its ecological role is primarily as a parasite, impacting the growth of its host plants.
