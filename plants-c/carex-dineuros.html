Carex dineuros

Carex dineuros, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex dineuros plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
