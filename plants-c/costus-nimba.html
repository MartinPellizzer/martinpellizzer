Costus nimba

Costus nimba, commonly known as the mountain costus, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes. The plant features tall, narrow leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow and purple coloration. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid, warm climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
