Coursetia boliviana

Coursetia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian coursetia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
