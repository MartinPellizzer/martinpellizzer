Carex rhynchoperigynium

Carex rhynchoperigynium, commonly known as the narrow-sheath sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex rhynchoperigynium plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
