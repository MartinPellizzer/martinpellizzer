Convolvulus aphyllus

Convolvulus aphyllus, commonly known as the leafless bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of true leaves, with only small, scale-like structures present at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
