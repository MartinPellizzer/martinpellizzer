Catasetum carolinianum

Catasetum carolinianum, commonly known as the Carolina orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange with dark markings. The plant produces a unique structure called a "catasetum," which is a specialized pollen sac that is released during pollination. It prefers warm, humid environments and is often found in shaded, moist woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a popular species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
