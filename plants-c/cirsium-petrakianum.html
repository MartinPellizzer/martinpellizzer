Cirsium petrakianum

Cirsium petrakianum, commonly known as the Petrak's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, violet-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers bloom in late summer.

Cirsium petrakianum is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
