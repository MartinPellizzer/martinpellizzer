Codonopsis lanceolata var. emaculata

Codonopsis lanceolata var. emaculata, commonly known as the slender codonopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in forested areas and has lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, and it produces small, oval berries that contain the seeds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
