Crepidium cyanobrachium

Crepidium cyanobrachium, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers with a distinctive blue-green hue. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique coloration and growth habits, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
