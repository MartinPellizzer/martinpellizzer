Cissampelos tomentosa

Cissampelos tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly cissampelos, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to reddish fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitats.
