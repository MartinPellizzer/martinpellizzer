Cryptocarya macrocarpa

Cryptocarya macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed.

The tree is often found in lowland tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
