Caltha umbrosa

Caltha umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a waxy coating.

Caltha umbrosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
