Crotalaria medicaginea var. medicaginea

Crotalaria medicaginea var. medicaginea, commonly known as the African blue lupine, is a perennial legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological benefits make it an important species in both horticulture and agroecosystems.
