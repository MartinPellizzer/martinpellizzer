Calycanthus tomentosus

Calycanthus tomentosus, commonly known as the southern waxflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, showy flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, with a distinctive, candle-like shape. The plant has ovate, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, woolly layer of hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

The botanical characteristics of Calycanthus tomentosus make it a unique and attractive addition to native plant collections.
