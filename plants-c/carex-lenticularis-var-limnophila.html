Carex lenticularis var. limnophila

Carex lenticularis var. limnophila, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are generally green to bluish-green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, characteristic of the Carex genus.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic life.
