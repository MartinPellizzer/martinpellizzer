Coleus maculosus

Coleus maculosus, commonly known as the spotted coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with intricate patterns of red, purple, and white. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking leaves.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners and indoor plant enthusiasts.
