Calamus toli-toliensis

Calamus toli-toliensis, commonly known as the Toli-Toli calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers.

The plant's rhizomes are sometimes harvested for their aromatic properties and used in local medicine.
