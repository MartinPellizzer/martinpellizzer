Crataegus oxyacantha semperflorens

Crataegus oxyacantha semperflorens, commonly known as the everflowering hawthorn, is a cultivar of the hawthorn plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season, giving it its "everflowering" trait. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its hardy nature and seasonal blooms make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
