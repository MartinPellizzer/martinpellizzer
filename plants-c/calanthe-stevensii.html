Calanthe stevensii

Calanthe stevensii, commonly known as the Stevens' calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in shaded, humid environments. Calanthe stevensii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to forest biodiversity.
