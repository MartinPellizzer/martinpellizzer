Capparis umbonata

Capparis umbonata, commonly known as the African sour orange, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has thick, woody stems and elliptical leaves with a rough texture. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to orange when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has a sour taste, hence its common name.
