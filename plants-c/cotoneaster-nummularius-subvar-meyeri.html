Cotoneaster nummularius subvar. meyeri

Cotoneaster nummularius subvar. meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer cotoneaster, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, rounded, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green in color, providing an attractive evergreen appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a border plant due to its dense growth habit and ornamental value.

Cotoneaster nummularius subvar. meyeri is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
