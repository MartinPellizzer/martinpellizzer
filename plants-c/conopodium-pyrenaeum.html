Conopodium pyrenaeum

Conopodium pyrenaeum, commonly known as the Pyrenean bladder-sheath, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbrella-like structures.

Conopodium pyrenaeum is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
