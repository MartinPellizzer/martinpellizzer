Cissus dissecta

Cissus dissecta, commonly known as the African mountain vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by green, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
