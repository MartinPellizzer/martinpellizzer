Carex cespitosa var. microstachys

Carex cespitosa var. microstachys, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flower clusters.

The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense stands.
