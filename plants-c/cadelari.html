Cadelari

Cadelari, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Cadelari has a taproot system and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Its flowers are followed by fluffy, white seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.
