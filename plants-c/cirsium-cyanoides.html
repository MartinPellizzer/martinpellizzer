Cirsium cyanoides

Cirsium cyanoides, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits that disperse seeds via the wind.

Cirsium cyanoides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
