Clidemia trichogona

Clidemia trichogona, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a member of the Melastomataceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Clidemia trichogona is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats, making it an invasive species in some areas.
