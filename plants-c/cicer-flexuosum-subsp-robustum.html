Cicer flexuosum subsp. robustum

Cicer flexuosum subsp.

robustum, commonly known as the robust wild vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its robust growth habit, with erect stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used as forage for livestock.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
