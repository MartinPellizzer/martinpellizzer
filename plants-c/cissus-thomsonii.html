Cissus thomsonii

Cissus thomsonii, commonly known as the rock fig or stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Cissus thomsonii is well adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
