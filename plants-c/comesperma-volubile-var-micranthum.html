Comesperma volubile var. micranthum

Comesperma volubile var. micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered climbing gooseberry, is a species native to Australia.

It is a trailing shrub that produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and alternate leaves with serrated margins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant for both ecological and culinary purposes.
