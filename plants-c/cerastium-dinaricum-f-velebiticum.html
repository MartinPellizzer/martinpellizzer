Cerastium dinaricum f. velebiticum

Cerastium dinaricum f. velebiticum, commonly known as the Dinaric mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Dinaric Alps.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and covered in fine pubescence, while its flowers are pink or white.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptation to harsh mountain environments and its role in local ecosystems.
