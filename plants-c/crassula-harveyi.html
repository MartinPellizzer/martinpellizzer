Crassula harveyi

Crassula harveyi, commonly known as Harvey's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant forms compact, low-growing rosettes and produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Harvey's crassula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and easy care requirements.
