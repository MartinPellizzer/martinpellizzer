Chondroscaphe eburnea

Chondroscaphe eburnea, commonly known as the white-flowered chondroscaphe, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
