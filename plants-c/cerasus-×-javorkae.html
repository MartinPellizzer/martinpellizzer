Cerasus × javorkae

Cerasus × javorkae, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Prunus × javorkae, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during the spring when it produces clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–5 meters and features ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit, a small cherry-like drupe, is edible but less commonly cultivated for consumption.

Cerasus × javorkae is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional Japanese culture for ornamental purposes.
