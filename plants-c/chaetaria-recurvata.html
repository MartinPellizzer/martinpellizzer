Chaetaria recurvata

Chaetaria recurvata, commonly known as the curly hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves that curl and twist, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a slender, drooping spikelet that bears small, two-ranked spikelets.

Chaetaria recurvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
