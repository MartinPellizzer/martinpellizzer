Convolvulus jamaicensis var. major

Convolvulus jamaicensis var. major, commonly known as the Jamaican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often opening in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common sight in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
