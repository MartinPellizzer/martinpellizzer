Centaurea margaritacea subsp. gerberi

Centaurea margaritacea subsp.

gerberi, commonly known as the Gerber's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
