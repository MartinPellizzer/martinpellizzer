Calamagrostis effusa

Calamagrostis effusa, commonly known as the silvergrass or silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers in late summer, which are often used in floral arrangements. Calamagrostis effusa thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
