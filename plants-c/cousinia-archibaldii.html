Cousinia archibaldii

Cousinia archibaldii, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Cousinia archibaldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
