Cleome pachystigma

Cleome pachystigma, commonly known as the woolly cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Cleome pachystigma is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
