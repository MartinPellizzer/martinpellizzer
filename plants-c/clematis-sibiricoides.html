Clematis sibiricoides

Clematis sibiricoides, commonly known as the Siberian Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in early summer. The plant produces small, woody follicles that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a climbing plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate blooms and graceful growth habit.
