Crataegus neosmithii

Crataegus neosmithii, commonly known as Smith's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other hawthorn species.
