Carex norvegica subsp. conicorostrata

Carex norvegica subsp.

conicorostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
