Cucumis melo var. nuksewan

Cucumis melo var. nuksewan, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a variety of melon native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, green rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is a climbing vine with palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its high water content and distinct flavor.
