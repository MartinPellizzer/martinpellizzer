Cyperus semitrifidus

Cyperus semitrifidus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus semitrifidus is adapted to moist soils and can tolerate periodic flooding, making it a common species in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
