Carex glauca var. arenosa

Carex glauca var. arenosa, commonly known as bluejoint grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its bluish-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex glauca var. arenosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
