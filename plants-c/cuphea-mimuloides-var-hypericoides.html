Cuphea mimuloides var. hypericoides

Cuphea mimuloides var. hypericoides, commonly known as the flame flower or fire plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
