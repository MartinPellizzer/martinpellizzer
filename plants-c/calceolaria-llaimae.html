Calceolaria llaimae

Calceolaria llaimae, commonly known as the Llaima slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in late summer.

Calceolaria llaimae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
