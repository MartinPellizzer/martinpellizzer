Carex subtumida

Carex subtumida, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to North America and typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly curled at the edges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Carex subtumida plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
