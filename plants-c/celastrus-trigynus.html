Celastrus trigynus

Celastrus trigynus, commonly known as the European bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, simple, and usually elliptic or ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by bright orange or red berries that are attractive to birds. Its stems are woody and can become tangled, making it a persistent weed in gardens and natural areas.

Celastrus trigynus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
