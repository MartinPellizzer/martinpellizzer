Carex appressa var. virgata

Carex appressa var. virgata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated.

This variant is distinguished by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, making it a valuable plant for wetland restoration projects.
