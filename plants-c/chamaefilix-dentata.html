Chamaefilix dentata

Chamaefilix dentata, commonly known as the toothed chamaefilix, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Chamaefilix dentata is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for alpine gardens.
