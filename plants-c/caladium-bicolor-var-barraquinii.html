Caladium bicolor var. barraquinii

Caladium bicolor var. barraquinii, commonly known as the Crimson Beauty, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, heart-shaped leaves that display shades of red, pink, and white, often with a contrasting green border. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant for its dramatic foliage rather than its flowers, which are small and inconspicuous.

This variety is popular in gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
