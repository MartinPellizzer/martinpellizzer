Cymbidium oreophilum

Cymbidium oreophilum, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, erect orchid with long, narrow leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically blooms in winter or early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cymbidium oreophilum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in horticultural displays.
