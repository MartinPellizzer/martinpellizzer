Cryptocarya bidwillii

Cryptocarya bidwillii, commonly known as the Australian laurel.

It is a flowering plant species native to eastern Australia, characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is often found in the understorey of native forests.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
