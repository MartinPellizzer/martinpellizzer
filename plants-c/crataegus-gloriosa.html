Crataegus gloriosa

Crataegus gloriosa, commonly known as the glory crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 feet and features thorny branches, which are a distinctive botanical feature. The plant produces showy, pink to white flowers in early spring, followed by small, red or yellow fruits in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland or open areas.
