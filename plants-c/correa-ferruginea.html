Correa ferruginea

Correa ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty correa, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Correa ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
