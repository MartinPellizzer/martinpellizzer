Carex sphaerogyna

Carex sphaerogyna, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex sphaerogyna plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
