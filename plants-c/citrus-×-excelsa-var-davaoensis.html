Citrus × excelsa var. davaoensis

Citrus × excelsa var. davaoensis, commonly known as Davao kumquat, is a hybrid citrus plant native to the Philippines.

It is a small evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 3–5 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that give way to round, orange-colored fruits with a thick, aromatic peel. The fruit is prized for its edible peel and segmented pulp, both of which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.
