Cirsium spitzelii

Cirsium spitzelii, commonly known as Spitzel's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial species characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and large, showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Cirsium spitzelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
