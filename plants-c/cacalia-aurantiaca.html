Cacalia aurantiaca

Cacalia aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Cacalia aurantiaca thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
