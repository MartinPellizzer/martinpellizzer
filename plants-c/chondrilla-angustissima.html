Chondrilla angustissima

Chondrilla angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf chondrilla, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and dry grasslands. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Chondrilla angustissima is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
