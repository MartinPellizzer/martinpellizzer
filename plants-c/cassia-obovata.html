Cassia obovata

Cassia obovata, commonly known as golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are grouped in spikes and bloom in late summer. The leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
