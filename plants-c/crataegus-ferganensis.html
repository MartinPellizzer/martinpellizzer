Crataegus ferganensis

Crataegus ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
