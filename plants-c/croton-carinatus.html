Croton carinatus

Croton carinatus, commonly known as the African croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful foliage. The plant features alternate, simple, and usually serrated leaves that can vary in color from green to red or orange. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in cyathia, which is a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton carinatus is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking leaf patterns.
