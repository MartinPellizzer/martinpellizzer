Campomanesia xanthocarpa

Campomanesia xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow mangaba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by yellow-orange fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Its fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, juicy pulp, making it a valuable resource in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
