Cynanchum racemosum var. racemosum

Cynanchum racemosum var. racemosum, commonly known as the racemose milkweed, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in racemose inflorescences, which are characteristic of its name. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The plant is known for its latex sap, which is milky and can cause skin irritation.
