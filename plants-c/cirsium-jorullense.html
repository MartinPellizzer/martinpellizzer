Cirsium jorullense

Cirsium jorullense, commonly known as the Jorullense thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
