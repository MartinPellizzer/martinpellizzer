Crocus insularis var. medius

Crocus insularis var. medius, commonly known as the Sicilian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.

This variety is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in Mediterranean horticulture.
