Convolvuloides elongata

Convolvuloides elongata, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically open in the morning. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
