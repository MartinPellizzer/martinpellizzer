Caladium praetermissum

Caladium praetermissum, commonly known as the Brazilian shield leaf.

It is a tropical plant native to South America, characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with intricate patterns of red, white, and green. The leaves are typically large, often reaching up to 30 cm in diameter, and are borne on long, sturdy petioles. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant foliage.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique and colorful leaf structures.
