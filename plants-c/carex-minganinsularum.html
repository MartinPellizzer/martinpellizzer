Carex minganinsularum

Carex minganinsularum, commonly known as the Mingan Island sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in boreal and subarctic environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
