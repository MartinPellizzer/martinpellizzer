Campanula pusilla subsp. jaubertiana

Campanula pusilla subsp.

jaubertiana, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters, featuring delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or limestone habitats, where it forms dense clusters.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
