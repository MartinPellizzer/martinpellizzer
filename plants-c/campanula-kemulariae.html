Campanula kemulariae

Campanula kemulariae, commonly known as the Kemularia bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula kemulariae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers and hardy nature.
