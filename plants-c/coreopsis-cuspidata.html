Coreopsis cuspidata

Coreopsis cuspidata, commonly known as tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Coreopsis cuspidata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
