Cocos maldivica

Cocos maldivica, commonly known as the Maldivian coconut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The tree produces coconuts, which are encased in a hard outer shell and contain a sweet, white flesh. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its economic and cultural significance.

Its fruits are used in various culinary and traditional applications across the regions where it is found.
