Clitoria

Clitoria, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its striking violet or blue flowers, which are often shaped like butterflies. Clitoria species are typically climbing or trailing plants with trifoliate leaves.

They are valued for their ornamental beauty and are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
