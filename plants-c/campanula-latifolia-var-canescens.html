Campanula latifolia var. canescens

Campanula latifolia var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features showy blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically gray-green and covered in a fine, woolly hair, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
