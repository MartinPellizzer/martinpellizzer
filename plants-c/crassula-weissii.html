Crassula weissii

Crassula weissii, commonly known as the Weiss's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the spring or early summer.

Crassula weissii is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
