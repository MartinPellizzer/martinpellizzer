Croton galeopsifolius

Croton galeopsifolius, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display a vibrant yellow or green coloration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique foliage, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
