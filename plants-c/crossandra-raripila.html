Crossandra raripila

Crossandra raripila, commonly known as the rare flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Crossandra raripila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
