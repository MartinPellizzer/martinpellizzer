Cereus azureus

Cereus azureus, commonly known as the Azure Cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming at night. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to xerophytic gardens and a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
