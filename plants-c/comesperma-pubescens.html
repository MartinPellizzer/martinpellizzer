Comesperma pubescens

Comesperma pubescens, commonly known as the hairy comesperma, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
