Clavija reticulata

Clavija reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined flax, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Clavija reticulata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its fibrous stems, which have been used historically for textile production.
