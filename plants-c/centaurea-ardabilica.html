Centaurea ardabilica

Centaurea ardabilica, commonly known as the Ardabil thistle, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its height can range from 30 to 100 centimeters, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea ardabilica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
