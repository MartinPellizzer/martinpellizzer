Cepa rubra

Cepa rubra, commonly known as the red onion, is a bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its fibrous, layered bulbs that store nutrients and water, allowing it to thrive in various climates. The plant produces upright, hollow stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green or bluish-green. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in umbels, and bloom in the summer months.

The bulb is the primary edible part, valued for its pungent flavor and culinary versatility.
