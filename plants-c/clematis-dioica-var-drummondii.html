Clematis dioica var. drummondii

Clematis dioica var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the central and southern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
