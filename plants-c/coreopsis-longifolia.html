Coreopsis longifolia

Coreopsis longifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers full sun to partial shade. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies, meadows, and along roadsides.

Coreopsis longifolia is a hardy perennial that is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
