Capparis comosa

Capparis comosa, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, greenish fruits resembling small capers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Capparis comosa is known for its aromatic foliage and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
