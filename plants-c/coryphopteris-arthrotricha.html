Coryphopteris arthrotricha

Coryphopteris arthrotricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Coryphopteris arthrotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
