Clitoria arborescens

Clitoria arborescens, commonly known as the winged bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, trifoliate leaves and produces vibrant violet or purple pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems that can grow up to several meters in length. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used as a food source in various parts of Asia.

Clitoria arborescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing abilities in agricultural systems.
