Carex setispica

Carex setispica, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, cylindrical spike that bears male and female flowers in separate regions.

Carex setispica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
