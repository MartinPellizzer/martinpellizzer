Crocus tingitanus

Crocus tingitanus, commonly known as the Spanish crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late autumn or early winter. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh conditions, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Crocus tingitanus is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
