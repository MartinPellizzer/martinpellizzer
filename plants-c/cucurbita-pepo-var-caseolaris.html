Cucurbita pepo var. caseolaris

Cucurbita pepo var. caseolaris, commonly known as the scalloped cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and scalloped-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when immature and tender. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers, and it is often cultivated for its edible fruit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and mild, crisp flavor.
