Crataegus monogyna var. typica

Crataegus monogyna var. typica, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits called haws, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often used in hedgerows and landscapes for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
