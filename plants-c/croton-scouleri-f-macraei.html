Croton scouleri f. macraei

Croton scouleri f. macraei, commonly known as the Macrae's croton, is a unique variant of the Croton genus native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often bright green with contrasting veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
