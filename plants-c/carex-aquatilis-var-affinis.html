Carex aquatilis var. affinis

Carex aquatilis var. affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly rough texture. Its flowering spike is cylindrical, bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
