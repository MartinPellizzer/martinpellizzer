Crocus ziyaretensis

Crocus ziyaretensis, commonly known as the Turkish crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to Turkey and the surrounding regions of the eastern Mediterranean. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Crocus ziyaretensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.
