Cicer flexuosum

Cicer flexuosum, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a leguminous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Cicer flexuosum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, sunny environments.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
