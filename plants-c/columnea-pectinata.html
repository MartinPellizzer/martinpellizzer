Columnea pectinata

Columnea pectinata, commonly known as the "Cup-and-Saucer Plant," is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that resemble a cup and saucer, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity. It is often cultivated as a hanging plant due to its trailing growth habit and is popular in both indoor and outdoor gardens.

Columnea pectinata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
