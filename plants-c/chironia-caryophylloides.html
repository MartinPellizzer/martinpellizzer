Chironia caryophylloides

Chironia caryophylloides, commonly known as the fragrant bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
