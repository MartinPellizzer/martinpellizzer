Campanula bayerniana

Campanula bayerniana, commonly known as the Bavarian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
