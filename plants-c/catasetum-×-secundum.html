Catasetum × secundum

Catasetum × secundum, commonly known as the orchid with the unique pollination mechanism, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers feature a specialized structure called a "labellum," which plays a key role in its unique pollination process.

Catasetum × secundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
