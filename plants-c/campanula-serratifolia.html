Campanula serratifolia

Campanula serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are serrated and lance-shaped, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in alpine or rocky environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
