Cistanche tinctoria

Cistanche tinctoria, commonly known as the Chinese parasitic desert plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially depends on host plants for water and nutrients while performing photosynthesis. Native to arid regions of China, it thrives in sandy soils and has a distinctive yellowish-brown stem with no leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers and is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its unique botanical characteristics make it an intriguing subject in both ecological and medicinal studies.
