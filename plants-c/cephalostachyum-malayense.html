Cephalostachyum malayense

Cephalostachyum malayense, commonly known as the Malay bamboo or Malayan bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender, and often clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
