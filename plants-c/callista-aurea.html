Callista aurea

Callista aurea, commonly known as the golden bell, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Callista aurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its vibrant color and unique flower structure.
