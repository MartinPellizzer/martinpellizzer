Cerasus × serratifolia var. rosea

Cerasus × serratifolia var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered cherry.

This ornamental plant is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus, characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens. The plant typically grows as a small deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Its foliage is dark green, and it produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications.
