Cynara lamyroides

Cynara lamyroides, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, purple-blue bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into spiky, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Cynara lamyroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
