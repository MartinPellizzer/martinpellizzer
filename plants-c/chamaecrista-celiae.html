Chamaecrista celiae

Chamaecrista celiae, commonly known as the Celia's burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive, spiny pods. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas.

Chamaecrista celiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
