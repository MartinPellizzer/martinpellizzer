Cardamine caroides

Cardamine caroides, commonly known as slender water crowfoot, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, streams, and marshes. The plant has finely divided, floating leaves that are often arranged in a whorl around the stem. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a head, usually appearing in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its delicate structure and preference for moist, nutrient-rich environments.
