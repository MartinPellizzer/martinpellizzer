Courbaril

Courbaril, commonly known as *Mora excelsa*, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into edible, round fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. Its wood is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, the leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
