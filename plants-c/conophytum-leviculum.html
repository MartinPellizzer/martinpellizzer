Conophytum leviculum

Conophytum leviculum, commonly known as the rock fig or dwarf rock fig, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Conophytum leviculum is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
