Calanthe victoria-regina

Calanthe victoria-regina, commonly known as the Queen Victoria Calanthe, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a sought-after ornamental plant in botanical gardens.

The species is named in honor of Queen Victoria, reflecting its historical significance in horticultural circles.
