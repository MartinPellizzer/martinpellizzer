Curcuma clovisii

Curcuma clovisii, commonly known as the Madagascar turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric species. The plant features rhizomatous underground stems that are rich in curcuminoids, giving it its characteristic yellow color. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, while the flowers are typically orange or yellow.

The rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other curcuma species.
