Ceropegia filipendula

Ceropegia filipendula, commonly known as the silver leaf or white ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that are often visited by specific pollinators such as moths and flies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rockeries.
