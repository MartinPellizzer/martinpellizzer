Cayratia elongata

Cayratia elongata, commonly known as the Chinese maypole vine, is a climbing plant native to Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
