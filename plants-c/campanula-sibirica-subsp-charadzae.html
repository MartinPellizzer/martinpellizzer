Campanula sibirica subsp. charadzae

Campanula sibirica subsp.

charadzae, commonly known as the Charadza bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and slightly hairy, adapting to its high-altitude environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
