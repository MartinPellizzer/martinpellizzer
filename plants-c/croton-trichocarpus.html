Croton trichocarpus

Croton trichocarpus, commonly known as the white croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often green with a distinctive white or yellow midrib. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
