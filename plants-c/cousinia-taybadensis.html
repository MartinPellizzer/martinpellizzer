Cousinia taybadensis

Cousinia taybadensis, commonly known as the Taybad hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow ray flowers and tufted foliage. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. It often grows in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.

Cousinia taybadensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its ecosystem.
