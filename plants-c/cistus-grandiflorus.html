Cistus grandiflorus

Cistus grandiflorus, commonly known as the largeflower rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features woody stems and aromatic, evergreen leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Cistus grandiflorus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
