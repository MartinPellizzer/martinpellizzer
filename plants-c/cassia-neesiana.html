Cassia neesiana

Cassia neesiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble cascading showers of flowers, hence its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia neesiana is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
