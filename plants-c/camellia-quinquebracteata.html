Camellia quinquebracteata

Camellia quinquebracteata, commonly known as the five-bract camellia, is a species native to China, particularly in the provinces of Sichuan and Yunnan.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, large, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features thick, leathery leaves that are glossy and elliptical in shape. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Camellia quinquebracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
