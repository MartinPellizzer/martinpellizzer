Campulosus plumosus

Campulosus plumosus, commonly known as the feathery campulosus, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive feathery foliage and showy inflorescences.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and can range in color from white to pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campulosus plumosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
