Cleome herrerae

Cleome herrerae, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy, spindly flower clusters. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow or orange flowers. Cleome herrerae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is known for its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.

It prefers well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
