Carex tonkinensis

Carex tonkinensis, commonly known as Tonkin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Carex tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
