Celsia sublanata

Celsia sublanata, commonly known as the velvety celsia, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are covered in a fine, soft pubescence that gives them a velvety texture, which is a key botanical feature. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in temperate regions. It belongs to the Celastraceae family, which includes various shrubs and climbers.

Celsia sublanata is notable for its small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, adding to its ornamental value.
