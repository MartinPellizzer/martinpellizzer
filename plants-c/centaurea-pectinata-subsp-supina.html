Centaurea pectinata subsp. supina

Centaurea pectinata subsp.

supina, commonly known as the creeping centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial with slender stems and small, spiny leaves.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive red center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings.
