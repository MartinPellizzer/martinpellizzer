Caladenia bicalliata

Caladenia bicalliata, commonly known as the yellow spider orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and vibrant yellow petals that resemble spider-like structures, giving it its common name. The plant typically blooms during the spring and summer months, producing intricate flowers with a distinctive, elongated labellum. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
