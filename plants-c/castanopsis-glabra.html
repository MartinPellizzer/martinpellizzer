Castanopsis glabra

Castanopsis glabra, commonly known as the smooth tanoak, is a species of evergreen tree native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical, leathery leaves that are glossy on top and dull underneath. Its small, clustered flowers are followed by hard, spiny acorns that mature over several years.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
