Chlorophytum flaccidum

Chlorophytum flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa, characterized by its long, arching leaves that resemble spider legs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Its ability to propagate through offsets makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
