Carex acuta f. stygia

Carex acuta f. stygia, commonly known as the slender tussock sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex acuta.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in riparian zones and bogs.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
