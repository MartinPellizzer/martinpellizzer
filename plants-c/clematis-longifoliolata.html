Clematis longifoliolata

Clematis longifoliolata, commonly known as the long-leaved clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis longifoliolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
