Caragana scythica

Caragana scythica, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and compound leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by pea-like pods containing several seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils with low moisture.

Caragana scythica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil stabilization and reforestation efforts.
