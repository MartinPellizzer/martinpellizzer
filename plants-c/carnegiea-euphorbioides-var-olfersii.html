Carnegiea euphorbioides var. olfersii

Carnegiea euphorbioides var. olfersii, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce transpiration.

This species is well adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient and visually appealing addition to desert landscapes.
