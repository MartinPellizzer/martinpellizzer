Craccina versicolor

Craccina versicolor, commonly known as the variable craccina, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, colorful bracts that often resemble petals. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Craccina versicolor is known for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant foliage.
