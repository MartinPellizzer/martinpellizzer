Cyperus subpapuanus

Cyperus subpapuanus, commonly known as the Papuan flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is typically found in wetland or swampy environments. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered flower heads. Its leaves are linear and often have a waxy coating, which helps in reducing water loss.

Cyperus subpapuanus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
