Chamaecrista leptadenia

Chamaecrista leptadenia, commonly known as narrow-leaf ticktree, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
