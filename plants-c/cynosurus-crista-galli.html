Cynosurus crista-galli

Cynosurus crista-galli, commonly known as the crowned sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, wetlands, and woodland edges. Its flowering period occurs in the summer, producing yellowish-brown inflorescences.

Cynosurus crista-galli is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various invertebrates.
