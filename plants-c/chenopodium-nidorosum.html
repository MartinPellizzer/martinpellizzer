Chenopodium nidorosum

Chenopodium nidorosum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its succulent, often spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain single seeds.

Chenopodium nidorosum is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is also considered a weed in agricultural settings.
