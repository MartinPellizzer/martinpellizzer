Coryphantha vivipara f. sonorensis

Coryphantha vivipara f. sonorensis, commonly known as the Sonoran hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It features a dense cluster of cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in xeriscaped gardens.
