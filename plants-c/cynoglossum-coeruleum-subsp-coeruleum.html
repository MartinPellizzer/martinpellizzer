Cynoglossum coeruleum subsp. coeruleum

Cynoglossum coeruleum subsp.

coeruleum, commonly known as blue hare's foot, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe. It features hairy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens and wildflower habitats.
