Casearia ophiticola

Casearia ophiticola, commonly known as the swamp casearia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in swampy areas.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
