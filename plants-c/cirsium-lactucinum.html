Cirsium lactucinum

Cirsium lactucinum, commonly known as the common thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered with stiff, prickly hairs.

The plant produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits that are dispersed by wind.
