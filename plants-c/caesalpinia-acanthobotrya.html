Caesalpinia acanthobotrya

Caesalpinia acanthobotrya, commonly known as the African firethorn, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright orange or red inflorescences that resemble flames. The plant produces long, bean-like pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
