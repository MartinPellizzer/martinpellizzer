Chrysocoryne uniflora

Chrysocoryne uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered chrysocoryne, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that often display a vibrant green to blue-green hue. The plant produces a single, showy yellow flower at the end of each stem, which blooms during the warmer months. Chrysocoryne uniflora thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in its native range.
