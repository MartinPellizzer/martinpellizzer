Cotyledon elizae

Cotyledon elizae, commonly known as the "Eli's cotyledon," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
