Caricina paniculata

Caricina paniculata, commonly known as the red panic grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery inflorescences. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

Caricina paniculata is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in disturbed areas.
