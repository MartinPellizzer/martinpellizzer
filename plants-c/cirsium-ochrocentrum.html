Cirsium ochrocentrum

Cirsium ochrocentrum, commonly known as the yellow centranthus thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cirsium ochrocentrum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
