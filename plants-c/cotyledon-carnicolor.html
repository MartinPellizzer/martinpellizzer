Cotyledon carnicolor

Cotyledon carnicolor, commonly known as the red-veined cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves with striking red veins, which are a defining botanical characteristic. The plant belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its leaves, adapting to arid environments. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, with a coloration that ranges from green to reddish-brown.

Cotyledon carnicolor is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
