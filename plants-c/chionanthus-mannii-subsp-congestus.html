Chionanthus mannii subsp. congestus

Chionanthus mannii subsp.

congestus, commonly known as the clustered fringeflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America. It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer.

The plant is a shrub or small tree, often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
