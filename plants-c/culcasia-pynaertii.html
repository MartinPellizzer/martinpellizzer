Culcasia pynaertii

Culcasia pynaertii, commonly known as the African giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic life. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or white, bloom in the early morning and close by midday.

Culcasia pynaertii plays an important ecological role by supporting biodiversity and contributing to water filtration in its native habitats.
