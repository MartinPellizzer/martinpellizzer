Cryptantha vinctens

Cryptantha vinctens, commonly known as the hooked cryptantha, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, mat-forming growth habit. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the fruit is a legume that splits open to release its seeds.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in arid environments, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
