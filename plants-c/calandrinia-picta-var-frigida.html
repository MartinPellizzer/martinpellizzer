Calandrinia picta var. frigida

Calandrinia picta var. frigida, commonly known as the frosty purslane, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, bright pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, colorful blooms and drought-resistant properties.
