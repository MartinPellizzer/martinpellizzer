Cyathea primaeva

Cyathea primaeva, commonly known as the dawn's cyathea, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, with its fronds unfurling in a spiral manner as they mature. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

Cyathea primaeva plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
