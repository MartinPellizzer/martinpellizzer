Crepis wilsonii

Crepis wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, spoon-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in early summer.

Crepis wilsonii is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
