Calendula incanescens

Calendula incanescens, commonly known as the white calendula, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Calendula incanescens is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
