Cytisus pubescens

Cytisus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, arching branches covered in small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets with a soft, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Cytisus pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
