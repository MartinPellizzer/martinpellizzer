Carica baccata

Carica baccata, commonly known as the wax apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, waxy, purple fruits that turn yellow when ripe. The plant produces broad, heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
