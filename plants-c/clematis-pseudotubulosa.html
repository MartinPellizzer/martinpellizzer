Clematis pseudotubulosa

Clematis pseudotubulosa, commonly known as the purple clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically violet or purple, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis pseudotubulosa is valued for its ornamental value and can be cultivated in gardens with proper support.
