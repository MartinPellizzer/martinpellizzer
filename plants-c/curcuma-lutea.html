Curcuma lutea

Curcuma lutea, commonly known as the yellow turmeric, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish rhizomes. The plant produces tall, leafy stems with small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomes are rich in curcumin, a compound responsible for its vibrant color and medicinal properties.

Curcuma lutea is widely used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.
