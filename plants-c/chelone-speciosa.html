Chelone speciosa

Chelone speciosa, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink to white in color, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred shape. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

Chelone speciosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
