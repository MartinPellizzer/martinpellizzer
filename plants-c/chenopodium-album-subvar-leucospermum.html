Chenopodium album subvar. leucospermum

Chenopodium album subvar. leucospermum, commonly known as white goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by oblong, bead-like seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed with potential ecological and medicinal significance.
