Campanula calycantha

Campanula calycantha, commonly known as the bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branching stems covered with soft, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often slightly hairy. Its root system consists of fibrous roots, and it prefers well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Campanula calycantha is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
