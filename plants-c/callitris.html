Callitris

Callitris, commonly known as the white cypress-pine, is a genus of coniferous evergreen trees native to Australia and New Guinea.

These plants are characterized by their slender, cylindrical trunks and dense, scale-like foliage. Callitris species typically grow in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh climates. They produce small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, and their wood is often used in construction and furniture-making.

The genus includes several species, each with unique morphological features and ecological roles.
