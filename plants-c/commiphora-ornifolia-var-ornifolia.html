Commiphora ornifolia var. ornifolia

Commiphora ornifolia var. ornifolia, commonly known as the ornate myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often adorned with distinctive white or pale green markings. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally in various medicinal and ritual applications.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
