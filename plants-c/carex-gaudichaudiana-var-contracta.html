Carex gaudichaudiana var. contracta

Carex gaudichaudiana var. contracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms dense clumps and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
