Cornus xanthotricha

Cornus xanthotricha, commonly known as the golden thread dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth margin, turning yellow in the fall.

Cornus xanthotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
