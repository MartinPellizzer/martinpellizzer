Cedrus deodara f. pendula

Cedrus deodara f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping deodar cedar, is a unique cultivar of the deodar cedar tree.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. This variety is native to the Himalayas and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. The leaves are needle-like, dark green, and arranged in a flat spray, while the cones are typically reddish-brown and maturing in the second year.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
