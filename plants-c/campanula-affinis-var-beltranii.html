Campanula affinis var. beltranii

Campanula affinis var. beltranii, commonly known as the Beltranii bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
