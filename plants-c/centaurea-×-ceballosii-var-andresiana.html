Centaurea × ceballosii var. andresiana

Centaurea × ceballosii var. andresiana, commonly known as the Andes centaury, is a hybrid plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and spiny, bristly foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its flowers are arranged in dense, conical clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
