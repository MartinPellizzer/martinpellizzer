Croton althaeifolius

Croton althaeifolius, commonly known as the Indian croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a distinctive yellow or red vein pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical areas.
