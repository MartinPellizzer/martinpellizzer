Cassia wrightii

Cassia wrightii, commonly known as the yellow brittlebush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and brittle, papery leaves that easily break off. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Cassia wrightii produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological research.
