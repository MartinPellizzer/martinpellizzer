Canarium antonii

Canarium antonii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark red or reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing several seeds.

Canarium antonii is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
