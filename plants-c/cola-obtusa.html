Cola obtusa

Cola obtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical West Africa, where it grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, reddish flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain stimulant-rich seeds used in traditional medicine and beverages.

Cola obtusa is closely related to other cola species, such as Cola acuminata and Cola nitida.
