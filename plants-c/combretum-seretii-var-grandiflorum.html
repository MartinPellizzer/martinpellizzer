Combretum seretii var. grandiflorum

Combretum seretii var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered combretum, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
