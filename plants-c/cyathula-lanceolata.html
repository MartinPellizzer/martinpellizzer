Cyathula lanceolata

Cyathula lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cyathula, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. The plant features a distinctive spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a cyathium, which is characteristic of the Araceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Cyathula lanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
