Crucifera brassica

Crucifera brassica, commonly known as cabbage, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its distinctive four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

This plant is an annual or biennial herb with a robust root system and thick, fleshy leaves that vary in shape and color depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its edible leaves, stems, and flowers. The plant contains glucosinolates, which contribute to its spicy flavor and have potential health benefits.

Its seeds are also used in the production of mustard and other condiments.
