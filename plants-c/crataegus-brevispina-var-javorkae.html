Crataegus brevispina var. javorkae

Crataegus brevispina var. javorkae, commonly known as the Javornik hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
