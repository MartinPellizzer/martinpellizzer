Carex cruciata var. rubrobrunnea

Carex cruciata var. rubrobrunnea, commonly known as the red-bronze sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish or reddish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is distinguished by its unique coloration of the inflorescence, which gives it its common name.
