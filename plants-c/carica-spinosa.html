Carica spinosa

Carica spinosa, commonly known as the spiny papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive spiny stems. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and similar in appearance to the common papaya. Carica spinosa is often cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in vitamin C and other nutrients.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
