Crinum americanum subsp. robustum

Crinum americanum subsp.

robustum, commonly known as the robust crinum, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family. It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, and is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers.

The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves and produces bulb-like structures called corms for storage and propagation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
