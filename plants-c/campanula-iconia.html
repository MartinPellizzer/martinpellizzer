Campanula iconia

Campanula iconia, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Campanula iconia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
