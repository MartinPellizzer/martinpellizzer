Caelestina suffruticosa

Caelestina suffruticosa, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers throughout the summer. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Caelestina suffruticosa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
