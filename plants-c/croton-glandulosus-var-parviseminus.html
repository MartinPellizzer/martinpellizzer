Croton glandulosus var. parviseminus

Croton glandulosus var. parviseminus, commonly known as the small-seeded croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves, which are often covered in small, sticky secretions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release minute, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its unique foliage.
