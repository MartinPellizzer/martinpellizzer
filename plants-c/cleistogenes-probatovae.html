Cleistogenes probatovae

Cleistogenes probatovae, commonly known as the Probatov's bluemink grass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers, a common trait among grasses. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low precipitation and high temperature fluctuations.

Cleistogenes probatovae plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is of interest for its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
