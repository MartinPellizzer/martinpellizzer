Chionochloa conspicua

Chionochloa conspicua, commonly known as the alpine hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. This plant thrives in cold, moist environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowering stems produce panicles of small, greenish-white flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Chionochloa conspicua plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine wildlife.
