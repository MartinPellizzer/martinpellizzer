Cuscuta epithymum subsp. obtusata

Cuscuta epithymum subsp.

obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients. It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host.

The subspecies is characterized by its obtuse, rounded leaves, which are usually scale-like and inconspicuous. It is often found growing on a variety of herbaceous plants in open, disturbed habitats.

This species is native to North America and is considered an annual plant with a short life cycle.
