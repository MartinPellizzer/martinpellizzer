Canna denudata

Canna denudata, commonly known as the naked cannas, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna denudata is often used in gardens for its vibrant foliage and long blooming period.
