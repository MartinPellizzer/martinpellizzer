Carlina complanata

Carlina complanata, commonly known as the flatleaf carline thorn, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single flowers. The plant features rough, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Carlina complanata is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in harsh alpine environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
