Calibrachoa linoides subsp. linoides

Calibrachoa linoides subsp.

linoides, commonly known as the silverleaf calibrachoa, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant features silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated in gardens and containers for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

This species is also valued for its drought tolerance and adaptability to various soil types.
