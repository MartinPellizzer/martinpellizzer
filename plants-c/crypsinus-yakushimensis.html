Crypsinus yakushimensis

Crypsinus yakushimensis, commonly known as the Yakushiman Islands fern, is a rare and unique plant native to the Japanese island of Yakushima.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant thrives in the humid, shaded environments of the subtropical forests where it is found. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and its role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.

Due to its rarity, it is often studied for its potential in botanical research and conservation efforts.
