Carex royleana

Carex royleana, commonly known as the Kashmir rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Pakistan, and thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and filtering water.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
