Cardiospermum macrophyllum

Cardiospermum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved heartseed or sweet dew, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to reveal bright red, fleshy seeds enclosed in a hard, woody capsule. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
