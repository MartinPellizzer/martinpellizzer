Carex capitata f. arctogena

Carex capitata f. arctogena, commonly known as the Arctic sedge, is a perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and dark green, with a slightly rough texture.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in tundra and mountainous areas.
