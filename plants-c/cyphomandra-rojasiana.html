Cyphomandra rojasiana

Cyphomandra rojasiana, commonly known as the Rosy Passionfruit, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to passionfruit but have a distinct flavor profile. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
