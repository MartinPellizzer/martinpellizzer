Crataegus aboriginum

Crataegus aboriginum, commonly known as the Australian hawthorn, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in various habitats including woodlands and coastal areas. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for some wildlife.

The wood of Crataegus aboriginum is hard and durable, making it useful for traditional and modern applications.
