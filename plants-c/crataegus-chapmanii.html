Crataegus chapmanii

Crataegus chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
