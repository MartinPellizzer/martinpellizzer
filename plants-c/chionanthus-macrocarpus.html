Chionanthus macrocarpus

Chionanthus macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture.

Chionanthus macrocarpus prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
