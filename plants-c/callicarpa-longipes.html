Callicarpa longipes

Callicarpa longipes, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall, which are edible for humans and wildlife. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The berries are also used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
