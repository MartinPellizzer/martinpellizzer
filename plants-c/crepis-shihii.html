Crepis shihii

Crepis shihii, commonly known as the Shihii mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, exhibiting a low-growing, mat-forming habit. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, while the flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in dense clusters.

Crepis shihii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
