Carduus argemonoides

Carduus argemonoides, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.

The plant has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
