Cyperus bonariensis

Cyperus bonariensis, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus bonariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
