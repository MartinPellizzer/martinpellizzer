Citrus × aurantium var. melitensis

Citrus × aurantium var. melitensis, commonly known as the Maltese lemon, is a citrus variant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thick, aromatic peel. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is prized for its high acidity and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It is well adapted to warm, dry climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
