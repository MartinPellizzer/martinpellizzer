Cyphia transvaalensis

Cyphia transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and sturdy.

Cyphia transvaalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
