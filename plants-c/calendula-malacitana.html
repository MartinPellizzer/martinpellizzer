Calendula malacitana

Calendula malacitana, commonly known as the Malagasy marigold, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky areas and has a compact, bushy habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Calendula malacitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
