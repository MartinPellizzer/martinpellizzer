Cucurbita maxima var. turgida

Cucurbita maxima var. turgida, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a cultivar of the species Cucurbita maxima.

It is characterized by its large, round, and often misshapen fruits that can weigh several hundred pounds. The plant is an annual vine with large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season to reach its full potential.

The fruit is primarily cultivated for its size and is used in various culinary and competitive contexts.
