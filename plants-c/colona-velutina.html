Colona velutina

Colona velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Colona velutina is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
