Chionanthus lucens

Chionanthus lucens, commonly known as the white fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear clusters of small, petal-like bracts.

Chionanthus lucens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
