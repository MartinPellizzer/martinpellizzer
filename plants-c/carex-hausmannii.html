Carex hausmannii

Carex hausmannii, commonly known as the Hausmann's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its grass-like leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer.

Carex hausmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
