Colchicum pannonicum

Colchicum pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian colchic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a large, showy inflorescence with waxy, purple to pink petals, and its leaves are basal, forming a rosette. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows.

The bulbs of Colchicum pannonicum contain toxic alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological and medicinal significance.
