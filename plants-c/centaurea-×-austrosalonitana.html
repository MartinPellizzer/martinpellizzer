Centaurea × austrosalonitana

Centaurea × austrosalonitana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
