Cardamine californica subsp. cuneata

Cardamine californica subsp.

cuneata, commonly known as the Sierra buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and streamside areas.

The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in spring. Its flowers are surrounded by specialized leaf-like structures called bracts.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its aesthetic appeal in native plant gardens.
