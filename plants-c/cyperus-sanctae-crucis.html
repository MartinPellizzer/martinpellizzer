Cyperus sanctae-crucis

Cyperus sanctae-crucis, commonly known as the Holy Cross sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus sanctae-crucis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various water conditions.
