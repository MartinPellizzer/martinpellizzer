Cryptomeria mucronata

Cryptomeria mucronata, commonly known as the Japanese cedar, is a coniferous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its wood is valued for its durability and aromatic properties, making it useful in construction and traditional crafts.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and temperate climates, often found in mountainous regions.
