Chamaecyparis pisifera filifera

Chamaecyparis pisifera filifera, commonly known as the Japanese white cedar or Japanese cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are dense and dark green. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens.

Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing year-round visual interest.
