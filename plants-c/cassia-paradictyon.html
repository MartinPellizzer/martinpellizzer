Cassia paradictyon

Cassia paradictyon, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia paradictyon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
