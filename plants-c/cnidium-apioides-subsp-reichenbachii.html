Cnidium apioides subsp. reichenbachii

Cnidium apioides subsp.

reichenbachii, commonly known as the Chinese wild ginger, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

The plant features slender, branched stems and lanceolate leaves with a distinct, pungent odor. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in complex umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits.

The subspecies is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional Chinese medicine.
