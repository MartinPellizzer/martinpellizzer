Crotalaria teretifolia

Crotalaria teretifolia, commonly known as the slender cowpea or narrowleaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and alternate leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers clustered in racemes, which are followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Crotalaria teretifolia is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in certain agricultural contexts.
