Citriobatus spinescens

Citriobatus spinescens, commonly known as the spiny citriobatus, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive spiny foliage.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae, although it is not a true cactus, and is native to arid regions of South America. The plant features elongated, fleshy leaves that are covered in sharp, protective spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure.

This plant is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a resilient and intriguing subject for botanical study.
