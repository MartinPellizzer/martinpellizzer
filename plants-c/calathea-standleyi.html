Calathea standleyi

Calathea standleyi, commonly known as the velvet leaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, and often variegated foliage, which displays intricate patterns of green, silver, and purple. The leaves are typically oval or heart-shaped, with a waxy texture that gives them a unique appearance. It thrives in humid environments with indirect light and requires consistent moisture in its soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
