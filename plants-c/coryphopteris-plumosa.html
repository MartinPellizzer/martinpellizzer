Coryphopteris plumosa

Coryphopteris plumosa, commonly known as the feather fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with soft, silky textures that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Coryphopteris plumosa is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
