Chrysanthemum × sinense var. vestitum

Chrysanthemum × sinense var. vestitum, commonly known as the silverleaf chrysanthemum, is a hybrid variety characterized by its distinctive silvery-white variegation on its foliage.

This plant is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to China, where it has been cultivated for centuries. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered with a dense layer of fine, silky hairs that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange, typically blooming in the fall.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
