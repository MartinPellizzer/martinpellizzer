Crassula × langleyensis

Crassula × langleyensis, commonly known as the compact crassula, is a hybrid succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and ability to form dense clumps make it an attractive and low-maintenance ornamental plant.
