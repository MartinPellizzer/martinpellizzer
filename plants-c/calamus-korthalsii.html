Calamus korthalsii

Calamus korthalsii, commonly known as the Korthals' rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clustered, orange-colored fruits. Its leaves are typically pinnate, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Calamus korthalsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in basketry and furniture making.
