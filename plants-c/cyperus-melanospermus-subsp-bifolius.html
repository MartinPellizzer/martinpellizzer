Cyperus melanospermus subsp. bifolius

Cyperus melanospermus subsp.

bifolius, commonly known as blackseed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often brown or black in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

The subspecies bifolius is distinguished by its two leaflets per node, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.
