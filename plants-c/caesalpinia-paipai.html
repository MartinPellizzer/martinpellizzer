Caesalpinia paipai

Caesalpinia paipai, commonly known as the pink glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright pink to red bract-based inflorescences, which resemble feathery plumes and are highly ornamental. The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Caesalpinia paipai is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
