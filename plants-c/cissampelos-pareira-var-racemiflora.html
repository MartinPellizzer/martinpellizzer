Cissampelos pareira var. racemiflora

Cissampelos pareira var. racemiflora, commonly known as the racemiflorous moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its fruits are elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
