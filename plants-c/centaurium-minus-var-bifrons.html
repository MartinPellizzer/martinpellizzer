Centaurium minus var. bifrons

Centaurium minus var. bifrons, commonly known as the narrowleaf centaury, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features slender, erect stems that bear small, lance-shaped leaves arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear several flower clusters near the tips.

Centaurium minus var. bifrons is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
