Cinchona canescens

Cinchona canescens, commonly known as the white cinchona, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. Its bark contains alkaloids, including quinine, which has historically been used in medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.
