Commiphora habessinica subsp. tanganyikensis

Commiphora habessinica subsp.

tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Tanganyikan myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the eastern African region, particularly Tanzania and southern Uganda. It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants.

The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and leathery, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fragrant, resinous gum resins that are used in traditional medicine and incense.

The species is valued for its unique aromatic properties and ecological role in its native habitat.
