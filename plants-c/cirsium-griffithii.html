Cirsium griffithii

Cirsium griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with strong, rigid spines along the edges.

Cirsium griffithii is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
