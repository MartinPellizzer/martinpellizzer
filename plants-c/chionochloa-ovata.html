Chionochloa ovata

Chionochloa ovata, commonly known as the ovate silvergrass, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, often forming dense tussocks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often concealed within the leaf sheaths.

Chionochloa ovata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
