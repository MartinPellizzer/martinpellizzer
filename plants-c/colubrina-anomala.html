Colubrina anomala

Colubrina anomala, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, wetlands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Colubrina anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist, sandy environments.
