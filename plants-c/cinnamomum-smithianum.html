Cinnamomum smithianum

Cinnamomum smithianum, commonly known as the Smith's cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-brown bark that resembles cinnamon. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, dark purple fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, spicy scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited commercial use compared to other cinnamon species.
