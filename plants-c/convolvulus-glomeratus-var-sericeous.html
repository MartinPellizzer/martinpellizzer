Convolvulus glomeratus var. sericeous

Convolvulus glomeratus var. sericeous, commonly known as silverweed or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its "sericeous" designation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its delicate appearance.
