Crucifera alyssum

Crucifera alyssum, commonly known as sweet alyssum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters of four, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often dispersed by wind. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
