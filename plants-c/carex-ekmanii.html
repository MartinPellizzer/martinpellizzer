Carex ekmanii

Carex ekmanii, commonly known as the Ekman sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Carex ekmanii has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
