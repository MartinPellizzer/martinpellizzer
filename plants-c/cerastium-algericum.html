Cerastium algericum

Cerastium algericum, commonly known as Algerian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy surface, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Cerastium algericum is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant.
