Cassia cuneifolia

Cassia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. Cassia cuneifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
