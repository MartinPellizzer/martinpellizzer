Cicercula cicera var. serotina

Cicercula cicera var. serotina, commonly known as the late vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in racemes. The plant has a robust root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.

Its seeds are typically harvested in late summer, making it a valuable forage crop in certain agricultural systems.
