Chassalia lutescens

Chassalia lutescens, commonly known as the yellow chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark brown disk florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. It produces a single, showy flower head that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chassalia lutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
