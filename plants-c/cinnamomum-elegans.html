Cinnamomum elegans

Cinnamomum elegans, commonly known as Ceylon cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces thick, reddish-brown bark that is harvested for its highly fragrant cinnamon spice. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The tree thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
