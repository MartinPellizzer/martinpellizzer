Cyperus inflexus

Cyperus inflexus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water edges.

Cyperus inflexus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
