Cheiranthus × luteus

Cheiranthus × luteus, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun conditions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cheiranthus × luteus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
