Chamaespartium tridentatum subsp. cantabricum

Chamaespartium tridentatum subsp.

cantabricum, commonly known as the Cantabrian thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three lobes, and it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.
