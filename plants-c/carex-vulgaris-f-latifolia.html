Carex vulgaris f. latifolia

Carex vulgaris f. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it is a common species in various wetland ecosystems across temperate regions.
