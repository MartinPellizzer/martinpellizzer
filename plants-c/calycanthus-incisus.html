Calycanthus incisus

Calycanthus incisus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name "incisus," which means "cutting" in Latin. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
