Cestrum toledii

Cestrum toledii, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming primarily at night. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Cestrum toledii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.
