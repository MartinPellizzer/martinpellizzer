Cheirostylis kanarensis

Cheirostylis kanarensis, commonly known as the Kanarensis orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Indian subcontinent.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and multiple pseudobulbs. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
