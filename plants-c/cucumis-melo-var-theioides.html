Cucumis melo var. theioides

Cucumis melo var. theioides, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pale green fruit with a thin, crisp rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmate leaves and produces yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is valued for its flavor and versatility in culinary uses.
