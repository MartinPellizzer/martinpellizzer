Crotalaria anisophylla

Crotalaria anisophylla, commonly known as the African butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers that resemble butterflies, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has compound leaves with alternating pairs of leaflets. Crotalaria anisophylla is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.
