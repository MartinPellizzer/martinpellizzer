Coix

Coix, commonly known as Job's tears, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hard, triangular seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its tall, slender stalks can reach heights of up to 2 meters, and it produces clusters of small, greenish flowers.

The seeds are rich in nutrients and have been used for thousands of years in both food and herbal remedies.
