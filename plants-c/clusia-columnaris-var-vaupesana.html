Clusia columnaris var. vaupesana

Clusia columnaris var. vaupesana, commonly known as the yellow columnar clusia, is a species native to South America, particularly Colombia and Venezuela.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, columnar growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.

The leaves are also noted for their ability to store water, making the plant drought-tolerant.
