Cousinia bonvaleti

Cousinia bonvaleti, commonly known as the Bonvalet's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually silver-gray, covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Cousinia bonvaleti produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its alpine habitat.
