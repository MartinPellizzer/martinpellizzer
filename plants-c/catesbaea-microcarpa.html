Catesbaea microcarpa

Catesbaea microcarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green surface. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a strong, spicy flavor.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
