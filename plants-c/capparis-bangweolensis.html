Capparis bangweolensis

Capparis bangweolensis, commonly known as the Bangweol Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, yellowish-orange fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale green, with five petals.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to harsh climates.
