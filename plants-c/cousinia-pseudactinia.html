Cousinia pseudactinia

Cousinia pseudactinia, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, often silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Cousinia pseudactinia is known for its resilience and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
