Cereus sepium

Cereus sepium, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its striking appearance and unique blooming habit make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
