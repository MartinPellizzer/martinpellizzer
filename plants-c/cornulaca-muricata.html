Cornulaca muricata

Cornulaca muricata, commonly known as the spiny cornflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments, storing water in its succulent stems and leaves.

Cornulaca muricata is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance.
