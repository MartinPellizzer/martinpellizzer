Crepinia obtusifolia

Crepinia obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved crepina, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features basal rosettes of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
