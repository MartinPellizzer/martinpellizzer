Centradenia floribunda

Centradenia floribunda, commonly known as the red-flowered centradenia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, simple leaves that are often elliptical in shape. Its botanical features include aromatic leaves and woody stems, which contribute to its ornamental value.

Centradenia floribunda is valued in horticulture for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to different soil types.
