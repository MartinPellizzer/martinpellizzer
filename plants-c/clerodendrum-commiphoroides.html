Clerodendrum commiphoroides

Clerodendrum commiphoroides, commonly known as the fragrant clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by dark purple to black berries. Its leaves and flowers contain volatile oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties and have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
