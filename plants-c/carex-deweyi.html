Carex deweyi

Carex deweyi, commonly known as Dewey's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. Dewey's sedge typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
