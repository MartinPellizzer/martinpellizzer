Caylusea latifolia

Caylusea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf caylusea, is a unique species native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its wide, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the cooler months, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Caylusea latifolia is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it thrives with minimal care.
