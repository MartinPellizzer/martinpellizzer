Cepa esculenta

Cepa esculenta, commonly known as the wild leek, is a flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its fragrant, garlic-like odor. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often have a mottled appearance.

Cepa esculenta is often cultivated for its edible bulbs, which are prized in gourmet cooking.
