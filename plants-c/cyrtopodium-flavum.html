Cyrtopodium flavum

Cyrtopodium flavum, commonly known as the Yellow Hooked Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cyrtopodium flavum is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
