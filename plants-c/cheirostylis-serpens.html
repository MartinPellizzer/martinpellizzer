Cheirostylis serpens

Cheirostylis serpens, commonly known as the serpent orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow with dark markings. Its root system is fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in its natural habitat.

Cheirostylis serpens is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and along stream banks.
