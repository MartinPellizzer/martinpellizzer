Campanumoea japonica

Campanumoea japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanumoea japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
