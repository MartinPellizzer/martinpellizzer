Chamaecrista nictitans var. paraguariensis

Chamaecrista nictitans var. paraguariensis, commonly known as paraguayan coral bean, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is often used in landscaping and agroforestry.
