Codiaeum phillipsii

Codiaeum phillipsii, commonly known as the flame leaf or croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, and often variegated foliage, which can display shades of red, orange, yellow, and green. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. Its leaves are typically lobed or palmately divided, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers.

Codiaeum phillipsii thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
