Craspedodictyum grande

Craspedodictyum grande, commonly known as the giant spiny leaf cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, spiny, paddle-shaped stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the dry soil.

Craspedodictyum grande is drought-tolerant and requires minimal watering, making it well-suited for arid environments.
