Campanula sparsiflora

Campanula sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Campanula sparsiflora is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
