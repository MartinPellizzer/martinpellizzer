Cyperus pseudodiaphanus

Cyperus pseudodiaphanus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, ditches, and along waterways.

Cyperus pseudodiaphanus is often considered a weed in agricultural and aquatic settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
