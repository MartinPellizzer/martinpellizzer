Crassocephalum haematophyllum

Crassocephalum haematophyllum, commonly known as the bloodleaf or red leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its striking red and green variegated foliage. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
