Calophyllum sangkae

Calophyllum sangkae, commonly known as the Malayan torchwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces woody fruits that contain a single, hard-shelled seed. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

Calophyllum sangkae is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
