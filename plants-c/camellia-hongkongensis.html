Camellia hongkongensis

Camellia hongkongensis, commonly known as the Hong Kong camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China and the island of Hong Kong.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pink and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and features glossy, dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.

Camellia hongkongensis is also valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
