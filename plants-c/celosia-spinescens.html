Celosia spinescens

Celosia spinescens, commonly known as the flame flower or crested grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, feather-like bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered within the colorful bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Celosia spinescens is also known for its ability to reseed itself, contributing to its widespread presence in various climates.
