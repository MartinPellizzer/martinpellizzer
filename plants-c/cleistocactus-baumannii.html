Cleistocactus baumannii

Cleistocactus baumannii, commonly known as the "cactus of the desert," is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly hairs, which help to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming during the cooler months. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
