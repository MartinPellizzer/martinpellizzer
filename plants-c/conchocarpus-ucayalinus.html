Conchocarpus ucayalinus

Conchocarpus ucayalinus, commonly known as the Andean sycamore, is a tree native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
