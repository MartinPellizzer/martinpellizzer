Crotalaria recta subsp. simplex

Crotalaria recta subsp.

simplex, commonly known as the simple crownflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves.

The plant produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
