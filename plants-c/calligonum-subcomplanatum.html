Calligonum subcomplanatum

Calligonum subcomplanatum, commonly known as the flat-leaved saxifrage, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Its robust structure and drought tolerance make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
