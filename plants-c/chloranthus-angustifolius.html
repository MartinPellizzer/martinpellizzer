Chloranthus angustifolius

Chloranthus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with a short, upright stem and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
