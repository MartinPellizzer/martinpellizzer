Chryselium

Chryselium, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow ray flowers and is native to various regions in the Americas. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Chryselium species often thrive in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
