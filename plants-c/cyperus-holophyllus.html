Cyperus holophyllus

Cyperus holophyllus, commonly known as the white flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically white or pale in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus holophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic habitats.
