Circaea caulescens f. ramosissima

Circaea caulescens f. ramosissima, commonly known as the branched wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.
