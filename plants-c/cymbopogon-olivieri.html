Cymbopogon olivieri

Cymbopogon olivieri, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that emit a strong, citrusy aroma. The plant is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are extracted from its rhizomes and lower stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Lemon grass is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
