Crataegus greggiana var. pepo

Crataegus greggiana var. pepo, commonly known as the Gregg's hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall and is often used in traditional medicine. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native range.
