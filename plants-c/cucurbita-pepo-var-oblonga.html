Cucurbita pepo var. oblonga

Cucurbita pepo var. oblonga, commonly known as the acorn squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruits.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers, with both male and female flowers present on the same plant. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of hairy texture. The fruit develops from the female flowers and matures to a deep orange color, often with a distinctive ridged or knobby surface.

This variety is cultivated for its edible seeds and flesh, which are rich in nutrients and commonly used in culinary applications.
