Clematis akoensis

Clematis akoensis, commonly known as the Akoensis Clematis, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, with a distinctive, intricate structure. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for consumption. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Clematis akoensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
