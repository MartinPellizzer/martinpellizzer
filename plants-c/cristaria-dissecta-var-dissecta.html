Cristaria dissecta var. dissecta

Cristaria dissecta var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected water shield, is a species of aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are finely divided, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are often submerged, while the stems may extend above the water surface.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
