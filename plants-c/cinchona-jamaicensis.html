Cinchona jamaicensis

Cinchona jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican quinine tree, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is renowned for its bark, which contains compounds such as quinine, historically used in the treatment of malaria.

It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil, often found in coastal lowlands and mountainous regions.
