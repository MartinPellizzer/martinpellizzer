Copaifera panamensis

Copaifera panamensis, commonly known as Panama copaiba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly Panama and parts of Colombia. The plant is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences.

The tree is valued for its aromatic resins, which have been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
