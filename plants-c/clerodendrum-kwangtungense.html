Clerodendrum kwangtungense

Clerodendrum kwangtungense, commonly known as the Guangdong glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

Clerodendrum kwangtungense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
