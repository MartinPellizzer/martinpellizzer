Caryophyllata officinalis

Caryophyllata officinalis, commonly known as the medicinal carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Caryophyllata officinalis has been historically valued for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
