Chytroma

Chytroma, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique aquatic plant that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant lacks true roots and instead relies on rhizoids for anchoring and nutrient absorption. Chytroma is often found in shaded, muddy areas and is known for its ability to tolerate low light conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for water quality and its ecological importance in supporting aquatic life.
