Costus ulugurensis

Costus ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru costus, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the Uluguru Mountains of Tanzania.

It is a rhizomatous herb characterized by its tall, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, pale yellow to white spadices with prominent, fragrant flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
