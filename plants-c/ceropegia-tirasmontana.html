Ceropegia tirasmontana

Ceropegia tirasmontana, commonly known as the Madagascar star vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces tubular, fragrant blooms that are typically pink or white, attracting specific pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.
