Crypsinopsis curtidens

Crypsinopsis curtidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cryptomeria, is a rare evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along its branches. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often found in high-altitude forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
