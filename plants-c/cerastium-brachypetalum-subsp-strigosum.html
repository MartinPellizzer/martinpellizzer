Cerastium brachypetalum subsp. strigosum

Cerastium brachypetalum subsp.

strigosum, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a distinctive hairy texture, and it produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
