Crataegus hirtella

Crataegus hirtella, commonly known as the hairy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and small white to pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is often found in moist woodlands and along streambanks.
