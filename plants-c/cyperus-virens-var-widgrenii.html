Cyperus virens var. widgrenii

Cyperus virens var. widgrenii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, clustered flower heads that are often hidden among the foliage.

This variant is distinguished by its slender leaves and compact growth habit compared to other Cyperus species.
