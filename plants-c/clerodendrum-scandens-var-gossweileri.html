Clerodendrum scandens var. gossweileri

Clerodendrum scandens var. gossweileri, commonly known as the Gossweiler's glorybower, is a vigorous climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The flowers are typically borne in terminal panicles and are attractive to pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a woody stem with thorn-like protrusions and can grow up to several meters in height.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
