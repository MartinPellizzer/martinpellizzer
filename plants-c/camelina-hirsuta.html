Camelina hirsuta

Camelina hirsuta, commonly known as hairy camelina, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, as well as small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Camelina hirsuta is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open fields.
