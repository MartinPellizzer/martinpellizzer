Compsanthus maculatus

Compsanthus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted leaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often spotted or streaked with red or purple markings. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or yellow, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its colorful foliage and showy blooms.
