Cyperus laticulmis

Cyperus laticulmis, commonly known as the broad-ribbed flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Cyperus laticulmis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive, textured foliage.
