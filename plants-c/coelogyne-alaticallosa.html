Coelogyne alaticallosa

Coelogyne alaticallosa, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a waxy coating.

The flowers, which range in color from white to yellow and orange, bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a sought-after ornamental plant.
