Chamaecrista capensis

Chamaecrista capensis, commonly known as the Cape burr legume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny seed pods that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Chamaecrista capensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
