Cyperus maculatus

Cyperus maculatus, commonly known as the variegated flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant features grass-like leaves with striking variegation, often displaying white or yellow markings. It produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green or brown.

Cyperus maculatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
