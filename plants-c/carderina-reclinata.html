Carderina reclinata

Carderina reclinata, commonly known as the hanging fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, cascading fronds that give it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant thrives in humid environments and requires indirect sunlight to flourish. Its leaves are typically light green with a slightly serrated edge.

Carderina reclinata is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
