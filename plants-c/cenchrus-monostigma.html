Cenchrus monostigma

Cenchrus monostigma, commonly known as the burr grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its dense growth habit and the presence of spiny, awned lemmas on its inflorescences, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and agricultural fields. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread readily, making it both a pioneer species and a potential weed.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its adaptability and resilience in arid environments.
