Corchorus polygonatum

Corchorus polygonatum, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lanceolate leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration, especially on the underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and typically bloom in late summer.

The roots are fibrous and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
