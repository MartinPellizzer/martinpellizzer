Coleocephalocereus aureispinus

Coleocephalocereus aureispinus, commonly known as the golden spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow spines and a generally green to pale blue stem. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and gardeners in dry climates.
