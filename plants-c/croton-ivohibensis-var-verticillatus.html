Croton ivohibensis var. verticillatus

Croton ivohibensis var. verticillatus, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, and often variegated leaves that display a range of hues including yellow, red, and green. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Its leaves are arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its vibrant foliage and unique form.
