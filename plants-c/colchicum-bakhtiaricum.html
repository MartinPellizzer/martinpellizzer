Colchicum bakhtiaricum

Colchicum bakhtiaricum, commonly known as the Bakhtiar colchic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are usually pale purple to pink, with six petals that are reflexed at the tips.

Colchicum bakhtiaricum is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in challenging ecological conditions.
