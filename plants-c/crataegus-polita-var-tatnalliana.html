Crataegus polita var. tatnalliana

Crataegus polita var. tatnalliana, commonly known as the Tatnall hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and features thorned branches, which are characteristic of the Crataegus genus. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with finely toothed margins and a glossy upper surface.

This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
