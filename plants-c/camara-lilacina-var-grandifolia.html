Camara lilacina var. grandifolia

Camara lilacina var. grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflora bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to lilac, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nectar source for pollinators and its ornamental value in gardens.
