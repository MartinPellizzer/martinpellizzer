Cassytha remotiflora

Cassytha remotiflora, commonly known as the creeping grapevine, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, serpentine stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread rapidly along the ground or through trees. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cassytha remotiflora is often found in moist, shaded environments and can be invasive in certain regions.
