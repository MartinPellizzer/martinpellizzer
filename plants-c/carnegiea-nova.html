Carnegiea nova

Carnegiea nova, commonly known as the Mexican prickly pear cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a "tuna," is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.

The Carnegiea nova plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
