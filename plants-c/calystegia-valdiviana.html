Calystegia valdiviana

Calystegia valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in moist, temperate regions and can be invasive in certain environments.

Calystegia valdiviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
