Carex muricata proles fumosa

Carex muricata proles fumosa, commonly known as the smoky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to grayish flowers, giving it its "smoky" appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
