Cirsium × grandiflorum var. legrandii

Cirsium × grandiflorum var. legrandii, commonly known as the Grandiflorum thistle, is a hybrid plant species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy purple to pink flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, green bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
