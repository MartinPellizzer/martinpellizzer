Capsella bursa-pastoris var. agrestis

Capsella bursa-pastoris var. agrestis, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers and distinctive heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often considered a weed. It has a short life cycle, completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
