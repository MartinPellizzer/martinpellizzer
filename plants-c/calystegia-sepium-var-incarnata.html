Calystegia sepium var. incarnata

Calystegia sepium var. incarnata, commonly known as the red bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
