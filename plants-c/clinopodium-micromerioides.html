Clinopodium micromerioides

Clinopodium micromerioides, commonly known as dwarf creeping mint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

Clinopodium micromerioides thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
