Chalybea brevipedunculata

Chalybea brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked ironwood, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Chalybea brevipedunculata is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.
