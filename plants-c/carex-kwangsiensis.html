Carex kwangsiensis

Carex kwangsiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features cylindrical, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex kwangsiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
