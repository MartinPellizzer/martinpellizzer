Cerastium lanatum

Cerastium lanatum, commonly known as the downy mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are densely covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its hairy foliage and delicate flowers make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
