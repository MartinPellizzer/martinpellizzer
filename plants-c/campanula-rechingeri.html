Campanula rechingeri

Campanula rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Campanula rechingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
