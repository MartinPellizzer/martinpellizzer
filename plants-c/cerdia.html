Cerdia

Cerdia, commonly known as the desert lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often bloom in the early morning. The plants typically have strap-like leaves and produce bulbs or corms for storage of nutrients. Cerdia species are adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, showcasing remarkable drought tolerance.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
