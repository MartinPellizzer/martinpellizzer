Chaenorhinum flexuosum subsp. maroccanum

Chaenorhinum flexuosum subsp.

maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan flowering plant, is a herbaceous annual native to North Africa. It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological role in arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
