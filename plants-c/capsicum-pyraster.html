Capsicum pyraster

Capsicum pyraster, commonly known as the bird pepper or the wild pepper, is a species native to the Americas.

It is a shrub-like plant that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits. These fruits are typically red or orange when mature and contain numerous seeds.

Capsicum pyraster is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
