Casearia pseudophiticola

Casearia pseudophiticola, commonly known as the false yellowwood, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
