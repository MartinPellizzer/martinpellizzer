Corylus avellana var. contorta

Corylus avellana var. contorta, commonly known as the horned hazel, is a variant of the common hazel that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its twisted, gnarled branches and small, spiny husks that encase its edible nuts. The plant produces small, yellowish male catkins and female flowers that develop into the characteristic hazelnuts. It is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its nuts, which are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
