Cortusa matthioli f. brotheri

Cortusa matthioli f. brotheri, commonly known as the dwarf garden bellflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and lavender, blooming in early summer. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a rosette formation, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to small garden spaces.
