Cyperus andschoa

Cyperus andschoa, commonly known as the Andes sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly the Andean regions, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Cyperus andschoa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
