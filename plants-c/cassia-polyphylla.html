Cassia polyphylla

Cassia polyphylla, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has compound leaves that are bipinnate, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
