Cyperus sanguinolentus f. melanocephalus

Cyperus sanguinolentus f. melanocephalus, commonly known as the black-headed flatsedge, is a variant of the cyperus genus characterized by its distinctive dark purple or black floral bracts.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its morphology includes narrow, grass-like leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the end of its flowering stems.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
