Chondrorhyncha aromatica

Chondrorhyncha aromatica, commonly known as the fragrant root plant, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features a robust, fibrous root system that stores essential oils, contributing to its aromatic properties. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth surface and a faintly hairy underside. Its small, clustered flowers are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring.

The essential oils extracted from its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory effects.
