Calyptranthes rostellata

Calyptranthes rostellata, commonly known as the silverleaf calyptranthes, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal or lowland forests.

Calyptranthes rostellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
