Crocus reineggeri

Crocus reineggeri, commonly known as the Reinegger's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. It typically blooms in early spring and has narrow, grass-like leaves.

Due to its striking appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ornamental species.
