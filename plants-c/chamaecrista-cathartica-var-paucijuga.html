Chamaecrista cathartica var. paucijuga

Chamaecrista cathartica var. paucijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf crownbeard, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems and small, pinnately compound leaves with a distinctive grayish-green hue. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are typically arranged in a loose, branching pattern. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, pod-like fruits that split open when mature.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as woods and thickets.
