Cupressus × ovensii

Cupressus × ovensii, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a hybrid coniferous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Cupressus arizonica and Cupressus sarmentosa, with scale-like leaves arranged in flat sprays. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its dense foliage and attractive form, often used in landscaping.
