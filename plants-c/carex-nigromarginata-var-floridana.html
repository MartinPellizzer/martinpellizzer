Carex nigromarginata var. floridana

Carex nigromarginata var. floridana, commonly known as Florida black margined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with dark green leaves that have a distinctive black margin. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
