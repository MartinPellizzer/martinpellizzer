Cyanotis thwaitesii

Cyanotis thwaitesii, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10-30 cm. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cyanotis thwaitesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
