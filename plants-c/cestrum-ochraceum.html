Cestrum ochraceum

Cestrum ochraceum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small, black seeds.

Cestrum ochraceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
