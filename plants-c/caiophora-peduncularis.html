Caiophora peduncularis

Caiophora peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It is a carnivorous plant, capable of trapping and digesting small aquatic organisms.

Caiophora peduncularis is commonly found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and ditches.
