Chenopodium album subsp. iranicum

Chenopodium album subsp.

iranicum, commonly known as wild spinach or fat-hen, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is known for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional cuisines in various regions.
