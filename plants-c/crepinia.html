Crepinia

Crepinia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other similar plants. The plants typically have showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white or pink petals. They are often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Crepinia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
