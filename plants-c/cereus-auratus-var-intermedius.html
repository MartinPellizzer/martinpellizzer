Cereus auratus var. intermedius

Cereus auratus var. intermedius, commonly known as the Intermediate Crown of Thorns, is a species of cactus native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking, white, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
