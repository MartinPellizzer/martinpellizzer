Cavanilla punctata

Cavanilla punctata, commonly known as the spotted cavanilla, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often branched and somewhat woody. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

The flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and are known to attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
