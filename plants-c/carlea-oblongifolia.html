Carlea oblongifolia

Carlea oblongifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features striking, radially symmetrical flowers with bright yellow centers and white petals, often surrounded by a ring of narrow, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 cm and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carlea oblongifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
