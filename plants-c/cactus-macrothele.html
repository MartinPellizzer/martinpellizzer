Cactus macrothele

Cactus macrothele, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in the spring.

The cactus is adapted to full sun and requires well-drained soil to thrive.
