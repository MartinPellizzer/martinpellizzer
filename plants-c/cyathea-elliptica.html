Cyathea elliptica

Cyathea elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and longevity. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Cyathea elliptica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and air-purifying qualities.
