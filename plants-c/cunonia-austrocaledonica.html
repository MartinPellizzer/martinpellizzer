Cunonia austrocaledonica

Cunonia austrocaledonica, commonly known as the Southern Kaguas, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, smooth seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
