Chrysanthemum galae

Chrysanthemum galae, commonly known as the Galae chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the fall, and its leaves are usually hairy and alternate. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Chrysanthemum galae is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some cultures.
