Centaurea uniflora var. tineana

Centaurea uniflora var. tineana, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and small, bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
