Crotalaria incana

Crotalaria incana, commonly known as the slender crotalaria, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a pubescent surface, and it produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers clustered in racemes. Its seed pods are cylindrical and dehiscent, releasing seeds when mature.

Crotalaria incana is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain environments.
