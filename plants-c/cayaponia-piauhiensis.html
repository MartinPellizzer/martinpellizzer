Cayaponia piauhiensis

Cayaponia piauhiensis, commonly known as the Piauhi tree, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
