Crepis acuminata var. pleurocarpa

Crepis acuminata var. pleurocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
