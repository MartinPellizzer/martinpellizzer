Crataegus × dippeliana

Crataegus × dippeliana, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid plant within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, small white or pink flowers, and red or orange berries that appear in the fall. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world. It thrives in a range of soil types and is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
