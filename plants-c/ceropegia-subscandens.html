Ceropegia subscandens

Ceropegia subscandens, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia subscandens is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appearance and ease of care.
