Centaurea ceratophylla subsp. ceratophylla

Centaurea ceratophylla subsp.

ceratophylla, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny bracts that resemble horns, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, arid environments. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, with a striking contrast to the green foliage.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
