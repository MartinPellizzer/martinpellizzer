Ceratotheca

Ceratotheca, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, and often hairy pods that enclose the seeds. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The flowers are usually small and clustered, with bright yellow petals that attract pollinators.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
