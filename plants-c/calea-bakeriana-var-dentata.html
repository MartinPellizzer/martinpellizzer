Calea bakeriana var. dentata

Calea bakeriana var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking white to pale blue ray florets. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with distinctive toothed margins. It produces showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Calea bakeriana var. dentata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, sunny soils.
