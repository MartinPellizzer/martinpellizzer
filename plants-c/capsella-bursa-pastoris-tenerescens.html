Capsella bursa-pastoris tenerescens

Capsella bursa-pastoris tenerescens, commonly known as the common chickweed or velvetleaf, is a low-growing annual herb belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

This species is widely distributed and thrives in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and urban areas.
