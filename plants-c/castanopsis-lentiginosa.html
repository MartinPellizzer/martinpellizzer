Castanopsis lentiginosa

Castanopsis lentiginosa, commonly known as the black oak.

It is a species of evergreen tree belonging to the Fagaceae family, characterized by its dark, fissured bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are typically enclosed in a woody cupule. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and Thailand.

Castanopsis lentiginosa is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in reforestation efforts due to its ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
