Cakile aegyptia var. susica

Cakile aegyptia var. susica, commonly known as the Egyptian rockcress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal and desert environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh conditions.

It is also known for its ability to tolerate high salinity, making it a hardy species in challenging climates.
