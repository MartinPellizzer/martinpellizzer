Chrysanthemum gaubae

Chrysanthemum gaubae, commonly known as the African chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Chrysanthemum gaubae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
