Croton cubanus

Croton cubanus, commonly known as the Cuban croton, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often displaying vibrant shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

Due to its striking appearance, it is popular in horticulture but requires well-drained soil and protection from frost.
