Comarum angustifolium var. parvifolium

Comarum angustifolium var. parvifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved water plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments, where it thrives in shallow water or damp soil. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters during the summer months, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing aquatic habitats and supporting aquatic life.
