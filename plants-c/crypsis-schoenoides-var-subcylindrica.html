Crypsis schoenoides var. subcylindrica

Crypsis schoenoides var. subcylindrica, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in racemes during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and native plant gardens.
