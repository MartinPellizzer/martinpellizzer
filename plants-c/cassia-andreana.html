Cassia andreana

Cassia andreana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces pods that contain flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
