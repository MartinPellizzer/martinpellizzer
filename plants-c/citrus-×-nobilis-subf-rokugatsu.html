Citrus × nobilis subf. rokugatsu

Citrus × nobilis subf.

rokugatsu, commonly known as the Japanese kumquat, is a citrus fruit native to Japan and southern China. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a thin, edible peel.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while the flowers are fragrant and white.

The fruit ripens in late autumn and is prized for its sweet, tangy flavor and nutritional value.
