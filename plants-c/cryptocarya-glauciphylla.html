Cryptocarya glauciphylla

Cryptocarya glauciphylla, commonly known as the silverleaf cryptocarya, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of rainforests and has glossy, dark green foliage with a silvery underside. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
