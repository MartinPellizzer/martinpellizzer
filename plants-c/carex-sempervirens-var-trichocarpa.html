Carex sempervirens var. trichocarpa

Carex sempervirens var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that contain small, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural landscapes.
