Calceolaria trilobata

Calceolaria trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of red or orange. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments, often growing in rocky or forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
