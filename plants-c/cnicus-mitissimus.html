Cnicus mitissimus

Cnicus mitissimus, commonly known as the lesser gooseweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters and features creeping stems with distinctive yellow flowers that bloom from June to August. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are smaller and more lanceolate. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in meadows, and on waste ground.

Cnicus mitissimus is known for its adaptability and is considered a common weed in many regions.
