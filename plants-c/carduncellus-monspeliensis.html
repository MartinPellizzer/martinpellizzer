Carduncellus monspeliensis

Carduncellus monspeliensis, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in inulin, a type of dietary fiber. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable crop.

Its historical use by Native American tribes highlights its cultural and nutritional significance.
