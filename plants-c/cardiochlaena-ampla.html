Cardiochlaena ampla

Cardiochlaena ampla, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
