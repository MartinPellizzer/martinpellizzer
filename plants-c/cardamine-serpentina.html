Cardamine serpentina

Cardamine serpentina, commonly known as serpent's root, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Cardamine serpentina is often found in rich, loamy soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
