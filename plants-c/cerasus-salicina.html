Cerasus salicina

Cerasus salicina, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia.

It produces small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant flowers in early spring with clusters of white or pale pink blossoms, which are followed by the development of sweet, juicy fruits. The cherries are typically red or dark red when mature and are widely cultivated for their flavor and culinary use.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice in temperate climates.
