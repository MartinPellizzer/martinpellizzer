Caralluma hottentotorum

Caralluma hottentotorum, commonly known as the Hooded Cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like spines that protect the plant from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are fleshy and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Caralluma hottentotorum is also known for its traditional use in some cultures for its purported medicinal properties.
