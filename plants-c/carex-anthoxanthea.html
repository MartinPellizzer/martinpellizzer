Carex anthoxanthea

Carex anthoxanthea, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Native to parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are usually bright green, and its flowering stems are often shorter than the foliage.

Carex anthoxanthea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
