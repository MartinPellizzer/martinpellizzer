Capsella bursa-pastoris emarginata

Capsella bursa-pastoris emarginata, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
