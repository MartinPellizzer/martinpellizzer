Calamus verschaffeltii

Calamus verschaffeltii, commonly known as the Verschaffelt's arrowroot, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, followed by the development of elongated, spindle-shaped fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its starchy rhizomes, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Calamus verschaffeltii is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
