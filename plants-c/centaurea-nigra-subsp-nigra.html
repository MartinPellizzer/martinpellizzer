Centaurea nigra subsp. nigra

Centaurea nigra subsp.

nigra, commonly known as black knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers and is often found in dry, disturbed soils.

The plant has upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing small, clustered inflorescences.

Black knapweed is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun and is often used in wildflower gardens.
