Coreopsis tinctoria var. atropurpurea

Coreopsis tinctoria var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy blooms that attract pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both ornamental and ecological value.
