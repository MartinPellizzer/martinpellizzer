Chamaefistula valerioi

Chamaefistula valerioi, commonly known as the Valerio's thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems covered in small, spiny leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
