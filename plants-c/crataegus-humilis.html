Crataegus humilis

Crataegus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 5 meters and features thorny branches. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

Crataegus humilis is often found in open woodlands, along streambanks, and in disturbed soils.
