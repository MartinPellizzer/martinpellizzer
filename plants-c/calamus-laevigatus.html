Calamus laevigatus

Calamus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. Calamus laevigatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its rhizomes are also valued for their aromatic properties.
