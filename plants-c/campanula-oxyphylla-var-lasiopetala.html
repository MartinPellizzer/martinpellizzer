Campanula oxyphylla var. lasiopetala

Campanula oxyphylla var. lasiopetala, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in cooler climates.
