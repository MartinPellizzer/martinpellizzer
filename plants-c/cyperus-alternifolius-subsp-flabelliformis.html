Cyperus alternifolius subsp. flabelliformis

Cyperus alternifolius subsp.

flabelliformis, commonly known as the fan rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a fan-like pattern, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or near water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a water garden plant.
