Cereus silvestrii var. crassicaulis

Cereus silvestrii var. crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed night blooming cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. These flowers emit a subtle fragrance and are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.

Its slow growth rate and striking appearance make it a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
