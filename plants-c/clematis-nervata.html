Clematis nervata

Clematis nervata, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Clematis nervata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
