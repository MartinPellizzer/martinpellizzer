Calamagrostis × bihariensis

Calamagrostis × bihariensis, commonly known as the silvergrass or Himalayan reed, is a perennial grass native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silvery-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and meadows. It produces fluffy, feathery seed heads in the summer, which contribute to its ornamental value.

Calamagrostis × bihariensis is valued in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
