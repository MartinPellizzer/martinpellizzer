Carex tonnerrei

Carex tonnerrei, commonly known as the French sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in wet or damp environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the spring. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist conditions.

Carex tonnerrei is also known for its ability to help stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland areas.
