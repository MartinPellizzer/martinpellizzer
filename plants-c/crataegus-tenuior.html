Crataegus tenuior

Crataegus tenuior, commonly known as the slender hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red, juicy berries in the fall. Its leaves are usually 2 to 6 centimeters long and have a serrated margin.

Crataegus tenuior is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
