Cerasus avium erythrocarpa

Cerasus avium erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited cherry, is a species of Prunus native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright red berries that ripen in the summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and bloom before the leaves in early spring.
