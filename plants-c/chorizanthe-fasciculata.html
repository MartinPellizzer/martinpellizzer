Chorizanthe fasciculata

Chorizanthe fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered choris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Chorizanthe fasciculata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
