Croton penduliflorus

Croton penduliflorus, commonly known as the weeping croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers, which give it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Croton penduliflorus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
