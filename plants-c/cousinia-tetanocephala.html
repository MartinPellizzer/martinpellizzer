Cousinia tetanocephala

Cousinia tetanocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed cousinia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia tetanocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
