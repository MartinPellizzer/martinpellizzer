Cyperus pseudoleptocladus

Cyperus pseudoleptocladus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges.

Cyperus pseudoleptocladus produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.
