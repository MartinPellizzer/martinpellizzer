Cinnamomum obtusifolium var. loureiroi

Cinnamomum obtusifolium var. loureiroi, commonly known as the Malabar cinnamon or Ceylon cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the laurel family, Lauraceae.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic bark and leaves. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctively aromatic scent.

The bark, which is the primary commercial product, is harvested for its spice and medicinal properties.
