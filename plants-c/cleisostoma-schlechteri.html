Cleisostoma schlechteri

Cleisostoma schlechteri, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several fragrant, zygomorphic flowers.

Cleisostoma schlechteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
