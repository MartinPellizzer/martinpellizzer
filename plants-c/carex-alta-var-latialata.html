Carex alta var. latialata

Carex alta var. latialata, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and three-ranked leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps with slender, erect stems. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to other Carex species, making it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
