Cirsium myrianthum

Cirsium myrianthum, commonly known as the many-flowered thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure. Cirsium myrianthum typically grows in dry grasslands, rocky soils, and disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
