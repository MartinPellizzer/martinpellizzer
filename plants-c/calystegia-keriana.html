Calystegia keriana

Calystegia keriana, commonly known as the Chinese morning glory, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. Its flowers open in the morning and close at night, hence its common name.

Calystegia keriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
