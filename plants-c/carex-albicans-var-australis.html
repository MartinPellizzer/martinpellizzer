Carex albicans var. australis

Carex albicans var. australis, commonly known as the Australian white sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to southern Australia and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and pale, fluffy flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. It has narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that are often white or pale yellow in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
