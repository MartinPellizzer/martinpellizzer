Cryptocarya affinis

Cryptocarya affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sassafras, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in lowland forests or along riverbanks. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin, and it produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
