Chrysanthemum corsicum

Chrysanthemum corsicum, commonly known as the Corsican chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which typically range in color from white to yellow and sometimes orange. The plant has dark green, lobed leaves and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Chrysanthemum corsicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
