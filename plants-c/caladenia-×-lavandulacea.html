Caladenia × lavandulacea

Caladenia × lavandulacea, commonly known as the lavender-leafed spider orchid, is a hybrid orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats, often in coastal areas. Its flowers are usually pale purple or pink, with a distinctive, narrow spur that resembles a spider's leg.

Caladenia × lavandulacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
