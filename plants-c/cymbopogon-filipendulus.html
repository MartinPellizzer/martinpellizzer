Cymbopogon filipendulus

Cymbopogon filipendulus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, aromatic stalks that emit a strong citrus scent. The plant's leaves are long and narrow, often used in cooking and traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Lemon grass is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
