Calandrinia sericea var. sessiliflora

Calandrinia sericea var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the silverleaf portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, bright pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its succulent leaves and drought tolerance contribute to its adaptability in arid environments.
