Calamagrostis neglecta var. confinis

Calamagrostis neglecta var. confinis, commonly known as the western grass or narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet meadows, stream banks, and other riparian habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences in the summer. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and tolerance for wet conditions, making it a valuable plant for wetland restoration.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for ecological landscaping and wildlife habitat support.
