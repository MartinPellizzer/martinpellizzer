Calceolaria luteocalyx

Calceolaria luteocalyx, commonly known as the yellow-flowered slipper orchid, is a striking member of the Calceolaria genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Calceolaria luteocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
