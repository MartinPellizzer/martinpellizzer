Cinnamomum cuspidatum

Cinnamomum cuspidatum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by egg-shaped berries. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth surface and a strong, spicy scent.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
