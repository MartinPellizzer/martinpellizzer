Callirhoe involucrata var. novomexicana

Callirhoe involucrata var. novomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico blanketflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
