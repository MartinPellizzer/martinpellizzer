Carpopogon monospermus

Carpopogon monospermus, commonly known as the grass pink, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Its inflorescence is a type of panicle, with each flower having three stamens and a single pistil.

The grass pink is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands, where it thrives in full sun.
