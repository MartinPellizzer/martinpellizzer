Cherleria eglandulosa

Cherleria eglandulosa, commonly known as the smooth cherleria, is a small evergreen shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, red berries that are edible. Its botanical features make it suitable for use in ornamental gardening and as a source of medicinal compounds.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
