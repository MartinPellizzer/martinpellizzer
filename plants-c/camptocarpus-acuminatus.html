Camptocarpus acuminatus

Camptocarpus acuminatus, commonly known as the slender mallow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.

Camptocarpus acuminatus is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
