Capparis flexuosa var. cordifolia

Capparis flexuosa var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in shrubland or coastal dunes.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
