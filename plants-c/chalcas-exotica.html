Chalcas exotica

Chalcas exotica, commonly known as the rare tropical orchid, is a flowering plant native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful blooms that range from deep purples to vibrant yellows. The plant has aerial roots that allow it to climb and attach to tree trunks in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically small but highly fragrant, attracting specific pollinators like bees and moths.

Due to habitat loss, Chalcas exotica is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation purposes.
