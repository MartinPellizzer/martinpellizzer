Cydonia silvestris var. maliformis

Cydonia silvestris var. maliformis, commonly known as the wild quince.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its rough, bark-like stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which give way to yellow, citrus-like fruits in the fall. The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a deep orange color, indicating maturity.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
