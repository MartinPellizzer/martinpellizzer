Carex macella var. paraguayensis

Carex macella var. paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
