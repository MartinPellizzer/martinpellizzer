Cardamine trichocarpa

Cardamine trichocarpa, commonly known as hairy watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with fine, hairy textures. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The plant is known for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
