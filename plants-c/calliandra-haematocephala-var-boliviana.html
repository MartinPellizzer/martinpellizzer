Calliandra haematocephala var. boliviana

Calliandra haematocephala var. boliviana, commonly known as the bloodflower or Bolivian calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink inflorescences that resemble clusters of small flowers, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its stems are typically woody and branched. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ability to attract pollinators.

The species is also valued for its potential use in soil stabilization and as a source of nectar for bees.
