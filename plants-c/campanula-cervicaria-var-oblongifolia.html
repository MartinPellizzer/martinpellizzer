Campanula cervicaria var. oblongifolia

Campanula cervicaria var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a hairy texture, and are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
