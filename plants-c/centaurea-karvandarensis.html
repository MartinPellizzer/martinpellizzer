Centaurea karvandarensis

Centaurea karvandarensis, commonly known as Karvandar centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bright blue or purple flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
