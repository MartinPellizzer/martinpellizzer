Caladenia pendens subsp. pendens

Caladenia pendens subsp.

pendens, commonly known as the hanging spider orchid, is an orchid species native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, pendulous flowers that dangle from the stem, often in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. It produces a single, narrow leaf and has a short, erect stem.

The species is known for its striking appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
