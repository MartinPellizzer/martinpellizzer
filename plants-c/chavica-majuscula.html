Chavica majuscula

Chavica majuscula, commonly known as the large chavica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Chavica majuscula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
