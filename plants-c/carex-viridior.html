Carex viridior

Carex viridior, commonly known as green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its grass-like leaves are usually dark green and have a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of sedges.

Carex viridior produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often grouped into clusters at the top of the plant.
