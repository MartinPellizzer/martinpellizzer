Catolesia

Catolesia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features a central cone-like structure surrounded by ray florets, typical of the sunflower family. Catolesia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its medicinal properties have also been studied for their potential health benefits.
