Comparettia newyorkorum

Comparettia newyorkorum, commonly known as the New York orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the tropical rainforests of Central America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its unique botanical features, including its specialized root system and symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, are crucial for its survival in its natural habitat.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is critically threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
