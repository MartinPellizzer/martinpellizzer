Crocus hartmannianus

Crocus hartmannianus, commonly known as the Hartmann's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Alps, particularly in Austria and Italy. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, recurved petal shape. It typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The corms of this plant are adapted to cold climates and can remain dormant during harsh winters.
