Calathea laxa

Calathea laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf calathea, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled, and often variegated leaves that display a range of colors including green, silver, and pink. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its unique leaf patterns that change with light exposure. Calathea laxa prefers bright, indirect light and consistently moist, well-draining soil.

It is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
