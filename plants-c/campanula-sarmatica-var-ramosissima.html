Campanula sarmatica var. ramosissima

Campanula sarmatica var. ramosissima, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems that are often branched, giving it a bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
