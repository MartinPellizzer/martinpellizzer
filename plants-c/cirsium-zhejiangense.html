Cirsium zhejiangense

Cirsium zhejiangense, commonly known as the Zhejiang thistle, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
