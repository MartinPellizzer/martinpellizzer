Chamaesyce humifusa var. glabra

Chamaesyce humifusa var. glabra, commonly known as smooth sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to North America.

It typically thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open areas such as dunes, streambanks, and wet meadows. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and rounded, with a fleshy texture. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of tiny greenish-white blossoms that appear in clusters.

It is a hardy species that is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
