Calystegia malacophylla subsp. pedicellata

Calystegia malacophylla subsp.

pedicellata, commonly known as the pedicellate bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern China. It features trailing stems with heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a prominent central spur. It blooms from summer to early autumn, producing small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinct pedicellate (stalked) flowers, which distinguish it from other members of the genus.
