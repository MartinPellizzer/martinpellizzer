Callicarpa giraldii var. subcanescens

Callicarpa giraldii var. subcanescens, commonly known as the silverleaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that appear in late summer. The leaves are typically gray-green and covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
