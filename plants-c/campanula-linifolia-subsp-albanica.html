Campanula linifolia subsp. albanica

Campanula linifolia subsp.

albanica, commonly known as the Albanian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Campanula linifolia subsp.

albanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
