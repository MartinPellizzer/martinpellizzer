Chresta exsucca

Chresta exsucca, commonly known as the desert rose, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features fleshy, branching stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically small and pointed, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Chresta exsucca is well adapted to hot, sunny environments and requires minimal water once established.
