Crassula concinna

Crassula concinna, commonly known as the Jelly Bean Plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Crassula concinna is popular as a low-maintenance houseplant due to its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
