Cryptotaenia japonica f. dissecta

Cryptotaenia japonica f. dissecta, commonly known as Japanese giant knotweed, is a variant of the Cryptotaenia japonica species.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacey appearance. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, adding a subtle aesthetic to the plant's overall appearance.
