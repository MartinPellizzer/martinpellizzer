Chytroma simiorum var. latifolia

Chytroma simiorum var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf chytroma, is a unique plant species characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 15 cm in length.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The varietal form, latifolia, distinguishes it from other chytroma species by its wider leaf structure and slower growth rate.
