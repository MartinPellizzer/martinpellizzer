Chione venosa

Chione venosa, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a rosette of silvery, fuzzy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
