Caragana brevispina

Caragana brevispina, commonly known as the narrowthorned shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, thorny branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Caragana brevispina is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in poor soils and cold climates.
