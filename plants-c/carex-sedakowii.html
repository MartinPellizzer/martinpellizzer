Carex sedakowii

Carex sedakowii, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. Carex sedakowii is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
