Carex vulgaris var. anomala

Carex vulgaris var. anomala, commonly known as the bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in acidic, water-saturated soils and is often found in peat bogs and along the edges of ponds.

Carex vulgaris var. anomala plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
