Cedronella aurantiaca

Cedronella aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange cedron, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Cedronella aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant aroma.
