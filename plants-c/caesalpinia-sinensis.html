Caesalpinia sinensis

Caesalpinia sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like inflorescences that appear in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender racemes, and it produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

Caesalpinia sinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
