Carex lucorum var. emmonsii

Carex lucorum var. emmonsii, commonly known as the Emmons' sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and is often found in riparian zones.
