Ceropegia valida

Ceropegia valida, commonly known as the African violet or velvet leaf, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and thick, waxy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. The plant produces delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
