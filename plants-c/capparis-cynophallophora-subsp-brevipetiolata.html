Capparis cynophallophora subsp. brevipetiolata

Capparis cynophallophora subsp.

brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-stemmed caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in umbels.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible but have a sharp, peppery taste.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.
