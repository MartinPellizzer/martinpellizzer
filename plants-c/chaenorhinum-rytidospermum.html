Chaenorhinum rytidospermum

Chaenorhinum rytidospermum, commonly known as the goldenhead flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
