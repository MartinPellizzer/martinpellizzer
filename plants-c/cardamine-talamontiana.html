Cardamine talamontiana

Cardamine talamontiana, commonly known as the Talamont's water crowfoot, is a small perennial herb belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
