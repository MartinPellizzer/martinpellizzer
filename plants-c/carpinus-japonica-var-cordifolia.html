Carpinus japonica var. cordifolia

Carpinus japonica var. cordifolia, commonly known as the Japanese hornbeam or Japanese ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often heart-shaped at the base, giving it its varietal name "cordifolia.". The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, round fruits that are a food source for various wildlife. Its hard, dense wood is highly valued for its strength and durability in traditional crafts and construction.

The plant is also noted for its adaptability to a variety of soil types and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
