Chazaliella coffeosperma subsp. longipedicellata

Chazaliella coffeosperma subsp.

longipedicellata, commonly known as the long pedicellate chazaliella, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated pedicels, which support the inflorescences. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils.
