Capparis indochinensis

Capparis indochinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese sour apple, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub or small tree structure, with thick, often spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, sour berry that contains several seeds.

Capparis indochinensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
