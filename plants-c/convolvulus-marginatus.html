Convolvulus marginatus

Convolvulus marginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces attractive, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin, and its stems are hairy and often trailing or climbing. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are known for their fragrance and nectar-rich petals that attract pollinators.
