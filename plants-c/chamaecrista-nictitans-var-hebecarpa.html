Chamaecrista nictitans var. hebecarpa

Chamaecrista nictitans var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the red leadtree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, bright red or orange pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit includes a woody stem with a spreading or upright form, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant foliage and showy seed pods.
