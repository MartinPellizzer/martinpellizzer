Chionanthus elegans

Chionanthus elegans, commonly known as the flowering dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in late spring, often in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed.

Chionanthus elegans thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
