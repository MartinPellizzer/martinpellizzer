Calamintha clinopodium subsp. atlantica

Calamintha clinopodium subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as Atlantic mint, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the Iberian Peninsula and northwest Africa. It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a mint-like scent, and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of blue to violet.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct ecological and morphological characteristics compared to other members of the Calamintha genus.
