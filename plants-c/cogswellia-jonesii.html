Cogswellia jonesii

Cogswellia jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its columnar stems that are typically covered in white, woolly hairs, providing insulation against extreme temperatures. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and conservation efforts.
