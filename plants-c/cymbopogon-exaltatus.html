Cymbopogon exaltatus

Cymbopogon exaltatus, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its ornamental value is due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in low-maintenance conditions.

It is also used in horticulture for erosion control and as a landscape plant.
