Citrullus lanatus var. citroides

Citrullus lanatus var. citroides, commonly known as the honeydew melon, is a botanical variety within the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, spherical fruit with a smooth, pale green rind and a sweet, pale yellow to white flesh. The plant is a trailing vine that produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, sunny climates. It requires well-drained soil and regular watering for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its high water content and mild, sweet flavor, making it a popular choice for fresh consumption.
