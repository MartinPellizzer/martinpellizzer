Croton macradenis

Croton macradenis, commonly known as the large-leaf croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are typically green with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
