Cousinia komidjanensis

Cousinia komidjanensis, commonly known as the Komijan's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia komidjanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
