Cyperus compressus f. depauperatus

Cyperus compressus f. depauperatus, commonly known as the sparse flat sedge, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically shorter and more sparse compared to the typical form of Cyperus compressus. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a useful plant for stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
