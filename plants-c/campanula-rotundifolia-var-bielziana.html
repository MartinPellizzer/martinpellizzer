Campanula rotundifolia var. bielziana

Campanula rotundifolia var. bielziana, commonly known as the roundleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, rounded leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
