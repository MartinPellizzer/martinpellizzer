Clinopodium alpinum subsp. nebrodensis

Clinopodium alpinum subsp.

nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebroden alpine betony, is a perennial herb native to the Alps and surrounding mountainous regions. It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
