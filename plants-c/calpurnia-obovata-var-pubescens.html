Calpurnia obovata var. pubescens

Calpurnia obovata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy calpurnia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive appearance, and it often thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in various cultures.
