Centaurium erythraea subsp. rumelicum

Centaurium erythraea subsp.

rumelicum, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, ascending stems that bear small, bright red flowers clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and ecologically significant species in its native habitats.
