Carduus brachycephalus

Carduus brachycephalus, commonly known as the short-headed thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a spiny, spherical stem with dense, prickly leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish to brownish, and arranged in a compact head, typical of the thistle genus.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
