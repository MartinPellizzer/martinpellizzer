Carex rigida var. saxatilis

Carex rigida var. saxatilis, commonly known as the rigid sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. This variety is often found in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in spikelets.

The plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various small organisms.
