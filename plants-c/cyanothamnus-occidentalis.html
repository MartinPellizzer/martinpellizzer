Cyanothamnus occidentalis

Cyanothamnus occidentalis, commonly known as the Western bluebush, is a low-growing shrub native to western Australia.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, blue to violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has aromatic leaves and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Cyanothamnus occidentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
