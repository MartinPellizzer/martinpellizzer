Cyperus pseudobrunneus var. hilsenbergii

Cyperus pseudobrunneus var. hilsenbergii, commonly known as the Hilsenberg sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
