Crataegus laevigata subsp. microphylla

Crataegus laevigata subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy.

The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
