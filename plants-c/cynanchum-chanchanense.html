Cynanchum chanchanense

Cynanchum chanchanense, commonly known as the Chanchan wild cucumber, is a perennial climbing plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and distinctive floral structures. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits.

Its botanical significance lies in its traditional use in Andean medicine for treating various ailments, though its chemical composition remains under study.
