Crepis mollis subsp. succisifolia

Crepis mollis subsp.

succisifolia, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, forming a cluster at the top of the stem.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces numerous seeds, contributing to its widespread distribution.
