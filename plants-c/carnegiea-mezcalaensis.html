Carnegiea mezcalaensis

Carnegiea mezcalaensis, commonly known as the Mexican cardon, is a species of columnar cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has a thick, ribbed trunk with prominent areoles that bear clusters of white, woolly flowers. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Carnegiea mezcalaensis plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various animals.
