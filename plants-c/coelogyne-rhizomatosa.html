Coelogyne rhizomatosa

Coelogyne rhizomatosa, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit, with fleshy, creeping stems that allow it to spread across the forest floor. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Coelogyne rhizomatosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking floral displays.
