Cassia kirkbridei

Cassia kirkbridei, commonly known as the Kirkbride's cassia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments.

Cassia kirkbridei is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
