Callitriche austinii

Callitriche austinii, commonly known as Austin's water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams. The plant has tiny, star-shaped leaves that are often floating or partially submerged. It produces small, white flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Callitriche austinii is an important part of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
