Cirsium zarkosii

Cirsium zarkosii, commonly known as the Zarkos thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowering heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the daisy family.

Cirsium zarkosii is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.
