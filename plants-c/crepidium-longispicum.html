Crepidium longispicum

Crepidium longispicum, commonly known as the long-spurred orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, greenish-yellow flowers with long, slender sepals and petals. Its unique floral structure is adapted for specific pollinators, such as bees and moths.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
