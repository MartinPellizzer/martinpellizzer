Chrysophyllum psilophyllum

Chrysophyllum psilophyllum, commonly known as the African starfruit or yellow star-apple, is a tropical tree belonging to the Sapotaceae family.

It is native to West Africa and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically harvested when they turn a bright yellow or orange color. The fruit has a sweet, acidic flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary preparations.

It also has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
