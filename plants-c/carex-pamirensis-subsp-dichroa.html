Carex pamirensis subsp. dichroa

Carex pamirensis subsp.

dichroa, commonly known as the Pamir sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with greenish-yellow flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
