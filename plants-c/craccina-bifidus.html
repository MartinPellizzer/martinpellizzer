Craccina bifidus

Craccina bifidus, commonly known as the split-leaved craccina, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive bifid (split) leaves, which are usually narrow and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats.

Craccina bifidus is known for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic ecosystems.
