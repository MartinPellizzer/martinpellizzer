Cherleria sibirica

Cherleria sibirica, commonly known as Siberian cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, tart berries that are edible but not commonly cultivated for fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and open woodlands.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant in harsh, temperate environments.
