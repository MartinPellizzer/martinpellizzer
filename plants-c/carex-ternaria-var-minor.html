Carex ternaria var. minor

Carex ternaria var. minor, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster. Its flowering spikes are usually short and subtended by leaf-like bracts.

The botanical characteristics of Carex ternaria var. minor make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
