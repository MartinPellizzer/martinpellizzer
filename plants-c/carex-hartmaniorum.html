Carex hartmaniorum

Carex hartmaniorum, commonly known as Hartman's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters. Native to the northeastern United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant is typically perennial and forms dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
