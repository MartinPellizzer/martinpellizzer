Cyathea percussa

Cyathea percussa, commonly known as the percussion fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Cyathea percussa has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-lived component of forest ecosystems.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in New Zealand's native flora.
