Cerasus hortensis var. nigra

Cerasus hortensis var. nigra, commonly known as the black cherry or black cherry plum, is a variety of the cherry plum species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black fruits, which are typically harvested in late summer. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its edible berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy green surface and turn reddish in autumn.
