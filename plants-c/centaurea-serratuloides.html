Centaurea serratuloides

Centaurea serratuloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and bright purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also considered an invasive weed in some parts of the world.
