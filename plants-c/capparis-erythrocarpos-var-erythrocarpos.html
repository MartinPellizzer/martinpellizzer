Capparis erythrocarpos var. erythrocarpos

Capparis erythrocarpos var. erythrocarpos, commonly known as the red-fruited caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible but often tart and acidic. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky garden environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to Mediterranean landscapes.
