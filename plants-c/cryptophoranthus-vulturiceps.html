Cryptophoranthus vulturiceps

Cryptophoranthus vulturiceps, commonly known as the death's-head hawkmoth plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate floral structure that resembles a hawkmoth in both form and coloration. The plant produces nectar-rich blossoms that attract specific pollinators, primarily nocturnal moths. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat.

Due to its rarity and specialized ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its natural range.
