Coriandrum melphitense

Coriandrum melphitense, commonly known as the Melphitense coriander, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, spicy aroma similar to regular coriander.
