Chicharronia intermedia

Chicharronia intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic, containing cardiac glycosides that are used in traditional medicine.

Chicharronia intermedia is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
