Centaurea orientalis var. armata

Centaurea orientalis var. armata, commonly known as the Armenian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is a perennial herb with upright, branching stems that bear small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, composite flower heads. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in its native ecosystem.
