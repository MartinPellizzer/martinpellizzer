Carex parryana var. brevisquama

Carex parryana var. brevisquama, commonly known as the short-sheath sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typical of sedges. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and marshy areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
