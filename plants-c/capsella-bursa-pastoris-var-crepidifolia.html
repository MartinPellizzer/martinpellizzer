Capsella bursa-pastoris var. crepidifolia

Capsella bursa-pastoris var. crepidifolia, commonly known as the crested shepherd's purse, is a variant of the common shepherd's purse.

It is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods. The leaves are usually lobed and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique seed pod shape.
