Croton flavens var. mucronatus

Croton flavens var. mucronatus, commonly known as the blunt-tipped croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored with shades of yellow, red, and green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
