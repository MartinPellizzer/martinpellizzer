Carex cespitosa f. ramosa

Carex cespitosa f. ramosa, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is typically found in wet or moist environments. The plant forms compact clumps with short, rhizomatous stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
