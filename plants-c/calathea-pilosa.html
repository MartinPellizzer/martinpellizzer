Calathea pilosa

Calathea pilosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with intricate patterns and a rich green coloration. The leaves are often hairy or fuzzy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and indirect light.

Calathea pilosa is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
