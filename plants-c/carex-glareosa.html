Carex glareosa

Carex glareosa, commonly known as the shining sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex glareosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
