Cassia goniodes

Cassia goniodes, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Cassia goniodes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
