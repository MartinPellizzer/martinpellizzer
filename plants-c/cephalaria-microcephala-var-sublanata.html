Cephalaria microcephala var. sublanata

Cephalaria microcephala var. sublanata, commonly known as the woolly cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
