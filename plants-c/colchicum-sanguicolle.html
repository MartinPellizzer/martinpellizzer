Colchicum sanguicolle

Colchicum sanguicolle, commonly known as the blood-red colchicum, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces large, showy flowers in shades of red and purple. The plant belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is known for its toxic alkaloids, which have historically been used in medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often growing in woodland or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and gardeners alike.
