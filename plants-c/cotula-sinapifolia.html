Cotula sinapifolia

Cotula sinapifolia, commonly known as the hairy cotula, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and slightly aromatic, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Cotula sinapifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
