Cyperus rufus

Cyperus rufus, commonly known as red flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically flowering during the summer months. Cyperus rufus thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas and along the edges of ponds.

It is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
