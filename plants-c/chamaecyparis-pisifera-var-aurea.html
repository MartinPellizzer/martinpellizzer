Chamaecyparis pisifera var. aurea

Chamaecyparis pisifera var. aurea, commonly known as the golden cypress, is a variant of the Japanese cedar that exhibits striking golden foliage.

It is a coniferous evergreen with a pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that turn a vibrant gold in the sunlight. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of its coniferous classification. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens.

Its unique coloration and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and botanical collections.
