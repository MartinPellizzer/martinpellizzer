Carex ornithopoda var. alpina

Carex ornithopoda var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their fibrous root systems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous environments, often forming dense clumps. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces three-sided, triangular seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
