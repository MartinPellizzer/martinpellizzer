Convolvulus muricatus

Convolvulus muricatus, commonly known as the rough morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a trailing or climbing annual herb characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The rough morning glory is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners.
