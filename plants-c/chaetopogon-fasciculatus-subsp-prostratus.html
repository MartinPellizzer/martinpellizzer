Chaetopogon fasciculatus subsp. prostratus

Chaetopogon fasciculatus subsp.

prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate chaetopogon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
