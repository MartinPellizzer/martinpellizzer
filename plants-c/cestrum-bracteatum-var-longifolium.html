Cestrum bracteatum var. longifolium

Cestrum bracteatum var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white and purple. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.
