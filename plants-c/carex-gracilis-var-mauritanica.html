Carex gracilis var. mauritanica

Carex gracilis var. mauritanica, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
