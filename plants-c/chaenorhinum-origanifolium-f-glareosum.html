Chaenorhinum origanifolium f. glareosum

Chaenorhinum origanifolium f. glareosum, commonly known as the golden-leafed horned poppy, is a rare and striking variant of the horned poppy species.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its typical red or orange flowers. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is a biennial or short-lived perennial, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after addition to botanical gardens and specialized collections.
