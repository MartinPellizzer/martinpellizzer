Chaetogastra cubensis var. brevistrigillosa

Chaetogastra cubensis var. brevistrigillosa, commonly known as the Cuban hairy grass, is a perennial grass species native to Cuba.

It is characterized by its short, stiff, and hairy leaf blades, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
