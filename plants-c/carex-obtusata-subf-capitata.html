Carex obtusata subf. capitata

Carex obtusata subf.

capitata, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
