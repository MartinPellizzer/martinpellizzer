Ceropegia pumila

Ceropegia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf string of hearts, is a small, trailing succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green or yellow, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and hanging baskets.
