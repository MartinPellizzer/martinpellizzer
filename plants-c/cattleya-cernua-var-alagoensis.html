Cattleya cernua var. alagoensis

Cattleya cernua var. alagoensis, commonly known as the Alagoas cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

This variety is prized for its beauty and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
