Crassula garibina

Crassula garibina, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are often pale green with a bluish tint, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the cooler months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Crassula garibina is valued for its ornamental appeal and easy care requirements.
