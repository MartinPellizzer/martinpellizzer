Calea uniflora

Calea uniflora, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white, tubular flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has velvety, dark green leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Calea uniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
