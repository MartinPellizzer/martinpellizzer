Crataegus monogyna subsp. transalpina

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

transalpina, commonly known as the Transalpine hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring.

The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
