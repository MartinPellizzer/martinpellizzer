Cacalia chlorolepis

Cacalia chlorolepis, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and somewhat hairy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
