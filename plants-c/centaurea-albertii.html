Centaurea albertii

Centaurea albertii, commonly known as Albert's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Centaurea albertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
