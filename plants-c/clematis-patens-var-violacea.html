Clematis patens var. violacea

Clematis patens var. violacea, commonly known as the violet-flowered clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
