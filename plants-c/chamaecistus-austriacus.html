Chamaecistus austriacus

Chamaecistus austriacus, commonly known as Austrian rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Alps.

It features woody stems covered in dense, glandular hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a white, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
