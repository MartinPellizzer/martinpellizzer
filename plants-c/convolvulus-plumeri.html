Convolvulus plumeri

Convolvulus plumeri, commonly known as the yellow woodbine, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, decorative vine.
