Carex nigra var. distachya

Carex nigra var. distachya, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features dark green, strap-shaped leaves and produces spikes of small, brownish flowers in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
