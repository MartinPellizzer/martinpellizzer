Carex nitida

Carex nitida, commonly known as the glossy sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually brown or reddish in color. Carex nitida thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
