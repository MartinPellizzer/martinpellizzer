Chamaecrista desvauxii var. mollissima

Chamaecrista desvauxii var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky crownflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
