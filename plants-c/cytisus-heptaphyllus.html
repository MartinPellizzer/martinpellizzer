Cytisus heptaphyllus

Cytisus heptaphyllus, commonly known as the seven-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of seven leaflets, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
