Centaurea hellenica f. intermedia

Centaurea hellenica f. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Greek cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and ecological landscapes.
