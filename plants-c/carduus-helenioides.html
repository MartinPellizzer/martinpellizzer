Carduus helenioides

Carduus helenioides, commonly known as the European thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in harsh environments, and its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
