Cotoneaster niger subsp. ammanii

Cotoneaster niger subsp.

ammanii, commonly known as black cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that persist through winter, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are usually oval and glossy, with a dense growth habit that provides habitat for small wildlife.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
