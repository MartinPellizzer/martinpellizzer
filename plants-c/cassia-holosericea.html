Cassia holosericea

Cassia holosericea, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.

Cassia holosericea thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
