Cacalia lindheimeri

Cacalia lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
