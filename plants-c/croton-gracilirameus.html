Croton gracilirameus

Croton gracilirameus, commonly known as the slender-branched croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. Croton gracilirameus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value is due to its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
