Cercocarpus betuloides subsp. macrourus

Cercocarpus betuloides subsp.

macrourus, commonly known as the Sierra stonecress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky habitats, including slopes and canyons.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, and the fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
