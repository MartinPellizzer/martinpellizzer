Clusia criuva subsp. sellowiana

Clusia criuva subsp.

sellowiana, commonly known as the glossy abutilon, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy in appearance.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its resistance to drought.
