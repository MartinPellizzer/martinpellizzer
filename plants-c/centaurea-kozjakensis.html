Centaurea kozjakensis

Centaurea kozjakensis, commonly known as the Kozjak's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
