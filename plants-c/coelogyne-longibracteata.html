Coelogyne longibracteata

Coelogyne longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bracted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces showy, often brightly colored blooms in shades of white, yellow, and orange. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments.

Coelogyne longibracteata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
