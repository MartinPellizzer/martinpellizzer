Cyathea demissa

Cyathea demissa, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its growth in nutrient-rich soils.

Cyathea demissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
