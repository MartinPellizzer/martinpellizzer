Centaurium erythraea subsp. rhodense

Centaurium erythraea subsp.

rhodense, commonly known as red-centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers.

The flowers are typically red or reddish-purple, with five petals and prominent yellow veins. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
