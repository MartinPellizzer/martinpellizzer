Carex qingdaoensis

Carex qingdaoensis, commonly known as the Qingdao sedge, is a species of sedge native to China.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Carex qingdaoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet areas.
