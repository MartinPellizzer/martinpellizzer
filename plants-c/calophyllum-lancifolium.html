Calophyllum lancifolium

Calophyllum lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
