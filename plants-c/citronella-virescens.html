Citronella virescens

Citronella virescens, commonly known as green citronella, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is closely related to other members of the genus Cymbopogon, which includes species used for their essential oils.

Citronella virescens is primarily cultivated for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
