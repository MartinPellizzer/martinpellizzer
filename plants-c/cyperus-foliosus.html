Cyperus foliosus

Cyperus foliosus, commonly known as the flatleaf flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus foliosus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.
