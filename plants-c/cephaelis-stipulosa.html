Cephaelis stipulosa

Cephaelis stipulosa, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cephaelis stipulosa is valued for both its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.
