Capparis batianoffii

Capparis batianoffii, commonly known as the Batianoff's caper, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to capers, though less commonly used in culinary applications. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some horticultural gardens.
