Cakile maritima var. geniculata

Cakile maritima var. geniculata, commonly known as the coastal bladderwrack, is a variant of the bladderwrack species native to coastal regions.

It is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline and often wind-swept environments. The plant exhibits a succulent, leafy structure with a distinctive bladdery appearance due to its specialized air chambers. It typically grows in intertidal zones, where it withstands tidal fluctuations and high salinity.

Its morphology reflects evolutionary adaptations to coastal conditions, making it an important component of maritime ecosystems.
