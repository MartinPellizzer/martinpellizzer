Cistus × corbariensis var. australis

Cistus × corbariensis var. australis, commonly known as the Australian rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant and has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
