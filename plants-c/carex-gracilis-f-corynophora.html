Carex gracilis f. corynophora

Carex gracilis f. corynophora, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a variant of the slender sedges, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit.

It is a perennial plant native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions, often found in wetlands, streambanks, and forest understories. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Carex gracilis f. corynophora is valued in ecological restoration projects for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and support local biodiversity.
