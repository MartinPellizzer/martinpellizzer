Cirsium lanceolatum subsp. crinitum

Cirsium lanceolatum subsp.

crinitum, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, serrated texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
