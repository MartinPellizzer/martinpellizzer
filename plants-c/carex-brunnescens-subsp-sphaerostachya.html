Carex brunnescens subsp. sphaerostachya

Carex brunnescens subsp.

sphaerostachya, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces distinctive, spherical spikelets that are often golden in color.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of ponds and streams. It has narrow, cylindrical leaves that are usually a dark green color, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
