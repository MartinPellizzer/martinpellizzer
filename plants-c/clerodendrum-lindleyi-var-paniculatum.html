Clerodendrum lindleyi var. paniculatum

Clerodendrum lindleyi var. paniculatum, commonly known as the panicle clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences that produce small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
