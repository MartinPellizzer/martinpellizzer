Commicarpus decipiens

Commicarpus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are covered in hooked bristles, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
