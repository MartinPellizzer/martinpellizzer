Caladenia cristata

Caladenia cristata, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-like floral structure, which is adorned with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in open habitats such as heathlands and grasslands. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting a variety of pollinators with their nectar-rich sepals.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Caladenia cristata is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
