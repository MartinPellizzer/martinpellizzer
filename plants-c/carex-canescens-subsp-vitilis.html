Carex canescens subsp. vitilis

Carex canescens subsp.

vitilis, commonly known as the white-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to white in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
