Codariocalyx conicus

Codariocalyx conicus, commonly known as the giant flame vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is often used in ornamental gardening and can become invasive if not properly managed.
