Commelina agrostophylla

Commelina agrostophylla, commonly known as the grass-leaf morning glory, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species with striking floral displays. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers with three petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
