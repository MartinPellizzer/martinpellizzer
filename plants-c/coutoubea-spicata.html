Coutoubea spicata

Coutoubea spicata, commonly known as the spicate coutoubea, is a deciduous shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments in the wild.

Coutoubea spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
