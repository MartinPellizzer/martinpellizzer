Carum meifolium

Carum meifolium, commonly known as meadow caraway, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in herbal remedies. Native to temperate regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its aromatic leaves and seeds contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
