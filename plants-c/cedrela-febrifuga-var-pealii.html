Cedrela febrifuga var. pealii

Cedrela febrifuga var. pealii, commonly known as the Peal's yellow cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and long, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. However, due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
