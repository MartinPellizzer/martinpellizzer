Conyza altissima

Conyza altissima, commonly known as greater fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, yellow to white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Conyza altissima is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is sometimes considered a weed due to its invasive tendencies.
