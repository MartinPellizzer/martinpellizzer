Carex zizaniifolia

Carex zizaniifolia, commonly known as bluejoint grass, is a perennial sedge native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Carex zizaniifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
