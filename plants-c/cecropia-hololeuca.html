Cecropia hololeuca

Cecropia hololeuca, commonly known as the giant cecropia, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive, hollow stems that are often used by animals for shelter. The plant has a symbiotic relationship with certain ants, which protect it from herbivores in exchange for shelter and food. Its rapid growth rate makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

The plant's ability to thrive in disturbed soils contributes to its widespread distribution in tropical forests.
