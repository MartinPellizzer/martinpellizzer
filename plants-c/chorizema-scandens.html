Chorizema scandens

Chorizema scandens, commonly known as the climbing chervil, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has slender, trailing stems that can climb or trail along the ground. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, heart-shaped leaflets.

Chorizema scandens is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and fragrance.
