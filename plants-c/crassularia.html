Crassularia

Crassularia, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a genus of succulent plants belonging to the family Crassulaceae.

These plants are characterized by their fleshy, often thick leaves that store water, allowing them to thrive in arid environments. Crassularia species are typically low-growing and form dense mats, with small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. They are native to various regions in Asia, Europe, and Africa, and many species are popular in rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The genus includes over 150 species, many of which are easy to cultivate and display a wide range of ornamental features.
