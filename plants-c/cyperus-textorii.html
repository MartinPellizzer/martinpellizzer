Cyperus textorii

Cyperus textorii, commonly known as the spiny flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered with sharp spines, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cyperus textorii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
