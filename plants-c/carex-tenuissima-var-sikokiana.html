Carex tenuissima var. sikokiana

Carex tenuissima var. sikokiana, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a low-growing, clump-forming plant native to Japan.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a silvery-green color, giving it a distinct, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous brown flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in a spiky inflorescence. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
