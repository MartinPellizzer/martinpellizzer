Cissampelos auriculata

Cissampelos auriculata, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a climbing plant native to Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow, pendant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners and horticulturists.
