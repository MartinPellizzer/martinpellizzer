Celtis laevigata var. brevipes

Celtis laevigata var. brevipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boxelder, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged samaras that disperse via wind. It is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, showcasing its adaptability to varied soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
