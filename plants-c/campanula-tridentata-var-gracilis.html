Campanula tridentata var. gracilis

Campanula tridentata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and trifoliate, giving the plant its species name "tridentata.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
