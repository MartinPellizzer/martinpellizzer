Celastrus saharae

Celastrus saharae, commonly known as the Saharan staff-vine, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions with its deep root system and ability to store water. Its leaves are small and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Celastrus saharae plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
