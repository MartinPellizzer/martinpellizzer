Carex paludicola

Carex paludicola, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. This plant thrives in wetland environments, such as bogs and marshes, where it prefers consistently moist to water-saturated soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in these habitats.

Carex paludicola plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
