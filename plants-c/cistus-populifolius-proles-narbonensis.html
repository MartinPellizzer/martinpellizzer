Cistus populifolius proles narbonensis

Cistus populifolius proles narbonensis, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, where it contributes to the local flora.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
