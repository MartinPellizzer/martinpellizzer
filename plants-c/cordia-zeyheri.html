Cordia zeyheri

Cordia zeyheri, commonly known as the sour fig or wild fig, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dune environments.

Cordia zeyheri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
