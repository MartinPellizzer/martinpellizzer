Cucurbita melopepo var. aurantiiformis

Cucurbita melopepo var. aurantiiformis, commonly known as the orange gourd, is a variant of the bottle gourd species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, bottle-shaped fruits that range in color from pale yellow to vibrant orange. The plant is a vigorous climber with large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and it is valued for its use in traditional cooking and as a container.

The botanical features of this variety make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and cultural appreciation.
