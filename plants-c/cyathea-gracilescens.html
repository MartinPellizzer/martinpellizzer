Cyathea gracilescens

Cyathea gracilescens, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dimorphic, with sterile and fertile fronds differing in shape and structure.

Cyathea gracilescens plays an important ecological role in tropical forests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
