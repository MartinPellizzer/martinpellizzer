Coryphantha ancistracantha

Coryphantha ancistracantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hedgehog cactus, is a species of cacti native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in spines and small, narrow leaves that are often reduced to spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
