Curcuma kwangsiensis var. puberula

Curcuma kwangsiensis var. puberula, commonly known as the Guangxi turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with three outer petals and three inner petals that are narrower.

The rhizomes of this species are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional herbal remedies.
