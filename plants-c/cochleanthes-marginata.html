Cochleanthes marginata

Cochleanthes marginata, commonly known as the marginate orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
