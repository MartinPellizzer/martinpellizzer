Carduus medius var. broteroi

Carduus medius var. broteroi, commonly known as the Broteroi thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a single, tall, purple-tinged flower head that blooms in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
