Centaurea × moehrleniana

Centaurea × moehrleniana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bright blue or purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features serrated, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
