Carex ampullacea var. nutans

Carex ampullacea var. nutans, commonly known as the nodding sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish to brownish flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
