Campanula rapunculoides var. lunariifolia

Campanula rapunculoides var. lunariifolia, commonly known as the moonflower bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that open in the evening. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine down.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooming pattern.
