Carex ischnostachya var. subtumida

Carex ischnostachya var. subtumida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that bears small, spike-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
