Campylospermum perseifolium

Campylospermum perseifolium, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
