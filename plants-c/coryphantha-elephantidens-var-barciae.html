Coryphantha elephantidens var. barciae

Coryphantha elephantidens var. barciae, commonly known as the Barcia's ghost cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and Angola. This succulent plant features short, cylindrical stems covered in white to pale blue spines, giving it a ghostly appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late summer.

The plant is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
