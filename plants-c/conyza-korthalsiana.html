Conyza korthalsiana

Conyza korthalsiana, commonly known as the common blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It features slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces numerous tiny seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Conyza korthalsiana is also known for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
