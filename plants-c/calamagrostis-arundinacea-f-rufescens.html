Calamagrostis arundinacea f. rufescens

Calamagrostis arundinacea f. rufescens, commonly known as the red stemmed reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems that become more pronounced as the plant matures. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This ornamental grass is valued for its striking fall color and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
