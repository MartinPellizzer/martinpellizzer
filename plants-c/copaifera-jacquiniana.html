Copaifera jacquiniana

Copaifera jacquiniana, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark that exudes a resinous sap. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. The tree produces elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
