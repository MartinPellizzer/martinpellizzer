Clusia guatemalensis var. guatemalensis

Clusia guatemalensis var. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, often with a yellowish or reddish margin. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Clusia guatemalensis var. guatemalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
