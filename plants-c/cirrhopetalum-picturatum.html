Cirrhopetalum picturatum

Cirrhopetalum picturatum, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that display intricate patterns of purple, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes many species of bellflowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

Cirrhopetalum picturatum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous habitats where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
