Chuquiraga raimondiana

Chuquiraga raimondiana, commonly known as the Andean burweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or arid soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
