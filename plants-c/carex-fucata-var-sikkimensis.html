Carex fucata var. sikkimensis

Carex fucata var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream sides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the alpine and subalpine ecosystems in its native range.
