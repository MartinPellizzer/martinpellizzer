Campylotropis macrostyla var. griffithii

Campylotropis macrostyla var. griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's broad-leaved crotalaria, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a bushy growth habit. Its seeds are encased in a distinctive pod, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
