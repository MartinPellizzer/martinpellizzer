Cephalaria mauritanica

Cephalaria mauritanica, commonly known as the Moroccan goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cephalaria mauritanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
