Cyathea madagascariensis

Cyathea madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive root system that helps it anchor in rocky or forested soils. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cyathea madagascariensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
