Calamagrostis carchiensis

Calamagrostis carchiensis, commonly known as the Carchian grass, is a species of grass native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, silver-green foliage that reflects the harsh mountain environment. This plant thrives in cold, arid climates and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its growth pattern is adapted to withstand strong winds and limited soil nutrients.

Calamagrostis carchiensis plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystem and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
