Chamaefistula acuminata

Chamaefistula acuminata, commonly known as the narrowleaf boxwood, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, compact foliage with dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are typically 1 to 2 inches long. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its slow growth rate and ability to tolerate pruning make it a popular choice for hedges and topiaries.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their astringent properties.
