Cyperus aggregatus

Cyperus aggregatus, commonly known as swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Cyperus aggregatus thrives in moist to aquatic environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ability to tolerate waterlogged soils makes it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
