Cranichis asplundii

Cranichis asplundii, commonly known as the Asplund's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the high altitudes of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
