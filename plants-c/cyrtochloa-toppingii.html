Cyrtochloa toppingii

Cyrtochloa toppingii, commonly known as the Topping's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in humid, tropical conditions.

Cyrtochloa toppingii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
