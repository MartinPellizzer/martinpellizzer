Calamagrostis bicolor

Calamagrostis bicolor, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and striking purple flower spikes that appear in late summer. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes, particularly in water feature areas.

Calamagrostis bicolor plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
