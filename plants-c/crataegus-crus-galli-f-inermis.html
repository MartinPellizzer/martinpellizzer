Crataegus crus-galli f. inermis

Crataegus crus-galli f. inermis, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, thornless stems and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
