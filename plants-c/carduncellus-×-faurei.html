Carduncellus × faurei

Carduncellus × faurei, commonly known as the hybrid cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and large, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically in cooking and medicine.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in its foliage and flowering patterns.
