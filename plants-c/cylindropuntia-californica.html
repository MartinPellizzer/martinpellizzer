Cylindropuntia californica

Cylindropuntia californica, commonly known as the California prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines and are typically green in color. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a succulent structure that allows it to store water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, juicy interior.
