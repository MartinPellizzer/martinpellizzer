Cyperus flavescens var. piceus

Cyperus flavescens var. piceus, commonly known as black flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, often with a pale or brownish hue.

Cyperus flavescens var. piceus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
