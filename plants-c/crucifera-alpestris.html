Crucifera alpestris

Crucifera alpestris, commonly known as the alpine crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Crucifera alpestris is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
