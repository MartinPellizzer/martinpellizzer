Crataegus tinctoria

Crataegus tinctoria, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, elliptical leaves, and showy white to pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries in the fall, which are edible but have a tart flavor. Crataegus tinctoria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in dyeing fabrics.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
