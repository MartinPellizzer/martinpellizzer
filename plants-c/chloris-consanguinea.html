Chloris consanguinea

Chloris consanguinea, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland margins. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its fine texture and color.

Chloris consanguinea is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
