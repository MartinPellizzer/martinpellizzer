Clusia guabalensis

Clusia guabalensis, commonly known as the Guabalense Glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae, also known as the Croton family, and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that are often followed by red or orange berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

Clusia guabalensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
