Cochlearia × briggsii

Cochlearia × briggsii, commonly known as the hybrid sea kale, is a perennial flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and distinctive leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or shingle beaches, where it tolerates salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are fleshy and often covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss.

Cochlearia × briggsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in coastal ecosystems.
