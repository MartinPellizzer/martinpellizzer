Conospermum caeruleum subsp. marginatum

Conospermum caeruleum subsp.

marginatum, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, blue to purple flower spikes that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive white or pale margin.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal gardens.
