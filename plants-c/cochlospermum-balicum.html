Cochlospermum balicum

Cochlospermum balicum, commonly known as the Philippine flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange floral bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cochlospermum balicum is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in traditional medicine.
