Centaurea scabiosa var. alpina

Centaurea scabiosa var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine cudweed, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its bracts are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Centaurea scabiosa var. alpina is often used in ornamental gardening for its distinctive appearance and hardiness.
