Calla picta

Calla picta, commonly known as the painted arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive, arrow-shaped leaf that is often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces a large, spadix surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe that comes in various colors, including red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The foliage and inflorescence contribute to its popularity in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
