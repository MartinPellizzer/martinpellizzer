Calamagrostis macilenta

Calamagrostis macilenta, commonly known as slender reedgrass, is a grass species native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in moist or wet soil, often in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds and streams. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are clustered in panicles.

Calamagrostis macilenta plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland wildlife.
