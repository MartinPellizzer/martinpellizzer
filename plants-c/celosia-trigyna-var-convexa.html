Celosia trigyna var. convexa

Celosia trigyna var. convexa, commonly known as the plume cockscomb, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, feathery inflorescences that resemble a plume or a comb, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers come in a variety of vibrant colors, including red, pink, and orange.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
