Croton yerbalium var. hirsutus

Croton yerbalium var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The hairy croton is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
