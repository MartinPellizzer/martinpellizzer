Cirsium laeve

Cirsium laeve, commonly known as the smooth thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the stems are usually smooth, giving it its common name.

Cirsium laeve is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flowers.
