Carduus × grenieri nothosubsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the Alpine Canada thistle. This plant is a hybrid species within the genus Carduus, characterized by its robust, spiny foliage and yellow composite flowers.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. The leaves are often lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the plant produces a tall, erect stem.

It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous areas of Europe and parts of North America.
