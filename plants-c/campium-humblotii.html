Campium humblotii

Campium humblotii, commonly known as the dwarf silverbush, is a small shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Campium humblotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
