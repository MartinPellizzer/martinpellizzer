Cereus speciosissimus var. curtisii

Cereus speciosissimus var. curtisii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a popular epiphytic cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its segmented, branching stems that produce vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the holiday season. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is known for its ability to bloom reliably when provided with proper care, including cool temperatures and reduced watering during the winter months.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a favorite among houseplant enthusiasts.
