Cogswellia laevigata

Cogswellia laevigata, commonly known as the red-tailed palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, single trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into dark blue, fleshy fruits. Its slow growth rate and unique aesthetic make it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Cogswellia laevigata is well-suited to bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to thrive.
