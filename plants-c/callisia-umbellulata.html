Callisia umbellulata

Callisia umbellulata, commonly known as the silver fan plant, is a tropical perennial belonging to the Asphodelaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. Native to Central and South America, it thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
