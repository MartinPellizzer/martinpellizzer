Calandrinia discolor

Calandrinia discolor, commonly known as the pink fairy flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small, annual herb with distinctive pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and adaptability have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical studies.
