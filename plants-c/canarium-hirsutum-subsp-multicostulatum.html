Canarium hirsutum subsp. multicostulatum

Canarium hirsutum subsp.

multicostulatum, commonly known as the hairy candlenut. It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

The plant is characterized by its hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
