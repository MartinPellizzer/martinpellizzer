Clematis ochotensis

Clematis ochotensis, commonly known as the Siberian Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated edge. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its vigorous growth habit requires support, such as trellises or fences, to flourish effectively.
