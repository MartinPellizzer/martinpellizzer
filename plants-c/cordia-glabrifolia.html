Cordia glabrifolia

Cordia glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often smooth and glossy. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in terminal panicles, producing a distinctive aromatic scent.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
