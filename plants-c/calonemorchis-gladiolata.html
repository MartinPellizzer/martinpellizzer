Calonemorchis gladiolata

Calonemorchis gladiolata, commonly known as the gladiolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with vibrant blooms in shades of red, yellow, orange, and white. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to store nutrients and water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions.

Calonemorchis gladiolata is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and long-lasting flowers.
