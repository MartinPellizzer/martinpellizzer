Citrosma guianensis var. divergentifolia

Citrosma guianensis var. divergentifolia, commonly known as the Guianese citrosma, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval-shaped fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
