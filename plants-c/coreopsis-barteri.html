Coreopsis barteri

Coreopsis barteri, commonly known as Barter's coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. The plant features bright yellow flowers with ray florets and a central disk, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Barter's coreopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
