Carduncellus vulgaris

Carduncellus vulgaris, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture and are covered in prickly hairs.

The plant is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
