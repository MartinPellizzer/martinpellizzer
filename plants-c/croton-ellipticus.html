Croton ellipticus

Croton ellipticus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, often glossy leaves that vary in color from green to reddish hues. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

Croton ellipticus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
