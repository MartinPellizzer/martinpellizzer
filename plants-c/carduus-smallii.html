Carduus smallii

Carduus smallii, commonly known as the Small's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive lobed shape, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Carduus smallii is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects.
