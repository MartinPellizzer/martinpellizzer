Campylotropis pauciflora

Campylotropis pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered bent legume, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its fruits are legumes that contain several small, dark seeds.

Campylotropis pauciflora is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
