Crataegus melanocarpa var. heterophylla

Crataegus melanocarpa var. heterophylla, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, ovate leaves that vary in shape, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red, persistent fruits that provide food for wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
