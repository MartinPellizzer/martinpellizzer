Calycogonium domatiatum

Calycogonium domatiatum, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, green pods that resemble small globes, which are adapted to retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding visual interest to its harsh habitat. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, further aiding in water conservation.

Calycogonium domatiatum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
