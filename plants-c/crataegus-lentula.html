Crataegus lentula

Crataegus lentula, commonly known as the small hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, featuring thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include small, five-lobed leaves and a woody, often multi-stemmed structure.

Crataegus lentula is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
