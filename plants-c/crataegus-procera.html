Crataegus procera

Crataegus procera, commonly known as the tall hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
