Camissonia boothii subsp. decorticans

Camissonia boothii subsp.

decorticans, commonly known as the decorticate camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Camissoniaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its decorticated (peeled) leaves, which are often reduced to a thin layer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland habitats.

This plant is adapted to dry environments and is typically found in open, sunny areas.
