Caesalpinia arborea

Caesalpinia arborea, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
