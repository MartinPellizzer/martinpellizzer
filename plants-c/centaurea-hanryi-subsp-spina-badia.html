Centaurea hanryi subsp. spina-badia

Centaurea hanryi subsp.

spina-badia, commonly known as the Badia thorned centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
