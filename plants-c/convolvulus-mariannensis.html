Convolvulus mariannensis

Convolvulus mariannensis, commonly known as the Mariana bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along coastal areas and in disturbed soils. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants known for their climbing or trailing growth patterns.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
