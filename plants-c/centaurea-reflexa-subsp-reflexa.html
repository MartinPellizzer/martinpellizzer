Centaurea reflexa subsp. reflexa

Centaurea reflexa subsp.

reflexa, commonly known as the slender cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and often hairy, while the stems are slender and branching.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
