Coussapoa cornifolia

Coussapoa cornifolia, commonly known as the horned fern, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, branched fronds that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spore-bearing structures. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
