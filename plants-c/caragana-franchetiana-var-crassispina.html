Caragana franchetiana var. crassispina

Caragana franchetiana var. crassispina, commonly known as the thick-leaved shrub willow, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and compound leaves that are broader and more robust than those of other Caragana species. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles during the spring, followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and rocky soils.

Its hardy nature makes it suitable for erosion control and as a ornamental plant in suitable climates.
