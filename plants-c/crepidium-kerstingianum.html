Crepidium kerstingianum

Crepidium kerstingianum, commonly known as the Kersting's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a sympodial growth habit. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces a single, erect stem bearing several flowering spikes.

The flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinct lip, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
