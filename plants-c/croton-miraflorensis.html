Croton miraflorensis

Croton miraflorensis, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that come in various shades of green, red, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by unique, spiky fruit capsules.

Croton miraflorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
