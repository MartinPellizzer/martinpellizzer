Cacalia chamaedrys

Cacalia chamaedrys, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

Cacalia chamaedrys is well adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.
