Cotoneaster thimphuensis

Cotoneaster thimphuensis, commonly known as the Bhutanese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayan region of Bhutan.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in white or pink hues, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a notable species in Bhutan's flora.
