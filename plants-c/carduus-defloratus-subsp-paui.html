Carduus defloratus subsp. paui

Carduus defloratus subsp.

paui, commonly known as the pale carduus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The subspecies paui is distinguished by its pale coloration and slightly different morphology compared to other subspecies of Carduus defloratus.
