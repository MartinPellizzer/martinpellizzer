Colchicum autumnale var. major

Colchicum autumnale var. major, commonly known as the Meadow Saffron, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features large, waxy, purple or reddish-purple flowers that are typically borne on tall, slender stems. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often appear after the flowers have faded.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals if ingested.
