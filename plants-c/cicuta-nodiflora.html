Cicuta nodiflora

Cicuta nodiflora, commonly known as water hemlock, is a highly toxic member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white, umbrella-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas and is often found near water bodies. It contains potent toxic compounds, including cicutoxin, which can cause severe poisoning in humans and animals.

Due to its toxicity, it is considered one of the most dangerous plants in North America.
