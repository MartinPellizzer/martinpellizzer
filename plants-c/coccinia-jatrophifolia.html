Coccinia jatrophifolia

Coccinia jatrophifolia, commonly known as the African eggplant or wild cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash. The plant produces small, green, oval-shaped fruits that resemble eggs, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
