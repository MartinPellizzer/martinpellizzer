Cynanchum baronii

Cynanchum baronii, commonly known as the baron's milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing numerous small, hairy seeds. It thrives in moist, shady habitats and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
