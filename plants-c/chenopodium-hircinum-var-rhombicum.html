Chenopodium hircinum var. rhombicum

Chenopodium hircinum var. rhombicum, commonly known as red goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, rhombic-shaped leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates.

The species is also known for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
