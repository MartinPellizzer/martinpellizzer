Coluteastrum procumbens

Coluteastrum procumbens, commonly known as creeping bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant found in wetlands and shallow water bodies.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender, branching stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are slender and often have a bladdery structure, which may aid in buoyancy.

It is a member of the Lentibulariaceae family, known for its carnivorous tendencies in some species.
