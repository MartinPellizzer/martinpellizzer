Carduus auriculatus

Carduus auriculatus, commonly known as the auriculate thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flower heads are surrounded by a series of bracts.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
