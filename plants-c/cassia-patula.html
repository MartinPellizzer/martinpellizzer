Cassia patula

Cassia patula, commonly known as the slender cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cassia patula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
