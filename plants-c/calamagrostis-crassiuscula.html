Calamagrostis crassiuscula

Calamagrostis crassiuscula, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

Calamagrostis crassiuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
