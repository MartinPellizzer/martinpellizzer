Cardamine pulchella

Cardamine pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful watercress, is a delicate annual herbaceous plant native to moist environments in North America.

It typically grows in shaded, wet habitats such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features slender, branching stems with alternate, trifoliate leaves that are often glossy and deeply lobed. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
