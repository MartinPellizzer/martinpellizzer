Crepis bifida

Crepis bifida, commonly known as the divided hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, with a distinctive pappus of bristles for wind dispersal. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy habitats and disturbed soils.

Crepis bifida is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
