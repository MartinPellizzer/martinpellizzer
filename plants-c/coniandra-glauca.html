Coniandra glauca

Coniandra glauca, commonly known as the blue coniandra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Coniandra glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
