Camellia rosmannii

Camellia rosmannii, commonly known as the Rosmann's camellia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in Yunnan Province.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and numerous stamens, making it a visually striking ornamental plant.

Camellia rosmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
