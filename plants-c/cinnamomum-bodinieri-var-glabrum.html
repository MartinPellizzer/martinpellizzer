Cinnamomum bodinieri var. glabrum

Cinnamomum bodinieri var. glabrum, commonly known as the glabrous cinnamon tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Vietnam and Cambodia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forest environments. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by reddish-brown berries containing kidney-shaped seeds.

This variety is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
