Crepidium arachnoideum subsp. arachnoideum

Crepidium arachnoideum subsp.

arachnoideum, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the humid tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate inflorescence that resembles a spider's web.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are small and often pale green or white, with a delicate, thread-like appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
