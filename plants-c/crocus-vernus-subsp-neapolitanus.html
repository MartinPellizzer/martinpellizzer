Crocus vernus subsp. neapolitanus

Crocus vernus subsp.

neapolitanus, commonly known as the Neapolitan crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Iris family.
