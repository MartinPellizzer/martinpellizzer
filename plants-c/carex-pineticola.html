Carex pineticola

Carex pineticola, commonly known as the mountain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or alpine environments. Carex pineticola produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
