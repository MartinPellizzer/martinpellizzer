Clerodendrum grandiflorum subsp. cajalbanense

Clerodendrum grandiflorum subsp.

cajalbanense, commonly known as the Jamaican glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica. It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, tubular flowers surrounded by large, showy bracts.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a rough texture with a serrated margin.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
