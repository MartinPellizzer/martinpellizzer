Cyathea alsophilum

Cyathea alsophilum, commonly known as the Alsop's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered with persistent, fibrous roots. The plant has large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea alsophilum plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
