Calligonum litwinowii

Calligonum litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinow's tamarisk, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. This plant is well adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates. It produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate poor soil conditions.
