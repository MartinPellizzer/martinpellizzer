Cereus squarrosus

Cereus squarrosus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its leaves are reduced to small spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Cereus squarrosus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
