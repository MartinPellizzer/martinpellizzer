Crepis occidentalis var. costata

Crepis occidentalis var. costata, commonly known as the western oatgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system consists of rhizomes that help it establish in various soil types, including rocky and disturbed areas.

The species is often found in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
