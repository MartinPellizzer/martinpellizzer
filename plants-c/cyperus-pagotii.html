Cyperus pagotii

Cyperus pagotii, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically clustered at the ends of its stems. Cyperus pagotii thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along waterways.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive, fountain-like growth habit.
