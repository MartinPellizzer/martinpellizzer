Camellia brevistyla

Camellia brevistyla, commonly known as the short-styled camellia, is a species native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The flowers are typically pink to red in color and have a distinctive short style, giving the plant its scientific name. Camellia brevistyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.
