Cistus pilulifer

Cistus pilulifer, commonly known as the pillwort rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, evergreen leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, pill-like seed pods. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
