Calanthe hamata

Calanthe hamata, commonly known as the spurred calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features a single, upright stem that bears several flowers, each with a distinctive spur at the base of the lip. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calanthe hamata thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated for its beauty and unique floral structure.
