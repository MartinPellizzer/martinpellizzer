Courbonia decumbens

Courbonia decumbens, commonly known as the Florida holly or swamp holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and along streams, adapting to moist, acidic soils. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its flowers are inconspicuous, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, while its berries are red and attractive to wildlife.

Courbonia decumbens plays an important ecological role in supporting local fauna and stabilizing wetland environments.
