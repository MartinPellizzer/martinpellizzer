Corylus thunbergii

Corylus thunbergii, commonly known as the Chinese hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its serrated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish male flowers. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a prickly husk, which ripen in the autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Corylus thunbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
