Croton scouleri var. genuinus

Croton scouleri var. genuinus, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a variant of the Croton genus native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a waxy texture and a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique appearance.
