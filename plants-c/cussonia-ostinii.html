Cussonia ostinii

Cussonia ostinii, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white spadices surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the arum family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is also valued for its edible roots, which are used in traditional cuisines.
