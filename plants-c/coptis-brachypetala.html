Coptis brachypetala

Coptis brachypetala, commonly known as the short-petalled goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces smooth, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or alpine environments.

Coptis brachypetala is noted for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic alkaloids that require careful handling.
