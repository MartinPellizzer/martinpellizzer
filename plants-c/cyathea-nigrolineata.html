Cyathea nigrolineata

Cyathea nigrolineata, commonly known as the black-line tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive black or dark brown midrib running down the center of its fronds, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cyathea nigrolineata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
