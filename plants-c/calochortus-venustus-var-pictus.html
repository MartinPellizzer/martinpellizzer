Calochortus venustus var. pictus

Calochortus venustus var. pictus, commonly known as the painted glory-of-the-snow, is a striking bulbous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with intricate patterns resembling brushstrokes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a cut flower.

Calochortus venustus var. pictus is known for its resilience and ability to bloom reliably in suitable climates.
