Centaurea haynaldiiformis

Centaurea haynaldiiformis, commonly known as the Haynald's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a rough texture, and its flowers are yellow with purple or brown ray florets.

Centaurea haynaldiiformis is often found in disturbed areas and can be an indicator of arid or semi-arid environments.
