Caragana boisii

Caragana boisii, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for many years.

Caragana boisii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in arid regions.
