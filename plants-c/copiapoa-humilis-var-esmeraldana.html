Copiapoa humilis var. esmeraldana

Copiapoa humilis var. esmeraldana, commonly known as the Emerald Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stems that are covered in dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
