Cymbopogon giganteus var. densiflorus

Cymbopogon giganteus var. densiflorus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fragrant foliage that emits a strong lemon scent. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense inflorescences. Its essential oils, rich in citral, are widely used in culinary, aromatic, and medicinal applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and versatility in various industries.
