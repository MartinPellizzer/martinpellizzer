Cyathea obtecta

Cyathea obtecta, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive shield-like appearance on the underside.

Cyathea obtecta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
