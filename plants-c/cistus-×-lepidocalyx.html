Cistus × lepidocalyx

Cistus × lepidocalyx, commonly known as the hybrid rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and its flowers have a distinctive, citrus-like scent.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
