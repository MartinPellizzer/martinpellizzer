Conyza purpurea

Conyza purpurea, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it can grow up to 2 to 3 feet tall.

Conyza purpurea is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
