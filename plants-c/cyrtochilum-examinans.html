Cyrtochilum examinans

Cyrtochilum examinans, commonly known as the examined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically features a sympodial growth pattern, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink, depending on the specific variety.

Cyrtochilum examinans thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
