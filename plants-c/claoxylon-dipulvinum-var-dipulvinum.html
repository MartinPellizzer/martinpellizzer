Claoxylon dipulvinum var. dipulvinum

Claoxylon dipulvinum var. dipulvinum, commonly known as the broad-leafed claoxylon, is a tropical tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
