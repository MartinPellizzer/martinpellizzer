Carex eriophylla

Carex eriophylla, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils, often in disturbed or open habitats such as sandy soils or rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system that helps it retain moisture in dry environments.

Carex eriophylla is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
