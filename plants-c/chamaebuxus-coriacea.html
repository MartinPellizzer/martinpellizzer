Chamaebuxus coriacea

Chamaebuxus coriacea, commonly known as the African leatherleaf, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are thick and waxy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
