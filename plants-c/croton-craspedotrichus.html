Croton craspedotrichus

Croton craspedotrichus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and striking, brightly colored leaves that range from green to red. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its unique appearance.
