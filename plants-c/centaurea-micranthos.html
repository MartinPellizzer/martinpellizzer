Centaurea micranthos

Centaurea micranthos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has slender, hairy stems with narrow, linear leaves. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea micranthos is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.
