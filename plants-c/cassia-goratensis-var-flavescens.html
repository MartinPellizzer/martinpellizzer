Cassia goratensis var. flavescens

Cassia goratensis var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The golden cassia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
