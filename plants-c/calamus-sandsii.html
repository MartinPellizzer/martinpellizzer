Calamus sandsii

Calamus sandsii, commonly known as the sand calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araucariaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in lowland forests.

Due to habitat loss, Calamus sandsii is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
