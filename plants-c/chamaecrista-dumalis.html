Chamaecrista dumalis

Chamaecrista dumalis, commonly known as the desert false acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments with deep root systems and a slow growth rate.

Chamaecrista dumalis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
