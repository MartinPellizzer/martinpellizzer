Convolvulus essequebensis

Convolvulus essequebensis, commonly known as the Cape night-flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple. The plant produces small, dry fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The Cape night-flower is known for its nocturnal blooming habit, attracting moths and other night-active pollinators.
