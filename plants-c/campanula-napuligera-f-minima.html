Campanula napuligera f. minima

Campanula napuligera f. minima, commonly known as the miniature bellflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the alpine regions of southern Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, typical of many alpine species. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its delicate appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
