Cyperus esculentus f. angustispicatus

Cyperus esculentus f. angustispicatus, commonly known as narrow-spike sedges, is a variant of the common sedge.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in humid conditions. Its botanical features include a robust rhizome system and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
