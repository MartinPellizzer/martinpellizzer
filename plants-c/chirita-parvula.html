Chirita parvula

Chirita parvula, commonly known as the small-leaved chirita, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds.
