Conyza obliqua

Conyza obliqua, commonly known as Common Cocklebur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Conyza obliqua is known for its potential as a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
