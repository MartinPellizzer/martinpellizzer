Crepis hyemalis subsp. hackelii

Crepis hyemalis subsp.

hackelii, commonly known as the winter crepis, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, dandelion-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. It is perennial, with a fibrous root system that helps it survive harsh winter conditions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
