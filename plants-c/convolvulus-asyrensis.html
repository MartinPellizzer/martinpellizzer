Convolvulus asyrensis

Convolvulus asyrensis, commonly known as the desert bindweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring and early summer. Its fibrous roots help it survive in poor soil conditions, and it is often found growing along rocky outcrops or in sandy soils.

Convolvulus asyrensis is a hardy species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems.
