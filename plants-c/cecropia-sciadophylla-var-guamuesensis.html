Cecropia sciadophylla var. guamuesensis

Cecropia sciadophylla var. guamuesensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan ceiba, is a member of the Malvaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a fast-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters, characterized by its buttressed roots and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces showy, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
