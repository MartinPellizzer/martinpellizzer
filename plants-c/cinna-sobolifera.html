Cinna sobolifera

Cinna sobolifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

Cinna sobolifera is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
