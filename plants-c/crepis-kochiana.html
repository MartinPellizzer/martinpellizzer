Crepis kochiana

Crepis kochiana, commonly known as the Koch's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant has fibrous roots and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically visited by a variety of pollinators.
