Celtis solenostigma

Celtis solenostigma, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by small, round berries that turn dark purple when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained areas, making it adaptable to different environmental conditions.

Celtis solenostigma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
