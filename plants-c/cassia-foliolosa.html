Cassia foliolosa

Cassia foliolosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often narrow and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. Cassia foliolosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the legume family, Fabaceae.
