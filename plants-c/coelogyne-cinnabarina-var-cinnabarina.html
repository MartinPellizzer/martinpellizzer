Coelogyne cinnabarina var. cinnabarina

Coelogyne cinnabarina var. cinnabarina, commonly known as the Cinnabar Orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in conditions with seasonal dry periods.

The flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a sought-after ornamental plant in horticulture.
