Cyperus Compressus Var. Pectiniformis

Cyperus compressus var. pectiniformis is a lesser-known member of the sedge family, Cyperaceae, known for its unique and striking appearance. This variant is distinguished by its dense, grass-like foliage and a subtle, almost feathery texture that sets it apart from its more common relatives. Native to regions with moist, sandy soils, it thrives in coastal and wetland environments. Despite its ornamental appeal, it remains underappreciated in horticulture due to its limited availability and specialized growing conditions. Its rarity and beauty make it a fascinating subject for plant enthusiasts and botanists alike.
