Cyanospermum

Cyanospermum, commonly known as the blue spirillum, is a genus of cyanobacteria that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

These microorganisms are capable of photosynthesis and are often found in aquatic environments. They possess a spiral-shaped morphology, which distinguishes them from other cyanobacterial species. Cyanospermum is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to nutrient cycling in ecosystems.

Their simple cellular structure and ancient evolutionary lineage make them important subjects in the study of early life forms.
