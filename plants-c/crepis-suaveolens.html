Crepis suaveolens

Crepis suaveolens, commonly known as sweet hairy grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. The species is noted for its aromatic leaves, which release a sweet scent when crushed.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
