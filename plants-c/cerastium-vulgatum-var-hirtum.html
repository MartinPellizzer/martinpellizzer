Cerastium vulgatum var. hirtum

Cerastium vulgatum var. hirtum, commonly known as hairy mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers with five petals and hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are spoon-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cool, damp conditions.
