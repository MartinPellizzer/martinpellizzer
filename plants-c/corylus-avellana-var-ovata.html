Corylus avellana var. ovata

Corylus avellana var. ovata, commonly known as the oval hornbeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The plant produces small, spiky husks that enclose edible nuts, which are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
