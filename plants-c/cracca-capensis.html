Cracca capensis

Cracca capensis, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are encased in a spiny, fleshy calyx. These berries are edible when ripe and have a tart, tangy flavor.

Cracca capensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and mountainous areas.
