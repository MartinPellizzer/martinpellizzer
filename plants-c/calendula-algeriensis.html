Calendula algeriensis

Calendula algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian marigold, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to North Africa, particularly Algeria, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features bright yellow to orange flowers with prominent central disks, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It is often found growing in rocky soils and along dry riverbeds.

Calendula algeriensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
