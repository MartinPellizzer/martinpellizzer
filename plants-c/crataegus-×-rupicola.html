Crataegus × rupicola

Crataegus × rupicola, commonly known as the rock hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.
