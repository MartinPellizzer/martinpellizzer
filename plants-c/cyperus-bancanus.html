Cyperus bancanus

Cyperus bancanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its slender, grass-like leaves are often used in traditional basketry and papermaking.

Cyperus bancanus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.
