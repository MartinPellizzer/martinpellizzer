Cassia tetraphylla var. colombiana

Cassia tetraphylla var. colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
