Carex caflischii

Carex caflischii, commonly known as the Caflisch sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine environments. Its small, clustered flowers appear in late summer, and it is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Carex caflischii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in its native range.
