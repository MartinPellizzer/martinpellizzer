Codonopsis clematidea var. obtusa

Codonopsis clematidea var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved monkshood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that are typically broader than those of the typical species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
