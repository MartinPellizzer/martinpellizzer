Carex morrowii var. temnolepis

Carex morrowii var. temnolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial sedge native to North America.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and dark green, with a distinctive appearance that resembles grass or rushes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
