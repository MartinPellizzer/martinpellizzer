Cistus × sahucii

Cistus × sahucii, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which is part of the family Ericaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with hot, dry summers. It produces small, woody fruits called capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Cistus × sahucii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
