Coelogyne leucantha var. heterophylla

Coelogyne leucantha var. heterophylla, commonly known as the White-lipped Orchid, is a striking orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and large, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees, thriving in humid, tropical climates. Its flowers are often borne in clusters and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.

The varietal form, heterophylla, is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral characteristics.
