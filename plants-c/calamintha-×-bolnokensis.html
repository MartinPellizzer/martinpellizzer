Calamintha × bolnokensis

Calamintha × bolnokensis, commonly known as the Siberian germander, is a hybrid shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is often found in rocky or dry soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its essential oils contain compounds like thymol and carvacrol, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Calamintha × bolnokensis is valued in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
