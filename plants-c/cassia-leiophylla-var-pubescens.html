Cassia leiophylla var. pubescens

Cassia leiophylla var. pubescens, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a dense covering of fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, fluffy flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, flat pods containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its unique foliage and delicate floral display make it a desirable addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
