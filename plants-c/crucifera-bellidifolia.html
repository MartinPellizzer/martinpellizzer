Crucifera bellidifolia

Crucifera bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellidifoliate crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on slender stems. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly lobed or serrated margin. This plant is native to certain regions of the Mediterranean and thrives in well-drained soils.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
