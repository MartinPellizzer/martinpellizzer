Cerastium kunthii

Cerastium kunthii, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early summer. Its botanical structure is well adapted to withstand cold temperatures and high-altitude conditions.
