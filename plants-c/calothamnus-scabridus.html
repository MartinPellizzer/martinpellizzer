Calothamnus scabridus

Calothamnus scabridus, commonly known as the rough everlasting or rough calothamnus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, red or pink flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in a rough, scaly texture, giving it its common name.

Calothamnus scabridus is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in coastal or inland regions.
