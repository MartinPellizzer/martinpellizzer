Cytisus bracteolatus

Cytisus bracteolatus, commonly known as the bracteolate broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, woody stems with small, trifoliate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
