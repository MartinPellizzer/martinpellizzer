Croton hancei

Croton hancei, commonly known as the Hance's croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically large, with a vibrant green to reddish hue, and often have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
