Carex spadiceovaginata

Carex spadiceovaginata, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear yellowish-brown flowers, typical of many sedges. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex spadiceovaginata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
