Commiphora bricchettii

Commiphora bricchettii, commonly known as the Bicchetti's myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine. The plant features thick, leathery leaves and produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in the flora of Socotra.
