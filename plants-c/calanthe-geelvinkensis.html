Calanthe geelvinkensis

Calanthe geelvinkensis, commonly known as the Geelvink's calanthe, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Indonesian island of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a combination of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy texture, and it prefers shaded, humid environments.

Calanthe geelvinkensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
