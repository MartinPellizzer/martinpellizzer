Corethrogyne californica var. obovata

Corethrogyne californica var. obovata, commonly known as the California corethrogyne, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features narrow, obovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to the Mediterranean climate of its native range.
