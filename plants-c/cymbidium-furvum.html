Cymbidium furvum

Cymbidium furvum, commonly known as the golden cymbidium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers with dark red or purple markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and often covered in a waxy coating.

Cymbidium furvum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
