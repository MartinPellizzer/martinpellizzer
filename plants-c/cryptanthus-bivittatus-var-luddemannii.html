Cryptanthus bivittatus var. luddemannii

Cryptanthus bivittatus var. luddemannii, commonly known as the Luddemann's candy cane plant, is a strikingly ornamental species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, striped foliage that resembles a candy cane. The plant features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically bright red, pink, or orange, with contrasting white or yellow stripes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
