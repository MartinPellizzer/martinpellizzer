Croton microstachyus

Croton microstachyus, commonly known as the small-stalked croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to shades of red and yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Croton microstachyus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
