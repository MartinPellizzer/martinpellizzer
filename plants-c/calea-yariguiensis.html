Calea yariguiensis

Calea yariguiensis, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescences that open at night. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that emit a fragrant scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Calea yariguiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological significance in supporting night-active insects.
