Cereus dasyacanthus

Cereus dasyacanthus, commonly known as the giant night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

The species is often cultivated for its striking appearance and unique blooming behavior.
