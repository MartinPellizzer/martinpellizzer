Cassia trachypus

Cassia trachypus, commonly known as the Indian laburnum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and serrated.

Cassia trachypus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
