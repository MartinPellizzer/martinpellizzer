Chamaedorea integrifolia

Chamaedorea integrifolia, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen or bamboo palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America.

It features tall, slender stems with arching, feather-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue berries, though it is more valued for its ornamental foliage than its fruit.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its air-purifying qualities and elegant appearance.
