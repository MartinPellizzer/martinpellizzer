Cerasus pumila canadensis

Cerasus pumila canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian dwarf cherry, is a small deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces edible, red or pink berries that are typically consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
