Cajanus trinervius

Cajanus trinervius, commonly known as the three-nerved cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. Cajanus trinervius is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed.

It also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
