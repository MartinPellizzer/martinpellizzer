Clinopodium sirnakense

Clinopodium sirnakense, commonly known as Armenian oregano, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of eastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its leaves are small, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls and range in color from white to pale purple.

Clinopodium sirnakense is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic effects.
