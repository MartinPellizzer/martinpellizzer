Calathea regalis

Calathea regalis, commonly known as the royal calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, patterned leaves that exhibit a range of colors, including deep green, silver, and red. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its foliage is often described as having a metallic sheen, which enhances its ornamental appeal.

Calathea regalis requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
