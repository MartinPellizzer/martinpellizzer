Cupressus macnabiana

Cupressus macnabiana, commonly known as the Macnab's cedar, is a species of conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in landscapes and natural habitats.
