Centropogon loretensis

Centropogon loretensis, commonly known as the Loretto grass.

It is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Cuba and the Bahamas. The plant belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny florets, typical of grass species.

Centropogon loretensis is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often growing in disturbed or open habitats.
