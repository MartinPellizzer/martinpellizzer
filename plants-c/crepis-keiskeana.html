Crepis keiskeana

Crepis keiskeana, commonly known as the Keiske's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the flowers are small, yellowish-green with a central disc.

It is a perennial species that produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.
