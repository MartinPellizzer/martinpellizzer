Chamaefistula sophera

Chamaefistula sophera, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features greyish-barked stems and heart-shaped, aromatic leaves that emit a strong scent when crushed. The plant produces fragrant, pale blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its showy blooms and adaptability.

However, it is important to note that this species is sometimes confused with other similar-looking plants in the Oleaceae family.
