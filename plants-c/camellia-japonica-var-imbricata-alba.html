Camellia japonica var. imbricata-alba

Camellia japonica var. imbricata-alba, commonly known as the white Japanese camellia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in winter and early spring, adding a dramatic touch to gardens during the colder months. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its pale blooms. It prefers partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is also valued for its cold hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of climates.
