Calliandra acapulcensis

Calliandra acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink or red inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is well-suited for use in gardens and as a landscape plant.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
