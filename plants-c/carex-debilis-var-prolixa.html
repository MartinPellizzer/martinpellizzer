Carex debilis var. prolixa

Carex debilis var. prolixa, commonly known as slender sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to greenish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tidal or freshwater habitats.

Carex debilis var. prolixa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
