Carex thibetica var. pauciflora

Carex thibetica var. pauciflora, commonly known as the Tibetan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments, it plays an important role in local ecosystems.
