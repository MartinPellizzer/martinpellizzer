Cornus drummondii f. priceae

Cornus drummondii f. priceae, commonly known as the Price's dogwood, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a slightly rough texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to drought conditions.
