Carex leporina subsp.

atlasica, commonly known as the Atlas mouse-eared moss. It is a perennial sedge native to the mountainous regions of North Africa and the Iberian Peninsula.

This plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as rocky crevices and stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
