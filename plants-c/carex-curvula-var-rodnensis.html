Carex curvula var. rodnensis

Carex curvula var. rodnensis, commonly known as the Rodnenskaya sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually pale green to brown, which contain the flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
