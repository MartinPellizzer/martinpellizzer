Crepis integra var. platyphylla

Crepis integra var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, basal rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
