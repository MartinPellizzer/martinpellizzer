Centaurea ruscinonensis

Centaurea ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonese knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
