Ceranthera subintegrifolia

Ceranthera subintegrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and is often found growing in shaded, humid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists worldwide.
