Capsicum cordiforme var. cerasicarpon

Capsicum cordiforme var. cerasicarpon, commonly known as the heart-shaped chili pepper, is a variant of the Capsicum cordiforme species.

It is native to the Andean region of South America and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bilaterally symmetrical, typical of the Solanaceae family.

The fruits are generally red or orange when mature and are used in local cuisines for their spicy flavor.
