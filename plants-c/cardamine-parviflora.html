Cardamine parviflora

Cardamine parviflora, commonly known as small-flowered watercress, is a flowering plant native to moist environments in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants. The plant typically grows in shaded, wet habitats such as stream banks and woodland floors. It produces small, white or greenish flowers and has trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
