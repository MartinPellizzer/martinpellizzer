Canarium laevigatum

Canarium laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing oily seeds. It is often cultivated for its valuable resin, which is used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The tree also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
