Chevreulia lycopodioides

Chevreulia lycopodioides, commonly known as the African clubmoss, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the reproductive organs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
