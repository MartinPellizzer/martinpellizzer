Circaea alpina f. composita

Circaea alpina f. composita, commonly known as the composite cinquefoil, is a variant of the alpine cinquefoil.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and covered in fine, white hairs.

This form is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
