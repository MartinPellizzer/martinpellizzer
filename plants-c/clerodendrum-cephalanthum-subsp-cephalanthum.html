Clerodendrum cephalanthum subsp. cephalanthum

Clerodendrum cephalanthum subsp.

cephalanthum, commonly known as the red-flowered clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia. It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small, white flowers, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its stems are often woody and branched.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
