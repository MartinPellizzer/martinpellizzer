Camellia japonica f. leucantha

Camellia japonica f. leucantha, commonly known as the White Camellia, is a variant of the Japanese camellia characterized by its striking white flowers.

This evergreen shrub is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained, acidic soil. The flowers, which bloom in winter, are large and showy, often with a subtle pink hue at the base. It is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant.

The plant is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively slow growth rate.
