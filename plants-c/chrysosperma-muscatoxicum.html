Chrysosperma muscatoxicum

Chrysosperma muscatoxicum, commonly known as the muskgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, low-growing foliage and small, yellowish-green composite flowers. The plant produces small, spherical seed heads that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and swampy areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
