Castanopsis hupehensis var. spinulosa

Castanopsis hupehensis var. spinulosa, commonly known as the spinulose chinquapin, is a deciduous evergreen tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its dense, spiny bracts surrounding its flowers. The tree produces small, oval nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
