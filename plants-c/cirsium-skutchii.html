Cirsium skutchii

Cirsium skutchii, commonly known as Skutch's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and gravelly soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, and it produces small, achene fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Cirsium skutchii is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
