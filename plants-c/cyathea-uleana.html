Cyathea uleana

Cyathea uleana, commonly known as the Ulean tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk and produces new fronds throughout the growing season.

Cyathea uleana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
