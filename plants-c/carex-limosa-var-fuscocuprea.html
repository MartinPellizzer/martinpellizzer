Carex limosa var. fuscocuprea

Carex limosa var. fuscocuprea, commonly known as the brown-cupped sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems. Its leaves are usually dark green with a reddish tint, and the inflorescence is often a dense, brownish cluster.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
