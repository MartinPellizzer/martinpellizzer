Colymbada argecillensis

Colymbada argecillensis, commonly known as the Argecillan marsh orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific wetland habitats in Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, waterlogged soils, often found in peat bogs or marshy areas. Its reproductive strategy relies on specialized mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological niche.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
