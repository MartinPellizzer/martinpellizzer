Cyperus rhenanus

Cyperus rhenanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are wind-pollinated.

Cyperus rhenanus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
