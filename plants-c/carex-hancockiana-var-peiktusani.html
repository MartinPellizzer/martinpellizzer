Carex hancockiana var. peiktusani

Carex hancockiana var. peiktusani, commonly known as the Hancock's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown, and it is noted for its ability to thrive in poorly drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
