Commiphora nigrescens

Commiphora nigrescens, commonly known as black myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic resin, which is harvested from its bark and stems. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a waxy surface, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The resin produced by Commiphora nigrescens has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and as a fragrance in incense and perfumes.
