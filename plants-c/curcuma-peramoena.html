Curcuma peramoena

Curcuma peramoena, commonly known as the purple ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces fragrant blooms that attract pollinators.

The rhizomes of Curcuma peramoena are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.
