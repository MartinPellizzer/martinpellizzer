Citharexylum fruticosum var. brittonii

Citharexylum fruticosum var. brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's citharexylum, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
