Cordylanthus kingii subsp. densiflorus

Cordylanthus kingii subsp.

densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower woollypod, is a perennial herb native to California. It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, pink to purplish flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, and it produces legume-like pods that contain seeds.

This subspecies is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.
