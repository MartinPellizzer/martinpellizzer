Conospermum taxifolium var. leianthum

Conospermum taxifolium var. leianthum, commonly known as the silver-leaf conospermum, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for native landscaping.
