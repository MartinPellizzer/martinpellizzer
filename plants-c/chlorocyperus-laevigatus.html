Chlorocyperus laevigatus

Chlorocyperus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, flat leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
