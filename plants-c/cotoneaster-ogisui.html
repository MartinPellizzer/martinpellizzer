Cotoneaster ogisui

Cotoneaster ogisui, commonly known as the Japanese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Cotoneaster ogisui is relatively hardy and adaptable to various soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
