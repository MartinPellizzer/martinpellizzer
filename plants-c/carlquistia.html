Carlquistia

Carlquistia, commonly known as the desert lily, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

These plants are characterized by their succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. Carlquistia species typically have a rosette growth form, which helps them conserve water in dry environments. They are adapted to survive in extreme conditions with minimal rainfall and high temperatures.

The genus is named in honor of the botanist Stanley W. Carlquist, who contributed significantly to the study of desert flora.
