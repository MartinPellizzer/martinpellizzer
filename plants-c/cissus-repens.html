Cissus repens

Cissus repens, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a trailing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus repens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability.
