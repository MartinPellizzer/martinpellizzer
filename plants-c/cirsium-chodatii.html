Cirsium chodatii

Cirsium chodatii, commonly known as the Chodat's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as hillsides and grasslands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Cirsium chodatii is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
