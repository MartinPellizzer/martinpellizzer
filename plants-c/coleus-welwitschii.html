Coleus welwitschii

Coleus welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschii coleus, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which are often mistaken for flowers. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Coleus welwitschii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its eye-catching appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
