Carex oshimensis f. variegata

Carex oshimensis f. variegata, commonly known as Variegated Japanese sedge, is a compact, low-growing plant native to East Asia.

It features attractive, waxy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that add interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a versatile choice for garden borders or containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of care make it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners.
