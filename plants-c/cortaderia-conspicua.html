Cortaderia conspicua

Cortaderia conspicua, commonly known as the silver feather grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender clumps of silver-white, feathery foliage that gives it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, pale pink to white flowers in the summer months, adding a soft, ethereal touch to the landscape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its architectural form and ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and garden design.
