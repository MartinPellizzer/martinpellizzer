Cyperus pervillei

Cyperus pervillei, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along water edges, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers, which are often green or brown in color.

Cyperus pervillei plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
