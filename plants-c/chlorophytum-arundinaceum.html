Chlorophytum arundinaceum

Chlorophytum arundinaceum, commonly known as the snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a perennial evergreen plant characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or yellow variegation. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it an ideal houseplant. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense clumps over time.

Chlorophytum arundinaceum is also noted for its air-purifying properties, as it can absorb toxins from the surrounding environment.
