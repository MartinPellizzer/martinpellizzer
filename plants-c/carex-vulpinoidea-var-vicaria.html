Carex vulpinoidea var. vicaria

Carex vulpinoidea var. vicaria, commonly known as the fox sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, three-sided spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds and streams. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense stands.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
