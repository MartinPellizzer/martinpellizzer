Carpinus londoniana var. londoniana

Carpinus londoniana var. londoniana, commonly known as the London hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to southern England.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical significance in urban landscapes.
