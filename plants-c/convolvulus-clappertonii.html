Convolvulus clappertonii

Convolvulus clappertonii, commonly known as the Clapperton's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is a climbing or trailing plant with opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink. The plant belongs to the family Convolvulaceae, which includes many species of morning-glories and bindweeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and can thrive in arid environments.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain regions.
