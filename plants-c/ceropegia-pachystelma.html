Ceropegia pachystelma

Ceropegia pachystelma, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces small, delicate flowers with a unique, tubular shape. The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its stems and roots, making it drought-tolerant. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
