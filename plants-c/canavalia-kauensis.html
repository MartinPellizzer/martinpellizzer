Canavalia kauensis

Canavalia kauensis, commonly known as the Kauai bean, is a flowering plant native to Hawaii.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of the Hawaiian Islands. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year.

The seeds are encased in fibrous pods and are used in traditional Hawaiian practices.
