Coix chinensis var. formosana

Coix chinensis var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan Job's tears, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically white or pale green. It is native to East Asia, particularly Taiwan, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The seeds, often called "coix seeds," are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
