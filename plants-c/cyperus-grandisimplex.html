Cyperus grandisimplex

Cyperus grandisimplex, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Cyperus grandisimplex plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
