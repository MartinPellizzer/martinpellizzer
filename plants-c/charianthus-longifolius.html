Charianthus longifolius

Charianthus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved charianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it forms dense mats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
