Cryptostylis fulva var. subregularis

Cryptostylis fulva var. subregularis, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow and orange floral display. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in rainforest or heathland habitats. Its flowers are borne on tall, erect stems and are known for their intricate, spurred petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
