Cynosurus echinatus var. hystrix

Cynosurus echinatus var. hystrix, commonly known as the hairy tussock grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features wiry, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly rough to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect and bird life.
