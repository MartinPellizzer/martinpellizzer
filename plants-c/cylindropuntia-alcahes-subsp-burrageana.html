Cylindropuntia alcahes subsp. burrageana

Cylindropuntia alcahes subsp.

burrageana, commonly known as the Burrageana cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that are often segmented and covered in clusters of small, spine-like structures.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
