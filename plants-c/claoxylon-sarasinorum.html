Claoxylon sarasinorum

Claoxylon sarasinorum, commonly known as the Sarawak ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
