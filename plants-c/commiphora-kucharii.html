Commiphora kucharii

Commiphora kucharii, commonly known as Indian myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elongated, elliptical leaves that are leathery in texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody capsules that contain aromatic resinous gum. This resin, known as kuchari oil, is highly valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Commiphora kucharii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, well-drained soils.
