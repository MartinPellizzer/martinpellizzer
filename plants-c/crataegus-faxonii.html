Crataegus faxonii

Crataegus faxonii, commonly known as the Faxon's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to various soil types and climates.
