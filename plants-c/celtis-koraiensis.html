Celtis koraiensis

Celtis koraiensis, commonly known as the Korean hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by spherical berries that mature to a dark purple color. Celtis koraiensis typically grows in forested areas and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for traditional uses.
