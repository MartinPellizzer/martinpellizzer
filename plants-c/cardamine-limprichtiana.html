Cardamine limprichtiana

Cardamine limprichtiana, commonly known as the Limpricht's watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, alternate leaves that are typically divided into small, lobed segments. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Its root system is fibrous, and it is often found growing in nutrient-rich, humusy soils.
