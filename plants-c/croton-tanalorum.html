Croton tanalorum

Croton tanalorum, commonly known as the red croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically palmately lobed and can display a range of hues, including green, red, and yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
