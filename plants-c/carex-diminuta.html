Carex diminuta

Carex diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a small, perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Carex diminuta is also an important food source for various wildlife species.
