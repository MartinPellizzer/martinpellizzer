Centaurea takhtajanii

Centaurea takhtajanii, commonly known as the Takhtajan's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Centaurea takhtajanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
