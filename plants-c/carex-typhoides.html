Carex typhoides

Carex typhoides, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.

Carex typhoides is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.
