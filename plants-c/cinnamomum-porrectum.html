Cinnamomum porrectum

Cinnamomum porrectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon or porrectum cinnamon, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and aromatic bark. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its bark is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
