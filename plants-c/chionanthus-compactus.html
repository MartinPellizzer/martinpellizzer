Chionanthus compactus

Chionanthus compactus, commonly known as the compact chionanthus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

The flowers emit a fragrant aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
