Carex bartlettii

Carex bartlettii, commonly known as Bartlett's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its grass-like leaves are usually narrow and arranged in three ranks, characteristic of the sedge family.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.
