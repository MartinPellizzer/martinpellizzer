Cyphocarpa quadrangula

Cyphocarpa quadrangula, commonly known as the four-angled sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-angled stems and small, pale green to grayish leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and it is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Cyphocarpa quadrangula is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is well-suited to harsh environmental conditions.
