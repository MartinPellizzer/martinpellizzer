Crataegus monogyna subf. filicifolia

Crataegus monogyna subf.

filicifolia, commonly known as the common hawthorn or simply hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are edible but often used in jams and wines. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with serrated edges, and it is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
