Carex victorinii

Carex victorinii, commonly known as the Victorin's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wet meadows, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated, typical of many sedges.
