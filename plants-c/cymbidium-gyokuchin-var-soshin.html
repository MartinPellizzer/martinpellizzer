Cymbidium gyokuchin var. soshin

Cymbidium gyokuchin var. soshin, commonly known as the Shoshin Cymbidium, is a hybrid orchid prized for its elegant appearance and fragrance.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to East Asia, particularly Japan. The plant features tall, upright pseudobulbs that support broad, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, are often adorned with intricate patterns and are highly sought after in horticulture.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions, making it a popular choice for both gardeners and collectors.
