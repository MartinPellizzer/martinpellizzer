Cereus myriocaulon

Cereus myriocaulon, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stem that resembles a globe, often with a distinctive red or pink coloration. The plant produces striking white or pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
