Cassia grantii var. pilosula

Cassia grantii var. pilosula, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage and clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
