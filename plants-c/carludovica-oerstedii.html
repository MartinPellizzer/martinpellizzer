Carludovica oerstedii

Carludovica oerstedii, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp spines and bear clusters of bright red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are typically produced in late spring or early summer.

Carludovica oerstedii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
