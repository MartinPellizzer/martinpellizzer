Campanula leblebicii

Campanula leblebicii, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. It features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its blooms appear in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
