Cyperus pseudosomaliensis

Cyperus pseudosomaliensis, commonly known as the false somali sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and bear small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Cyperus pseudosomaliensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded environments.
