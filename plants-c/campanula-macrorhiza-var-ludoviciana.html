Campanula macrorhiza var. ludoviciana

Campanula macrorhiza var. ludoviciana, commonly known as the Louisiana bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
