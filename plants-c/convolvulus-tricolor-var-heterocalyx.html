Convolvulus tricolor var. heterocalyx

Convolvulus tricolor var. heterocalyx, commonly known as the three-color morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its vibrant, tri-colored flowers that typically display shades of red, yellow, and white.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to regions in Africa and Asia. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces trumpet-shaped blooms that open in the morning. It thrives in sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds make it both a desirable and potentially invasive species in certain environments.
