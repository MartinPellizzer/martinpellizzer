Campylotheca australis

Campylotheca australis, commonly known as the Australian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is often used for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
