Corymbia torta

Corymbia torta, commonly known as the red gum or red blood gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers are typically pale yellow and appear in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds when mature.
