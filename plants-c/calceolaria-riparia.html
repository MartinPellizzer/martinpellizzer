Calceolaria riparia

Calceolaria riparia, commonly known as the river calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that are often violet or blue in color. The plant typically grows near water sources, such as rivers and streams, which is reflected in its common name. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Calceolaria riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
