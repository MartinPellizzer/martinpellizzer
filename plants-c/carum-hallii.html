Carum hallii

Carum hallii, commonly known as Hall's caraway, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, and its leaves are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. Hall's caraway is valued for its aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
