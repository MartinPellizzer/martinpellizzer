Corybas leucotyle

Corybas leucotyle, commonly known as the white hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to New Zealand.

It is a small, terrestrial herb with a unique floral structure characterized by a hood-like labellum. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a greenish-white coloration and a prominent, hooded lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest undergrowth.

Corybas leucotyle is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism involving flies.
