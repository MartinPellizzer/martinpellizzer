Centaurea brachtii

Centaurea brachtii, commonly known as the Bracht's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiky, bract-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea brachtii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
