Campanula thaliana

Campanula thaliana, commonly known as the mouse ear campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are hairy and resemble the ears of a mouse, giving it its common name.

Campanula thaliana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
