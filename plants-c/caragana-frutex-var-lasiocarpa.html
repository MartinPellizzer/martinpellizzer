Caragana frutex var. lasiocarpa

Caragana frutex var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the silky sandbar willow, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in dry conditions.

This species is also valued for its role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion.
