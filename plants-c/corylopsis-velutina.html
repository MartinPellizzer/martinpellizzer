Corylopsis velutina

Corylopsis velutina, commonly known as the silky flame azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves that are elliptical in shape with a smooth texture. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Corylopsis velutina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to add color to early spring gardens.
