Cupaniopsis oxypetala

Cupaniopsis oxypetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Cupaniopsis oxypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
