Carthamus salicifolius

Carthamus salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant produces seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native environment.
