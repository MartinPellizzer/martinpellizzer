Cymbidium cyperifolium

Cymbidium cyperifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous areas. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are broad and flat, giving it its common name. It produces tall spikes of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often blooming in late winter to early spring.

Cymbidium cyperifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
