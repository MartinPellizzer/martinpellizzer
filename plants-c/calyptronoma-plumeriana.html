Calyptronoma plumeriana

Calyptronoma plumeriana, commonly known as the red-veined begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
