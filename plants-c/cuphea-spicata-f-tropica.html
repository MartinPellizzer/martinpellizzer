Cuphea spicata f. tropica

Cuphea spicata f. tropica, commonly known as the tropical firecracker plant, is a striking ornamental species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange bracts that surround clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy, spikelike inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its unique appearance and prolonged blooming period make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, heat-tolerant plants.
