Cupressus arizonica subsp. stephensonii

Cupressus arizonica subsp.

stephensonii, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a subspecies native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its scale-like leaves and aromatic wood.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. It produces small, spherical cones and has a dense, pyramidal shape.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
