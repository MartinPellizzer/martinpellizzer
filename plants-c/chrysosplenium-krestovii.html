Chrysosplenium krestovii

Chrysosplenium krestovii, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically silver-white underneath, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Chrysosplenium krestovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
