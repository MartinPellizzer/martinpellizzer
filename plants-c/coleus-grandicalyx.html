Coleus grandicalyx

Coleus grandicalyx, commonly known as the red-leaved coleus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, with leaves that range from deep red to purple and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is closely related to other ornamental coleus species. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is popular as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
