Carya tomentosa f. ficoides

Carya tomentosa f. ficoides, commonly known as the smooth walnut.

This variant of the black walnut is characterized by its smoother, less hairy leaves compared to the typical species. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are wind-pollinated, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a thick, husky shell. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
