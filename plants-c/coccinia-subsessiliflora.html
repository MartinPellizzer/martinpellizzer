Coccinia subsessiliflora

Coccinia subsessiliflora, commonly known as the small-leaved gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and melons. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange, spherical fruits. Its fruits are edible when ripe and have a mild, sweet flavor.
