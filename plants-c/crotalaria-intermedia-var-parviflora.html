Crotalaria intermedia var. parviflora

Crotalaria intermedia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered crown flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with two lobes. The plant has palmately compound leaves and produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and agricultural research.
