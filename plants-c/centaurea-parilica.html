Centaurea parilica

Centaurea parilica, commonly known as the Parilica thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features spiny leaves and produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Centaurea parilica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
