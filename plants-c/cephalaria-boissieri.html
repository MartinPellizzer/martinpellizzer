Cephalaria boissieri

Cephalaria boissieri, commonly known as the Alpine cephalaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clustered, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Cephalaria boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
