Crossoglossa dalessandroi

Crossoglossa dalessandroi, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that resemble a cross or a tongue, hence its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid, shaded understory of the forest. Its botanical features include long, slender petals and a distinct, spurred labellum that plays a role in pollination.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts to protect its dwindling population.
