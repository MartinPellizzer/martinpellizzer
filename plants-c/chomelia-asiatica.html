Chomelia asiatica

Chomelia asiatica, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several elliptical leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are enclosed in a woody capsule that splits open when mature.
