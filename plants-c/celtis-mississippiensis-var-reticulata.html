Celtis mississippiensis var. reticulata

Celtis mississippiensis var. reticulata, commonly known as the Mississippi hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark blue berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

It is often found in moist bottomlands and along stream banks, thriving in a variety of soil types.
