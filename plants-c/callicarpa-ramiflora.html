Callicarpa ramiflora

Callicarpa ramiflora, commonly known as the red-berried beauty or Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking, bright red berries that appear in the fall, which are a significant feature of its botanical profile. These berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.

Callicarpa ramiflora is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and ornamental fruit, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
