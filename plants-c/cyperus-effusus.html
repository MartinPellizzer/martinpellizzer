Cyperus effusus

Cyperus effusus, commonly known as swamp spikerush, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water areas. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often covered in soft, silky hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, three-sided flower clusters.

Cyperus effusus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
