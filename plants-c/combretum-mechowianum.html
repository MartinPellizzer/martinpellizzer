Combretum mechowianum

Combretum mechowianum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
