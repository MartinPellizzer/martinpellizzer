Calceolaria tomentosa

Calceolaria tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, fuzzy leaves and unique slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calceolaria tomentosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and adaptability to garden settings.
