Cuscuta myricoides

Cuscuta myricoides, commonly known as the mistletoe dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is often found on conifers and other woody plants, particularly in temperate regions. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat its berries, contributing to its spread.

Cuscuta myricoides plays a role in ecosystem dynamics by influencing host plant growth and providing food for various wildlife species.
