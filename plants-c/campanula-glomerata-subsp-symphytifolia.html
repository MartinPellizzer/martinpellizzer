Campanula glomerata subsp. symphytifolia

Campanula glomerata subsp.

symphytifolia, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
