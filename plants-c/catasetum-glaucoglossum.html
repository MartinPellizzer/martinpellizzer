Catasetum glaucoglossum

Catasetum glaucoglossum, commonly known as the blue-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and purple floral lips, which are highly specialized for pollination by specific insects. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous showy flowers, often arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Catasetum glaucoglossum is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
