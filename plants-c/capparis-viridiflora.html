Capparis viridiflora

Capparis viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in rocky, coastal environments.

The species is notable for its unique floral coloration and ecological significance in its native habitat.
