Cardiospermum duarteanum

Cardiospermum duarteanum, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds with a distinctive spiral pattern. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
