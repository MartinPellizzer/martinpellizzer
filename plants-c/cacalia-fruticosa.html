Cacalia fruticosa

Cacalia fruticosa, commonly known as the everlasting bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Cacalia fruticosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
