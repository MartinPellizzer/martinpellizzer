Chesneya microphylla

Chesneya microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved chesneya, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its showy, clustered inflorescences consist of bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Chesneya microphylla is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
