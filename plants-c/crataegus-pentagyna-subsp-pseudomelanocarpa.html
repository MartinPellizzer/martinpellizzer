Crataegus pentagyna subsp. pseudomelanocarpa

Crataegus pentagyna subsp.

pseudomelanocarpa, commonly known as the blackthorn or European blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, dark berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including woodlands, hedgerows, and open areas. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces white or pink flowers in early spring.

The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
