Cyperus procerus var. trachycladus

Cyperus procerus var. trachycladus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, inconspicuous flowers.

The botanical features of this species make it well-adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
