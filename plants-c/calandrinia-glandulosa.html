Calandrinia glandulosa

Calandrinia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular purslane, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in arid and coastal environments due to its ability to retain water.

Calandrinia glandulosa is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
