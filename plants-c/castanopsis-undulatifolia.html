Castanopsis undulatifolia

Castanopsis undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaved tanoak, is a species of evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of southeastern China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts, and is characterized by its hard, durable wood. The plant has lance-shaped, glossy leaves with a wavy margin, and it produces small, cup-shaped flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits.

It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in mixed forests at higher elevations.
