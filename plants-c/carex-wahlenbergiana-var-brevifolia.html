Carex wahlenbergiana var. brevifolia

Carex wahlenbergiana var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
