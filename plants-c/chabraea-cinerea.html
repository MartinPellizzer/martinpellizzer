Chabraea cinerea

Chabraea cinerea, commonly known as the silver mistflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Chabraea cinerea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid climates.
