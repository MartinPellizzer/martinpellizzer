Clinopodium pamphylicum subsp. pamphylicum

Clinopodium pamphylicum subsp.

pamphylicum, commonly known as Turkish thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features aromatic, small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers in terminal spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued for their antiseptic and aromatic qualities.
