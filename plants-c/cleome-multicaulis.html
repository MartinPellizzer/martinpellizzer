Cleome multicaulis

Cleome multicaulis, commonly known as the common cleome, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its stems are often hairy and can branch profusely, contributing to its bushy appearance.

Cleome multicaulis is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
