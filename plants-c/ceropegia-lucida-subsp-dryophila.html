Ceropegia lucida subsp. dryophila

Ceropegia lucida subsp.

dryophila, commonly known as the ghost flower or ghost plant, is a climbing or trailing succulent native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, waxy coating.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
