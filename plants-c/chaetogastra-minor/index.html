Chaetogastra Minor

Chaetogastra minor is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is known for its small, intricate blooms that appear in late spring and early summer. Despite its beauty, this species remains largely overlooked by botanists and the general public alike. Its limited distribution and fragile nature make it a subject of interest for conservationists. The plant's unique characteristics offer valuable insights into the adaptation strategies of alpine flora.
