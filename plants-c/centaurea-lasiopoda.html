Centaurea lasiopoda

Centaurea lasiopoda, commonly known as the woolly centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant features narrow, linear leaves covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Centaurea lasiopoda is often found in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas.
