Carex lowei

Carex lowei, commonly known as the Low's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Carex lowei has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
