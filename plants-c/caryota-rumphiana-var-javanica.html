Caryota rumphiana var. javanica

Caryota rumphiana var. javanica, commonly known as the Javan palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and tall, slender trunk. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling small, round mangoes. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The leaves are also used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials in some regions.
