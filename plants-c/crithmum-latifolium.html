Crithmum latifolium

Crithmum latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf sea fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often growing in rocky or limestone habitats. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and seeds, are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Crithmum latifolium plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
