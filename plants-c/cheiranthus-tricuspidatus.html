Cheiranthus tricuspidatus

Cheiranthus tricuspidatus, commonly known as the three-lobed wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
