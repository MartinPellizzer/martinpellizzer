Calamus mawaiensis

Calamus mawaiensis, commonly known as the Mawai reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the island of Mawar in Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Calamus mawaiensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

This species is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
