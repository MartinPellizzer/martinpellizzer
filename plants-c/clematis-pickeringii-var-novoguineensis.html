Clematis pickeringii var. novoguineensis

Clematis pickeringii var. novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It features showy, often fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
