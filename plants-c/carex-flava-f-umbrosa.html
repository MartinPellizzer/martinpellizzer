Carex flava f. umbrosa

Carex flava f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving sedge, is a variant of the yellow sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of small, greenish-yellow flowers that mature into brown, spike-like structures.
