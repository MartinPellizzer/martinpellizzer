Centranthus angustifolius subsp. rifanus

Centranthus angustifolius subsp.

rifanus, commonly known as narrowleaf redhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branching stems that are typically covered in dense, woolly hairs.

The leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue and a rough texture. Its flowers are clustered in dense, showy spikes, often displaying shades of pink to red.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
