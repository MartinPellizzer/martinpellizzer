Cakile lanceolata var. millspaughii

Cakile lanceolata var. millspaughii, commonly known as the Millspaugh's bladderwrack, is a coastal plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous species characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline, often in intertidal zones. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a bluish-green hue, while the stems are prostrate or spreading.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to salt spray and periodic submersion, making it a resilient member of coastal ecosystems.
