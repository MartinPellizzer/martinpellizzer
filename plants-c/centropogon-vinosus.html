Centropogon vinosus

Centropogon vinosus, commonly known as the purple false pigeonwings, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.
