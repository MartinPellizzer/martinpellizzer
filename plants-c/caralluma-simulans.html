Caralluma simulans

Caralluma simulans, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom at night. Its leaves are succulent, allowing it to store water efficiently in its native habitat.

Caralluma simulans is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
