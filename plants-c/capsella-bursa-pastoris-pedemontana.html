Capsella bursa-pastoris pedemontana

Capsella bursa-pastoris pedemontana, commonly known as the common chickweed, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with small, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and oval in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a distinctive, heart-shaped silique that splits open to release the seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is widespread across temperate regions.
