Citrus × limonelloides

Citrus × limonelloides, commonly known as the kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically eaten whole, including the peel, which is rich in vitamin C. The plant has fragrant, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The fruit's unique flavor and nutritional content make it a popular choice in both culinary and medicinal applications.
