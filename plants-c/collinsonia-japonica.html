Collinsonia japonica

Collinsonia japonica, commonly known as Japanese collinsonia, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
