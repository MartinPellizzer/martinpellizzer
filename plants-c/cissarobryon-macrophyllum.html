Cissarobryon macrophyllum

Cissarobryon macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant mossy fern, is a unique plant characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to several feet in length.

It is native to the humid, shaded environments of tropical rainforests, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soil. The plant's foliage is often covered in a dense layer of moss, which helps retain moisture and provides a habitat for various microorganisms. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are typically found on the underside of its fronds, releasing spores into the air for dissemination.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Cissarobryon macrophyllum is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
