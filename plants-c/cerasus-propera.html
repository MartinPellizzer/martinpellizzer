Cerasus propera

Cerasus propera, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits known as cherries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it has a shallow root system that makes it adaptable to various soil types.

Cerasus propera is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
