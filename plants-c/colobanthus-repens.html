Colobanthus repens

Colobanthus repens, commonly known as Antarctic pearlwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It thrives in harsh environments such as Antarctica, New Zealand, and southern South America. The plant has small, succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with four petals, and appear in clusters.

Colobanthus repens is adapted to survive in extreme climates, making it a resilient and hardy species.
