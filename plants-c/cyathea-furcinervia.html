Cyathea furcinervia

Cyathea furcinervia, commonly known as the broad-veined tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its fronds are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
