Chrysosplenium sinicum

Chrysosplenium sinicum, commonly known as the Chinese chrysosplenium, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and have a delicate, sometimes hairy texture.

Chrysosplenium sinicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
