Cussambium

Cussambium, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and yellow, often floating on the surface of still or slow-moving water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that provide a habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the surrounding soil, preventing erosion.

Cussambium is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
