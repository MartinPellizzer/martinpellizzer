Conyza elata

Conyza elata, commonly known as blueweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

Conyza elata is known for its ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions.
