Chamaecrista browniana

Chamaecrista browniana, commonly known as the brown's ticktree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Chamaecrista browniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
