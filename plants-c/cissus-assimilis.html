Cissus assimilis

Cissus assimilis, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing cissus, is a tropical vine native to regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its trailing habit and its ability to climb using tendrils that emerge from the nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits resembling figs. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture and a slightly serrated margin.

Cissus assimilis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
