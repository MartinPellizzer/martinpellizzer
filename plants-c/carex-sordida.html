Carex sordida

Carex sordida, commonly known as the sordid sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Native to wetlands and moist soils in North America, it thrives in environments with consistent moisture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
