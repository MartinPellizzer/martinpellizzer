Cassia gigantifolia

Cassia gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces bright yellow, fragrant flowers in clusters, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
