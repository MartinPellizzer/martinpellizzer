Canna glauca var. annaei

Canna glauca var. annaei, commonly known as the Blue Canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a bluish tint, and its flowers are showy, with vibrant blue or purple petals. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its bold foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Canna glauca var. annaei is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
