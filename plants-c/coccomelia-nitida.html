Coccomelia nitida

Coccomelia nitida, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods that burst to disperse seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of desert flora.
