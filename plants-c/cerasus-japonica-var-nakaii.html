Cerasus japonica var. nakaii

Cerasus japonica var. nakaii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Prunus serrulata var. nakaii, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the emergence of leaves. The plant has elliptical leaves that are serrated along the edges, and its fruits are small, dark red cherries that ripen in summer. It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic value and is a symbol of renewal and the arrival of spring.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
