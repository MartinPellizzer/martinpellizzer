Cybianthus bahiensis

Cybianthus bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia cybianthus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, exhibiting adaptations to salt spray and strong winds. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Cybianthus bahiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
