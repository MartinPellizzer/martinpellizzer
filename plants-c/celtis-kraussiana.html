Celtis kraussiana

Celtis kraussiana, commonly known as the African hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. Celtis kraussiana is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
