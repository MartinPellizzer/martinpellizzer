Conyza parvifolia

Conyza parvifolia, commonly known as smallflower weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields. The plant has small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and hairy. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.

Conyza parvifolia is often considered a weed due to its invasive potential in agricultural and natural areas.
