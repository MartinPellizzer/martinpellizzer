Cotula filiformis

Cotula filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf dandelion, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, delicate leaves that are thread-like in appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cotula filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
