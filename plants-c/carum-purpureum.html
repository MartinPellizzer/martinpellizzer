Carum purpureum

Carum purpureum, commonly known as purple caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, white or purple flowers that bloom in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, spicy aroma.

The seeds, which are small and oval, are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
