Carex leribensis

Carex leribensis, commonly known as the Leribe sedge, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grass-like plants with three-sided stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex leribensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
