Cistus chinamadensis f. griseus

Cistus chinamadensis f. griseus, commonly known as the grayleaf rockrose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
