Crotalaria saltiera

Crotalaria saltiera, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five petals and a prominent central spur. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or coastal soils and is often found in disturbed areas along the shoreline.

Crotalaria saltiera is adapted to salty environments and is sometimes used in coastal restoration projects.
