Crataegus oxyacantha var. horrida

Crataegus oxyacantha var. horrida, commonly known as the rough hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along roadsides.
