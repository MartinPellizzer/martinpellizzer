Cleome heptaphylla

Cleome heptaphylla, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is an annual or short-lived perennial, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Cleome heptaphylla is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
