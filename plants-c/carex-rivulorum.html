Carex rivulorum

Carex rivulorum, commonly known as the river sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are typically green and grow in dense clusters. Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex rivulorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
