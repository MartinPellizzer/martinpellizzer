Commiphora marlothii

Commiphora marlothii, commonly known as the marlothii myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which is used in traditional medicine. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. It produces small yellow flowers and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The resin, known as myrrh, has been historically valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
