Calliandra carbonaria

Calliandra carbonaria, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, feathery inflorescences that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it both visually striking and ecologically beneficial.
