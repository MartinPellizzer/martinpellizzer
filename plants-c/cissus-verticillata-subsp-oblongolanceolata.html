Cissus verticillata subsp. oblongolanceolata

Cissus verticillata subsp.

oblongolanceolata, commonly known as the climbing fig or snake plant, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Africa. It features opposite, elliptic to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fleshy, edible berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it a popular choice for ornamental and horticultural purposes.
