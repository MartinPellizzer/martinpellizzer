Calamintha herba-barona

Calamintha herba-barona, commonly known as sweet germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Calamintha herba-barona thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
