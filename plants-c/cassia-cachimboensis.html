Cassia cachimboensis

Cassia cachimboensis, commonly known as the Cachimbo Cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest in Brazil.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, flat seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia cachimboensis thrives in humid, tropical environments and is adapted to the dense canopy of the rainforest.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential pharmaceutical applications.
