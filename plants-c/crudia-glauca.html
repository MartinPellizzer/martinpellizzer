Crudia glauca

Crudia glauca, commonly known as the bluebush, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, waxy leaves that often appear bluish or greyish due to a fine layer of trichomes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient component of its native ecosystems.
