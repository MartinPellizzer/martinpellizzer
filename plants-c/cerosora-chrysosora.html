Cerosora chrysosora

Cerosora chrysosora, commonly known as the golden starflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Cerosora chrysosora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
