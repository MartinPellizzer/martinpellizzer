Cottendorfia atrorosea

Cottendorfia atrorosea, commonly known as the black rose cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems that are covered in a fine, fuzzy coating. The plant produces striking pink or rose-colored flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Cottendorfia atrorosea is relatively rare and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance.
