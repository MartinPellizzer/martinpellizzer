Caryota angustifolia

Caryota angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The leaves are typically dark green and have a feathery appearance, making the plant visually striking. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are sometimes consumed by local populations.
