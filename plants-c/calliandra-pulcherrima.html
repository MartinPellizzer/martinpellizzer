Calliandra pulcherrima

Calliandra pulcherrima, commonly known as the coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red or pink flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant has feathery, trifoliate leaves and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Calliandra pulcherrima make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
