Ceratostylis spathulata var. spathulata

Ceratostylis spathulata var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy habitats and has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in moist, shaded garden environments.
