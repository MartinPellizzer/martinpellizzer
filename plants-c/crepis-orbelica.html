Crepis orbelica

Crepis orbelica, commonly known as the Orbelica mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and spoon-shaped, while its flowers are small and yellowish-white.

Crepis orbelica is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
