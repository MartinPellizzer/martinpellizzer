Chenopodium vulvaria var. rhombicum

Chenopodium vulvaria var. rhombicum, commonly known as wild spinach or lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is notable for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional cuisines.
