Corymborkis assamica

Corymborkis assamica, commonly known as the Assam orchid, is a species of orchid native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with yellow lips, which bloom during the summer months. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Corymborkis assamica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
