Crataegus cupulifera

Crataegus cupulifera, commonly known as the clustered hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it typically grows in woodland edges or thickets.

Crataegus cupulifera is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native habitats.
