Campanula rumenica

Campanula rumenica, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, and it produces a basal rosette of foliage.

Campanula rumenica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
