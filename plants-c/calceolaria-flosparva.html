Calceolaria flosparva

Calceolaria flosparva, commonly known as the small flowered slipper orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Calceolaria flosparva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
