Carex glauca var. sphaerostachys

Carex glauca var. sphaerostachys, commonly known as blue sedge, is a variant of the blue sedge species native to North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to yellow-green flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
