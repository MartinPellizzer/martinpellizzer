Clerodendrum cephalanthum var. torrei

Clerodendrum cephalanthum var. torrei, commonly known as the red-leaved clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which contrasts with its green stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
