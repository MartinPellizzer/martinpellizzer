Chrysoma cuneata var. spathulata

Chrysoma cuneata var. spathulata, commonly known as the Narrowleaf chrysoma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its delicate appearance and adaptability.
