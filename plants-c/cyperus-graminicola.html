Cyperus graminicola

Cyperus graminicola, commonly known as the swamp grass or narrowleaf sedgeland, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize moist soils effectively.

Cyperus graminicola plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
