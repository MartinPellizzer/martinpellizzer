Cotyledon amplexicaulis

Cotyledon amplexicaulis, commonly known as the hugging cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

The plant is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
