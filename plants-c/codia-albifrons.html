Codia albifrons

Codia albifrons, commonly known as the white-frond codia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes many aquatic and semi-aquatic plants. The plant is characterized by its striking white, waxy leaves that are often used in ornamental horticulture. Its flowers are small and clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, which is typical of the Araceae family.

Codia albifrons thrives in humid environments and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
