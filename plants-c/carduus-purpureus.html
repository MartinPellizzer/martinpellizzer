Carduus purpureus

Carduus purpureus, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its purple flowers and spiny leaves, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant grows up to 1.5 meters tall, with a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various soil types. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
