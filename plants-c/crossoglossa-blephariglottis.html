Crossoglossa blephariglottis

Crossoglossa blephariglottis, commonly known as the starry bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, with a star-like pattern on the petals. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
