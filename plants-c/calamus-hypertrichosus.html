Calamus hypertrichosus

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizome and large, arrow-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Calamus hypertrichosus is often found in wetland or swampy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
