Croton reticulatus

Croton reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with intricate, net-like veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a branched structure and thick, waxy foliage.

Croton reticulatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant leaf coloration and unique foliage patterns.
