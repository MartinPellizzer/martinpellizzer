Carex × strigosula

Carex × strigosula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves that are typically arranged in a triangular cross-section. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and stream banks.

Carex × strigosula is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
