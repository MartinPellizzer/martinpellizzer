Celtis koraiensis var. aurantiaca

Celtis koraiensis var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the Korean hackberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Korea.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, red-orange fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The varietal name 'aurantiaca' refers to its distinctive orange-colored fruits.
