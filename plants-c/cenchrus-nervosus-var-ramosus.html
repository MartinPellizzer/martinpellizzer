Cenchrus nervosus var. ramosus

Cenchrus nervosus var. ramosus, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often branch profusely. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often have a distinctive nervose pattern, contributing to its common name.

This species is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
