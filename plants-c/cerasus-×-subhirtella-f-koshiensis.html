Cerasus × subhirtella f. koshiensis

Cerasus × subhirtella f. koshiensis, commonly known as the Koshi cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It is a hybrid of Prunus subhirtella and Prunus × yedoensis, characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, double flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are small and tart, often used in jams and desserts.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
