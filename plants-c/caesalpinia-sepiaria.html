Caesalpinia sepiaria

Caesalpinia sepiaria, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, giving it its striking visual appearance. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically bisexual, with five petals and numerous stamens, and it produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds.

Caesalpinia sepiaria thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
