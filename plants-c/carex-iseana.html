Carex iseana

Carex iseana, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces viable seeds that aid in its propagation.

Carex iseana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
