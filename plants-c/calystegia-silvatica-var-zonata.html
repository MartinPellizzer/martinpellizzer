Calystegia silvatica var. zonata

Calystegia silvatica var. zonata, commonly known as the zoned wood bindweed, is a perennial vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or light green border, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rapid growth and aggressive spreading habit make it a noted weed in some regions.
