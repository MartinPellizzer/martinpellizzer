Celastrus paniculatus

Celastrus paniculatus, commonly known as the Indian Satinflower, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the summer. The plant bears orange-red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Celastrus paniculatus has medicinal uses in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
