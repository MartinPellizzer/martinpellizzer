Caralluma dummeri f. colorata

Caralluma dummeri f. colorata, commonly known as the colorful nightshade or desert nightshade, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of India.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves are typically spiny and adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
