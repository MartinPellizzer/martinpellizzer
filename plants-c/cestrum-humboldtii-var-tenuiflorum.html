Cestrum humboldtii var. tenuiflorum

Cestrum humboldtii var. tenuiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
