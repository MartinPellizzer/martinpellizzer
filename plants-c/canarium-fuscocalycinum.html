Canarium fuscocalycinum

Canarium fuscocalycinum, commonly known as the dark-stemmed candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing oily seeds. Its latex is toxic and has been used traditionally in various cultural and medicinal practices.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and historical significance in local communities.
