Caiophora cernua

Caiophora cernua, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Caiophora cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
