Chavica rumphii

Chavica rumphii, commonly known as the Malabar cowpea, is a flowering plant native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Chavica rumphii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
