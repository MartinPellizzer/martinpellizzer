Cereus coerulescens var. melanacanthus

Cereus coerulescens var. melanacanthus, commonly known as the black-spined night bloomer, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green and covered with white, spiny tubercles. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, often with a sweet scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
