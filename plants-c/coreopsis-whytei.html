Coreopsis whytei

Coreopsis whytei, commonly known as Texas tawny coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
