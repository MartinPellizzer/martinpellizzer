Cogswellia torreyi

Cogswellia torreyi, commonly known as the Torrey's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in rocky soils and canyons.
