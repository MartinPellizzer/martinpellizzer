Cuphea distichophylla

Cuphea distichophylla, commonly known as the two-leaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cuphea distichophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
