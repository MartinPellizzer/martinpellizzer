Croton pavana

Croton pavana, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in tropical regions.
