Chaetothylax rzedowskii

Chaetothylax rzedowskii, commonly known as the Mexican spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the cloud forests of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, twisted flower structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky outcrops or in forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and it has a limited geographic range, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
