Cocos virgata

Cocos virgata, commonly known as the Virgin Islands palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly the U.S. Virgin Islands and Puerto Rico. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline.

Cocos virgata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various species.
