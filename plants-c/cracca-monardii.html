Cracca monardii

Cracca monardii, commonly known as the showy milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of vibrant orange flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic to many animals, including livestock. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cracca monardii plays an important role in supporting pollinators, particularly butterflies and bees.
