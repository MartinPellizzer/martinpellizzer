Corydalis persica var. persica

Corydalis persica var. persica, commonly known as Persian candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has delicate, linear leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its blooming period typically occurs in early spring, adding a touch of color to gardens in cooler climates.
