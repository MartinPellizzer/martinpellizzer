Cuphea lanceolata f. typica

Cuphea lanceolata f. typica, commonly known as the common cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
