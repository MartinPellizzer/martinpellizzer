Ceanothus rigidus var. albus

Ceanothus rigidus var. albus, commonly known as the white ceanothus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a drought-resistant structure.

The white ceanothus is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
