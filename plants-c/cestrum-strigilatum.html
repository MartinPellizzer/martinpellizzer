Cestrum strigilatum

Cestrum strigilatum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, or purple. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cestrum strigilatum is also known for its nocturnal blooming habit, attracting pollinators such as moths and bats.
