Chrysophyllum rugosum

Chrysophyllum rugosum, commonly known as the rough-leaved starfruit, is a tropical plant belonging to the Sapotaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, waxy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into the edible star-shaped fruit. The plant is native to regions in Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, while the plant itself has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.

Chrysophyllum rugosum is typically cultivated for both its fruit and its ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
