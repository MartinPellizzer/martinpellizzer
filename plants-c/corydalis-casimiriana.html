Corydalis casimiriana

Corydalis casimiriana, commonly known as the Casimir's fumitory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has slender, ascending stems and produces small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
