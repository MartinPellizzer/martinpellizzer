Cynanchum hamatum

Cynanchum hamatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant contains toxic compounds, including cardiac glycosides, which can be harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
