Creodus

Creodus, commonly known as the common tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Tamaricaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Creodus is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
