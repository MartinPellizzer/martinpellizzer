Cypripedium × cardinale

Cypripedium × cardinale, commonly known as the cardinal lady's tresses, is a hybrid orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers with a distinctive green throat, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a dense rosette. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands, often growing on the forest floor. It has a long flowering period, typically from late spring to early summer, and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
