Clethra nicaraguensis

Clethra nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan clethra, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
