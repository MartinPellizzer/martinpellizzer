Cerastium lanigerum f. pauciflorum

Cerastium lanigerum f. pauciflorum, commonly known as the scarce-flowered mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in early spring and attract pollinators like bees and flies.
