Cynanchum sobradoi

Cynanchum sobradoi, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds known for their milky sap. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in follicles that open to release silky-haired seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

Cynanchum sobradoi is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
