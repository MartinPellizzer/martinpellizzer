Crataegus oxyacantha subsp. monogyna

Crataegus oxyacantha subsp.

monogyna, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits called pomes, which are edible but often tart.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its cardiovascular benefits.
