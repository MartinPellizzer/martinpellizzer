Cynanchum sublanceolatum var. kinokuniense

Cynanchum sublanceolatum var. kinokuniense, commonly known as Japanese milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its fruits are follicles that release seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
