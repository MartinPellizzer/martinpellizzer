Cassia splendida

Cassia splendida, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, the Americas, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces long, narrow pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia splendida has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
