Centaurea heerffelii

Centaurea heerffelii, commonly known as the Herffel's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

Centaurea heerffelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
