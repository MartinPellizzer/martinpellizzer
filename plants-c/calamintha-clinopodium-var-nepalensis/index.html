Calamintha Clinopodium Var. Nepalensis

Nepal mint, scientifically known as Calamintha clinopodium var. nepalensis, is a fragrant herb native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and parts of northern India. This plant belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is valued for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories at high altitudes. The plant's leaves are commonly used in traditional medicine for their calming and digestive benefits. Its unique scent and adaptability make it a valuable species in both ecological and herbal contexts.
