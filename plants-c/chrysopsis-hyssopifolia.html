Chrysopsis hyssopifolia

Chrysopsis hyssopifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Chrysopsis hyssopifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
