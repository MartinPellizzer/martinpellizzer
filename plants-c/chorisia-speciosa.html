Chorisia speciosa

Chorisia speciosa, commonly known as the angel's trumpet, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a distinctive, hollow, tree-like stem that is often branched and covered in a thick, waxy coating. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Chorisia speciosa is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in warm climates.
