Cremastus sceptrum var. latifolia

Cremastus sceptrum var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved crested bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a yellow or orange throat. The leaves are broad and ovate, typically arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
