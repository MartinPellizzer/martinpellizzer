Crepis taygetica

Crepis taygetica, commonly known as the Greek wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow or pale yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Crepis taygetica is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions.
