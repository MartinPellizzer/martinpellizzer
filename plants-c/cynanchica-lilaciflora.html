Cynanchica lilaciflora

Cynanchica lilaciflora, commonly known as the lilac-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of fragrant, lilac-colored flowers. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Cynanchica lilaciflora is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
