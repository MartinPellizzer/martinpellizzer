Celastrus elevativenius

Celastrus elevativenius, commonly known as the climbing staff-vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange berries, which are an important food source for birds.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it both useful in horticulture and a potential invasive species in some areas.
