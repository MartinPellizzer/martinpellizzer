Convolvulus moricandii

Convolvulus moricandii, commonly known as the Moricandia convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a trailing perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry climates.
