Calamus mirabilis

Calamus mirabilis, commonly known as the miraculous canes, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a waxy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers.

Calamus mirabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
