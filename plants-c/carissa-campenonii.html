Carissa campenonii

Carissa campenonii, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Carissa campenonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
