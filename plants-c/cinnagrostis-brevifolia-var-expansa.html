Cinnagrostis brevifolia var. expansa

Cinnagrostis brevifolia var. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf cinnagrostis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a distinctive hairy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
