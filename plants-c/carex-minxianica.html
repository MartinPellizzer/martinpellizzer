Carex minxianica

Carex minxianica, commonly known as the Minxian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex minxianica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
