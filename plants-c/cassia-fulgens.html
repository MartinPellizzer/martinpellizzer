Cassia fulgens

Cassia fulgens, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia fulgens thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and showy appearance make it a favorite in landscaping and horticulture.
