Capparis gonaivensis

Capparis gonaivensis, commonly known as the Haitian sour fig, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically sour and yellowish in color. The plant's flowers are usually pale green to white, with five petals and a distinct central column. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and dry forests.

Capparis gonaivensis plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
