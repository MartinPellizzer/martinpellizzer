Clematopsis scabiosifolia subsp. kirkii

Clematopsis scabiosifolia subsp.

kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's clematopsis, is a member of the Rosaceae family. It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and foliage.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring, typically in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
