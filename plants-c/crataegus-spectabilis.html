Crataegus spectabilis

Crataegus spectabilis, commonly known as the showy crabsapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 leaflets, and its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits known as pomes, which are edible but often tart. Crataegus spectabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
