Celtis opegrapha

Celtis opegrapha, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, yellowish fruits that mature in the fall.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
