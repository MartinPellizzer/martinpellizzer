Carphephorus paniculatus

Carphephorus paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled grass, is a tropical perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, feathery panicles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in loose, branching clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
