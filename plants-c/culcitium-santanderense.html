Culcitium santanderense

Culcitium santanderense, commonly known as the Santander sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, compact growth habit.

Culcitium santanderense is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers that develop in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
