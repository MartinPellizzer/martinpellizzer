Chamaesyce bifida

Chamaesyce bifida, commonly known as the splitleaf sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a fern-like appearance. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the spring.

The species is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh desert climates.
