Canarium harveyi var. sapidum

Canarium harveyi var. sapidum, commonly known as the Malabar nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries containing a single seed. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, subtropical climates.
