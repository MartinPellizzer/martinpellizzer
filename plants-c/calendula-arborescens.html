Calendula arborescens

Calendula arborescens, commonly known as the tree marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Calendula arborescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
