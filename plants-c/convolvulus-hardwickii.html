Convolvulus hardwickii

Convolvulus hardwickii, commonly known as the hardwick's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features trailing stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
