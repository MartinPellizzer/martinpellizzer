Clinopodium aegyptiacum

Clinopodium aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian oregano, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a fuzzy texture and a strong, pungent aroma, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Clinopodium aegyptiacum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
