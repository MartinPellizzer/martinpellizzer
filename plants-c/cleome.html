Cleome

Cleome, commonly known as spider flower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Cleomaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plants typically have simple, alternate leaves and are often annual or biennial in nature. Cleome species are native to various regions in the Americas and are popular in gardens for their ornamental value.

They thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making them easy to cultivate.
