Chaptalia vegaensis

Chaptalia vegaensis, commonly known as the Patagonian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which appear in clusters, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
