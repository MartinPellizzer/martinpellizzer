Calceolaria alternifolia

Calceolaria alternifolia, commonly known as the zigzag calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct zigzag pattern along their edges. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

Calceolaria alternifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
