Camaridium chimalapanum

Camaridium chimalapanum, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its striking red and orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
