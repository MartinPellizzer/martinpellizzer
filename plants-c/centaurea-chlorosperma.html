Centaurea chlorosperma

Centaurea chlorosperma, commonly known as the greenhead thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and the flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical cluster.

Centaurea chlorosperma is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
