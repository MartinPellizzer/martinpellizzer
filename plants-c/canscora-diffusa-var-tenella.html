Canscora diffusa var. tenella

Canscora diffusa var. tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marshmallow, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in indigenous healing practices.
