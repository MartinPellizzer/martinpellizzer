Crepidium burbidgei

Crepidium burbidgei, commonly known as the Burridge's orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces compact, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to its arid environment. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its resilience in harsh conditions.

The species is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
