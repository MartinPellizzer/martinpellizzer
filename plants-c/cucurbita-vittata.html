Cucurbita vittata

Cucurbita vittata, commonly known as the warty gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinct, lobed leaves.

This species is native to Central and South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, warty fruit surface. The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and the fruits can vary in color from green to yellow or orange. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
