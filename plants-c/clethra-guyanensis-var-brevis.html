Clethra guyanensis var. brevis

Clethra guyanensis var. brevis, commonly known as the short-stemmed sweet pepper tree, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves and produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
