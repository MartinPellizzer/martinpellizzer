Clematis acutangula f. major

Clematis acutangula f. major, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are twining and can grow up to 10 meters in length, making it suitable for trellises or arches.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
