Convolvulus pilosellifolius var. pilosellifolius

Convolvulus pilosellifolius var. pilosellifolius, commonly known as the woolly leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a trailing or climbing vine, with tendrils that help it attach to supports. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
