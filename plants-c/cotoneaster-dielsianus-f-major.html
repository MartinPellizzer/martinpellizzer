Cotoneaster dielsianus f. major

Cotoneaster dielsianus f. major, commonly known as the large-flowered cotoneaster, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. The plant bears red, fleshy berries that are typically consumed by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
