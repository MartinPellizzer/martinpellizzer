Croton echinocarpus

Croton echinocarpus, commonly known as the spiky croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fruits that enclose its seeds, giving it its scientific name *echinocarpus*, meaning "spiny fruit.". The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and unique fruit structure.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
