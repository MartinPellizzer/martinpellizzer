Cassia arrojadoana

Cassia arrojadoana, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia arrojadoana is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
