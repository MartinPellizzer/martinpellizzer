Cissus carnosa

Cissus carnosa, commonly known as the bone leaf or stonecrop.

It is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, white, granular bumps. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries.

Cissus carnosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
