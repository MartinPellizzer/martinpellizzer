Calamagrostis micrantha var. sierrae

Calamagrostis micrantha var. sierrae, commonly known as Sierra bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green foliage and tall, flowering stalks that produce small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and streambanks. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in mountainous regions.

Sierra bluejoint grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
