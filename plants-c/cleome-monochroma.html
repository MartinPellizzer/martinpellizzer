Cleome monochroma

Cleome monochroma, commonly known as the white cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Cleome monochroma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
