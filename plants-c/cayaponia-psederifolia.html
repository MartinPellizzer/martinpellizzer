Cayaponia psederifolia

Cayaponia psederifolia, commonly known as the Maya apple, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, green, apple-like fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and it produces fruits that ripen in the summer months.

The plant is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in tropical gardens.
