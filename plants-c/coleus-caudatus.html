Coleus caudatus

Coleus caudatus, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Coleus caudatus thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
