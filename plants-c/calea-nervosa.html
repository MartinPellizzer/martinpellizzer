Calea nervosa

Calea nervosa, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, ornamental inflorescences that resemble butterflies, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, tubular corolla. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, while the flowers bloom in clusters at the top of the stem.

Calea nervosa is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
