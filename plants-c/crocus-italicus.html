Crocus italicus

Crocus italicus, commonly known as the Italian crocus, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive, often fragrant, scent. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

Crocus italicus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
