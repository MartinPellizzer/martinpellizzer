Cybianthus guyanensis subsp. multipunctatus

Cybianthus guyanensis subsp.

multipunctatus, commonly known as the Guyana thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and showy yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in compact clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
