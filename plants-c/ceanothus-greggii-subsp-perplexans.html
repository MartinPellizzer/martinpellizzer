Ceanothus greggii subsp. perplexans

Ceanothus greggii subsp.

perplexans, commonly known as the Desert Ceanothus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its small, leathery leaves are aromatic and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
