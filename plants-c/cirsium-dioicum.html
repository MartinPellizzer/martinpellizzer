Cirsium dioicum

Cirsium dioicum, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are usually gray-green with sharp, prickly margins, and the flowers are arranged in a spherical head.

Cirsium dioicum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
