Convolvulus dryadum

Convolvulus dryadum, commonly known as the dryad's convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
