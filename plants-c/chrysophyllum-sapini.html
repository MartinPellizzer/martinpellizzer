Chrysophyllum sapini

Chrysophyllum sapini, commonly known as the African cherry or *Tetrapleura tetraptera*, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it valuable in both ecological and medicinal contexts.

Chrysophyllum sapini thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
