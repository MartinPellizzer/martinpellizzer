Crataegus × rufula

Crataegus × rufula, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–8 meters. The plant features thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a small, red or orange berry that matures in the fall.

Crataegus × rufula is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and other disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.
