Campanula tenella var. stenocodon

Campanula tenella var. stenocodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
