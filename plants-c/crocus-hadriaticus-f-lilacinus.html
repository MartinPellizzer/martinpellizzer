Crocus hadriaticus f. lilacinus

Crocus hadriaticus f. lilacinus, commonly known as the Adriatic crocus, is a botanical variant of the crocus genus native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking lilac-colored flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cup-shaped blooms with a distinctive, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical significance in horticultural traditions.
