Crateva urbaniana

Crateva urbaniana, commonly known as the Indian caper or velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
