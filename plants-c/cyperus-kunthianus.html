Cyperus kunthianus

Cyperus kunthianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family, and it often serves as a habitat for various insects and small aquatic organisms.

Cyperus kunthianus is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
