Carex hornschuchiana var. eckeroeensis

Carex hornschuchiana var. eckeroeensis, commonly known as the Eckeroeensis sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. It has narrow, dark green leaves that form a dense mat, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
