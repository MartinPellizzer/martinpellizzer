Chamitis complicata

Chamitis complicata, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss.

Chamitis complicata is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
