Chrysanthemum odoratum

Chrysanthemum odoratum, commonly known as sweet chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are finely divided and have a silvery underside, while the stems are often hairy.

Chrysanthemum odoratum is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and in traditional medicine.
