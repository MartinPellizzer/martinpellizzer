Callicarpa woodii

Callicarpa woodii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of violet to purple berries that mature in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive fruiting bodies. The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
