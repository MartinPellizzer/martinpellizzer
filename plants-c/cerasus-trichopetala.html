Cerasus trichopetala

Cerasus trichopetala, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese wild cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by bright red, edible berries. Its leaves are typically ovate with a serrated edge and a glossy green surface.

Cerasus trichopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
