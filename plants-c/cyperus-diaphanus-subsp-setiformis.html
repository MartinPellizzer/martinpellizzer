Cyperus diaphanus subsp. setiformis

Cyperus diaphanus subsp.

setiformis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae). It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences.

This subspecies typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually unisexual, with separate male and female structures, a common trait in the Cyperaceae family.

The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland habitats.
