Campanula rotundifolia var. arctica

Campanula rotundifolia var. arctica, commonly known as Arctic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has rounded, hairy leaves that are adapted to cold climates and low light conditions. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine tundra environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in polar ecosystems.
