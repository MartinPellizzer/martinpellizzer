Cyperus amuricus var. japonicus

Cyperus amuricus var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas, along stream banks, and in flooded fields. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common weed in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.

The species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and filter water, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
