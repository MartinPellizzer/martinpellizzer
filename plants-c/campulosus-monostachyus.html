Campulosus monostachyus

Campulosus monostachyus, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
