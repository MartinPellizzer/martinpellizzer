Coelogyne rossiana

Coelogyne rossiana, commonly known as the Ross's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that often display a combination of white, yellow, and red hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, storing water and nutrients for periods of drought.

Coelogyne rossiana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
