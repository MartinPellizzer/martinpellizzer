Cuscuta saccharata

Cuscuta saccharata, commonly known as the sugar dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers and has thread-like stems that wrap around host plants such as grasses and shrubs. The plant's seeds are dispersed through the wind, allowing it to colonize new areas effectively. Its life cycle is closely tied to the growth and survival of its host, making it an important component of certain ecosystems.

Despite its parasitic nature, Cuscuta saccharata plays a role in ecological interactions and can sometimes be found in agricultural settings.
