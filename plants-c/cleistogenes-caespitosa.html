Cleistogenes caespitosa

Cleistogenes caespitosa, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically concealed within the foliage. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common component of grassland ecosystems.

Blue grama plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife and livestock.
