Cuphea melvilla

Cuphea melvilla, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea melvilla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
