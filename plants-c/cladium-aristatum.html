Cladium aristatum

Cladium aristatum, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves with serrated edges that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow water or saturated soils, forming dense stands that provide habitat for various wildlife species. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas effectively.

Cladium aristatum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and filtering water.
