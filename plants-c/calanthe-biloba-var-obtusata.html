Calanthe biloba var. obtusata

Calanthe biloba var. obtusata, commonly known as the obtuse-lipped calanthe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
