Callitriche insularis

Callitriche insularis, commonly known as the island water-star, is a small aquatic plant native to certain regions of the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has small, floating leaves that are oval-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tiny, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Callitriche insularis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through its biological activity.
