Chenopodium × borbasioides f. hircinifolium

Chenopodium × borbasioides f. hircinifolium, commonly known as the woolly goosefoot, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while its seeds can be processed into a flour-like substance.

The species exhibits variability in leaf shape and hair density, contributing to its adaptability in different environments.
