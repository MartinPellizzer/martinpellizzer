Clematis koreana f. chiisanensis

Clematis koreana f. chiisanensis, commonly known as the Korean Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Korea.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its graceful appearance.
