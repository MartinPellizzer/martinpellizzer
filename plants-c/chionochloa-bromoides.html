Chionochloa bromoides

Chionochloa bromoides, commonly known as the alpine hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, hair-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in cold, high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small alpine organisms.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a valuable species for studying plant resilience in harsh climates.
