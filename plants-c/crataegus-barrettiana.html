Crataegus barrettiana

Crataegus barrettiana, commonly known as the Barrett hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It typically grows in open woodlands and rocky areas, adapting well to a variety of soil conditions.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
