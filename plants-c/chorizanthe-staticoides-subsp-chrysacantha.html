Chorizanthe staticoides subsp. chrysacantha

Chorizanthe staticoides subsp.

chrysacantha, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, giving it its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.

The species is adapted to hot, dry conditions and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
