Crataegus cuneifolia

Crataegus cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Crataegus cuneifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
