Carex macrocephala f. kobomugi

Carex macrocephala f. kobomugi, commonly known as the Kobomugi sedge, is a variant of the broad-leaf sedge native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces large, showy spikelets that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a waxy texture, while the inflorescence consists of two distinct spikelets.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to garden settings.
