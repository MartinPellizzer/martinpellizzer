Cyperus brevifolius subsp. leiolepis

Cyperus brevifolius subsp.

leiolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae). It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting diverse invertebrate and bird life.
