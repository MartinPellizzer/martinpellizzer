Coelogyne veitchii

Coelogyne veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and cylindrical, supporting the plant's growth and storage of nutrients.

The flowers bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing and sought-after species in horticulture.
