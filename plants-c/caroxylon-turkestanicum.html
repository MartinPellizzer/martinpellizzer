Caroxylon turkestanicum

Caroxylon turkestanicum, commonly known as the Central Asian thorny tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and steppe regions.

Caroxylon turkestanicum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
