Camellia kaempferia

Camellia kaempferia, commonly known as the white camellia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is characterized by its showy, waxy white flowers with red stamens. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are glossy, elliptical, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Camellia kaempferia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
