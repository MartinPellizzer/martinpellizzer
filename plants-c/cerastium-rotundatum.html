Cerastium rotundatum

Cerastium rotundatum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its stems are often prostrate, and it produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes other flowering plants with similar structural characteristics.
