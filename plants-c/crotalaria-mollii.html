Crotalaria mollii

Crotalaria mollii, commonly known as the velvetflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. Crotalaria mollii is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
