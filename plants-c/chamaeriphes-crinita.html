Chamaeriphes crinita

Chamaeriphes crinita, commonly known as the hairy chamaerhiphys, is a unique plant species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, providing additional protection against intense sunlight and drought.

Chamaeriphes crinita is well adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
