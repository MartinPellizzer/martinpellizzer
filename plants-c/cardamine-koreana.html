Cardamine koreana

Cardamine koreana, commonly known as Korean watercress, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its leaves are often used in traditional Korean cuisine and herbal medicine for their mild peppery flavor and potential health benefits.
