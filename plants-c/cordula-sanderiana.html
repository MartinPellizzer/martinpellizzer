Cordula sanderiana

Cordula sanderiana, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, bloom in the spring and early summer.

Cordula sanderiana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
