Cyclopogon nutantiflorus

Cyclopogon nutantiflorus, commonly known as the drooping-flowered cyclopogon, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, which helps it to conserve water in its native habitat.

Cyclopogon nutantiflorus is typically found in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
