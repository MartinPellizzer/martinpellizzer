Callicarpa stenophylla

Callicarpa stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered purple berries that appear in the fall, which are a key feature for identification. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Callicarpa stenophylla is valued in landscaping for its ornamental berries and attractive foliage.
