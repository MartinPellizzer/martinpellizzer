Chrysopogon leucotrichus

Chrysopogon leucotrichus, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-colored foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chrysopogon leucotrichus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects.
