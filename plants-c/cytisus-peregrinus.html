Cytisus peregrinus

Cytisus peregrinus, commonly known as the wandering broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often sprawling stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Cytisus peregrinus is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
