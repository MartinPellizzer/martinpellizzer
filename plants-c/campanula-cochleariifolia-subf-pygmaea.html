Campanula cochleariifolia subf. pygmaea

Campanula cochleariifolia subf.

pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a compact and ornamental species. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
