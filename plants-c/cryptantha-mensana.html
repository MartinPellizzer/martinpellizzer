Cryptantha mensana

Cryptantha mensana, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its seed pods contain small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh climates.

The species is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
