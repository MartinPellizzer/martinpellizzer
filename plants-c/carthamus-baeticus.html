Carthamus baeticus

Carthamus baeticus, commonly known as the Baetican safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces large, showy blooms that typically appear in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

Carthamus baeticus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
