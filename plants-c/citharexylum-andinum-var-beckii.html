Citharexylum andinum var. beckii

Citharexylum andinum var. beckii, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and botanical collections.
