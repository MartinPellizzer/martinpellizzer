Carex virgata var. secta

Carex virgata var. secta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
