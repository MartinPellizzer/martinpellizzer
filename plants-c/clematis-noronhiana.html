Clematis noronhiana

Clematis noronhiana, commonly known as the Brazilian clematis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and climbing nature, often reaching heights of several meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its foliage consists of alternate, simple, and serrated leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture.

Clematis noronhiana is well-suited for trellises or as a climbing plant in gardens with adequate support.
