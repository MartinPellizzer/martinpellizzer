Camellia luteopallida

Camellia luteopallida, commonly known as the yellow camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to six feet. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its bright yellow blooms.

Camellia luteopallida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
