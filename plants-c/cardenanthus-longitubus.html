Cardenanthus longitubus

Cardenanthus longitubus, commonly known as the long-tube poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which are typically tubular in shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
