Cyperus buchananii

Cyperus buchananii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Cyperus buchananii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
