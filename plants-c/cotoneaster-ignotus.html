Cotoneaster ignotus

Cotoneaster ignotus, commonly known as the unknown cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
