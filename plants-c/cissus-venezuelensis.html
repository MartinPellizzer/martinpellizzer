Cissus venezuelensis

Cissus venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its stems are woody and capable of rooting at the nodes, allowing it to climb and spread effectively.

Cissus venezuelensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
