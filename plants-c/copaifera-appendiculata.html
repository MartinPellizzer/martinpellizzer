Copaifera appendiculata

Copaifera appendiculata, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and has compound leaves with elongated leaflets. Its most notable feature is the production of a valuable resin, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a diverse array of wildlife.
