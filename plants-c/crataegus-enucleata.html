Crataegus enucleata

Crataegus enucleata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring slender branches and ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

Its leaves and fruits are often used in traditional herbal medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
