Coronilla globosa

Coronilla globosa, commonly known as the globular vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has a compact, globular growth habit, with dense clusters of leaves and flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
