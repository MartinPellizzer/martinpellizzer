Calamus trichrous

Calamus trichrous, commonly known as the hairy calamus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Calamus trichrous is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in wetland or swampy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
