Centratherum metzianum

Centratherum metzianum, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its root system is fibrous and can form dense colonies, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
