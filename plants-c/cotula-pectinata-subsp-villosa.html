Cotula pectinata subsp. villosa

Cotula pectinata subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is a low-growing perennial with a compact, mat-forming structure.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
