Cerinthe pyrenaica

Cerinthe pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean cerinthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tubular and come in shades of blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cerinthe pyrenaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in mountain ecosystems.
