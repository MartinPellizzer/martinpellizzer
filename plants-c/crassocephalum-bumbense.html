Crassocephalum bumbense

Crassocephalum bumbense, commonly known as the Bumbense cudweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, hairy leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
