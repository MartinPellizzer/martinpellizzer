Cestrum ochraceum var. macrophyllum

Cestrum ochraceum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved yellow bell, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are relatively large compared to other species in the Cestrum genus. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
