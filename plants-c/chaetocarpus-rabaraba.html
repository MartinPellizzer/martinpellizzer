Chaetocarpus rabaraba

Chaetocarpus rabaraba, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and spiny, leaf-like bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along shorelines. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, while its stems are erect and often covered in small, hooked hairs.

Chaetocarpus rabaraba is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
