Cardamine cretica

Cardamine cretica, commonly known as lesser watercress, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, white or pale green, and arranged in a single umbel.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.
