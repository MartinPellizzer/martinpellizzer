Cirrhopetalum wightii

Cirrhopetalum wightii, commonly known as the Wight's star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, while the inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
