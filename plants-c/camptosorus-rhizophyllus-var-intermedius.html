Camptosorus rhizophyllus var. intermedius

Camptosorus rhizophyllus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate root-leaf fern, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the Camptosorus genus.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a creeping rhizome with specialized root-like structures that anchor it to the substrate. The plant exhibits a distinctive foliage pattern, with fronds that are often divided into narrow, linear segments. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
