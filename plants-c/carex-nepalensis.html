Carex nepalensis

Carex nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem.

Carex nepalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
