Cnidoscolus hasslerianus

Cnidoscolus hasslerianus, commonly known as the "Mexican stinging leaf" or "hassler's stinging leaf," is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact with the skin. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
