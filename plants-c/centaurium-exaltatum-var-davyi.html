Centaurium exaltatum var. davyi

Centaurium exaltatum var. davyi, commonly known as the Sierra centaury, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in native plant gardens.
