Calamagrostis phragmitoides var. rubicunda

Calamagrostis phragmitoides var. rubicunda, commonly known as the red reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to wetland and marshy areas of North America.

It features slender, arching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often reddish in color, especially at the base. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers in late summer, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in riparian zones, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking reddish hues and graceful appearance.
