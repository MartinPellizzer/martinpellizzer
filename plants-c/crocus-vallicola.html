Crocus vallicola

Crocus vallicola, commonly known as the alpine crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
