Cryptanthus tiradentesensis

Cryptanthus tiradentesensis, commonly known as the Brazilian carpet flower, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Haemodoraceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
