Calophyllum longifolium

Calophyllum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and large, elongated leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas, where it thrives in humid, warm climates.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
