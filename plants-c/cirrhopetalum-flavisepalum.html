Cirrhopetalum flavisepalum

Cirrhopetalum flavisepalum, commonly known as the golden sepal orchid, is a tropical plant characterized by its vibrant yellow sepals and petals.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to regions in Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small but striking, often blooming in clusters during the rainy season.

The botanical features of this orchid make it a popular specimen among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
