Cyperus longus f. myriostachyus

Cyperus longus f. myriostachyus, commonly known as the many-stamened flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale yellow to brownish flowers, which are typically arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Cyperus genus.
