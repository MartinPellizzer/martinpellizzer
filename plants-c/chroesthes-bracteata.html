Chroesthes bracteata

Chroesthes bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
