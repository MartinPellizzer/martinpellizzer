Calamintha betulifolia subsp. cilicica

Calamintha betulifolia subsp.

cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician birch-leaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and showy flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a serrated margin.

The small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.
