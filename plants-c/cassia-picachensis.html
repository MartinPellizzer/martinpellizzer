Cassia picachensis

Cassia picachensis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, flat, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its bright, showy flowers.

Cassia picachensis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
