Comanthera flexuosa

Comanthera flexuosa, commonly known as the waxy begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant and visually striking plants.
