Colubrina reclinata

Colubrina reclinata, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and coastal areas. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Colubrina reclinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, sandy soils.
