Cedrus libani var. brevifolia

Cedrus libani var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved cedar of Lebanon, is a subspecies of the Lebanon cedar.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, particularly in parts of Syria and Lebanon. This evergreen conifer is characterized by its short, thick leaves and pyramidal shape. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its historical and ecological significance, it is also valued for its ornamental and timber qualities.
