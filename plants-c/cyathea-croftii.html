Cyathea croftii

Cyathea croftii, commonly known as the Croft's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically a deep green, with a leathery texture, and the plant can live for several decades.

Cyathea croftii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
