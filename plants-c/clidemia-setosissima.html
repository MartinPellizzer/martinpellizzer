Clidemia setosissima

Clidemia setosissima, commonly known as the hairy mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant attaches itself to host trees, deriving some of its nutrients through specialized roots called haustoria. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are typically clustered and bloom during the wet season.

Clidemia setosissima plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
