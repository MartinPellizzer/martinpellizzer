Capparis fischeri

Capparis fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which is characterized by its unique floral structures and often thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly succulent texture.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.
