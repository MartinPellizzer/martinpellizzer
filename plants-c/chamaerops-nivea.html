Chamaerops nivea

Chamaerops nivea, commonly known as the white date palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that resemble dates. Chamaerops nivea is adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray, making it a hardy species.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and parks in warm climates.
