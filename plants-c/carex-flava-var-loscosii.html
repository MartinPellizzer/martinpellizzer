Carex flava var. loscosii

Carex flava var. loscosii, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along streambanks. Its bright yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, forming dense spikes that are often used in floral arrangements.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates.
