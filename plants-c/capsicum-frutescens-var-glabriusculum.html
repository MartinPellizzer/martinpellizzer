Capsicum frutescens var. glabriusculum

Capsicum frutescens var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the bird pepper or chili pepper, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small, shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of about 30–60 cm. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines for their spicy flavor. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is well-suited for cultivation in USDA hardiness zones 9–11.

The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy surface.
