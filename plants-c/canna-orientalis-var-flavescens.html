Canna orientalis var. flavescens

Canna orientalis var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features large, broad leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside, and its most notable characteristic is its vibrant, orange to yellow inflorescence. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.

Canna orientalis var. flavescens is a perennial that can grow up to 3 meters tall, making it a dramatic addition to tropical landscapes.
