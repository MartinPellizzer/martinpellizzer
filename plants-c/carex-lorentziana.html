Carex lorentziana

Carex lorentziana, commonly known as the Lorentzian sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, erect leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
