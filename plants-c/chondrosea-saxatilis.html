Chondrosea saxatilis

Chondrosea saxatilis, commonly known as the rock sponge or sea sponge, is a unique marine organism belonging to the family Chondrosiidae.

It is characterized by its spongy, porous structure that allows it to filter water for nutrients. This species typically inhabits rocky substrates in shallow coastal waters, where it thrives in turbulent conditions. Its body is composed of a flexible skeleton made of spongin fibers and spicules, providing structural support.

Chondrosea saxatilis plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and offering habitat for various small marine organisms.
