Centaurea seusana

Centaurea seusana, commonly known as the Balearic centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly the Balearic Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its vibrant blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea seusana is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
