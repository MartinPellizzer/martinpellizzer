Capparis horrida var. erythrodasys

Capparis horrida var. erythrodasys, commonly known as the red-fruited caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thorny stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are edible when ripe and have a tangy, sour flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
