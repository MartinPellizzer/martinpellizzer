Crinum australasicum var. arenarium

Crinum australasicum var. arenarium, commonly known as the coastal crinum, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia and New Guinea.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal winds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in harsh coastal environments.
