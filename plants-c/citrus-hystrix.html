Citrus hystrix

Citrus hystrix, commonly known as the citronella shrub, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sharp, thorny stems and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which is typically green and bitter, is not commonly consumed by humans but is used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
