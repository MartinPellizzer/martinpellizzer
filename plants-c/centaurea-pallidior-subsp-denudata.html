Centaurea pallidior subsp. denudata

Centaurea pallidior subsp.

denudata, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
