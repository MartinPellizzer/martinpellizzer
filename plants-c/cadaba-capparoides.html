Cadaba capparoides

Cadaba capparoides, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various animals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Cadaba capparoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
