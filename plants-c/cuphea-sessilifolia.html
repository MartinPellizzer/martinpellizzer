Cuphea sessilifolia

Cuphea sessilifolia, commonly known as the wax plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glandular, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea sessilifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
