Cissus incisa var. andrewsii

Cissus incisa var. andrewsii, commonly known as the African bush willow, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are opposite and have a waxy, leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological purposes.
