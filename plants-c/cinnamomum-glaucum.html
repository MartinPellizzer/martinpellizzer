Cinnamomum glaucum

Cinnamomum glaucum, commonly known as the blue camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glaucous blue-gray foliage and aromatic bark, which contains essential oils. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The leaves and bark are used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.
