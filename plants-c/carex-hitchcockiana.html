Carex hitchcockiana

Carex hitchcockiana, commonly known as Hitchcock's sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular, often glaucous leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and stream banks.

Hitchcock's sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
