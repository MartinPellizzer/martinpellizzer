Comolia serpyllacea

Comolia serpyllacea, commonly known as common yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are typically linear and serrated, often forming a basal rosette.

Comolia serpyllacea is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.
