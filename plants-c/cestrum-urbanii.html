Cestrum urbanii

Cestrum urbanii, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a strong, sweet scent.

Cestrum urbanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nighttime blooming habit.
