Convolvulus lachnospermus

Convolvulus lachnospermus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, creeping stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
