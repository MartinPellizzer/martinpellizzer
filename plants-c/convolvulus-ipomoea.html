Convolvulus ipomoea

Convolvulus ipomoea, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heliotropic (sun-tracking) flowers that open in the morning and close at dusk. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of blue, purple, and white. It is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, using its tendrils to support itself.

Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
