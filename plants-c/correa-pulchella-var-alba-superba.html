Correa pulchella var. alba-superba

Correa pulchella var. alba-superba, commonly known as the white superba correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various garden conditions.
