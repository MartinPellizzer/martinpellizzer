Celosia palmeri

Celosia palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that resemble a comb or a plume, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other ornamental and edible plants. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and xeriscaping.
