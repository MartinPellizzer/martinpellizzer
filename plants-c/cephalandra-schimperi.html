Cephalandra schimperi

Cephalandra schimperi, commonly known as the Schimperi's ivy or the creeping ivy, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its trailing vines and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage.

Cephalandra schimperi requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to grow successfully.
