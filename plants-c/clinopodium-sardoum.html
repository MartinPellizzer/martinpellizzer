Clinopodium sardoum

Clinopodium sardoum, commonly known as Sardinian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are fragrant and have a mint-like scent, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.

Clinopodium sardoum is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat digestive issues and respiratory ailments.
