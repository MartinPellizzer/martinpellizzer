Crataegus monogyna var. stricta

Crataegus monogyna var. stricta, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits called haws, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and open scrublands.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedgerow construction.
