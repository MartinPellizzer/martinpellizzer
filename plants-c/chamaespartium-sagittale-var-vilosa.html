Chamaespartium sagittale var. vilosa

Chamaespartium sagittale var. vilosa, commonly known as the slender yew, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Taxaceae family, characterized by its coniferous appearance and unique reproductive structures. The plant produces small, reddish berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by birds. Its leaves are needle-like and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
