Cissus sicyoides f. foliolata

Cissus sicyoides f. foliolata, commonly known as the variegated rock fig, is a unique cultivar of the rock fig plant.

It is characterized by its striking variegation, with leaves displaying a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. This plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. It has a trailing growth habit and produces small, edible figs that are often used in traditional medicine.

The foliage is thick and waxy, making it an attractive ornamental plant for indoor or outdoor cultivation.
