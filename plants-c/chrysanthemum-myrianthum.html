Chrysanthemum myrianthum

Chrysanthemum myrianthum, commonly known as the mountain chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually grayish-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from white to yellow.

Chrysanthemum myrianthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
