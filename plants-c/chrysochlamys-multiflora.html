Chrysochlamys multiflora

Chrysochlamys multiflora, commonly known as the golden glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
