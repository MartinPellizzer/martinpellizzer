Cyathocline birmanica

Cyathocline birmanica, commonly known as the Burmese water lily, is a aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its floating, broad leaves that provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning. Its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of still water bodies.

Cyathocline birmanica plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
