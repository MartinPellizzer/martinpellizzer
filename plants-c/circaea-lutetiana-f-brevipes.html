Circaea lutetiana f. brevipes

Circaea lutetiana f. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed white campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, often prostrate stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter stems compared to the typical form of Circaea lutetiana.
