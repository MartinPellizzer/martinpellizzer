Crypsinus hastatus f. dichotomus

Crypsinus hastatus f. dichotomus, commonly known as the dichotomous holly, is a variant of the holly species characterized by its unique branching pattern.

This plant is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are typically glossy and oval-shaped, with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red berries, which are an important food source for birds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
