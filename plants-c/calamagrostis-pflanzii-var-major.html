Calamagrostis pflanzii var. major

Calamagrostis pflanzii var. major, commonly known as the tall bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces spikelets that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
