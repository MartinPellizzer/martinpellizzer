Caccinia macranthera var. crassifolia

Caccinia macranthera var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved caccinia, is a species native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its growth habit is often bushy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
