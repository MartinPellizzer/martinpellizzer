Calanthe caulodes

Calanthe caulodes, commonly known as the velvet root calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

Calanthe caulodes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
