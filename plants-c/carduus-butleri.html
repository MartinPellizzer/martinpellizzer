Carduus butleri

Carduus butleri, commonly known as the Butler's thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
