Cistus × skanbergii f. albiflorus

Cistus × skanbergii f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered rock rose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Cistus in the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers are typically fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
