Cyrtopodium braemii

Cyrtopodium braemii, commonly known as the Braem's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers typically bloom in spring and feature a combination of white, yellow, and green hues.

Cyrtopodium braemii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
