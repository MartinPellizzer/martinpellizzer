Catasetum laminatum var. maculatum

Catasetum laminatum var. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often feature a distinctive spotted or mottled pattern on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of fragrant, brightly colored flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
