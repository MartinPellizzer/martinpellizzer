Cassia gallinaria

Cassia gallinaria, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Cassia gallinaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
