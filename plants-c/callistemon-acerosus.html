Callistemon acerosus

Callistemon acerosus, commonly known as the rough-leaved bottlebrush, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink spike-like inflorescences, which are composed of numerous stamen clusters. The plant has narrow, rough-textured leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Callistemon acerosus is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects due to its nectar-rich flowers.
