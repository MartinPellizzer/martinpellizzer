Capparis cordifolia

Capparis cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow, capitate flowers. The plant is evergreen and typically grows in rocky or coastal environments. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain seeds.

Capparis cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
