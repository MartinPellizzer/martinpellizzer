Cymbidium cruentum

Cymbidium cruentum, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically blooms in winter or early spring, producing fragrant, spurred flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Cymbidium cruentum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
