Calamintha × joffrei

Calamintha × joffrei, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a hybrid perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces aromatic seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calamintha × joffrei is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
