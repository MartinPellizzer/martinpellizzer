Cistus × florentinus f. subsalviifolius

Cistus × florentinus f. subsalviifolius, commonly known as the Salvia-leaved Rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fuzzy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
