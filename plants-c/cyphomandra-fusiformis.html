Cyphomandra fusiformis

Cyphomandra fusiformis, commonly known as the long bean or Brazilian bean, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruit that resembles a bean pod. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by the edible, green, elongated fruits. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated for its edible pods, which are used in various culinary traditions.
