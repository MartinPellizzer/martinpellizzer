Cullumia Setosa Var. Adnata

Cullumia setosa var. adnata is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of the southwestern united states. it belongs to the rockcress family and is known for its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. this variant is distinguished by its compact growth habit and dense, wooly leaves that help it survive in harsh, arid conditions. due to habitat loss and overcollection, it is considered threatened and requires conservation efforts. its unique characteristics make it an important subject for botanical study and ecological preservation.
