Cucurbita pepo var. radiata

Cucurbita pepo var. radiata, commonly known as the scalloped pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, scalloped-shaped leaves and vibrant, orange to yellow fruits. The plant is a trailing vine that produces large, showy flowers, both male and female, which are essential for pollination. Its fruits are often used in culinary applications and ornamental displays due to their unique appearance.

The botanical features of this variety make it a popular choice for both gardeners and horticultural enthusiasts.
