Campanula tubiflora

Campanula tubiflora, commonly known as the tuberous bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has tuberous roots that help it survive in harsh, alpine conditions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Campanula tubiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
