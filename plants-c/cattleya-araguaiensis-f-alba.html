Cattleya araguaiensis f. alba

Cattleya araguaiensis f. alba, commonly known as the White Cattleya, is a rare and striking orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves, and it produces showy, pendulous blooms during the flowering season.

This cultivar is highly valued for its beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
