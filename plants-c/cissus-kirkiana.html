Cissus kirkiana

Cissus kirkiana, commonly known as the creeping fig or African shield fern, is a tropical vine native to Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits. Cissus kirkiana is known for its ability to climb using adventitious roots that form along its stems.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
