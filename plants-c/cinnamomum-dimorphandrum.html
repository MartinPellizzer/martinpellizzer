Cinnamomum dimorphandrum

Cinnamomum dimorphandrum, commonly known as the two-form cinnamon tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soils.
