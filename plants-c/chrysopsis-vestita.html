Chrysopsis vestita

Chrysopsis vestita, commonly known as the silverleaf goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant is distinguished by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chrysopsis vestita is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
