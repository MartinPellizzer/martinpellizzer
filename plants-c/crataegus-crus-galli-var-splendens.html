Crataegus crus-galli var. splendens

Crataegus crus-galli var. splendens, commonly known as the showy crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring bright red or orange flowers in early spring and showy red or yellow fruits in autumn. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

The fruits are edible but typically used in jams and jellies rather than consumed raw.
