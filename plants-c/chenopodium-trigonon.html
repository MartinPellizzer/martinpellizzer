Chenopodium trigonon

Chenopodium trigonon, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are encased in a hard, triangular capsule. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Chenopodium trigonon is known for its adaptability and can be both a weed and a forage plant for livestock.
