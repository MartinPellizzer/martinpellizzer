Cyrtanthera citrina

Cyrtanthera citrina, commonly known as the yellow sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
