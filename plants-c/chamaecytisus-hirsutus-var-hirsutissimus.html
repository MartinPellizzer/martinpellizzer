Chamaecytisus hirsutus var. hirsutissimus

Chamaecytisus hirsutus var. hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used as a decorative plant in landscaping.
