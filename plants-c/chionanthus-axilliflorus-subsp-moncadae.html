Chionanthus axilliflorus subsp. moncadae

Chionanthus axilliflorus subsp.

moncadae, commonly known as the Moncada's fringe tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Cuba. It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical, with a smooth margin.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its showy inflorescences.
