Cousinia kirrindica

Cousinia kirrindica, commonly known as the Kirrindic hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, succulent leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow to orange, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia kirrindica is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in difficult climates.
