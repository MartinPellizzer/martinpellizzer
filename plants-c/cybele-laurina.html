Cybele laurina

Cybele laurina, commonly known as the sacred laurel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. Cybele laurina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical association with ancient religious practices.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
