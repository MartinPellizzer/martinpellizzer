Centaurea margarita-alba

Centaurea margarita-alba, commonly known as the white marguerite, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, white ray florets surrounded by a ring of yellow-orange disk flowers, forming a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-30 cm, with slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky habitats.

The flowers bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
