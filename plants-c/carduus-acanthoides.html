Carduus acanthoides

Carduus acanthoides, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in harsh environments.
