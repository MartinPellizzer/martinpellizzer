Croton malvoides

Croton malvoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses in tropical and subtropical regions.
