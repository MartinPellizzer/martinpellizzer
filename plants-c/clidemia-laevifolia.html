Clidemia laevifolia

Clidemia laevifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white or purple, which bloom during the wet season. Clidemia laevifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils make it a notable component of tropical ecosystems.
