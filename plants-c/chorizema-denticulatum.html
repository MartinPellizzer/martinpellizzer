Chorizema denticulatum

Chorizema denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its leaves are reduced to spines, while its stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry climates.

The flowers bloom in the morning and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.
