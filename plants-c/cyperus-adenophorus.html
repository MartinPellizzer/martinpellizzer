Cyperus adenophorus

Cyperus adenophorus, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. Cyperus adenophorus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions and is used in some areas for ornamental purposes or as a food source for wildlife.
