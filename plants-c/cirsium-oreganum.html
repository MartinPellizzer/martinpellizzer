Cirsium oreganum

Cirsium oreganum, commonly known as the Oregon thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Cirsium oreganum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
