Caprifolium dimorphum

Caprifolium dimorphum, commonly known as the two-form honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring. Its berries are small, red, and edible, often consumed by birds.

Caprifolium dimorphum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
