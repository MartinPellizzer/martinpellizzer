Celastrus euphlebiphyllus

Celastrus euphlebiphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed staff-vine, is a member of the Celastraceae family.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves with a narrow base. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruiting structures.
