Cardamine angulata var. kamtschatica

Cardamine angulata var. kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamtschatka watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with slender, branching stems that often grow in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three lobes. It is native to the temperate regions of Asia, particularly the Russian Far East and parts of Japan.

This variety is valued for its peppery flavor and is sometimes cultivated as a leafy green vegetable.
