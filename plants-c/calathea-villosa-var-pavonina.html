Calathea villosa var. pavonina

Calathea villosa var. pavonina, commonly known as the velvet leaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, and variegated foliage with deep green and silver patterns. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that enhances its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
