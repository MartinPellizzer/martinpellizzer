Carex euryphylla

Carex euryphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Carex euryphylla is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
