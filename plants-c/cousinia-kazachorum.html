Cousinia kazachorum

Cousinia kazachorum, commonly known as the Kazakh cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and steppes.

Cousinia kazachorum is adapted to withstand drought and cold temperatures, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
