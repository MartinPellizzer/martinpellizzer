Conophytum depressum subsp. perdurans

Conophytum depressum subsp.

perdurans, commonly known as the "Lentil Plant," is a succulent native to South Africa. It features a compact, rounded body with fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, typical of arid environments.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
