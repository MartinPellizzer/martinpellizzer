Clematis montana var. grandiflora

Clematis montana var. grandiflora, commonly known as the mountain clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

This variety is known for its vigorous growth and ability to climb trellises or walls, adding vertical interest to gardens.
