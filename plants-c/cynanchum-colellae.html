Cynanchum colellae

Cynanchum colellae, commonly known as the eastern dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
