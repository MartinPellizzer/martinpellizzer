Cyperus kabarensis

Cyperus kabarensis, commonly known as the Kabare flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in eastern and central parts of the continent. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering stems are usually slender and produce small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus kabarensis is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its ability to thrive in moist conditions and its aesthetic appeal.
