Calochortus maculosus

Calochortus maculosus, commonly known as the spotted glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant native to California and parts of Nevada.

It belongs to the lily family (Liliaceae) and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, often in areas with full sun exposure. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Calochortus maculosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
