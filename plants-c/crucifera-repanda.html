Crucifera repanda

Crucifera repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, pinnately compound leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically useful.
