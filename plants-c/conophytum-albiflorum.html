Conophytum albiflorum

Conophytum albiflorum, commonly known as the Whiteflowered Stone Plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
