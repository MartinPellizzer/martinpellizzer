Chloranthus glaber

Chloranthus glaber, commonly known as the smooth chloranthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and are often clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy in natural habitats.
