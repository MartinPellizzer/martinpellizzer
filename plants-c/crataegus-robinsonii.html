Crataegus robinsonii

Crataegus robinsonii, commonly known as Robinson's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and South Carolina. The plant is a small to medium-sized deciduous shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 15 meters. It produces showy white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated edges, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
