Calystegia atriplicifolia subsp. buttensis

Calystegia atriplicifolia subsp.

buttensis, commonly known as the Scottish bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces yellow flowers with five petals.

The leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive serrated edge. This subspecies is often found in grassy areas and along riverbanks.

It is considered a semi-parasitic plant, deriving some of its nutrients from the roots of neighboring vegetation.
