Cynanchum davuricum

Cynanchum davuricum, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers in summer, followed by small, follicle-like fruits. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and forest-steppe habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
