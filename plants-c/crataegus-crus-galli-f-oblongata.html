Crataegus crus-galli f. oblongata

Crataegus crus-galli f. oblongata, commonly known as the common hawthorn or mayflower, is a cultivar of the hawthorn plant.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves and showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It is typically found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed soils across Europe and parts of Asia.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
