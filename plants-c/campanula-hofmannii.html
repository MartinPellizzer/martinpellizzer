Campanula hofmannii

Campanula hofmannii, commonly known as the Hofmann's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula hofmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
