Carduus onopordioides var. albidens

Carduus onopordioides var. albidens, commonly known as the white thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves and striking white to pale purple flower heads. The plant produces small, spiky bracts that protect its composite flowers, which consist of both ray and disk florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for gardening purposes.
