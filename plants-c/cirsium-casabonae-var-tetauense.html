Cirsium casabonae var. tetauense

Cirsium casabonae var. tetauense, commonly known as the Tetauense thistle, is a perennial plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
