Crataegus monogyna f. pilifera

Crataegus monogyna f. pilifera, commonly known as the hairy hawthorn, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense growth habit and distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are typically oval to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a slightly hairy surface, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is native to Europe and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for hedgerows and ornamental gardens.

The hairy hawthorn is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for wildlife.
