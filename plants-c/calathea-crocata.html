Calathea crocata

Calathea crocata, commonly known as the crocodile plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, and deeply veined leaves that resemble the patterns of a crocodile's skin. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its foliage is often variegated with shades of green, silver, and pink, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
