Carex × reducta

Carex × reducta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. It is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
