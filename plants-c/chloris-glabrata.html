Chloris glabrata

Chloris glabrata, commonly known as slender grass or slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a fine texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Chloris glabrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
