Centaurea × jacea f. dumeticola

Centaurea × jacea f. dumeticola, commonly known as the rough centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces bright yellow to orange-red flowers arranged in dense, radial heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a rough texture due to the presence of stiff, bristly hairs.

This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
