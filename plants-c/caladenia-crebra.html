Caladenia crebra

Caladenia crebra, commonly known as the slender spider-orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, intricately patterned flower that resembles a spider. The plant typically grows in heathland and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color with dark markings, and they bloom during the spring season.

Caladenia crebra is a protected species, reflecting its ecological significance and the need for conservation efforts.
