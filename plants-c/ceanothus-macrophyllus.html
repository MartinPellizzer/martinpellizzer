Ceanothus macrophyllus

Ceanothus macrophyllus, commonly known as the bigleaf ceanothus, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 10 inches in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Ceanothus macrophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
