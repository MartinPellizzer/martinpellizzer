Canthium

Canthium, commonly known as the spiny sida, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It consists of shrubs or small trees native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plants are characterized by their spiny stems and alternate leaves with serrated margins. They produce small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Canthium species are often used in traditional medicine and have ecological significance as food sources for various animals.
