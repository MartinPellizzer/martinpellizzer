Cypripedium fargesii

Cypripedium fargesii, commonly known as the Farges' lady's slipper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive slipper-shaped flower. The plant typically grows in cool, shaded environments, often in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, with a showy pouch-like structure that attracts pollinators.

Cypripedium fargesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest in botanical research due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
