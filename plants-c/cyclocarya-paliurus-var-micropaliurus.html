Cyclocarya paliurus var. micropaliurus

Cyclocarya paliurus var. micropaliurus, commonly known as the small-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to temperate climates. Its flowers are white or pink, and it produces edible berries that are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The variety micropaliurus is distinguished by its smaller fruit size compared to the typical form of Cyclocarya paliurus.
