Carex interjecta

Carex interjecta, commonly known as the intermediate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, and it blooms during the summer months. Carex interjecta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
