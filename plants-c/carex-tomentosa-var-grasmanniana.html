Carex tomentosa var. grasmanniana

Carex tomentosa var. grasmanniana, commonly known as Grasmann's tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its dense growth habit makes it a valuable plant for erosion control and habitat for various invertebrates.
