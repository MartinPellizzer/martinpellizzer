Calliandra biflora

Calliandra biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered calliandra, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that consist of two clusters of bright red or pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Calliandra biflora is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
