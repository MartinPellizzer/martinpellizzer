Cymbidium cerinum

Cymbidium cerinum, commonly known as the woolly orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cymbidium cerinum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
