Commiphora flabellulifera

Commiphora flabellulifera, commonly known as the fan-leaved myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in the Socotra Archipelago.
