Chasmone pusilla

Chasmone pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf chasmone, is a small, flowering plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and yellowish, appearing in clusters at the ends of its stems.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
