Centrolobium tomentosum

Centrolobium tomentosum, commonly known as the velvet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets covered in a fine, woolly pubescence. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by legume-like pods containing seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
