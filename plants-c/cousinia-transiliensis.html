Cousinia transiliensis

Cousinia transiliensis, commonly known as the Transilvanian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia transiliensis produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
