Cotoneaster kaschkarovii

Cotoneaster kaschkarovii, commonly known as the Karakorum cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, red or orange berries that appear in the fall. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its compact growth habit and ornamental berries make it a desirable plant for rock gardens and alpine collections.

Cotoneaster kaschkarovii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
