Carex × vierhapperi

Carex × vierhapperi, commonly known as the Vierhapper's sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, characterized by its grass-like appearance and dense, clumping growth habit.

It typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, which are usually green to brown in color. This plant is often found in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.

Carex × vierhapperi is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that contributes to ecological diversity in wetland ecosystems.
