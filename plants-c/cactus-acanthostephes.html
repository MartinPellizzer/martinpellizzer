Cactus acanthostephes

Cactus acanthostephes, commonly known as the desert hedge cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green, succulent body. Its root system is shallow and adapted to quickly absorb water after rainfall.

Overall, it is well-suited to harsh desert environments and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
