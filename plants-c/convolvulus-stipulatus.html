Convolvulus stipulatus

Convolvulus stipulatus, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically hairy and alternate along the stem. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in the morning and closing by midday. It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Convolvulus stipulatus is an annual herb that thrives in well-drained soils and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
