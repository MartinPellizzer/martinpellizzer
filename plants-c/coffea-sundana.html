Coffea sundana

Coffea sundana, commonly known as the Java coffee plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces red berries containing seeds that are used to make coffee. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique flavor profile, Coffea sundana is valued in specialty coffee markets.
