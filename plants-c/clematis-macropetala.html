Clematis macropetala

Clematis macropetala, commonly known as the large-petioled clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features showy, fragrant, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into distinctive, bladder-like fruits. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Clematis macropetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
