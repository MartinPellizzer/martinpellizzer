Ceropegia tenuiflora

Ceropegia tenuiflora, commonly known as the string of hearts or rabbit's foot plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia tenuiflora is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
