Cynanchum chouxii

Cynanchum chouxii, commonly known as Chinese milkweed or yew vine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive seed pods containing silky-haired seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
