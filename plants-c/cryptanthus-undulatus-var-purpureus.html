Cryptanthus undulatus var. purpureus

Cryptanthus undulatus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple carpet plant, is a low-growing succulent native to South America.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with velvety, oval-shaped leaves that are typically purple or reddish in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
