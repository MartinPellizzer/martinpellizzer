Commiphora kilimandscharica

Commiphora kilimandscharica, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the eastern African region, particularly the highlands of Kenya and Tanzania.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces a fragrant resin, which is tapped from its bark and used in traditional medicine and incense.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native environment.
