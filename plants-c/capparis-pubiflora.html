Capparis pubiflora

Capparis pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters. These flowers give way to round, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe.

Capparis pubiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in culinary applications.
