Cereus viridiflorus var. tubulosus

Cereus viridiflorus var. tubulosus, commonly known as the green-flowered cactus, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces striking, greenish-white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and cactus enthusiasts.
