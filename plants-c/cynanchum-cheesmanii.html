Cynanchum cheesmanii

Cynanchum cheesmanii, commonly known as the cheesman's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique floral structure and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
