Caladenia tentaculata

Caladenia tentaculata, commonly known as the tentacle orchid, is a species of orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that are typically borne on tall, slender stems. The plant produces a single, erect flower spike with a single or occasionally two flowers, each featuring a distinctive, elongated labellum. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Caladenia tentaculata is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique floral morphology and ecological significance.
