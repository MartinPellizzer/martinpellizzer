Cyathea obtusiloba

Cyathea obtusiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in specialized structures called sori. Cyathea obtusiloba thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
