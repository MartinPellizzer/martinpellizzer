Callista megaceras

Callista megaceras, commonly known as the giant callista, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, featuring vibrant colors such as red, orange, and yellow. The plant has a woody stem with thick, leathery leaves that help it conserve water in its native habitat. Its flowers are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences, making it a visually impressive species.

Callista megaceras is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its unique appearance and long-lasting blooms.
