Clematis parviloba var. ganpiniana

Clematis parviloba var. ganpiniana, commonly known as the Ganpinian Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, recurved shape. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate blooms and graceful growth habit.
