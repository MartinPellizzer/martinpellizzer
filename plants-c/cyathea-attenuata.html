Cyathea attenuata

Cyathea attenuata, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea attenuata plays an important ecological role in tropical forests by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
