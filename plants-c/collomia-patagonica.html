Collomia patagonica

Collomia patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, which helps in reducing water loss.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
