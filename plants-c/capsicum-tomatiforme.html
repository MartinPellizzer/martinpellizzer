Capsicum tomatiforme

Capsicum tomatiforme, commonly known as the tomato-shaped pepper, is a unique species within the Capsicum genus.

It is characterized by its rounded, tomato-like fruit shape, which sets it apart from other pepper varieties. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, and the fruit ripens to a bright red color.

This plant is native to South America and is valued for its ornamental and culinary potential.
