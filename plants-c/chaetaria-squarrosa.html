Chaetaria squarrosa

Chaetaria squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrose chaetaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, spindly stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant produces yellow or orange disc flowers surrounded by pale green to brown ray florets. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Chaetaria squarrosa is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.
