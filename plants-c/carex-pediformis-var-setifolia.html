Carex pediformis var. setifolia

Carex pediformis var. setifolia, commonly known as the slender thread sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it a common sight in wetland habitats.

The variety setifolia is distinguished by its slender, thread-like leaves and is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant appearance.
