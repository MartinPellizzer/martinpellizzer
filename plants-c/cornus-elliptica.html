Cornus elliptica

Cornus elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clustered inflorescences during the spring. Its berries, which are red or black, are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Cornus elliptica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
