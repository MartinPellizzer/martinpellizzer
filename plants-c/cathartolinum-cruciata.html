Cathartolinum cruciata

Cathartolinum cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaved cathartolinum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive cross-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is found in temperate regions. It produces small, rounded fruits known as siliques, which contain several seeds.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
