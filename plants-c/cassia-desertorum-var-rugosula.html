Cassia desertorum var. rugosula

Cassia desertorum var. rugosula, commonly known as the desert cassia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Cassia desertorum var. rugosula is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
