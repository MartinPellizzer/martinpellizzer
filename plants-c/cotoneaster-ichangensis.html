Cotoneaster ichangensis

Cotoneaster ichangensis, commonly known as the Chinese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Cotoneaster ichangensis is also valued for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
