Chirita peduncularis

Chirita peduncularis, commonly known as the clustered ivy or creeping ivy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features trailing stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground or climb supports. The plant has small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators like bees.

Chirita peduncularis is often used in ornamental gardening for its ability to cover ground and its delicate appearance.
