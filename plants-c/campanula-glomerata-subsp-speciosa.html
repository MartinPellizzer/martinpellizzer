Campanula glomerata subsp. speciosa

Campanula glomerata subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the showy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters, making it a striking addition to gardens and natural habitats.
