Centaurea ciliata

Centaurea ciliata, commonly known as the silky centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely hairy leaves that give it a silvery appearance, and its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters. Centaurea ciliata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
