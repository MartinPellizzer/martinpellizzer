Cynanchum fraternum

Cynanchum fraternum, commonly known as wild ginseng or American bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a follicle that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used by indigenous peoples for various health-related purposes.
