Campanula plicatula var. urticifolia

Campanula plicatula var. urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging campanula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, sometimes stinging leaves that can cause irritation upon contact with skin. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
