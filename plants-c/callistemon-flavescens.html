Callistemon flavescens

Callistemon flavescens, commonly known as the yellow bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive crimson to yellow flowers that resemble bottlebrushes, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native birds and insects.
