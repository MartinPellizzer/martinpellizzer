Carex mandarinorum

Carex mandarinorum, commonly known as the Mandarin sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges or streambanks. It has narrow, dark green leaves that form compact clumps.

Carex mandarinorum is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
