Clematis × globulosa

Clematis × globulosa, commonly known as the globe-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and is prized for its showy, globe-shaped clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in late spring to early summer, which are often surrounded by velvety, glandular hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Clematis × globulosa is also notable for its ability to climb using its tendrils, adding vertical interest to landscapes.
