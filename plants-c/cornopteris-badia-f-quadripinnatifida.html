Cornopteris badia f. quadripinnatifida

Cornopteris badia f. quadripinnatifida, commonly known as the four-pinnate wood fern, is a fern species characterized by its intricate frond structure.

It features a bipinnate to quadripinnate frond, with multiple divisions creating a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive, finely divided structure.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
