Coris monspeliensis var. syrtica

Coris monspeliensis var. syrtica, commonly known as the Syrtic sandwort, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions.
