Canna indica var. saturaterubra

Canna indica var. saturaterubra, commonly known as the red flame cannas, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features large, broad leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish tint, and its most notable characteristic is its vibrant, fiery red flowers. The plant is a member of the Cannaceae family and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can grow up to several feet in height.

The flowers bloom in summer and are known for their ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
