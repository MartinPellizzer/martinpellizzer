Clematis strigillosa

Clematis strigillosa, commonly known as the woolly clematis, is a perennial vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Clematis strigillosa is also valued for its ability to climb trellises and support structures, adding vertical interest to landscapes.
