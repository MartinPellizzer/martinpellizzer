Cruciata coronata var. pilosa

Cruciata coronata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy crowned cruciata, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive crown-like inflorescence and hairy foliage, which gives it its varietal name "pilosa.". The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It is a perennial species with a compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or low shrubs.

The botanical features of this plant make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
