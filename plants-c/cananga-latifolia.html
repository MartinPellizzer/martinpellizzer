Cananga latifolia

Cananga latifolia, commonly known as the cream of tartar tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its production of a valuable essential oil derived from its flowers, which is widely used in perfumery and aromatherapy. The fruit, a woody capsule, contains seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Cananga latifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
