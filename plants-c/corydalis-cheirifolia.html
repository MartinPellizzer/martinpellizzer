Corydalis cheirifolia

Corydalis cheirifolia, commonly known as the Moroccan poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and a fibrous root system, typical of many members of the Fumariaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
