Coelogyne planiscapa var. grandis

Coelogyne planiscapa var. grandis, commonly known as the giant flat-leafed orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its natural habitat with seasonal rainfall. Its leaves are broad and flat, typically arranged in two rows along the stem.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its striking floral display.
