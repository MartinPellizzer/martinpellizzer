Crotalaria topouensis

Crotalaria topouensis, commonly known as the Topou's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Crotalaria topouensis is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
