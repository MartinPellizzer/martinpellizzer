Cousinia porphyrochrysea

Cousinia porphyrochrysea, commonly known as the purple-leafed cushion flower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing cushions of foliage, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high altitudes and extreme temperature fluctuations.

Cousinia porphyrochrysea is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
