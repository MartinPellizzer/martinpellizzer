Crepis viscidula subsp. viscidula

Crepis viscidula subsp.

viscidula, commonly known as the sticky hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are covered in a sticky substance, which helps in deterring herbivores.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
