Cirsium tamastoloniferum

Cirsium tamastoloniferum, commonly known as the western thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and a robust, branched stem. The plant has deeply lobed, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
