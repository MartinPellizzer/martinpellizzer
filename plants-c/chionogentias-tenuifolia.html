Chionogentias tenuifolia

Chionogentias tenuifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features narrow, silvery-gray leaves that are densely packed and covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows and scree slopes.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
