Crepis grandifolia

Crepis grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features basal rosettes of silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets. Its inflorescence is a single, tall, branched stem bearing numerous flower heads.

Crepis grandifolia is characterized by its robust growth and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
