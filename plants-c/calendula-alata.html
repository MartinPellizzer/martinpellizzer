Calendula alata

Calendula alata, commonly known as the winged sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of its hairy stems. The plant grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Calendula alata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
