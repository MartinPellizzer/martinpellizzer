Carex bonplandii var. robustior

Carex bonplandii var. robustior, commonly known as the robust sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowering spikes are usually erect and produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
