Cuphea plumbaginea

Cuphea plumbaginea, commonly known as the bluecup plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant color and compact growth habit.

Cuphea plumbaginea is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
