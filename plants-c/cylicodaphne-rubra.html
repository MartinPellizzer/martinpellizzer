Cylicodaphne rubra

Cylicodaphne rubra, commonly known as the red-stemmed cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk that often exhibits a reddish hue, especially when young. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its reproductive structures include a cone-like structure that produces both male and female spores.

Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
