Cineraria britteniae

Cineraria britteniae, commonly known as the Britten's cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage is usually grayish-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to gardens.
