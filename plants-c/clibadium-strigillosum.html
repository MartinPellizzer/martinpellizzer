Clibadium strigillosum

Clibadium strigillosum, commonly known as the rough senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant has serrated, hairy leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, twisted pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Clibadium strigillosum is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage crop in some areas.
