Cirsium hranicense

Cirsium hranicense, commonly known as the Hranice thistle, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry meadows and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with coarse teeth, and its flowers are arranged in a spherical head.

Cirsium hranicense is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
