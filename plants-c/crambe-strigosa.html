Crambe strigosa

Crambe strigosa, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Crambe strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
