Calycosia aneitensis

Calycosia aneitensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces achene fruits.

Calycosia aneitensis is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
