Cytisus subleiocarpus

Cytisus subleiocarpus, commonly known as the golden broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in coastal and rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
