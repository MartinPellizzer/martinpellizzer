Cyperus micreilema

Cyperus micreilema, commonly known as the dwarf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus micreilema is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
