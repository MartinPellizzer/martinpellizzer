Crepis pawlowskii

Crepis pawlowskii, commonly known as the Pawlowski's hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, linear leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation across its native habitat.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in alpine flora studies.
