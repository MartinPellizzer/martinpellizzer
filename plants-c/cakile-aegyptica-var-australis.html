Cakile aegyptica var. australis

Cakile aegyptica var. australis, commonly known as the Australian bladderwrack, is a perennial plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, an adaptation to its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by insects. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in sandy or rocky soils.

Cakile aegyptica var. australis is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray and high temperatures.
