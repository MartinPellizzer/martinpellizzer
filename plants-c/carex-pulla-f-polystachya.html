Carex pulla f. polystachya

Carex pulla f. polystachya, commonly known as the purple sedge, is a variant of the Carex pulla species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a deep green color, with a distinct purplish hue on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, cylindrical spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
