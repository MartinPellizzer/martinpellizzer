Cleome johnstonii

Cleome johnstonii, commonly known as the Texas spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy, spindly flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Cleome johnstonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
