Calyptranthes concinna

Calyptranthes concinna, commonly known as the silverleaf calyptranthes, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy soils.

Calyptranthes concinna is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
