Cyphomandra diversifolia subsp. chlorantha

Cyphomandra diversifolia subsp.

chlorantha, commonly known as the green passionfruit, is a member of the Solanaceae family. It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces small, green fruits that are typically harvested when they turn yellow, though they remain edible even when green. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and emit a mild, aromatic scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and as a source of edible fruit in certain regions.
