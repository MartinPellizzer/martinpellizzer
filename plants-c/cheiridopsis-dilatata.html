Cheiridopsis dilatata

Cheiridopsis dilatata, commonly known as the broad-leaved ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
