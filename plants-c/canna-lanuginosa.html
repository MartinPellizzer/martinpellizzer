Canna lanuginosa

Canna lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in soft, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, orange-red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna lanuginosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
