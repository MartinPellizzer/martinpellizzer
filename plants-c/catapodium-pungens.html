Catapodium pungens

Catapodium pungens, commonly known as the spiny grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its sharp, spiny leaves and cylindrical seed heads that are often reddish in color. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
