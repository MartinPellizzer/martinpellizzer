Carex tomentosa subsp. globifera

Carex tomentosa subsp.

globifera, commonly known as the globeflower sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, often arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
