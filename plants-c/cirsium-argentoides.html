Cirsium argentoides

Cirsium argentoides, commonly known as silverleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, white hairs that reflect light. The plant produces spiny, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, serrated edge.

Cirsium argentoides is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
