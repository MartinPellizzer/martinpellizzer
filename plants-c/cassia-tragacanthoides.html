Cassia tragacanthoides

Cassia tragacanthoides, commonly known as Indian gum tree, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in dense spikes, which are followed by elongated, woody pods. It is valued for its gum, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, demonstrating adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
