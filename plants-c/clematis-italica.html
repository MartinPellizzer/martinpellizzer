Clematis italica

Clematis italica, commonly known as the Italian Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to southern Europe.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, green berries that mature to a dark blue or black color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis italica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
