Calliandra anomala

Calliandra anomala, commonly known as the pink coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered together. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm climates.

Calliandra anomala is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it useful in soil enrichment.
