Ceanothus ovatus f. pubescens

Ceanothus ovatus f. pubescens, commonly known as the softleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and showy clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, which contribute to its common name.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
