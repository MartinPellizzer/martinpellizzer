Capsella bursa-pastoris leontodon

Capsella bursa-pastoris leontodon, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb with a distinctive appearance.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a hairy texture. The plant is characterized by its capsule-like seed pods, which contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along pathways.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
