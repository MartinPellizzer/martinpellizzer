Croton stenophyllus

Croton stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
