Cerasus regalis var. serotina

Cerasus regalis var. serotina, commonly known as the late cherry or late bloom cherry, is a variety of the sweet cherry tree.

It is characterized by its late flowering period, which typically occurs after most other cherry varieties. The plant produces small, dark red to black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
