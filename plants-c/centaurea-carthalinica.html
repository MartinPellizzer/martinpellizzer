Centaurea carthalinica

Centaurea carthalinica, commonly known as the golden centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow flowers with red or purple markings, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Centaurea carthalinica is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
