Coleus unguentarius

Coleus unguentarius, commonly known as the sticky coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, often glandular leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
