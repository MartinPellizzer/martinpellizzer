Coleus silvaticus

Coleus silvaticus, commonly known as the wild coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are usually hairy and vary in color from green to purple, adding to its ornamental value.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.
