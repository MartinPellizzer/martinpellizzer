Conyza adnata

Conyza adnata, commonly known as the common ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Conyza adnata is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
