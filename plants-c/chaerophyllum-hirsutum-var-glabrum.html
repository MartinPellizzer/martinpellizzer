Chaerophyllum hirsutum var. glabrum

Chaerophyllum hirsutum var. glabrum, commonly known as smooth hogweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are usually hairy but can become smooth in certain conditions, hence the variety name *glabrum*.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
