Cerasus samydoides

Cerasus samydoides, commonly known as the Japanese cherry.

This species is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan, characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy flowers. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and typically grows to a height of 3-5 meters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
