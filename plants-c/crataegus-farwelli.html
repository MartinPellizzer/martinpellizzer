Crataegus farwelli

Crataegus farwelli, commonly known as Farwell's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, small leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a small red berry, is an important food source for various wildlife species.

Crataegus farwelli is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
