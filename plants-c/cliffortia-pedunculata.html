Cliffortia pedunculata

Cliffortia pedunculata, commonly known as the Cape snowbells, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Cliffortia pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
