Crupina vulgaris proles morisii

Crupina vulgaris proles morisii, commonly known as the Morisii crupina, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils due to its salt tolerance and hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it an adaptable and resilient species in various environmental conditions.
