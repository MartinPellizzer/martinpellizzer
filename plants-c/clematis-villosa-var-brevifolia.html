Clematis villosa var. brevifolia

Clematis villosa var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has distinctive hairy stems and heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
