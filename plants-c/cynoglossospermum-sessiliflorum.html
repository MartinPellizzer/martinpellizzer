Cynoglossospermum sessiliflorum

Cynoglossospermum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flower milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.

Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
