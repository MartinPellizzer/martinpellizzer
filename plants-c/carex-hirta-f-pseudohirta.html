Carex hirta f. pseudohirta

Carex hirta f. pseudohirta, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a variant of the common sedge found in moist, temperate environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. This subspecies is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

The plant is perennial and thrives in partial to full shade, making it a valuable addition to wetland ecosystems.
