Chrysosplenium peltatum

Chrysosplenium peltatum, commonly known as the golden spleenwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Primulaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, hairy leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, showy yellow or orange flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
