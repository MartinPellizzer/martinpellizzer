Cleistanthus eburneus

Cleistanthus eburneus, commonly known as the white-flowered cleistanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its fragrant blooms. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
