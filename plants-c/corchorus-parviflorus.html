Corchorus parviflorus

Corchorus parviflorus, commonly known as small-flowered dock, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, temperate environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its stems are often reddish and can be harvested for their fibrous material, which has been used traditionally in papermaking and textile production.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
