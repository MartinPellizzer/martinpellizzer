Chamelaucium virgatum

Chamelaucium virgatum, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal environments, characterized by its yellow to orange flowers. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces fluffy, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is well adapted to sandy soils and salty air, making it a hardy species in coastal regions.

Chamelaucium virgatum plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
