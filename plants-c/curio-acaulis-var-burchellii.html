Curio acaulis var. burchellii

Curio acaulis var. burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's curio, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
