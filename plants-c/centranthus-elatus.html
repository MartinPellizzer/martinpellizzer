Centranthus elatus

Centranthus elatus, commonly known as the tall redflowered gentian, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or violet in color, with five lobes and a prominent corona. Its leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Centranthus elatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
