Cereus engelmannii var. fulvispinus

Cereus engelmannii var. fulvispinus, commonly known as the golden spiny cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, yellowish spines. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are typically borne at the tips of the stems. It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water.

Its unique morphology and flowering habit make it a distinctive and visually striking plant in its native habitat.
