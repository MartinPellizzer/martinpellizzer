Calodracon venusta

Calodracon venusta, commonly known as the splendor bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps reflect sunlight in its high-altitude habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
