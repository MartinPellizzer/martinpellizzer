Carthamus glaber

Carthamus glaber, commonly known as the slender safflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, branching stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, dry seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem.

Carthamus glaber is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
