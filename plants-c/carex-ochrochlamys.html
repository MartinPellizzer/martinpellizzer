Carex ochrochlamys

Carex ochrochlamys, commonly known as the yellow-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color, often arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex ochrochlamys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
