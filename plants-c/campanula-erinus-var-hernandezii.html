Campanula erinus var. hernandezii

Campanula erinus var. hernandezii, commonly known as the Hernandez's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.

This variety is particularly valued for its resilience in coastal and sunny environments.
