Croptilon divaricatum var. hirtellum

Croptilon divaricatum var. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy croptilon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is characterized by its distinctive varietal traits, including the dense pubescence that gives it its common name.
