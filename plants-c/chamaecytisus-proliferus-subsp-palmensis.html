Chamaecytisus proliferus subsp. palmensis

Chamaecytisus proliferus subsp.

palmensis, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are adapted to the dry, coastal climate of its native habitat. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Genista genus, sharing similar structural and floral features.
