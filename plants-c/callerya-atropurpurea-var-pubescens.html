Callerya atropurpurea var. pubescens

Callerya atropurpurea var. pubescens, commonly known as the purple-flowered callerya, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple or reddish-pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft pubescence, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant for shaded garden areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for horticultural collections and botanical gardens.
