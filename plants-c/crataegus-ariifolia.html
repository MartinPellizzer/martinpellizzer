Crataegus ariifolia

Crataegus ariifolia, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Crataegus ariifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
