Cymbidium striatum

Cymbidium striatum, commonly known as the streaked orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. Its leaves are strap-shaped, glossy, and arranged in two rows around the pseudobulb.

Cymbidium striatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
