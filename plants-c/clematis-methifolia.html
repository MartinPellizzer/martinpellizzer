Clematis methifolia

Clematis methifolia, commonly known as the sweet autumn clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its stems are twining and can grow up to 15 feet long, making it suitable for trellises or pergolas.

Clematis methifolia thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering during dry periods.
