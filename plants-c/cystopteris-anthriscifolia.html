Cystopteris anthriscifolia

Cystopteris anthriscifolia, commonly known as the fern with the appearance of the cow parsley plant, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. The fronds are typically light green and have a feathery appearance, with a smooth, glossy surface. This plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
