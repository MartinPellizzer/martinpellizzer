Copiapoa ferox

Copiapoa ferox, commonly known as the fierce copiapoa, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and dense, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white, woolly flowers. The plant produces large, football-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to extreme arid conditions with a slow growth rate and can survive long periods without rainfall.

Copiapoa ferox is a popular specimen for cactus enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and resilience.
