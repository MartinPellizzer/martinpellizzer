Croton campinarensis

Croton campinarensis, commonly known as the Campina Croton, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Venezuela.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Croton campinarensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
