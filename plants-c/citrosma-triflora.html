Citrosma triflora

Citrosma triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered citrosma, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes other citrus and aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellowish in color. Its fruits are small, round, and have a rough, leathery skin.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
