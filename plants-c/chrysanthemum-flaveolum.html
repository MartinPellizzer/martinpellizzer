Chrysanthemum flaveolum

Chrysanthemum flaveolum, commonly known as the yellow chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets, which contribute to its ornamental value.

Chrysanthemum flaveolum is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a cut flower.
