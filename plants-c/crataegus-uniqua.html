Crataegus uniqua

Crataegus uniqua, commonly known as the unique hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 leaflets, and its showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The fruit is a red, fleshy pome that matures in the fall, often attracting birds and other wildlife. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in ecological systems.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
