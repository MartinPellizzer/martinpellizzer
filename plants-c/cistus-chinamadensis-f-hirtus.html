Cistus chinamadensis f. hirtus

Cistus chinamadensis f. hirtus, commonly known as the hairy Chinese rockrose, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is distinguished by its hairy foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "hirtus.".
