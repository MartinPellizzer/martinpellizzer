Cinnagrostis spicigera var. cephalotes

Cinnagrostis spicigera var. cephalotes, commonly known as the purpletop wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, with a distinctive hairy texture.

It is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in restoration projects and as a forage crop.
