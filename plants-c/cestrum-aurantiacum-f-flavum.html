Cestrum aurantiacum f. flavum

Cestrum aurantiacum f. flavum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and produces small, red, berry-like fruits. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by hummingbirds, making it an important species in ecological studies.

The yellow bellflower is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
