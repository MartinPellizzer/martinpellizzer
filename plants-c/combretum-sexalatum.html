Combretum sexalatum

Combretum sexalatum, commonly known as the red bush willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
