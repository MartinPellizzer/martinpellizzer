Cinchona calisaya var. boliviana

Cinchona calisaya var. boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It is valued for its historical role in the production of quinine, a compound used in the treatment of malaria.

The leaves and bark contain alkaloids, making it an important plant in both traditional and medicinal contexts.
