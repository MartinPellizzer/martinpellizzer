Corylus avellana var. glabrata

Corylus avellana var. glabrata, commonly known as the hairless hazelnut, is a variety of the common hazelnut.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless bark and smaller, more compact nuts compared to the typical hazelnut. This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows. The leaves are typically oval with a serrated margin and turn yellow in autumn.

It produces edible nuts that are often used in culinary applications and traditional herbal remedies.
