Cheiranthus × cheiri var. thyrsoideus

Cheiranthus × cheiri var. thyrsoideus, commonly known as the tree poppy, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both annual and perennial growth habits.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant features a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age, giving it a tree-like appearance. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, with four petals and a distinctive hooded shape.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
