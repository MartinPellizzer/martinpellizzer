Cissus hassleriana

Cissus hassleriana, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or white underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in greenhouses.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.
