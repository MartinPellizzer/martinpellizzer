Cirsium arizonicum var. rothrockii

Cirsium arizonicum var. rothrockii, commonly known as the Rothrock's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and showy purple to pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

This variety is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
