Conospermum flexuosum f. minus-diffusum

Conospermum flexuosum f. minus-diffusum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
