Cytisus hirsutus var. seminudus

Cytisus hirsutus var. seminudus, commonly known as the naked buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features wiry, thorned stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically narrow and leathery, with a grayish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

This plant is adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
