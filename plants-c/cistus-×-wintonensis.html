Cistus × wintonensis

Cistus × wintonensis, commonly known as the Winton rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. It has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Cistus × wintonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
