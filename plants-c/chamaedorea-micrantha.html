Chamaedorea micrantha

Chamaedorea micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo palm, is a compact, evergreen plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant has a dense, bushy appearance and is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue berries, though these are not typically cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Chamaedorea micrantha is valued for its elegant foliage and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
