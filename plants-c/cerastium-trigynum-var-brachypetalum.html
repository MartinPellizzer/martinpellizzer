Cerastium trigynum var. brachypetalum

Cerastium trigynum var. brachypetalum, commonly known as the short-petaled mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
