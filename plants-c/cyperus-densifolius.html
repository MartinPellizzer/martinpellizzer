Cyperus densifolius

Cyperus densifolius, commonly known as denseleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth surface, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus densifolius is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
