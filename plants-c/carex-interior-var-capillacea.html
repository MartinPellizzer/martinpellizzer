Carex interior var. capillacea

Carex interior var. capillacea, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
