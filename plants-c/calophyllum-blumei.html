Calophyllum blumei

Calophyllum blumei, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a single seed surrounded by a white, aromatic pulp, which is edible and highly valued.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
