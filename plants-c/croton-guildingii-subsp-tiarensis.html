Croton guildingii subsp. tiarensis

Croton guildingii subsp.

tiarensis, commonly known as the Tiarensis Croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with a glossy green surface and bright yellow veins.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
