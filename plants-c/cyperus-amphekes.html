Cyperus amphekes

Cyperus amphekes, commonly known as the broadleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and cylindrical flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Cyperus amphekes has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-lobed seed capsules.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
