Calceolaria collina subsp. collina

Calceolaria collina subsp.

collina, commonly known as the mountain slipper orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andes of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-like floral structure, which resembles a slipper or pouch, giving it its common name.

The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetric flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with contrasting patterns. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
