Cissus repens var. luzoniensis

Cissus repens var. luzoniensis, commonly known as the Philippine grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like berries. It thrives in humid, tropical conditions and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
