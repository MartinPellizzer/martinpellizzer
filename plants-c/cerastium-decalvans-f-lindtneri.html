Cerastium decalvans f. lindtneri

Cerastium decalvans f. lindtneri, commonly known as the Lindtner's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers, typically in early summer. It is native to Central and Southern Europe and thrives in rocky or gravelly soils.

This cultivar is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
