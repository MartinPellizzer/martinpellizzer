Coleus speciosus

Coleus speciosus, commonly known as the flame bamboo or red bamboo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with hues of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale pink or white, arranged in spikes at the ends of its stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaves.

Coleus speciosus is relatively easy to grow and can be propagated through stem cuttings.
