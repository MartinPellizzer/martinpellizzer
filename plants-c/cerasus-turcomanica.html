Cerasus turcomanica

Cerasus turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkmen cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits similar to cherries, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
