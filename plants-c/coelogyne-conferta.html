Coelogyne conferta

Coelogyne conferta, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a mix of white, yellow, and orange hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Coelogyne conferta is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique fragrance.
