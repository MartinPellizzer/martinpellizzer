Carex nakaharae

Carex nakaharae, commonly known as Nakahara's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

Carex nakaharae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
