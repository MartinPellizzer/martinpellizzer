Camelina barbareifolia

Camelina barbareifolia, commonly known as the slender camelina, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often hairy. The plant grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Camelina barbareifolia is known for its potential as a source of oilseed and has been studied for its ecological and agricultural value.
