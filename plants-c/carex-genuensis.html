Carex genuensis

Carex genuensis, commonly known as the Gueen's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region, where it thrives in moist to wet soils. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and dark green, with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize the soil. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical shape.

Carex genuensis is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other damp habitats, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
