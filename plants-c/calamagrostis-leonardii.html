Calamagrostis leonardii

Calamagrostis leonardii, commonly known as Leonard's reed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver- or blue-gray in color. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces panicles of small, greenish-brown flowers in the summer, which are often overlooked but contribute to its ecological role.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
