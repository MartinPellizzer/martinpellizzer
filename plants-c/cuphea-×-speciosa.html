Cuphea × speciosa

Cuphea × speciosa, commonly known as the flamebeard plant, is a hybrid species native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular red flowers with a distinctive beard-like structure at the base. The plant features lance-shaped, velvety green leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is well-suited for container gardening and landscape beds due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Cuphea × speciosa is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
