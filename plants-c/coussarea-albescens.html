Coussarea albescens

Coussarea albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered coussarea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish drupe that contains several seeds.

Coussarea albescens is often found in moist, shaded areas of lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
