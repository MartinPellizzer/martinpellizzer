Crataegus cupressocollina

Crataegus cupressocollina, commonly known as the Colorado crataegus, is a species of hawthorn native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and mountainous regions. The plant features small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in the fall and is an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
