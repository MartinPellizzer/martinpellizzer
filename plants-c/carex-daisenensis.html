Carex daisenensis

Carex daisenensis, commonly known as the Daisen sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet areas.

Carex daisenensis is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic and functional benefits in water gardens and natural landscapes.
