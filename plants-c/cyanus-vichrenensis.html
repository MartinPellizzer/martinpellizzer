Cyanus vichrenensis

Cyanus vichrenensis, commonly known as the Vichrean Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the dry, high-altitude environments where it thrives.

Cyanus vichrenensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
