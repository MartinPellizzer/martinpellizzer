Cereus pellucidus

Cereus pellucidus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and spiny appearance. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers during the night, which is a unique adaptation for pollination by nocturnal insects. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
