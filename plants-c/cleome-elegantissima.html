Cleome elegantissima

Cleome elegantissima, commonly known as the "Spider Flower," is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that features showy, branched inflorescences with small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy and branching. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cleome elegantissima is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
