Carolinea alba

Carolinea alba, commonly known as the white carolinea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a soft, ethereal appearance to its foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery down, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Carolinea alba is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
