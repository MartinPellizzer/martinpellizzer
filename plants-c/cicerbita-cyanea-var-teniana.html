Cicerbita cyanea var. teniana

Cicerbita cyanea var. teniana, commonly known as the blue baneberry, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet berries that appear in clusters, surrounded by white, petal-like bracts. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes buttercups and lilies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value is often appreciated in gardens, though it is toxic to humans and animals.
