Crataegus oxyacantha var. tortuosa

Crataegus oxyacantha var. tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its twisted, often contorted branches that give it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red berries in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows or woodland edges.

Due to its unique form, it is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
