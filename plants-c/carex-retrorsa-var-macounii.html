Carex retrorsa var. macounii

Carex retrorsa var. macounii, commonly known as Macoun's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and the plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
