Caesalpinia pubescens

Caesalpinia pubescens, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, velvety, pubescent foliage. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, often resembling those of the legume family. Its fruit is a long, pod-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

Caesalpinia pubescens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
