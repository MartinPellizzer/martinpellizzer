Convolvulus discolor

Convolvulus discolor, commonly known as the variable morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Convolvulus discolor is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
