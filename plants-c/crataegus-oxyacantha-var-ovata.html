Crataegus oxyacantha var. ovata

Crataegus oxyacantha var. ovata, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, orange, or yellow berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and gardens.
