Cassia gardneri

Cassia gardneri, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Cassia gardneri is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is an important species in Australian flora and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.
