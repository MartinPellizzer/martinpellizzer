Castanea sativa var. hamulata

Castanea sativa var. hamulata, commonly known as the Hungarian sweet chestnut.

This variant is characterized by its robust growth and large, edible nuts encased in thick, spiny husks. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its nut production. The leaves are typically broad and serrated, providing a dense canopy.

It is a subspecies of the common chestnut, adapted to specific environmental conditions found in its native range.
