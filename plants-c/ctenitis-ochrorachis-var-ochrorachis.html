Ctenitis ochrorachis var. ochrorachis

Ctenitis ochrorachis var. ochrorachis, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that are typically dark green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces spores on the undersides of its fronds, which are arranged in clusters called sori. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
