Capparis subsessilis

Capparis subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in coastal and sandy environments. The plant features succulent, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain seeds.

Capparis subsessilis is often used in local horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh conditions.
