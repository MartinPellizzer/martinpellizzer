Caltha sagittata f. latifolia

Caltha sagittata f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often a striking green or variegated with white or yellow markings. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in upright inflorescences, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in wet conditions, making it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
