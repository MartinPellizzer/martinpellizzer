Cerastium tetrandrum var. algeriense

Cerastium tetrandrum var. algeriense, commonly known as Algerian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Algerian mouse-ear is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
