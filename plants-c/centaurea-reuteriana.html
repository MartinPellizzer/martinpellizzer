Centaurea reuteriana

Centaurea reuteriana, commonly known as the Reuter's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts that surround the seed heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea reuteriana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
