Citrus × myrtifolia

Citrus × myrtifolia, commonly known as the citron.

It is a hybrid citrus plant that originates from the cross between the citron (Citrus medica) and the pomelo (Citrus maxima). The plant is characterized by its large, thick, and fragrant citrus fruits, which are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are glossy and dark green, and the plant is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
