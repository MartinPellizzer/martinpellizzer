Cassia australis

Cassia australis, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features bipinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its woody stems are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Cassia australis is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for native pollinators.
