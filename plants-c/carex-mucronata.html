Carex mucronata

Carex mucronata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of inconspicuous flowers. Carex mucronata typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
