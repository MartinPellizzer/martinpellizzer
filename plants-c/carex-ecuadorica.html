Carex ecuadorica

Carex ecuadorica, commonly known as Ecuador sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the Andean region of Ecuador and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or high-altitude habitats.

Carex ecuadorica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
