Chamaecrista nictitans var. glaberrima

Chamaecrista nictitans var. glaberrima, commonly known as the glabrous crown wing, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive light green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by flat, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in garden settings.
