Carex brunnescens var. gracilior

Carex brunnescens var. gracilior, commonly known as slender brown sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of brownish flowers. This variety is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
