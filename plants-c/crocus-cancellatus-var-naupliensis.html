Crocus cancellatus var. naupliensis

Crocus cancellatus var. naupliensis, commonly known as the Nauplion crocus, is a rare and delicate species native to the Aegean region of Greece.

It is characterized by its striking purple and white petals with a distinctive cancellated pattern, resembling a lattice or sieve. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears a cluster of small, cup-shaped flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
