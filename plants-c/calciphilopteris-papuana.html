Calciphilopteris papuana

Calciphilopteris papuana, commonly known as the Papuan calciphile fern, is a species of fern that thrives in calcareous soils.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Papua New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained, alkaline environments and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
