Cerastium banaticum subsp. banaticum

Cerastium banaticum subsp.

banaticum, commonly known as the Banat sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, dunes, and along roadsides.
