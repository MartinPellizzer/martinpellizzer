Cereus flavispinus

Cereus flavispinus, commonly known as the yellow-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom at night, a feature common among many cacti in the Cactaceae family. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss in its harsh environment.

Cereus flavispinus is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
