Coelogyne maingayi

Coelogyne maingayi, commonly known as the white-lipped coelogyne, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Coelogyne maingayi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and fragrance.
