Cassia bahamensis var. latifolia

Cassia bahamensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the Bahamian cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty air and sandy soils. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces pods that contain flat, elliptical seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
