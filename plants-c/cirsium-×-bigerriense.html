Cirsium × bigerriense

Cirsium × bigerriense, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.

However, due to its prickly foliage, it can be challenging to handle without protective gear.
