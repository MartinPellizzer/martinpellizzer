Cirsium lucens

Cirsium lucens, commonly known as the shining thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical structure includes a robust taproot and a hollow, ribbed stem that supports its upright growth.
