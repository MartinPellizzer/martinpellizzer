Capsicum indicum var. angulosum

Capsicum indicum var. angulosum, commonly known as the bird's eye chili, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pointed, and often red or orange fruits that are highly aromatic and spicy. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses due to its high capsaicin content.
