Cucumis hirsutus var. ovatus

Cucumis hirsutus var. ovatus, commonly known as the rough gourd or hairy cucumber, is a variant of the species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a vine-like plant characterized by its hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves. The fruit is typically elongated, with a rough, spiny surface and a pale green to yellowish color. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a mild, cucumber-like flavor.
