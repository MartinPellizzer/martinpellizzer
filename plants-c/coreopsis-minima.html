Coreopsis minima

Coreopsis minima, commonly known as dwarf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of only 6 to 12 inches. The plant features slender stems and produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Coreopsis minima thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
