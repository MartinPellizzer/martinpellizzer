Ceodes cauliflora

Ceodes cauliflora, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cauliflorous growth habit, where flowers and fruits develop directly from the stems rather than the leaves. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
