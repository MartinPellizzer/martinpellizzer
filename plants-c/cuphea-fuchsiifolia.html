Cuphea fuchsiifolia

Cuphea fuchsiifolia, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Cuphea fuchsiifolia is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
