Cyathea nesiotica

Cyathea nesiotica, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the plant's structure and facilitating nutrient absorption.

Cyathea nesiotica plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's forests, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
