Crataegus mollis var. meridionalis

Crataegus mollis var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features thorny branches and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for wildlife, providing food and habitat for various birds and insects.
