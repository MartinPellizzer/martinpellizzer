Cucumis nigristriatus

Cucumis nigristriatus, commonly known as the black-striped cucumber, is a rare and less-studied species within the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark green, ridged fruits that often have a striking black or dark green striping pattern. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, a common trait in the Cucurbitaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution and low genetic diversity, Cucumis nigristriatus is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
