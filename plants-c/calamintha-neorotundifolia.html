Calamintha neorotundifolia

Calamintha neorotundifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, narrow, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue to violet, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Calamintha neorotundifolia is often found in rocky or limestone soils and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
