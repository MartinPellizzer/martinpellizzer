Callitriche fuliginea

Callitriche fuliginea, commonly known as blackwater waterstar, is a small aquatic plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has delicate, star-shaped flowers that are often submerged or floating. Its leaves are opposite, oval, and have a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through oxygen production.
