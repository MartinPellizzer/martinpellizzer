Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. ellipticum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. ellipticum, commonly known as Ceylon cinnamon, is a variant of the cinnamon tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves and aromatic bark, which is highly valued in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its essential oils, rich in cinnamaldehyde, contribute to its distinctive flavor and therapeutic properties.

This variety is often cultivated for its bark, which is harvested and dried to produce the spice known as cinnamon.
