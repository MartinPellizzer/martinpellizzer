Cordia chacoensis

Cordia chacoensis, commonly known as Chaco cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to South America, particularly found in Argentina, Paraguay, and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry or semi-arid regions.
