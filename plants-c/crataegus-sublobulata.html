Crataegus sublobulata

Crataegus sublobulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorny branches and oval to elliptical leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a distinct lobed shape, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Crataegus sublobulata is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
