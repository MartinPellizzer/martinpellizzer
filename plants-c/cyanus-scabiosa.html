Cyanus scabiosa

Cyanus scabiosa, commonly known as the painted lady or Scotch thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers, which are typically arranged in a composite head with many small florets. The plant has a hairy, rough texture and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cyanus scabiosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
