Cirsium arisanense f. purpurescens

Cirsium arisanense f. purpurescens, commonly known as the Arisan thistle, is a rare and striking plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple-tinged leaves. The plant produces showy, purple-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires protection to ensure its survival.
