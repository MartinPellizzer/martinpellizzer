Cineraria gracilis

Cineraria gracilis, commonly known as slender cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and sometimes hairy, and it produces small, white or pale blue flowers.

Cineraria gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
