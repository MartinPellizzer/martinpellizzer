Commiphora oblongifolia

Commiphora oblongifolia, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. The plant is known for its aromatic resin, which has been traditionally used in incense and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands.

Commiphora oblongifolia is valued for its ecological role and cultural significance in its native regions.
