Carduus neomexicanus

Carduus neomexicanus, commonly known as the New Mexico thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its spiny, hairy leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
