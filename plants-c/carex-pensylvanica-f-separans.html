Carex pensylvanica f. separans

Carex pensylvanica f. separans, commonly known as the Pennsylvania sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers that are typically arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
