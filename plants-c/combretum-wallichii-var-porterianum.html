Combretum wallichii var. porterianum

Combretum wallichii var. porterianum, commonly known as the Porters' flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often giving it a fiery appearance. The leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical, with a smooth margin. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also notable for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions and partial shade.
