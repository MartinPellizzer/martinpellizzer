Croton somalensis

Croton somalensis, commonly known as the Somali croton, is a shrub native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.
