Cnidoscolus loefgrenii

Cnidoscolus loefgrenii, commonly known as the Mexican arrowroot or giant arrowhead, is a perennial herb native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spiny, three-lobed fruits. Its tuberous roots are edible and have been used traditionally for their starchy content.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and its ornamental value in landscaping.
