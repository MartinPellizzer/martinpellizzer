Callicarpa rivularis

Callicarpa rivularis, commonly known as the waterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its small, red berries that resemble tiny grapes, which are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found along stream banks and in wet woodlands.

Callicarpa rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and foliage.
