Chamaedorea incrustata

Chamaedorea incrustata, commonly known as the velvet palm or velvet fern, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery foliage that gives it a lush, ornamental appearance. The leaves are typically dark green and have a velvety texture, contributing to its common name. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.

The plant is also valued for its ability to purify the air and enhance indoor environments.
