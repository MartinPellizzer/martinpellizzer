Crepidium cordiglottis

Crepidium cordiglottis, commonly known as the heart-shaped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lanceolate, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to green flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the warmer months. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
