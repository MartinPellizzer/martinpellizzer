Cyathea melanoclada

Cyathea melanoclada, commonly known as the black-stemmed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk that is often dark in color, giving it its common name. The plant has large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

Cyathea melanoclada is known for its slow growth and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
