Capparis jacobinae

Capparis jacobinae, commonly known as the Jacobina caper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Capparaceae and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Capparis jacobinae is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky areas.
