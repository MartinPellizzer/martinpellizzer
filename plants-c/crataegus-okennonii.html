Crataegus okennonii

Crataegus okennonii, commonly known as the Okonkwo hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Crataegus okennonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
