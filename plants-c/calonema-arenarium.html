Calonema arenarium

Calonema arenarium, commonly known as the sandstone saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Calonema arenarium is often found growing on rocky outcrops and sandy soils, showcasing its ability to colonize harsh, nutrient-poor habitats.
