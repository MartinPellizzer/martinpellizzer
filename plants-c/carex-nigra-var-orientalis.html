Carex nigra var. orientalis

Carex nigra var. orientalis, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variety is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
