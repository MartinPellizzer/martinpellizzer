Cynoctonum paniculatum

Cynoctonum paniculatum, commonly known as the white soursop, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is similar in appearance to a soursop but has a distinct, aromatic flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
