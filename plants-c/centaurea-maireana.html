Centaurea maireana

Centaurea maireana, commonly known as Maire's coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red flowers, which are typically arranged in a conical shape at the top of slender stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Centaurea maireana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
