Convolvulus ovalifolius

Convolvulus ovalifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it a common ornamental and invasive species in certain regions.
