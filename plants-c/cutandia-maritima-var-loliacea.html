Cutandia maritima var. loliacea

Cutandia maritima var. loliacea, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in saline environments. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
