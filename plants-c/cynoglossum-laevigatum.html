Cynoglossum laevigatum

Cynoglossum laevigatum, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often mistaken for those of the related forget-me-nots. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Cynoglossum laevigatum is an annual or biennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
