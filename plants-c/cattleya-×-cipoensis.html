Cattleya × cipoensis

Cattleya × cipoensis, commonly known as the Cipo Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. The plant is an epiphyte, meaning it grows on the surface of trees and absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Cattleya × cipoensis is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
