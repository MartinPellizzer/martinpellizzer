Croton brittonii

Croton brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's croton, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid landscapes.
