Cyperus globosus f. khasianus

Cyperus globosus f. khasianus, commonly known as the Khasi globe sedge, is a variant of the globe sedge native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its compact, rounded inflorescence that resembles a globe. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dark green coloration, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
