Catalpa × erubescens f. adina

Catalpa × erubescens f. adina, commonly known as the Red catalpa, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical features.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are typically borne in long, drooping clusters. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value. This cultivar is a result of natural or artificial crossbreeding between different catalpa species.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
