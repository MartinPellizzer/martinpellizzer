Cordia guaranitica var. pedunculosa

Cordia guaranitica var. pedunculosa, commonly known as the Brazilian red cedar, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and long, pendulous fruiting clusters that contain small, sticky seeds. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The wood of this species is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties, making it a significant resource in traditional and modern applications.
