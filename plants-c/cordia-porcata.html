Cordia porcata

Cordia porcata, commonly known as the porcine cordia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by orange or red berries. Its fruit is an important food source for various birds and mammals in its native habitat.

Cordia porcata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
