Calanthe lamellata

Calanthe lamellata, commonly known as the broad-leafed calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are often arranged in a raceme and have three sepals and three petals, with the lateral petals being larger and more showy.

Calanthe lamellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
