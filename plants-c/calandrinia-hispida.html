Calandrinia hispida

Calandrinia hispida, commonly known as the hairy purslane, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is an annual herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and stems covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Calandrinia hispida is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
