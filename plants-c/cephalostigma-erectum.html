Cephalostigma erectum

Cephalostigma erectum, commonly known as the upright false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a slender, upright stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and yellow, forming a dense cluster at the top of the stem.

Cephalostigma erectum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.
