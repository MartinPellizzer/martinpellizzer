Calanthe pubescens

Calanthe pubescens, commonly known as the hairy calanthe, is a perennial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and typically produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil.

Calanthe pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
