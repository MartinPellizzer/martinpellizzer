Cytisus supinus var. noeanus

Cytisus supinus var. noeanus, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens.
