Carex sachalinensis var. sikokiana

Carex sachalinensis var. sikokiana, commonly known as the Sakhalin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers in the summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
