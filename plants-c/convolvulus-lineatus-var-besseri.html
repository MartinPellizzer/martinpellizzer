Convolvulus lineatus var. besseri

Convolvulus lineatus var. besseri, commonly known as the Bessera bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is noted for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
