Callista infundibuliformis

Callista infundibuliformis, commonly known as the bell-shaped callista, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a succulent, shrub-like structure. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

The flowers bloom in shades of yellow and orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
