Cissus omburensis

Cissus omburensis, commonly known as the African leafcreeper, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cissus omburensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
