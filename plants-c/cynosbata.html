Cynosbata

Cynosbata, commonly known as the common thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, often growing in rocky or dry soils. Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.

Cynosbata is also valued in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and tonic properties.
