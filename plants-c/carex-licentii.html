Carex licentii

Carex licentii, commonly known as the Licent's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of Europe and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Carex licentii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
