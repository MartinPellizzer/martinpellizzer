Caltha chionophila

Caltha chionophila, commonly known as the snowdrop buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features a low-growing rosette of glossy, dark green leaves that remain close to the ground. The plant produces delicate, white flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Caltha chionophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
