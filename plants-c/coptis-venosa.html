Coptis venosa

Coptis venosa, commonly known as the veined goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant contains alkaloids, including berberine, which gives it medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine make it a notable species in botanical studies.
