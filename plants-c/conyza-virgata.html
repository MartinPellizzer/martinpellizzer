Conyza virgata

Conyza virgata, commonly known as the common cudweed, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It is native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia, and often grows in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Conyza virgata is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions.
