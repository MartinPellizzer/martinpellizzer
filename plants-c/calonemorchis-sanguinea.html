Calonemorchis sanguinea

Calonemorchis sanguinea, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red petals and sepals, which make it visually distinctive. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
