Cestrum dielsii

Cestrum dielsii, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Cestrum dielsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
