Carex barbata

Carex barbata, commonly known as the bearded sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. Carex barbata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
