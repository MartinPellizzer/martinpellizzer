Circaea lutetiana var. glaberrima

Circaea lutetiana var. glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are white or pale pink, often with five petals. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is characterized by its reduced pubescence compared to other varieties of Circaea lutetiana.
