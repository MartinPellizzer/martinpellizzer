Cinnamomum chingii

Cinnamomum chingii, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum chingii, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinctive cinnamon-like scent.

The bark is thin, reddish-brown, and often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
