Clethra bridgesii

Clethra bridgesii, commonly known as the Florida sweet pepper.

This evergreen shrub is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and parts of Georgia. It features fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate appearance and adaptability to moist, acidic soils.
