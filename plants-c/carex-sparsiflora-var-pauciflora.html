Carex sparsiflora var. pauciflora

Carex sparsiflora var. pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized inflorescence structures. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its foliage is linear and grass-like, with dark green leaves that often form dense clumps.

The inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated and not showy.
