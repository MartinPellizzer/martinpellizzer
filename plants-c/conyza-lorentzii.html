Conyza lorentzii

Conyza lorentzii, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Conyza lorentzii is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
