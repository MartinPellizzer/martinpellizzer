Curcuma rhomba

Curcuma rhomba, commonly known as the rhombic turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive rhombic-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its rhizomes contain curcuminoids, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Curcuma rhomba is typically grown in tropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
