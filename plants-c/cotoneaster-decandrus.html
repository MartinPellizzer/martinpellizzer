Cotoneaster decandrus

Cotoneaster decandrus, commonly known as the Chinese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red berries that are attractive to birds, contributing to its ecological value. Cotoneaster decandrus typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its winter interest and compact growth habit.
