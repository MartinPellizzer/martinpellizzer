Colletia valdiviana

Colletia valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, featuring warm, dry summers and mild, wet winters.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
