Campanula pseudolanceolata f. elatior

Campanula pseudolanceolata f. elatior, commonly known as the Greater Bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in garden settings with proper soil and moisture conditions.
