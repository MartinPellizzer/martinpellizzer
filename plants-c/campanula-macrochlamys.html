Campanula macrochlamys

Campanula macrochlamys, commonly known as the large-chalice bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula macrochlamys is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to rocky or alpine gardens.
