Chrysoglossum maculatum

Chrysoglossum maculatum, commonly known as the spotted rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and produces showy, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the year under optimal conditions.
