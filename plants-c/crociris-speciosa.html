Crociris speciosa

Crociris speciosa, commonly known as the showy crocus, is a member of the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant features tall, erect stems with large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

Crociris speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant spring blooms.
