Crataegus monogyna var. nordica

Crataegus monogyna var. nordica, commonly known as the northern hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red berries in the autumn, which are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and coastal areas.

The northern hawthorn is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
