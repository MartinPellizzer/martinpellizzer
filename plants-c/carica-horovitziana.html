Carica horovitziana

Carica horovitziana, commonly known as the Horovitzian papaya, is a species of flowering plant in the Caricaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, fleshy, orange-colored fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing before the fruit develops.
