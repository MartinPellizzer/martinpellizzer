Cordia ovalis

Cordia ovalis, commonly known as the oval cordia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Cordia ovalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
