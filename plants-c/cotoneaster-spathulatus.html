Cotoneaster spathulatus

Cotoneaster spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth upper surface. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Cotoneaster spathulatus prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
