Claytonia pusilla

Claytonia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf spring beauty, is a small flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies.
