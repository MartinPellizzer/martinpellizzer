Chamaecladon nigrescens

Chamaecladon nigrescens, commonly known as the blackleaf chamaecladon, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that turn a deep blackish-purple color as they mature. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and native plant gardens.
