Caucalis fallax

Caucalis fallax, commonly known as the false wintercress, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed or toothed, with small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Caucalis fallax is noted for its ability to thrive in shaded to partially shaded conditions, making it a versatile plant in various ecological settings.
