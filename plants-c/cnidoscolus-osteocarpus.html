Cnidoscolus osteocarpus

Cnidoscolus osteocarpus, commonly known as the horse chestnut or Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces distinctive, spiny fruit capsules that contain edible seeds. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its showy foliage. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
