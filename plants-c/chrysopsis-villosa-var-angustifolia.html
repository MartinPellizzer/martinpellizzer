Chrysopsis villosa var. angustifolia

Chrysopsis villosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf chrysanthemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features linear, hairy leaves that are typically narrow and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces yellow ray flowers and yellow disk flowers, characteristic of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a drought-tolerant, ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
