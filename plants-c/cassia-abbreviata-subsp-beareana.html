Cassia abbreviata subsp. beareana

Cassia abbreviata subsp.

beareana, commonly known as the dwarf cassia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and emit a faint, pleasant aroma.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a slender, elongated pod that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact size and seasonal blooms.
