Carum flabellifolium

Carum flabellifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf caraway, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender stems and large, fern-like, compound leaves that give it its common name. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the family.

The seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other caraway species.
