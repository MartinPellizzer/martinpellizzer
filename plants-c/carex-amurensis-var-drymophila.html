Carex amurensis var. drymophila

Carex amurensis var. drymophila, commonly known as the Amur sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, typical of the Carex genus. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in swamps, bogs, and along riverbanks. It has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
