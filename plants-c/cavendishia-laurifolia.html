Cavendishia laurifolia

Cavendishia laurifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cavendishia laurifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
