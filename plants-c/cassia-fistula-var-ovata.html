Cassia fistula var. ovata

Cassia fistula var. ovata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, seed-like structures. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
