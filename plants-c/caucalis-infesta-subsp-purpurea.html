Caucalis infesta subsp. purpurea

Caucalis infesta subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as purple sandwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage.

The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
