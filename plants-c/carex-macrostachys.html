Carex macrostachys

Carex macrostachys, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex macrostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
