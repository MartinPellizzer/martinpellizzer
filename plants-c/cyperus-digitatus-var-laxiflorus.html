Cyperus digitatus var. laxiflorus

Cyperus digitatus var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas, along stream banks, or in poorly drained soils.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural or aquatic settings.
