Citharexylum spinosum f. subserratum

Citharexylum spinosum f. subserratum, commonly known as the serrated citharexylum, is a variant of the native Australian plant Citharexylum spinosum.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins, which set it apart from the typical form. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and adaptability in horticultural settings.
