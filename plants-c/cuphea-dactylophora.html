Cuphea dactylophora

Cuphea dactylophora, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens in warmer climates.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors contribute to its ornamental value.
