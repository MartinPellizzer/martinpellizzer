Cardiochilos

Cardiochilos, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, colorful flowers that range from white to purple. Cardiochilos species are epiphytic, meaning they grow on other plants without being parasitic, often found in the canopy of rainforests.

These orchids are popular among horticulturists for their ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
