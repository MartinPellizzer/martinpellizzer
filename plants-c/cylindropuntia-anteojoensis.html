Cylindropuntia anteojoensis

Cylindropuntia anteojoensis, commonly known as the Anteojo cholla, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Baja California, Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in clusters of small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green, spiky structure. Its adaptations include reduced leaves and thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh desert conditions.

The Anteojo cholla plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
