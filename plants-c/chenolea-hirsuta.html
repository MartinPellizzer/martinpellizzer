Chenolea hirsuta

Chenolea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy chenopod, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Chenolea hirsuta is adapted to thrive in harsh environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy tissues.

It is often found in sandy soils and is used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
