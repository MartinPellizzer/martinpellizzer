Carex fissa var. fissa

Carex fissa var. fissa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and a tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Carex fissa var. fissa is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
