Celtis occidentalis var. reticulata

Celtis occidentalis var. reticulata, commonly known as the netleaf hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive netted venation pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by small, round berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

The tree is valued for its adaptability and ornamental qualities in landscaping.
