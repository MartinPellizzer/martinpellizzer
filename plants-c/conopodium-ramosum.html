Conopodium ramosum

Conopodium ramosum, commonly known as the clustered bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, bladder-like structures that aid in trapping microscopic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and ditches. Its leaves are thin and ribbon-like, often arranged in whorls around the stem.

Conopodium ramosum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
