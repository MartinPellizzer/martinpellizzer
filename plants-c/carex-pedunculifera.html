Carex pedunculifera

Carex pedunculifera, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
