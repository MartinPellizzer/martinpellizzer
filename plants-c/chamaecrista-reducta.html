Chamaecrista reducta

Chamaecrista reducta, commonly known as the small crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Chamaecrista reducta is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility in its native habitat.
