Chlorophytum krauseanum

Chlorophytum krauseanum, commonly known as the snake plant, is a hardy, evergreen perennial native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a yellowish or white midrib. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it an ideal houseplant. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers on slender stalks, which are followed by orange berries.

Chlorophytum krauseanum is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to indoor air quality.
