Cylindrophyllum dyeri

Cylindrophyllum dyeri, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and dry sclerophyll forests.

Cylindrophyllum dyeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
