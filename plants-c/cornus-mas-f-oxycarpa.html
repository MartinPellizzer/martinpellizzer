Cornus mas f. oxycarpa

Cornus mas f. oxycarpa, commonly known as the sharp-fruited cornel.

It is a subspecies of the cornel dogwood, characterized by its distinctive, sharp-pointed fruits. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in woodland and scrub habitats. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, red berries that are edible but not widely consumed.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
