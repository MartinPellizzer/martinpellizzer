Cassia nigricans

Cassia nigricans, commonly known as black cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bright green and alternate along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Cassia nigricans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common species in various ecological and agricultural settings.
