Campulosus gracilior

Campulosus gracilior, commonly known as the slender campulose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to brown, typically blooming in the late summer to early autumn. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it is well adapted to dry conditions with deep roots and reduced leaf surface.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
