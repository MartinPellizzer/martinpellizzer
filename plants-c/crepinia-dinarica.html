Crepinia dinarica

Crepinia dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric crepis, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
