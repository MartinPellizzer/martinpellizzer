Crinum crassicaule

Crinum crassicaule, commonly known as the broad-leaved crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically in late summer to early autumn. Its bulbous root system allows it to store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in various soil types.

Crinum crassicaule is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats across its native range.
