Cyperus tristachyus

Cyperus tristachyus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along water bodies.

Cyperus tristachyus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
