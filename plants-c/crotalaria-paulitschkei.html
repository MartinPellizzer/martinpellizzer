Crotalaria paulitschkei

Crotalaria paulitschkei, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and palmate leaves, which are divided into five lobes. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, and it is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Crotalaria paulitschkei is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, enhancing soil fertility.
