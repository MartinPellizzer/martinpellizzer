Cylindropuntia maldonadensis

Cylindropuntia maldonadensis, commonly known as the Maldonado's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by insects and birds. It has adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions with deep roots and the ability to store water in its stems.

This cactus is often found in rocky soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
