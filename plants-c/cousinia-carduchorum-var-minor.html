Cousinia carduchorum var. minor

Cousinia carduchorum var. minor, commonly known as the Kashmiri goat's beard, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, pale yellow to white composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. It produces small, elongated achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
