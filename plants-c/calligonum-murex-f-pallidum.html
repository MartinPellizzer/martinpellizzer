Calligonum murex f. pallidum

Calligonum murex f. pallidum, commonly known as the pale sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late summer. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in harsh environments.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to sandy soils and its role in stabilizing dunes in its native habitat.
