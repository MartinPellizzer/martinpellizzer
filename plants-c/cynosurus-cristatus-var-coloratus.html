Cynosurus cristatus var. coloratus

Cynosurus cristatus var. coloratus, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white or purple-tinged inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in heathlands and moorlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that enhances its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
