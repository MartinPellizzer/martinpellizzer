Clematis multistriata

Clematis multistriata, commonly known as the white clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 15 feet in length. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Clematis multistriata prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
