Chrysophyllum pallescens

Chrysophyllum pallescens, commonly known as the pale chrysophyllum, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, sweet-tasting fruits resembling small apples.

Chrysophyllum pallescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the nutritional benefits of its fruit.
