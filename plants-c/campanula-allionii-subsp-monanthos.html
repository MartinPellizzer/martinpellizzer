Campanula allionii subsp. monanthos

Campanula allionii subsp.

monanthos, commonly known as the single-flower bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
