Chionanthus dussii

Chionanthus dussii, commonly known as the Mexican whiteflower, is a deciduous shrub native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.
