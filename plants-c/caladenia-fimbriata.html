Caladenia fimbriata

Caladenia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed spider-orchid, is an exquisite flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, spider-like appearance with intricate, fimbriated petals. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, are pollinated primarily by thynnid wasps.

Caladenia fimbriata is a protected species due to habitat loss and is often found in conservation reserves.
