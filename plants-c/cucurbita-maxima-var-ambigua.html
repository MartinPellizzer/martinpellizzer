Cucurbita maxima var. ambigua

Cucurbita maxima var. ambigua, commonly known as the warty pumpkin, is a variant of the common squash.

It is characterized by its irregular, warty surface and varying colors, often including shades of green, yellow, and orange. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, known for its vine-like growth habit and large, lobed leaves. It produces large, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and culinary uses in various cuisines.
