Cupaniopsis anacardioides f. parvifolia

Cupaniopsis anacardioides f. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cupaniopsis, is a shrub or small tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes cashew and poison ivy. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. Its flowers are inconspicuous, clustered in umbels, and are followed by small, red, fleshy fruits.

This species is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
