Cocos comosa

Cocos comosa, commonly known as the hairy coconut palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces coconuts, which are encased in a fibrous husk and contain both water and milk. Its roots are fibrous and help in stabilizing the soil in coastal areas.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
