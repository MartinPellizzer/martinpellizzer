Corypha nana

Corypha nana, commonly known as the dwarf palm, is a small, slow-growing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, fan-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and compact size.

Corypha nana thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
