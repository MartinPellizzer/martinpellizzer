Calia arizonica subsp. formosa

Calia arizonica subsp.

formosa, commonly known as the Arizona calia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers.

The plant typically blooms in the spring, with flowers ranging in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and a popular choice for xeriscaping.
