Cucumis melo var. carmelitarum

Cucumis melo var. carmelitarum, commonly known as the Carmelita melon, is a variety of muskmelon characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruit.

The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and typically grows as a trailing vine with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and the fruit develops from the female flowers. The skin of the Carmelita melon is generally thin and pale green, with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and minerals.
