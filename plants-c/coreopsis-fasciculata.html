Coreopsis fasciculata

Coreopsis fasciculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a fuzzy texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance ornamental plants.
