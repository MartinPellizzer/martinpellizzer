Cirsium callilepis

Cirsium callilepis, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and large, showy purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its habitat includes dry grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in full sun.

The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can be found in various regions of the Andes.
