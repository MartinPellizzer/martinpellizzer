Convolvulus sinuatodentatus

Convolvulus sinuatodentatus, commonly known as the wavy-toothed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive wavy, toothed margins. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus sinuatodentatus is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, using its tendrils to support its growth.
