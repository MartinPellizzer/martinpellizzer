Carpinus fargesiana var. hwai

Carpinus fargesiana var. hwai, commonly known as the Hwai hornbeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a glossy upper surface. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, spherical fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
