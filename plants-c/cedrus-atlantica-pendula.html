Cedrus atlantica pendula

Cedrus atlantica pendula, commonly known as the weeping cedar of the Atlas Mountains, is a unique variant of the Atlas cedar.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree has scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. It produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is native to the mountainous regions of North Africa and is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience.
