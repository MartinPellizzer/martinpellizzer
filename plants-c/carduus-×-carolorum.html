Carduus × carolorum

Carduus × carolorum, commonly known as the common cocklebur.

This hybrid plant is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, spherical fruit heads. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various parts of Europe and North America. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by the development of the distinctive prickly burs.

It is often considered a weed but has been used historically for its fibers and as a source of medicinal compounds.
