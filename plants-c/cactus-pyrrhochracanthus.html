Cactus pyrrhochracanthus

Cactus pyrrhochracanthus, commonly known as the red-spined cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces spiny areoles covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing at the base of the spines during the warmer months.

Despite its hardy nature, it requires well-draining soil and minimal watering to thrive.
