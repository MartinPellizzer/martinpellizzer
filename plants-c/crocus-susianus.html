Crocus susianus

Crocus susianus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, saucer-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its bulbs are perennial, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Crocus susianus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
