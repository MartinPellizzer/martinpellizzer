Campanula polyclada

Campanula polyclada, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from blue to purple. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Campanula polyclada is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
