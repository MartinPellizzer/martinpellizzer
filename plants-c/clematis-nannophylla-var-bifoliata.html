Clematis nannophylla var. bifoliata

Clematis nannophylla var. bifoliata, commonly known as the small-leaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant flowering display.
