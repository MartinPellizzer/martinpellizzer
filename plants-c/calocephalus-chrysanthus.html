Calocephalus chrysanthus

Calocephalus chrysanthus, commonly known as the goldenhead dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and erect.

Calocephalus chrysanthus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
