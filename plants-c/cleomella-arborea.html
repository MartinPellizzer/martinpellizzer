Cleomella arborea

Cleomella arborea, commonly known as the tree cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or white, with five petals and a distinctive, spurred structure. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

The plant is adapted to dry climates and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.
