Couepia paraensis subsp. glaucescens

Couepia paraensis subsp.

glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous couepia, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lanceolate leaves with a distinctive bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
