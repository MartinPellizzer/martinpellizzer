Cineraria elatior

Cineraria elatior, commonly known as the greater cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Cineraria elatior is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
