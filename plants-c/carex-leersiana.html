Carex leersiana

Carex leersiana, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex leersiana prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially sunny environments.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
