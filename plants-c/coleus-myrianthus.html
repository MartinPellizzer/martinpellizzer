Coleus myrianthus

Coleus myrianthus, commonly known as the velvetleaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that displays a range of colors including green, red, and purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture that gives it a distinctive appearance.

Coleus myrianthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
