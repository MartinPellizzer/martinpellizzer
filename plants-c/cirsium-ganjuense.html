Cirsium ganjuense

Cirsium ganjuense, commonly known as Ganju thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates. It produces small, achene fruits that are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural areas.
