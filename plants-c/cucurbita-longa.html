Cucurbita longa

Cucurbita longa, commonly known as the long squash or bottle gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical shape and smooth, waxy skin that can range from light green to pale yellow. The plant produces large, yellow flowers and is typically cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary traditions. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and often used in soups, stews, and as a container for cooking.
