Cnestis trifolia

Cnestis trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Cnestis trifolia is a climbing vine that can reach significant lengths, making it a notable presence in its native habitats.
