Carex saxatilis var. rotundata

Carex saxatilis var. rotundata, commonly known as the rounded sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
