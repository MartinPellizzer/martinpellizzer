Cephalanthus cavaleriei

Cephalanthus cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese sweet pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
