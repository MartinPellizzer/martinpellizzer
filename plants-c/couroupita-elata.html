Couroupita elata

Couroupita elata, commonly known as the yellow mombin, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces fleshy, orange-red fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

Couroupita elata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
