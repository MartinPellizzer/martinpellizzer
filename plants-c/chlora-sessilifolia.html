Chlora sessilifolia

Chlora sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf chlora, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest regions. The plant has simple, broad leaves that are sessile, meaning they attach directly to the stem without a stalk. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, Chlora sessilifolia is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
