Calophyllum tetrapterum var. tetrapterum

Calophyllum tetrapterum var. tetrapterum, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark brown to black bark and large, elliptical leaves that are glossy and opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The wood of this species is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a significant resource in local economies.
