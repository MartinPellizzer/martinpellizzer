Crinum lineare

Crinum lineare, commonly known as the linear crinum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Crinum lineare is also known for its strong, pleasant fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
