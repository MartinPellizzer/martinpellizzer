Centaurea konkae

Centaurea konkae, commonly known as the Konkae knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Centaurea konkae is known for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local pollinators.
