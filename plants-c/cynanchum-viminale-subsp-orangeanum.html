Cynanchum viminale subsp. orangeanum

Cynanchum viminale subsp.

orangeanum, commonly known as the Orange Milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant features slender, vine-like stems that can climb or trail along the ground. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, producing a mild milky sap that is characteristic of the Asclepiadaceae family.
