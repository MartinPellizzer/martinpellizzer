Cyperus capillifolius

Cyperus capillifolius, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in areas with standing water or saturated soil. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive hairy texture, while its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus capillifolius plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
