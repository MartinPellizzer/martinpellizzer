Conyza leucodasys

Conyza leucodasys, commonly known as whiteleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces small, white to pale yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is characterized by its branched stems and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Conyza leucodasys is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
