Cousinia wolgensis

Cousinia wolgensis, commonly known as the Wolgen's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are usually basal, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Cousinia wolgensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
