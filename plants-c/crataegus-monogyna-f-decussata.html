Crataegus monogyna f. decussata

Crataegus monogyna f. decussata, commonly known as the common hawthorn or whitethorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the autumn, which are a food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval or elliptic, with serrated edges, and it is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedging and as a source of medicinal compounds.
