Cimicifuga simplex var. yezoensis

Cimicifuga simplex var. yezoensis, commonly known as Yezo bugbane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, white to pale purple flowers. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and is known for its showy, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
