Cirsium radians

Cirsium radians, commonly known as the lesser thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in a spherical cluster. Cirsium radians thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
