Capparis quinqueflora

Capparis quinqueflora, commonly known as the five-flowered caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. Its fruits are capsule-like and contain several seeds.

Capparis quinqueflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
