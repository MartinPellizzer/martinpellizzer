Coryphantha clavata var. ancistracantha

Coryphantha clavata var. ancistracantha, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular shape and bright green, ribbed stems. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, apple-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
