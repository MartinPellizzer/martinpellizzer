Circaea caulescens var. pilosula

Circaea caulescens var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each leaflet being hairy and oval-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
