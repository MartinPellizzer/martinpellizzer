Cephalostachyum capitatum var. decompositum

Cephalostachyum capitatum var. decompositum, commonly known as the decomposed head bamboo, is a unique variant of the native bamboo species found in specific regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, and often decomposed appearance of the inflorescence, which sets it apart from other bamboo varieties. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it can thrive in the partial light conditions. The plant's botanical features include slender, segmented stems and small, clustered inflorescences that are often covered in a layer of dead plant material.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
