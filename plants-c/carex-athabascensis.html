Carex athabascensis

Carex athabascensis, commonly known as the Athabasca sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as bogs and marshes, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex athabascensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
