Castanea sativa var. spicata

Castanea sativa var. spicata, commonly known as the spiny chestnut, is a variant of the sweet chestnut native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny calyx that persists on the fruit even after maturity. The plant produces large, edible nuts enclosed in a thick, spiky husk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental features and historical use in traditional landscaping.
