Campanula arvatica subsp. adsurgens

Campanula arvatica subsp.

adsurgens, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and hairy, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
