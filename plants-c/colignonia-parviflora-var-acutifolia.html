Colignonia parviflora var. acutifolia

Colignonia parviflora var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved colignonia, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, giving the plant its "acutifolia" designation. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate floral display.
