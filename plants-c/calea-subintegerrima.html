Calea subintegerrima

Calea subintegerrima, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Mexican calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically borne in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing plant in both natural and cultivated settings.
