Centaurea haenseleri subsp. epapposa

Centaurea haenseleri subsp.

epapposa, commonly known as the Turkish knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
