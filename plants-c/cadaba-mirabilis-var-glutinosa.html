Cadaba mirabilis var. glutinosa

Cadaba mirabilis var. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky cadaba, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a sticky, resinous substance. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in clusters. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

The sticky resin has been used traditionally for various purposes, including as an adhesive or in medicinal applications.
