Crataegus blockwayae

Crataegus blockwayae, commonly known as the Blockway hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and Florida. This shrub or small tree typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, red berries that are edible for birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
