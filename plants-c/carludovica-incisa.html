Carludovica incisa

Carludovica incisa, commonly known as the jaguar cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure. Carludovica incisa is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
