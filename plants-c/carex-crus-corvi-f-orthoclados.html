Carex crus-corvi f. orthoclados

Carex crus-corvi f. orthoclados, commonly known as the black-root sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, rhizomes and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a single, cylindrical spike that bears small, greenish flowers.

The botanical features of this sedge make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
