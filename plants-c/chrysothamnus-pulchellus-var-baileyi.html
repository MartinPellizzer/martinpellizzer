Chrysothamnus pulchellus var. baileyi

Chrysothamnus pulchellus var. baileyi, commonly known as Bailey's golden coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers and aromatic foliage, which are typical features of the genus Chrysothamnus. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
