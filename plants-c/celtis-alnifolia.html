Celtis alnifolia

Celtis alnifolia, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that ripen to a dark purple color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
