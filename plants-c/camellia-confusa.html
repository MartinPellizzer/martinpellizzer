Camellia confusa

Camellia confusa, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive yellow or green center. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Camellia confusa is also valued for its ability to produce edible seeds, which are used in traditional Chinese cuisine.
