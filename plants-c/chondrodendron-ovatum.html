Chondrodendron ovatum

Chondrodendron ovatum, commonly known as the Amazonian moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant produces distinctive, kidney-shaped fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique reproductive system involving both flowers and fruits.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its traditional medicinal uses.
