Canavalia brasiliensis

Canavalia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian bean or wild pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in various soil types.

Canavalia brasiliensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
