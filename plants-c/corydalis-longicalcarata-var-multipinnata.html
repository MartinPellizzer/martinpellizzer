Corydalis longicalcarata var. multipinnata

Corydalis longicalcarata var. multipinnata, commonly known as the multilobed corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, creeping stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often adorned with distinctive lobes. The plant produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowering stems are usually shorter than the leaves, and the plant thrives in well-drained, moist soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and unique floral structure.
