Chamaecrista tephrosiifolia

Chamaecrista tephrosiifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and distinctive, elongated pods that contain several seeds. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
