Croton tranomarensis

Croton tranomarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a prominent central vein. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
