Cerastium latifolium subsp. biadegii

Cerastium latifolium subsp.

biadegii, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central Asia. It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June.

The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
