Calochortus pulchellus var. parviflorus

Calochortus pulchellus var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered mariposa lily, is a perennial herb native to California.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that are typically white to pale pink, with a distinctive, showy dorsal sepal. The plant grows from a bulbous rootstock and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal bluffs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a member of the Liliaceae family.
