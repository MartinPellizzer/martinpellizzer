Chrysanthemum alashanense

Chrysanthemum alashanense, commonly known as the Alashan chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow to orange petals. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures. It is often found in semi-desert and steppe environments, contributing to local biodiversity.

Due to its unique ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
