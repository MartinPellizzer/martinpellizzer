Cichorium glabratum

Cichorium glabratum, commonly known as the smooth chicory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its blue or purple composite flowers. The plant has basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairless, giving it its species name "glabratum.". Its edible leaves and flowers are often used in salads or as a coffee substitute, while the roots can be roasted and brewed.

Cichorium glabratum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.
