Cytisus lupinifolius

Cytisus lupinifolius, commonly known as the lupine leaf broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, thorny branches. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus lupinifolius is adapted to poor, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
