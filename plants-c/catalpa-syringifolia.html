Catalpa syringifolia

Catalpa syringifolia, commonly known as the Chinese catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces long, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its winged seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Catalpa syringifolia is also known for its resistance to certain pests and diseases.
