Clerodendrum thomsoniae

Clerodendrum thomsoniae, commonly known as the Thomson's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Clerodendrum thomsoniae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
