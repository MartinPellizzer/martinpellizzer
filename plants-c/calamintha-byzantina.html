Calamintha byzantina

Calamintha byzantina, commonly known as Byzantine germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a mint-like scent.

Calamintha byzantina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming properties.
