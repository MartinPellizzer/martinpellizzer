Calanthe ferruginea

Calanthe ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty calanthe, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy rhizome and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a spike of pale yellow to brownish flowers with showy, spurred petals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest undergrowth.

Calanthe ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens.
