Cyclamen graecum subsp. mindleri

Cyclamen graecum subsp.

mindleri, commonly known as the Greek cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant. Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, adding a touch of color during the colder months. Cyclamen graecum subsp.

mindleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
