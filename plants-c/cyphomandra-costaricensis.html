Cyphomandra costaricensis

Cyphomandra costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has elliptical, hairy leaves. Its fruits are edible when ripe, though they can be mildly toxic if unripe.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas in the understory of tropical forests.
