Clinopodium creticum

Clinopodium creticum, commonly known as Crete mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Greece and other parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in dry, sunny environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Clinopodium creticum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
