Ceropegia loureiroi

Ceropegia loureiroi, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, cylindrical stems covered in a silvery, powdery coating. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia loureiroi is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
