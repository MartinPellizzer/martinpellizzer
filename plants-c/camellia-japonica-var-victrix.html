Camellia japonica var. victrix

Camellia japonica var. victrix, commonly known as the Japanese Camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and its showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter and early spring. The flowers typically range in color from red to pink and white, with a unique, double-petaled structure. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand colder temperatures compared to other camellia species.
