Calamus nudus

Calamus nudus, commonly known as the naked reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, unbranched stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a smooth or slightly ribbed surface.

Calamus nudus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
