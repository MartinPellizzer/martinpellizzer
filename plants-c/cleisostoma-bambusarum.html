Cleisostoma bambusarum

Cleisostoma bambusarum, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, tropical environments, where it thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are long and narrow, often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Cleisostoma bambusarum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom multiple times throughout the year.
