Convolvulus ambigens

Convolvulus ambigens, commonly known as the ambigous bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a trailing perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain environments.
