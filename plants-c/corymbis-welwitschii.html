Corymbis welwitschii

Corymbis welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's corymbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in corymbs, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Corymbis welwitschii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
