Cereus royenii var. armatus

Cereus royenii var. armatus, commonly known as the armed night bloomer, is a cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems and its ability to produce large, fragrant white flowers at night. The plant is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements. Its flowers typically open in the evening and remain open for only a few hours.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
