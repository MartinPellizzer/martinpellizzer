Carex sempervirens subsp. laevis

Carex sempervirens subsp.

laevis, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are soft to the touch due to the presence of fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and wetland restoration projects.
