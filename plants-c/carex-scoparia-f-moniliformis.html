Carex scoparia f. moniliformis

Carex scoparia f. moniliformis, commonly known as the bearded sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive hairy or bearded appearance along the edges, giving it its common name.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils.
