Cinnamomum rosiflorum

Cinnamomum rosiflorum, commonly known as the rose-flowered cinnamon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces reddish-brown bark, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, contain compounds like cinnamaldehyde and eugenol.

Cinnamomum rosiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its contribution to the spice trade.
