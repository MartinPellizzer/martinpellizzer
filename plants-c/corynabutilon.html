Corynabutilon

Corynabutilon, commonly known as the false sandmat, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in hot, dry climates with minimal rainfall. The plant's structure is adapted to conserve water, with thick leaves that store moisture.

It is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
