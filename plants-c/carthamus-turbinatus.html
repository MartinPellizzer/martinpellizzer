Carthamus turbinatus

Carthamus turbinatus, commonly known as the turban thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Carthamus turbinatus is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
