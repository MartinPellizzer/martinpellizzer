Carlephyton

Carlephyton, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its striking silvery foliage.

Its leaves are broad and waxy, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Carlephyton is native to regions with dry climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
