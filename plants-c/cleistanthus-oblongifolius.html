Cleistanthus oblongifolius

Cleistanthus oblongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf cleistanthus, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
