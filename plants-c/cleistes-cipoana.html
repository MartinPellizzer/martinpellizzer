Cleistes cipoana

Cleistes cipoana, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that resemble a closed bud. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Cleistes cipoana is noted for its short blooming period, usually occurring in the spring or early summer.
