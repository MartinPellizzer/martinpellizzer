Calpurnia aurea subsp. aurea

Calpurnia aurea subsp.

aurea, commonly known as the golden calpurnia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple branches and small, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months and attract various pollinators. Its flowers are tubular and have a distinctive golden hue, while its leaves are covered in fine hairs. Calpurnia aurea subsp.

aurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
