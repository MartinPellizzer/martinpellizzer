Caryophyllus atrorubens

Caryophyllus atrorubens, commonly known as the black-red carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, dark red to almost black petals that contrast with its green foliage. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and floral arrangements.
