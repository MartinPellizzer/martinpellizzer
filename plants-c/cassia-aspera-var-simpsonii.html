Cassia aspera var. simpsonii

Cassia aspera var. simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's rough cassia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, flat seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
