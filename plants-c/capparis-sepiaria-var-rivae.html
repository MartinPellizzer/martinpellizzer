Capparis sepiaria var. rivae

Capparis sepiaria var. rivae, commonly known as the Red Sea caper, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Red Sea.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a succulent, spiny structure adapted to its harsh habitat. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, spherical fruits. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, with a distinctive aromatic scent, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Capparis genus, which are known for their unique floral and fruit characteristics.
