Cotoneaster klotzii

Cotoneaster klotzii, commonly known as the Klotzii cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features small, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Cotoneaster klotzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its seasonal color changes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
