Clematis orientalis subsp. graveolens

Clematis orientalis subsp.

graveolens, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a palmate pattern.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, often with a white throat, during late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
