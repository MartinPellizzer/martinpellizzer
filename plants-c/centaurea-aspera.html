Centaurea aspera

Centaurea aspera, commonly known as the rough centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features wiry, branching stems and narrow, gray-green leaves covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Centaurea aspera is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
