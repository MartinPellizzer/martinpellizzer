Carex dessaueri

Carex dessaueri, commonly known as the Dessauer's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to moist habitats in North America, it thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
