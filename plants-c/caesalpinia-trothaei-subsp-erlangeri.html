Caesalpinia trothaei subsp. erlangeri

Caesalpinia trothaei subsp.

erlangeri, commonly known as the Erlanger's Crown, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
