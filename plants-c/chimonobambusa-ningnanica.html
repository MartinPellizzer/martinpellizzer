Chimonobambusa ningnanica

Chimonobambusa ningnanica, commonly known as the Ningnan bamboo, is a species of flowering bamboo native to southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, culm-like stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
