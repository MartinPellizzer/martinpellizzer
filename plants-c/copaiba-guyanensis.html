Copaiba guyanensis

Copaiba guyanensis, commonly known as yellow copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall with smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
