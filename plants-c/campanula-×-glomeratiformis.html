Campanula × glomeratiformis

Campanula × glomeratiformis, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue, violet, or white. The plant typically produces small, oval leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its hybrid nature, Campanula × glomeratiformis exhibits a variety of forms and flower colors, enhancing its versatility in horticultural settings.
