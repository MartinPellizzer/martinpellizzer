Chenopodium ambrosioides var. comosum

Chenopodium ambrosioides var. comosum, commonly known as wormseed goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically pubescent and have a slightly succulent texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, and its fruit is a dry, three-angled achene with a tuft of silky hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The plant is sometimes considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
