Calliandra bromelioides

Calliandra bromelioides, commonly known as the bromelioid calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences composed of clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and unique foliage structure.
