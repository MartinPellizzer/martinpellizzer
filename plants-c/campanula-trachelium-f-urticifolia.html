Campanula trachelium f. urticifolia

Campanula trachelium f. urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging bellflower, is a unique botanical variant of the common bellflower.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. The plant features hairy, serrated leaves that can cause a mild skin irritation, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
