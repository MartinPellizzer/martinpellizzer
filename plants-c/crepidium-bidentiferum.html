Crepidium bidentiferum

Crepidium bidentiferum, commonly known as the two-toothed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a slender, often branched stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale yellow, with distinctive two-toothed structures on the lip.

Crepidium bidentiferum is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
