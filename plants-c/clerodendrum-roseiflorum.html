Clerodendrum roseiflorum

Clerodendrum roseiflorum, commonly known as the pink trumpet bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
