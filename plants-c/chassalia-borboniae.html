Chassalia borboniae

Chassalia borboniae, commonly known as the Bourbon chassalia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within the rainforests of the island. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Chassalia borboniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
