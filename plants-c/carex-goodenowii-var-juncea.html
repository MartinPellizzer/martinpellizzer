Carex goodenowii var. juncea

Carex goodenowii var. juncea, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. This variety is native to North America, particularly found in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and it serves as a habitat and food source for various invertebrates.
