Cedrela fissilis

Cedrela fissilis, commonly known as Brazilian walnut or saibah, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its large, simple leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces woody, elongated fruits that contain one or two seeds, which are encased in a hard, fibrous coat. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a commercially important species.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
