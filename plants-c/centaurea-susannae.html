Centaurea susannae

Centaurea susannae, commonly known as the Susanna's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and spiny bracts that form a distinctive crown-like structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

Centaurea susannae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
