Cirsium lacaitae

Cirsium lacaitae, commonly known as the Sicilian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristle-like hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Cirsium lacaitae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
