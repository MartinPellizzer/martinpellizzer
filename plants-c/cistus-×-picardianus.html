Cistus × picardianus

Cistus × picardianus, commonly known as the Picardian rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which is part of the family Ericaceae, known for its aromatic and often evergreen species. The plant is characterized by its dense, woody growth and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Cistus × picardianus is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant in gardens.
