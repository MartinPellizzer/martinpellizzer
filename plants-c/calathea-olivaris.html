Calathea olivaris

Calathea olivaris, commonly known as the Olive Calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that display a range of green, silver, and brown hues. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ornamental value. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Calathea olivaris is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
