Cyperus sanguinolentus subsp. teysmannii

Cyperus sanguinolentus subsp.

teysmannii, commonly known as the red sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
