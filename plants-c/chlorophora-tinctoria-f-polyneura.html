Chlorophora tinctoria f. polyneura

Chlorophora tinctoria f. polyneura, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree species native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant has alternate, simple, and usually elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is also noted for its ecological importance in forest ecosystems and its role in traditional medicine.
