Croton leytensis

Croton leytensis, commonly known as the Leyte croton, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from bright yellow to deep red. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in inflorescences, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically growing in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
