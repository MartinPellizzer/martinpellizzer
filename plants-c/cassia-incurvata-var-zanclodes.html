Cassia incurvata var. zanclodes

Cassia incurvata var. zanclodes, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow floral spikes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their aromatic properties.
