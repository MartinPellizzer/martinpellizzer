Cratylia argentea

Cratylia argentea, commonly known as silver cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white pubescent leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a forage crop or for its ornamental value.

Cratylia argentea also has nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
