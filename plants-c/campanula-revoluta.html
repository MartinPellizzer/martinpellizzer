Campanula revoluta

Campanula revoluta, commonly known as the revolute bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
