Cacalia squamulosa

Cacalia squamulosa, commonly known as the silky bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Cacalia squamulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
