Cirsium giganteum

Cirsium giganteum, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple or pinkish flower heads. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with sharp, serrated edges and a thick, waxy coating. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single, terminal head, and it blooms from late summer to early autumn.

Cirsium giganteum is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.
