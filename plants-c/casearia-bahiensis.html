Casearia bahiensis

Casearia bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered, white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are a notable feature. The berries are edible for some bird species but are generally considered unpalatable to humans.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and vibrant fruit.
