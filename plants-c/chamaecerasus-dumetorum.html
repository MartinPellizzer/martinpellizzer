Chamaecerasus dumetorum

Chamaecerasus dumetorum, commonly known as the dwarf snowberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit, with waxy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its winter interest.
