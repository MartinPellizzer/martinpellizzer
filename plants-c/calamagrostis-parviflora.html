Calamagrostis parviflora

Calamagrostis parviflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, spike-like clusters of pale blue to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough, textured surface.

Calamagrostis parviflora plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
