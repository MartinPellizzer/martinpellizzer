Cogniauxia cordifolia

Cogniauxia cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaved fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its leaves are usually a deep green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Cogniauxia cordifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
