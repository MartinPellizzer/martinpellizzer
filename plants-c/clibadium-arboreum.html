Clibadium arboreum

Clibadium arboreum, commonly known as the Brazilian burrbean, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain edible seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
