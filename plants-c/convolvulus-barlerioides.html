Convolvulus barlerioides

Convolvulus barlerioides, commonly known as the Barlerioides bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive tubular throat. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it an aggressive weed in some regions.
