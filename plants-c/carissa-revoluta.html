Carissa revoluta

Carissa revoluta, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a rounded, yellow berry that turns red when ripe, containing several seeds.

Carissa revoluta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
