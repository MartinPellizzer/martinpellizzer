Callistachys pyrenaica

Callistachys pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.
