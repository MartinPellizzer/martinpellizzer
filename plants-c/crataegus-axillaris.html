Crataegus axillaris

Crataegus axillaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant ecosystems and a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
