Caesalpinia nitida

Caesalpinia nitida, commonly known as the fire bush, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and compound leaves that are pinnately arranged. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers clustered in elongated spikes, which are followed by flat, leathery pods. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various tropical and subtropical climates.

Caesalpinia nitida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
