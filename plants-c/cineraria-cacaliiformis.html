Cineraria cacaliiformis

Cineraria cacaliiformis, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of silver-green, woolly leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The flowers are not only visually striking but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
