Cyperus cunninghamii subsp. cheradicus

Cyperus cunninghamii subsp.

cheradicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia. It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often with a pale green or brownish hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
