Clinopodium mutabile

Clinopodium mutabile, commonly known as variable mint or variable oregano, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and can be found in meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are often gray-green and have a strong, mint-like aroma.

Clinopodium mutabile is known for its variable morphology, which contributes to its common name.
