Carissa myrtoides

Carissa myrtoides, commonly known as the star apple or yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique fruit structure with a star-shaped appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in warm climates.
