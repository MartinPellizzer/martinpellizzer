Connarus griffonianus var. subsericeus

Connarus griffonianus var. subsericeus, commonly known as the silky connarus, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically leathery and have a smooth, almost silky texture, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
