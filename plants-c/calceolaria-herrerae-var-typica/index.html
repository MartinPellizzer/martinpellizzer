Calceolaria Herrerae Var. Typica

Calceolaria herrerae var. typica is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of Ecuador. Known for its unique, pouch-like flowers that resemble tiny cups, this species captivates botanists and plant enthusiasts alike. Its vivid colors and intricate structure make it a standout among the Calceolaria genus. Due to its limited habitat and environmental threats, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts. This article explores its biology, habitat, and the challenges it faces in the wild.
