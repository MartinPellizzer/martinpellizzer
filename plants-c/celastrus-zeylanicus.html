Celastrus zeylanicus

Celastrus zeylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon creeper, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a woody vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
