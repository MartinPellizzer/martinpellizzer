Callista canaliculata

Callista canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and ribbed, giving it its common name, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Callista canaliculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
