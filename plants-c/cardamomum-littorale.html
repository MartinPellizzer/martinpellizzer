Cardamomum littorale

Cardamomum littorale, commonly known as the coastal cardamom, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found growing near the shoreline. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its seeds, enclosed in a three-lobed pod, are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant's adaptability to saline soils makes it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
