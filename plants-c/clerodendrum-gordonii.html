Clerodendrum gordonii

Clerodendrum gordonii, commonly known as the Gordon's flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The bark is smooth and can be grayish or reddish in color, often exuding a milky sap when damaged.
