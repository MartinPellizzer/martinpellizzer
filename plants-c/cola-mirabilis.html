Cola mirabilis

Cola mirabilis, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits. The plant is notable for its seeds, which are enclosed in a hard, woody shell and contain the stimulant compound caffeine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree plays an important ecological role and has cultural significance in various African communities.
