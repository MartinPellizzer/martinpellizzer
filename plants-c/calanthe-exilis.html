Calanthe exilis

Calanthe exilis, commonly known as the slender calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown.

Calanthe exilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
