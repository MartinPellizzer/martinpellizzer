Camptosema grandiflorum

Camptosema grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered camptosema, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
