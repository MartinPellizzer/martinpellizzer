Couroupita guianensis

Couroupita guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese couroupita, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces bright yellow fruits that contain edible seeds, often used in local cuisines. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
