Clematis quadribracteolata

Clematis quadribracteolata, commonly known as the four-bract clematis, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, usually blue or violet flowers. The plant produces four bracts beneath each flower cluster, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clematis quadribracteolata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
