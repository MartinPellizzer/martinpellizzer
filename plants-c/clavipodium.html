Clavipodium

Clavipodium, commonly known as the clubfoot grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and compact, often sparsely leafy foliage. The plants typically produce small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Clavipodium species are native to various regions of Africa and are often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
