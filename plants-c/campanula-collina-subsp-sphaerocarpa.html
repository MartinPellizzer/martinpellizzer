Campanula collina subsp. sphaerocarpa

Campanula collina subsp.

sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer.

The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
