Carex digitalis var. vanweckii

Carex digitalis var. vanweckii, commonly known as the Vanweck's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spikes of small, spikelet-bearing flowers. This variety is native to southern Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It typically grows in open woodlands or along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
