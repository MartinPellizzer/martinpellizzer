Coleus hijazensis

Coleus hijazensis, commonly known as the Hijaz coleus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often brightly colored, ranging from red to purple, which makes it a striking ornamental plant.

Despite its aesthetic appeal, it is not commonly cultivated outside its native range due to its specific environmental requirements.
