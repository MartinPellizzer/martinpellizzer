Cassia cavalcantina

Cassia cavalcantina, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that resemble cascading showers. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically compound with oval-shaped leaflets. Cassia cavalcantina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
