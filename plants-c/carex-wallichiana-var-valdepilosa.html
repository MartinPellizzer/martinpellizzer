Carex wallichiana var. valdepilosa

Carex wallichiana var. valdepilosa, commonly known as the Alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
