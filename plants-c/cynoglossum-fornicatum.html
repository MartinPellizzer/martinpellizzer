Cynoglossum fornicatum

Cynoglossum fornicatum, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Cynoglossum fornicatum is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
