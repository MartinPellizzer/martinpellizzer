Crocus gargaricus var. panchrysus

Crocus gargaricus var. panchrysus, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, golden-yellow flowers with six petals that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.

The bulbs are also used in traditional medicine and as a source of saffron-like pigments.
