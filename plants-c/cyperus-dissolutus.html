Cyperus dissolutus

Cyperus dissolutus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in swamps, ditches, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are usually hairless or slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of small, three-lobed spikelets that are often borne on short stalks.

Cyperus dissolutus is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
