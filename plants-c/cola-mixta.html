Cola mixta

Cola mixta, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive red, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant. Its botanical features include a sturdy trunk, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

Cola mixta is also valued for its economic importance in local communities.
