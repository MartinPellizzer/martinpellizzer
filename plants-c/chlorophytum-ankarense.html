Chlorophytum ankarense

Chlorophytum ankarense, commonly known as the African peace lily, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically green with a white or silver midrib, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas or indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value and ease of care have made it a popular choice for both gardeners and plant enthusiasts.
