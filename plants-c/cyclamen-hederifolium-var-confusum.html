Cyclamen hederifolium var. confusum

Cyclamen hederifolium var. confusum, commonly known as the woolly cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive wavy margin and a dense covering of fine hairs. The flowers are typically pink or mauve, with a spurred petal at the base, and are borne on tall, sturdy stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
