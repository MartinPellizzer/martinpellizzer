Croton elaeagni var. antsirananae

Croton elaeagni var. antsirananae, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its bark is rough and often fissured, and it has a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
