Crocus vernus var. fusiformis

Crocus vernus var. fusiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland or grassy areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.

This variety is closely related to other crocus species but is distinguished by its slender foliage and compact flower structure.
