Capparis oxyphylla

Capparis oxyphylla, commonly known as the prickly date caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and distinctive, spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Capparis oxyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and resilience to harsh conditions.
