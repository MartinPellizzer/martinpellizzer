Cornus canadensis var. intermedia

Cornus canadensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate silky dogwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy surface.
