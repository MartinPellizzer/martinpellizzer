Chaetospora mauritii

Chaetospora mauritii, commonly known as the Mauritius spore plant, is a unique species belonging to the family Lepidomycetaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial fungus that produces distinctive, spore-bearing structures called chaetospores. These spores are characterized by their long, slender, and often twisted filaments, which aid in their dispersal. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for mycological study.
