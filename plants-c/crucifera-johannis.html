Crucifera johannis

Crucifera johannis, commonly known as the Johann's Cross, is a rare and ancient plant species belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive cruciform flowers, which typically feature four petals arranged in a cross shape. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, a trait common among members of its family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often found in temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution and historical significance, it is considered a botanical curiosity and subject of scientific study.
