Cirsium × gutnickianum

Cirsium × gutnickianum, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets.

Cirsium × gutnickianum is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in other damp habitats.
