Cymbidium seidenfadenii

Cymbidium seidenfadenii, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, branched stems with numerous clusters of fragrant, lance-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cymbidium seidenfadenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
