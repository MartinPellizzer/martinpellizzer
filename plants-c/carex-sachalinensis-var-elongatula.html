Carex sachalinensis var. elongatula

Carex sachalinensis var. elongatula, commonly known as the elongated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly curled at the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish-brown flowers that are clustered in three rows.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
