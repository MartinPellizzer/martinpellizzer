Canavalia sericea var. munroi

Canavalia sericea var. munroi, commonly known as the silky cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with long, silky pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds and ornamental value.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is also used in crop rotation to improve soil fertility.
