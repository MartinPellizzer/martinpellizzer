Croton pallidulus

Croton pallidulus, commonly known as the pale croton, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-yellow and clustered in cyathia, a feature common in the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton pallidulus is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
