Crataegus phaenopyrum f. fastigiata

Crataegus phaenopyrum f. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright hawthorn, is a compact, slow-growing shrub or small tree with a dense, columnar growth habit.

It features small, oval leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, and it produces white flowers in early spring followed by red berries in the fall. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its upright form and seasonal interest.
