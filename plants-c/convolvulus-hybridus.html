Convolvulus hybridus

Convolvulus hybridus, commonly known as the common bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, purple, or white. The plant is a perennial vine that can grow up to several meters in length, with tendrils that allow it to climb and spread aggressively. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant produces small, dry fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Convolvulus hybridus is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and persistence.
