Catapodium rigescens

Catapodium rigescens, commonly known as rigid catapodium, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.
