Campanula trachelium var. leucantha

Campanula trachelium var. leucantha, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
