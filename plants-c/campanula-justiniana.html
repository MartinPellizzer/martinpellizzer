Campanula justiniana

Campanula justiniana, commonly known as the Justinian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender stems that produce clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula justiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
