Calceolaria platyzyga

Calceolaria platyzyga, commonly known as the flatleaf slipper orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-like structure that resembles a slipper, which is a key feature of the Calceolaria genus. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in cooler, moist environments. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Calceolaria platyzyga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
