Cyperus levis

Cyperus levis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus levis is often considered a weed in agricultural settings but plays a role in wetland ecosystems.
