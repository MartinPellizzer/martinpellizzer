Camellia lucii

Camellia lucii, commonly known as the golden camellia, is a rare and ornamental flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are leathery to the touch. Camellia lucii prefers well-drained, acidic soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

Its unique coloration and seasonal blooming make it a prized addition to botanical collections and gardens.
