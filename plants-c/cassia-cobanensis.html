Cassia cobanensis

Cassia cobanensis, commonly known as the Coban cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with several pairs of leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, making it visually striking. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that mature to a reddish-brown color.

Cassia cobanensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland tropical regions.
