Cunila nepalensis

Cunila nepalensis, commonly known as Nepal betony, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous forests and along stream banks.

Cunila nepalensis is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing effects.
