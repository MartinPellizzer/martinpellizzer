Calamus guangxiensis

Calamus guangxiensis, commonly known as Guangxi calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to southern China, particularly the Guangxi region, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of the palm family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
