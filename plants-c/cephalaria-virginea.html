Cephalaria virginea

Cephalaria virginea, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its most striking feature is the purple to pinkish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Cephalaria virginea is often found in dry grasslands and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
