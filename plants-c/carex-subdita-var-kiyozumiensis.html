Carex subdita var. kiyozumiensis

Carex subdita var. kiyozumiensis, commonly known as the Kiyozumi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and habitat preferences, making it a notable species in botanical studies.
