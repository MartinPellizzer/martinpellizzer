Chlorophytum cameronii var. purpuratum

Chlorophytum cameronii var. purpuratum, commonly known as the Purple Viper's Bugloss, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features long, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a distinctive purple or reddish hue along the edges. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and delicate floral display.
