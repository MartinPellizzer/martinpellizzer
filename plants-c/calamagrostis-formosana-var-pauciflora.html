Calamagrostis formosana var. pauciflora

Calamagrostis formosana var. pauciflora, commonly known as the Taiwan reed grass, is a perennial grass native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically found at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
