Centranthus calcitrapae subsp. orbiculatus

Centranthus calcitrapae subsp.

orbiculatus, commonly known as the Mediterranean globe thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features upright, sparsely branched stems that bear dense clusters of blue to purple, globe-shaped flowers.

The leaves are gray-green, covered in a whitish powdery coating, and are typically lance-shaped with a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
