Colchicum bertolonii var. cupanii

Colchicum bertolonii var. cupanii, commonly known as the cupanii autumn crocus, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant produces large, showy flowers with three petals and three sepals, which are often adorned with distinctive markings. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, emerging from a bulbous underground stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
