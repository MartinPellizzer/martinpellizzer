Cactus latimamma

Cactus latimamma, commonly known as the giant cactus, is a large, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 10 meters in height, covered in thick, waxy bark. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the dry season, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its deep root system allows it to store water efficiently, making it highly drought-resistant.

Despite its size, it plays a crucial role in providing habitat and food for desert wildlife.
