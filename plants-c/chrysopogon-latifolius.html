Chrysopogon latifolius

Chrysopogon latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf grass or Indian grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, linear leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture and landscaping.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a resilient choice for ecological restoration projects.
