Cyanus cheiranthifolius var. purpurascens

Cyanus cheiranthifolius var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces striking purple to pinkish-red composite flowers. The plant thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing at high altitudes with well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are more prominent than the disc florets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
