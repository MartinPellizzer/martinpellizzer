Codonopsis inflata

Codonopsis inflata, commonly known as the inflated bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds within its fleshy, bladder-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
