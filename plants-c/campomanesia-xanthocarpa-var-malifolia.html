Campomanesia xanthocarpa var. malifolia

Campomanesia xanthocarpa var. malifolia, commonly known as the yellow custard apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a smooth, yellow or orange berry with a sweet, aromatic pulp that is often eaten fresh or used in jams and beverages.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
