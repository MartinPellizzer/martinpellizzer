Camptosema nobile

Camptosema nobile, commonly known as the noble camptosema, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Camptosema nobile is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability.
