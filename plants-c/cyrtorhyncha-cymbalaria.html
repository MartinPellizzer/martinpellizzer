Cyrtorhyncha cymbalaria

Cyrtorhyncha cymbalaria, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern near the base of the plant.

Cyrtorhyncha cymbalaria thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats.
