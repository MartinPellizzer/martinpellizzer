Convolvulus glaucifolius

Convolvulus glaucifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The leaves are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many other flowering vines and herbs.
