Cassia caiapo

Cassia caiapo, commonly known as yellow flame tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces long, slender pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
