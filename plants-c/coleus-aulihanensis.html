Coleus aulihanensis

Coleus aulihanensis, commonly known as the Philippine coleus, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often marked with striking patterns of red, purple, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
