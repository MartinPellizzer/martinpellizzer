Coleus argenteus

Coleus argenteus, commonly known as silver coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that feature a mix of green, white, and silver hues. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or blue, which bloom in late summer.

Coleus argenteus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid conditions.
