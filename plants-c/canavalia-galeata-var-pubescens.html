Canavalia galeata var. pubescens

Canavalia galeata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy jackbean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which are typically pubescent. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in disturbed areas and is used in some regions for forage or traditional purposes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
