Cerastium alexandrei

Cerastium alexandrei, commonly known as Alexandrian chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Cerastium alexandrei thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
