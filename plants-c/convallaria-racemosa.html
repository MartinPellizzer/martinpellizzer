Convallaria racemosa

Convallaria racemosa, commonly known as lily of the valley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along a slender stem. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and forest understories.

The berries it produces are bright red and contain toxic seeds, making it a plant that requires caution when handling.
