Carex × limula var. decolorans

Carex × limula var. decolorans, commonly known as the decoloring sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, inconspicuous flowers.

The leaves often exhibit a distinctive bluish-green color that fades to a lighter green as the plant matures, giving it its common name.
