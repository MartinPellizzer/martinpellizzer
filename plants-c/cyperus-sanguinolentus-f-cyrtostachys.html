Cyperus sanguinolentus f. cyrtostachys

Cyperus sanguinolentus f. cyrtostachys, commonly known as the red sedge, is a variant of the sedge genus Cyperus.

It is characterized by its striking red-tinged foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water margins.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
