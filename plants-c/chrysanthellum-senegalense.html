Chrysanthellum senegalense

Chrysanthellum senegalense, commonly known as the Senegalese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open woodlands or along riverbanks. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a prominent central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Chrysanthellum senegalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
