Cyathea mossambicensis

Cyathea mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mossambique tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically evergreen and can reach lengths of up to two meters.

Cyathea mossambicensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
