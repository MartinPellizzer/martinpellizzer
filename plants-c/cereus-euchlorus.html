Cereus euchlorus

Cereus euchlorus, commonly known as the pale-flowered night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and produces large, white flowers that open at night. The plant has a columnar growth habit and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are typically fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Cereus euchlorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
