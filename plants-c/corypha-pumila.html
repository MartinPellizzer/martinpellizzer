Corypha pumila

Corypha pumila, commonly known as the dwarf palm, is a small, evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its compact size and clustered fronds. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 3 meters, making it ideal for ornamental use in gardens. Its leaves are fan-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Corypha pumila thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
