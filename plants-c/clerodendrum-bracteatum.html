Clerodendrum bracteatum

Clerodendrum bracteatum, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red to orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are typically bisexual and are accompanied by small, leaf-like bracts that enhance its visual appeal.

Clerodendrum bracteatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
