Crocus penicillatus

Crocus penicillatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, narrow corona. It typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

Crocus penicillatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
