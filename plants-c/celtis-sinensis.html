Celtis sinensis

Celtis sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Ulmariales and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible for some bird species. Celtis sinensis is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for wildlife, as well as its use in traditional medicine.
