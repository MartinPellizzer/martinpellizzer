Campanula parryi

Campanula parryi, commonly known as Parry's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is part of the Campanulaceae family.

Campanula parryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
