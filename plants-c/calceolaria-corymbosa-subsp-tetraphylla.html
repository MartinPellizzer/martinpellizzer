Calceolaria corymbosa subsp. tetraphylla

Calceolaria corymbosa subsp.

tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf calceolaria, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous areas with high humidity. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique floral structure and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
