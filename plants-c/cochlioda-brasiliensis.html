Cochlioda brasiliensis

Cochlioda brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom in the spring.

Cochlioda brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
