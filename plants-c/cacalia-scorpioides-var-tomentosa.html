Cacalia scorpioides var. tomentosa

Cacalia scorpioides var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic study.
