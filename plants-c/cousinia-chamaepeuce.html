Cousinia chamaepeuce

Cousinia chamaepeuce, commonly known as the mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy, often blue or purple-colored foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Cousinia chamaepeuce is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and visual interest.
