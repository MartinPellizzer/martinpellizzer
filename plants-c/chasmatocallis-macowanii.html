Chasmatocallis macowanii

Chasmatocallis macowanii, commonly known as the Macowan's spider orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces intricate, spider-like flowers with a unique coloration pattern that ranges from pale yellow to deep orange. Its blooms are typically small, measuring around 1-2 centimeters in diameter, and are arranged in a loose, racemose inflorescence.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
