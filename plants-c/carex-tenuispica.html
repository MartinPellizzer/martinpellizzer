Carex tenuispica

Carex tenuispica, commonly known as slender spike sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and is often found in temperate regions.

Carex tenuispica plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
