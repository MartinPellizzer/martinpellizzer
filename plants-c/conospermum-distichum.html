Conospermum distichum

Conospermum distichum, commonly known as the pink conospermum, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are typically pink or red, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer.

Conospermum distichum is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
