Cornus racemosa f. nielsenii

Cornus racemosa f. nielsenii, commonly known as the Nielsen's dogwood, is a rare and cultivated form of the flowering dogwood.

It is characterized by its showy white bracts that surround small yellowish flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands or stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
