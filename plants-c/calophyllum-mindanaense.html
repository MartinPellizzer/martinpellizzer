Calophyllum mindanaense

Calophyllum mindanaense, commonly known as Mindanao calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by spherical, red berries containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for ecological importance, it is of interest for conservation and horticultural purposes.
