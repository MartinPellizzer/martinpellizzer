Cerasus avium f. duracina

Cerasus avium f. duracina, commonly known as the sour cherry, is a botanical variant of the sweet cherry species.

It is characterized by its smaller, more acidic fruits compared to the typical sweet cherries. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It produces white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, tart cherries in late summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for use in jams and culinary applications.
