Cotoneaster narynensis

Cotoneaster narynensis, commonly known as the Naryn cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often found in rocky or mountainous areas and is adapted to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
