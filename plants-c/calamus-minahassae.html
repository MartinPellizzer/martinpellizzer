Calamus minahassae

Calamus minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, typical of many palms. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional uses.

Calamus minahassae thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical forests.
