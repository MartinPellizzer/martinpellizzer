Centaurea chaixiana

Centaurea chaixiana, commonly known as the Chaix's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring blue to purple ray flowers surrounding yellow disk flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually erect and branched.

Centaurea chaixiana is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
