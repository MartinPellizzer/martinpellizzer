Clinacanthus angustus

Clinacanthus angustus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved devil's tongue, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are typically pink or purple and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
