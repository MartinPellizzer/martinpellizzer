Citrus medica var. acida

Citrus medica var. acida, commonly known as the sour orange, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature. The plant has thick, leathery leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is highly acidic and is often used in cooking, marmalades, and as a flavoring agent.

The sour orange is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
