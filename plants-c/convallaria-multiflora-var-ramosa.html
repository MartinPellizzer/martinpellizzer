Convallaria multiflora var. ramosa

Convallaria multiflora var. ramosa, commonly known as the fragrant lily-of-the-valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that emit a sweet, fragrant aroma. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that grow in clusters along the stem. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
