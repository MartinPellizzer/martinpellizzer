Cirsium strictum

Cirsium strictum, commonly known as the upright thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, woolly leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads that consist of many small florets, which bloom in summer. Cirsium strictum is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower structures.
