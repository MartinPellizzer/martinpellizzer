Campanula arcuata var. subrhomboidalis

Campanula arcuata var. subrhomboidalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy, and its flowers are bell-shaped, typically ranging from blue to violet in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The variety subrhomboidalis is distinguished by its narrower leaf shape compared to other varieties of Campanula arcuata.
