Cyrtandra × arguta

Cyrtandra × arguta, commonly known as the hybrid whiteflower heliotrope, is a flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyrtandra hainiana and Cyrtandra arguta, both native to tropical regions of Asia. The plant is characterized by its showy, tubular white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Its foliage is usually heart-shaped with a glossy texture, adding to its ornamental value in horticulture.
