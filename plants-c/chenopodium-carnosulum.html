Chenopodium carnosulum

Chenopodium carnosulum, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves that are rich in nutrients. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its flowers are small and greenish, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is cultivated for its nutritious leaves and is valued in both traditional and modern cuisines.
