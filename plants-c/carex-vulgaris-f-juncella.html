Carex vulgaris f. juncella

Carex vulgaris f. juncella, commonly known as the common sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. This plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
