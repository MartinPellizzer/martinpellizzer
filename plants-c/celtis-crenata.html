Celtis crenata

Celtis crenata, commonly known as the Japanese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark green leaves with wavy edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, smooth fruits. Celtis crenata is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in urban environments.
