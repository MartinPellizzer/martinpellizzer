Cyperus sublaevicarinatus

Cyperus sublaevicarinatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-lobed spikelets, which are often subtended by bracts.

Cyperus sublaevicarinatus is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
