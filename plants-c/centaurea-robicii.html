Centaurea robicii

Centaurea robicii, commonly known as the rock centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Centaurea robicii is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to Mediterranean climates.
