Cucumis debilis

Cucumis debilis, commonly known as the wild cucumber, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, spiky fruits that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blossoms.

It is often found growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides, adapting well to various environmental conditions.
