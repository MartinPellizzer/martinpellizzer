Chrysochlamys allenii

Chrysochlamys allenii, commonly known as the golden chrysochlamys, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that ranges from dark green to almost black in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscaped areas.
