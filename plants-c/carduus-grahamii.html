Carduus grahamii

Carduus grahamii, commonly known as Graham's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are spiny and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Graham's thistle plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to survive in arid environments.
