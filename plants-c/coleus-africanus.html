Coleus africanus

Coleus africanus, commonly known as the African coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that range in color from red to orange and purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent when crushed.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
