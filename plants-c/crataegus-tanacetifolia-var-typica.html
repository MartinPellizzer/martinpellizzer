Crataegus tanacetifolia var. typica

Crataegus tanacetifolia var. typica, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white to pink flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces thorny branches, which are typical of the Crataegus genus. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
