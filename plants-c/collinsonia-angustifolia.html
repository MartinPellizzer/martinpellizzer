Collinsonia angustifolia

Collinsonia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bluetail.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States, characterized by its blue-violet flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is known for its attractive, clustered flower heads. It has a short growing season and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

The species is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
