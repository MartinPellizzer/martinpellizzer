Coreopsis canescentifolia

Coreopsis canescentifolia, commonly known as the woolly coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
