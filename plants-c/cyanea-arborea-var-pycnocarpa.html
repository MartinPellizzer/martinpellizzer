Cyanea arborea var. pycnocarpa

Cyanea arborea var. pycnocarpa, commonly known as the dense-fruited Cyanea, is a species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, mesic to wet forest habitats. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fleshy, red or purple berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
