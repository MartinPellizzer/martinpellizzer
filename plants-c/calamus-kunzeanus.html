Calamus kunzeanus

Calamus kunzeanus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sweetflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, though it is not a true palm. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often aromatic and have a pleasant, sweet scent. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
