Centaurium × aschersonianum

Centaurium × aschersonianum, commonly known as the hybrid everlasting or common centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 20–60 cm, featuring slender, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, linear to lanceolate, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Centaurium × aschersonianum is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
