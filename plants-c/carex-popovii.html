Carex popovii

Carex popovii, commonly known as Popov's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant features triangular stems and produces spike-like inflorescences with small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Carex popovii is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.
