Cyathea calva

Cyathea calva, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that supports its growth and helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to several meters.

Cyathea calva plays an important ecological role in tropical forests by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
