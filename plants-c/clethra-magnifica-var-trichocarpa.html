Clethra magnifica var. trichocarpa

Clethra magnifica var. trichocarpa, commonly known as the silverleaf clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its attractive, fragrant white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are elliptical, with a silvery underside that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
