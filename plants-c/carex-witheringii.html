Carex witheringii

Carex witheringii, commonly known as the Withering sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex witheringii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration.
