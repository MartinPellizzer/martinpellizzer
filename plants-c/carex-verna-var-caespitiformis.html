Carex verna var. caespitiformis

Carex verna var. caespitiformis, commonly known as the spring sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense, clump-like growths, giving it its varietal name "caespitiformis," meaning "in a tufted or clump form.". It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Carex verna var. caespitiformis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and forest edges.
