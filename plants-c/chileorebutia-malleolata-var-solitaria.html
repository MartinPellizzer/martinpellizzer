Chileorebutia malleolata var. solitaria

Chileorebutia malleolata var. solitaria, commonly known as the Solitary Chileorebutia, is a unique cactus species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that often bear a single, large, funnel-shaped flower at the apex. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a compact growth habit that makes it well-suited for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
