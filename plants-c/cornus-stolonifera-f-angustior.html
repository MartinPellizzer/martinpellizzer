Cornus stolonifera f. angustior

Cornus stolonifera f. angustior, commonly known as the narrowleaf dogwood, is a shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly toothed edge. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
