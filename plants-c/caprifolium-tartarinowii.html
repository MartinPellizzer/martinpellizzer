Caprifolium tartarinowii

Caprifolium tartarinowii, commonly known as the Tartarian honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring. Its berries are small, dark red, and edible, though they are typically not consumed by humans.

Caprifolium tartarinowii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
