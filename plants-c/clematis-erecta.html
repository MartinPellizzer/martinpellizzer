Clematis erecta

Clematis erecta, commonly known as the upright clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive, intricate structure. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis erecta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in gardens.
