Crepis muricata

Crepis muricata, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. The plant has basal leaves that are rough and hairy, and its flowering heads are small, yellowish, and surrounded by bracts. It produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Crepis muricata is typically perennial and thrives in well-drained soils.
