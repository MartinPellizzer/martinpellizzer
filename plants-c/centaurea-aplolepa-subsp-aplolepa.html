Centaurea aplolepa subsp. aplolepa

Centaurea aplolepa subsp.

aplolepa, commonly known as the narrowleaf star-thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads.

The plant produces small, bracts that surround its flowering heads, which are often mistaken for petals. It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structures.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
