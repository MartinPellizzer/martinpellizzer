Caltha palustris subsp. barthei

Caltha palustris subsp.

barthei, commonly known as the Barthe's water buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe. It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are usually submerged or floating on the water surface. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and is often found in peat bogs and marshes.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic biodiversity and its aesthetic appeal in wetland gardens.
