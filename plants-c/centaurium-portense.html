Centaurium portense

Centaurium portense, commonly known as the coastal centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry grasslands.

Centaurium portense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and colorful blooms.
