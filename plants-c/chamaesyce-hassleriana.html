Chamaesyce hassleriana

Chamaesyce hassleriana, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual or perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and arranged in clusters.

Chamaesyce hassleriana thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.
