Crinum laurentii

Crinum laurentii, commonly known as the African lily, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring strap-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces large, showy white or pale pink flowers with a strong, spicy scent, often blooming in summer. Its bulbous root system is essential for its growth and propagation.

Crinum laurentii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
