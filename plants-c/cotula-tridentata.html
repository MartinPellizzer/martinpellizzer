Cotula tridentata

Cotula tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed cotula, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive three-lobed shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cotula tridentata is typically short-lived, with a compact growth habit and is sometimes used in horticulture for its delicate appearance.
