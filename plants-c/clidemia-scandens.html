Clidemia scandens

Clidemia scandens, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a woody vine that typically grows along forest edges and in disturbed areas, often climbing or trailing along the ground. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped, hairy, and have a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Clidemia scandens is known for its adaptability and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in various conditions.
