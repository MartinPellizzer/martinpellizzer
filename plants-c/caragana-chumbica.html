Caragana chumbica

Caragana chumbica, commonly known as the Tibetan barbed bush, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves that are adapted to cold and arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or barren landscapes.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for local wildlife.
