Clausena san-ki var. calciphila

Clausena san-ki var. calciphila, commonly known as the limestone citrus or calciphile citrus, is a variant of the genus Clausena native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. The plant thrives in calcareous soils, making it particularly adapted to rocky and limestone-rich environments. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated margin.

This variant is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
