Cupressus fallax

Cupressus fallax, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar shape and scale-like foliage that resembles a pine. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its year-round foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
