Campanopsis procumbens var. oppositifolia

Campanopsis procumbens var. oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in specialty gardens.
