Ceropegia tenuicaulis

Ceropegia tenuicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. The plant produces a type of succulent root known as arrowroot, which is used as a starch source. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia tenuicaulis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
