Cassia conferta var. simulans

Cassia conferta var. simulans, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Cassia conferta var. simulans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
