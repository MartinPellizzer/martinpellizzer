Cyperus jubaeiflorus

Cyperus jubaeiflorus, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its erect, triangular stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Cyperus jubaeiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
