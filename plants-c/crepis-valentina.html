Crepis valentina

Crepis valentina, commonly known as the Valentine's crepis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in meadows and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its slender stems and small, grass-like leaves contribute to its elegant appearance.

Crepis valentina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a wildflower in natural habitats.
