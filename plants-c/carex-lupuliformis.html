Carex lupuliformis

Carex lupuliformis, commonly known as the bitter sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Carex lupuliformis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
