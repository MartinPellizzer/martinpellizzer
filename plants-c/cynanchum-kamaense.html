Cynanchum kamaense

Cynanchum kamaense, commonly known as Kama false hellebore, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive hooded corolla, and it produces follicles that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
