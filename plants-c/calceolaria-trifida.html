Calceolaria trifida

Calceolaria trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually violet or purple and bloom in the spring.

Calceolaria trifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
