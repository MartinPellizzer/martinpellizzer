Centaurea × aurigerana

Centaurea × aurigerana, commonly known as the golden centaury, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed heads that are often reddish-brown in color. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
