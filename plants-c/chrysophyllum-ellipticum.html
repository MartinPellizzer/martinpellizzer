Chrysophyllum ellipticum

Chrysophyllum ellipticum, commonly known as the African cherry or elliptic wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, encased in a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
