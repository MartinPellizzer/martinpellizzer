Cornus scabrida

Cornus scabrida, commonly known as rough cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, characterized by its rough, bark-like texture on its stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges.

Cornus scabrida is often found in woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade.
