Cirsium pseudocreticum

Cirsium pseudocreticum, commonly known as the Cretan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Cirsium pseudocreticum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
