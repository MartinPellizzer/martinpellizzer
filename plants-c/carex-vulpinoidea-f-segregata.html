Carex vulpinoidea f. segregata

Carex vulpinoidea f. segregata, commonly known as the fox squirrel sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly rough texture, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability.
