Crataegus araioclada

Crataegus araioclada, commonly known as the Japanese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Crataegus araioclada is also noted for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
