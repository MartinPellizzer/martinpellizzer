Crotalaria orthoclada

Crotalaria orthoclada, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves and yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod and are often used as a source of protein in some regions.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and agriculturally useful.
