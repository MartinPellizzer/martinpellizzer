Cerasus maximowiczii

Cerasus maximowiczii, commonly known as the Manchurian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Cerasus maximowiczii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
