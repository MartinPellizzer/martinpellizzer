Cerastium triviale var. glandulosum

Cerastium triviale var. glandulosum, commonly known as sticky chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive glandular texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and garden beds.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and easy maintenance.
