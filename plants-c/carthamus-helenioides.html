Carthamus helenioides

Carthamus helenioides, commonly known as the Helen's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural areas.
