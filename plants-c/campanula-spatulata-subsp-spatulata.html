Campanula spatulata subsp. spatulata

Campanula spatulata subsp.

spatulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to broadly ovate leaves with a distinctive broad, rounded base.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
