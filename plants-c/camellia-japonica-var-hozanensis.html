Camellia japonica var. hozanensis

Camellia japonica var. hozanensis, commonly known as the Hozanensis camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is typically evergreen, with a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for ornamental gardening. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, often blooming in late winter to early spring.

This variety is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
