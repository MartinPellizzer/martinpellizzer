Cyperus nuerensis

Cyperus nuerensis, commonly known as the Nuer sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous structure that helps it thrive in moist environments.

Cyperus nuerensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic ecosystems.
