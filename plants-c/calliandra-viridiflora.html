Calliandra viridiflora

Calliandra viridiflora, commonly known as green-flowered calliandra, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. Its flowers are rich in nectar, making it an important food source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Calliandra viridiflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, enhancing soil fertility.
