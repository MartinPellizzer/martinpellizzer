Cerastium minutum

Cerastium minutum, commonly known as lesser mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, dry fruits called capsules.

Cerastium minutum is often found in calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
