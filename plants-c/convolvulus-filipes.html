Convolvulus filipes

Convolvulus filipes, commonly known as slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus filipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
