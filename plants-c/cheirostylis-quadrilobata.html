Cheirostylis quadrilobata

Cheirostylis quadrilobata, commonly known as the four-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant has linear to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spike and have a unique four-lobed structure in the lip.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
