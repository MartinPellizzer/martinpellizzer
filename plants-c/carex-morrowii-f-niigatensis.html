Carex morrowii f. niigatensis

Carex morrowii f. niigatensis, commonly known as the Niigata sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, upright clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or brown, which appear in late spring to early summer. It is native to Japan and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for water gardens and wetland landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
