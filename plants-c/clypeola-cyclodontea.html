Clypeola cyclodontea

Clypeola cyclodontea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clypeola, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically hairy stems. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense, rounded clusters that bloom in late summer.

Clypeola cyclodontea is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and coastal areas.
