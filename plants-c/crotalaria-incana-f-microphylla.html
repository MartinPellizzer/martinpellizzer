Crotalaria incana f. microphylla

Crotalaria incana f. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved cowpea or narrow-leaved crotalaria, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow upright or prostrate, depending on environmental conditions. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically narrow with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are showy, yellow with a distinctive black or purple spot at the base, and are arranged in racemes.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable forage crop in agricultural systems.
