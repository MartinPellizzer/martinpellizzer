Carex muricata subsp. contigua

Carex muricata subsp.

contigua, commonly known as the rough-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden and bog plantings.
