Cassia agnes

Cassia agnes, commonly known as golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by long, slender pods. Cassia agnes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
