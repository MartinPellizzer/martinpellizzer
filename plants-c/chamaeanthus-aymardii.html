Chamaeanthus aymardii

Chamaeanthus aymardii, commonly known as the Aymard's rock cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically features dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Chamaeanthus aymardii thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.
