Clematis ibarensis subsp. ibarensis

Clematis ibarensis subsp.

ibarensis, commonly known as the Ibaran Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Spain. It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and produces showy, blue to violet-colored flowers in late spring.

The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires support due to its climbing growth form.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
