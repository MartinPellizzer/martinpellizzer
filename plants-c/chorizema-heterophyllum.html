Chorizema heterophyllum

Chorizema heterophyllum, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and ability to store water in its leaves. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Chorizema heterophyllum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its ecosystem.
