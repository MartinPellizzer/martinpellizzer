Capparis duchesnei var. parvifolia

Capparis duchesnei var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens in Mediterranean climates.
