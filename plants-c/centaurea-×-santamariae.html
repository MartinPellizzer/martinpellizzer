Centaurea × santamariae

Centaurea × santamariae, commonly known as the Santamaria's knapweed, is a hybrid species belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to pink flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, conical inflorescences. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Centaurea × santamariae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its showy blooms.
