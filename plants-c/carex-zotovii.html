Carex zotovii

Carex zotovii, commonly known as Zotov's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and spike-like flower clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
