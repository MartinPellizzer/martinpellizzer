Carex rigida var. juncea

Carex rigida var. juncea, commonly known as slender hardstem sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly rough texture. Its flowering spikes are usually erect and contain small, inconspicuous flowers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
