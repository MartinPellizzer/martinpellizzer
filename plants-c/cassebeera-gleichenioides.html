Cassebeera gleichenioides

Cassebeera gleichenioides, commonly known as the red-tailed palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, round fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
