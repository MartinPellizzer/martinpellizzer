Carthamus hispanicus subsp. araneosus

Carthamus hispanicus subsp.

araneosus, commonly known as the Spanish safflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers.

The plant produces fibrous, spiny stems and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
