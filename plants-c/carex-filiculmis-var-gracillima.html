Carex filiculmis var. gracillima

Carex filiculmis var. gracillima, commonly known as slender filiculmis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value to gardens.
