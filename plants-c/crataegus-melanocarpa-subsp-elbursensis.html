Crataegus melanocarpa subsp. elbursensis

Crataegus melanocarpa subsp.

elbursensis, commonly known as the Elburs hawthorn, is a subspecies native to the Elburs Mountains in Iran. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain.

The plant produces white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a glossy upper surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.
