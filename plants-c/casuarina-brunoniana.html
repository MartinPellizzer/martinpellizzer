Casuarina brunoniana

Casuarina brunoniana, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Sheoak, is a member of the Casuarinaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant has distinctive needle-like leaves and produces small, woody cones that contain the seeds. It is well adapted to salt-laden winds and can thrive in harsh coastal environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological resilience make it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
