Crataegus callosa

Crataegus callosa, commonly known as the rough crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Crataegus callosa is often found in open woods, thickets, and along stream banks, thriving in a variety of soil types.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and wildlife habitat.
