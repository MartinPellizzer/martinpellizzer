Cyathea molliuscula

Cyathea molliuscula, commonly known as the velvet tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The fronds are covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
