Cucurbita maxima farinae

Cucurbita maxima farinae, commonly known as the pumpkin, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, spherical or elongated fruits with thick, fibrous skins and a sweet, edible pulp. The plant is a vine that climbs using tendrils and produces yellow flowers that develop into the familiar orange-colored fruits. Its leaves are large, lobed, and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The fruit is rich in nutrients, making it a valuable crop for both food and industrial uses.
