Cariniana decandra

Cariniana decandra, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its elongated, woody fruits that contain the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a broad, buttressed trunk and dense, glossy green leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

The plant plays a vital ecological role in the rainforest, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
