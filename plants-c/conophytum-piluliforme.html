Conophytum piluliforme

Conophytum piluliforme, commonly known as the pill-shaped conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its rounded, fleshy leaves that resemble small pills. The plant stores water in its thick, segmented leaves, allowing it to survive in arid environments. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, typically in clusters.

Conophytum piluliforme is often grown as a decorative succulent due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
