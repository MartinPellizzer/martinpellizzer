Carpolobia versicolor

Carpolobia versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that range from yellow to red, often with contrasting patterns. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Carpolobia versicolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.
