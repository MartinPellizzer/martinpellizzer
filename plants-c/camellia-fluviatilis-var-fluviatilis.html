Camellia fluviatilis var. fluviatilis

Camellia fluviatilis var. fluviatilis, commonly known as the river camellia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Vietnam and Cambodia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows near water bodies such as rivers and streams. The plant features dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are camellia-like, with showy, pink to red petals that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
