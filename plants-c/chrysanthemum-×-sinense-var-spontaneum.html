Chrysanthemum × sinense var. spontaneum

Chrysanthemum × sinense var. spontaneum, commonly known as the wild chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30–100 cm, featuring upright stems and hairy leaves that are lance-shaped. The plant produces numerous daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange, blooming from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
