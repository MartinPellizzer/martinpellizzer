Cotoneaster niger subsp. moravicus

Cotoneaster niger subsp.

moravicus, commonly known as the Moravian black cotoneaster, is a shrub native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its dark, glossy berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter.

The plant typically grows in woodlands and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and winter interest.
