Cassia × desolata

Cassia × desolata, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red inflorescences that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, winged fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia × desolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types and climates.
