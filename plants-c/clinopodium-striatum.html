Clinopodium striatum

Clinopodium striatum, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or streamside habitats. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive silvery-white underside.

Small, pale blue to purple flowers appear in whorled clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
