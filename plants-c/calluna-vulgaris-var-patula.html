Calluna vulgaris var. patula

Calluna vulgaris var. patula, commonly known as the creeping heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as ground cover.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
