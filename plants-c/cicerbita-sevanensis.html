Cicerbita sevanensis

Cicerbita sevanensis, commonly known as the Sevan bittercress, is a flowering plant native to the Lake Sevan region in Armenia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white or pale yellow.

Cicerbita sevanensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
