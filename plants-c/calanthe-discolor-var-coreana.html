Calanthe discolor var. coreana

Calanthe discolor var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean calanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lanceolate, dark green leaves and produces showy, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
