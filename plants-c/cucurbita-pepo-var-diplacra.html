Cucurbita pepo var. diplacra

Cucurbita pepo var. diplacra, commonly known as the Mexican ridge gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine that produces elongated, ridged fruits with a warty surface. The plant is native to Central and South America and is often cultivated for its edible fruits. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates.

The fruit can be used in various culinary applications, including soups and stews.
