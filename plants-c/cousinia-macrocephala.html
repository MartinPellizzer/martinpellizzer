Cousinia macrocephala

Cousinia macrocephala, commonly known as the giant cup plant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy leaves and a robust root system adapted to survive in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Cousinia macrocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
