Chrysophyllum novoguineense

Chrysophyllum novoguineense, commonly known as the Guinean star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, yellowish skin and a sweet, juicy pulp that contains numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
