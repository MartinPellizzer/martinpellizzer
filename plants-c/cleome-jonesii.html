Cleome jonesii

Cleome jonesii, commonly known as the Mexican Marvel, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with branching stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cleome jonesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and ease of growth.
