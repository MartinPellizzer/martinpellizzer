Carex alata var. ferruginea

Carex alata var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusted arrow sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with reddish or rust-colored markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the sedge family.
