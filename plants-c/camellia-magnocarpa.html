Camellia magnocarpa

Camellia magnocarpa, commonly known as the giant fruit camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy, red-orange fruits that are edible and contain numerous seeds. Camellia magnocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates.

It is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits.
