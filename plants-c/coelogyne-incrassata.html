Coelogyne incrassata

Coelogyne incrassata, commonly known as the thick-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or as a terrestrial plant in humid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange with red or purple markings, and it produces a distinctive, fragrant scent.

Coelogyne incrassata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
