Caragana microphylla

Caragana microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf shrub or silky caragana, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor, well-drained soils, making it suitable for reclamation projects.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
