Campanula rhomboidalis var. angustifolia

Campanula rhomboidalis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a rough texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
