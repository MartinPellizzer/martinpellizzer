Chaetochlamys ciliata

Chaetochlamys ciliata, commonly known as the fringed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive fringed appearance along the edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers and delicate foliage.
