Carex normalis var. tincta

Carex normalis var. tincta, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial sedge native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinted leaves that add a striking visual element to gardens and natural habitats. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical spikes that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex normalis var. tincta is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize soil in wet or boggy areas.
