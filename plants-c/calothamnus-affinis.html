Calothamnus affinis

Calothamnus affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically red or orange in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bright red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Calothamnus affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
