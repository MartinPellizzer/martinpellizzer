Campanula cochleariifolia var. tenella

Campanula cochleariifolia var. tenella, commonly known as the small bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-gray hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and delicate appearance, suitable for both ornamental and ecological landscaping.
