Conyza laxiflora

Conyza laxiflora, commonly known as the slenderleaf blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters. It is characterized by its wind-pollinated inflorescences and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Conyza laxiflora is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
