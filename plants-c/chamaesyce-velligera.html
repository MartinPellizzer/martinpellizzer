Chamaesyce velligera

Chamaesyce velligera, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

This species is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
