Centunculus pentandrus

Centunculus pentandrus, commonly known as the five-stamen buttercup, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers with five stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are palmately arranged.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
