Cerastium semidecandrum subvar. abortivum

Cerastium semidecandrum subvar. abortivum, commonly known as the short-stalked mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and oval-shaped, while the flowers are pink to purple and bloom in early summer. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
