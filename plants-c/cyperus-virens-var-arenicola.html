Cyperus virens var. arenicola

Cyperus virens var. arenicola, commonly known as sand flats sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to coastal and sandy environments.

It thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and tidal flats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and fluctuating water levels.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
