Carex semiplena var. kirigaminensis

Carex semiplena var. kirigaminensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are often slightly curled at the tips. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
