Ctenitis boryana

Ctenitis boryana, commonly known as the Bory's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, frond-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive, spirally arranged stem with a fibrous, woody texture. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a soft, light green color and a delicate, feathery appearance.

Ctenitis boryana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
