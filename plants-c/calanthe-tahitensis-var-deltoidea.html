Calanthe tahitensis var. deltoidea

Calanthe tahitensis var. deltoidea, commonly known as the Tahitian calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, orchid-like flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a yellow lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
