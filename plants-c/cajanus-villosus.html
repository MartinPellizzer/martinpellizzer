Cajanus villosus

Cajanus villosus, commonly known as the hairy groundnut, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are often used for their nutritional value.

Cajanus villosus is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in agricultural systems.
