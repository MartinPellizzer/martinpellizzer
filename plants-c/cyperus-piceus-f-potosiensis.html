Cyperus piceus f. potosiensis

Cyperus piceus f. potosiensis, commonly known as the Potosi flatsedge, is a variant of the common flatsedge native to South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland habitats, often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
