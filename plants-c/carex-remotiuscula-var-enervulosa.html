Carex remotiuscula var. enervulosa

Carex remotiuscula var. enervulosa, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
