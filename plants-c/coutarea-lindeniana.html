Coutarea lindeniana

Coutarea lindeniana, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic wood, which has been used in traditional crafts and medicinal applications.
