Ceiba lupuna

Ceiba lupuna, commonly known as the yellow ceiba, is a large tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its buttressed roots and towering height, often exceeding 30 meters. The tree produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and contributing to its longevity.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting diverse flora and fauna.
