Cupressus triquetra

Cupressus triquetra, commonly known as the Three-angled cypress, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are arranged in crossed pairs. The tree produces small, round cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its foliage is dense and provides habitat for various wildlife species.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different soil conditions.
