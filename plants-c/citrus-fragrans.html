Citrus fragrans

Citrus fragrans, commonly known as the sweet orange.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, yellow-orange fruits. The fruit contains a juicy pulp surrounding a segmented white pith and numerous seeds.

It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in food, beverages, and traditional medicine.
