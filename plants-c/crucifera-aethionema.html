Crucifera aethionema

Crucifera aethionema, commonly known as the Ethiopian mustard, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like broccoli and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

The seeds of Crucifera aethionema are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
