Circaea canadensis

Circaea canadensis, commonly known as Canadian white-campion, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and a woody, branched stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.
