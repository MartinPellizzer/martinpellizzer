Campsis radicans f. aurea

Campsis radicans f. aurea, commonly known as the golden flame trumpetvine, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is a hybrid form of the scarlet flame (Campsis radicans) and is characterized by its vibrant golden-orange tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blossoms. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance ornamental plants.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract hummingbirds and butterflies, adding ecological value to the landscape.
