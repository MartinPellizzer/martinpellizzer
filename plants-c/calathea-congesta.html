Calathea congesta

Calathea congesta, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that display intricate patterns of green, silver, and purple. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.

Calathea congesta prefers humid environments and well-draining soil to maintain its health and vibrant appearance.
