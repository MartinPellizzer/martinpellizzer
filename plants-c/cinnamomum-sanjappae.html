Cinnamomum sanjappae

Cinnamomum sanjappae, commonly known as the Sanjappae cinnamon or Malabar cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain a single seed, which is used for propagation. Its bark and leaves are valued for their aromatic properties and have been traditionally used in local medicine and as a spice.

However, it is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of conservation interest.
