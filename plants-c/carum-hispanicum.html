Carum hispanicum

Carum hispanicum, commonly known as Spanish cumin, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces oval, dark brown seeds that are used in traditional cooking and herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its historical use dates back to ancient times, where it was valued for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
