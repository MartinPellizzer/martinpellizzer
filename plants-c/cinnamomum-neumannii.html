Cinnamomum neumannii

Cinnamomum neumannii, commonly known as the Java cinnamon or Cinnamomum neumannii, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is valued for its aromatic bark, which is used to produce a type of cinnamon with a distinct flavor and aroma. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its essential oils, extracted from the bark and leaves, are widely used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its economic and aromatic properties.
