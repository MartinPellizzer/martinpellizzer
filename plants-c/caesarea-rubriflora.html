Caesarea rubriflora

Caesarea rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered caesaria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with small, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Caesarea rubriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
