Clusia guatemalensis var. orizabae

Clusia guatemalensis var. orizabae, commonly known as the Orizaba clusia, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate partial shade.
