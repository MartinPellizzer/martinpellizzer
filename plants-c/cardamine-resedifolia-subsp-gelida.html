Cardamine resedifolia subsp. gelida

Cardamine resedifolia subsp.

gelida, commonly known as the alpine watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It thrives in moist, cool environments such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes, often found in mountainous areas of Europe and Asia.

The plant has slender, creeping stems that produce small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it is known for its peppery, spicy flavor.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in traditional herbal cuisine.
