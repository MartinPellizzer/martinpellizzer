Clematis fulva

Clematis fulva, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically bright green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are woody and often have a reddish hue, and it is known for its rapid growth habit.

Clematis fulva is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
