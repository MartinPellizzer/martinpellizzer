Castanopsis carlesii var. spinulosa

Castanopsis carlesii var. spinulosa, commonly known as the hairy chinquapin, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage and produces small, spiny nuts enclosed in a woody cupule. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
