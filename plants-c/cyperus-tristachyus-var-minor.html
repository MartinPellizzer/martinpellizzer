Cyperus tristachyus var. minor

Cyperus tristachyus var. minor, commonly known as dwarf umbrella sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers on short, erect spikes. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along water bodies. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
