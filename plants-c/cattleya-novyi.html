Cattleya novyi

Cattleya novyi, commonly known as the Novi Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has long, strap-like leaves and grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid environments. Its blooming period usually occurs during the summer months, and it is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

The species is also noted for its ability to produce a high number of pseudobulbs, which contribute to its robust growth and longevity.
