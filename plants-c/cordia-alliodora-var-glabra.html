Cordia alliodora var. glabra

Cordia alliodora var. glabra, commonly known as the white cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
