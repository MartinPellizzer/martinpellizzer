Cryptocarya splendens

Cryptocarya splendens, commonly known as the golden sycamore, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or greyish hue, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Cryptocarya splendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
