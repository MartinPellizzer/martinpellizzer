Chrysophyllum soboliferum

Chrysophyllum soboliferum, commonly known as the African cherry or African wild cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a single seed.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
