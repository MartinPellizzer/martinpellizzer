Chaetogastra solmsii

Chaetogastra solmsii, commonly known as the Solms's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Chaetogastra solmsii plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to coastal environments.
