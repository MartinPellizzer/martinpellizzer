Cinnamomum pachypodum

Cinnamomum pachypodum, commonly known as the African cinnamon or slender cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by elliptical, dark green leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
