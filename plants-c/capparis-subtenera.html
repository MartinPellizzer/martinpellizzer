Capparis subtenera

Capparis subtenera, commonly known as the slender caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, exhibiting a compact, thorny structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale blue, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are small, oval, and often covered in fine hairs, while its fruits are capsule-like and contain several seeds.

Capparis subtenera is adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
