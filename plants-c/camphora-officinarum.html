Camphora officinarum

Camphora officinarum, commonly known as camphor laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark that contain camphor. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small white flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and distinctive scent, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and commercial properties.
