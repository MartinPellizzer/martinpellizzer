Celastrus metzianus

Celastrus metzianus, commonly known as the Mexican staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments within forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
