Copaiva officinalis

Copaiva officinalis, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its aromatic resin, which is widely used in traditional medicine. The plant produces a sticky, turpentine-like sap that has been utilized for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
