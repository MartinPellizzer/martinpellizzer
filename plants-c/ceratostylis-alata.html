Ceratostylis alata

Ceratostylis alata, commonly known as the spreading horned orchid, is a herbaceous orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swampy areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The orchid's unique floral structure includes prominent, horn-like appendages on the petals, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.
