Crassula exilis subsp. sedifolia

Crassula exilis subsp.

sedifolia, commonly known as the "Laceleaf Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features compact, rosette-shaped growth with thin, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating.

The leaves are typically a vibrant green, sometimes with a reddish tinge under stress or in full sun. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the warmer months.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens or container growing due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
