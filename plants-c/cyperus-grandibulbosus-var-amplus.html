Cyperus grandibulbosus var. amplus

Cyperus grandibulbosus var. amplus, commonly known as the broadbulb flat sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow waters, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are characteristic of the genus Cyperus.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
