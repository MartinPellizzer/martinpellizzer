Cistus parviflorus var. spathulatus

Cistus parviflorus var. spathulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features woody stems and aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
