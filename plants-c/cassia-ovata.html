Cassia ovata

Cassia ovata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. Cassia ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval and slightly serrated.
