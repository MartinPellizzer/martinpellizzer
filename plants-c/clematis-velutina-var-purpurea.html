Clematis velutina var. purpurea

Clematis velutina var. purpurea, commonly known as purple velvet clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a distinctive violet hue. The plant has coarsely toothed, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety down. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis velutina var. purpurea is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its cascading blooms.
