Combretum stenanthum

Combretum stenanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
