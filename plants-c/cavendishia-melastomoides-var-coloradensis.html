Cavendishia melastomoides var. coloradensis

Cavendishia melastomoides var. coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado Cavendishia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
