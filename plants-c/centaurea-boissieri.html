Centaurea boissieri

Centaurea boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea boissieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
