Cerastium atrovirens

Cerastium atrovirens, commonly known as blackleaf mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are pink to violet, clustered in umbels, and bloom in early spring.

Cerastium atrovirens is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodland edges and stream banks.
