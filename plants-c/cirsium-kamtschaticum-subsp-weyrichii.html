Cirsium kamtschaticum subsp. weyrichii

Cirsium kamtschaticum subsp.

weyrichii, commonly known as the Kamtschatic mountain thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped with serrated edges and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
