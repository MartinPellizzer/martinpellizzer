Chenopodium diversifolium var. montuosum

Chenopodium diversifolium var. montuosum, commonly known as the mountain goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinctive bluish-green color. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are enclosed in three-angled, dry fruits. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
