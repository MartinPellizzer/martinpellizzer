Carex parasitica

Carex parasitica, commonly known as the parasitic sedge, is a unique plant species that exhibits parasitic behavior.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, but differs by relying on other plants for nutrients. Unlike typical sedges, Carex parasitica lacks chlorophyll and cannot perform photosynthesis. Instead, it attaches to the roots of host plants, drawing water and nutrients from them.

This parasitic lifestyle makes it an interesting subject in the study of plant ecology and symbiotic relationships.
