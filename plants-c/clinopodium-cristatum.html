Clinopodium cristatum

Clinopodium cristatum, commonly known as mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. Clinopodium cristatum thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
