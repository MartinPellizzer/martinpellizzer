Casuarina incana

Casuarina incana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved she-oak, is a evergreen tree or shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Casuarinaceae and is characterized by its unique, needle-like leaves that are clustered in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain seeds. Casuarina incana is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy species often used for dune stabilization.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
