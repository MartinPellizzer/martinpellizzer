Cereus gonzalezii

Cereus gonzalezii, commonly known as the Gonzalez's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and sparse, spiny foliage. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming at night. It is well adapted to hot, dry environments and requires minimal water to thrive.

Cereus gonzalezii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
