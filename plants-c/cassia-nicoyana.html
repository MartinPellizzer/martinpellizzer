Cassia nicoyana

Cassia nicoyana, commonly known as the Nicoyan cassia, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing seeds covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Cassia nicoyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
