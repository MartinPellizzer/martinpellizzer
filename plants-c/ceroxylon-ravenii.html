Ceroxylon ravenii

Ceroxylon ravenii, commonly known as the Rarotonga palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the Cook Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to salt spray and sandy soils. Its unique morphology makes it a notable species in the palms of the Pacific.

Ceroxylon ravenii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
