Convolvulus tricolor var. hortensis

Convolvulus tricolor var. hortensis, commonly known as the tri-color morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and strikingly colorful, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically display shades of red, white, and blue. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.
