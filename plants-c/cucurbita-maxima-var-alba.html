Cucurbita maxima var. alba

Cucurbita maxima var. alba, commonly known as the white pumpkin, is a variant of the common squash species.

It is characterized by its large, round, and predominantly white to pale yellow fruit, which can reach weights of several kilograms. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, known for its climbing vines and lobed leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow with a distinct five-petaled structure, and it produces both male and female flowers on the same plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses, particularly in certain cultural cuisines.
