Clinopodium pamphylicum subsp. alanyense

Clinopodium pamphylicum subsp.

alanyense, commonly known as Alanyan woodruff, is a perennial herb native to Turkey. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils in open habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant, mint-like aroma.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale blue, blooming in the summer months.
