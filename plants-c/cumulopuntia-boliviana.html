Cumulopuntia boliviana

Cumulopuntia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian star cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes mountains of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by fleshy, red fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a deep root system adapted to arid conditions.

Cumulopuntia boliviana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
