Carex vaginata subsp. quasivaginata

Carex vaginata subsp.

quasivaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and pointed, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to brown in color.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
