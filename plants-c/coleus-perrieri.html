Coleus perrieri

Coleus perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with leaves that can display shades of red, orange, and purple. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is closely related to other ornamental coleus species. It typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.
