Crataegus lobata

Crataegus lobata, commonly known as the Pacific crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It features small, oval leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring. The plant bears small, red or yellow fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The Pacific crabapple is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
