Crataegus ambigua subsp. transcaspica

Crataegus ambigua subsp.

transcaspica, commonly known as the Transcaspian hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, often white or pink flowers.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
