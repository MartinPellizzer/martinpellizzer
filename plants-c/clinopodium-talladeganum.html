Clinopodium talladeganum

Clinopodium talladeganum, commonly known as Talladega mint, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong minty fragrance when crushed.

Clinopodium talladeganum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
