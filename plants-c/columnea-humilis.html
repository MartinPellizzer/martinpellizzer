Columnea humilis

Columnea humilis, commonly known as the lowly columnea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers typically come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in hanging baskets.

The plant's foliage is usually dark green with a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.
