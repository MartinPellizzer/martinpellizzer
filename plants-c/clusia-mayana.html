Clusia mayana

Clusia mayana, commonly known as the evergreen glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a smooth, leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its adaptability to different soil types and resistance to drought make it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Clusia mayana is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded or sunny conditions, making it versatile for landscaping purposes.
