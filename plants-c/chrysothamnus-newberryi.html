Chrysothamnus newberryi

Chrysothamnus newberryi, commonly known as Newberry's goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Newberry's goldenbush is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
