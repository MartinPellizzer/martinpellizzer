Cucurbita maxima var. turbaniformis

Cucurbita maxima var. turbaniformis, commonly known as the turban squash, is a cultivated variant of the giant pumpkin.

It is characterized by its distinctive, turban-like shape with a broad, flattened base and a rounded top. The fruit typically has a rough, warty skin that can range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. This variety is prized for its large size and is often used in both culinary and decorative applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is a member of the Cucurbita genus.
