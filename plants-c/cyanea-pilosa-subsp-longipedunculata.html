Cyanea pilosa subsp. longipedunculata

Cyanea pilosa subsp.

longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate Cyanea, is a member of the Rubiaceae family. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves.

The flowers are small, clustered in terminal panicles, and typically have five petals. The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated peduncles, which are the stalks supporting the flower clusters.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
