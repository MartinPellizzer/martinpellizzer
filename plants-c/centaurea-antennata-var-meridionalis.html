Centaurea antennata var. meridionalis

Centaurea antennata var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in a cluster. The leaves are typically narrow and pointed, with a rough texture. This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

It is an annual or biennial plant that produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.
