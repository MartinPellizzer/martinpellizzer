Cathartolinum rupestre

Cathartolinum rupestre, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid regions.

It typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as cliffs, gravelly soils, and rocky outcrops. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Cathartolinum rupestre is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and adaptive species.
