Cyanthillium

Cyanthillium, commonly known as the blue daisy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue petals and yellow central disk, which resemble a daisy. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cyanthillium is native to southern Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
