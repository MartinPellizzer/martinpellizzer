Cochlearia orientalis

Cochlearia orientalis, commonly known as the Oriental horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar characteristics. The plant features striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, often with a distinctive horn-like shape. Its leaves are basal, lobed, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cochlearia orientalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
