Carteretia paniculata

Carteretia paniculata, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils. It produces a single, branched inflorescence that can reach up to two meters in height.

Carteretia paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
