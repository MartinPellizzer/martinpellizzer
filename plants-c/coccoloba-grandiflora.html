Coccoloba grandiflora

Coccoloba grandiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf glorybower, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, orange-red flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with five to seven leaflets, and the flowers are clustered in panicles, adding to its ornamental value.

Coccoloba grandiflora is valued for its vibrant foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
