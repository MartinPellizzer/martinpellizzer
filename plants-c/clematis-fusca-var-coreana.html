Clematis fusca var. coreana

Clematis fusca var. coreana, commonly known as the Korean dusky clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Korea.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
