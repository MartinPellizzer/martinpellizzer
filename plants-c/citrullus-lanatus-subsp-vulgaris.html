Citrullus lanatus subsp. vulgaris

Citrullus lanatus subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as watermelon, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family. It is characterized by its sprawling vine growth habit and large, lobed leaves.

The plant produces bright green, striped fruits with a thick rind and juicy, sweet red or yellow flesh. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is rich in water, vitamins, and antioxidants, making it both nutritious and widely consumed.
