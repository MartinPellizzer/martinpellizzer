Celosia semperflorens

Celosia semperflorens, commonly known as flame flower or cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant, plume-like inflorescences that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its long flowering period, often blooming throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
