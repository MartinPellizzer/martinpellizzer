Cymbopogon tamba

Cymbopogon tamba, commonly known as Tamba grass, is a species of perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest margins. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Cymbopogon tamba is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
