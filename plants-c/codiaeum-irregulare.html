Codiaeum irregulare

Codiaeum irregulare, commonly known as the "Rainbow Plant," is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of red, yellow, orange, and green. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin and a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Codiaeum irregulare is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
