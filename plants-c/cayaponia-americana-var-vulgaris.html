Cayaponia americana var. vulgaris

Cayaponia americana var. vulgaris, commonly known as the American cayaponia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
