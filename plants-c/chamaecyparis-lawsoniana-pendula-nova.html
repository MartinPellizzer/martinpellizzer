Chamaecyparis lawsoniana pendula-nova

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana pendula-nova, commonly known as the Lawson's cypress weeping form, is a cultivar of the Lawson's cypress, characterized by its distinctive weeping growth habit.

It features long, cascading branches that create a soft, elegant appearance, making it a popular ornamental tree. The foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are a deep green, providing a dense canopy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring moderate watering.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
