Caladium esculentum bataviensis

Caladium esculentum bataviensis, commonly known as the Batavias spinach, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is cultivated for its edible leaves, which are similar in taste and texture to spinach. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is gaining popularity in both horticulture and agriculture.
