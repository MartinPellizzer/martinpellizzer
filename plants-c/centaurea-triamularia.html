Centaurea triamularia

Centaurea triamularia, commonly known as the Triangular Centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, upright growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with reddish or purplish markings.

Centaurea triamularia is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
