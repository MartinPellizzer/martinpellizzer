Croton montis-silam

Croton montis-silam, commonly known as the Silam mountain croton, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from bright green to deep red, often with intricate patterns. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton montis-silam is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
