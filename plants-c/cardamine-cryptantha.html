Cardamine cryptantha

Cardamine cryptantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-20 centimeters, with slender stems and small, alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and often hairy. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters and blooming in early spring.

The plant is often found in stream banks, wet meadows, and other damp habitats where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
