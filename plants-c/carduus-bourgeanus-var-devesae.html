Carduus bourgeanus var. devesae

Carduus bourgeanus var. devesae, commonly known as the Mediterranean thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or disturbed soils and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its robust root system that allows it to thrive in arid conditions. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological adaptability make it a notable species in its native range.
