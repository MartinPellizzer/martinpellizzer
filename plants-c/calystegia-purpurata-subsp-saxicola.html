Calystegia purpurata subsp. saxicola

Calystegia purpurata subsp.

saxicola, commonly known as the rock nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a trailing perennial vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
