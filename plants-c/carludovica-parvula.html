Carludovica parvula

Carludovica parvula, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to purple. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, orange-red fruit that splits open when mature.

Carludovica parvula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
