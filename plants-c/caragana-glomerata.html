Caragana glomerata

Caragana glomerata, commonly known as the Siberian pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is highly drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or loamy soils.

Caragana glomerata is often used for soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
