Cordia globosa var. humilis

Cordia globosa var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf globe tree, is a small, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptic or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its ornamental value comes from its compact form and attractive foliage.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
