Cynaroides

Cynaroides, commonly known as the African thistle, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to various regions of Africa and are characterized by their distinctive, spiny leaves and yellow to orange composite flowers. Cynaroides species typically grow in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions. They often have a compact growth habit with hairy foliage, which helps reduce water loss.

Some species are used in traditional medicine, though their botanical study remains limited compared to more widely researched plants.
