Cleome foliolosa

Cleome foliolosa, commonly known as the leafy cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom throughout the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Cleome foliolosa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
