Columnea grandiflora

Columnea grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy texture.

Columnea grandiflora thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to flourish.
