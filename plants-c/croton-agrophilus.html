Croton agrophilus

Croton agrophilus, commonly known as the African croton, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that can range from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

Croton agrophilus is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
