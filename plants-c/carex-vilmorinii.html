Carex vilmorinii

Carex vilmorinii, commonly known as the Vilmorin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which is part of the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Carex vilmorinii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
