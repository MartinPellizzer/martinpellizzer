Cyananthus inflatus var. sylvestris

Cyananthus inflatus var. sylvestris, commonly known as the mountain bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth texture, while its stems are upright and branched.
