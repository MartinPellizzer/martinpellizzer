Centaurea × croatica

Centaurea × croatica, commonly known as the Croatian knapweed, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads surrounded by vibrant red bracts. The plant typically grows in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces.

Centaurea × croatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its colorful display.
