Carex straminea var. alata

Carex straminea var. alata, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Native to moist habitats in North America, this plant thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
