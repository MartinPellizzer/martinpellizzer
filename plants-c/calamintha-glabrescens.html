Calamintha glabrescens

Calamintha glabrescens, commonly known as smooth catnip, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their mild sedative properties.

Calamintha glabrescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
