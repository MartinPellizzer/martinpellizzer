Camphorata monandra

Camphorata monandra, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-white foliage that resembles snow, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its leaves are aromatic and contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic uses.

Camphorata monandra is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
