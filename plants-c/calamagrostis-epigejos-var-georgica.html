Calamagrostis epigejos var. georgica

Calamagrostis epigejos var. georgica, commonly known as the Georgia grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, silver-blue foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. This variety thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats. It produces fluffy, silver-green seed heads in the fall, which contribute to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
