Celosia namaensis

Celosia namaensis, commonly known as the Namibian plume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, feather-like bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with the colorful bracts serving as the primary ornamental feature.

Celosia namaensis is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
