Citrus medica var. sarcodactylis

Citrus medica var. sarcodactylis, commonly known as the finger lime, is a unique citrus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finger-like, elongated fruits that resemble a cluster of fingers, hence its common name. The fruit has a thick, bumpy rind and contains a high number of small, translucent, gelatinous seeds. The plant is a shrub or small tree with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible, tangy fruit used in culinary applications.
