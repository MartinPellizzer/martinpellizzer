Carduus ptarmicifolius

Carduus ptarmicifolius, commonly known as the tansy leaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant features a robust, spiny stem and broad, deeply lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
