Catasetum wailesii

Catasetum wailesii, commonly known as the Wailes' orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces specialized structures called "labellum" which aid in pollination by specific wasps. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Catasetum wailesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
