Cyclamen coum var. abchasicum

Cyclamen coum var. abchasicum, commonly known as the Abchazian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive dark green color and prominent veins. The plant produces delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the winter months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cyclamen coum var. abchasicum is well-suited for rock gardens and shady borders due to its compact growth habit.
