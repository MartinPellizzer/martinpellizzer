Cirsium sieboldii f. sieboldii

Cirsium sieboldii f. sieboldii, commonly known as Siebold's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disc florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
