Callistemon rugulosus var. flavovirens

Callistemon rugulosus var. flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features striking, bright red or crimson flowers that bloom in winter, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
