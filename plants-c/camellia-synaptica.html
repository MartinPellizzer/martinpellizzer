Camellia synaptica

Camellia synaptica, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers. Camellia synaptica thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
