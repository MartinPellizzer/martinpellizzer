Clematis coerulea var. grandiflora

Clematis coerulea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features climbing stems with palmate leaves that are often lobed and have a soft texture. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
