Cornus canadensis

Cornus canadensis, commonly known as red stem dogwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a slender, reddish stem that is often branched and covered with small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and appear in clusters, followed by red or orange berries.

Cornus canadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
