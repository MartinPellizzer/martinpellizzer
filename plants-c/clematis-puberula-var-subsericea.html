Clematis puberula var. subsericea

Clematis puberula var. subsericea, commonly known as the silky clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 15 feet in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a soft, downy texture that gives it its common name.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for trellises and garden structures.
