Crotalaria barnabassii

Crotalaria barnabassii, commonly known as the African cowpea or barnabas's crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are typically trifoliate. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Crotalaria barnabassii is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
