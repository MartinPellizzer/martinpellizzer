Coleus tetragonus

Coleus tetragonus, commonly known as the four-angled coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its square or four-angled stems, which are a distinctive botanical feature. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. Its leaves are often vibrant in color, ranging from green to shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Coleus tetragonus thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and indoor settings.
