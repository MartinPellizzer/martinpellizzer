Campomanesia widgreniana

Campomanesia widgreniana, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
