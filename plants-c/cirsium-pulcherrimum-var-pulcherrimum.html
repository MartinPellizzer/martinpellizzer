Cirsium pulcherrimum var. pulcherrimum

Cirsium pulcherrimum var. pulcherrimum, commonly known as the showy thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
