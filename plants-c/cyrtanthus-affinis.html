Cyrtanthus affinis

Cyrtanthus affinis, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, sturdy stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Cyrtanthus affinis is known for its early spring blooming habit, adding a vibrant touch to gardens in the cooler months.
