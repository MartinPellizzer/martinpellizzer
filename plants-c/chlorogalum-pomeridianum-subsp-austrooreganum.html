Chlorogalum pomeridianum subsp. austrooreganum

Chlorogalum pomeridianum subsp.

austrooreganum, commonly known as the Oregon white globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its striking white, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland valleys.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
