Comolia ovalifolia var. denudata

Comolia ovalifolia var. denudata, commonly known as the naked leaf comolia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, oval-shaped leaves that are often reduced or absent in some varieties, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can tolerate periods of drought.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
