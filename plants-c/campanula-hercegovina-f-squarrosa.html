Campanula hercegovina f. squarrosa

Campanula hercegovina f. squarrosa, commonly known as the Hercegovaian bellflower, is a compact, perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
