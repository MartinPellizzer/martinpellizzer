Crepidium fulvum

Crepidium fulvum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking yellow flowers with purple markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the spring season.

Crepidium fulvum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
