Cacalia linaria var. pumila

Cacalia linaria var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf linaria, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticulture.
