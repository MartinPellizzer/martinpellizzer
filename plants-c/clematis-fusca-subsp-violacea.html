Clematis fusca subsp. violacea

Clematis fusca subsp.

violacea, commonly known as the purple clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. It is a deciduous vine characterized by its woody stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
