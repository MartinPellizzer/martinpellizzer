Correa lawrenciana var. lawrenciana

Correa lawrenciana var. lawrenciana, commonly known as the coastal correa, is a species native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal environments.
