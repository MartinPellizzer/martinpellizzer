Cissus sarcospathula

Cissus sarcospathula, commonly known as the rock fig or rock cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Cissus sarcospathula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
