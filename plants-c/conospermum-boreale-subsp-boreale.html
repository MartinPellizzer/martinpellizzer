Conospermum boreale subsp. boreale

Conospermum boreale subsp.

boreale, commonly known as the Northern Banksia, is a shrub native to the cool temperate regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes, which are typically pink to red in color.

The plant produces woody follicles that contain seeds, a feature typical of the Proteaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and open forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular subject in horticultural and botanical studies.
