Crocus kofudagensis

Crocus kofudagensis, commonly known as the Kofu dafu crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms. The plant produces showy, often violet-colored flowers that emerge from narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
