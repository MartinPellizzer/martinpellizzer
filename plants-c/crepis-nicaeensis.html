Crepis nicaeensis

Crepis nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

The flowers are yellow with ray florets, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
