Calamagrostis canadensis var. arcta

Calamagrostis canadensis var. arcta, commonly known as black joint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It features tall, upright clumps with dark purple to black stems that contrast strikingly with its silver-green foliage. The plant produces feathery, silver-white plumes in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and marshes.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for water garden and native plant landscaping.
