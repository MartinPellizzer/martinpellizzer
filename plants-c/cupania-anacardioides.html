Cupania anacardioides

Cupania anacardioides, commonly known as the Brazilian apple or the sweet chestnut tree, is a species native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, sweet, red drupes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is often used in local cuisines.
