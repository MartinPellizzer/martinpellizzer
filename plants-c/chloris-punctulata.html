Chloris punctulata

Chloris punctulata, commonly known as the dotted grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often bear a dense covering of small, white to pale green hairs. The leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens and for erosion control.
