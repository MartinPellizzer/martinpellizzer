Centaurea crupina var. maculata

Centaurea crupina var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and open fields.

Its striking appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
