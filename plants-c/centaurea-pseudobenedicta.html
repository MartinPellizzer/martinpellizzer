Centaurea pseudobenedicta

Centaurea pseudobenedicta, commonly known as the false blessed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its striking blue-violet flowers are surrounded by pale yellow rays, making it visually distinctive.

It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
