Chelonopsis deflexa var. matsudae

Chelonopsis deflexa var. matsudae, commonly known as the Matsudae's chelonopsis, is a unique variant of the chelonopsis genus, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

This plant is native to specific regions in East Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate, making it a prized specimen among collectors.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
