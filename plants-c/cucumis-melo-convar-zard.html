Cucumis melo convar. zard

Cucumis melo convar. zard, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive netted rind.

The plant produces large, orange to yellow fruits with a sweet, aromatic flesh. It is typically cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins A and C. The plant prefers warm climates and well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.
