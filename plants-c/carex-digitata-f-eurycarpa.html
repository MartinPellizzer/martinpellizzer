Carex digitata f. eurycarpa

Carex digitata f. eurycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited bog sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, typical of sedges. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and bogs, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
