Calophyllum pulcherrimum

Calophyllum pulcherrimum, commonly known as the beauty leaf, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and often display vibrant red, orange, or yellow hues, especially when mature. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
