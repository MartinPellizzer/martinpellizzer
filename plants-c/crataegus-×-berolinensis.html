Crataegus × berolinensis

Crataegus × berolinensis, commonly known as the Berlin hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The Berlin hawthorn is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
