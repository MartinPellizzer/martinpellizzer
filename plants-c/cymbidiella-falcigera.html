Cymbidiella falcigera

Cymbidiella falcigera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous areas. Its root system is fibrous, and it often forms dense colonies in suitable habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
