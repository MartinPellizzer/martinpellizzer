Clypeola hispidula

Clypeola hispidula, commonly known as the hairy shield orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open woodlands and heathlands. The plant features a short, erect stem with a single, ovate to elliptical leaf that is covered in fine, hairy pubescence. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and borne in a loose spike, with a distinctive, shield-shaped labellum.

Clypeola hispidula is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique morphology and specific habitat preferences.
