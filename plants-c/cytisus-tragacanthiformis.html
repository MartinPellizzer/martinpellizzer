Cytisus tragacanthiformis

Cytisus tragacanthiformis, commonly known as the Persian broom, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its woody stems are often covered with small, scale-like leaves, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
