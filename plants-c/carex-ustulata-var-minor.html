Carex ustulata var. minor

Carex ustulata var. minor, commonly known as the small burnet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
