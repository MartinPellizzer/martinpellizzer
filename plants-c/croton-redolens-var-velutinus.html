Croton redolens var. velutinus

Croton redolens var. velutinus, commonly known as velvet croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are often adorned with striking red or orange veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
