Camilleugenia

Camilleugenia, commonly known as the pink trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Camilleugenia is typically found in moist, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
