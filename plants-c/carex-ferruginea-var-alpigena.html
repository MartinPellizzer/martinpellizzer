Carex ferruginea var. alpigena

Carex ferruginea var. alpigena, commonly known as the alpine rust sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, upright leaves. The plant produces small, clustered brownish flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar environmental conditions.
