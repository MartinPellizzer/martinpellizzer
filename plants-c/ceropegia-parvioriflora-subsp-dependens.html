Ceropegia parvioriflora subsp. dependens

Ceropegia parvioriflora subsp.

dependens, commonly known as the hanging ceropegia, is a climbing perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features tendrils that allow it to climb and attach to supports, making it well-suited for hanging baskets or trained structures.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
