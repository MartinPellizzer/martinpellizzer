Citharexylum fruticosum var. subvillosum

Citharexylum fruticosum var. subvillosum, commonly known as the silky citharexylum, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its foliage is widely used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
