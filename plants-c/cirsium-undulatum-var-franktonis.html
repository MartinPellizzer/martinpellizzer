Cirsium undulatum var. franktonis

Cirsium undulatum var. franktonis, commonly known as the Frankton thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple flower heads and deeply lobed, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
