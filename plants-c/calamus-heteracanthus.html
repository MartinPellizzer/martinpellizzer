Calamus heteracanthus

Calamus heteracanthus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant has slender, upright stems that often exhibit a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture.

Calamus heteracanthus produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.
