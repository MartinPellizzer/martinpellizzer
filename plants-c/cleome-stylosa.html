Cleome stylosa

Cleome stylosa, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy, spindly flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and stems that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in gardens.

Cleome stylosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
