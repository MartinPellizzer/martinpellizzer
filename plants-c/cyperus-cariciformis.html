Cyperus cariciformis

Cyperus cariciformis, commonly known as the spiked sedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are often reddish or purplish in color.

Cyperus cariciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
