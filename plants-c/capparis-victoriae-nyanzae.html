Capparis victoriae-nyanzae

Capparis victoriae-nyanzae, commonly known as the East African snowball, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has woody stems and pinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in montane forests.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in its native habitat.
