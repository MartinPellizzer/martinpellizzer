Centaurea × szenicensis

Centaurea × szenicensis, commonly known as the Hungarian centaury, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Carpathian Basin and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, and its flowering period occurs in the summer months.

Centaurea × szenicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
