Crepis pratensis

Crepis pratensis, commonly known as meadow hoary cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is typically found in moist meadows and grasslands across Europe and parts of Asia.

Crepis pratensis is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
