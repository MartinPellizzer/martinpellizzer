Chamaerhodos baicalensis

Chamaerhodos baicalensis, commonly known as the Baikal bugloss, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
