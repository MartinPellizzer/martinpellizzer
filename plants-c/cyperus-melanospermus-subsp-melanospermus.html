Cyperus melanospermus subsp. melanospermus

Cyperus melanospermus subsp.

melanospermus, commonly known as blackseed sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, triangular stems.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or black in color. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded environments, often found in marshes and along waterways.

Its seeds are dark, hard, and serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.
