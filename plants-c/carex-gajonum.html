Carex gajonum

Carex gajonum, commonly known as the Gajon sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grassy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Carex gajonum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
