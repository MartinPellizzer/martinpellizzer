Carex xanthophysa

Carex xanthophysa, commonly known as the yellow-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bright yellow-green in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale to yellowish flowers in the summer.

Carex xanthophysa is often found in wetlands and is an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
