Crotalaria crassipes

Crotalaria crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmately compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Crotalaria crassipes is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
