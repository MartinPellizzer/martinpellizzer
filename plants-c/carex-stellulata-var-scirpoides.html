Carex stellulata var. scirpoides

Carex stellulata var. scirpoides, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones or along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
