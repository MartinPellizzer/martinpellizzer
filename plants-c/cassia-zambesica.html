Cassia zambesica

Cassia zambesica, commonly known as flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a woody, branching stem that can grow quite tall. Its flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
