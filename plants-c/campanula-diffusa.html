Campanula diffusa

Campanula diffusa, commonly known as the spreading bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, adorned with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its flowering period typically occurs from July to September, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade.

Campanula diffusa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
