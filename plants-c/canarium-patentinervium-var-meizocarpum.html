Canarium patentinervium var. meizocarpum

Canarium patentinervium var. meizocarpum, commonly known as the golden fruit tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy dark green leaves with prominent parallel veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries. The fruit is notable for its high oil content and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
