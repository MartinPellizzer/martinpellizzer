Coluppa

Coluppa, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Coluppa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
