Chionanthus minutiflorus

Chionanthus minutiflorus, commonly known as the smallflower snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

The fruit is a small, capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.
