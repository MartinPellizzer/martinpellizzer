Cymbidium sanderae

Cymbidium sanderae, commonly known as the Sanderae orchid, is a rare and elegant species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and tall, branched flower spikes. The plant produces fragrant, long-lasting flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive yellow or green throat. It thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by collectors and horticulturists.
