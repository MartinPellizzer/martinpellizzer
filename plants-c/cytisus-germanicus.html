Cytisus germanicus

Cytisus germanicus, commonly known as German broom, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus germanicus is often found in open habitats like woodlands, heaths, and coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
