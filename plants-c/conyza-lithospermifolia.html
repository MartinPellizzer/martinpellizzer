Conyza lithospermifolia

Conyza lithospermifolia, commonly known as desert composite, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Conyza lithospermifolia is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can thrive in disturbed habitats.
