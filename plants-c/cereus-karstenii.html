Cereus karstenii

Cereus karstenii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and its ability to produce large, showy white flowers at night. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert conditions. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
