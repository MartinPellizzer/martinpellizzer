Canthium melanophengos

Canthium melanophengos, commonly known as the black-fruited spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often hairy stems and alternate, simple leaves. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically dark-colored.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
