Cyphophoenix elegans

Cyphophoenix elegans, commonly known as the elegant date palm, is a species native to the southern regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates, though it is less productive than other date palm species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
