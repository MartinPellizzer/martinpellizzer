Crataegus blanda

Crataegus blanda, commonly known as the smooth crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet and features small, oval leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, red or yellow fruits in the summer. Its botanical characteristics include smooth, often grayish bark and a preference for well-drained soils in full sun.

Crataegus blanda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators and providing food for wildlife.
