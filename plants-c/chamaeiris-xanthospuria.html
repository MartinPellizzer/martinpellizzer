Chamaeiris xanthospuria

Chamaeiris xanthospuria, commonly known as the yellow-spored rockrose, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and typically have a rough texture.

The species is notable for its unique yellowish spores produced by its cones, which contribute to its common name.
