Cotoneaster melanocarpus var. laxiflorus

Cotoneaster melanocarpus var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the laxiflorous cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. It blooms in late spring with clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are borne in terminal cymes.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
