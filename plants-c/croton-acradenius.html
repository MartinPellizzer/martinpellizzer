Croton acradenius

Croton acradenius, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a range of hues including red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Croton acradenius typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires well-drained soil and bright, indirect light to thrive.
