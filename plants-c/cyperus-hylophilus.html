Cyperus hylophilus

Cyperus hylophilus, commonly known as the broadleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Cyperus hylophilus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its growth habit makes it a common ornamental and a potential invasive species in some areas.
