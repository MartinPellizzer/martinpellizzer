Combesia muscosa var. anguina

Combesia muscosa var. anguina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved combesia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread along the ground, forming dense mats. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and the plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its botanical structure supports its role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
