Capsicum indicum elaeocarpon

Capsicum indicum elaeocarpon, commonly known as the Indian chili or wild chili, is a species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, elongated chili pepper that can vary in color from green to red, depending on maturity.
