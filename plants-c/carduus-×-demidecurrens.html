Carduus × demidecurrens

Carduus × demidecurrens, commonly known as the intermediate thistle, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Carduus tenuifolius and Carduus nutans.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.

Its botanical features include a robust root system and a flowering period that usually occurs in late summer to early autumn.
