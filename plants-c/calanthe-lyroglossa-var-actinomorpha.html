Calanthe lyroglossa var. actinomorpha

Calanthe lyroglossa var. actinomorpha, commonly known as the actinomorphic calanthe, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it thrives in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and possesses lanceolate, dark green leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in a raceme and feature a distinctive, actinomorphic (radially symmetrical) structure with showy, often brightly colored petals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
