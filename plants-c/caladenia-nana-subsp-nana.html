Caladenia nana subsp. nana

Caladenia nana subsp.

nana, commonly known as the dwarf spider orchid, is a small terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia. It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters and features a single, slender flowering stem.

The flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive, narrow labellum that resembles a spider's body. This subspecies is known for its compact size and delicate appearance, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is typically found in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands.
