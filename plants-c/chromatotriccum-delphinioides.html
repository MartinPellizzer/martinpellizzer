Chromatotriccum delphinioides

Chromatotriccum delphinioides, commonly known as the water clover, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, branching stems that float on the water's surface, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant is characterized by its ability to absorb nutrients from the water, making it a common sight in slow-moving freshwater environments. Its leaves are typically green, but they can change color in response to light and nutrient availability.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and helping to maintain water clarity.
