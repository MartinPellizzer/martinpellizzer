Cynoglossum officinale var. areolatum

Cynoglossum officinale var. areolatum, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its flowers are typically bell-shaped with a distinctive white or yellowish throat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
