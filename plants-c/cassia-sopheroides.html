Cassia sopheroides

Cassia sopheroides, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets arranged oppositely.

Cassia sopheroides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
