Carex moupinensis

Carex moupinensis, commonly known as the Moupin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks.

Its botanical structure is adapted to thrive in humid, temperate climates with well-drained soil.
