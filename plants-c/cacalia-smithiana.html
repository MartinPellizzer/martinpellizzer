Cacalia smithiana

Cacalia smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.
