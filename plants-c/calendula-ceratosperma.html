Calendula ceratosperma

Calendula ceratosperma, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, saucer-shaped yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive horn-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release the seeds.

Calendula ceratosperma is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
