Chysis aurea var. limminghei

Chysis aurea var. limminghei, commonly known as the golden chysis, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and are highly attractive to pollinators. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a humid, shaded environment to flourish.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species in botanical conservation efforts.
