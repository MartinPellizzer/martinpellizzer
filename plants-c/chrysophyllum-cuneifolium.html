Chrysophyllum cuneifolium

Chrysophyllum cuneifolium, commonly known as the African cherry or mabira, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, orange-red berries. The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
