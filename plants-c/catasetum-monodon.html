Catasetum monodon

Catasetum monodon, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous flower spikes. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers with a unique structure that includes a specialized lip for pollination. Catasetum monodon thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and botanical collections.
