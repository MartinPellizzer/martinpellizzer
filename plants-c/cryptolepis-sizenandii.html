Cryptolepis sizenandii

Cryptolepis sizenandii, commonly known as African snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red or orange berries containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The plant has a long history of traditional medicinal use, particularly for its purported antimalarial properties.
