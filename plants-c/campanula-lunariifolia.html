Campanula lunariifolia

Campanula lunariifolia, commonly known as the moonflower campanula, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Native to Central Asia, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flowering behavior.
