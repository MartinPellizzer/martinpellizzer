Cynara lanceata

Cynara lanceata, commonly known as the lanceleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its robust, drought-tolerant nature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
