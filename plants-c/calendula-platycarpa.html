Calendula platycarpa

Calendula platycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit calendula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and narrow, elongated fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are often used in herbal remedies.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
