Colubrina faraloatra var. sinuata

Colubrina faraloatra var. sinuata, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly wavy margin, and produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant is often found in wetlands, hammocks, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soils. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
