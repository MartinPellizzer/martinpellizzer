Chlorophytum delagoense

Chlorophytum delagoense, commonly known as the African tiger grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green with distinctive white or yellow stripes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, often appearing in late summer. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage, which adds visual interest to gardens.
