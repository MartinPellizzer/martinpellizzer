Canarium radlkoferi

Canarium radlkoferi, commonly known as the Lombok nut or Lombok tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the Indonesian island of Lombok.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, elongated fruits that resemble tamarinds.

The seeds within the fruits are encased in a hard, woody shell and are used in local traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
