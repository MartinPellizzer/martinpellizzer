Cyperus barrancae

Cyperus barrancae, commonly known as the barranca flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of freshwater bodies. Cyperus barrancae has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
