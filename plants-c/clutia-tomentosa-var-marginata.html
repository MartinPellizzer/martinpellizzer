Clutia tomentosa var. marginata

Clutia tomentosa var. marginata, commonly known as the white-veined clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a distinctive white or pale green margin. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
