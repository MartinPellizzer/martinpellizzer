Caucalis tenerrima

Caucalis tenerrima, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pale blue or white, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
