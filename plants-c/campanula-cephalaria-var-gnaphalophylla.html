Campanula cephalaria var. gnaphalophylla

Campanula cephalaria var. gnaphalophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
