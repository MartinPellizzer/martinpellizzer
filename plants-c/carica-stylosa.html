Carica stylosa

Carica stylosa, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Caricaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. The plant produces a distinctive, spiky fruit with a fragrant, creamy flesh that is rich in nutrients. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruit and has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
