Cachrys asperula subsp. haussknechtii

Cachrys asperula subsp.

haussknechtii, commonly known as the Hoosier mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a fibrous root system. It is part of the mint family (Lamiaceae), which is known for its aromatic properties and often hairy foliage.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly rough texture and specific geographic distribution within its range.
