Calophyllum retusum

Calophyllum retusum, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, orange-colored fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Calophyllum retusum is valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction due to its durability and attractive grain.
