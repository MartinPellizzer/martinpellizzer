Crocus vernus var. acutiflorus

Crocus vernus var. acutiflorus, commonly known as the sharp-flowered crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland edges and meadows. It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The bulbs are relatively hardy and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial shade.
