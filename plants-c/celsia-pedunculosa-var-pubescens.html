Celsia pedunculosa var. pubescens

Celsia pedunculosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy celsia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pubescent, hairy leaves that give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant morphology and adaptation.
