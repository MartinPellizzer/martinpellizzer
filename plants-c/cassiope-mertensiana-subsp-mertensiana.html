Cassiope mertensiana subsp. mertensiana

Cassiope mertensiana subsp.

mertensiana, commonly known as the alpine candytuft, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the alpine regions of North America. It features dense clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh mountain conditions.
