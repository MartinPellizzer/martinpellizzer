Crantzia corymbosa

Crantzia corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes many showy, herbaceous plants. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense, rounded clusters at the ends of the stems.

Crantzia corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and compact growth habit.
