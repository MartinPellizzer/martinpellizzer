Combretum albidiflorum

Combretum albidiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and compound leaves that are opposite and pinnate. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
