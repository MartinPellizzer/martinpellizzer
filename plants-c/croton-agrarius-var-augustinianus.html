Croton agrarius var. augustinianus

Croton agrarius var. augustinianus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features vibrant, often colorful leaves that can range from green to red, yellow, or orange, depending on the season and environmental conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes grown as a garden plant, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
