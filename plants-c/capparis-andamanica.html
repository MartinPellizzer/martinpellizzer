Capparis andamanica

Capparis andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman crabapple, is a flowering plant native to the Andaman Islands.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant's leaves are simple, ovate, and have a glossy green surface. After flowering, it develops small, red, apple-like fruits that are edible but tart.

Capparis andamanica is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
