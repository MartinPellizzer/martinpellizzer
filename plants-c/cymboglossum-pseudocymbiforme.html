Cymboglossum pseudocymbiforme

Cymboglossum pseudocymbiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
