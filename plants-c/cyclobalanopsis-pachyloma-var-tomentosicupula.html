Cyclobalanopsis pachyloma var. tomentosicupula

Cyclobalanopsis pachyloma var. tomentosicupula, commonly known as the woolly-caledron oak, is a variant of the cyclobalanopsis genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its dense, woolly undergrowth of young shoots and leaves. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a leathery texture and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, cup-shaped fruits that contain one or two seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in montane forests.
