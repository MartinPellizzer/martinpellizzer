Cnicus saanensis

Cnicus saanensis, commonly known as the San Francisco dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically hairy as well. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience in arid environments.
