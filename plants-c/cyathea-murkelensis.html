Cyathea murkelensis

Cyathea murkelensis, commonly known as the Murkel's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

Cyathea murkelensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
