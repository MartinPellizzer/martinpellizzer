Crataegus laurentiana var. dissimilifolia

Crataegus laurentiana var. dissimilifolia, commonly known as the Laurentian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream valleys.
