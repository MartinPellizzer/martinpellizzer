Coccoloba lasseri

Coccoloba lasseri, commonly known as the rock nettle, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and limestone soils. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in inflorescences. It produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

Coccoloba lasseri is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
