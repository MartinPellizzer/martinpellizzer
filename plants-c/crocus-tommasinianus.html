Crocus tommasinianus

Crocus tommasinianus, commonly known as the Tommasinian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its early spring blooming period. The plant features showy, violet-colored flowers with six petals and a distinctive, narrow, grass-like leaf structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Crocus tommasinianus is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
