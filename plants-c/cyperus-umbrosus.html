Cyperus umbrosus

Cyperus umbrosus, commonly known as the dark sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in three rows, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus umbrosus is often used in ornamental gardening for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its attractive foliage.
