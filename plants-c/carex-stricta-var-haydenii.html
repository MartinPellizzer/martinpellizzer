Carex stricta var. haydenii

Carex stricta var. haydenii, commonly known as the Hayden's tussock sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, spiky inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges. Its leaves are usually narrow and sword-shaped, with a distinct midrib.

The flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, contributing to its ecological role in wetland habitats.
