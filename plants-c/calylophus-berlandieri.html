Calylophus berlandieri

Calylophus berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to blue, often appearing in the early morning. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
