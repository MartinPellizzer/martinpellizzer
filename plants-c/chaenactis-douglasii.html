Chaenactis douglasii

Chaenactis douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's brittlestem, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in arid or rocky environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry conditions. Its stems are often brittle and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer.
