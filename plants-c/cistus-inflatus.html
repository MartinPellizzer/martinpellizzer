Cistus inflatus

Cistus inflatus, commonly known as the inflated rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to rocky and arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
