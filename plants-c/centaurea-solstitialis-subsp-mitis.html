Centaurea solstitialis subsp. mitis

Centaurea solstitialis subsp.

mitis, commonly known as the slender sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.

Due to its adaptability, it can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in arid landscapes.
