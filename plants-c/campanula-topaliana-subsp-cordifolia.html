Campanula topaliana subsp. cordifolia

Campanula topaliana subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in alpine settings.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
