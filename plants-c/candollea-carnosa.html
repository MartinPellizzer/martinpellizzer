Candollea carnosa

Candollea carnosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. Its fronds are usually light green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Candollea carnosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
