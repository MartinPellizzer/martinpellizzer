Carex sikokiana

Carex sikokiana, commonly known as the Sikokian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex sikokiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland conditions.
