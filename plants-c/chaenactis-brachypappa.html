Chaenactis brachypappa

Chaenactis brachypappa, commonly known as the short-stemmed fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow to white in color.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
