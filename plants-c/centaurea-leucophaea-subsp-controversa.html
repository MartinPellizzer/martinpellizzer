Centaurea leucophaea subsp. controversa

Centaurea leucophaea subsp.

controversa, commonly known as the white-centred knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers with a white center.

The species belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy as well.

This subspecies is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
