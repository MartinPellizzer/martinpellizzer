Carum gracile

Carum gracile, commonly known as slender caraway, is a small herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in umbel-shaped clusters. Its seeds are often used in culinary applications, similar to other caraway species.

Carum gracile is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
