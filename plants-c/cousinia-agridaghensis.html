Cousinia agridaghensis

Cousinia agridaghensis, commonly known as the Afghan cottonweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, while its flowers are small and white or pale purple.

Cousinia agridaghensis is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
