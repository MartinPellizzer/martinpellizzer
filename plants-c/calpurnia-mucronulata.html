Calpurnia mucronulata

Calpurnia mucronulata, commonly known as the small-lobed calpurnia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Calpurnia mucronulata is often found in open woodland and savanna habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
