Carapa borneensis

Carapa borneensis, commonly known as Borneo copal or yellow copal, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and simple, opposite leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is valued for its resin, which is used in traditional medicine and as a natural adhesive.
