Cortia papyracea

Cortia papyracea, commonly known as the paperleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble paper. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cortia papyracea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
