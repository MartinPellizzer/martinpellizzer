Cupressus reinwardtii

Cupressus reinwardtii, commonly known as the Himalayan cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar form and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers followed by cone-like structures that contain seeds. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Cupressus reinwardtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
