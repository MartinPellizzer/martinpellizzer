Carex liparocarpos

Carex liparocarpos, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of Europe, Asia, and North America. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its grass-like leaves are usually dark green and have a narrow, cylindrical shape.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
