Carex purshii

Carex purshii, commonly known as Pursh's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually brownish or reddish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex purshii plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
