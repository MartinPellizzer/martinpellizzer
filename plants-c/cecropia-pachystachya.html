Cecropia pachystachya

Cecropia pachystachya, commonly known as the giant ceiba, is a fast-growing tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and towering height, often reaching up to 40 meters. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It is highly adaptable to various soil types and thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Its rapid growth makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
