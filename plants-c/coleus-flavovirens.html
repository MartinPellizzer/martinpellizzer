Coleus flavovirens

Coleus flavovirens, commonly known as the golden-green coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that features a mix of green, yellow, and white colors. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant and unique leaf patterns.
