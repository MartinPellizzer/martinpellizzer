Christisonia bicolor

Christisonia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The species is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that appear in early spring. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions.

Christisonia bicolor is known for its distinctive bi-colored leaves, which are usually green on the upper surface and white or pale on the underside.
