Carex chaetorhiza

Carex chaetorhiza, commonly known as the hairy root sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically flowering in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex chaetorhiza is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland ecosystems.
