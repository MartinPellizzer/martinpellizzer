Campanula hellenica

Campanula hellenica, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula hellenica is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
