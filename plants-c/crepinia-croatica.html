Crepinia croatica

Crepinia croatica, commonly known as the Croatian crepe myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in summer, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a compact, shrub-like growth habit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lythraceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
