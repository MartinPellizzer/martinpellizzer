Cakile aegyptia var. hispanica

Cakile aegyptia var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, perennial herb that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, round fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

This species is also known for its ability to colonize disturbed areas, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
