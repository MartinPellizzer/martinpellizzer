Convolvulus laurifolius

Convolvulus laurifolius, commonly known as the laurel-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial vine that produces clusters of small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a waxy, slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
