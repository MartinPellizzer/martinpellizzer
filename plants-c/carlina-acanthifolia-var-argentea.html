Carlina acanthifolia var. argentea

Carlina acanthifolia var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly foliage and striking purple to pink flower heads. The plant produces composite flowers, with both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
