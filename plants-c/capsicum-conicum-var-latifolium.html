Capsicum conicum var. latifolium

Capsicum conicum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf pepper.

This variant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and elongated, slender fruit that can range in color from green to red when mature. It is native to South America, particularly the Andean regions, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

Its botanical features make it suitable for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
