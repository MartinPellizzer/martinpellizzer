Carex serotina subsp. pulchella

Carex serotina subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the showy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like flower clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are generally narrow and dark green, while the inflorescence consists of two distinct spikelets.

This subspecies is often noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
