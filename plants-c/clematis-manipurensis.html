Clematis manipurensis

Clematis manipurensis, commonly known as the Manipur clematis, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, usually white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in cymes. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires careful attention to its specific growing conditions.
