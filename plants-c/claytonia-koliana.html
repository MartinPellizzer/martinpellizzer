Claytonia koliana

Claytonia koliana, commonly known as the Sierra snowplant, is a flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky areas. The plant features rosettes of oval, fleshy leaves that are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. Its flowers are small, pinkish-white, and clustered in a terminal inflorescence, often blooming in late summer.

Claytonia koliana is adapted to cold, moist conditions and plays a role in the ecological diversity of alpine ecosystems.
