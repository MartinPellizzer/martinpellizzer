Carduus defloratus subsp. argemone

Carduus defloratus subsp.

argemone, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while the plant is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
