Calcitrapa tinctoria

Calcitrapa tinctoria, commonly known as the red root or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its roots are the primary part used, containing compounds that have been historically used for dyeing fabrics.

Calcitrapa tinctoria is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
