Claoxylon erythrophyllum var. brassii

Claoxylon erythrophyllum var. brassii, commonly known as the red-fruited celtuce, is a variant of the celtuce plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, waxy leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by bright red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to those of the common celtuce. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content, making it a popular choice in local cuisines.
