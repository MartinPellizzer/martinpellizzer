Crataegus invisa

Crataegus invisa, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet, featuring thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it has a distinctive, compact growth habit.

Crataegus invisa is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
