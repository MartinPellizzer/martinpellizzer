Capparis sicula subsp. herbacea

Capparis sicula subsp.

herbacea, commonly known as the Sicilian caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features woody stems covered in small, thorny branches and produces yellow, tubular flowers.

The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds.

This subspecies is closely related to other caper species and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
