Celastrus barbarus

Celastrus barbarus, commonly known as American bittersweet, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and yellow-orange flowers that appear in late summer. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its stems are woody and can become tangled, making it a persistent weed in forested areas.

Celastrus barbarus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
