Conomorpha laurifolia

Conomorpha laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel leaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that resemble those of laurel plants, hence its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.

Conomorpha laurifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
