Carex pamirensis subsp. angustispicata

Carex pamirensis subsp.

angustispicata, commonly known as the narrow-spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine habitats. It produces small, clustered flowers in late summer, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies is notable for its narrow, spiky inflorescence and its adaptation to high-altitude environments.
