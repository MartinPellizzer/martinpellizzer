Cirsium carduophilum

Cirsium carduophilum, commonly known as the heartleaf thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped, which gives it its common name, and it produces small, cylindrical flower heads.

Cirsium carduophilum is often found in disturbed areas and can be a common weed in agricultural fields.
