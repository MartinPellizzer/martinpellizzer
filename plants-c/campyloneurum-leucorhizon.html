Campyloneurum leucorhizon

Campyloneurum leucorhizon, commonly known as the white-stemmed fern, is a species of moss belonging to the family Pterobryaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and its pale green to white-colored stems. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. This moss thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or tree trunks in temperate regions.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for microorganisms in its ecosystem.
