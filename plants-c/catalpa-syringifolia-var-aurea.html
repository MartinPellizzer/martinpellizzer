Catalpa syringifolia var. aurea

Catalpa syringifolia var. aurea, commonly known as the golden catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of white to yellow. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinctive, velvety texture. This variety is prized for its ornamental value, particularly for its vibrant foliage and floral display.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for landscape planting.
