Chryseis suaveolens

Chryseis suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces tiny, disk-shaped flowers that are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Chryseis suaveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance.
