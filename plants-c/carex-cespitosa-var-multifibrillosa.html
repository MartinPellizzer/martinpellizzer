Carex cespitosa var. multifibrillosa

Carex cespitosa var. multifibrillosa, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces cylindrical spikes of small, spikelet-bearing flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a fibrous texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
