Colocasia esculenta var. antiquorum

Colocasia esculenta var. antiquorum, commonly known as the African arrowleaf, is a tropical perennial plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water bodies. Its leaves are edible when cooked, though they contain calcium oxalate crystals that can cause irritation.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
