Cyperus castaneus

Cyperus castaneus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, clustered flowers, typically in shades of purple or brown.

Cyperus castaneus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
