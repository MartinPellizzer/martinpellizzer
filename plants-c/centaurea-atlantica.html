Centaurea atlantica

Centaurea atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Centaurea atlantica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and hardy nature.
