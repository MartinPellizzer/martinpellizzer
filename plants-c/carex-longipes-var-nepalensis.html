Carex longipes var. nepalensis

Carex longipes var. nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepalese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and adjacent areas of northern India.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forest understories or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
