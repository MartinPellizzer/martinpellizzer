Cardamine hirsuta proles sylvatica

Cardamine hirsuta proles sylvatica, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and delicate, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinct appearance.

It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its edible leaves, though it can be toxic in higher concentrations.
