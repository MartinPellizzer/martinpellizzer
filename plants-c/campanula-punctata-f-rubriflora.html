Campanula punctata f. rubriflora

Campanula punctata f. rubriflora, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers with a red or pink throat, which gives it its specific epithet "rubriflora.". The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often spotted with white or pale green markings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a specimen plant for its unique floral coloration.
