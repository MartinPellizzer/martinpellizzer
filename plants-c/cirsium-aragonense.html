Cirsium aragonense

Cirsium aragonense, commonly known as the Aragon thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium aragonense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
