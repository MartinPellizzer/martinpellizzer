Clinopodium microphyllum

Clinopodium microphyllum, commonly known as narrow-leaved mint or mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, opposite leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or purple flowers.

Clinopodium microphyllum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
