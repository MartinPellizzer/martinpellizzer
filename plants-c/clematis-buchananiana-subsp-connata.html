Clematis buchananiana subsp. connata

Clematis buchananiana subsp.

connata, commonly known as the Ecuadorian clematis, is a flowering plant native to the Andes of Ecuador. It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to horticulturists and conservationists alike.
