Cleome obtusa var. gamboensis

Cleome obtusa var. gamboensis, commonly known as the Gambo's cleome, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

Cleome obtusa var. gamboensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
