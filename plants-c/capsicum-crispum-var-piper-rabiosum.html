Capsicum crispum var. piper-rabiosum

Capsicum crispum var. piper-rabiosum, commonly known as the Trinidad moruga scorpion pepper.

This variety is a type of chili pepper native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago. It is characterized by its long, thin, scorpion-like shape and vibrant red or orange color when mature. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lobed leaves.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its intense heat and unique flavor profile.
