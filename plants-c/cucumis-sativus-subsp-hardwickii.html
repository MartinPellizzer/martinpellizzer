Cucumis sativus subsp. hardwickii

Cucumis sativus subsp.

hardwickii, commonly known as the hardwick cucumber, is a subspecies of the common cucumber native to South Asia. It is characterized by its long, slender fruits that are typically harvested when still immature and tender.

The plant produces yellow flowers and has a vine-like growth habit, similar to other cucurbit species. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and the fruits have a mild, crisp texture with a slightly bitter taste.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its edible young fruits, which are used in various culinary applications.
