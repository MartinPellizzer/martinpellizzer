Cyamus nelumbo

Cyamus nelumbo, commonly known as the lotus, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom above the water surface. The plant has a distinctive underground stem called a rhizome, which helps it anchor in muddy substrates. Its leaves are broad and floating, providing shade and oxygen to the surrounding water.

Cyamus nelumbo is also known for its symbolic significance in various cultures and its use in traditional medicine and cuisine.
