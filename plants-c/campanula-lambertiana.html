Campanula lambertiana

Campanula lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
