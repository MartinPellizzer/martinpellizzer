Carex prescottiana var. fargesii

Carex prescottiana var. fargesii, commonly known as the Farges sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
