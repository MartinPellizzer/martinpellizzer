Capparis subacuta

Capparis subacuta, commonly known as the narrowleaf caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are often clustered in branched inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and have a slightly succulent texture, which helps it withstand arid conditions.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
