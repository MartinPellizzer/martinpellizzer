Cynanchum canoi

Cynanchum canoi, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruits are follicles that contain toxic milky sap, which can cause skin irritation.

Cynanchum canoi is often found in open woods and disturbed sites, and it has been used in traditional medicine.
