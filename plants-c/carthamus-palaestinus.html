Carthamus palaestinus

Carthamus palaestinus, commonly known as the Palestine safflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The seeds of Carthamus palaestinus are small and can be harvested for various traditional and agricultural uses.
