Cyclamen intermedium

Cyclamen intermedium, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a deep green color and striking, bowl-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant produces a single flower per stem, which blooms in early spring and lasts for several weeks. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Cyclamen intermedium is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its decorative foliage and delicate blossoms.
