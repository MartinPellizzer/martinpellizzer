Carex luzulina var. ablata

Carex luzulina var. ablata, commonly known as the bluish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
