Cassia javanica subsp. agnes

Cassia javanica subsp.

agnes, commonly known as golden shower tree or *Saraca asoca*, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear numerous yellow-orange flowers, which are highly fragrant.

The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that give it a delicate, feathery appearance.
