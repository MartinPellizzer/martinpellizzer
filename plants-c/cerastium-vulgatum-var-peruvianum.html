Cerastium vulgatum var. peruvianum

Cerastium vulgatum var. peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in dense, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a fine layer of woolly hair.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
