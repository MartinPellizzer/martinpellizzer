Cornus canadensis f. rosea

Cornus canadensis f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered Canadian dogwood, is a variant of the Canadian dogwood native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with distinctive, showy pink to rose-colored bracts that surround its small, yellowish flowers. The plant features a compact, bushy habit with dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a preference for moist, shaded environments in forest understories.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its unique floral display.
