Cistus alyssoides

Cistus alyssoides, commonly known as the lesser rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are generally gray-green with a fuzzy texture. It is part of the Ericaceae family and is known for its aromatic properties.

Cistus alyssoides is also valued for its resilience to drought and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
