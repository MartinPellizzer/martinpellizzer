Chisocheton hackenbergii

Chisocheton hackenbergii, commonly known as the African tamarind tree, is a member of the Clusiaceae family.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savannas and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in various parts of Africa.
