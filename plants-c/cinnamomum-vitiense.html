Cinnamomum vitiense

Cinnamomum vitiense, commonly known as the Vanuatu cinnamon or Vitiense cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the South Pacific islands, particularly Vanuatu.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark reddish-brown bark that is harvested for its aromatic properties, similar to traditional cinnamon. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically found in lowland rainforests.
