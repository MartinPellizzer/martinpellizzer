Cucumis operculatus

Cucumis operculatus, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, green fruit that resembles a horn, hence its common name. The plant is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and the fruit develops from a single ovary.

The plant is often cultivated for its unique appearance and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
