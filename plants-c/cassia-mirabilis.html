Cassia mirabilis

Cassia mirabilis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, seed-rich pods.

Cassia mirabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
