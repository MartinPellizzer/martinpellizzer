Carex subramosa

Carex subramosa, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to submerged soil conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex subramosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
