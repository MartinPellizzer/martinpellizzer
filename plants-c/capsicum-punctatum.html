Capsicum punctatum

Capsicum punctatum, commonly known as the bird pepper or yellow dwarf pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture.

Capsicum punctatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, making it popular in both gardens and culinary applications.
