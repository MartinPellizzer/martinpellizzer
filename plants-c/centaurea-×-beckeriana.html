Centaurea × beckeriana

Centaurea × beckeriana, commonly known as the Becker's knapweed, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with slender stems and dense clusters of purple to pinkish-red flowers. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 feet and produces small, bract-like leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in prairies and open fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
