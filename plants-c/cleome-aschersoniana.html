Cleome aschersoniana

Cleome aschersoniana, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Cleome aschersoniana is a hardy annual that is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
