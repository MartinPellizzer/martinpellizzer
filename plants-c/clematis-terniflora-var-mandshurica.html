Clematis terniflora var. mandshurica

Clematis terniflora var. mandshurica, commonly known as the Mandshurian Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated margin. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white or pale blue. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
