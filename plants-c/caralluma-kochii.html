Caralluma kochii

Caralluma kochii, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of India and Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of yellow or orange flowers that bloom at night. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent structure and deep root system.

Caralluma kochii is also known for its traditional use in Ayurvedic medicine for its purported health benefits.
