Calamagrostis purpurascens subsp. sesquiflora

Calamagrostis purpurascens subsp.

sesquiflora, commonly known as the purple bluestem grass, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple tints during the growing season.

The plant produces spikelets that are often arranged in panicles, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in prairie and wetland habitats.
