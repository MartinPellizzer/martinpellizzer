Cyperus dipsaceus

Cyperus dipsaceus, commonly known as purple rush, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often found in wet or marshy habitats.

Cyperus dipsaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a water garden plant.
