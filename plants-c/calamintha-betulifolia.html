Calamintha betulifolia

Calamintha betulifolia, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces a delicate, aromatic scent, and its seeds are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Calamintha betulifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
