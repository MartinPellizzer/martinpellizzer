Clematis alpina subvar. tenuiloba

Clematis alpina subvar. tenuiloba, commonly known as the Alpine Clematis, is a perennial vine native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems that climb by means of hooked tendrils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three lance-shaped leaflets that are often hairy.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.
