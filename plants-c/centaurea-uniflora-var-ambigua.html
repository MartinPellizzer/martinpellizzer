Centaurea uniflora var. ambigua

Centaurea uniflora var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous singleflower centaury, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
