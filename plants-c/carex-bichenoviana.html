Carex bichenoviana

Carex bichenoviana, commonly known as the Bichenovian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flower heads during the summer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of wetland habitats.
