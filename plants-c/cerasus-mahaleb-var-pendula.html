Cerasus mahaleb var. pendula

Cerasus mahaleb var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or pendulous cherry, is a variety of the Prunus mahaleb species.

It is characterized by its graceful, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by red or black berries in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is often used in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal beauty.
