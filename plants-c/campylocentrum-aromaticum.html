Campylocentrum aromaticum

Campylocentrum aromaticum, commonly known as the honeycomb ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, and aromatic leaves that emit a spicy scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
