Comesperma rhyoliticum

Comesperma rhyoliticum, commonly known as the rhyolite comesperma, is a unique plant species native to specific regions with volcanic soil.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Its growth habit is low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, Comesperma rhyoliticum is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
