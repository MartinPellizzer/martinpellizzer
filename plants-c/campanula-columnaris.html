Campanula columnaris

Campanula columnaris, commonly known as the columnar bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
