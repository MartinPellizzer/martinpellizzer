Cicer adonis

Cicer adonis, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and arid environments. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cicer adonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
