Columnea latisepala

Columnea latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled flame flower, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Columnea latisepala is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its striking appearance and ease of care.
