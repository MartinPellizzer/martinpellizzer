Cucumis melo subsp. conomon

Cucumis melo subsp.

conomon, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its thick, ribbed rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is a vine that produces large, round fruits with a distinctive netted appearance on the outer surface.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This subspecies is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins A and C.
