Cucumis melo var. siamensis

Cucumis melo var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam melon, is a variant of the cantaloupe belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, and thrives in warm, tropical climates. It produces yellow to orange flowers and is typically cultivated for its edible fruit.

The Siam melon is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
