Centaurea hausmannii

Centaurea hausmannii, commonly known as the Hausmann's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Centaurea hausmannii is often found in rocky or disturbed areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
