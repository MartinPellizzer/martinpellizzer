Crataegus × oxystevenii

Crataegus × oxystevenii, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the genus Crataegus, which includes various species of hawthorns known for their ornamental value and ecological importance. The plant typically features thorny stems, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, white or pink flowers in spring. It produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

This hybrid is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
