Crataegus oxyacantha subsp. laevigata

Crataegus oxyacantha subsp.

laevigata, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits known as haws, which are an important food source for birds and mammals.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along stream banks.
