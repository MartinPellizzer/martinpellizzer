Carex rosea f. debilis

Carex rosea f. debilis, commonly known as the slender rose marsh sedge.

It is a low-growing perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with pink or reddish hues. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
