Cissus coccinea

Cissus coccinea, commonly known as the red-leafed devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with pink or purple. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, red berries that attract birds and other wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Cissus coccinea is also valued for its ability to climb and its low maintenance requirements.
