Ceropegia inflata

Ceropegia inflata, commonly known as the inflated ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, inflated, tubular flowers that resemble small balloons. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to greenish-white blooms, which are often adorned with fine, hair-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia inflata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
