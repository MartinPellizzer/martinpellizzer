Cytisus infestus subsp. intermedius

Cytisus infestus subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and scrublands.

The plant features slender, often spiny stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for erosion control.
