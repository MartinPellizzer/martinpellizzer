Coriandrum globosum

Coriandrum globosum, commonly known as globe coriander, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and small, pale green leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces spherical, white to pinkish flower heads that contain numerous tiny florets. Its seeds, which are used in culinary applications, are small and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill.
