Cladostachys muricata

Cladostachys muricata, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems covered in short, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental and erosion control purposes.
