Crepis hackelii

Crepis hackelii, commonly known as the Hackel's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions. Its leaves are usually hairy and shaped like a mouse's ears, giving it its common name.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
