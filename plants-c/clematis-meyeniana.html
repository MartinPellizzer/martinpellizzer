Clematis meyeniana

Clematis meyeniana, commonly known as the New Zealand vine, is a vigorous climbing plant native to New Zealand.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically appearing in late winter to early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and requires support for its long, flexible stems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive if not managed properly.
