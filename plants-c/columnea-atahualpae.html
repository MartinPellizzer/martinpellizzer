Columnea atahualpae

Columnea atahualpae, commonly known as the Atahualpa flameflower, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a climbing plant with striking, tubular red flowers that are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds. The leaves are elliptical and glossy, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat. It thrives in partial shade and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
