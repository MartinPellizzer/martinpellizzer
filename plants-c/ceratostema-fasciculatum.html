Ceratostema fasciculatum

Ceratostema fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Ceratostema fasciculatum is relatively low maintenance and prefers well-drained soil.
