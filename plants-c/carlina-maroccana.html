Carlina maroccana

Carlina maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan cudweed, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in a single head with ray florets.

Carlina maroccana is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
