Cucurbita pepo var. fraterna

Cucurbita pepo var. fraterna, commonly known as the Mexican ridge gourd, is a flowering vine native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like squash and melons. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers and elongated, ridged fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and sprawling.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a mild, slightly sweet flavor.
