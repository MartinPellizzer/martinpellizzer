Carex oederi var. glomerata

Carex oederi var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces dense, cylindrical clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
