Calamintha esquirolii

Calamintha esquirolii, commonly known as Esquiroli's calamint, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Calamintha esquirolii thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.
