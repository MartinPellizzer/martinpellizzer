Campomanesia adamantium var. nana

Campomanesia adamantium var. nana, commonly known as dwarf star apple, is a compact shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and home landscapes.
