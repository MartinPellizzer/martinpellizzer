Cyananthus petiolatus var. pallidocoeruleus

Cyananthus petiolatus var. pallidocoeruleus, commonly known as the pale sky-blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range from pale blue to sky blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate and colorful blooms.
