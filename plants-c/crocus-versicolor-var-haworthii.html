Crocus versicolor var. haworthii

Crocus versicolor var. haworthii, commonly known as the Haworth's crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a distinctive bicolor pattern. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sunny to partially shaded soils. It blooms in early spring, typically between February and April, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is relatively hardy and suitable for rock gardens or containers.
