Carelia latifolia

Carelia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf carelia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and herbal practices.
