Crataegus okanaganensis var. longispina

Crataegus okanaganensis var. longispina, commonly known as the Okanagan hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but generally considered tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
