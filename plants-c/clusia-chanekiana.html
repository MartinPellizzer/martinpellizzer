Clusia chanekiana

Clusia chanekiana, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clusia chanekiana is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and urban environments.
