Canala rubiifolia

Canala rubiifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf canella, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-red foliage, which contrasts with its green stems and leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Canala rubiifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
