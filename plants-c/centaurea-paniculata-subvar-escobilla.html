Centaurea paniculata subvar. escobilla

Centaurea paniculata subvar. escobilla, commonly known as the Escobilla bluemink, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight bluish-gray hue. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
