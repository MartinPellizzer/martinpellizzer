Coelodiscus glabriusculus

Coelodiscus glabriusculus, commonly known as the smooth-surfaced sand mat, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a fibrous root system and produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptation to survive in harsh, dry environments.
