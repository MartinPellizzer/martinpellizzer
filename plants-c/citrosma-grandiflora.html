Citrosma grandiflora

Citrosma grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered citrosma, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are small, round, and have a thick, leathery rind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
