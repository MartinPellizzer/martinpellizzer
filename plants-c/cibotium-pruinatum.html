Cibotium pruinatum

Cibotium pruinatum, commonly known as the silver tree, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance in its native habitat.
