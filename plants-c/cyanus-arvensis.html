Cyanus arvensis

Cyanus arvensis, commonly known as the common blueweed, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Cyanus arvensis is known for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
