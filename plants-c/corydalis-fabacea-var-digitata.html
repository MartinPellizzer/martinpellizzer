Corydalis fabacea var. digitata

Corydalis fabacea var. digitata, commonly known as the digitate corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with delicate, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically pink or purple and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
