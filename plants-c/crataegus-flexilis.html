Crataegus flexilis

Crataegus flexilis, commonly known as the flexible hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Crataegus flexilis thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, along stream banks, and in open fields.
