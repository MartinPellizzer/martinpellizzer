Cirsium faucium

Cirsium faucium, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and scrub habitats. Its scientific name reflects its historical association with the region of Fauces, a historical area in Italy.

Cirsium faucium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
