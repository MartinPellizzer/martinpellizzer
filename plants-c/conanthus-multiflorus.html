Conanthus multiflorus

Conanthus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered conanthus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Conanthus multiflorus is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
