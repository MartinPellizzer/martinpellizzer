Cerastium semidecandrum f. majus

Cerastium semidecandrum f. majus, commonly known as the greater mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that are oval to kidney-shaped, often with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in gardens and natural habitats.
