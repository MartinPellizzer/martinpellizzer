Celtis aurantiaca

Celtis aurantiaca, commonly known as the Chinese laurustin or red-fruited hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals.

Celtis aurantiica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
