Cattleya lawrenceana var. concolor

Cattleya lawrenceana var. concolor, commonly known as the Purple Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and white flowers, which are often fragrant and bloom during the summer months. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, typically arranged in a fan shape.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively ease of cultivation.
