Commiphora tecomaca

Commiphora tecomaca, commonly known as the Mexican myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dark green, elliptical leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by reddish or yellowish resinous exudates.

The resin, known as tecomaca or Mexican myrrh, has been used traditionally for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
