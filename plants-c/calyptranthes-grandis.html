Calyptranthes grandis

Calyptranthes grandis, commonly known as the large-leaved calyptranthes, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Calyptranthes grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
