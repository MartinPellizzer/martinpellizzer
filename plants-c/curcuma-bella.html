Curcuma bella

Curcuma bella, commonly known as the golden ginger or dwarf turmeric, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and cardamom. The plant features rhizomatous roots that produce bright yellow to orange-colored flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its foliage consists of broad, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture.

Curcuma bella is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
