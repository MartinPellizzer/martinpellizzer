Chorobanche

Chorobanche, commonly known as goatweed, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Chenopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with small, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are small, dry, and often dispersed by wind. Chorobanche is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is sometimes considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
