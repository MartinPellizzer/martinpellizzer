Calea hypericifolia

Calea hypericifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals that open at night and close in the morning. The plant has a distinctive violet-blue hue and emits a faint, sweet fragrance during the evening. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a velvety texture.

Calea hypericifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique nocturnal blooming behavior.
