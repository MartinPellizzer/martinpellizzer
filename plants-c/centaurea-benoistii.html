Centaurea benoistii

Centaurea benoistii, commonly known as the Benoist's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its compact, upright growth habit. It produces small, narrow leaves and has a short flowering period during the summer months.

Centaurea benoistii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public landscapes.
