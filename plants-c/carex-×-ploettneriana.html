Carex × ploettneriana

Carex × ploettneriana, commonly known as the silver hair sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Carex genus in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a silvery-blue hue, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
