Combretum humbertianum

Combretum humbertianum, commonly known as the Humbert's flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that resemble flames, typically appearing in bright orange or yellow hues. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent and may have a distinctive scent. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in traditional medicine.
