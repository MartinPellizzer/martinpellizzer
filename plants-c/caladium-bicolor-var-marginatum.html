Caladium bicolor var. marginatum

Caladium bicolor var. marginatum, commonly known as the painted tongue, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display vivid patterns of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically variegated with contrasting colors, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial shade.

This plant is often used in containers or as a ground cover in shaded garden areas.
