Cola selengana

Cola selengana, commonly known as African cola or wild cola, is a member of the Sterculiaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of West and Central Africa. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into hard, woody pods. The seeds within these pods are the primary part used, containing caffeine and other alkaloids.

It plays a significant role in traditional medicine and is also used in the production of certain beverages.
