Chamaesyce aequata

Chamaesyce aequata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.
