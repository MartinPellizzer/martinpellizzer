Clematis bellojocensis

Clematis bellojocensis, commonly known as the Bellojocensis Clematis, is a climbing vine native to specific regions in South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its attractive blooms and vigorous growth habit.
