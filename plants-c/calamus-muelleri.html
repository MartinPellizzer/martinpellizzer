Calamus muelleri

Calamus muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's canes, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves. Calamus muelleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
