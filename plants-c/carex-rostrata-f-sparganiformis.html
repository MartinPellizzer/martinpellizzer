Carex rostrata f. sparganiformis

Carex rostrata f. sparganiformis, commonly known as the spiky sedge, is a variant of the common rostrata sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical structure makes it well-adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
