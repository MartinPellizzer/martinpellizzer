Coptis anemonifolia var. dissecta

Coptis anemonifolia var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
