Convolvulus lineatus var. angustifolius

Convolvulus lineatus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, winding stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological contexts.
