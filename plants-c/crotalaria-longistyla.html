Crotalaria longistyla

Crotalaria longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with two prominent lobes on the upper lip. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically palmately lobed. Crotalaria longistyla belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
