Charidia mimosoides

Charidia mimosoides, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The silky acacia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
