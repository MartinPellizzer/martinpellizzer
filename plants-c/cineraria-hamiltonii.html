Cineraria hamiltonii

Cineraria hamiltonii, commonly known as the rock cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Cineraria hamiltonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
