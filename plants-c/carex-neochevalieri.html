Carex neochevalieri

Carex neochevalieri, commonly known as the Nechevalier sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. This perennial plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist to aquatic habitats. Its grass-like leaves are usually narrow and cylindrical, with a dense arrangement of small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
