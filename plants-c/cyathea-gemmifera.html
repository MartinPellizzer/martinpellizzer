Cyathea gemmifera

Cyathea gemmifera, commonly known as the gemmiferous tree fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Cyatheaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant produces spores on the undersides of its fertile fronds, which are arranged in clusters called sori. Cyathea gemmifera thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
