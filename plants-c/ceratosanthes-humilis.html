Ceratosanthes humilis

Ceratosanthes humilis, commonly known as the small-flowered horned poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features distinctive, horn-shaped bracts that enclose its small, showy flowers, which are typically pink to purple in color. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that release tiny seeds when mature. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woods and along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
