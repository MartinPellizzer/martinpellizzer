Cacalia sulcata

Cacalia sulcata, commonly known as the Sulcate Bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with a distinctive yellow or green throat. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
