Chassalia hiernii

Chassalia hiernii, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, cylindrical flower heads.

Chassalia hiernii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to full sun exposure.
