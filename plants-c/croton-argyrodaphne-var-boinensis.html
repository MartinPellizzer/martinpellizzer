Croton argyrodaphne var. boinensis

Croton argyrodaphne var. boinensis, commonly known as the silver-leaf croton, is a tropical plant characterized by its striking, variegated foliage.

It features broad, elliptical leaves with a silvery underside and bright green upper surface, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiky, orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variant is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique leaf patterns and adaptability to indoor conditions.
