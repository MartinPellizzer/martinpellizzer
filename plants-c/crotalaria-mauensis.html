Crotalaria mauensis

Crotalaria mauensis, commonly known as the Mau's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Crotalaria mauensis is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with upright stems and hairy foliage.
