Capsicum indicum var. vulgatum

Capsicum indicum var. vulgatum, commonly known as the chili pepper or bell pepper, is a species native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its fleshy, edible fruits that vary in color from green to red, yellow, or orange. The plant is an annual herb with lobed leaves and produces small, yellow flowers. Its fruit develops from the flower and is used extensively in global cuisines for its flavor and heat.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
