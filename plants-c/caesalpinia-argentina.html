Caesalpinia argentina

Caesalpinia argentina, commonly known as the Argentine trumpet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, tubular flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caesalpinia argentina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
