Cardamine griffithii var. grandifolia

Cardamine griffithii var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered watercress, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features trifoliate leaves with finely divided lobes and produces small, white or greenish flowers. Its roots are fibrous, and it spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in aquatic and wetland ecosystems.
