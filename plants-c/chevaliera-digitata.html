Chevaliera digitata

Chevaliera digitata, commonly known as the fingered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features distinctive, finger-like leaves that give it its common name, and it produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Chevaliera digitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
