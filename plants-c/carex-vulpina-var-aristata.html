Carex vulpina var. aristata

Carex vulpina var. aristata, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features triangular, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
