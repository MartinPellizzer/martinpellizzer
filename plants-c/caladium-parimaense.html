Caladium parimaense

Caladium parimaense, commonly known as the Parima arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Brazil.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants like taro and calla lilies. The plant is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored in shades of green, red, and white. Caladium parimaense thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
