Camellia cordifolia

Camellia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, double or semi-double flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. Camellia cordifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in traditional medicine.
