Cyathea frondosa

Cyathea frondosa, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of fronds that gives it a tree-like appearance, despite being a fern. Cyathea frondosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its rhizome system allows it to store water and nutrients, contributing to its ability to survive in humid, tropical climates.
