Carex montana var. oxyandra

Carex montana var. oxyandra, commonly known as the sharp-edged sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
