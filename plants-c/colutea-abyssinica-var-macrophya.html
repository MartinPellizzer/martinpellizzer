Colutea abyssinica var. macrophya

Colutea abyssinica var. macrophya, commonly known as the Ethiopian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia and parts of East Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
