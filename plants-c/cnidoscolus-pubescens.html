Cnidoscolus pubescens

Cnidoscolus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy stinging leaf or stinging tree, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves that can cause a painful rash upon contact. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid lowland areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
