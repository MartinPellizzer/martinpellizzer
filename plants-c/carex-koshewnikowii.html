Carex koshewnikowii

Carex koshewnikowii, commonly known as the Koshewnikow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
