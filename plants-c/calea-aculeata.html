Calea aculeata

Calea aculeata, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, spiny stems and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its scientific name, *Calea aculeata*, refers to its leaf structure, with "calea" meaning "to cover" and "aculeata" meaning "armed with spines.".

It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
