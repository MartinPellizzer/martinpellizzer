Cytisus thommasini

Cytisus thommasini, commonly known as the Thomas's broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
