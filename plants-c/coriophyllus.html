Coriophyllus

Coriophyllus, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Coriophyllus is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
