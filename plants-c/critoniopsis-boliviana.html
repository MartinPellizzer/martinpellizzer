Critoniopsis boliviana

Critoniopsis boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat. Its botanical features include a thick, fleshy stem that stores water and reduces transpiration.

Critoniopsis boliviana is often found in mountainous areas where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
