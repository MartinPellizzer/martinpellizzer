Cleomella arborea var. globosa

Cleomella arborea var. globosa, commonly known as the globe cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, woody stems that bear clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
