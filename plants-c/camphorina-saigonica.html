Camphorina saigonica

Camphorina saigonica, commonly known as the Saigon camphor tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that have a distinctive aromatic scent. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

The tree is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its leaves and used in various medicinal and aromatic applications.
