Caladenia saccata

Caladenia saccata, commonly known as the Purple Sheath Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking purple and white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open forests and heathlands. Its flowers are arranged in a spike and feature a unique, pouch-like structure called a labellum.

Caladenia saccata is a small to medium-sized orchid, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.
