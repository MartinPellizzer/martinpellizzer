Conyza microglossa

Conyza microglossa, commonly known as the small-lipped weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in clusters, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Conyza microglossa is often found in disturbed soils and can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
