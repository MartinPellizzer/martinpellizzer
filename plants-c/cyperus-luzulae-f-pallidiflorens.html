Cyperus luzulae f. pallidiflorens

Cyperus luzulae f. pallidiflorens, commonly known as the pale-flowered flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, preferring moist soils and full sun to partial shade. Its pale yellow flowers are less showy compared to other sedges, but it is valued for its ecological role in wetland habitats.

This subspecies is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic life.
