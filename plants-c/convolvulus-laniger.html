Convolvulus laniger

Convolvulus laniger, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, blue to purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves. Native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, it thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often found growing along roadsides and in disturbed areas, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
