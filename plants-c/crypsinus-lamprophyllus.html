Crypsinus lamprophyllus

Crypsinus lamprophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leafed cryptant, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which is covered in fine, reflective hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.
