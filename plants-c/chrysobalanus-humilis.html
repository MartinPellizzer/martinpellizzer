Chrysobalanus humilis

Chrysobalanus humilis, commonly known as the dwarf chrysobalanus, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters and has glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are covered in a hairy coating. Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystems, often found in moist, shaded areas.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
