Crassula lycopodioides f. acuminata

Crassula lycopodioides f. acuminata, commonly known as the Acuminata Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The leaves are typically fleshy and often have a bluish or grayish hue, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often in spring.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry, well-drained soils.
