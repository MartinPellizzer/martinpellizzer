Croton cornutus

Croton cornutus, commonly known as the horned croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often colorful leaves that can range from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Croton cornutus thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in rainforests or near water sources.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
