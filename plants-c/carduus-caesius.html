Carduus caesius

Carduus caesius, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features silver-green, spiny leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Carduus caesius is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
