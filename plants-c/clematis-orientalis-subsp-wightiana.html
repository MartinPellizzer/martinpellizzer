Clematis orientalis subsp. wightiana

Clematis orientalis subsp.

wightiana, commonly known as the Chinese vine or Wight's clematis, is a deciduous climbing plant native to China. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, fragrant scent.

The plant produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical characteristics make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in native habitats.
