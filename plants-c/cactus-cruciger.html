Cactus cruciger

Cactus cruciger, commonly known as the cross-cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems that are often ribbed and covered in clusters of small, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Native to the arid regions of South America, it has adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions.

Its slow growth and ability to store water in its thick stems make it a resilient and fascinating botanical specimen.
