Carex semicylindrica

Carex semicylindrica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a triangular cross-section, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its flowering spike is usually short and compact, with small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.
