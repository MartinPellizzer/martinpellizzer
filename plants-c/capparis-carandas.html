Capparis carandas

Capparis carandas, commonly known as the Indian caper, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, edible fruits known as capers. Its botanical features include a woody structure, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for well-drained soils.

Capparis carandas is often found in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
