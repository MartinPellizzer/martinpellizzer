Cicer isauricum

Cicer isauricum, commonly known as the Isaurian vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its pods contain edible seeds that have been used historically for food and animal feed.

Cicer isauricum is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
