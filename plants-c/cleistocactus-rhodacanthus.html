Cleistocactus rhodacanthus

Cleistocactus rhodacanthus, commonly known as the red-armed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, cylindrical cactus with a distinctive red or pinkish coloration on its stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique appearance and ability to store water in its thick stems make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
