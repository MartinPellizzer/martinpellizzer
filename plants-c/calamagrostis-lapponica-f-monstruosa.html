Calamagrostis lapponica f. monstruosa

Calamagrostis lapponica f. monstruosa, commonly known as the giant tussock grass, is a striking ornamental grass native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It features tall, silver-blue foliage that forms dense, tussock-like clumps, creating a dramatic visual impact in gardens. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Its ornamental value lies in its unique coloration and architectural form, making it a popular choice for wetland or bog gardens.

It also serves as a habitat for various insects and small birds, contributing to ecological diversity.
