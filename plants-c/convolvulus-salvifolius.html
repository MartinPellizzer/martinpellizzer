Convolvulus salvifolius

Convolvulus salvifolius, commonly known as the lemony convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive lemony scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its ability to cover ground effectively.
