Cassia chiapensis

Cassia chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its seeds are encased in pods and can be used in traditional medicine.

Cassia chiapensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
