Ceanothus americanus var. intermedius

Ceanothus americanus var. intermedius, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and often have a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

This species is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
