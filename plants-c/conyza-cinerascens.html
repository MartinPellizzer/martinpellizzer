Conyza cinerascens

Conyza cinerascens, commonly known as white dandelion, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces achenes with pappus, which are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, sharing features with other daisy family members.
