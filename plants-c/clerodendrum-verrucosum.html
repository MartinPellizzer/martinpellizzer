Clerodendrum verrucosum

Clerodendrum verrucosum, commonly known as the rough-leaved clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
