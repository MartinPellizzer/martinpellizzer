Centaurea aplolepa

Centaurea aplolepa, commonly known as the narrowleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spherical seed heads that resemble globes, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Centaurea aplolepa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
