Casearia longifolia

Casearia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf casearia, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by oval, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
