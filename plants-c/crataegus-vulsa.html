Crataegus vulsa

Crataegus vulsa, commonly known as the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Crataegus vulsa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
