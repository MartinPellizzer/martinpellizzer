Cacalia inulifolia

Cacalia inulifolia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, purple to violet ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
