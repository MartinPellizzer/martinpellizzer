Canarium gawadaense

Canarium gawadaense, commonly known as the Mindanao canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Canarium gawadaense is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
