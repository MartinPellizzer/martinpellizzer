Carex pseudololiacea

Carex pseudololiacea, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas.

The plant is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including parts of North America and Eurasia.
