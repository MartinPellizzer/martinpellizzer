Celosia interrupta

Celosia interrupta, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, plume-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

Celosia interrupta thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
