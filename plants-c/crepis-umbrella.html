Crepis umbrella

Crepis umbrella, commonly known as the common mouse-ear.

It is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae, characterized by its distinctive rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in grassy areas and along roadsides.

Crepis umbrella is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
