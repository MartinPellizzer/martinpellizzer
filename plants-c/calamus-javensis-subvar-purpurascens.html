Calamus javensis subvar. purpurascens

Calamus javensis subvar. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stem that exhibits a distinctive purplish hue, especially when young. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, followed by triangular, winged fruits. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets that give it a feathery appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its unique aesthetic features.
