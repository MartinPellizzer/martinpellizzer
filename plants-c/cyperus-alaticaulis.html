Cyperus alaticaulis

Cyperus alaticaulis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on short, leafy stems, and the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Cyperus alaticaulis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
