Croton antisyphiliticus var. weddellianus

Croton antisyphiliticus var. weddellianus, commonly known as the Weddellian croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that range from green to red, yellow, and orange, often displaying striking variegation. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its efficacy.
