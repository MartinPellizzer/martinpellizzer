Carlina biebersteinii

Carlina biebersteinii, commonly known as the Sierra madre composite, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It produces showy blue to purple flower heads that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Carlina biebersteinii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
