Cremastus grallatorius

Cremastus grallatorius, commonly known as the climbing lygodium, is a rare fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds and a unique ability to climb using its rhizomes. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind pollination.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
