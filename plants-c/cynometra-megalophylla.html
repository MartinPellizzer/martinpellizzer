Cynometra megalophylla

Cynometra megalophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, providing structural support and protection.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine and construction.
