Colchicum autumnale var. algeriense

Colchicum autumnale var. algeriense, commonly known as the Algerian autumn crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking violet or purple flowers in the autumn. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and a single, erect stem bearing a single flower. Its bulbs contain toxic compounds, including colchicine, which has medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
