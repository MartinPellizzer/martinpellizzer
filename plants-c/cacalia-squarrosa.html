Cacalia squarrosa

Cacalia squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia squarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
