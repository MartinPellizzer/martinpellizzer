Calceolaria lanuginosa

Calceolaria lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and distinctive slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine areas. Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collections.

Calceolaria lanuginosa is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
