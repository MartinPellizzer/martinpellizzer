Camellia rusticana f. albiflora

Camellia rusticana f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered camellia, is a unique cultivar characterized by its pale to fully white petals.

This variety originates from the broader Camellia rusticana species, which is native to China. The flowers are typically large and showy, with a delicate, almost porcelain-like appearance. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its white blooms.
