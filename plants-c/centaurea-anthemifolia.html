Centaurea anthemifolia

Centaurea anthemifolia, commonly known as the sweet William or the common centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are often surrounded by blue or purple bracts. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing clusters of small, showy flowers.

Centaurea anthemifolia is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
