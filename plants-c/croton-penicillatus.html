Croton penicillatus

Croton penicillatus, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from red to orange and yellow, often with a metallic sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
