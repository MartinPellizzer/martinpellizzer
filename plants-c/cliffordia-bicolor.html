Cliffordia bicolor

Cliffordia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored cliffordia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Cliffordia bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
