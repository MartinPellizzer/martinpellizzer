Crocus vernus var. fucatus

Crocus vernus var. fucatus, commonly known as the purple crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from deep purple to violet in color, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, and it thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.

The variety is distinguished by its darker, more intense flower color compared to the typical crocus vernus.
