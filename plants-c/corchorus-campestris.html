Corchorus campestris

Corchorus campestris, commonly known as common kochia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is valued for its fibrous stems, which have been used in traditional textiles.
