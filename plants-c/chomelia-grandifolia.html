Chomelia grandifolia

Chomelia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered chomelia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like texture, and its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
