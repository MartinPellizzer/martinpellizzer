Cestrum ambatense

Cestrum ambatense, commonly known as the Madagascar nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Cestrum ambatense is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and unique blooming pattern.
