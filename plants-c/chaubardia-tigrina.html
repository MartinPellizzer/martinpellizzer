Chaubardia tigrina

Chaubardia tigrina, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble tiger stripes, giving it its common name. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Chaubardia tigrina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
