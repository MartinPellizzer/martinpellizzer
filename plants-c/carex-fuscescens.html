Carex fuscescens

Carex fuscescens, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its dark green, grass-like leaves are arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are often wind-pollinated.
