Convolvulus andinus

Convolvulus andinus, commonly known as Andean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Convolvulus andinus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
