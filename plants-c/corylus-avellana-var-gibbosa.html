Corylus avellana var. gibbosa

Corylus avellana var. gibbosa, commonly known as the European hazel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces catkins in early spring, with male and female flowers on separate plants. Its edible nuts are enclosed in a spiny husk and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
