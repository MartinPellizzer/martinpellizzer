Centaurea gracillima

Centaurea gracillima, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea gracillima is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
