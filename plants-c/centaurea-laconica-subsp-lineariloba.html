Centaurea laconica subsp. lineariloba

Centaurea laconica subsp.

lineariloba, commonly known as the Laconica thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, sparsely branched stems that are covered in fine, white to pale blue hairs.

The plant produces striking purple to pink flower heads, which are composed of both ray and disk florets. Its leaves are lanceolate, gradually narrowing to a point, and are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and scrublands.
