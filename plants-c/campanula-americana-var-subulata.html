Campanula americana var. subulata

Campanula americana var. subulata, commonly known as the American bellflower or slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, spire-like leaves and bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Campanula americana var. subulata is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in well-drained soil.
