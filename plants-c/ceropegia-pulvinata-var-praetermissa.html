Ceropegia pulvinata var. praetermissa

Ceropegia pulvinata var. praetermissa, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features long, arching leaves that are typically variegated with white and green patterns. The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its unique leaf structure and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
