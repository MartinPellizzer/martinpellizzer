Cephalaria pungens

Cephalaria pungens, commonly known as the spiked cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, yellow-orange flower heads consist of many small florets arranged in a disc shape.

Cephalaria pungens is typically found in rocky or dry habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
