Campanula patula proles neglecta

Campanula patula proles neglecta, commonly known as the neglected bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas or along stream banks. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant.

Campanula patula proles neglecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
