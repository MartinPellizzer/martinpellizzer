Clidemia macropora

Clidemia macropora, commonly known as the broadleaf mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. Clidemia macropora is also known for its ability to spread through vegetative means, making it both a common and sometimes invasive species in its native habitats.
