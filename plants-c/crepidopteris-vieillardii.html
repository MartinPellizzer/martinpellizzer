Crepidopteris vieillardii

Crepidopteris vieillardii, commonly known as the giant reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and robust growth habit. The plant can reach heights of up to 6 meters, making it a striking feature in wetland environments. It thrives in moist, sunny conditions and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense stands that provide habitat for various wildlife.
