Clematis gracilifolia

Clematis gracilifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are typically slender and elongated, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental climbers.

Its botanical characteristics make it both a visually appealing and ecologically valuable plant in its native habitat.
