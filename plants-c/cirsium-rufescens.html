Cirsium rufescens

Cirsium rufescens, commonly known as the redstem thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and pale purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in fine, prickly hairs.

Cirsium rufescens plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often found in prairie and woodland ecosystems.
