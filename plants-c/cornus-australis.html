Cornus australis

Cornus australis, commonly known as the southern dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Cornus australis thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
