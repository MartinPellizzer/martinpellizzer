Centropogon rubroaureus

Centropogon rubroaureus, commonly known as the red-and-gold grass, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and vibrant, golden-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies. Its striking coloration makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

The flowers are small and clustered, contributing to its overall visual appeal and ecological significance.
