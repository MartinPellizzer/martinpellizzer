Chamaeraphis italica var. verticillata

Chamaeraphis italica var. verticillata, commonly known as the spiral rush, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of leaves that spiral around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the base of the stems. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
