Convolvulus undulifolius

Convolvulus undulifolius, commonly known as the wavy-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with wavy edges and trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it both a beautiful and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
