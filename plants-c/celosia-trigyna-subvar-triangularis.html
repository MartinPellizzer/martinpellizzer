Celosia trigyna subvar. triangularis

Celosia trigyna subvar. triangularis, commonly known as the flame flower or cockscomb, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, plume-like inflorescences that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow, resembling the comb of a rooster. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, providing a contrast to the showy floral displays.

Celosia trigyna subvar. triangularis is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
