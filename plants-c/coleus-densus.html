Coleus densus

Coleus densus, commonly known as the dense coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense foliage and vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is closely related to other members of the Coleus genus. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers in clusters.

Coleus densus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
