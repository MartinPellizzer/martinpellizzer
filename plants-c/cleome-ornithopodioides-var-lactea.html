Cleome ornithopodioides var. lactea

Cleome ornithopodioides var. lactea, commonly known as the white cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The milky sap that exudes from the stems and leaves is a notable botanical feature of this species.
