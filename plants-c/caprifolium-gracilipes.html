Caprifolium gracilipes

Caprifolium gracilipes, commonly known as the slender honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and yellow. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Caprifolium gracilipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
