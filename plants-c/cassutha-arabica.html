Cassutha arabica

Cassutha arabica, commonly known as the Arabian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture.

Cassutha arabica is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it forms dense mats or climbs over shrubs and trees.
