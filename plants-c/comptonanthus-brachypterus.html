Comptonanthus brachypterus

Comptonanthus brachypterus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved comptonanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded habitats. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense colonies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
