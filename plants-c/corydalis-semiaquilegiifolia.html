Corydalis semiaquilegiifolia

Corydalis semiaquilegiifolia, commonly known as the Pacific poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It features slender, hairy stems and produces delicate, nodding flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the tips of the stems. Corydalis semiaquilegiifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.
