Carex praecox f. rumelica

Carex praecox f. rumelica, commonly known as the early sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in a dense cluster, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
