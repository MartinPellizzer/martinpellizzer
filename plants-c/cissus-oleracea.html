Cissus oleracea

Cissus oleracea, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and small, reddish stems that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cissus oleracea is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its nutritional value in traditional cuisines.
