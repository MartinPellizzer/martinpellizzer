Chascolytrum altimontanum

Chascolytrum altimontanum, commonly known as the Andean mountain onion, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth pattern and bulbous structures. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to the cool, dry conditions of its natural habitat.

Chascolytrum altimontanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some Andean communities.
