Celastrus yuloensis

Celastrus yuloensis, commonly known as the Yulo climbing vine, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a woody vine characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
