Conocephalus dubius

Conocephalus dubius, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small, evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and tiny, scale-like leaves that form a dense, low-growing mat. Native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions, it thrives in woodland and forest floors. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are clustered at the tips of its stems, a feature typical of primitive vascular plants.

Its simple structure and ancient lineage make it an important subject in the study of early plant evolution.
