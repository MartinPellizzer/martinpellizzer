Calamus kentiiformis

Calamus kentiiformis, commonly known as the Kent's calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, palmate leaves that can reach up to several meters in length. The inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, greenish-white flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
