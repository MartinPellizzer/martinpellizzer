Cissus ternatomultifida

Cissus ternatomultifida, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can be used for propagation.

The botanical features of Cissus ternatomultifida make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
