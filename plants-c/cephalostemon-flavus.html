Cephalostemon flavus

Cephalostemon flavus, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found climbing or trailing along forest floors.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
