Crepinella weberbaueri

Crepinella weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's crepinella, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale purple in color. Its leaves are simple, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Crepinella weberbaueri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
