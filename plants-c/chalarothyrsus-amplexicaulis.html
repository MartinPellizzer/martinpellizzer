Chalarothyrsus amplexicaulis

Chalarothyrsus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping thorned vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, clasping leaves that encircle the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are follicles that split open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its thorny structure, it is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
