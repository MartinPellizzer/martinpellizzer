Carex filicina f. depauperata

Carex filicina f. depauperata, commonly known as the slender sedge.

This plant is a variant of Carex filicina, characterized by its reduced foliage and slender, grass-like leaves. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or wet meadows. The inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike of small, greenish flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native habitat.
