Cuscuta umbellata

Cuscuta umbellata, commonly known as the common dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a vine-like structure covered in tiny, hairless stems. The plant spreads by releasing seeds that germinate on the surface of host plants, attaching via specialized root-like structures called haustoria. It is found in various regions across North America and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its life cycle is closely tied to its host, making it an ecologically significant but sometimes problematic species.
