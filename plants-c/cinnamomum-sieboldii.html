Cinnamomum sieboldii

Cinnamomum sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese cinnamon or Siebold's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for aromatic purposes.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
