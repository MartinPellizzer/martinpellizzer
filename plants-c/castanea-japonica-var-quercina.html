Castanea japonica var. quercina

Castanea japonica var. quercina, commonly known as Japanese chestnut oak, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, and it produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks.
