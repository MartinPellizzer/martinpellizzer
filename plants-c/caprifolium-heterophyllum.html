Caprifolium heterophyllum

Caprifolium heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable honeysuckle, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Caprifolium heterophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
