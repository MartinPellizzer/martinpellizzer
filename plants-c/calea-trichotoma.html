Calea trichotoma

Calea trichotoma, commonly known as the "Mexican ghost flower," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence that resembles a ghostly face. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Calea trichotoma is also known for its strong, sometimes unpleasant odor, which is thought to attract certain pollinators.
