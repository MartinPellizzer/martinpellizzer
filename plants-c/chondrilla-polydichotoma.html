Chondrilla polydichotoma

Chondrilla polydichotoma, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions, making it well-adapted to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Chondrilla polydichotoma is often found in sandy soils and is considered a pioneer species in disturbed areas.
