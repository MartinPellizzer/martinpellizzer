Cucubalus baccifer var. japonicus

Cucubalus baccifer var. japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces orange to reddish fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor, depending on ripeness. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
