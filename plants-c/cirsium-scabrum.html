Cirsium scabrum

Cirsium scabrum, commonly known as rough thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a rough, scabrous texture, giving it its common name.

The flowers are usually visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.
