Chrysothamnus viscidiflorus var. stenolepis

Chrysothamnus viscidiflorus var. stenolepis, commonly known as sticky goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, woody stems covered in a sticky resin, which is a distinguishing botanical characteristic. The plant produces small, yellowish to golden flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that aids in reducing water loss.

This variety is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and sagebrush steppe habitats.
