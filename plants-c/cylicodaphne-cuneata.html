Cylicodaphne cuneata

Cylicodaphne cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sago palm, is a slow-growing, evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown surface and produces feathery, compound leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant is characterized by its pinnate leaves, which are arranged in a fan shape and have a triangular cross-section. Cylicodaphne cuneata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
