Cuscuta europaea

Cuscuta europaea, commonly known as the European dodder, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a fibrous, thread-like structure that attaches to its host. The plant's seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through human activity. It can infest a variety of crops and wild plants, causing significant agricultural damage.

Its life cycle is closely tied to its host, with the plant often dying when the host is removed.
