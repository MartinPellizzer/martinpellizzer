Canavalia luzonica

Canavalia luzonica, commonly known as the Luzon bean or Malunggay, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pods containing edible seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are a good source of protein. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Canavalia luzonica also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
