Cyperus triphyllus

Cyperus triphyllus, commonly known as the three-leaved flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an aggressive colonizer in suitable habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
