Cattleya brysiana

Cattleya brysiana, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often growing in a fan shape.

Cattleya brysiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
