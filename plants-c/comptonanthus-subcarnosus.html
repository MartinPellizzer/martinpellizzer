Comptonanthus subcarnosus

Comptonanthus subcarnosus, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the rainy season. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Comptonanthus subcarnosus is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and visually appealing species in its native habitat.
