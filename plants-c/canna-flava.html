Canna flava

Canna flava, commonly known as the yellow canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a distinctive yellowish-green hue. The plant produces showy, spadix-shaped flowers surrounded by bright orange or yellow bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Canna flava is a perennial herb that can grow up to 2 meters tall, making it a dramatic focal point in tropical landscapes.
