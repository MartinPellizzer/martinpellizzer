Carex xiangxiensis

Carex xiangxiensis, commonly known as the Xiangxi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, typical of the genus Carex.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
