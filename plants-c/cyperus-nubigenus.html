Cyperus nubigenus

Cyperus nubigenus, commonly known as the cloud sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or aquatic environments, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and flowering spikes. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, three-lobed flowers, which are often green or reddish in color.

Cyperus nubigenus is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds and lakes.
