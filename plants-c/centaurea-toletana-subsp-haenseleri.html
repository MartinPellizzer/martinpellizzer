Centaurea toletana subsp. haenseleri

Centaurea toletana subsp.

haenseleri, commonly known as the Toleto centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in regions such as Spain and Portugal. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky bracts around its flowers.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
