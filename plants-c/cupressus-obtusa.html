Cupressus obtusa

Cupressus obtusa, commonly known as the Japanese cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in crossed pairs along the branches. Its cones are small and woody, typically containing two to three seeds.

Cupressus obtusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens and landscapes worldwide.
