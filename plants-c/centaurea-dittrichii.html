Centaurea dittrichii

Centaurea dittrichii, commonly known as the Dittrich's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has narrow, linear leaves. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Centaurea dittrichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
