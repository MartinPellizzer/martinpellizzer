Cephalocereus mezcalaensis

Cephalocereus mezcalaensis, commonly known as the mezcala cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and clusters of white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has spiny areoles and produces small, red fruits that are edible. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
