Collinsonia serotina

Collinsonia serotina, commonly known as lateflower bluebeard, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

Collinsonia serotina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
