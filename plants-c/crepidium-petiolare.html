Crepidium petiolare

Crepidium petiolare, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower per stem, which blooms during the spring or early summer.

The orchid's rhizome is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
