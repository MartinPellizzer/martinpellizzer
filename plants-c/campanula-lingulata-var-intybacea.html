Campanula lingulata var. intybacea

Campanula lingulata var. intybacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often found in grassy areas, woodland edges, and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
