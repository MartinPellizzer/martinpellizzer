Cyathea quadripinnatifida

Cyathea quadripinnatifida, commonly known as the four-feather tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are pinnately compound, with multiple levels of branching, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant plays a role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
