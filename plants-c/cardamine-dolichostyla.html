Cardamine dolichostyla

Cardamine dolichostyla, commonly known as the long-styled water crowfoot, is a small perennial herb belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are deeply divided into narrow, grass-like segments. The plant produces delicate, nodding flowers with five sepals and numerous stamens, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.

Its unique floral structure and habitat preferences make it an important species for aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
