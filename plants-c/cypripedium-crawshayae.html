Cypripedium crawshayae

Cypripedium crawshayae, commonly known as the Crawshay's lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of northern Vietnam.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its delicate pink and white petals are adorned with intricate patterns, making it a visually captivating species.

Due to habitat loss and overcollection, Cypripedium crawshayae is classified as critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
