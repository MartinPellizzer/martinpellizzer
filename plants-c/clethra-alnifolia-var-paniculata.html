Clethra alnifolia var. paniculata

Clethra alnifolia var. paniculata, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, grayish bark. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
