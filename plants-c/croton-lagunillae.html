Croton lagunillae

Croton lagunillae, commonly known as the Guanica croton, is a shrub native to the Caribbean, specifically found in Puerto Rico and the U.S. Virgin Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves that can range from yellow to red and orange. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Croton lagunillae typically grows in moist, tropical environments and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
