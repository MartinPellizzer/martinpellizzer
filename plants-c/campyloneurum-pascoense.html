Campyloneurum pascoense

Campyloneurum pascoense, commonly known as the Pasco fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, curved appearance. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Campyloneurum pascoense is often found in the southeastern United States, particularly in Florida and Georgia.
