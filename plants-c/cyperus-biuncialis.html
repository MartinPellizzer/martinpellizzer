Cyperus biuncialis

Cyperus biuncialis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 inch wide, with a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in marshy areas, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Cyperus biuncialis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
