Cerastium arvensiforme var. bienne

Cerastium arvensiforme var. bienne, commonly known as the two-flowered mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing numerous tiny flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.
