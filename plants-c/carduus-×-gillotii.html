Carduus × gillotii

Carduus × gillotii, commonly known as the gillotii thistle, is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It has a taproot system and is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
