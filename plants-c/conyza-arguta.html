Conyza arguta

Conyza arguta, commonly known as the common fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a branching stem covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellowish-white composite flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and open areas.

Conyza arguta is known for its ability to spread rapidly through wind-dispersed seeds.
