Clematis alpina var. koreana

Clematis alpina var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean mountain clematis, is a perennial vine native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
