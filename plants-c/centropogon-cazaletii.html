Centropogon cazaletii

Centropogon cazaletii, commonly known as the Cazalet's centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly seen in its natural habitat.
