Campanula kachethica

Campanula kachethica, commonly known as the Kachetian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula kachethica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
