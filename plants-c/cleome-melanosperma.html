Cleome melanosperma

Cleome melanosperma, commonly known as black-seeded cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The fruit is a capsule that contains small, black seeds, which are used in some culinary applications.
