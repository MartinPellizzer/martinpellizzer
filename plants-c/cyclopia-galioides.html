Cyclopia galioides

Cyclopia galioides, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring.

Cyclopia galioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
