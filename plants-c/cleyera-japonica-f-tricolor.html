Cleyera japonica f. tricolor

Cleyera japonica f. tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored Japanese holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which display a striking combination of green, white, and red hues. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique foliage and seasonal interest make it a valuable addition to gardens and woodland settings.
