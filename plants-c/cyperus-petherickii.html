Cyperus petherickii

Cyperus petherickii, commonly known as the Petherick's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Cyperus petherickii is often found in coastal regions and can tolerate saline conditions, making it an important component of estuarine ecosystems.

Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing tiny seeds that are dispersed by water or wind.
