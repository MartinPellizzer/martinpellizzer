Cucumis melo var. odessanus

Cucumis melo var. odessanus, commonly known as the Odessan melon, is a botanical variety of the melon plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruit with a pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmate leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
