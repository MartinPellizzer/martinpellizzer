Cerasus regalis

Cerasus regalis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the emergence of leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits known as cherries, which are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The botanical features of Cerasus regalis make it a popular choice for gardens and public landscapes.
