Campanula rotundifolia var. alpina

Campanula rotundifolia var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its round, lobed leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula rotundifolia var. alpina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic alpine environments.
