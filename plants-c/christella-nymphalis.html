Christella nymphalis

Christella nymphalis, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
