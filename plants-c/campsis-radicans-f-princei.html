Campsis radicans f. princei

Campsis radicans f. princei, commonly known as the Prince's flame bush, is a cultivar of the trumpet vine.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in full sun.
