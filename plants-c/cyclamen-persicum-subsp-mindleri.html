Cyclamen persicum subsp. mindleri

Cyclamen persicum subsp.

mindleri, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant. It has a tuberous root system that allows it to survive periods of dormancy. Cyclamen persicum subsp.

mindleri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
