Corchorus neocaledonicus var. estellatus

Corchorus neocaledonicus var. estellatus, commonly known as Estellate cupweed, is a variant of the cupweed species native to New Caledonia.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
