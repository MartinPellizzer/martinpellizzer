Cordia dwyeri

Cordia dwyeri, commonly known as the Dwyer's cordia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has simple, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. Cordia dwyeri is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
