Chloris confertifolia

Chloris confertifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense clusters, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Chloris confertifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
