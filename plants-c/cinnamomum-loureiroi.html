Cinnamomum loureiroi

Cinnamomum loureiroi, commonly known as cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its bark and leaves are used to make the spice cassia, which is widely used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
