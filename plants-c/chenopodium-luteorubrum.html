Chenopodium luteorubrum

Chenopodium luteorubrum, commonly known as yellow goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its alternate, ovate to triangular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its seeds are typically dispersed by wind. Chenopodium luteorubrum is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in agricultural fields.

It is a hardy perennial that can thrive in a variety of soil types and is sometimes considered a weed.
