Cheiloclinium cognatum

Cheiloclinium cognatum, commonly known as the white-flowered tamarind, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing edible, sweet-smelling seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and tropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
