Crinum riparium

Crinum riparium, commonly known as the river crinum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows along riverbanks and wetlands, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green and glossy. Its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a strong, spicy fragrance.

Crinum riparium is known for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding, making it an important species in riparian ecosystems.
