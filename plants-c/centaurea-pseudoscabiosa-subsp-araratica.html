Centaurea pseudoscabiosa subsp. araratica

Centaurea pseudoscabiosa subsp.

araratica, commonly known as the Araratic centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a compact growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, with a striking central disc surrounded by ray florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
