Cyperus radians

Cyperus radians, commonly known as the spiny flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its slender, triangular stems are often adorned with spiky, grass-like leaves.

Cyperus radians produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, contributing to its ecological role in wetland habitats.
