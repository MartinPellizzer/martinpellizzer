Colchicum sibthorpii var. euboeum

Colchicum sibthorpii var. euboeum, commonly known as the Euboean autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Aegean region of Greece.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the autumn season. The plant produces large, showy blooms on tall, slender stems, often with three to five petals. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, emerging from a bulbous corm.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique flowering display.
