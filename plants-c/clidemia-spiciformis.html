Clidemia spiciformis

Clidemia spiciformis, commonly known as the Hawaiian leafy spurge, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
