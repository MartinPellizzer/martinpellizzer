Campanula scheuchzeri var. villarsiana

Campanula scheuchzeri var. villarsiana, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly coating, which helps protect it from harsh weather conditions. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
