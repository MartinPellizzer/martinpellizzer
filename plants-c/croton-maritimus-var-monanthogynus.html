Croton maritimus var. monanthogynus

Croton maritimus var. monanthogynus, commonly known as the coastal croton, is a shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive yellow or greenish hue. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its adaptability to saline and sandy soils makes it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.

The plant is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine.
