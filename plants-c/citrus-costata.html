Citrus costata

Citrus costata, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its thick, rough bark and fragrant, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, yellowish-orange peel and a tart, acidic pulp. It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in cooking, medicine, and as a source of essential oils.

Citrus costata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various climates.
