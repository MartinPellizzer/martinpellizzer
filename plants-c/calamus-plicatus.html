Calamus plicatus

Calamus plicatus, commonly known as the pleated reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and plicate (pleated) leaf sheaths. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. Calamus plicatus thrives in wet, marshy habitats and is often found in swampy areas near rivers.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native wetland ecosystems.
