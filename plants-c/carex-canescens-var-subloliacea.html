Carex canescens var. subloliacea

Carex canescens var. subloliacea, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a variant of the common hairgrass species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikelets that are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
