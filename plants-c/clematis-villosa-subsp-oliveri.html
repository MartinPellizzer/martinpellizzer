Clematis villosa subsp. oliveri

Clematis villosa subsp.

oliveri, commonly known as the Oliver's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, often climbing or trailing over supports.

The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically borne in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with serrated edges, and it has a fibrous, woody root system.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
