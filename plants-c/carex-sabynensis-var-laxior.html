Carex sabynensis var. laxior

Carex sabynensis var. laxior, commonly known as the laxior sedge, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in loose, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
