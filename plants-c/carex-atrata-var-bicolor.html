Carex atrata var. bicolor

Carex atrata var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green to blackish stems and leaves that transition to a lighter green or yellowish hue along their edges. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

Its unique coloration and adaptability make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
