Calotheca niliaca

Calotheca niliaca, commonly known as the rock lichen, is a species of lichen that thrives in arid and rocky environments.

It belongs to the family Parmeliaceae and is characterized by its crust-like growth form, which adheres tightly to rock surfaces. The thallus of Calotheca niliaca is typically pale to dark green, with a distinctive, often lobed or areolate surface. This lichen is often used as an indicator species for environmental conditions due to its sensitivity to air pollution.
