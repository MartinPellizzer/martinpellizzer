Cirsium eriophorum subsp. odontolepis

Cirsium eriophorum subsp.

odontolepis, commonly known as the toothed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more pronounced tooth-like leaf margins compared to other Cirsium species.
