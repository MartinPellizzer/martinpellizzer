Capsicum annuum subsp. microcarpon

Capsicum annuum subsp.

microcarpon, commonly known as the dwarf pepper or bird pepper, is a compact, bushy plant native to Central and South America. It produces small, round, and often brightly colored fruits, typically red, yellow, or orange when mature.

The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and relatively small leaves. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild to spicy flavor in culinary applications.
