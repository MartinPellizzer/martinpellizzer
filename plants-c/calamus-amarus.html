Calamus amarus

Calamus amarus, commonly known as bitter arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The botanical features of Calamus amarus make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
