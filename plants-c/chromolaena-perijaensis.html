Chromolaena perijaensis

Chromolaena perijaensis, commonly known as the Perijan false chinchona, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense heads. It is often found in dry to moist forest understories and is adapted to thrive in partial shade.

Chromolaena perijaensis is also known for its medicinal properties, used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
