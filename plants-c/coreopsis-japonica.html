Coreopsis japonica

Coreopsis japonica, commonly known as Japanese coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with a dark center, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its origin, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
