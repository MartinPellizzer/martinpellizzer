Cytisus capitatus var. bisflorens

Cytisus capitatus var. bisflorens, commonly known as the yellow broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, often branching stems covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its leaves are small, alternate, and trifoliate, with a leathery texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.
