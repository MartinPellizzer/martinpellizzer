Cantua loxensis

Cantua loxensis, commonly known as the Sierra Valley flax, is a perennial herb native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and open slopes.

Cantua loxensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
