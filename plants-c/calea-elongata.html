Calea elongata

Calea elongata, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

Calea elongata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
