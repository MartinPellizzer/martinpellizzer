Cephalocereus totolapensis

Cephalocereus totolapensis, commonly known as the Totolapan cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white or light-colored spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
