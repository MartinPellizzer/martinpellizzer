Centaurea sylvatica var. inermis

Centaurea sylvatica var. inermis, commonly known as the slender wood centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
