Colletia intricata

Colletia intricata, commonly known as the intricate cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water for survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted for absorbing moisture from the soil.

Colletia intricata is often used in horticulture due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
