Clerodendrum paniculatum

Clerodendrum paniculatum, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces clusters of these flowers in panicles, which are often adorned with small, leaf-like bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, adding to its visual appeal.
