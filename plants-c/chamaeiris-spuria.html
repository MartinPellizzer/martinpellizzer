Chamaeiris spuria

Chamaeiris spuria, commonly known as the common sand thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in open, disturbed habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters.

Chamaeiris spuria is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
