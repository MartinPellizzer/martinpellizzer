Chamaecyparis lycopodioides

Chamaecyparis lycopodioides, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or false cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and scale-like leaves that are dense and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
