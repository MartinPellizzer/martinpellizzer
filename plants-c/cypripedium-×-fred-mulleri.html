Cypripedium × fred-mulleri

Cypripedium × fred-mulleri, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium micranthum.

It is characterized by its large, showy, slipper-shaped花朵, which are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, including China and the Russian Far East. It thrives in cool, moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
