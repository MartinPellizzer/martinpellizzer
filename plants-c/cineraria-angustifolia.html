Cineraria angustifolia

Cineraria angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Cineraria angustifolia is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and poor soil.
