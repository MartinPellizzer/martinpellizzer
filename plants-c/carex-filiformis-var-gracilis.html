Carex filiformis var. gracilis

Carex filiformis var. gracilis, commonly known as slender filiform sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. Native to temperate regions, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream edges. Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
