Chloranthus japonicus var. koreanus

Chloranthus japonicus var. koreanus, commonly known as the Korean goosefoot, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides.
