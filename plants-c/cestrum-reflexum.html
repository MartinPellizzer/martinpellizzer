Cestrum reflexum

Cestrum reflexum, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that open in the evening and close by morning. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical with a smooth margin.

Cestrum reflexum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its nocturnal blooms.
