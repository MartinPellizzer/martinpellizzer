Ceratotheca sesamoides

Ceratotheca sesamoides, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pods. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into hairy, inflated pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its seed pods, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
