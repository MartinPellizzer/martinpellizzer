Cassia tetraphylla var. linearis

Cassia tetraphylla var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, linear leaves that are opposite or alternate in arrangement, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that split open to release the seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
