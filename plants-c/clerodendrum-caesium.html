Clerodendrum caesium

Clerodendrum caesium, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
