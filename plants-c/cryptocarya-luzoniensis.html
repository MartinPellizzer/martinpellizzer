Cryptocarya luzoniensis

Cryptocarya luzoniensis, commonly known as Luzon's cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical berries that turn red when mature.

Cryptocarya luzoniensis is often found in lowland to montane forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
