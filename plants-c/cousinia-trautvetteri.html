Cousinia trautvetteri

Cousinia trautvetteri, commonly known as the Transcaucasian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia trautvetteri is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
