Cyperus varicus

Cyperus varicus, commonly known as the variable flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its slender, triangular stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus varicus is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
