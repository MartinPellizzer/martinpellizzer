Calea orizabaensis var. websteri

Calea orizabaensis var. websteri, commonly known as the Mexican skyflower, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that resemble small, intricate flowers, often with a striking blue or purple hue. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its unique floral structure. It thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
