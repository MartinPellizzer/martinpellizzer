Carex inamii

Carex inamii, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Carex inamii is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden settings.
