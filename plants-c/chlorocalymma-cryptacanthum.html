Chlorocalymma cryptacanthum

Chlorocalymma cryptacanthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers on sturdy stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements have made it a favored plant among gardeners.
