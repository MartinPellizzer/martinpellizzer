Cupressus arizonica subsp. nevadensis

Cupressus arizonica subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Apache Creek cypress, is a subspecies of the Arizona cypress native to the southwestern United States. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its scale-like leaves and distinctive, rounded crown.

The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a valued ornamental plant.
