Chaenactis heterocarpha var. curta

Chaenactis heterocarpha var. curta, commonly known as the short-fruited plainscore, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer.
