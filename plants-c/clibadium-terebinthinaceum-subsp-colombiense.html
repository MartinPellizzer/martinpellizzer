Clibadium terebinthinaceum subsp. colombiense

Clibadium terebinthinaceum subsp.

colombiense, commonly known as the Colombian sycamore, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
