Centratherum mayurii

Centratherum mayurii, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in spring, giving the plant its vivid name. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth surface. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
