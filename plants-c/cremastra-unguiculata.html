Cremastra unguiculata

Cremastra unguiculata, commonly known as the northern star flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Cremastra unguiculata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a food source for certain pollinators.
