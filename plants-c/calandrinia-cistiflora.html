Calandrinia cistiflora

Calandrinia cistiflora, commonly known as the rock morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Calandrinia cistiflora is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
