Crepis connexa

Crepis connexa, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, rough, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, often grouped in clusters.

Crepis connexa is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
