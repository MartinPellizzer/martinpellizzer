Chamaemelum nobile f. discoideum

Chamaemelum nobile f. discoideum, commonly known as the cushion chamomile, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, rounded foliage composed of finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers with a central brownish disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine contribute to its value in both horticulture and traditional remedies.
