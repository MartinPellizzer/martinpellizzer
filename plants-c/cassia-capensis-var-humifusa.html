Cassia capensis var. humifusa

Cassia capensis var. humifusa, commonly known as the dwarf senna, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers clustered in racemes. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and is often found growing in open, sunny habitats. Its botanical features include small, papery pods that contain several seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
