Coffea thonneri

Coffea thonneri, commonly known as the Ethiopian coffee tree, is a species native to Ethiopia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries containing the seeds used for coffee. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is considered a valuable species in the study of coffee plant genetics.
