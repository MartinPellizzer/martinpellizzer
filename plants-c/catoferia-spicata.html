Catoferia spicata

Catoferia spicata, commonly known as the spiky cat o' nine tails, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage that forms a compact, bushy structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration in dry conditions.

Catoferia spicata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
