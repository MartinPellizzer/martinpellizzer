Cryptocarya loureirii

Cryptocarya loureirii, commonly known as the Brazilian laurel, is a species of evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant leaves that emit a subtle, spicy scent. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Cryptocarya loureirii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
