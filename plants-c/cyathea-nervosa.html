Cyathea nervosa

Cyathea nervosa, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to several meters.

Cyathea nervosa plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to nutrient cycling.
