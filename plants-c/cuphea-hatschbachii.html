Cuphea hatschbachii

Cuphea hatschbachii, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea hatschbachii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
