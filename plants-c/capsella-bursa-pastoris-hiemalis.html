Capsella bursa-pastoris hiemalis

Capsella bursa-pastoris hiemalis, commonly known as winter common chickweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to temperate regions.

It features small, alternate leaves that are typically hairy and have a heart-shaped base. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its fruits are distinctive spherical seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
