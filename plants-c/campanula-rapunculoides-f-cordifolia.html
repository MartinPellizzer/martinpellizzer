Campanula rapunculoides f. cordifolia

Campanula rapunculoides f. cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf bellflower, is a compact perennial herb native to Europe.

It features rosettes of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in garden settings.
