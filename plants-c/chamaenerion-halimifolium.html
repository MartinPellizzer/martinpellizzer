Chamaenerion halimifolium

Chamaenerion halimifolium, commonly known as the sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to withstand salty air and strong winds. Its flowering stems are often covered in small, glandular hairs that help reduce water loss.

Chamaenerion halimifolium thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.
