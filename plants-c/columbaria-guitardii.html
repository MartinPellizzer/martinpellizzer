Columbaria guitardii

Columbaria guitardii, commonly known as the mountain rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Pyrenees and surrounding regions.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant features woody stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are narrow, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Columbaria guitardii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
