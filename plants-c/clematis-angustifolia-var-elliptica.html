Clematis angustifolia var. elliptica

Clematis angustifolia var. elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with compound leaves that are elliptical in shape, giving it its botanical name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
