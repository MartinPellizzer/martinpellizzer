Cucurbita moschata var. naudinii

Cucurbita moschata var. naudinii, commonly known as the Mexican pumpkin, is a variant of the winter squash species characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruits.

The plant is native to Mexico and Central America, where it has been cultivated for centuries. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and typically have a rough, hairy texture. The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, a common trait in cucurbit species.

This variety is valued for its edible, sweet, and nutty-flavored seeds, as well as its ornamental appeal.
