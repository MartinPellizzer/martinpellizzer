Crotalaria undulata

Crotalaria undulata, commonly known as the wavy crownflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaf patterns. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Crotalaria undulata is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
