Carex orientalis

Carex orientalis, commonly known as the oriental sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and ribbed, with a dark green coloration. Its flowering spikes are usually cylindrical and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex orientalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
