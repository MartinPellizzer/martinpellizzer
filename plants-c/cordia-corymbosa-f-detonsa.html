Cordia corymbosa f. detonsa

Cordia corymbosa f. detonsa, commonly known as the mangrove apple or sea apple, is a variant of the mangrove species native to tropical coastal regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it an important component of mangrove ecosystems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its roots form extensive networks that help stabilize coastal soils and prevent erosion.

This subspecies is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in various cultures.
