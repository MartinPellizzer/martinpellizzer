Circaea alpina var. fertilis

Circaea alpina var. fertilis, commonly known as the alpine chickweed, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are oval-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
