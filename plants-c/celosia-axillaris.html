Celosia axillaris

Celosia axillaris, commonly known as the flame flower or cotton wool plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames or cotton. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the colorful bracts serve as the primary ornamental feature.

Celosia axillaris thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
