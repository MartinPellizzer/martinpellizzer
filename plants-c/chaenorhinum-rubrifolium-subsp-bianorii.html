Chaenorhinum rubrifolium subsp. bianorii

Chaenorhinum rubrifolium subsp.

bianorii, commonly known as the red-leafed toadflax, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive red or reddish-brown foliage, which contrasts with its pale blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and delicate blooms.
