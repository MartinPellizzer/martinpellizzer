Croton bolivarensis

Croton bolivarensis, commonly known as the Bolivian croton, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that often display a mix of red, yellow, and green hues. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Croton bolivarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
