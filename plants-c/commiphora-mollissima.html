Commiphora mollissima

Commiphora mollissima, commonly known as wild myrrh, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and produces a resinous sap that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes other resin-producing trees like frankincense and myrrh. Its bark and resin are valued for their aromatic properties and potential medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in dry, warm climates and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
