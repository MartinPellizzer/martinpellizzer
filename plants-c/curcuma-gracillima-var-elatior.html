Curcuma gracillima var. elatior

Curcuma gracillima var. elatior, commonly known as the slender turmeric or golden ginger, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
