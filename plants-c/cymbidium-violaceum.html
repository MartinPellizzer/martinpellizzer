Cymbidium violaceum

Cymbidium violaceum, commonly known as the purple cymbidium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect pseudobulbs and long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant prefers cool, humid environments and is often grown as a decorative ornamental. Its flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens and indoor spaces.

Cymbidium violaceum is valued for its beauty and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
