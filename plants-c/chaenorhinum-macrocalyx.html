Chaenorhinum macrocalyx

Chaenorhinum macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red petals that are often adorned with a distinctive horn-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its flowers bloom in summer, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, black seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
