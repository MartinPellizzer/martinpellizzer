Chylocalyx perfoliatus

Chylocalyx perfoliatus, commonly known as the perfoliate chylocalyx, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive perfoliate leaves, which have a unique structure where the stem passes through the leaf blade, creating a perforation. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
