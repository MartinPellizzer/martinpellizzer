Clematis altheifolia

Clematis altheifolia, commonly known as the white clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round berries that mature to a dark purple color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Clematis altheifolia is also valued for its ability to climb trellises and structures, adding vertical interest to landscapes.
