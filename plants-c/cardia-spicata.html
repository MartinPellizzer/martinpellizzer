Cardia spicata

Cardia spicata, commonly known as the spikeflower or spiky cardia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive spiky, tubular flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cardia spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
