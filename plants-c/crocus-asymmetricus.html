Crocus asymmetricus

Crocus asymmetricus, commonly known as the asymmetrical crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, asymmetrical flower shape, which sets it apart from other crocus species. The plant produces showy, violet to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in rocky, well-drained soils, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
