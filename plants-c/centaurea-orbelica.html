Centaurea orbelica

Centaurea orbelica, commonly known as the Orbeli's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and bristly foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its scientific name honors the botanist Jean-Baptiste Orbeli, who contributed to the study of the genus Centaurea.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
