Crepis acuminata

Crepis acuminata, commonly known as the acuminated hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the plant produces achenes that are dispersed by wind.

Crepis acuminata is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often found in temperate regions.
