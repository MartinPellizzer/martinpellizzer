Campanula persicifolia var. laevicaulis

Campanula persicifolia var. laevicaulis, commonly known as the smooth-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are smooth and ovate, with a slightly hairy underside. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
