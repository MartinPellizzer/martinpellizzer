Condalia mirandana

Condalia mirandana, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Condalia mirandana is also valued for its drought tolerance and adaptability to various soil types.
