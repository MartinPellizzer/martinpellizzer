Crataegus digyna

Crataegus digyna, commonly known as the red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries that persist into winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces white or pinkish flowers in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Crataegus digyna is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local ecosystems.
