Convolvulus batatas

Convolvulus batatas, commonly known as sweet potato, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the edible part of the plant, and its sprawling, vine-like growth habit. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically yellow or purple with five petals. The plant thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil, making it a staple in many tropical and subtropical regions.

Its versatility in cultivation and nutritional value contribute to its global significance as a food crop.
