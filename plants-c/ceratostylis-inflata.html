Ceratostylis inflata

Ceratostylis inflata, commonly known as the inflated tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy, inflated flower that resembles a small balloon. The plant has fleshy, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. It blooms during the rainy season, producing a distinctive, often fragrant flower with a unique, tubular shape.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shady, moist environments.
