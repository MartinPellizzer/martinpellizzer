Cornus sibirica var. kesselringii

Cornus sibirica var. kesselringii, commonly known as the Siberian dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking white bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in colder climates.
