Crotalaria intermedia

Crotalaria intermedia, commonly known as the Indian crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and palmately divided leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive spurred petal, and it produces small, hairy pods containing several seeds.

Crotalaria intermedia is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in certain agricultural systems.
