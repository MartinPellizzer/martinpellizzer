Calophyllum burmanni var. bracteatum

Calophyllum burmanni var. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has compound leaves with opposite arrangement. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The varietal form bracteatum is distinguished by its unique bract structures surrounding the flowers.
