Cucumis melo var. zhukovskyi

Cucumis melo var. zhukovskyi, commonly known as the Zhukovsky melon, is a rare and ancient variety of melon native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thick, aromatic rind and a sweet, juicy interior. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmate leaves and yellow flowers. It is valued for its unique flavor and historical significance in traditional agriculture.

Due to its limited distribution and cultivation, it is considered a botanically interesting and endangered species.
