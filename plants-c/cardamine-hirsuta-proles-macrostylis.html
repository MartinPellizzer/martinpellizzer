Cardamine hirsuta proles macrostylis

Cardamine hirsuta proles macrostylis, commonly known as the hairy watercress or broad-styled watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves are arranged in a basal rosette.

The flowers produce small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
