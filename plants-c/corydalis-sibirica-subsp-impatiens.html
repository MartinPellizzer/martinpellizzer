Corydalis sibirica subsp. impatiens

Corydalis sibirica subsp.

impatiens, commonly known as Siberian Corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
