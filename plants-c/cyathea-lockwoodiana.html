Cyathea lockwoodiana

Cyathea lockwoodiana, commonly known as the Lockwood's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system allows it to store moisture and nutrients, supporting its growth in nutrient-poor soils.

Cyathea lockwoodiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
