Calceolaria uniflora var. silvestris

Calceolaria uniflora var. silvestris, commonly known as the forest calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pouch-like flowers that range in color from violet to red, resembling a small lantern. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
