Coronilla repanda subsp. montserratii

Coronilla repanda subsp.

montserratii, commonly known as the Montserrat sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to sunny, arid environments. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soils in Mediterranean regions.
