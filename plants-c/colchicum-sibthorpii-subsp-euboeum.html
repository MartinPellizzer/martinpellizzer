Colchicum sibthorpii subsp. euboeum

Colchicum sibthorpii subsp.

euboeum, commonly known as the Euboean colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Aegean region of Greece. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

The plant produces striking, showy purple to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are basal, broad, and often have a waxy texture.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
