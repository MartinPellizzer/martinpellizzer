Cyrtopodium punctatum var. salvadorense

Cyrtopodium punctatum var. salvadorense, commonly known as the Salvadorian spurred orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and produces erect, hairy pseudobulbs. The plant has linear to lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and covered with a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically yellow with red or purple markings, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
