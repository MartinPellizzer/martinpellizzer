Clethra papuana var. papuana

Clethra papuana var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan clethra, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its compact shrub form and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland forest environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
