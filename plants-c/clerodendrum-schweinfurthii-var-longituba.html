Clerodendrum schweinfurthii var. longituba

Clerodendrum schweinfurthii var. longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed clefleaf, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are woody and often branched. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in horticulture for its striking floral display.
