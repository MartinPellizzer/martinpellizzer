Crucifera coerulescens

Crucifera coerulescens, commonly known as the blue crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. The plant has serrated leaves that are usually alternate along the stem, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds. Native to temperate regions, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
