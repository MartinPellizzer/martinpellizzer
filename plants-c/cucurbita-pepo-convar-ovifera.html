Cucurbita pepo convar. ovifera

Cucurbita pepo convar. ovifera, commonly known as the common gourd, is a widely cultivated species within the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its diverse range of shapes, sizes, and colors, with fruits that can be spherical, elongated, or bottle-shaped. The plant typically has large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow, vine-like growth habits. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This species is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary and ornamental applications.
