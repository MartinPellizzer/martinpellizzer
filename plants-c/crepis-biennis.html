Crepis biennis

Crepis biennis, commonly known as the annual hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces a single, tall flowering stem with numerous small flowers arranged in a head.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread widely in various habitats.
