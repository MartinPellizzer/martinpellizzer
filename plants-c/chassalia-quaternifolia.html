Chassalia quaternifolia

Chassalia quaternifolia, commonly known as the four-leaved chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clustered, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Chassalia quaternifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blooms.
