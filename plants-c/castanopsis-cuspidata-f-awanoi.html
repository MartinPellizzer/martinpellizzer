Castanopsis cuspidata f. awanoi

Castanopsis cuspidata f. awanoi, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut or Awano chestnut, is a variant of the evergreen oak.

It is a small to medium-sized tree native to Japan, particularly the Awano region of Shikoku. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with the fruit being a spiny, woody capsule containing one or two seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
