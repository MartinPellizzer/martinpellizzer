Canna cubensis

Canna cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban canna, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, colorful leaves and showy, spadix-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. Its flowers come in shades of red, yellow, and orange, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Canna cubensis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
