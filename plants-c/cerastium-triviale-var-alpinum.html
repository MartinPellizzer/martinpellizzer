Cerastium triviale var. alpinum

Cerastium triviale var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has hairy, succulent stems and leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it retain moisture in harsh environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to cold climates and high altitudes.
