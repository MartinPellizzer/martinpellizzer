Caladenia bryceana subsp. cracens

Caladenia bryceana subsp.

cracens, commonly known as the Bryce's Spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Western Australia. It produces a single, slender stem that bears a single, strikingly colored flower with a distinctive spider-like appearance.

The flower typically has a greenish-yellow labellum and a dark red or purple dorsal sepal. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and sandplains.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
