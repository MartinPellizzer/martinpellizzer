Cambessedesia tiradentensis

Cambessedesia tiradentensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum tiradentense*, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, red berries that resemble cherries. The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces clusters of small white flowers. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
