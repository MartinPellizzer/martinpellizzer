Cucumis luzonicus

Cucumis luzonicus, commonly known as the Luzon cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Philippines.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces small, edible fruits resembling cucumbers. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellow and are followed by elongated, green fruits that can be harvested when immature.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and ornamental value.
