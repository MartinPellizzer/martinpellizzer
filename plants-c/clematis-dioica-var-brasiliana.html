Clematis dioica var. brasiliana

Clematis dioica var. brasiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian virgin's bower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or fences.
