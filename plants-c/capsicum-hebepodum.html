Capsicum hebepodum

Capsicum hebepodum, commonly known as the woolly capsicum, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, sharing relatives with other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers.
