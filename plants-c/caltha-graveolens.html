Caltha graveolens

Caltha graveolens, commonly known as the yellow marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist meadows and wetlands in Europe and Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Caltha graveolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
