Chrysophyllum africanum var. orientale

Chrysophyllum africanum var. orientale, commonly known as the African starfruit or yellow starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia and parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible, star-shaped fruits that are yellow when ripe and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The leaves and fruit are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
