Carex goodenowii f. subsetacea

Carex goodenowii f. subsetacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spiky inflorescences. This subspecies is typically found in wetland or moist soil environments, thriving in areas with consistent water availability. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its growth habit makes it a common component of riparian and marsh ecosystems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
