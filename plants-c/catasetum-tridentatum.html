Catasetum tridentatum

Catasetum tridentatum, commonly known as the Tridentate Catasetum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with a specialized column for pollination. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The orchid's botanical significance lies in its role in the ecological systems of its native habitats and its value in horticultural cultivation.
