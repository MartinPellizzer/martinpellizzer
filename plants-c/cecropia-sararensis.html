Cecropia sararensis

Cecropia sararensis, commonly known as the Sararensis Cecropia, is a fast-growing tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive, hollow stems. The plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as secondary forests and along riverbanks. It plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
