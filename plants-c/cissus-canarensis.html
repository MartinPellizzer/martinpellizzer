Cissus canarensis

Cissus canarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian rock fig, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Cissus canarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
