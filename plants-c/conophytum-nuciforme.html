Conophytum nuciforme

Conophytum nuciforme, commonly known as the "Cape tulip," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical leaves that resemble small bulbs. The plant produces vibrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.
