Cercocarpus pallidus

Cercocarpus pallidus, commonly known as the silverleaf curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its unique leaf structure and ecological role make it an important species in its native range.
