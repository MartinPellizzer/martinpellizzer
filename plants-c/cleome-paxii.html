Cleome paxii

Cleome paxii, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling spider-like structures, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cleome paxii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and full sun.
