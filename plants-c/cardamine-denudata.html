Cardamine denudata

Cardamine denudata, commonly known as narrowleaf watercress, is a small perennial herb native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It typically grows in rocky or streamside habitats, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often hairless, with a slightly succulent texture. Its small white flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
