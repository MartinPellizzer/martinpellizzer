Cerastium behmianum

Cerastium behmianum, commonly known as the Behm's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes.

Cerastium behmianum is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is well adapted to cool, humid climates.
