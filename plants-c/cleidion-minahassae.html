Cleidion minahassae

Cleidion minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa clethra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clethraceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often serrated leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
