Callitris tasmanica

Callitris tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian white cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, scale-like foliage. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of gymnosperms. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its slow growth rate and longevity make it a significant component of Tasmania's unique flora.
