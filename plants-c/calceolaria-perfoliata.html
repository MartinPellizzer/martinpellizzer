Calceolaria perfoliata

Calceolaria perfoliata, commonly known as the perfoliate calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that resemble small cups, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern and can be hairy or glandular.

Calceolaria perfoliata is valued in horticulture for its vibrant flowers and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
