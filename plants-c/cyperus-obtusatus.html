Cyperus obtusatus

Cyperus obtusatus, commonly known as the broadleaf flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Cyperus obtusatus produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are usually green or brown in color.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
