Chenopodium foliosum

Chenopodium foliosum, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its lobed, succulent leaves that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by dry, three-angled fruits containing seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as a leafy vegetable.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both agriculture and ecological studies.
