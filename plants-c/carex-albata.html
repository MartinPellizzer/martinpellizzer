Carex albata

Carex albata, commonly known as the white sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow or white flowers, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex albata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
