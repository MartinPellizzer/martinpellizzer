Corydalis flexuosa subsp. pseudoheterocentra

Corydalis flexuosa subsp.

pseudoheterocentra, commonly known as the bent-stem corydalis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, wiry stems that often curve and twist, giving it a distinctive, almost spiral appearance. Its flowers are small, funnel-shaped, and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The subspecies is notable for its unique floral morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
