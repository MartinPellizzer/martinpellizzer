Cassia densiflora

Cassia densiflora, commonly known as golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical and subtropical regions.
