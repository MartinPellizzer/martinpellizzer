Chrysanthemum × cuneifolium

Chrysanthemum × cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chrysanthemum, is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely cut, narrow leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in a range of colors. Native to East Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
