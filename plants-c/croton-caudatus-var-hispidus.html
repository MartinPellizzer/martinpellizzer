Croton caudatus var. hispidus

Croton caudatus var. hispidus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
