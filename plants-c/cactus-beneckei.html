Cactus beneckei

Cactus beneckei, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its fruit, resembling an apple, is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.

Cactus beneckei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
