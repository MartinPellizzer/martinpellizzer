Calyptranthes tussaciana

Calyptranthes tussaciana, commonly known as the Tussacana or Tussacana leaf.

It is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in cool, humid conditions and is often found in high-altitude cloud forests.

The leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, waxy texture, making it a unique addition to botanical gardens.
