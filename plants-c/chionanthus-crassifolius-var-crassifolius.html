Chionanthus crassifolius var. crassifolius

Chionanthus crassifolius var. crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically 5–10 cm long and 2–4 cm wide. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers in panicles during late spring to early summer. Its leaves are thick and leathery, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland areas.
