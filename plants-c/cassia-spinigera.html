Cassia spinigera

Cassia spinigera, commonly known as the spiny cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, spindle-shaped pods that contain numerous seeds. Cassia spinigera is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and savannas.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
