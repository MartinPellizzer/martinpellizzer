Cristaria insularis var. insularis

Cristaria insularis var. insularis, commonly known as the Island Crinum, is a flowering plant native to certain tropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
