Cyathea nigricans

Cyathea nigricans, commonly known as the black tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. Its rhizome system supports the plant's structure and allows it to store water and nutrients.

Cyathea nigricans plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
