Cordyline brasiliensis

Cordyline brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar, is a striking ornamental plant native to Brazil.

It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, strap-like leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

Its resilience and adaptability make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
