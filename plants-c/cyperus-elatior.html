Cyperus elatior

Cyperus elatior, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, umbrella-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers. Cyperus elatior typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a water garden plant.
