Cinnagrostis trichodonta var. trichodonta

Cinnagrostis trichodonta var. trichodonta, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, wet soils.
