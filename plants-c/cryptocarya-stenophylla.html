Cryptocarya stenophylla

Cryptocarya stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen tree native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Cryptocarya stenophylla plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
