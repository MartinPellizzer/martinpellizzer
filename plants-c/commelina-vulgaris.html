Commelina vulgaris

Commelina vulgaris, commonly known as spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant has strap-shaped leaves and produces small, three-petaled flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina vulgaris is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a common weed and a valuable ornamental plant.
