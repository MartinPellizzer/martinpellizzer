Clematis recta subvar. fruticosa

Clematis recta subvar. fruticosa, commonly known as the shrub clematis, is a deciduous vine or shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round fruits known as follicles, which contain seeds covered in a silky coating.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail.
