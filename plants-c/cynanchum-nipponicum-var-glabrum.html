Cynanchum nipponicum var. glabrum

Cynanchum nipponicum var. glabrum, commonly known as Japanese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, ribbed fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
