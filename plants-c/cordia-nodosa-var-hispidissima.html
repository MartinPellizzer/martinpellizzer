Cordia nodosa var. hispidissima

Cordia nodosa var. hispidissima, commonly known as the hairy cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and dark green, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
