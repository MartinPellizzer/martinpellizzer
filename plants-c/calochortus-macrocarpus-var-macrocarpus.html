Calochortus macrocarpus var. macrocarpus

Calochortus macrocarpus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant marsh marigold, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are upright and sturdy.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with adequate water.
