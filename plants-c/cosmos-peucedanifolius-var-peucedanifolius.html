Cosmos peucedanifolius var. peucedanifolius

Cosmos peucedanifolius var. peucedanifolius, commonly known as the common cosmos, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and large, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a purple or pink center. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and landscapes.
