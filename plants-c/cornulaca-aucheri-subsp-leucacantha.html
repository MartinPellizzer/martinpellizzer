Cornulaca aucheri subsp. leucacantha

Cornulaca aucheri subsp.

leucacantha, commonly known as the white cornflower, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance.

The subspecies leucacantha is distinguished by its pale floral coloration compared to other subspecies of Cornulaca.
