Cordylophorum

Cordylophorum, commonly known as the water chestnut, is a aquatic plant that thrives in shallow freshwater environments.

It features a distinctive, edible, nut-like structure that develops underwater. The plant has broad, floating leaves that help in photosynthesis, while its roots anchor it in the mud. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Cordylophorum is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines.
