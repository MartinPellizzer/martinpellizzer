Carumbium moerenhoutianum

Carumbium moerenhoutianum, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender stalks, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Carumbium moerenhoutianum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
