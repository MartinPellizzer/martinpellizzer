Cracca senna

Cracca senna, commonly known as the yellow senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its yellow, pod-like flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and is known for its medicinal properties. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their laxative effects.

The plant's ability to thrive in arid conditions makes it an important component of its native ecosystems.
