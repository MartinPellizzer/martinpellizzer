Calamintha acinos

Calamintha acinos, commonly known as alpine germander, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Calamintha acinos is valued for its medicinal uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
