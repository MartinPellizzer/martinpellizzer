Caprifolium nepalense

Caprifolium nepalense, commonly known as the Nepal honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have serrated edges, while its stems are slender and woody.

Caprifolium nepalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
