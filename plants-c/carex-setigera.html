Carex setigera

Carex setigera, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage, with leaves that often have a zigzag pattern along their edges. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Carex setigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
