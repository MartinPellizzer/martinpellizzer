Centaurea praecox

Centaurea praecox, commonly known as early centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Centaurea praecox is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
