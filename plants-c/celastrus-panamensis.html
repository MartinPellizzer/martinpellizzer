Celastrus panamensis

Celastrus panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of tropical flora.
