Chenopodium trigynum

Chenopodium trigynum, commonly known as goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features alternate, broadly ovate leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture on the upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are enclosed in three-winged fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Chenopodium trigynum is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
