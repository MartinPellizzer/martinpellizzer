Cirsium longepedunculatum

Cirsium longepedunculatum, commonly known as the long peduncled thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this perennial plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges, and the plant produces achenes as its seeds.

The species is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
