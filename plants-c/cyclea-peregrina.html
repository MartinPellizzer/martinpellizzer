Cyclea peregrina

Cyclea peregrina, commonly known as the Indian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, red berries containing several seeds. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.

Cyclea peregrina is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and berries.
