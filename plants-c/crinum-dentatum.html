Crinum dentatum

Crinum dentatum, commonly known as the toothed crinum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often marked with white or yellow stripes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Crinum dentatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrance and striking floral display.
