Cissus cuspidata

Cissus cuspidata, commonly known as the needleleaf goosefoot, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by heart-shaped, edible berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cissus cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
