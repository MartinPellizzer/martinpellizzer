Camphora wrightii var. multinervia

Camphora wrightii var. multinervia, commonly known as the Mexican camphor tree, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a large, evergreen tree characterized by its thick, aromatic bark and dark, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing smooth, dark seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a prominent venation pattern, which is a key identifying feature.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
