Cestichis laurisilvatica

Cestichis laurisilvatica, commonly known as the laurel silverscale, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with mild winters and warm summers. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is dark green and glossy, and its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. It produces small, round berries that are typically dark blue to black in color.

The leaves are aromatic, often used in perfumes and traditional medicine for their essential oils.
