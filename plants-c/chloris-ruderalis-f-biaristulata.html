Chloris ruderalis f. biaristulata

Chloris ruderalis f. biaristulata, commonly known as the two-branched red grass, is a variant of the common grass species Chloris ruderalis.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-branched inflorescence, which sets it apart from other forms of the species. This plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in urban areas, where it thrives in nutrient-rich environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like flowers.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
