Candollea floribunda

Candollea floribunda, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to yellow spines. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for arid gardens.

Its unique appearance and vibrant blooms make it a striking addition to succulent collections.
