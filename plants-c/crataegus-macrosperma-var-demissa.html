Crataegus macrosperma var. demissa

Crataegus macrosperma var. demissa, commonly known as the Mexican hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It features compound leaves with 5 to 9 elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or white, arranged in corymbs. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
