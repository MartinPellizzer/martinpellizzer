Colona velutinosa

Colona velutinosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It is native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is often found in moist, disturbed habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many areas.
