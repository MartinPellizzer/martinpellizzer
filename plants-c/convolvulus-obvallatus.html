Convolvulus obvallatus

Convolvulus obvallatus, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny environments. Its vines can climb or trail, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and natural habitats.
