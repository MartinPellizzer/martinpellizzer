Citrullus lanatus

Citrullus lanatus, commonly known as watermelon, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and vibrant, striped fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into the familiar red or yellow fleshed fruits. Watermelon is botanically classified as a fruit, though it is often consumed as a vegetable in culinary contexts.

It is rich in water, vitamins, and antioxidants, making it both nutritious and hydrating.
