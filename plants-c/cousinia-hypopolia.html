Cousinia hypopolia

Cousinia hypopolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Cousinia hypopolia is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy species found in alpine and steppe habitats.
