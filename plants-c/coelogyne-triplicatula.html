Coelogyne triplicatula

Coelogyne triplicatula, commonly known as the Three-lobed Orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often with a waxy texture, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Coelogyne triplicatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
