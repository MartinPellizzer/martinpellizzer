Croton cascarilloides

Croton cascarilloides, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often milky sap-containing species. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences. It produces orange to red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
