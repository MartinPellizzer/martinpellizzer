Calamagrostis lanceolata var. gracilis

Calamagrostis lanceolata var. gracilis, commonly known as slender threadgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and tall, airy plumes that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along streambanks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of diverse wetland ecosystems.
