Crataegus acerifolia

Crataegus acerifolia, commonly known as the mapleleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of maple trees, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
