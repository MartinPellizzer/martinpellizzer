Castanea edwii

Castanea edwii, commonly known as the Edw's chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and other chestnut species. The tree produces oval, spiny nuts enclosed in a thick, hairy husk, which mature over a period of several months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, and they turn yellow in the fall.

Castanea edwii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
