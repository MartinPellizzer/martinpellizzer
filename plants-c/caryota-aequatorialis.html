Caryota aequatorialis

Caryota aequatorialis, commonly known as the Ecuador palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits.

Caryota aequatorialis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
