Campanula willdenowiana

Campanula willdenowiana, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
