Cestrum laurifolium var. neglectum

Cestrum laurifolium var. neglectum, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade or the evergreen trumpet vine, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that remain evergreen throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. These flowers are adapted to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
