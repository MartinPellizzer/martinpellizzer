Clinopodium sphenophyllum

Clinopodium sphenophyllum, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often branched, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in late summer.

Clinopodium sphenophyllum is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
