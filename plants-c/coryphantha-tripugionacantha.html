Coryphantha tripugionacantha

Coryphantha tripugionacantha, commonly known as the Mexican golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of spines and a yellowish-green stem. The plant features are small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry climates with minimal water requirements.

Its unique shape and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
