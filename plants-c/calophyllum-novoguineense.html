Calophyllum novoguineense

Calophyllum novoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea calophyllum, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Calophyllaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of New Guinea and other parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small and yellowish, typically clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in humid, lowland environments.
