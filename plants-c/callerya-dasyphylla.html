Callerya dasyphylla

Callerya dasyphylla, commonly known as the white-flowered callerya, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blossoms.
