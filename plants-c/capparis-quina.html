Capparis quina

Capparis quina, commonly known as the Indian Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its woody shrub structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, acidic berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Capparis quina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
