Centaurea paniculata subsp. langeana

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

langeana, commonly known as the Langeana bluemink, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces a slender, branching stem with narrow, linear leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
