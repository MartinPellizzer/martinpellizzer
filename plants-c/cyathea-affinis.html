Cyathea affinis

Cyathea affinis, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its fibrous roots, which help anchor the plant in moist, shaded environments.

Cyathea affinis is often found in humid forests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
