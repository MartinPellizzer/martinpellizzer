Capeochloa cincta subsp. cincta

Capeochloa cincta subsp.

cincta, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa. It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

The plant features slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray. Its flowering inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Capeochloa cincta subsp.

cincta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
