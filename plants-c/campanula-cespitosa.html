Campanula cespitosa

Campanula cespitosa, commonly known as the tufted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, clump-forming stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula cespitosa is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
