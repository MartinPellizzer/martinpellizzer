Cerasus × subhirtella

Cerasus × subhirtella, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically used in jams and preserves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
