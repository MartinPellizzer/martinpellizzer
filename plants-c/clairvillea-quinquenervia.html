Clairvillea quinquenervia

Clairvillea quinquenervia, commonly known as the five-veined corymbia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive five-veined leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in corymbs, often blooming in the summer months. Its bark is smooth and greyish, while its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
