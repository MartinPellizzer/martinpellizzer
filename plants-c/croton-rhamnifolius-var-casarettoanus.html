Croton rhamnifolius var. casarettoanus

Croton rhamnifolius var. casarettoanus, commonly known as the Casaretto's croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that vary in color from green to reddish-brown, depending on the stage of growth. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.
