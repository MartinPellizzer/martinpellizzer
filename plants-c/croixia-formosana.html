Croixia formosana

Croixia formosana, commonly known as the Formosan crocus, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
