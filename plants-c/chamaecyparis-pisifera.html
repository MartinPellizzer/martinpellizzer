Chamaecyparis pisifera

Chamaecyparis pisifera, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or Hinoki cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and its aromatic, needle-like leaves that are dark green on top and silvery white underneath. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a valuable addition to botanical gardens and private collections.
