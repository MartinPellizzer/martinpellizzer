Carex revoluta

Carex revoluta, commonly known as the curled sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes various sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curled at the base, giving it its common name. It typically grows in wet or moist soils, such as marshes or along stream banks.

Carex revoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
