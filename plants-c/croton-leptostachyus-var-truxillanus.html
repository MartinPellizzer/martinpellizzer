Croton leptostachyus var. truxillanus

Croton leptostachyus var. truxillanus, commonly known as the Truxillan croton, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
