Calophyllum ferrugineum var. orientale

Calophyllum ferrugineum var. orientale, commonly known as the oriental flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces woody, elongated fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction.
