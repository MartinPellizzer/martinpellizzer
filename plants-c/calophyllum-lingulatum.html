Calophyllum lingulatum

Calophyllum lingulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are followed by woody, orange-colored fruits containing several seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
