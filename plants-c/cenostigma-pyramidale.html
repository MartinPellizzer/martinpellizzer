Cenostigma pyramidale

Cenostigma pyramidale, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation programs.
