Cissus rotundifolia var. ferrugineopubescens

Cissus rotundifolia var. ferrugineopubescens, commonly known as the rusty-hair arrowroot, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive rust-colored pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, starchy tubers. Its stems are often used for ornamental purposes due to their attractive foliage.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
