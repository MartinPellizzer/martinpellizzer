Cattleya nalderiana

Cattleya nalderiana, commonly known as the Nalder's cattleya, is a tropical orchid native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant features long, arching inflorescences and has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

This species is prized by orchid growers for its beauty and fragrance, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
