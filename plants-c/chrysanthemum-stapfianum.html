Chrysanthemum stapfianum

Chrysanthemum stapfianum, commonly known as the Stapf's chrysanthemum, is a species native to China, particularly found in the Yunnan province.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale pink petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of dark green, lance-shaped leaves, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

This species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
