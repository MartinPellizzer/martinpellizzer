Centaurea chouletteana

Centaurea chouletteana, commonly known as the Choulette's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its bright yellow flowers are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
