Centropetalum hartwegii

Centropetalum hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's centropetalum, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with distinctive, showy flowers that bloom in shades of violet and blue. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are arranged in a unique centripetal pattern, with petals radiating inward toward the center of the inflorescence.

This botanical feature gives it a unique appearance and makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
