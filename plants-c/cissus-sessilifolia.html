Cissus sessilifolia

Cissus sessilifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or oval in form. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by spherical, red berries. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cissus sessilifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb structures.
