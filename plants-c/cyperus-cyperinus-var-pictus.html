Cyperus cyperinus var. pictus

Cyperus cyperinus var. pictus, commonly known as the variegated flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that often display striking variegation patterns. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and disturbed soils.

Its growth habit and appearance make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in aquatic and wetland habitats.
