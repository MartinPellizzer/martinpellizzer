Camellia honbaensis

Camellia honbaensis, commonly known as the Honbaensis camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers. The flowers, which are typically pink or red, bloom in winter or early spring, making it a striking ornamental plant. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.

This plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
