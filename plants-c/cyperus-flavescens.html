Cyperus flavescens

Cyperus flavescens, commonly known as yellow flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered in fine, hairy structures, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus flavescens is often considered a weed in agricultural and aquatic settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
