Carex krullii

Carex krullii, commonly known as Krull's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex krullii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
