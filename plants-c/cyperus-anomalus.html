Cyperus anomalus

Cyperus anomalus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Cyperus anomalus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
