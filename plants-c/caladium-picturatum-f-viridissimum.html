Caladium picturatum f. viridissimum

Caladium picturatum f. viridissimum, commonly known as the Emerald Queen caladium, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its uniquely veined, heart-shaped leaves.

The foliage displays a vibrant mix of green hues with intricate patterns of white and cream, creating a visually appealing contrast. This cultivar is native to South America and thrives in warm, humid climates with ample sunlight. It is often used in garden landscapes for its aesthetic value and is propagated through tuberous roots.

The plant is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
