Corchoropsis crenata var. hupehensis

Corchoropsis crenata var. hupehensis, commonly known as the Hupeh nettle, is a variant of the nettle family (Urticaceae) native to China.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Urticaceae family. Its leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
