Carduus segetum

Carduus segetum, commonly known as the corn cockle, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and agricultural fields, often found in crop rotations. Its leaves are rough and lobed, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive purple center.

The plant is known for its ornamental value but is also considered a weed in some agricultural contexts.
