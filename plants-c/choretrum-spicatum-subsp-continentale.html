Choretrum spicatum subsp. continentale

Choretrum spicatum subsp.

continentale, commonly known as the European marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies.

The plant produces tall, slender stems with clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
