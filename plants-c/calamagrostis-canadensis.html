Calamagrostis canadensis

Calamagrostis canadensis, commonly known as Canadian reed grass, is a tall, clump-forming perennial grass native to North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a fine texture. The plant produces feathery, silver-gray plumes in the fall, which add ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Canadian reed grass is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
