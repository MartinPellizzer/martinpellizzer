Cassia leptoclada

Cassia leptoclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often compound leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. It produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody pods containing several seeds.

Cassia leptoclada is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes due to its various chemical compounds.
