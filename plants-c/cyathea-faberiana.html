Cyathea faberiana

Cyathea faberiana, commonly known as the Faber's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant is evergreen and can grow up to several meters in height, with a slow growth rate. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

The fronds are typically dark green and can be used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
