Conyza stachyoides

Conyza stachyoides, commonly known as the hairyleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has opposite, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in open, sunny areas.

Conyza stachyoides is also known for its potential medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
