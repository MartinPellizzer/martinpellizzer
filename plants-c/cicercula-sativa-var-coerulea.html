Cicercula sativa var. coerulea

Cicercula sativa var. coerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered evening primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in prairies and open woodlands, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
