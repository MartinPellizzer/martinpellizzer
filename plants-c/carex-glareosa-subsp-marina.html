Carex glareosa subsp. marina

Carex glareosa subsp.

marina, commonly known as saltmarsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions. It thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, greenish-brown spikes.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various organisms.
