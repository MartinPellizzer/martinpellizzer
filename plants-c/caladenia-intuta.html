Caladenia intuta

Caladenia intuta, commonly known as the pink-stained spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a single, slender stem that bears one or two strikingly colored flowers. Its flowers are usually pink or purple with a distinctive, intricate pattern resembling a spider's web.

Caladenia intuta is known for its short flowering period, usually in late winter to early spring.
