Cousinia boissieri

Cousinia boissieri, commonly known as the Armenian hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. It produces small, elongated achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Cousinia boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
