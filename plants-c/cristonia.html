Cristonia

Cristonia, commonly known as the "Cristonia plant," is a member of the Aizoaceae family, native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and produces small, vibrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil. Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Cristonia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
