Cassia hookeriana

Cassia hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's cassia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Cassia hookeriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
