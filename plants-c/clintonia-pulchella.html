Clintonia pulchella

Clintonia pulchella, commonly known as the white fringeflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers with prominent yellow stamens, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

Clintonia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
