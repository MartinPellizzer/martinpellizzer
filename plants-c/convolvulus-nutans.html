Convolvulus nutans

Convolvulus nutans, commonly known as the drooping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often opening in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it versatile for various landscaping applications.
