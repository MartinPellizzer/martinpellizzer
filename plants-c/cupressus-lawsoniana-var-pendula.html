Cupressus lawsoniana var. pendula

Cupressus lawsoniana var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Lawson cypress, is a cultivar of the Lawson cypress that exhibits a distinctive weeping habit.

It features long, cascading branches that hang downward, creating a unique, elegant appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically a deep green, providing a dense, lush foliage. It is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate form and year-round visual interest.
