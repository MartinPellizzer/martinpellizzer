Commiphora berryi

Commiphora berryi, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It produces small, yellowish berries that are typically harvested when mature. The plant is characterized by its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a glossy appearance.

The botanical features of Commiphora berryi make it a notable species within the Burseraceae family.
