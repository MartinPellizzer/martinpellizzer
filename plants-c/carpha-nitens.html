Carpha nitens

Carpha nitens, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Carpha nitens thrives in moist soils and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
