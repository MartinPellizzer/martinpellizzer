Cirsium eriophorum subsp. morisianum

Cirsium eriophorum subsp.

morisianum, commonly known as the Morisian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial species characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural areas.
