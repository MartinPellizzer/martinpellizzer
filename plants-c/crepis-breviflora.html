Crepis breviflora

Crepis breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered hoary cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features hairy, often silver-gray leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Crepis breviflora is generally short-lived, with a height ranging from 10 to 30 centimeters.
