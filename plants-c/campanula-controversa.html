Campanula controversa

Campanula controversa, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 inches and has hairy, branched stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Campanula controversa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
