Cyathea barbata

Cyathea barbata, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant produces both sterile and fertile fronds, with the latter bearing spore-producing structures called sori. Cyathea barbata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
