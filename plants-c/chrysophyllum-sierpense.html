Chrysophyllum sierpense

Chrysophyllum sierpense, commonly known as the Sierpenski's starfruit, is a tropical plant belonging to the Sapotaceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces edible, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow when ripe, though they can also be orange or red. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both decorative and culinary purposes.
