Caamembeca andina

Caamembeca andina, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high Andes of South America.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and high-altitude conditions. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in rocky or well-drained soils.

Caamembeca andina is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in Andean cultures.
