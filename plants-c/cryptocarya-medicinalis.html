Cryptocarya medicinalis

Cryptocarya medicinalis, commonly known as the mountain laurel or Australian mountain ash, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue, edible berries that are a source of food for various wildlife.

Cryptocarya medicinalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
