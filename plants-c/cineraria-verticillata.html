Cineraria verticillata

Cineraria verticillata, commonly known as the silver cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

Cineraria verticillata prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
