Carex shikokiana

Carex shikokiana, commonly known as the Shikoku sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the summer months.

Carex shikokiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
