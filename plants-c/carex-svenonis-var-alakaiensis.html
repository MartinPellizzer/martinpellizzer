Carex svenonis var. alakaiensis

Carex svenonis var. alakaiensis, commonly known as the Alakaiensis sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
