Calyptranthes obtusifolia

Calyptranthes obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf calyptranthes, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

Calyptranthes obtusifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shady environments.
