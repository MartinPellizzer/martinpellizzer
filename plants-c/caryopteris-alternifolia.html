Caryopteris alternifolia

Caryopteris alternifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its attractive foliage and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
