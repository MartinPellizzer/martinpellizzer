Clusia humboldtii

Clusia humboldtii, commonly known as the wax plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia humboldtii is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its resilience to drought.
