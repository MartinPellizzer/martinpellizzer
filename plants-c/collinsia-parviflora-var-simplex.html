Collinsia parviflora var. simplex

Collinsia parviflora var. simplex, commonly known as the slender checkerspot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is part of the Plantaginaceae family and thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is also known for its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
