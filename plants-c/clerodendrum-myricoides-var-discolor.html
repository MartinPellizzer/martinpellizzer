Clerodendrum myricoides var. discolor

Clerodendrum myricoides var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated flame bush, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that ranges from yellow to red and green, creating a vibrant display throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy appearance make it a valuable addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
