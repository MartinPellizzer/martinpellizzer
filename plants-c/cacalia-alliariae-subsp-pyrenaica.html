Cacalia alliariae subsp. pyrenaica

Cacalia alliariae subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Pyrenees region. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in mountainous ecosystems.
