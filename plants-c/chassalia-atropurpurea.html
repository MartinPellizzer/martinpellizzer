Chassalia atropurpurea

Chassalia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple chassalia, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple to reddish-pink flower heads, which resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its appeal in horticultural settings.
