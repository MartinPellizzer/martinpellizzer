Collomia coccinea

Collomia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy stems and leaves. Its flowers are tubular and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

Collomia coccinea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in wildflower gardens.
