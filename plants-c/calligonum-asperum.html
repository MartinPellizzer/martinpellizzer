Calligonum asperum

Calligonum asperum, commonly known as the rough calligonum, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, grayish-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in poor soil, making it a hardy species for harsh environments.

Calligonum asperum is often used in landscaping for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
