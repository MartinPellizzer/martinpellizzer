Cissus oliveri

Cissus oliveri, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a climbing plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, round berries. Cissus oliveri thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its ability to grow on various surfaces makes it a popular choice for vertical gardening and hanging baskets.
