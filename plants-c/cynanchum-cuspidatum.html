Cynanchum cuspidatum

Cynanchum cuspidatum, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
