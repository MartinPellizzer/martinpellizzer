Copernicia prunifera

Copernicia prunifera, commonly known as the carnauba wax palm, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant is particularly noted for producing a type of wax known as carnauba wax, which is widely used in various industrial applications. Its growth is typically slow, and it can live for several decades.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
