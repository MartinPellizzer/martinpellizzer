Chamaedorea ferruginea

Chamaedorea ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty feather palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in areas with high humidity and well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pinnate leaves with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration on the underside. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and is often used as an ornamental plant in indoor and outdoor settings.

Due to its slow growth rate and attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for tropical gardens and interior landscapes.
