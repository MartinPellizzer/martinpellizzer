Cryptomeria nigricans

Cryptomeria nigricans, commonly known as the black firs, is a species of coniferous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark green, needle-like leaves and conical shape, which contribute to its ornamental value. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain winged seeds, typical of many conifer species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes.
