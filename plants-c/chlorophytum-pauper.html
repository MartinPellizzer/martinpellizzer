Chlorophytum pauper

Chlorophytum pauper, commonly known as the common spider plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers on slender stalks, which are followed by clusters of green, bell-shaped berries. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its ease of care and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Chlorophytum pauper is also known for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
