Cineraria glandulosa

Cineraria glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, slightly sticky texture. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
