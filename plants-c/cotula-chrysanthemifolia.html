Cotula chrysanthemifolia

Cotula chrysanthemifolia, commonly known as the golden cotula, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cotula chrysanthemifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
