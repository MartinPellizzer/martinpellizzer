Chromolaena guiengolensis

Chromolaena guiengolensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian tridacna, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It is native to tropical regions of the Pacific and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a problematic species in some ecosystems.
