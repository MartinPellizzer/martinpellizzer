Claoxylon scottianum

Claoxylon scottianum, commonly known as the Scott's claoxylon, is a rare and ancient tree species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Claoxylaceae and is characterized by its thick, buttressed roots and large, leathery leaves. The plant is known for its slow growth and longevity, with some specimens living for several centuries. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while its fruits are fleshy and attract various wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
