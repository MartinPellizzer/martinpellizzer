Coryanthes maculata var. parkeri

Coryanthes maculata var. parkeri, commonly known as the spotted coryanthe, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and colorful floral structures that resemble a crown, with a distinctive central spur. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with contrasting patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
