Chionanthus vitiensis

Chionanthus vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis Fringeflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, white to cream-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faintly fragrant scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The Vitiensis Fringeflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
