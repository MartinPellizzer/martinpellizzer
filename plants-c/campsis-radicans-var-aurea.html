Campsis radicans var. aurea

Campsis radicans var. aurea, commonly known as the yellow trumpetbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow tubular flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn red in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in its spread.
