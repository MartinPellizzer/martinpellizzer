Convolvulus natalensis var. integrifolia

Convolvulus natalensis var. integrifolia, commonly known as Natal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth, entire margin, which distinguishes it from other varieties. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden settings for its vibrant blooms.
