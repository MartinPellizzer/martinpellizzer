Crateva fragrans

Crateva fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its milky sap and distinctive, palmate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in umbels, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fragrance of its flowers is a notable botanical feature, attracting various pollinators.
