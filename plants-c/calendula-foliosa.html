Calendula foliosa

Calendula foliosa, commonly known as the leafy calendula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are known for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant benefits.

Calendula foliosa is often cultivated in gardens for both ornamental and therapeutic purposes.
