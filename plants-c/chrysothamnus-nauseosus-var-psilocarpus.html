Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. psilocarpus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. psilocarpus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange-yellow inflorescences and silvery-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

The species is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and its ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
