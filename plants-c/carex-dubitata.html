Carex dubitata

Carex dubitata, commonly known as the uncertain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, often shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex dubitata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
