Calamus schlechterianus

Calamus schlechterianus, commonly known as the Schlechter's calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a tall, slender palm-like plant characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clustered inflorescences. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white in color.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its botanical significance lies in its ecological role within its native habitat.
