Crataegus kansuensis f. aurantiaca

Crataegus kansuensis f. aurantiaca, commonly known as the Kansu hawthorn var. aurantiaca, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Kansu hawthorn.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red foliage, which stands out during the autumn season. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China, specifically the Gansu province. It produces small, white flowers in the spring followed by red berries in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its unique leaf coloration.
