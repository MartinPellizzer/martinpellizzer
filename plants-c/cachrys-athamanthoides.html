Cachrys athamanthoides

Cachrys athamanthoides, commonly known as the dwarf snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Cachrys athamanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
