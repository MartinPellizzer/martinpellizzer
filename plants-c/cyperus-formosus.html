Cyperus formosus

Cyperus formosus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Philippines, and thrives in moist, often wet environments such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.

Cyperus formosus is typically short-lived and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
