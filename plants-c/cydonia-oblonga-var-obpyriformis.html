Cydonia oblonga var. obpyriformis

Cydonia oblonga var. obpyriformis, commonly known as the Indian date palm, is a variety of the date palm characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curved fruit shape.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces large, fibrous fruits that are rich in sugars and have a sweet, honey-like flavor. Its leaves are large, feather-like, and can grow up to several meters in length.

The tree is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional agriculture.
