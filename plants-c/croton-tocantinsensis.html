Croton tocantinsensis

Croton tocantinsensis, commonly known as the Tocantins croton, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, red, and yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
