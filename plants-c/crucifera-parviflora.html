Crucifera parviflora

Crucifera parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, round pods that contain seeds. It is native to temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens and botanical collections.
