Carex cylindrica

Carex cylindrica, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and bear small, spikelet-like flowers.

Carex cylindrica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
