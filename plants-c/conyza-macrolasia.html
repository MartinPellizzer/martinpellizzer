Conyza macrolasia

Conyza macrolasia, commonly known as the greater ragweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and has finely divided, grass-like leaves. It is known for its allergenic pollen, which can cause significant respiratory issues in humans.

Conyza macrolasia thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.
