Cracca platycarpa

Cracca platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited cracca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan, and thrives in moist, woodland environments. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, and its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape with a smooth margin. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Its fruit is a three-lobed, fleshy capsule that splits open to release the seeds.
