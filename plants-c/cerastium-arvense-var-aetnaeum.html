Cerastium arvense var. aetnaeum

Cerastium arvense var. aetnaeum, commonly known as the Aetna mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
