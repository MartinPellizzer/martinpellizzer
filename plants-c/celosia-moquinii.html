Celosia moquinii

Celosia moquinii, commonly known as the flame flower or red dwarf celosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, plume-like inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant is typically compact, with narrow, grass-like leaves and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of growth.

Celosia moquinii is also known for its ability to reseed itself, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, colorful blooms.
