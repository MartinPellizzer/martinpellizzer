Cerastium lanthes

Cerastium lanthes, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are hairy and oval-shaped, adapted to withstand cold and harsh alpine conditions.

Cerastium lanthes thrives in rocky soils and is often found in meadows and alpine tundra.
