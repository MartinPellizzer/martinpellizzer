Cynometra ramiflora var. bifoliolata

Cynometra ramiflora var. bifoliolata, commonly known as the two-leaf sterculia, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are divided into two pairs of small, opposite leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
