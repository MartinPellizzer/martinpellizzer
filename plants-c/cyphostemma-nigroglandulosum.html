Cyphostemma nigroglandulosum

Cyphostemma nigroglandulosum, commonly known as the black glandular cyphostemma, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
