Cylindrolobus aurantiacus

Cylindrolobus aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous covering. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
