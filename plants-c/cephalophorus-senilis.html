Cephalophorus senilis

Cephalophorus senilis, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Cephalophorus senilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
