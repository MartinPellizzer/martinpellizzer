Chironia ixifera

Chironia ixifera, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like growth form and produces small, colorful flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to absorb moisture from the soil efficiently.

Chironia ixifera is often found growing on rocky outcrops and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.
