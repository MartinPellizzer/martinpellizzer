Chlorophytum subhispidum var. glabrum

Chlorophytum subhispidum var. glabrum, commonly known as the spider plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its long, arching leaves that often form a rosette shape. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its foliage is typically sword-shaped with a smooth, waxy texture, and it is known for its air-purifying qualities.
