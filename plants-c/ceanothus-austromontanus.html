Ceanothus austromontanus

Ceanothus austromontanus, commonly known as the coast ceanothus, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to coastal climates. Its root system helps stabilize sandy or rocky soils, contributing to its ecological importance in coastal habitats.

Ceanothus austromontanus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
