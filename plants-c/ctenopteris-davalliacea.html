Ctenopteris davalliacea

Ctenopteris davalliacea, commonly known as the Davalliacean sea palm, is a species of marine fern belonging to the family Ctenopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a central stem with fronds that are often spirally arranged. The plant thrives in shallow coastal waters, where it attaches itself to rocky substrates using its rhizomes. Its fronds are typically narrow and elongated, with a distinctive pattern of small, scale-like structures along the midrib.

This species plays an important ecological role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and shelter for various marine organisms.
