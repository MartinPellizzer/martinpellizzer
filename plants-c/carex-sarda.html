Carex sarda

Carex sarda, commonly known as Sardian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Carex in the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

Carex sarda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
