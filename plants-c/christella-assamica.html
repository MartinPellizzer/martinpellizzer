Christella assamica

Christella assamica, commonly known as the Assam bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It is often found in alpine meadows and forest understories at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
