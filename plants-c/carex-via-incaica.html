Carex via-incaica

Carex via-incaica, commonly known as the Inca sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky environments such as mountain slopes and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex via-incaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
