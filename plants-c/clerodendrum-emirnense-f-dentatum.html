Clerodendrum emirnense f. dentatum

Clerodendrum emirnense f. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed clerodendrum, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that have distinctive serrated or toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers, which are often followed by ornamental, spiny capsules. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
