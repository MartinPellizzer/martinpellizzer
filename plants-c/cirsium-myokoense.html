Cirsium myokoense

Cirsium myokoense, commonly known as the Myoko thistle, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cirsium myokoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
