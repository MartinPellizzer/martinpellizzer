Crepis multicaulis

Crepis multicaulis, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces numerous yellow flowers arranged in dense, flattened clusters called capitula. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Crepis multicaulis is typically herbaceous, with a short lifespan and a preference for temperate climates.
