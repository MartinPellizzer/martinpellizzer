Cerastium semidecandrum var. linneanum

Cerastium semidecandrum var. linneanum, commonly known as the Linnean sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It typically features prostrate or ascending stems that are covered in dense, white to pale green hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine pubescence.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
