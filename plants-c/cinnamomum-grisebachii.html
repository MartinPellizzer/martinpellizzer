Cinnamomum grisebachii

Cinnamomum grisebachii, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum grisebachii, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to China, particularly in the provinces of Yunnan and Sichuan, where it grows in mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Its bark is used in traditional Chinese medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and medicinal research.
