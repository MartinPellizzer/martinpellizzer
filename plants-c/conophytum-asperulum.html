Conophytum asperulum

Conophytum asperulum, commonly known as the rock fig or stone plant, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often forming dense clusters. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Conophytum asperulum is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its low maintenance and unique appearance.
