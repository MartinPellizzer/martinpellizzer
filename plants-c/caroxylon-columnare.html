Caroxylon columnare

Caroxylon columnare, commonly known as the columnar cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has spiny, ribbed stems that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Caroxylon columnare is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy plant in desert conditions.
