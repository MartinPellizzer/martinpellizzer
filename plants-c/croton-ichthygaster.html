Croton ichthygaster

Croton ichthygaster, commonly known as the fish-scale croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful leaves that have a distinctive fish-scale appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
