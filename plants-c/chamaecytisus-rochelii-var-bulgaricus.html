Chamaecytisus rochelii var. bulgaricus

Chamaecytisus rochelii var. bulgaricus, commonly known as the Bulgarian broom, is a shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
