Cissus hotteana

Cissus hotteana, commonly known as the African devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, white, glandular dots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its rapid growth and trailing habit make it an attractive ornamental plant.
