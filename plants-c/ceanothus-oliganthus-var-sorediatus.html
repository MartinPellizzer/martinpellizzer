Ceanothus oliganthus var. sorediatus

Ceanothus oliganthus var. sorediatus, commonly known as the coastal ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
