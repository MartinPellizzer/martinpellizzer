Centaurea balbisiana

Centaurea balbisiana, commonly known as the Balbisian thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea balbisiana is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its aggressive growth habit.
