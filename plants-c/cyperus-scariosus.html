Cyperus scariosus

Cyperus scariosus, commonly known as the paper flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are often covered with a waxy, papery layer that helps reduce water loss.

Cyperus scariosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a stabilizing plant in aquatic ecosystems.
