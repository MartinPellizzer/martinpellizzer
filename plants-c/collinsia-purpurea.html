Collinsia purpurea

Collinsia purpurea, commonly known as the purple checkerspot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and vibrant purple and white coloration. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has slender, branching stems and produces small, showy flowers in spring.

Collinsia purpurea is an important species for pollinators and is often found in conservation efforts due to its declining populations.
