Calathea curaraya

Calathea curaraya, commonly known as the curaraya calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns. The leaves are typically broad and have a velvety texture, often displaying shades of green, pink, and white. This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
