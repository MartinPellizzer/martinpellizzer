Celtis flagellaris

Celtis flagellaris, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by small, spherical berries.

Celtis flagellaris is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in urban environments.
