Coelogyne papillitepala

Coelogyne papillitepala, commonly known as the Papillate Coelogyne, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant features a pseudobulb structure with fleshy leaves that help store water, adapting it to its tropical and sometimes dry habitats. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with red or purple markings, making it a popular choice for horticultural collections.

Coelogyne papillitepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
