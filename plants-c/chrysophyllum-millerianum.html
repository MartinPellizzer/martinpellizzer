Chrysophyllum millerianum

Chrysophyllum millerianum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp that contains small, starchy seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is consumed both fresh and in processed forms.
