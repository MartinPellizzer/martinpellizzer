Cyanotis homblei

Cyanotis homblei, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish tint, contributing to its common name.

Cyanotis homblei thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.
