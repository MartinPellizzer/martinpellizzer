Crocus annulatus subvar. argenteus

Crocus annulatus subvar. argenteus, commonly known as the silver-crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive silvery or pale green sheen. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It blooms in early spring, typically between February and April, depending on the climate.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-flowering display.
