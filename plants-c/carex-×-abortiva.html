Carex × abortiva

Carex × abortiva, commonly known as the hybrid marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex × abortiva plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
