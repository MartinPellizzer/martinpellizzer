Crataegus pruinosa var. leiophylla

Crataegus pruinosa var. leiophylla, commonly known as the smooth-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and its dense, thorned branches. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
