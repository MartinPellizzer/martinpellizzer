Cercocarpus betuloides var. betuloides

Cercocarpus betuloides var. betuloides, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, oval leaves that turn red in the fall, and it produces clusters of red, berry-like fruits that persist through winter. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. It has a compact, multi-stemmed growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suited to xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
