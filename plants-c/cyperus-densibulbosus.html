Cyperus densibulbosus

Cyperus densibulbosus, commonly known as the densebulb sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, typically bearing brownish or reddish flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus densibulbosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
