Camptorrhiza strumosa

Camptorrhiza strumosa, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in native plant gardens.
