Carthamus lanatus var. baeticus

Carthamus lanatus var. baeticus, commonly known as the Baetican thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
