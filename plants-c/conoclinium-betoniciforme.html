Conoclinium betoniciforme

Conoclinium betoniciforme, commonly known as Mexican trumpetweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

It is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
