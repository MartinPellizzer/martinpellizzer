Celosia adoensis

Celosia adoensis, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves rather than flowers. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and prefers well-drained soil.

Celosia adoensis is known for its long-lasting blooms and is popular in gardens and floral arrangements.
