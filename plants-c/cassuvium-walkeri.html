Cassuvium walkeri

Cassuvium walkeri, commonly known as the walking cucumber, is a unique plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of tendrils that help it secure itself to supports. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and distinctive, elongated fruits that resemble cucumbers. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

Cassuvium walkeri is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates.
