Coix tubulosa

Coix tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular coix, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical inflorescence, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often with a slight sheen, and it produces small, greenish flowers.

Coix tubulosa is primarily grown for its starchy seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
