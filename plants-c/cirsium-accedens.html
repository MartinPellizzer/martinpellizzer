Cirsium accedens

Cirsium accedens, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features a rosette of spiny, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is characterized by its rough, prickly texture and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.

Cirsium accedens is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
