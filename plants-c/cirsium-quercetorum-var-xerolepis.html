Cirsium quercetorum var. xerolepis

Cirsium quercetorum var. xerolepis, commonly known as the dryland thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a deep taproot system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
