Chrysopteris multiserialis

Chrysopteris multiserialis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a tropical plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble dandelion-like blooms. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to Southeast Asia.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and landscapes.
