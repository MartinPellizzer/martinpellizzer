Cyperus cyprius

Cyperus cyprius, commonly known as the Cyprus sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies such as ponds and streams. Its slender, triangular stems are covered in fine, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus cyprius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
