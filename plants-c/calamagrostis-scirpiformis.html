Calamagrostis scirpiformis

Calamagrostis scirpiformis, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, ditches, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-based flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
