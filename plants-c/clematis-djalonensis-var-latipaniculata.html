Clematis djalonensis var. latipaniculata

Clematis djalonensis var. latipaniculata, commonly known as the broad-panicle clematis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often armed with hooked prickles. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and greenhouses.
