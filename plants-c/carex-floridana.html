Carex floridana

Carex floridana, commonly known as Florida sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are grass-like, usually narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer.

Carex floridana plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
