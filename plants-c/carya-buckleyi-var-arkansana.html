Carya buckleyi var. arkansana

Carya buckleyi var. arkansana, commonly known as the Arkansas hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, nut-like drupes that are important for wildlife.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
