Carex melanostoma

Carex melanostoma, commonly known as black-stem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

Carex melanostoma plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
