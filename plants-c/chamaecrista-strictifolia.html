Chamaecrista strictifolia

Chamaecrista strictifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets arranged in pairs, and it produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique seed pods, which are often used in horticulture.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and can be found in various ecological settings across its range.
