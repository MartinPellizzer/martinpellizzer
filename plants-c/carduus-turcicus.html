Carduus turcicus

Carduus turcicus, commonly known as Turkish thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

The plant has been used historically for its medicinal properties, though it is generally considered a weed in agricultural settings.
