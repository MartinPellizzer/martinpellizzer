Cyperus pseudothyrsiflorus

Cyperus pseudothyrsiflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its slender, grass-like stems are often found in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

The species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic wildlife.
