Crocus vernus subvar. albiflorus

Crocus vernus subvar. albiflorus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland areas or meadows. It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The subspecies is distinguished by its predominantly white flowers, setting it apart from the more common yellow-flowered C. vernus.
