Chenopodium triangulare var. polygonoides

Chenopodium triangulare var. polygonoides, commonly known as wild spinach or lambsquarters, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is an annual herb characterized by its alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional cuisines.
