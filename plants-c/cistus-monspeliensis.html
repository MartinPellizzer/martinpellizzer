Cistus monspeliensis

Cistus monspeliensis, commonly known as rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it drought-resistant and hardy. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant also has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
