Carex lagopina var. scaber

Carex lagopina var. scaber, commonly known as the rough-haired sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are rough to the touch. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows.

Carex lagopina var. scaber plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
