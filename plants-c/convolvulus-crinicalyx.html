Convolvulus crinicalyx

Convolvulus crinicalyx, commonly known as the silky bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a slight purple tinge. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
