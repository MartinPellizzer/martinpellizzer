Carex paniculata var. favratii

Carex paniculata var. favratii, commonly known as the favratii sedge, is a perennial plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves. The plant produces inflorescences of small, clustered flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, low-maintenance plants.
