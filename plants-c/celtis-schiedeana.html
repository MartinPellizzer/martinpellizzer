Celtis schiedeana

Celtis schiedeana, commonly known as the Schiedea's hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils near rivers or in forested areas.

Celtis schiedeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
