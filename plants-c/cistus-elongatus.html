Cistus elongatus

Cistus elongatus, commonly known as the longleaf rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Cistus elongatus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
