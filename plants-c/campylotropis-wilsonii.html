Campylotropis wilsonii

Campylotropis wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's spurred vetch, is a perennial legume native to Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod that dehisces to release small, kidney-shaped seeds.

It is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
