Crataegus × brevipes

Crataegus × brevipes, commonly known as the short-stalked hawthorn, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, small white or pink flowers, and red or orange berries that appear in the fall. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and is adapted to temperate climates. It has a rounded shrub form and can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

Crataegus × brevipes is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
