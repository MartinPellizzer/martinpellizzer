Chalybea mutisiana

Chalybea mutisiana, commonly known as the silverleaf chalybea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, pale green to white flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
