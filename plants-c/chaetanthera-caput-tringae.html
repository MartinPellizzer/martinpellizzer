Chaetanthera caput-tringae

Chaetanthera caput-tringae, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red sandal tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by woody, oblong fruits containing several seeds.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, including the presence of aromatic compounds and its historical use in traditional medicine.
