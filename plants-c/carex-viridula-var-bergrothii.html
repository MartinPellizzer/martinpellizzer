Carex viridula var. bergrothii

Carex viridula var. bergrothii, commonly known as Bergroth's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, greenish-brown flowers. Native to parts of Asia, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. It is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other moist habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects and wetland gardens.
