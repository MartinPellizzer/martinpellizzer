Croton oblongus

Croton oblongus, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored in shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Croton oblongus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
