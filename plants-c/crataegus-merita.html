Crataegus merita

Crataegus merita, commonly known as the Armenian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Crataegus merita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.
