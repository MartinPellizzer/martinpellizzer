Clematis phanerophlebia

Clematis phanerophlebia, commonly known as the broadleaf clematis, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces showy, usually blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Clematis phanerophlebia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
