Cotyledon rhombifolia

Cotyledon rhombifolia, commonly known as the diamond leaf or rhomboid cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are rhombus-shaped, often with a waxy coating to store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Cotyledon rhombifolia is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
