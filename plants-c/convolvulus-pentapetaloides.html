Convolvulus pentapetaloides

Convolvulus pentapetaloides, commonly known as the five-petaled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
