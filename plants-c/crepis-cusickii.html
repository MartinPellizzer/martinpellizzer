Crepis cusickii

Crepis cusickii, commonly known as the Cusick's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, producing small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Crepis cusickii is often found in sagebrush steppe and is adapted to arid environments.
