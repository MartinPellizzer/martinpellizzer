Carex oederi f. oedocarpa

Carex oederi f. oedocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedges, is a variant of the Carex oederi species.

It is characterized by its broad, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. This plant is typically found in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous growth habit and the ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.

The plant plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
