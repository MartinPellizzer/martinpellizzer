Cruciata ciliata var. nana

Cruciata ciliata var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf snowflake, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to rocky or alpine environments. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine displays.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add a touch of winter interest to their landscapes.
