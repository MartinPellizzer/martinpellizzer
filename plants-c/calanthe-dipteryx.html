Calanthe dipteryx

Calanthe dipteryx, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its unique floral structure and adaptability to humid, tropical climates.
