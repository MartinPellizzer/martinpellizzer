Caesalpinia epifanioi

Caesalpinia epifanioi, commonly known as the Epifanio's flame tree, is a small to medium-sized leguminous shrub or tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange feathery inflorescences that appear during the dry season. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
