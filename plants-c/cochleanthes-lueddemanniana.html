Cochleanthes lueddemanniana

Cochleanthes lueddemanniana, commonly known as the Lueddemann's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and display a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

Cochleanthes lueddemanniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
