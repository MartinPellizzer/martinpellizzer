Carex atrata

Carex atrata, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Carex atrata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
