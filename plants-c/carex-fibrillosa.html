Carex fibrillosa

Carex fibrillosa, commonly known as the fibrous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and covered with fine, thread-like fibers. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are often reddish or brownish in color.

Carex fibrillosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
