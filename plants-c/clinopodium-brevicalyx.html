Clinopodium brevicalyx

Clinopodium brevicalyx, commonly known as narrow-leaved mintweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive minty aroma, and its flowers are usually pale blue to purple in color. It produces small, four-lobed flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, and it is often found in association with other drought-tolerant plants.

Clinopodium brevicalyx is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
