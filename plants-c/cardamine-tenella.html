Cardamine tenella

Cardamine tenella, commonly known as narrowleaf watercress, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its small white or greenish flowers appear in early spring and are clustered in umbels.

The plant is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
