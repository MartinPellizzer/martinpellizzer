Centaurea × bourlieri

Centaurea × bourlieri, commonly known as the hybrid glaucous coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

The species is a hybrid between Centaurea cyanus and Centaurea solstitialis, which contributes to its unique botanical traits.
