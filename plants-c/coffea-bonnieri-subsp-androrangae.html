Coffea bonnieri subsp. androrangae

Coffea bonnieri subsp.

androrangae, commonly known as the Andorangae coffee, is a species native to the highlands of Ethiopia. It is a shrub or small tree that produces red, fleshy berries containing seeds used for coffee production.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and moderate rainfall. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are small, white, and fragrant, typically blooming during the dry season.
