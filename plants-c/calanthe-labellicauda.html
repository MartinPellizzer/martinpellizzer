Calanthe labellicauda

Calanthe labellicauda, commonly known as the golden calanthe, is a rare and elegant orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive golden hue at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
