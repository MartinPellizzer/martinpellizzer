Costus sanguineus

Costus sanguineus, commonly known as red ginger or bloodroot, is a tropical plant native to Africa and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features tall, leafy stalks with striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
