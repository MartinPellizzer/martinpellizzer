Cephalophyllum truncatum

Cephalophyllum truncatum, commonly known as the rounded-headed cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and distinctive, rounded, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry environments with minimal water, featuring thick, fleshy stems that store moisture. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cephalophyllum truncatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
