Cypripedium aureum

Cypripedium aureum, commonly known as the golden slipper orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a single, showy flower with a golden-yellow pouch-shaped lip and three greenish-yellow sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a short, tuberous rhizome. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its delicate, fragrant appearance.

Cypripedium aureum is also listed as a threatened species in some regions due to habitat loss and over-collection.
