Calceolaria hispida

Calceolaria hispida, commonly known as the hairy slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it the common name "hairy slipper orchid.".

Calceolaria hispida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique blooms.
