Chondroscaphe atrilinguis

Chondroscaphe atrilinguis, commonly known as the black-veined rock cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. Its small, bright yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
