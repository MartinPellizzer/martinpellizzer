Conyza trihecatactis

Conyza trihecatactis, commonly known as the three-rayed weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, composite flowers that typically bloom in clusters, giving it a dandelion-like appearance. The plant has finely divided, hairy leaves that are usually alternate and can vary in shape depending on the growth conditions. It is native to various regions in Africa and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Conyza trihecatactis is known for its ability to spread rapidly through wind-dispersed seeds, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
