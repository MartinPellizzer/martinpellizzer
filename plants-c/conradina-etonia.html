Conradina etonia

Conradina etonia, commonly known as Etonia coneflower, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, slightly fuzzy appearance.
