Calacinum peruvianum

Calacinum peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian calacina, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting adaptations such as thick, fleshy stems for water storage. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Calacinum peruvianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
