Cyanus dominii subsp. sokolensis

Cyanus dominii subsp.

sokolensis, commonly known as the Alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are often arranged in a single head, with ray florets that bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
