Carex vulgaris var. lipocarpa

Carex vulgaris var. lipocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy areas in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in riparian zones and along the edges of ponds.

This species plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
