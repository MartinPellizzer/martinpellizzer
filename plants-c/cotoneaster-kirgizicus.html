Cotoneaster kirgizicus

Cotoneaster kirgizicus, commonly known as the Kirghiz cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Cotoneaster kirgizicus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
