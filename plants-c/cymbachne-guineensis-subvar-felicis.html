Cymbachne guineensis subvar. felicis

Cymbachne guineensis subvar. felicis, commonly known as the African fountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
