Campylotropis pinetorum subsp. pinetorum

Campylotropis pinetorum subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the pine bladdernut, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring.

The plant produces small, bladder-like fruits that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
