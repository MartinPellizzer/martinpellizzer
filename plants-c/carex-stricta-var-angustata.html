Carex stricta var. angustata

Carex stricta var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial sedge native to wetland environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are often reddish-brown in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life contributes to its ecological significance.
