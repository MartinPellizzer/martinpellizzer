Chrysiphiala aurantiaca

Chrysiphiala aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange and yellow flowers, which resemble flames, and its broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light. It has a fibrous root system and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific care requirements, it is considered a challenging plant for novice gardeners.
