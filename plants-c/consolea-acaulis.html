Consolea acaulis

Consolea acaulis, commonly known as the naked stem cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that are often covered in spines. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
