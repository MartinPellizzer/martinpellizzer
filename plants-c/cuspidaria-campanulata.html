Cuspidaria campanulata

Cuspidaria campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower cuspidaria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, tubular flowers that typically range in color from white to blue. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.
