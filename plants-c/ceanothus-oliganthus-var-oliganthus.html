Ceanothus oliganthus var. oliganthus

Ceanothus oliganthus var. oliganthus, commonly known as the coastal ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing coastal dunes.
