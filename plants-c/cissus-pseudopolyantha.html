Cissus pseudopolyantha

Cissus pseudopolyantha, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and waxy, heart-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or white underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its rapid growth makes it popular as a houseplant and for covering trellises or walls.

Cissus pseudopolyantha is also known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal care.
