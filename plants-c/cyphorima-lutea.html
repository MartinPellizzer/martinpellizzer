Cyphorima lutea

Cyphorima lutea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its flowers are often short-lived but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators.

Cyphorima lutea is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its cultural significance in traditional practices.
