Carex asperifructus

Carex asperifructus, commonly known as the rough-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to wet or moist habitats, it thrives in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has rough, textured fruits that are adapted for dispersal by water or animals.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
