Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. mohavensis

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. mohavensis, commonly known as Mohave goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and silver-green foliage, which help it thrive in arid environments. The plant has a compact growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
