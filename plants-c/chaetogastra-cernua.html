Chaetogastra cernua

Chaetogastra cernua, commonly known as the drooping hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Chaetogastra cernua thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
