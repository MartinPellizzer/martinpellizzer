Carex schoenoides

Carex schoenoides, commonly known as silverhair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically silver-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex schoenoides is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse ecosystems.
