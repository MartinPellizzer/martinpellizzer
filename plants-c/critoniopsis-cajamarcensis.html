Critoniopsis cajamarcensis

Critoniopsis cajamarcensis, commonly known as the Peruvian goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats at high altitudes. It has small, narrow leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
