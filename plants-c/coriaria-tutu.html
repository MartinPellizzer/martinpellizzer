Coriaria tutu

Coriaria tutu, commonly known as the New Zealand coriander, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Coriariaceae and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
