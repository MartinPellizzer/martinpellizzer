Carex capillaris subsp. porsildiana

Carex capillaris subsp.

porsildiana, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in tundra or mountainous habitats. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies in its native environment.
