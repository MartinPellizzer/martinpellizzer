Cyclopogon chloroleucus var. australis

Cyclopogon chloroleucus var. australis, commonly known as the southern bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
