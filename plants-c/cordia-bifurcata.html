Cordia bifurcata

Cordia bifurcata, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a species of flowering plant in the family Meliaceae.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. This plant is typically found in coastal areas, where it thrives in brackish or saline water. Its roots form a complex network that helps stabilize shorelines and filter water.

Cordia bifurcata plays an important ecological role in mangrove ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
