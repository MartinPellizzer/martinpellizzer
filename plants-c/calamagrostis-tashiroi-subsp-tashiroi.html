Calamagrostis tashiroi subsp. tashiroi

Calamagrostis tashiroi subsp.

tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's reed, is a perennial grass native to Japan. It typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

The plant features tall, slender stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny, feathery flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
