Carex × prolixa

Carex × prolixa, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical flower clusters. Native to wetlands and marshy areas, it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
