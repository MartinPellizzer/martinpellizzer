Chamaerops arborea

Chamaerops arborea, commonly known as the slender fan palm, is a species of palm native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves that form a fan shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences. Its fruits are round, green berries that turn red when mature.

Chamaerops arborea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in coastal and rocky environments.
