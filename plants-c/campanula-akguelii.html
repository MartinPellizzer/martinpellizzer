Campanula akguelii

Campanula akguelii, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Campanula akguelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
