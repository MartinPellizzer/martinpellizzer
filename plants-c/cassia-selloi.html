Cassia selloi

Cassia selloi, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia selloi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.
