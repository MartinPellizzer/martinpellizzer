Caladenia xantha

Caladenia xantha, commonly known as the golden spider-orchid, is a striking orchid native to south-eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and its distinctive, spider-like flower with a vivid yellow hue. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open habitats, often in heathlands or open forests. Caladenia xantha is a terrestrial orchid that produces a single, showy flower per stem, which blooms from late winter to early spring.
