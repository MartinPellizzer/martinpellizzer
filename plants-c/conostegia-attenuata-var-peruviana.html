Conostegia attenuata var. peruviana

Conostegia attenuata var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian silverleaf, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
