Carex reuteriana subsp. mauritanica

Carex reuteriana subsp.

mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, riverbanks, and damp meadows. It produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
