Calamagrostis glaucescens

Calamagrostis glaucescens, commonly known as blue joint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences with small, spikelet flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
