Chrysocoma radiata

Chrysocoma radiata, commonly known as the yellow-flowered candelabra, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its branching structure and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

Chrysocoma radiata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant color and unique form.
