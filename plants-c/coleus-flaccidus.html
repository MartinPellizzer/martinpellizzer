Coleus flaccidus

Coleus flaccidus, commonly known as the hanging coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and trailing habit make it popular for hanging baskets and containers.
