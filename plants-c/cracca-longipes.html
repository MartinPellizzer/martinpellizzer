Cracca longipes

Cracca longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed cracca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
