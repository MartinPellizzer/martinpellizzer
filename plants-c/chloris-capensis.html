Chloris capensis

Chloris capensis, commonly known as African lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
