Cocculus miersiana

Cocculus miersiana, commonly known as the Mexican devil's bit, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a hairy, spindle-shaped pod that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
