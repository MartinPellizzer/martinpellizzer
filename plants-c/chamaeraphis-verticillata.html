Chamaeraphis verticillata

Chamaeraphis verticillata, commonly known as the clustered glassworts, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically at the ends of the stems. It is native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and is often found in saline or sandy soils.

Chamaeraphis verticillata is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
