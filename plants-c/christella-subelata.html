Christella subelata

Christella subelata, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or forested habitats, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep crimson, often with a distinctive red throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact, bushy appearance.

Christella subelata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
