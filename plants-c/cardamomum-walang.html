Cardamomum walang

Cardamomum walang, commonly known as the Philippine cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that eventually develop into reddish-brown pods containing aromatic seeds. These seeds are valued for their intense flavor and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
