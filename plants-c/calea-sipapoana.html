Calea sipapoana

Calea sipapoana, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Calea sipapoana is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
