Callicarpa farinosa

Callicarpa farinosa, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife.
