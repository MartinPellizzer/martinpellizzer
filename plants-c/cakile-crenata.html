Cakile crenata

Cakile crenata, commonly known as sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant has slender, succulent stems and small, lobed leaves that are adapted to withstand salty air and strong winds. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, typical of the family.

Cakile crenata is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils along shorelines.
