Crataegus insidiosa

Crataegus insidiosa, commonly known as the insidious hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, where it typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant produces showy white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.

Crataegus insidiosa is also valued for its ecological role, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
