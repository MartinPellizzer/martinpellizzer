Convolvulus chondrilloides var. burzianus

Convolvulus chondrilloides var. burzianus, commonly known as the Burzian sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has hairy, succulent stems and leaves adapted to conserve water in dry environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
