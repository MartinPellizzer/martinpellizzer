Clerodendrum subpandurifolium

Clerodendrum subpandurifolium, commonly known as the Chinese flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually green with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits.

Clerodendrum subpandurifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
