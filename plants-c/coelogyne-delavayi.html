Coelogyne delavayi

Coelogyne delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters on long, arching inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The orchid prefers high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
