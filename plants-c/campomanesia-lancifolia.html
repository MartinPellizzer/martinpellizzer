Campomanesia lancifolia

Campomanesia lancifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits resembling small cherries. The fruits are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making them popular in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, warm climate for optimal growth.
