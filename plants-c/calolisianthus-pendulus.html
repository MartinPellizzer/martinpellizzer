Calolisianthus pendulus

Calolisianthus pendulus, commonly known as the hanging lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that dangle from long, slender stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are typically pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
