Coluteastrum pubescens

Coluteastrum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy colutea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Coluteastrum pubescens is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and coastal areas.
