Clematis montana var. tongluensis

Clematis montana var. tongluensis, commonly known as the Tonglu mountain clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a distinct, fragrant aroma. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb trellises or fences with its strong, twisting stems.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
