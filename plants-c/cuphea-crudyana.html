Cuphea crudyana

Cuphea crudyana, commonly known as the red flame cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea crudyana is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to add visual interest throughout the growing season.
