Chaetobromus involucratus

Chaetobromus involucratus, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species with showy inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is adapted to hot, dry environments.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in arid landscapes.
