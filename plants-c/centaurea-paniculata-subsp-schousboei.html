Centaurea paniculata subsp. schousboei

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

schousboei, commonly known as the Schousboe's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features erect, branching stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces showy, composite flower heads with purple-blue ray florets and yellow disk florets. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.
