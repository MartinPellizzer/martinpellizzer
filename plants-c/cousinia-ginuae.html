Cousinia ginuae

Cousinia ginuae, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and adjacent areas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flower heads that resemble bells. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid climates. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia ginuae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
