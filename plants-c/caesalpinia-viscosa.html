Caesalpinia viscosa

Caesalpinia viscosa, commonly known as the hairy glorybower, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hairy stipules and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Caesalpinia viscosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
