Cistus vaginatus var. hirsutissimus

Cistus vaginatus var. hirsutissimus, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
