Cardia obtusifolia

Cardia obtusifolia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal rosettes of broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
