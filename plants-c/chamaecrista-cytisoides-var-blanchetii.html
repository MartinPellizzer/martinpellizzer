Chamaecrista cytisoides var. blanchetii

Chamaecrista cytisoides var. blanchetii, commonly known as the narrowleaf crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves are typical of the species, and it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and its ecological importance for pollinators.
