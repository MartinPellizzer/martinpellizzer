Chaenorhinum minus subsp. litorale

Chaenorhinum minus subsp.

litorale, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help reflect sunlight and reduce water loss in its saline environment.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize dunes and prevent erosion in coastal ecosystems.
