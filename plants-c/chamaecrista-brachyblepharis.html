Chamaecrista brachyblepharis

Chamaecrista brachyblepharis, commonly known as the narrow-eyed cassia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces bright yellow flowers in clusters. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, typical of the legume family.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
