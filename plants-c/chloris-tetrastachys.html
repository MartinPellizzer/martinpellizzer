Chloris tetrastachys

Chloris tetrastachys, commonly known as the four-stamen grass, is a species of grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces compact inflorescences with four stamens, giving it its scientific name. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Chloris tetrastachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
