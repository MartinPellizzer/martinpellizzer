Cerasus capricida

Cerasus capricida, commonly known as the goat cherry, is a species of shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and its flowers are pink to white in color.

Cerasus capricida is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its historical use in traditional medicine.
