Chalazocarpus hirsuta

Chalazocarpus hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy chalazocarpus, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial shrub native to specific regions with a preference for well-drained soils. The plant exhibits a hairy texture on its stems and leaves, which is a key identifying trait. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a unique reproductive structure.

The fruit is a specialized type known as a chalaza, which plays a role in seed dispersal.
