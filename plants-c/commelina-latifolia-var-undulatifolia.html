Commelina latifolia var. undulatifolia

Commelina latifolia var. undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaf Commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a wavy margin, which is a distinguishing characteristic of the varietal form. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers clustered in umbels, typical of the Commelina genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that aids in its spread and survival in wet habitats.
