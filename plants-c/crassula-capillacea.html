Crassula capillacea

Crassula capillacea, commonly known as the "Laceleaf Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and requiring minimal watering.

Crassula capillacea is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
