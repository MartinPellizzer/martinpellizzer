Carex latifolia

Carex latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three ranks, and it produces spikes of small, greenish flowers. Carex latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
