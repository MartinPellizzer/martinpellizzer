Coix lingulata

Coix lingulata, commonly known as Job's tears, is a member of the Poaceae family, native to East Asia.

It is a grass-like plant that produces small, bead-like seeds encased in a tough, fibrous husk. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in various cultures.

The botanical features of Coix lingulata make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable.
