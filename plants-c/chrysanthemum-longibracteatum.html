Chrysanthemum longibracteatum

Chrysanthemum longibracteatum, commonly known as the longibracteatum chrysanthemum, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange. Its foliage is typically gray-green with hairy surfaces, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This variety is relatively hardy and suitable for garden use in temperate climates.
