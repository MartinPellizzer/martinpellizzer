Campanula macrorhiza var. trianthemos

Campanula macrorhiza var. trianthemos, commonly known as the large-rooted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a robust root system, which contributes to its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are upright and branched.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
