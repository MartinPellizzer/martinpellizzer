Convolvulus fulcratus

Convolvulus fulcratus, commonly known as the fulcrate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its trailing or climbing habit. The plant produces funnel-shaped, trumpet-like flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and hairy, while the stems are hairy and often twining.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
