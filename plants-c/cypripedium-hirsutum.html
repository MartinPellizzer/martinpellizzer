Cypripedium hirsutum

Cypripedium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy lady's slipper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, showy, slipper-shaped flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a long, underground rhizome that supports its growth. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and the flowers range in color from white to pink or purple.

Cypripedium hirsutum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest in botanical research due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
