Combretum longispicatum

Combretum longispicatum, commonly known as the long-spined combretum, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions.
