Caltha ucrainica

Caltha ucrainica, commonly known as the Ukrainian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes many showy flowering plants. The plant features striking yellow flowers with prominent, reflexed sepals that resemble petals. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a hairy surface that aids in moisture retention.

Caltha ucrainica typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows.
