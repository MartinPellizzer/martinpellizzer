Cupressus lawsoniana var. erecta

Cupressus lawsoniana var. erecta, commonly known as the Lawson's cypress, is a cultivar of the western red cedar.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant has scale-like leaves that are closely packed, giving it a smooth, textured appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by woody cones.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
