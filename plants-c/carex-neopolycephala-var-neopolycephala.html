Carex neopolycephala var. neopolycephala

Carex neopolycephala var. neopolycephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological value.
