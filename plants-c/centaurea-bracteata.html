Centaurea bracteata

Centaurea bracteata, commonly known as the bracted cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its striking blue to purple flowers are surrounded by bracts, which give it its common name.

Centaurea bracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and hardy nature.
