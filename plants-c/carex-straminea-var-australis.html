Carex straminea var. australis

Carex straminea var. australis, commonly known as the Australian sedge, is a perennial plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
