Cryptotaenia canadensis subsp. japonica

Cryptotaenia canadensis subsp.

japonica, commonly known as Japanese cowparsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley.

The plant features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
