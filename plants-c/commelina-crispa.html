Commelina crispa

Commelina crispa, commonly known as the crinkled spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a crinkled texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina crispa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
