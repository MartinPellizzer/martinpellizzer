Cucurbita pepo var. brasilica

Cucurbita pepo var. brasilica, commonly known as the Brazilian pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces large, oblong fruits with a thick, smooth rind and a sweet, nutty flavor. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are both male and female. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
