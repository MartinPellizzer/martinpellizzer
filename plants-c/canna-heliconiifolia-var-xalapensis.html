Canna heliconiifolia var. xalapensis

Canna heliconiifolia var. xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican cannonball plant, is a tropical perennial known for its large, showy inflorescences.

It features striking, brightly colored bracts that can range from red to orange, often mistaken for flowers. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
