Capsicum bauhini

Capsicum bauhini, commonly known as the Brazilian chili or yellow bell pepper, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces yellow or orange fruits that are typically harvested when they are still firm and unripe. Its foliage consists of broad, lobed leaves that are green and often covered in fine hairs.

Capsicum bauhini is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, making it popular in both gardens and culinary use.
