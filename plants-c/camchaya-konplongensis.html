Camchaya konplongensis

Camchaya konplongensis, commonly known as the rare mountain orchid, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and delicate, orchid-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, adapted to maximize light absorption in its dim habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Camchaya konplongensis is considered a vulnerable species.
