Crotalaria agatiflora subsp. imperialis

Crotalaria agatiflora subsp.

imperialis, commonly known as the imperial rattlepod, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in racemes, often with a striking contrast against its green foliage.

The plant produces elongated, ridged pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Crotalaria genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
