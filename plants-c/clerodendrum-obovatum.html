Clerodendrum obovatum

Clerodendrum obovatum, commonly known as the broadleaf chaste tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate to obovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a globose capsule containing numerous small, dark seeds.

Clerodendrum obovatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
