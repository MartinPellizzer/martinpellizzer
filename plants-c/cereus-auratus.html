Cereus auratus

Cereus auratus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and spiny, greenish-gray leaves. The plant produces striking white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. These flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a single night.

Cereus auratus is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for succulent gardens.
