Carex bessarabica

Carex bessarabica, commonly known as Bessarabian sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flower heads known as spikes. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet conditions.
