Carduus carthamoides

Carduus carthamoides, commonly known as the common sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to various regions around the world. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is often considered a weed but has some historical uses in traditional medicine.
