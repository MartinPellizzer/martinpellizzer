Centaurea subsericans

Centaurea subsericans, commonly known as the silky centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white to grayish seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton or down. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Centaurea subsericans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
