Cousinia sefidiana

Cousinia sefidiana, commonly known as the Persian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia sefidiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic features.
