Campanula schelkownikowii

Campanula schelkownikowii, commonly known as the Schelkunov's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
