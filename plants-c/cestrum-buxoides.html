Cestrum buxoides

Cestrum buxoides, commonly known as the dwarf yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and somewhat succulent.

Cestrum buxoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
