Chamaecrista multiseta

Chamaecrista multiseta, commonly known as the hairy palmetto or sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with compound leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dunes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
