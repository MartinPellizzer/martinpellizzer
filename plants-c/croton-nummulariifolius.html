Croton nummulariifolius

Croton nummulariifolius, commonly known as the silver croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
