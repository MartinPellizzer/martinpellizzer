Chresta heteropappa

Chresta heteropappa, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its seed pods are spherical and contain numerous small, hard seeds.

Chresta heteropappa is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
