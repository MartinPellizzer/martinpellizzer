Carex duvaliana var. alterniflora

Carex duvaliana var. alterniflora, commonly known as the alternate-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers often occurring on separate spikes.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
