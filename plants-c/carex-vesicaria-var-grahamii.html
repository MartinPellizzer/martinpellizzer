Carex vesicaria var. grahamii

Carex vesicaria var. grahamii, commonly known as Graham's bladderleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, pointed leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
