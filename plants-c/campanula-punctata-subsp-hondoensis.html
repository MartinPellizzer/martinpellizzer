Campanula punctata subsp. hondoensis

Campanula punctata subsp.

hondoensis, commonly known as the Hondo bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and streamside habitats. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
