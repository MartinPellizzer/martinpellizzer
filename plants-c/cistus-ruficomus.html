Cistus ruficomus

Cistus ruficomus, commonly known as the red-stemmed rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces showy pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in a fine, woolly texture.

Cistus ruficomus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
