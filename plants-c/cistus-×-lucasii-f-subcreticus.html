Cistus × lucasii f. subcreticus

Cistus × lucasii f. subcreticus, commonly known as the subcretic rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
