Cistus symphytifolius var. canus

Cistus symphytifolius var. canus, commonly known as the white-flowered rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silvery-gray, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

This variety is often found in coastal and mountainous areas where it contributes to the local flora.
