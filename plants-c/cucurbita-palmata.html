Cucurbita palmata

Cucurbita palmata, commonly known as the palmate gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and vibrant, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green, though some varieties can be left to ripen to a deep orange or red. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Cucurbita palmata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and unique fruit shape.
