Caltha palustris var. himalaica

Caltha palustris var. himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan marsh marigold, is a variant of the marsh marigold native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy habitats and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist soil.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
