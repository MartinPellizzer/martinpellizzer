Colchicum versicolor

Colchicum versicolor, commonly known as the variable colchicum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and meadows, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that emerge in early spring.

Colchicum versicolor is a perennial species that produces its flowers in late summer, followed by distinctive, helmet-shaped seed capsules.
