Campsis adrepens

Campsis adrepens, commonly known as the red-barked catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, peeling red bark that becomes more prominent with age. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically orange to red in color, blooming in the summer. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip.

The tree is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
