Ceanothus megacarpus subsp. insularis

Ceanothus megacarpus subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the Santa Catalina Island ceanothus, is a shrub native to the Channel Islands of California. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat. It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
