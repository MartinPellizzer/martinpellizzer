Cyperus gigantobulbes

Cyperus gigantobulbes, commonly known as the giant bulbous sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and large, bulbous base that stores water and nutrients. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
