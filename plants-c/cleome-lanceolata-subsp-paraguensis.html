Cleome lanceolata subsp. paraguensis

Cleome lanceolata subsp.

paraguensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to South America. It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
