Crocus brickellii

Crocus brickellii, commonly known as the Florida crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Crocus brickellii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in native plant gardens.
