Capparis kirkii

Capparis kirkii, commonly known as the East African caper, is a flowering plant native to regions of East Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and savannah habitats. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers with a strong, pungent odor, which attracts pollinators such as bees and wasps. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant has a woody, thorny stem with compound leaves.

Capparis kirkii is valued for its ornamental potential and has traditional uses in some local communities.
