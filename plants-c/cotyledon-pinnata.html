Cotyledon pinnata

Cotyledon pinnata, commonly known as the "Laceleaf" or "Cotyledon with pinnate leaves," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves arranged in a pinnate pattern, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant is characterized by its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves, making it drought-tolerant. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, and its leaves often display striking patterns of white and green.

This plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.
