Calamus lanatus

Calamus lanatus, commonly known as the hairy calamus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes that are often covered in a dense, hairy coating. The plant produces tall, narrow leaves that are typically green and sometimes have a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in inflorescences at the base of the plant.

Calamus lanatus is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in wetland or swampy habitats.
