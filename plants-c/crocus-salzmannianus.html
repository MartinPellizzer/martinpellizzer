Crocus salzmannianus

Crocus salzmannianus, commonly known as the Salzmann's crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.

Crocus salzmannianus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
