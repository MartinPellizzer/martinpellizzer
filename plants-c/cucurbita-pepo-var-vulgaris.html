Cucurbita pepo var. vulgaris

Cucurbita pepo var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a herbaceous, annual vine that produces large, lobed leaves and distinctive, often orange or yellow, spherical or oblong fruits. The plant is characterized by its monoecious nature, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. The fruit develops from the female flowers and can vary in size and shape depending on the variety.

It is widely cultivated for its edible seeds, flesh, and uses in both culinary and medicinal applications.
