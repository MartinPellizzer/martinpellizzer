Cestrum morretense

Cestrum morretense, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but should be consumed in moderation. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.
