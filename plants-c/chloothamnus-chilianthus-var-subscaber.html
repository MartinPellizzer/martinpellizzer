Chloothamnus chilianthus var. subscaber

Chloothamnus chilianthus var. subscaber, commonly known as the rough chilianthus, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features wiry, branching stems that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered with a fine layer of pubescence.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous habitats.
