Cytisus boissieri

Cytisus boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pea-like flowers and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, elliptical leaflets.

Cytisus boissieri is often found in rocky or arid habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
