Cineraria ramosa

Cineraria ramosa, commonly known as the branched cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the stems are often branched, giving the plant its common name.

Cineraria ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
