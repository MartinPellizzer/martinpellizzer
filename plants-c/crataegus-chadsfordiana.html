Crataegus chadsfordiana

Crataegus chadsfordiana, commonly known as the Chad's Ford hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
