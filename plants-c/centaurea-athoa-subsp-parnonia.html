Centaurea athoa subsp. parnonia

Centaurea athoa subsp.

parnonia, commonly known as the Parnon thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads.

The plant features a robust central stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical head, and it produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
