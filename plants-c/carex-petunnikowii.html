Carex petunnikowii

Carex petunnikowii, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of lakes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects and as a decorative plant in water gardens.
