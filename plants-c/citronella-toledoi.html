Citronella toledoi

Citronella toledoi, commonly known as the Mexican citronella, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the genus Cymbopogon in the grass family Poaceae. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a strong lemony scent. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Citronella toledoi is valued for its essential oils, which are used in natural insect repellents.
