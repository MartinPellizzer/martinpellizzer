Croton oreades var. occidentalis

Croton oreades var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western croton, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features striking, palmate leaves with deep lobes, often in shades of green, red, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscapic gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in arid landscapes.
