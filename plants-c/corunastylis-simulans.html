Corunastylis simulans

Corunastylis simulans, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that resemble those of other orchid species. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as under forest canopies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
