Crypsinus bellivenosus

Crypsinus bellivenosus, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance, featuring small, pale green leaves and a single, showy white or pink flower. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on the branches of trees. Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes and is considered endangered.

Its botanical significance lies in its rare floral structure and ecological role in its native ecosystem.
