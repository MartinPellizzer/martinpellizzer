Cattleya × blesensis

Cattleya × blesensis, commonly known as the Blesens hybrid, is a hybrid orchid resulting from the cross between Cattleya bicolor and Cattleya nobilis.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture from the air.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in horticulture for its beauty and resilience.
