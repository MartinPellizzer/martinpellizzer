Clematis pubipes

Clematis pubipes, commonly known as the pubescent clematis, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and showy, usually white or pale blue flowers. The plant features palmately lobed leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically clustered in cymes and bloom in the spring or early summer.

Clematis pubipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
