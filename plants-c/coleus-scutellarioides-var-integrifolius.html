Coleus scutellarioides var. integrifolius

Coleus scutellarioides var. integrifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved coleus, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display shades of red, purple, and green, often with striking variegation. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically entire, giving it its specific epithet "integrifolius.". It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, usually in shades of blue or purple, though they are less showy than the foliage.

This plant thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental in gardens and indoor settings.
