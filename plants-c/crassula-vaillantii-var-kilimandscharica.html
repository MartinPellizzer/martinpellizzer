Crassula vaillantii var. kilimandscharica

Crassula vaillantii var. kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro crassula, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscape designs.
