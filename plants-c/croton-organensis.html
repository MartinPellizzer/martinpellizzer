Croton organensis

Croton organensis, commonly known as the Sierra Leone croton, is a flowering plant native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that vary in shape and texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
