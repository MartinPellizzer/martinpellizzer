Cyanus variegatus

Cyanus variegatus, commonly known as the variegated blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in open, sunny habitats. The plant produces striking blue to violet composite flowers, which are arranged in a flat-topped cluster called a capitulum. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white.

Cyanus variegatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
