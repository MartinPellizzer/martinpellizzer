Cerasus dolichadenia

Cerasus dolichadenia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family, native to Japan. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, tart cherry, often used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
