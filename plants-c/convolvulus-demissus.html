Convolvulus demissus

Convolvulus demissus, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, pale blue or violet flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The flowers bloom in spring and are followed by capsule fruits that contain several seeds.
