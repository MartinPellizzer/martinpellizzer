Centipeda

Centipeda, commonly known as hedge bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. Centipeda is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

It is known for its resilience and ability to spread rapidly through both seeds and rhizomes.
