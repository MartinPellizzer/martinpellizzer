Carex gaudichaudiana var. appendiculata

Carex gaudichaudiana var. appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps.

Botanically, it is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, waterlogged soils.
