Calanthe rhodochila

Calanthe rhodochila, commonly known as the pink-stemmed calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers with pink and white petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and the inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers.

Calanthe rhodochila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
