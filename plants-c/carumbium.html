Carumbium

Carumbium, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with dense, spiny branches. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are highly nutritious and often consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Carumbium thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
