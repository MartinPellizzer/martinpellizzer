Centaurea moschata

Centaurea moschata, commonly known as sweet centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, spiky seed heads that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a slightly aromatic scent.

Centaurea moschata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is considered a valuable plant in traditional herbal medicine.
