Cleome cuneifolia

Cleome cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf cleome, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, typically blooming from summer to fall. Cleome cuneifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long flowering period.
