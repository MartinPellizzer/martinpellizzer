Celastrus heterophyllus

Celastrus heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable leaf bindweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, which can range from heart-shaped to oval, depending on the environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are capsules that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Celastrus heterophyllus is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
