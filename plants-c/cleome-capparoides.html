Cleome capparoides

Cleome capparoides, commonly known as the spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow up to 3 feet tall, adorned with clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that resemble spiders. The plant's leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Cleome capparoides is known for its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
