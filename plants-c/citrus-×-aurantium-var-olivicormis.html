Citrus × aurantium var. olivicormis

Citrus × aurantium var. olivicormis, commonly known as the bitter orange, is a citrus species characterized by its thick, aromatic peel and segmented, juicy white pulp.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is generally bitter and not typically consumed raw, though it is valued for its essential oils and zest.

The variety is also used in the production of marmalade and as a source of citric acid.
