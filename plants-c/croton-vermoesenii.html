Croton vermoesenii

Croton vermoesenii, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and diverse leaf structures. The plant typically has alternate, simple, or palmate leaves that vary in shape and size. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its unique foliage.
