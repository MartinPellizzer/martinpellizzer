Coleus thalassoscopicus

Coleus thalassoscopicus, commonly known as the sea coleus, is a tropical plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often features shades of red, purple, and green. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to enhance coastal landscapes.
