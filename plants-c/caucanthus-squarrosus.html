Caucanthus squarrosus

Caucanthus squarrosus, commonly known as the squarrosus caucanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are often lobed or notched, and the stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

Caucanthus squarrosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
