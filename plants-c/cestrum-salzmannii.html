Cestrum salzmannii

Cestrum salzmannii, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant is renowned for its large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pink. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The plant is adapted to warm climates and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
