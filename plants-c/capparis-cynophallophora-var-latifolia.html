Capparis cynophallophora var. latifolia

Capparis cynophallophora var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and pinnately compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, round, green berries. These berries are typically harvested when fully mature and can be used in culinary applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in rocky or coastal environments.
