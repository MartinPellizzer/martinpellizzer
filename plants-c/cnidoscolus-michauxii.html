Cnidoscolus michauxii

Cnidoscolus michauxii, commonly known as the Mexican stinging leaf or stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with stinging hairs that can cause irritation upon contact. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces berries that are edible when ripe.
