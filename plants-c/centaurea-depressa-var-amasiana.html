Centaurea depressa var. amasiana

Centaurea depressa var. amasiana, commonly known as the Amasian blueweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
