Carum capense

Carum capense, commonly known as Cape garlic or African garlic, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, herbaceous foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Carum capense thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
