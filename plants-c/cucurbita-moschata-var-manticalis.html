Cucurbita moschata var. manticalis

Cucurbita moschata var. manticalis, commonly known as the Mexican pumpkin, is a variant of the winter squash native to Central America.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruits with a smooth, pale yellow to orange skin. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its sweet, nutty flavor and is often used in traditional cuisines.
