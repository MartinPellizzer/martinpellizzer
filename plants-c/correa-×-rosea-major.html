Correa × rosea-major

Correa × rosea-major, commonly known as the pink correa, is a hybrid plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with aromatic leaves that have a distinctive citrus scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to attract native pollinators such as bees and birds.
