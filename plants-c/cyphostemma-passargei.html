Cyphostemma passargei

Cyphostemma passargei, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its thin, twining stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, horn-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cyphostemma passargei is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
