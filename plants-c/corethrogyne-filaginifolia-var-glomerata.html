Corethrogyne filaginifolia var. glomerata

Corethrogyne filaginifolia var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered corethrogyne, is a rare and endemic plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, clustered growth habit and distinctive foliage with elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pale green or white, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
