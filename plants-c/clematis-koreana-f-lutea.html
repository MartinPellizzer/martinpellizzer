Clematis koreana f. lutea

Clematis koreana f. lutea, commonly known as the Korean yellow clematis, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features climbing stems with trifoliate leaves, and its flowers have five sepals that are typically yellow or cream in color. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
