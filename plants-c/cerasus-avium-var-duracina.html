Cerasus avium var. duracina

Cerasus avium var. duracina, commonly known as the cherry plum, is a variant of the European cherry plum species.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant produces small, sweet-tart fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it has fragrant white flowers in spring.

The botanical characteristics of this variety make it suitable for ornamental gardening and small-scale fruit cultivation.
