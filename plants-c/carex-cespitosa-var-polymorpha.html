Carex cespitosa var. polymorpha

Carex cespitosa var. polymorpha, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland conservation efforts.
