Campomanesia goetheana

Campomanesia goetheana, commonly known as the Goethe's wax apple, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellowish-orange fruits that are similar in appearance to apples but have a waxy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
