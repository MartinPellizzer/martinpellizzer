Cirsium chynysicum

Cirsium chynysicum, commonly known as the Siberian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its life cycle includes a period of dormancy during winter, and it reproduces through both seeds and vegetative propagation.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
