Cirsium americanum var. callilepis

Cirsium americanum var. callilepis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry to moist soils in open woodlands and prairies. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used by Native American tribes for various health purposes.
