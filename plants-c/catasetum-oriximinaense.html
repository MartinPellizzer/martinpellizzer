Catasetum oriximinaense

Catasetum oriximinaense, commonly known as the Oriximina orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces specialized structures called pollinia, which are involved in its unique pollination mechanism. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Catasetum oriximinaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
