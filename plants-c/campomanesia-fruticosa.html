Campomanesia fruticosa

Campomanesia fruticosa, commonly known as pitanga or Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically orange to red in color when ripe, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.
