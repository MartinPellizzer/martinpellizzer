Crepis longipes

Crepis longipes, commonly known as the long-footed hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are elongated and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Crepis longipes is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
