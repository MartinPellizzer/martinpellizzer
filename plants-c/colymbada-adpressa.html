Colymbada adpressa

Colymbada adpressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water shield, is a small aquatic plant found in shallow, freshwater habitats.

It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged, with the upper surface often bearing a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, which are often partially submerged. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the sediment in its wetland environment.

Colymbada adpressa plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water filtration.
