Carex elyniformis

Carex elyniformis, commonly known as the Ely's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, greenish-brown flowers.

Carex elyniformis is often found in open, disturbed sites and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
