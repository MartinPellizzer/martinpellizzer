Carex subebracteata

Carex subebracteata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. This species typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a distinct midrib, while its flowers are arranged in spikelets.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
