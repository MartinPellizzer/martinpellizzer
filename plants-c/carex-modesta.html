Carex modesta

Carex modesta, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Carex modesta has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

It is valued in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
