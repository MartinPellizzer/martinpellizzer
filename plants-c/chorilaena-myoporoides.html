Chorilaena myoporoides

Chorilaena myoporoides, commonly known as the silverleaf myopor, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in coastal areas. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Chorilaena myoporoides is drought-tolerant and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
