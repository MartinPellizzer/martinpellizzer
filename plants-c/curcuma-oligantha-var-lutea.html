Curcuma oligantha var. lutea

Curcuma oligantha var. lutea, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange rhizomes. The plant produces tall, leafy stems with small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their curcumin content.

The botanical features of this variety make it a valuable species in both horticulture and herbal applications.
