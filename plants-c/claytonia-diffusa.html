Claytonia diffusa

Claytonia diffusa, commonly known as the pink rockcress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features basal rosettes of ovate, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range from pink to white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
