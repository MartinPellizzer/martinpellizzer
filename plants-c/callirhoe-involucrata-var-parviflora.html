Callirhoe involucrata var. parviflora

Callirhoe involucrata var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered skyrocket, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, pink to red tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures that encase the flower cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
