Crataegus krima

Crataegus krima, commonly known as the Krima hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its small, red berries are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

The Krima hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
