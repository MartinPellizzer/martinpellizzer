Cephalaria velutina

Cephalaria velutina, commonly known as the velvet cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy, silver-green foliage that gives it its common name, and it produces yellow-orange flowers in late summer. Cephalaria velutina thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
