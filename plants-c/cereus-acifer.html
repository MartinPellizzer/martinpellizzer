Cereus acifer

Cereus acifer, commonly known as the apple cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a spiny cactus that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces striking, night-blooming flowers that are usually white or pale yellow. Its stems are cylindrical and covered in sharp spines, which help protect it from herbivores.

The fruit, known as the "apple cactus fruit," is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.
