Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. nanus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

nanus, commonly known as the dwarf yellow rabbitbrush, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States. It features silver-gray foliage with small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established.

Its botanical characteristics include narrow, linear leaves and a mat-forming structure that helps it survive in arid environments.
