Campanula × gisleri

Campanula × gisleri, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12–24 inches and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula × gisleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
