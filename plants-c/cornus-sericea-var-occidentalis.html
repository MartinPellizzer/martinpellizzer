Cornus sericea var. occidentalis

Cornus sericea var. occidentalis, commonly known as western silky dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

This species is often found in moist habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
