Crataegus × rosacea

Crataegus × rosacea, commonly known as the rose thornapple or rose hawthorn, is a hybrid plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It is native to the eastern United States and thrives in woodland areas with well-drained soil.

Crataegus × rosacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
