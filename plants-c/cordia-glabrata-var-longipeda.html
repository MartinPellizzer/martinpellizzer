Cordia glabrata var. longipeda

Cordia glabrata var. longipeda, commonly known as the long-fruited gloryflower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
