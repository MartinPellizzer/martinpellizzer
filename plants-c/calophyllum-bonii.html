Calophyllum bonii

Calophyllum bonii, commonly known as the Madagascar beautyberry, is a member of the Clusiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized tree native to Madagascar, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are ornamental and attract birds. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
