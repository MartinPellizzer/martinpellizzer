Cyperus dilutus

Cyperus dilutus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus dilutus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its ability to thrive in moist conditions and its attractive, grass-like appearance.
