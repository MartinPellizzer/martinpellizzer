Centaurea scabiosa f. integrifolia

Centaurea scabiosa f. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers with prominent central disks. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have smooth margins, distinguishing it from other varieties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
