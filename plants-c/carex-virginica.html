Carex virginica

Carex virginica, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often green or brown in color.

Carex virginica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
