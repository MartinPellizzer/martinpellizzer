Carica portoricensis

Carica portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican papaya, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, orange-colored fruit that is rich in enzymes like papain, which is used in food and medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
