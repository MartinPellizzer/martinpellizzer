Chenopodium laterale

Chenopodium laterale, commonly known as pigweed, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in many regions.

Chenopodium laterale is also known for its nutritional value, containing high levels of protein and minerals.
