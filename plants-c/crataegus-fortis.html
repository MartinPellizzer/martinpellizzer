Crataegus fortis

Crataegus fortis, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are edible but often used in jams and wines.

Crataegus fortis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting wildlife, particularly birds, during the winter months.
