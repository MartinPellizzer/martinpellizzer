Cerastium malachiforme

Cerastium malachiforme, commonly known as the silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving the plant a silvery appearance.

Cerastium malachiforme is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.
