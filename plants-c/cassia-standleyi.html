Cassia standleyi

Cassia standleyi, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its habitat. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a tuft of silky hair. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

Cassia standleyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
