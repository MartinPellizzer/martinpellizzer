Crepis sahendii

Crepis sahendii, commonly known as Sahendian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, appearing in autumn, contributing to its ecological role in pollination.
