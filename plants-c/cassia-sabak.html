Cassia sabak

Cassia sabak, commonly known as yellow flame, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia sabak thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and aromatic foliage.
