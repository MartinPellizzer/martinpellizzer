Corylus × vilmorinii

Corylus × vilmorinii, commonly known as the Vilmorin hazelnut, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Corylus colurna and Corylus avellana.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2–4 meters, featuring compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces catkins and edible nuts enclosed in a cupule, which are often used in culinary and horticultural applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental qualities and nut production.

The species is also notable for its resistance to certain diseases, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
