Crambe putaria

Crambe putaria, commonly known as the narrowleaf crambe, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are small and oval, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Crambe putaria is adapted to grow in sandy or calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
