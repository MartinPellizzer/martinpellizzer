Centaurea × cavarae

Centaurea × cavarae, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which resemble those of its parent species, Centaurea cyanus and Centaurea solstitialis. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves and showy composite flowers that bloom in summer.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a common sight in gardens and along roadsides.
