Cyrtochilum bictoniense

Cyrtochilum bictoniense, commonly known as the Bicton's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive pouch-like structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil. Cyrtochilum bictoniense has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single spike of flowers.

It is a rare and lesser-known species, often found in high-altitude regions.
