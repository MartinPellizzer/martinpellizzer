Croton salutaris

Croton salutaris, commonly known as the sacred croton, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from bright yellow to deep red, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes worldwide.
