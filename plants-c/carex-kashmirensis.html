Carex kashmirensis

Carex kashmirensis, commonly known as the Kashmir sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a panicle inflorescence.

Carex kashmirensis is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems.
