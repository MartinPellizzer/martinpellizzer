Carex gracilis var. minor

Carex gracilis var. minor, commonly known as slender sedge, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly curved. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
