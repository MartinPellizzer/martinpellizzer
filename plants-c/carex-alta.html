Carex alta

Carex alta, commonly known as tall sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular, often hairy leaves that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and pond edges.

Carex alta plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
