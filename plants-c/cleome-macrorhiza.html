Cleome macrorhiza

Cleome macrorhiza, commonly known as the giant cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Cleome macrorhiza is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
