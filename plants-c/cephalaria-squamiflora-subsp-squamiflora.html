Cephalaria squamiflora subsp. squamiflora

Cephalaria squamiflora subsp.

squamiflora, commonly known as the woolly cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and tall, sparsely branched stems.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
