Crocus hyemalis

Crocus hyemalis, commonly known as the winter crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It produces showy, early blooming flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often emerging through snow in late winter or early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to cold climates, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers are typically short-lived but are among the first signs of the coming season.

Crocus hyemalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.
