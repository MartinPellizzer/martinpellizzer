Chamaesyce cozumelensis

Chamaesyce cozumelensis, commonly known as Cozumel sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cozumel in the Mexican Caribbean.

It is a low-growing herb with opposite, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
