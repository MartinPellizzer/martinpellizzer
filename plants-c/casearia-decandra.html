Casearia decandra

Casearia decandra, commonly known as the Indian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fleshy, violet-colored berries that appear in late summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in racemes, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Casearia decandra is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
