Convolvulus laciniatus var. hirsutus

Convolvulus laciniatus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a vigorous climber with hairy stems and foliage, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique floral display.
