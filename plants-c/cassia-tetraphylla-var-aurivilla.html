Cassia tetraphylla var. aurivilla

Cassia tetraphylla var. aurivilla, commonly known as golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, fluffy flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, contributing to biodiversity in the ecosystem.
