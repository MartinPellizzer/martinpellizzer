Cornus hongkongensis var. gigantea

Cornus hongkongensis var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant Chinese dogwood, is a striking ornamental shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white bracts that resemble petals, which appear in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and is often used in landscape design for its ornamental appeal.
