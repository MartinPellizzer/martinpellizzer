Carex atrofusca subsp. atrofusca

Carex atrofusca subsp.

atrofusca, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America. It is characterized by its dark green, grass-like foliage and produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown.

The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with rhizomes that help it spread vegetatively. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
