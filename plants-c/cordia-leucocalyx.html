Cordia leucocalyx

Cordia leucocalyx, commonly known as white-flowered cordia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
