Cyphomandra tegorea

Cyphomandra tegorea, commonly known as the purple husk pepper.

It is a climbing vine native to South America, characterized by its distinctive purple, fleshy fruits that resemble small, elongated peppers. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruits are edible when ripe, though they are not commonly cultivated for commercial use.
