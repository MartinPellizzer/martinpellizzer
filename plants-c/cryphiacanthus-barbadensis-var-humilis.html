Cryphiacanthus barbadensis var. humilis

Cryphiacanthus barbadensis var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically surrounded by a spathe, a modified leaf-like structure. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
