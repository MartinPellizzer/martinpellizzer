Crepis urundica

Crepis urundica, commonly known as the Himalayan mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It features small, spoon-shaped leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals.

Crepis urundica is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.
