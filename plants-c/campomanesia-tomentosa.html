Campomanesia tomentosa

Campomanesia tomentosa, commonly known as pitanga or Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, slightly astringent flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
