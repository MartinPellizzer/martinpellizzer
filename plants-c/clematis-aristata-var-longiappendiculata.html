Clematis aristata var. longiappendiculata

Clematis aristata var. longiappendiculata, commonly known as the long-stalked clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features slender, climbing stems with palmately compound leaves consisting of three to five leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of blue to purple. Its botanical characteristics include long, slender pedicels and a distinctive, hairy appearance on its stems and leaves.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
