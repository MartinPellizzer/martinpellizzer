Ceanothus decumbens

Ceanothus decumbens, commonly known as the low ceanothus, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and inland regions, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in the spring. Its leaves are evergreen, narrow, and often covered in fine hairs.

Ceanothus decumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
