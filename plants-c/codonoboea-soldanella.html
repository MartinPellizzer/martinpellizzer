Codonoboea soldanella

Codonoboea soldanella, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually violet or blue in color, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Codonoboea soldanella thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
