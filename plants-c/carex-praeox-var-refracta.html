Carex praeox var. refracta

Carex praeox var. refracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by their triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variant is distinguished by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescence, which typically consists of spikes of tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
