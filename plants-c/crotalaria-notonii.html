Crotalaria notonii

Crotalaria notonii, commonly known as the southern crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are often accompanied by palmate leaves. Its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny pod, a characteristic feature of the Crotalaria genus.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
