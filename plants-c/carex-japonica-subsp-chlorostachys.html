Carex japonica subsp. chlorostachys

Carex japonica subsp.

chlorostachys, commonly known as the green-stem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
