Campanula incanescens subsp. khorasanica

Campanula incanescens subsp.

khorasanica, commonly known as the Khorasan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
