Carex ornithopoda var. ornithopodioides

Carex ornithopoda var. ornithopodioides, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects.

The plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
