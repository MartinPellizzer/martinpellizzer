Combretodendron quadrialatum

Combretodendron quadrialatum, commonly known as the four-winged flame tree, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, often displaying vibrant red or orange hues. The plant has pinnate leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental species in tropical landscapes.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional construction and crafts.
