Chrysanthemum indicum var. litorale

Chrysanthemum indicum var. litorale, commonly known as the coastal chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. It is adapted to tolerate salt spray and mild coastal winds, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it suitable for use in coastal gardens and as a natural barrier against erosion.
