Cornicina nummularia

Cornicina nummularia, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silvery, spoon-shaped leaves that reflect sunlight, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
