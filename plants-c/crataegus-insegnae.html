Crataegus insegnae

Crataegus insegnae, commonly known as the Sierra foothill hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to California.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has thorny branches and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Crataegus insegnae plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
