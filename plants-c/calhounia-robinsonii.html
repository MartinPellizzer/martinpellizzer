Calhounia robinsonii

Calhounia robinsonii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

Calhounia robinsonii plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides nectar for pollinators.
