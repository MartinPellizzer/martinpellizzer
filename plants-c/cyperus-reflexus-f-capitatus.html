Cyperus reflexus f. capitatus

Cyperus reflexus f. capitatus, commonly known as the clustered flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of a compact cluster of spikelets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands.

This plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions and is adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
