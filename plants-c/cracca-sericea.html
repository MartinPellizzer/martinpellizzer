Cracca sericea

Cracca sericea, commonly known as silver cracca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy surface.

Cracca sericea is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
