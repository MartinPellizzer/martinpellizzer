Cayaponia longiloba

Cayaponia longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed cayaponia, is a climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, and its roots are known to fix nitrogen in the soil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
