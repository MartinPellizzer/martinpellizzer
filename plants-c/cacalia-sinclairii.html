Cacalia sinclairii

Cacalia sinclairii, commonly known as the Sinclair's bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia sinclairii is known for its early spring blooming period, adding vibrant color to its native environment.
