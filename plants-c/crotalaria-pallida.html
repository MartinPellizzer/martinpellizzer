Crotalaria pallida

Crotalaria pallida, commonly known as the pale cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. Its seeds are encased in slender, twisted pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria pallida is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in various environments.
