Cleyera serrulata

Cleyera serrulata, commonly known as the serrated cleyera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
