Chaenostoma pedicellatum

Chaenostoma pedicellatum, commonly known as the clustered water plantain, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with small white flowers that emerge above the water surface. Its rhizomes form dense clusters, helping to stabilize the substrate and prevent erosion.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and fish.
