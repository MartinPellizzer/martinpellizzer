Clinopodium julianoides

Clinopodium julianoides, commonly known as Julian's mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct mint-like fragrance when crushed.

It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
