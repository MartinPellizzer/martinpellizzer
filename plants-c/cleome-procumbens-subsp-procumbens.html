Cleome procumbens subsp. procumbens

Cleome procumbens subsp.

procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate cleome, is a low-growing annual plant native to the southeastern United States. It features slender, branching stems that lie close to the ground, often forming a mat-like structure.

The plant produces small, yellow, cruciform flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.
