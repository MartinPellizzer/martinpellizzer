Cosmostigma acuminatum

Cosmostigma acuminatum, commonly known as the spiked cosmos, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to purple bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
