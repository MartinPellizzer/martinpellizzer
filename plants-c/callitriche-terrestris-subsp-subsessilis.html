Callitriche terrestris subsp. subsessilis

Callitriche terrestris subsp.

subsessilis, commonly known as the slender water starwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in ponds, ditches, and stream edges.

The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are usually arranged in two rows along the stem. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is a perennial species that plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for invertebrates and stabilizing water margins.
