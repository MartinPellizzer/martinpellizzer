Calamus sarasinorum

Calamus sarasinorum, commonly known as the Sarasin's calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves, which are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers.

The plant's rhizomes are sometimes harvested for their aromatic properties and used in local medicinal practices.
