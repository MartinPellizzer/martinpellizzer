Cupressus glabra

Cupressus glabra, commonly known as the smooth cedar, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the branches. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its foliage is aromatic and has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Cupressus glabra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
