Calceolaria villosa

Calceolaria villosa, commonly known as the hairy calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers cool climates. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety appearance.

Calceolaria villosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, showy blooms.
