Cupania cinerea

Cupania cinerea, commonly known as the silvercup tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its greyish-white bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Cupania cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
