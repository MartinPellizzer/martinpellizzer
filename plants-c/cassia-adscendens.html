Cassia adscendens

Cassia adscendens, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear numerous golden-yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds of Cassia adscendens are encased in a hairy, spiny pod and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
