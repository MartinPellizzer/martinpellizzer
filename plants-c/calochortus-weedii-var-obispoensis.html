Calochortus weedii var. obispoensis

Calochortus weedii var. obispoensis, commonly known as the Obispo's mariposa lily, is a flowering plant native to California, particularly in the Central Valley region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing delicate, short-lived flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along the edges of wetlands.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
