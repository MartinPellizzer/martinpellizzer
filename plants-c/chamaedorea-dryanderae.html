Chamaedorea dryanderae

Chamaedorea dryanderae, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen or the feather palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and pinnate leaves that resemble feathers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its elegant appearance. It produces small, yellowish-pink flowers followed by dark blue berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed.

Chamaedorea dryanderae is valued for its air-purifying qualities and low maintenance requirements.
