Cleome eyerdamii

Cleome eyerdamii, commonly known as the Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners looking for ornamental plants.

Cleome eyerdamii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
