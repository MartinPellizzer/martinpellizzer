Cyclopia squamosa

Cyclopia squamosa, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange flower heads. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cyclopia squamosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
