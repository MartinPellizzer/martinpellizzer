Cephalaria leucantha var. angustiloba

Cephalaria leucantha var. angustiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf whitehead, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white to pale yellow flower heads. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces numerous ray florets that give it a dandelion-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
