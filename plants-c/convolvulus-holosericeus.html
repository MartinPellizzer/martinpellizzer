Convolvulus holosericeus

Convolvulus holosericeus, commonly known as the silky blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has hairy, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden use.

The flowers bloom throughout the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
