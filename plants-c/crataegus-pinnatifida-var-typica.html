Crataegus pinnatifida var. typica

Crataegus pinnatifida var. typica, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, pinnately compound leaves, and showy white to pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
