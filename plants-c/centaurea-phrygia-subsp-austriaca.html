Centaurea phrygia subsp. austriaca

Centaurea phrygia subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian cornflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It has erect, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
