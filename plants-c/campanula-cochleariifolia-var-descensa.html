Campanula cochleariifolia var. descensa

Campanula cochleariifolia var. descensa, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
