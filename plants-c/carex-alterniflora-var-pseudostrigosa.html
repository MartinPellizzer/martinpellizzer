Carex alterniflora var. pseudostrigosa

Carex alterniflora var. pseudostrigosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and distinctive flowering structures called spikelets. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
