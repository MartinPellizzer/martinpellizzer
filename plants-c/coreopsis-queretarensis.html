Coreopsis queretarensis

Coreopsis queretarensis, commonly known as the Mexican coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Coreopsis queretarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
