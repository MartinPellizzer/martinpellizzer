Curcuma pygmaea

Curcuma pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf turmeric, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and cardamom, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are broad and lance-shaped. Curcuma pygmaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
