Calea axillaris var. urticifolia

Calea axillaris var. urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle calea, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs that can cause mild skin irritation, similar to stinging nettle.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical and subtropical regions.
