Carex rufovariegata

Carex rufovariegata, commonly known as the variegated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant features bright green leaves with striking yellow or white variegation, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or shaded woodland areas.

Carex rufovariegata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
