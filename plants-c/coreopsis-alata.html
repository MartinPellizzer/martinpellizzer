Coreopsis alata

Coreopsis alata, commonly known as the Western New Mexico coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, branched stems and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often covered in fine white or grayish down. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Coreopsis alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
