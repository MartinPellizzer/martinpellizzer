Croton martii var. longifolius

Croton martii var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that can range from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
