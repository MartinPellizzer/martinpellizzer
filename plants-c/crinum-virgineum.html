Crinum virgineum

Crinum virgineum, commonly known as the Virgin's Crinum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant has strap-shaped, narrow leaves that form a dense rosette at the base. It produces a tall, leafless stem that bears the floral display, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Crinum virgineum is adapted to wetland environments and is often found in swamps and marshes.
