Clinopodium trapezuntinum

Clinopodium trapezuntinum, commonly known as the Greek oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in culinary applications, similar to other members of the oregano genus.

Clinopodium trapezuntinum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.
