Crataegus monogyna subsp. brevispina

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

brevispina, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that ripen in autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its flowers and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their cardiovascular benefits.
