Cleome sessilifolia

Cleome sessilifolia, commonly known as the winged cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wing-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Cleome sessilifolia is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
