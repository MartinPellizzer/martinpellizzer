Circaea × intermedia f. minor

Circaea × intermedia f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and hairy, narrow leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.

Its compact form makes it suitable for rock gardens and shaded borders in landscaping.
