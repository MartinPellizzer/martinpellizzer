Cyperus × amuricocompressus

Cyperus × amuricocompressus, commonly known as the Amur flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

It produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually inconspicuous, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
