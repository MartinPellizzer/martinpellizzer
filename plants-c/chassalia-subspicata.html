Chassalia subspicata

Chassalia subspicata, commonly known as the slender chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a compact cluster, and bloom during the warmer months.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
