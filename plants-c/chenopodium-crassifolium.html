Chenopodium crassifolium

Chenopodium crassifolium, commonly known as thickleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its thick, succulent leaves that are typically lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Chenopodium crassifolium is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is also considered a weed in agricultural settings.
