Cyperus radiatus

Cyperus radiatus, commonly known as the spike rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, ponds, and along stream banks. Cyperus radiatus has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a tight spiral around the stem.

It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
