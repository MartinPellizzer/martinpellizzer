Chusquea fruticosa

Chusquea fruticosa, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Chusquea fruticosa is often used in traditional landscaping and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
