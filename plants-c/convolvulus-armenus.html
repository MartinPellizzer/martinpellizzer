Convolvulus armenus

Convolvulus armenus, commonly known as Armenian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that allow it to climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
