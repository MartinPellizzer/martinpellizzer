Chimarrhis dioica

Chimarrhis dioica, commonly known as the coastal wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is well adapted to coastal environments. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dunes and cliff faces.

Chimarrhis dioica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in coastal landscaping.
