Callicarpa tikusikensis

Callicarpa tikusikensis, commonly known as the Alaska chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Arctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black berries that are typically clustered in groups of three. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible fruits. Callicarpa tikusikensis thrives in cold climates and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

Its berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used by indigenous peoples for both food and traditional medicine.
