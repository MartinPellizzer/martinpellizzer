Carex nemurensis

Carex nemurensis, commonly known as the ghost sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex nemurensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
