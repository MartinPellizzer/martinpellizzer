Carex vicaria

Carex vicaria, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant is typically short-lived and spreads through rhizomes, forming dense clumps.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
