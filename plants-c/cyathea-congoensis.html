Cyathea congoensis

Cyathea congoensis, commonly known as the Congo tree fern, is a tall, evergreen fern native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is covered with persistent, dark brown, fibrous roots. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cyathea congoensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
