Cuttsia viburnea

Cuttsia viburnea, commonly known as the Australian holly, is a deciduous shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Cuttsia viburnea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
