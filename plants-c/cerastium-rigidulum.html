Cerastium rigidulum

Cerastium rigidulum, commonly known as the rigid chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cerastium rigidulum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
