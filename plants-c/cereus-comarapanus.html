Cereus comarapanus

Cereus comarapanus, commonly known as the Christmas star cactus, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of bright, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms during the holiday season, producing vibrant pink or red flowers. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Due to its striking appearance and seasonal blooms, it is a popular ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.
