Cnicus angustifolius

Cnicus angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved burnet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, erect stems and finely divided, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Cnicus angustifolius is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
