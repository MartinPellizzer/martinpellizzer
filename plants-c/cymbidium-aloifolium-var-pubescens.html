Cymbidium aloifolium var. pubescens

Cymbidium aloifolium var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with tall, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, typically during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
