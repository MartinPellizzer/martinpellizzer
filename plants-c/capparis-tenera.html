Capparis tenera

Capparis tenera, commonly known as the desert fig or sweet caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands and parts of North Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating high drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to coastal and rocky habitats.
