Campanula biebersteiniana

Campanula biebersteiniana, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula biebersteiniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and hardy nature, suitable for gardens in cooler climates.
