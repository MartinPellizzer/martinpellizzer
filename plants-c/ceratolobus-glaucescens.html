Ceratolobus glaucescens

Ceratolobus glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its pale green to bluish-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in spring, which are clustered in globular heads. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Ceratolobus glaucescens is a hardy plant that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
