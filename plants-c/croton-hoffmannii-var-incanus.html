Croton hoffmannii var. incanus

Croton hoffmannii var. incanus, commonly known as the silver croton, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are typically large, elongated, and covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery-white hairs, giving it a shimmering appearance. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
