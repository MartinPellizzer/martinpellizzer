Chesneya intermedia

Chesneya intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican giant gentian, is a striking perennial plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, showy blue to violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Gentianaceae family, known for its vibrant floral displays and medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, providing a textured contrast to its brilliant blossoms.

Chesneya intermedia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
