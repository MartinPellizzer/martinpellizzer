Cardamine latifolia var. raphanifolia

Cardamine latifolia var. raphanifolia, commonly known as broadleaf watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its leaves are broad and slightly succulent, with a peppery flavor, and it produces small white or pale yellow flowers in early spring.

The species is valued for its edible leaves and has been used in traditional cuisine and herbal medicine.
