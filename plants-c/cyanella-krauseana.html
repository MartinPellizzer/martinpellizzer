Cyanella krauseana

Cyanella krauseana, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a striking member of the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually blue-green in color, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Cyanella krauseana is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
