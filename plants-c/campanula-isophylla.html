Campanula isophylla

Campanula isophylla, commonly known as the equal-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Campanula isophylla is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases, making it a hardy choice for gardeners.
