Carex pamiroalaica

Carex pamiroalaica, commonly known as the Pamir sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex pamiroalaica is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas.
