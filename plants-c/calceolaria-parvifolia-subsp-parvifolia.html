Calceolaria parvifolia subsp. parvifolia

Calceolaria parvifolia subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved slipper orchid, is a member of the Campanulaceae family. It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Peru and Bolivia.

The plant is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that resemble tiny slippers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed along the stem.
