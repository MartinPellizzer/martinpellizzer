Cervicina campanuloides

Cervicina campanuloides, commonly known as the bell-shaped cervicin, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a smooth margin.

Cervicina campanuloides is often found in the temperate regions of eastern North America.
