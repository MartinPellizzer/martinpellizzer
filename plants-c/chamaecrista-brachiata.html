Chamaecrista brachiata

Chamaecrista brachiata, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions and is adapted to poor soil conditions.

Chamaecrista brachiata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
