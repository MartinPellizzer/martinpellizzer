Cynorkis hologlossa var. angustilabia

Cynorkis hologlossa var. angustilabia, commonly known as the narrow-lipped cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and a crown of large, pinnate leaves that are dark green and often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs, typical of cycads. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
