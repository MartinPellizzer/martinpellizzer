Chaetospermum glutinosum

Chaetospermum glutinosum, commonly known as the sticky hairgrass, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, sticky hairs on the leaves and stems. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in green or reddish inflorescences.

The plant's ability to thrive in waterlogged soils makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
