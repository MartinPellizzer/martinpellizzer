Cephalaria mauritanica var. atlantica

Cephalaria mauritanica var. atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
