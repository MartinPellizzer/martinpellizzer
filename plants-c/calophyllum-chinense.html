Calophyllum chinense

Calophyllum chinense, commonly known as the Chinese mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and broad, pinnate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by round, purple fruits with a segmented, white flesh. The fruit is highly aromatic and contains a single large seed, making it a valuable crop in its native region.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
