Carex cantabrorum

Carex cantabrorum, commonly known as the Cantabrian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex cantabrorum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
