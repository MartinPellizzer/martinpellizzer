Campanula capitata

Campanula capitata, commonly known as the "Bellflower" or "Snowbell," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
