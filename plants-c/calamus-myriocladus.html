Calamus myriocladus

Calamus myriocladus, commonly known as the many-leaved calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender reed-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height, with long, narrow leaves arranged in a fan shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaf sheaths. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Calamus myriocladus thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
