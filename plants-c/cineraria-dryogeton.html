Cineraria dryogeton

Cineraria dryogeton, commonly known as the rock cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-resistant plants.
