Cephalaria laevigata subf. puberula

Cephalaria laevigata subf.

puberula, commonly known as the hairy cephalaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe. It features tall, erect stems covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it its common name.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers arranged in loose, terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along woodland edges.
