Cyathea camerooniana var. aethiopica

Cyathea camerooniana var. aethiopica, commonly known as the Cameroon hakea, is a species of tree fern native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes produce roots that help stabilize the soil, making it an important component of forest ecosystems.

The fronds are often used in traditional medicine and as thatching materials by local communities.
