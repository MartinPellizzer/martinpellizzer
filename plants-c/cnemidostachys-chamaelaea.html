Cnemidostachys chamaelaea

Cnemidostachys chamaelaea, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, orchid-like flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular species among botanists and gardeners.
