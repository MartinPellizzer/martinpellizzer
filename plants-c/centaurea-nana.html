Centaurea nana

Centaurea nana, commonly known as dwarf coneflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Centaurea nana is also known for its ability to spread through self-seeding, contributing to its naturalized presence in various landscapes.
