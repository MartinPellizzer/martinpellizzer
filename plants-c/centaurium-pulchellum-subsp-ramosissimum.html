Centaurium pulchellum subsp. ramosissimum

Centaurium pulchellum subsp.

ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
