Calophyllum megistanthum

Calophyllum megistanthum, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa, including Kenya and Tanzania.

It is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and long, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

Due to its valuable wood and traditional uses, it is also of interest in ecological and conservation studies.
