Colchicum aegyptiacum

Colchicum aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically basal and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces a distinctive corm-like structure that serves as its storage organ.

Colchicum aegyptiacum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
