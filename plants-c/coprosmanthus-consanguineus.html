Coprosmanthus consanguineus

Coprosmanthus consanguineus, commonly known as the Chinese lantern plant, is a flowering shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces small, round, orange berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Coprosmanthus consanguineus is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant fall display.
