Canna lutea var. maculata

Canna lutea var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, brightly colored leaves, which often feature distinctive red or purple markings. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Canna lutea var. maculata is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
