Columbaria canescens

Columbaria canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf shrub, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its woody stems are often branched and can grow up to several feet in height, making it a distinctive feature in desert landscapes.

Columbaria canescens is adapted to poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
