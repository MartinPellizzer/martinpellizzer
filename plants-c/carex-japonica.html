Carex japonica

Carex japonica, commonly known as Japanese sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
