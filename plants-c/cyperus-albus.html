Cyperus albus

Cyperus albus, commonly known as the white flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale brown flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus albus is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various water conditions.
