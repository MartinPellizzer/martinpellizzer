Chenopodium gaudichaudianum

Chenopodium gaudichaudianum, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and it is typically found in rocky or arid environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important species in ecological studies of arid ecosystems.
