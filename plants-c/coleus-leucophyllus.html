Coleus leucophyllus

Coleus leucophyllus, commonly known as the white-leaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its striking, large, heart-shaped leaves that are often white or variegated. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is particularly notable for its vibrant coloration and ability to add visual interest to shaded garden spaces.
