Cynoglossum wallichii var. glochidiatum

Cynoglossum wallichii var. glochidiatum, commonly known as the woolly blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It features a basal rosette of velvety, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces striking violet to blue flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variant is distinguished by its glandular hairs and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
