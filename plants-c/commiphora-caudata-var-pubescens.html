Commiphora caudata var. pubescens

Commiphora caudata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy Myrrh tree, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The hairy Myrrh tree thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.
