Ceropegia capensis

Ceropegia capensis, commonly known as the Cape ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its succulent, trailing stems and fleshy leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia capensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
