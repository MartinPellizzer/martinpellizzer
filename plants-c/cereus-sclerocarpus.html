Cereus sclerocarpus

Cereus sclerocarpus, commonly known as the night bloomer or moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pink. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
