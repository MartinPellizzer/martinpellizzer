Callisia montana

Callisia montana, commonly known as the mountain spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the stems are upright and branching.
