Cynanchum viminale subsp. viminale

Cynanchum viminale subsp.

viminale, commonly known as the creeping milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and slender, often hairy stems that root at the nodes.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of xerophytic vegetation.
