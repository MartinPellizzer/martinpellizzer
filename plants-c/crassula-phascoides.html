Crassula phascoides

Crassula phascoides, commonly known as the rock rose or dwarf crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green, arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Crassula phascoides is popular as a decorative houseplant due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.
