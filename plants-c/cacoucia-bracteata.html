Cacoucia bracteata

Cacoucia bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous characteristics and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its pods are typically elongated and contain edible seeds, making it a valuable crop in some parts of the world.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.
