Calamagrostis planifolia

Calamagrostis planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf reed, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are flat on both sides. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshes.

Calamagrostis planifolia is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
