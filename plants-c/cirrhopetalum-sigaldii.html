Cirrhopetalum sigaldii

Cirrhopetalum sigaldii, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals that resemble small bells. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its unique beauty.
