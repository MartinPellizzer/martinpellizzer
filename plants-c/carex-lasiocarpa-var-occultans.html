Carex lasiocarpa var. occultans

Carex lasiocarpa var. occultans, commonly known as the hidden-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are often covered with fine, hair-like structures, giving it its scientific name.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
