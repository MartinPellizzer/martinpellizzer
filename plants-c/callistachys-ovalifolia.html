Callistachys ovalifolia

Callistachys ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf spurge.

It is a shrub native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its narrow, oval-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are usually arranged oppositely on the stems, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
