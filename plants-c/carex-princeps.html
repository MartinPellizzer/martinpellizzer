Carex princeps

Carex princeps, commonly known as the prince's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex princeps is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
