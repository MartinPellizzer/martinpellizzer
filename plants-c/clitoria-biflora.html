Clitoria biflora

Clitoria biflora, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive butterfly-shaped blue or violet flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and features trifoliate leaves with three leaflets. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, containing both male and female reproductive structures.

Clitoria biflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting agricultural practices.
