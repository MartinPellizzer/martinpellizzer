Circaea delavayi

Circaea delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's wintergreen, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often prostrate or ascending. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
