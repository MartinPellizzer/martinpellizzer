Cyathea trinitensis

Cyathea trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant is evergreen and can grow up to several meters in height, with a distinctive crown of feathery leaves. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the upright structure of the plant.

Cyathea trinitensis plays a role in tropical ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
