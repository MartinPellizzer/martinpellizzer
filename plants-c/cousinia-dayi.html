Cousinia dayi

Cousinia dayi, commonly known as the Tibetan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow flowers are often clustered in a composite head, and it has hairy, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss.

Cousinia dayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
