Commelina erecta f. cana

Commelina erecta f. cana, commonly known as the white-veined spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or white flowers with prominent veins. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial with erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually clustered in spikes and are pollinated by insects.
