Calycopeplus ephedroides

Calycopeplus ephedroides, commonly known as the Chinese ephedra, is a shrub native to arid regions of China.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant contains alkaloids, particularly ephedrine, which has been historically used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also of interest in modern pharmacology.
