Crepis czerepanovii

Crepis czerepanovii, commonly known as the Czerepanov's hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its habitat.
