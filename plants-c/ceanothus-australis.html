Ceanothus australis

Ceanothus australis, commonly known as the Australian bluebottle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Ceanothus australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
