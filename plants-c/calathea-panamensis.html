Calathea panamensis

Calathea panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian prayer plant, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, often displaying a mix of green, silver, and pink hues. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
