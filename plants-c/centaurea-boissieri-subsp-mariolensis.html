Centaurea boissieri subsp. mariolensis

Centaurea boissieri subsp.

mariolensis, commonly known as the Mariolensian centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and vibrant blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
