Cladanthus geslinii

Cladanthus geslinii, commonly known as the Geslin's clavicle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy textures, and its leaves are typically lanceolate with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense, disc-like formations, typical of the daisy family.

Cladanthus geslinii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
