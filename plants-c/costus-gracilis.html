Costus gracilis

Costus gracilis, commonly known as slender costus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

Costus gracilis prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
