Chlamysporum triandrum

Chlamysporum triandrum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to the western Pacific region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, reddish-brown bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.
