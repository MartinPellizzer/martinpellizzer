Clerodendrum semiserratum

Clerodendrum semiserratum, commonly known as the Chinese flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

The species belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
