Camptostylus aristatus

Camptostylus aristatus, commonly known as the redstem dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pink bracts that surround small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often reddish beneath, and it produces small, dark berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Camptostylus aristatus plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
