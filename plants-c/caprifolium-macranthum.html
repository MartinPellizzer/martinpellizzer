Caprifolium macranthum

Caprifolium macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically clustered in pairs. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces small, black berries that are edible and contain seeds.

Caprifolium macranthum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
