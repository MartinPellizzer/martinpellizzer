Caltha laeta var. czarnohorensis

Caltha laeta var. czarnohorensis, commonly known as the Czar's Glory, is a variant of the buttercup family (Ranunculaceae) native to the Carpathian region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking yellow flowers with numerous stamens. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, glossy appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
