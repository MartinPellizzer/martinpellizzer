Chloranthus brachystachys var. melanocarpus

Chloranthus brachystachys var. melanocarpus, commonly known as the black-fruited boxwood, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is characterized by its small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, black drupe, which is distinctive and contributes to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its compact growth habit and unique fruiting characteristics.
