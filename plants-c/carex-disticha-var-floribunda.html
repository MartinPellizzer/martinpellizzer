Carex disticha var. floribunda

Carex disticha var. floribunda, commonly known as the flowering sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy.

The inflorescence consists of two to three flowering spikes, which can range in color from green to reddish-brown.
