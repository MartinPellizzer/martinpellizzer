Capraria biflora subsp. biflora

Capraria biflora subsp.

biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered capraria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and rocky habitats, characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers.

The plant has opposite, narrow leaves and produces two flowers per stem, giving it its subspecies name. It is often found in sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Capraria biflora subsp.

biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
