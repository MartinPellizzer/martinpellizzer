Citrus neocaledonica

Citrus neocaledonica, commonly known as the Tahitian lime or kaffir lime, is a tropical citrus species native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant fruits with a distinctive bumpy, green rind. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is widely used in cooking for its aromatic zest and juice.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
