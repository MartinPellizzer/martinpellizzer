Candollea preissii

Candollea preissii, commonly known as the giant forestiera, is a striking member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of northern Mexico and the southwestern United States. This plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. It produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers during the spring and summer months.

Candollea preissii is well adapted to dry conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping in suitable climates.
