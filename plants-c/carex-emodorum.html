Carex emodorum

Carex emodorum, commonly known as the purplestem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces three-sided, three-rowed berries.

Carex emodorum plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
