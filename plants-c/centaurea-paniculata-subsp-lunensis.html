Centaurea paniculata subsp. lunensis

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

lunensis, commonly known as the lunar centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branching stems and produces striking, purple-blue flower heads that resemble small, delicate globes.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
