Cucubalus striatus

Cucubalus striatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed balsam, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its alternate, elliptical leaves with a distinctive striated surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional use.
