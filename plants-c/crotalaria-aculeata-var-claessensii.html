Crotalaria aculeata var. claessensii

Crotalaria aculeata var. claessensii, commonly known as the prickly crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spiny pods and small, yellow flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three leaflets and produces seed pods that are typically covered in sharp, bristly hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
