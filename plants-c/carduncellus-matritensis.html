Carduncellus matritensis

Carduncellus matritensis, commonly known as the Madrid thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open landscapes such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are spiny and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellowish in color.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
