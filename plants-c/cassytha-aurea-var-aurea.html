Cassytha aurea var. aurea

Cassytha aurea var. aurea, commonly known as golden cassytha, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to twine around other plants make it both a useful ground cover and a potential invasive species.
