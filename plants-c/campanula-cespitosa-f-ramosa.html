Campanula cespitosa f. ramosa

Campanula cespitosa f. ramosa, commonly known as the branched bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with many branching stems, giving it a bushy appearance. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
