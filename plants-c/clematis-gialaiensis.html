Clematis gialaiensis

Clematis gialaiensis, commonly known as the Gialai Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous climbing growth habit. The plant produces showy, usually fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of palmately compound leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.

Clematis gialaiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
