Colchicum alpinum var. merenderoides

Colchicum alpinum var. merenderoides, commonly known as the alpine colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
