Camphora porrecta

Camphora porrecta, commonly known as the Chinese camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the樟科 (Lauraceae) family and is characterized by its aromatic, rough bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into oval, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Its most notable feature is the production of camphor, a volatile oil used in traditional medicine and as a preservative.

Camphora porrecta is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is often planted for its ornamental appeal.
