Centaurea jacea var. diploidea

Centaurea jacea var. diploidea, commonly known as the doublehead thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, showy composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The leaves are typically lance-shaped, with rough, hairy surfaces and often have a bluish or purplish hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
