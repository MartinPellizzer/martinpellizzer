Carex bhutanensis

Carex bhutanensis, commonly known as the Bhutan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Bhutan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Carex bhutanensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates in its native ecosystem.
