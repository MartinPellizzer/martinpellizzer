Cephalocleistocactus pallidus

Cephalocleistocactus pallidus, commonly known as the pale flowered cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle visual contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small groups, and it is well adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is of interest to both botanists and cactus enthusiasts.
