Carex leersii var. angustata

Carex leersii var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish or reddish flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
