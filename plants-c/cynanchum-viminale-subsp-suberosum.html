Cynanchum viminale subsp. suberosum

Cynanchum viminale subsp.

suberosum, commonly known as the hairy milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that become corky with age, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its milky sap, which is a common feature among plants in the milkweed family.
