Cyrtopodium poecilum

Cyrtopodium poecilum, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a dense, mat-forming growth habit with long, slender stems that bear numerous small, pale pink to white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Cyrtopodium poecilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its delicate floral display.
