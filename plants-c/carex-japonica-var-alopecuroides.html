Carex japonica var. alopecuroides

Carex japonica var. alopecuroides, commonly known as Japanese sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes or along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
