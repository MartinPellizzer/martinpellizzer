Casearia sylvestris var. wydleri

Casearia sylvestris var. wydleri, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright blue, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed.
