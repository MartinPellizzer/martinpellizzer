Cephaelis attenuata

Cephaelis attenuata, commonly known as the pink fairy fan, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
