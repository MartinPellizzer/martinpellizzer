Castilleja fissifolia subsp. pumila

Castilleja fissifolia subsp.

pumila, commonly known as the smallleaf Indian paintbrush, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the western United States. It has slender, hairy stems and produces showy, red-orange to yellow floral clusters.

The plant lacks true leaves and relies on its host for water and nutrients. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native range.
