Carex praecox f. laxa

Carex praecox f. laxa, commonly known as the slender flowering sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
