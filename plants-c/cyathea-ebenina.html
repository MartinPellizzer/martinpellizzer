Cyathea ebenina

Cyathea ebenina, commonly known as the ebony tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its fronds are often used in traditional medicine and basket weaving.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in rainforests.
