Calycolpus nipense

Calycolpus nipense, commonly known as the tropical evergreen tree, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds and mammals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
