Cytisus tetragonus

Cytisus tetragonus, commonly known as the four-angled broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, four-angled stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
