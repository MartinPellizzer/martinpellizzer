Cousinia outichaschensis f. glauciifolia

Cousinia outichaschensis f. glauciifolia, commonly known as the glaucous-flowered cousinia, is a botanical variant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which is often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating known as a glaucous bloom. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, typical of the tribe Cardueae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphological features and potential ornamental value.
