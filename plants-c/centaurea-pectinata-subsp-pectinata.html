Centaurea pectinata subsp. pectinata

Centaurea pectinata subsp.

pectinata, commonly known as the purple centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and bright purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, bract-like leaves along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas. Centaurea pectinata subsp.

pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
