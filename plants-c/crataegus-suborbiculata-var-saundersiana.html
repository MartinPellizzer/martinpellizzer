Crataegus suborbiculata var. saundersiana

Crataegus suborbiculata var. saundersiana, commonly known as the Saunders' hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its rounded, often thornless leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its botanical features include dense, often spreading branches and a compact growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
