Crotalaria acutiflora var. grandiflora

Crotalaria acutiflora var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered crotalaria, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive hooded shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and potential as a forage crop, it is sometimes grown in agricultural and horticultural settings.
