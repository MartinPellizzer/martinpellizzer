Crinum douglasii

Crinum douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's glory lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a spike at the top of the stem. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Crinum douglasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
