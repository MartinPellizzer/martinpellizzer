Clitoria ternatea f. leucopetala

Clitoria ternatea f. leucopetala, commonly known as the white winged bean, is a variant of the common butterfly pea.

It is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its pale to white flowers that contrast with its deep green foliage. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.

The white variant is prized for its unique color and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
