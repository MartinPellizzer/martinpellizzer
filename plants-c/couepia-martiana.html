Couepia martiana

Couepia martiana, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or the wild starfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that resemble starfruits, though they are not related to the common starfruit (Carambola). Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinct floral structure.

Couepia martiana is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
