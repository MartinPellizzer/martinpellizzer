Carex comans var. pulchella

Carex comans var. pulchella, commonly known as the showy sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
