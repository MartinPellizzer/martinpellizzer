Capparis gaudichaudiana

Capparis gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the sweet-smelling caper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but often have a slightly tart flavor. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Capparis gaudichaudiana is often found in coastal and tropical forest environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
