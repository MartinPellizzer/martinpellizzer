Conchograecum curvipes

Conchograecum curvipes, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its unique spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom sequentially along the spiral structure. Its leaves are typically strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Conchograecum curvipes thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
