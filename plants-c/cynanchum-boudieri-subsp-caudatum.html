Cynanchum boudieri subsp. caudatum

Cynanchum boudieri subsp.

caudatum, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
