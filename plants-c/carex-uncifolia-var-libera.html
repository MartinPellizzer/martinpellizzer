Carex uncifolia var. libera

Carex uncifolia var. libera, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wet or moist environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It thrives in full to partial sun and requires consistent moisture.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
