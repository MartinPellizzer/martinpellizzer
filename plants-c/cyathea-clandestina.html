Cyathea clandestina

Cyathea clandestina, commonly known as the hidden tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, with the trunk covered in remnants of old fronds that contribute to its distinctive appearance. Cyathea clandestina is typically found in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives in the understory.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
