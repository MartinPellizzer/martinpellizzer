Cissus ulmifolia

Cissus ulmifolia, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in shaded areas.

Its rapid growth makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.
