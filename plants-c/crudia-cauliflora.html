Crudia cauliflora

Crudia cauliflora, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
