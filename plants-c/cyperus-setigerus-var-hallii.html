Cyperus setigerus var. hallii

Cyperus setigerus var. hallii, commonly known as the Hall's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster, which is a defining feature of the genus Cyperus.

This variant is distinguished by its specific morphological traits, making it identifiable within the broader species complex of Cyperus setigerus.
