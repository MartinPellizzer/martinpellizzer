Cardamine scoriarum

Cardamine scoriarum, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit with a short stem and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator species of alpine biodiversity.
