Castela retusa

Castela retusa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or castela, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. Castela retusa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
