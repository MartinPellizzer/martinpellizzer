Capparis barcellonensis

Capparis barcellonensis, commonly known as the Spanish figwort, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Capparaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive flowers and often prickly foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it has a shrub-like growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
