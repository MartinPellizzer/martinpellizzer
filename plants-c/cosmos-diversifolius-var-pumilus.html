Cosmos diversifolius var. pumilus

Cosmos diversifolius var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf cosmos, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, feathery foliage with a broad, rounded shape and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, making it a compact and ornamental choice for gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular selection for native plant gardens and butterfly habitats.
