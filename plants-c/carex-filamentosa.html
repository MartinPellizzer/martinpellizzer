Carex filamentosa

Carex filamentosa, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes over 5,000 species of sedges, grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
