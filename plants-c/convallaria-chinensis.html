Convallaria chinensis

Convallaria chinensis, commonly known as Chinese lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink blossoms. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Convallaria chinensis is often cultivated for its beauty and is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
