Campyloptera carnea

Campyloptera carnea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Campyloptera carnea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
