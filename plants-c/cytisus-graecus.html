Cytisus graecus

Cytisus graecus, commonly known as the Greek broom, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, branching stems covered in small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in temperate climates.

Cytisus graecus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
