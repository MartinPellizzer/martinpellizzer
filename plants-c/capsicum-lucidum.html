Capsicum lucidum

Capsicum lucidum, commonly known as the golden bell pepper.

It is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange fruits that mature to a deep red. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals, and the fruits are elongated, bell-shaped, and often have a sweet, mild flavor.

Capsicum lucidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
