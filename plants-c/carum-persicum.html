Carum persicum

Carum persicum, commonly known as Persian cumin, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels. The plant is characterized by its branched, hairy stems and is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. Carum persicum thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical classification places it in the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill.
