Calla moschata

Calla moschata, commonly known as the musk calla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive spatulate leaves and showy inflorescence. The plant produces a single, large white spathe that encloses a spadix, which is often fragrant. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
