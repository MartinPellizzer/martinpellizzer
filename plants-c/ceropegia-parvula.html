Ceropegia parvula

Ceropegia parvula, commonly known as the dwarf string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia parvula is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
