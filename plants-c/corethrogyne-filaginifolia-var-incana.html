Corethrogyne filaginifolia var. incana

Corethrogyne filaginifolia var. incana, commonly known as the silky corethrogyne, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Its unique foliage and drought tolerance make it a distinctive addition to desert gardens.
