Carex praecox f. monostachya

Carex praecox f. monostachya, commonly known as the early sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its monostachyous inflorescence, which consists of a single, elongated spike of flowers.
