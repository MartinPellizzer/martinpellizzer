Centaurea × beregiensis

Centaurea × beregiensis, commonly known as the Berezovskaya centaury, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright red or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
