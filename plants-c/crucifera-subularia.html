Crucifera subularia

Crucifera subularia, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which is characterized by its cross-shaped flowers and seed pods. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in a waxy coating, while its bright yellow flowers appear in early spring.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.
