Croton antisyphiliticus var. cordiifolius

Croton antisyphiliticus var. cordiifolius, commonly known as the heartleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its unique foliage.
