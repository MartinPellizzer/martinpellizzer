Carmona microphylla

Carmona microphylla, commonly known as the Mexican heather or dwarf hibiscus, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It features dense, compact growth with small, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
