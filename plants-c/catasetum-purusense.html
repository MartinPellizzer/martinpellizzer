Catasetum purusense

Catasetum purusense, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that can range from yellow to red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers have a unique structure with a specialized lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Catasetum purusense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
