Crocus officinalis

Crocus officinalis, commonly known as the saffron crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, often violet or purple flowers with three petals and three sepals. The plant's most valuable part is its stigmas, which are harvested to produce saffron, a highly prized spice and dye. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cool, dry climate for optimal growth.

Crocus officinalis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in herbal medicine.
