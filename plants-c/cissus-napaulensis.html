Cissus napaulensis

Cissus napaulensis, commonly known as the Indian rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its tendrils and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Cissus napaulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
