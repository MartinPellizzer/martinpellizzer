Calogyne porphyrea

Calogyne porphyrea, commonly known as the Porphyry Nightshade, is a striking plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive purple calyx. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine, silky hairs, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is of interest to horticulturists and botanists alike.
