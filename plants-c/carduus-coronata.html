Carduus coronata

Carduus coronata, commonly known as the crown thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, forming a dense, globe-like structure. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, serrated edge.

Carduus coronata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
