Cuphea elliptica

Cuphea elliptica, commonly known as the wax cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, waxy leaves and showy, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is typically evergreen and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and unique foliage.

Cuphea elliptica is relatively low maintenance and suitable for container gardening or as a border plant.
