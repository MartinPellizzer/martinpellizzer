Callitriche hermaphroditica subsp. truncata

Callitriche hermaphroditica subsp.

truncata, commonly known as the narrowleaf water starwort, is a small aquatic plant characterized by its submerged, ribbon-like leaves. It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and ditches.

The plant is hermaphroditic, meaning each flower contains both male and female reproductive structures. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, which can influence aquatic ecosystems.
