Carex breviculmis var. fibrillosa

Carex breviculmis var. fibrillosa, commonly known as the fibrous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow, with a distinct midrib and a fibrous texture that gives it its common name.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
