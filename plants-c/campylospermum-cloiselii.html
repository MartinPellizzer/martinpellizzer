Campylospermum cloiselii

Campylospermum cloiselii, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal conditions.
