Calanthe discolor var. viridialba

Calanthe discolor var. viridialba, commonly known as the green-veined calanthe, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive greenish-white coloration and a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically in late spring or early summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
