Chascanum sulcatum

Chascanum sulcatum, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually broad and waxy, adapted to the harsh mountain climate.

Chascanum sulcatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
