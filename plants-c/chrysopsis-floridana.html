Chrysopsis floridana

Chrysopsis floridana, commonly known as the Florida goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Chrysopsis floridana plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
