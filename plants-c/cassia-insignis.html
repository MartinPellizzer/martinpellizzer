Cassia insignis

Cassia insignis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant produces small, flat, winged pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple narrow leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia insignis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
