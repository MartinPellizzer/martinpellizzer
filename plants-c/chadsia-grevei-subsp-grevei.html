Chadsia grevei subsp. grevei

Chadsia grevei subsp.

grevei, commonly known as the Namib Desert lily, is a perennial herb native to the Namib Desert in Namibia. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapted to the arid environment.

The plant produces a single, tall flowering stem that bears white or pale pink flowers. It blooms during the brief rainy season, typically in winter, and is pollinated by insects.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
