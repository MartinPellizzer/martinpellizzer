Cassia persoonii

Cassia persoonii, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds.

Cassia persoonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
