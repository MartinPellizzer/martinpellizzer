Claytonia alsinoides var. heterophylla

Claytonia alsinoides var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable springbeauty, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are more elongated and less hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable addition to native plant gardens.
