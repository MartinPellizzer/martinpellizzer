Chloris leptantha

Chloris leptantha, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It features slender, tufted stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers in spikes during the spring and summer months. It is often found in open grasslands, sandy soils, and coastal areas.

Chloris leptantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
