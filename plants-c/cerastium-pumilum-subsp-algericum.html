Cerastium pumilum subsp. algericum

Cerastium pumilum subsp.

algericum, commonly known as Algerian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats.

Algerian mouse-ear is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
