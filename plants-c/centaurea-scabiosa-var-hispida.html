Centaurea scabiosa var. hispida

Centaurea scabiosa var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers with prominent, spiky central cones, typical of the genus Centaurea. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.
