Coleus meyeri

Coleus meyeri, commonly known as the New Guinea impatiens, is a flowering plant native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often adorned with striking patterns. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Coleus meyeri thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and container planting.
