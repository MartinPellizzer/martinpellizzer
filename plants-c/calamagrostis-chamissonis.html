Calamagrostis chamissonis

Calamagrostis chamissonis, commonly known as the Chamisson's reed, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, fluffy flowers that appear in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in moist environments.
