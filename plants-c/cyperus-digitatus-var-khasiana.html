Cyperus digitatus var. khasiana

Cyperus digitatus var. khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in humid and tropical climates. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in lowland areas near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
