Cleome viridiflora

Cleome viridiflora, commonly known as greenflower cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cleome viridiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
