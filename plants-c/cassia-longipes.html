Cassia longipes

Cassia longipes, commonly known as the longipe Cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia longipes thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
