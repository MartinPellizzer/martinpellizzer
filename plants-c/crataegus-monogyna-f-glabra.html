Crataegus monogyna f. glabra

Crataegus monogyna f. glabra, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and it is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The common hawthorn is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
