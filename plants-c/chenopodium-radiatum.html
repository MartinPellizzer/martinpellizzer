Chenopodium radiatum

Chenopodium radiatum, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and a distinctive white vein pattern. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by dry, triangular seed capsules. Lambsquarters thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, gardens, and along roadsides.

It is a common weed but also serves as a food source for various animals and birds.
