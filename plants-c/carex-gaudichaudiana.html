Carex gaudichaudiana

Carex gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils with partial to full shade. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex gaudichaudiana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
