Cynanchum gerrardi subsp. ingae

Cynanchum gerrardi subsp.

ingae, commonly known as the Narrowleaf Milkweed Vine, is a perennial climbing plant native to the southeastern United States. It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which is known for its milkweed species.

The plant features slender, twining stems and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces distinctive seed pods that contain toxic, silky-haired seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
