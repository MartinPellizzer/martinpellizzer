Camptosorus rhizophyllus

Camptosorus rhizophyllus, commonly known as the giant rhizophyll, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized root system.

It is native to the humid tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in rich, moist soils. The plant features a short, thick stem with long, fleshy roots that can extend several meters underground. These roots are not only used for anchoring but also for absorbing nutrients and water efficiently.

Its botanical structure makes it an important subject in studies of plant adaptation and symbiotic relationships.
