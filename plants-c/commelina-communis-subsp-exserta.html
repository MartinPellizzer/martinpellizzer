Commelina communis subsp. exserta

Commelina communis subsp.

exserta, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy.

The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common species in ecological studies of wetland flora.
