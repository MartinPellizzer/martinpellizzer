Carex concolor

Carex concolor, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, upright leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex concolor plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
