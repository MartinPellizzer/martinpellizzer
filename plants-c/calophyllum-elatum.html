Calophyllum elatum

Calophyllum elatum, commonly known as the Tahitian tamanu tree, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces a unique, oily resin that is harvested from its bark and used in traditional medicine and cosmetics. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves, and a drupaceous fruit containing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in skincare products due to its anti-inflammatory and healing benefits.
