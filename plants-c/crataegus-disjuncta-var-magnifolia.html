Crataegus disjuncta var. magnifolia

Crataegus disjuncta var. magnifolia, commonly known as the showy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its flowers and fruits are important food sources for various wildlife species.

The showy hawthorn is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.
