Cirsium × ochroleucum

Cirsium × ochroleucum, commonly known as the white thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, pale to white flower heads that resemble those of other thistle species. The plant produces yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically spiny and have a rough, hairy texture, which helps deter herbivores.

Cirsium × ochroleucum is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, showcasing its adaptability to different environmental conditions.
