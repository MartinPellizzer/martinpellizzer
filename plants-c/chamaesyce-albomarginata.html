Chamaesyce albomarginata

Chamaesyce albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin sandmat, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, sprawling stems and small, alternate leaves that often have a distinctive white or pale margin. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species often found in sandy or rocky habitats.
