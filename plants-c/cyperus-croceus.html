Cyperus croceus

Cyperus croceus, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces bright yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in dense, spike-like formations. Cyperus croceus thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in disturbed soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
