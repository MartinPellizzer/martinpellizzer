Chrozophora plicata

Chrozophora plicata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Chrozophora plicata is also known for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
