Cycnium bricchettii

Cycnium bricchettii, commonly known as the Bricchetti's gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and palmate leaves.

It produces yellow flowers that transition into elongated, spiny fruits, which are typically harvested when mature. The plant thrives in warm, sunny environments with well-drained soil, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions. Its botanical features include hairy stems and lobed foliage, which contribute to its distinct appearance.

This species is often grown for ornamental purposes or as a food source in certain cuisines.
