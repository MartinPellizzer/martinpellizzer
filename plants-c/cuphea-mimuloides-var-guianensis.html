Cuphea mimuloides var. guianensis

Cuphea mimuloides var. guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan Cuphea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink and orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves and produces showy bracts that enhance its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
