Calliandra spruceana

Calliandra spruceana, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces clusters of pink to red flowers. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in warm climates.

Calliandra spruceana also has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
