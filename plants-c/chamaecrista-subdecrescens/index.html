Chamaecrista Subdecrescens

Coral bean, scientifically known as Chamaecrista subdecrescens, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States. It is prized for its vibrant, flame-like flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer. The plant's feathery foliage and long, slender stems make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and resilience. While it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, it requires careful attention to avoid overwatering. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a standout addition to native plant gardens and landscapes.
