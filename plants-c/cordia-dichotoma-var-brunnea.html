Cordia dichotoma var. brunnea

Cordia dichotoma var. brunnea, commonly known as the brown cordia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
