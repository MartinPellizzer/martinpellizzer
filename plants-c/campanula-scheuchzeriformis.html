Campanula scheuchzeriformis

Campanula scheuchzeriformis, commonly known as the Scheuchzer's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in a fine, soft hair, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.
