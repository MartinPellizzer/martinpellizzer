Crassula mesembryanthoides subsp. hispida

Crassula mesembryanthoides subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the Wooly Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, woolly appearance.

The leaves are typically green or bluish-green, with a dense covering of fine white hairs. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the warmer months.

This hardy plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
