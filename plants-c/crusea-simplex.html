Crusea simplex

Crusea simplex, commonly known as the simple crusea, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

It is native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming low, spreading mats that thrive in partial shade. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Crusea simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with proper care.
