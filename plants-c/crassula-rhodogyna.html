Crassula rhodogyna

Crassula rhodogyna, commonly known as the pink crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically green with a pinkish or reddish tint. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
