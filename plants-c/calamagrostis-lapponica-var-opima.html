Calamagrostis lapponica var. opima

Calamagrostis lapponica var. opima, commonly known as the Arctic marsh grass, is a perennial grass species native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces tall, airy panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. This plant thrives in wet to moist environments, such as bogs, marshes, and along the edges of lakes. Its growth habit is typically clump-forming, and it is often found in arctic and alpine tundra habitats.

The plant's botanical features make it well adapted to cold climates and poor soil conditions.
