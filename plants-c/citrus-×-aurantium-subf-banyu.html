Citrus × aurantium subf. banyu

Citrus × aurantium subf.

banyu, commonly known as the Banyu citrus or Banyu mandarin, is a botanical variant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, round fruits with thin, smooth peels and a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are dark green, oval-shaped, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The Banyu citrus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for both its fruit and medicinal properties.
