Cryptocoryne crispatula var. albida

Cryptocoryne crispatula var. albida, commonly known as the white-leaf cryptocoryne, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, ribbon-like leaves that are typically pale green to white in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in low-light conditions and is often used in planted aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care. It prefers soft, acidic water with moderate nutrient levels and can grow in both shallow and deeper substrates.

Cryptocoryne crispatula var. albida is known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of water parameters, making it a popular choice among aquarium enthusiasts.
