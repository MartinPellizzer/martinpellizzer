Cavendishia gracilis

Cavendishia gracilis, commonly known as the slender cavendishia, is a rare and striking member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of Colombia and is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves that form a rosette. The plant produces vibrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is a sought-after species among bromeliad enthusiasts.
