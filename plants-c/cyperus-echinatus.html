Cyperus echinatus

Cyperus echinatus, commonly known as the spiny flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or muddy soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered with short, stiff hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Cyperus echinatus is known for its adaptability to wetland environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
