Cestrum talamancaense

Cestrum talamancaense, commonly known as the Talamancan jessamine, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while its stems are woody and often thorny.

The flowers, which are usually white or yellow, attract pollinators such as moths and bats due to their strong scent and nectar-rich blooms.
