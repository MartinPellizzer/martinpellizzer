Chloris pluriflora

Chloris pluriflora, commonly known as the common grassvetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense panicles. Chloris pluriflora is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
