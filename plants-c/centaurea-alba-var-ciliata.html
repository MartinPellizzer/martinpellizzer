Centaurea alba var. ciliata

Centaurea alba var. ciliata, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with hairy surfaces that give them a silvery appearance. This variety is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
