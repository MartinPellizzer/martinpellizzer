Cyperus hakonensis

Cyperus hakonensis, commonly known as the Hakonese flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus hakonensis is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its ability to form dense colonies and its adaptability to various water conditions.
