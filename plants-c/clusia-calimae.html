Clusia calimae

Clusia calimae, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It is well adapted to humid environments and thrives in partial to full shade.

Clusia calimae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
