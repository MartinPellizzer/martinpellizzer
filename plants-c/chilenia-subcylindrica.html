Chilenia subcylindrica

Chilenia subcylindrica, commonly known as the Chilean cylinder plant, is a unique species native to the coastal regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted to reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Chilenia subcylindrica is well-suited to rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes or dry shrublands.
