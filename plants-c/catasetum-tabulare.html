Catasetum tabulare

Catasetum tabulare, commonly known as the flat-leaf orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and striking, showy flowers that often have a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are known for their intricate structure, with specialized pollination mechanisms.

Catasetum tabulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
