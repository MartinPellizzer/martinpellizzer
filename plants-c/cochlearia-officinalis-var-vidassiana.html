Cochlearia officinalis var. vidassiana

Cochlearia officinalis var. vidassiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf scurvygrass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, alternate leaves that are often linear to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in coastal and maritime environments, often growing on sandy or gravelly soils.

This variety is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine due to its high vitamin C content.
