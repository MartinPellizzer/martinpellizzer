Capparis peduncularis

Capparis peduncularis, commonly known as the Indian caper or wild caper, is a shrub native to southern and southeastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, greenish fruits that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Capparis peduncularis is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils.
