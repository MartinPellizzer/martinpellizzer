Coleus platyphyllus

Coleus platyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its square stems and opposite leaf arrangement. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Coleus platyphyllus is primarily grown as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage rather than its flowers.
