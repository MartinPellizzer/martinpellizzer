Cirsium tuberosum proles anglicum

Cirsium tuberosum proles anglicum, commonly known as the English thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its root system is tuberous, which allows it to store nutrients and survive harsh conditions.

The plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
