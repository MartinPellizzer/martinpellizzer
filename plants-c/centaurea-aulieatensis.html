Centaurea aulieatensis

Centaurea aulieatensis, commonly known as the Aulieat's coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea aulieatensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
