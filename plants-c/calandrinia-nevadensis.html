Calandrinia nevadensis

Calandrinia nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear and gray-green, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Calandrinia nevadensis is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in desert environments.
