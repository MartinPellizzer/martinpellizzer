Crepis lapsanifolia

Crepis lapsanifolia, commonly known as the Lapland hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Crepis lapsanifolia produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its role in pollinator habitats.
