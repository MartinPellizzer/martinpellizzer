Catasetum dalastranum

Catasetum dalastranum, commonly known as the Dalastran orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are notable for their unique structure, featuring a specialized lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Catasetum dalastranum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
