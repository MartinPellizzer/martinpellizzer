Croton martinicensis

Croton martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique croton, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its foliage rather than its flowers.
