Chrysosplenium crenulatum var. atrodiscum

Chrysosplenium crenulatum var. atrodiscum, commonly known as black-veined chrysosplenium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive dark green, veined leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
