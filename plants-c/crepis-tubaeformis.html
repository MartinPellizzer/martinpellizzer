Crepis tubaeformis

Crepis tubaeformis, commonly known as the tubular sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, while its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive tubular shape.

Crepis tubaeformis is an annual plant that is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
