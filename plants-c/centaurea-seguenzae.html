Centaurea seguenzae

Centaurea seguenzae, commonly known as Seguenza's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and slender, upright stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight hairy texture.

Centaurea seguenzae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
