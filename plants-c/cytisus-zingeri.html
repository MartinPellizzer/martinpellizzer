Cytisus zingeri

Cytisus zingeri, commonly known as the Zinger's broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, often spiny stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae (legume) family. Its leaves are small, trifoliate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cytisus zingeri is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
