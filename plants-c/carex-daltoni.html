Carex daltoni

Carex daltoni, commonly known as Dalton's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex daltoni is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
