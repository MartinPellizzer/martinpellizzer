Conospermum ellipticum var. imbricatum

Conospermum ellipticum var. imbricatum, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Wallaby Bush, is a species native to southeastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry sclerophyllous forests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
