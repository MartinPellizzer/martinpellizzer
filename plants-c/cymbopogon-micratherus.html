Cymbopogon micratherus

Cymbopogon micratherus, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slender blades and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant contains essential oils, particularly citral, which contribute to its distinctive fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental and aromatic properties, it is cultivated for use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
