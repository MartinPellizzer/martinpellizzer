Clematis versicolor f. pubescens

Clematis versicolor f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent clematis, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China. The plant features compound leaves with three leaflets and produces fragrant, usually blue or purple flowers. Its pubescent (hairy) stems and foliage are notable botanical features.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
