Cururu curassavica

Cururu curassavica, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers, which are typically tubular and attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Cururu curassavica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in garden settings.
