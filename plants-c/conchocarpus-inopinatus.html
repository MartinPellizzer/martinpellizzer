Conchocarpus inopinatus

Conchocarpus inopinatus, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Its flowers are typically green or brown, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Conchocarpus inopinatus plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
