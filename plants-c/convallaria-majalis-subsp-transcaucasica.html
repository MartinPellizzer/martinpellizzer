Convallaria majalis subsp. transcaucasica

Convallaria majalis subsp.

transcaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The subspecies is closely related to the more widely known Convallaria majalis, but it exhibits distinct morphological characteristics.
