Crassula harveyi var. typica

Crassula harveyi var. typica, commonly known as the Harvey's Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and green, with a waxy texture to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor settings.
