Cyperus haspan var. adenophorus

Cyperus haspan var. adenophorus, commonly known as the woolly flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often covered in soft, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas, along stream banks, or in flooded fields.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
