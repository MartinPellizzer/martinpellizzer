Cattleya vestalis

Cattleya vestalis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on trees in its natural habitat.

Cattleya vestalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
