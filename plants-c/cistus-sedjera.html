Cistus sedjera

Cistus sedjera, commonly known as the Algerian rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Algeria.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and clusters of small, tubular pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Cistus sedjera is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
