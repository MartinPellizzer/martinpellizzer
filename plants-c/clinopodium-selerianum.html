Clinopodium selerianum

Clinopodium selerianum, commonly known as Seler's mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves. The plant features square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Seler's mint is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
