Crataegus arguta

Crataegus arguta, commonly known as the prickly ash or American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Crataegus arguta is adaptable to various soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
