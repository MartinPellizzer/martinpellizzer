Carex flavescens

Carex flavescens, commonly known as yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers in spikelets. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and pond edges.

Carex flavescens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
