Carduncellus caeruleus var. incisus

Carduncellus caeruleus var. incisus, commonly known as the blue thistle or blue cardoon, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features robust, spiny stems and large, showy blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant's leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and ecological benefits.
