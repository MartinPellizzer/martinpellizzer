Cirsium ficifolium

Cirsium ficifolium, commonly known as the bull thistle, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe, it has been introduced to North America and is considered an invasive species in many regions. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its leaves are usually broad and have a distinctive lobed shape, with a rough, hairy texture.
