Crepis kandavanensis

Crepis kandavanensis, commonly known as the Kandavan hairgrass, is a rare and endemic species native to the alpine regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in high-altitude environments. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is considered a vulnerable species.
