Calamus calospathus

Calamus calospathus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant features long, slender rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface.

The inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.
