Canna edulis

Canna edulis, commonly known as the edible canna, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful leaves and striking, spiky flower spikes that range in color from red to yellow. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomes are the primary edible part, often cooked and used in various traditional dishes.

Canna edulis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to provide food in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
