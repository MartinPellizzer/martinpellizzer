Cynorkis trilinguis

Cynorkis trilinguis, commonly known as the three-colored orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the warm season. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its unique floral display.
