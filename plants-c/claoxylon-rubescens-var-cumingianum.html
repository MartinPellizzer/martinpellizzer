Claoxylon rubescens var. cumingianum

Claoxylon rubescens var. cumingianum, commonly known as the Cuming's ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
