Calophyllum papuanum

Calophyllum papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan calophyllum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Calophyllaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Papua. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It grows as a small to medium-sized tree, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
