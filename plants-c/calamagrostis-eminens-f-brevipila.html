Calamagrostis eminens f. brevipila

Calamagrostis eminens f. brevipila, commonly known as the compact sweetgrass, is a grass species characterized by its dense, short growth habit.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often rolled at the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, feathery flowers. It is native to North America and thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and wet meadows.

This ornamental grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
