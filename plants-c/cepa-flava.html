Cepa flava

Cepa flava, commonly known as the yellow onion, is a bulbous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its layered, fleshy bulbs that store nutrients and water, allowing it to thrive in temperate climates. The plant produces grass-like leaves and tall, hollow stems that bear white or greenish flowers. Its bulbs are widely cultivated for culinary use due to their pungent aroma and flavor.

Cepa flava is a perennial plant that is often grown as an annual in agriculture.
