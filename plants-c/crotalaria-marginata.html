Crotalaria marginata

Crotalaria marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it an important species in ecological restoration efforts.
