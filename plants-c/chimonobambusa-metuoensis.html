Chimonobambusa metuoensis

Chimonobambusa metuoensis, commonly known as the Metuo bamboo, is a species of bamboo native to China, specifically found in the Yunnan Province.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its hollow, jointed stalks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is typically found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
