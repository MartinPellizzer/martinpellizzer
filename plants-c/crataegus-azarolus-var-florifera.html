Crataegus azarolus var. florifera

Crataegus azarolus var. florifera, commonly known as the flowering hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces thorned branches, which are characteristic of the Crataegus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The flowering hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
