Cestrum anagyris var. tomentosum

Cestrum anagyris var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly cestrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its pleasant evening fragrance.
