Celtis conferta subsp. amblyphylla

Celtis conferta subsp.

amblyphylla, commonly known as the smallleaf boxleaf elm, is a deciduous tree native to parts of eastern Asia, including China and Korea. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, spherical fruits. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in moist, temperate forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
