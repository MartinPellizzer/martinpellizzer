Centaurea nigra subsp. debeauxii

Centaurea nigra subsp.

debeauxii, commonly known as the black knapweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, dark purple to black flowers arranged in dense, spherical heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
