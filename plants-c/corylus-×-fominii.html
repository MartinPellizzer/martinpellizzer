Corylus × fominii

Corylus × fominii, commonly known as the Fomin hazelnut, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between the hazel (Corylus avellana) and the filbert (Corylus maxima).

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts enclosed in a spiky husk. The plant typically grows to a height of 2–5 meters and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its nuts are often used in culinary applications and can also be propagated through seeds or cuttings.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and agricultural potential.
