Calanthe vestita var. fournieri

Calanthe vestita var. fournieri, commonly known as the Fournier's velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with white petals and a deep violet lip. The plant typically blooms in spring, with flowers that are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Calanthe vestita var. fournieri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
