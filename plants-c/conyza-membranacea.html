Conyza membranacea

Conyza membranacea, commonly known as ragweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces a large amount of lightweight pollen, which is a common allergen for humans. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Conyza membranacea is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
