Cyperus mexicanus

Cyperus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically brown or reddish in color. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an invasive species in some areas.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
