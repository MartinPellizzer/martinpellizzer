Cyperus leucanthus

Cyperus leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus leucanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
