Clerodendrum grandiflorum

Clerodendrum grandiflorum, commonly known as the largeflowered cleerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
