Calamagrostis colorata var. variegata

Calamagrostis colorata var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated moorgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that display striking variegation of green, white, and gold. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for wetland or bog gardens.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for adding texture and color to landscapes.
