Carex lanceolata var. subpediformis

Carex lanceolata var. subpediformis, commonly known as the lance-shaped sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its foliage is usually bright green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

The variety subpediformis is distinguished by its elongated, pedunculate inflorescence, which sets it apart from other Carex species.
