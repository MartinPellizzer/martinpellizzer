Crataegus transmississippiensis

Crataegus transmississippiensis, commonly known as the plains hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies, woodlands, and along roadsides.
