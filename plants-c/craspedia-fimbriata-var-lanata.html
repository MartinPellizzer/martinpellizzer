Craspedia fimbriata var. lanata

Craspedia fimbriata var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
