Cissus thalictrifolia

Cissus thalictrifolia, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus thalictrifolia is also valued for its ability to climb and its potential use in traditional medicine.
