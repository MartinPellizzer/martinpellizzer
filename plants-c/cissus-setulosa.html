Cissus setulosa

Cissus setulosa, commonly known as the rock fig or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.

Cissus setulosa is also valued for its ability to climb and its air-purifying properties.
