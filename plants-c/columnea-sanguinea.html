Columnea sanguinea

Columnea sanguinea, commonly known as the blood vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, often contrasting with its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Columnea sanguinea is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional uses in local medicine.
