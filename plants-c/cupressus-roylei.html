Cupressus roylei

Cupressus roylei, commonly known as the Himalayan cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes worldwide.
