Carlina brachylepis var. lanigera

Carlina brachylepis var. lanigera, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
