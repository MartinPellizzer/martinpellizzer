Cacalia decumbens

Cacalia decumbens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with creeping stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded areas.

Cacalia decumbens is often found in dry slopes and rocky habitats, making it a hardy and resilient species.
