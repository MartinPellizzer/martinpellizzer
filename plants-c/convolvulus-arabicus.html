Convolvulus arabicus

Convolvulus arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, trailing stems that often form a dense mat on the ground. Its small, funnel-shaped flowers are typically pale yellow or white, with five petals and a distinctive tubular throat. The plant produces small, dry fruits called capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
