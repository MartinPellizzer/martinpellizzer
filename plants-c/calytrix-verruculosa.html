Calytrix verruculosa

Calytrix verruculosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a shrub-like appearance with rough, hairy leaves. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Calytrix verruculosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
