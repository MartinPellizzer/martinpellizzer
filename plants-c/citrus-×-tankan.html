Citrus × tankan

Citrus × tankan, commonly known as the kumquat.

It is a hybrid citrus fruit that originated from crosses between the mandarin and the pomelo. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is small, typically less than an inch in diameter, and has a thin, edible skin.

The fruit is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit, often used in culinary applications.
