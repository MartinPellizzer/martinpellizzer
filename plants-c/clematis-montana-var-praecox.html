Clematis montana var. praecox

Clematis montana var. praecox, commonly known as the early mountain clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
