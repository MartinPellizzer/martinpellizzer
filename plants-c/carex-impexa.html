Carex impexa

Carex impexa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex impexa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
