Celsia incana

Celsia incana, commonly known as the silverleaf celsia, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Celsia incana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
