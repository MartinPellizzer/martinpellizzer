Cordia cordifolia

Cordia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf cordia, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, the Caribbean, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds and other animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Cordia cordifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
