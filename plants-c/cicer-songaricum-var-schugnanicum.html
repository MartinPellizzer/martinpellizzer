Cicer songaricum var. schugnanicum

Cicer songaricum var. schugnanicum, commonly known as the Schugnan lentil, is a rare and ancient legume native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elongated seeds that are typically brown or gray in color. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers and has a bushy growth habit with trifoliate leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its historical significance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical and agricultural research.
