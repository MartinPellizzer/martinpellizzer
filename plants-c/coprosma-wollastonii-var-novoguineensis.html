Coprosma wollastonii var. novoguineensis

Coprosma wollastonii var. novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea coprosma, is a species native to tropical regions of New Guinea.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical gardens.
