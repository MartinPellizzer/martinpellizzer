Cyperus andreanus var. excelsior

Cyperus andreanus var. excelsior, commonly known as the tall flatsedge, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along water edges, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Cyperus andreanus var. excelsior is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
