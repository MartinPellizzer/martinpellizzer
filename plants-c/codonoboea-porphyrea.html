Codonoboea porphyrea

Codonoboea porphyrea, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Codonoboea porphyrea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
