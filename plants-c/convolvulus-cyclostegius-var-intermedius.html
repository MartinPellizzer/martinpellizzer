Convolvulus cyclostegius var. intermedius

Convolvulus cyclostegius var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features slender, trailing stems that produce heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant bears trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it versatile for various garden settings.
