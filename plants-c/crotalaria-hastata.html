Crotalaria hastata

Crotalaria hastata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant has hairy stems and produces small, elongated pods containing seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some areas for forage or as a cover crop.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
