Carex riparia f. reticulosa

Carex riparia f. reticulosa, commonly known as the reticulate marsh sedge, is a variant of the marsh sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered with a distinctive reticulate pattern, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

This form is adapted to moist soils and is often found in marshes, streambanks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
