Chrysochamela

Chrysochamela, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall, making it a prominent feature in gardens and landscapes.
