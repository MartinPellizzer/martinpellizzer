Celtis ehrenbergiana

Celtis ehrenbergiana, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and ornamental value.

Celtis ehrenbergiana is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resistance to urban environmental stressors.
