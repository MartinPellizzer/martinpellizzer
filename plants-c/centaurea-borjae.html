Centaurea borjae

Centaurea borjae, commonly known as the Borja's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Centaurea borjae is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
