Cheirinia lanceolata

Cheirinia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and bell-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Cheirinia lanceolata is a perennial species that produces small, tubular flowers attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
