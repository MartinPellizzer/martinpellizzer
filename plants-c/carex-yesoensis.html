Carex yesoensis

Carex yesoensis, commonly known as the Yesso sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of its stems.

Carex yesoensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
