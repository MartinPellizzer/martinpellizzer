Catasetum × eliasii

Catasetum × eliasii, commonly known as the Eliasii Catasetum, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that feature a unique structure with a specialized pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are known for their intricate morphology, including a labellum that plays a key role in attracting and guiding pollinators.

Catasetum × eliasii is valued by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and the complexity of its reproductive biology.
