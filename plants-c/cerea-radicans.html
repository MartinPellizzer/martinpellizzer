Cerea radicans

Cerea radicans, commonly known as the spreading root plant, is a unique species characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

It produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense mats on the ground. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
