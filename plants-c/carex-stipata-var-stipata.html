Carex stipata var. stipata

Carex stipata var. stipata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex stipata var. stipata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
