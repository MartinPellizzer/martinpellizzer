Cambessedesia membranacea subsp. membranacea

Cambessedesia membranacea subsp.

membranacea, commonly known as the membranaceous camphor tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers.

The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic trees such as cinnamon and laurel. Its bark is thick and peels in strips, revealing a reddish inner layer.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
