Chamaemelum conoclinium

Chamaemelum conoclinium, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in meadows and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used historically in herbal medicine and as a natural insect repellent.
