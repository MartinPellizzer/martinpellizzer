Coelogyne cinnabarina var. sanguineum

Coelogyne cinnabarina var. sanguineum, commonly known as the red-flowered cinnabar orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, red or orange-red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a pseudobulbous structure with fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in tropical climates. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
