Calytrix granulosa

Calytrix granulosa, commonly known as the rough calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant has small, granular leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. It is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees.

Calytrix granulosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sandy soils.
