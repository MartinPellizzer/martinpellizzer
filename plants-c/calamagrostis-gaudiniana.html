Calamagrostis gaudiniana

Calamagrostis gaudiniana, commonly known as the Gaudin's reed, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Calamagrostis gaudiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
