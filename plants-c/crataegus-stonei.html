Crataegus stonei

Crataegus stonei, commonly known as the Stone's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
