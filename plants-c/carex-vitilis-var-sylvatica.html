Carex vitilis var. sylvatica

Carex vitilis var. sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variety is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a valuable subject for ecological studies.
