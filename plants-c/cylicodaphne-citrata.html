Cylicodaphne citrata

Cylicodaphne citrata, commonly known as the lemon-scented daphne, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and fragrance.
