Costus speciosus var. angustifolius

Costus speciosus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flameplant, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in dense, showy inflorescences. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
