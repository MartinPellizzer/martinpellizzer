Celtis conferta

Celtis conferta, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Elm family (Ulmaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible berries that are a food source for various birds and mammals. Celtis conferta is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to different soil types.

It is also valued for its ability to thrive in urban environments.
