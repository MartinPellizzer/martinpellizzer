Cassia tomentosa var. paucijuga

Cassia tomentosa var. paucijuga, commonly known as the woolly cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves with a dense, woolly covering that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in elongated, pod-like structures that split open when mature.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
