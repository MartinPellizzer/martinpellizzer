Capparis fengii

Capparis fengii, commonly known as the Chinese caper, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its prickly stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. Its leaves are typically succulent and can be used as a vegetable in some regional dishes.

Capparis fengii thrives in warm, arid environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.
