Cyperus monandrus

Cyperus monandrus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green or brown. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in waterlogged soils.

Cyperus monandrus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
