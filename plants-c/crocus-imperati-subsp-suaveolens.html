Crocus imperati subsp. suaveolens

Crocus imperati subsp.

suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in Mediterranean climates, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its stronger fragrance compared to other crocus species.
