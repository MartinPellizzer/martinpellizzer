Cadetia subhastata

Cadetia subhastata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features slender stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Cadetia subhastata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
