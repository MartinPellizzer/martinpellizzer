Cucubalus multiflorus

Cucubalus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered cucumber, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and gourds. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in abundance. Its fruits are typically elongated, with a rough, warty skin and a sweet, edible flesh.

Due to its ornamental value and potential culinary uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
