Chaenorhinum rubrifolium subsp. raveyi

Chaenorhinum rubrifolium subsp.

raveyi, commonly known as the red-leafed horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking red foliage and delicate, papery flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its subspecies status highlights its distinct morphological features compared to the broader species.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
