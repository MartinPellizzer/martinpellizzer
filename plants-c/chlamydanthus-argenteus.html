Chlamydanthus argenteus

Chlamydanthus argenteus, commonly known as the silver thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly foliage and spiny, purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Chlamydanthus argenteus thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
