Clematis magniflora

Clematis magniflora, commonly known as the sweet autumn clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces blue to purple berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis magniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
