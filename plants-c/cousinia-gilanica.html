Cousinia gilanica

Cousinia gilanica, commonly known as the Gilan fescue, is a flowering plant native to the Caspian region of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite inflorescences, which are typical of the daisy family. Cousinia gilanica is adapted to moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.
