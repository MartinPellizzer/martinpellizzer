Chrysothamnus viscidiflorus subsp. latifolius

Chrysothamnus viscidiflorus subsp.

latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a sprawling growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Its foliage and flowers provide nectar for various pollinators, making it ecologically significant in its habitat.
