Chamaecrista carobinha

Chamaecrista carobinha, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Chamaecrista carobinha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
