Calamintha officinalis var. alba

Calamintha officinalis var. alba, commonly known as white hedge hyssop, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in whorls along the upper stem. Its leaves are narrow, serrated, and emit a strong, mint-like fragrance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical use in herbal medicine.
