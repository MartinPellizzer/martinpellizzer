Commicarpus verticillatus var. puberulus

Commicarpus verticillatus var. puberulus, commonly known as the hairy clustered legume, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, pod-like fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

This species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is of interest in ecological studies.
