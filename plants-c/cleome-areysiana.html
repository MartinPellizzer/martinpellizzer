Cleome areysiana

Cleome areysiana, commonly known as the yellow spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small spiders, hence its common name. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Cleome areysiana is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to two meters, making it a notable presence in its native habitat.
