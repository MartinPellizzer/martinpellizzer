Clusia polyantha

Clusia polyantha, commonly known as the wax tree or golden wax plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia polyantha is also known for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
