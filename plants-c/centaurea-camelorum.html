Centaurea camelorum

Centaurea camelorum, commonly known as the desert knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely hairy stems and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. Centaurea camelorum is typically found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its resilience to drought.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental species suitable for xeriscaping.
