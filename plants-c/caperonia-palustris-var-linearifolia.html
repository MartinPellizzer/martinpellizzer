Caperonia palustris var. linearifolia

Caperonia palustris var. linearifolia, commonly known as the slender swamp cinquefoil, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
