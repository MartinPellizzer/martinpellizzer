Cyperus eragrostis

Cyperus eragrostis, commonly known as flatleaf sedges, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, often growing in wet or moist environments such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, which are usually found at the tips of the stems.

Cyperus eragrostis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for various small organisms.
