Calycanthus floridus var. oblongus

Calycanthus floridus var. oblongus, commonly known as the Southern Sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.
