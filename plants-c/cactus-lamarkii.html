Cactus lamarkii

Cactus lamarkii, commonly known as the Lamark's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Cactus lamarkii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique structure and resilience.
