Carex rubrobrunnea var. rubrobrunnea

Carex rubrobrunnea var. rubrobrunnea, commonly known as the red-brown sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically a mix of red and brown hues, especially in the fall. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams.

This species is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
