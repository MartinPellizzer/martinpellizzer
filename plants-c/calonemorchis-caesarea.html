Calonemorchis caesarea

Calonemorchis caesarea, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
