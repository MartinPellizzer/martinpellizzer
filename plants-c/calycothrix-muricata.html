Calycothrix muricata

Calycothrix muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in stiff, spiny hairs, which provide a defensive mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a rough, textured surface, contributing to its common name.

Calycothrix muricata thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland shrubland habitats.
