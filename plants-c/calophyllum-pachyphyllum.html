Calophyllum pachyphyllum

Calophyllum pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or white in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is often valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
