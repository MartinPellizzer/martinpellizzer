Cactus ruficeps

Cactus ruficeps, commonly known as the red-headed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish stems and spiny, fleshy structures adapted for water storage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in environments with minimal rainfall. Its flowers, which appear in late spring, are usually yellow or orange and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
