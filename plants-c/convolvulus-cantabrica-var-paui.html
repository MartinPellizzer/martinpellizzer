Convolvulus cantabrica var. paui

Convolvulus cantabrica var. paui, commonly known as the Cantabrian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial with trailing stems that produce clusters of small, funnel-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple, and they bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
