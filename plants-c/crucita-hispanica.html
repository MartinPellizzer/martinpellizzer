Crucita hispanica

Crucita hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish crucifix or Spanish thorn, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, thorn-like branches. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Crucita hispanica is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.
