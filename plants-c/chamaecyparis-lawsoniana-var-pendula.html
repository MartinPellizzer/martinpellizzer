Chamaecyparis lawsoniana var. pendula

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cedar, is a cultivar of the Lawson cypress.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. This evergreen conifer has scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various climates.
