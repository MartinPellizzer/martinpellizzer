Cirsium nipponicum var. sadoense

Cirsium nipponicum var. sadoense, commonly known as the Sado thistle, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, pale purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
