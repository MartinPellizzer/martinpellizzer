Cerasus jenkinsii

Cerasus jenkinsii, commonly known as the Jenkins cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. The plant is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters, adding ornamental value to the plant.

Cerasus jenkinsii is often cultivated for both its edible fruits and its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
