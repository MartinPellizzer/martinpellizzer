Cephalomanes curvatum

Cephalomanes curvatum, commonly known as the curved-headed manes, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, curving inflorescence that resembles a mane, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a mix of white and green hues.

The botanical features of Cephalomanes curvatum make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
