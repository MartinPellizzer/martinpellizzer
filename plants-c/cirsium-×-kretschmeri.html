Cirsium × kretschmeri

Cirsium × kretschmeri, commonly known as the Kretschmer's thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that resemble those of other thistle species. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not properly managed.
