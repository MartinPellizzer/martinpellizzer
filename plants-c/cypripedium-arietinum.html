Cypripedium arietinum

Cypripedium arietinum, commonly known as the golden slipper orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including China, India, and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that resemble a slipper, with a golden-yellow coloration and intricate patterns. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it typically grows in shaded, moist environments.

Cypripedium arietinum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
