Cryptandra inconspicua

Cryptandra inconspicua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cryptandra, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green to yellowish in color. Cryptandra inconspicua is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants.

It is relatively uncommon and is often found in heathlands and woodland habitats.
