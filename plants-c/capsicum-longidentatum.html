Capsicum longidentatum

Capsicum longidentatum, commonly known as the long-toothed pepper, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that produces small, elongated fruits with a distinctive serrated or toothed appearance along the ridges. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its flowers are yellow and bilaterally symmetric, typical of the Solanaceae family.

The fruit is usually harvested when it turns from green to yellow or red, depending on the variety and maturity stage.
