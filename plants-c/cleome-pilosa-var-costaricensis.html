Cleome pilosa var. costaricensis

Cleome pilosa var. costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems and showy, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

The flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
