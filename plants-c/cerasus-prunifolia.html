Cerasus prunifolia

Cerasus prunifolia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet-tart fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn vibrant yellow in autumn.

Cerasus prunifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
