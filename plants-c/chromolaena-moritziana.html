Chromolaena moritziana

Chromolaena moritziana, commonly known as the tropical bluebush, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Chromolaena moritziana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
