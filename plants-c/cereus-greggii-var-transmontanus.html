Cereus greggii var. transmontanus

Cereus greggii var. transmontanus, commonly known as the Transmontanus night bloomer, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is a member of the Cactaceae family. The plant is known for its striking, large white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
