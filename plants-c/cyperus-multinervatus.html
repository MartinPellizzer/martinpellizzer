Cyperus multinervatus

Cyperus multinervatus, commonly known as the narrow-veined flatsedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant often grows in wetland or moist soil environments. Its root system helps in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in aquatic habitats.

Cyperus multinervatus is also used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in some regions.
