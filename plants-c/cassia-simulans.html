Cassia simulans

Cassia simulans, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces long, narrow leaves with a glossy green surface and a slightly serrated margin. Cassia simulans thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and seasonal display make it a notable species in tropical and subtropical landscapes.
