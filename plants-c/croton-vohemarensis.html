Croton vohemarensis

Croton vohemarensis, commonly known as the Vohemaren's croton, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, often colorful foliage, which can vary from green to red or yellow depending on the variety. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits.

Croton vohemarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
