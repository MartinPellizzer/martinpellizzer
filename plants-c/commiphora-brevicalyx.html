Commiphora brevicalyx

Commiphora brevicalyx, commonly known as the small-leaved myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and simple, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing aromatic resinous seeds.

The resin exuded from its bark has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
