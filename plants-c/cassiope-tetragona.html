Cassiope tetragona

Cassiope tetragona, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Pacific Northwest.

It is a low-growing shrub with dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves and showy pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and cool, shaded environments. Its unique structure and habitat make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.

Cassiope tetragona is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
