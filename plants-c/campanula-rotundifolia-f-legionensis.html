Campanula rotundifolia f. legionensis

Campanula rotundifolia f. legionensis, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a variant of the common bellflower native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rounded, lobed leaves and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
