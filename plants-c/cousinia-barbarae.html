Cousinia barbarae

Cousinia barbarae, commonly known as the Armenian hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Cousinia barbarae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
