Crataegus cuprea

Crataegus cuprea, commonly known as the red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus cuprea thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
