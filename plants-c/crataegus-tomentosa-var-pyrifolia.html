Crataegus tomentosa var. pyrifolia

Crataegus tomentosa var. pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white or gray pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include a thorny stem, simple leaves, and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
