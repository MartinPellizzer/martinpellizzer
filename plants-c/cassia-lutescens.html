Cassia lutescens

Cassia lutescens, commonly known as golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open woodland or along forest edges. Cassia lutescens is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.

It also has traditional uses in some cultures for medicinal purposes.
