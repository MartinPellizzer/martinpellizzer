Cyperus triceps var. obtusiflorus

Cyperus triceps var. obtusiflorus, commonly known as the broad-flower sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily in suitable habitats.
