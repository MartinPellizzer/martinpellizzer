Colutea fruticosa

Colutea fruticosa, commonly known as the shrubby corydalis, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, branching shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, typically with three to five leaflets, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Colutea fruticosa is often found in rocky or woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
