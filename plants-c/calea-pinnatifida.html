Calea pinnatifida

Calea pinnatifida, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, nocturnal-opening white flowers that emit a mild fragrance. The plant has a rosette-like arrangement of lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its botanical name, Calea, refers to the Latin word for "flower," highlighting its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its unique blooming behavior.
