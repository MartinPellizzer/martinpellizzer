Cassia scabrella

Cassia scabrella, commonly known as the rough cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, oblong seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Cassia scabrella is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
