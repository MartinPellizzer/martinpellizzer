Chloroluma saldanhaei

Chloroluma saldanhaei, commonly known as the Saldanha's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, delicate inflorescence with translucent petals. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are typically small, with a striking combination of white and green hues, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered critically endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
