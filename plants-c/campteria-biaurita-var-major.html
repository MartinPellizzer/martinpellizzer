Campteria biaurita var. major

Campteria biaurita var. major, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy bell-shaped flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in length, typically blooming in shades of blue or violet. The plant features sturdy, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
