Croton baillonianus var. submembranaceus

Croton baillonianus var. submembranaceus, commonly known as the red croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage.
