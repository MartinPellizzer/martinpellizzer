Ceropegia chlorantha

Ceropegia chlorantha, commonly known as the white-flowered ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow or white flowers that are often tubular in shape. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia chlorantha is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
