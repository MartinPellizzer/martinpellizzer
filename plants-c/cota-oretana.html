Cota oretana

Cota oretana, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cota oretana is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and resilience in dry, rocky soils.
