Chamaecrista fasciata

Chamaecrista fasciata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive velvety texture and its clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dry forests.

Chamaecrista fasciata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
