Clinopodium candidissimum

Clinopodium candidissimum, commonly known as white wood betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically arranged in whorls along the stem, blooming in shades of white to pale purple.

Clinopodium candidissimum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.
