Carex pseudobicolor

Carex pseudobicolor, commonly known as the pink sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Native to parts of North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pinkish in color.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
