Carex rhizina

Carex rhizina, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex rhizina has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
