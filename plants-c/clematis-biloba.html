Clematis biloba

Clematis biloba, commonly known as the twinleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with two opposite leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis biloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
