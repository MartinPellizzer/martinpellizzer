Calceolaria tetraphylla

Calceolaria tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leafed slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its striking, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and colorful blooms.

Calceolaria tetraphylla is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
