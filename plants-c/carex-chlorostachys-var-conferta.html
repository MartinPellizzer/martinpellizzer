Carex chlorostachys var. conferta

Carex chlorostachys var. conferta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are generally a dark green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
