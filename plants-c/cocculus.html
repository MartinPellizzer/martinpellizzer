Cocculus

Cocculus, commonly known as the devil's fig, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

It consists of several species, with Cocculus carolinus being the most well-known. The plant is characterized by its woody vines and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruit is a fleshy, indehiscent capsule that contains several seeds.

The botanical features of Cocculus make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ethnobotanical research.
