Cuphea cordifolia

Cuphea cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea cordifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
