Ceratonia chilensis

Ceratonia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean carob tree, is a leguminous plant native to southern Chile.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a thick, fissured bark. Its flowers are typically pale yellow and appear in clusters, while the fruit is a long, woody pod containing edible seeds.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions and is sometimes cultivated for its nutritious seeds and ornamental value.
