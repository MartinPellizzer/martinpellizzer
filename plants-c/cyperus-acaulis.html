Cyperus acaulis

Cyperus acaulis, commonly known as the spike rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. Cyperus acaulis typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of aquatic and wetland ecosystems.
