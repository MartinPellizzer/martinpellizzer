Clusia planchoniana var. oblongifolia

Clusia planchoniana var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed glory bush, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically elliptical or oblong in shape, often with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are borne in terminal panicles, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate partial shade.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
