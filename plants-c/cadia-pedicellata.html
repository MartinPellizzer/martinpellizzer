Cadia pedicellata

Cadia pedicellata, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help to reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
