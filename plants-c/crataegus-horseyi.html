Crataegus horseyi

Crataegus horseyi, commonly known as the Horsey hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
