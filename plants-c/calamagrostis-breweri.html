Calamagrostis breweri

Calamagrostis breweri, commonly known as Brewer's grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It produces feathery, silver-green plumes in late summer, which are visually striking.

Calamagrostis breweri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
