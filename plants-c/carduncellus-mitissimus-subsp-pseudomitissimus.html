Carduncellus mitissimus subsp. pseudomitissimus

Carduncellus mitissimus subsp.

pseudomitissimus, commonly known as the lesser cardoon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
