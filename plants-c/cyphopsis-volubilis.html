Cyphopsis volubilis

Cyphopsis volubilis, commonly known as the climbing lily, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is characterized by its scrambling growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters. Its leaves are strap-like and often have a bluish-green hue.

Cyphopsis volubilis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a climbing plant in shaded areas.
