Cyphostemma dasycarpum

Cyphostemma dasycarpum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellow, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along rocky outcrops and in dry woodlands.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
