Crupina insularis

Crupina insularis, commonly known as the island crupina, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in spikes and are usually pink or purple in color.

Crupina insularis is adapted to saline and arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
