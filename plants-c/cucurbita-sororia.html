Cucurbita sororia

Cucurbita sororia, commonly known as the Mexican moonseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, pumpkins, and gourds. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by green, oval-shaped fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

While not widely cultivated for food, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
