Crepidium myosotis

Crepidium myosotis, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and bloom during the late spring to early summer season.

Crepidium myosotis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
