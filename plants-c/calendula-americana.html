Calendula americana

Calendula americana, commonly known as the American marigold, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom from early summer to fall and are often used in herbal remedies and as a natural dye.

Calendula americana is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for skin healing and anti-inflammatory effects.
