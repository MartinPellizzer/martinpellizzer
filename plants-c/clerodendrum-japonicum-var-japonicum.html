Clerodendrum japonicum var. japonicum

Clerodendrum japonicum var. japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese glorybower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that produces striking clusters of tubular, fragrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
