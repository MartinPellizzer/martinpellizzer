Cyclosorus macropterus

Cyclosorus macropterus, commonly known as the broad-leafed water clover, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It features delicate, floating leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in ponds and slow-moving streams.

Cyclosorus macropterus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
