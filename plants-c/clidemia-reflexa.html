Clidemia reflexa

Clidemia reflexa, commonly known as the silverleaf mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Clidemia reflexa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some tropical gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
