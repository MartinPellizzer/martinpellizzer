Commiphora monstruosa

Commiphora monstruosa, commonly known as the giant myrrh tree, is a large, evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, rugged bark and can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. Its leaves are typically large, leathery, and elliptical in shape.

Commiphora monstruosa thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.
