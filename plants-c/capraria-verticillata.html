Capraria verticillata

Capraria verticillata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features whorled leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. It blooms in the spring and is often found in coastal areas and dry habitats.

Capraria verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
