Carex halleriana var. occultata

Carex halleriana var. occultata, commonly known as the hidden sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in wet or moist environments, making it a common sight in marshy areas and along stream banks. The plant's small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or brown, and it produces small, triangular seeds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
