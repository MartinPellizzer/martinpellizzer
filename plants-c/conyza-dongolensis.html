Conyza dongolensis

Conyza dongolensis, commonly known as the African ironweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to sub-Saharan Africa and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Conyza dongolensis is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
