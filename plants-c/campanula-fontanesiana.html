Campanula fontanesiana

Campanula fontanesiana, commonly known as the Fontanesian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
