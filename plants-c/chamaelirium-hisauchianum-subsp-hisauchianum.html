Chamaelirium hisauchianum subsp. hisauchianum

Chamaelirium hisauchianum subsp.

hisauchianum, commonly known as the Hisauchian False Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of East Asia. It typically grows in forested areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

The plant features slender, arching stems that bear small, greenish-white flowers in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
