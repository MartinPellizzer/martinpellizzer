Caltha polypetala

Caltha polypetala, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May, depending on the region. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in a dense cluster at the tip of a leafy stem, and they are often surrounded by a ring of bracts.

Caltha polypetala thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
