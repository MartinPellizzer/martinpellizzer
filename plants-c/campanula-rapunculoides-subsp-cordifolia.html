Campanula rapunculoides subsp. cordifolia

Campanula rapunculoides subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features basal and opposite leaves that are typically heart-shaped, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
