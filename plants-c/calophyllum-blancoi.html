Calophyllum blancoi

Calophyllum blancoi, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are encased in a thick, pinkish-purple rind. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
