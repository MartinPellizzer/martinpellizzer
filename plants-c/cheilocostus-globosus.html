Cheilocostus globosus

Cheilocostus globosus, commonly known as the globe ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, tuberous rhizomes and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, spicy fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
