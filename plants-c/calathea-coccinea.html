Calathea coccinea

Calathea coccinea, commonly known as the red-veined calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is prized for its striking, colorful foliage, featuring deep green leaves with vivid red and orange veins. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its unique leaf patterns make it a popular choice for indoor gardening enthusiasts.
