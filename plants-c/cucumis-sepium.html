Cucumis sepium

Cucumis sepium, commonly known as wild cucumber, is a herbaceous vine native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It produces large, green, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cooking. The plant has hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are alternate along the stem. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is typically spiky and turns yellow or orange when mature.
