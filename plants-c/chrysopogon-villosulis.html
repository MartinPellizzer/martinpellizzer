Chrysopogon villosulis

Chrysopogon villosulis, commonly known as the silky grass or silky beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and slender, often tufted inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Chrysopogon villosulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate appearance.
