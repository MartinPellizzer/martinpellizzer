Cyperus nyererei

Cyperus nyererei, commonly known as the African sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and along riverbanks. Its flowering spikes are usually cylindrical and contain small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus nyererei is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
