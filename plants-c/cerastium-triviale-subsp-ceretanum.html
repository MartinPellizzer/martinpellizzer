Cerastium triviale subsp. ceretanum

Cerastium triviale subsp.

ceretanum, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, hairy leaves.

The flowers are typically white or pink, borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
