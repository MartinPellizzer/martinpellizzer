Calanthe griffithii

Calanthe griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of China and northern India.

It is characterized by its upright, rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive purple and white coloration. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, with its flowers arranged in a raceme along the stem. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in two rows along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calanthe griffithii is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in shaded, moist environments.
