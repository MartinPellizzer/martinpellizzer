Cyperus globosus proles bubanii

Cyperus globosus proles bubanii, commonly known as the Bubanii flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
