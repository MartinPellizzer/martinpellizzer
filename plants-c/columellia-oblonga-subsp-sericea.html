Columellia oblonga subsp. sericea

Columellia oblonga subsp.

sericea, commonly known as the silky columellia, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

The flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and typically have a pale green to yellowish color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
