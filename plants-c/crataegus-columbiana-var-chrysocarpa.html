Crataegus columbiana var. chrysocarpa

Crataegus columbiana var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange berries that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white to pinkish flowers in early spring. Its botanical features include thorned stems, small ovate leaves, and a compact growth habit.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
