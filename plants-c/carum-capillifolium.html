Carum capillifolium

Carum capillifolium, commonly known as mountain parsley, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and small white or yellowish flowers that bloom in umbels. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Carum capillifolium is valued for its delicate fragrance and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
