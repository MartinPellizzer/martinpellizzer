Chirita bilabium

Chirita bilabium, commonly known as the two-lipped chirita, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, purple, or white. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value.
