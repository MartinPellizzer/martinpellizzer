Carex scitula

Carex scitula, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Carex scitula is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
