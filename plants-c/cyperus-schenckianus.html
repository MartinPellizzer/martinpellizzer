Cyperus schenckianus

Cyperus schenckianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually inconspicuous and wind-pollinated.

Cyperus schenckianus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in water features.
