Cousinia behboudiana

Cousinia behboudiana, commonly known as the Persian onion or Persian garlic, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Cousinia behboudiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local herbal medicine.
