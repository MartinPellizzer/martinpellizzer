Calligonum leucocladum var. strictum

Calligonum leucocladum var. strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf arrowgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale blue flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
