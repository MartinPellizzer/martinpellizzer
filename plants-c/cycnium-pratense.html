Cycnium pratense

Cycnium pratense, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Cycnium pratense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
