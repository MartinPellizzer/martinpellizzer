Calamus javensis var. polyphyllus

Calamus javensis var. polyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that often have a distinct waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a feature common in many members of the Araceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
