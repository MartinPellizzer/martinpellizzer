Cyperus rehmannianus f. minor

Cyperus rehmannianus f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf flatsedge, is a compact, herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas, ponds, or along waterways. Its botanical features include a short, rhizomatous stem and pale green to silver-gray foliage, making it a distinctive addition to aquatic or wetland gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wetland biodiversity.
