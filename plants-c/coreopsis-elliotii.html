Coreopsis elliotii

Coreopsis elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with a dark central disk, blooming from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Elliot's coreopsis thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
