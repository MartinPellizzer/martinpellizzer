Cactus muehlenpfordtii

Cactus muehlenpfordtii, commonly known as the Muehlenpfordt cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warm season, which are often followed by small, red fruits. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
