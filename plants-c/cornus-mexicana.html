Cornus mexicana

Cornus mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in the summer, attracting various birds and small mammals.

Cornus mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
