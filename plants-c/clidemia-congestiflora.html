Clidemia congestiflora

Clidemia congestiflora, commonly known as the Hawaiian miltweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. Clidemia congestiflora is an important component of native Hawaiian ecosystems, though it is considered an invasive species in some areas.
