Cerastium ovatum

Cerastium ovatum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, hairy leaves that are spoon-shaped and clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Cerastium ovatum is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its delicate appearance and widespread distribution.
