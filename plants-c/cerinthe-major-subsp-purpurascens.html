Cerinthe major subsp. purpurascens

Cerinthe major subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered cerinthe, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces slender, branching stems. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.
