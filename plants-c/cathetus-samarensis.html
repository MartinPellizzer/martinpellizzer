Cathetus samarensis

Cathetus samarensis, commonly known as the Samarensis Pitcher Plant, is a unique carnivorous plant native to the mountainous regions of Mindanao in the Philippines.

It is characterized by its elongated, tubular leaves that function as pitfall traps to capture and digest insects. The plant's botanical features include a slender, erect stem with dark green, glossy foliage. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and nutrient-poor soil.

Its specialized morphology reflects adaptations to its nutrient-deficient habitat, making it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
