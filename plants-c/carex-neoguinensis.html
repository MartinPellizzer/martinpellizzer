Carex neoguinensis

Carex neoguinensis, commonly known as the New Guinea sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex neoguinensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the tips of its stems.
