Cornus alba var. spaethii

Cornus alba var. spaethii, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red stems that become more vivid in the fall. The plant produces small white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
