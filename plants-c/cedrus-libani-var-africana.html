Cedrus libani var. africana

Cedrus libani var. africana, commonly known as the African cedar, is a subspecies of the Lebanon cedar.

It is a coniferous tree native to the mountains of North Africa, particularly in regions like Morocco and Algeria. The plant has needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are typically small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which has been used historically in construction and craftsmanship.
