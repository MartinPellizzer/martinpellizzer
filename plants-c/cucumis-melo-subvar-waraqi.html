Cucumis melo subvar. waraqi

Cucumis melo subvar. waraqi, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanically distinct variety of melon native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruits with a smooth, pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous vine with palmate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subvariety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
