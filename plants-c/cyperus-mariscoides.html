Cyperus mariscoides

Cyperus mariscoides, commonly known as marsh flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with three-ranked leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus mariscoides plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
