Cereus wercklei

Cereus wercklei, commonly known as the Werckle's night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and spines that are often pale or whitish in color. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance to attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
