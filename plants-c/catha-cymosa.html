Catha cymosa

Catha cymosa, commonly known as the African coffee plant, is a shrub native to the Horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.

Its leaves and seeds contain psychoactive compounds, making it culturally and pharmacologically significant in certain regions.
