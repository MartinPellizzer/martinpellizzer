Cryptocarya patentinervis

Cryptocarya patentinervis, commonly known as the broad-veined cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves with prominent, spreading veins. It typically grows in coastal and lowland forests, often in moist, shaded environments.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, and the fruit is a dark purple drupe.
