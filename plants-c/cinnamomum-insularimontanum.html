Cinnamomum insularimontanum

Cinnamomum insularimontanum, commonly known as the Moluccan cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the islands of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinct scent. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained, fertile soils. Its leaves are elongated and dark green, while its flowers are small and white, often clustered in spikes.

This species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.
