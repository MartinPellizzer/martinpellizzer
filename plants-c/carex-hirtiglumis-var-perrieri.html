Carex hirtiglumis var. perrieri

Carex hirtiglumis var. perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like flower clusters in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
