Calamus hartmannii

Calamus hartmannii, commonly known as Hartmann's arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clustered fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both practical and aesthetic purposes.
