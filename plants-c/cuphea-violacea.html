Cuphea violacea

Cuphea violacea, commonly known as the velvet tangerine plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround small, tubular flowers. The plant produces showy inflorescences that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

Cuphea violacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
