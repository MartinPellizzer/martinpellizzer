Curcuma coerulea

Curcuma coerulea, commonly known as blue ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are highly ornamental. Its rhizomes, while not as widely used for medicinal purposes as other curcuma species, are sometimes utilized in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
