Carapichea cardenasiana

Carapichea cardenasiana, commonly known as the Cuchumatan or Cuchumata, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, mountainous environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, usually in shades of white or pale yellow.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal blooms.
