Carex bigelowii subsp. paishanensis

Carex bigelowii subsp.

paishanensis, commonly known as the Paishan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland and riparian ecosystems.
