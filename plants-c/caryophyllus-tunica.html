Caryophyllus tunica

Caryophyllus tunica, commonly known as the tunica carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pink to white. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is native to regions of southern Africa, where it has adapted to dry, coastal climates.
