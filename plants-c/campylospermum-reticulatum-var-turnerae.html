Campylospermum reticulatum var. turnerae

Campylospermum reticulatum var. turnerae, commonly known as Turnera's net-veined mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, leathery leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern, and its flowers are typically white or greenish-white. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It is native to parts of Central and South America and is adapted to humid, tropical environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems where it is found.
