Calamagrostis chalybaea var. sibirica

Calamagrostis chalybaea var. sibirica, commonly known as Siberian reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features slender, bluish-green leaves that form dense clumps, and it produces feathery, silver-white plumes in late summer. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
