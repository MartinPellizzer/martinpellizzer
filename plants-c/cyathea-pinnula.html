Cyathea pinnula

Cyathea pinnula, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its fronds are typically a deep green, with a leathery texture, and it produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds.

Cyathea pinnula is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
