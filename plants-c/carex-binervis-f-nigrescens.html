Carex binervis f. nigrescens

Carex binervis f. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its dark green to almost black stems are a distinguishing feature, giving it its common name.

It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
