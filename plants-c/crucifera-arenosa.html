Crucifera arenosa

Crucifera arenosa, commonly known as sand mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
