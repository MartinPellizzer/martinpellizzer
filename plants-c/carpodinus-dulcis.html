Carpodinus dulcis

Carpodinus dulcis, commonly known as the sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Sweet clover is often used in agriculture as a forage crop and for soil enrichment.

Its flowers are also harvested for honey production due to their high nectar content.
