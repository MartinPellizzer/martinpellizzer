Calamintha georgiana

Calamintha georgiana, commonly known as George's catnip, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional herbal remedies.

Calamintha georgiana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
