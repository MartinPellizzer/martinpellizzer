Cordyline terminalis var. ferrea

Cordyline terminalis var. ferrea, commonly known as the red cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its distinctive, sword-shaped leaves that are typically a deep red or purple color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its vibrant foliage makes it an eye-catching addition to any tropical or subtropical setting.
