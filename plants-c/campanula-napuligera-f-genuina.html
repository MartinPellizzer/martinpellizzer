Campanula napuligera f. genuina

Campanula napuligera f. genuina, commonly known as the Alpina bellflower, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardiness.
