Casearia petraea

Casearia petraea, commonly known as the rock casearia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glandular. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
