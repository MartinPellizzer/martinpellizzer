Cynanchum denigratum

Cynanchum denigratum, commonly known as black swallowwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in umbels, which are followed by follicles containing seeds with tufts of white hairs.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and it has a somewhat invasive growth habit in some regions.
