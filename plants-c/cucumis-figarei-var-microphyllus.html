Cucumis figarei var. microphyllus

Cucumis figarei var. microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf fig leaf gourd, is a variant of the fig leaf gourd species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and elongated, thin fruits that are typically harvested when immature. The plant thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth. Its botanical features include a trailing or climbing habit, with pale green stems and waxy, textured foliage.

The fruit is edible when young, often used in various culinary preparations in its native regions.
