Cassia punctulifera

Cassia punctulifera, commonly known as the spotted cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets that often have a distinctive spotted or dotted appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, which are typical of the legume family. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia punctulifera thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
