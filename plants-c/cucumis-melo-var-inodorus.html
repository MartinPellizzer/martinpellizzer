Cucumis melo var. inodorus

Cucumis melo var. inodorus, commonly known as the casaba melon, is a variety of Cucurbitaceae family plant characterized by its distinctive appearance and flavor.

It typically features a round, pale yellow rind with a smooth texture and a sweet, mild, and slightly honeyed flavor. The fruit has a dense, crisp flesh that is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its versatility and is commonly cultivated in regions with suitable growing conditions.
