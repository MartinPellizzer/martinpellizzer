Castrea

Castrea, commonly known as the yellow wood or yellow cedar, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Central and South America. The plants are characterized by their aromatic foliage and showy, often fragrant flowers. Castrea species typically grow as trees or shrubs, with smooth, often reddish bark.
