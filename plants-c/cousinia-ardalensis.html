Cousinia ardalensis

Cousinia ardalensis, commonly known as the Armenian fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense mats or low shrubs. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in clusters.

Cousinia ardalensis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in areas with poor soil conditions.
