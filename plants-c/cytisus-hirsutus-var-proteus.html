Cytisus hirsutus var. proteus

Cytisus hirsutus var. proteus, commonly known as the velvet broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its adaptability and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for garden use.
