Cardamine schaffneri

Cardamine schaffneri, commonly known as Schaffner's watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a peppery or spicy aroma.

It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by tiny, round pods containing several seeds.
