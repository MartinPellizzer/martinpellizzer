Crataegus monogyna subsp. insegnae

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

insegnae, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring.

The plant produces red berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and open woodland.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
