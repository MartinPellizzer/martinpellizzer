Cassia bracteata

Cassia bracteata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia bracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
