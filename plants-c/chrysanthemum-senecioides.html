Chrysanthemum senecioides

Chrysanthemum senecioides, commonly known as the swamp chrysanthemum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, and has a sprawling growth habit. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow to orange composite flowers appear in late summer to early fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
