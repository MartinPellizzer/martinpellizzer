Ceropegia multiflora f. pubescens

Ceropegia multiflora f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ceropegia, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, trailing stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is often grown as a decorative plant due to its unique foliage and ability to cascade from hanging baskets.
