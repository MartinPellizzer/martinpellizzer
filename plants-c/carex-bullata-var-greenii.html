Carex bullata var. greenii

Carex bullata var. greenii, commonly known as the greenii sedge.

This variety is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Its leaves are usually a vibrant green, and it is valued in wetland restoration projects for its ecological benefits.
