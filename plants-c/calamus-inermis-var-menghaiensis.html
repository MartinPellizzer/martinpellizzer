Calamus inermis var. menghaiensis

Calamus inermis var. menghaiensis, commonly known as Menghai bamboo, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its long, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional practices in some cultures.
