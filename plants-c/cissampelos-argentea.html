Cissampelos argentea

Cissampelos argentea, commonly known as silverleaf moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery, waxy coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
