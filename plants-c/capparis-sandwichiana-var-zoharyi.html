Capparis sandwichiana var. zoharyi

Capparis sandwichiana var. zoharyi, commonly known as the Zohary's caper, is a shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, spiny leaves and yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are often used in local cuisine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, coastal climates.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a notable species in the region's flora.
