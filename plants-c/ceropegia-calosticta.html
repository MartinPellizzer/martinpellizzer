Ceropegia calosticta

Ceropegia calosticta, commonly known as the Zulu moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish. Its unique floral structure attracts specific pollinators, such as moths and bats.

Ceropegia calosticta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
