Cacalia fimbrillifera

Cacalia fimbrillifera, commonly known as the fringed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

The flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch of color to the landscape.
