Coelogyne josephii

Coelogyne josephii, commonly known as Joseph's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with fleshy leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically blooms during the winter months, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Coelogyne josephii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
