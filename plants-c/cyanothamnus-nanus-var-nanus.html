Cyanothamnus nanus var. nanus

Cyanothamnus nanus var. nanus, commonly known as the small bluebottle, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, typical of the Rutaceae family. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
