Chorizanthe californica

Chorizanthe californica, commonly known as the California burmallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, globe-shaped seed heads that resemble small, fuzzy balls. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked.

The plant is drought-tolerant and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
