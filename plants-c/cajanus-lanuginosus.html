Cajanus lanuginosus

Cajanus lanuginosus, commonly known as the hairy pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is often grown for its protein-rich seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable crop for sustainable agriculture.
