Crotalaria robynsii

Crotalaria robynsii, commonly known as the Carolina morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is an annual herb that produces striking violet to blue flowers with five petals. The plant has a slender, branching stem and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in the early morning, hence its common name, and it is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides.

Crotalaria robynsii is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
