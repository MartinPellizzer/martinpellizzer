Cirsium × stoderianum

Cirsium × stoderianum, commonly known as the Stöder's thistle, is a hybrid plant species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads, which are typically composite and consist of both ray and disk florets. Native to Europe, this plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. Its growth habit is perennial, with a robust root system that allows it to persist in various environmental conditions.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable example of hybridization in the genus Cirsium.
