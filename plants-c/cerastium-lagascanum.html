Cerastium lagascanum

Cerastium lagascanum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Cerastium lagascanum is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
