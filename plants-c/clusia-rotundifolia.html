Clusia rotundifolia

Clusia rotundifolia, commonly known as the fatshed or broadleaf poinsettia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, waxy leaves that are typically broad and rounded. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous yellow flowers, which are surrounded by colorful bracts that resemble petals. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Clusia rotundifolia is also known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of soil conditions and is relatively easy to care for.
