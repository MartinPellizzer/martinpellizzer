Cuscuta papillosa var. ambigua

Cuscuta papillosa var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has thin, thread-like stems that wrap around its hosts. The plant is native to North America and can be found growing on a variety of host species, including grasses and other flowering plants. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, and human activity, allowing it to spread widely.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken or even kill its host plants if left unchecked.
