Campylotropis tomentosipetiolata

Campylotropis tomentosipetiolata, commonly known as the woolly petioled milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves with long, petioled stipules. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Its root system fixes nitrogen, making it an important species in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
