Convolvulus capituliferus var. suberectus

Convolvulus capituliferus var. suberectus, commonly known as the upright bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate to ascending growth habit, with hairy stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming from spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
