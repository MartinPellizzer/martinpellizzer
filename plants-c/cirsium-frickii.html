Cirsium frickii

Cirsium frickii, commonly known as the Frick's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Cirsium frickii is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.
