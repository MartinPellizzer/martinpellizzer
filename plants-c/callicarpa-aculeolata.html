Callicarpa aculeolata

Callicarpa aculeolata, commonly known as the rough-leaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces showy, violet-purple berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges and a rough texture.

Callicarpa aculeolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
