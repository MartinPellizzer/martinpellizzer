Chenopodium × jehlikii

Chenopodium × jehlikii, commonly known as the hybrid goosefoot, is a species that results from the cross between Chenopodium album and Chenopodium berlandieri.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often ribbed and have a slightly tangy flavor. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Chenopodium × jehlikii is known for its adaptability and is often used as a forage plant for livestock.
