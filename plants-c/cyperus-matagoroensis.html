Cyperus matagoroensis

Cyperus matagoroensis, commonly known as the Matagoro flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and seasonally flooded areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Cyperus matagoroensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
