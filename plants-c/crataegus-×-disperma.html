Crataegus × disperma

Crataegus × disperma, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
