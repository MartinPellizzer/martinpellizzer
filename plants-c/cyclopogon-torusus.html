Cyclopogon torusus

Cyclopogon torusus, commonly known as the Torus mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Cyclopogon torusus is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas.
