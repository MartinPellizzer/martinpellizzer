Celtis henriquezii

Celtis henriquezii, commonly known as the Mexican hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, white flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, dark blue berries that are edible for some wildlife.

It is often found in dry to moist forests and is valued for its hardiness and ornamental qualities.
