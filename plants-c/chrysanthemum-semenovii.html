Chrysanthemum semenovii

Chrysanthemum semenovii, commonly known as the Semenov's chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers and robust, herbaceous stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its yellow to orange flower heads are composed of numerous small florets, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Chrysanthemum semenovii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
