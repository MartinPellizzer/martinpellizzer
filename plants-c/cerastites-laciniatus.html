Cerastites laciniatus

Cerastites laciniatus, commonly known as the cut-leaved sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
