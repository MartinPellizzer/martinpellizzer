Coleus exilis

Coleus exilis, commonly known as the slender coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage rather than its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often heart-shaped or ovate, with a velvety texture and a wide range of colors, including red, purple, and green.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
