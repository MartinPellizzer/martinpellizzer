Conospermum caeruleum subsp. caeruleum

Conospermum caeruleum subsp.

caeruleum, commonly known as the blue conospermum, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flower spikes that appear in spring.

The plant has narrow, grey-green leaves and is well adapted to dry, rocky soils. It is often found in heathlands and coastal areas, thriving in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
