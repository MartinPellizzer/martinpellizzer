Cimicifuga taiwanensis

Cimicifuga taiwanensis, commonly known as Taiwan bugbane, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the inflorescence.

Cimicifuga taiwanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
