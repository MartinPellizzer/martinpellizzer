Cordyline murchisoniae

Cordyline murchisoniae, commonly known as the Murchison cordyline, is a striking plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and rosette of large, strap-like leaves. The leaves are typically a deep green with a reddish tinge, especially when exposed to full sun. This plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
