Calanthe textorii var. longicalcarata

Calanthe textorii var. longicalcarata, commonly known as the long-spurred calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and striking, fragrant flowers with long, slender spurs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
