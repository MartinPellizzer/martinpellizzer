Carex subbracteata var. tolucensis

Carex subbracteata var. tolucensis, commonly known as the Tolucan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to wetland habitats and its role in stabilizing soil in such environments.
