Clematis venusta

Clematis venusta, commonly known as the velvet clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its deep green, velvety foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis venusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
