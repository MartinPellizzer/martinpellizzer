Crocanthemum corymbosum

Crocanthemum corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered tansy, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

Crocanthemum corymbosum is known for its ability to thrive in partial to full shade and its preference for well-drained soils.
