Caesalpinia mollis

Caesalpinia mollis, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features pinnately compound leaves and produces vibrant yellow or orange inflorescences that resemble plumes. Its flowers are followed by pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Caesalpinia mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
