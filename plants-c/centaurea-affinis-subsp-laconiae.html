Centaurea affinis subsp. laconiae

Centaurea affinis subsp.

laconiae, commonly known as the Laconian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow to orange-red composite flowers. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid environments and it is often found in southern Europe and parts of western Asia.
