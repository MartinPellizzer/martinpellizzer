Calendula graminifolia

Calendula graminifolia, commonly known as meadow saffron, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like stems and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Calendula graminifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
