Cordia macuirensis

Cordia macuirensis, commonly known as the Macuire's cordia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and sometimes hairy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.
