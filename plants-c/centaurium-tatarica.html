Centaurium tatarica

Centaurium tatarica, commonly known as Tatarian centaury, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it often grows in moist meadows and along stream banks.

Centaurium tatarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
