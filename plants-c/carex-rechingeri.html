Carex rechingeri

Carex rechingeri, commonly known as Rechinger's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to specific regions in Central Asia, this plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often densely packed, forming a compact rosette structure.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
