Centaurea fatoui

Centaurea fatoui, commonly known as the Fatou's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has slender, branched stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Centaurea fatoui is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
