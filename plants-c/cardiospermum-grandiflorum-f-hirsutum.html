Cardiospermum grandiflorum f. hirsutum

Cardiospermum grandiflorum f. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf.

This variant is characterized by its hairy foliage and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. It produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
