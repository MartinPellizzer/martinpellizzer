Chondrophora howardii

Chondrophora howardii, commonly known as the Howard's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, ghostly white flowers that bloom in late summer, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a short, rhizomatous root system and produces small, green leaves that are often hidden beneath the soil. It is considered a threatened species due to habitat loss and limited distribution.

Its botanical significance lies in its specialized ecological niche and its role in the conservation of rare orchid species.
