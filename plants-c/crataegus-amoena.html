Crataegus amoena

Crataegus amoena, commonly known as the sweet thorn or white thorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals but are generally not consumed by humans.

Crataegus amoena is valued in horticulture for its ornamental features and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
