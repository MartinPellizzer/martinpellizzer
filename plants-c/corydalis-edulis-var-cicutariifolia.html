Corydalis edulis var. cicutariifolia

Corydalis edulis var. cicutariifolia, commonly known as the slender corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features delicate, creeping stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
