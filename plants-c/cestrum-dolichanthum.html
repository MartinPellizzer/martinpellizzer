Cestrum dolichanthum

Cestrum dolichanthum, commonly known as the long-flowered cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, and features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers, which bloom in clusters, are usually white or pale blue, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, shiny seeds.
