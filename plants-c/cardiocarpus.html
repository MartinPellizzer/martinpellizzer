Cardiocarpus

Cardiocarpus, commonly known as the heartseed or false indigo, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive heart-shaped seeds, which are enclosed in a pod-like fruit. Cardiocarpus species are typically found in temperate regions and are often shrubs or small trees. They have compound leaves and produce small, usually blue or purple flowers.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine or as ornamental plants.
