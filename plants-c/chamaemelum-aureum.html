Chamaemelum aureum

Chamaemelum aureum, commonly known as golden yarrow, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically bright yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Chamaemelum aureum thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.
