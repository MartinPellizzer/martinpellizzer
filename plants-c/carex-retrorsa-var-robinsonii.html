Carex retrorsa var. robinsonii

Carex retrorsa var. robinsonii, commonly known as Robinson's sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. This variety is native to specific regions in North America, particularly in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with other wetland plants.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
