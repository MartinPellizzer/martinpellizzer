Centaurea alpestris

Centaurea alpestris, commonly known as Alpine knapweed, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats at high elevations. The plant features bright yellow to orange flowers arranged in a composite head, surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Centaurea alpestris plays a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas and serves as a food source for various pollinators.
