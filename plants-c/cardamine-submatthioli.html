Cardamine submatthioli

Cardamine submatthioli, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water-crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are typically submerged or floating on the surface of freshwater habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a loose cluster, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Cardamine submatthioli is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
