Cinnamomum jussieui var. oblongifolium

Cinnamomum jussieui var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed cinnamon tree, is a variant of the cinnamon genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark, leathery berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a spice.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties in horticulture.
