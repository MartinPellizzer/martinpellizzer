Capurodendron tampinense var. tampinense

Capurodendron tampinense var. tampinense, commonly known as the Tampinense flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange floral bracts that resemble flames, which appear during the dry season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure for optimal growth.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
