Cardamomum cereum

Cardamomum cereum, commonly known as black cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, aromatic pods that contain small, dark brown seeds. The plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated at high altitudes. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The seeds are prized for their strong, spicy aroma and are used extensively in both culinary and medicinal applications.
