Caryocar amygdaliforme

Caryocar amygdaliforme, commonly known as the Brazilian almond or patawa, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by large, round fruits with a hard, woody shell. The seeds inside the fruit are edible and have a nutty flavor, often used in local cuisine.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
