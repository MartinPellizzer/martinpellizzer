Colchicum schimperianum

Colchicum schimperianum, commonly known as the Schimper's colchicum, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a single, showy inflorescence with reflexed petals, and its leaves are basal, grass-like, and often persist through winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
