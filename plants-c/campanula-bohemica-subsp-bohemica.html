Campanula bohemica subsp. bohemica

Campanula bohemica subsp.

bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in woodland and meadow habitats, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
