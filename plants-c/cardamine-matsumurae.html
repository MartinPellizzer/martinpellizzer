Cardamine matsumurae

Cardamine matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese water shield, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features a rosette of basal leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers are arranged in a single umbel and bloom in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
