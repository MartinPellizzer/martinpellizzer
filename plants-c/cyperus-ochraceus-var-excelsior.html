Cyperus ochraceus var. excelsior

Cyperus ochraceus var. excelsior, commonly known as the tall flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish or brownish in color.

Cyperus ochraceus var. excelsior is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
