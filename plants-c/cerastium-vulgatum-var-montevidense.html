Cerastium vulgatum var. montevidense

Cerastium vulgatum var. montevidense, commonly known as the Montevideo mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
