Ceropegia mahabalei

Ceropegia mahabalei, commonly known as the Mahabaleshwar ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a succulent vine characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
