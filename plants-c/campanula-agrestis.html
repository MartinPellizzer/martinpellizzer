Campanula agrestis

Campanula agrestis, commonly known as the field bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender stems that produce clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and grayish-green in appearance.

Campanula agrestis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
