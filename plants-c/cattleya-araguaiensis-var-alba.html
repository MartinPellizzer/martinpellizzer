Cattleya araguaiensis var. alba

Cattleya araguaiensis var. alba, commonly known as the white Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, white flowers with a slight pinkish tinge and a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often reaching lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
