Chrysosplenium octandrum

Chrysosplenium octandrum, commonly known as the eight-angled spleenwort, is a member of the Saxifragaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five sepals and five petals, typically in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its unique leaf shape and delicate floral structure make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
