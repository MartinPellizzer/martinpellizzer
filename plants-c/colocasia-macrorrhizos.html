Colocasia macrorrhizos

Colocasia macrorrhizos, commonly known as the giant taro, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, with a glossy green surface and a deep veining pattern. The plant produces spadices, which are spathes that enclose the actual flowers, typically in shades of purple or white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its edible corms.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.
