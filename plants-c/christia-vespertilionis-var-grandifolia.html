Christia vespertilionis var. grandifolia

Christia vespertilionis var. grandifolia, commonly known as the evening bat plant, is a rare and specialized species native to specific regions with unique microclimates.

It is characterized by its large, showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as moths and bats. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, aiding in moisture retention.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected natural reserves.
