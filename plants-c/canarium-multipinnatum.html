Canarium multipinnatum

Canarium multipinnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves with multiple pairs of lateral lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
