Crepis desertorum

Crepis desertorum, commonly known as the desert hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Crepis desertorum is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.
