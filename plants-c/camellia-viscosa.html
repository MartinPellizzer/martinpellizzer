Camellia viscosa

Camellia viscosa, commonly known as the sticky camellia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, sticky aril. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Camellia viscosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
