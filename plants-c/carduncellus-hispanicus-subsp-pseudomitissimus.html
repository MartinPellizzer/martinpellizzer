Carduncellus hispanicus subsp. pseudomitissimus

Carduncellus hispanicus subsp.

pseudomitissimus, commonly known as the Spanish cardoon, is a thistle-like plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its hairy stems and robust growth make it a notable species in both wild and cultivated settings.
