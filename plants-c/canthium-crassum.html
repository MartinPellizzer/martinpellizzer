Canthium crassum

Canthium crassum, commonly known as the broad-leaved spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with thick, fleshy stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often grouped into cyathia, which are cup-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Canthium crassum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
