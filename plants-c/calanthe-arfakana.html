Calanthe arfakana

Calanthe arfakana, commonly known as the Arfak mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Calanthe arfakana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a subject of interest in botanical research due to its unique adaptations to its high-altitude habitat.
