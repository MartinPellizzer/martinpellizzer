Chamaecrista cuprea

Chamaecrista cuprea, commonly known as red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or subshrub characterized by its upright growth habit and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

Chamaecrista cuprea is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
