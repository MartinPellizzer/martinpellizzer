Croton attenuatus

Croton attenuatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to red, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage and floral display make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, tropical aesthetics.
