Campanula perpallens

Campanula perpallens, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it typically blooms in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula perpallens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
