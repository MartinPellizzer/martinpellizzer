Corallocarpus velutinus

Corallocarpus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet leaf or velvet tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy texture.

The flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often clustered in inflorescences, and the plant produces small, spherical fruits that are edible.
