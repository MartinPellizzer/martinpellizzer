Cyminosma oblongifolia

Cyminosma oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cumin tree, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique aroma and historical use, it has been cultivated for centuries in traditional herbal practices.
