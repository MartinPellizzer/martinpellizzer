Canna leptochila

Canna leptochila, commonly known as the slender cannas, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, and its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of brightly colored bracts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by the colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Canna leptochila thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for various pollinators and other wildlife.
