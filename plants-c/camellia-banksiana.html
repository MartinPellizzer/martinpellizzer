Camellia banksiana

Camellia banksiana, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant typically produces large, cup-shaped blooms in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a yellowish center. Camellia banksiana prefers partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and its long-lasting flowers.
