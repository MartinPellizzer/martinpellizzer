Colymbada dichroantha subsp. alpigena

Colymbada dichroantha subsp.

alpigena, commonly known as the alpine colymbada, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to high-altitude environments.
