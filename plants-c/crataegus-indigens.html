Crataegus indigens

Crataegus indigens, commonly known as the Mexican hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and it can reach heights of up to 6 meters.

Crataegus indigens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
