Costus arabicus

Costus arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian costus, is a perennial herb native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
