Clusia uvitana

Clusia uvitana, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.

Clusia uvitana is also known for its adaptability to partial shade and its use in landscaping.
