Caladenia patersonii var. dilatata

Caladenia patersonii var. dilatata, commonly known as the Paterson's Spider Orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, spider-like appearance with a single, erect flower that features a distinctive, elongated labellum. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils within open woodlands or heathlands. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with a complex structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

This variety is noted for its rarity and is often found in isolated populations, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
