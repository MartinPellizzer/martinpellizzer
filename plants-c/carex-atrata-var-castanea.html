Carex atrata var. castanea

Carex atrata var. castanea, commonly known as the chestnut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly glossy. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, clustered flowers, which are usually brown or reddish in color.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
