Carduus carlinoides var. nevadensis

Carduus carlinoides var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a basal rosette of leaves and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
