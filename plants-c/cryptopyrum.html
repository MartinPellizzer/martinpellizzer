Cryptopyrum

Cryptopyrum, commonly known as the field garlic or wild garlic, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaryllidaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like foliage and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils in temperate regions. Cryptopyrum species are often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
