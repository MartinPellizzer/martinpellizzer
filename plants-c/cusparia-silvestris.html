Cusparia silvestris

Cusparia silvestris, commonly known as the wild spiky grass, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive, spiky seed heads that resemble small, elongated cones. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed soils and thrives in full sun conditions. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Cusparia silvestris is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
