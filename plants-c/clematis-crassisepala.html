Clematis crassisepala

Clematis crassisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled clematis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a vine or shrub, with woody stems and palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Clematis crassisepala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
