Canthium oligophlebium

Canthium oligophlebium, commonly known as the narrow-veined spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and often spiny structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have prominent parallel veins, giving it its scientific name.

The flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
