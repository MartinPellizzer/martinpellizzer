Claytonia ptychosperma

Claytonia ptychosperma, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with fleshy, basal leaves and showy, violet or purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
