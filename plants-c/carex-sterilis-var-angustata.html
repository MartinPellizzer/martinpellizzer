Carex sterilis var. angustata

Carex sterilis var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three ranks, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a terminal inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Narrowleaf sedge plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
