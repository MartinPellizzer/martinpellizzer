Carmichaelia ramosa

Carmichaelia ramosa, commonly known as the coastal kowhai, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are often visited by native birds and insects. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and sometimes hairy, adapting to harsh coastal conditions.

Carmichaelia ramosa plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting local biodiversity.
