Cavendishia caudata

Cavendishia caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long, pendant-like flower spikes that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom sequentially along the inflorescence. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Cavendishia caudata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique and dramatic appearance.
