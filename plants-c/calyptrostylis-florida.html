Calyptrostylis florida

Calyptrostylis florida, commonly known as the Florida calyptrostylis, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a yellow or greenish hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

Calyptrostylis florida prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
