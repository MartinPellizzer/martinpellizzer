Canna coccinea var. floribunda

Canna coccinea var. floribunda, commonly known as the red flame cannas, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fiery red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish tint. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
