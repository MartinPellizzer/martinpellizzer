Camissonia pterosperma

Camissonia pterosperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Camissoniaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Camissonia pterosperma is adapted to Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers.
