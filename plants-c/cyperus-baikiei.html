Cyperus baikiei

Cyperus baikiei, commonly known as the Baikie's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its slender, triangular stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus baikiei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
