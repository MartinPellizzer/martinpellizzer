Cytisus ratisbonensis subsp. elongatus

Cytisus ratisbonensis subsp.

elongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring.

The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is known for its hardy nature.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
