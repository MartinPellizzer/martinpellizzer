Croton parksii

Croton parksii, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its striking foliage, it is cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
