Crataegus lapeyrousii

Crataegus lapeyrousii, commonly known as the Lapeyrouse hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in the fall.

Crataegus lapeyrousii is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
