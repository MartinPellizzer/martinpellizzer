Crepis elymaitica

Crepis elymaitica, commonly known as the Elymian hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by achene fruits with pappus for wind dispersal.

Crepis elymaitica is often found in disturbed or marginal areas, showing a preference for well-drained soils.
