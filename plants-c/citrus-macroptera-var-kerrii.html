Citrus macroptera var. kerrii

Citrus macroptera var. kerrii, commonly known as Kerri's kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The fruit is a small, orange-colored drupe with a thick, leathery rind that turns yellow when ripe. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tart flavor, and is often used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and unique fruit characteristics.
