Couepia rankiniae

Couepia rankiniae, commonly known as the Rankin's couepia, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its lanceolate leaves and dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, yellowish fruits. Couepia rankiniae is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments and is often found in understorey vegetation.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
