Cochlearia salsa

Cochlearia salsa, commonly known as the salt marsh cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, often found in salt marshes and coastal dunes. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

Cochlearia salsa is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
