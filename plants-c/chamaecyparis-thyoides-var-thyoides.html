Chamaecyparis thyoides var. thyoides

Chamaecyparis thyoides var. thyoides, commonly known as Atlantic white-cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the eastern coast of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

This species is valued for its durability and historical use in construction and shipbuilding.
