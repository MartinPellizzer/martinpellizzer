Callista binocularis

Callista binocularis, commonly known as the twin-eyed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble two eyes. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Callista binocularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
