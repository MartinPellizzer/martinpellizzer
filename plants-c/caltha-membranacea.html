Caltha membranacea

Caltha membranacea, commonly known as the membranous buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Caltha membranacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
