Crataegus pygmaea

Crataegus pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a small deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Crataegus pygmaea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
