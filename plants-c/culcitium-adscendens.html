Culcitium adscendens

Culcitium adscendens, commonly known as the climbing cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
