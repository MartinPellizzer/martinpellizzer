Centaurea montana var. adscendens

Centaurea montana var. adscendens, commonly known as the ascending mountain bluemink, is a variant of the mountain bluemink that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly the Alps and Carpathians. The plant features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, forming dense, fluffy inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny soils.
