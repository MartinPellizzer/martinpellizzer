Crotalaria shuklae

Crotalaria shuklae, commonly known as Shukla's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features palmately compound leaves and produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods, and it is often grown for its ornamental value.

Crotalaria shuklae is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
