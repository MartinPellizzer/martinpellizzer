Croton redolens var. genuinus

Croton redolens var. genuinus, commonly known as the fragrant croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that vary in color from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
