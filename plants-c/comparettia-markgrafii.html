Comparettia markgrafii

Comparettia markgrafii, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

This herbaceous plant features striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are highly fragrant.

Its unique appearance and fragrance make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
