Calliandra pittieri

Calliandra pittieri, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red bract-like flowers that are often mistaken for petals. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with bacteria. Its feathery foliage and showy inflorescences make it a popular ornamental plant.

Calliandra pittieri is also valued for its drought tolerance and adaptability to various soil types.
