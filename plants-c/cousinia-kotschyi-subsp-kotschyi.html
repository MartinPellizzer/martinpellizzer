Cousinia kotschyi subsp. kotschyi

Cousinia kotschyi subsp.

kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and basal rosettes of silver-gray, hairy leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow to orange composite flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures. Cousinia kotschyi subsp.

kotschyi is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
