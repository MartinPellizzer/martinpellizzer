Chamaecrista crenulata

Chamaecrista crenulata, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with palmate leaves that have lobes resembling a heart shape. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for coastal ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
