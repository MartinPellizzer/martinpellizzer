Cnicus ochroleucus

Cnicus ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers bloom in early summer, producing five petals that are often slightly notched.

Cnicus ochroleucus is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
