Carapa velutina

Carapa velutina, commonly known as velvet tree or Brazilian oil tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody fruits containing oily seeds. The seeds are rich in oil, which has been traditionally used for medicinal and industrial purposes.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat for various wildlife species in its native range.
