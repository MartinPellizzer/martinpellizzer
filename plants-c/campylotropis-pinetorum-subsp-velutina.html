Campylotropis pinetorum subsp. velutina

Campylotropis pinetorum subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a native, flowering shrub.
