Carex schottii

Carex schottii, commonly known as Schott's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
