Croton peltophorus var. cuzcoanus

Croton peltophorus var. cuzcoanus, commonly known as the Cuzco croton, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored leaves that display a variety of patterns and hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
