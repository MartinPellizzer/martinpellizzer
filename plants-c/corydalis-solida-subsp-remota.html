Corydalis solida subsp. remota

Corydalis solida subsp.

remota, commonly known as the slender corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia. It features delicate, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters along the stems.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders in horticulture.
