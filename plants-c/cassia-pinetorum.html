Cassia pinetorum

Cassia pinetorum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 8 to 12 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its flowers are followed by long, slender pods that contain several seeds.

Cassia pinetorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
