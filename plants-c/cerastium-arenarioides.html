Cerastium arenarioides

Cerastium arenarioides, commonly known as sand sandwort, is a low-growing flowering plant native to sandy and rocky habitats in Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Cerastium arenarioides is often found in coastal dunes, gravelly soils, and disturbed areas, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
