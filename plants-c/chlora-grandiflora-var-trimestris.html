Chlora grandiflora var. trimestris

Chlora grandiflora var. trimestris, commonly known as the Three-Season Orchid, is a striking perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 6 feet in height, adorned with clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant blooms in three distinct seasons—spring, summer, and fall—hence its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its long-lasting floral display.
