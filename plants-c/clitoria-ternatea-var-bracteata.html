Clitoria ternatea var. bracteata

Clitoria ternatea var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate winged bean, is a variant of the winged bean plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped, violet-blue flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds and can be used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.

The bracteate form is distinguished by its modified leaves that resemble bracts, enhancing its visual appeal.
