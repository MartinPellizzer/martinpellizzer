Chondrodendron scabrum

Chondrodendron scabrum, commonly known as the Andean cactus or the horned cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, including Ecuador, Peru, and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, adapted to its arid and high-altitude environment. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in the axils of the stems.

Chondrodendron scabrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
