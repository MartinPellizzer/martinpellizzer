Codonocalyx velleriflorus

Codonocalyx velleriflorus, commonly known as the silky flower vine, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
