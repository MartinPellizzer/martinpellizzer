Calligonum dielsianum

Calligonum dielsianum, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with low precipitation.

Its resilience makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
