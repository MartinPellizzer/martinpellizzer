Chrysanthemum × schousboeanum

Chrysanthemum × schousboeanum, commonly known as the garden chrysanthemum, is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its diverse range of flower colors, including yellow, orange, red, and white, and comes in various forms such as pompons, anemones, and spider varieties. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of 30 to 150 cm, with finely divided, feather-like leaves. Its flowers are composed of ray and disk florets, and it is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.

Chrysanthemum × schousboeanum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
