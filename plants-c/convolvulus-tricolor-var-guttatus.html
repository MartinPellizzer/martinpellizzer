Convolvulus tricolor var. guttatus

Convolvulus tricolor var. guttatus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and strikingly colorful flowers that range from blue to purple and pink. The plant produces small, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing along roadsides and in disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
