Cerastium latifolium var. glaucum

Cerastium latifolium var. glaucum, commonly known as the broadleaf mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are rounded and covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving them a silvery appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes.

This plant is often used in garden settings for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
