Centaurea zanonii

Centaurea zanonii, commonly known as the Zanonii knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features bright red to orange flowers, often with a distinctive central disk surrounded by bristly rays. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Centaurea zanonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
