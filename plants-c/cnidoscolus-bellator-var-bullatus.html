Cnidoscolus bellator var. bullatus

Cnidoscolus bellator var. bullatus, commonly known as the Bullatus Burr cucumber, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of stinging hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to edible, spiny fruits resembling small cucumbers. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical regions.
