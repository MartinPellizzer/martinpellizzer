Conchium ceratophyllum

Conchium ceratophyllum, commonly known as the horned water plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive, horn-like structures on its stems.

It is native to freshwater environments in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in slow-moving or still water. The plant has a branching, ribbon-like appearance with small, scale-like leaves that help it float and absorb nutrients. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in water gardens and ponds.
