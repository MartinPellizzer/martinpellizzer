Citrus × aurantium subf. benikawa

Citrus × aurantium subf.

benikawa, commonly known as Benikawa mandarin, is a hybrid citrus species native to Japan. It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, smooth peel and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The fruit is prized for its flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
