Ceropegia gordonii

Ceropegia gordonii, commonly known as the Gordon's African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, violet-colored blooms that are typically borne on leafless stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia gordonii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
