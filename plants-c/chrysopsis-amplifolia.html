Chrysopsis amplifolia

Chrysopsis amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf chrysopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its yellow flowers bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
