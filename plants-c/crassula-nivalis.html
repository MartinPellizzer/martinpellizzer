Crassula nivalis

Crassula nivalis, commonly known as the snowflake plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid environments.

Crassula nivalis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
