Cactus pringlei

Cactus pringlei, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and prominent, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white flowers. The plant has a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are typically yellow or orange in color.

Cactus pringlei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
