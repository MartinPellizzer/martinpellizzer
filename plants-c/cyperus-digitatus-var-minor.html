Cyperus digitatus var. minor

Cyperus digitatus var. minor, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a triangular cross-section, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in dense, cylindrical formations.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist soils.
