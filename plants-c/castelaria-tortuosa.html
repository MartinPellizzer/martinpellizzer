Castelaria tortuosa

Castelaria tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted castelaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Castelaria tortuosa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
