Campylotropis macrocarpa f. lanceolata

Campylotropis macrocarpa f. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate sweetclover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant is a perennial shrub that thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland edges and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
