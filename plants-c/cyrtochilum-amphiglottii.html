Cyrtochilum amphiglottii

Cyrtochilum amphiglottii, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy rhizomes and its distinctive, broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, arranged in a loose raceme. Cyrtochilum amphiglottii thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique and attractive species within the Orchidaceae family.
