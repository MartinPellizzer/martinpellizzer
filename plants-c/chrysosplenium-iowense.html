Chrysosplenium iowense

Chrysosplenium iowense, commonly known as Iowa golden spleenwort, is a small, herbaceous perennial belonging to the Saxifragaceae family.

It is native to the central United States, particularly Iowa, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features delicate, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape, with a bright green color and a waxy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in cymes, and produce yellowish-green petals that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
