Corispermum hookeri var. pseudodeclinatum

Corispermum hookeri var. pseudodeclinatum, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, pale green to yellowish leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green or white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
