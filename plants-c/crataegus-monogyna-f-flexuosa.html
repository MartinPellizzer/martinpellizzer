Crataegus monogyna f. flexuosa

Crataegus monogyna f. flexuosa, commonly known as the creeping hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its prostrate or creeping growth habit, making it well-suited for ground cover. The plant features small, oval leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in early spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the autumn.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
