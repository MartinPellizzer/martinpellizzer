Carex verna f. argyrata

Carex verna f. argyrata, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it a valuable plant for erosion control and wetland restoration.
