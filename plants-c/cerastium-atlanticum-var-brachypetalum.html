Cerastium atlanticum var. brachypetalum

Cerastium atlanticum var. brachypetalum, commonly known as Atlantic stellaria, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the coastal regions of the northeastern United States.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, typically from April to June. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal winds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal vegetation, contributing to soil stabilization and ecological diversity.
