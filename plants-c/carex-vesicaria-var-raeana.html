Carex vesicaria var. raeana

Carex vesicaria var. raeana, commonly known as the smooth marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to reddish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
