Conchocarpus gaudichaudianus

Conchocarpus gaudichaudianus, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, heavy wood and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its hard, durable timber.

The species also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
