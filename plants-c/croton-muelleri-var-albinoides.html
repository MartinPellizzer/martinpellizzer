Croton muelleri var. albinoides

Croton muelleri var. albinoides, commonly known as the white-flowered croton, is a striking tropical shrub characterized by its unique foliage and floral display.

It features broad, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in summer, adding a delicate contrast to its foliage. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
