Calanthe izu-insularis

Calanthe izu-insularis, commonly known as the Izu Islands Calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Japan.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb that supports its growth in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces showy, lance-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulb.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
