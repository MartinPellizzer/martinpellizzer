Corydalis daucifolia

Corydalis daucifolia, commonly known as the sea holly or sea hollyflower, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its blue to violet flowers and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils. The plant has a compact growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of delicate, spurred flowers.

Corydalis daucifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal conditions.
