Colchicum albofenestratum

Colchicum albofenestratum, commonly known as the white-veined colchicum, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, showy flowers with white petals and distinctive veining, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a short stem that supports its large, cup-shaped inflorescence, which contains several flowers. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are usually broad and lanceolate in shape.

Colchicum albofenestratum is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
