Ceanothus × vanrensselaeri

Ceanothus × vanrensselaeri, commonly known as the coast ceanothus, is a hybrid shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for coastal climates. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing year-round interest in the garden.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
