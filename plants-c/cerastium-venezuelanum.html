Cerastium venezuelanum

Cerastium venezuelanum, commonly known as the Venezuelan mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in dense, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often covered in fine, soft hairs.

Cerastium venezuelanum thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal regions.
