Chaetanthera cochlearifolia

Chaetanthera cochlearifolia, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and rockeries in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
