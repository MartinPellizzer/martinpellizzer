Conopodium denudatum subsp. daucifolium

Conopodium denudatum subsp.

daucifolium, commonly known as the slender burrweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features slender, erect stems with alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, and it produces burrs with hooked bracts that aid in seed dispersal.

The species is characterized by its unique morphology and adaptation to arid environments.
