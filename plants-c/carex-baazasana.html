Carex baazasana

Carex baazasana, commonly known as the Baazasana sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are usually green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Carex baazasana is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
