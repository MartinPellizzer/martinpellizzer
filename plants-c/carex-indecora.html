Carex indecora

Carex indecora, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer.

Carex indecora is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
