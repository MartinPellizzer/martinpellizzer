Cucurbita pepo var. grisea

Cucurbita pepo var. grisea, commonly known as the gray squash, is a variant of the common squash species.

It is characterized by its pale green to grayish skin and oblong, slightly elongated fruit shape. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, producing yellow flowers and edible fruits. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in culinary applications, particularly in traditional cuisines.
