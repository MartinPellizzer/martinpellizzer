Cestrum flavovirens

Cestrum flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is usually dark green and glossy, adding to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
