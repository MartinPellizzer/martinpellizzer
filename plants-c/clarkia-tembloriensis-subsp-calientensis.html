Clarkia tembloriensis subsp. calientensis

Clarkia tembloriensis subsp.

calientensis, commonly known as the Calientic Clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, adapting to harsh desert conditions. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is part of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
