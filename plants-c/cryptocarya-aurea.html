Cryptocarya aurea

Cryptocarya aurea, commonly known as the golden cryptocarya, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon trees, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight waxy coating. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by yellowish or orange fruits that are edible for certain native animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique fruiting habit.
