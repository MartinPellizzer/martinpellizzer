Clinopodium albanicum

Clinopodium albanicum, commonly known as albanian mint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are fragrant and have a mint-like scent, which is due to the presence of essential oils.

Clinopodium albanicum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
