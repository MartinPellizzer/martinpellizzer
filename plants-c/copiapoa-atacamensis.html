Copiapoa atacamensis

Copiapoa atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama cholla, is a cactus native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is adapted to extreme desert conditions with a deep root system and thick, water-storing stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
