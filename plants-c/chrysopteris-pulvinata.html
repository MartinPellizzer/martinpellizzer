Chrysopteris pulvinata

Chrysopteris pulvinata, commonly known as the golden leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves that contrast beautifully with its dark green stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by orange-red fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
