Chamaepitys laevigata

Chamaepitys laevigata, commonly known as the western redcedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark, which becomes scaly and fissured with age. The tree has flat, spruce-like needles and produces small, winged seeds in cones. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in mountainous regions.

The wood of Chamaepitys laevigata is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay.
