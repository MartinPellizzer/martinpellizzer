Codiaeum variegatum var. moluccanum

Codiaeum variegatum var. moluccanum, commonly known as the flame maple or moluccan croton, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Moluccas in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of red, orange, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
