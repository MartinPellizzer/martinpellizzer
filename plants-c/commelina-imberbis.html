Commelina imberbis

Commelina imberbis, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers with three petals, often accompanied by three white bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina imberbis is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
