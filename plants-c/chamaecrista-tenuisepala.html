Chamaecrista tenuisepala

Chamaecrista tenuisepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, pinnate leaves with narrow, linear leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by distinctive, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
