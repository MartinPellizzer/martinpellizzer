Centaurea pratensis subsp. debeauxii

Centaurea pratensis subsp.

debeauxii, commonly known as the Beaux's common centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in meadows and grasslands, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to purple flower heads. Its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
