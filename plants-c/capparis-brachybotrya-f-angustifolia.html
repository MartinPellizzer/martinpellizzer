Capparis brachybotrya f. angustifolia

Capparis brachybotrya f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
