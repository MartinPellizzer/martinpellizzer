Crassula hemisphaerica var. recurva

Crassula hemisphaerica var. recurva, commonly known as the Wooly Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, woolly leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with bright, indirect light.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
