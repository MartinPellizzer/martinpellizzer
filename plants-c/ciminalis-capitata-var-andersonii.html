Ciminalis capitata var. andersonii

Ciminalis capitata var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
