Chaenorhinum crassifolium var. pityusicum

Chaenorhinum crassifolium var. pityusicum, commonly known as the Canary Island Snapdragon, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous annual with lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
