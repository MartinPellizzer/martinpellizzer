Cyanopis erigeroides

Cyanopis erigeroides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cyanopis erigeroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
