Cyperus palmatus

Cyperus palmatus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and dense, umbrella-shaped clusters of small, pale green to white flowers. Cyperus palmatus typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
