Calamina humilis

Calamina humilis, commonly known as the low calamina, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species within its native range.
