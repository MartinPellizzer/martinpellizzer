Clematis albicoma var. coactilis

Clematis albicoma var. coactilis, commonly known as the white clematis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with slender, woody stems and compound leaves. Its botanical features include small, white petals and a hairy calyx, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add vertical interest to their landscapes.
