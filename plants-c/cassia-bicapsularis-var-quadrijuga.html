Cassia bicapsularis var. quadrijuga

Cassia bicapsularis var. quadrijuga, commonly known as the four-winged cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with four pairs of leaflets, giving it the "quadrijuga" designation. The plant produces small, yellow, fragrant flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a distinctive, four-winged capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
