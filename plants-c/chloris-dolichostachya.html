Chloris dolichostachya

Chloris dolichostachya, commonly known as the long-stemmed grass or slender chloris, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, elongated inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Chloris dolichostachya is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
