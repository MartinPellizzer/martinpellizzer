Calamagrostis nana subsp. nana

Calamagrostis nana subsp.

nana, commonly known as dwarf silvergrass, is a low-growing grass species native to temperate regions of Asia. It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are often described as having a silky texture.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued in horticulture for its attractive appearance and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
