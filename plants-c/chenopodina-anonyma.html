Chenopodina anonyma

Chenopodina anonyma, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

Chenopodina anonyma plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
