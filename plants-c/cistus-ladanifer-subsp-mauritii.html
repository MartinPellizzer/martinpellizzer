Cistus ladanifer subsp. mauritii

Cistus ladanifer subsp.

mauritii, commonly known as the Mauritius rockrose, is a shrub native to the island of Mauritius. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces a resinous substance known as ladanum, which is used in perfumery and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and Mediterranean climates.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is protected in its native region.
