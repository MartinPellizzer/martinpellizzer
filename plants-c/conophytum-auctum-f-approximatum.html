Conophytum auctum f. approximatum

Conophytum auctum f. approximatum, commonly known as the rock fig or stone plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
