Cirsium × bifrons

Cirsium × bifrons, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are arranged in a rounded cluster called a capitulum, and the plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its striking appearance.

Cirsium × bifrons is also known for its ability to hybridize with other thistle species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
