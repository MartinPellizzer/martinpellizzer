Cactus zuccarinii

Cactus zuccarinii, commonly known as the Zuccarini cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with thick, ribbed stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in hot, dry climates.
