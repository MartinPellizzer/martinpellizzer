Clematis chrysocoma var. laxistrigosa

Clematis chrysocoma var. laxistrigosa, commonly known as the golden clematis, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in length, often twining around supports.

The foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.
