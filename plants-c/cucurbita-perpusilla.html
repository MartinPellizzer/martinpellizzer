Cucurbita perpusilla

Cucurbita perpusilla, commonly known as the little bear gourd, is a small, vining plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and gourds. The plant produces small, pale green fruits that are typically less than 2 inches in diameter. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and historical significance in traditional medicine.
