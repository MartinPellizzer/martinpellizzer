Carex walkeri var. turrita

Carex walkeri var. turrita, commonly known as the Turritate sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
