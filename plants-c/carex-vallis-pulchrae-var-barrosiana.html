Carex vallis-pulchrae var. barrosiana

Carex vallis-pulchrae var. barrosiana, commonly known as the Barrosian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, three-sided seed capsules.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
