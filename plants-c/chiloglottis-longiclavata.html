Chiloglottis longiclavata

Chiloglottis longiclavata, commonly known as the long-clawed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred labellum and elongated, club-like petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowering period occurs during the cooler months, producing small, greenish-white flowers.

Chiloglottis longiclavata is a member of the Orchidaceae family and exhibits specialized pollination mechanisms involving specific insect species.
