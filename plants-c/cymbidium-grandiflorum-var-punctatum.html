Cymbidium grandiflorum var. punctatum

Cymbidium grandiflorum var. punctatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with erect, leafy stems that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The leaves are strap-like, broad, and often have a waxy texture. The flowers are typically white or pale pink with dark red or purple markings, giving them their distinctive appearance.

This orchid thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
