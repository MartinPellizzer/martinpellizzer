Cassia katangensis var. biensis

Cassia katangensis var. biensis, commonly known as the Katanga cassia, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. It produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The bark and leaves of this species have been used in traditional medicine for their potential therapeutic properties.
