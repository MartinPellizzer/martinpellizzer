Carex siroumensis

Carex siroumensis, commonly known as the Siroumense sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. Native to specific regions in Africa, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
