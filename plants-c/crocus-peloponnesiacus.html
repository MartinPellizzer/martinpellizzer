Crocus peloponnesiacus

Crocus peloponnesiacus, commonly known as the Peloponnesian crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Peloponnese in southern Greece.

It is a member of the Iris family, characterized by its compact, bulbous underground stem and delicate, early blooming flowers. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often appearing in late autumn or early winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable addition to Mediterranean flora.
