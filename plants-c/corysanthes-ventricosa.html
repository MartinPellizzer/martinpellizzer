Corysanthes ventricosa

Corysanthes ventricosa, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

Corysanthes ventricosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
