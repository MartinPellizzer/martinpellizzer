Croton clavuliger

Croton clavuliger, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and resilience in various environmental conditions.
