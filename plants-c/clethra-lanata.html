Clethra lanata

Clethra lanata, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Clethra lanata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
