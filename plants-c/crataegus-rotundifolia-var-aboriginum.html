Crataegus rotundifolia var. aboriginum

Crataegus rotundifolia var. aboriginum, commonly known as the Australian hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rounded, often lobed leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for some animals but generally not for humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland or open habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
