Carex fischeri

Carex fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of North America, particularly in the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. It produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are often brown or reddish in hue.

Carex fischeri prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
