Casuarina drummondiana

Casuarina drummondiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sheoak, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, needle-like foliage that forms dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, woody cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Casuarina drummondiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
