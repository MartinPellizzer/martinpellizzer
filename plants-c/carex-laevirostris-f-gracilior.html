Carex laevirostris f. gracilior

Carex laevirostris f. gracilior, commonly known as the slender-leaved sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
