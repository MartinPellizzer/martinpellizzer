Coris monspeliensis subsp. fontqueri

Coris monspeliensis subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as the Fontqueri's rockrose, is a member of the Geraniaceae family. It is a shrub characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers.

Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is adapted to sunny, arid environments and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
