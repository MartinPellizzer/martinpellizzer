Chrysojasminum humile var. microphyllum

Chrysojasminum humile var. microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf golden jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or undergrowth. Its leaves are often elliptical or ovate, with a smooth surface and a slight sheen.

The flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
