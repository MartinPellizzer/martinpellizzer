Celastrus kusanoi var. glaber

Celastrus kusanoi var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth-flowered bittersweet, is a variant of the Celastrus genus native to Japan.

It is a deciduous climbing shrub characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries that mature in the fall. It thrives in woodland areas and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
