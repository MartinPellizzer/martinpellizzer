Caragana afghanica

Caragana afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan shrub or Afghan bitterbrush, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in drought conditions.

Caragana afghanica is often used for erosion control and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
