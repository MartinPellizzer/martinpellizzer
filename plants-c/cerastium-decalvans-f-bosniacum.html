Cerastium decalvans f. bosniacum

Cerastium decalvans f. bosniacum, commonly known as the Bosnian mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented fruits. The plant typically features hairy stems and leaves that are oval to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are white or pale pink and attract various pollinators.
