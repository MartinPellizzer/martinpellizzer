Carex artinux

Carex artinux, commonly known as the artichoke sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by bracts.

Carex artinux thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
