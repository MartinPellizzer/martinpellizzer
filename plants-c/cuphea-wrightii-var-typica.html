Cuphea wrightii var. typica

Cuphea wrightii var. typica, commonly known as the scarlet wheelcup, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, wheel-shaped inflorescences that display vibrant red or orange hues. The plant features glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance and aid in moisture retention. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
