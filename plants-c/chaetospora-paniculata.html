Chaetospora paniculata

Chaetospora paniculata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that resemble coral or brush, hence its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Chaetospora paniculata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm climates.
