Clematis armandi var. retusifolia

Clematis armandi var. retusifolia, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, often with a slightly toothed margin. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its climbing habit and vibrant floral display.
