Cynosurus giganteus

Cynosurus giganteus, commonly known as giant tussock grass, is a tall, perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It forms dense, tufted clumps with long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its robust growth makes it an important component of the local ecosystem and a valuable forage plant for livestock.
