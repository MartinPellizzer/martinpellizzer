Chenopodium pseudoborbasii f. borbasiiforme

Chenopodium pseudoborbasii f. borbasiiforme, commonly known as the Borszcz or spinach weed, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically in late summer. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
