Campanula massalskyi

Campanula massalskyi, commonly known as the Massalskyi bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Campanula massalskyi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
