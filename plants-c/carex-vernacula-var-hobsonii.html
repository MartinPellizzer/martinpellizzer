Carex vernacula var. hobsonii

Carex vernacula var. hobsonii, commonly known as the Hobson's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
