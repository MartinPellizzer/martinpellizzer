Canarium asperum subsp. papuanum

Canarium asperum subsp.

papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to New Guinea and parts of Indonesia. It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits that contain a single seed. The fruit's oil is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
