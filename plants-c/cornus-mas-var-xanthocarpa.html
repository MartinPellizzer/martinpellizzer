Cornus mas var. xanthocarpa

Cornus mas var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow cornel.

This variant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its yellowish-orange fruits, which contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by the distinctive yellow berries.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
