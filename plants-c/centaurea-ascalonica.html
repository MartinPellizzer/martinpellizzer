Centaurea ascalonica

Centaurea ascalonica, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant purple-blue flowers. The plant features serrated, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, spiky seed heads. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Centaurea ascalonica is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
