Caragana sukiensis

Caragana sukiensis, commonly known as the Mongolian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caragana sukiensis plays a role in preventing soil erosion and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
