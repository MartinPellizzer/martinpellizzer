Cercocarpus intricatus var. typicus

Cercocarpus intricatus var. typicus, commonly known as the Sierra Madre buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces distinctive red or orange berries that are a key feature of its botanical appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its native range.
