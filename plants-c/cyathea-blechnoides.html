Cyathea blechnoides

Cyathea blechnoides, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a distinctive root system that forms a dense network underground, aiding in nutrient absorption. Its fronds are typically evergreen and can grow up to several meters in length.

Cyathea blechnoides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks.
