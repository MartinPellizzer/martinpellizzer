Carex hakodatensis

Carex hakodatensis, commonly known as Hakodate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and reeds. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers.

Carex hakodatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
