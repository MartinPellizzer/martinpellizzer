Carlina Hispanica Subsp. Globosa

Carlina hispanica subsp. globosa is a lesser-known yet fascinating plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, commonly known as the sunflower or daisy family. This subspecies is distinguished by its unique spherical flower heads and robust, hairy stems. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions. Its ecological significance and ornamental value make it a subject of growing interest among botanists and gardeners alike.
