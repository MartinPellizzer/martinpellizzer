Charpentiera densiflora

Charpentiera densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered charpentiera, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils within tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are usually simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture.

Charpentiera densiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
