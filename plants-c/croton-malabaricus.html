Croton malabaricus

Croton malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar croton, is a tropical shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from red to yellow and green, depending on the season and environmental conditions. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough texture, while the flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in cyathia.

Croton malabaricus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
