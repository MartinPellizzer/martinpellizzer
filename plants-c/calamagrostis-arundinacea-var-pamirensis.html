Calamagrostis arundinacea var. pamirensis

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. pamirensis, commonly known as the Pamir reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
