Calamagrostis neglecta subsp. micrantha

Calamagrostis neglecta subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelets that are usually pale blue or greenish.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
