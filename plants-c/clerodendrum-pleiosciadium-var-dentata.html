Clerodendrum pleiosciadium var. dentata

Clerodendrum pleiosciadium var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
