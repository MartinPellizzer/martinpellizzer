Crataegus × decipiens

Crataegus × decipiens, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in hedgerows for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
