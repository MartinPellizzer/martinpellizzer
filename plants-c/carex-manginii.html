Carex manginii

Carex manginii, commonly known as the manginii sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes and stream sides.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
