Calamus asteracanthus

Calamus asteracanthus, commonly known as the star-pointed arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on its bracts. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

Calamus asteracanthus is valued both for its medicinal properties and as a decorative plant in humid environments.
