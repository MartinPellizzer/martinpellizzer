Crambe edentula var. balchanica

Crambe edentula var. balchanica, commonly known as the Balchanica crambe, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers with four petals. The plant has palmate leaves with lobes that are often toothless, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
