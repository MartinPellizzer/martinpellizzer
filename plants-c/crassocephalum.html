Crassocephalum

Crassocephalum, commonly known as the "cow-calf plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, and often hairy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or broad-oval. The flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a prominent central disk surrounded by ray florets. Many species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

These plants are often used in traditional medicine and some species are cultivated as ornamental plants.
