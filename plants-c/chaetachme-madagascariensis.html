Chaetachme madagascariensis

Chaetachme madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass or velvet grass, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
