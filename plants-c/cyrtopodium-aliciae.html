Cyrtopodium aliciae

Cyrtopodium aliciae, commonly known as the Alice's coryphoe, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white with yellow markings, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Cyrtopodium aliciae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
