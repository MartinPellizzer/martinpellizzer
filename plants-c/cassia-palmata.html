Cassia palmata

Cassia palmata, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palm-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces pods that contain seeds. Cassia palmata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and vibrant blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
