Concocidium endocharis

Concocidium endocharis, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
