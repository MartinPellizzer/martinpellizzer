Cattleya bicolor f. alba

Cattleya bicolor f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and sepals, with a contrasting yellow lip that adds to its visual appeal. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the late spring or early summer.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and collectors.
