Corchorus echinatus

Corchorus echinatus, commonly known as the spiny kochia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, spiny capsules that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes other plants known for their medicinal and agricultural uses.
