Camellia remotiserrata

Camellia remotiserrata, commonly known as the remote serrated camellia, is a species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Camellia remotiserrata is a member of the Theaceae family, which includes other camellia species.
