Cucurbita cucullata

Cucurbita cucullata, commonly known as the hooded gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like shape and rough, warty surface. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, followed by fruits that can vary in color from green to orange. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in traditional culinary practices.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
