Citrus × limetta

Citrus × limetta, commonly known as the Meyer lemon.

It is a hybrid citrus fruit that originated in the early 20th century through the crossbreeding of the lemon and the mandarin. The plant is a small evergreen shrub with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is typically smaller than a regular lemon, with a thin, yellow-orange skin and a sweet, tangy flavor.

It is widely cultivated for both ornamental purposes and culinary use.
