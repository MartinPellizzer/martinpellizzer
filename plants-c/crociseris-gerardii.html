Crociseris gerardii

Crociseris gerardii, commonly known as the Gerard's goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies and marshes. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators.

Gerard's goldenrod is an important species for ecological diversity and is often used in restoration projects.
