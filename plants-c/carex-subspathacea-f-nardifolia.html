Carex subspathacea f. nardifolia

Carex subspathacea f. nardifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a variant of the sedge species Carex subspathacea.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a muted green in color. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or bog environments. It has a clump-forming growth habit and produces small, spiky inflorescences during the flowering season.

The foliage and structure of Carex subspathacea f. nardifolia make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
