Cytisus violaceus

Cytisus violaceus, commonly known as the purple broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and pairs of opposite, pinnate leaves. The plant produces bright purple or violet flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus violaceus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
