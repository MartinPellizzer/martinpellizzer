Calliandra asplenioides

Calliandra asplenioides, commonly known as the fern-like calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and prolonged blooming period. Calliandra asplenioides is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
