Catalpa fargesii f. duclouxii

Catalpa fargesii f. duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux's catalpa, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the catalpa tree native to China.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often a striking light green with a silvery underside. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters during the spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
