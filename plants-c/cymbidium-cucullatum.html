Cymbidium cucullatum

Cymbidium cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are strap-shaped and evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Cymbidium cucullatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
