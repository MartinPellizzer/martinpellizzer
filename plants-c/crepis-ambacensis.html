Crepis ambacensis

Crepis ambacensis, commonly known as the Ambacensis mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands. It features small, basal leaves and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in a head.
