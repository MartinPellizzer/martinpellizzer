Cyphia botrys

Cyphia botrys, commonly known as the clustered cyphia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cyphia botrys is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
