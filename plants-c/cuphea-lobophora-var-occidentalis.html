Cuphea lobophora var. occidentalis

Cuphea lobophora var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silky pubescence.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, sunny climates.
