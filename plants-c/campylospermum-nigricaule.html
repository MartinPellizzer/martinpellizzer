Campylospermum nigricaule

Campylospermum nigricaule, commonly known as the black-stemmed campylospermum, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
