Cissus parkeri

Cissus parkeri, commonly known as the African dewberry, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in small, white, waxy dots. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and ability to climb make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor displays.
