Clibadium angustifolium

Clibadium angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant has alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits known as capsules.

The plant is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.
