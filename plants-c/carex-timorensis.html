Carex timorensis

Carex timorensis, commonly known as the Timor sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the island of Timor and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically found in wetland or marshy habitats. It has fibrous roots and is often used in traditional landscaping due to its hardy nature.

Carex timorensis is valued for its ability to thrive in moist environments and its ornamental appeal.
