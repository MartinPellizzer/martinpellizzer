Carpinus orientalis var. macrocarpa

Carpinus orientalis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that are typically clustered in groups of three. Its flowers are inconspicuous, with male and female flowers occurring on separate trees.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
