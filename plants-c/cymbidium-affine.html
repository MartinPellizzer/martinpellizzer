Cymbidium affine

Cymbidium affine, commonly known as the common orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in winter or early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually green and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Cymbidium affine is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home garden setting.
