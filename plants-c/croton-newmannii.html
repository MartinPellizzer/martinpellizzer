Croton newmannii

Croton newmannii, commonly known as the Newmann's croton, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
