Cynara cardunculus var. sativa

Cynara cardunculus var. sativa, commonly known as the cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is cultivated for its edible stems and leaves, which are often used in cooking, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

The cardoon is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in herbal medicine.
