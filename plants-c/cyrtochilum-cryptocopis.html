Cyrtochilum cryptocopis

Cyrtochilum cryptocopis, commonly known as the cryptic orchid, is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, intricate flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern, often featuring a combination of white, green, and yellow hues. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for survival.

Cyrtochilum cryptocopis is a rare and specialized species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
