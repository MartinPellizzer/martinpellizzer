Carex clavata subsp. cylindracea

Carex clavata subsp.

cylindracea, commonly known as the slender club-sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.
