Carex disticha var. longibracteata

Carex disticha var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bracted sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
