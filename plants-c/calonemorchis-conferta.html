Calonemorchis conferta

Calonemorchis conferta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and mountainous areas. The plant features a slender, erect stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and usually have a pale green or white color with a subtle fragrance.

Calonemorchis conferta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
