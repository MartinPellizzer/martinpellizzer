Cissus spectabilis

Cissus spectabilis, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, silver-green leaves that have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
