Cereus × ruber

Cereus × ruber, commonly known as the red torch cactus, is a hybrid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny areoles, typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
