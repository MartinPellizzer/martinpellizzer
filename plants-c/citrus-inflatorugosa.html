Citrus inflatorugosa

Citrus inflatorugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf kumquat.

This plant is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and small, orange-colored fruits. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in culinary applications.

The plant is relatively low maintenance and thrives in warm, sunny climates.
