Calocephalus globosus

Calocephalus globosus, commonly known as the globe-headed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical seed head, which resembles a dandelion puffball. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is known for its yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, while the stems are upright and branching.

Calocephalus globosus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
