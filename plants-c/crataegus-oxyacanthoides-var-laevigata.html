Crataegus oxyacanthoides var. laevigata

Crataegus oxyacanthoides var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-flowered hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, adding to its ornamental value in gardens.
