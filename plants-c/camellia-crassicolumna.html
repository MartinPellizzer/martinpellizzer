Camellia crassicolumna

Camellia crassicolumna, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pink and white. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Camellia crassicolumna is also valued for its ability to produce edible oil from its seeds.
