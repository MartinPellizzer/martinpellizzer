Cereus × funkii

Cereus × funkii, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a hybrid cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically spiny and ribbed. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, often in shades of white or pale pink. These flowers emit a strong fragrance and are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.

Cereus × funkii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.
