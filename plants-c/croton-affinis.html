Croton affinis

Croton affinis, commonly known as the red-root croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage, with leaves that can range from green to red or yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its roots are thick and fleshy, which contribute to its common name.

Croton affinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
