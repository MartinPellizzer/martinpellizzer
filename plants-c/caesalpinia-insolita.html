Caesalpinia insolita

Caesalpinia insolita, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

The botanical features of Caesalpinia insolita make it a popular choice for gardens and public spaces due to its vibrant floral display.
