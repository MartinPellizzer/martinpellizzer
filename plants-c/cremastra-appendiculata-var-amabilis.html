Cremastra appendiculata var. amabilis

Cremastra appendiculata var. amabilis, commonly known as the showy false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring. Its flowers are clustered in a terminal umbel, and the plant is often found in moist woodlands and shaded areas.

The leaves and roots are toxic, containing cardiac glycosides that can be harmful if ingested.
