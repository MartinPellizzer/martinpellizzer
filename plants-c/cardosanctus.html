Cardosanctus

Cardosanctus, commonly known as the sacred cardoon, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, silvery-green leaves. The plant features a tall, sturdy stem with large, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its flowers, which bloom in the summer, produce showy yellow or orange blooms that attract pollinators.

Cardosanctus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical significance in both culinary and medicinal practices.
