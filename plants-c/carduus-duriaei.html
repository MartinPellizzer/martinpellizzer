Carduus duriaei

Carduus duriaei, commonly known as the Duriai thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. Its flowering heads are composed of many small florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Carduus duriaei is adapted to withstand drought and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
