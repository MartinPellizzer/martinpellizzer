Centaurea achaia subsp. achaia

Centaurea achaia subsp.

achaia, commonly known as the Achaean cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces vibrant blue to purple flowers in late summer. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
