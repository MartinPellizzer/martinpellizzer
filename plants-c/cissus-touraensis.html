Cissus touraensis

Cissus touraensis, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable environments.
