Clerodendrum schmidtii var. macrophyllum

Clerodendrum schmidtii var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant produces opposite, ovate leaves that are usually large and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
