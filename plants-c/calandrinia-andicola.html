Calandrinia andicola

Calandrinia andicola, commonly known as Andean portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that produces vibrant red or orange flowers, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves and stems, adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments. It is often found in high-altitude areas with well-drained soils.

Calandrinia andicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its colorful blooms.
