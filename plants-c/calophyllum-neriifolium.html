Calophyllum neriifolium

Calophyllum neriifolium, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its dark, heavy wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers followed by small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and has traditional uses in various cultural practices.
