Cynanchica affinis

Cynanchica affinis, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which is known for its milkweed species. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a hairy texture.

Cynanchica affinis is often found in open, sunny areas and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
