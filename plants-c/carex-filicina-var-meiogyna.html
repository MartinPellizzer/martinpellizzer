Carex filicina var. meiogyna

Carex filicina var. meiogyna, commonly known as the Meiogyna sedge, is a variant of the filicina sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like foliage and flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dark green coloration, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer.

This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
