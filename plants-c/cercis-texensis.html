Cercis texensis

Cercis texensis, commonly known as the Texas redbud, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

The Texas redbud is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to support local wildlife.
