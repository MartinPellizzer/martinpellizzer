Cerastium mairei

Cerastium mairei, commonly known as the Maire's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
