Clypeola montana

Clypeola montana, commonly known as the mountain clypeola, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and exhibits a compact growth habit with upright stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale blue, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Clypeola montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
