Cornucopiae altissimum

Cornucopiae altissimum, commonly known as the celestial bounty vine, is a striking plant characterized by its cascading clusters of vibrant, jewel-toned flowers that resemble miniature cornucopias.

Native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, it thrives in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and dark green, often speckled with silver markings that catch the sunlight. The plant produces an abundance of nectar-rich blooms, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and high-altitude farms worldwide.
