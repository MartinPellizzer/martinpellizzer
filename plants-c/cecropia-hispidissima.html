Cecropia hispidissima

Cecropia hispidissima, commonly known as the hairy cecropia, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, often hairy bark and large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cecropia hispidissima is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its ecological importance in supporting various insect and bird species.
