Cyperus psilostachys var. subrufus

Cyperus psilostachys var. subrufus, commonly known as the red-stemmed flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and flooded soils, and is often found in disturbed areas. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by water or wind.

The species is known for its adaptability to various moisture conditions, making it a common sight in wetland ecosystems.
