Cymbidium tortisepalum var. longibracteatum

Cymbidium tortisepalum var. longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract cymbidium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear numerous lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
