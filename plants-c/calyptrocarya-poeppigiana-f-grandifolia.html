Calyptrocarya poeppigiana f. grandifolia

Calyptrocarya poeppigiana f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered calyptrocarya, is a rare and distinctive plant native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features large, showy yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
