Cleistocactus winteri subsp. colademono

Cleistocactus winteri subsp.

colademono, commonly known as the Colademono cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina and southern Chile. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in dense, white to pale green spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, often before the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rocky or desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
