Cardamine hirsuta proles borbonica

Cardamine hirsuta proles borbonica, commonly known as the Bourbon hairy bittercress, is a perennial herb native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Thailand and Vietnam.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance.

The species is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.
