Chamaecyparis squarrosa

Chamaecyparis squarrosa, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or kurzel, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, scale-like leaves, and distinctive reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Chamaecyparis squarrosa is also valued for its aromatic wood, which has been used in traditional Japanese construction and crafts.
