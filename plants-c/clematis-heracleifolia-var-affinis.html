Clematis heracleifolia var. affinis

Clematis heracleifolia var. affinis, commonly known as the redstem clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated edge, and produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation. Its vigorous growth habit requires support structures, and it is valued for its ornamental flowers and autumn foliage.

Clematis heracleifolia var. affinis is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
