Citrus hystrix subsp. acida

Citrus hystrix subsp.

acida, commonly known as the sour orange, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, peels and small, sour fruits that are typically used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The plant is a shrub or small tree with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is valued for its high acidity and aromatic properties, making it an important crop in certain regions.
