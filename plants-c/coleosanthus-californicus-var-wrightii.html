Coleosanthus californicus var. wrightii

Coleosanthus californicus var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal sage scrub habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It has small, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

This variety is known for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local pollinators.
