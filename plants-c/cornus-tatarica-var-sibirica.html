Cornus tatarica var. sibirica

Cornus tatarica var. sibirica, commonly known as Siberian cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of eastern Europe and western Asia. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are ovate and have a serrated edge, turning red in the autumn.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in harsh environments.
