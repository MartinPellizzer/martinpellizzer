Crassula muscosa f. pseudolycopodioides

Crassula muscosa f. pseudolycopodioides, commonly known as the moss-like crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that resemble moss. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-draining soil. It prefers bright, indirect light and requires minimal water, making it suitable for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
