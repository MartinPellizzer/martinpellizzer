Calceolaria lasiocarpa

Calceolaria lasiocarpa, commonly known as the wooly slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that help it retain moisture in its high-altitude habitat. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Calceolaria lasiocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive blooms.
