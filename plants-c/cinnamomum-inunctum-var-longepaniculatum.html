Cinnamomum inunctum var. longepaniculatum

Cinnamomum inunctum var. longepaniculatum, commonly known as the longpanicle cinnamon.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a fragrant bark that is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their distinctive flavor.

The variety is distinguished by its elongated panicles, which are clusters of flowers.
