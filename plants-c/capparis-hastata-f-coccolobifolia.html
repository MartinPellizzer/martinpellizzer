Capparis hastata f. coccolobifolia

Capparis hastata f. coccolobifolia, commonly known as the Coccolobifolia Caper, is a rare and unique hybrid plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble those of the coccolobium genus. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, tart fruits.

Its botanical significance lies in its hybrid origin and its role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
