Condea anitae

Condea anitae, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, with upright stems and clustered foliage. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Condea anitae is known for its ability to thrive in humid environments, though it requires careful watering to avoid root rot.
