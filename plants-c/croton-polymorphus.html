Croton polymorphus

Croton polymorphus, commonly known as the variable croton, is a species of flowering plant in the spurge family, Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, which can range from elliptical to ovate or even triangular. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, and its bark contains milky sap that is toxic. Croton polymorphus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
