Cenchrus trisetus

Cenchrus trisetus, commonly known as the three-bristled grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged in panicles, with the lemma bearing three short bristles.

Cenchrus trisetus is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, open habitats.
