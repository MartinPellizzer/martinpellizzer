Cattleya sanguiloba

Cattleya sanguiloba, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking red and pink flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has long, arching stems that bear multiple flower spikes, each with several showy blooms. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in a fan-like pattern, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant flowers.

Cattleya sanguiloba is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific climate requirements.
