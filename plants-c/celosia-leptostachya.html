Celosia leptostachya

Celosia leptostachya, commonly known as the flame flower or crested grass.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance. It typically grows in open areas and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Celosia leptostachya is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and can be propagated through seeds or cuttings.
