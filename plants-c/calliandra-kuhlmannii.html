Calliandra kuhlmannii

Calliandra kuhlmannii, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink or red inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, feather-like flowers. The plant is a deciduous shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with compound leaves and a taproot system. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calliandra kuhlmannii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
