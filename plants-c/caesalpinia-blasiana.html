Caesalpinia blasiana

Caesalpinia blasiana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, feathery flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. Its seed pods are long, narrow, and woody, containing several seeds.

Caesalpinia blasiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
