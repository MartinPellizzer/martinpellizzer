Croton oreades var. periphoradenius

Croton oreades var. periphoradenius, commonly known as the mountain croton, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a rough texture and prominent veins. This variant is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often striking botanical features.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.
