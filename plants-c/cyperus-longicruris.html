Cyperus longicruris

Cyperus longicruris, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are often hidden among the foliage.

Cyperus longicruris is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
