Cousinia × androssowii

Cousinia × androssowii, commonly known as the Androssowii globemallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming basal leaves and tall, branched stems that bear showy, globular inflorescences. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, with five petals and numerous stamens, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

The plant is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
