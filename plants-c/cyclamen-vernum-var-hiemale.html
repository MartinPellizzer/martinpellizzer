Cyclamen vernum var. hiemale

Cyclamen vernum var. hiemale, commonly known as the winter cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It produces basal rosettes of velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant flowers in late autumn to early spring, producing delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
