Condalia warnockii

Condalia warnockii, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Condalia warnockii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
