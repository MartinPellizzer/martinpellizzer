Centaurea jacea subsp. angustifolia

Centaurea jacea subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features slender, upright stems and produces striking blue to purple radial flowers with prominent yellow centers.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
