Clematis acerifolia

Clematis acerifolia, commonly known as the mapleleaf clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate, maple-like leaves that are typically lobed and have a distinctive, finely toothed margin. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, usually in shades of white, pink, or lavender. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Clematis acerifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be trained to grow on trellises or fences.
