Chamaecytisus capitatus

Chamaecytisus capitatus, commonly known as the creeping tea or sweet honey plant, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
