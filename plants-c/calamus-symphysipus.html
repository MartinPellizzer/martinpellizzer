Calamus symphysipus

Calamus symphysipus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed calamus, is a perennial aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and narrow, ribbon-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes and ponds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is notable for its adaptability to wetland environments and its role in maintaining aquatic ecosystem stability.
