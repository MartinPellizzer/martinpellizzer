Carex crassicaulis

Carex crassicaulis, commonly known as broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
