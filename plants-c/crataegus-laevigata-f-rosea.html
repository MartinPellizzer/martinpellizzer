Crataegus laevigata f. rosea

Crataegus laevigata f. rosea, commonly known as the pink hawthorn, is a cultivar of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to pale red flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its foliage consists of oval, dark green leaves with serrated edges.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
