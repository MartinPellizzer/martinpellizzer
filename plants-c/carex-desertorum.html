Carex desertorum

Carex desertorum, commonly known as the desert sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its ecosystem.

Carex desertorum is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
