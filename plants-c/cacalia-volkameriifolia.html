Cacalia volkameriifolia

Cacalia volkameriifolia, commonly known as the Volkameriifolia bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
