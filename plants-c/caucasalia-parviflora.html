Caucasalia parviflora

Caucasalia parviflora, commonly known as the Caucasian dwarf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in well-drained conditions. It is a perennial species that prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
