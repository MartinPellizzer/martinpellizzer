Cogswellia daucifolia

Cogswellia daucifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf dandelion, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
