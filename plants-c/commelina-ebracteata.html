Commelina ebracteata

Commelina ebracteata, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
