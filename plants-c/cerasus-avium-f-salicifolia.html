Cerasus avium f. salicifolia

Cerasus avium f. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf cherry, is a unique variant of the bird cherry species.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are elongated and narrow, giving it a distinct appearance compared to typical cherry trees. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its foliage remains fresh and vibrant throughout the growing season, making it a desirable ornamental plant.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively low maintenance in suitable climates.
