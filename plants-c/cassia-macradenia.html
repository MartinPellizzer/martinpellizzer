Cassia macradenia

Cassia macradenia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, oval-shaped pods that contain several seeds. Cassia macradenia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets arranged along a central stem.
