Cephalostachyum pallidum

Cephalostachyum pallidum, commonly known as the pale leaching bamboo, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, clump-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white inflorescences that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its delicate foliage and unique flowering display.
