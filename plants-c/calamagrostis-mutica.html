Calamagrostis mutica

Calamagrostis mutica, commonly known as the rough bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers that are often tinged with purple or brown.

Calamagrostis mutica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
