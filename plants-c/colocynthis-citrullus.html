Colocynthis citrullus

Colocynthis citrullus, commonly known as the bitter apple or wild cucumber, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to green, spiny fruits resembling small cucumbers. The fruit contains a toxic, bitter pulp and seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.
