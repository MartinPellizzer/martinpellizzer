Caraipa heterocarpa

Caraipa heterocarpa, commonly known as the Caraipa fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, and its roots are known to have medicinal properties. The fruit, which is edible, contains seeds enclosed in a hard, woody capsule.

This species is valued for both its ecological role and potential applications in traditional medicine.
