Cytisus hirsutus var. capitatus

Cytisus hirsutus var. capitatus, commonly known as the clustered broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has slender, wiry stems covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
