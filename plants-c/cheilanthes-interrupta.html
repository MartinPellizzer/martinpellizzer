Cheilanthes interrupta

Cheilanthes interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted lip fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, intricately divided fronds that typically have a bluish-green hue. Native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is drought-tolerant and often grows on limestone or sandstone outcrops.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in xerophytic ecosystems.
