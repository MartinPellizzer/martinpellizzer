Caragana nepalensis

Caragana nepalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan shrub or Nepal bush clover, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is well adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in rocky soils and high altitudes. It plays a vital role in preventing soil erosion and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Caragana nepalensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
