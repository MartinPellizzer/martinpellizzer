Campylotropis polyantha

Campylotropis polyantha, commonly known as the Chinese peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens and landscapes.
