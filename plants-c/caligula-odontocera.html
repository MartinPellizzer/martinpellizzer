Caligula odontocera

Caligula odontocera, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and silver-gray, fleshy leaves that are adapted to salty, windy environments. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing along shorelines and dunes.

Caligula odontocera is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
