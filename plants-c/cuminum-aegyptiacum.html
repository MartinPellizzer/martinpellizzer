Cuminum aegyptiacum

Cuminum aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has feathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and cooking. Its seeds, harvested when fully mature, are valued for their strong, spicy flavor and various medicinal properties.

Cuminum aegyptiacum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy and adaptable crop.
