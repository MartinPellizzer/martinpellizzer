Carex exerta

Carex exerta, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
