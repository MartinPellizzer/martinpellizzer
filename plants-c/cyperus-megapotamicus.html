Cyperus megapotamicus

Cyperus megapotamicus, commonly known as the Amazon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its morphology includes slender, triangular stems and small, three-ranked leaves.

Cyperus megapotamicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
