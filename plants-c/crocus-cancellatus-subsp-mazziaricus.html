Crocus cancellatus subsp. mazziaricus

Crocus cancellatus subsp.

mazziaricus, commonly known as the Mazziarica crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family. It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern Italy and parts of Sicily.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive, pale yellow to white flowers with a speckled pattern on the petals. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clumps.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
