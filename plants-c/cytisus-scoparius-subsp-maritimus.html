Cytisus scoparius subsp. maritimus

Cytisus scoparius subsp.

maritimus, commonly known as the coastal broom, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa. It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves and produces long, cylindrical pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates.

This subspecies is adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.
