Coccoloba caribaea

Coccoloba caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually pink or white, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, waxy texture.
