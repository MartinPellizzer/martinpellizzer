Crossandra puberula var. smithii

Crossandra puberula var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
