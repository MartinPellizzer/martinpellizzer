Crateva simplicifolia

Crateva simplicifolia, commonly known as the Indian butter bean or velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. Its growth habit includes both annual and perennial forms, depending on the climate.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional systems of medicine.
