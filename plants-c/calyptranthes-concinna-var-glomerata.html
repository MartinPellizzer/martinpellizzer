Calyptranthes concinna var. glomerata

Calyptranthes concinna var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered calyptranthes, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid climates.
