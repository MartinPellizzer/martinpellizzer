Cassia leschenaultiana var. pacifica

Cassia leschenaultiana var. pacifica, commonly known as golden wattle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, fluffy flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is a symbol of Australian biodiversity and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value.
