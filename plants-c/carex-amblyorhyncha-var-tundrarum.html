Carex amblyorhyncha var. tundrarum

Carex amblyorhyncha var. tundrarum, commonly known as the tundra sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. It plays an important role in tundra ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

Its morphology reflects its evolutionary adaptation to harsh environmental conditions.
