Chrysothamnus parryi subsp. bolanderi

Chrysothamnus parryi subsp.

bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, yellow-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
