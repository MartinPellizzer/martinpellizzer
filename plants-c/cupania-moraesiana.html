Cupania moraesiana

Cupania moraesiana, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries, which are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species and is of interest for conservation and horticultural research.
