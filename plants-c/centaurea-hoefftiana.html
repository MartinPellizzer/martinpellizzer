Centaurea hoefftiana

Centaurea hoefftiana, commonly known as the Hoefft's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center, blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Centaurea hoefftiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
