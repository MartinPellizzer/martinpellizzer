Clematis ranunculoides var. cordata

Clematis ranunculoides var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white during the summer months. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
