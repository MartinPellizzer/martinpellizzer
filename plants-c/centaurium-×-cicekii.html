Centaurium × cicekii

It is a natural hybrid resulting from the cross between Centaurium erythraea and Centaurium umbrosum. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.
