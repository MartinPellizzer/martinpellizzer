Crotalaria flavicoma

Crotalaria flavicoma, commonly known as the yellow crown flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Crotalaria flavicoma is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

However, some species in the genus are known to be toxic, so caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
