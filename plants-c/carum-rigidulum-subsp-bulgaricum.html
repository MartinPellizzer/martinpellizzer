Carum rigidulum subsp. bulgaricum

Carum rigidulum subsp.

bulgaricum, commonly known as Bulgarian caraway, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and compound umbels.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its small, white flowers are followed by oval, aromatic seeds that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The subspecies is distinguished by its rigid stems and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other Carum species.
