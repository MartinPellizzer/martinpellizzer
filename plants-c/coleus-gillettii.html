Coleus gillettii

Coleus gillettii, commonly known as the red-stemmed coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often brightly colored in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, clustered in whorls along the stems.

Coleus gillettii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
