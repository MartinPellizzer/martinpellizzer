Carex ericetorum subsp. approximata

Carex ericetorum subsp.

approximata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and heathlands.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. It is often found in shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is part of the Carex genus, which includes many other sedges with similar ecological and botanical characteristics.
