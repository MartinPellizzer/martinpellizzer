Crataegus assurgens

Crataegus assurgens, commonly known as the smooth crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature.

Crataegus assurgens is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
