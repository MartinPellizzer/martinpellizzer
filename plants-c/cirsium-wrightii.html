Cirsium wrightii

Cirsium wrightii, commonly known as Wright's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, showy purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with rough textures. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
