Carex cespitosa f. retorta

Carex cespitosa f. retorta, commonly known as the curled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant features narrow, cylindrical leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it its distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

The inflorescence consists of a single, erect spike of small, three-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
