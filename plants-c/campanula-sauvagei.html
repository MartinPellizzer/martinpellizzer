Campanula sauvagei

Campanula sauvagei, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Campanula sauvagei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
