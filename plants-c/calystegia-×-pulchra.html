Calystegia × pulchra

Calystegia × pulchra, commonly known as the showy morning glory, is a hybrid vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often opening in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, often fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Calystegia × pulchra is commonly cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
