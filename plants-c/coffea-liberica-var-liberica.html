Coffea liberica var. liberica

Coffea liberica var. liberica, commonly known as the Liberica coffee, is a species native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and robust, woody stems. The plant produces red, fleshy berries containing two seeds, which are used for coffee production. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is less commonly cultivated than Arabica or Robusta but is valued for its unique flavor profile.
