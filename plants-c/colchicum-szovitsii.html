Colchicum szovitsii

Colchicum szovitsii, commonly known as the Szovits' colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically purple or violet in color, with six petals that are reflexed at the tips.

Colchicum szovitsii is adapted to grow in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
