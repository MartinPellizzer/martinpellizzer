Cydonia japonica var. gaujardii

Cydonia japonica var. gaujardii, commonly known as the Japanese quince.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia, characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that ripen in late summer, which are typically used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
