Crataegus coccinea var. flabellata

Crataegus coccinea var. flabellata, commonly known as the red-flowered hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that appear in early spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges. Its botanical features include a woody stem with thorny branches, and it is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
