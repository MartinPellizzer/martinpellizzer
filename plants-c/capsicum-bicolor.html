Capsicum bicolor

Capsicum bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bi-colored fruits, which typically feature a mix of yellow and red hues. The plant is an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that mature to a vibrant red.

Capsicum bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild to spicy flavor, making it popular in both culinary and horticultural contexts.
