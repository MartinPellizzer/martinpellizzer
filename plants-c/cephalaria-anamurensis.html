Cephalaria anamurensis

Cephalaria anamurensis, commonly known as the Turkish cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
