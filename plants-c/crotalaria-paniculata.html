Crotalaria paniculata

Crotalaria paniculata, commonly known as the panicled cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant features erect, branched stems and produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers that are often accompanied by distinctive leaflets. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
