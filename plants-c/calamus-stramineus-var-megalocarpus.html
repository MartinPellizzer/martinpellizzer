Calamus stramineus var. megalocarpus

Calamus stramineus var. megalocarpus, commonly known as the giant calamus, is a variant of the calamus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, rhizomatous plant that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its large, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clustered, elongated inflorescences with small, yellowish flowers, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
