Campanula spicata subvar. canescens

Campanula spicata subvar. canescens, commonly known as the gray bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to central and eastern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically grayish-green and covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow.
