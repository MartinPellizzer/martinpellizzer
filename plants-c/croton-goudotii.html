Croton goudotii

Croton goudotii, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic plants. The plant features alternate, simple, and often lobed leaves that vary in shape depending on the species. Its flowers are small and clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, typical of the genus Croton.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
