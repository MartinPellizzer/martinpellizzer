Camellia illia

Camellia illia, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant produces elliptical, woody seed pods that contain small, hard seeds. Camellia illia thrives in partially shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in traditional landscapes.
