Croton laetifolius

Croton laetifolius, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display shades of yellow, red, and orange, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes other members known for their milky sap. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

Croton laetifolius thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
