Chailletia setosa

Chailletia setosa, commonly known as the woolly chailletia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
