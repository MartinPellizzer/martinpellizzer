Calceolaria juncalensis

Calceolaria juncalensis, commonly known as the mountain calceolaria, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, resembling small cups or bells. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to the harsh climate of its native habitat.

Calceolaria juncalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
