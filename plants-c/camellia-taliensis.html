Camellia taliensis

Camellia taliensis, commonly known as the Taliensis camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its leathery, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking, double or semi-double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in winter and early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

Camellia taliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
