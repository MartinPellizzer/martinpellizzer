Cotoneaster niger subsp. slavicus

Cotoneaster niger subsp.

slavicus, commonly known as Black Cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and small, glossy leaves that turn red in the fall.

The plant produces clusters of white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its attractive appearance and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and horticulture.
