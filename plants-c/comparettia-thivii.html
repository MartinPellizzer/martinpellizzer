Comparettia thivii

Comparettia thivii, commonly known as the Thivi's cymbidium, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with multiple blooms, each featuring a spurred lip and a velvety texture. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence.

Comparettia thivii prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
