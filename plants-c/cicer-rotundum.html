Cicer rotundum

Cicer rotundum, commonly known as the globular vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, pod-like fruits that contain several small, edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in early summer.

Cicer rotundum is adapted to dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
