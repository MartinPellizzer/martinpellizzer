Cyperorchis rosea

Cyperorchis rosea, commonly known as the rose orchid, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence and are often visited by pollinators like bees and moths.

Cyperorchis rosea is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
