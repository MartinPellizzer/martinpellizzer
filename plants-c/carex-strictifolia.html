Carex strictifolia

Carex strictifolia, commonly known as stiffleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its upright, grass-like foliage is characterized by narrow, stiff leaves that are often dark green in color.

Carex strictifolia produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
