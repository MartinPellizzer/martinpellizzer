Convolvulus valentinus f. simulans

Convolvulus valentinus f. simulans, commonly known as the Valentine's convolvulus, is a flowering plant in the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant pink or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Its growth habit is typically prostrate or trailing, making it suitable for hanging baskets or ground cover.
