Clethra gelida

Clethra gelida, commonly known as the frosty clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are elliptical and turn a striking red or orange in the fall.

Clethra gelida is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
