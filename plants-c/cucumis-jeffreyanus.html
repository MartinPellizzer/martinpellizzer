Cucumis jeffreyanus

Cucumis jeffreyanus, commonly known as the Jeffrey's cucumber, is a rare and little-known member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in parts of Kazakhstan and Afghanistan. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically green and have a mild, cucumber-like flavor. It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, palmately lobed leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
