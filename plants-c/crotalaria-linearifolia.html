Crotalaria linearifolia

Crotalaria linearifolia, commonly known as the slender cowpea or linearleaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop and has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

Crotalaria linearifolia is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
