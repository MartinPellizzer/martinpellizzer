Caragana frutescens var. pendula

Caragana frutescens var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping barberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a distinctive cascading growth habit with long, drooping branches that create a soft, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Its compact size and unique form make it a popular choice for hedging and landscape design.
