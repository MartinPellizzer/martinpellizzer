Capparis spinosa subsp. cordifolia

Capparis spinosa subsp.

cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features woody stems and produces distinctive, fleshy, yellowish flowers.

The leaves are heart-shaped, which gives the subspecies its common name, and are often covered in fine hairs. The plant is known for its edible flower buds, which are used in cooking, and its ability to thrive in rocky, coastal environments.

It plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
