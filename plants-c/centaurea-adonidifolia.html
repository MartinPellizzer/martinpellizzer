Centaurea adonidifolia

Centaurea adonidifolia, commonly known as the dwarf coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial species that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiny, conical flower heads that are typically pink to red in color. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
