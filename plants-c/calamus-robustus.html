Calamus robustus

Calamus robustus, commonly known as the robust sedge, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Calamus robustus typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
