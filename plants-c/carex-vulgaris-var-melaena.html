Carex vulgaris var. melaena

Carex vulgaris var. melaena, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, stems and leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. Native to wetlands and moist soils in temperate regions, it thrives in environments with consistent moisture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
