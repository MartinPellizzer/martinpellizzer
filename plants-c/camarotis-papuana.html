Camarotis papuana

Camarotis papuana, commonly known as the Papuan orchid, is a species of orchid native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as purple, pink, and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil.

Camarotis papuana plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
