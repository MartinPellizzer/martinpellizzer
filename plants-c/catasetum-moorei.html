Catasetum moorei

Catasetum moorei, commonly known as the Moore's catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with a specialized column. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are known for their intricate morphology, including a movable pollinia that is transferred during pollination.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
