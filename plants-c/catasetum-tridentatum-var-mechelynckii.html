Catasetum tridentatum var. mechelynckii

Catasetum tridentatum var. mechelynckii, commonly known as the Mechelynck's Catasetum, is a rare orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that are typically yellow or orange with intricate patterns. The plant produces a unique structure called a "column," which is modified to facilitate pollination by specific wasp species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil and requires consistent moisture.

Its botanical significance lies in its specialized pollination mechanism and limited geographic distribution.
