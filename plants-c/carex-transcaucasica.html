Carex transcaucasica

Carex transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as wet meadows and forested areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Carex transcaucasica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
