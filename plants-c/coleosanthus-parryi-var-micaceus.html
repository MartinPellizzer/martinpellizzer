Coleosanthus parryi var. micaceus

Coleosanthus parryi var. micaceus, commonly known as the golden spider lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant features strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm, humid climates.
