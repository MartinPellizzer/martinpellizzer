Chrysanthemum maximum

Chrysanthemum maximum, commonly known as the garden chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including yellow, orange, red, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 150 centimeters and has finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Chrysanthemum maximum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
