Croton rhamnifolius var. conduplicatus

Croton rhamnifolius var. conduplicatus, commonly known as the double-leaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring leaves with two pairs of lobes, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often a vibrant green, with some varieties displaying variegation.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
