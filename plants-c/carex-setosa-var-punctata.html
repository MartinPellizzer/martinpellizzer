Carex setosa var. punctata

Carex setosa var. punctata, commonly known as the dotted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, spiky seed heads.

The varietal name "punctata" refers to the small, dark spots on its leaves, which are a distinguishing feature of this variety.
