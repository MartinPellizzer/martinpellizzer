Carex × beckmannii

Carex × beckmannii, commonly known as the Beckmann's sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often brown or reddish at maturity. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex × beckmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
