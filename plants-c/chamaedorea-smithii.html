Chamaedorea smithii

Chamaedorea smithii, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen bamboo, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is a compact, slow-growing plant that typically reaches a height of 3 to 5 feet. Native to Mexico, it thrives in shaded, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its slender, upright stems are adorned with narrow, dark green leaves that give it a lush, tropical appearance.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its air-purifying qualities and low maintenance requirements.
