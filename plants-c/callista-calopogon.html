Callista calopogon

Callista calopogon, commonly known as the grass pink, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, grass-like leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

The grass pink is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
