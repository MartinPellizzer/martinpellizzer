Coryanthes panamensis

Coryanthes panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the humid forests of Panama.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are highly specialized, featuring a complex structure with a labellum that plays a role in pollination.

Coryanthes panamensis is known for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
