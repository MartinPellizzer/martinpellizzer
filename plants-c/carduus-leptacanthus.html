Carduus leptacanthus

Carduus leptacanthus, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features spiny, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

It is often found in grasslands and along the edges of woodlands.
