Cephalanthus dioicus

Cephalanthus dioicus, commonly known as the sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rubiacae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and emits a pleasant, spicy scent. Its fruit is a small, dark red drupe that matures in the fall.

Cephalanthus dioicus is often found in moist, well-drained soils near streams and wetlands.
