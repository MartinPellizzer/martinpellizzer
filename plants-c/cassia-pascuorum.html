Cassia pascuorum

Cassia pascuorum, commonly known as the meadow cassia, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in open habitats like meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soil.

Cassia pascuorum is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
