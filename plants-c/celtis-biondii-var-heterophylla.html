Celtis biondii var. heterophylla

Celtis biondii var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable boxleaf elm, is a deciduous tree native to parts of China.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that vary in shape and size across different parts of the tree. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
