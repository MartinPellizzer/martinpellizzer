Cereus dichroacanthus

Cereus dichroacanthus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems and is known for its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant is a succulent, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular choice for gardens and collectors.
