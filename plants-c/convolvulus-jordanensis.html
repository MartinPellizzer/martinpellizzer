Convolvulus jordanensis

Convolvulus jordanensis, commonly known as Jordanian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows up to 30 cm in height, featuring hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Convolvulus jordanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
