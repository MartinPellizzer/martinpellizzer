Cupania rigida

Cupania rigida, commonly known as the rigid cupania, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
