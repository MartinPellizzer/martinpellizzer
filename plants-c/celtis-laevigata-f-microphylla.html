Celtis laevigata f. microphylla

Celtis laevigata f. microphylla, commonly known as the smoothleaf hackberry, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 3 inches in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, greenish fruits that mature to a purplish-black color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat and food for wildlife.
