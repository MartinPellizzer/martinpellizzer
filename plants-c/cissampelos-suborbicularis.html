Cissampelos suborbicularis

Cissampelos suborbicularis, commonly known as the broad-leaved moon vine, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with five petals, which are typically borne in clusters along the stems. Its fruits are round, green berries that turn red when mature, containing several small, flat seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
