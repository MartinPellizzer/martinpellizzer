Ctenitis buchholzii

Ctenitis buchholzii, commonly known as the Buchholz's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing cones, are located at the base of the fronds.

Ctenitis buchholzii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
