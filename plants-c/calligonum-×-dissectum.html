Calligonum × dissectum

Calligonum × dissectum, commonly known as the hybrid saltbush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and its ability to thrive in saline and alkaline soils. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by small, edible berries. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Calligonum × dissectum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soils in harsh environments.
