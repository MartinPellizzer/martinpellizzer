Croton acuminatus

Croton acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Croton acuminatus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
