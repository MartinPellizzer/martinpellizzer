Cassia microsenna

Cassia microsenna, commonly known as small-leaved cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Cassia microsenna also has traditional uses in some cultures for medicinal purposes.
