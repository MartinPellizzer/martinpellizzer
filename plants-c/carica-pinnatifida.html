Carica pinnatifida

Carica pinnatifida, commonly known as the pinnate papaya, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, musky flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
