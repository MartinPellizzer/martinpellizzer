Cynosurus cristatus var. tenuissimus

Cynosurus cristatus var. tenuissimus, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting invertebrate life.
