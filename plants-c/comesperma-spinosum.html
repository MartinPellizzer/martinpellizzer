Comesperma spinosum

Comesperma spinosum, commonly known as the spinous comesperma, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. It is typically found in sandy or rocky soils in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
