Crotalaria anagyroides var. pauciflora

Crotalaria anagyroides var. pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in warm climates.
