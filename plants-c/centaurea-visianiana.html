Centaurea visianiana

Centaurea visianiana, commonly known as the Visianian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its vibrant blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
