Capsicum annuum var. cerasiforme

Capsicum annuum var. cerasiforme, commonly known as the cherry pepper, is a variant of the common chili pepper species.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often brightly colored fruits, typically red, yellow, or orange when mature. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and the fruit develops from a single ovary.

The plant has a bushy growth habit and is relatively easy to grow in home gardens.
