Clematis chrysocoma var. sericea

Clematis chrysocoma var. sericea, commonly known as the silky clematis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with finely divided leaflets that give the plant a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not particularly palatable.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
