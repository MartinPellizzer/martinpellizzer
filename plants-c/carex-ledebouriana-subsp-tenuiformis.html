Carex ledebouriana subsp. tenuiformis

Carex ledebouriana subsp.

tenuiformis, commonly known as slender-form carex, is a perennial sedge native to arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia. It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in cold environments.
