Crataegus fontanesiana

Crataegus fontanesiana, commonly known as the Fontanesia hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
