Callicarpa formosana var. chinensis

Callicarpa formosana var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically medium green and turn reddish in autumn. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The berries are not only visually striking but also have historical uses in traditional medicine.
