Critoniopsis sagasteguii

Critoniopsis sagasteguii, commonly known as the Sagastegui's flax, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used traditionally for their strong, durable fibers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
