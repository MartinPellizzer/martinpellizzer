Capsicum pubescens var. oviforme

Capsicum pubescens var. oviforme, commonly known as the "Peruvian chili" or "huacatay," is a species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovary-shaped fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are typical of the Capsicum genus. Its flowers are yellow and bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals.

The fruit is used in traditional Andean cuisine and has a distinct, sharp flavor with a hint of citrus.
