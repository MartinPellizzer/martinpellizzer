Calamagrostis pulchella

Calamagrostis pulchella, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive purple-tinted inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Calamagrostis pulchella is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
