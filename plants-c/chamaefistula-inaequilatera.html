Chamaefistula inaequilatera

Chamaefistula inaequilatera, commonly known as the unequal-leaved boxwood, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its hardy nature and compact growth make it suitable for hedges and topiary.
