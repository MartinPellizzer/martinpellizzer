Cirsium pastoris

Cirsium pastoris, commonly known as the meadow thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry meadows and grasslands. The plant has a robust root system and can tolerate poor soil conditions.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
