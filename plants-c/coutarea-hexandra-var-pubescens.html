Coutarea hexandra var. pubescens

Coutarea hexandra var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a woody stem and produces clusters of small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities but requires careful handling due to its toxic properties.
