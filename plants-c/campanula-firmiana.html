Campanula firmiana

Campanula firmiana, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Campanula firmiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
