Ceropegia pilulifera

Ceropegia pilulifera, commonly known as the bead vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, bead-like tubercles along its stems. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple in color, which attract specific pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its roots can form symbiotic relationships with certain fungi.

Ceropegia pilulifera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
