Camellia annamensis

Camellia annamensis, commonly known as the Annam camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant produces waxy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Camellia annamensis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable subject for both horticultural study and conservation efforts.
