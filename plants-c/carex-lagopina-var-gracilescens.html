Carex lagopina var. gracilescens

Carex lagopina var. gracilescens, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
