Cynanchum dalhousiae

Cynanchum dalhousiae, commonly known as the Dalhousie milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing the seeds.

It is often found in rocky or arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
