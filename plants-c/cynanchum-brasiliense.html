Cynanchum brasiliense

Cynanchum brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds and related species. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cynanchum brasiliense is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
