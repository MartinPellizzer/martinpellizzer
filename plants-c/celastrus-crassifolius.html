Celastrus crassifolius

Celastrus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf staffvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by elliptical, leathery fruits. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
