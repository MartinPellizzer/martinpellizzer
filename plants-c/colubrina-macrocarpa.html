Colubrina macrocarpa

Colubrina macrocarpa, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 feet tall, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.
