Cynanchum stellatum

Cynanchum stellatum, commonly known as starvine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural interest.

Cynanchum stellatum is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade.
