Campanula formanekiana

Campanula formanekiana, commonly known as the Formanek's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Campanula formanekiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
