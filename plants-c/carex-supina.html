Carex supina

Carex supina, commonly known as the prostrate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with cylindrical, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually borne on short, leaf-like bracts.

Carex supina is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas, stream banks, and wet meadows.
