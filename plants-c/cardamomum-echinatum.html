Cardamomum echinatum

Cardamomum echinatum, commonly known as the spiny cardamom, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that are followed by dark, spherical fruits containing aromatic seeds. These seeds are prized for their intense flavor and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Due to its ornamental value and unique scent, it is also cultivated in some botanical gardens.
