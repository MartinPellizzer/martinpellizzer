Coronilla varia f. multipinnata

Coronilla varia f. multipinnata, commonly known as the variable crownvetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple pairs of pinnate leaflets, giving it the subspecies name *multipinnata*. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant in sustainable agriculture and land restoration projects.
