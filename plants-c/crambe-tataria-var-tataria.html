Crambe tataria var. tataria

Crambe tataria var. tataria, commonly known as Tatarian mustard, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and have a distinctive mustard-like odor. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer.

Its capsule fruits contain numerous elliptical seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of oilseed.
