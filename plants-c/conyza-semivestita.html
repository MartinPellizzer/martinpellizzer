Conyza semivestita

Conyza semivestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf conyza, is an annual flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, white seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to different habitats, contributing to its widespread distribution.
