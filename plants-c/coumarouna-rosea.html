Coumarouna rosea

Coumarouna rosea, commonly known as the rosewood tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, brown seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
