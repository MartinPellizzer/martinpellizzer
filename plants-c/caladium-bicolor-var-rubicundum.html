Caladium bicolor var. rubicundum

Caladium bicolor var. rubicundum, commonly known as the flameleaf caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with deep red, orange, and yellow veins against a green or white background. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and partial shade.

It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant for gardens and indoor spaces due to its vibrant leaf patterns.
