Chlora crenulata

Chlora crenulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaved fern, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, wavy-edged leaves that are typically bright green and arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale yellow, which appear in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
