Cirsium × tataricum

Cirsium × tataricum, commonly known as the Siberian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple or pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can form dense colonies. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Due to its invasive nature in some areas, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
