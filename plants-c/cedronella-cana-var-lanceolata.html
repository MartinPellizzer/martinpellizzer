Cedronella cana var. lanceolata

Cedronella cana var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf cedronella, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often with a subtle citrus-like aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
