Callista ovata

Callista ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological significance.

Callista ovata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and foliage.
