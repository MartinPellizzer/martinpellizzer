Cephalocarpus ptariensis

Cephalocarpus ptariensis, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, red berries that are distinctive in appearance. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
