Calamintha megalantha

Calamintha megalantha, commonly known as giant calamintha, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their essential oils. The plant features square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or blue, arranged in whorled clusters.

Calamintha megalantha is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
