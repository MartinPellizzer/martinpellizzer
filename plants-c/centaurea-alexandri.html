Centaurea alexandri

Centaurea alexandri, commonly known as Alexandrian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, conical inflorescences. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are often gray-green in color, and it thrives in dry, sunny environments. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it produces small, achene fruits.

Centaurea alexandri is often found in rocky soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
