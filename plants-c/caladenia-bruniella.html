Caladenia bruniella

Caladenia bruniella, commonly known as the Bruny Island spider orchid, is an endemic species of the Australian flora.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance. The plant typically grows in heathlands and forested areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
