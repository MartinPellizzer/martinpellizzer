Carduus nutans var. granatensis

Carduus nutans var. granatensis, commonly known as the Granatense thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its flowering heads are composed of many small florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
