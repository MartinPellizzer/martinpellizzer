Couroupita magnifica

Couroupita magnifica, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The plant also has cultural significance in some regions, where it is used in traditional medicine.
