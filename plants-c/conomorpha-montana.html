Conomorpha montana

Conomorpha montana, commonly known as the mountain conomorpha, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, white to pale blue flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil in mountainous areas, contributing to ecosystem health.

Conomorpha montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
