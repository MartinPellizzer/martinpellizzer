Cyrtomium caryotideum var. anomophyllum

Cyrtomium caryotideum var. anomophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf holly fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, upright rosette. The fronds are usually dark green with a glossy surface and have a distinctive, finely divided appearance. This fern thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid, semi-shaded conditions.
