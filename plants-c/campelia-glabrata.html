Campelia glabrata

Campelia glabrata, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually oval in shape. It produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the showy fruit.

Campelia glabrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
