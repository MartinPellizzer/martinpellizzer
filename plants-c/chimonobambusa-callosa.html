Chimonobambusa callosa

Chimonobambusa callosa, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or thick-skinned bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern China and is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems covered in a thick, waxy layer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib and a rough, textured surface.

Chimonobambusa callosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its unique appearance.
