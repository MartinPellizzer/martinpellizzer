Carex kitaibeliana var. capillata

Carex kitaibeliana var. capillata, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features grass-like foliage with narrow, dark green leaves that are softly hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of small, greenish-yellow flowers that mature into brown, spike-like structures.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
