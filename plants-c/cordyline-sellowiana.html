Cordyline sellowiana

Cordyline sellowiana, commonly known as the Sellow's cabbage tree, is a striking evergreen plant native to Madagascar.

It features a tall, slender trunk with a fibrous, bark-like covering and large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cordyline sellowiana is also valued for its ability to produce small, white flowers that appear at the base of the leaves.
