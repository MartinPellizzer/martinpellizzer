Croton macrobothrys subsp. macrobothrys

Croton macrobothrys subsp.

macrobothrys, commonly known as the giant croton, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries, which are a feature of its ornamental value. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also popular as a houseplant due to its vibrant appearance and ability to tolerate indoor conditions.
