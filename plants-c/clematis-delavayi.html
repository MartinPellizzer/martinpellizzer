Clematis delavayi

Clematis delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in temperate climates.
