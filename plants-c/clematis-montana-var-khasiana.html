Clematis montana var. khasiana

Clematis montana var. khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi clematis, is a flowering vine native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and its ability to climb using its tendrils. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is usually dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant in shaded areas.
