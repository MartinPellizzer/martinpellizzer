Cypripedium × celia

Cypripedium × celia, commonly known as the Celia's lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white with a distinctive pouch-like structure. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in cool, shaded environments. It has thick, fleshy rhizomes and broad, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
