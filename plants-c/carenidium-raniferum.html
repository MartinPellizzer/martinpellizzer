Carenidium raniferum

Carenidium raniferum, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Carenidium raniferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
