Carex Alterna

Carex alterna is a unique sedge species known for its distinctive foliage and adaptability to various wetland environments. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. This plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that alternate in direction, giving it its name. Its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat makes it ecologically significant. Carex alterna is both a hardy and aesthetically pleasing addition to wetland restoration projects.
