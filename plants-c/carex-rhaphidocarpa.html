Carex rhaphidocarpa

Carex rhaphidocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of greenish-brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Carex rhaphidocarpa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
