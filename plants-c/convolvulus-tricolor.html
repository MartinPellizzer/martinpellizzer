Convolvulus tricolor

Convolvulus tricolor, commonly known as the three-color morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tri-colored flowers that range from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

Convolvulus tricolor is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive blooms and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
