Cinnamomum pyrifolium

Cinnamomum pyrifolium, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or litsea cubeba, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fragrant, oval-shaped fruits. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and fruits, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Cinnamomum pyrifolium thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
