Calostigma guilleminianum

Calostigma guilleminianum, commonly known as the Guillemin's bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with five petals that are often marked with darker veins.

Calostigma guilleminianum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and borders.
