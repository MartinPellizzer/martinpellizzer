Cyrtopera woodfordii var. pachystelidia

Cyrtopera woodfordii var. pachystelidia, commonly known as the Woolly Cyrtopera, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides insulation against the harsh desert climate. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
