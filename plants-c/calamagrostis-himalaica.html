Calamagrostis himalaica

Calamagrostis himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and riverbanks. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces plume-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the grass family. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.

Calamagrostis himalaica is also known for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its natural habitat.
