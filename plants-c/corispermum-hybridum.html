Corispermum hybridum

Corispermum hybridum, commonly known as the hybrid sea rocket, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in coastal areas and saline soils, adapting well to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies related to salt tolerance and coastal vegetation.
