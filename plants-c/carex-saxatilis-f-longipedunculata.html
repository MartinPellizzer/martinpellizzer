Carex saxatilis f. longipedunculata

Carex saxatilis f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stalked sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces cylindrical spikes of small, greenish flowers. This subspecies is typically found in moist, rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil in its natural environment.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland and mountain ecosystems.
