Camellia subintegra

Camellia subintegra, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive pinkish hue. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Camellia subintegra is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming period.
