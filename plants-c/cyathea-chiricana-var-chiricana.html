Cyathea chiricana var. chiricana

Cyathea chiricana var. chiricana, commonly known as the Chirican Chamaedorea, is a species of tree fern native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes produce roots that help anchor it to the soil and absorb nutrients.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their aesthetic appeal in horticulture.
