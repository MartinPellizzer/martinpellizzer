Chaetochloa trichorhachis

Chaetochloa trichorhachis, commonly known as the hairy panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
