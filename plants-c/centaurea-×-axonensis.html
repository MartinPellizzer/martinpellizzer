Centaurea × axonensis

Centaurea × axonensis, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Centaurea × axonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
