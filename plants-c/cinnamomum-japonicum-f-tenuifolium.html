Cinnamomum japonicum f. tenuifolium

Cinnamomum japonicum f. tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Japanese cinnamon, is a variant of the Japanese cinnamon tree.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically a bright green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by dark blue or black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

This variety is valued for its aromatic wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
