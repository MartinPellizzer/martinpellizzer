Cacalia subverticillata

Cacalia subverticillata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
