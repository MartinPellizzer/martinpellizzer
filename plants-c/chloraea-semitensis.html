Chloraea semitensis

Chloraea semitensis, commonly known as the slender flame vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically orange-red in color and are borne in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
