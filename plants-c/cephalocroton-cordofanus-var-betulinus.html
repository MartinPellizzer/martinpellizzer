Cephalocroton cordofanus var. betulinus

Cephalocroton cordofanus var. betulinus, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
