Cynorkis quinqueloba

Cynorkis quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed cypripedium, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a large, pouch-like lip and intricate patterns. The plant has a tall, erect stem with several broad, lance-shaped leaves. It blooms in late spring or early summer, producing a single flower per stem.

Cynorkis quinqueloba is adapted to grow in cool, shaded forest environments, often in moist, well-drained soil.
