Cyperus annuus

Cyperus annuus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often surrounded by bracts. Cyperus annuus is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
