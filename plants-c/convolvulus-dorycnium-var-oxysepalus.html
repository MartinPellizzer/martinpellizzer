Convolvulus dorycnium var. oxysepalus

Convolvulus dorycnium var. oxysepalus, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
