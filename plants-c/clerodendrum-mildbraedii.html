Clerodendrum mildbraedii

Clerodendrum mildbraedii, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to red in color, which bloom in summer. Its botanical features include the presence of glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
