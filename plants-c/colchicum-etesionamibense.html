Colchicum etesionamibense

Colchicum etesionamibense, commonly known as the Etesionamibense Colchic, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions in the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, often violet-colored flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a bulbous structure that serves as its storage organ, typical of many colchic species. It thrives in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species requiring conservation efforts.
