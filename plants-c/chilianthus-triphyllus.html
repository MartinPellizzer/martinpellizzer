Chilianthus triphyllus

Chilianthus triphyllus, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Chilianthus triphyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
