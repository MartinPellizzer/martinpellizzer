Cinnamomum mendozae

Cinnamomum mendozae, commonly known as Mendoza's cinnamon, is a species of tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.

The tree typically grows in moist, tropical lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 15 meters.
