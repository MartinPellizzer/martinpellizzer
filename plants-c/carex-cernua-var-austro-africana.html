Carex cernua var. austro-africana

Carex cernua var. austro-africana, commonly known as the African sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of water bodies. Its foliage is usually dark green, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers during the spring or early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
