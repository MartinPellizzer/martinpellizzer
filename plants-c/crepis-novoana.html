Crepis novoana

Crepis novoana, commonly known as the New Zealand mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest margins and stream banks.

Crepis novoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
