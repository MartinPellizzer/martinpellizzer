Cyperus pseudovariegatus

Cyperus pseudovariegatus, commonly known as the variegated flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, variegated leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas, pond edges, and disturbed sites. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Cyperus pseudovariegatus is also valued for its ability to help stabilize soil and improve water quality in wetland ecosystems.
