Cactus recurvus

Cactus recurvus, commonly known as the twisted spine cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny exterior. Its spines are modified leaves that help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Overall, Cactus recurvus exemplifies the adaptive strategies of desert flora.
