Carex × ewingii

Carex × ewingii, commonly known as the Ewing's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex × ewingii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
