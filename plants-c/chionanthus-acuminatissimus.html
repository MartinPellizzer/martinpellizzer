Chionanthus acuminatissimus

Chionanthus acuminatissimus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
