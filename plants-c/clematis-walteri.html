Clematis walteri

Clematis walteri, commonly known as Walter's Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces striking, large, showy flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is characterized by its long, climbing stems. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis walteri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
