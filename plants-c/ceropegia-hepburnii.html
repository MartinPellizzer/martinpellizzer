Ceropegia hepburnii

Ceropegia hepburnii, commonly known as the Blue Snowflake, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces delicate, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia hepburnii is also noted for its ability to bloom profusely during the winter months.
