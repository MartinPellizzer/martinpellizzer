Calluna vulgaris subf. aureifolia

Calluna vulgaris subf.

aureifolia, commonly known as the golden heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to western Europe. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange.

The plant features needle-like leaves that are typically bright yellow or golden, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments.

This subshrub is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
