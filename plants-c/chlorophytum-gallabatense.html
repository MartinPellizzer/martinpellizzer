Chlorophytum gallabatense

Chlorophytum gallabatense, commonly known as the Galabat spider plant, is a rare and unique species native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its long, arching leaves that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
