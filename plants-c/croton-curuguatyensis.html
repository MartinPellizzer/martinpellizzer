Croton curuguatyensis

Croton curuguatyensis, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves, which can be green, yellow, or red depending on the variety. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Croton curuguatyensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
