Cardamine kamtschatica

Cardamine kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamtschatka watercress, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a peppery aroma.

It produces small white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elongated pods containing tiny seeds.
