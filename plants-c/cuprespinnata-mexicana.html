Cuprespinnata mexicana

Cuprespinnata mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican cupressus, is a species of coniferous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its scale-like leaves and woody cones. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 15 meters.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
