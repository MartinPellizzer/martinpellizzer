Centaurea dumortieri

Centaurea dumortieri, commonly known as the Dumortier's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in regions such as Spain, Portugal, and southern France. Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
