Capsicum annuum var. acuminatum

Capsicum annuum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the bird's eye chili, is a variety of the common chili pepper native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, pointed, and typically red or orange fruits, which are renowned for their intense heat and flavor. The plant is an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for culinary use, particularly in Asian and Caribbean cuisines.
