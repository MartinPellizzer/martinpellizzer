Cupressus macrocarpa var. angulata

Cupressus macrocarpa var. angulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lacebark, is a variant of the Australian lacebark tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are arranged in crossed pairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to southeastern Australia and thrives in coastal and sandy soils. It produces small, spherical cones and has a slow growth rate, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique foliage and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
