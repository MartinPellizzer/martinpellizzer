Centaurea laciniata

Centaurea laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and deeply lobed, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea laciniata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wildflower displays.
