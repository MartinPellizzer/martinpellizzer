Cupressus pseudosquarrosa

Cupressus pseudosquarrosa, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tall form and scale-like leaves arranged in flat sprays. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes worldwide.
