Chionanthus calcicola

Chionanthus calcicola, commonly known as the limestone honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils, often found in limestone areas. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It is a low-growing shrub that plays an important role in its native ecosystem.
