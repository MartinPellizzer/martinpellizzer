Calantica

Calantica, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Calantica thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
