Callitriche peploides

Callitriche peploides, commonly known as velvet water-starwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Callitrichaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and ditches. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually occurring in clusters, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Callitriche peploides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through its filtration capabilities.
