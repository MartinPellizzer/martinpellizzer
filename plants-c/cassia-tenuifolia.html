Cassia tenuifolia

Cassia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia tenuifolia is also cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
