Carum armenum

Carum armenum, commonly known as Armenian cumin, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and western Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill, and is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications, valued for their pungent, spicy aroma.

Carum armenum thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
