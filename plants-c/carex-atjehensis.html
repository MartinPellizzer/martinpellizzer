Carex atjehensis

Carex atjehensis, commonly known as the Atjeh sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia. This plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Carex atjehensis is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystem.
