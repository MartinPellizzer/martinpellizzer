Conioselinum morrisonense

Conioselinum morrisonense, commonly known as Morrison's wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is typically found growing in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in traditional medicine by local communities.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
