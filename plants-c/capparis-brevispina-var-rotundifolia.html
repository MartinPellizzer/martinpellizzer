Capparis brevispina var. rotundifolia

Capparis brevispina var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed caper, is a variant of the caper plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, fleshy stems. The leaves are rounded and often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, green to reddish-purple berries.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
