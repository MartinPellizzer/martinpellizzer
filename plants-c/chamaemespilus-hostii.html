Chamaemespilus hostii

Chamaemespilus hostii, commonly known as the Mexican olive, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
