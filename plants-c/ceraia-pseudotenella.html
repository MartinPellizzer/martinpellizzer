Ceraia pseudotenella

Ceraia pseudotenella, commonly known as the false sand dollar, is a marine invertebrate belonging to the family Tetraodontidae.

It is characterized by its calcareous, globular shell that resembles a sand dollar, though it is not a true sand dollar. Its reproductive strategy involves external fertilization, with females laying eggs that are fertilized by the males in the open water.
