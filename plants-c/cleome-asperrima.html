Cleome asperrima

Cleome asperrima, commonly known as rough cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellowish-white flowers with five petals, which bloom from summer to fall. Cleome asperrima is a hardy annual that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial.
