Cerothamnus heterophyllus

Cerothamnus heterophyllus, commonly known as the coastal goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its yellowish-gold flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal environments with salt spray.

Cerothamnus heterophyllus plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
