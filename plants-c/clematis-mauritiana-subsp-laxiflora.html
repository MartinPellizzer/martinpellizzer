Clematis mauritiana subsp. laxiflora

Clematis mauritiana subsp.

laxiflora, commonly known as the Mauritius clematis, is a climbing vine native to the island of Mauritius. It features slender, twining stems and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring.

The flowers are typically blue to violet in color, with five petals and numerous stamens. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
