Crepis poeppigii

Crepis poeppigii, commonly known as the Puebla mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and hairy, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a compact growth habit and produces small, pale yellow to white flowers.
