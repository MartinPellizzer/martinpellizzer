Caladenia bryceana

Caladenia bryceana, commonly known as the Bryce's Spider-orchid, is a striking orchid native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and its unique, spider-like flower structure. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing flowers that are predominantly green with purple and brown markings. Caladenia bryceana thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and threatened species.
