Crataegus × calciphila

Crataegus × calciphila, commonly known as the limestone hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in calcareous soils, making it a notable species in limestone-rich environments. The plant typically exhibits small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in autumn. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and it can grow as a shrub or small tree.

Crataegus × calciphila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
