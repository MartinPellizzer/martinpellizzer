Cudrania crenata

Cudrania crenata, commonly known as the warty sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Cudrania crenata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
