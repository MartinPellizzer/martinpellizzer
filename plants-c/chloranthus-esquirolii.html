Chloranthus esquirolii

Chloranthus esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's chloranthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and somewhat obscure species in botanical studies.
