Cocculus megaspermus

Cocculus megaspermus, commonly known as the giant spurred cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green fruits that mature to a dark purple or black color, often containing a single large seed. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a unique, complex floral structure.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
