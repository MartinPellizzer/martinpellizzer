Crocus macrobolbos

Crocus macrobolbos, commonly known as the giant crocus, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces large, showy flowers with bright yellow petals and a distinctive purple center. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, allowing it to bloom in early spring.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.
