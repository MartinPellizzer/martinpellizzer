Calceolaria sotarensis

Calceolaria sotarensis, commonly known as the Sotara slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique calyx-like structure that resembles a slipper, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
