Chimanthus

Chimanthus, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate appearance with pale, translucent petals. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Chimanthus has a short-lived flower that blooms only once every few years, making it a rare sight in the wild.

Due to its habitat specificity and conservation status, it is considered endangered and is often protected in its native regions.
