Croton haitiensis

Croton haitiensis, commonly known as Haitian croton, is a flowering plant species native to the Caribbean, specifically Haiti and the Dominican Republic.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with yellow petals. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it may be invasive in some regions.
