Cuphea nitidula

Cuphea nitidula, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Cuphea nitidula is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
