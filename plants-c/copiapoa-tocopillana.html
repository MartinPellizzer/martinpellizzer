Copiapoa tocopillana

Copiapoa tocopillana, commonly known as the Tocopilla cactus, is a species native to the Atacama Desert in northern Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive rib structure and a gray-green coloration. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular specimen among cactus enthusiasts and botanists.
