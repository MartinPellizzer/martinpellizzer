Campanula glomerata var. oblongata

Campanula glomerata var. oblongata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
