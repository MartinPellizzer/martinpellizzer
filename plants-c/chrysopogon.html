Chrysopogon

Chrysopogon, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Sweetgrass is often found in moist, temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in wetlands and meadows. The plant is valued for its aromatic foliage, which has been used traditionally by various cultures for ceremonial and medicinal purposes.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
