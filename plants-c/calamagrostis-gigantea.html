Calamagrostis gigantea

Calamagrostis gigantea, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It features slender, arching leaves and can grow up to 6 feet in height, making it a striking addition to landscapes. The plant produces feathery, silver-gray plumes in late summer, which are both ornamental and beneficial for pollinators. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found near water bodies such as marshes and ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to erosion control in wetland environments.
