Carissa grandiflora

Carissa grandiflora, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow bell.

It is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its dense, thorny stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers that give way to orange-red berries. These berries are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
