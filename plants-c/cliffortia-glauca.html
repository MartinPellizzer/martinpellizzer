Cliffortia glauca

Cliffortia glauca, commonly known as the bluebells of the Cape, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky, coastal environments. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to withstand coastal conditions.

Cliffortia glauca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.
