Campanula pusilla subsp. ficarioides

Campanula pusilla subsp.

ficarioides, commonly known as the fairy bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and suitability in woodland gardens.
