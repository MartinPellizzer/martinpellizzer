Convallaria cirrhifolia

Convallaria cirrhifolia, commonly known as the sweet woodruff, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage has been historically used in herbal remedies and as a flavoring agent.
