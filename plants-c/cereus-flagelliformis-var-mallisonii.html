Cereus flagelliformis var. mallisonii

Cereus flagelliformis var. mallisonii, commonly known as the Christmas Cactus, is a succulent plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its branching, segmented stems that resemble a whip, hence its common name. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a popular ornamental plant among gardeners.
