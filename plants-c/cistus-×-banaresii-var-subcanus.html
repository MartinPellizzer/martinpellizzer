Cistus × banaresii var. subcanus

Cistus × banaresii var. subcanus, commonly known as the silver-haired rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens. Its aromatic leaves and drought tolerance make it a resilient addition to Mediterranean landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
