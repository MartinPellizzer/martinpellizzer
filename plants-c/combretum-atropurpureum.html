Combretum atropurpureum

Combretum atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish-brown flowers that appear in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement, and its bark is smooth and often fissured. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and forest margins.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
