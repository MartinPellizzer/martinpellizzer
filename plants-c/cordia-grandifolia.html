Cordia grandifolia

Cordia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved cordia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks.

Cordia grandifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in some cultures.
