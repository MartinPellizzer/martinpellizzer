Campanula rotundifolia subsp. moravica

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

moravica, commonly known as the Moravian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its round, palmate leaves and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
