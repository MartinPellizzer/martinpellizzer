Chironia vulgaris var. lychnoides

Chironia vulgaris var. lychnoides, commonly known as the common evening primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features showy yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

The oil from its seeds is valued for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
