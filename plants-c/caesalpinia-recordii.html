Caesalpinia recordii

Caesalpinia recordii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling a shower of light. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are compound and divided into small, feather-like leaflets. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing several seeds enclosed in a fibrous covering.

Caesalpinia recordii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
