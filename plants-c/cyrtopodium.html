Cyrtopodium

Cyrtopodium, commonly known as the slipper orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. They are characterized by their unique, often colorful, slipper-shaped flowers that resemble a small pouch. Cyrtopodium species typically grow as epiphytes or terrestrial plants, often found in shaded, humid environments.

Their botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a complex reproductive system involving specialized pollination mechanisms.
