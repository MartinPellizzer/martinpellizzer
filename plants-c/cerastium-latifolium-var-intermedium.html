Cerastium latifolium var. intermedium

Cerastium latifolium var. intermedium, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers with five petals and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It produces numerous small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shady garden conditions.
