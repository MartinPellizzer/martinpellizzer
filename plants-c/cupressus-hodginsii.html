Cupressus hodginsii

Cupressus hodginsii, commonly known as the Coulter pine, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and its distinctive, rounded cones. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its botanical features include a straight trunk with a conical crown and reddish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the Pacific Northwest.
