Coleosanthus densus

Coleosanthus densus, commonly known as the dense leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along its stems. The plant produces white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are usually red or yellow, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments.
