Cotula conica

Cotula conica, commonly known as the conical cotula, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading herb with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and they bloom in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cotula conica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.
