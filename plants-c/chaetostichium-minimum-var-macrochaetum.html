Chaetostichium minimum var. macrochaetum

Chaetostichium minimum var. macrochaetum, commonly known as the large-haired mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented stems. The plant features dense, hairy foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with four petals arranged symmetrically.

It is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
