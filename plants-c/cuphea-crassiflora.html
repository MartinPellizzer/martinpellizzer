Cuphea crassiflora

Cuphea crassiflora, commonly known as the roughcup cuphea, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features hairy, rough-textured leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warmer climates.

Cuphea crassiflora is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
