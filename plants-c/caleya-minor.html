Caleya minor

Caleya minor, commonly known as the lesser caleya, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pale blue or white flowers during the summer months. It is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils.

Caleya minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
