Cracca sylvatica

Cracca sylvatica, commonly known as the woodland cracca, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in umbels. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and shaded environments, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Cracca sylvatica is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in treating respiratory and digestive ailments.
