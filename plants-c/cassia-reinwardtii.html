Cassia reinwardtii

Cassia reinwardtii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while its roots are deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in tropical climates.

Cassia reinwardtii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
