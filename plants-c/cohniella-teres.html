Cohniella teres

Cohniella teres, commonly known as the narrow-leafed burr grass, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Cohniella teres is adapted to dry, disturbed habitats and is often found in open areas with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a common species in arid and semi-arid regions.
