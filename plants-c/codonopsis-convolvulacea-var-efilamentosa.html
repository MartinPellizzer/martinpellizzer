Codonopsis convolvulacea var. efilamentosa

Codonopsis convolvulacea var. efilamentosa, commonly known as the thread-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a perennial species characterized by its slender, twining stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The leaves are narrow and elongated, often with a hairy texture, and the plant produces small, showy flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
