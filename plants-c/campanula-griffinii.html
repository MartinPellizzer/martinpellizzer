Campanula griffinii

Campanula griffinii, commonly known as Griffin's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
