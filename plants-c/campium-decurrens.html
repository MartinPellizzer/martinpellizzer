Campium decurrens

Campium decurrens, commonly known as the running campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. The plant features slender, trailing stems that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pink or purple, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Campium decurrens is known for its ability to spread vegetatively, making it both a common weed and a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
