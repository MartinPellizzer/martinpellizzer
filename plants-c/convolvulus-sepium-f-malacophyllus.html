Convolvulus sepium f. malacophyllus

Convolvulus sepium f. malacophyllus, commonly known as the soft-leaved morning glory, is a variant of the common morning glory.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in gardens and natural habitats.
