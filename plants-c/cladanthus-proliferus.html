Cladanthus proliferus

Cladanthus proliferus, commonly known as the spreading thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation. Its leaves are alternate and often have a rough texture.

Cladanthus proliferus is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
