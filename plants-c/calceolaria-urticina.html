Calceolaria urticina

Calceolaria urticina, commonly known as the stinging calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that resemble small cups. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that can cause skin irritation, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Calceolaria urticina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
