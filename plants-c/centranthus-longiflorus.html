Centranthus longiflorus

Centranthus longiflorus, commonly known as the tall centranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant produces bright pink to red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Centranthus longiflorus is known for its long blooming period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
