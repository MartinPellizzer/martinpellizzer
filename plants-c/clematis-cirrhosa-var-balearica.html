Clematis cirrhosa var. balearica

Clematis cirrhosa var. balearica, commonly known as the Balearic clematis, is a perennial vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three ovate to lanceolate leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
