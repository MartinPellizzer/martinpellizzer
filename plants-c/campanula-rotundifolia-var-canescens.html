Campanula rotundifolia var. canescens

Campanula rotundifolia var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, hairy leaves and bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, woolly appearance.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
