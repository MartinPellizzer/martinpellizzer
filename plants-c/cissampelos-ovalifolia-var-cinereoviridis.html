Cissampelos ovalifolia var. cinereoviridis

Cissampelos ovalifolia var. cinereoviridis, commonly known as the ash-leaf moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive ash-gray underside, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Its stems are woody and often covered in a fine, pale pubescence.

It is commonly found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
