Calamagrostis simlensis

Calamagrostis simlensis, commonly known as the Simlens tallgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis simlensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
