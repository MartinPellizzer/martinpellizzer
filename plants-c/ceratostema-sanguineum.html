Ceratostema sanguineum

Ceratostema sanguineum, commonly known as the red-flowered ceratostema, is a perennial shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features woody stems and elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
