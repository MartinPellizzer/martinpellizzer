Cinnastrum poiforme

Cinnastrum poiforme, commonly known as the Indian snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool climates. The plant features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Cinnastrum poiforme is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in specific alpine conditions.
