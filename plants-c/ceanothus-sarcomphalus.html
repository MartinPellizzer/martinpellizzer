Ceanothus sarcomphalus

Ceanothus sarcomphalus, commonly known as the blue curl ceanothus, is a shrub native to California and southern Oregon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Ceanothus sarcomphalus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
