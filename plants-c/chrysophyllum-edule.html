Chrysophyllum edule

Chrysophyllum edule, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically pear-shaped, with a smooth, yellowish skin that turns reddish when ripe. The pulp is juicy, sweet, and aromatic, containing a central cavity with seeds.

It is valued both for its edible fruit and its ornamental qualities in landscaping.
