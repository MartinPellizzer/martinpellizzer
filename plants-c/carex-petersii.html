Carex petersii

Carex petersii, commonly known as Peters' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters, typical of sedges. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
