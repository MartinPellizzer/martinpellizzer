Cichorium intybus subsp. balearicum

Cichorium intybus subsp.

balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic chicory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its rosette of blue to purple leaves and a tall, flowering stem that bears blue or purple flowers.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible leaves and roots, which are used in various culinary traditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for Mediterranean climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
