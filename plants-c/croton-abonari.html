Croton abonari

Croton abonari, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves, which can vary in shape from ovate to lanceolate. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family, and are usually yellow or greenish in color.

The bark and leaves of Croton abonari contain various chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
