Calamintha urticifolia

Calamintha urticifolia, commonly known as stinging nettle mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are hairy and can cause a mild stinging sensation when touched, similar to stinging nettle.

Calamintha urticifolia is often used in herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
