Cyperus proteus

Cyperus proteus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are often clustered at the base.

Cyperus proteus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
