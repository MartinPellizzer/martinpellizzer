Crepis modocensis subsp. modocensis

Crepis modocensis subsp.

modocensis, commonly known as Modoc thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other composite flowers.
