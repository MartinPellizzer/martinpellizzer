Cissus fugosioides

Cissus fugosioides, commonly known as the African stonecrop, is a trailing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, waxy leaves that help it store water. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that are followed by small, round, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Cissus fugosioides is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its trailing growth habit.
