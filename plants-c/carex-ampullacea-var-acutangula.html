Carex ampullacea var. acutangula

Carex ampullacea var. acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in two tiers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden settings.
