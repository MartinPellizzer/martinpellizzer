Commelina haitiensis

Commelina haitiensis, commonly known as Haitian spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Commelina haitiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
