Cinnamomum guizhouense

Cinnamomum guizhouense, commonly known as Guizhou cinnamon, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Guizhou Province in China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at moderate to high elevations. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
