Centronia crassiramus

Centronia crassiramus, commonly known as the broad-armed centronia, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It is native to southern Africa, where it typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a sturdy, branching stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense clusters, making it an attractive addition to gardens.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
