Cissus opaca

Cissus opaca, commonly known as the African Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its stems can develop roots at the nodes, allowing it to climb or trail along surfaces.

Cissus opaca is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
