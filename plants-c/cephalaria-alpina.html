Cephalaria alpina

Cephalaria alpina, commonly known as alpine cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, yellowish to reddish flower heads.

Cephalaria alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators in high-altitude ecosystems.
