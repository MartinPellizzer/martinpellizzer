Caltha natans var. arctica

Caltha natans var. arctica, commonly known as the arctic marsh marigold, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in wet, boggy habitats. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in water. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, containing both male and female reproductive structures.

The species is adapted to cold climates and thrives in nutrient-poor, moist environments.
