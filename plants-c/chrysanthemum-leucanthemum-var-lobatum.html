Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. lobatum

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. lobatum, commonly known as the marguerite daisy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals surrounding a yellow center, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has a bushy growth habit, with hairy stems and finely divided, lobed leaves that are often gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
