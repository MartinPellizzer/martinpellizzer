Cyclopia falcata

Cyclopia falcata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to dry and windy conditions.

Cyclopia falcata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
