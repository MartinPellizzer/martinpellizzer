Crotalaria iringana

Crotalaria iringana, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
