Clematis orientalis var. pauciflora

Clematis orientalis var. pauciflora, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, showy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Clematis orientalis var. pauciflora is relatively hardy and requires support for its climbing habit.
