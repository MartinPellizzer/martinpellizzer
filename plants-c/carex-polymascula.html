Carex polymascula

Carex polymascula, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-stamened flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex polymascula plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
