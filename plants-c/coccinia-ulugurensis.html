Coccinia ulugurensis

Coccinia ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru cucumber, is a climbing vine native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically green and resemble miniature cucumbers. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are trailing and often hairy.

Coccinia ulugurensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential nutritional value.
