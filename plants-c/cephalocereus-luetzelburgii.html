Cephalocereus luetzelburgii

Cephalocereus luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Luetzelburg's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems covered in small, spiny areoles, which bear clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces fruit that is typically a red or orange color, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and minimal water.

Its unique structure and ecological role make it an important part of its native desert ecosystem.
