Calogyne quadrifida

Calogyne quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leaf arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Calogyne quadrifida is also valued for its air-purifying properties and is relatively easy to care for.
