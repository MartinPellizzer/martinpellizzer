Carex neurophora

Carex neurophora, commonly known as the slender flat-sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex neurophora plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
