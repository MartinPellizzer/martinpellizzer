Calothamnus quadrifidus subsp. petraeus

Calothamnus quadrifidus subsp.

petraeus, commonly known as the rock everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy species suitable for drought-tolerant gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant color and long-lasting flowers.
