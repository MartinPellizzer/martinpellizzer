Critonia chrysocephala

Critonia chrysocephala, commonly known as the golden-headed cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are covered in spines, which help to reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and botanical gardens.
