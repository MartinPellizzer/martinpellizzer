Cynoglossum torvum

Cynoglossum torvum, commonly known as the rough bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped, toothed leaves. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Cynoglossum torvum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
