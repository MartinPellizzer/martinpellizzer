Cornus dichotoma

Cornus dichotoma, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red twigs that become prominent in winter. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds.

Cornus dichotoma is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
