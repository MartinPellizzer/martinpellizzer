Cassia hayesiana

Cassia hayesiana, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are opposite in arrangement.

Cassia hayesiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
