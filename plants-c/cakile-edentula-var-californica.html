Cakile edentula var. californica

Cakile edentula var. californica, commonly known as the California sandmat, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of California.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy and gravelly soils, often found in dunes and coastal bluffs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by pale green, edible pods. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments.

The species is notable for its ability to stabilize sand dunes and is considered an important part of the local ecosystem.
