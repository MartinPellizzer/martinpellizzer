Cestrum meridanum

Cestrum meridanum, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in late winter to early spring. These flowers are adapted for pollination by night-flying insects such as moths.

The plant is also known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
