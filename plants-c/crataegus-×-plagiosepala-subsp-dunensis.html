Crataegus × plagiosepala subsp. dunensis

Crataegus × plagiosepala subsp.

dunensis, commonly known as the Dunedin hawthorn, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in autumn.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a glossy upper surface.

It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
