Coleus congestus

Coleus congestus, commonly known as the clustered coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in shades of red, purple, and orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often large, hairy, and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

Coleus congestus is popular as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
