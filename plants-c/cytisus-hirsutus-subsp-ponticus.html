Cytisus hirsutus subsp. ponticus

Cytisus hirsutus subsp.

ponticus, commonly known as the Pontic broom, is a shrub native to southeastern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are pinnate, consisting of several small, oval leaflets, and the plant produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
