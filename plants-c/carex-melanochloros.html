Carex melanochloros

Carex melanochloros, commonly known as black-stem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular, dark green leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clustered spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Carex melanochloros plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
