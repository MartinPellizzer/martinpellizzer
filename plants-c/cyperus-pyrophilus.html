Cyperus pyrophilus

Cyperus pyrophilus, commonly known as the fireplant, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets.

Cyperus pyrophilus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, where it thrives in shallow water or damp soil.
