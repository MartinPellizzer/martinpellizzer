Clusia cajambrensis

Clusia cajambrensis, commonly known as the Jamaican glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Clusia cajambrensis is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
