Cornus oblonga var. glabrescens

Cornus oblonga var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
