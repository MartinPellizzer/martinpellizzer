Clematis cirrhosa subsp. heterophylla

Clematis cirrhosa subsp.

heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf clematis, is a species native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, which can range from trifoliate to palmately compound, depending on environmental conditions.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its adaptability and ornamental value contribute to its use in horticulture across its native range.
