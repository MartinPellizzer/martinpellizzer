Crupina vulgaris var. microcephala

Crupina vulgaris var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed crupina, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and coastal areas due to its tolerance for harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species suitable for various ecological and ornamental purposes.
