Chamaecrista pteropoda

Chamaecrista pteropoda, commonly known as the winged bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with wing-like leaflets and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Chamaecrista pteropoda is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in sustainable agriculture.
