Crataegus coccinea var. typica

Crataegus coccinea var. typica, commonly known as the red thornapple or red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red berries that persist through winter, providing food for birds and other wildlife. The plant has thorny branches and small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are white or pink and appear in early spring before the leaves.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
