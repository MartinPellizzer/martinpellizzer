Capsicum nigrum

Capsicum nigrum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries. The plant produces small white flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is used to make black pepper, which is a common spice worldwide.

The botanical features of Capsicum nigrum make it an important species in both horticulture and the spice industry.
