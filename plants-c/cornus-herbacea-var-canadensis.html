Cornus herbacea var. canadensis

Cornus herbacea var. canadensis, commonly known as Canadian dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruits are small, red drupes that ripen in the fall.

It is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
