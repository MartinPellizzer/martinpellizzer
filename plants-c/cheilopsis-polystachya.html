Cheilopsis polystachya

Cheilopsis polystachya, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Cheilopsis polystachya is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
