Calamagrostis ovata var. nivalis

Calamagrostis ovata var. nivalis, commonly known as the snowgrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in cold, moist environments. The plant produces slender, silver-blue to white plumes that give it a distinctive appearance, especially during the late summer and autumn months. It is often found in wet meadows, bogs, and along the edges of lakes and rivers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes for its aesthetic and habitat-supporting qualities.
