Cephalantheropsis calanthoides

Cephalantheropsis calanthoides, commonly known as the slender green-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and a single, slender flower with a distinctive green-veined lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
