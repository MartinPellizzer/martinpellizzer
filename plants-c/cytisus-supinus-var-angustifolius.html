Cytisus supinus var. angustifolius

Cytisus supinus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved broom, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow and leathery, typically arranged in pairs along the stems. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil improvement.
