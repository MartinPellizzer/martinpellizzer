Crataegus × macrocarpa var. cebinensis

Crataegus × macrocarpa var. cebinensis, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a hybrid plant native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, often red or yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
