Cissus discolor marmorata

Cissus discolor marmorata, commonly known as the marble cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves, which feature a marbled pattern of green and white. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by reddish berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
