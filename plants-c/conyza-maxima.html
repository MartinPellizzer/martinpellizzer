Conyza maxima

Conyza maxima, commonly known as greater capeweed, is an annual flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough, serrated texture. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seeds.

Conyza maxima is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
