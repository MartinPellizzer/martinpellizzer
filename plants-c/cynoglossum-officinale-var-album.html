Cynoglossum officinale var. album

Cynoglossum officinale var. album, commonly known as white hound's tongue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features distinctive, broad, tongue-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
