Colocasia antiquorum f. purpurea

Colocasia antiquorum f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple giant taro, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, with a deep purple to reddish-purple hue. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its dramatic foliage rather than its small, inconspicuous flowers. It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other familiar plants like peace lilies and calla lilies.

Its foliage is not only visually appealing but also serves as a habitat for various small insects and invertebrates.
