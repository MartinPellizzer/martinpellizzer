Chusquea tenuiglumis var. laxiuscula

Chusquea tenuiglumis var. laxiuscula, commonly known as the slender-leafed weeping bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that form dense, clumping growth patterns. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, feathery appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique, cascading form.
