Cynanchum boveanum subsp. boveanum

Cynanchum boveanum subsp.

boveanum, commonly known as the Bovean dogbane, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are usually lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs.
