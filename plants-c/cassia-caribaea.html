Cassia caribaea

Cassia caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean senna, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, with pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Cassia caribaea is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
