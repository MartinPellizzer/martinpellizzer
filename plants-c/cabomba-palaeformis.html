Cabomba palaeformis

Cabomba palaeformis, commonly known as the ancient waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to southern Africa.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often hidden beneath the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with soft, acidic substrates.

Due to its historical significance and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and aquatic ecosystem researchers.
