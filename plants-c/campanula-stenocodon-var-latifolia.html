Campanula stenocodon var. latifolia

Campanula stenocodon var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically broader than those of the typical variety, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
