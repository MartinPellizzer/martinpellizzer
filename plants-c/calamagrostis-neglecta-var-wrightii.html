Calamagrostis neglecta var. wrightii

Calamagrostis neglecta var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bluish-green leaves. This variety thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
