Cinnamomum floccosum

Cinnamomum floccosum, commonly known as the silky camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, silky white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing smooth, black seeds. Its aromatic bark and leaves have been used traditionally in medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
