Calamagrostis hieronymi

Calamagrostis hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymus reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver- or gray-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ornamental grass in landscaped areas.

Calamagrostis hieronymi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
