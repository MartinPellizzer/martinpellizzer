Capparis cynophallophora var. puberula

Capparis cynophallophora var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces edible, round, green fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The hairy caper is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
