Chamaecytisus czerniaevii

Chamaecytisus czerniaevii, commonly known as the Czerniaev's broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woody stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with a leathery texture and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
