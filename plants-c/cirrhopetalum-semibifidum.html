Cirrhopetalum semibifidum

Cirrhopetalum semibifidum, commonly known as the bifid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
