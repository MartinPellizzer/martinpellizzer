Calamagrostis neglecta var. mongolica

Calamagrostis neglecta var. mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to Mongolia and parts of northern China.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
