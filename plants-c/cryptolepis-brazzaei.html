Cryptolepis brazzaei

Cryptolepis brazzaei, commonly known as the African bittersweet, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain toxic seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and riverbanks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in various African cultures for treating ailments.
