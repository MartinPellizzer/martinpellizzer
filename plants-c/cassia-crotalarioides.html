Cassia crotalarioides

Cassia crotalarioides, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange floral bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia crotalarioides is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
