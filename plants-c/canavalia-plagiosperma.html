Canavalia plagiosperma

Canavalia plagiosperma, commonly known as the evergreen cowpea, is a tropical legume native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and showy, purple or red flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, spiny pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in various culinary traditions. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important plant in agroforestry and sustainable farming practices.
