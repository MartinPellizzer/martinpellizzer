Casearia microphylla

Casearia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved casearia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers, followed by distinctive, woody, elongated fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
