Callicarpa cana var. repanda

Callicarpa cana var. repanda, commonly known as the redcurrant bush, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental and edible gardening.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract various pollinators and wildlife.
