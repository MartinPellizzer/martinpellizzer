Carex amurensis

Carex amurensis, commonly known as the Amur sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex amurensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
