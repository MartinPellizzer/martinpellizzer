Chrysopsis hirtella

Chrysopsis hirtella, commonly known as the hairy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green bracts, and the leaves are hairy, giving it its common name.

It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
