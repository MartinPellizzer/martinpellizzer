Carthamus eriocephalus

Carthamus eriocephalus, commonly known as the Persian coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, yellow-orange ray flowers that resemble a cone. The plant produces small, dark brown seeds enclosed in a spiny, globe-shaped receptacle. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
