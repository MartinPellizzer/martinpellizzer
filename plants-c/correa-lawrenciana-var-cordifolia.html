Correa lawrenciana var. cordifolia

Correa lawrenciana var. cordifolia, commonly known as the Heartleaf Correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy, evergreen leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is well adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.

The flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
