Crotalaria sessiliflora f. garhwalensis

Crotalaria sessiliflora f. garhwalensis, commonly known as the Garhwal false flax, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stem and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of the Fabaceae family. It is native to the Himalayan regions of India and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
