Centaurea microptilon var. emporitana

Centaurea microptilon var. emporitana, commonly known as the Emporitana Bluet, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers and slender, branched stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
