Campsiandra macrocarpa

Campsiandra macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The tree produces yellow, star-shaped flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit, resembling a starfruit, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production in suitable climates.
