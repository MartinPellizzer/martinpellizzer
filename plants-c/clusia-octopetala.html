Clusia octopetala

Clusia octopetala, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Clusia octopetala is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought and salt spray, making it a popular choice for coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
