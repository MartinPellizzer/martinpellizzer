Calamagrostis pinetorum

Calamagrostis pinetorum, commonly known as the pinegrass, is a perennial grass native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowering stems produce panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

Calamagrostis pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
