Cassine peragua

Cassine peragua, commonly known as the swamp cassine, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. Cassine peragua is often found in wetland areas and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitats.
