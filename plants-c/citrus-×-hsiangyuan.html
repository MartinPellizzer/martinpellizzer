Citrus × hsiangyuan

Citrus × hsiangyuan, commonly known as the Hsiangyuan mandarin, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from China.

It is characterized by its small, round, and thick-skinned fruits that are typically orange in color. The plant produces a high amount of aromatic essential oils in its peel, contributing to its distinct fragrance. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires a significant amount of sunlight.

The fruit is prized for its sweet and tangy flavor, making it a popular choice for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
