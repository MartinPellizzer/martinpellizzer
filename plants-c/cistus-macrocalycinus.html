Cistus macrocalycinus

Cistus macrocalycinus, commonly known as the large-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, leathery leaves and clusters of pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Cistus macrocalycinus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
