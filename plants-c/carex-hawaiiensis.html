Carex hawaiiensis

Carex hawaiiensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

Carex hawaiiensis is characterized by its unique ecological role in Hawaiian ecosystems and is often found in areas with high humidity and limited sunlight.
