Chamaecyparis obtusa var. obtusa

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. obtusa, commonly known as Japanese cedar or Hinoki cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens.

Its slow growth rate and longevity make it a valued specimen in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
