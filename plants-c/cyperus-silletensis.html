Cyperus silletensis

Cyperus silletensis, commonly known as the Sillet's flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and flooded soils. It has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
