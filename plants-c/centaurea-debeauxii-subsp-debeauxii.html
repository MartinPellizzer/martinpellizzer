Centaurea debeauxii subsp. debeauxii

Centaurea debeauxii subsp.

debeauxii, commonly known as the Beaux's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to pink composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has upright stems with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bracts at the base of its flower heads.

This subspecies is often found in Mediterranean regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
