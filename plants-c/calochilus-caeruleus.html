Calochilus caeruleus

Calochilus caeruleus, commonly known as the blue-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue and purple flowers with prominent veins, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in woodland areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Calochilus caeruleus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
