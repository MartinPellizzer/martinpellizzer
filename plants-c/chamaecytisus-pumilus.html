Chamaecytisus pumilus

Chamaecytisus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Chamaecytisus pumilus is also known for its adaptability to coastal environments and its resistance to salt spray.
