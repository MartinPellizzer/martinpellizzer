Calamagrostis montana

Calamagrostis montana, commonly known as mountain reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and reaches heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves and produces panicles of pale green to golden yellow flowers in late summer. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.

Calamagrostis montana is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
