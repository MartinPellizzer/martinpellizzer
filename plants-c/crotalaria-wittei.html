Crotalaria wittei

Crotalaria wittei, commonly known as the yellow crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds through the wind.

Crotalaria wittei is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
