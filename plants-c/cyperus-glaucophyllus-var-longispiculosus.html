Cyperus glaucophyllus var. longispiculosus

Cyperus glaucophyllus var. longispiculosus, commonly known as the long-spined flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in botanical taxonomy.
