Caladium rubellum

Caladium rubellum, commonly known as the red-veined caladium, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins contrasting against a pale green or white background. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Caladium rubellum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

It is typically grown as an annual in regions with cold winters, where it is prized for its decorative value in gardens and landscapes.
