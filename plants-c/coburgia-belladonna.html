Coburgia belladonna

Coburgia belladonna, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its large, showy, pale purple to white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant features velvety, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a strong, unpleasant odor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing as a shrub or small tree.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is a popular ornamental plant in gardens and collections.
