Calyptranthes cordifolia

Calyptranthes cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf calyptranthes, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and emit a mild fragrance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in warmer climates.
