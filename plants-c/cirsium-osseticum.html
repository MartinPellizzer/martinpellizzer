Cirsium osseticum

Cirsium osseticum, commonly known as the Ossetian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are covered in stiff, prickly hairs, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are usually purple or pink, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.
