Camellia japonica f. lancifolia

Camellia japonica f. lancifolia, commonly known as the Japanese camellia with lanceolate leaves.

This variety is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that give it a distinct appearance compared to other camellia species. It produces showy, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for garden borders or container planting.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, requiring consistent moisture during the growing season.
