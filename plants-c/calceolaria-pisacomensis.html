Calceolaria pisacomensis

Calceolaria pisacomensis, commonly known as the Peruvian slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found growing in rocky soils at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.
