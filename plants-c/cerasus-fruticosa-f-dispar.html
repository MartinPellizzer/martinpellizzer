Cerasus fruticosa f. dispar

Cerasus fruticosa f. dispar, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or shrub cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller leaves compared to the typical species. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or dark red in color. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive flowers and foliage.

This form is well-suited for garden use in containers or as part of a hedgerow.
