Calamus lobbianus

Calamus lobbianus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spadices, a common feature among many palm relatives. Calamus lobbianus is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
