Ciminalis glauca

Ciminalis glauca, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that provide a striking contrast to its floral display. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.

Botanically, it is characterized by its erect stem and its ability to thrive in partial to full sun.
