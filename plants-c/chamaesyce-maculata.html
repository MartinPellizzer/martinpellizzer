Chamaesyce maculata

Chamaesyce maculata, commonly known as the spotted spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features succulent, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
