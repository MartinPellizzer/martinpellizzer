Celastrus macrophyllus

Celastrus macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf bindweed, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often larger than those of related species, with a glossy green surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are elongated, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Celastrus macrophyllus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
