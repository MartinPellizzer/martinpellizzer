Cranichis similis

Cranichis similis, commonly known as the common twayblade, is a small, perennial orchid native to North America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with two opposite leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Cranichis similis is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi, which play a crucial role in its nutrient uptake.
