Caltha howellii

Caltha howellii, commonly known as Howell's Crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, which includes buttercups and other flowering plants. The plant features delicate, nodding yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. Its leaves are divided into three parts, forming a characteristic trifoliate shape.

Caltha howellii thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.
