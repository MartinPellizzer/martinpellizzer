Cerasus × oneyamensis var. takasawana

Cerasus × oneyamensis var. takasawana, commonly known as the Takasawana cherry, is a variety of Prunus species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of small, pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with a rounded crown and smooth, reddish bark. Its leaves are oval-shaped, dark green, and turn a vibrant red in the autumn.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
