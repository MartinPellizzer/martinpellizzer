Colutea paulsenii

Colutea paulsenii, commonly known as the Paulsen's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its seed pods contain small, round seeds that are often dispersed by wind.

Colutea paulsenii is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
