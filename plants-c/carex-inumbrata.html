Carex inumbrata

Carex inumbrata, commonly known as shadow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in early summer. Carex inumbrata thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats, often forming dense colonies.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small mammals.
