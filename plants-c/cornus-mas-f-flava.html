Cornus mas f. flava

Cornus mas f. flava, commonly known as the yellow cornel.

This plant is a flowering shrub belonging to the Cornaceae family, characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in woodland and scrub habitats. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the fruit is a small, red drupe.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
