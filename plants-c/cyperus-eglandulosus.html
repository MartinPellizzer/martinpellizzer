Cyperus eglandulosus

Cyperus eglandulosus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or muddy habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often difficult to distinguish from other sedges. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on wind pollination.

Cyperus eglandulosus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions and is adapted to thrive in environments with high humidity and saturated soil.
