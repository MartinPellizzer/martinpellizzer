Convolvulus reticulatus

Convolvulus reticulatus, commonly known as the netleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive netted venation. The plant produces trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus reticulatus is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
