Clerodendrum rumphianum

Clerodendrum rumphianum, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant also has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
