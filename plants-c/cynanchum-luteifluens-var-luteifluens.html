Cynanchum luteifluens var. luteifluens

Cynanchum luteifluens var. luteifluens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered milkweed, is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive corona. Its latex is milky and can be toxic, containing various cardioactive compounds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
