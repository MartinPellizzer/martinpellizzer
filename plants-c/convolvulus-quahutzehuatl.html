Convolvulus quahutzehuatl

Convolvulus quahutzehuatl, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heliotropic leaves that follow the sun's movement. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it both a decorative and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
