Centaurea ptarmicifolia

Centaurea ptarmicifolia, commonly known as the lesser centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
