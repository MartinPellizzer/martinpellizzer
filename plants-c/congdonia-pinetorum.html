Congdonia pinetorum

Congdonia pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest moss, is a rare and delicate species of moss that thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically found in coniferous forests.

It has a low-growing, carpet-like structure with closely packed, slender shoots that form dense mats on the forest floor. The plant's leaves are elongated and tapering, with a smooth surface and a distinct midrib, and they remain upright throughout the growing season. Congdonia pinetorum reproduces through spores, which are released from small, capsule-like structures that develop on the stems.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered an indicator species for undisturbed, old-growth pine forests.
