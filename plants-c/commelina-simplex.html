Commelina simplex

Commelina simplex, commonly known as the common spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, arranged in a whorl at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina simplex is a member of the Commelinaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils.
