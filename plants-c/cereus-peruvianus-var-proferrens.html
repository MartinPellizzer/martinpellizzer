Cereus peruvianus var. proferrens

Cereus peruvianus var. proferrens, commonly known as the Peruvian torch cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of white, night-blooming flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its stems are covered in areoles, which bear both spines and clusters of small, white flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
