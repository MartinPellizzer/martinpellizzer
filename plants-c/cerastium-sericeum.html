Cerastium sericeum

Cerastium sericeum, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Cerastium sericeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
