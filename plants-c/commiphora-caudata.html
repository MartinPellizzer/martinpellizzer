Commiphora caudata

Commiphora caudata, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its aromatic resin, which has been traditionally used in incense and medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Commiphora caudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional practices.
