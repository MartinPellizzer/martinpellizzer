Capparis laurifolia

Capparis laurifolia, commonly known as the fragrant caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with glossy, lance-shaped leaves that emit a mild fragrance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by edible, green berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Capparis laurifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
