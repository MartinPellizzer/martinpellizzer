Colocasia odora var. rubra

Colocasia odora var. rubra, commonly known as the red-scented elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 60 cm in diameter, often with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces striking purple flowers with a fragrant, sweet scent, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments and requires well-draining soil rich in organic matter.

This variety is popular in ornamental gardening for its dramatic foliage and unique floral display.
