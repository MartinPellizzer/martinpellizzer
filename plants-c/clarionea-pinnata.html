Clarionea pinnata

Clarionea pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf clarionea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are deeply divided into narrow, thread-like segments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Clarionea pinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
