Callista metuliflora

Callista metuliflora, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that resemble those of the passionfruit. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a sweet, aromatic scent. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and hummingbirds.

Callista metuliflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
