Crepis bertiscea

Crepis bertiscea, commonly known as the Bertis's bentgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this species thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
