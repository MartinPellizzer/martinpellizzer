Chrysophyllum pauciflorum

Chrysophyllum pauciflorum, commonly known as the little-flowered chrysophyllum, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and have a sweet, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Chrysophyllum pauciflorum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
