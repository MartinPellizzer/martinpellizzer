Cryptomeria japonica var. radicans

Cryptomeria japonica var. radicans, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or kuril cedar, is a variant of the Japanese cedar native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pendulous branches that give it a weeping appearance. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual contrast. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is valued for its unique form and is often used in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
