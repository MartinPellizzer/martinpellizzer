Campanula pusilla var. modesta

Campanula pusilla var. modesta, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a small, herbaceous perennial that thrives in woodland and shaded garden environments.

It features delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact growth habit with upright, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It is native to parts of Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.

Campanula pusilla var. modesta prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.
