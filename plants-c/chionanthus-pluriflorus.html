Chionanthus pluriflorus

Chionanthus pluriflorus, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Chionanthus pluriflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
