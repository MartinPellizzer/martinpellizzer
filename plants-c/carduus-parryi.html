Carduus parryi

Carduus parryi, commonly known as Parry's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical head.

Carduus parryi plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
