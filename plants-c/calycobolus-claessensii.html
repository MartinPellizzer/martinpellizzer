Calycobolus claessensii

Calycobolus claessensii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.
