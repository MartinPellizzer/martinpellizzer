Cyperus pseudobrunneus

Cyperus pseudobrunneus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, making it common in marshy areas and along waterways. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it often serves as a host for various insect species.

Cyperus pseudobrunneus is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
