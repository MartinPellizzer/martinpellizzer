Cirsium × hunyadense

Cirsium × hunyadense, commonly known as the Hungarian thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Cirsium × hunyadense is native to Hungary and surrounding regions in Central Europe.
