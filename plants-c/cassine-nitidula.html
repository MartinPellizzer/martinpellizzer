Cassine nitidula

Cassine nitidula, commonly known as the silky cassine, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elliptical, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near freshwater habitats.

Cassine nitidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
