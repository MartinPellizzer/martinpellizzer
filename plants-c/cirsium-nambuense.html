Cirsium nambuense

Cirsium nambuense, commonly known as the Nambu thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in stiff, bristly hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium nambuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in its native environment.
