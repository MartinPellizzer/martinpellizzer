Chamaemelum cartilagineum

Chamaemelum cartilagineum, commonly known as the cartilaginous chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and feathery leaves. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny conditions. Its essential oils, derived from the flowers, are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Chamaemelum cartilagineum is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
