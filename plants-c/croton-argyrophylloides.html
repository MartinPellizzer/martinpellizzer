Croton argyrophylloides

Croton argyrophylloides, commonly known as the silver-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that often have a silvery or variegated appearance due to the presence of fine, white or gray hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its ornamental value is primarily due to its foliage, though it can also be grown for its unique floral display.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
