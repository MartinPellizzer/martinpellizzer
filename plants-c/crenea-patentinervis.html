Crenea patentinervis

Crenea patentinervis, commonly known as the broad-veined water plant, is a species of aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive leaf structure.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with prominent, parallel veins that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. Its leaves are often floating or submerged, depending on the water conditions.

Crenea patentinervis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
