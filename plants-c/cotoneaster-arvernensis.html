Cotoneaster arvernensis

Cotoneaster arvernensis, commonly known as the Arvernian cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to France.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, red berries that persist through winter. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white and appear in clusters.

Cotoneaster arvernensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
