Capsicum petenense

Capsicum petenense, commonly known as the Petén pepper, is a species native to Central America, particularly Guatemala and Belize.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and has a mild, peppery flavor.
