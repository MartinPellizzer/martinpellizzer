Campanula polymorpha f. angustifolia

Campanula polymorpha f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender stems that often grow in a trailing or upright habit, depending on the environment. The leaves are elongated and narrow, typically with a rough or hairy texture, and the flowers are bell-shaped, usually blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
