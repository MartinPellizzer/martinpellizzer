Chamaedorea exorrhiza

Chamaedorea exorrhiza, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen or the Mexican feather plant, is a tropical evergreen palm native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pinnate leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its health.

Chamaedorea exorrhiza is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
