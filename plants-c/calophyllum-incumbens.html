Calophyllum incumbens

Calophyllum incumbens, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its botanical features include a thick, rough bark and a dense canopy, making it a notable species in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
