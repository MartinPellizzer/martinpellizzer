Carex brongniartii var. densa

Carex brongniartii var. densa, commonly known as dense sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in dense, compact clusters. Its inflorescence consists of a spiky, cylindrical structure that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
