Cistus undulatus

Cistus undulatus, commonly known as the undulating rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. It is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Cistus undulatus is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
