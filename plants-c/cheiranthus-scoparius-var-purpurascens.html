Cheiranthus scoparius var. purpurascens

Cheiranthus scoparius var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant purple or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found along walls or in dry habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

The flowers are arranged in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
