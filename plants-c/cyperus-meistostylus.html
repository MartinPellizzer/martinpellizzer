Cyperus meistostylus

Cyperus meistostylus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous, and it is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Due to its adaptability, it can be found in various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
