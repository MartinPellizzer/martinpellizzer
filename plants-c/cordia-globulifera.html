Cordia globulifera

Cordia globulifera, commonly known as the globe tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by distinctive round, orange-red fruits. Its botanical features include a shallow root system and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
