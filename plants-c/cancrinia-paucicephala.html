Cancrinia paucicephala

Cancrinia paucicephala, commonly known as the dwarf sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated in coastal gardens for its ornamental value.
