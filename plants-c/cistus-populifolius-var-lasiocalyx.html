Cistus populifolius var. lasiocalyx

Cistus populifolius var. lasiocalyx, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
