Cleome aculeata var. diffusa

Cleome aculeata var. diffusa, commonly known as the prickly cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and palmate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
