Caladium bicolor subvar. rosaceum

Caladium bicolor subvar. rosaceum, commonly known as the Rosy Glow caladium, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display vibrant shades of pink, red, and white. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth. Its foliage is the primary ornamental feature, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.

It is typically grown as an annual in temperate climates but can be perennial in tropical regions.
