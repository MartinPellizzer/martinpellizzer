Cyanococcus vacillans

Cyanococcus vacillans, commonly known as the wavering blue moss, is a unique species of cyanobacteria that forms dense, carpet-like colonies.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant's structure consists of filamentous cells that intertwine to create a cohesive mat. It is capable of photosynthesis and can fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Its vibrant blue-green color is due to the presence of phycocyanin, a pigment common in cyanobacteria.
