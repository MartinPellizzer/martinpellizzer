Cinnamomum culitlawan

Cinnamomum culitlawan, commonly known as the Philippine cinnamon or culitlawan tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as cinnamon and laurel. The tree is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval-shaped berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its essential oils.
