Cyperus ochraceus

Cyperus ochraceus, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green to yellowish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Cyperus ochraceus is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

It is an important plant in ecological studies due to its role in wetland ecosystems and its adaptability to various water conditions.
