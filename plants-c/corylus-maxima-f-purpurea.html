Corylus maxima f. purpurea

Corylus maxima f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple husk hazelnut, is a decorative variant of the European hazelnut.

It is characterized by its striking purple-brown husks that contrast with its edible, nutty seeds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its foliage is often a deep green, turning reddish in the fall, adding to its ornamental value.

This variety is prized in gardens for its unique appearance and winter interest.
