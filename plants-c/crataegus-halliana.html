Crataegus halliana

Crataegus halliana, commonly known as the Hall's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other hawthorn species.

Its adaptability to various soil types and partial shade makes it a common sight in woodland edges and thickets.
