Carex eggertii

Carex eggertii, commonly known as the Eggert's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Carex eggertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
