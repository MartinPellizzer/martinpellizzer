Cirsium wallowense

Cirsium wallowense, commonly known as the Wallowa thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.

Cirsium wallowense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and wildlife.
