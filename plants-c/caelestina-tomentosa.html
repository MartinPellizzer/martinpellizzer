Caelestina tomentosa

Caelestina tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

Caelestina tomentosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
