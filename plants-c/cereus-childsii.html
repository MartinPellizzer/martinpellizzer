Cereus childsii

Cereus childsii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that are typically green and fleshy. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often blooming during the holiday season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cereus childsii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
