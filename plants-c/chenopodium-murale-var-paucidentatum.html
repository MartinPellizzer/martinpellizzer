Chenopodium murale var. paucidentatum

Chenopodium murale var. paucidentatum, commonly known as sandmat, is a herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution in dry environments.
