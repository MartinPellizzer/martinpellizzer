Citrus decumana

Citrus decumana, commonly known as the Sicilian lemon.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. The plant produces small, yellow, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to lemons but have a distinct flavor. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, with a strong aromatic scent.

Citrus decumana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of citrus fruits.
