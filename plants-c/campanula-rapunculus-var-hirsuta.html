Campanula rapunculus var. hirsuta

Campanula rapunculus var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
