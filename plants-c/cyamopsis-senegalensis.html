Cyamopsis senegalensis

Cyamopsis senegalensis, commonly known as wild oat, is a perennial grass native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Cyamopsis senegalensis is also valued for its potential as a forage crop and its ability to prevent soil erosion.
