Caragana inermis

Caragana inermis, commonly known as the smooth caragana, is a deciduous shrub native to the steppes and semi-desert regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, arid environments and has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions. It produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caragana inermis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and hardiness.
