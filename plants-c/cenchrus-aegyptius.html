Cenchrus aegyptius

Cenchrus aegyptius, commonly known as the Egyptian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions due to its deep root system.

Cenchrus aegyptius is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
