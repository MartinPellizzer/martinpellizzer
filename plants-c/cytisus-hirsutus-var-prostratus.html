Cytisus hirsutus var. prostratus

Cytisus hirsutus var. prostratus, commonly known as creeping yellow broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming a dense mat of vegetation. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, hairy, elliptical leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.
