Cereus calvescens

Cereus calvescens, commonly known as the night bloomer or moon cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, spindly stem and its ability to produce large, white flowers at night. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water.

Its unique blooming habit makes it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
