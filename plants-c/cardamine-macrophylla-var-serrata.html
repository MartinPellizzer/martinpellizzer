Cardamine macrophylla var. serrata

Cardamine macrophylla var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated water buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges, typically submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and marshes.

Its leaves are often fully submerged, while its stems may be partially emergent.
