Cyathea keysseri

Cyathea keysseri, commonly known as the Keyser's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its upright structure and enabling it to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Cyathea keysseri plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
