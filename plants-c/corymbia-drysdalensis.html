Corymbia drysdalensis

Corymbia drysdalensis, commonly known as the Drysdale apple.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family, native to Western Australia. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. It produces small, round, red or yellow fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy soils.
