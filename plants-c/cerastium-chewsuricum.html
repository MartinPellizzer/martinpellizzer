Cerastium chewsuricum

Cerastium chewsuricum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
