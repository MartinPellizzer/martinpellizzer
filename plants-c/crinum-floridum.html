Crinum floridum

Crinum floridum, commonly known as the Florida crinum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the top of the stem. Crinum floridum is adapted to wetland environments and can thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.
