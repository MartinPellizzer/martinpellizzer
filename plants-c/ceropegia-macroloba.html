Ceropegia macroloba

Ceropegia macroloba, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or arrowleaf ceropegia, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract specific pollinators like moths and flies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
