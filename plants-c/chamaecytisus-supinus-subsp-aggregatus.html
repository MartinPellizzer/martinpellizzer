Chamaecytisus supinus subsp. aggregatus

Chamaecytisus supinus subsp.

aggregatus, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense, wiry stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

The plant produces small, yellow,蝶形花 (dial-petaled) flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
