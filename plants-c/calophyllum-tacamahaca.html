Calophyllum tacamahaca

Calophyllum tacamahaca, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that give way to bright orange, fleshy fruits. The fruit's interior contains several segments filled with a white, edible pulp.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
