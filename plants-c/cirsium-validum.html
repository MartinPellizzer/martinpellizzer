Cirsium validum

Cirsium validum, commonly known as the tall thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Cirsium validum is known for its robust growth and is often found in open habitats where it can thrive with minimal care.
