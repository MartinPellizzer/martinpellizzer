Cousinia tirinensis

Cousinia tirinensis, commonly known as the Tirinensis globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Cousinia tirinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
