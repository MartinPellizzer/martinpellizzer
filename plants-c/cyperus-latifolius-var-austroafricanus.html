Cyperus latifolius var. austroafricanus

Cyperus latifolius var. austroafricanus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas, along riverbanks, and in poorly drained soils.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
