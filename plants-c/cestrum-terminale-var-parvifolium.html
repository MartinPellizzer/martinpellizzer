Cestrum terminale var. parvifolium

Cestrum terminale var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight pubescence.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its nocturnal blooming habit and attractive foliage.
