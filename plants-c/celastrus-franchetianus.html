Celastrus franchetianus

Celastrus franchetianus, commonly known as the Chinese staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to China.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
