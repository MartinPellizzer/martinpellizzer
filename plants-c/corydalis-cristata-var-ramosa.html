Corydalis cristata var. ramosa

Corydalis cristata var. ramosa, commonly known as the frilled poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, frilled, papery flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its unique floral display.
