Cissus maranguensis

Cissus maranguensis, commonly known as the African dewberry, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing habit and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with a velvety underside, and its stems are often armed with tendrils for support. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

Cissus maranguensis is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
