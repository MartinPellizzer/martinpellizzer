Cinnamomum panayense

Cinnamomum panayense, commonly known as Panay cinnamon, is a rare evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval-shaped berries. Its essential oils, extracted from the bark and leaves, are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a critically endangered species.
