Cyrtocarpa velutinifolia

Cyrtocarpa velutinifolia, commonly known as the velvet-leafed cyrtocarpa, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Cyrtocarpa velutinifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.
