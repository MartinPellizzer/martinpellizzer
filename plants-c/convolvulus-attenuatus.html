Convolvulus attenuatus

Convolvulus attenuatus, commonly known as the slender bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a trailing or climbing annual herb characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a yellow or greenish throat. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
