Catapodium rigidum subsp. majus

Catapodium rigidum subsp.

majus, commonly known as the rigid tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color, which can vary depending on environmental conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed sites.

Botanically, it is notable for its robust root system and its ability to tolerate a range of environmental stresses.
