Carex straminea f. multa

Carex straminea f. multa, commonly known as the variegated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
