Cattleya × leucoglossa

Cattleya × leucoglossa, commonly known as the white-lipped cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with white petals and a distinctive pink or red lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are highly prized for their beauty and fragrance.

Cattleya × leucoglossa is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its vibrant colors and ease of cultivation.
