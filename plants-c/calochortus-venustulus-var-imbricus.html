Calochortus venustulus var. imbricus

Calochortus venustulus var. imbricus, commonly known as the imbricated mariposa lily, is a striking bulbous perennial native to California.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and sometimes orange, with a distinctive, often recurved petal. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats, such as chaparral or grassland. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate and elegant blooms.
