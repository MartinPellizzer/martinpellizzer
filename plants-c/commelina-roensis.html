Commelina roensis

Commelina roensis, commonly known as the Java arrowhead, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive arrowhead pattern, and its flowers are small, blue-violet, and clustered in umbels. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Commelina roensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
