Copernicia nigra

Copernicia nigra, commonly known as the black copernicia, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark, leathery fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts, and the plant plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various animals.

Copernicia nigra is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
