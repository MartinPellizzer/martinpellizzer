Carex nexa

Carex nexa, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex nexa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
