Chaetothylax phyllostachyus

Chaetothylax phyllostachyus, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in moist, tropical environments. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with culms that are hollow and segmented.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
