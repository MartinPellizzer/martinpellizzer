Crataegus obovatifolia

Crataegus obovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Crataegus obovatifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
