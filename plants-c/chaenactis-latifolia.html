Chaenactis latifolia

Chaenactis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has basal leaves that are broad and lobed. Its showy, pink to purple flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often found in open slopes and meadows, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
