Cryptocoryne scurrilis

Cryptocoryne scurrilis, commonly known as the dwarf cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant thrives in low to moderate light conditions and is often found in slow-moving freshwater habitats. It is popular in aquariums due to its hardiness and ability to tolerate a wide range of water parameters.

Cryptocoryne scurrilis is also known for its ability to absorb nitrates, making it beneficial for maintaining water quality in planted tanks.
