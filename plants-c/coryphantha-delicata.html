Coryphantha delicata

Coryphantha delicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pincushion cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by reddish-brown fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a compact growth habit that makes it well adapted to arid environments.

Coryphantha delicata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
