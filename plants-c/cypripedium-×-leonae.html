Cypripedium × leonae

Cypripedium × leonae, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium micranthum.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pouch-like flower that typically ranges in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive dark red or purple lip. The plant is native to regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces a single flower per stem, making it a striking ornamental plant.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, supporting specific pollinators and contributing to biodiversity in its native habitats.
