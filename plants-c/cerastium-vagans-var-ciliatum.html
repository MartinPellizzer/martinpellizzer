Cerastium vagans var. ciliatum

Cerastium vagans var. ciliatum, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant features dense, hairy leaves that are often ciliate, giving it its specific variety name. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.
