Carex pediformis var. obliqua

Carex pediformis var. obliqua, commonly known as the slanted sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly curved. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, typically in shades of green or brown, which appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
