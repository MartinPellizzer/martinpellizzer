Centaurea spectabilis

Centaurea spectabilis, commonly known as the showy cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by bright yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its height ranges from 30 to 90 centimeters, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Centaurea spectabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public landscapes.
