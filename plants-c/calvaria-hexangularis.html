Calvaria hexangularis

Calvaria hexangularis, commonly known as the six-sided skullcap, is a unique flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive hexagonal seed pods, which give the plant its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, textured surface.

Calvaria hexangularis is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
