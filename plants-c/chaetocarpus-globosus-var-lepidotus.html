Chaetocarpus globosus var. lepidotus

Chaetocarpus globosus var. lepidotus, commonly known as the velvethead sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the southern coasts of Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal environments. The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate salt spray and harsh coastal conditions.
