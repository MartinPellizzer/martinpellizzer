Cornus alternifolia var. ochroleuca

Cornus alternifolia var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white cornel dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically green with a white or pale underside. The plant produces small, white flowers in upright clusters during the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance, providing food for birds and insects.
