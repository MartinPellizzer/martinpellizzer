Calycanthus floridus var. oblongifolius

Calycanthus floridus var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the sweetshrub or southern sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fragrant, red-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible for birds but not for humans.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
