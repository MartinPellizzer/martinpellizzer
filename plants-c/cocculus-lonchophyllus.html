Cocculus lonchophyllus

Cocculus lonchophyllus, commonly known as the long-leaf cocculus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. Its leaves are typically elongated and have a prominent midrib, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
