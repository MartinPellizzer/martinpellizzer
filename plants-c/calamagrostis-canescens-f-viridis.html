Calamagrostis canescens f. viridis

Calamagrostis canescens f. viridis, commonly known as the green-haired reed, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green color, giving it its common name. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet that is usually pale green or brown in color.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
