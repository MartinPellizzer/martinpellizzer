Comarostaphylis diversifolia

Comarostaphylis diversifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed rock heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in whorls around the stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom in spring.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
