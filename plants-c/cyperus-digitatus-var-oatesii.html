Cyperus digitatus var. oatesii

Cyperus digitatus var. oatesii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

This species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
