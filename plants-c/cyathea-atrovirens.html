Cyathea atrovirens

Cyathea atrovirens, commonly known as the black-veined tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, compound fronds that are dark green with prominent, contrasting veins. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, making it a striking feature in tropical landscapes.

Cyathea atrovirens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
