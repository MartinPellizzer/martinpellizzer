Chenopodium polyspermum var. controversans

Chenopodium polyspermum var. controversans, commonly known as the common goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
