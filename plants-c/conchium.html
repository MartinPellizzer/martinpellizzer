Conchium

Conchium, commonly known as the bladder wort, is a genus of aquatic plants belonging to the family Bladderworts.

These plants are characterized by their small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms, aiding in their nutrient acquisition. Conchium species are typically found in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. They have a simple morphology with submerged leaves and specialized structures for capturing prey.

The genus is known for its unique adaptations that allow it to thrive in nutrient-poor aquatic habitats.
