Chironia capsularis

Chironia capsularis, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant features showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
