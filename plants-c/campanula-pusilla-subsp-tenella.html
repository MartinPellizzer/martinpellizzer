Campanula pusilla subsp. tenella

Campanula pusilla subsp.

tenella, commonly known as the small bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or woodland environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
