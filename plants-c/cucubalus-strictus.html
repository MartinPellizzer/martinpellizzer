Cucubalus strictus

Cucubalus strictus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, orange-red capsule that splits open when mature.

Cucubalus strictus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
