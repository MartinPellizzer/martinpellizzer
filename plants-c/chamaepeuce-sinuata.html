Chamaepeuce sinuata

Chamaepeuce sinuata, commonly known as the red sandreed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, grass-like foliage and striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is highly drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to desert environments. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its hardiness and minimal water requirements.
