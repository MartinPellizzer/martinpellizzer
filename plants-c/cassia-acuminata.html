Cassia acuminata

Cassia acuminata, commonly known as the sicklepod or Indian laburnum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant produces bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets.

Cassia acuminata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
