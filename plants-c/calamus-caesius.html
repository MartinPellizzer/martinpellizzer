Calamus caesius

Calamus caesius, commonly known as the blue bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Calamus caesius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
