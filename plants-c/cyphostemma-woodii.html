Cyphostemma woodii

Cyphostemma woodii, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitats.
