Cargillia laxa

Cargillia laxa, commonly known as the slender sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments.

Cargillia laxa is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate mild drought conditions.
