Clematis fusca var. middendorffii

Clematis fusca var. middendorffii, commonly known as the black clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, woody stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 feet, often twining around supports. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.
