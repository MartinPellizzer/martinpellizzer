Cucumis flexuosus var. reflexus

Cucumis flexuosus var. reflexus, commonly known as the curled cucumber, is a unique variant of the cucumber species characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and reflexed fruit shape.

This plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and the stems are hairy and trailing. The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and the fruit develops in a spiral or curled pattern.

This botanical feature makes it a subject of interest for both horticultural and scientific study.
