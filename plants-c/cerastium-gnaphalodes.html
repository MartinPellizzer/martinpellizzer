Cerastium gnaphalodes

Cerastium gnaphalodes, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
