Calceolaria hispida subsp. hispida

Calceolaria hispida subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the hairy calceolaria, is a herbaceous plant native to South America. It features distinctive calyces that resemble small pouches, which are characteristic of the Calceolaria genus.

The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with contrasting markings. Its leaves are typically hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
