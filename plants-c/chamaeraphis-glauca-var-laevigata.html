Chamaeraphis glauca var. laevigata

Chamaeraphis glauca var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth glassworts, is a succulent plant native to coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid and salty conditions.
