Cytisus supinus var. bulgaricus

Cytisus supinus var. bulgaricus, commonly known as the Bulgarian dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Balkans.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its compact form. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
