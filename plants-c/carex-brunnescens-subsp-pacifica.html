Carex brunnescens subsp. pacifica

Carex brunnescens subsp.

pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific bracken sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of western North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, brownish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is characterized by its ability to thrive in coastal climates with high humidity and moderate temperatures.
