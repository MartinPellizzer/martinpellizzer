Campanula jacquinii subsp. rumeliana

Campanula jacquinii subsp.

rumeliana, commonly known as the Rumelian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region. It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
