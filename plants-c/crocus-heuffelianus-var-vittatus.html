Crocus heuffelianus var. vittatus

Crocus heuffelianus var. vittatus, commonly known as the spotted crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces small, cup-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet with distinctive yellow or white spots. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate and colorful flowers.
