Ceropegia vignaldiana

Ceropegia vignaldiana, commonly known as the Lion's Tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract specific pollinators like flies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among succulent enthusiasts and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
