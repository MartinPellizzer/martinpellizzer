Citrus reticulata var. austera

Citrus reticulata var. austera, commonly known as the dwarf mandarin or kumquat, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and small, orange-colored fruits that are typically eaten whole, including the peel. The plant is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of climates. It produces small white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the fruiting season in the summer.

The plant is also known for its hardiness and adaptability to container gardening.
