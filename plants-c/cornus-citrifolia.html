Cornus citrifolia

Cornus citrifolia, commonly known as the cornelian cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its bark is smooth and can be reddish or grayish in color, and it has a moderate growth rate.

Cornus citrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
