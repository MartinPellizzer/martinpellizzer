Campnosperma

Campnosperma, commonly known as the bottlebrush tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that resemble a bottlebrush, typically composed of numerous stamen clusters. The leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a glossy surface. Native to Australia, these plants are often cultivated for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in various climates.

They also play a role in supporting local wildlife by providing nectar for birds and insects.
