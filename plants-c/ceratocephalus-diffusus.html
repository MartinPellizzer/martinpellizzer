Ceratocephalus diffusus

Ceratocephalus diffusus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers and spiky, leaf-like bracts that surround the flower heads. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It has a short growth period, typically blooming during the winter months.

Ceratocephalus diffusus is adapted to coastal conditions, with mechanisms to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
