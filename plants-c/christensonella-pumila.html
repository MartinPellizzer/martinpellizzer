Christensonella pumila

Christensonella pumila, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandwort, is a small, flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has compact, low-growing stems with small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Christensonella pumila is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
