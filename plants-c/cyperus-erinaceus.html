Cyperus erinaceus

Cyperus erinaceus, commonly known as spiny flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, often surrounded by bracts. Cyperus erinaceus thrives in wet or marshy habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

It is sometimes used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability.
