Campanula saxifraga subsp. saxifraga

Campanula saxifraga subsp.

saxifraga, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with a fine, fuzzy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in cooler climates.
