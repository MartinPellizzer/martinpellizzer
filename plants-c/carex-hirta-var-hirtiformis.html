Carex hirta var. hirtiformis

Carex hirta var. hirtiformis, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it a distinctive texture and appearance.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
