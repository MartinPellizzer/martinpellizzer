Cocculus taiwanianus

Cocculus taiwanianus, commonly known as the Taiwan morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces distinctive, warty, orange-brown fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
