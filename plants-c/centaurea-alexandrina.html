Centaurea alexandrina

Centaurea alexandrina, commonly known as Egyptian knapweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and produces bright yellow to orange flowers with distinctive purple-blue bracts. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in soft, white or grayish pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Centaurea alexandrina is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
