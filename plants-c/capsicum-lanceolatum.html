Capsicum lanceolatum

Capsicum lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf chili or wild pepper, is a species native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate, hence the species name, and are usually arranged alternately along the stem. The flowers are small and yellow, with five petals, and the fruit is a bell-shaped chili pepper that can range in color from green to red.

This plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various cuisines and traditional medicine.
