Cacalia muricata

Cacalia muricata, commonly known as the rough cacalia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, lobed leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Cacalia muricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
