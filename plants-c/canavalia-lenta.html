Canavalia lenta

Canavalia lenta, commonly known as the slow-growing cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Canavalia lenta is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it a useful plant in sustainable agriculture.
