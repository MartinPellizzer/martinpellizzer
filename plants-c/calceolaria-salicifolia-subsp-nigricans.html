Calceolaria salicifolia subsp. nigricans

Calceolaria salicifolia subsp.

nigricans, commonly known as the blackleaf calceolaria, is a perennial herb native to South America. It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often slightly hairy.

The plant produces striking, tubular flowers with a distinctive calyx that resembles a slipper, typically in shades of purple or violet. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or shaded areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
