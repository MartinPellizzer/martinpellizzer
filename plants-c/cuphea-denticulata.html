Cuphea denticulata

Cuphea denticulata, commonly known as the toothed cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble small cups. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It produces vibrant red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Cuphea denticulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
