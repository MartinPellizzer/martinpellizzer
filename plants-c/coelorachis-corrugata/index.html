Coelorachis Corrugata

Coelorachis corrugata is a rare and intriguing fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. Known for its distinctive, deeply lobed fronds and unique, corrugated texture, this plant stands out among its fern relatives. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in dense forest undergrowth where it competes for limited light. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Coelorachis corrugata is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from habitat loss. Its study offers valuable insights into the adaptation strategies of understory plants in tropical ecosystems.
