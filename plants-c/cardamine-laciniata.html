Cardamine laciniata

Cardamine laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. Its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

The plant is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
