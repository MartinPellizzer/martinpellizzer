Cerasus sargentii

Cerasus sargentii, commonly known as Sargent's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Cerasus sargentii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological benefits to local wildlife.
