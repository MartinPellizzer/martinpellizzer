Centaurea subciliaris subsp. acarnanica

Centaurea subciliaris subsp.

acarnanica, commonly known as the Acarnanian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and bristly leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is a perennial herb that produces small, cylindrical seed heads, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
