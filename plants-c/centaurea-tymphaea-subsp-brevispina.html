Centaurea tymphaea subsp. brevispina

Centaurea tymphaea subsp.

brevispina, commonly known as the short-spined centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces bright blue to purple flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or calcareous habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its compact size and ornamental value in gardens and wildflower displays.
