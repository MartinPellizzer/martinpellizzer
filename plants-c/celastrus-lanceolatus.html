Celastrus lanceolatus

Celastrus lanceolatus, commonly known as the American beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall, which are a distinctive botanical feature. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and turn reddish in autumn. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, typically blooming in the spring.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
