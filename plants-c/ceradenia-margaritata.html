Ceradenia margaritata

Ceradenia margaritata, commonly known as the pearl orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, delicate flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Ceradenia margaritata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
