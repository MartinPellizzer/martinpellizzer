Cracca triphylla

Cracca triphylla, commonly known as the three-leafed cracca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with three leaflets. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically opposite or whorled, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Cracca triphylla is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
