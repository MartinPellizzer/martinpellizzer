Cereus robinii var. deeringii

Cereus robinii var. deeringii, commonly known as the Deering's night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to the United States, particularly Texas.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it thrives in arid environments with well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
