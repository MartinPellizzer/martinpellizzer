Calamus spectabilis

Calamus spectabilis, commonly known as the showy calamus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, arranged in dense clusters along the stem. Calamus spectabilis thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular plant in horticulture, though it requires careful management due to its invasive potential in some areas.
