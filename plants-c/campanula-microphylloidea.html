Campanula microphylloidea

Campanula microphylloidea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
