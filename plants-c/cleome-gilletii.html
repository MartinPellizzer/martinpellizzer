Cleome gilletii

Cleome gilletii, commonly known as the spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that resemble spider legs. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome gilletii thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its unique floral display and ease of cultivation.
