Carex matsumurae var. variegata

Carex matsumurae var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated matsunara sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, dark green leaves that have distinctive white or yellow variegation along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This ornamental plant is valued for its attractive foliage and is commonly used in landscaping and garden design.
