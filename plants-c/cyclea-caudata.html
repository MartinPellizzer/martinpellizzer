Cyclea caudata

Cyclea caudata, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cyclea caudata is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
