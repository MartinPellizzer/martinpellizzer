Cirsium × jaegeri

Cirsium × jaegeri, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cirsium vulgare and Cirsium palustre, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex inflorescence and the presence of both ray and disc florets.
