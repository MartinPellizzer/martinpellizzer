Carex dispalatha

Carex dispalatha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually brown or reddish in hue, which appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex dispalatha is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
