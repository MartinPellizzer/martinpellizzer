Capparis arborescens

Capparis arborescens, commonly known as the tree caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its thorny branches and thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Capparis arborescens is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
