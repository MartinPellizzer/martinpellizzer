Convolvulus valentinus var. typicus

Convolvulus valentinus var. typicus, commonly known as the Valentine's convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and seasonal flowering.
