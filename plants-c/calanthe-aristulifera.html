Calanthe aristulifera

Calanthe aristulifera, commonly known as the lance-leaf calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, elongated lip.

Calanthe aristulifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
