Calceolaria dentata subsp. cummingiana

Calceolaria dentata subsp.

cummingiana, commonly known as the toothed slipper orchid, is a member of the Calceolariaceae family. It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

The plant is characterized by its distinctive slipper-shaped flowers, which are typically purple or violet in color. Its leaves are toothed and often arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its unique and attractive blooms.
