Camelina austriaca

Camelina austriaca, commonly known as Austrian mustard, is a small annual plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard species. The plant produces small yellow flowers and has slender, branching stems. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Camelina austriaca is also valued for its potential in phytoremediation due to its ability to accumulate heavy metals.
