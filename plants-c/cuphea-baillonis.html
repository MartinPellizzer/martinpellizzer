Cuphea baillonis

Cuphea baillonis, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Cuphea baillonis is also known for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
