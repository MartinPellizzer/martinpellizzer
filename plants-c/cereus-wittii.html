Cereus wittii

Cereus wittii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night and emit a sweet fragrance. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.

Cereus wittii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and unique flowering behavior.
