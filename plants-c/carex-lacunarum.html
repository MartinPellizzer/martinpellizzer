Carex lacunarum

Carex lacunarum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.

Carex lacunarum prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
