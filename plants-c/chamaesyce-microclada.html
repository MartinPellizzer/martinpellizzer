Chamaesyce microclada

Chamaesyce microclada, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has small, fleshy, spade-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
