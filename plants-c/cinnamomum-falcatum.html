Cinnamomum falcatum

Cinnamomum falcatum, commonly known as the Florida cinnamon or sweet cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves that have a distinctive, almost sword-like shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that contain winged seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in the wetlands of Florida and the Caribbean.
