Carex praecox f. podolica

Carex praecox f. podolica, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in early spring. Native to temperate regions of Europe, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
