Cyathostemma grandifolium

Cyathostemma grandifolium, commonly known as the giant leaf cycad, is a unique plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, which is typical of cycads, and it has a slow growth rate. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
