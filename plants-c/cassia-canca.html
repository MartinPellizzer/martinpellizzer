Cassia canca

Cassia canca, commonly known as yellow flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia canca is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
