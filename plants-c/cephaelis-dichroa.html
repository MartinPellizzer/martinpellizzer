Cephaelis dichroa

Cephaelis dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored celandine, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, ascending stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in terminal racemes and are characterized by their two-toned petals, often pink and white.

Cephaelis dichroa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
