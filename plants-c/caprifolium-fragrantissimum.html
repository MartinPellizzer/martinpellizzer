Caprifolium fragrantissimum

Caprifolium fragrantissimum, commonly known as the sweet-scented honeysuckle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its berries are small and usually red or black when mature.

Caprifolium fragrantissimum thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.
