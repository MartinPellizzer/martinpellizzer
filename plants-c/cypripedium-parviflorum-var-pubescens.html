Cypripedium parviflorum var. pubescens

Cypripedium parviflorum var. pubescens, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy, slipper-shaped flower that is typically pink or white with a greenish throat. The plant has a single, erect stem that bears one or two flowers, with broad, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest floors, and is often found in rich, organic soil.

The species is also notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and its role as an indicator of undisturbed ecosystems.
