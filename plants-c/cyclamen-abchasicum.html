Cyclamen abchasicum

Cyclamen abchasicum, commonly known as the Abkhazian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Abkhazia.

It features distinctive heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and vibrant, spurred flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and shaded environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its tuberous roots allow it to survive harsh winters, and it is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Cyclamen abchasicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific care requirements.
