Carex scita var. scabrinervia

Carex scita var. scabrinervia, commonly known as the scabrous-veined sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers.

The leaves are often covered with a distinctive scabrous texture, giving the plant its varietal name.
