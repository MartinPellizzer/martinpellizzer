Cyperus filicinus f. cleaveri

Cyperus filicinus f. cleaveri, commonly known as the Cleaver's flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus characterized by its distinctive morphological features.

It is a herbaceous plant with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

This subspecies is often found in regions with high humidity and saturated soils, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
