Crataegus corusca f. blothra

Crataegus corusca f. blothra, commonly known as the blothra hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the hawthorn genus native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, red-pink berry that matures in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in natural and cultivated landscapes.
