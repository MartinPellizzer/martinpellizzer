Cyathea holdridgeana

Cyathea holdridgeana, commonly known as the Holdridge's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

Cyathea holdridgeana plays a significant role in tropical ecosystems by offering habitat and contributing to soil stability.
