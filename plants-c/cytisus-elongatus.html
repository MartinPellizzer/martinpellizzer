Cytisus elongatus

Cytisus elongatus, commonly known as the longpod broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, pointed leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to rocky or sandy environments.

Cytisus elongatus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
