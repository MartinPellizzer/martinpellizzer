Calotis cuneifolia

Calotis cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive burr-like fruit that aids in seed dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.

Calotis cuneifolia is drought-tolerant and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
