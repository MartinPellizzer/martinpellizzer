Cinnamomum paiei

Cinnamomum paiei, commonly known as Kew's cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red berries containing winged seeds. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like cinnamaldehyde, contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Cinnamomum paiei thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic properties.
