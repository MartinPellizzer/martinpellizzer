Correa reflexa var. nummulariifolia

Correa reflexa var. nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf correa, is a compact shrub native to south-eastern Australia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
