Cucurbita pepo var. americana

Cucurbita pepo var. americana, commonly known as the Mexican pumpkin, is a species of flowering plant in the gourd family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and vibrant orange to yellow fruits. The plant typically grows on vines that can spread across the ground or climb supports. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This variety is valued for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary and traditional practices.
