Cassia choriophylla var. latifolia

Cassia choriophylla var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
