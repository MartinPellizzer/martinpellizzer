Cyparissia australis

Cyparissia australis, commonly known as the Australian cypress, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by woody, cone-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Cyparissia australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
