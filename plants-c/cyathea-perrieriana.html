Cyathea perrieriana

Cyathea perrieriana, commonly known as the Perrier's tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a large, evergreen tree fern characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, and the plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds.

Cyathea perrieriana is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
