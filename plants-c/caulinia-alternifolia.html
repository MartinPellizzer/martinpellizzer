Caulinia alternifolia

Caulinia alternifolia, commonly known as the alternate-leaf caulinia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale purple.

Caulinia alternifolia is noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
