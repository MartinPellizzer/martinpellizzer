Cnidoscolus marcgravii

Cnidoscolus marcgravii, commonly known as the Brazilian arrowroot or arrowleaf, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
