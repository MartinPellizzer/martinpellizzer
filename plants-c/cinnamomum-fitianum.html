Cinnamomum fitianum

Cinnamomum fitianum, commonly known as the Hainan cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China, particularly Hainan Island.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval-shaped berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a spice.

Cinnamomum fitianum is valued for its unique fragrance and medicinal properties.
