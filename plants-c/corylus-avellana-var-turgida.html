Corylus avellana var. turgida

Corylus avellana var. turgida, commonly known as the European hazelnut or common hazelnut, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets that are oval to lance-shaped and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish male catkins and female flowers in clusters, which develop into edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. The nuts are typically harvested in the fall and are used both for culinary purposes and in traditional medicine.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
