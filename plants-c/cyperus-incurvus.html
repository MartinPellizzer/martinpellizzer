Cyperus incurvus

Cyperus incurvus, commonly known as the curly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. Cyperus incurvus thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
