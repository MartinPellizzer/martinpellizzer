Capparis anomala

Capparis anomala, commonly known as the Indian caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its thorny stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible, round, green fruits. Its leaves and young shoots are used in traditional cuisines, while the plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities.

Capparis anomala thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.
