Crataegus oxyacantha var. coriacea

Crataegus oxyacantha var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-fruited hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a red to orange berry that ripens in autumn, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
