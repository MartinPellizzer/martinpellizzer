Crataegus rosiformis var. carstica

Crataegus rosiformis var. carstica, commonly known as the Carpathian hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but typically consumed by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
