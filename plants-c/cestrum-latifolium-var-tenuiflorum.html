Cestrum latifolium var. tenuiflorum

Cestrum latifolium var. tenuiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flower trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually 5 to 10 centimeters in length. Its most distinctive feature is its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often fragrant at night.

The flowers are adapted for pollination by moths and bats, making it an important plant in nocturnal ecosystems.
