Cryptocoryne amicorum

Cryptocoryne amicorum, commonly known as the friendship cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in shaded, nutrient-rich substrates. Its rhizomes form dense clusters, helping to stabilize the substrate and provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Cryptocoryne amicorum is popular in aquariums due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
