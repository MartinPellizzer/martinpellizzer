Cryptandra sieberi var. latifolia

Cryptandra sieberi var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cryptandra, is a shrub native to the southwestern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
