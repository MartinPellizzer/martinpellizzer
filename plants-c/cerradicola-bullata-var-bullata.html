Cerradicola bullata var. bullata

Cerradicola bullata var. bullata, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has a deciduous habit, shedding its leaves during the dry season, and features a sturdy, often multi-trunked stem. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, and the plant thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
