Cotinus coriarius

Cotinus coriarius, commonly known as the smoke bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery panicles that produce a smoky appearance during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves that are typically green on top and grayish-white underneath. It blooms with small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cotinus coriarius is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
