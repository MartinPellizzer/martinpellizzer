Caesalpinia tsoongii

Caesalpinia tsoongii, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red-orange flower clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

Caesalpinia tsoongii is adapted to a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
