Cyperus indecorus var. decurvatus

Cyperus indecorus var. decurvatus, commonly known as the curly flat sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight curl, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are typically found at the base of the plant.
