Crataegus ornata

Crataegus ornata, commonly known as the ornate hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
