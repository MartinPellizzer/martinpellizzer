Cotyledon farinulenta

Cotyledon farinulenta, commonly known as the Woolly Cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Cotyledon farinulenta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
