Campanula patula peterfii

Campanula patula peterfii, commonly known as the Peterfii bellflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in areas of Kazakhstan and Mongolia. The plant features showy blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Campanula patula peterfii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, cascading flowers.
