Campanula sibirica var. divergens

Campanula sibirica var. divergens, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
