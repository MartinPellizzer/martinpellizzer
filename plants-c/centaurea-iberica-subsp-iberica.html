Centaurea iberica subsp. iberica

Centaurea iberica subsp.

iberica, commonly known as the Iberian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as scrublands and forest edges.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, conical inflorescences, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
