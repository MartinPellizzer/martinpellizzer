Caesalpinia bonducella var. inaequiaculeata

Caesalpinia bonducella var. inaequiaculeata, commonly known as the unequal-armed crown bean, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features distinctive, palmately divided leaves with unequal lobes, giving it its varietal name. Its flowers are typically bright yellow with reddish markings, and it produces elongated, woody pods containing kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
