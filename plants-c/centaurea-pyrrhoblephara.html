Centaurea pyrrhoblephara

Centaurea pyrrhoblephara, commonly known as the red-eyed centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers with dark red centers, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Centaurea pyrrhoblephara is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
