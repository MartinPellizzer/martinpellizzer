Campanula sibirica var. taurica

Campanula sibirica var. taurica, commonly known as the Tauric bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Russia and parts of Ukraine.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and borders.
