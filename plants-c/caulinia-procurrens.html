Caulinia procurrens

Caulinia procurrens, commonly known as the creeping caulinia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Caulinia procurrens is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
