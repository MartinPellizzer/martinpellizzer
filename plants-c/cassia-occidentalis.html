Cassia occidentalis

Cassia occidentalis, commonly known as the western cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate, and its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Cassia occidentalis is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage and flowers.
