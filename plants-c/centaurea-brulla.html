Centaurea brulla

Centaurea brulla, commonly known as the Brulla's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Centaurea brulla is known for its hardy nature and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
