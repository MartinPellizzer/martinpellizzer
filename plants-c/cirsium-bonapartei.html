Cirsium bonapartei

Cirsium bonapartei, commonly known as the Bonaparte's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, erect stems and clustered, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Cirsium bonapartei is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
