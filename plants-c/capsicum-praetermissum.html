Capsicum praetermissum

Capsicum praetermissum, commonly known as the wild chili pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, slender fruits. Its fruits are typically green when unripe and may turn red or orange when mature.

This species is valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of capsaicin.
