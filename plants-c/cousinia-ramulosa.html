Cousinia ramulosa

Cousinia ramulosa, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is extensive, helping it to anchor in loose, dry soils.

Cousinia ramulosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique texture and drought tolerance.
