Centaurea pseudoleucolepis

Centaurea pseudoleucolepis, commonly known as the purple cornflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are upright and branched. Native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

Centaurea pseudoleucolepis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
