Cyathea ferruginea

Cyathea ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and storage for nutrients.

The fronds are evergreen and can persist for several years, contributing to the plant's distinctive appearance.
