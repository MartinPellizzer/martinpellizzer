Clethra divaricata

Clethra divaricata, commonly known as the fragrant sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, capsule that splits open to release tiny, dark seeds.

Clethra divaricata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
