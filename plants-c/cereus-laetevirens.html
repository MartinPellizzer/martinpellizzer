Cereus laetevirens

Cereus laetevirens, commonly known as the Happy Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming at night. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
