Calamus collarifer

Calamus collarifer, commonly known as the black root or black cane reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, rhizomes, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth texture, while its inflorescence consists of clustered, spike-like flowers.

Calamus collarifer is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in wetlands and marshy areas.
