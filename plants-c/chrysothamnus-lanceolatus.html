Chrysothamnus lanceolatus

Chrysothamnus lanceolatus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange inflorescences and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and shrublands. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is adapted to dry conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
