Cerastium semidecandrum subsp. balearicum

Cerastium semidecandrum subsp.

balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, white flowers with five petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
