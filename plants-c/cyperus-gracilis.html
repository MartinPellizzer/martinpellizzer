Cyperus gracilis

Cyperus gracilis, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. Native to wetland and marshy environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
