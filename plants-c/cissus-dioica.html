Cissus dioica

Cissus dioica, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on different plants. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cissus dioica thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found growing along forest edges or in disturbed areas.
