Calamagrostis lapponica var. gorodkowii

Calamagrostis lapponica var. gorodkowii, commonly known as the Gorodkowii reed, is a perennial grass native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically silver-gray in color. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
