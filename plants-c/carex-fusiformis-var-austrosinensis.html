Carex fusiformis var. austrosinensis

Carex fusiformis var. austrosinensis, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually broad at the base and tapering toward the tip. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
