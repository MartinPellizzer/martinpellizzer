Coa

Coa, commonly known as the coffee plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It produces red berries that contain the seeds used to make coffee. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface.

Coa plants can grow up to 10 feet tall, though they are often pruned to encourage fruit production.
