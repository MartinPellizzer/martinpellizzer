Calliandra calycina

Calliandra calycina, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright red, pink, or yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, giving it a vibrant and ornamental appearance. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Calliandra calycina is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
