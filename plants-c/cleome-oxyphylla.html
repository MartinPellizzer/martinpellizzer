Cleome oxyphylla

Cleome oxyphylla, commonly known as the redstem cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Cleome oxyphylla is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
