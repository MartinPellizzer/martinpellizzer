Ceratostema harmsianum

Ceratostema harmsianum, commonly known as the Harms' bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
