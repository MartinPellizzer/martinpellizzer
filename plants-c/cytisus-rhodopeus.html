Cytisus rhodopeus

Cytisus rhodopeus, commonly known as the Rhodope shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Rhodope Mountains in southeastern Europe.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus rhodopeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
