Crataegus monogyna f. denudata

Crataegus monogyna f. denudata, commonly known as the naked-stemmed hawthorn, is a distinct form of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous stems and reduced leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by red, persistent fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
