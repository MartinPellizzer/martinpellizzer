Cinclia

Cinclia, commonly known as the white cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Cinclia has compound leaves with five leaflets, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
