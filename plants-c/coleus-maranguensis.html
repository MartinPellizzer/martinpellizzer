Coleus maranguensis

Coleus maranguensis, commonly known as the Maranguensis coleus, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Coleus maranguensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
