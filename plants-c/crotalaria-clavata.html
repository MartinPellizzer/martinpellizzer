Crotalaria clavata

Crotalaria clavata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable crop for soil enrichment. It grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields.

Crotalaria clavata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
