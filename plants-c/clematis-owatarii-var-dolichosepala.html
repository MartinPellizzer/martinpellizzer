Clematis owatarii var. dolichosepala

Clematis owatarii var. dolichosepala, commonly known as the Japanese vine or long-sepaled clematis, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to Japan.

It features slender, woody stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, making it suitable for trellises or arbors. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to lilac, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are ovate to elliptical in shape.

The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
