Croton litoralis subsp. rugelianus

Croton litoralis subsp.

rugelianus, commonly known as the coastal croton, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short peduncles. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and coastal winds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in coastal gardens.
