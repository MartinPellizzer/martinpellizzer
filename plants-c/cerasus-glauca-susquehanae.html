Cerasus glauca susquehanae

Cerasus glauca susquehanae, commonly known as the Susquehanna chokecherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white to pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream valleys.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in native ecosystems.
