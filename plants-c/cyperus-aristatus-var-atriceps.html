Cyperus aristatus var. atriceps

Cyperus aristatus var. atriceps, commonly known as the black rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a dark purple or blackish hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen, and it thrives in wet or marshy environments.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking appearance and adaptability to wet conditions.
