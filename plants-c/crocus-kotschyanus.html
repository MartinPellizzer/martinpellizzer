Crocus kotschyanus

Crocus kotschyanus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant produces a single, showy flower with three petals and three sepals, typical of the Iridaceae family. Its corm is small and round, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

The Persian crocus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming period.
