Cacalia cotoneaster

Cacalia cotoneaster, commonly known as the European cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Cacalia cotoneaster is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
