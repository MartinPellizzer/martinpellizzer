Cinnamomum gracillimum

Cinnamomum gracillimum, commonly known as the slender cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, and its bark is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its aromatic properties and ornamental appeal.
