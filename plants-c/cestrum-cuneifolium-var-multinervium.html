Cestrum cuneifolium var. multinervium

Cestrum cuneifolium var. multinervium, commonly known as the Multinervium Cestrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing or shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a prominent vein pattern that gives the variety its name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also noted for its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
