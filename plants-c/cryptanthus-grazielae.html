Cryptanthus grazielae

Cryptanthus grazielae, commonly known as the "Rainbow Grass" or "Beaded Beauty," is a strikingly colorful plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact rosette of grass-like leaves. The leaves are typically bright red, orange, or yellow, creating a vibrant display that resembles a cluster of beads. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette.

This plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vivid coloration.
