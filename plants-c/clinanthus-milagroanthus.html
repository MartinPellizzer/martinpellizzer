Clinanthus milagroanthus

Clinanthus milagroanthus, commonly known as the miracle flower, is a rare and striking plant native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to bright yellow, often resembling the sun. The plant has a delicate, herbaceous structure with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
