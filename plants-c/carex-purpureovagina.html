Carex purpureovagina

Carex purpureovagina, commonly known as purple-vagina sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive purple hue in the bracts.

Carex purpureovagina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
