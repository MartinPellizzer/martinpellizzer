Calliandra grisebachiana

Calliandra grisebachiana, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences composed of clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
