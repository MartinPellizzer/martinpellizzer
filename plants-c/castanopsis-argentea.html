Castanopsis argentea

Castanopsis argentea, commonly known as the silver-leaf tara or silver-leaf chestnut, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
