Ceanothus eglandulosus

Ceanothus eglandulosus, commonly known as the slender ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a valuable species for wildlife, providing nectar for pollinators and cover for small animals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
