Clematis leiocarpa

Clematis leiocarpa, commonly known as the silky clematis, is a woody vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 15 feet in length, supported by adventitious roots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a soft, velvety texture.

Clematis leiocarpa thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
