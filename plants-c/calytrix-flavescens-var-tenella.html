Calytrix flavescens var. tenella

Calytrix flavescens var. tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter and produces small, fleshy fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
