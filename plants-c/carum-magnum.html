Carum magnum

Carum magnum, commonly known as giant parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and dill. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fern-like, deeply lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Carum magnum is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
