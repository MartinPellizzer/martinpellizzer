Carex goodenowii f. chlorostachya

Carex goodenowii f. chlorostachya, commonly known as the green-stem sedges, is a variant of the Carex genus characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant with grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often greenish in color, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in temperate regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
