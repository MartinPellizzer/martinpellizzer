Chassalia lancifolia

Chassalia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Chassalia lancifolia is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
