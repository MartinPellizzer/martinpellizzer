Cyathea tussacii

Cyathea tussacii, commonly known as the Tussac fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, contributing to its ability to store water and nutrients.

Cyathea tussacii plays a significant role in the ecosystem by providing habitat and shelter for various wildlife species.
