Cynoxylon floridum var. pendulum

Cynoxylon floridum var. pendulum, commonly known as the hanging sandcherry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible for birds and some small mammals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

This variety is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
