Crassula connata var. eremica

Crassula connata var. eremica, commonly known as the Desert Crassula, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that are often blue-gray in color, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
