Coleus Parishii

Coleus parishii is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. Known for its vibrant, colorful foliage, it captivates botanists and plant enthusiasts alike. This species belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is closely related to other popular ornamental plants. Its unique leaf patterns and vivid hues make it a sought-after addition to specialized gardens. Despite its beauty, Coleus parishii remains relatively obscure, highlighting the need for greater conservation efforts.
