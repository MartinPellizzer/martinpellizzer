Centaurea maculata

Centaurea maculata, commonly known as the spotted knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking purple to pink flowers with distinctive white markings. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces numerous small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind. Centaurea maculata is a perennial herb that thrives in a variety of soil types and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

It is also known for its ability to spread aggressively, making it both a valuable wildflower and a potential invasive species.
