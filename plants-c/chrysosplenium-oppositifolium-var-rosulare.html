Chrysosplenium oppositifolium var. rosulare

Chrysosplenium oppositifolium var. rosulare, commonly known as the rosette saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense rosette of basal leaves that are typically woolly or hairy in texture. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of leafy stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its foliage is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine in some cultures.
