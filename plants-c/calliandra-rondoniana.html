Calliandra rondoniana

Calliandra rondoniana, commonly known as the Rondonian calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy inflorescences that resemble clouds. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in tropical climates.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
