Campanula tillischii

Campanula tillischii, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often glandular and slightly hairy.

Campanula tillischii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like flowers.
