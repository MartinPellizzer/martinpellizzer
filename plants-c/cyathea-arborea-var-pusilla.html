Cyathea arborea var. pusilla

Cyathea arborea var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and a crown of large, feathery fronds. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is covered with remnants of dead leaf bases. Its fronds are typically green and can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters.

This variety is well-suited for cultivation in shaded, moist environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
