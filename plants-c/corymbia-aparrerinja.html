Corymbia aparrerinja

Corymbia aparrerinja, commonly known as the Arnhem Land bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, flaky bark that often displays a reddish hue, and its lance-shaped leaves that are usually pale green and hairy underneath. The tree produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its hard, heavy wood has been traditionally used by Indigenous Australians for tool-making and ceremonial purposes.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and heat.
