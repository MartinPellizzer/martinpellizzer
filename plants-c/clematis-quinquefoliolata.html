Clematis quinquefoliolata

Clematis quinquefoliolata, commonly known as the five-leafed clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it its common name, and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, blue, and purple. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can reach lengths of up to 20 feet.

Clematis quinquefoliolata is relatively hardy and suitable for USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
