Cestrum fulvescens

Cestrum fulvescens, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange to reddish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly rough texture.
