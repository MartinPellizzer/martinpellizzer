Caladenia simulans

Caladenia simulans, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble other orchid species. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that mimics the appearance of other orchids.

Caladenia simulans is a short-lived perennial, often flowering once before dying, making it a rare and valuable species in the wild.
