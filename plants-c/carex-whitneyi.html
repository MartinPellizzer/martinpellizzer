Carex whitneyi

Carex whitneyi, commonly known as Whitney's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, streambanks, and other riparian habitats. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Whitney's sedge plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
