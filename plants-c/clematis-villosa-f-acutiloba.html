Clematis villosa f. acutiloba

Clematis villosa f. acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, climbing stems with a hairy texture, and its leaves are palmately compound with sharply toothed lobes. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or lilac. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in gardens.
