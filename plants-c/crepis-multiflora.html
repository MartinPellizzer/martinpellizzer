Crepis multiflora

Crepis multiflora, commonly known as the common mouse-ear.

It is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Crepis multiflora is often used in wildflower gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions.
