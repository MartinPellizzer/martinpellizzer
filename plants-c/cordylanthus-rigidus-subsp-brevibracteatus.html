Cordylanthus rigidus subsp. brevibracteatus

Cordylanthus rigidus subsp.

brevibracteatus, commonly known as the narrow-bracted rigid buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its shorter bracts compared to other subspecies of Cordylanthus. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

This plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
