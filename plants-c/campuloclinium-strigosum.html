Campuloclinium strigosum

Campuloclinium strigosum, commonly known as hairy false nettle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes plants commonly known as nettles. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects.

Campuloclinium strigosum is often found in moist, wooded areas and can spread through rhizomes.
