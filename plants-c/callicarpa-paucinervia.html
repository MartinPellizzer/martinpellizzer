Callicarpa paucinervia

Callicarpa paucinervia, commonly known as the small-veined beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces vibrant purple to red berries in the fall, which are a key feature for its ornamental value. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a smooth texture with a mild aromatic scent.

Callicarpa paucinervia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
