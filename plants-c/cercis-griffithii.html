Cercis griffithii

Cercis griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's redbud, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
