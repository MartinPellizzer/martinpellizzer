Clompanus colorata

Clompanus colorata, commonly known as the rainbow bellflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, from deep purples to bright yellows. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hair, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its unique floral structure and colorful display make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
