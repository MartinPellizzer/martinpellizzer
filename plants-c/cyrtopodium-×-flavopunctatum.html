Cyrtopodium × flavopunctatum

Cyrtopodium × flavopunctatum, commonly known as the golden dot orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often yellow or orange, flower spikes that bear showy, fragrant blossoms. The plant typically produces small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and its pseudobulbs are often adorned with distinctive yellow or orange spots. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cyrtopodium × flavopunctatum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
