Cordia vignei

Cordia vignei, commonly known as the red balsa tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a spherical, fleshy capsule that turns red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
