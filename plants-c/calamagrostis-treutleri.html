Calamagrostis treutleri

Calamagrostis treutleri, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces fluffy, feathery inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis treutleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
