Carex drymophila var. udensis

Carex drymophila var. udensis, commonly known as the Udens' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their distinctive flowering structures. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and along stream banks.
