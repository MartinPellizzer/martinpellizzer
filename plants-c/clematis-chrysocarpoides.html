Clematis chrysocarpoides

Clematis chrysocarpoides, commonly known as the golden fruit clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive yellow center. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis chrysocarpoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
