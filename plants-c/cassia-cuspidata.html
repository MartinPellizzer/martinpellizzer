Cassia cuspidata

Cassia cuspidata, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia cuspidata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
