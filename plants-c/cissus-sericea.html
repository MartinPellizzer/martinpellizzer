Cissus sericea

Cissus sericea, commonly known as silver shield ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cissus sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
