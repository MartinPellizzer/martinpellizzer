Calamagrostis trichantha

Calamagrostis trichantha, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, greenish flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Calamagrostis trichantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
