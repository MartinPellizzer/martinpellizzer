Campanula rapunculus var. bracteosa

Campanula rapunculus var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
