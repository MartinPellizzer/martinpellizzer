Cistus × escartianus nothosubsp. escartianus

Cistus × escartianus nothosubsp.

escartianus, commonly known as the Escartian rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in open woodlands and scrublands, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
