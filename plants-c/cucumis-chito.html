Cucumis chito

Cucumis chito, commonly known as the Chinese ridge gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its long, ridged, green fruits that resemble cucumbers. The plant produces yellow flowers and has palmately lobed leaves. The fruit is typically harvested when immature for culinary use, though it can be allowed to mature for ornamental purposes.

It is valued for its versatility in cooking and its adaptability to various growing conditions.
