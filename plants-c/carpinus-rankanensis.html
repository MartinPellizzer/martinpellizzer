Carpinus rankanensis

Carpinus rankanensis, commonly known as the Philippine hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, which are followed by winged fruits. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as mountain forests.

Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for local use.
