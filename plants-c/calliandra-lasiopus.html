Calliandra lasiopus

Calliandra lasiopus, commonly known as the showy calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry climates and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological balance.

Additionally, the plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
