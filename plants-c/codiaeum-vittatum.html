Codiaeum vittatum

Codiaeum vittatum, commonly known as the flame tree or croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display a range of hues including red, orange, yellow, and green. The leaves are typically variegated with intricate patterns, making the plant a popular choice for ornamental purposes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The plant is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens where it can add a striking visual element.
