Cinnamomum phoebe

Cinnamomum phoebe, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or sweet cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its essential oils are used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
