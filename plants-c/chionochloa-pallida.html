Chionochloa pallida

Chionochloa pallida, commonly known as the silver grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that reflect light. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with poor soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
