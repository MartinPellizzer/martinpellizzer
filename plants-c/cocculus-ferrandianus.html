Cocculus ferrandianus

Cocculus ferrandianus, commonly known as the scarlet bower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous growth and dense foliage. The plant produces striking red and orange flowers that are often used in ornamental horticulture. Its fruit is a distinctive, fleshy pod that contains several seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
