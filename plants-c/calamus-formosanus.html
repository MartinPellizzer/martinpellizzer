Calamus formosanus

Calamus formosanus, commonly known as the Formosan calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Taiwan.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it relies on wind pollination. Its rhizomes are sometimes harvested for their aromatic properties and used in local herbal medicine.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland and marsh habitats.
