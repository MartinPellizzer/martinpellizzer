Chonemorpha inflata

Chonemorpha inflata, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, balloon-like fruits that contain seeds. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks or in moist woodlands.

The botanical features of Chonemorpha inflata make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
