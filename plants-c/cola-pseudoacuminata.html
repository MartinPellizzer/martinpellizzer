Cola pseudoacuminata

Cola pseudoacuminata, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a member of the Sterculiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into hard, capsule-like fruits containing caffeine-rich seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported stimulant and antispasmodic properties.
