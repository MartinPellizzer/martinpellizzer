Cereus donatii

Cereus donatii, commonly known as the night bloomer or moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
