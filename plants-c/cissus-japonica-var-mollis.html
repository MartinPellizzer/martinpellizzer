Cissus japonica var. mollis

Cissus japonica var. mollis, commonly known as Japanese devil's ivy, is a trailing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by green, spherical berries. It is a member of the family Vitaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental foliage.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular houseplant.
