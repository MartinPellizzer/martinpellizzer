Calamagrostis czukczorum

Calamagrostis czukczorum, commonly known as the Siberian reed, is a tall grass native to the colder regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green blades and tufted flowering heads that appear in late summer. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and marshy areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it a valuable species for erosion control.

The plant is also appreciated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
