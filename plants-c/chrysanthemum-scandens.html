Chrysanthemum scandens

Chrysanthemum scandens, commonly known as the climbing chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its trailing or climbing habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in a range of colors including yellow, white, and pink. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Chrysanthemum scandens is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
