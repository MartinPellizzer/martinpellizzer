Carex lachenalii var. glareosa

Carex lachenalii var. glareosa, commonly known as the shining sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often glaucous, giving them a bluish-white appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
