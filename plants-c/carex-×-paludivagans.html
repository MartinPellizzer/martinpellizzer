Carex × paludivagans

Carex × paludivagans, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. Its reproductive structures consist of small, three-parted flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex × paludivagans is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
