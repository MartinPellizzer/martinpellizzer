Carex wui

Carex wui, commonly known as the Wui sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions in East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Carex wui thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
