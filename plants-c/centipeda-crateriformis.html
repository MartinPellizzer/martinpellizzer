Centipeda crateriformis

Centipeda crateriformis, commonly known as the cup-shaped centipeda, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, cup-like inflorescence that resembles a shallow bowl. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small and white, arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the plant.
