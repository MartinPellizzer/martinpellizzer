Coleus adenanthus

Coleus adenanthus, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features striking, velvety, silver-green leaves that are often adorned with red or purple veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, purple, or pink. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often grown as an ornamental plant for its foliage.

Its foliage is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
