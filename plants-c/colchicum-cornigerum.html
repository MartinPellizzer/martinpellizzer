Colchicum cornigerum

Colchicum cornigerum, commonly known as the Cornish colchic, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a member of the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a tall, leafless stem that can reach up to 60 cm in height. Its leaves are basal, broad, and often have a waxy texture.

Colchicum cornigerum is typically found in damp, shaded areas such as woodland floors and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
