Cucubalus baccifer var. albiflorus

Cucubalus baccifer var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered Indian date palm, is a variant of the Indian date palm native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers, which eventually develop into edible, fleshy fruits similar to dates. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
