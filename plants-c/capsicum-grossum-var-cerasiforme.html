Capsicum grossum var. cerasiforme

Capsicum grossum var. cerasiforme, commonly known as the red bell pepper.

This variety is a cultivar of the Capsicum genus, characterized by its large, elongated fruits that typically range from red to orange in color. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are yellow, and the fruit develops from a single ovary, typical of the Solanaceae family.

The plant is valued for its culinary use, particularly in dishes requiring a sweet, mild flavor.
