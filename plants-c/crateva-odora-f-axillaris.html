Crateva odora f. axillaris

Crateva odora f. axillaris, commonly known as the fragrant false indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form suitable for garden settings.
