Crotalaria ledermannii

Crotalaria ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's cowpea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant grows as an annual herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural settings.

However, it can also be toxic to livestock due to the presence of pyrrolizidine alkaloids.
