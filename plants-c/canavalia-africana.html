Canavalia africana

Canavalia africana, commonly known as African burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits. The plant is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its seeds are encased in spiny pods, which aid in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur or clothing.

Canavalia africana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
