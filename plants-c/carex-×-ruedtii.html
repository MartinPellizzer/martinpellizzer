Carex × ruedtii

Carex × ruedtii, commonly known as the hybrid sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Carex acutata and Carex lyngbyei, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swales, and pond margins. Its leaves are grass-like, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters.

Carex × ruedtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
