Calathea toroi

Calathea toroi, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with intricate patterns of green, silver, and purple. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often featuring a waxy texture that enhances its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Calathea toroi is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
