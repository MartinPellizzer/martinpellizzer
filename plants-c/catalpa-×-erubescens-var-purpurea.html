Catalpa × erubescens var. purpurea

Catalpa × erubescens var. purpurea, commonly known as the Purple Catalpa, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that turn a striking yellow in the fall. The tree produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in early summer. Its fruit is a long, cylindrical pod that splits open to release its seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
