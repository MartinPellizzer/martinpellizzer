Chamaecrista moorei

Chamaecrista moorei, commonly known as the Moore's burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Chamaecrista moorei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
