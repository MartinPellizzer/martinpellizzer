Calamus latisectus

Calamus latisectus, commonly known as the broad-leafed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, elongated leaves that can reach up to two meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, three-lobed fruits.

Calamus latisectus is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appearance.
