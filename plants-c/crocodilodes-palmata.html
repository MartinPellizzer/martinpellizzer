Crocodilodes palmata

Crocodilodes palmata, commonly known as the crocodile palm, is a distinctive species native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
