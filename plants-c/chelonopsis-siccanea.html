Chelonopsis siccanea

Chelonopsis siccanea, commonly known as the dryland chelone, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is deep and extensive, enabling it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Chelonopsis siccanea is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it thrives with minimal care.
