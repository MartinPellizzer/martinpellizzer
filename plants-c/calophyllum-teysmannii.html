Calophyllum teysmannii

Calophyllum teysmannii, commonly known as the Taysmann's flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces woody, elliptical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction.
