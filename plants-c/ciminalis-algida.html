Ciminalis algida

Ciminalis algida, commonly known as the cold mountain cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and it has a low-growing, mat-forming habit.

Ciminalis algida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
