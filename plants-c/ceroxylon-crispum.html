Ceroxylon crispum

Ceroxylon crispum, commonly known as the Andean wax palm, is a striking evergreen tree native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant is known for its slow growth and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically bright green and have a distinctive crinkled texture, which gives the plant its scientific name.

Ceroxylon crispum is also notable for its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.
