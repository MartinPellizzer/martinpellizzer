Ceanothus perplexans

Ceanothus perplexans, commonly known as the California lilac, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Ceanothus perplexans plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
