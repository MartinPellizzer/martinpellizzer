Crepis schreberi

Crepis schreberi, commonly known as the Schreber's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.

Crepis schreberi is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local pollinator populations.
