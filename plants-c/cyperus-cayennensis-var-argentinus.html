Cyperus cayennensis var. argentinus

Cyperus cayennensis var. argentinus, commonly known as the Argentine flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along riverbanks or in swampy areas. It produces small, clustered flower heads that are often hidden among the foliage.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
