Cydista spectabilis

Cydista spectabilis, commonly known as the showy cydista, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

Cydista spectabilis is often found in marshy areas and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
