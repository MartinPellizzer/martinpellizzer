Clerodendrum schweinfurthii var. conradsii

Clerodendrum schweinfurthii var. conradsii, commonly known as the African balm, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
