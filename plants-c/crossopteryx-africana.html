Crossopteryx africana

Crossopteryx africana, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making, as well as for its medicinal properties.

The plant also plays an important role in soil conservation and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
