Campanula glomerata subsp. aggregata

Campanula glomerata subsp.

aggregata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically basal, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
