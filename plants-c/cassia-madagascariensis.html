Cassia madagascariensis

Cassia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a reddish or yellowish hue. The plant produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry, open woodlands.

Cassia madagascariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
