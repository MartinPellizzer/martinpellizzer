Cassia aciphylla

Cassia aciphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cassia, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Cassia aciphylla is often found in coastal dunes and other harsh environments, showcasing its resilience to extreme weather.
