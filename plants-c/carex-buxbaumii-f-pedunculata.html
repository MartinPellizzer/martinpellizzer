Carex buxbaumii f. pedunculata

Carex buxbaumii f. pedunculata, commonly known as the Buxbaum's sedge, is a variant of the Buxbaum's sedge, which is native to South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant features narrow, cylindrical leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

This ornamental plant is often used in wetland gardens and is valued for its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
