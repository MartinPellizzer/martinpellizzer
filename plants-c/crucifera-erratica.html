Crucifera erratica

Crucifera erratica, commonly known as the wandering mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers.

This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides or in open fields. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge. The fruit is a dry, segmented pod called a silique, which contains several small, round seeds.

It is an annual plant that completes its life cycle within a single growing season.
