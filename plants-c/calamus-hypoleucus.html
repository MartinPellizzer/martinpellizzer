Calamus hypoleucus

Calamus hypoleucus, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its long, narrow rhizomes and arrow-shaped leaves. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in wet, marshy environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically surrounded by a spadix and a modified leaf called a spathe.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic materials.
