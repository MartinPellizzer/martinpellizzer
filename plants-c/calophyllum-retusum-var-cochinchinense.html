Calophyllum retusum var. cochinchinense

Calophyllum retusum var. cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is used in various woodworking applications.
