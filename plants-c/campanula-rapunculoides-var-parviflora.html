Campanula rapunculoides var. parviflora

Campanula rapunculoides var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile garden plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
