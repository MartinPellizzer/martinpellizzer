Catapodium unilaterale

Catapodium unilaterale, commonly known as the one-sided catapodium, is a unique grass species characterized by its asymmetric growth pattern.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is native to specific regions of South America. The plant exhibits a distinctive morphology with one side of its stems and leaves developing more prominently than the other. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

Due to its rarity and unique structure, it is of interest to botanists and researchers studying plant asymmetry.
