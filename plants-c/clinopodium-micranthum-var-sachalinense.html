Clinopodium micranthum var. sachalinense

Clinopodium micranthum var. sachalinense, commonly known as Sachalin Mint, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula and parts of eastern Russia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink to purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped berries.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming and digestive properties.
