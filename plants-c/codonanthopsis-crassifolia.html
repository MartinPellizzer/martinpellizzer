Codonanthopsis crassifolia

Codonanthopsis crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved moss rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, often blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.

Its compact size and low maintenance requirements make it an attractive addition to xeriscaping and container gardening.
