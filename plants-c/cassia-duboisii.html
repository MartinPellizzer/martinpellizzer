Cassia duboisii

Cassia duboisii, commonly known as the white cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Cassia duboisii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
