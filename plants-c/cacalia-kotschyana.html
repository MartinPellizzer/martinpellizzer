Cacalia kotschyana

Cacalia kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschy's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia kotschyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
