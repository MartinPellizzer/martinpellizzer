Cynanchum viminale subsp. australe

Cynanchum viminale subsp.

australe, commonly known as Australian greenbriar, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape.

The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.

It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.
