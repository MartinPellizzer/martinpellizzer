Cincinalis

Cincinalis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the genus *Ceratonia* in the legume family, Fabaceae. The plant is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that resemble cascading showers, hence its common name. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces woody pods containing edible seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
