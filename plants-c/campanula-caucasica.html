Campanula caucasica

Campanula caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
