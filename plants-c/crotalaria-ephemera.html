Crotalaria ephemera

Crotalaria ephemera, commonly known as the ephemeral crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite and pinnately compound, with slender, branching stems.

The plant is noted for its short life cycle, completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.
