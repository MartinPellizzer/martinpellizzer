Centaurium umbellatum subsp. grandiflorum

Centaurium umbellatum subsp.

grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flower centaury, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant features a slender, upright stem with opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is its umbel-like inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, bright yellow flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
