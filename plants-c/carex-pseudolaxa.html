Carex pseudolaxa

Carex pseudolaxa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically gray-green in color. This plant thrives in wet to mesic environments, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Carex pseudolaxa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
