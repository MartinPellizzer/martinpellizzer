Crepidiastrum denticulatum subsp. ramosissimum

Crepidiastrum denticulatum subsp.

ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy leaf mallow, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features compound leaves with serrated edges and a dense, sprawling growth habit.

The plant produces showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and foliage, which contribute to its adaptability in arid environments.
