Chamaesium markgrafianum

Chamaesium markgrafianum, commonly known as the Markgraf's chamaesium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct reddish or purple hue, especially along the veins.

The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters that attract various pollinators.
