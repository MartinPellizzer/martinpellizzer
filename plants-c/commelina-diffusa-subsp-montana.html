Commelina diffusa subsp. montana

Commelina diffusa subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features striking blue to violet flowers with white petals, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of its stems.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
