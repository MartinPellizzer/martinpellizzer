Carex panda

Carex panda, commonly known as the panda sedge, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brown flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Carex panda is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
