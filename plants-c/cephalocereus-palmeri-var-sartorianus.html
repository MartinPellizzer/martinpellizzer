Cephalocereus palmeri var. sartorianus

Cephalocereus palmeri var. sartorianus, commonly known as the Palmer's hedgehog cactus, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a rounded, shrub-like structure. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
