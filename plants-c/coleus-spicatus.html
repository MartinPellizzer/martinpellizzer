Coleus spicatus

Coleus spicatus, commonly known as the spike coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are often ornamental, displaying a wide range of hues such as red, orange, and yellow.

Coleus spicatus thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
