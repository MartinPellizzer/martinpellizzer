Cucurbita pepo subvar. alba

Cucurbita pepo subvar. alba, commonly known as the Mexican hubab or white squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white, round or oblong fruits with a smooth, thin rind. The plant is an annual vine that produces both male and female flowers on separate plants. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine hairy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a mild, sweet flavor and are used in various culinary traditions.
