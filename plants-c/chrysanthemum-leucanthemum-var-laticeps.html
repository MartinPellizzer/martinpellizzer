Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. laticeps

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. laticeps, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, white to pale yellow ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets, giving it a daisy-like appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks.
