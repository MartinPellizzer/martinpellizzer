Canavalia tortilis

Canavalia tortilis, commonly known as the twisted bean or wild lima bean, is a climbing leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive zigzag or twisted appearance, which is a key botanical characteristic. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Canavalia tortilis is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some regions.
