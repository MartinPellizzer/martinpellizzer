Cirsium heterotrichum

Cirsium heterotrichum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Cirsium heterotrichum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
