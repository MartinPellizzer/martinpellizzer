Campanula macrorhiza subsp. catalanica

Campanula macrorhiza subsp.

catalanica, commonly known as Catalan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as cliffs and woodland edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to the typical form of Campanula macrorhiza.
