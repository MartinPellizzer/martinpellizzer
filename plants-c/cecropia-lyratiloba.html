Cecropia lyratiloba

Cecropia lyratiloba, commonly known as the lyrate ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are typically light green and have a lyrate shape, giving it its common name. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed or degraded habitats. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, three-angled fruits containing numerous seeds.

The tree plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
