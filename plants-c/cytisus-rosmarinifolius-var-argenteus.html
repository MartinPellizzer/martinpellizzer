Cytisus rosmarinifolius var. argenteus

Cytisus rosmarinifolius var. argenteus, commonly known as silverleaf broom, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, multi-branched stems that often grow along the ground, forming dense mats. The leaves are small, lance-shaped, and covered in a silvery-white bloom of fine hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. Its fragrant, yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, arranged in racemes.

This plant is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
