Campanula bellidifolia subsp. argunensis

Campanula bellidifolia subsp.

argunensis, commonly known as the Argun mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
