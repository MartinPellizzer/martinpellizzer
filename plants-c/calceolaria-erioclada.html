Calceolaria erioclada

Calceolaria erioclada, commonly known as the velvet leaf calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to red, resembling small cups. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are velvety and dark green, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calceolaria erioclada is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
