Cosmia quadrivalvis

Cosmia quadrivalvis, commonly known as the four-valved cosmos, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces showy, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Cosmia quadrivalvis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
