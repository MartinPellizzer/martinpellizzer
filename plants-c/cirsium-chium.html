Cirsium chium

Cirsium chium, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant features spiny leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Cirsium chium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
