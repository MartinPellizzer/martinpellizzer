Chenopodium fruticosum

Chenopodium fruticosum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Chenopodium fruticosum is often used in traditional medicine and can be found in various ecological studies due to its adaptability.
