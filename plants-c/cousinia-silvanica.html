Cousinia silvanica

Cousinia silvanica, commonly known as the mountain cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. The plant features basal leaves that are often woolly or hairy, and its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in a composite head. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower structure.

Cousinia silvanica is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
