Chamaesyce makinoi var. liukiuensis

Chamaesyce makinoi var. liukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu sandmat, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming a dense mat. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are often subtended by leaf-like bracts.

This plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments.
