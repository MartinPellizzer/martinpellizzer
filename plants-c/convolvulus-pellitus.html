Convolvulus pellitus

Convolvulus pellitus, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with trailing stems that produce clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and characteristic species of its native environment.
