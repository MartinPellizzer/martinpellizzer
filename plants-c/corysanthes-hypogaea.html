Corysanthes hypogaea

Corysanthes hypogaea, commonly known as the underground orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its subterranean growth habit, with the plant body largely concealed beneath the soil surface. The species produces a single, showy flower that emerges from the ground, often in response to seasonal changes. Its specialized structure allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, adapting to its native environment effectively.

The plant's rare and ephemeral blooming makes it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
