Chionodoxa gigantea

Chionodoxa gigantea, commonly known as the giant crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It produces striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its ability to naturalize easily in well-drained soils. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Chionodoxa gigantea is often used in rock gardens and as a spring-flowering ornamental in gardens.
