Cephalocroton albicans

Cephalocroton albicans, commonly known as the white-headed croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive white-pink flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and traditional uses.
