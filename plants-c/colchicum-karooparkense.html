Colchicum karooparkense

Colchicum karooparkense, commonly known as the Karoopark colchic, is a flowering plant native to the Karoopark region of South Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking violet-blue flowers during the summer months. The plant belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and short, erect flowering stems. Its bulbs contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially hazardous to livestock and humans.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its mountainous habitat.
