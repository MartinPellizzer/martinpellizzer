Cicer reticulatum

Cicer reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a legume belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock. Cicer reticulatum thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration efforts.
