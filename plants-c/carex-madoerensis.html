Carex madoerensis

Carex madoerensis, commonly known as Madoer's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Madoer's sedge is often found in steppes and desert margins, playing a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
