Centaurea nigra subsp. pratensis

Centaurea nigra subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as black knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces dark purple to black flowers in late summer. Its flowers are surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets, creating a striking visual contrast.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
