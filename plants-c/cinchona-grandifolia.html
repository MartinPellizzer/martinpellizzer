Cinchona grandifolia

Cinchona grandifolia, commonly known as the quinine tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates. It is well-known for its bark, which contains the alkaloid quinine, historically used to treat malaria.

Due to its medicinal importance, it has been cultivated in various parts of the world for centuries.
