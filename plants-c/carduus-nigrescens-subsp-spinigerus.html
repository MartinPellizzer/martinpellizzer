Carduus nigrescens subsp. spinigerus

Carduus nigrescens subsp.

spinigerus, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. It has a robust root system and is often found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides.

The subspecies spinigerus is distinguished by its more hairy foliage compared to other subspecies of Carduus nigrescens.
