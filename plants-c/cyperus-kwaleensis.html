Cyperus kwaleensis

Cyperus kwaleensis, commonly known as the Kwale flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and flooded grasslands, and is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its morphology includes narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences.

Cyperus kwaleensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
