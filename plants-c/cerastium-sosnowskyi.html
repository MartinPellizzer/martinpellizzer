Cerastium sosnowskyi

Cerastium sosnowskyi, commonly known as the Sosnowsky's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are rounded and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.

Cerastium sosnowskyi is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to high-altitude environments.
