Chamaele decumbens f. japonica

Chamaele decumbens f. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese creeping bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
