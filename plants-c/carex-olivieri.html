Carex olivieri

Carex olivieri, commonly known as the olive sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks.

Carex olivieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
