It is characterized by its pale yellow to white thallus, which is typically crustose in growth form. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on bark or soil surfaces in temperate regions.

Its reproductive structures, known as soredia, are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across suitable habitats.
