Calamintha nepeta var. mollis

Calamintha nepeta var. mollis, commonly known as sweet cicely or honey herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and emit a subtle, sweet scent when crushed. The plant produces small, delicate white or pale blue flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb.
