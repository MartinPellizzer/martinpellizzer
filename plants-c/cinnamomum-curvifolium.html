Cinnamomum curvifolium

Cinnamomum curvifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is the primary source of its essential oil and spice. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its aromatic oils and timber.
