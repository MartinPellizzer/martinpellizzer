Ceraia philippinensis

Ceraia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its roots are specialized for nutrient absorption, and it plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
