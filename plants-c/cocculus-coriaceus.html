Cocculus coriaceus

Cocculus coriaceus, commonly known as the rough cocculus, is a perennial climbing plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark blue to black berries. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit contains a single seed, which is enclosed in a thick, fleshy pulp.
