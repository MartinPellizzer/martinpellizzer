Costus louisii

Costus louisii, commonly known as the Louisiana costus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves and produces inflorescences of small, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Costus louisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
