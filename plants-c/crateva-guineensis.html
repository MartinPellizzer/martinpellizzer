Crateva guineensis

Crateva guineensis, commonly known as the African star fruit or golden apple, is a tropical shrub belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in various cuisines. Its botanical features include a thick, woody stem and a short, cylindrical fruit with a star-shaped base.

Crateva guineensis is native to Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
