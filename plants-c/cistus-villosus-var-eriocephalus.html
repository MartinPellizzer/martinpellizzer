Cistus villosus var. eriocephalus

Cistus villosus var. eriocephalus, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is drought-tolerant. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
