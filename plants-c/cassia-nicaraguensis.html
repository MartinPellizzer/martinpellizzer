Cassia nicaraguensis

Cassia nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaragua cassia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. It produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.

Cassia nicaraguensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
