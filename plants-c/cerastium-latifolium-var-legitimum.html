Cerastium latifolium var. legitimum

Cerastium latifolium var. legitimum, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are arranged opposite along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and meadows.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies for its mild diuretic properties.
