Convolvulus malcolmii

Convolvulus malcolmii, commonly known as the Malcolm's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers typically bloom in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
