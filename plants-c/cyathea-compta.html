Cyathea compta

Cyathea compta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with long, narrow segments that contribute to its elegant look.

Cyathea compta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
