Campuloclinium corymbosum

Campuloclinium corymbosum, commonly known as the Florida false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

The plant is often found in swampy areas and is adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
