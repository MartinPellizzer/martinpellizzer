Carduus gayanus var. braun-blanquetii

Carduus gayanus var. braun-blanquetii, commonly known as the Braun-Blanquet's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a single head.

This variety is distinguished by its specific morphology and habitat preferences, making it a notable species in botanical studies.
