Cayratia ciliifera

Cayratia ciliifera, commonly known as the silky vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Cayratia ciliifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
