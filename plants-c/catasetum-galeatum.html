Catasetum galeatum

Catasetum galeatum, commonly known as the golden cattleya, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often golden-yellow flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a complex reproductive structure with specialized pollinia.

Catasetum galeatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is popular among orchid enthusiasts.
