Carex prionocarpa

Carex prionocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of sedge native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by water or wind.

Carex prionocarpa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
