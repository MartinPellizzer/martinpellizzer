Cunninghamia kawakamii

Cunninghamia kawakamii, commonly known as the Japanese stone pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Pinaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, oval cones that mature to release seeds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
