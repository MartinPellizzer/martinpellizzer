Crotalaria boudetii

Crotalaria boudetii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, dark seeds used in traditional medicine and as a protein source. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Crotalaria boudetii is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
