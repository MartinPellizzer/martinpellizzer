Croton cyanospermus

Croton cyanospermus, commonly known as the blue seed croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green leaves, which are a result of high concentrations of chlorophyll and other pigments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
