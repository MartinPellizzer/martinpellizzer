Cassia semaphora

Cassia semaphora, commonly known as the signal tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain small, dark seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Cassia semaphora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
