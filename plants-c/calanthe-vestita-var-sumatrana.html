Calanthe vestita var. sumatrana

Calanthe vestita var. sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen, strap-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several flowers, which bloom during the wet season.

Calanthe vestita var. sumatrana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
