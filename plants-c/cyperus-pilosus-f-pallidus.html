Cyperus pilosus f. pallidus

Cyperus pilosus f. pallidus, commonly known as the pale flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and slender, cylindrical stems. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that are often pale in color, contributing to its common name.

This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along water bodies, and is often found in disturbed or semi-aquatic habitats.
