Crassula colligata subsp. colligata

Crassula colligata subsp.

colligata, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder.

The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped white flowers during the winter months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor cultivation.
