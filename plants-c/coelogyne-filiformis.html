Coelogyne filiformis

Coelogyne filiformis, commonly known as the thread orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like pseudobulbs and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Coelogyne filiformis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
