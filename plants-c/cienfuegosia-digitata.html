Cienfuegosia digitata

Cienfuegosia digitata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cienfuegosia digitata is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
