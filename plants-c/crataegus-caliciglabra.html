Crataegus caliciglabra

Crataegus caliciglabra, commonly known as the smooth thornapple, is a species of hawthorn native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges and a smooth texture.

The plant is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full sun.
