Comolia ovalifolia

Comolia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf comolia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
