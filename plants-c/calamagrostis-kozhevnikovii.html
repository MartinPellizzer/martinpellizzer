Calamagrostis kozhevnikovii

Calamagrostis kozhevnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Calamagrostis kozhevnikovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
