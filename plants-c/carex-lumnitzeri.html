Carex lumnitzeri

Carex lumnitzeri, commonly known as the Lumnitzer sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Carex lumnitzeri is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to periodic flooding, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
