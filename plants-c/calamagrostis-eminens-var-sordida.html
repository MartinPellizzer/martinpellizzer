Calamagrostis eminens var. sordida

Calamagrostis eminens var. sordida, commonly known as the gray reed, is a variant of the tall reed grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its grayish-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. This perennial grass thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its narrow leaves and panicle-like inflorescence make it a striking addition to wetland gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various wildlife species.
