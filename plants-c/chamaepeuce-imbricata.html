Chamaepeuce imbricata

Chamaepeuce imbricata, commonly known as the rock holly, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often arranged in a compact, bushy form.

Chamaepeuce imbricata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
