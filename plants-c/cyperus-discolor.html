Cyperus discolor

Cyperus discolor, commonly known as the silver rush, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves. Cyperus discolor typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often near water bodies.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
