Calamaria triquetra

Calamaria triquetra, commonly known as the three-cornered leek, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale purple flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calamaria triquetra is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
