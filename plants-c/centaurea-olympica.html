Centaurea olympica

Centaurea olympica, commonly known as the Olympic Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea olympica is often found in alpine environments and is considered a symbol of the Olympic Games.
