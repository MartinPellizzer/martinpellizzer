Cyathea nitidula

Cyathea nitidula, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The fronds are typically dark green and have a velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cyathea nitidula is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
