Crataegus intricata f. fortunata

Crataegus intricata f. fortunata, commonly known as the Fortunata hawthorn, is a compact, thorned shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers in early spring. The plant bears red, berry-like fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact size and seasonal blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
