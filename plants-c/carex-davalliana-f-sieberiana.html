Carex davalliana f. sieberiana

Carex davalliana f. sieberiana, commonly known as the Sieber's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
