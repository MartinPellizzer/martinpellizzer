Clematis wightiana var. pilosissima

Clematis wightiana var. pilosissima, commonly known as the silky clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The foliage is typically lobed and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral displays.
