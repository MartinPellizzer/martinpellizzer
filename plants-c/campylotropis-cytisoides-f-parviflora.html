Campylotropis cytisoides f. parviflora

Campylotropis cytisoides f. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered false acacia, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
