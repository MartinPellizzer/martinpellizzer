Carex townsendii

Carex townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America, particularly found in the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and forest edges.

Carex townsendii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
