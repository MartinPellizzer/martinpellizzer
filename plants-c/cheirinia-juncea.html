Cheirinia juncea

Cheirinia juncea, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cheirinia juncea is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in low-nutrient, dry conditions.
