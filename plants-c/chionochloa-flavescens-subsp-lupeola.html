Chionochloa flavescens subsp. lupeola

Chionochloa flavescens subsp.

lupeola, commonly known as the yellow tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically yellowish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in grasslands and along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native habitats.
