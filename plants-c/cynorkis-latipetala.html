Cynorkis latipetala

Cynorkis latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petalled orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with broad, velvety petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its roots are often tuberous, which helps it store water and nutrients.

The flowers bloom in late summer or early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
