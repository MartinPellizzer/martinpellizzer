Cyananthus wardii

Cyananthus wardii, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its adaptation to cold climates.

Cyananthus wardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
