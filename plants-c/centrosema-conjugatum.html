Centrosema conjugatum

Centrosema conjugatum, commonly known as the smallflower velvetbean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, hairy pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agroforestry systems for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
