Carum elegans var. minimum

Carum elegans var. minimum, commonly known as the dwarf parsley.

It is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in umbels. It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires well-drained soil and partial shade to thrive.
