Cassia smaragdina

Cassia smaragdina, commonly known as emerald cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright green, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces long, slender pods containing small, flat seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia smaragdina is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
