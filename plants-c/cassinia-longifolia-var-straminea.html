Cassinia longifolia var. straminea

Cassinia longifolia var. straminea, commonly known as the yellow everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.

The variety straminea is distinguished by its pale yellow flowers and slender stems.
