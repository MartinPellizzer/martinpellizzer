Corallodiscus conchifolius

Corallodiscus conchifolius, commonly known as the spiral frond seaweed, is a species of red algae belonging to the family Corallodiscaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped thallus, which is composed of a central axis with branching filaments. The plant typically grows in shallow marine environments, often attached to rocky substrates. Its structure provides habitat for various marine organisms, contributing to the biodiversity of coastal ecosystems.

The species is widely distributed in tropical and subtropical regions of the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
