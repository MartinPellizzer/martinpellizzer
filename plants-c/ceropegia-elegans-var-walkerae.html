Ceropegia elegans var. walkerae

Ceropegia elegans var. walkerae, commonly known as the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or pink markings. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white, which are pollinated by specific moths. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
