Cassia caeciliae

Cassia caeciliae, commonly known as the white cassia, is a shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, seed-filled pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Cassia caeciliae is notable for its unique appearance and is considered an endemic species to Socotra.
