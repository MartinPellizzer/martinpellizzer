Clusia plurivalvis

Clusia plurivalvis, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color. Clusia plurivalvis is a hardy shrub that thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
