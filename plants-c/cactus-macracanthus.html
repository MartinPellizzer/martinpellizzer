Cactus macracanthus

Cactus macracanthus, commonly known as the giant barrel cactus, is a species native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 20 feet tall, which stores water to help it survive in arid conditions. The plant has spiny areoles that bear clusters of white flowers, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it produces edible, fleshy fruits that are consumed by various animals.

The cactus is well adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient and iconic desert plant.
