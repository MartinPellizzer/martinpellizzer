Campylanthus salsoloides var. penduliflorus

Campylanthus salsoloides var. penduliflorus, commonly known as the weeping flaxflower, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its drooping, pendulous inflorescences that bear small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has silver-gray foliage with a woolly texture, adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Its unique floral display makes it a visually striking addition to xeriscaped gardens.
