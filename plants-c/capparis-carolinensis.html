Capparis carolinensis

Capparis carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina crabapple, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet. The plant features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is adapted to moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
