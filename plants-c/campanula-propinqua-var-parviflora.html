Campanula propinqua var. parviflora

Campanula propinqua var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender stems that grow upright, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
