Cirsium asiaticum

Cirsium asiaticum, commonly known as the Asian thistle, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are covered in stiff hairs and have a distinctive serrated edge.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
