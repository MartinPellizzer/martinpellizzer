Carex potamostauros

Carex potamostauros, commonly known as the river sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as riverbanks, marshes, and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex potamostauros plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
