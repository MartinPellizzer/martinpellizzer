Cinnamomum bourgeauvianum

Cinnamomum bourgeauvianum, commonly known as the Madagascar cinnamon or *Cinnamomum bourgeauvianum*, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark that resembles cinnamon. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and oval, leathery leaves with a strong, spicy scent. Its bark is harvested for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

However, it is relatively rare and faces threats from habitat loss and overharvesting.
