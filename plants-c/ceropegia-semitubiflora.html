Ceropegia semitubiflora

Ceropegia semitubiflora, commonly known as the tuberous ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, tuberous roots that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and drought-tolerant landscaping.
