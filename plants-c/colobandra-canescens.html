Colobandra canescens

Colobandra canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf colobandra, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, which give it its distinctive appearance and help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Colobandra canescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
