Carolus chasei

Carolus chasei, commonly known as the Chase's orchid, is a rare and unique flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful blooms that often feature intricate patterns and vibrant hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them, and thrives in high humidity and indirect sunlight. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by specific insect species, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.

Due to habitat destruction and over-collection, Carolus chasei is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
