Carex × mihashii

Carex × mihashii, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and compact, cylindrical inflorescence. Native to East Asia, particularly Japan, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, water-tolerant plant.
