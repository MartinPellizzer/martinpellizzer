Ceropegia swaziorum

Ceropegia swaziorum, commonly known as the Swazi milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Ceropegia. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
