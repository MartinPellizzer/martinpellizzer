Carduus reuterianus f. baeticus

Carduus reuterianus f. baeticus, commonly known as the Iberian thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a robust perennial herb characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, rough leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which attract various pollinators. It is native to southern Europe, particularly the Iberian Peninsula, and thrives in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
