Calamagrostis levis

Calamagrostis levis, commonly known as the silky bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blue-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, fluffy flowers that are often blue or purple in color.

Calamagrostis levis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
