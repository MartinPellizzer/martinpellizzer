Croton trachycaulis

Croton trachycaulis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, ridged stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its unique foliage.
