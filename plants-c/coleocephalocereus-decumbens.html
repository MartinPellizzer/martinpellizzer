Coleocephalocereus decumbens

Coleocephalocereus decumbens, commonly known as the sunburst cactus, is a species native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that often lie flat on the ground, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
