Cullumia aculeata var. sublanata

Cullumia aculeata var. sublanata, commonly known as the woolly cullumia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides a unique texture and appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white or pink, and bloom during the spring and early summer.

This variety is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in harsh, sun-exposed conditions.
