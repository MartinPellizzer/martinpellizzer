Caesalpinia macrantha

Caesalpinia macrantha, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flower clusters that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods that split open when mature.

Caesalpinia macrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
