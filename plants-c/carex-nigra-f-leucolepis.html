Carex nigra f. leucolepis

Carex nigra f. leucolepis, commonly known as the white-fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, grass-like foliage. The plant produces cylindrical inflorescences with white to pale green bracts, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique coloration and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
