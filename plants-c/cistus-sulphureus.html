Cistus sulphureus

Cistus sulphureus, commonly known as the sulphur rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers and hairy, aromatic leaves. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cistus sulphureus is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
