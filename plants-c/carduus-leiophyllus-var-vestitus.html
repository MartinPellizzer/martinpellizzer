Carduus leiophyllus var. vestitus

Carduus leiophyllus var. vestitus, commonly known as the silky thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust stem that is often covered in silky, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces purple to violet flower heads that are surrounded by spiny, lobed leaves. Its leaves are typically gray-green in color and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
