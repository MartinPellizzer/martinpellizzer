Cousinia mulgediifolia

Cousinia mulgediifolia, commonly known as the Kashmir copper grass, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Cousinia mulgediifolia is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
