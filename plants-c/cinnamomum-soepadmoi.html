Cinnamomum soepadmoi

Cinnamomum soepadmoi, commonly known as the Java cinnamon or *Cinnamomum soepadmoi*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the Indonesian archipelago, particularly found in Java and Sumatra. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its bark, similar to true cinnamon, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

However, it is less commonly cultivated compared to other cinnamon species due to its specific growing requirements.
