Carex microsaccus

Carex microsaccus, commonly known as the small-sac sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves and small, clustered spikelets. Native to various regions in North America, it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by water or wind.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
