Caricina canescens

Caricina canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by the wind.

Caricina canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
