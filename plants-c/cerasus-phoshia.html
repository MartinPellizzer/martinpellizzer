Cerasus phoshia

Cerasus phoshia, commonly known as the Chinese wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries but are typically less sweet. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cerasus phoshia is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and floral display.
