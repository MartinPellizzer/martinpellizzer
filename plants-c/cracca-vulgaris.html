Cracca vulgaris

Cracca vulgaris, commonly known as common cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and clusters of pink or red flowers. Cracca vulgaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
