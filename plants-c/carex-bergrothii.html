Carex bergrothii

Carex bergrothii, commonly known as Bergroth's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia and Kazakhstan. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine or subalpine environments.

Carex bergrothii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
