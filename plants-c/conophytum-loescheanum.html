Conophytum loescheanum

Conophytum loescheanum, commonly known as the "Lobed Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often lobed or toothed, adapted for storing water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
