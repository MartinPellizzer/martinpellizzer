Cedrela subandina

Cedrela subandina, commonly known as the Andean cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and smooth, grayish bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is also valued for its timber.
