Convolvulus longipedicellatus

Convolvulus longipedicellatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Convolvulus longipedicellatus is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
