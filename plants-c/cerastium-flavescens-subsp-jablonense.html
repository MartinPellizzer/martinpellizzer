Cerastium flavescens subsp. jablonense

Cerastium flavescens subsp.

jablonense, commonly known as the Yellow mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with a dense layer of fine pubescence.

It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
