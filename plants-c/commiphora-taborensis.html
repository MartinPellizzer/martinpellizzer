Commiphora taborensis

Commiphora taborensis, commonly known as the Tabor resin tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine. The tree typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in dry or semi-arid habitats. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its unique chemical compounds, including various terpenoids and flavonoids.
