Cacalia cylindriflora

Cacalia cylindriflora, commonly known as the cylindrical false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, yellow, and arranged in a cylindrical shape, giving it its common name.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to dry conditions.
