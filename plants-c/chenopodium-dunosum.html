Chenopodium dunosum

Chenopodium dunosum, commonly known as desert spinach, is an annual herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of powdery wax. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Chenopodium dunosum is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes used as a food source in traditional diets.
