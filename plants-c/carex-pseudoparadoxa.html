Carex pseudoparadoxa

Carex pseudoparadoxa, commonly known as the strange sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and wetlands.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
