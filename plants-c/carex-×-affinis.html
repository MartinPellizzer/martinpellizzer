Carex × affinis

Carex × affinis, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Carex × affinis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
