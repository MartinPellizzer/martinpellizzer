Cyperus subambiguus

Cyperus subambiguus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, a common feature among sedges.

Cyperus subambiguus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
