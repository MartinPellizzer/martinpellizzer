Crotalaria eriocarpa var. viminalis

Crotalaria eriocarpa var. viminalis, commonly known as the velvetleaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in disturbed soils. Its seeds are encased in a pod-like fruit, and the plant is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in certain ecological contexts.
