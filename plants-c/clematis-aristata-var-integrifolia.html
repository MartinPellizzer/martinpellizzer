Clematis aristata var. integrifolia

Clematis aristata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Sierra cliff clematis, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its woody vines and showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically compound, with ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are often serrated or lobed. This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in rocky or well-drained soils.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
