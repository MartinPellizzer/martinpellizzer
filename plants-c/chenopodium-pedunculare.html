Chenopodium pedunculare

Chenopodium pedunculare, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, often hairy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is commonly found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It has a short, branched stem and produces small, dry fruits known as utricles.

Chenopodium pedunculare is often used in traditional medicine and is also a common weed in agricultural settings.
