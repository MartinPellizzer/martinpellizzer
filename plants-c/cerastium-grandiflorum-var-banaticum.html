Cerastium grandiflorum var. banaticum

Cerastium grandiflorum var. banaticum, commonly known as the Banat mountain chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves and stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native habitat.
