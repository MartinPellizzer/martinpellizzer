Castilleja talamancensis

Castilleja talamancensis, commonly known as the Talamancan milkvetch, is a hemiparasitic plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its bright red to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It forms symbiotic relationships with certain host plants, drawing nutrients through its roots.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for horticultural purposes.
