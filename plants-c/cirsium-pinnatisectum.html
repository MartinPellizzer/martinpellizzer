Cirsium pinnatisectum

Cirsium pinnatisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, also known as the sunflower family, and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Its foliage is deeply pinnatisected, giving it a finely divided appearance.

The plant is notable for its defensive spines, which deter herbivores and aid in its survival in various habitats.
