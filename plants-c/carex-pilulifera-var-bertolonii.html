Carex pilulifera var. bertolonii

Carex pilulifera var. bertolonii, commonly known as the Bertoloni's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
