Cereus minensis

Cereus minensis, commonly known as the Easter Cactus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that branch into clusters of fleshy, leaf-like structures. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming during the spring and early summer. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Cereus minensis thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
