Campylocentrum microphyllum

Campylocentrum microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other hibiscus species.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
