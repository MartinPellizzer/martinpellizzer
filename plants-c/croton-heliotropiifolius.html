Croton heliotropiifolius

Croton heliotropiifolius, commonly known as the sunflower leaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of sunflowers. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant, colorful foliage, which can range from yellow to red and orange, depending on the variety.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
