Cleome pallida

Cleome pallida, commonly known as the white cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features simple, opposite leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils.

Cleome pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
