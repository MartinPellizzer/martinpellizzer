Casimiroa sapota f. macrocarpa

Casimiroa sapota f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant sapote, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces a heavy, fleshy fruit with a sweet, creamy pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications due to its unique flavor and texture.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
