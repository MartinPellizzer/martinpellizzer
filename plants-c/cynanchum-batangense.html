Cynanchum batangense

Cynanchum batangense, commonly known as the Batang milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruits are follicles containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also of interest in traditional Chinese medicine.
