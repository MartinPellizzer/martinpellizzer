Cirsium vlassovianum f. album

Cirsium vlassovianum f. album, commonly known as the white thistle, is a variant of the common thistle characterized by its pale to white flowers.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features spiny leaves with a rough texture and a robust root system. Its flowers are typically solitary and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The f. album form is distinguished by its reduced pigmentation, giving it a distinctive appearance compared to the typical purple-flowered variety.
