Chondrocarpus repandus

Chondrocarpus repandus, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor environments such as wetlands and swampy areas.

Chondrocarpus repandus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
