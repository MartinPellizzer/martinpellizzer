Chloropetalum denticulatum

Chloropetalum denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cyathia, typical of the spurge family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its sap contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
