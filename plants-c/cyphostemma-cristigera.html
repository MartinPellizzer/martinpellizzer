Cyphostemma cristigera

Cyphostemma cristigera, commonly known as the cristate cyphostemma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ridged, and branching stems that give it a unique, almost sculptural appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in arid or semi-arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its striking foliage.
