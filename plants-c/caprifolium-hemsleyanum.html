Caprifolium hemsleyanum

Caprifolium hemsleyanum, commonly known as the Hemsley's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens in autumn.
