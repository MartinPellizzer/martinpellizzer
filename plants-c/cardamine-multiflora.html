Cardamine multiflora

Cardamine multiflora, commonly known as the mountain lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of kidney-shaped leaves with a distinctive white and green mottled pattern. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

Cardamine multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
