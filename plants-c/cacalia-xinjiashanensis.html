Cacalia xinjiashanensis

Cacalia xinjiashanensis, commonly known as the Xinjiashan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
