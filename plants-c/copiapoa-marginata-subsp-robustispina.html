Copiapoa marginata subsp. robustispina

Copiapoa marginata subsp.

robustispina, commonly known as the robust spined cactus, is a columnar cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide insulation and protection from herbivores.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in spring, which attract pollinators like bees and bats. It thrives in arid conditions with minimal rainfall, storing water in its fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular species among cactus enthusiasts and conservationists.
