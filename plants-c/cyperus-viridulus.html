Cyperus viridulus

Cyperus viridulus, commonly known as green flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the base of the plant. Cyperus viridulus thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

It is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed soils.
