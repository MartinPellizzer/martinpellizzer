Campanula ochroleuca var. alpestris

Campanula ochroleuca var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found at high altitudes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
