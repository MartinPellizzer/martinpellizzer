Cirsium elatius

Cirsium elatius, commonly known as the greater thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Cirsium elatius is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
