Carex tenuiformis var. neofilipes

Carex tenuiformis var. neofilipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 millimeter wide, giving it a delicate appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
