Cyperus diffusus var. multispicatus

Cyperus diffusus var. multispicatus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering structures consist of small, clustered spikes that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus diffusus var. multispicatus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
