Croton multiglandulosus

Croton multiglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage with leaves that exhibit a variety of shades and textures. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Croton multiglandulosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
