Cryptocarya subfalcata

Cryptocarya subfalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cryptocarya, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly New South Wales and Queensland, where it grows in subtropical and tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically reaches a height of 3 to 5 meters and has a dense, evergreen canopy.

Cryptocarya subfalcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic qualities.
