Carex vulpina var. tenuior

Carex vulpina var. tenuior, commonly known as the slender tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its slender, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
