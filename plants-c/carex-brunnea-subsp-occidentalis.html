Carex brunnea subsp. occidentalis

Carex brunnea subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy, with a triangular cross-section. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, three-sided flowering structures.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
