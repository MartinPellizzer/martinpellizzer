Capparis nitida

Capparis nitida, commonly known as the white caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible flower buds.

Capparis nitida thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky garden environments.
