Cystium pseudocicer

Cystium pseudocicer, commonly known as the Persian clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
