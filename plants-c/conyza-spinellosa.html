Conyza spinellosa

Conyza spinellosa, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is an annual flowering plant native to Africa, South America, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant has hairy, serrated leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Conyza spinellosa is sometimes considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
