Crassula expansa

Crassula expansa, commonly known as the "Laceleaf Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact and ornamental appearance. The leaves are typically a deep green color with a waxy texture, helping the plant store water efficiently. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the summer months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its low maintenance requirements.
