Clusia multilineata

Clusia multilineata, commonly known as the shining glory bush, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clusia multilineata is also known for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
