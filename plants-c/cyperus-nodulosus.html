Cyperus nodulosus

Cyperus nodulosus, commonly known as the knotted flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. Cyperus nodulosus typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, often followed by three-lobed, boat-shaped seeds.
