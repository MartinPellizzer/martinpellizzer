Clematis viorna var. obtusifoliola

Clematis viorna var. obtusifoliola, commonly known as the broad-leaved clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are typically green on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces fragrant, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and climbing structures.

Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and showy blooms.
