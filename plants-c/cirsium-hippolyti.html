Cirsium hippolyti

Cirsium hippolyti, commonly known as the Saint Helen's thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and dark green, lobed leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers in composite flower heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
