Celastrus stylosus var. stylosus

Celastrus stylosus var. stylosus, commonly known as the slender staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by orange to red berries in the fall. Its stems are often twining and have a somewhat woody texture, allowing it to climb and spread along vegetation.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
