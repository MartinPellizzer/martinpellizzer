Cyperus subxerophilus

Cyperus subxerophilus, commonly known as the desert flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowering structures called spikelets. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

Cyperus subxerophilus is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and can thrive in environments with limited rainfall.
