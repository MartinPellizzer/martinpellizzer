Crossandra strobilifera

Crossandra strobilifera, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Crossandra strobilifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
