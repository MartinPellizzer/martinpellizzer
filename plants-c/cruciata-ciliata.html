Cruciata ciliata

Cruciata ciliata, commonly known as the fringed crucifix, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in moist, calcareous soils. The plant features distinctive, palmately lobed leaves with finely toothed margins. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically have five petals.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
