Chimanthus amygdalina

Chimanthus amygdalina, commonly known as the almond orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, evergreen stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces white or pale pink blooms that emit a subtle almond scent, giving it its common name. Chimanthus amygdalina thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.
