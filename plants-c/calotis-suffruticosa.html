Calotis suffruticosa

Calotis suffruticosa, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, perennial herb that typically forms a dense mat of silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Calotis suffruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
