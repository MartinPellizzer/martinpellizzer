Cinnamomum esquirolii

Cinnamomum esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive cinnamon scent when crushed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited commercial use compared to other cinnamon species.
