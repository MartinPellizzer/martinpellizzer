Cinchona maynensis

Cinchona maynensis, commonly known as the yellow quinine tree, is a species of the Rubiaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds, and it is well adapted to high-altitude environments. It is notable for its historical use in the production of quinine, a compound used to treat malaria.

However, due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is now considered a vulnerable species.
