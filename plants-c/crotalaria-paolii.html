Crotalaria paolii

Crotalaria paolii, commonly known as the Paolii cowpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds, which are used in traditional agriculture. Crotalaria paolii is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

However, some species within the genus can be toxic to livestock, requiring caution in its use.
