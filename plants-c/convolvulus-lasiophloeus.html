Convolvulus lasiophloeus

Convolvulus lasiophloeus, commonly known as the roughleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous annual or perennial, often climbing or trailing along the ground. It produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
