Cereus chiloensis var. nigripilis

Cereus chiloensis var. nigripilis, commonly known as the black-haired chilote cactus, is a species native to Chile and Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its dark green stems and dense, blackish-gray spines. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often on rocky soils. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, appearing at the base of the stems during the spring and early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
