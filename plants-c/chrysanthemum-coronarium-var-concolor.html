Chrysanthemum coronarium var. concolor

Chrysanthemum coronarium var. concolor, commonly known as the pale chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers with a central disc surrounded by ray florets, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
