Carex recta

Carex recta, commonly known as the upright sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clumps, typically reaching heights of 3 to 5 feet. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex recta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
