Cyrtochilum tricorne

Cyrtochilum tricorne, commonly known as the triangular orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, triangular-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small, usually white or pale pink, and have a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Cyrtochilum tricorne is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
