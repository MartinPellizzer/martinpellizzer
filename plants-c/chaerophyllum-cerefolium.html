Chaerophyllum cerefolium

Chaerophyllum cerefolium, commonly known as the cow parsley or bishop's weed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in umbrella-like clusters. The plant is known for its mild, parsley-like scent and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
