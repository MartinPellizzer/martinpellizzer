Corylus americana var. calyculata

Corylus americana var. calyculata, commonly known as the California hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets, and its catkin-like male flowers are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a cupule, which is a modified leaf structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
