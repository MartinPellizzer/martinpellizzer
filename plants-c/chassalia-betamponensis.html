Chassalia betamponensis

Chassalia betamponensis, commonly known as the Madagascar chassalia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the island's humid forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
