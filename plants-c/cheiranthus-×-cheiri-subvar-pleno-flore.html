Cheiranthus × cheiri subvar. pleno-flore

Cheiranthus × cheiri subvar. pleno-flore, commonly known as the double-flowered wallflower, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, known for its showy, double blooms that are more densely packed than those of its parent species. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped, serrated leaves. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with a strong, spicy fragrance.

This subvariety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
