Crassocephalum togoense

Crassocephalum togoense, commonly known as the Togo hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with prominent dark veins. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Crassocephalum togoense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
