Caprifolium hypoglaucum

Caprifolium hypoglaucum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are fragrant, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer and is edible for both humans and wildlife.
