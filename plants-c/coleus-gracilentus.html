Coleus gracilentus

Coleus gracilentus, commonly known as the silver leaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that features a mix of green, white, and silver tones. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding visual interest to any garden or indoor space. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper watering and light conditions.
