Cnicus benedictus var. kostchyi

Cnicus benedictus var. kostchyi, commonly known as the Armenian blessed thistle, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
