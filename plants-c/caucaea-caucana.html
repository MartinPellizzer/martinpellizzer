Caucaea caucana

Caucaea caucana, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy slopes, thriving in cool and moist climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Caucaea caucana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
