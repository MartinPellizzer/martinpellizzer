Calophanes verticillaris

Calophanes verticillaris, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
