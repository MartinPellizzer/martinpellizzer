Coreopsis frondosa

Coreopsis frondosa, commonly known as the fringed coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces daisy-like yellow flowers with fringed petals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
