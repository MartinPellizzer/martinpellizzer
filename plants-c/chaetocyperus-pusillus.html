Chaetocyperus pusillus

Chaetocyperus pusillus, commonly known as the small bristled sedge, is a small perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chaetocyperus pusillus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
