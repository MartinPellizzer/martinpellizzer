Camellia corallina

Camellia corallina, commonly known as the coral camellia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and requires partial to full sun exposure.

Camellia corallina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and evergreen foliage.
