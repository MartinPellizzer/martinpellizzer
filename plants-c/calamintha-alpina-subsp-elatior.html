Calamintha alpina subsp. elatior

Calamintha alpina subsp.

elatior, commonly known as alpine calamint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are fragrant and have been used historically in herbal remedies for their calming properties.
