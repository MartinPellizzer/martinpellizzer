Cassia aristulifera

Cassia aristulifera, commonly known as the broad-leafed cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically broad and have a glossy green appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, seed-filled pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Cassia aristulifera is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
