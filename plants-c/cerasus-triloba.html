Cerasus triloba

Cerasus triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black when mature. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and wildlife benefits.
