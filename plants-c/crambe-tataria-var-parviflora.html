Crambe tataria var. parviflora

Crambe tataria var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered tatarian crambe, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
