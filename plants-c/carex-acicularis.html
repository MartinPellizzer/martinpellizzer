Carex acicularis

Carex acicularis, commonly known as the needleleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a sharp, needle-like appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green to brown in color, and it flowers in the summer months. Carex acicularis prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
