Capparis transvaalensis

Capparis transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is found in regions with a Mediterranean climate. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
