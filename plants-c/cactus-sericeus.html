Cactus sericeus

Cactus sericeus, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spineless stems covered in dense, silver-gray hairs that help reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Cactus sericeus plays a role in supporting local wildlife, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small animals.
