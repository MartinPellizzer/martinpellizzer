Carex diffusa

Carex diffusa, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Carex diffusa plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
