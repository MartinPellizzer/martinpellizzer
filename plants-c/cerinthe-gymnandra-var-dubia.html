Cerinthe gymnandra var. dubia

Cerinthe gymnandra var. dubia, commonly known as the hybrid cerinthe, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
