Carex banksiana

Carex banksiana, commonly known as the white sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grasses. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
