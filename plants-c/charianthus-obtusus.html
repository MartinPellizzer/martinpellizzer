Charianthus obtusus

Charianthus obtusus, commonly known as the bluntleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces large, bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and have a blunt or rounded tip, giving it its scientific name.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
