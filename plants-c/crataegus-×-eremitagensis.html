Crataegus × eremitagensis

Crataegus × eremitagensis, commonly known as the wild hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, small white or pink flowers, and red or orange berries that ripen in the fall. The plant typically grows in woodland areas, along stream banks, and in other moist, well-drained soils. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
