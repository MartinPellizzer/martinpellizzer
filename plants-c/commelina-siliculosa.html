Commelina siliculosa

Commelina siliculosa, commonly known as the silique commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers consist of three pale blue petals and three white sepals.

Commelina siliculosa is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
