Campylotropis hersii

Campylotropis hersii, commonly known as the narrowleaf burrbean, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnately arranged with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its fruit is a type of legume pod that becomes woody and contains several seeds.

Campylotropis hersii is often found in moist, open woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
