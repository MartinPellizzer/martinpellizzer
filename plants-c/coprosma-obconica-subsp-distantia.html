Coprosma obconica subsp. distantia

Coprosma obconica subsp.

distantia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coprosma, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It features small, oval to lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
