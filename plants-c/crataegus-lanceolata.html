Crataegus lanceolata

Crataegus lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its thorny stems and clustered growth habit.
