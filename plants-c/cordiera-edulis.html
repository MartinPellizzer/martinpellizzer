Cordiera edulis

Cordiera edulis, commonly known as the edible cordiera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are small, round, and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Cordiera edulis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses in local cuisines.
