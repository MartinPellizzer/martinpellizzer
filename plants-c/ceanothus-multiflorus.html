Ceanothus multiflorus

Ceanothus multiflorus, commonly known as the California bluecurls, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of sky-blue, curl-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

Ceanothus multiflorus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
