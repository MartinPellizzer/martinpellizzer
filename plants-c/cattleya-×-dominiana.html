Cattleya × dominiana

Cattleya × dominiana, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant features strap-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in a rosette formation. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
