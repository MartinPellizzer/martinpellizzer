Cassytha coronata

Cassytha coronata, commonly known as the crowned cassytha, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It features slender, wiry stems that twine around other plants, often forming dense mats. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are oval to elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, typically appearing in late winter to early spring.

Cassytha coronata is often found in wet or disturbed habitats, such as rainforests and creek sides.
