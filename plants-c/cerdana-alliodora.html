Cerdana alliodora

Cerdana alliodora, commonly known as the sweet-scented wood or alliandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and as a source of essential oils.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland tropical rainforests.
