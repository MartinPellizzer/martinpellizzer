Clematis gilbertiana

Clematis gilbertiana, commonly known as the Gilbert's clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features slender, climbing stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 feet. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

Clematis gilbertiana thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil for optimal growth.
