Centaurea castellanoides subsp. castellanoides

Centaurea castellanoides subsp.

castellanoides, commonly known as the Castilian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features striking blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bristly, spiny leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy composite flower head, which is characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
