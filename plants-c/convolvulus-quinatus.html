Convolvulus quinatus

Convolvulus quinatus, commonly known as the five-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves and trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that open in the morning. The plant is a climbing vine with hairy stems and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens despite its invasive potential in certain regions.
