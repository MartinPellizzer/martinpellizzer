Chlorophytum blepharophyllum subsp. rubropygmaeum

Chlorophytum blepharophyllum subsp.

rubropygmaeum, commonly known as the red-veined spider plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically green with striking red or purple veins.

The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright blue or purple berries. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
