Carex flaccosperma

Carex flaccosperma, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top. Native to wetland and marshy environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, and its seeds are dispersed by water or wind.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
