Calytrix arborescens

Calytrix arborescens, commonly known as the tree calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in winter, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Calytrix arborescens is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive floral display.
