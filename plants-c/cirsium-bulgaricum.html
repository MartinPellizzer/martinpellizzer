Cirsium bulgaricum

Cirsium bulgaricum, commonly known as the Bulgarian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The flowers are visited by various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native range.
