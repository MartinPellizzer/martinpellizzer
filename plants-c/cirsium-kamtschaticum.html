Cirsium kamtschaticum

Cirsium kamtschaticum, commonly known as the Kamtschatka thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Russia and northern China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with dark green, serrated foliage and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and deep, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Cirsium kamtschaticum is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
