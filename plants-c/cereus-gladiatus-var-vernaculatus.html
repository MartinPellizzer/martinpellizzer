Cereus gladiatus var. vernaculatus

Cereus gladiatus var. vernaculatus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is known for its striking, large white flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water. It thrives in full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
