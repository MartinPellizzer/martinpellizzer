Cyclamen cyclaminus

Cyclamen cyclaminus, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is typically grown as a winter annual, blooming in cool temperatures and thriving in well-drained soil. Its tuberous roots allow it to survive in dry conditions, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Cyclamen cyclaminus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
