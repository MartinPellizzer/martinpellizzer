Clinopodium umbrosum var. souliei

Clinopodium umbrosum var. souliei, commonly known as the dark clinique or southern clinopodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and emit a strong mint-like fragrance when crushed.

It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.
