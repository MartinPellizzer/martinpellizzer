Coffea multibracteata

Coffea multibracteata, commonly known as the multibracteata coffee plant, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree with elliptical, glossy leaves and white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that contain one or two seeds, which are used to make coffee.

It is notable for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its potential for cultivation and genetic diversity.
