Croton enantiophyllus

Croton enantiophyllus, commonly known as the sacred croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored leaves that can vary in shape and texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.

Despite its toxicity, it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
