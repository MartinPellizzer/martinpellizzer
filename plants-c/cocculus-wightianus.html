Cocculus wightianus

Cocculus wightianus, commonly known as the Indian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to purple. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
