Chloris anoplia

Chloris anoplia, commonly known as slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Chloris anoplia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
