Campanula mollis var. lasiantha

Campanula mollis var. lasiantha, commonly known as the soft bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
