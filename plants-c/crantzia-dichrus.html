Crantzia dichrus

Crantzia dichrus, commonly known as the two-colored crantzia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. Crantzia dichrus typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
