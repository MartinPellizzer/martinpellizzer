Cheilosa

Cheilosa, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small cups. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Cheilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
