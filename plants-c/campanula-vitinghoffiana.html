Campanula vitinghoffiana

Campanula vitinghoffiana, commonly known as the Vitinghoff's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

Campanula vitinghoffiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
