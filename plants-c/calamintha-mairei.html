Calamintha mairei

Calamintha mairei, commonly known as Maire's calamint, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant features slender, square stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are fragrant and often used in herbal teas or as a culinary herb.

Calamintha mairei thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
