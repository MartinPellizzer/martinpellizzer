Calea scaberrima

Calea scaberrima, commonly known as the "sobremesa" or "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, rough leaves and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it an intriguing subject for botanical study.
