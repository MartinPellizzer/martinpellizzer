Carex chichijimensis

Carex chichijimensis, commonly known as the Chichijima sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan, specifically the Ryukyu Islands, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
