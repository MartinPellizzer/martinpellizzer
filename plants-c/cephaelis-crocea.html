Cephaelis crocea

Cephaelis crocea, commonly known as the red flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. The plant produces striking red-orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture.

Cephaelis crocea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
