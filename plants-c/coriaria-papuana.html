Coriaria papuana

Coriaria papuana, commonly known as the Papuan gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Coriariaceae family and is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
