Citrus × vulgaris

Citrus × vulgaris, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a hybrid citrus fruit originating from the cross between the pomelo and mandarin.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery rind and segmented, juicy pulp that is rich in vitamin C. The plant is a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and it is widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes.

The botanical features of Citrus × vulgaris make it a valuable species in horticulture and agriculture.
