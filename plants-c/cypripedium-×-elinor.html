Cypripedium × elinor

Cypripedium × elinor, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a unique adaptation for pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland habitats, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
