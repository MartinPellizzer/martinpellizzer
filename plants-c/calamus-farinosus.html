Calamus farinosus

Calamus farinosus, commonly known as the powdered reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are typically borne at the base of the leaves. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth surface and a slight sheen.

Calamus farinosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
