Campanula rapunculus var. reclinata

Campanula rapunculus var. reclinata, commonly known as the nodding bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that droop downward, giving it its common name. The leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
