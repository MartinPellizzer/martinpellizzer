Cyanopis villosa

Cyanopis villosa, commonly known as the hairy blueberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southeastern China and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and blue-black berries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are white and often clustered, producing a mild fragrance.

Cyanopis villosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
