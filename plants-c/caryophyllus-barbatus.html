Caryophyllus barbatus

Caryophyllus barbatus, commonly known as the barbed caryophyll, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant is characterized by its distinctive barbed bracts that surround its flowers, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or white in color.

Caryophyllus barbatus is typically found in rocky or arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil.
