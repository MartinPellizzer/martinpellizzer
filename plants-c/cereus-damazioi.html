Cereus damazioi

Cereus damazioi, commonly known as the Damazo's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of cacti.
