Carex lepidocarpa f. laevigata

Carex lepidocarpa f. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-surfaced sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and triangular stems. The leaves are typically narrow and have a smooth texture, distinguishing it from other Carex species. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along water edges.

This plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
