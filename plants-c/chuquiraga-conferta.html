Chuquiraga conferta

Chuquiraga conferta, commonly known as the Andean burr bush, is a shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is often used in local traditional medicine and horticulture.
