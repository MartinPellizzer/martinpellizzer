Calamagrostis arenaria subsp. arundinacea

Calamagrostis arenaria subsp.

arundinacea, commonly known as silvergrass or sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It features narrow, silver-blue leaves and produces fluffy, pale green to brown seed heads in late summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. It has a clumping growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Its foliage and seed heads provide habitat and food for various insects and birds.
