Calais tenella

Calais tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
