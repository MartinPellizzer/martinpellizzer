Calamagrostis suizanensis

Calamagrostis suizanensis, commonly known as the Suizan reed, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant features tall, slender culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-white underneath. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish to brownish flowers.

Calamagrostis suizanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
