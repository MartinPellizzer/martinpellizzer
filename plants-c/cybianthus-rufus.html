Cybianthus rufus

Cybianthus rufus, commonly known as the redflower tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its bright red petals and yellow center. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is drought-tolerant. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
