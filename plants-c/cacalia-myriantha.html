Cacalia myriantha

Cacalia myriantha, commonly known as the many-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to lavender, often with a distinctive purple throat. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Cacalia myriantha is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
