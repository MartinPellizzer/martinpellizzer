Carex lagopina subsp. baetica

Carex lagopina subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Baetican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond margins, and stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowering spikes are usually short and produce small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
