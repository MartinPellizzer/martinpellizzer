Carex lucorum subsp. austrolucorum

Carex lucorum subsp.

austrolucorum, commonly known as the Australian bulrush, is a perennial sedge native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of water bodies.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of spikelets that are often submerged or floating. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for wetland restoration projects.
