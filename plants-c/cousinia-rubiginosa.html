Cousinia rubiginosa

Cousinia rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the stems are reddish in color, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cousinia rubiginosa is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
