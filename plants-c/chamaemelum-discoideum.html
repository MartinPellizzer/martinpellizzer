Chamaemelum discoideum

Chamaemelum discoideum, commonly known as the slender false chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Chamaemelum discoideum is often used in herbal medicine for its mild sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
