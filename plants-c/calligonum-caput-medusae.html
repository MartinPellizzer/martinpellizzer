Calligonum caput-medusae

Calligonum caput-medusae, commonly known as the "Medusa's Head," is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, displaying a compact, bushy form. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Calligonum caput-medusae is well adapted to drought and can thrive in areas with limited rainfall.
