Celtis bungeana var. pubipedicella

Celtis bungeana var. pubipedicella, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, greenish fruits. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in urban landscaping.
