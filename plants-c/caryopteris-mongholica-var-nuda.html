Caryopteris mongholica var. nuda

Caryopteris mongholica var. nuda, commonly known as nakedleaf bluemouth, is a deciduous shrub native to northern China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairless, giving it its "nuda" designation. The plant produces clusters of blue-violet flowers in late summer, which are borne on slender, erect stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its drought tolerance and attractive foliage make it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
