Cargillia megalocarpa

Cargillia megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant seed tree, is a unique plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its large, woody structure and can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces massive, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to the cold, high-altitude environment.

Cargillia megalocarpa is of interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations and ecological significance in its native habitat.
