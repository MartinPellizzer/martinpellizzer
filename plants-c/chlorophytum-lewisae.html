Chlorophytum lewisae

Chlorophytum lewisae, commonly known as the "snake plant" or "mother-in-law's tongue," is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or yellow variegation. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers on long, slender stalks during the summer months.

Chlorophytum lewisae is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to improved indoor air quality.
