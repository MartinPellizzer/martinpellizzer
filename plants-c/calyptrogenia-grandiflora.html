Calyptrogenia grandiflora

Calyptrogenia grandiflora, commonly known as the giant calyptrate, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It features a unique calyptra, a hood-like structure that covers the flower bud, which is a distinctive botanical trait. The flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators with its vibrant colors.
