Canarium microphyllum

Canarium microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaf candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its oil-rich seeds and has traditional uses in various cultures.
