Caryospermum arborescens

Caryospermum arborescens, commonly known as the red balsam, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, characterized by its upright stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caryospermum arborescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
