Cyperus fiebrigii

Cyperus fiebrigii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Cyperus fiebrigii is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
