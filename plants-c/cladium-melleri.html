Cladium melleri

Cladium melleri, commonly known as the sawgrass, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often lined with serrations, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which help stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

Cladium melleri plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and water filtration.
