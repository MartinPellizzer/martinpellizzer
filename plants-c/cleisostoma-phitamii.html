Cleisostoma phitamii

Cleisostoma phitamii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive red lip. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are ovate and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces fragrant blooms during the rainy season.

Cleisostoma phitamii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
