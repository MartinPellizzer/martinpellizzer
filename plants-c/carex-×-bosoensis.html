Carex × bosoensis

Carex × bosoensis, commonly known as the Boso sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical inflorescences.

It is native to regions of East Asia, including Japan and China, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense clumps.

Carex × bosoensis is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
