Clerodendrum transvaalense

Clerodendrum transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal glorybower, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces vibrant clusters of tubular flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

The plant is well-suited for warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
