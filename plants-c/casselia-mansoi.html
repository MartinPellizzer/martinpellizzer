Casselia mansoi

Casselia mansoi, commonly known as the blue waxy apple, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a unique visual appeal to its appearance. The plant produces small, apple-like fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may turn red or yellow depending on the variety. Casselia mansoi thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
