Crocus circumscissus

Crocus circumscissus, commonly known as the split crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Crocus.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces showy, early spring blooms with petals that are typically purple or pink in color. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh environments, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
