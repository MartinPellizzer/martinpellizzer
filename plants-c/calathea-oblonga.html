Calathea oblonga

Calathea oblonga, commonly known as the arrowleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and contrasting colors. The plant thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its foliage changes color in response to light exposure, a phenomenon known as nyctinasty.

Calathea oblonga requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
