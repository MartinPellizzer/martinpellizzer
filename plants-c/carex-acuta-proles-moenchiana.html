Carex acuta proles moenchiana

Carex acuta proles moenchiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a species of sedge characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical, often triangular stems.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually arranged in a dense, compact clump. Its flowering structure consists of spikelets that bear small, three-parted flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
