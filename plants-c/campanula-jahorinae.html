Campanula jahorinae

Campanula jahorinae, commonly known as the Jahorina bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

Campanula jahorinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
