Cormus domestica var. paradisiaca

Cormus domestica var. paradisiaca, commonly known as the giant banana plant, is a striking member of the Musaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach several meters in length, providing a lush, tropical appearance. The plant produces clusters of large, yellow bananas that are typically harvested when they are fully mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions as both a decorative and edible plant.
