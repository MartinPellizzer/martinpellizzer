Caprifolium decipiens

Caprifolium decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that attract pollinators. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Caprifolium decipiens is known for its ability to mimic the appearance of other plants, which may aid in its survival through deceptive pollination strategies.
