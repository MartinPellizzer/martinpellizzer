Climacoptera lachnophylla

Climacoptera lachnophylla, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme environments.
