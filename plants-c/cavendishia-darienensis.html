Cavendishia darienensis

Cavendishia darienensis, commonly known as the Darien orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Panama and Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a pseudobulb structure that helps store water and nutrients.

Cavendishia darienensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
