Cestrum elegans var. totutla

Cestrum elegans var. totutla, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
