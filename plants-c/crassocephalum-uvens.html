Crassocephalum uvens

Crassocephalum uvens, commonly known as the African violet or violetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive helmet-shaped structure at the base of the petals. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Crassocephalum uvens is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
