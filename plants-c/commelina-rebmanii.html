Commelina rebmanii

Commelina rebmanii, commonly known as the slender dayflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that are clustered in umbels, often with a white or yellowish spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina rebmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
