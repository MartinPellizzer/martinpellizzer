Campanula patula f. velenowskyi

Campanula patula f. velenowskyi, commonly known as the Velenowsky's bellflower, is a cultivar of the bellflower plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long-lasting blooms.
