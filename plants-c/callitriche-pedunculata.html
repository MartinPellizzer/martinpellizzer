Callitriche pedunculata

Callitriche pedunculata, commonly known as water starwort, is a small aquatic plant that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the sediment in aquatic habitats.

Callitriche pedunculata plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
