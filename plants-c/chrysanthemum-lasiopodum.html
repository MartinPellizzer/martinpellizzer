Chrysanthemum lasiopodum

Chrysanthemum lasiopodum, commonly known as the woolly chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
