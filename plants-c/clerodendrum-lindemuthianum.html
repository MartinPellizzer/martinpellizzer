Clerodendrum lindemuthianum

Clerodendrum lindemuthianum, commonly known as the red-flowered clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also notable for its ability to produce small, berry-like fruits that contain seeds.
