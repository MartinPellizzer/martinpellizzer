Castilla costaricana

Castilla costaricana, commonly known as the Guatemalan beauty or Costa Rican castilla, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
