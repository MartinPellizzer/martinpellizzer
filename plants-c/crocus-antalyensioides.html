Crocus antalyensioides

Crocus antalyensioides, commonly known as the Antalya crocus, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing bright yellow or orange petals with a central stigma. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species.
