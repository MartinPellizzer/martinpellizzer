Convallaria majalis f. mappii

Convallaria majalis f. mappii, commonly known as the Mappian Lily, is a rare and striking variant of the lily of the valley.

This cultivar is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It is a hybrid form that exhibits unique foliage and floral characteristics compared to the typical species. The plant is evergreen and thrives in shaded, moist environments, making it a prized addition to woodland gardens.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
