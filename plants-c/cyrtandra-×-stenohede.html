Cyrtandra × stenohede

Cyrtandra × stenohede, commonly known as the hybrid stenohede, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Cyrtandra tenuifolia and Cyrtandra stenohyla, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant typically produces showy, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its foliage is usually lance-shaped with a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
