Clematis montana

Clematis montana, commonly known as the mountain clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Clematis montana is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
