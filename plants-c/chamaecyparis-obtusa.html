Chamaecyparis obtusa

Chamaecyparis obtusa, commonly known as the Japanese cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its flat, scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and durable timber.

Its foliage provides habitat for various wildlife species, contributing to its ecological significance.
