Consolida phrygia subsp. thessalonica

Consolida phrygia subsp.

thessalonica, commonly known as the Thessalonica poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Balkan region. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
