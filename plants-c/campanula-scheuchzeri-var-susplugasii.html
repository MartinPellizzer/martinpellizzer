Campanula scheuchzeri var. susplugasii

Campanula scheuchzeri var. susplugasii, commonly known as the Susplugasii bellflower, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh mountain environment.
