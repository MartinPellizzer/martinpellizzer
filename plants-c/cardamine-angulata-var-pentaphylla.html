Cardamine angulata var. pentaphylla

Cardamine angulata var. pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf water-cress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream sides and forest floors. Its leaves are trifoliate or pinnately divided, with a distinctive angular appearance.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
