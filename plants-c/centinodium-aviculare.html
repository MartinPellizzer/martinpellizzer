Centinodium aviculare

Centinodium aviculare, commonly known as the silverleaf cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branching, spiny stems that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, enabling it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife species.
