Cyperus burchellii

Cyperus burchellii, commonly known as the common flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Cyperus burchellii is adaptable and can be found in a variety of habitats, including wetlands, fields, and along riverbanks.
