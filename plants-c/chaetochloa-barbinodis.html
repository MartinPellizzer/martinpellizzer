Chaetochloa barbinodis

Chaetochloa barbinodis, commonly known as the hairy leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a stabilizing plant in aquatic environments.
