Crataegus pumila

Crataegus pumila, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a small deciduous shrub native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features dense, thorny branches. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated edges and a glossy surface.

Crataegus pumila is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and rocky areas, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
