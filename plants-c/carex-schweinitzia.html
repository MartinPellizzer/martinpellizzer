Carex schweinitzia

Carex schweinitzia, commonly known as Schweinitz's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to North America, particularly the eastern United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands and forested areas. The plant is often found in shaded environments and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for various wildlife species.
