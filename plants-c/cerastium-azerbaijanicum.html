Cerastium azerbaijanicum

Cerastium azerbaijanicum, commonly known as Azerbaijani chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are usually opposite and ovate. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

Cerastium azerbaijanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
