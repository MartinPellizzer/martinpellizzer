Cinnamomum melliodorum

Cinnamomum melliodorum, commonly known as the yellow cinnamon or sweet cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that exudes a sweet, spicy scent when cut. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a strong, camphor-like aroma.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
