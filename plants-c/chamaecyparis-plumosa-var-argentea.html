Chamaecyparis plumosa var. argentea

Chamaecyparis plumosa var. argentea, commonly known as the silver-tipped cedar or silver plumosa cypress, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer with a distinctive, weeping form.

Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are a striking silver-white to bluish-gray, providing a unique visual contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a specimen plant.
