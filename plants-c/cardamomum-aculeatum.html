Cardamomum aculeatum

Cardamomum aculeatum, commonly known as the spiny cardamom, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and aromatic industries.
