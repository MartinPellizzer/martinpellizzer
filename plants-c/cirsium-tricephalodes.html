Cirsium tricephalodes

Cirsium tricephalodes, commonly known as the three-headed thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant produces a single flower head that splits into three distinct parts, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Cirsium tricephalodes is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
