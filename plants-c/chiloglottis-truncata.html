Chiloglottis truncata

Chiloglottis truncata, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-like floral structure and is typically found in heathlands and open forests. The plant has a short, stocky rhizome and produces a single, erect stem with a few leaves. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive, hooded labellum.

Chiloglottis truncata is a protected species, often requiring specific environmental conditions for successful cultivation.
