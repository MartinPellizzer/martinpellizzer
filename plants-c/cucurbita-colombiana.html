Cucurbita colombiana

Cucurbita colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian pumpkin, is a species native to South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourd and squash plants. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, with the fruit typically being a round or elongated pumpkin. It is characterized by its thick, smooth rind and sweet, edible flesh.

This species is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses in its native region.
