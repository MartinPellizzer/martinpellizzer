Crotalaria brasiliensis

Crotalaria brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cowpea or devil's flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with two lobes. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its pods contain seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
