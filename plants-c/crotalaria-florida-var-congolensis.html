Crotalaria florida var. congolensis

Crotalaria florida var. congolensis, commonly known as the Congo flame flower, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its striking, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its stems are typically hairy and branching. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
