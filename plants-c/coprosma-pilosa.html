Coprosma pilosa

Coprosma pilosa, commonly known as the hairy coprosma, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is typically found in coastal and forested areas. It produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

Coprosma pilosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
