Cotyledon egglii

Cotyledon egglii, commonly known as the Easter cactus, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are often segmented and covered in small, pointed spines. The plant produces vibrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are typically small and rounded, and it is known for its ability to store water in its stems.

Cotyledon egglii prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
