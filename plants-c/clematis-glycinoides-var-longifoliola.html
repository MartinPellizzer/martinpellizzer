Clematis glycinoides var. longifoliola

Clematis glycinoides var. longifoliola, commonly known as the Japanese climbing bower, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
