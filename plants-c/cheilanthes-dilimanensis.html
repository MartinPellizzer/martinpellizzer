Cheilanthes dilimanensis

Cheilanthes dilimanensis, commonly known as the Diliman rock fern, is a species of fern found primarily in the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its fronds are leathery and have a distinctive, intricate pattern of sori, which are clusters of spores.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance in its native habitat.
