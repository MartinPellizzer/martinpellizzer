Calophyllum versteegii

Calophyllum versteegii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria and Cameroon.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing several seeds.

It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
