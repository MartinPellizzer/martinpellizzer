Capparis cynophallophora f. latifolia

Capparis cynophallophora f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf caper, is a variant of the caper plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible, rounded fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential as a food source.
