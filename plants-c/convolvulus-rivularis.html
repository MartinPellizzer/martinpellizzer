Convolvulus rivularis

Convolvulus rivularis, commonly known as water bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows near water sources such as rivers, streams, and wetlands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pale pink to white flowers with five petals. Its stems are often prostrate or trailing, and it has a fibrous root system that helps it thrive in moist environments.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
