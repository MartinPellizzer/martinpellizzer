Ciconium glabrifolium

Ciconium glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved ciconium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Ciconium glabrifolium is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
