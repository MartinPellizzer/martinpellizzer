Cylindropuntia verschaffeltii

Cylindropuntia verschaffeltii, commonly known as the Mexican fire cholla, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiky structure. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
