Crassula lycopodioides f. fragilis

Crassula lycopodioides f. fragilis, commonly known as the Fragile Crassula, is a unique succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers during the warmer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Due to its delicate foliage, it requires careful handling to avoid damage.
