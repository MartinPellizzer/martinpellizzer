Calochilus caesius

Calochilus caesius, commonly known as the blue-tinged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its striking, often violet or blue-purplish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and are known for their intricate, spurred petals.

Calochilus caesius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
