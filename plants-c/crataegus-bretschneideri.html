Crataegus bretschneideri

Crataegus bretschneideri, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different climates, making it a resilient species.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties contribute to its significance in horticulture and herbal practices.
