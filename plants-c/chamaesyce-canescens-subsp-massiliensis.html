Chamaesyce canescens subsp. massiliensis

Chamaesyce canescens subsp.

massiliensis, commonly known as the Massilian sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to southern Europe. It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal or inland dunes.

The plant features small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is well-adapted to harsh, arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
