Campanula integerrima

Campanula integerrima, commonly known as the rock campanula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun to partial shade.

Campanula integerrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
