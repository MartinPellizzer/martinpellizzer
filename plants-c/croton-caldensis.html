Croton caldensis

Croton caldensis, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Croton caldensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
