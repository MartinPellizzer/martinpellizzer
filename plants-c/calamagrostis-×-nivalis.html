Calamagrostis × nivalis

Calamagrostis × nivalis, commonly known as the silvergrass or snowgrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which gives it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its culms are slender and somewhat arching, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed.

Calamagrostis × nivalis is valued in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
