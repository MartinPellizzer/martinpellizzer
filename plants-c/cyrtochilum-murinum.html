Cyrtochilum murinum

Cyrtochilum murinum, commonly known as the mouse orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It features a slender, hairy stem and produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a unique, mouse-like appearance. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract specific pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Cyrtochilum murinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
