Cereus torulosus

Cereus torulosus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, thick stem and bright, showy flowers. The plant is typically cultivated for its ornamental value, with its vibrant flowers often appearing in shades of yellow, orange, or red. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Despite its attractive appearance, it is a short-lived species, often blooming only once in its lifetime.
