Caragana tragacanthoides var. pallasiana

Caragana tragacanthoides var. pallasiana, commonly known as the Pallas's caragana, is a shrub native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system.

This species is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of forage for livestock.
