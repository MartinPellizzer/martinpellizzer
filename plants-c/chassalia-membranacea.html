Chassalia membranacea

Chassalia membranacea, commonly known as the parchment lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces distinctive, membranous bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Chassalia membranacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
