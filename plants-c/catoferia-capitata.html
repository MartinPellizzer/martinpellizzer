Catoferia capitata

Catoferia capitata, commonly known as the common cat's ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, broad leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Catoferia capitata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
