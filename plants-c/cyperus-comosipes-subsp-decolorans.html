Cyperus comosipes subsp. decolorans

Cyperus comosipes subsp.

decolorans, commonly known as the silver grass or decoloring sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a silvery or bluish hue.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in late spring or early summer. It thrives in wet or moist soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and water feature plantings.
