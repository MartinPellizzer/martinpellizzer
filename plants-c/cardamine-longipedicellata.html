Cardamine longipedicellata

Cardamine longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stemmed watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants. The plant features small, delicate leaves and produces clusters of tiny white flowers. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in wet soil along stream banks and in swampy areas.

The species is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to fluctuating water levels.
