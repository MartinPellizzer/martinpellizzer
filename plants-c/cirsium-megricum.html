Cirsium megricum

Cirsium megricum, commonly known as the meadow thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

Cirsium megricum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
