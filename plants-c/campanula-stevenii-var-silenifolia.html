Campanula stevenii var. silenifolia

Campanula stevenii var. silenifolia, commonly known as the silvery bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in cooler climates.
