Calea brevifolia

Calea brevifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open at night. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Calea brevifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique nocturnal blooming habit.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.
