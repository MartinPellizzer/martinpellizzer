Capparis cynophallophora f. ovalifolia

Capparis cynophallophora f. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf caper, is a variant of the caper plant characterized by its oval-shaped leaves.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in southern Europe and parts of North Africa. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, green fruits. Its leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some culinary uses in traditional recipes.
