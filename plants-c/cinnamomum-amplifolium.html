Cinnamomum amplifolium

Cinnamomum amplifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct aromatic scent. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, spindle-shaped fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
