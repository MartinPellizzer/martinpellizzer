Cururu sinuata

Cururu sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuata vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The Sinuata vine thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
