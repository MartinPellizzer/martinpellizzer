Convolvulus leiocalycinus var. stocksii

Convolvulus leiocalycinus var. stocksii, commonly known as the Stocks' blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often twining. This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
