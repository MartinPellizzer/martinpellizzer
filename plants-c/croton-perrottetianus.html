Croton perrottetianus

Croton perrottetianus, commonly known as the Perrottetian croton, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from yellow to red. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
