Coffea dongnaiensis

Coffea dongnaiensis, commonly known as Dong Nai coffee, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and is known for its small, red berries that contain the seeds used to produce coffee. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences, and bloom during the rainy season.
