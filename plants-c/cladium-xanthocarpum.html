Cladium xanthocarpum

Cladium xanthocarpum, commonly known as the yellow-fruited sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in shallow water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by yellow, boat-shaped fruits that are adapted for water dispersal. Cladium xanthocarpum plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for environments with fluctuating water levels and poor soil conditions.
