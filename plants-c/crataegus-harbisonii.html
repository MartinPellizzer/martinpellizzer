Crataegus harbisonii

Crataegus harbisonii, commonly known as Harbison's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas of Georgia and South Carolina. This shrub or small tree typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features thorny branches. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins, and it produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
