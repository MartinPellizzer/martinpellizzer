Cirsium × reichardtii

Cirsium × reichardtii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, purple-tinged flower heads and robust, upright stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cirsium × reichardtii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage and showy flowers.
