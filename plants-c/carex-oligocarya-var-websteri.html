Carex oligocarya var. websteri

Carex oligocarya var. websteri, commonly known as Webster's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
