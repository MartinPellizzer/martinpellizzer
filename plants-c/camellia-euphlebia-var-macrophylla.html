Camellia euphlebia var. macrophylla

Camellia euphlebia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf camellia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and China.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
