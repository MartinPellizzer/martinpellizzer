Clitoria capitata

Clitoria capitata, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple pea-shaped flowers that resemble butterflies, hence its common name. The plant is a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and produces clusters of vibrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye due to their rich pigments.

The botanical features of Clitoria capitata make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
