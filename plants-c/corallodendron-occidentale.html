Corallodendron occidentale

Corallodendron occidentale, commonly known as the western coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking coral-like inflorescences, which consist of clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with glossy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
