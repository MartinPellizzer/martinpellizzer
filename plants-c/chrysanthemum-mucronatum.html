Chrysanthemum mucronatum

Chrysanthemum mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Chrysanthemum mucronatum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
