Callicarpa tomex

Callicarpa tomex, commonly known as the red beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces striking clusters of bright red berries that persist into winter, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. These berries are not only attractive but also serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.

Callicarpa tomex thrives in partial shade to full sun and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
