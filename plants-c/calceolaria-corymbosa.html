Calceolaria corymbosa

Calceolaria corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while the stems are often hairy.

Calceolaria corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
