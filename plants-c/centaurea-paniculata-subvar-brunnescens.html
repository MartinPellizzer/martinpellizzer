Centaurea paniculata subvar. brunnescens

Centaurea paniculata subvar. brunnescens, commonly known as the brown-centred thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces dense, conical inflorescences composed of purple-blue flowers, which bloom in summer. Its bracts are typically brownish and contribute to its common name.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
