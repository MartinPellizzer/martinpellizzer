Clematis mandshurica f. lancifolia

Clematis mandshurica f. lancifolia, commonly known as the lance-leaved Clematis, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
