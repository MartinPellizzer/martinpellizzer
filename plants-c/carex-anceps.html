Carex anceps

Carex anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedges, is a species of sedge native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Carex anceps is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ability to thrive in poorly drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
