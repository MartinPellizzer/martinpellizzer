Centrosema latissimum

Centrosema latissimum, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used as a food source. It thrives in warm climates and is often used in agricultural systems for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
