Clematis aristata subsp. tasmanica

Clematis aristata subsp.

tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian Clematis, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania. It is a woody vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and extended blooming period.
