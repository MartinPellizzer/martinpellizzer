Coleus bipartitus

Coleus bipartitus, commonly known as the two-colored coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bicolor leaves that typically display a combination of green, white, and red hues. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. Its leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Coleus bipartitus thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
