Campomanesia sepalifolia

Campomanesia sepalifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree or erva-cidra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub with opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
