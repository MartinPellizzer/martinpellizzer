Cirsium cymosum var. canovirens

Cirsium cymosum var. canovirens, commonly known as the Canovirens thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, branched inflorescence that bears purple to pink flower heads. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant has a robust root system and is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils.
