Cylindrolobus neglectus

Cylindrolobus neglectus, commonly known as the slender tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It features small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its woody stems are often covered in a whitish coating, which helps reflect sunlight and minimize heat stress.

Cylindrolobus neglectus is well-suited to low-nutrient soils and can thrive in areas with limited rainfall.
