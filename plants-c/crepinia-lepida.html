Crepinia lepida

Crepinia lepida, commonly known as the slender crepe jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are often eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in coastal areas or sandy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
