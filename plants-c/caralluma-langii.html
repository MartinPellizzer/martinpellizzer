Caralluma langii

Caralluma langii, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of India and Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features fleshy, spiny stems and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water in harsh environments.

Caralluma langii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
