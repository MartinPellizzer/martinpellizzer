Chrysophyllum masoalense

Chrysophyllum masoalense, commonly known as the Madagascar cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of the rainforest.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
