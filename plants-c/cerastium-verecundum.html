Cerastium verecundum

Cerastium verecundum, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, commonly known as the carnation family. The plant features small, hairy, rounded leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.
