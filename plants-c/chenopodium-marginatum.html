Chenopodium marginatum

Chenopodium marginatum, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that often have a reddish hue, especially at the base. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and typically have a rough, sandpapery texture. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
