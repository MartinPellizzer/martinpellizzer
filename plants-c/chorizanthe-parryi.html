Chorizanthe parryi

Chorizanthe parryi, commonly known as Parry's choris, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which are often pink or red, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
