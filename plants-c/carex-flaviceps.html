Carex flaviceps

Carex flaviceps, commonly known as the yellow-headed sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, often with a yellowish hue, giving it its common name.

Carex flaviceps plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
