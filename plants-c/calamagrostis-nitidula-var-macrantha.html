Calamagrostis nitidula var. macrantha

Calamagrostis nitidula var. macrantha, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and distinctive, tall, feathery panicles that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a clumping growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.

Its foliage provides year-round visual interest, making it a popular choice for water feature landscaping.
