Carex acuta f. amblylepis

Carex acuta f. amblylepis, commonly known as the blunt-leaved sedge, is a variant of the narrowleaf sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. The plant features grass-like leaves that are generally shorter and broader than those of the typical Carex acuta. It typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swampy areas.

Its inflorescence consists of a spicate cluster of small, inconspicuous flowers.
