Callista eriiflora

Callista eriiflora, commonly known as the silky sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and the species is known for its aromatic properties.

Callista eriiflora is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments.
