Centaurea gubanovii

Centaurea gubanovii, commonly known as the Gubanov's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, bract-like leaves around the flower head.

Centaurea gubanovii is often found in open habitats such as steppes and forest edges.
