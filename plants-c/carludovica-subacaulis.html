Carludovica subacaulis

Carludovica subacaulis, commonly known as the Mexican sun cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
