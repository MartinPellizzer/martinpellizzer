Crataegus × luzinii

Crataegus × luzinii, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds and other wildlife.

Crataegus × luzinii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
