Crataegus augusta

Crataegus augusta, commonly known as the August crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The August crabapple is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
