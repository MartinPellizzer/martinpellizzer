Cerastium adamovicii

Cerastium adamovicii, commonly known as the Adamovici's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers during the spring and early summer, which are typically arranged in umbel-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
