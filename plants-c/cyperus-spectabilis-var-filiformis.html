Cyperus spectabilis var. filiformis

Cyperus spectabilis var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, thread-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its botanical features include rhizomes that help in spreading and stabilizing the soil.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
