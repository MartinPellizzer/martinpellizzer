Cymbopogon stolzii

Cymbopogon stolzii, commonly known as Stolz's lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a citrus scent. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences and is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cymbopogon stolzii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
