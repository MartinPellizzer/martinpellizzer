Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. bernardinus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. bernardinus, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-green foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, dry fruits called achene. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and steppe environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
