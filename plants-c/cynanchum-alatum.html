Cynanchum alatum

Cynanchum alatum, commonly known as the winged milkweed, is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, including China, Korea, and Russia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, ovate leaves with distinct wing-like structures along the veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbel-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Cynanchum alatum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.
