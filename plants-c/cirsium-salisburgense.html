Cirsium salisburgense

Cirsium salisburgense, commonly known as the Salzburg thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It has a robust root system and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun.
