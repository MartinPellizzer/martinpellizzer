Croton salviifolius

Croton salviifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
