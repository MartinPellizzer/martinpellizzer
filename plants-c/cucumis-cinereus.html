Cucumis cinereus

Cucumis cinereus, commonly known as the silver cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, pale green fruits that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The silver cucumber is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to adapt to harsh desert conditions.
