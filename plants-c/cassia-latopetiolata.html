Cassia latopetiolata

Cassia latopetiolata, commonly known as the broad-leaved cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a deep green color. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the legume family. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia latopetiolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
