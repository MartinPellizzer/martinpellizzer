Cyrtochilum lehmannii

Cyrtochilum lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained soil, often found in cloud forests.

Cyrtochilum lehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
