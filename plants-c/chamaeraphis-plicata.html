Chamaeraphis plicata

Chamaeraphis plicata, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is characterized by its distinctive zigzag or wavy leaf margins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. It is well adapted to arid environments and has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Chamaeraphis plicata is also known for its use in traditional herbal medicine and as a decorative plant in dryland gardens.
