Caragana brevifolia

Caragana brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved shrub vetch, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia and parts of Russia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and its yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caragana brevifolia is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh landscapes.
