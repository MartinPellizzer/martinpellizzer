Calochilus imberbis

Calochilus imberbis, commonly known as the hairless spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, featuring a rhizome and a rosette of linear leaves. The plant produces a single, slender flower spike bearing a single, showy orchid flower with a distinctive labellum. It blooms during the cooler months, typically between July and September.

Calochilus imberbis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and delicate appearance.
