Cassia brevifolia var. minutula

Cassia brevifolia var. minutula, commonly known as dwarf cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, papery flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a slender, elongated pod that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its compact growth habit.
