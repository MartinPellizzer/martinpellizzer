Curcuma kurzii

Curcuma kurzii, commonly known as the Khasi turmeric, is a rhizomatous plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange rhizomes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a distinctive, narrow bract.

The rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional herbal remedies.
