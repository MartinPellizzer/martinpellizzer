Chrysocoma phosphorica

Chrysocoma phosphorica, commonly known as the phosphorescent chrysocoma, is a unique plant species characterized by its bioluminescent properties.

Native to specific regions in South Africa, it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant's leaves and stems emit a faint blue-green glow, which is attributed to the presence of luciferin and luciferase compounds. This bioluminescence is most noticeable at night, making it a rare and fascinating botanical phenomenon.

Its limited distribution and specialized habitat contribute to its status as an endangered species.
