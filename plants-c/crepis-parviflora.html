Crepis parviflora

Crepis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and the flowers are yellow with prominent central cones.

Crepis parviflora is often found in open habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
