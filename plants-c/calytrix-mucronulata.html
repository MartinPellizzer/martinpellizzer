Calytrix mucronulata

Calytrix mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calytrix, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and typically appear in clusters, adding ornamental value to the plant.

It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy and resilient species.
