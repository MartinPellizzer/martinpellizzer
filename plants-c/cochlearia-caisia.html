Cochlearia caisia

Cochlearia caisia, commonly known as the sea kale, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in saline or sandy soils, thriving in harsh maritime environments. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Cochlearia caisia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
