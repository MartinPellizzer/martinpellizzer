Cirsium vulgare var. album

Cirsium vulgare var. album, commonly known as the white thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, silver-green leaves with coarse teeth. The plant produces showy white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

The plant is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
