Carduus × erucagineus

Carduus × erucagineus, commonly known as the Scotch thistle, is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
