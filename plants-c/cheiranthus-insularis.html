Cheiranthus insularis

Cheiranthus insularis, commonly known as the island mustards, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It is an annual or biennial species, completing its life cycle within one or two growing seasons.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in certain areas.
