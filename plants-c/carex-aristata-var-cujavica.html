Carex aristata var. cujavica

Carex aristata var. cujavica, commonly known as the Andean sedge, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, typically in shades of brown or purple, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
