Cassia stenosepala

Cassia stenosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled cassia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
