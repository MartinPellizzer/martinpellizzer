Chromolepis heterophylla

Chromolepis heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf chromolepis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the lower Mississippi Valley. The plant is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves that can be either entire or lobed depending on the growth conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.
