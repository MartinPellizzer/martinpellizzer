Caladenia longicauda var. eminens

Caladenia longicauda var. eminens, commonly known as the tall spider orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to south-western Australia.

This variant is characterized by its elongated, slender stem and showy, spider-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces a single, elongated flower spike with several tubular, spurred flowers that are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
