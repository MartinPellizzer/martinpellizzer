Chenopodium berlandieri f. subhastatum

Chenopodium berlandieri f. subhastatum, commonly known as buffalo bur, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, prickly leaves and spherical, spiny fruit heads that resemble small burs. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and prairie habitats. Its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces small greenish-white flowers in clusters.

The species is known for its adaptability and resilience, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural environments.
