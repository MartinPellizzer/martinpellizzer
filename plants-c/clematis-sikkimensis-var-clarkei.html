Clematis sikkimensis var. clarkei

Clematis sikkimensis var. clarkei, commonly known as the Sikkim Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It features slender, twining stems and heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, often with a distinctive red or purple calyx. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
