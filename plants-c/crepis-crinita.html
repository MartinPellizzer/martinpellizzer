Crepis crinita

Crepis crinita, commonly known as the hairy crabgrass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces small, yellow flower heads that are surrounded by pale green bracts. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Crepis crinita is an annual plant that thrives in temperate climates and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
