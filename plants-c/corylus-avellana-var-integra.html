Corylus avellana var. integra

Corylus avellana var. integra, commonly known as the European hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray-barked stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces catkin-like male flowers and female flowers that develop into edible hazelnuts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or hedgerows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting wildlife and traditional agriculture.
