Corothamnus glaber

Corothamnus glaber, commonly known as the naked thornstep, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
