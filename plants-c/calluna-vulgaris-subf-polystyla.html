Calluna vulgaris subf. polystyla

Calluna vulgaris subf.

polystyla, commonly known as the Scottish heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the British Isles. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are evergreen throughout the year.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
