Christella sumatrana

Christella sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran Christella, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Sumatra.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
