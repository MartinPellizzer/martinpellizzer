Cirsium japonicum

Cirsium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese thistle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features spiny, gray-green leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers in late summer. The plant is characterized by its tall, upright stems and is often found in meadows and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flowers.

Cirsium japonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
