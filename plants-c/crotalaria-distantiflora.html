Crotalaria distantiflora

Crotalaria distantiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with prominent veining. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and potentially useful in agricultural settings.
