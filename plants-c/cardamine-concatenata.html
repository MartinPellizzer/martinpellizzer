Cardamine concatenata

Cardamine concatenata, commonly known as the clustered water crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white flowers are arranged in a loose cluster and bloom in early spring.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and preference for wet, calcareous soils.
