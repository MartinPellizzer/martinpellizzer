Campanula morettiana

Campanula morettiana, commonly known as the Morettiana bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence.

Campanula morettiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
