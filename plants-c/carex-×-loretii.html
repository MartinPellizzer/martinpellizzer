Carex × loretii

Carex × loretii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carex lyngbyei and Carex morvarica, characterized by its robust growth and dense foliage. The plant typically forms clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often green to blue-green in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, making it a common sight in marshy areas and along water edges.

Carex × loretii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various small organisms.
