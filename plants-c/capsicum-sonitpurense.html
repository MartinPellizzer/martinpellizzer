Capsicum sonitpurense

Capsicum sonitpurense, commonly known as the Sonitpurense chili.

It is a species of Capsicum native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is a herbaceous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellow flowers that develop into red, elongated fruits.

The fruits are used in local cuisines and have a mild to medium heat level, making them a valued crop in traditional agriculture.
