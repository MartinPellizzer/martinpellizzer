Carex crassinervia

Carex crassinervia, commonly known as broad-veined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces three-sided, triangular seeds.

Carex crassinervia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
