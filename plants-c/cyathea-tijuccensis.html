Cyathea tijuccensis

Cyathea tijuccensis, commonly known as the Tijuca hakea, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize the soil, making it an important component of its ecosystem.

Cyathea tijuccensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
