Cedrela macrocarpa

Cedrela macrocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and smooth, pale to reddish-brown bark. The tree produces large, showy yellow flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing one or two seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic with a leathery texture.

Cedrela macrocarpa is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
