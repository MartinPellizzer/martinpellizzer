Croton commiphoroides

Croton commiphoroides, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, often hairy leaves and showy, yellow or orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
