Cucurbita paradoxa

Cucurbita paradoxa, commonly known as the puzzling gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, warty, and often irregularly shaped fruits. The plant produces broad, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its vines can grow up to 10 meters long, and the fruit can reach weights of up to 10 kilograms.

The gourd is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used historically for various practical purposes.
