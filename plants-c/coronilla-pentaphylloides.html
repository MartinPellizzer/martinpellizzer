Coronilla pentaphylloides

Coronilla pentaphylloides, commonly known as the five-leafed crownvetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate and pentaphylloid leaves, which consist of three to five leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
