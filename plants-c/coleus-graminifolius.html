Coleus graminifolius

Coleus graminifolius, commonly known as the grass-leaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Coleus graminifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
