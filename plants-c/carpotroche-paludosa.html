Carpotroche paludosa

Carpotroche paludosa, commonly known as the marsh carpotroche, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, branching stems that are covered in small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in late spring to early summer.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
