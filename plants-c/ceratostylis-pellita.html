Ceratostylis pellita

Ceratostylis pellita, commonly known as the pink torch orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a single, erect spike bearing numerous small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and swampy areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, while its leaves are narrow and strap-like.

The orchid's flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, making it a notable species in its native habitat.
