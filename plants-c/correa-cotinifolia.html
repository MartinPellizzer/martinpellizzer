Correa cotinifolia

Correa cotinifolia, commonly known as the silky correa, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
