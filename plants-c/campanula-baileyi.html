Campanula baileyi

Campanula baileyi, commonly known as Bailey's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Campanula baileyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
