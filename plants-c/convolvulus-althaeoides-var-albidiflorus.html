Convolvulus althaeoides var. albidiflorus

Convolvulus althaeoides var. albidiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
