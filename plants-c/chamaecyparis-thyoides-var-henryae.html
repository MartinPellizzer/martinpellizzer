Chamaecyparis thyoides var. henryae

Chamaecyparis thyoides var. henryae, commonly known as the Chinese white cedar, is a rare and ornamental conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are a striking silver-blue color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for specific horticultural conditions.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
