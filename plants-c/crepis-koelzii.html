Crepis koelzii

Crepis koelzii, commonly known as the Koelz's hawk's-beard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in a single, showy head.

Crepis koelzii is noted for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy species in its native range.
