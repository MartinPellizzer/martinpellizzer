Carex umbrosa subsp. sabynensis

Carex umbrosa subsp.

sabynensis, commonly known as the Sabyn's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate periodic flooding and poor soil conditions.

Botanically, it is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.
