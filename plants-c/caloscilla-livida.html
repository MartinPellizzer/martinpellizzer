Caloscilla livida

Caloscilla livida, commonly known as the pale bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Caloscilla livida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
