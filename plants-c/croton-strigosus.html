Croton strigosus

Croton strigosus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and striking, often colorful, bracts that resemble flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough, textured surface.

Croton strigosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical and subtropical gardens.
