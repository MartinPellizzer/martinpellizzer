Carex glandulifolia

Carex glandulifolia, commonly known as the glandular sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Carex glandulifolia is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse ecosystems.
