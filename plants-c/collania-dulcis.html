Collania dulcis

Collania dulcis, commonly known as the sweetleaf or sweetbush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. Its flowers are typically borne in terminal panicles, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Collania dulcis is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and mild, sweet scent.
