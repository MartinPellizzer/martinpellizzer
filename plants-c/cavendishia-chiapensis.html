Cavendishia chiapensis

Cavendishia chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas Cavendish, is a rare and striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Chiapas, Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescence that can display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and often have a waxy coating to help retain moisture.

Cavendishia chiapensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific environmental requirements.
