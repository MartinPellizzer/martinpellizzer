Croton rarus

Croton rarus, commonly known as the rare croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage, which often displays shades of yellow, red, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
