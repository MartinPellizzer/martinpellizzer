Chamaemelum inodorum

Chamaemelum inodorum, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny white or pink flowers. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Chamaemelum inodorum is also a popular garden plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
