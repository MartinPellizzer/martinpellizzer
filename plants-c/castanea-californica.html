Castanea californica

Castanea californica, commonly known as the California black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Fagaceae, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spiny capsules containing one or two nuts. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing habitat for various insects and animals.

The nuts are rich in oils and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicine.
