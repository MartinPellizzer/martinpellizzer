Catha crenata

Catha crenata, commonly known as khat, is a flowering plant native to the horn of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and gourds, though it is more closely related to the genus Catha. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth, with serrated, lobed leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a slender, green pod containing small, white seeds.

The plant is cultivated for its leaves, which contain stimulant compounds like cathinone and cathine, making it a traditional stimulant in certain cultures.
