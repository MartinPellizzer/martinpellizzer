Cardamine heyneana

Cardamine heyneana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are often submerged or partially submerged, adapting to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Cardamine heyneana is noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, wet soils.
