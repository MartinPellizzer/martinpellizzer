Chamaecerasus albertii

Chamaecerasus albertii, commonly known as the Albert's snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white, fragrant flowers that appear in early spring, giving it a snowball-like appearance. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, and it produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Chamaecerasus albertii is also valued for its ability to tolerate cold climates and partial shade.
