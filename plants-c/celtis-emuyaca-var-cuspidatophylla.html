Celtis emuyaca var. cuspidatophylla

Celtis emuyaca var. cuspidatophylla, commonly known as the cuspidate-leaf hackberry, is a variant of the hackberry tree native to parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are elliptical with a pointed tip, and they are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, dark blue berries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
