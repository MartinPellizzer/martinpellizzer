Carex esquiroliana

Carex esquiroliana, commonly known as the Esquiroli's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream sides. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, typical of sedges. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.

Carex esquiroliana is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
