Cycas javana

Cycas javana, commonly known as the Java cycad, is a perennial evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with the male cones being yellow and the female cones dark purple. Cycas javana is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
