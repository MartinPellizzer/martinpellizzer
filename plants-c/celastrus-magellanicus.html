Celastrus magellanicus

Celastrus magellanicus, commonly known as the Magellan's staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
