Centrosema arenarium f. glabrescens

Centrosema arenarium f. glabrescens, commonly known as the coastal centrosema, is a variant of the species native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. It plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and preventing erosion due to its extensive root system.

Additionally, it is a valuable forage plant for livestock in certain regions.
