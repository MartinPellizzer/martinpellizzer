Cerastium cerastoides f. glandulosum

Cerastium cerastoides f. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rock crevices.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in temperate regions of Europe and parts of North America.
