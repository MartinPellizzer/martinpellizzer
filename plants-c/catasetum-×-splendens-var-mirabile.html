Catasetum × splendens var. mirabile

Catasetum × splendens var. mirabile, commonly known as the magnificent orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear vibrant, often colorful flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are notable for their unique structure, including specialized pollination mechanisms that attract specific insect pollinators.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
