Caccinia crassifolia f. persica

Caccinia crassifolia f. persica, commonly known as Persian thickleaf, is a shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It features dense, thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
