Crocus oreogenus

Crocus oreogenus, commonly known as the mountain crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming period, often emerging in late winter or early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and purple, which are typically borne on tall, slender stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

The mountain crocus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
