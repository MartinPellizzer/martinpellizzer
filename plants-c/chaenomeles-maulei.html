Chaenomeles maulei

Chaenomeles maulei, commonly known as the Maule cherry, is a species of flowering plant native to Chile.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy pink to red flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to cherries but are not widely cultivated for consumption. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are simple and alternate, with a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
