Carpinus fangiana

Carpinus fangiana, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The wood of Carpinus fangiana is hard and durable, making it valuable for carpentry and furniture making.
