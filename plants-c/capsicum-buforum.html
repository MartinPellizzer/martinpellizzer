Capsicum buforum

Capsicum buforum, commonly known as the wild pepper or bird pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its small, bright red or yellow fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white and are followed by the distinctive peppery fruits.

Capsicum buforum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for use in traditional medicine.
