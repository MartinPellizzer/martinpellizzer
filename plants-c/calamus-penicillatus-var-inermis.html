Calamus penicillatus var. inermis

Calamus penicillatus var. inermis, commonly known as the unarmed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, elongated leaves that are often green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family, and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is native to Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.

Due to its delicate structure, it requires careful cultivation to thrive in garden settings.
