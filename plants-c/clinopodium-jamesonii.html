Clinopodium jamesonii

Clinopodium jamesonii, commonly known as Jameson's mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and square stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong mint-like fragrance, while its small purple flowers bloom in summer.

Clinopodium jamesonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
