Canna gaboniensis

Canna gaboniensis, commonly known as the African canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, resembling those of the ginger family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Canna gaboniensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
