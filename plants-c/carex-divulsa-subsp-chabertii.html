Carex divulsa subsp. chabertii

Carex divulsa subsp.

chabertii, commonly known as the Chabert's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which are typical features of sedges.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in wet or semi-wet conditions.
