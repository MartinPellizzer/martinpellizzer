Carex longibracteata

Carex longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex longibracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
