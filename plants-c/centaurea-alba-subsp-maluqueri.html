Centaurea alba subsp. maluqueri

Centaurea alba subsp.

maluqueri, commonly known as the White Cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant produces achene fruits with pappus hairs, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in Mediterranean flora.
