Centaurea alba subsp. ciliata

Centaurea alba subsp.

ciliata, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is often associated with open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
