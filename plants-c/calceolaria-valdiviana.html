Calceolaria valdiviana

Calceolaria valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the temperate rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, which help retain moisture.

Calceolaria valdiviana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
