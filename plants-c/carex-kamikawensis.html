Carex kamikawensis

Carex kamikawensis, commonly known as the Kamikawa sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The flowering stems produce small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typical of the sedge family.
