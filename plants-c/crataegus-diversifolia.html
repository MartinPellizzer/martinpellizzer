Crataegus diversifolia

Crataegus diversifolia, commonly known as the variable hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that vary in shape and size, often with serrated edges. The plant produces white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Crataegus diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
