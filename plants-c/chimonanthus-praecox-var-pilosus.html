Chimonanthus praecox var. pilosus

Chimonanthus praecox var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy witch hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the stems are covered in fine, hairy pubescence. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blossoms and autumn foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
