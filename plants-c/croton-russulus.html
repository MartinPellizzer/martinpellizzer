Croton russulus

Croton russulus, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a small shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, palmate leaves with deep green veins and a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage, which can vary in color depending on light conditions.
