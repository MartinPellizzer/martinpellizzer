Cyathea munita

Cyathea munita, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.

Cyathea munita thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
