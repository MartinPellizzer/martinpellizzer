Centaurea spinulosa subsp. adpressa

Centaurea spinulosa subsp.

adpressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, with ray florets and a distinctive central disk.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
