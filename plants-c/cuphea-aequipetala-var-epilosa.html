Cuphea aequipetala var. epilosa

Cuphea aequipetala var. epilosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are pollinated by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species for attracting pollinators.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
