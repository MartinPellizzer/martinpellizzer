Campylocentrum multiflorum

Campylocentrum multiflorum, commonly known as the clustered starflower, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are typically evergreen, leathery, and arranged in a compact, rounded shape.

Campylocentrum multiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
