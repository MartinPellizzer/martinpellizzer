Carex clavata subsp. tritacea

Carex clavata subsp.

tritacea, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

The subspecies tritacea is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and habitat preferences compared to the main species.
