Clematis recta subsp. nannophylla

Clematis recta subsp.

nannophylla, commonly known as the small-leaf clematis, is a perennial vine native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 5 meters.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets.

This species prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for garden cultivation.
