Calamaria melanopoda

Calamaria melanopoda, commonly known as the black-leaved calamaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calamaria melanopoda is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
