Cestrum elegans var. smithii

Cestrum elegans var. smithii, commonly known as the Sweet Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 6 meters in height, with opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.
