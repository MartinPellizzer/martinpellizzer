Centaurea ochrolepis

Centaurea ochrolepis, commonly known as the yellow-centred knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its bright yellow flowers are surrounded by a distinctive reddish or purple bract, giving it its common name.

Centaurea ochrolepis is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soils.
