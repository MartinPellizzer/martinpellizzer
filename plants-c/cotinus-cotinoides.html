Cotinus cotinoides

Cotinus cotinoides, commonly known as the American smoke bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its feathery, pinkish to reddish inflorescences that resemble smoke, which appear in late summer. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite and often have a serrated margin. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cotinus cotinoides is valued for its ornamental value and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
