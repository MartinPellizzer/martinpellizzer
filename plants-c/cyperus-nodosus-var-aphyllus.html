Cyperus nodosus var. aphyllus

Cyperus nodosus var. aphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and often reduced, giving it a distinctive appearance compared to other Cyperus species.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering structures typical of sedges.
