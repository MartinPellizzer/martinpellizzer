Cachrys morisonii

Cachrys morisonii, commonly known as the Morrison's thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Cachrys morisonii thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
