Calamus inflatus

Calamus inflatus, commonly known as the inflated calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark brown, elongated fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or marshy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
