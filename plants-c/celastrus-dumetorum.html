Celastrus dumetorum

Celastrus dumetorum, commonly known as the Indian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia.

It is an annual herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and agricultural fields. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in umbels. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
