Cyanotis sericea

Cyanotis sericea, commonly known as silverleaf waterplantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically in clusters at the base of the foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as along streams or in damp woodlands.

Cyanotis sericea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
