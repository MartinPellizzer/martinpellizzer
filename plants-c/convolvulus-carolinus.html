Convolvulus carolinus

Convolvulus carolinus, commonly known as Carolina morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems that produce vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and woodland edges.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it both a weed and a decorative plant.
