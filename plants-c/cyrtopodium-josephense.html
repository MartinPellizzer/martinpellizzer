Cyrtopodium josephense

Cyrtopodium josephense, commonly known as the Joseph's coryphoe, is a species of orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellow and green coloration. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, thriving in cool, humid environments with well-draining soil. Its pseudobulbs store water, allowing it to survive in fluctuating environmental conditions.

Cyrtopodium josephense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
