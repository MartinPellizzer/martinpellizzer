Campanula patula f. bicolor

Campanula patula f. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored bellflower, is a cultivar of the Campanula genus native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive bicolor flowers, typically featuring a white throat and blue or violet petals. The plant is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. It produces upright, branching stems with lance-shaped leaves and blooms from late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
