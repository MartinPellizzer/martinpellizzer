Centaurea montana subsp. variegata

Centaurea montana subsp.

variegata, commonly known as the Alpine cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and serrated leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in cooler climates.
