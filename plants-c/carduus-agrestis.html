Carduus agrestis

Carduus agrestis, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual or biennial herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are covered in hooked bracts, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.

The species is known for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
