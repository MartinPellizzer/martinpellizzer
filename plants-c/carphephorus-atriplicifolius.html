Carphephorus atriplicifolius

Carphephorus atriplicifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowleaf, is a tropical grass species native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves, which are typically broad and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often adorned with pale blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
