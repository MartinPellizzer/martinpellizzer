Carex rariflora f. baeostachya

Carex rariflora f. baeostachya, commonly known as the sparse-stamen sedge, is a variant of the rare-flower sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
