Clethra punctata

Clethra punctata, commonly known as the spotted clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are elliptical, serrated, and turn reddish in the fall. The plant prefers moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
