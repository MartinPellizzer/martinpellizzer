Cybianthus occigranatensis

Cybianthus occigranatensis, commonly known as the granate cybianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive red-orange center, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
