Cichorium endivia subsp. divaricatum

Cichorium endivia subsp.

divaricatum, commonly known as Italian chicory, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of dark green, spoon-shaped leaves.

The plant produces blue or purple flowers in the second year, which are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are typically harvested for culinary use, either raw or cooked, and are known for their slightly bitter taste.

The plant thrives in cool climates and prefers well-drained soil.
