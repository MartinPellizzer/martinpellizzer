Conomitra linearis

Conomitra linearis, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It possesses small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic prey such as water fleas and small crustaceans. The plant's leaves are long and ribbon-like, adapted for both photosynthesis and prey capture. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with abundant sunlight.

Its unique morphology and predatory behavior make it an intriguing subject in botanical studies.
