Cassebeeria parviflora

Cassebeeria parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of tiny yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
