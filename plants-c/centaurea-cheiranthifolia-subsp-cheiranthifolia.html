Centaurea cheiranthifolia subsp. cheiranthifolia

Centaurea cheiranthifolia subsp.

cheiranthifolia, commonly known as the rock centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiny seed pods. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
