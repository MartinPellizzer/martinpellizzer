Conyza laxa

Conyza laxa, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually hairy and lance-shaped. Conyza laxa is known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which allows it to spread easily and often makes it a weed in agricultural areas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
