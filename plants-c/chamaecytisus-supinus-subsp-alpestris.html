Chamaecytisus supinus subsp. alpestris

Chamaecytisus supinus subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine broom, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
