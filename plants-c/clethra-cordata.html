Clethra cordata

Clethra cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet pepper, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces small, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in pendulous inflorescences. These flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often grown in gardens for its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
