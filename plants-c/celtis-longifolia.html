Celtis longifolia

Celtis longifolia, commonly known as the European hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by small, round, greenish fruits that ripen to a dark blue or black color. It is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and along streams, thriving in a variety of soil types.

Celtis longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat and food for various wildlife species.
