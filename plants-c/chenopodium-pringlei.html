Chenopodium pringlei

Chenopodium pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.

Chenopodium pringlei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
