Ceropegia attenuata

Ceropegia attenuata, commonly known as the string of hearts, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features trailing stems with heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or pink markings. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which are pollinated by specific flies. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ceropegia attenuata is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
