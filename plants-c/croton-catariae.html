Croton catariae

Croton catariae, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that resemble the shape of a goat's beard, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for Mediterranean climates.

Croton catariae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
