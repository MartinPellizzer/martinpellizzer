Caiophora scandens var. orientalis

Caiophora scandens var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental climbing caiophora, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant, decorative plants.
