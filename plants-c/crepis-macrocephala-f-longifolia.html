Crepis macrocephala f. longifolia

Crepis macrocephala f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, silver-gray leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single head. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
