Cupamenis chamedrifolia

Cupamenis chamedrifolia, commonly known as the creeping woodruff, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

Cupamenis chamedrifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
