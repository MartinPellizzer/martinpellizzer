Carex aterrima subsp. medwedewii

Carex aterrima subsp.

medwedewii, commonly known as the dark sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is typically found in wet or moist environments such as bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
