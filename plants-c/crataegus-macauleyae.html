Crataegus macauleyae

Crataegus macauleyae, commonly known as the Macauley hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, specifically found in Georgia and Florida. This shrub or small tree typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.
