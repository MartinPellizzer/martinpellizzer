Crotalaria incana var. nicaraguensis

Crotalaria incana var. nicaraguensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive bean-like pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This species is also noted for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
