Coccyganthe subintegra

Coccyganthe subintegra, commonly known as the Cape ironwood, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its thick, waxy leaves. It produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
