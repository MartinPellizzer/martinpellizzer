Claytonia scammaniana

Claytonia scammaniana, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, showy flowers in shades of red and pink. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, white coating that gives them a silvery appearance.

The scarlet pimpernel is also known for its rapid blooming cycle, with flowers opening in the morning and closing by midday.
