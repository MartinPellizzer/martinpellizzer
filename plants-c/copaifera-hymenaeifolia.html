Copaifera hymenaeifolia

Copaifera hymenaeifolia, commonly known as the yellow copaiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow bark and large, elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, elongated capsules containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also recognized for its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
