Campanula expansa

Campanula expansa, commonly known as the broad bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula expansa is known for its resilience and ability to bloom throughout the summer months.
