Centaurea aspromontana

Centaurea aspromontana, commonly known as the Aspromontana thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the daisy family.

Centaurea aspromontana is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
