Cousinia czerniakowskae

Cousinia czerniakowskae, commonly known as the Czerniakowski's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. It produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across its native habitat.

Cousinia czerniakowskae is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its region.
