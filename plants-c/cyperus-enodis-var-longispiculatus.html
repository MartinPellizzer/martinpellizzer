Cyperus enodis var. longispiculatus

Cyperus enodis var. longispiculatus, commonly known as the slender spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often elongated and spiky, giving it its common name.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
