Capraria integerrima

Capraria integerrima, commonly known as the Cape rose or white sweetpea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Capraria integerrima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
