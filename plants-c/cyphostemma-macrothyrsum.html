Cyphostemma macrothyrsum

Cyphostemma macrothyrsum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
