Cheirostylis sarcopus

Cheirostylis sarcopus, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and a unique, spurred lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often producing a fragrant scent that attracts specific pollinators.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
