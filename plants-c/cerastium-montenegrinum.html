Cerastium montenegrinum

Cerastium montenegrinum, commonly known as the Montenegro chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and often covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

Cerastium montenegrinum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.
