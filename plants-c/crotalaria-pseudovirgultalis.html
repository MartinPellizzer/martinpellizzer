Crotalaria pseudovirgultalis

Crotalaria pseudovirgultalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
