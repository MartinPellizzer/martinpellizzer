Ceropegia ciliata

Ceropegia ciliata, commonly known as the Woolly Ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, fleshy leaves covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are often pale green or white, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
