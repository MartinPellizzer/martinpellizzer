Chaptalia cordata var. ferrugineo-tomentosa

Chaptalia cordata var. ferrugineo-tomentosa, commonly known as the rusty-tomentose cordate chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or open woodlands.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying plant adaptation and biodiversity.
