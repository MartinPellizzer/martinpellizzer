Cotula microcephala

Cotula microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Cotula microcephala is also a food source for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
