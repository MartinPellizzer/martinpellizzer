Crucifera columnae

Crucifera columnae, commonly known as the columnar crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit, which distinguishes it from other members of the crucifer group. The plant produces small, four-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Crucifera columnae is native to specific regions in South America and is adapted to grow in rocky or arid environments.
