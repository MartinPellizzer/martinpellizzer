Carex dispalata

Carex dispalata, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to certain regions of North America, it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
