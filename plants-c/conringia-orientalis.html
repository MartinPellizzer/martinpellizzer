Conringia orientalis

Conringia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Central Asia and parts of Europe, often found in dry, rocky habitats. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Conringia orientalis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
