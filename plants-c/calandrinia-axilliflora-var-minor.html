Calandrinia axilliflora var. minor

Calandrinia axilliflora var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.
