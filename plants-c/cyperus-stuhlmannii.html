Cyperus stuhlmannii

Cyperus stuhlmannii, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its tall, cylindrical stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green to brown in color.

Cyperus stuhlmannii is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
