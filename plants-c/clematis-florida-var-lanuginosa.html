Clematis florida var. lanuginosa

Clematis florida var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy clematis, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has finely divided, palmately lobed leaves that are covered in a soft, downy texture. Its stems are twining and can grow up to 15 feet long, making it an excellent choice for trellises or fences.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering during the growing season.
