Caragana halodendron

Caragana halodendron, commonly known as the saltbush or halophilous caragana, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its seeds are enclosed in small, woody pods, and the plant is often used for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.

Caragana halodendron is well-suited to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low precipitation.
