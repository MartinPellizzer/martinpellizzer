Cereus sartorianus

Cereus sartorianus, commonly known as the night bloomer or moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and spiny, segmented structure. The plant produces striking white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for succulent collections.
