Cephaelis appendiculata

Cephaelis appendiculata, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
