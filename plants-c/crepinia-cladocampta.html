Crepinia cladocampta

Crepinia cladocampta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces tall, erect stems with showy, fragrant white or pale purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its clustered inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spike. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Crepinia cladocampta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
