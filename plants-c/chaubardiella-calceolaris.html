Chaubardiella calceolaris

Chaubardiella calceolaris, commonly known as the starry bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, star-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

The flowers are usually blue to purple in color, adding ornamental value to alpine gardens.
