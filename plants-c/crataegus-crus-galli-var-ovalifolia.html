Crataegus crus-galli var. ovalifolia

Crataegus crus-galli var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
