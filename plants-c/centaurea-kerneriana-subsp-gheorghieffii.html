Centaurea kerneriana subsp. gheorghieffii

Centaurea kerneriana subsp.

gheorghieffii, commonly known as the Transylvanian star-thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a robust taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
