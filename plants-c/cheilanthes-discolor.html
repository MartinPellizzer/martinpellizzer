Cheilanthes discolor

Cheilanthes discolor, commonly known as the pale fern, is a species of rock fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green fronds that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and in dry, well-drained soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the undersides of the leaves, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Cheilanthes discolor is notable for its ability to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
