Carex mackenziana

Carex mackenziana, commonly known as Mackenzie's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, sword-shaped, and often have a distinctive blue-green color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.
