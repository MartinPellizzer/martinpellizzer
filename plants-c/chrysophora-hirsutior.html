Chrysophora hirsutior

Chrysophora hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated for both horticultural and traditional purposes.
