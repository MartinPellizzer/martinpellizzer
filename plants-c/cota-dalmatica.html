Cota dalmatica

Cota dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cota dalmatica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
