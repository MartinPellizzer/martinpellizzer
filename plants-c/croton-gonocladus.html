Croton gonocladus

Croton gonocladus, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from bright red to orange and yellow. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are often lobed or serrated. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Croton gonocladus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
