Cryptandra tridentata

Cryptandra tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed cryptandra, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Cryptandra tridentata is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
