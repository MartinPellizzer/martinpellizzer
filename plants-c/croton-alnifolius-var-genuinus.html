Croton alnifolius var. genuinus

Croton alnifolius var. genuinus, commonly known as the Indian croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often displaying a striking combination of green, yellow, and red hues. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
