Campyloneurum chrysopodum

Campyloneurum chrysopodum, commonly known as the golden flax fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The botanical features of this fern make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
