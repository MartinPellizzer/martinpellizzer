Cyperus biceps

Cyperus biceps, commonly known as the two-headed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense clumps near water bodies. Cyperus biceps produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green or brown, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
