Cattleya × albanensis

Cattleya × albanensis, commonly known as the Albanian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as pink, red, and white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom in clusters, making it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.

Cattleya × albanensis thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
