Cassia leschenaultiana var. wallichiana

Cassia leschenaultiana var. wallichiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from April to June. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
