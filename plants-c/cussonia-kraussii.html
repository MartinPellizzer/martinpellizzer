Cussonia kraussii

Cussonia kraussii, commonly known as the Natal arrowroot, is a striking evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its value in both horticulture and ecological settings.
