Carex testacea

Carex testacea, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers, typical of many sedges. Carex testacea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
