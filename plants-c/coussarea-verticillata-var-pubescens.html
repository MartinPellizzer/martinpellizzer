Coussarea verticillata var. pubescens

Coussarea verticillata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy coussarea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered with fine pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
