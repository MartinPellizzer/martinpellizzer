Cerasus × furuseana

Cerasus × furuseana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese apricot, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly for its showy spring blossoms. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, edible fruits that are similar to cherries.

The species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
