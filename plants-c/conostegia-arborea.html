Conostegia arborea

Conostegia arborea, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
