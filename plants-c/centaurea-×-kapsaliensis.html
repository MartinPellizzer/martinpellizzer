Centaurea × kapsaliensis

Centaurea × kapsaliensis, commonly known as the Kapsali hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
