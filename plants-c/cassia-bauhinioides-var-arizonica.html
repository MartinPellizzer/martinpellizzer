Cassia bauhinioides var. arizonica

Cassia bauhinioides var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona false acacia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, and it produces bright yellow, fluffy flower clusters in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Its seeds are encased in flat, leathery pods that split open when mature.
