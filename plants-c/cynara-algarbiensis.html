Cynara algarbiensis

Cynara algarbiensis, commonly known as the Algarbi thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisine. Its yellow flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cynara algarbiensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in arid environments.
