Campanula legionensis

Campanula legionensis, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Campanula legionensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
