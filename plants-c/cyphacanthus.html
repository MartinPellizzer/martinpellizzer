Cyphacanthus

Cyphacanthus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves and showy, long-lasting flowers that come in a variety of colors. Cyphacanthus species are typically low-growing and thrive in humid, warm environments. They are popular as houseplants due to their ease of care and ornamental value.

The genus includes several species, with Cyphacanthus tetragonus being one of the most commonly cultivated.
