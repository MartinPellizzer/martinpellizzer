Clinanthus incarnatus

Clinanthus incarnatus, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red petals that resemble small bells, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Clinanthus incarnatus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
