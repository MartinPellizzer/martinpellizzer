Chelone glabra f. tomentosa

Chelone glabra f. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly white hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features striking, showy flowers that range from white to pale pink, with a prominent central pistil and five reflexed petals. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
