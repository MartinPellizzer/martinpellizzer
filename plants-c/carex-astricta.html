Carex astricta

Carex astricta, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense, cylindrical spikelets, which are clustered at the top of the plant.

Carex astricta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
