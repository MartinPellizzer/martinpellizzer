Cyperus exaltatus var. amoenus

Cyperus exaltatus var. amoenus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often adorned with clusters of small, grass-like leaves. The plant produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, branched inflorescences. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in wetlands, ponds, and along waterways.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and landscape designs.
