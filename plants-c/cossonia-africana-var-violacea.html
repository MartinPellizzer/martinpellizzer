Cossonia africana var. violacea

Cossonia africana var. violacea, commonly known as the purple cossonia, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant produces showy, pale violet to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in Mediterranean climates.

Its unique foliage and floral display make it a popular choice for both landscaping and cut flower arrangements.
