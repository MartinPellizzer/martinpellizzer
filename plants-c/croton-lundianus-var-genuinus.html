Croton lundianus var. genuinus

Croton lundianus var. genuinus, commonly known as the East African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that often display shades of red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant in some regions.
