Centaurea kalambakensis

Centaurea kalambakensis, commonly known as the Kalambaken star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Kalambaken region of Greece.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has spiny leaves and a compact growth habit, making it easily distinguishable from other thistle species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species, highlighting its ecological significance.
