Chamaejasme stachyoides

Chamaejasme stachyoides, commonly known as the silverleaf chamaejasme, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its succulent leaves are adapted for water storage, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Chamaejasme stachyoides is often found in rocky soils and is a common component of desert and chaparral ecosystems.
