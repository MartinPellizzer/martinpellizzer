Cyclamen coum

Cyclamen coum, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive harsh winters.

Cyclamen coum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
