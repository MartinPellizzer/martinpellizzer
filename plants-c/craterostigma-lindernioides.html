Craterostigma lindernioides

Craterostigma lindernioides, commonly known as the mountain rockrose, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the rocky slopes of southern Africa.

It features dense, compact growth with small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers, often in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom in spring. It is well adapted to arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and succulent-like foliage.

Its unique morphology and hardiness make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
