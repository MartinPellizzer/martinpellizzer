Cacalia rubescens

Cacalia rubescens, commonly known as the redstem beggarticks, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, ditches, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cacalia rubescens is known for its ability to spread rapidly through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.
