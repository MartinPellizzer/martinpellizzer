Canna meridensis

Canna meridensis, commonly known as the Mexican canna, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cannaceae family and is characterized by its large, colorful, and showy leaves and inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with broad, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. Its flowers are usually orange or red, with a distinctive yellow center, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Canna meridensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to add vibrant color to gardens.
