Conyza cuneifolia

Conyza cuneifolia, commonly known as rough horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers.

Conyza cuneifolia is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
