Cyperus sabulosus

Cyperus sabulosus, commonly known as the sand flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, often in sandy or muddy soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually green or brown in color. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in shallow water or saturated soil conditions.

Cyperus sabulosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
