Chaenorhinum rubrifolium var. imintalense

Chaenorhinum rubrifolium var. imintalense, commonly known as the red-leafed horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
