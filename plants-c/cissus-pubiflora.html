Cissus pubiflora

Cissus pubiflora, commonly known as the velvet leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can grow up to several meters in length, allowing it to climb trees and other structures.

Cissus pubiflora is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
