Crepis nemetzii

Crepis nemetzii, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or white.

Crepis nemetzii is often found in high-altitude environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
