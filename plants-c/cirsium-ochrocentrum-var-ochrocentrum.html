Cirsium ochrocentrum var. ochrocentrum

Cirsium ochrocentrum var. ochrocentrum, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
