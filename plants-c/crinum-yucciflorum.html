Crinum yucciflorum

Crinum yucciflorum, commonly known as the Yucca-flowered Crinum, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped white or pink flowers. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Crinum yucciflorum is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its resilience and aesthetic appeal make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural habitats.
