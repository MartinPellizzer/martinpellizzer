Carpinus × hybrida

Carpinus × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid hornbeam, is a deciduous tree that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species, Carpinus betulus and Carpinus caroliniana.

It typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. The tree produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, winged fruits that mature in the fall.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types, making it a versatile choice for ornamental landscaping.
