Centaurea × segobricensis

Centaurea × segobricensis, commonly known as the Segobric wild thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and vibrant purple or pink composite flower heads. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its spread across suitable environments.

It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
