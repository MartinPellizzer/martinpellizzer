Cassia × simplicifolia

Cassia × simplicifolia, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a hybrid plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments, and the flowers are typically fragrant.

Cassia × simplicifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
