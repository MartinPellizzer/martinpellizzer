Canarium zeylanicum

Canarium zeylanicum, commonly known as the candlenut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil, while the resin has been historically used for making candles and varnishes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
