Chloris ridleyi

Chloris ridleyi, commonly known as the ridge grass, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Chloris ridleyi is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
