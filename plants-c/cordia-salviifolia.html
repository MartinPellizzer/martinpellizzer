Cordia salviifolia

Cordia salviifolia, commonly known as the sacred fig or Indian rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common species in urban and rural landscapes.
