Campanula orphanidea

Campanula orphanidea, commonly known as the rare orchid bellflower, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Campanula orphanidea is considered a rare and endangered species.
