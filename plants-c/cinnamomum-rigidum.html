Cinnamomum rigidum

Cinnamomum rigidum, commonly known as the rigid cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries containing seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The species is valued for its essential oils and is often cultivated for commercial use.
