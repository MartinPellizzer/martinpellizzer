Carpopogon

Carpopogon, commonly known as the panic grasses, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in disturbed soils or open habitats. They are characterized by their tufted growth habit and spikelet-based inflorescences. Many species within this genus are used for forage or in ecological restoration projects due to their hardy nature.

Some species also produce edible grains that are utilized in traditional cuisines.
