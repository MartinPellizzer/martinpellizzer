Cissus integrifolia

Cissus integrifolia, commonly known as the diamond vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, diamond-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like fruits. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil and partial shade.

Cissus integrifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying properties.
