Conyza eupatorioides

Conyza eupatorioides, commonly known as whiteleaf ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Conyza eupatorioides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
