Cyphostemma homblei

Cyphostemma homblei, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. It is often found in rocky areas and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.
