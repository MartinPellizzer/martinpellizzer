Chrysanthellum pusillum

Chrysanthellum pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf chrysanthemum, is a small flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 inches, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
