Chamaecrista × blanchetiformis

Chamaecrista × blanchetiformis, commonly known as the hybrid crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, opposite leaflets and its showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion, and it is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.

Its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia makes it beneficial for soil fertility.
