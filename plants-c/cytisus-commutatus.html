Cytisus commutatus

Cytisus commutatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive, feathery appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and dry woodlands.
