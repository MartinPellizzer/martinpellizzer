Chamaecrista monticola

Chamaecrista monticola, commonly known as the mountain burr legume, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Chamaecrista monticola is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
