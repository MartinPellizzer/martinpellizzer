Cactus bolivianus

Cactus bolivianus, commonly known as the Bolivian cactus, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water, which helps it survive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, dry climates. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, appearing during the warmer months.

Cactus bolivianus plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
