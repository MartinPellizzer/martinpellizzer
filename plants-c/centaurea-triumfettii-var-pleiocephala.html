Centaurea triumfettii var. pleiocephala

Centaurea triumfettii var. pleiocephala, commonly known as the showy centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats like grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grass-like appearance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
