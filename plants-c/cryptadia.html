Cryptadia

Cryptadia, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique genus of flowering plants native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink, often appearing as if they are floating in the air. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. Cryptadia species are typically found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops, where they endure harsh environmental conditions.

Due to their rarity and specific habitat requirements, many species of Cryptadia are considered endangered.
