Clappia suaedifolia

Clappia suaedifolia, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its spiny, leaf-like bracts that encase the small, yellow flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and along shorelines. Its hardy nature allows it to withstand harsh maritime conditions.

Clappia suaedifolia is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in stabilizing coastal environments.
