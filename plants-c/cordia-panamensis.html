Cordia panamensis

Cordia panamensis, commonly known as Panama cordia, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.
