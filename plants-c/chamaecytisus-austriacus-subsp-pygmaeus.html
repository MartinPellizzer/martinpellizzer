Chamaecytisus austriacus subsp. pygmaeus

Chamaecytisus austriacus subsp.

pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It features small, bright green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring.

The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in sunny, open habitats. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
