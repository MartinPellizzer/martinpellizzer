Chenopodium incanum var. incanum

Chenopodium incanum var. incanum, commonly known as pigweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped with a rough texture and often have a bluish or grayish hue due to the presence of fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
