Calamus vidalianus

Calamus vidalianus, commonly known as the yellow bamboo or vidalia reed, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are typically a bright green color, and it can grow up to several meters in height. Calamus vidalianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rhizomes are sometimes harvested for their aromatic properties.
