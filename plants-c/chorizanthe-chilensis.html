Chorizanthe chilensis

Chorizanthe chilensis, commonly known as Chilean jointweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, which helps reduce water loss.

Chorizanthe chilensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
