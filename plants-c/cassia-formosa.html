Cassia formosa

Cassia formosa, commonly known as golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves that are pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it produces long, slender pods that contain seeds.

Cassia formosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
