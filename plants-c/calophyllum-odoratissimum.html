Calophyllum odoratissimum

Calophyllum odoratissimum, commonly known as the sweet-scented sandarac tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers that are fragrant and attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the tree is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and incense.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
