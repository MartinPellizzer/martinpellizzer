Cladrastis tashiroi

Cladrastis tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's trumpet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cladrastis tashiroi is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
