Croton caudatus var. harmandii

Croton caudatus var. harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's croton, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a rough, textured surface. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
