Cucumis melo var. ispahanensis

Cucumis melo var. ispahanensis, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon plant characterized by its large, round fruit with a thick, green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to Central Asia, particularly regions around the Caspian Sea. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine, producing yellow flowers that develop into the edible fruit. Its fruit is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications, valued for its high water content and mild flavor.

The variety is well-suited to warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
