Cucurbita pepo var. sphaeroleuca

Cucurbita pepo var. sphaeroleuca, commonly known as the white-fruited pumpkin, is a cultivated variety of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its smooth, light green to white skin and round, spherical shape. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. It typically grows as a trailing or climbing vine with palmately lobed leaves.

The fruit is used in various culinary and ornamental applications due to its unique appearance and mild flavor.
