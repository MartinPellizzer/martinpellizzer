Cavendishia dulcis

Cavendishia dulcis, commonly known as the sweet banana plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a banana flower. The plant produces edible, sweet fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are broad and can reach several feet in length, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cavendishia dulcis thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
