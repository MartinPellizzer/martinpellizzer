Cerastium banaticum var. kellereri

Cerastium banaticum var. kellereri, commonly known as the Kellerer's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy, ovate leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their delicate appearance.
