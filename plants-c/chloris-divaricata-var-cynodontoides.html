Chloris divaricata var. cynodontoides

Chloris divaricata var. cynodontoides, commonly known as the slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in dense spikes, which are typically borne at the base of the plant. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
