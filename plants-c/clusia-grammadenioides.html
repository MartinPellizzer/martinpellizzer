Clusia grammadenioides

Clusia grammadenioides, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Clusia grammadenioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade in landscaping.
