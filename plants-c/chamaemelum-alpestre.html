Chamaemelum alpestre

Chamaemelum alpestre, commonly known as alpine chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and aromatic, finely divided leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a mild, chamomile-like scent.

Chamaemelum alpestre is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
