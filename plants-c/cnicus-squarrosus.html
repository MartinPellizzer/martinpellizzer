Cnicus squarrosus

Cnicus squarrosus, commonly known as the rough hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its stems are erect and covered in stiff, bristly hairs.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.
