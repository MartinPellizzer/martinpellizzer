Cedrela microcarpa var. grandifoliola

Cedrela microcarpa var. grandifoliola, commonly known as the broadleaf cedrela, is a tropical tree species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often glossy and have a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The tree is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
