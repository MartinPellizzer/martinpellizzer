Citrus australasica

Citrus australasica, commonly known as the Australian lemon.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia, characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature. The fruit's peel is thick and contains a high amount of essential oils, contributing to its strong citrus aroma.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic compounds.
