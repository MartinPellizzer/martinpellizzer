Convolvulus ochraceus

Convolvulus ochraceus, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are trailing or ascending.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
