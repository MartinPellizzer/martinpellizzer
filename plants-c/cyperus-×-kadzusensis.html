Cyperus × kadzusensis

Cyperus × kadzusensis, commonly known as the Kadzusensis flatsedge, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and a dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of its flowering stems. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its adaptability, it can become invasive in certain regions, impacting native plant communities.
