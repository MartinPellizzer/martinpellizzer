Coelorachis rugosa

Coelorachis rugosa, commonly known as the rough-hairy orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or near streams. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a spurred lip that attracts pollinators like bees and wasps.

Coelorachis rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting various insect populations.
