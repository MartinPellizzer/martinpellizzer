Cyrilla racemiflora var. racemifera

Cyrilla racemiflora var. racemifera, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal and wetland areas.
