Coronilla varia f. recta

Coronilla varia f. recta, commonly known as the erect form of the variable coronilla, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as a forage crop or ornamental plant.
