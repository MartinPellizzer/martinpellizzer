Convolvulus semenovii

Convolvulus semenovii, commonly known as the Semenov's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.
