Chrysobalanus cuspidatus

Chrysobalanus cuspidatus, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut or cuspidate chrysobalanus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Chrysobalaneceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing single seeds.

It is often found in moist lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
