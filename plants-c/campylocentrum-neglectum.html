Campylocentrum neglectum

Campylocentrum neglectum, commonly known as the wild passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, fragrant flowers that resemble those of the passionfruit. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
