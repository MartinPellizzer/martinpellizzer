Crassula ammophila

Crassula ammophila, commonly known as the sand crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, dense clusters of blue-green leaves. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or summer.

Crassula ammophila is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
