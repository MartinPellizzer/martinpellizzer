Carex barbarae

Carex barbarae, commonly known as the Barbarian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Carex barbarae typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
