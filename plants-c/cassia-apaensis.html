Cassia apaensis

Cassia apaensis, commonly known as the African cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a silky, white coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Cassia apaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
