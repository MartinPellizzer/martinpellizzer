Clematis montevidensis

Clematis montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense Clematis, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are alternate, compound, and typically have three leaflets.

Clematis montevidensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
