Cochlearia polonica

Cochlearia polonica, commonly known as the Polish cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to Europe, particularly found in Poland and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Cochlearia polonica is also known for its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
