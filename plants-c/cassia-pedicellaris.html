Cassia pedicellaris

Cassia pedicellaris, commonly known as the white cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellow or white flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods that split open when mature.

Cassia pedicellaris is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
