Convolvulus kurdistanicus

Convolvulus kurdistanicus, commonly known as Kurdish morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It is a climbing herb that produces vibrant blue or purple flowers, which bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
