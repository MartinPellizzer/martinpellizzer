Crocus mouradi

Crocus mouradi, commonly known as the Mouradi crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iris family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of North Africa, particularly Morocco and Algeria. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive purple or blue tinge. It blooms in early spring, typically between February and April, and is adapted to cold, rocky environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
