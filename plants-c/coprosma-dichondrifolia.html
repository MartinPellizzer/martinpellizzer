Coprosma dichondrifolia

Coprosma dichondrifolia, commonly known as the silver coprosma, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a silvery-gray color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in umbels, which are followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Coprosma dichondrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for landscaping purposes.
