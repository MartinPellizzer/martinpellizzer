Cylindropuntia hypsophila

Cylindropuntia hypsophila, commonly known as the cliff prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in spines and are adapted to store water. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in arid environments.

Cylindropuntia hypsophila plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
