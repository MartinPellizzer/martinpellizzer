Campomanesia glareophila

Campomanesia glareophila, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glossy bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries containing several seeds. Its fruits are edible, with a tart, acidic flavor, and are often consumed fresh or used in local cuisine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
