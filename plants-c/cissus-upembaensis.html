Cissus upembaensis

Cissus upembaensis, commonly known as the Upemba gooseberry, is a climbing vine native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are usually opposite and have a glossy, dark green appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
