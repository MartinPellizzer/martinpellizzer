Condalia spathulata

Condalia spathulata, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of white flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming in spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery or grayish coating. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
