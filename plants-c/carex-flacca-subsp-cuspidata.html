Carex flacca subsp. cuspidata

Carex flacca subsp.

cuspidata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

This subspecies is native to regions of North America, particularly in the eastern United States. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant's rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for various wildlife species.
