Clitoria guianensis var. guianensis

Clitoria guianensis var. guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan butterfly pea, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to blue flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
