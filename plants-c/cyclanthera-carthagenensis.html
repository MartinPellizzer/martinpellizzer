Cyclanthera carthagenensis

Cyclanthera carthagenensis, commonly known as the Carthage cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces striking, pale yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. The plant's fruit is a elongated, spiny capsule that contains edible, fleshy seeds. Cyclanthera carthagenensis is characterized by its pinnate leaves and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
