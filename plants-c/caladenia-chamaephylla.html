Caladenia chamaephylla

Caladenia chamaephylla, commonly known as the small-flowered spider-orchid, is an Australian native orchid belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, intricate flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in heathlands and sandy soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the late winter to early spring, producing pale pink to white blooms.

Caladenia chamaephylla is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological significance in its native habitat.
