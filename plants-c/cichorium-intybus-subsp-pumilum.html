Cichorium intybus subsp. pumilum

Cichorium intybus subsp.

pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf chicory, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are typically lance-shaped and often have a bluish-gray hue.

The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its leaves are edible and can be used in salads or brewed into a coffee-like beverage.
