Crucifera perfoliata

Crucifera perfoliata, commonly known as the perfoliate crucifer, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are often fused around the stem, creating a perfoliate appearance. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers arranged in racemes, typical of the cruciferous family. It is native to various regions in Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, open habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
