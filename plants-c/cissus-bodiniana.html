Cissus bodiniana

Cissus bodiniana, commonly known as the climbing stonecress, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, fleshy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and often have a reddish or purple hue, which can vary depending on light conditions.

Cissus bodiniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid environments.
