Coleus gracilis

Coleus gracilis, commonly known as the slender coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage rather than its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, red, and yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Coleus gracilis prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking visual appeal.
