Conandron ramondioides var. pilosus

Conandron ramondioides var. pilosus, commonly known as the hairy conandron, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-green foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
