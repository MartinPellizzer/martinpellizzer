Celosia dichotoma var. pilosa

Celosia dichotoma var. pilosa, commonly known as the cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, plume-like inflorescences that resemble a rooster's comb, giving it its common name. The plant is an annual herb that grows up to 1 meter tall, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.

The flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow, making it a popular choice for floral arrangements.
