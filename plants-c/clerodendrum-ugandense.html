Clerodendrum ugandense

Clerodendrum ugandense, commonly known as the Uganda glorybower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, forested areas.

It produces small, dark brown fruits that contain several seeds, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
