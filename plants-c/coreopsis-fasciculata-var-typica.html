Coreopsis fasciculata var. typica

Coreopsis fasciculata var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a basal rosette, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
