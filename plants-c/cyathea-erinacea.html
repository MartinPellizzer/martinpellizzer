Cyathea erinacea

Cyathea erinacea, commonly known as the silver tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a thick, fibrous trunk that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Cyathea erinacea plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
