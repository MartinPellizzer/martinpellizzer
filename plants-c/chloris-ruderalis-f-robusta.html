Chloris ruderalis f. robusta

Chloris ruderalis f. robusta, commonly known as the robust annual grass, is a variant of the common dog's-tail grass.

It is characterized by its sturdy, erect growth habit and dense, spike-like inflorescence. This plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a prominent midrib.

It is an annual species that produces small, pale green to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
