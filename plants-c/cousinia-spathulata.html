Cousinia spathulata

Cousinia spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Cousinia spathulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
