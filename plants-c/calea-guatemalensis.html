Calea guatemalensis

Calea guatemalensis, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant, white flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular florets arranged in a composite head. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

Calea guatemalensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
