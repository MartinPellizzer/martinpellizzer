Cerasus variabilis

Cerasus variabilis, commonly known as the variable cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional Chinese medicine, it is often cultivated in gardens and horticultural collections.
