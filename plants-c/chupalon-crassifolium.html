Chupalon crassifolium

Chupalon crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf chupalon, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and for soil stabilization in degraded areas.
