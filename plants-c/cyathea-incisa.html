Cyathea incisa

Cyathea incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and its fronds are sometimes employed in ornamental horticulture.

Cyathea incisa thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.
