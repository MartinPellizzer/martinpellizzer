Clematis intricata var. purpurea

Clematis intricata var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple clematis, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. Its stems are slender and twining, allowing it to climb and trail along supports.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
