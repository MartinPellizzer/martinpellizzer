Calonemorchis haemantha

Calonemorchis haemantha, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and a rosette of linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers arranged in a raceme, with showy, spurred petals and a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
