Cineraria microglossa

Cineraria microglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
