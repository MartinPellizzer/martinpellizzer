Crocus olivieri

Crocus olivieri, commonly known as the olive crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, delicate flowers with yellow petals and a distinctive purple center. It typically blooms in late autumn or early winter, making it one of the earliest flowering plants of the season.

Crocus olivieri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
