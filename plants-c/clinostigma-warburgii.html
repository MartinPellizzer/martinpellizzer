Clinostigma warburgii

Clinostigma warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's clinostigma, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, often in well-drained soils. It is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

Due to its ecological importance and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
