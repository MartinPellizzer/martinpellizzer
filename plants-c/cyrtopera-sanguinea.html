Cyrtopera sanguinea

Cyrtopera sanguinea, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins and its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with a subtle pink or red hue, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Cyrtopera sanguinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
