Carex sacrosancta var. tamakii

Carex sacrosancta var. tamakii, commonly known as the sacred sedge, is a perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown, depending on the season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
