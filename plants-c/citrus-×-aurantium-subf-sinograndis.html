Citrus × aurantium subf. sinograndis

Citrus × aurantium subf.

sinograndis, commonly known as the Chinese pomelo or Shaddock, is a hybrid citrus species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruits that can weigh up to 10 kilograms.

The plant is a small to medium-sized tree with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is typically harvested when fully ripe, and it is valued for both its culinary and medicinal uses.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
