Carex asa-grayi

Carex asa-grayi, commonly known as Gray's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to the western United States, it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or mountainous areas. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, which develop into three-angled, three-rowed berries.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
