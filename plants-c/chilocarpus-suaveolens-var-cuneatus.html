Chilocarpus suaveolens var. cuneatus

Chilocarpus suaveolens var. cuneatus, commonly known as the sweet-smelling holly.

It is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe with a smooth, glossy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
