Crataegus parryana

Crataegus parryana, commonly known as Parry hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny branches, simple leaves, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and mammals but generally not for humans.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
