Chamaeraphis italica var. viridis

Chamaeraphis italica var. viridis, commonly known as the green sea oats, is a halophyte plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It has slender, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in coastal areas and estuaries, where it helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate high levels of salt and moisture.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable species for coastal restoration projects.
