Cirsium setidens var. chanroenicum

Cirsium setidens var. chanroenicum, commonly known as the Chanroenic thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
