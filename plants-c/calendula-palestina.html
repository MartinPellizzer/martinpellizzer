Calendula palestina

Calendula palestina, commonly known as the Palestinian marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of Israel and Palestine.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Calendula palestina is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
