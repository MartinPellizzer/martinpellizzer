Chenopodium frigidum

Chenopodium frigidum, commonly known as yellow goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are enclosed in dry, three-lobed fruits. Chenopodium frigidum is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
