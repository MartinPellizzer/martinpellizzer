Convolvulus dasycephalus

Convolvulus dasycephalus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to pink flowers that bloom in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus dasycephalus is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
