Cousinia erivanensis

Cousinia erivanensis, commonly known as the Erivan mountain cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in its dry habitat.

Cousinia erivanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
