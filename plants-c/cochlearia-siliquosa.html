Cochlearia siliquosa

Cochlearia siliquosa, commonly known as the Silique Cabbage, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant features small, white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically lobed and hairy. It produces elongated, slender pods that contain the seeds.

Cochlearia siliquosa is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
