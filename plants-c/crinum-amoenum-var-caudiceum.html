Crinum amoenum var. caudiceum

Crinum amoenum var. caudiceum, commonly known as the long-stemmed crinum, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in umbels at the top of tall, slender stems. The plant has strap-like, lanceolate leaves that are often glaucous and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

The species is valued for its ornamental flowers and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
