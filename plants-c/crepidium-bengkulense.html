Crepidium bengkulense

Crepidium bengkulense, commonly known as the Bengkulu orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its native habitat.

Its leaves are usually linear and grass-like, and it prefers shaded, moist environments in the undergrowth of forests.
