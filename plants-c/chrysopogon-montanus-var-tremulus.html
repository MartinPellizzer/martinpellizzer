Chrysopogon montanus var. tremulus

Chrysopogon montanus var. tremulus, commonly known as the trembling grass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce light green to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is noted for its delicate, swaying growth habit, which gives it its common name.
