Cracca humilis

Cracca humilis, commonly known as the dwarf cracca, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
