Coleus acariformis

Coleus acariformis, commonly known as the spider plant or velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Coleus acariformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
