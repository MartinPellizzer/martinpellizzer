Cassia compitalis

Cassia compitalis, commonly known as the Jerusalem thorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are bright yellow, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is drought-tolerant and often grows in rocky or sandy soils.

Cassia compitalis is also known for its thorny stems, which provide protection against herbivores.
