Clematopsis argentea

Clematopsis argentea, commonly known as silver clematopsis, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Clematopsis argentea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
