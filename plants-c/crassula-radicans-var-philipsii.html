Crassula radicans var. philipsii

Crassula radicans var. philipsii, commonly known as the Philips' Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a slight reddish tinge under bright light. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with ample sunlight.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.
