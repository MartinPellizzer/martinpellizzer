Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. normalis

Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. normalis, commonly known as the common reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. normalis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
