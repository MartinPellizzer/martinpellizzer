Cussonia thyrsoidea

Cussonia thyrsoidea, commonly known as the African arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its thick, smooth, and often greyish bark. The plant features large, palmate leaves that are typically dark green and have a velvety texture. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Cussonia thyrsoidea is also known for its edible roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
