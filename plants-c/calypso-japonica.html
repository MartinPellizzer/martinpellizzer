Calypso japonica

Calypso japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Calypso japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
