Chionogentias townsonii

Chionogentias townsonii, commonly known as the Townsend's snow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Chionogentias townsonii plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
