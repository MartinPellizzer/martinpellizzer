Calea papposa

Calea papposa, commonly known as the "Mexican skyflower," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its distinctive, fluffy, white pappus that resembles clouds, giving it its common name. Its leaves are hairy and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

Calea papposa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
