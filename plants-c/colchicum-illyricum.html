Colchicum illyricum

Colchicum illyricum, commonly known as the Illyrian colchic, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a short, stout stem and produces large, showy flowers with recurved petals. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and it is adapted to grow in rocky or calcareous soils.

Colchicum illyricum is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
