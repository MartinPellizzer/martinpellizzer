Cereus undulatus

Cereus undulatus, commonly known as the undulating candle cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a distinctive undulating or wavy appearance along its stem. The plant features spines that are typically white to yellow in color, providing a striking contrast to its green body. It produces large, showy night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pink.

This species is well adapted to desert environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
