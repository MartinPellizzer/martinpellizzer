Cardamine sandbergii

Cardamine sandbergii, commonly known as Sandberg's watercress, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This herbaceous perennial typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its leaves are trifoliate, with small, rounded lobes, and it produces tiny white or greenish flowers in early spring.

The plant is valued for its delicate, peppery flavor and is sometimes used in local cuisine or as a medicinal herb.
