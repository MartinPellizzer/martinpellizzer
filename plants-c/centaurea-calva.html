Centaurea calva

Centaurea calva, commonly known as the slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its small, blue to purple flowers. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale achenes as its seeds.

Centaurea calva is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
