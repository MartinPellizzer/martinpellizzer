Claytonia lanceolata

Claytonia lanceolata, commonly known as the mountain sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, fleshy, and often reddish leaves. The plant produces upright stems bearing small, white to pinkish flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
