Cassia uniflora var. ramosa

Cassia uniflora var. ramosa, commonly known as the clustered golden glory, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, often branching stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval leaflets arranged in feathery patterns. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental in warm climates.
