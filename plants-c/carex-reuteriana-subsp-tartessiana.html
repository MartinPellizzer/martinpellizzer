Carex reuteriana subsp. tartessiana

Carex reuteriana subsp.

tartessiana, commonly known as the Canary Islands sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
