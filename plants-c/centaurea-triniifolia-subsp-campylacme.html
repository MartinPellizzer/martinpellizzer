Centaurea triniifolia subsp. campylacme

Centaurea triniifolia subsp.

campylacme, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
