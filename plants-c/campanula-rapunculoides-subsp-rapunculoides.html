Campanula rapunculoides subsp. rapunculoides

Campanula rapunculoides subsp.

rapunculoides, commonly known as the bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
