Cyphomandra pinnata

Cyphomandra pinnata, commonly known as the purple passionfruit vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers that give way to edible, orange-colored fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a fibrous, aromatic stem.

Cyphomandra pinnata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique fruit.
