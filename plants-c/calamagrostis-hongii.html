Calamagrostis hongii

Calamagrostis hongii, commonly known as Hong's reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of spikelets that are typically greenish or purplish in color, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in temperate regions.

Calamagrostis hongii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
