Cuphea erectifolia f. angustifolia

Cuphea erectifolia f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact size.
