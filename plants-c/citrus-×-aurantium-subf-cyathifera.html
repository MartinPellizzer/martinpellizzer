Citrus × aurantium subf. cyathifera

Citrus × aurantium subf.

cyathifera, commonly known as the papeda, is a citrus hybrid characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and aromatic leaves. It produces small, round, yellow-orange fruits with a thick, leathery rind.

The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it is known for its high essential oil content.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and for making perfumes and culinary products.
