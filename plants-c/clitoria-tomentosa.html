Clitoria tomentosa

Clitoria tomentosa, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple butterfly-shaped flowers, which are clustered in racemes. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of natural dye.

The flowers are also used in culinary applications, particularly in beverages and desserts.
