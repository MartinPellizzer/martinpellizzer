Cylindropuntia alcahes subsp. gigantensis

Cylindropuntia alcahes subsp.

gigantensis, commonly known as the giant barrel cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. This subspecies is characterized by its massive, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 15 feet in height, covered in thick, rib-like structures.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically pollinated by bees and other insects. Its spines are modified leaves that help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

The giant barrel cactus is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its succulent stems and playing a vital role in desert ecosystems.
