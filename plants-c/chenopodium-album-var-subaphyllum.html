Chenopodium album var. subaphyllum

Chenopodium album var. subaphyllum, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in agricultural fields.

This species is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
