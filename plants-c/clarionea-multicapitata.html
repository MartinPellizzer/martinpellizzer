Clarionea multicapitata

Clarionea multicapitata, commonly known as the multi-headed clarion, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, colorful flowers that bloom in a variety of hues, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a bushy growth habit with woody stems that can reach several feet in height. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Clarionea multicapitata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
