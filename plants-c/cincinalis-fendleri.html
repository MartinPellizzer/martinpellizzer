Cincinalis fendleri

Cincinalis fendleri, commonly known as the Desert Willow, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its flowering period typically occurs in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
