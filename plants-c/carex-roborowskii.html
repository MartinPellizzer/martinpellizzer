Carex roborowskii

Carex roborowskii, commonly known as the Roborowskii sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and establish dense colonies.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant for its architectural form and resilience.
