Crataegus brevispina

Crataegus brevispina, commonly known as the short-spined hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters, featuring thorny branches and dense, rounded foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and it is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and open forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
