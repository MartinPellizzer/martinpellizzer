Cladomyza uncinata

Cladomyza uncinata, commonly known as the hooked lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Cladoniaceae.

It is characterized by its frond-like lobes that are often curved or hooked, giving it its common name. The thallus is typically leathery and can vary in color from yellowish to dark brown. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on tree bark or soil in temperate regions.

This lichen is known for its slow growth and is often used in ecological studies to monitor air quality.
