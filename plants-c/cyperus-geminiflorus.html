Cyperus geminiflorus

Cyperus geminiflorus, commonly known as the twinflower flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often subtended by leaf-like bracts. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common species in aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.

Cyperus geminiflorus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
