Clematis armandi var. biondiana

Clematis armandi var. biondiana, commonly known as the golden clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from pale yellow to golden orange. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
