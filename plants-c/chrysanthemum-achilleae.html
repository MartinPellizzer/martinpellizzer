Chrysanthemum achilleae

Chrysanthemum achilleae, commonly known as the African chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance contribute to its appeal in horticulture.
