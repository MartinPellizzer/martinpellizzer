Calophyllum kunstleri var. longifolium

Calophyllum kunstleri var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The tree produces yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, elongated fruits containing a single seed. Its bark is thick and often fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
