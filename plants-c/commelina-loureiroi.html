Commelina loureiroi

Commelina loureiroi, commonly known as the blue commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, blue flowers.

Commelina loureiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
