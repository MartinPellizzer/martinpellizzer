Cavendishia strobilifera

Cavendishia strobilifera, commonly known as the candlewood plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, spire-like inflorescence that resembles a candle, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered along a central stem, which is often covered in a dense layer of leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance.

Cavendishia strobilifera is also valued for its ability to collect and store water in its leaf rosette, making it well adapted to its natural habitat.
