Convolvulus tenuifolius

Convolvulus tenuifolius, commonly known as slenderleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces trumpet-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
