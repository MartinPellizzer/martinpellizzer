Cardamine ochroleuca

Cardamine ochroleuca, commonly known as the white violets or white watercress.

This perennial herb is native to moist, shaded environments in North America. It features small, delicate leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white wax. The flowers are white or pale yellow and appear in clusters, often resembling those of other members of the Ranunculaceae family.

The plant is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and shaded forest floors, where it thrives in cool, damp conditions.
