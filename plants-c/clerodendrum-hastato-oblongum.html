Clerodendrum hastato-oblongum

Clerodendrum hastato-oblongum, commonly known as the broad-leaved flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. These flowers are typically borne in terminal panicles and are adapted for pollination by insects.

Clerodendrum hastato-oblongum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types provided it receives adequate sunlight.
