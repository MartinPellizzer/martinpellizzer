Camara aculeata var. normalis

Camara aculeata var. normalis, commonly known as the prickly nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in short, stiff hairs and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant bears dark purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
