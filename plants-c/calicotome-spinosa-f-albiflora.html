Calicotome spinosa f. albiflora

Calicotome spinosa f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered spinous calicotome, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to southern Africa, particularly in regions with arid or semi-arid climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
