Coelogyne psittacina var. huttonii

Coelogyne psittacina var. huttonii, commonly known as the Hutton's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy pseudobulbs and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a red or orange lip. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
