Cerastium purpusii

Cerastium purpusii, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence.

Cerastium purpusii thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
