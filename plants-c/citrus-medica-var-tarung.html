Citrus medica var. tarung

Citrus medica var. tarung, commonly known as the Kaffir lime, is a citrus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, green, and deeply wrinkled peel, which is highly aromatic and used extensively in cooking. The fruit is small, typically less than 2 inches in diameter, and has a segmented, bumpy appearance. The peel contains essential oils that contribute to its distinctive fragrance and flavor.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both culinary and aromatic purposes.
