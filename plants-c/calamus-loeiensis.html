Calamus loeiensis

Calamus loeiensis, commonly known as the Loei calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Thailand, Cambodia, and Vietnam. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color.

Calamus loeiensis is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological importance in its native habitats.
