Ctenopteris eximia

Ctenopteris eximia, commonly known as the lace fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that are divided into numerous small, finger-like segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests and along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of bright green, compound leaves.

Ctenopteris eximia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
