Calendula prolifera

Calendula prolifera, commonly known as the common marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Calendula prolifera is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
