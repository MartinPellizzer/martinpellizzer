Campanula haynaldii

Campanula haynaldii, commonly known as the Haynald's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula haynaldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, bell-like flowers.
