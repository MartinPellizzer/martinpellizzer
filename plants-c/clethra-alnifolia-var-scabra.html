Clethra alnifolia var. scabra

Clethra alnifolia var. scabra, commonly known as roughleaf sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rough, grayish bark and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically orange or red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
