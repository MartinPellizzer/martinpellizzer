Cucurbita pepo var. pastalis

Cucurbita pepo var. pastalis, commonly known as the Mexican ridge gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruits that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers typical of the gourd family. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in traditional Mexican cuisine, either cooked or raw.
