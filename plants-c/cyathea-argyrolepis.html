Cyathea argyrolepis

Cyathea argyrolepis, commonly known as the silver-veined tree fern, is a species of tree fern belonging to the family Cyatheaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are typically dark green with a silvery or pale underside, giving the plant its common name. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
