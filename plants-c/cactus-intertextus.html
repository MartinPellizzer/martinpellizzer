Cactus intertextus

Cactus intertextus, commonly known as the twisted cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its roots are shallow and widespread, enabling it to absorb moisture from light rains.

Despite its tough exterior, Cactus intertextus plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
