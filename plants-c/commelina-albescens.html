Commelina albescens

Commelina albescens, commonly known as the white commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking white flowers with a blue-violet throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are tuberous.

Commelina albescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
