Carex sellowiana

Carex sellowiana, commonly known as Sellow's sedge, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early to mid-summer.

Carex sellowiana is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
