Carex rigida var. frigida

Carex rigida var. frigida, commonly known as the hardy sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and other wetland habitats. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
