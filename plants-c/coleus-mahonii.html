Coleus mahonii

Coleus mahonii, commonly known as the heartleaf plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Coleus mahonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
