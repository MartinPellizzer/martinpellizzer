Chloothamnus reholttumianus

Chloothamnus reholttumianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as small, narrow leaves to reduce water loss. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hardy nature and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
