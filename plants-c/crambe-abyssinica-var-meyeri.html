Crambe abyssinica var. meyeri

Crambe abyssinica var. meyeri, commonly known as the Abyssinian crambe, is a herbaceous plant native to Ethiopia.

It features yellow flowers with prominent, hairy sepals and is characterized by its upright growth habit. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential agricultural uses.
