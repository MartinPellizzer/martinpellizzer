Clematis montana var. fasciculiflora

Clematis montana var. fasciculiflora, commonly known as the mountain clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically white to pale blue, appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental climbers.

Its botanical characteristics make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial in native habitats.
