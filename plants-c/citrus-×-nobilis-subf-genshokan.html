Citrus × nobilis subf. genshokan

Citrus × nobilis subf.

genshokan, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin, is a hybrid citrus species native to East Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with thin, smooth, and slightly pebbled rind.

The fruit typically has a sweet and aromatic flavor with a high juice content. It is cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value, thriving in warm, subtropical climates.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring.
