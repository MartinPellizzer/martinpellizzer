Calystegia occidentalis

Calystegia occidentalis, commonly known as the western morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a trailing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open fields. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
