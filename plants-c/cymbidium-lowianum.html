Cymbidium lowianum

Cymbidium lowianum, commonly known as the Low's orchid, is a species of orchid native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of fragrant, lance-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it a notable addition to winter gardens. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cymbidium lowianum is often cultivated for its beauty and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
