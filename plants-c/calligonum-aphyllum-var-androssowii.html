Calligonum aphyllum var. androssowii

Calligonum aphyllum var. androssowii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and gray-green, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow, which are followed by dry, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
