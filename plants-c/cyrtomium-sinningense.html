Cyrtomium sinningense

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Vietnam, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. Its spore-producing structures, called sorangia, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Cyrtomium sinningense is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and as a houseplant.
