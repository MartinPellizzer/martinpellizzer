Cirsium jonasii

Cirsium jonasii, commonly known as Jonas's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage is covered in stiff, barbed bracts that serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
