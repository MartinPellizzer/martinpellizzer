Cicer oxyodon

Cicer oxyodon, commonly known as the Persian chickpea, is a leguminous plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces spherical pods containing several seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Cicer oxyodon is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, improving soil fertility.
