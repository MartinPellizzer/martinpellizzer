Clitoria javanica

Clitoria javanica, commonly known as the winged bean, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically violet or blue in color. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces edible pods that contain both beans and a starchy seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Clitoria javanica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
