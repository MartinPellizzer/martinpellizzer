Cissus vinifera

Cissus vinifera, commonly known as the grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, translucent glands. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, grape-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus vinifera is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and vigorous growth habit.
