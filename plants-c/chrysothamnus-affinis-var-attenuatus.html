Chrysothamnus affinis var. attenuatus

Chrysothamnus affinis var. attenuatus, commonly known as the slender goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and narrow, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and is used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
