Ceropegia sinaica

Ceropegia sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East, particularly the Sinai Peninsula.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited to semi-arid environments.

Ceropegia sinaica is also noted for its ability to produce a milky sap, which is a common feature among plants in the Asclepiadaceae family.
