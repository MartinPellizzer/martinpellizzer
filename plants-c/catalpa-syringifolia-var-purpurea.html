Catalpa syringifolia var. purpurea

Catalpa syringifolia var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The flowers are typically pale purple to deep purple, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The tree produces long, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
