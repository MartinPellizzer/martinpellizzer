Castela emoryi

Castela emoryi, commonly known as Emory's castella, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls. Its growth habit is typically low and sprawling, with multiple stems arising from the base.

Castela emoryi is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
