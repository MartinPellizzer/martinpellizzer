Campanula punctata var. rubriflora

Campanula punctata var. rubriflora, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers with distinctive red markings, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Campanula punctata var. rubriflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
