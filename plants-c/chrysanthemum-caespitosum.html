Chrysanthemum caespitosum

Chrysanthemum caespitosum, commonly known as the mouseear chrysanthemum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage with small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as ladybugs and lacewings.
