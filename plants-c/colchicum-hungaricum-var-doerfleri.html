Colchicum hungaricum var. doerfleri

Colchicum hungaricum var. doerfleri, commonly known as the Hungarian colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive spurred petal. The plant produces a bulbous corm, which serves as its primary storage organ. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
