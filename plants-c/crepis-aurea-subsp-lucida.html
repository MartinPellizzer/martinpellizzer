Crepis aurea subsp. lucida

Crepis aurea subsp.

lucida, commonly known as the shining hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its bright yellow flowers are surrounded by pale green, leaf-like structures called phyllaries.

The subspecies lucida is distinguished by its more elongated leaves and a more prominent central stem compared to other subspecies of Crepis aurea.
