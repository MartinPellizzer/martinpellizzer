Cistus heterophyllus subsp. carthaginensis

Cistus heterophyllus subsp.

carthaginensis, commonly known as the Carthaginian rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.

Its essential oils are valued for their use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
