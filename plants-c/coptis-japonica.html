Coptis japonica

Coptis japonica, commonly known as Japanese goldthread, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. Its underground stems contain the alkaloid berberine, which has medicinal properties.

Coptis japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in herbal medicine.
