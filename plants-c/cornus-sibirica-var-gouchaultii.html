Cornus sibirica var. gouchaultii

Cornus sibirica var. gouchaultii, commonly known as the Siberian cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The berries that follow the flowers are red and edible, often consumed by birds and small mammals.
