Chilocalyx macrophyllus

Chilocalyx macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a floating canopy on the water surface. The plant's flowers are showy, with white petals that open in the early morning and close by midday. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to support its extensive growth.

Chilocalyx macrophyllus plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by offering habitat and shelter for various water organisms.
