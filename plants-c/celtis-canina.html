Celtis canina

Celtis canina, commonly known as the dogwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that are edible for some wildlife but not typically consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Celtis canina is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
