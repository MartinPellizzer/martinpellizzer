Centaurea mesopotamica

Centaurea mesopotamica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Centaurea mesopotamica is often found in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
