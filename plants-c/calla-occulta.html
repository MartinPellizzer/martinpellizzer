Calla occulta

Calla occulta, commonly known as the hidden calla, is a rare and elusive plant species belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, spatulate-shaped leaves and a single, elegant white spathe that encloses the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along streams. Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often difficult to locate in the wild.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
