Carex rariflora f. brevipedunculata

Carex rariflora f. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the narrow-stalked sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along streambanks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
