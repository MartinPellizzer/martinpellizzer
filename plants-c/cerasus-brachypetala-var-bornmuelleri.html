Cerasus brachypetala var. bornmuelleri

Cerasus brachypetala var. bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Chinese dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
