Cordia hartwissiana

Cordia hartwissiana, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
