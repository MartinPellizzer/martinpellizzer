Calytrix flavescens

Calytrix flavescens, commonly known as the yellow calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a shrub-like appearance with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces fruit that is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

Calytrix flavescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
