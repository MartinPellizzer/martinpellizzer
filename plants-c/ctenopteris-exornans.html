Ctenopteris exornans

Ctenopteris exornans, commonly known as the ornate cliff fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricately branched fronds that are often adorned with small, tooth-like projections. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on rocky outcrops or in moist forest floors. Its fronds can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a feathery appearance that adds to its ornamental value.

Ctenopteris exornans is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
