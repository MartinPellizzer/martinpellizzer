Cynanchum jamaicense

Cynanchum jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are clustered in umbels and display a mix of white and green hues.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations, including certain species of butterflies.
