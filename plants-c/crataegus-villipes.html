Crataegus villipes

Crataegus villipes, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a sour taste. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Crataegus villipes thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
