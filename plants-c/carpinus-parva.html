Carpinus parva

Carpinus parva, commonly known as the dwarf hornbeam, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 5 meters, featuring a dense, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, while the fruit is a small, winged nut enclosed in a spiky husk.

Carpinus parva is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
