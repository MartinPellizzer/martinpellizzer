Carex pseudocyperus var. haenkeana

Carex pseudocyperus var. haenkeana, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. This variety is native to regions in South America, particularly in Brazil, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
