Cyperus ingratus

Cyperus ingratus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other similar plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering stems are usually erect and produce small, clustered flowers.

Cyperus ingratus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
