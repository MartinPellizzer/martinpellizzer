Crambe parviflora

Crambe parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered crambe, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features slender stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Crambe parviflora is often found in grasslands and along the edges of woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
