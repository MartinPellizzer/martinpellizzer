Careya sphaerica

Careya sphaerica, commonly known as the globe sycamore or Indian sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and used in traditional medicine.
