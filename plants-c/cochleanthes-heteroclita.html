Cochleanthes heteroclita

Cochleanthes heteroclita, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. It typically blooms in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

Cochleanthes heteroclita is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
