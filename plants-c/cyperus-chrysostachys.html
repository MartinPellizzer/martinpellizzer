Cyperus chrysostachys

Cyperus chrysostachys, commonly known as golden flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often golden or yellowish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, commonly found in marshy areas, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus chrysostachys is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to wet environments.
