Chamaezelum

Chamaezelum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers and is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Chamaezelum is known for its strong aromatic scent and has been used historically for its medicinal properties.
