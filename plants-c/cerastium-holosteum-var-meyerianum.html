Cerastium holosteum var. meyerianum

Cerastium holosteum var. meyerianum, commonly known as Meyer's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a soft appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variant is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate, mat-forming habit.
