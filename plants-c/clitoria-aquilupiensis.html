Clitoria aquilupiensis

Clitoria aquilupiensis, commonly known as the sacred bean or butterfly pea, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple or blue pea-like flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its medicinal properties and ornamental value.
