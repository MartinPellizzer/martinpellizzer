Celtis weddelliana

Celtis weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into spherical, fuzzy fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Celtis weddelliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
