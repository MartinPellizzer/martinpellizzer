Cicendia hyssopifolia

Cicendia hyssopifolia, commonly known as the blue hyssop, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Cicendia hyssopifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
