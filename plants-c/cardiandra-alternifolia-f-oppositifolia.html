Cardiandra alternifolia f. oppositifolia

Cardiandra alternifolia f. oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved heartleaf, is a unique variant of the Cardiandra genus.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, which distinguishes it from the typical alternate leaf pattern of the species. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often velvety in texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical interest lies in its morphological variation and potential use in horticultural collections.
