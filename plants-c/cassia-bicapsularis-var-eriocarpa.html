Cassia bicapsularis var. eriocarpa

Cassia bicapsularis var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the hairy capsule cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid environments.
