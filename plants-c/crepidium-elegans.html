Crepidium elegans

Crepidium elegans, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in a dense spike. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
