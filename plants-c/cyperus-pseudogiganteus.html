Cyperus pseudogiganteus

Cyperus pseudogiganteus, commonly known as the tall flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Cyperus pseudogiganteus is adapted to wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas, swamps, and along riverbanks.
