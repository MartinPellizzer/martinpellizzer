Ceratocephala syriaca

Ceratocephala syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or yellow throat. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Ceratocephala syriaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
