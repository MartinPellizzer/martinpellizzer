Canthium gracile

Canthium gracile, commonly known as slender spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and often simple leaf structures. The plant typically features small, green leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

Canthium gracile is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of moist to dry habitats.
