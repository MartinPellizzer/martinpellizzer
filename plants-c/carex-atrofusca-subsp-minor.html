Carex atrofusca subsp. minor

Carex atrofusca subsp.

minor, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and bog environments in North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant has dark green, strap-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact rosette. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, with male and female flowers often occurring on separate stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
