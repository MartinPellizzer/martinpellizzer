Caragana frutex subsp. mollis

Caragana frutex subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the softleaf shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by pea-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in landscaping and erosion control.
