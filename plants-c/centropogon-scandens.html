Centropogon scandens

Centropogon scandens, commonly known as the climbing centropogon, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vine-like growth habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red and orange. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological significance.
