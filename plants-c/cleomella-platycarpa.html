Cleomella platycarpa

Cleomella platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited cleome, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, yellow or white flowers. The plant produces capsule-like fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cleomella platycarpa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
