Calceolaria vulpina

Calceolaria vulpina, commonly known as the fox's tail or wolf's tail, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its striking, pouch-like flowers that resemble a fox's tail. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions, often in rocky or forested habitats. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Calceolaria vulpina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
