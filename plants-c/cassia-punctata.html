Cassia punctata

Cassia punctata, commonly known as golden shower tree or yellow flame, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Cassia punctata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
