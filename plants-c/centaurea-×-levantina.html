Centaurea × levantina

Centaurea × levantina, commonly known as the Levantine centaury, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves and spiny stems. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea × levantina is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
