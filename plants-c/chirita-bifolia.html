Chirita bifolia

Chirita bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaved begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pair of opposite, heart-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Chirita bifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care in suitable conditions.
