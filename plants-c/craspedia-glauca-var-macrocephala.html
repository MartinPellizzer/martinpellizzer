Craspedia glauca var. macrocephala

Craspedia glauca var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland dunes. The plant features showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage is generally covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
