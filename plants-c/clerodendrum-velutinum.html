Clerodendrum velutinum

Clerodendrum velutinum, commonly known as velvet cleaver, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
