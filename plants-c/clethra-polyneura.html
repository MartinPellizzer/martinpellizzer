Clethra polyneura

Clethra polyneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Clethra polyneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
