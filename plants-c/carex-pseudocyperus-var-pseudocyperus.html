Carex pseudocyperus var. pseudocyperus

Carex pseudocyperus var. pseudocyperus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly pointed, with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
