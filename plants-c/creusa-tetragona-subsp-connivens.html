Creusa tetragona subsp. connivens

Creusa tetragona subsp.

connivens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved creusa, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to botanical collections.
