Cestrum brevifolium var. gracillimum

Cestrum brevifolium var. gracillimum, commonly known as the slender-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically orange-red in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers.
