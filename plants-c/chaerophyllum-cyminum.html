Chaerophyllum cyminum

Chaerophyllum cyminum, commonly known as Persian cumin or wild cumin, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces umbels of small, white to greenish-yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cumin.
