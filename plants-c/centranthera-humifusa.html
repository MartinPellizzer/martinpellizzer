Centranthera humifusa

Centranthera humifusa, commonly known as the low centranthera, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. The plant features slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes and produce small, white to pale pink flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Centranthera humifusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
