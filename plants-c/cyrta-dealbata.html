Cyrta dealbata

Cyrta dealbata, commonly known as the white-flowered cycad, is a unique plant species native to certain parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow cones rather than flowers, which are used for reproduction. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, dry climate.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species.
