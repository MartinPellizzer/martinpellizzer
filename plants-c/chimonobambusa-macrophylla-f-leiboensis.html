Chimonobambusa macrophylla f. leiboensis

Chimonobambusa macrophylla f. leiboensis, commonly known as the Leibo bamboo, is a unique variant of the giant bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and robust culms that can grow to impressive heights. This plant thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its botanical significance lies in its distinct morphology and genetic diversity within the Chimonobambusa genus.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and sustainable use in traditional crafts.
