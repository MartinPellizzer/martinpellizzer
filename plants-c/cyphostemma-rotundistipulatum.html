Cyphostemma rotundistipulatum

Cyphostemma rotundistipulatum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with rounded tips. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habit, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
