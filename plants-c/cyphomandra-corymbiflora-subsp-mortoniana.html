Cyphomandra corymbiflora subsp. mortoniana

Cyphomandra corymbiflora subsp.

mortoniana, commonly known as the Morton's bitter apple, is a member of the Solanaceae family. It is native to the Andes region of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

The plant produces small, yellow, egg-shaped fruits that are typically bitter and not suitable for human consumption. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and the plant has a trailing growth habit.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
