Colladoa distachya

Colladoa distachya, commonly known as the two-branched grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-branched flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
