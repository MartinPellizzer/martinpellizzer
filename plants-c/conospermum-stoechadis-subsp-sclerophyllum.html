Conospermum stoechadis subsp. sclerophyllum

Conospermum stoechadis subsp.

sclerophyllum, commonly known as the coastal honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its woody, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in coastal heathlands and is resilient to salt spray and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
