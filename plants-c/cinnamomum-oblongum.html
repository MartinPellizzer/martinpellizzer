Cinnamomum oblongum

Cinnamomum oblongum, commonly known as Chinese cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its bark, which is the source of the spice cinnamon, is rich in essential oils and has a distinctive woody aroma.

The plant is valued both for its medicinal properties and its use in culinary applications.
