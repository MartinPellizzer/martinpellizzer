Cheiranthus versicolor

Cheiranthus versicolor, commonly known as the variable carnation, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its showy, bi-colored flowers that typically range from pink to white and yellow. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cheiranthus versicolor thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and rockeries.
