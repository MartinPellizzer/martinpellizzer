Chaetostachydium filiforme

Chaetostachydium filiforme, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows or along stream banks. Its delicate structure makes it a common component of wetland vegetation.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
