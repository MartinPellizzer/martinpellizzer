Crotalaria polysperma subsp. stewartii

Crotalaria polysperma subsp.

stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's crotalaria, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family Fabaceae. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, elliptical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It has compound leaves with small leaflets and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
