Clusia chusqueae

Clusia chusqueae, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are usually dark green and glossy, providing an ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia chusqueae is also known for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
