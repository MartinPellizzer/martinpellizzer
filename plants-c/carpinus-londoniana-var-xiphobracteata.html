Carpinus londoniana var. xiphobracteata

Carpinus londoniana var. xiphobracteata, commonly known as the London hornbeam variant with xiphobracteata characteristics, is a lesser-known cultivar of the hornbeam genus.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctively narrow and pointed base. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit, making it suitable for small garden spaces. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, showing some tolerance to urban conditions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
