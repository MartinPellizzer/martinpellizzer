Carex beyrichiana

Carex beyrichiana, commonly known as the Beyrich's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
