Cyanotis wightii

Cyanotis wightii, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Cyanotis wightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
