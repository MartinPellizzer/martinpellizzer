Calamus ralumensis

Calamus ralumensis, commonly known as the Ralumensian reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellowish flowers, which are typically borne at the base of the leaves.

Calamus ralumensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
