Cabralea clausseniana

Cabralea clausseniana, commonly known as the redwood tree of the Andes, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and large, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its unique adaptations allow it to thrive in the cool, moist conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Cabralea clausseniana plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
