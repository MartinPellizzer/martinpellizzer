Celosia recurva

Celosia recurva, commonly known as the flame flower or crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by colorful, papery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Celosia recurva is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
