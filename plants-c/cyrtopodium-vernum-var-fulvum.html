Cyrtopodium vernum var. fulvum

Cyrtopodium vernum var. fulvum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit and produces erect, leafy stems. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, fragrant scent. Its pseudobulbs are clustered and covered in a dense layer of pale green to brownish hair.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
