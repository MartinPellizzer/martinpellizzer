Chloris brandegeei

Chloris brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegge's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it drought-tolerant.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
