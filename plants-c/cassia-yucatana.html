Cassia yucatana

Cassia yucatana, commonly known as the Yucatan cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, making it a visually striking species. Its leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia yucatana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral display.
