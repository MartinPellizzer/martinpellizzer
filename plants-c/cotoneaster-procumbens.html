Cotoneaster procumbens

Cotoneaster procumbens, commonly known as the creeping cotoneaster, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of bright red berries in the fall. The plant is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.

Cotoneaster procumbens is also known for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
