Calathea comosa

Calathea comosa, commonly known as the "White Vein" plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green leaves with intricate white and silver veins that create a visually appealing pattern. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its foliage is often used in interior design due to its ornamental value.

Calathea comosa requires consistent humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
