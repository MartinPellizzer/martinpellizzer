Crataegus mexicana var. carrierei

Crataegus mexicana var. carrierei, commonly known as the Mexican hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
