Conophytum labyrintheum

Conophytum labyrintheum, commonly known as the labyrinth cactus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy stem that resembles a cactus, although it is actually a member of the Aizoaceae family. The plant features small, spiny tubercles and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
