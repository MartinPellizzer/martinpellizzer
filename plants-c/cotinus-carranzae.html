Cotinus carranzae

Cotinus carranzae, commonly known as the Carranza's smoke bush, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pinkish-red inflorescences that resemble smoke, hence its common name. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Cotinus carranzae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
