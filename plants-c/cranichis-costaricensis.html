Cranichis costaricensis

Cranichis costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
