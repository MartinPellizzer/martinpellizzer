Chondrodendron

Chondrodendron, commonly known as the "moonflower" or "night-blooming cereus," is a genus of flowering plants in the cacti family, Cactaceae.

Chondrodendron species are characterized by their spiny stems and showy, nocturnal flowers that open at night and emit a fragrant scent.
