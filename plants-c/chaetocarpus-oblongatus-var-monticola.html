Chaetocarpus oblongatus var. monticola

Chaetocarpus oblongatus var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain chaetocarpus, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces distinctive, hairy pods that contain seeds with a fibrous covering. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations to extreme environments.
