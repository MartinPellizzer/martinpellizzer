Ceropegia parviflora

Ceropegia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that are often clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia parviflora is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
