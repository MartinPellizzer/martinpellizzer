Clematis aethusifolia

Clematis aethusifolia, commonly known as the silky clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features slender, woody stems that can grow up to 15 meters in length, often twining around supports. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5-7 oval-shaped leaflets with a silvery underside.

The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
