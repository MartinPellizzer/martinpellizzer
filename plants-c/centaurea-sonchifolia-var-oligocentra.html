Centaurea sonchifolia var. oligocentra

Centaurea sonchifolia var. oligocentra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are composed of ray florets and tubular disc florets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
