Calanthe apostasioides

Calanthe apostasioides, commonly known as the mountain calanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers with a distinct lip, which is a defining feature of many orchids. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Calanthe apostasioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in horticulture for its elegance and unique floral structure.
