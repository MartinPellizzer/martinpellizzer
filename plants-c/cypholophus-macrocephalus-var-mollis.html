Cypholophus macrocephalus var. mollis

Cypholophus macrocephalus var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-headed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to blue, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, softly textured leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
