Cryptoglottis serpyllifolia

Cryptoglottis serpyllifolia, commonly known as lesser bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has slender, creeping stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Cryptoglottis serpyllifolia is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
