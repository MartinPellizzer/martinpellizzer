Cousinia amicorum

Cousinia amicorum, commonly known as the friendship flower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia amicorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
