Crassula rudolfii

Crassula rudolfii, commonly known as the Rudolf's Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Crassula rudolfii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and aesthetic appeal.
