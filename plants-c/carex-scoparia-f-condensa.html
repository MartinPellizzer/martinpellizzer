Carex scoparia f. condensa

Carex scoparia f. condensa, commonly known as the dense sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and cylindrical, with a prominent midrib, and it often forms dense clumps.

The inflorescence consists of two to three spikelets, which are often reddish or brownish in color.
