Cyathea caracasana var. meridensis

Cyathea caracasana var. meridensis, commonly known as the Caracas hakea, is a fern native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the family Hymenophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.
