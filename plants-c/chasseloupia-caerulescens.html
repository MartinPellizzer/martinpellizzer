Chasseloupia caerulescens

Chasseloupia caerulescens, commonly known as the blue willow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and small, blue-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
