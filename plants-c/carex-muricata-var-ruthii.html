Carex muricata var. ruthii

Carex muricata var. ruthii, commonly known as the Ruth's rough sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular, rough-textured leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
