Cirsium undulatum var. albescens

Cirsium undulatum var. albescens, commonly known as the white thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial species belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple or white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with wavy edges, and its flowers are usually arranged in a single head.

The white thistle is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
