Calamus melanoloma

Calamus melanoloma, commonly known as black-root calamus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow rhizomes that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as marshes and ponds.

Calamus melanoloma is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
