Calamagrostis angustifolia

Calamagrostis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf grass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often silver-white underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences with small, spikelet flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

Calamagrostis angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
