Chrysopogon aciculatus var. elatior

Chrysopogon aciculatus var. elatior, commonly known as the tall needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
