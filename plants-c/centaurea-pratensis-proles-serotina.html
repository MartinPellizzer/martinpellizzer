Centaurea pratensis proles serotina

Centaurea pratensis proles serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering field centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant typically grows in open grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its flowering period occurs late in the growing season, usually from late summer to early autumn.

The plant produces slender, branching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
