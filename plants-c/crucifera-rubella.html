Crucifera rubella

Crucifera rubella, commonly known as the red-cabbage mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers.

This plant typically grows in temperate regions and is known for its edible leaves and bright red or purple flowers. Its stems are often hairy, and the leaves are lobed, with a slightly bitter taste. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and use in traditional cuisine.
