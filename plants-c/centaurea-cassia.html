Centaurea cassia

Centaurea cassia, commonly known as the Cassia Centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. Its striking flowers are blue to purple in hue, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Centaurea cassia is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
