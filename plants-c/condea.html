Condea

Condea, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. Condea is native to regions in Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in some areas.
