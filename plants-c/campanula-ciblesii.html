Campanula ciblesii

Campanula ciblesii, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Campanula ciblesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
