Celosia albida

Celosia albida, commonly known as the white cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that are typically pink to red in color. The plant features lanceolate, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Celosia albida thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its short growing season and ability to reseed itself contribute to its widespread cultivation in warm climates.
