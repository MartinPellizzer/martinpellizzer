Cerastium longipedunculatum

Cerastium longipedunculatum, commonly known as the long peduncled mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often sprawling and bear elongated peduncles that support its delicate blooms.

Cerastium longipedunculatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
