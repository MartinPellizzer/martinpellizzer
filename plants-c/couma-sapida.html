Couma sapida

Couma sapida, commonly known as the edible wild bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds, which are often consumed after soaking to remove bitterness. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

The seeds are rich in protein and can be used in various traditional dishes.
