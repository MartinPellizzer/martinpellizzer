Cotoneaster vulgaris var. minutifolius

Cotoneaster vulgaris var. minutifolius, commonly known as the dwarf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, compact growth with small, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a finely toothed margin.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
