Cirsium × grandiflorum

Cirsium × grandiflorum, commonly known as the Great Coneflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces large, purple or pink composite flowers with a prominent central disk surrounded by ray florets. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Cirsium × grandiflorum is often found in prairies, open woods, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
