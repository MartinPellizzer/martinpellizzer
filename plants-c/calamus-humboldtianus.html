Calamus humboldtianus

Calamus humboldtianus, commonly known as the Humboldt's calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae) and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a tall, herbaceous plant that can grow up to several meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces a spadix, a type of inflorescence typical of the Aroid family, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Calamus humboldtianus is characterized by its robust rhizomes, which are often harvested for their aromatic properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.
