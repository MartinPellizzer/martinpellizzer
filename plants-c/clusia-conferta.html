Clusia conferta

Clusia conferta, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellowish-orange fruits containing several seeds. Clusia conferta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to tolerate urban environments.
