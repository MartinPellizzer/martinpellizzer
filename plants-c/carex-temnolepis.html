Carex temnolepis

Carex temnolepis, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, followed by three-sided, triangular seed capsules.

Carex temnolepis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
