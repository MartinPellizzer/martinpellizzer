Cassebeeria rotundifolia

Cassebeeria rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf cassia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or on rocky soils.

Cassebeeria rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
