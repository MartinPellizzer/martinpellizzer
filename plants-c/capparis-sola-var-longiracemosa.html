Capparis sola var. longiracemosa

Capparis sola var. longiracemosa, commonly known as the long raceme caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, thorny branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in elongated racemes. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough, leathery texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
