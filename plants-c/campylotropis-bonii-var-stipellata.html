Campylotropis bonii var. stipellata

Campylotropis bonii var. stipellata, commonly known as the stippled bush clover, is a perennial legume native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive stippled appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for ornamental gardens.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in ecological landscapes.
