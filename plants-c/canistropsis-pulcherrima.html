Canistropsis pulcherrima

Canistropsis pulcherrima, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its segmented stems that resemble those of true cacti. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming during the holiday season. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Canistropsis pulcherrima is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
