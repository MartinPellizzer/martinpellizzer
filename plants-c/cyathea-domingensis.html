Cyathea domingensis

Cyathea domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, with a short rhizome that supports the trunk and a crown of fronds that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyathea domingensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
