Citrus × limonellus

Citrus × limonellus, commonly known as the kaffir lime, is a hybrid citrus plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinct, aromatic leaves and small, inedible fruit. The plant is a member of the Rutaceae family and is closely related to other citrus species like lemons and limes. Its leaves are widely used in cooking for their strong, citrusy aroma.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
