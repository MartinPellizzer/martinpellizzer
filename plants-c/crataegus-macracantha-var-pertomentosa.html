Crataegus macracantha var. pertomentosa

Crataegus macracantha var. pertomentosa, commonly known as the fragrant crataegus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white to pink. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with finely serrated margins, and the plant produces red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
