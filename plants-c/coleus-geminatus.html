Coleus geminatus

Coleus geminatus, commonly known as the geminated coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, pink, and purple. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple.

Coleus geminatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and ease of growth.
