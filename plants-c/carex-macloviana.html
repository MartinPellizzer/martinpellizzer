Carex macloviana

Carex macloviana, commonly known as the Maclovian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex macloviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
