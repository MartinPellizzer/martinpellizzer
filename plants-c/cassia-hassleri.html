Cassia hassleri

Cassia hassleri, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

Cassia hassleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
