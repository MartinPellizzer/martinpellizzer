Crataegus helderbergensis

Crataegus helderbergensis, commonly known as the Heldersberg hawthorn, is a small to medium-sized deciduous shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and animals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
