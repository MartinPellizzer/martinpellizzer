Calendula aegyptiaca

Calendula aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has hairy, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Calendula aegyptiaca is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in herbal remedies.
