Centaurea aggregata

Centaurea aggregata, commonly known as the common knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Centaurea aggregata produces small, purple to pink flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.
