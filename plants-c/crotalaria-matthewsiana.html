Crotalaria matthewsiana

Crotalaria matthewsiana, commonly known as the African heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five lobes and produces showy yellow flowers that are often surrounded by a distinctive purple or violet calyx. It is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Additionally, it is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.
