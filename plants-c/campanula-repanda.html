Campanula repanda

Campanula repanda, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Campanula repanda is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and its ability to attract pollinators.
