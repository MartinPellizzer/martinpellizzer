Centaurea tschuchrukidzei

Centaurea tschuchrukidzei, commonly known as the Tschuchrukidze's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its striking blue to purple flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
