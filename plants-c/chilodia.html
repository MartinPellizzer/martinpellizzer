Chilodia

Chilodia, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that resemble those of the true grass family. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along the edges of ponds. Chilodia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.

Its striking floral display also makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in moist climates.
