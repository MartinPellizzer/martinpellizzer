Cyperus shimadae

Cyperus shimadae, commonly known as the Japanese flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has slender, triangular stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop in spikelets.

Cyperus shimadae is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
