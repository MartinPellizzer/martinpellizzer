Chamaefistula gigantifolia

Chamaefistula gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to form thick, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
