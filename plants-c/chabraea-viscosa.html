Chabraea viscosa

Chabraea viscosa, commonly known as the sticky chabraea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a sticky resin. The plant produces fragrant yellowish-white flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique resinous properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural adhesives.
