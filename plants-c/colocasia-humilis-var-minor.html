Colocasia humilis var. minor

Colocasia humilis var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, making it a striking addition to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Its foliage is often a deep green with a slightly glossy texture, adding visual interest to the landscape.
