Carpupica odorata

Carpupica odorata, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The flowers are primarily visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
