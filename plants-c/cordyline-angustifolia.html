Cordyline angustifolia

Cordyline angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cabbage tree, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Cordyline angustifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
