Cephalaria mauritanica var. rifana

Cephalaria mauritanica var. rifana, commonly known as the Rif mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing in mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
