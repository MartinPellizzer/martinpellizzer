Ceiba insignis

Ceiba insignis, commonly known as the giant ceiba or ceiba tree, is a towering tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and a massive, umbrella-shaped crown. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a thick trunk that often has a fluted appearance. Its flowers are pale yellow and appear in clusters, while its fruits are woody capsules containing fibrous seeds.

The ceiba tree plays a significant ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
