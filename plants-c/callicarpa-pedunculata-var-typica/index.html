Callicarpa Pedunculata Var. Typica

Callicarpa pedunculata var. typica is a lesser-known but strikingly beautiful species of the honeysuckle family. Native to parts of Asia, this plant is often found in moist, shaded environments within forests. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of vibrant red berries that appear in the fall, attracting a variety of wildlife. The plant is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological benefits. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively obscure in horticultural circles compared to its more widely cultivated relatives.
