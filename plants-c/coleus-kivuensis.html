Coleus kivuensis

Coleus kivuensis, commonly known as the Kivu coleus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of East Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for horticultural purposes.
