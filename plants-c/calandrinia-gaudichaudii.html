Calandrinia gaudichaudii

Calandrinia gaudichaudii, commonly known as the red sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Calandrinia gaudichaudii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.
