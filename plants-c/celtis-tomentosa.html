Celtis tomentosa

Celtis tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly apple tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and rounded leaves with a fuzzy upper surface. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals.

Its fruit is an important food source in its native habitat and contributes to the ecological diversity of the region.
