Centaurea zangulensis

Centaurea zangulensis, commonly known as the Zangul thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple or red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its distribution across its native range.

Centaurea zangulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
