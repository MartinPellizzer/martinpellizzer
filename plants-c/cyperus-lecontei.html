Cyperus lecontei

Cyperus lecontei, commonly known as the leconte's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often pale green or brown. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Cyperus lecontei is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
