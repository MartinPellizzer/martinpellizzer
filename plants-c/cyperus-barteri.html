Cyperus barteri

Cyperus barteri, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus barteri thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along waterways, and is often found in disturbed soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
