Clematis menglaensis

Clematis menglaensis, commonly known as the Mengla clematis, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy flowers. The plant produces fragrant, typically blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Clematis menglaensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
