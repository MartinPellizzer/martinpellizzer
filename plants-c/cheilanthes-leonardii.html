Cheilanthes leonardii

Cheilanthes leonardii, commonly known as the Leonard's rockcap, is a perennial fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Cheilanthes leonardii is a hardy species that requires minimal water and is often found in crevices or on exposed rock surfaces.
