Citrus japonica

Citrus japonica, commonly known as the Japanese citrus or yuzu, is a small evergreen tree native to East Asia.

It produces small, fragrant fruits that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves and is characterized by its distinctive, aromatic flowers. Its fruit is typically harvested when it is still green, though it can ripen to a yellowish color.

Citrus japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its contribution to traditional herbal medicine.
