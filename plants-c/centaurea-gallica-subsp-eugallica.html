Centaurea gallica subsp. eugallica

Centaurea gallica subsp.

eugallica, commonly known as the French centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features erect, branching stems that are covered in fine, grayish hairs.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flower heads, composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite flower structure. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight reddish tinge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
