Clematis alpina var. verticillaris

Clematis alpina var. verticillaris, commonly known as the Alpine Clematis, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a sunny to partially shaded location.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to alpine conditions.
