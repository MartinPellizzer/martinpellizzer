Croton sanguis-draconis

Croton sanguis-draconis, commonly known as dragon's blood tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, the Caribbean, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and a short, branching trunk. One of its most notable features is the reddish, resinous sap it produces, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a dye.

This sap, known as dragon's blood, is also used in varnishes and as a natural remedy for various ailments.
