Colchicum sibthorpii

Colchicum sibthorpii, commonly known as the Kashmir snowflake, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, snow-white flowers that resemble small stars. The plant produces a single, nodding flower per stem, which blooms in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette.

Colchicum sibthorpii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
