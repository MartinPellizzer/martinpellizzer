Carduncellus arborescens

Carduncellus arborescens, commonly known as the tree cardoon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a tall, woody stem with large, palmate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Carduncellus arborescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
