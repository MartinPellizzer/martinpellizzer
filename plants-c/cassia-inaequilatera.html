Cassia inaequilatera

Cassia inaequilatera, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in clusters, giving it a fiery appearance. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they have a feathery, compound structure with small, oval leaflets.

Cassia inaequilatera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
