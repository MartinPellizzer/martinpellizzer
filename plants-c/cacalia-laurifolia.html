Cacalia laurifolia

Cacalia laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel leaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that resembles the leaves of laurel plants. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cacalia laurifolia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
