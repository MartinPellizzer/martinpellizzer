Convolvulus oxyphyllus subsp. oxycladus

Convolvulus oxyphyllus subsp.

oxycladus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
