Capparis elaeagnoides var. zizyphoides

Capparis elaeagnoides var. zizyphoides, commonly known as the Indian date tree or false date palm, is a shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, pinnately compound leaves, and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, date-like flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Capparaceae family.
