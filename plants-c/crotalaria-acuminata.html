Crotalaria acuminata

Crotalaria acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cowpea or the devil's flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow or orange pods that contain seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
