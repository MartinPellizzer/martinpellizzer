Calanthe viridifusca

Calanthe viridifusca, commonly known as the green-root calanthe, is a rare and elegant orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-white petals and a distinctive, elongated lip that sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky substrates or in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its lush, foliage-heavy appearance.

Calanthe viridifusca is prized by botanists and horticulturists for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
