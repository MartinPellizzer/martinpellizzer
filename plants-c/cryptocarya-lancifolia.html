Cryptocarya lancifolia

Cryptocarya lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and have a rough texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Cryptocarya lancifolia is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
