Cinnamomum linearifolium

Cinnamomum linearifolium, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum cassia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern China, Vietnam, and Indonesia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by dark brown, elliptical berries. Its bark is rich in volatile oils, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its economic importance in the spice trade.
