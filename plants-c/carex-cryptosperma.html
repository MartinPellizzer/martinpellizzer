Carex cryptosperma

Carex cryptosperma, commonly known as the black-root sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by their triangular stems and narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Carex cryptosperma has dark, fibrous rhizomes and produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green to brown in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
