Cornus asperifolia var. drummondii

Cornus asperifolia var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's roughleaf dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, grayish bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
