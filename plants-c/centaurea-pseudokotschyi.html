Centaurea pseudokotschyi

Centaurea pseudokotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its blue to violet petals are surrounded by ray florets, giving it a striking appearance.

Centaurea pseudokotschyi is often found in open areas such as slopes and disturbed soils, where it thrives with minimal water.
