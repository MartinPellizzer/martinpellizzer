Coffea ligustrifolia

Coffea ligustrifolia, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries containing the seeds used for coffee. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, warm climate for optimal growth.
