Chrysopogon filipes var. arundinaceus

Chrysopogon filipes var. arundinaceus, commonly known as bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
