Cyrtandra flabellifolia

Cyrtandra flabellifolia, commonly known as the fanleaf heliconia, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves that form a fan-like structure, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant produces vibrant, spadix-like inflorescences that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Cyrtandra flabellifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in local ecosystems as a habitat for various insects and birds.
