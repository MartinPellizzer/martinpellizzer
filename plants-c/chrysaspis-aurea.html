Chrysaspis aurea

Chrysaspis aurea, commonly known as the golden chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Chrysaspis aurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
