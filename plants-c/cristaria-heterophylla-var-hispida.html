Cristaria heterophylla var. hispida

Cristaria heterophylla var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy broadleaf water plantain, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing white to pale pink blooms.

This variant is characterized by its distinct leaf morphology and adaptability to wetland habitats.
