Cerastium vulgatum var. grandiflorum

Cerastium vulgatum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as white mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals, and bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.
