Caltha palustris var. bosnica

Caltha palustris var. bosnica, commonly known as the Bosnian marsh marigold, is a variant of the marsh marigold native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The flowers are clustered in a loose inflorescence and are pollinated primarily by insects.
