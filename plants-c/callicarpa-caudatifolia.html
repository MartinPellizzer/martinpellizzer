Callicarpa caudatifolia

Callicarpa caudatifolia, commonly known as the long-tailed beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn red in the autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Callicarpa caudatifolia is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
