Curio ovoideus

Curio ovoideus, commonly known as the eggplant tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces oval-shaped, fleshy fruits that resemble eggs, hence its common name. Curio ovoideus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
