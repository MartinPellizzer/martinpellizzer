Colutea gifana

Colutea gifana, commonly known as the slender brookweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods that split open to release the seeds.

Colutea gifana is known for its ecological importance in supporting insect pollinators and contributing to soil stability in its habitat.
