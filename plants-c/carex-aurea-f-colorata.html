Carex aurea f. colorata

Carex aurea f. colorata, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a striking ornamental plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial sedge that features bright yellow foliage, which stands out in shaded or partially sunny garden settings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and can thrive in woodland or wetland environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and naturalistic garden designs.
