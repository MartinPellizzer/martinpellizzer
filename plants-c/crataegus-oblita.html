Crataegus oblita

Crataegus oblita, commonly known as the Tibetan hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant has small, oval leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces small red berries in the fall.

Crataegus oblita is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
