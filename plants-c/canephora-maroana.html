Canephora maroana

Canephora maroana, commonly known as the African coffee plant, is a species native to western and central Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 4 meters in height, with dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds used for coffee production. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soils.

Canephora maroana is valued for its robust coffee beans, which are often used in commercial coffee blends.
