Canna jacobiniflora

Canna jacobiniflora, commonly known as the Jacobiniflora canna, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful, and showy inflorescences that resemble spikes of flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Canna jacobiniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
