Chenopodium album var. incanum

Chenopodium album var. incanum, commonly known as common lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture and a bluish-gray or whitish coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense, clustered inflorescences, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and gardens.

This species is known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal medicine.
