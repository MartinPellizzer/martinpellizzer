Chickrassia

Chickrassia, commonly known as wild chicory, is a hardy perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of blue-green, spoon-shaped leaves and bright blue or purple flowering heads. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in disturbed areas. Chickrassia is known for its edible leaves, which have a slightly bitter taste, and its flowers, which attract pollinators.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting biodiversity.
