Cyperus luteolus

Cyperus luteolus, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, blade-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in tropical and subtropical regions. Its yellowish flowers are often clustered in dense spikes, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Cyperus luteolus plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
