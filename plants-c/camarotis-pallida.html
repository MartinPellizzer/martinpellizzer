Camarotis pallida

Camarotis pallida, commonly known as the pale bristled moth, is a species of moth belonging to the family Noctuidae.

It is characterized by its pale yellow wings with distinctive dark markings, which help in camouflage within its natural habitat. The larvae of this species feed on various plants, primarily grasses and sedges, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its environment. Botanically, it is associated with moist, temperate regions where its host plants thrive.

Its life cycle highlights the intricate relationships between plants and insect pollinators in natural ecosystems.
