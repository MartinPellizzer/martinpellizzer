Cordia hispida

Cordia hispida, commonly known as rough cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with small, stiff hairs.

The bark and leaves of Cordia hispida contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
