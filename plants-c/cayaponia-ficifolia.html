Cayaponia ficifolia

Cayaponia ficifolia, commonly known as the Mexican date tree, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to dates, though they are typically smaller and less sweet. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
