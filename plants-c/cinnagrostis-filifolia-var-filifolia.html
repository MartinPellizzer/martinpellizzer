Cinnagrostis filifolia var. filifolia

Cinnagrostis filifolia var. filifolia, commonly known as slender false flax, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and wet meadows. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
