Cyperus multispiceus

Cyperus multispiceus, commonly known as the many-fruited flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the base of the leaves. Cyperus multispiceus thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it can tolerate fluctuating water levels.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
