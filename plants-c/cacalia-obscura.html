Cacalia obscura

Cacalia obscura, commonly known as the dark cacalia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-white.

It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across arid environments.
