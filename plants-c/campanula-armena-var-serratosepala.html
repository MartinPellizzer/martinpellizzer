Campanula armena var. serratosepala

Campanula armena var. serratosepala, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice among gardeners interested in cultivating hardy, decorative plants.
