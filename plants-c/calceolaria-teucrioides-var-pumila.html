Calceolaria teucrioides var. pumila

Calceolaria teucrioides var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf calceolaria, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, resembling small cups or lanterns. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its unique floral structure and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
