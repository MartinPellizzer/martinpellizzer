Citrus × nobilis subf. koshotankan

Citrus × nobilis subf.

koshotankan, commonly known as the Koshotankan lemon, is a lesser-known citrus hybrid originating from Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thick, aromatic rind and a high concentration of essential oils.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is valued for its unique flavor and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.

Due to its limited cultivation, it remains a rare and specialized citrus variety.
