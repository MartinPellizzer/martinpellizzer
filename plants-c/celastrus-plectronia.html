Celastrus plectronia

Celastrus plectronia, commonly known as the Chinese bindweed, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries. Its stems are woody and often have a distinctive, fluted appearance.

Celastrus plectronia is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
