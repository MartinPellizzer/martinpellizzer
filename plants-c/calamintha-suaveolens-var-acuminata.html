Calamintha suaveolens var. acuminata

Calamintha suaveolens var. acuminata, commonly known as sweet germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a mild, mint-like scent.

Calamintha suaveolens var. acuminata is valued for its medicinal properties and ornamental appeal in gardens.
