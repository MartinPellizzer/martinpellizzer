Cyperus laevigatus var. caespitosus

Cyperus laevigatus var. caespitosus, commonly known as the tufted flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like stems that grow in dense clusters, often forming low, mat-like growths. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on short, leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly rough texture, and the plant is often found in wet or moist soils.

Cyperus laevigatus var. caespitosus is adapted to a variety of aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats, including marshes and pond edges.
