Cissus capreolata

Cissus capreolata, commonly known as the rock fig or climbing cissus, is a vining plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and ability to climb using aerial roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
