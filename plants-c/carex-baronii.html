Carex baronii

Carex baronii, commonly known as Baron's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silver-blue coloration.

Carex baronii produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by three-sided, triangular seed heads.
