Carex planiscapa

Carex planiscapa, commonly known as the flatleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, flat leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Carex planiscapa produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are often wind-pollinated.
