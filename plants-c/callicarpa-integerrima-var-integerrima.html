Callicarpa integerrima var. integerrima

Callicarpa integerrima var. integerrima, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant violet to purple berries that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and borne in clusters, which are followed by the showy berries.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.
