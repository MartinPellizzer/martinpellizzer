Cyperus nigricans var. simulans

Cyperus nigricans var. simulans, commonly known as the black flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive dark green color and a slightly rough texture.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
