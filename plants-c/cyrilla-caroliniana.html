Cyrilla caroliniana

Cyrilla caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy appearance. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that splits open to release seeds.

Cyrilla caroliniana is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.
