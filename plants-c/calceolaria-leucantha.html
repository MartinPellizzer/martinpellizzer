Calceolaria leucantha

Calceolaria leucantha, commonly known as the white hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-like hood that encloses the petals, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale pink, often with a velvety texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Calceolaria leucantha is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a potted plant.
