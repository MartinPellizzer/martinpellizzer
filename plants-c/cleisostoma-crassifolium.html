Cleisostoma crassifolium

Cleisostoma crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed cleisostoma, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features thick, leathery leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for cultivation.
