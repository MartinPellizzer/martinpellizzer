Cyperus aromaticus var. elatior

Cyperus aromaticus var. elatior, commonly known as the sweet grass or lemon grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tall, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in perfumery and traditional medicine. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of light conditions.
