Cadaba parvula

Cadaba parvula, commonly known as the small-leaved balsam, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in areas with high salinity. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cadaba parvula is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
