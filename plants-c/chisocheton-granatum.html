Chisocheton granatum

Chisocheton granatum, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Chisocheton granatum is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
