Coleus englerastrum

Coleus englerastrum, commonly known as the red-flowered coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and often display vibrant shades of red, purple, and green. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters at the ends of branches and are pollinated by insects.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
