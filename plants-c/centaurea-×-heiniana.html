Centaurea × heiniana

Centaurea × heiniana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous tiny florets arranged in a composite structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. Native to Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
