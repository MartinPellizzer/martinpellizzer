Calea longipes

Calea longipes, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its unique floral behavior. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a velvety texture.

Calea longipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
