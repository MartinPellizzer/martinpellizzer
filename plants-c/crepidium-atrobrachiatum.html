Crepidium atrobrachiatum

Crepidium atrobrachiatum, commonly known as the black-bracted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery leaves and a distinctive inflorescence that bears small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
