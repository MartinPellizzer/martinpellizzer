Chamaesyce urbanii

Chamaesyce urbanii, commonly known as the urban weed or city weed, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe.

It typically has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny greenish-white flowers. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and urban environments, making it a common sight in pavement cracks and along sidewalks. It is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize areas with minimal care.

The species is also known for its resilience to environmental stressors, which contributes to its widespread distribution.
