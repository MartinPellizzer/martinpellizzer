Calea lobata

Calea lobata, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, white, waxy flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its unique nocturnal blooming behavior. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Calea lobata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
