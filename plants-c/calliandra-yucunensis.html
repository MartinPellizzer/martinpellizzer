Calliandra yucunensis

Calliandra yucunensis, commonly known as the Yucun tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy flower clusters. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The leaves are bipinnate, and the flowers are often pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
