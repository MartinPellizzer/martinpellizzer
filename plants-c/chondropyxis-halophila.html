Chondropyxis halophila

Chondropyxis halophila, commonly known as the saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is native to regions with high salt content in the soil, such as coastal areas and arid zones. The plant has small, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss and tolerate high salinity. It produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Chondropyxis halophila is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils in harsh conditions.
