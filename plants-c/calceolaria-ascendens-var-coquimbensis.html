Calceolaria ascendens var. coquimbensis

Calceolaria ascendens var. coquimbensis, commonly known as the Coquimbo slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyces that resemble small slippers, which are a key feature of the Calceolaria genus. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils along the Pacific coast, thriving in maritime climates with cool temperatures and moderate rainfall. Its flowers are usually pale to deep blue or violet, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
