Carduus multiflorus

Carduus multiflorus, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Carduus multiflorus typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

It has been used historically in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
