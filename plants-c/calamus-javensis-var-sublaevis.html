Calamus javensis var. sublaevis

Calamus javensis var. sublaevis, commonly known as the smooth calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tropical evergreen plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic materials.

The varietal form sublaevis is distinguished by its smoother, less hairy leaves compared to other varieties.
