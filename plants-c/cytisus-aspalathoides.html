Cytisus aspalathoides

Cytisus aspalathoides, commonly known as the yellow bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant has small, leathery leaves and a woody stem, typical of many members of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
