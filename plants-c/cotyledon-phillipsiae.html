Cotyledon phillipsiae

Cotyledon phillipsiae, commonly known as the "Cape Tulip," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern, providing structural support and aiding in water storage.

Cotyledon phillipsiae is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
