Crotalaria caerulea

Crotalaria caerulea, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or bean family. The plant is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the early morning. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Crotalaria caerulea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
