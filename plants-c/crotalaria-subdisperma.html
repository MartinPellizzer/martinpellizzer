Crotalaria subdisperma

Crotalaria subdisperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf horsegram, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves and yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in cylindrical pods and are often used as a leguminous crop in some parts of the world.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in agricultural systems.
