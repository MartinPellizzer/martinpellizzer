Cissus welwitschii

Cissus welwitschii, commonly known as the Devil's Backbone, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features distinctive, waxy, dark green leaves that are elongated and often have a zigzag or serrated edge. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Cissus welwitschii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and resilience.
