Cyperus lanatus

Cyperus lanatus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

Cyperus lanatus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
