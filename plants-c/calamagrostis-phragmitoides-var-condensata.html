Calamagrostis phragmitoides var. condensata

Calamagrostis phragmitoides var. condensata, commonly known as silver reed, is a perennial grass native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features tall, slender culms that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

Its dense growth habit makes it an effective plant for erosion control and wetland restoration projects.
