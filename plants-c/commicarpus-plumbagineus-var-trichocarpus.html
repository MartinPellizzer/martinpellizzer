Commicarpus plumbagineus var. trichocarpus

Commicarpus plumbagineus var. trichocarpus, commonly known as the woolly thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
