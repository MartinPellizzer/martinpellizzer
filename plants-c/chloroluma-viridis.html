Chloroluma viridis

Chloroluma viridis, commonly known as the green chloroluma, is a unique plant species characterized by its vibrant green foliage.

It belongs to the family Chlorolaceae and is native to specific tropical regions. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are dense with chlorophyll, contributing to its striking coloration. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded environments.

The botanical interest of Chloroluma viridis lies in its unusual pigmentation and adaptability to humid, low-light conditions.
