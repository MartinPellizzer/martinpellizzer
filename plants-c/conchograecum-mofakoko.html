Conchograecum mofakoko

Conchograecum mofakoko, commonly known as the Zulu orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring and are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
