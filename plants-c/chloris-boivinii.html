Chloris boivinii

Chloris boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Chloris boivinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
