Carex lepidocarpa subsp. ferraria

Carex lepidocarpa subsp.

ferraria, commonly known as the serrated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in riparian zones and swampy areas.

The subspecies ferraria is distinguished by its serrated leaf margins and robust growth habit.
