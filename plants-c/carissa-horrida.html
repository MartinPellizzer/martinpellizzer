Carissa horrida

Carissa horrida, commonly known as the rough carissa, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe.

The plant is known for its thorny stems and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.
