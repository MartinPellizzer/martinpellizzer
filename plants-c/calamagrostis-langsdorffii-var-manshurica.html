Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. manshurica

Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. manshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian reed, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
