Ceratostylis papuana

Ceratostylis papuana, commonly known as the Papuan horned orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that resemble horns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its rhizomes are fibrous, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip.

Ceratostylis papuana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
