Cytisus albus f. durus

Cytisus albus f. durus, commonly known as the white broom or white genista, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, white, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and fragrant blossoms.
