Carinta

Carinta, commonly known as the wild chervil, is a delicate annual herb belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It features slender, branching stems and finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of parsley. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Carinta is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are sometimes used in culinary and medicinal applications.
