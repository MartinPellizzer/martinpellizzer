Centaurea × subdecurrens subsp. albuferae

Centaurea × subdecurrens subsp.

albuferae, commonly known as the Albufera centaury, is a hybrid species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and striking blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its flowers are composite, consisting of ray florets and tubular disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
