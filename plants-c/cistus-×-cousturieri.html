Cistus × cousturieri

Cistus × cousturieri, commonly known as the Corsican rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which includes various species of evergreen shrubs characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy flowers. This particular species is notable for its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
