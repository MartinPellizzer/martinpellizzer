Cistus monspeliensis var. parviflorus

Cistus monspeliensis var. parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its aromatic leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

The species is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
