Callianthe sulcatarinensis

Callianthe sulcatarinensis, commonly known as the Sulcatarinensis Cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Callianthe sulcatarinensis is valued for its resilience and is often cultivated in xerophytic gardens.
