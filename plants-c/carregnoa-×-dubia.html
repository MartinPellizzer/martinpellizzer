Carregnoa × dubia

Carregnoa × dubia, commonly known as the hybrid fig tree, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carregnoa resinosa and Ficus carica.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and the production of edible, fig-like fruits. The plant is typically found in regions with a warm, temperate climate, often thriving in well-drained soils. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a bridge between two distinct fig species, showcasing genetic diversity.

The fruit of Carregnoa × dubia is often used in local cuisines and has been studied for its potential agricultural and medicinal applications.
