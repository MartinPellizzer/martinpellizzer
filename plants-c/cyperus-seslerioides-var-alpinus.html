Cyperus seslerioides var. alpinus

Cyperus seslerioides var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to cold, mountainous environments.
