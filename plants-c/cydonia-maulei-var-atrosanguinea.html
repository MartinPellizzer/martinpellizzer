Cydonia maulei var. atrosanguinea

Cydonia maulei var. atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black blood quince, is a variant of the quince species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark red to almost black bark and foliage, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, aromatic fruits in the fall. Its botanical features include a shrub or small tree form with smooth, fissured bark and alternate, ovate leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and unique fruit characteristics.
