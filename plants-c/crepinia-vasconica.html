Crepinia vasconica

Crepinia vasconica, commonly known as the Vasconian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as hillsides and woodland edges. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Crepinia vasconica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
