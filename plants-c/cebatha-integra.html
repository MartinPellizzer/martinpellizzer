Cebatha integra

Cebatha integra, commonly known as the Indian nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging nettles. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and small, greenish-white flowers that grow in clusters. Its stems are covered in fine hairs that can cause a skin irritation when touched.

Cebatha integra is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
