Carex infuscata var. erostrata

Carex infuscata var. erostrata, commonly known as the darkened sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like structures that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
