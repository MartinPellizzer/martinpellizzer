Conyza procera

Conyza procera, commonly known as goatweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa, South Asia, and Australia.

It features a slender, branching stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with fine, hairy hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a serrated edge.

Conyza procera is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural environments.
