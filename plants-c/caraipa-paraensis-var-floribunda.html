Caraipa paraensis var. floribunda

Caraipa paraensis var. floribunda, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in shaded, humid environments. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
