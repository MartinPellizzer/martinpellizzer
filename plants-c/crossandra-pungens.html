Crossandra pungens

Crossandra pungens, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and glandular hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Crossandra pungens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
