Carex melinacra var. melinacra

Carex melinacra var. melinacra, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. This species typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a single, compact spike of small, three-lobed flowers.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
