Cremosperma hirsutissimum var. album

Cremosperma hirsutissimum var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered crepe myrtle, is a variant of the crepe myrtle species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its showy, white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has a shrub or small tree form, with exfoliating bark that reveals a mottled pattern. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, white hairs, contributing to its hirsute appearance.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
