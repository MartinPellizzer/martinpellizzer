Chromolaena bullata

Chromolaena bullata, commonly known as the silverleaf weed, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies.

Chromolaena bullata is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resistance to drought conditions.
