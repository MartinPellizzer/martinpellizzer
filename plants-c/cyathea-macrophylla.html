Cyathea macrophylla

Cyathea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and longevity. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive dimorphic structure where the lower fronds are smaller and the upper ones are larger.

Cyathea macrophylla plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various organisms.
