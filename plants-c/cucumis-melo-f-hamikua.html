Cucumis melo f. hamikua

Cucumis melo f. hamikua, commonly known as the honeydew melon, is a botanical variant of the cantaloupe belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green rind and sweet, aromatic, pale yellow to cream-colored flesh. The fruit typically has a rounded shape with a slight depression at the top, known as the "hub.". It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its high water content and mild flavor, making it a popular choice for fresh consumption.
