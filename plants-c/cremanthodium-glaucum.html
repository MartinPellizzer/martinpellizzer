Cremanthodium glaucum

Cremanthodium glaucum, commonly known as the bluehead fritillary, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are usually basal, with a glaucous coating that gives the plant its common name.

Cremanthodium glaucum is an important component of alpine flora and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
