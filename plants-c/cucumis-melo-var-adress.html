Cucumis melo var. adress

Cucumis melo var. adress, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon characterized by its elongated, slender shape and smooth, thin skin.

It typically has a pale green color with a slightly ribbed surface, and its flesh is crisp and juicy, often with a mild, sweet flavor. This variety is native to the Middle East and is cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant produces yellow flowers and is typically harvested when the fruit is fully matured and slightly firm.
