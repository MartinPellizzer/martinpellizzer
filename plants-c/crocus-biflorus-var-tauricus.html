Crocus biflorus var. tauricus

Crocus biflorus var. tauricus, commonly known as the Taurian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, nodding shape, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early-season color.

The species is also noted for its resilience to cold temperatures and its ability to naturalize over time.
