Clausena anisata

Clausena anisata, commonly known as wild mango or Indian wild mango, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by yellow, fleshy fruits resembling small mangoes. The fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
