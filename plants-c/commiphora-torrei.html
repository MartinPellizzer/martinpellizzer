Commiphora torrei

Commiphora torrei, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by unique, aromatic resinous exudates. These resins have been traditionally used for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
