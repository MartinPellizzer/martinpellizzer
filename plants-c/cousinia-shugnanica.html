Cousinia shugnanica

Cousinia shugnanica, commonly known as the Shugnan cushion flower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosette of silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Cousinia shugnanica is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
