Clematis lasiandra var. nagasawae

Clematis lasiandra var. nagasawae, commonly known as Nagasawa's Clematis, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, usually white or pale pink flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically hairy.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
