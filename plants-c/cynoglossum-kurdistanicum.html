Cynoglossum kurdistanicum

Cynoglossum kurdistanicum, commonly known as Kurdish dead-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.

Cynoglossum kurdistanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
