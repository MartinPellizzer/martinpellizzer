Cyperus wallichii

Cyperus wallichii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India and Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in moist environments.
