Carex argunensis subsp. alticola

Carex argunensis subsp.

alticola, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of Asia. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine meadows and mountainous areas. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate frost, making it a hardy species.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
