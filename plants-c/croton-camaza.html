Croton camaza

Croton camaza, commonly known as the red croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically broad and vary in color from green to red, orange, or yellow, depending on the variety. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
