Chrysochosma lemmonii

Chrysochosma lemmonii, commonly known as the desert goldstar, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small sunflowers. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture conditions.

The leaves are typically small, narrow, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.
