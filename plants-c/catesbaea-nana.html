Catesbaea nana

Catesbaea nana, commonly known as dwarf satinwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds.

Catesbaea nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
