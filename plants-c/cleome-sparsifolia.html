Cleome sparsifolia

Cleome sparsifolia, commonly known as the spider flower, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, spider-like blooms that are typically pink or white, with five narrow petals. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cleome sparsifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
