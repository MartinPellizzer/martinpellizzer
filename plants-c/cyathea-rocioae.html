Cyathea rocioae

Cyathea rocioae, commonly known as the Rocio's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Cyathea rocioae is known for its slow growth and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat.

Its unique morphology makes it a striking addition to both natural ecosystems and botanical collections.
