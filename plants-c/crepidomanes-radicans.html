Crepidomanes radicans

Crepidomanes radicans, commonly known as the creeping wall lettuce, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with fleshy, succulent leaves that form a dense mat on the ground. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and adaptable species suitable for various ecological niches.
