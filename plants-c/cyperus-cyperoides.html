Cyperus cyperoides

Cyperus cyperoides, commonly known as flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils and often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. It produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, which are usually inconspicuous and arranged in dense inflorescences.

Cyperus cyperoides plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
