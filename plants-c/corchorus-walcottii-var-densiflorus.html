Corchorus walcottii var. densiflorus

Corchorus walcottii var. densiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. Its leaves are broadly ovate with wavy margins and a rough texture.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
