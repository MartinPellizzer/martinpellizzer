Cinnamomum hartmanii

Cinnamomum hartmanii, commonly known as Hartman's cinnamon, is a species of tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and oval-shaped berries that contain seeds. Its bark is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
