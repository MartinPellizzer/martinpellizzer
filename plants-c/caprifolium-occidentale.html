Caprifolium occidentale

Caprifolium occidentale, commonly known as the western honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often appearing in clusters. These flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden environments.
