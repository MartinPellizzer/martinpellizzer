Caltha palustris lusus polysepala

Caltha palustris lusus polysepala, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a loose, spiral-like inflorescence. The plant has slender, creeping rhizomes and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats, and is often found in USDA hardiness zones 3 to 7.

Due to its unique floral structure and ecological significance, it is a valued plant in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
