Commiphora cyclophylla

Commiphora cyclophylla, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is notable for exuding a fragrant resin, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural and commercial purposes.
