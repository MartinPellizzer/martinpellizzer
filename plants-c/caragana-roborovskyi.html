Caragana roborovskyi

Caragana roborovskyi, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 pairs of small, oval leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and deep root systems.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for erosion control and as a source of forage in its native range.
