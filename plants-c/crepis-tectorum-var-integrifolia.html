Crepis tectorum var. integrifolia

Crepis tectorum var. integrifolia, commonly known as the common wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste places. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a distinctive silvery-blue underside.

The flowers bloom in spring and early summer, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
