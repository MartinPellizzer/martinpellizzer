Calamus scipionum

Calamus scipionum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by the development of coconut-like fruits. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Calamus scipionum plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
