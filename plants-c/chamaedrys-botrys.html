Chamaedrys botrys

Chamaedrys botrys, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive nodding flowers that resemble small snowflakes, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in woodland and shady garden environments, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

Chamaedrys botrys is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
