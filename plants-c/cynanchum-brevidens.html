Cynanchum brevidens

Cynanchum brevidens, commonly known as the short-stemmed dogbane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its opposite leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains various alkaloids, some of which have been studied for their medicinal properties. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can be found in forests and along stream banks.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
