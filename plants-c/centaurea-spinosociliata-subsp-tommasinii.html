Centaurea spinosociliata subsp. tommasinii

Centaurea spinosociliata subsp.

tommasinii, commonly known as the Tommasinii thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, reddish stems and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
