Cyperus ignoratus

Cyperus ignoratus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Cyperus ignoratus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

It is generally not cultivated for ornamental purposes but plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
