Campylotropis reticulata

Campylotropis reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern, and its small, pea-like flowers are typically pink or purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, where it plays a role in nitrogen fixation through its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
