Chenopodium salsum

Chenopodium salsum, commonly known as seaside spinach, is a halophytic plant adapted to grow in saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

Chenopodium salsum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh coastal conditions.
