Crataegus azarolus var. turcomanica

Crataegus azarolus var. turcomanica, commonly known as the Turcoman hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and small, oval leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing tolerance to drought and poor soil conditions.

This variety is of interest in horticulture for its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits.
