Calamintha incana f. calvescens

Calamintha incana f. calvescens, commonly known as woolly blueweed, is a subspecies of the herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage that is covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
