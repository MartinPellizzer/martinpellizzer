Cirsium nebraskense var. discissum

Cirsium nebraskense var. discissum, commonly known as the Nebraska thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Great Plains of North America.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and large, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces and a distinctive basal rosette. This species is adapted to dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairie habitats.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is considered a native wildflower in its range.
