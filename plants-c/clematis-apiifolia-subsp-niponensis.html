Clematis apiifolia subsp. niponensis

Clematis apiifolia subsp.

niponensis, commonly known as Japanese Clematis, is a flowering plant native to Japan. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers.

The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade. Its flowers, which are usually blue or purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slender stems and smaller, more delicate foliage compared to other Clematis species.
