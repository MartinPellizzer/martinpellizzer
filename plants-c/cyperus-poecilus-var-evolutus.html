Cyperus poecilus var. evolutus

Cyperus poecilus var. evolutus, commonly known as the broadleaf paper plant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, with a dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water edges.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact form and ability to thrive in humid environments.
