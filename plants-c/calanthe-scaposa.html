Calanthe scaposa

Calanthe scaposa, commonly known as the scapose calanthe, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a single, erect spike bearing several white or pale pink flowers.

Calanthe scaposa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
