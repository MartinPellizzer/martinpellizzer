Chirita fordii var. dolichotricha

Chirita fordii var. dolichotricha, commonly known as the long-haired chirita, is a species of flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a waxy texture and produces clusters of vibrant orange or red flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its varietal name, *dolichotricha*, refers to its long, hairy stems and leaves.
