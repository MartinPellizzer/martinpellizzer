Carex hudsonii lusus verticillata

Carex hudsonii lusus verticillata, commonly known as the Hudson's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation, which helps in stabilizing soil.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the sedge family.
