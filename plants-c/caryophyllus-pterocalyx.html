Caryophyllus pterocalyx

Caryophyllus pterocalyx, commonly known as the winged caryophyll, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes many species with showy, often pink or white flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, leaf-like bracts that resemble wings, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with five petals that are often reflexed at the tips.

Caryophyllus pterocalyx is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
