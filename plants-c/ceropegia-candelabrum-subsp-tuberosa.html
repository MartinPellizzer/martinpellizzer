Ceropegia candelabrum subsp. tuberosa

Ceropegia candelabrum subsp.

tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous candelabra ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing stems and tuberous roots, which store water and nutrients.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
