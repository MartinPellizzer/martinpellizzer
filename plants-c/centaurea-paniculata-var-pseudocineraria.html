Centaurea paniculata var. pseudocineraria

Centaurea paniculata var. pseudocineraria, commonly known as the false cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branching stems and striking blue to purple flowers. The leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green coloration. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
