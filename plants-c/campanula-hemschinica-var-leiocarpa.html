Campanula hemschinica var. leiocarpa

Campanula hemschinica var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
