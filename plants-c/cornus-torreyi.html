Cornus torreyi

Cornus torreyi, commonly known as Torrey's dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its showy white bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in the fall, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
