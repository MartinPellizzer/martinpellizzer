Camunium bengalense

Camunium bengalense, commonly known as the Bengal camunium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or near water bodies.

Camunium bengalense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
