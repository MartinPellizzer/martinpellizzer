Calamagrostis arundinacea subsp. distantiflora

Calamagrostis arundinacea subsp.

distantiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered reed grass, is a perennial grass native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicle-shaped inflorescences that produce small, fluffy flowers.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, riverbanks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value.

It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
