Cyperus unioloides

Cyperus unioloides, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Cyperus unioloides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
