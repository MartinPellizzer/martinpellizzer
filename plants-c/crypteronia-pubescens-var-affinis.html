Crypteronia pubescens var. affinis

Crypteronia pubescens var. affinis, commonly known as the African starwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded form and glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
