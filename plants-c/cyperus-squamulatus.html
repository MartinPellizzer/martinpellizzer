Cyperus squamulatus

Cyperus squamulatus, commonly known as the flatleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and wetlands, where it thrives in moist soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive flat, scaly appearance on the lower stems.

Cyperus squamulatus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various water conditions.
