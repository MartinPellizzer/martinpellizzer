Chrysothamnus scoparius

Chrysothamnus scoparius, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments. It is adapted to dry conditions and has small, hairy leaves to reduce water loss.

Chrysothamnus scoparius plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and animals.
