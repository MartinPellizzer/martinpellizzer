Cheirolophus canariensis subsp. subexpinnata

Cheirolophus canariensis subsp.

subexpinnata, commonly known as the Canary Island bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained, sunny environments and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in Mediterranean and subtropical flora.
