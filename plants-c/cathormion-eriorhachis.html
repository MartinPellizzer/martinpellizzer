Cathormion eriorhachis

Cathormion eriorhachis, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its yellow flowers are typically arranged in a single head, with ray florets surrounding disk florets.

Cathormion eriorhachis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
