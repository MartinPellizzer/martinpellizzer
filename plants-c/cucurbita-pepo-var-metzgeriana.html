Cucurbita pepo var. metzgeriana

Cucurbita pepo var. metzgeriana, commonly known as the Mexican gourd or metzgerian pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a trailing vine that produces small, ornamental fruits with a distinctive warty texture. The plant is native to Mexico and is often cultivated for its unique appearance rather than for culinary use. Its flowers are typically yellow with a strong, sweet fragrance.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.
