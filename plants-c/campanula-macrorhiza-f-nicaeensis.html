Campanula macrorhiza f. nicaeensis

Campanula macrorhiza f. nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
