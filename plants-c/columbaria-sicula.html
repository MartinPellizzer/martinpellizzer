Columbaria sicula

Columbaria sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian columbaria, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its roots are fibrous and well adapted to the dry, rocky soils of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, Columbaria sicula is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
