Crotalaria axillifloroides var. gracilis

Crotalaria axillifloroides var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and prominent veins. This species belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and possesses the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
