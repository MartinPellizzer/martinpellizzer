Centaurea jacea var. croatica

Centaurea jacea var. croatica, commonly known as Croatian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a composite head.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
