Chaetoseris macrantha

Chaetoseris macrantha, commonly known as the large-headed composite, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, silver-haired leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, composite heads. Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
