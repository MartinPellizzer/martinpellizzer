Cerasus varia

Cerasus varia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its ornamental flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits known as cherries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn vibrant colors in autumn.

Cerasus varia is widely cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks.
