Crucifera teesdalea

Crucifera teesdalea, commonly known as the Teesdale crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It is native to the mountainous regions of the United Kingdom and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
