Cyclamen littorale

Cyclamen littorale, commonly known as the coastal cyclamen, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near coastal areas. Its blooming period typically occurs in spring, adding a touch of color to rocky or sandy landscapes.

Cyclamen littorale is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
