Crotalaria galeottii

Crotalaria galeottii, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be an important component of ecological restoration projects.

Crotalaria galeottii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
