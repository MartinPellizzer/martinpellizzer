Cyperus laxespicatus

Cyperus laxespicatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other similar plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and along water edges. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually narrow and have a fibrous texture.

Cyperus laxespicatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
