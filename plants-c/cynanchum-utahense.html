Cynanchum utahense

Cynanchum utahense, commonly known as Utah milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has adapted to arid environments through its deep root system. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces capsule fruits that contain toxic seeds.

Due to its medicinal properties and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
