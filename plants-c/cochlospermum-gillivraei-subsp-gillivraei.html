Cochlospermum gillivraei subsp. gillivraei

Cochlospermum gillivraei subsp.

gillivraei, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America. It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early spring.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
