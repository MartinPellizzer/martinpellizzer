Calathea foliosa

Calathea foliosa, commonly known as the veined calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricately patterned leaves that display a variety of colors, including green, silver, and deep red. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Calathea foliosa is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage.
