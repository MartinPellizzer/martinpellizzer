Campomanesia obversa var. angustifolia

Campomanesia obversa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wax apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are glossy and typically have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellow or orange berries. The fruit has a waxy coating and is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisine.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
