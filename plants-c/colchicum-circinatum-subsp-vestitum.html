Colchicum circinatum subsp. vestitum

Colchicum circinatum subsp.

vestitum, commonly known as the dressed autumn crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive purple-tipped style, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy layer, giving it its subspecies name "vestitum," meaning "cloaked" or "dressed.". It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
