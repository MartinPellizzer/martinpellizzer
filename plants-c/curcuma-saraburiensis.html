Curcuma saraburiensis

Curcuma saraburiensis, commonly known as the Saraburi turmeric, is a rhizomatous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-orange rhizomes. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.
