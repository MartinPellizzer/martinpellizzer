Carex ustulata var. aterrima

Carex ustulata var. aterrima, commonly known as the dark-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream edges. Its dark green foliage and subtle flowering structures make it a distinctive feature in wetland ecosystems.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
