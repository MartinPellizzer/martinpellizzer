Calathea roseobracteata

Calathea roseobracteata, commonly known as the rose-bract calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that display intricate patterns of deep green, silver, and pink. The plant features small, pinkish bracts that surround its inconspicuous white flowers. Calathea roseobracteata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
