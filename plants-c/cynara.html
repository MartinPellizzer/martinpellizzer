Cynara

Cynara, commonly known as artichoke, is a thistle-like plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces purple flower heads, which are actually clusters of tiny florets. The edible part of the plant is the immature flower bud, which is harvested before it opens.

Cynara is native to the Mediterranean region and is cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
