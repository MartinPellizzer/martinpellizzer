Coleus lactiflorus

Coleus lactiflorus, commonly known as the milkweed coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature and can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
