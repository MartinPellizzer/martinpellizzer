Crataegus vallicola

Crataegus vallicola, commonly known as the valley hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as stream valleys and woodland edges. The plant features opposite, oval to elliptical leaves that are often pubescent beneath. Its flowers are white or pink, arranged in cymes, and it produces small, red to orange berries in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
