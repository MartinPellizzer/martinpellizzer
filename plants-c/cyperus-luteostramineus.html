Cyperus luteostramineus

Cyperus luteostramineus, commonly known as the yellow-striped flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with a waxy coating, giving them a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically pale green or yellowish in color.

Cyperus luteostramineus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, where it thrives in shallow water or saturated soil.
