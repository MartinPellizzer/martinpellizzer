Cuphea spicata f. grandifolia

Cuphea spicata f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered cuphea, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in dense spikes, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
