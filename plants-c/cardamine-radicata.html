Cardamine radicata

Cardamine radicata, commonly known as watercress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and small white or greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. It has a distinctive peppery flavor and is often used in salads or as a garnish.

The leaves are edible and contain various nutrients, including vitamins A and C.
