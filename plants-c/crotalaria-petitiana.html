Crotalaria petitiana

Crotalaria petitiana, commonly known as the small-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features erect stems with opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow with distinctive black markings, and it produces pods containing kidney-shaped seeds.

Crotalaria petitiana is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
