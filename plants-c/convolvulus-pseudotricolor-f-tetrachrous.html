Convolvulus pseudotricolor f. tetrachrous

Convolvulus pseudotricolor f. tetrachrous, commonly known as the four-colored morning glory, is a variant of the common morning glory that exhibits a striking four-color pattern in its flowers.

This plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The flowers typically display a gradient of colors, ranging from white to pink, red, and purple, creating a visually appealing display. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
