Chaetogastra weddelliana

Chaetogastra weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. The plant features fine, wiry leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
