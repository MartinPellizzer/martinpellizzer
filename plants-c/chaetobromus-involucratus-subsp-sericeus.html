Chaetobromus involucratus subsp. sericeus

Chaetobromus involucratus subsp.

sericeus, commonly known as the silky chaetobromus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from pale yellow to orange.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely hairy, or sericeous, leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
