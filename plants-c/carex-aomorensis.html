Carex aomorensis

Carex aomorensis, commonly known as the Aomori sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Japan, specifically the Aomori Prefecture, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Carex aomorensis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity in its native habitat.
