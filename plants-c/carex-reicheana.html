Carex reicheana

Carex reicheana, commonly known as the Reiche's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex reicheana plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
