Chorizandra multiarticulata

Chorizandra multiarticulata, commonly known as the many-jointed cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, cylindrical stems that are covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it stores water in its fleshy stems for survival in arid conditions.

Chorizandra multiarticulata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
