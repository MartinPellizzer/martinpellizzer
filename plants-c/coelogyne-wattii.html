Coelogyne wattii

Coelogyne wattii, commonly known as the Watt's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid forest environments. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single, lance-shaped leaf and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its blooms are often clustered in a raceme and can last for several weeks.

Coelogyne wattii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
