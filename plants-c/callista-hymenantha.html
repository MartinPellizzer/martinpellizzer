Callista hymenantha

Callista hymenantha, commonly known as the silverleaf lily, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish bloom, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid environment to flourish.

Callista hymenantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.
