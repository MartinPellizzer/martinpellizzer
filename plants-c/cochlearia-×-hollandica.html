Cochlearia × hollandica

Cochlearia × hollandica, commonly known as the Dutch cochlearia, is a hybrid plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically has hairy, lobed leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal or maritime environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
