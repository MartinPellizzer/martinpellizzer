Coffea canephora var. laurentii

Coffea canephora var. laurentii, commonly known as the African coffee bean, is a variety of the robusta coffee species.

It is native to Central and West Africa, where it thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soils. This plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, red berries containing two seeds. It is known for its strong flavor profile and higher caffeine content compared to other coffee varieties.

The plant is typically grown in shaded conditions and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.
