Cucularia bulbosa

Cucularia bulbosa, commonly known as the bladder mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a distinctive, inflated fruit that resembles a bladder, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cucularia bulbosa is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
