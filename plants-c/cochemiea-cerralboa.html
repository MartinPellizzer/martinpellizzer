Cochemiea cerralboa

Cochemiea cerralboa, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly, gray-green foliage and produces showy clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is a low-maintenance plant that is well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates.

Cochemiea cerralboa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
