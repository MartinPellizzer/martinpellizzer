Clusia schomburgkii

Clusia schomburgkii, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and often have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It is adapted to humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Clusia schomburgkii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage.
