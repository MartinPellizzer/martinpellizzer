Coreopsis grandiflora f. harveyana

Coreopsis grandiflora f. harveyana, commonly known as the Harvey's tickseed, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers with a distinctive red eye, blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
