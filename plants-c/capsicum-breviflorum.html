Capsicum breviflorum

Capsicum breviflorum, commonly known as the dwarf chili or small-flowered pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small to medium-sized fruits that range in color from yellow to orange and red. Its botanical features include simple, ovate leaves and a short, branched stem.

It is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes due to its unique appearance and mild flavor.
