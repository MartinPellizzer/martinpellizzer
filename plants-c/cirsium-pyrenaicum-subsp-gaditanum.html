Cirsium pyrenaicum subsp. gaditanum

Cirsium pyrenaicum subsp.

gaditanum, commonly known as the Pyrenean thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous regions. It produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits that disperse seeds via wind.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
