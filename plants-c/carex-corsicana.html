Carex corsicana

Carex corsicana, commonly known as the Corsican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica, Sardinia, and parts of southern Italy. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in stream banks and wet meadows. It has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and sword-shaped, with a dense, compact growth habit.

The flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and the plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.
