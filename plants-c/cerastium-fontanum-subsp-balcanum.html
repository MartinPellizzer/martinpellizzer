Cerastium fontanum subsp. balcanum

Cerastium fontanum subsp.

balcanum, commonly known as the Balkan mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy surface, adapted to the cool, moist environments it inhabits.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or gravelly soils along streambanks and in woodland edges.
