Croton antisyphiliticus var. intermedius

Croton antisyphiliticus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with a mix of green, yellow, and red hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
