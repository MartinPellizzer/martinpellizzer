Crocus subapenninus

Crocus subapenninus, commonly known as the Apennine crocus, is a species native to southern Europe, particularly the Apennine Mountains.

It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces striking violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in woodland edges and open slopes, thriving in temperate climates.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and have a short blooming period, making it a sought-after ornamental plant.
