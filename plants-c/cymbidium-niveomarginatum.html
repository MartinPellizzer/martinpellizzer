Cymbidium niveomarginatum

Cymbidium niveomarginatum, commonly known as the white-edged orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that produces tall, slender stems with clusters of fragrant, white flowers bordered with pale pink or white margins. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in two rows along the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
