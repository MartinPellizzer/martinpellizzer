Cereus pentlandii var. roseovenosus

Cereus pentlandii var. roseovenosus, commonly known as the rose-veined torch cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often adorned with distinctive pink or red veins. The plant produces showy, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pale pink, opening at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and unique flowering habits, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
