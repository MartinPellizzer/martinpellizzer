Capparis scortechinii var. scortechinii

Capparis scortechinii var. scortechinii, commonly known as the African sour fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

The berries are edible when ripe but have a sour taste, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
