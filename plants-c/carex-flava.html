Carex flava

Carex flava, commonly known as yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Carex flava thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
