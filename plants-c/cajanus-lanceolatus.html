Cajanus lanceolatus

Cajanus lanceolatus, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional value. It plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.

Cajanus lanceolatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
