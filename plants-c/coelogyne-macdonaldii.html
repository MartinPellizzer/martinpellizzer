Coelogyne macdonaldii

Coelogyne macdonaldii, commonly known as the Macdonald's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces racemes of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns and are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.

Coelogyne macdonaldii is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in its native habitat.
