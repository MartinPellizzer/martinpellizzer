Crinodendron boliviensis

Crinodendron boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian flame tree, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and seasonal flowering make it a notable species in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
