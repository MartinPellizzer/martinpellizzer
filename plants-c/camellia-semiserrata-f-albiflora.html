Camellia semiserrata f. albiflora

Camellia semiserrata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered camellia, is a botanical variant of the camellia plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves and produces elliptical, red or orange fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its horticultural value lies in its elegant appearance and ability to add winter color to landscapes.
