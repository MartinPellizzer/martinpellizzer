Clematis vitalba var. syriaca

Clematis vitalba var. syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian traveler's joy, is a climbing vine native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny to partially shaded locations with well-drained soil.

This variety is known for its vigorous growth and historical use in traditional medicine.
