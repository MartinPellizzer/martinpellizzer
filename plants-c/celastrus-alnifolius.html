Celastrus alnifolius

Celastrus alnifolius, commonly known as the Almondleaf bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a noxious weed in certain regions.
