Caladium kochii

Caladium kochii, commonly known as the "Cochineal Caladium," is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that display vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. Its foliage is typically variegated, with contrasting patterns that make it visually appealing.

Caladium kochii thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.
