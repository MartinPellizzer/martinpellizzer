Cassia chamaecrista var. cinerea

Cassia chamaecrista var. cinerea, commonly known as the silver cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a silvery-gray underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

The species is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
