Carex lupulina

Carex lupulina, commonly known as the hop sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along stream banks.

Carex lupulina is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
