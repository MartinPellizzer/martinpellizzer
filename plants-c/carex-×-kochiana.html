Carex × kochiana

Carex × kochiana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex × kochiana is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
