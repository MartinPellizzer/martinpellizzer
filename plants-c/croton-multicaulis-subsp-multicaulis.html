Croton multicaulis subsp. multicaulis

Croton multicaulis subsp.

multicaulis, commonly known as the branched croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
