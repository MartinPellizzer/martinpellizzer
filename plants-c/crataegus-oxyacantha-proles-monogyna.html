Crataegus oxyacantha proles monogyna

Crataegus oxyacantha proles monogyna, commonly known as the single-fruit hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval leaves, and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Native to Europe and western Asia, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
