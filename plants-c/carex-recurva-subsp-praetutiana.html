Carex recurva subsp. praetutiana

Carex recurva subsp.

praetutiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments.

The plant typically grows in shaded, damp areas such as woodlands and stream banks, where it forms dense clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
