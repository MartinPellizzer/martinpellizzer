Cleisostoma dichroanthum

Cleisostoma dichroanthum, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that display a striking two-tone appearance. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, while the flowers bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pink.

Cleisostoma dichroanthum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in humid, shaded environments.
