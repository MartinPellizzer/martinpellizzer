Cyperus teretifolius

Cyperus teretifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a tight spiral. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

Cyperus teretifolius is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
