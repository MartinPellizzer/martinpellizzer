Conyza bovei

Conyza bovei, commonly known as the Brazilian ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Conyza bovei is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
