Crepis pseudovirens

Crepis pseudovirens, commonly known as the false wiregrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-gray coloration.

Crepis pseudovirens is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
