Cyperus multicaulis

Cyperus multicaulis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often slightly hairy, and its leaves are narrow and linear. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus multicaulis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, grass-like appearance and adaptability to moist environments.
