Camellia wilkesiana

Camellia wilkesiana, commonly known as the Wilkes camellia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Camellia wilkesiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
