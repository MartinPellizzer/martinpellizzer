Cremosperma anisophyllum

Cremosperma anisophyllum, commonly known as the variable cypress, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, scale-like leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain small, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suited for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and resilient choice for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
