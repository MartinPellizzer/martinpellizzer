Chenopodiastrum

Chenopodiastrum, commonly known as goosefoot, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

These plants are typically annual or biennial herbs characterized by their distinctive broad, often succulent leaves that resemble a goose's foot. Chenopodiastrum species are often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. They produce small, clustered flowers that are usually green or red, and their seeds are dispersed by wind.

Some species are cultivated for their edible leaves or as ornamental plants due to their unique foliage.
