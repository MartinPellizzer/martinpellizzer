Carex tenerrima

Carex tenerrima, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Native to various regions in Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
