Carex cataractae

Carex cataractae, commonly known as the cascade sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in stream banks, marshes, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and grow in dense clusters. Its flowering spikes are cylindrical and produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex cataractae plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
