Cyphostemma allophylloides subsp. karuraense

Cyphostemma allophylloides subsp.

karuraense, commonly known as the Karura moonvine, is a climbing plant native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds.

This species is often found in woodland and scrub habitats, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance and traditional uses.
