Crataegus lennoniana

Crataegus lennoniana, commonly known as Lennon's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features thorny branches, which are a characteristic feature of the Crataegus genus. The plant produces white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and it is often found in moist, wooded areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
