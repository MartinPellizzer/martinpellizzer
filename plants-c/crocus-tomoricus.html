Crocus tomoricus

Crocus tomoricus, commonly known as the Japanese crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded corms as its storage organs. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value and early blooming habit make it a popular choice among gardeners.
