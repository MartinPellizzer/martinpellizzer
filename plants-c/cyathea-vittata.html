Cyathea vittata

Cyathea vittata, commonly known as the golden tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive golden-brown coloration on its trunk, which becomes more pronounced with age. Cyathea vittata is a slow-growing plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in New Zealand's flora.
