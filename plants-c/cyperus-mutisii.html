Cyperus mutisii

Cyperus mutisii, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its slender, grass-like stems are often found in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus mutisii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
