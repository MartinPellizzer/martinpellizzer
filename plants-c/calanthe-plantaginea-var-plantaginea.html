Calanthe plantaginea var. plantaginea

Calanthe plantaginea var. plantaginea, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its striking, pinkish-white inflorescence that resembles a tresses of hair. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy flowers arranged in a spiral along the stem. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
