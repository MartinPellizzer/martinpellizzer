Callisia serrulata

Callisia serrulata, commonly known as the serrated spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have serrated edges, which give the plant its scientific name. It produces vibrant purple or blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
