Cistus salviifolius proles arrigens

Cistus salviifolius proles arrigens, commonly known as the Salvia-leaved Rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
