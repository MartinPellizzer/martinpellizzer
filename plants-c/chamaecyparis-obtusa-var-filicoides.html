Chamaecyparis obtusa var. filicoides

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. filicoides, commonly known as the Japanese white cedar or Japanese cypress, is a variant of the Japanese cedar characterized by its narrow, feather-like foliage.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan, typically growing in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. The plant has scale-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a feathery appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous cones. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant form and year-round foliage.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and partial shade.
