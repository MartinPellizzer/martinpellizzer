Calceolaria angulata

Calceolaria angulata, commonly known as the angular slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with a unique pouch-like structure that attracts pollinators.

Calceolaria angulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
