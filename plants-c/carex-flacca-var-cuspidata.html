Carex flacca var. cuspidata

Carex flacca var. cuspidata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
