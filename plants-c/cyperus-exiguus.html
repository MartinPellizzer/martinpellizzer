Cyperus exiguus

Cyperus exiguus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Cyperus exiguus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions and is adapted to thrive in saturated soil conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to wetland biodiversity.
