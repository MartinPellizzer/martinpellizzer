Calopappus acerosus

Calopappus acerosus, commonly known as the rigid calopappus, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sunflower-like structure. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and along stream banks.

Calopappus acerosus is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.
