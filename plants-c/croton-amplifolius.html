Croton amplifolius

Croton amplifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

Croton amplifolius thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
