Crotalaria boranica subsp. boranica

Crotalaria boranica subsp.

boranica, commonly known as the African horsebean, is a flowering plant native to Africa. It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring erect stems and alternate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by bean-like pods containing several seeds. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
