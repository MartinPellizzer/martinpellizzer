Campylotheca polycephala

Campylotheca polycephala, commonly known as the clustered flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
