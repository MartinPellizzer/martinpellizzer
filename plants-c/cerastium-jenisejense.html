Cerastium jenisejense

Cerastium jenisejense, commonly known as the Siberian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in alpine and subarctic environments, where it withstands harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with similar environmental requirements.
