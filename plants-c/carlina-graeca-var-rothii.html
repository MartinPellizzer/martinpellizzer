Carlina graeca var. rothii

Carlina graeca var. rothii, commonly known as the Greek carline thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a woolly texture. The plant produces tall, sparsely branched stems that bear purple to pink flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
