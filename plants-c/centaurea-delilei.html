Centaurea delilei

Centaurea delilei, commonly known as the Delile's cornflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Centaurea delilei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
