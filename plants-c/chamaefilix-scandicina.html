Chamaefilix scandicina

Chamaefilix scandicina, commonly known as the creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as ground cover.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage.
