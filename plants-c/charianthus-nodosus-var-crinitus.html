Charianthus nodosus var. crinitus

Charianthus nodosus var. crinitus, commonly known as the hairy nodding bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, with a distinctive nodding habit. The plant has hairy foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "crinitus," meaning "hairy" in Latin. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
