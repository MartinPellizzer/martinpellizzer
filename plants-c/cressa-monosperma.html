Cressa monosperma

Cressa monosperma, commonly known as the African cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers and delicate, fern-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Cressa monosperma is also valued for its edible leaves, which have a peppery flavor similar to watercress.
