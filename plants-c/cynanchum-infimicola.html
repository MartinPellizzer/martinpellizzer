Cynanchum infimicola

Cynanchum infimicola, commonly known as the lesser milkweed vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which is known for its milkweed species, and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its latex sap is milky and can be toxic.

Cynanchum infimicola is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade and moist soil.
