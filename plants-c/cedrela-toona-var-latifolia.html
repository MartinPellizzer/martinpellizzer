Cedrela toona var. latifolia

Cedrela toona var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf toona, is a tropical tree species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are typically broad and have a smooth, glossy surface. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical fruits containing numerous flat, seed-like structures. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various forms of wildlife.
