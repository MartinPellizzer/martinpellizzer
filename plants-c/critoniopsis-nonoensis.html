Critoniopsis nonoensis

Critoniopsis nonoensis, commonly known as the Nono Island grass, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
