Camaridium cacaoense

Camaridium cacaoense, commonly known as the West African cacao tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of West Africa.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and produces small, white flowers that bloom directly on the trunk and branches. The plant is renowned for its fruit, which contains the seeds used to make cocoa products. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

Camaridium cacaoense plays a vital role in both local ecosystems and the global chocolate industry.
