Cycas revoluta var. inermis

Cycas revoluta var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth cycad, is a unique variant of the cycad species native to southern China.

It is characterized by its smooth, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces cones, with the male cones typically appearing at the base of the plant and the female cones at the top. It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ancient lineage, it is considered a living fossil and has significant ecological and evolutionary importance.
