Cyperus aureolus

Cyperus aureolus, commonly known as the golden bulb sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often yellow or brown in color. Cyperus aureolus is typically found in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
