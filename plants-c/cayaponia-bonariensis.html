Cayaponia bonariensis

Cayaponia bonariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum bonariense*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Erythroxylaceae and is characterized by its attractive, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are small, capsule-like berries.

Cayaponia bonariensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
