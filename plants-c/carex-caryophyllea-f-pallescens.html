Carex caryophyllea f. pallescens

Carex caryophyllea f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale-leaved sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex caryophyllea.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and pale green, distinguishing it from the typical green form of the species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
