Centaurea margaritacea subsp. dubjanskyi

Centaurea margaritacea subsp.

dubjanskyi, commonly known as the Dubjansky's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, composite-like inflorescences.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and extreme temperatures.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after species in horticulture, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.
