Clematis patens subsp. tientaiensis

Clematis patens subsp.

tientaiensis, commonly known as the Tientai Clematis, is a flowering plant native to China. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces white to pale blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it thrives in woodland or shady environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
