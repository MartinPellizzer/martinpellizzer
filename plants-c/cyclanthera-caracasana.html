Cyclanthera caracasana

Cyclanthera caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas cucumber or sweet gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces elongated, pale green fruits that are often harvested when immature and tender. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it has tendrils that aid in climbing.

The fruit is typically used in local cuisines and has a mild, sweet flavor.
