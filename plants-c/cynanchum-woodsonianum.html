Cynanchum woodsonianum

Cynanchum woodsonianum, commonly known as the woodson's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. It has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters.

Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant serves as a host for certain butterfly species.
