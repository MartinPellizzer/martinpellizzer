Convolvulus binghamiae

Convolvulus binghamiae, commonly known as the Bingham's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky areas and along the edges of grasslands.

Convolvulus binghamiae is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants with similar morphological features.
