Chrysosplenium echinus

Chrysosplenium echinus, commonly known as the spiny spleenwort, is a member of the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves.

It is a small evergreen perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges and a hairy texture. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are clustered in umbels, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Chrysosplenium echinus is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in shady conditions and its unique foliage.
