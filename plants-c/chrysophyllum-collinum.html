Chrysophyllum collinum

Chrysophyllum collinum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are typically harvested when they turn yellow or red, depending on the variety. The fruit has a sweet, creamy pulp with a mild flavor, and it is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
