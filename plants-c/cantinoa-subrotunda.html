Cantinoa subrotunda

Cantinoa subrotunda, commonly known as the curled leaf begonia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often curled leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers during the warm seasons. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
