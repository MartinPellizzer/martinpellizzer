Chelone erianthera

Chelone erianthera, commonly known as the purple swallow-wort, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chelone erianthera thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland gardens.
