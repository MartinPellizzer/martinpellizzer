Convolvulus cephalopodus subsp. bushiricus

Convolvulus cephalopodus subsp.

bushiricus, commonly known as the Bushiric convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly Iran and Afghanistan. It belongs to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
