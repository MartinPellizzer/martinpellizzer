Coronilla varia subsp. orientalis

Coronilla varia subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to regions of Europe and Asia. It features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.
