Cupressus arizonica f. compacta

Cupressus arizonica f. compacta, commonly known as the Apache Creek cypress, is a dense, shrub-like form of the Arizona cypress.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, twisted branches and small, scale-like leaves. Native to the southwestern United States, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.

Its cones are small and resemble those of other cypress species, contributing to its ecological and horticultural value.
