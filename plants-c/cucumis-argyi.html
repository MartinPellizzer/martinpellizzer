Cucumis argyi

Cucumis argyi, commonly known as the Chinese ridge gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant features lobed leaves and produces yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers. Its botanical structure includes a hairy stem and tendrils that help it climb.

The fruit is typically harvested when it is still green, though it can be used both raw and cooked in various culinary applications.
