Commiphora eminii subsp. trifoliolata

Commiphora eminii subsp.

trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Horn of Africa. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a cluster.

The plant produces a fragrant resin, similar to myrrh, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the genus Commiphora.
