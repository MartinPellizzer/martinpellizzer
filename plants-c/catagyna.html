Catagyna

Catagyna, commonly known as the catagyna plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The leaves are usually opposite and ovate, with a glossy surface. Catagyna species often have vibrant colors, ranging from red to orange, which make them popular in ornamental horticulture.
