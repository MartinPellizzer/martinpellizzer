Celtis mairei

Celtis mairei, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. Celtis mairei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
