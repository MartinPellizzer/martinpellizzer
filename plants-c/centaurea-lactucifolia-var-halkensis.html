Centaurea lactucifolia var. halkensis

Centaurea lactucifolia var. halkensis, commonly known as the Halken's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 60 cm tall, covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces bright blue to purple flowers with a distinctive ray floret and a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-gray hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
