Crataegus pinnatifida var. partita

Crataegus pinnatifida var. partita, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.
