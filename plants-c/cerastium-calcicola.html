Cerastium calcicola

Cerastium calcicola, commonly known as the limestone chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and calcareous soils.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically has hairy stems and leaves that are opposite or whorled, with a distinctive white or pale pink coloration. It thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in alpine or arctic regions.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
