Cycloptychis virgata

Cycloptychis virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's cyclops, is a rare flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped leaves that resemble a whirlpool, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
