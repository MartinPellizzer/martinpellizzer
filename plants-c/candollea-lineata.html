Candollea lineata

Candollea lineata, commonly known as the lineated orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory. Its leaves are usually linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, while the flowers are borne on tall, slender stems.

Candollea lineata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
