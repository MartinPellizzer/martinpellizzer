Centaurea × pseudosadleriana

Centaurea × pseudosadleriana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period, which extends from late spring to early autumn.
