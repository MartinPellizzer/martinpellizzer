Centronia sessilifolia

Centronia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf centronia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and simple, sessile leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, dry fruits.

Centronia sessilifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
