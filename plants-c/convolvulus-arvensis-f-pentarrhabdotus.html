Convolvulus arvensis f. pentarrhabdotus

Convolvulus arvensis f. pentarrhabdotus, commonly known as the five-lobed field bindweed, is a variant of the common field bindweed.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have five distinct lobes, giving it its specific epithet. It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its vines can become invasive in certain environments.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, where it thrives in sunny conditions.
