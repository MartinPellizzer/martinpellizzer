Camellia truncata

Camellia truncata, commonly known as the broadleaf camellia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its large, leathery, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. Its flowers are typically pink or white, with five to seven petals and numerous stamens, and they bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
