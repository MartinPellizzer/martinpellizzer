Chrysanthemum argyrophyllum

Chrysanthemum argyrophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, hairy foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and is well adapted to dry, rocky environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silver-white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance and resilience to drought.
