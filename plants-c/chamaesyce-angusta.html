Chamaesyce angusta

Chamaesyce angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow or greenish-white.

It is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
