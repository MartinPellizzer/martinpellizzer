Chrozophora gangetica

Chrozophora gangetica, commonly known as Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in elongated inflorescences. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found near water bodies.

Chrozophora gangetica is a member of the Malvaceae family, which includes other milkweed species.
