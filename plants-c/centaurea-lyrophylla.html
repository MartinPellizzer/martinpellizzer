Centaurea lyrophylla

Centaurea lyrophylla, commonly known as the rock centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Centaurea lyrophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
