Coronilla glycyrrhiza

Coronilla glycyrrhiza, commonly known as sweet clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions. It has a deep root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soils.

Sweet clover is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
