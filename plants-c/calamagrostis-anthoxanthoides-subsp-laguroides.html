Calamagrostis anthoxanthoides subsp. laguroides

Calamagrostis anthoxanthoides subsp.

laguroides, commonly known as the yellow bluestem, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that contrast with its green foliage.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
