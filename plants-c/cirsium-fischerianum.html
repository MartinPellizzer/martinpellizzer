Cirsium fischerianum

Cirsium fischerianum, commonly known as Fischer's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils.

Due to its prickly foliage, it is often avoided by herbivores, making it a resilient and hardy species.
