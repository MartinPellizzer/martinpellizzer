Cyathea ecuadorensis

Cyathea ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system supports the plant's structure and helps in nutrient absorption from the soil.

Cyathea ecuadorensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
