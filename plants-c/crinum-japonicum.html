Crinum japonicum

Crinum japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese crinum, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Japan and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a fan shape at the base. Its bulbs are perennial and can produce multiple stems and flowers each growing season.

Crinum japonicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
