Chionanthus mala-elengi subsp. mala-elengi

Chionanthus mala-elengi subsp.

mala-elengi, commonly known as the Malawian snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to the high altitudes of Malawi. It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and its clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
