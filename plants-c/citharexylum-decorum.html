Citharexylum decorum

Citharexylum decorum, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Citharexylum decorum is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
