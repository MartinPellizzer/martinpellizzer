Cinna crinita

Cinna crinita, commonly known as the curlytail palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to southern Florida and the Caribbean.

It is a small, slow-growing palm that typically reaches heights of 6 to 10 feet. The plant is characterized by its fan-shaped, dark green fronds that are adorned with long, curly fibers, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cinna crinita is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
