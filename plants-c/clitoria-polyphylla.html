Clitoria polyphylla

Clitoria polyphylla, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple pea-shaped flowers that resemble butterflies, hence its common name. The plant is a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and is often grown for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is commonly found in gardens and along roadsides.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.
