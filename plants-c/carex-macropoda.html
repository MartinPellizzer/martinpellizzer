Carex macropoda

Carex macropoda, commonly known as the large-fruit sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. Carex macropoda thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
