Curcuma flaviflora

Curcuma flaviflora, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-orange flowers. Its rhizomes, which are rich in curcuminoids, are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Curcuma flaviflora make it both ornamental and economically valuable.
