Coleus macrophyllus

Coleus macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which can range from red, orange, and yellow to purple and pink. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often large and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or white.

Coleus macrophyllus is primarily grown as an ornamental plant for its striking leaf colors and is often used in gardens and as a houseplant.
