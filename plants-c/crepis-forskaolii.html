Crepis forskaolii

Crepis forskaolii, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Its morphology reflects evolutionary adaptations to withstand harsh, high-altitude conditions.
