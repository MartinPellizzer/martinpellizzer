Cephalaria scoparia

Cephalaria scoparia, commonly known as the lesser cephalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, typically growing in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clustered inflorescences, and bloom during the summer months.

Cephalaria scoparia is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
