Cyperus aromaticus var. teres

Cyperus aromaticus var. teres, commonly known as the fragrant flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic when crushed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a potential candidate for phytoremediation.
