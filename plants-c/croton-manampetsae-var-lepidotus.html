Croton manampetsae var. lepidotus

Croton manampetsae var. lepidotus, commonly known as the Malagasy croton, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored with shades of red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
