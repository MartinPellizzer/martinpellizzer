Camphoromoea surinamensis

Camphoromoea surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, dark blue to black berries. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic and analgesic properties.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
