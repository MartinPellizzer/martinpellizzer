Cordia baracoensis

Cordia baracoensis, commonly known as the Baracoa cordia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region, particularly found in Cuba and the Bahamas.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture with a distinct scent.

Cordia baracoensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
