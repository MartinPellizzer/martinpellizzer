Clematis queenslandica

Clematis queenslandica, commonly known as the Queensland clematis, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
