Campanula tatrae

Campanula tatrae, commonly known as the Tatra bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Tatra Mountains in Central Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Campanula tatrae is a protected species due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.
