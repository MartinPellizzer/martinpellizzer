Cyperus vaginatus subsp. gymnocaulos

Cyperus vaginatus subsp.

gymnocaulos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats. It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in shallow water or damp soil and is often found in coastal areas and freshwater wetlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
