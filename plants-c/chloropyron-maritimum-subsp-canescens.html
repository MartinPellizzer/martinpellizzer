Chloropyron maritimum subsp. canescens

Chloropyron maritimum subsp.

canescens, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its blue or violet flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it withstand salty and windy conditions.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. It is a perennial herb with a robust root system that anchors it in harsh maritime environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to coastal ecosystems and a common sight in seaside habitats.
