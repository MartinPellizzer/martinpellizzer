Chlorophytum pygmaeum subsp. pygmaeum

Chlorophytum pygmaeum subsp.

pygmaeum, commonly known as the baby tears plant, is a small, evergreen perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features dense clusters of bright green, fleshy leaves that form a low-growing rosette.

The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
