Cissus dasyantha

Cissus dasyantha, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by translucent, waxy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.

Cissus dasyantha is also noted for its ability to root from cuttings, making it relatively easy to propagate.
