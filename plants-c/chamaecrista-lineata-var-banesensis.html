Chamaecrista lineata var. banesensis

Chamaecrista lineata var. banesensis, commonly known as the Banes senna, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, bipinnate leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces yellow to orange pea-like flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
