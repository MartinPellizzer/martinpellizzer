Cardamine sublyrata

Cardamine sublyrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watercress, is a small perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and streambanks. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a distinctive, narrow shape and small white flowers that appear in early spring. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions, making it an important part of riparian ecosystems.
