Cestrum mariquitense

Cestrum mariquitense, commonly known as the Mariquitense Trumpetflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
