Cyperus chamissoi

Cyperus chamissoi, commonly known as the Chamisso's flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually brown or greenish in color. It is a perennial herb that often grows in dense stands, contributing to the ecological structure of its wetland environment.

Cyperus chamissoi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
