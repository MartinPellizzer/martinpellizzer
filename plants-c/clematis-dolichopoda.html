Clematis dolichopoda

Clematis dolichopoda, commonly known as the long-stemmed clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other insects, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.

Clematis dolichopoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can be grown as a decorative climbing plant.
