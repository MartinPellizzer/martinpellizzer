Centaurea decipiens

Centaurea decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Centaurea decipiens is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
