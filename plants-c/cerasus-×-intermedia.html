Cerasus × intermedia

Cerasus × intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese wild cherry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus mandshurica and Prunus pseudocerasus.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, tart cherries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine and cuisine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
