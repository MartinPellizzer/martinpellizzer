Citrus × tachibana subf. nippokoreana

Citrus × tachibana subf.

nippokoreana, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin or Nippon mandarin, is a hybrid citrus plant native to Japan. It is characterized by its small, round, and fragrant fruits with a thin, smooth peel.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in subtropical climates and is valued for its edible fruit, which is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
