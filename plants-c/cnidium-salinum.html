Cnidium salinum

Cnidium salinum, commonly known as sea cnidium, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its umbellate inflorescence. The plant has aromatic, often pungent, leaves and stems, which contain various bioactive compounds. It thrives in coastal areas and salt marshes, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its resilience to high salinity makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
