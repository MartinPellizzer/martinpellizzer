Centaurea shelkovnikovii

Centaurea shelkovnikovii, commonly known as Shelkovnikov's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
