Chamaeanthus brigittae

Chamaeanthus brigittae, commonly known as the Brigitte's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing shrub with dense, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative plant in coastal landscapes.

Chamaeanthus brigittae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
