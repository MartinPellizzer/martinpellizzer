Cousinia kovalevskiana

Cousinia kovalevskiana, commonly known as the Kovalevskya's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It features basal leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing protection against desiccation.

Cousinia kovalevskiana produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
