Cissus anemonifolia

Cissus anemonifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically waxy and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus anemonifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and vigorous growth habit.
