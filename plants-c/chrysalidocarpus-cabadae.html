Chrysalidocarpus cabadae

Chrysalidocarpus cabadae, commonly known as the Cabada's palm, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Guam in the Pacific Ocean.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length, contributing to its striking appearance. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for conservation and ecological studies.
