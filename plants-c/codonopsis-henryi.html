Codonopsis henryi

Codonopsis henryi, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng or henry's bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
