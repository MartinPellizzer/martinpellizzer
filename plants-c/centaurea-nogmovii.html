Centaurea nogmovii

Centaurea nogmovii, commonly known as the Nogmovian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Centaurea nogmovii is considered a rare and endangered species.
