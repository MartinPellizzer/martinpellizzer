Coelogyne anomala

Coelogyne anomala, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its tropical habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and strap-shaped, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Coelogyne anomala is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
