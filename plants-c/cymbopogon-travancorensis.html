Cymbopogon travancorensis

Cymbopogon travancorensis, commonly known as Malabar grass, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale blue or white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Cymbopogon travancorensis is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
