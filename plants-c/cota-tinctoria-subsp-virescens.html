Cota tinctoria subsp. virescens

Cota tinctoria subsp.

virescens, commonly known as the green false heather, is a perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Asteraceae family.
