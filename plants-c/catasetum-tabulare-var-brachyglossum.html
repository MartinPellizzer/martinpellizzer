Catasetum tabulare var. brachyglossum

Catasetum tabulare var. brachyglossum, commonly known as the short-lipped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow with a prominent, fleshy spur. It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is part of the Catasetum genus, known for its unique pollination mechanisms.

This orchid prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
