Crataegus × lavallei

Crataegus × lavallei, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is a natural hybrid between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus virginea, exhibiting a mix of characteristics from both parent species. The plant is noted for its dense, thorny growth habit and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the fall and are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The hybrid is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
