Chrysothamnus serrulatus

Chrysothamnus serrulatus, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and linear, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive component of its native ecosystem.
