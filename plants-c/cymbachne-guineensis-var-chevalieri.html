Cymbachne guineensis var. chevalieri

Cymbachne guineensis var. chevalieri, commonly known as African grass or Guinea grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, erect culms and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability makes it an important component of many ecosystems and a useful forage plant.
