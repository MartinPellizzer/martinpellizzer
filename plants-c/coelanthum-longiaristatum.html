Coelanthum longiaristatum

Coelanthum longiaristatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Coelanthum longiaristatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
