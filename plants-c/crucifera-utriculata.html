Crucifera utriculata

Crucifera utriculata, commonly known as the bladderpod, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, green, bladder-like pods that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various regions of North America. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes.

The species is often used in ecological studies due to its role in soil stabilization and as a host plant for certain insects.
