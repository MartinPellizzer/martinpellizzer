Campanula lasiocarpa subsp. latisepala

Campanula lasiocarpa subsp.

latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and it produces small, elongated fruits known as capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
