Cayratia novemfolia

Cayratia novemfolia, commonly known as the nine-leaved cayratia, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in clusters of nine, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cayratia novemfolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
