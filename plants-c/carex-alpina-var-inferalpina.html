Carex alpina var. inferalpina

Carex alpina var. inferalpina, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in rocky or mountainous environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude habitats.
