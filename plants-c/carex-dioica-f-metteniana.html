Carex dioica f. metteniana

Carex dioica f. metteniana, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is typically found in moist soils along stream banks, marshes, and other wet areas.

The subspecies is characterized by its distinct morphology and ecological preferences, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
