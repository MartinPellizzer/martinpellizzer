Convolvulus diversifolius

Convolvulus diversifolius, commonly known as the variable bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in its native habitat.
