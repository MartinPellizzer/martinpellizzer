Cinnamomum angustifolium

Cinnamomum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
