Croton gracilipes f. tiliifolius

Croton gracilipes f. tiliifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a variant of the Croton genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
