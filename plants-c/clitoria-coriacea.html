Clitoria coriacea

Clitoria coriacea, commonly known as the rough butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, butterfly-like blue or purple flowers that are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. The plant has a woody stem and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture, which gives it its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clitoria coriacea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
