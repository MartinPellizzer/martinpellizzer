Crataegus grandiflora

Crataegus grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has pinnate leaves with serrated edges and produces thorny branches, which are typical of the Crataegus genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
