Carex koshewnikowii subsp. chitralensis

Carex koshewnikowii subsp.

chitralensis, commonly known as the Chitral sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense rosette structure that helps retain moisture.

The flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typical of the sedge family.
