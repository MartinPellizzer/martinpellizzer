Capparis lasiantha var. rhombifolia

Capparis lasiantha var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombifoliate caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and features elliptic to rhombic-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
