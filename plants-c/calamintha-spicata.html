Calamintha spicata

Calamintha spicata, commonly known as spiked caraway, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in spikes, and its leaves are often aromatic and slightly fuzzy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Calamintha spicata is also valued for its medicinal properties, with historical uses in traditional herbal remedies.
