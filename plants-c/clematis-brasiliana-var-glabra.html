Clematis brasiliana var. glabra

Clematis brasiliana var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth Brazilian clematis, is a deciduous vine native to South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its hardiness and ability to climb trellises or walls.
