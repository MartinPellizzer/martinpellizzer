Croton adenophorus var. loucoubensis

Croton adenophorus var. loucoubensis, commonly known as the Loucoubene croton, is a shrub native to parts of West Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that vary in shape and hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
