Cuphea inaequalifolia

Cuphea inaequalifolia, commonly known as the flame beak plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble beaks, which are clustered in inflorescences. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and unique flower structure.

Cuphea inaequalifolia is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil.
