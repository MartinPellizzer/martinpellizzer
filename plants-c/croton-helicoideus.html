Croton helicoideus

Croton helicoideus, commonly known as the spiral croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spirally arranged leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Croton helicoideus is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
