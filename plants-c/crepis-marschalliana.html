Crepis marschalliana

Crepis marschalliana, commonly known as the marsh hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are often pale yellow or white. It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils in meadows and wetlands.

Crepis marschalliana is native to Europe and parts of western Asia.
