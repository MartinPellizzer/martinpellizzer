Cynotoxicum suffruticosum

Cynotoxicum suffruticosum, commonly known as the creeping cynoglossum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, blue to purple in color, and are arranged in loose clusters atop the stems.

Cynotoxicum suffruticosum is characterized by its toxic properties, containing various alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock and humans.
