Calceolaria × guttata

Calceolaria × guttata, commonly known as the spotted calceolaria, is a hybrid plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that resemble small pouches, a feature typical of the Calceolaria genus. The plant produces vibrant, often colorful blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and may have a slightly hairy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.
