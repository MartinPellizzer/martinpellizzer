Coleosanthus corymbosus

Coleosanthus corymbosus, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the winter season. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Coleosanthus corymbosus thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
