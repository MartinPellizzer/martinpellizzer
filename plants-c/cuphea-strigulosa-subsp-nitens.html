Cuphea strigulosa subsp. nitens

Cuphea strigulosa subsp.

nitens, commonly known as the silverleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
