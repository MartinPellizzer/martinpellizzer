Coleobotrys alata

Coleobotrys alata, commonly known as the winged cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in white, waxy hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its unique feature is the presence of wing-like structures along the stem, which give it its common name.

Coleobotrys alata is adapted to survive in extreme conditions, making it a resilient and fascinating member of the cactus family.
