Cupania semidentata

Cupania semidentata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian elderberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, white flowers and red, fleshy fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The fruits are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
