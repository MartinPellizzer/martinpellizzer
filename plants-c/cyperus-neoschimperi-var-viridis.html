Cyperus neoschimperi var. viridis

Cyperus neoschimperi var. viridis, commonly known as green flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is distinguished by its vibrant green foliage, which sets it apart from other similar species within the Cyperus genus.
