Callista coventryana

Callista coventryana, commonly known as the Coventry's callista, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange ray florets that surround a central disc of smaller, tubular flowers. It typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Callista coventryana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
