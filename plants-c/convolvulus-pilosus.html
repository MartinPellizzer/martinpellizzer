Convolvulus pilosus

Convolvulus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Convolvulus pilosus is a perennial species that can spread aggressively through its extensive root system.
