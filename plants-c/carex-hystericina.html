Carex hystericina

Carex hystericina, commonly known as the silver hair sedge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that features a distinctive silvery-gray hue due to the presence of fine, hair-like structures on the leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous brown flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, upright stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex hystericina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
