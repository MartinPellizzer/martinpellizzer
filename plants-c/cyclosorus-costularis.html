Cyclosorus costularis

Cyclosorus costularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water clover, is a perennial aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, with a distinctive waxy coating that aids in water retention. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-yellow, and clustered in dense, branching inflorescences.

Cyclosorus costularis plays a role in stabilizing water bodies and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
