Clematis × fulgens

Clematis × fulgens, commonly known as the flame clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from red to orange, often with a yellow throat, and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces compound, pinnate leaves that are typically three to five leaflets, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis × fulgens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
