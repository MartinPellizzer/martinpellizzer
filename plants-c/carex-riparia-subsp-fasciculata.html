Carex riparia subsp. fasciculata

Carex riparia subsp.

fasciculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions. It typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other waterlogged habitats.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in dense clusters. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, inconspicuous flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
