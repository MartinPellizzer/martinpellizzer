Centaurea laconica

Centaurea laconica, commonly known as the Laconic thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its spiny leaves and purple-blue flower heads. Centaurea laconica is often found in open areas such as wastelands and scrublands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
