Cerinthe minor subsp. cleiostoma

Cerinthe minor subsp.

cleiostoma, commonly known as the narrowleaf cerinthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, which are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
