Cassia fruticosa var. benthamiana

Cassia fruticosa var. benthamiana, commonly known as golden shower tree or Indian laburnum, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
