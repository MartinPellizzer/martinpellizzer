Calpurnia robinioides

Calpurnia robinioides, commonly known as the Indian borage or wild coffee, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered with fine hairs.

Calpurnia robinioides is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savannas and rocky areas.
