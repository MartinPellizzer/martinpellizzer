Carex hirta var. pilosa

Carex hirta var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are densely covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This versatile plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland ecosystems.
