Carduncellus hispanicus subsp. intercedens

Carduncellus hispanicus subsp.

intercedens, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features a robust stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered with spiny leaves that are typically grayish-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters during the summer months, which are followed by spiky, spherical seed heads. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, rough texture, while its root system is fibrous and well-developed.

This species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.
