Crocus hyemalis var. foxii

Crocus hyemalis var. foxii, commonly known as the Foxii Crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces delicate, early blooming flowers with pale yellow to orange petals, typically appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to cold climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to bloom before many other spring flowers.

This variety is prized for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
