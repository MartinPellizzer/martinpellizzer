Crotalaria pringlei

Crotalaria pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces clusters of yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Crotalaria pringlei is also known for its toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to livestock.
