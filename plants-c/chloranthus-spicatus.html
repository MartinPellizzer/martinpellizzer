Chloranthus spicatus

Chloranthus spicatus, commonly known as the spike chloranthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spiky inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions of Asia and the Americas. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest in botanical studies due to its primitive floral characteristics.
