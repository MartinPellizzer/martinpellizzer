Chytranthus welwitschii

Chytranthus welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's chytranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
