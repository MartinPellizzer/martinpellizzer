Callista cunninghamii

Callista cunninghamii, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, forming dense clusters along the stems. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat and is also a symbol of Australia's national identity.
