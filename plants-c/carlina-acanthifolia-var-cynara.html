Carlina acanthifolia var. cynara

Carlina acanthifolia var. cynara, commonly known as the Cretan carline thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking yellow flowers arranged in a spherical inflorescence, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is closely related to other Carlina species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
