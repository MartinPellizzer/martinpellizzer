Coprosma ternata

Coprosma ternata, commonly known as the New Zealand gloryflower, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red or orange berries that are a food source for native birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest environments.

Coprosma ternata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
