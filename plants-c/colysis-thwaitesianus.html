Colysis thwaitesianus

Colysis thwaitesianus, commonly known as the Antarctic hair grass, is a flowering plant native to the extreme environments of Antarctica.

It is a small, low-growing species that thrives in the harsh conditions of the continent's dry valleys. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted to withstand the cold and low-nutrient soil. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to reduce water loss and protect against wind.

This resilient species plays a vital role in the fragile ecosystems of the Antarctic region.
