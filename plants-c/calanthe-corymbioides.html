Calanthe corymbioides

Calanthe corymbioides, commonly known as the clustered calanthe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features broad, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink blossoms. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe corymbioides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
