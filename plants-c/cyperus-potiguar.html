Cyperus potiguar

Cyperus potiguar, commonly known as the Potiguar sedge, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist soil conditions. Its slender stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus potiguar is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic ecosystems.
