Cacalia crotonoides

Cacalia crotonoides, commonly known as the crotonleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with hairy surfaces and a distinctive yellowish-green color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cacalia crotonoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
