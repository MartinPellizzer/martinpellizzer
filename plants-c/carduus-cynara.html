Carduus cynara

Carduus cynara, commonly known as the artichoke, is a thistle-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, purple-tinged leaves that form a dense rosette. The plant produces a showy purple flower head, which eventually develops into the edible artichoke bud. Its botanical features include a taproot system and a flowering structure known as a capitulum.

The plant is widely cultivated for its tender, edible flower buds, which are prized in culinary traditions around the world.
