Cissus stipulata

Cissus stipulata, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a flowering plant native to Africa, southern Asia, and Australia.

It is a vine that produces small, white, star-shaped flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with tendrils for climbing. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance for both shade and full sun. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and it is also popular as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it a common choice for green walls and hanging baskets.
