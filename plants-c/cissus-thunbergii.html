Cissus thunbergii

Cissus thunbergii, commonly known as the rock fig or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus thunbergii is also known for its ability to climb using its aerial roots, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
