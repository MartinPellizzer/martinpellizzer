Chrysothamnus gramineus

Chrysothamnus gramineus, commonly known as the golden sagebrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a low, sprawling growth habit with woody stems that persist through the winter.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
