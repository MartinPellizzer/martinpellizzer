Crataegus florens

Crataegus florens, commonly known as the flowering hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Crataegus florens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
