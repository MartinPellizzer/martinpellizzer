Cryptocentrum hoppii

Cryptocentrum hoppii, commonly known as the golden thread fern, is a rare and delicate fern native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bright yellowish-green fronds that give it its common name. The plant has a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread slowly across the forest floor. It thrives in moist, shaded environments with high humidity and well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
