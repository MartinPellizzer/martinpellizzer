Copaifera nana

Copaifera nana, commonly known as dwarf copaiba, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compact size and aromatic foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly due to the resin it exudes. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a pinnate structure, contributing to its ornamental value.

Copaifera nana thrives in moist, shaded environments and plays a role in the biodiversity of tropical ecosystems.
