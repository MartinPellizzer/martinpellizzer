Crepinia uncinella

Crepinia uncinella, commonly known as the hookleaf sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically significant in its habitat.

Crepinia uncinella is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
