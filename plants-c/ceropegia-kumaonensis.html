Ceropegia kumaonensis

Ceropegia kumaonensis, commonly known as the Kumaon milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in rocky and semi-arid environments, typically found at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
