Codonopsis graminifolia

Codonopsis graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-leaved monkshood, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, helmet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. Codonopsis graminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
