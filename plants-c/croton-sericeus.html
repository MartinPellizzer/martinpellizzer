Croton sericeus

Croton sericeus, commonly known as silver croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
