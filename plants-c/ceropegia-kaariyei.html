Ceropegia kaariyei

Ceropegia kaariyei, commonly known as the African violet or the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, pale green or white flowers. The plant produces specialized flowers that are adapted for pollination by specific insects, such as flies. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Ceropegia kaariyei is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
