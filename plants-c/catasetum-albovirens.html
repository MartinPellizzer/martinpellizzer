Catasetum albovirens

Catasetum albovirens, commonly known as the white-green orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white and green flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are known for their unique structure, featuring a specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insect pollinators.

Catasetum albovirens thrives in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.
