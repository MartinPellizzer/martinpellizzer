Crataegus × browicziana

Crataegus × browicziana, commonly known as the Browicziana hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. Native to Central Europe, it thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
