Crataegus monogyna f. intermedia

Crataegus monogyna f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its intermediate traits between the typical forms of Crataegus monogyna, often displaying a mix of leaf morphology and fruit size. This form is typically found in woodland edges and hedgerows, thriving in temperate climates. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its flowers are white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
