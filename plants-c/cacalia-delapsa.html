Cacalia delapsa

Cacalia delapsa, commonly known as the slender bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia delapsa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.
