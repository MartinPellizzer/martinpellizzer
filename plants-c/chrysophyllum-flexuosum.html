Chrysophyllum flexuosum

Chrysophyllum flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible chrysophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, drooping leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, citrus-like fruits that are highly valued in local cuisines. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
