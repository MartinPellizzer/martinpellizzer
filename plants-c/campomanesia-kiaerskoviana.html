Campomanesia kiaerskoviana

Campomanesia kiaerskoviana, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
