Cissus dysocarpa

Cissus dysocarpa, commonly known as the climbing cissus, is a woody vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by yellowish berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in warm climates.
