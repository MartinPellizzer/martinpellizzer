Crotalaria vialattei

Crotalaria vialattei, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant is an annual herb that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.
