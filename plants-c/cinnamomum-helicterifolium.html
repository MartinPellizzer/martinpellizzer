Cinnamomum helicterifolium

Cinnamomum helicterifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a species of tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is aromatic and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its potential economic importance in the spice trade.
