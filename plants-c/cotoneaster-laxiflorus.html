Cotoneaster laxiflorus

Cotoneaster laxiflorus, commonly known as the laxiflorous cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster laxiflorus is also valued for its use in landscaping and as a source of food for wildlife.
