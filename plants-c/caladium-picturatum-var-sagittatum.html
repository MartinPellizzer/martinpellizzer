Caladium picturatum var. sagittatum

Caladium picturatum var. sagittatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf caladium, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with vibrant, colorful patterns in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is the main ornamental feature, making it a popular choice for garden displays and container planting.

It is typically grown as an annual in temperate regions due to its sensitivity to frost.
