Calathea trinitensis

Calathea trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidadian calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with intricate patterns and variegation. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, often featuring a waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
