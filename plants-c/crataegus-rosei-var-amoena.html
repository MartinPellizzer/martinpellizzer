Crataegus rosei var. amoena

Crataegus rosei var. amoena, commonly known as the Amoena hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental choice for gardens and landscapes.
