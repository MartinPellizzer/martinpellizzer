Cryptomeria japonica var. sinensis

Cryptomeria japonica var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese red fir, is a variant of the Japanese cedar.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern China, characterized by its reddish-brown bark and needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish in color and contain seeds. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its timber and has ecological importance in its native habitats.
