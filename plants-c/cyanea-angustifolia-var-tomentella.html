Cyanea angustifolia var. tomentella

Cyanea angustifolia var. tomentella, commonly known as the Hawaiian treefern, is a variant of the Cyanea angustifolia species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, tree-like structure with a central trunk and a crown of feathery fronds. The plant has a distinctive trunk covered in a dense, woolly layer of brownish hairs, giving it a unique appearance. Its fronds are typically dark green and can grow quite large, contributing to its ornamental value.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests of Hawaii.
