Cardamomum solare

Cardamomum solare, commonly known as sun cardamom, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by orange-red berries. Its rhizomes, which are the primary part used, contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

Cardamomum solare is valued both for its culinary uses and traditional herbal applications.
