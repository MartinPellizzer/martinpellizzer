Carex stenophylla var. curaica

Carex stenophylla var. curaica, commonly known as the Curaica sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and cylindrical, with a dark green coloration. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green to brown in color, typical of the Carex genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
