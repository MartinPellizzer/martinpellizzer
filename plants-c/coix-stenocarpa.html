Coix stenocarpa

Coix stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited Job's tears, is a species of grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, brownish seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Coix stenocarpa is valued for its nutritional properties and has been cultivated for centuries in various Asian cultures.
