Cocculus mollis

Cocculus mollis, commonly known as the smooth cocculus, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, inflated fruit that resembles a helmet. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental species.
