Cyphostemma erythrocephalum

Cyphostemma erythrocephalum, commonly known as the red-headed cyphostemma, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and can thrive in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
