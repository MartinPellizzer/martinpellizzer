Corynella

Corynella, commonly known as the silverleaf corynella, is a small shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Corynella thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and sustainable gardening practices.
