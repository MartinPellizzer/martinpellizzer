Castanea crenata f. femina

Castanea crenata f. femina, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut or sweet chestnut, is a variety of the sweet chestnut tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and oval-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces edible, sweet nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are typically harvested in the autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in both culinary and horticultural applications.
