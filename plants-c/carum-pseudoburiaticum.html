Carum pseudoburiaticum

Carum pseudoburiaticum, commonly known as the Kashmiri caraway, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers and has finely divided, feathery leaves. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their aromatic properties.

Carum pseudoburiaticum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
