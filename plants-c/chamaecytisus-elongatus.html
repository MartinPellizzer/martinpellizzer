Chamaecytisus elongatus

Chamaecytisus elongatus, commonly known as the sweet shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, arching stems covered in fine, silvery hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
