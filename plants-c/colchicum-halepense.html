Colchicum halepense

Colchicum halepense, commonly known as the meadow colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

Colchicum halepense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
