Cistus elegans

Cistus elegans, commonly known as the sweet rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their fragrance and medicinal properties.

Cistus elegans plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity and is often found in coastal and mountainous habitats.
