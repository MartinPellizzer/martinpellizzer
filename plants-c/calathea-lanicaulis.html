Calathea lanicaulis

Calathea lanicaulis, commonly known as the woolly-stemmed calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, and hairy stems that give it its common name. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with intricate patterns of green, silver, and rust-colored markings. Calathea lanicaulis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
