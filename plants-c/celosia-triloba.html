Celosia triloba

Celosia triloba, commonly known as the flame flower or red dwarf celosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by colorful, papery bracts that serve as the primary ornamental feature. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its decorative appeal in gardens and floral arrangements.

Celosia triloba is relatively drought-tolerant and easy to grow, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
