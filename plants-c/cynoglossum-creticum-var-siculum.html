Cynoglossum creticum var. siculum

Cynoglossum creticum var. siculum, commonly known as Sicilian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in appearance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
