Carex turczaninowiana var. beaurepairoei

Carex turczaninowiana var. beaurepairoei, commonly known as the Beaurepaire's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
