Calligonum sinuosoaculeolatum

Calligonum sinuosoaculeolatum, commonly known as the sinuous aculeolate calligonum, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage, which are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are usually small, narrow, and have a distinctive sinuous or wavy margin.

Calligonum sinuosoaculeolatum is often used in desert landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
