Crassula uniflora

Crassula uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces a single, showy flower at the end of each stem, usually in shades of pink or red. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.

Due to its hardy nature, it is relatively easy to care for, though it is sensitive to overwatering.
