Carex vulpina var. divulsa

Carex vulpina var. divulsa, commonly known as the redstem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.
