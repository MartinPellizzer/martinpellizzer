Ceratostylis clavata

Ceratostylis clavata, commonly known as the club-styled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a slender rhizome with several erect, linear leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, with a distinctive club-shaped spur.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique morphology and adaptability to various soil types.
