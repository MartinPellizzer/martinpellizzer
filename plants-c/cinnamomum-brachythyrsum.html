Cinnamomum brachythyrsum

Cinnamomum brachythyrsum, commonly known as small-leaved cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark brown, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are valued for their culinary and medicinal properties.

This species is often cultivated for its aromatic qualities and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
