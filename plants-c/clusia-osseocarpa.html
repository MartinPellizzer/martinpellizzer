Clusia osseocarpa

Clusia osseocarpa, commonly known as the bone fruit or coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

Clusia osseocarpa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
