Cousinia kornhuberi

Cousinia kornhuberi, commonly known as the Kornhuber's tussock grass, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and waxy leaf coating.

Cousinia kornhuberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes.
