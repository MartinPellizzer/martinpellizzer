Cnidiocarpa coniifolia

Cnidiocarpa coniifolia, commonly known as the mountain thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, lantern-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
