Chassalia curviflora var. megacoma

Chassalia curviflora var. megacoma, commonly known as the giant chassalia, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is noted for its robust growth and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
