Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. alpicola

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine white chrysanthemum, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cooler climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
