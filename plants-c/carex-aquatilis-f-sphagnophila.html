Carex aquatilis f. sphagnophila

Carex aquatilis f. sphagnophila, commonly known as the sphagnophila sedge, is a variant of the aquatic sedge native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to submerged conditions, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its presence is an indicator of healthy aquatic ecosystems due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
