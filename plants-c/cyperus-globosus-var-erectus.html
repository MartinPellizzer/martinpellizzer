Cyperus globosus var. erectus

Cyperus globosus var. erectus, commonly known as the upright sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a cylindrical, grass-like stem that grows upright, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth, waxy surface.

It thrives in wet or moist soils and is often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.
