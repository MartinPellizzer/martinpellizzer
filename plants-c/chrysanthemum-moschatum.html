Chrysanthemum moschatum

Chrysanthemum moschatum, commonly known as the musk chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and aromatic, slightly hairy leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive musky scent, which gives it its common name.

The flowers bloom in the fall and are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants.
