Cornus controversa var. shikokumontana

Cornus controversa var. shikokumontana, commonly known as the Shikoku Cornus, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flowers, which appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its seasonal appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and dramatic floral display.
