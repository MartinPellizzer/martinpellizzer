Carissa schimperi

Carissa schimperi, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant bears bright yellow, berry-like fruits that are edible when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Carissa schimperi thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.
