Crocus kotschyi

Crocus kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 15-20 centimeters and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms in early spring, usually between March and May, and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.
