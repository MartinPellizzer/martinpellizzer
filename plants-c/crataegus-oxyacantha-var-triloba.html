Crataegus oxyacantha var. triloba

Crataegus oxyacantha var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed leaves, which are serrated and often have a hairy underside. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
