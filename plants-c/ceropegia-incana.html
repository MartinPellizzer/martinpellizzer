Ceropegia incana

Ceropegia incana, commonly known as the silver leaf string plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features slender, trailing stems that produce small, pale green leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces unique, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is popular as a decorative indoor plant.
