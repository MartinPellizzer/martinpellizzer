Carduus × moritzii subsp. burnatii

Carduus × moritzii subsp.

burnatii, commonly known as Burnat's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, usually purple or pinkish flower heads.

The plant produces both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
