Cleome purpurea

Cleome purpurea, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery blooms that range from pink to purple, resembling spider legs. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Cleome purpurea is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
