Capparis odoratissima

Capparis odoratissima, commonly known as the sweet-scented caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often covered in a fine, sticky substance. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Capparis odoratissima is known for its strong, pleasant fragrance, which is most noticeable when the plant is in bloom.
