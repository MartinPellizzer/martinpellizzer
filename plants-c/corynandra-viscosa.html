Corynandra viscosa

Corynandra viscosa, commonly known as the rough corymbia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Corynandra viscosa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
