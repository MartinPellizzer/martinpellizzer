Centaurea pannonica

Centaurea pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright blue or purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. It produces small, spiky seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal.

Centaurea pannonica is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant appearance and hardy nature.
