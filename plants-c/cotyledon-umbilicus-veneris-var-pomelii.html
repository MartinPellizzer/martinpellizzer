Cotyledon umbilicus-veneris var. pomelii

Cotyledon umbilicus-veneris var. pomelii, commonly known as the "moonstone plant," is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically pale green with a waxy texture. The leaves often display a distinctive pink or reddish hue when exposed to direct sunlight, which is a common trait in many succulents. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle ornamental touch to its appearance.

This variety is prized for its unique leaf coloration and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
