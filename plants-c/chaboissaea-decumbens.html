Chaboissaea decumbens

Chaboissaea decumbens, commonly known as the creeping chaboissaea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous species that typically spreads along the ground, forming dense mats. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and they bloom in clusters.
