Coix lacryma-jobi var. monilifer

Coix lacryma-jobi var. monilifer, commonly known as Job's tears or Chinese pearl barley, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, drooping inflorescence that resembles tears, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, round grains that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The plant is native to East Asia and is cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
