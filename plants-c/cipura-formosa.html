Cipura formosa

Cipura formosa, commonly known as the golden cipura, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Cipura formosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
