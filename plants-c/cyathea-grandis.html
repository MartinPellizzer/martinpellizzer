Cyathea grandis

Cyathea grandis, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds that can reach lengths of up to 5 meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and longevity, often living for several decades. Its rhizome system supports the development of its massive fronds, which are used for photosynthesis.

Cyathea grandis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
