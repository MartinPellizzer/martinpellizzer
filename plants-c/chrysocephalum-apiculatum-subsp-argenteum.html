Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp. argenteum

Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp.

argenteum, commonly known as silver everlasting, is a member of the Asteraceae family. It is native to Australia and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its silvery appearance.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
