Crucifera lepidium

Crucifera lepidium, commonly known as wild cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and near water sources. The plant has alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or yellow and bloom in early spring.

Wild cress is known for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
