Caragana media var. fuscata

Caragana media var. fuscata, commonly known as the Blackleaf shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in poor, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, followed by pea-like pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically dark green and turn reddish in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

It is also valued for its erosion control capabilities and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
