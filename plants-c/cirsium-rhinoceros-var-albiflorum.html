Cirsium rhinoceros var. albiflorum

Cirsium rhinoceros var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, silvery-gray, lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in a spherical shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
