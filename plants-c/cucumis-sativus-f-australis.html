Cucumis sativus f. australis

Cucumis sativus f. australis, commonly known as the Australian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more elongated fruits compared to the standard cucumber. The plant typically thrives in warmer climates and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its foliage is broad and lobed, contributing to its distinct appearance.

This form is often cultivated for its unique flavor and suitability for container gardening.
