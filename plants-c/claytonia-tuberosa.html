Claytonia tuberosa

Claytonia tuberosa, commonly known as the mountain sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of heart-shaped, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas. It has a tuberous root system that stores nutrients and helps it survive in its natural habitat.

Claytonia tuberosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
