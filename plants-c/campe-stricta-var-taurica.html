Campe stricta var. taurica

Campe stricta var. taurica, commonly known as the Tauric thistle, is a perennial plant native to the steppes of southern Russia and Ukraine.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple-tinged leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, open habitats.

Campe stricta var. taurica is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
