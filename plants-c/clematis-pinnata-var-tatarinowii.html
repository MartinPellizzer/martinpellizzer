Clematis pinnata var. tatarinowii

Clematis pinnata var. tatarinowii, commonly known as the Tatarian Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, which are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, usually in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beautiful blooms.
