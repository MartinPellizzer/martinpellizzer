Combretum macrophyllum

Combretum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often large and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its bark is typically smooth and greyish, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Combretum macrophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
