Cavendishia atroviolacea

Cavendishia atroviolacea, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple petals with contrasting green veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are small but fragrant, often blooming in clusters during the wet season.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
