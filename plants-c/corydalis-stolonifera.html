Corydalis stolonifera

Corydalis stolonifera, commonly known as the spreading corydalis, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its stoloniferous growth habit, producing runners that root at the nodes to form dense mats. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, nodding flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Corydalis stolonifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
