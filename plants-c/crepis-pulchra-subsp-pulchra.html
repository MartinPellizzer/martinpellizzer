Crepis pulchra subsp. pulchra

Crepis pulchra subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as the pretty hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, elongated achenes that are dispersed by wind.
