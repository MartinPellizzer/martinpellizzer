Camissonia claviformis subsp. rubescens

Camissonia claviformis subsp.

rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and pungent odor.

The subspecies is distinguished by its reddish stems and leaves that often have a bluish-gray hue. It typically grows in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, contributing to its reproductive success in its native range.
