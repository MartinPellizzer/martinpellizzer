Clitoria amoena

Clitoria amoena, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue or purple flowers that resemble butterflies, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has compound leaves with small leaflets. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are known for their vibrant color and fragrance.

Clitoria amoena is also valued for its ornamental use and has traditional medicinal applications in some cultures.
