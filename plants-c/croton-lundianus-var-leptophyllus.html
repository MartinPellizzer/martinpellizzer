Croton lundianus var. leptophyllus

Croton lundianus var. leptophyllus, commonly known as the slender-leaf croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
