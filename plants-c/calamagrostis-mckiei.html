Calamagrostis mckiei

Calamagrostis mckiei, commonly known as the Mckee reed, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, silver-blue foliage that adds a striking visual appeal to gardens. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils, making it well-suited for wetland or pond edge landscapes. It produces feathery plumes in the autumn, contributing to its ornamental value.

Calamagrostis mckiei is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
