Citrus melanocarpa

Citrus melanocarpa, commonly known as the black lemon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, round fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature, though they can also be yellow or orange. The fruit has a thick, aromatic rind and a tart, acidic pulp with a unique flavor profile. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in traditional medicine.

The plant prefers warm, humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
