Chenopodium acuminatum subsp. virgatum

Chenopodium acuminatum subsp.

virgatum, commonly known as wild spinach, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

This species is known for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional culinary practices.
