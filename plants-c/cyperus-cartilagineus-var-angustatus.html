Cyperus cartilagineus var. angustatus

Cyperus cartilagineus var. angustatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paper plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in wetlands and along water edges.

Its fibrous roots and rhizomes contribute to its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
