Campanula albanica subsp. albanica

Campanula albanica subsp.

albanica, commonly known as the Albanian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine down, while its stems are often hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
