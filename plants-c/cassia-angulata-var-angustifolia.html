Cassia angulata var. angustifolia

Cassia angulata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, angular stems and compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by elongated, slender pods containing flat, brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
