Carex seposita

Carex seposita, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures consist of small, three-lobed flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex seposita plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
