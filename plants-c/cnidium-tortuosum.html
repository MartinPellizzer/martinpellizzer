Cnidium tortuosum

Cnidium tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted cnidium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant is notable for its distinctive, winding, and twisted roots, which are the primary part used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a strong, pungent odor.

Cnidium tortuosum contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and coumarins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
