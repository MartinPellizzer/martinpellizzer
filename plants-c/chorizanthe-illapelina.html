Chorizanthe illapelina

Chorizanthe illapelina, commonly known as the Illapel choris, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Chile.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically pollinated by wind. It is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments with low nutrient soils.
