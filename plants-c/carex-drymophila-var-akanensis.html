Carex drymophila var. akanensis

Carex drymophila var. akanensis, commonly known as the Akan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
