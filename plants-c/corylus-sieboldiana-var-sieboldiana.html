Corylus sieboldiana var. sieboldiana

Corylus sieboldiana var. sieboldiana, commonly known as the Japanese hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring. The plant bears edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which ripen in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique nut-bearing habit.
