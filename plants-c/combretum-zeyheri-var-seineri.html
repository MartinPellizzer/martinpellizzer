Combretum zeyheri var. seineri

Combretum zeyheri var. seineri, commonly known as the red bushwillow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its bark is smooth and reddish-brown. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
