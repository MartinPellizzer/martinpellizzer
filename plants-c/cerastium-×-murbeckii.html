Cerastium × murbeckii

Cerastium × murbeckii, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as a ground cover in gardens.
