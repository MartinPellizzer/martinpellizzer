Chytraculia sartoriana

Chytraculia sartoriana, commonly known as the Sartorian chytra, is a lesser-known species within the chytrid group of fungi.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring small, motile zoospores that aid in its reproductive cycle. This organism thrives in moist, organic-rich environments, often found in decaying plant material. Its life cycle involves both aquatic and terrestrial phases, contributing to nutrient cycling in its habitat.

Due to limited research, much about its ecological role and distribution remains unclear.
