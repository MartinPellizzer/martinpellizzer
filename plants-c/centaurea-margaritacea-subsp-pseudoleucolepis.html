Centaurea margaritacea subsp. pseudoleucolepis

Centaurea margaritacea subsp.

pseudoleucolepis, commonly known as the rock centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, well-drained habitats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the stems are erect and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and colorful display.
