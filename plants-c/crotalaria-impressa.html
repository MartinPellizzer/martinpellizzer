Crotalaria impressa

Crotalaria impressa, commonly known as the Indian crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant has a taproot system and produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Crotalaria impressa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
