Cyperus trigynus

Cyperus trigynus, commonly known as the three-awn sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, three-lobed spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems. Cyperus trigynus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
