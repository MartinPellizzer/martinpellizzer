Calamagrostis sylvatica var. longifolia

Calamagrostis sylvatica var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved woodland grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright culms with long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, feathery flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
