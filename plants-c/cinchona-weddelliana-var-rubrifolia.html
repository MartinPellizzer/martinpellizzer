Cinchona weddelliana var. rubrifolia

Cinchona weddelliana var. rubrifolia, commonly known as the red-leaved quinine tree, is a species of the Rubiaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more prominent during the cooler months. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It is valued for its historical use in the production of quinine, though its medicinal applications are less prominent today.

The species is also appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique coloration.
