Citrus × limon var. abyssinica

Citrus × limon var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian lemon, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a thick, yellowish rind and a high concentration of essential oils. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is prized for its aromatic zest and juice, often used in culinary and aromatic applications.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
