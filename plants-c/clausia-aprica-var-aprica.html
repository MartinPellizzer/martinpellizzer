Clausia aprica var. aprica

Clausia aprica var. aprica, commonly known as the golden bellvista, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
