Cassia quebracho

Cassia quebracho, commonly known as Brazilian wattle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its hard, durable wood, which is highly valued in the timber industry. Cassia quebracho also has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
