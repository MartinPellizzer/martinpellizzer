Convolvulus compactus subsp. parnassicus

Convolvulus compactus subsp.

parnassicus, commonly known as the Parnassian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it often grows in rocky or alpine environments. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
