Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. parviflora

Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrowleaf tussockgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of pale green to golden yellow flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
