Campanula cochleariifolia subf. macedonica

Campanula cochleariifolia subf.

macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
