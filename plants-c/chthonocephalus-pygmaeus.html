Chthonocephalus pygmaeus

Chthonocephalus pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf subterranean mushroom, is a unique fungal species found in deep subterranean environments.

It thrives in nutrient-rich, dark, and humid underground habitats, often in caves or deep soil layers. This fungus is characterized by its small, delicate cap and pale, fibrous stem. It plays a crucial role in decomposing organic matter in its hidden ecosystem.

Due to its elusive nature, little is known about its full botanical characteristics and life cycle.
