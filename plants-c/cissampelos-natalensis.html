Cissampelos natalensis

Cissampelos natalensis, commonly known as Natal moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth habit makes it a common ornamental plant in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
