Cymbachne angustata var. africana

Cymbachne angustata var. africana, commonly known as African narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, savannas, and woodland edges. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns and can tolerate a range of soil conditions.

This grass is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
