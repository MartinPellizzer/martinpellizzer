Cryptocarya ovoidea

Cryptocarya ovoidea, commonly known as the oval-fruited cryptocarya, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and distinctive, oval-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a leathery texture and a fragrant scent when crushed.

The flowers are small and clustered, contributing to its ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
