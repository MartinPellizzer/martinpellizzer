Cassine latifolia var. heterophylla

Cassine latifolia var. heterophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf cassine, is a variant of the broadleaf cassine species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its large, broad leaves that can vary in shape depending on the environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
