Croton confinis

Croton confinis, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that range from red to orange and yellow, often displaying a striking variegation. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in both garden and landscape settings.
