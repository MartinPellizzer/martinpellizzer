Celastrus nossibaeus

Celastrus nossibaeus, commonly known as the Sierra Madre beauty, is a climbing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are a source of food for various wildlife species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments within its native habitat.
