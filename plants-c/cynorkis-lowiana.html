Cynorkis lowiana

Cynorkis lowiana, commonly known as the African orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming during the spring and summer months. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Cynorkis lowiana is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
