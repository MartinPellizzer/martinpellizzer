Carex gentilis var. oshimensis

Carex gentilis var. oshimensis, commonly known as the Oshimensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green coloration.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
