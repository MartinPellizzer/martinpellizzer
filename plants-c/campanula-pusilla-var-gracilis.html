Campanula pusilla var. gracilis

Campanula pusilla var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and charming appearance make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
