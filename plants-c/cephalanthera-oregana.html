Cephalanthera oregana

Cephalanthera oregana, commonly known as the Oregon lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and its distinctive, fragrant, white to pale pink flower spikes that resemble a train of small, rounded blossoms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and meadows, often near streams or in damp woodlands. It produces showy, intricate flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

The Oregon lady's tresses is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
