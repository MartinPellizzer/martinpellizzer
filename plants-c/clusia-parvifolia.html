Clusia parvifolia

Clusia parvifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Clusia parvifolia is valued for its ornamental foliage and its ability to tolerate urban environments.
