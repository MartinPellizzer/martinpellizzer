Cyclosorus perpiliferus

Cyclosorus perpiliferus, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in a fine, silky layer of spores, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant thrives in shallow, still freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with the spores being dispersed by water currents.

Cyclosorus perpiliferus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing aquatic sediments and providing habitat for microorganisms.
