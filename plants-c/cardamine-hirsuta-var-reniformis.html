Cardamine hirsuta var. reniformis

Cardamine hirsuta var. reniformis, commonly known as the broad-leaved water cress, is a variant of the hairy bittercress species.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its kidney-shaped leaves and small, white flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and have a peppery flavor, making it a popular choice for culinary use. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

This variant is often found in temperate regions and is part of the Brassicaceae family.
