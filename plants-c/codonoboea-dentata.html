Codonoboea dentata

Codonoboea dentata, commonly known as the serrated codonopsis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
