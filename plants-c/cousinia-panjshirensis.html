Cousinia panjshirensis

Cousinia panjshirensis, commonly known as the Panjshir mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Panjshir Valley in Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous roots. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in the region.
