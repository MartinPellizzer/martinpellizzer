Cincinalis vestita

Cincinalis vestita, commonly known as the naked cincinalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
