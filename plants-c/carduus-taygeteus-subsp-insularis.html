Carduus taygeteus subsp. insularis

Carduus taygeteus subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the Mediterranean thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.
