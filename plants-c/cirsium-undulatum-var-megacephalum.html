Cirsium undulatum var. megacephalum

Cirsium undulatum var. megacephalum, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, spiny, purple to pink flower heads that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant has deeply lobed, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

The species is known for its striking appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
