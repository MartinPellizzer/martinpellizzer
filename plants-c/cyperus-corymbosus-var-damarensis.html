Cyperus corymbosus var. damarensis

Cyperus corymbosus var. damarensis, commonly known as the Damarensis flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
