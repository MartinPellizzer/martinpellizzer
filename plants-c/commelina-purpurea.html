Commelina purpurea

Commelina purpurea, commonly known as purple arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking violet or purple spatulate leaves and small white or green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Commelina purpurea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
