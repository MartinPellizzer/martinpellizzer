Centropogon grandidentatus f. vulcanicus

Centropogon grandidentatus f. vulcanicus, commonly known as the volcanic centropogon, is a rare and unique variant of the centropogon genus.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and toothed leaves that are adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor volcanic soils. The plant exhibits a robust growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

This subspecies is found exclusively in specific volcanic regions, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
