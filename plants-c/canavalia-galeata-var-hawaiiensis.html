Canavalia galeata var. hawaiiensis

Canavalia galeata var. hawaiiensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to Hawaii.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant thrives in coastal and dryland environments, often found on sandy soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a woody stem with thorn-like projections.

The species is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
