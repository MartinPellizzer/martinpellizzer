Crinum politifolium

Crinum politifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf crinum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features strap-like, broad leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Crinum politifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
