Cocos petraea var. genuina

Cocos petraea var. genuina, commonly known as the rock coconut or desert coconut, is a rare and drought-tolerant plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are adapted to survive in extreme environmental conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from underground sources.

Due to its ecological significance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for studying plant adaptation to harsh climates.
