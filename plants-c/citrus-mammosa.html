Citrus mammosa

Citrus mammosa, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its thick, peeling bark and fragrant, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, round citrus fruits with a thick, leathery rind and a sour, acidic pulp. It is closely related to other citrus species like the mandarin and grapefruit.

Due to its high acidity and aromatic qualities, it is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
