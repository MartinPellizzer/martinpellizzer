Cinnamomum calcareum

Cinnamomum calcareum, commonly known as the limestone cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, dark blue berries. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with limestone soils. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The essential oils extracted from the plant contain compounds with antimicrobial properties.
