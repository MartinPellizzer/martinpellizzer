Chenopodium viride var. paniculatum

Chenopodium viride var. paniculatum, commonly known as green goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers in branched inflorescences, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is also known for its edible leaves, which have a slightly tangy flavor and are used in various culinary traditions.
