Cynoglossum cheirifolium

Cynoglossum cheirifolium, commonly known as the Egyptian hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a contrasting yellow or white throat. The plant has basal leaves that are kidney-shaped and hairy, while the upper leaves are lanceolate and gradually reduce in size. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cynoglossum cheirifolium is a perennial herb that blooms from spring to early summer.
