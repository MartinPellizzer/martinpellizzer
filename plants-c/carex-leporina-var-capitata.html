Carex leporina var. capitata

Carex leporina var. capitata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
