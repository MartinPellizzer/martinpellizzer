Cylindropuntia whipplei

Cylindropuntia whipplei, commonly known as the whipplei cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spineless cactus characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green and ribbed. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
