Chaetanthera dioica

Chaetanthera dioica, commonly known as the two-colored milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, a feature common among milkweeds. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
