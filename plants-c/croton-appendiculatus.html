Croton appendiculatus

Croton appendiculatus, commonly known as the red-stemmed croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful leaves that range from green to red and orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
