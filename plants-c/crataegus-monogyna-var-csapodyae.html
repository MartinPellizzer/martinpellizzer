Crataegus monogyna var. csapodyae

Crataegus monogyna var. csapodyae, commonly known as the Hungarian hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn native to Central Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in gardens and natural habitats.
