Cardamine parviflora var. manshurica

Cardamine parviflora var. manshurica, commonly known as the Far Eastern water crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features small, delicate leaves that are divided into three parts, and its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in a cluster. It produces small, rounded fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is often found in temperate regions with high humidity and well-drained soils.
