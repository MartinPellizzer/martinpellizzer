Calophyllum soulattri

Calophyllum soulattri, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing several seeds. Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
