Calamagrostis glauca var. latifolia

Calamagrostis glauca var. latifolia, commonly known as bluejoint reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and panicle-like inflorescence that matures to a golden hue. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration and landscape design.
