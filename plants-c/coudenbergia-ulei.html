Coudenbergia ulei

Coudenbergia ulei, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by legume-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodlands and savannas.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an important species for ecological and economic purposes.
