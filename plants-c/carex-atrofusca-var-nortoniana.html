Carex atrofusca var. nortoniana

Carex atrofusca var. nortoniana, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features dark green, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
