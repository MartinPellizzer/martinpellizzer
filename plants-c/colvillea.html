Colvillea

Colvillea, commonly known as the "false arum," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Colvillea corymbosa, which is native to South America. These plants are characterized by their striking, often colorful inflorescences that resemble those of true arums. The leaves are typically arrow-shaped and have a glossy appearance.

Colvillea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its unique and exotic foliage and flowers.
