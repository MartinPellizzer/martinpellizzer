Carex gersneri

Carex gersneri, commonly known as Gersner's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like flower clusters that are usually green or brown, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Carex gersneri is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in diverse wetland environments.
