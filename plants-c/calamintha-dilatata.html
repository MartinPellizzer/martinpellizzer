Calamintha dilatata

Calamintha dilatata, commonly known as sweet germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its fragrance is often described as sweet and minty, contributing to its historical use in herbal medicine.

Calamintha dilatata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional remedies.
