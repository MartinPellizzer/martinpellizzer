Crotonogyne giorgii

Crotonogyne giorgii, commonly known as the Giant Croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its size and unique foliage, it is a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor spaces.
