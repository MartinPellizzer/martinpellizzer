Copaiba confertiflora

Copaiba confertiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is part of the Burseraceae family, known for its aromatic resins. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The tree plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
