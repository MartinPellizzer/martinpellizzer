Crocus straussii

Crocus straussii, commonly known as the Strauss crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early flowering habit.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8.
