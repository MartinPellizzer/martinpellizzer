Cytisus scoparius subsp. cantabricus

Cytisus scoparius subsp.

cantabricus, commonly known as the Cantabrian broom, is a shrub native to the western Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
