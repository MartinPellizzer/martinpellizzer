Corylus sieboldiana

Corylus sieboldiana, commonly known as the Japanese hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its oval, serrated leaves and clusters of yellowish-green male catkins. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiky husk, which ripen in the autumn. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often growing in forested areas.

Corylus sieboldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
