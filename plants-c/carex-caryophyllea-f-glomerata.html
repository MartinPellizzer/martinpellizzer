Carex caryophyllea f. glomerata

Carex caryophyllea f. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their fibrous roots and three-ranked leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish to reddish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic insects.
