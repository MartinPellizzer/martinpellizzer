Cassia fastigiata

Cassia fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. Cassia fastigiata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
