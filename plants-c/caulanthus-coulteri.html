Caulanthus coulteri

Caulanthus coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's silversword, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves. The plant produces tall, branching stems that bear small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
