Centrosema pascuorum var. brevipes

Centrosema pascuorum var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed crownvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often used for soil stabilization due to its extensive root network. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for erosion control and as a cover crop in agricultural settings.
