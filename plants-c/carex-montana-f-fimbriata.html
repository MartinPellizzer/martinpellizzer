Carex montana f. fimbriata

Carex montana f. fimbriata, commonly known as the fimbriate marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often surrounded by bracts.

This variety is distinguished by its finely toothed or fringed margins on the leaf blades, giving it its specific epithet "fimbriata.".
