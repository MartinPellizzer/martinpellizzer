Cyperus subparadoxus

Cyperus subparadoxus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems that give it its common name. The plant produces small, three-lobed leaves and has compact, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus subparadoxus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
