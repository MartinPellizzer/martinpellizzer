Cydonia maulei var. grandiflora-rosea

Cydonia maulei var. grandiflora-rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, rose-like flowers that bloom in spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces yellow, edible fruits that are similar to quinces but smaller and more acidic. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

The variety is prized for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
