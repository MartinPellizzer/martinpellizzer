Cheirolepis persica

Cheirolepis persica, commonly known as Persian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers that appear in spring. The fruit, which is a pome, develops from the flower and is typically harvested in late summer or early autumn. The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

It is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its edible fruit.
