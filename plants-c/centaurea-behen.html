Centaurea behen

Centaurea behen, commonly known as Indian breadseed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, hard seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Centaurea behen is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in herbal remedies.
