Castanea japonica var. crenata

Castanea japonica var. crenata, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut with wavy leaves, is a variant of the Japanese chestnut tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves and produces small, edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible produce.

The tree is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and soil stabilization.
