Cinnamomum burmanni var. angustifolium

Cinnamomum burmanni var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cinnamon tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, dark blue-black berries. Its leaves and bark contain essential oils, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
