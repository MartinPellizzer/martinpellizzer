Cuscuta reflexa var. densiflora

Cuscuta reflexa var. densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a dense, branched structure with numerous tiny, thread-like stems. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to infest a wide range of host species, including agricultural crops and wild plants. It spreads through seeds and can form dense mats on its host, often causing significant damage.

Its botanical features make it an important subject in studies of parasitic plant ecology and host-parasite interactions.
