Coelogyne convallariiformis

Coelogyne convallariiformis, commonly known as the "Lily Orchid," is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchid species. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Coelogyne convallariiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
