Capparis intermedia

Capparis intermedia, commonly known as the common caper, is a flowering plant in the Capparaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in Mediterranean climates and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are used in various culinary applications. Its leaves are succulent and often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it conserve water in arid environments.

Capparis intermedia is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
