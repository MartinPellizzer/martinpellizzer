Castanea sativa var. mollissima

Castanea sativa var. mollissima, commonly known as the Chinese sweet chestnut, is a variety of the common chestnut tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, edible nuts enclosed in thick, spiny husks. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant prefers well-drained soils and temperate climates, often growing in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and as a source of nutritious nuts.
