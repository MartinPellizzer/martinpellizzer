Clematis viorna var. flaccida

Clematis viorna var. flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.

Its botanical characteristics make it a versatile and attractive addition to native plant landscapes.
