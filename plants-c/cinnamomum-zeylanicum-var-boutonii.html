Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. boutonii

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. boutonii, commonly known as Ceylon cinnamon, is a variant of the cinnamon tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is harvested and dried to produce the spice cinnamon. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a strong, spicy aroma.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.
