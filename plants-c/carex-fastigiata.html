Carex fastigiata

Carex fastigiata, commonly known as the upright sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and produces spikes of small, usually brown or greenish flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Carex fastigiata is often used in landscaping for its upright form and ability to thrive in partial shade.
