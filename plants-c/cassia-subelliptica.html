Cassia subelliptica

Cassia subelliptica, commonly known as the narrow-elliptic cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its seeds are encased in elongated pods that split open to release the seeds.

Cassia subelliptica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
