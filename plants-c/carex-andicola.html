Carex andicola

Carex andicola, commonly known as the Andicola sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in forested wetlands and streamside areas. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and narrow, with a dense growth habit. Its flowering spikes are usually short and inconspicuous, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex andicola plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
