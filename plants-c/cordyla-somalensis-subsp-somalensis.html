Cordyla somalensis subsp. somalensis

Cordyla somalensis subsp.

somalensis, commonly known as the Somali cordyla, is a shrub native to the Horn of Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of fuelwood.
