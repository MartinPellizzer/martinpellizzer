Carlina vulgaris subsp. malinvaudii

Carlina vulgaris subsp.

malinvaudii, commonly known as the Malinvaud's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, woolly leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, purple-blue flower heads that are surrounded by several rows of bracts. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
