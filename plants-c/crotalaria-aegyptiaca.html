Crotalaria aegyptiaca

Crotalaria aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with red veins and compound leaves. The plant is an annual herb that grows up to 1 meter in height, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability.

The seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
