Curcuma kunstleri var. rubra

Curcuma kunstleri var. rubra, commonly known as the red ginger or red turmeric, is a rhizomatous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It produces striking, fiery red flowers with a distinctive, fragrant aroma. The plant's leaves are broad and lance-shaped, typically reaching lengths of up to 1 meter. Its rhizomes, similar to those of turmeric, are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of this plant make it both ornamental and economically valuable.
