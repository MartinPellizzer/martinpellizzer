Coelogyne speciosa var. alba

Coelogyne speciosa var. alba, commonly known as the white orchid of Sumatra, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle pinkish tinge at the base. The plant features a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids, which stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, providing a lush green backdrop to the delicate flowers.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
