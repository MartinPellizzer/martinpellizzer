Crataegus pseudokyrtostyla

Crataegus pseudokyrtostyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, ovate leaves. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

It is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
