Chamaesyce fruticulosa var. hirtella

Chamaesyce fruticulosa var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy false nettle, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features dense, hairy foliage that is typically dark green with a rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Its hairy leaves and stems provide habitat for various small invertebrates.
