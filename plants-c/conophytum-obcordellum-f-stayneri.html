Conophytum obcordellum f. stayneri

Conophytum obcordellum f. stayneri, commonly known as the Stayner's conophytum, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy, and often brightly colored leaves. The plant typically forms a rosette shape, with leaves that are obcordate, meaning heart-shaped with the base narrow and the tip rounded. It produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months.

This species is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves to survive dry conditions.
