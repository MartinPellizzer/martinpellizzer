Chrysalidocarpus madagascariensis var. lucubensis

Chrysalidocarpus madagascariensis var. lucubensis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It features a short, thick trunk with a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by orange-red fruits that contain a single seed. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Its distinctive morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
