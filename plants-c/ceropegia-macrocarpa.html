Ceropegia macrocarpa

Ceropegia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant chain plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features long, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length, adorned with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange, which are pollinated by specific insects. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and hanging displays.
