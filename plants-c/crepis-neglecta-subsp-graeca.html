Crepis neglecta subsp. graeca

Crepis neglecta subsp.

graeca, commonly known as the Greek mouse-ear, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, dandelion-like yellow flowers. Its fruit is a cylindrical achene with a pappus, aiding in wind dispersal.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ecological adaptability.
