Cocculus bariensis

Cocculus bariensis, commonly known as the Indian devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, twisting stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds. Its botanical features make it a notable species in both traditional medicine and horticulture.

The plant is also known for its use in herbal remedies, though its toxicity requires careful handling.
