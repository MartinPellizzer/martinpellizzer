Calothamnus villosus var. ericifolius

Calothamnus villosus var. ericifolius, commonly known as the Ericifolius chamaecaryophyll.

It is a shrub native to Australia, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive foliage. The leaves are typically small, narrow, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually red or orange in color, which bloom in spring.

It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal regions.
