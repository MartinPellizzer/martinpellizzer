Carambola

Carambola, commonly known as starfruit, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Achariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit when sliced. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and have a smooth texture, while the flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The fruit develops from the flower and matures to a yellowish-orange color, often with a waxy coating.
