Chamaelirium obovale

Chamaelirium obovale, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in loose inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is part of the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and some toxic species.
