Centaurea furfuracea

Centaurea furfuracea, commonly known as the woolly centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with purple markings, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Centaurea furfuracea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
