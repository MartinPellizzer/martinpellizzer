Capparis versicolor

Capparis versicolor, commonly known as the variable caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant, yellow or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has woody stems and is typically shrub-like in growth habit. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

Capparis versicolor is known for its ability to thrive in coastal and saline environments.
