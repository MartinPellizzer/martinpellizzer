Calea cordata

Calea cordata, commonly known as the "Mexican heartleaf," is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Calea cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
