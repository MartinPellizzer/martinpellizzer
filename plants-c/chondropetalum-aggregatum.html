Chondropetalum aggregatum

Chondropetalum aggregatum, commonly known as the clustered clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones, which are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native habitat.
