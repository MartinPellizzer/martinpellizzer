Coelogyne triotos

Coelogyne triotos, commonly known as the Three-lobed Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Coelogyne triotos thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
