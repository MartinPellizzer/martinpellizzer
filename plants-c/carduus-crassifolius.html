Carduus crassifolius

Carduus crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry, disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits that disperse seeds via the wind.

Its robust structure and adaptive features make it a resilient species in various environments.
