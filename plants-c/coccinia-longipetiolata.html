Coccinia longipetiolata

Coccinia longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn orange when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with long petioles, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The vine thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued both for its nutritional content and ornamental appeal.
