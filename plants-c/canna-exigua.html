Canna exigua

Canna exigua, commonly known as the dwarf canna, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It features striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers with three petals and three sepals, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Canna exigua is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
