Chelone lyonii f. latifolia

Chelone lyonii f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved turtlehead, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its unique floral structure makes it an attractive addition to native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
