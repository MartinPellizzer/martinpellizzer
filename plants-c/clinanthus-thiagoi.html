Clinanthus thiagoi

Clinanthus thiagoi, commonly known as the Thiago's ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the cloud forests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in low light, a unique adaptation for attracting specific pollinators. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom during the early morning hours, coinciding with the activity of its primary pollinators. Clinanthus thiagoi has a short flowering period, making it difficult to study in the wild.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered critically endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts to protect this rare botanical specimen.
