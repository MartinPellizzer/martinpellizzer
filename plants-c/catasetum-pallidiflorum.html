Catasetum pallidiflorum

Catasetum pallidiflorum, commonly known as the pale-flowered catasetum, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a specialized lip that aids in pollination by specific wasps. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a distinct, spurred shape.

Catasetum pallidiflorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and interesting pollination mechanism.
