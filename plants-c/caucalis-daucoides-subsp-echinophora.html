Caucalis daucoides subsp. echinophora

Caucalis daucoides subsp.

echinophora, commonly known as the spiny hedge parsley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant produces small, hairy fruits that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
