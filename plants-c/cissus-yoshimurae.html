Cissus yoshimurae

Cissus yoshimurae, commonly known as the Yoshimura's devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and ability to thrive in humid environments. The plant has a rapid growth rate and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal. It prefers indirect sunlight and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Cissus yoshimurae is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ease of care.
