Cyphostemma gallaense

Cyphostemma gallaense, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Due to its unique appearance and growth habit, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
