Crataegus oxyacantha var. miniata

Crataegus oxyacantha var. miniata, commonly known as the red-fruited hawthorn, is a compact shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are often reddish beneath, and its showy flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are ornamental and attract wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its compact size and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to ornamental horticulture.
