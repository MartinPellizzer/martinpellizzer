Croton amaraliae

Croton amaraliae, commonly known as the red croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its diverse species with often unusual floral structures. The plant is noted for its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from bright red to deep green, depending on the variety. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a type of cup-like structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton amaraliae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
