Cicerbita corymbosa

Cicerbita corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bittercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters, giving it its common name. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is known for its peppery, somewhat bitter taste, which is characteristic of many members of its family.
