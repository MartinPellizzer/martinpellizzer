Cardamine luxurians

Cardamine luxurians, commonly known as the luxuriant water crowfoot, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features small, floating leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the water's surface.

Cardamine luxurians plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small fish.
