Corynostylis berteroi

Corynostylis berteroi, commonly known as the broad-leafed grass-tongue, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that blooms during the spring and early summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
