Cyperus hirtellus

Cyperus hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus hirtellus is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural and aquatic habitats due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
