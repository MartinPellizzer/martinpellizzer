Carex mendocinensis

Carex mendocinensis, commonly known as Mendo sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flowering structures. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, riparian zones, and wet meadows. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Carex mendocinensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
