Carpesium divaricatum var. abrotanoides

Carpesium divaricatum var. abrotanoides, commonly known as yellow cocklebur, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring serrated leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and a subject of ecological study.
