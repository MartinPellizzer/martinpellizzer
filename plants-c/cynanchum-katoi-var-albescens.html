Cynanchum katoi var. albescens

Cynanchum katoi var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the genus Cynanchum. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often with hairy surfaces.

It thrives in dry, open habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
