Calochilus australianus

Calochilus australianus, commonly known as the Australian spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, intricately shaped flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing pale green or yellowish flowers with prominent sepals and petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Calochilus australianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a species of interest in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
