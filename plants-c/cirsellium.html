Cirsellium

Cirsellium, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plants typically have hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped or ovate. They are native to North America and are often found in moist meadows, prairies, and along stream banks.

Cirsellium species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are also used in traditional herbal medicine.
