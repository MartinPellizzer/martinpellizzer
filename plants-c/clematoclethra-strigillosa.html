Clematoclethra strigillosa

Clematoclethra strigillosa, commonly known as the smoothleaf clethra, is a species of flowering plant in the family Clethraceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in moist, wooded areas. The plant is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in elongated inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
