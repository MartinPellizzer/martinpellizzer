Crataegus lipskyi

Crataegus lipskyi, commonly known as the Lyski's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and used in local cuisines. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Crataegus lipskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
