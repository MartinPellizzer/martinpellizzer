Crataegus oxyacantha f. integrifolia

Crataegus oxyacantha f. integrifolia, commonly known as the European hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with entire margins, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible for birds and some mammals.

It thrives in a variety of soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along riverbanks.
