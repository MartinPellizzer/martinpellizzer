Cuphea cyanea f. cordata

Cuphea cyanea f. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf cuphea, is a striking ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that give it its common name and contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to add color to landscapes.
