Carex echinata var. microstachys

Carex echinata var. microstachys, commonly known as the dwarf spike sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
