Cassia fragilis

Cassia fragilis, commonly known as the brittle cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain small, dark brown seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Cassia fragilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
