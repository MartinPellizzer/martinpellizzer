Centaurea rumelica

Centaurea rumelica, commonly known as the Balkan cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typical of the Centaurea genus. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bristly seeds in its flower heads. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
