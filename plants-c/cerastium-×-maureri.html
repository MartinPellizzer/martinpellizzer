Cerastium × maureri

Cerastium × maureri, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Cerastium × maureri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its ability to form dense, carpet-like ground cover.
