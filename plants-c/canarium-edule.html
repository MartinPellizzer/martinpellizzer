Canarium edule

Canarium edule, commonly known as the candlenut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single, hard-shelled seed. The seeds are rich in oil and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of culinary oil.

Additionally, the tree plays an important ecological role in coastal and forest ecosystems.
