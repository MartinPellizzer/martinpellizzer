Chlorocyperus erythrorhizus

Chlorocyperus erythrorhizus, commonly known as the red-rooted flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant's roots are distinctive, often appearing reddish due to high concentrations of iron, which gives it its common name. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
