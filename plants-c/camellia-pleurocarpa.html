Camellia pleurocarpa

Camellia pleurocarpa, commonly known as the broadleaf camellia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like China, Vietnam, and Thailand.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in winter and early spring.

Camellia pleurocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty and winter interest.
