Catonia

Catonia, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and includes several species that are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce bright yellow or orange composite flowers. Catonia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sun, making them suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
