Convolvulus auriculatus

Convolvulus auriculatus, commonly known as the earleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant grows along the ground, often in rocky or sandy soils, and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in harsh conditions. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other climbing and trailing plants.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution in arid regions.
