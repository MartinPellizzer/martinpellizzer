Clitoria arborea

Clitoria arborea, commonly known as the everlasting pea or tree morning glory, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, butterfly-like blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Clitoria arborea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
