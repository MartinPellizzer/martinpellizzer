Crataegus doddsii

Crataegus doddsii, commonly known as Dodds' hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly found in the Appalachian Mountains. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall, attracting various wildlife species.

This shrub plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing food and shelter for birds and small mammals.
