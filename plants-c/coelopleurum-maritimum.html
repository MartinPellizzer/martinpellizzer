Coelopleurum maritimum

Coelopleurum maritimum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray leaves that are adapted to salty, windy environments. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along the shoreline. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coelopleurum maritimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
