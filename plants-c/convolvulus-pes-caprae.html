Convolvulus pes-caprae

Convolvulus pes-caprae, commonly known as seaside bindweed, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and other temperate areas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale purple or white flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found along shorelines and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability to saline environments makes it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.
