Clematis virginiana var. genuina

Clematis virginiana var. genuina, commonly known as the Virginia Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in early summer. The plant produces small, round, red or black berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wildlife.
