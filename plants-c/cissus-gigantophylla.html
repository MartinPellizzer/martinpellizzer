Cissus gigantophylla

Cissus gigantophylla, commonly known as the giant-leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces small, white flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus gigantophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
