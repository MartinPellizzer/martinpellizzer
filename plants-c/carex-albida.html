Carex albida

Carex albida, commonly known as the white sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers in spikelets. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex albida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
