Conyza oblongifolia

Conyza oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils.

Conyza oblongifolia is often found in regions with warm climates and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
