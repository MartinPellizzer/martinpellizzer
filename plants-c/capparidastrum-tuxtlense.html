Capparidastrum tuxtlense

Capparidastrum tuxtlense, commonly known as the Tuxtlans caper, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in the Sierra de Tuxtlas region. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with woody stems and small, elliptical leaves.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
