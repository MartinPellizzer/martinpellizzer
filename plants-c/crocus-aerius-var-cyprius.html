Crocus aerius var. cyprius

Crocus aerius var. cyprius, commonly known as the Cyprus crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
