Chloris brevigluma

Chloris brevigluma, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and support local wildlife.
