Cyanus atropurpureus

Cyanus atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple petals and a central cone-like structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces showy blooms from late summer into early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
