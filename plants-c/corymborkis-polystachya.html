Corymborkis polystachya

Corymborkis polystachya, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to the high altitudes of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in a panicle-like arrangement. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy coating.

Corymborkis polystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
