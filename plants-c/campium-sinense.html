Campium sinense

Campium sinense, commonly known as the Chinese mistletoe, is a parasitic plant belonging to the family Viscaceae.

It grows on various host trees, primarily in eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually green or reddish in color. It produces small, white or yellowish flowers followed by berries that are often red or orange.

Campium sinense is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
