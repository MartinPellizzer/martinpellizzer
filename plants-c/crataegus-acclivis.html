Crataegus acclivis

Crataegus acclivis, commonly known as the mountain hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Crataegus acclivis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological systems in its native habitat.
