Cyanotis sarmentosa

Cyanotis sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping bluebell, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are oval and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often prostrate or trailing.

Cyanotis sarmentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
