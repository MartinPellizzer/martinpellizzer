Chartolepis lyrata

Chartolepis lyrata, commonly known as the lyreleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with white markings, which bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Chartolepis lyrata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
