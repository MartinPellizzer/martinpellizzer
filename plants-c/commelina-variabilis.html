Commelina variabilis

Commelina variabilis, commonly known as the spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous.

Commelina variabilis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often used in ornamental gardening.
