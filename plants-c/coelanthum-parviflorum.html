Coelanthum parviflorum

Coelanthum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact growth habit with a short, creeping rhizome and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, and it is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Coelanthum parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
