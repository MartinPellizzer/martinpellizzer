Cyperus pseudopilosus

Cyperus pseudopilosus, commonly known as the silky flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are often inconspicuous. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
