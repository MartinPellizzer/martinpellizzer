Carex schreberi subsp. ohmuelleriana

Carex schreberi subsp.

ohmuelleriana, commonly known as the Ohmueller's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often green or reddish in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
