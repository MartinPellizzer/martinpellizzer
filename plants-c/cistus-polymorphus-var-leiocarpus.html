Cistus polymorphus var. leiocarpus

Cistus polymorphus var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the smooth-capped rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
