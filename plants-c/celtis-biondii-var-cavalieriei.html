Celtis biondii var. cavalieriei

Celtis biondii var. cavalieriei, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained habitats.
