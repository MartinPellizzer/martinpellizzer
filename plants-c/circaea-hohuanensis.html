Circaea hohuanensis

Circaea hohuanensis, commonly known as the Hohuan sandwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates with well-drained conditions. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Circaea hohuanensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
