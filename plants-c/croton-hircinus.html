Croton hircinus

Croton hircinus, commonly known as the goat's croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage. The plant features alternate, palmately lobed leaves that can vary in shape and color depending on the variety. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in cyathia, which are cup-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton hircinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant leaf colors and adaptability to various growing conditions.
