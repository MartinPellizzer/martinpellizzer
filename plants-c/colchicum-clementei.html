Colchicum clementei

Colchicum clementei, commonly known as the Clement's colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with six petals and a distinctive spurred structure.

Colchicum clementei is adapted to grow in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in woodland or scrubland habitats.
