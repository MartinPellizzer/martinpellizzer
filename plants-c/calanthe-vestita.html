Calanthe vestita

Calanthe vestita, commonly known as the naked calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Calanthe vestita thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
