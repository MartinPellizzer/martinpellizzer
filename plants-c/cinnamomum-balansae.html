Cinnamomum balansae

Cinnamomum balansae, commonly known as the Balansae cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces berries that contain seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its essential oils, extracted from the bark and leaves, are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Cinnamomum balansae thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
