Coleosanthus veronicifolius

Coleosanthus veronicifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf.

It is a tropical plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. It is native to regions of South America and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
