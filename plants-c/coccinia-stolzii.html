Coccinia stolzii

Coccinia stolzii, commonly known as the African eggplant or African bitter melon, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squashes. The plant produces small, oval fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often trailing.

Coccinia stolzii is valued for its edible fruits, which have a slightly bitter taste and are used in various traditional dishes.
