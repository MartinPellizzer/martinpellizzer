Chaetospora fimbriolata

Chaetospora fimbriolata, commonly known as the fringed beard lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Pilocarpaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate thallus structure, which consists of a dense mat of filaments forming a crust-like growth on rocks and soil. The lichen exhibits a distinct, bright orange to yellowish color due to the presence of specific secondary metabolites. It is typically found in temperate regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments.

Its morphology and chemical composition make it an important subject in ecological and taxonomic studies.
