Crataegus munda var. pexa

Crataegus munda var. pexa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically pubescent beneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
