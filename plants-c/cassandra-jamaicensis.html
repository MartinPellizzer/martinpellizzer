Cassandra jamaicensis

Cassandra jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.
