Capnophyllum nodiflorum

Capnophyllum nodiflorum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant has a compact growth habit with nodding flowers that are often clustered together, giving it its common name. Native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and sunny to partially shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders in horticultural settings.
