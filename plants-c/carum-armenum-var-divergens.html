Carum armenum var. divergens

Carum armenum var. divergens, commonly known as Armenian cumin or Persian cumin, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems and small, aromatic leaves with a distinctively pungent scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its seeds, which are oval and yellowish-brown, are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
