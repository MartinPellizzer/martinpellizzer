Cerastium banaticum f. diklicii

Cerastium banaticum f. diklicii, commonly known as the Banat dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Carpathian region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered with fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This fern-like plant is often found in rocky or forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
