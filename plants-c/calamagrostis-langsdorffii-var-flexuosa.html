Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. flexuosa

Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible blue grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features slender, arching stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces panicles of blue-gray flowers in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that persists throughout the growing season.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
