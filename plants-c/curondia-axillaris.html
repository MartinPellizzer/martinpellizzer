Curondia axillaris

Curondia axillaris, commonly known as the wax plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a waxy coating, reducing water loss.

Curondia axillaris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
