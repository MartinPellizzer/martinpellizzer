Cousinia schindleriana

Cousinia schindleriana, commonly known as Schindler's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Cousinia schindleriana is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
