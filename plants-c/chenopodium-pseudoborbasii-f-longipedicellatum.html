Chenopodium pseudoborbasii f. longipedicellatum

Chenopodium pseudoborbasii f. longipedicellatum, commonly known as the long-beaked goosefoot, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive floral structures.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. The plant features elongated bracts and small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually succulent, with a rough texture and a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water conservation mechanisms.
