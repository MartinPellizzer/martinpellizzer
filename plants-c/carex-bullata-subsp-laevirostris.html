Carex bullata subsp. laevirostris

Carex bullata subsp.

laevirostris, commonly known as the smooth-beaked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, terminal spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The subspecies laevirostris is distinguished by its smooth, elongated bracts and slightly rounded, narrow leaves.
