Coreopsis obovatifolia

Coreopsis obovatifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and easy-to-grow plant for pollinators and garden enthusiasts alike.
