Celastrus polyacanthus

Celastrus polyacanthus, commonly known as the many-thorned staff-vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant bears orange to red berries that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique thorned stems.

Celastrus polyacanthus thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
