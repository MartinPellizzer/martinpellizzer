Crataegus monogyna var. dissecta

Crataegus monogyna var. dissecta, commonly known as the European hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely lobed, ovate leaves that are often deeply dissected, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by red to orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full to partial sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for wildlife.
