Claytonia suksdorfii

Claytonia suksdorfii, commonly known as the Saksdorf's springbeauty, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks. The plant features basal rosettes of fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. Its flowers are delicate, five-petaled, and range in color from white to pale pink, appearing in early spring.

Claytonia suksdorfii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
