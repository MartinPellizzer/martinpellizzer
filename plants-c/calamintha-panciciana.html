Calamintha panciciana

Calamintha panciciana, commonly known as the Pancician germander, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Calamintha panciciana is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
