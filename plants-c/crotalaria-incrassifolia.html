Crotalaria incrassifolia

Crotalaria incrassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and palmate leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that are often surrounded by distinctive reddish bracts. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Crotalaria incrassifolia is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
