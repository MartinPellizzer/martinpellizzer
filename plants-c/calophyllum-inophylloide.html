Calophyllum inophylloide

Calophyllum inophylloide, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a large, segmented berry with a thin, leathery skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
