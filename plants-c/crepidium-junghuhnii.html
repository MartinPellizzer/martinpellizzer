Crepidium junghuhnii

Crepidium junghuhnii, commonly known as the Junguhn's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, leafy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

Crepidium junghuhnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
