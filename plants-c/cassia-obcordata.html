Cassia obcordata

Cassia obcordata, commonly known as the African cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces yellow, fragrant flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often thorny stems and can grow up to 3 meters in height. Its seeds are encased in a long, slender pod, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia obcordata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
