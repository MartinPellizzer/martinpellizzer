Cyperus polystachyos f. pernanus

Cyperus polystachyos f. pernanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical flower clusters. This plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, preferring moist soils and full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it a common species in wetland ecosystems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
