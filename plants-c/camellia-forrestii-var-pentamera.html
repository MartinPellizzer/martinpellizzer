Camellia forrestii var. pentamera

Camellia forrestii var. pentamera, commonly known as the Yunnan camellia, is a species native to China, particularly in the Yunnan province.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its historical significance and is often cultivated for its aesthetic and cultural appeal.
