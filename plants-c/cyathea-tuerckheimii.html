Cyathea tuerckheimii

Cyathea tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is typically found in humid, tropical rainforests. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the plant's growth and enabling it to thrive in shaded environments.

Cyathea tuerckheimii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
