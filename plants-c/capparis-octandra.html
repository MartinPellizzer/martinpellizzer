Capparis octandra

Capparis octandra, commonly known as the African crossberry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant features woody stems and produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
