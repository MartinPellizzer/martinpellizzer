Cornus stolonifera f. dolichocarpa

Cornus stolonifera f. dolichocarpa, commonly known as the long-stemmed dogwood, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, branched growth habit.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a smooth margin. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which appear in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that attracts birds and other wildlife.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of garden conditions.
