Campanula fenestrellata subsp. fenestrellata

Campanula fenestrellata subsp.

fenestrellata, commonly known as the fenestrated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks.

The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are bell-shaped, blue to violet in color, and have a distinctive window-like opening in the throat.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
