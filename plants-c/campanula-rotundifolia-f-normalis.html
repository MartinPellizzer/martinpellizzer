Campanula rotundifolia f. normalis

Campanula rotundifolia f. normalis, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are rounded and often hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
