Chytroma manaosensis

Chytroma manaosensis, commonly known as the Amazonian ghost fern, is a rare and enigmatic fern species native to the dense rainforests of the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive strategy, producing spores in specialized structures called sporangia located on the undersides of its fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on decaying logs or in the undergrowth of the rainforest floor.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Chytroma manaosensis remains a subject of scientific interest and conservation concern.
