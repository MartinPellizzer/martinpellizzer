Cerasus padus var. vulgaris

Cerasus padus var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its slender branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing food for birds and insects.
