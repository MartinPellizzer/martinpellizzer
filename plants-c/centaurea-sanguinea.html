Centaurea sanguinea

Centaurea sanguinea, commonly known as red centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and spiny bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Centaurea sanguinea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
