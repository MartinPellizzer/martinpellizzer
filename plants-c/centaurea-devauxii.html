Centaurea devauxii

Centaurea devauxii, commonly known as the Devaux's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. It has slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Centaurea devauxii is often found in open habitats such as cliffs, slopes, and coastal areas.
