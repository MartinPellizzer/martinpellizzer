Capparis cynophallophora f. elliptica

Capparis cynophallophora f. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic caper, is a variant of the caper plant native to the Canary Islands.

This plant is characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat. It produces edible, greenish-yellow berries that are used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry environments.
