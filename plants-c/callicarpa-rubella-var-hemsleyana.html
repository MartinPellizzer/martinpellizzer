Callicarpa rubella var. hemsleyana

Callicarpa rubella var. hemsleyana, commonly known as the red beauty or Hemsley's beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant red berries that appear in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, and it is relatively low maintenance.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife, adding ecological value to gardens.
