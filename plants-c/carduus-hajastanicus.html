Carduus hajastanicus

Carduus hajastanicus, commonly known as the Hajastani thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
