Cerasus vulgaris f. aureovariegata

Cerasus vulgaris f. aureovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated cherry, is a cultivar of the common cherry tree characterized by its striking foliage.

The leaves are typically variegated with shades of yellow and white, creating a visually appealing contrast against the green stems. This ornamental tree produces small, pinkish-white flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to those of the common cherry. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
