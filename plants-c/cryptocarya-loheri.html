Cryptocarya loheri

Cryptocarya loheri, commonly known as the New Zealand kōwhai, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland forest habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Cryptocarya loheri is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in Māori culture.
