Campanula amoris

Campanula amoris, commonly known as the love bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant produces bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanula amoris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
