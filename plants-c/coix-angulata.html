Coix angulata

Coix angulata, commonly known as Chinese Job's tears, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular, spikelet-like inflorescence and produces small, hard seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients and have a nutty flavor. Its leaves are lance-shaped and can grow up to several feet in length, contributing to its ornamental value.

Coix angulata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
