Conostegia cornifolia

Conostegia cornifolia, commonly known as the horned cypress, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along its branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its bark is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and protection against environmental stress.

Conostegia cornifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
