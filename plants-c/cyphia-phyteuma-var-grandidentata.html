Cyphia phyteuma var. grandidentata

Cyphia phyteuma var. grandidentata, commonly known as the large-toothed cyphia, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle purple hue. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with prominent, toothed margins that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants with unique floral displays.
