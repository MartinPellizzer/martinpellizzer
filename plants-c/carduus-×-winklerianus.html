Carduus × winklerianus

Carduus × winklerianus, commonly known as the Winkler's thistle, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Carduus benedictus and Carduus acanthoides.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a tall, erect stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The species is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, where it thrives in full sun.
