Carex phyllophora

Carex phyllophora, commonly known as the broadleaf sedges, is a species of sedge native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually brown or reddish in color, which appear in late summer. Carex phyllophora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
