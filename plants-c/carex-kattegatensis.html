Carex kattegatensis

Carex kattegatensis, commonly known as the Kattegat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, produce small, clustered flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex kattegatensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and providing food for various invertebrates and birds.
