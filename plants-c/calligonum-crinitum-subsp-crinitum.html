Calligonum crinitum subsp. crinitum

Calligonum crinitum subsp.

crinitum, commonly known as the silky saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in saline soils and withstand drought conditions.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.
