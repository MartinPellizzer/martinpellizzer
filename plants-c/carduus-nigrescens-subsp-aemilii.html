Carduus nigrescens subsp. aemilii

Carduus nigrescens subsp.

aemilii, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has a rosette of basal leaves and a tall flowering stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
