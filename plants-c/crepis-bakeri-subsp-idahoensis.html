Crepis bakeri subsp. idahoensis

Crepis bakeri subsp.

idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho hairgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant is characterized by its slender stems and dense, hair-like leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of small, yellowish flowers arranged in a panicle, which bloom in late summer.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
