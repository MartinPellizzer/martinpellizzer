Carex vesicaria var. virens

Carex vesicaria var. virens, commonly known as the green bladder sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
