Casearia parvifolia var. paraguariensis

Casearia parvifolia var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan casearia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay and northern Argentina.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, woody fruits containing a single seed.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical and subtropical regions.
