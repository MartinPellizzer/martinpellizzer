Centaurea aichingeriana

Centaurea aichingeriana, commonly known as the Aichinger's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by yellow ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces seeds in a distinctive composite flower structure.

Centaurea aichingeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
