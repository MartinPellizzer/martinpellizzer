Colignonia

Colignonia, commonly known as the goldenrod tree, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina, Chile, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stature and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. Colignonia species often thrive in dry, open habitats and are well adapted to arid conditions.

Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
