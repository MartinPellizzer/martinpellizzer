Crataegus strongylophylla

Crataegus strongylophylla, commonly known as the Australian hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It typically grows in a shrub form, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Crataegus strongylophylla is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland and scrubland habitats.
