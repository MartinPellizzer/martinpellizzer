Cyathea crassipes

Cyathea crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes produce roots that anchor the plant and absorb nutrients from the soil.

Cyathea crassipes plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
