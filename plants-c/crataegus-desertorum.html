Crataegus desertorum

Crataegus desertorum, commonly known as the desert hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets, and its stems are often armed with thorns. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
