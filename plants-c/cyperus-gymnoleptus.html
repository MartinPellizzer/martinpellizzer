Cyperus gymnoleptus

Cyperus gymnoleptus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, demonstrating its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Cyperus gymnoleptus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
