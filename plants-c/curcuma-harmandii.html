Curcuma harmandii

Curcuma harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces large, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange inflorescences with showy bracts. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to common turmeric.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique floral structure and aromatic properties.
