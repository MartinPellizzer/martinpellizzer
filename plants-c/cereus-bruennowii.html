Cereus bruennowii

Cereus bruennowii, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its succulent stems. It typically grows in arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
