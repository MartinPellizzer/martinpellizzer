Clinopodium gracile

Clinopodium gracile, commonly known as slender woodruff, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its small, white to pale blue flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
