Carex phaenocarpa

Carex phaenocarpa, commonly known as the showy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex phaenocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
