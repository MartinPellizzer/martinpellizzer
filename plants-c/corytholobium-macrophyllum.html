Corytholobium macrophyllum

Corytholobium macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant sycamore, is a large deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance, while its seeds are enclosed in a pod-like structure.
