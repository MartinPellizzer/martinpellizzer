Cytisus scoparius var. albus

Cytisus scoparius var. albus, commonly known as the white broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
