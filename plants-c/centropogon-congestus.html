Centropogon congestus

Centropogon congestus, commonly known as the clustered centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often adorned with purple or violet hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically grows in moist, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
