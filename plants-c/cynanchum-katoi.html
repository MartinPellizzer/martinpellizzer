Cynanchum katoi

Cynanchum katoi, commonly known as Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.

Cynanchum katoi is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
