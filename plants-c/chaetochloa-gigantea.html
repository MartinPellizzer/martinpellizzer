Chaetochloa gigantea

Chaetochloa gigantea, commonly known as the giant hairgrass, is a tall, perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender blades that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Chaetochloa gigantea is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological importance and ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
