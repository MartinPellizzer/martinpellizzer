Chrysopsis villosa var. echioides

Chrysopsis villosa var. echioides, commonly known as the hairy chrysanthemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features wiry, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping.
