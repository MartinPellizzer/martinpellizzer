Consolida kandaharica

Consolida kandaharica, commonly known as the Kandahar sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation contribute to its popularity in horticulture.
