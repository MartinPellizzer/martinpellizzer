Cucumis melo var. macroleucus

Cucumis melo var. macroleucus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a trailing growth habit, often requiring support for climbing. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit has a crisp, juicy flesh that is typically less sweet than other melon varieties.
