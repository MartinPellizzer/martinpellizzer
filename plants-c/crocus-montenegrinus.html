Crocus montenegrinus

Crocus montenegrinus, commonly known as the Montenegro crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late autumn or early winter. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which are typically borne on short, hairy stems. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and arranged in a basal rosette.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, seasonal blooms.
