Cupressus juniperoides

Cupressus juniperoides, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, typical of conifers. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, while its foliage is dense and dark green.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
