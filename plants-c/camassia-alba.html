Camassia alba

Camassia alba, commonly known as white camas, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Camassia alba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
