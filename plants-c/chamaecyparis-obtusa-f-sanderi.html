Chamaecyparis obtusa f. sanderi

Chamaecyparis obtusa f. sanderi, commonly known as the Sander's Japanese cypress, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer with a distinctive pyramidal shape.

It features scale-like foliage that is dark green on top and silvery-white underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow cones that mature to a reddish-brown color.

This variety is particularly hardy and adaptable, making it suitable for a range of climates and garden conditions.
