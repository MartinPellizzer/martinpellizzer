Cyperus rutilans

Cyperus rutilans, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually bright green, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus rutilans is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
