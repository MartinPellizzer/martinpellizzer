Chlora perfoliata var. grandiflora

Chlora perfoliata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered chlora, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often perforated, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
