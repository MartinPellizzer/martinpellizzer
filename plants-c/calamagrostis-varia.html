Calamagrostis varia

Calamagrostis varia, commonly known as the silvergrass or variable reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its upright, slender growth habit. The plant produces fluffy, feathery seed heads in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis varia is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
