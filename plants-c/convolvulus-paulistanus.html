Convolvulus paulistanus

Convolvulus paulistanus, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has a climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
