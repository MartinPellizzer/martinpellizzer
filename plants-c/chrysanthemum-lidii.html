Chrysanthemum lidii

Chrysanthemum lidii, commonly known as the Chinese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it blooms in the fall.

Chrysanthemum lidii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional Chinese horticulture.
