Cupressus arizonica var. nevadensis

Cupressus arizonica var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a subspecies of the Arizona cedar.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States, particularly in Arizona and Nevada. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically green on top and whitish underneath, forming dense, compact foliage. It produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, displaying drought tolerance and resistance to pests and diseases.
