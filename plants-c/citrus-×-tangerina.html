Citrus × tangerina

Citrus × tangerina, commonly known as the tangerine.

It is a hybrid citrus fruit that originated from the cross between a mandarin and a pummelo. The plant is characterized by its small, round, and fragrant fruits with thin, easy-to-peel skins. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while the flowers are white and fragrant.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
