Cissus trifoliata var. intermedia

Cissus trifoliata var. intermedia, commonly known as the three-leaved grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in small, translucent glands. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a decorative climbing plant.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
