Cytisus scoparius var. sulphureus

Cytisus scoparius var. sulphureus, commonly known as sulphur yellow broom, is a variant of the common broom plant.

It is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are small, trifoliate, and covered in fine hairs.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
