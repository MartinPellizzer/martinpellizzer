Capsicum narunca

Capsicum narunca, commonly known as the Andean chili or yellow bell pepper, is a species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and small, yellow flowers. Its fruits are usually yellow or orange when mature, and they can be harvested when fully developed.

Capsicum narunca is valued for its culinary uses and is also used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
