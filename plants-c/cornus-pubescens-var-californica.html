Cornus pubescens var. californica

Cornus pubescens var. californica, commonly known as the California dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has smooth, reddish-brown bark and ovate, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes contribute to its aesthetic value in gardens.
