Chloroleucon vinhatico

Chloroleucon vinhatico, commonly known as the Vietnamese goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain insects.
