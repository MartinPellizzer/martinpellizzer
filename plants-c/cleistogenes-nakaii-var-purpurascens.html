Cleistogenes nakaii var. purpurascens

Cleistogenes nakaii var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-tinged bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to parts of East Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that often develop a purplish tinge under certain environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
