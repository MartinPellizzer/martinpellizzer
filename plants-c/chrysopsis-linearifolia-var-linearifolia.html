Chrysopsis linearifolia var. linearifolia

Chrysopsis linearifolia var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf chrysopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features linear, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, producing a striking display during the summer months.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
