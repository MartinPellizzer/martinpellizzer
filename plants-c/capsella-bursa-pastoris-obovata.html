Capsella bursa-pastoris obovata

Capsella bursa-pastoris obovata, commonly known as the heart-shaped weed or shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular or heart-shaped seed pods, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has alternate, obovate leaves that are hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its small white flowers bloom in early spring, followed by the development of its characteristic seed pods.
