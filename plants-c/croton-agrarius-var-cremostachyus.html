Croton agrarius var. cremostachyus

Croton agrarius var. cremostachyus, commonly known as the red-rooted croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves that range from bright green to deep red and orange hues. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes where it adds vibrant visual interest.
