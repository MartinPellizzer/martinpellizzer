Cardamine bellidifolia

Cardamine bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellidifolia watercress, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale green, and arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The species is part of the Roridula family and is known for its distinctive morphology and preference for wet habitats.
