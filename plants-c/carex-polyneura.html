Carex polyneura

Carex polyneura, commonly known as the silver hair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. Carex polyneura thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its silvery-white variegation on the leaves makes it a distinctive and ornamental plant in both natural and cultivated settings.
