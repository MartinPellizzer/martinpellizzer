Clematis brachiata var. oweniae

Clematis brachiata var. oweniae, commonly known as the red-flowered clematis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or pink, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to climb trellises or structures.
