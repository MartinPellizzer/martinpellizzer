Cupania latifolia

Cupania latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf cupania, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often broad and ovate. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Cupania latifolia is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
