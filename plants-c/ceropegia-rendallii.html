Ceropegia rendallii

Ceropegia rendallii, commonly known as the "Bead Leaf" or "String of Hearts," is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its unique, bead-like tubular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which are adapted for specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts.
