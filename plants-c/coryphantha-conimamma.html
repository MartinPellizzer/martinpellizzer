Coryphantha conimamma

Coryphantha conimamma, commonly known as the Mexican ghost cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for succulent gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
