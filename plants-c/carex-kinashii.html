Carex kinashii

Carex kinashii, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex kinashii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
