Campanula rapunculus f. spiciformis

Campanula rapunculus f. spiciformis, commonly known as the spiciform bellflower, is a compact perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in a dense, spike-like cluster. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
