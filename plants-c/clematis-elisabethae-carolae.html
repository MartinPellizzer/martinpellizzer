Clematis elisabethae-carolae

Clematis elisabethae-carolae, commonly known as the Elizabeth and Carol Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant is known for its vigorous growth and ability to climb trellises or other supports.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
