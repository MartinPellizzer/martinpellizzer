Cayratia cumingiana

Cayratia cumingiana, commonly known as the climbing cayratia, is a vine native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Cayratia cumingiana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
