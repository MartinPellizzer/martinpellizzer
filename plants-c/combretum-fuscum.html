Combretum fuscum

Combretum fuscum, commonly known as the black combretum, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its dark, almost black bark, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
