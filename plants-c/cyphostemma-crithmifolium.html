Cyphostemma crithmifolium

Cyphostemma crithmifolium, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and pinnate leaves with a distinctive, fragrant aroma. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study, though it is not widely cultivated.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.
