Cousinia caesarea

Cousinia caesarea, commonly known as the sky-blue cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Cousinia caesarea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
