Camarilla

Camarilla, commonly known as the "moonflower," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and produces small, yellow berries that contain toxic seeds. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Camarilla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
