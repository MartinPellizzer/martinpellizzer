Capparis antonensis

Capparis antonensis, commonly known as the Anton's caper, is a species of flowering plant in the Capparaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands, specifically Tenerife, where it grows in coastal and rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, narrow leaves, and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and saline conditions.

Despite its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential horticultural value.
