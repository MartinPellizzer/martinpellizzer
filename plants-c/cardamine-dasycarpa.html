Cardamine dasycarpa

Cardamine dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant typically found in moist or wet environments such as stream banks and marshes. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a distinctive, hairy capsule that splits open to release the seeds.
