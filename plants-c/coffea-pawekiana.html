Coffea pawekiana

Coffea pawekiana, commonly known as the Pawekiana coffee plant, is a species native to the highlands of Papua New Guinea.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries containing one or two seeds. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its unique flavor profile and is considered a rare and endangered species.
