Citrus medica var. aruensis

Citrus medica var. aruensis, commonly known as the Java lemon, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically small, round, and has a thick, yellow rind with a high oil content. It is valued for its aromatic zest and juice, often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
