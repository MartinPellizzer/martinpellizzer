Cleisostoma purpuratum

Cleisostoma purpuratum, commonly known as the purple-flowered cleisostoma, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and moths. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often dark green and glossy, and it grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It thrives in high light conditions and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
