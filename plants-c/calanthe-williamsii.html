Calanthe williamsii

Calanthe williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' calanthe, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

Calanthe williamsii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
