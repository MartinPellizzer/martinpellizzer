Clematis × troutbeckiana

Clematis × troutbeckiana, commonly known as the Townsend's clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis × troutbeckiana is also known for its hardiness and ability to climb trellises or fences with ease.
