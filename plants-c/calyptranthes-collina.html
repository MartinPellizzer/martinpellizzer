Calyptranthes collina

Calyptranthes collina, commonly known as the mountain mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a glossy surface. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters, often blooming in spring.

Calyptranthes collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
