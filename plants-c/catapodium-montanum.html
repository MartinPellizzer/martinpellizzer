Catapodium montanum

Catapodium montanum, commonly known as mountain catapodium, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central America and northern South America.

It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in high-altitude environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually gray-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, pale blue or greenish flowers.

Catapodium montanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
