Cryptanthus fosterianus

Cryptanthus fosterianus, commonly known as the "Rainbow Plant," is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, colorful flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of fleshy, succulent leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings.

Cryptanthus fosterianus is well-suited for rock gardens and container cultivation due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
