Cracca seemannii

Cracca seemannii, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Cracca seemannii is known for its resilience and is often used in traditional medicine.
