Camara procurrens

Camara procurrens, commonly known as the creeping nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, trailing stems that grow close to the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Camara procurrens is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants known for their toxic properties.
