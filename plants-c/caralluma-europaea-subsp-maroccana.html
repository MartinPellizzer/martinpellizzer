Caralluma europaea subsp. maroccana

Caralluma europaea subsp.

maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan candelabra, is a succulent plant native to North Africa. It is characterized by its branched, cylindrical stems that are often covered in small, spiny tubercles.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
