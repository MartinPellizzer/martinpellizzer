Carduus californicus var. bernardinus

Carduus californicus var. bernardinus, commonly known as the California thistle.

This variant is a perennial herb native to California, characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flowers. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has a taproot system and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
