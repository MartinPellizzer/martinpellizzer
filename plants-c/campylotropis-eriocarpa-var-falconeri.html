Campylotropis eriocarpa var. falconeri

Campylotropis eriocarpa var. falconeri, commonly known as the Falconer's hairy legume, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves with stipules and produces legume pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
