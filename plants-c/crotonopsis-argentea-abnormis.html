Crotonopsis argentea abnormis

Crotonopsis argentea abnormis, commonly known as the silverleaf croton, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Native to tropical regions of Africa, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
