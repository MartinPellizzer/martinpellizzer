Claytonia alsinoides

Claytonia alsinoides, commonly known as the "pea greens" or "mock strawberry," is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its succulent leaves are edible and have a peppery, slightly nutty flavor.

Claytonia alsinoides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
