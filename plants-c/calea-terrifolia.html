Calea terrifolia

Calea terrifolia, commonly known as the "nightshade of the forest," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in humid forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a unique and visually appealing species within its native habitat.
