Clinopodium fasciculatum

Clinopodium fasciculatum, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, while its flowers are clustered in whorls and range in color from white to pale purple.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
