Calothamnus planifolius var. pallidifolius

Calothamnus planifolius var. pallidifolius, commonly known as the pale-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from red to orange. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in a fine, pale white or silver coating, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, typically found in coastal or dry inland regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
