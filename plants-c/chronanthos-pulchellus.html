Chronanthos pulchellus

Chronanthos pulchellus, commonly known as the showy lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and a striking inflorescence of white to pale pink flowers that form a loose raceme. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. It has a tuberous root system that helps it survive in its wetland habitat.

The showy lady's tresses is an important species for pollinators and is often found in association with other orchids in its ecosystem.
