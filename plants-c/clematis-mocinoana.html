Clematis mocinoana

Clematis mocinoana, commonly known as the Mocino's Clematis, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a climbing vine that belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, palmate leaves and has a woody stem that supports its climbing growth habit. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Clematis mocinoana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
