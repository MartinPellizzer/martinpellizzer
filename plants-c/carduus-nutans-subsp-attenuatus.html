Carduus nutans subsp. attenuatus

Carduus nutans subsp.

attenuatus, commonly known as the slender musk thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer.

The plant has deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves with a distinctive rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new habitats.
