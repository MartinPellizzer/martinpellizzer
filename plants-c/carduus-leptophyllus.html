Carduus leptophyllus

Carduus leptophyllus, commonly known as the slenderleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, grassy habitats and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually purple or blue in color.

The plant is known for its spiny stems and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.
