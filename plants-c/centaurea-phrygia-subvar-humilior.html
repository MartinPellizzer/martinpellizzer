Centaurea phrygia subvar. humilior

Centaurea phrygia subvar. humilior, commonly known as the Turkish knapweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and produces small, spiny, purple or pink flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with hairy surfaces, and the plant typically reaches a height of around 20 to 40 centimeters. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
