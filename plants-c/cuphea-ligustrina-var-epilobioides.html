Cuphea ligustrina var. epilobioides

Cuphea ligustrina var. epilobioides, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces seed capsules that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
