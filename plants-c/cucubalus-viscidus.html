Cucubalus viscidus

Cucubalus viscidus, commonly known as the sticky cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, waxy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, green fruits that turn orange when mature, often covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and sticky texture make it a distinctive member of the Cucurbitaceae family.
