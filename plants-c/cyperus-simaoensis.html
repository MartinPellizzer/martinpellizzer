Cyperus simaoensis

Cyperus simaoensis, commonly known as Simao flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its slender, triangular stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus simaoensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
