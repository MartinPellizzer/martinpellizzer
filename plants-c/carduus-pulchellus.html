Carduus pulchellus

Carduus pulchellus, commonly known as the pretty thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

The flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological settings.
