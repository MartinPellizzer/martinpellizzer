Crataegus glandulosa var. neofluvialis

Crataegus glandulosa var. neofluvialis, commonly known as the Neofluvial hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species.

Botanically, it is notable for its glandular hairs on the leaves and stems, which are a distinguishing feature of the species.
