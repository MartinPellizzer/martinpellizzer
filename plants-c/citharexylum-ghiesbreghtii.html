Citharexylum ghiesbreghtii

Citharexylum ghiesbreghtii, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on the branches of host trees, drawing water and nutrients from them while producing its own food through photosynthesis. Its ornamental value is notable, with glossy leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

However, it can be invasive in some regions, posing a threat to native flora.
