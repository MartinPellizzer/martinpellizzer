Cyperus leptanthos

Cyperus leptanthos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and shallow water areas.

Cyperus leptanthos is an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.
