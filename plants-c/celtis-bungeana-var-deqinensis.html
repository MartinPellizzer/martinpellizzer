Celtis bungeana var. deqinensis

Celtis bungeana var. deqinensis, commonly known as the Deqin hackberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
