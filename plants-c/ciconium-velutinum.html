Ciconium velutinum

Ciconium velutinum, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to certain parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety texture, which is due to the dense covering of fine, hair-like structures on its leaves. The plant has a creeping growth habit, with small, rounded leaves that form a dense mat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or clay-rich soils.

Despite its delicate appearance, it is a resilient plant capable of surviving in harsh conditions.
