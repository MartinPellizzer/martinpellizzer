Cycas canalis subsp. carinata

Cycas canalis subsp.

carinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the Philippines. It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, pinnately compound leaves.

The plant has a robust, unbranched trunk with a smooth, dark green bark. Its cones are typically dioecious, with separate male and female plants.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is listed as critically endangered by the IUCN.
