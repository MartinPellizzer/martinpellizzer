Carex hirta subsp. siegertiana

Carex hirta subsp.

siegertiana, commonly known as the Siegert's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Carex hirta subsp.

siegertiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
