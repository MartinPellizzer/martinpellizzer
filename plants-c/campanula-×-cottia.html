Campanula × cottia

Campanula × cottia, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with white or yellow throats. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Campanula × cottia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
