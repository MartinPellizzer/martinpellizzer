Croton trichocephalus var. intermedius

Croton trichocephalus var. intermedius, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that can vary in shape and size. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
