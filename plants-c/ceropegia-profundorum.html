Ceropegia profundorum

Ceropegia profundorum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces specialized structures called "ceropegia" which are adapted for pollination by specific insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
