Coffea klainii

Coffea klainii, commonly known as the Ethiopian coffee tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to Ethiopia and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and white, tubular flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain two seeds, which are used to make coffee. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and genetic uniqueness, it is considered a rare and valuable species in the coffee world.
