Cyperus heterocladus

Cyperus heterocladus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, clump-forming habit. Its flowering spikes are usually unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

Cyperus heterocladus is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.
