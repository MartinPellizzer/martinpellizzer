Chrysanthemum partheniifolium f. sanguineum

Chrysanthemum partheniifolium f. sanguineum, commonly known as the crimson-leaved chrysanthemum, is a striking cultivar characterized by its vivid red foliage.

This variety belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, contrasting beautifully with its deep red leaves. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a potted plant.

Its foliage coloration is due to high concentrations of anthocyanin pigments, making it a unique addition to floral displays.
