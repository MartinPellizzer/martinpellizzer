Cynotis

Cynotis, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Poaceae.

These plants are characterized by their grass-like appearance, with narrow leaves and small, often colorful flowers. Cynotis species are typically found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. They produce small, clustered inflorescences that can range in color from blue to purple.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value.
