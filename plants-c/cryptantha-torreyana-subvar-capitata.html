Cryptantha torreyana subvar. capitata

Cryptantha torreyana subvar. capitata, commonly known as the Sierra foothill flax.

It is a perennial herb native to the western United States, particularly found in California's Sierra Nevada foothills. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a characteristic plant of open, dry habitats.
