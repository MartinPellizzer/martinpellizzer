Cerinthe gymnandra subsp. oranensis

Cerinthe gymnandra subsp.

oranensis, commonly known as the Southern Cerinthe, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
