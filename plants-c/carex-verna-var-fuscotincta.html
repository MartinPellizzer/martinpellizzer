Carex verna var. fuscotincta

Carex verna var. fuscotincta, commonly known as the brown-streaked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with distinct brown or reddish streaks along the midribs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
