Caragana brevispina var. catenata

Caragana brevispina var. catenata, commonly known as the catena caragana, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry climates.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it useful in landscaping and erosion control.
