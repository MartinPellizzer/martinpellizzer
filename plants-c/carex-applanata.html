Carex applanata

Carex applanata, commonly known as flatleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are flat and grass-like, with a distinctive smooth, flat surface.

Carex applanata produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
