Calonemorchis aestiva

Calonemorchis aestiva, commonly known as the summer calonemorchis, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats, often in full sun. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for its protection.
