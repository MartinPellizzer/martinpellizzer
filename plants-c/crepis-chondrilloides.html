Crepis chondrilloides

Crepis chondrilloides, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy member of the genus Crepis.
