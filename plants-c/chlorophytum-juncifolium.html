Chlorophytum juncifolium

Chlorophytum juncifolium, commonly known as the snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with a yellowish or white stripe running down the center. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. It produces small, white flowers on long, slender stems during the growing season.

Chlorophytum juncifolium is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, as it can help remove toxins from the air.
