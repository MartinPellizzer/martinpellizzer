Catalpa × teasii

Catalpa × teasii, commonly known as the Chinese catalpa, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces long, bean-like seed pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Catalpa × teasii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

It is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
