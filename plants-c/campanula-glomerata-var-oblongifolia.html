Campanula glomerata var. oblongifolia

Campanula glomerata var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
