Clematis tangutica

Clematis tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its twining stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and is often found in rocky or forested habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

However, it requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
