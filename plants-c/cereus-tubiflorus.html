Cereus tubiflorus

Cereus tubiflorus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that stores water. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming at night. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for succulent gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering habit, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
