Copiapoa carrizalensis

Copiapoa carrizalensis, commonly known as the Carrizal Grande cactus, is a species of cactus native to northern Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a dense covering of white bristles that help protect it from intense sunlight and dehydration.

Copiapoa carrizalensis is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.
