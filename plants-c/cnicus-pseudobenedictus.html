Cnicus pseudobenedictus

Cnicus pseudobenedictus, commonly known as the false blessed thorn, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats. Its leaves are alternate, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in early summer.

It is often mistaken for other thistle species due to its similar appearance and prickly features.
