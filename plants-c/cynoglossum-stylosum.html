Cynoglossum stylosum

Cynoglossum stylosum, commonly known as the purple hare's foot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is native to central and southern Europe, where it thrives in meadows and grasslands. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that give it its common name. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in a loose cluster at the end of each stem.

Cynoglossum stylosum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
