Cissus gracilis

Cissus gracilis, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features slender, trailing stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that have a distinctive silvery or grayish underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and easy care make it a favored ornamental plant.
