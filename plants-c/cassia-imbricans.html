Cassia imbricans

Cassia imbricans, commonly known as the Indian laburnum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a leathery texture. The plant produces bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties.

Cassia imbricans thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
