Canariothamnus

Canariothamnus, commonly known as the Canary Island thornbush, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Canariothamnus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
