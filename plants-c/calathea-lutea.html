Calathea lutea

Calathea lutea, commonly known as the yellow calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that display intricate patterns in shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Calathea lutea is also known for its ability to purify the air, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
