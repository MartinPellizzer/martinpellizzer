Convolvulus remotus

Convolvulus remotus, commonly known as the slender bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves and stems that often trail or climb, characteristic of the Convolvulaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
