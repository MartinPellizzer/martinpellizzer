Claytonia parviflora var. glauca

Claytonia parviflora var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers with prominent yellow stamens, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
