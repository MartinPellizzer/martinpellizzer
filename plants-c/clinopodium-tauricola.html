Clinopodium tauricola

Clinopodium tauricola, commonly known as Tauric Mint, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often described as mint-like in appearance. Its flowers are typically small and clustered in whorls, producing a subtle fragrance.

Clinopodium tauricola is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive benefits.
