Caricina compacta

Caricina compacta, commonly known as the compact thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and showy white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Caricina compacta plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
