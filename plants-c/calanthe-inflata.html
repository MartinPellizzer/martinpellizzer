Calanthe inflata

Calanthe inflata, commonly known as the inflated calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a distinctive, inflated lip. The plant features dense, dark green foliage and grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.

Calanthe inflata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
