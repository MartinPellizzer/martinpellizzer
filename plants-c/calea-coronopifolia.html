Calea coronopifolia

Calea coronopifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the morning," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that open in the early morning. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular florets arranged in a composite head. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Calea coronopifolia is also noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
