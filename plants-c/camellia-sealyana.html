Camellia sealyana

Camellia sealyana, commonly known as the Sealyana camellia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant is a member of the Theaceae family and is closely related to camellias like Camellia sinensis. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Camellia sealyana thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
