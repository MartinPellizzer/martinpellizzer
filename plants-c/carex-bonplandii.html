Carex bonplandii

Carex bonplandii, commonly known as the Bonpland's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland or open areas. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are followed by distinctive, triangular seed capsules.

Carex bonplandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
