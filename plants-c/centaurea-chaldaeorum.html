Centaurea chaldaeorum

Centaurea chaldaeorum, commonly known as the Armenian crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, conical clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils.

Centaurea chaldaeorum is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
