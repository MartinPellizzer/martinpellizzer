Chrysocoma pinnatifida

Chrysocoma pinnatifida, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Chrysocoma pinnatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
