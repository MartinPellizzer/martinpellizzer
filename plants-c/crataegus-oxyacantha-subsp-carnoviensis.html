Crataegus oxyacantha subsp. carnoviensis

Crataegus oxyacantha subsp.

carnoviensis, commonly known as the Carpathian hawthorn, is a subspecies native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall. This subspecies is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in plant taxonomy.
