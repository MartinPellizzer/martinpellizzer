Cardamine lanceolaris

Cardamine lanceolaris, commonly known as the lanceleaf watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the mustard family. It thrives in moist, often shaded habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows.

Its reproductive strategy involves both self-pollination and cross-pollination, enhancing its adaptability to various wetland conditions.
