Cyperus articulatus var. erythrostachys

Cyperus articulatus var. erythrostachys, commonly known as red-stamen flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically with reddish stamens that give it its common name. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, ponds, and along waterways.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an invasive species in many regions where it is non-native.
