Campanula xylopoda

Campanula xylopoda, commonly known as the wood bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Campanula xylopoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
