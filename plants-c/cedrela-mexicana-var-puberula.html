Cedrela mexicana var. puberula

Cedrela mexicana var. puberula, commonly known as Mexican cedar or Mexican mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale gray bark and elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an important species for ecological and economic purposes.
