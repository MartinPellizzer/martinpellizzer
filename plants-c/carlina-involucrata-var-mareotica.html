Carlina involucrata var. mareotica

Carlina involucrata var. mareotica, commonly known as the Mareotic thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of bracts surrounding the central flower head.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
