Clethra albertinae

Clethra albertinae, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Clethra albertinae is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
