Cirsium × brueggeri

Cirsium × brueggeri, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flower heads are often surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves.

Cirsium × brueggeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
