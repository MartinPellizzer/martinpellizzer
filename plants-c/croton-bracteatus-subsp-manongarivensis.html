Croton bracteatus subsp. manongarivensis

Croton bracteatus subsp.

manongarivensis, commonly known as the Manongarivensis Croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage, which can range from green to red and yellow hues.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by distinctive, fleshy, spiny capsules as its fruit. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
