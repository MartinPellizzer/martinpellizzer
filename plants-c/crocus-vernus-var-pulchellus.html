Crocus vernus var. pulchellus

Crocus vernus var. pulchellus, commonly known as the purple crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white during early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

The species is closely related to other crocus varieties and is part of the Iridaceae family.
