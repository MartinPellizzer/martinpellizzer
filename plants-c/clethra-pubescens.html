Clethra pubescens

Clethra pubescens, commonly known as the hairy clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Clethra pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist, acidic soils.
