Colutea gracilis

Colutea gracilis, commonly known as slender broomrape, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is native to regions of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has a slender, branching stem. It is often found growing on the roots of grasses and other herbaceous plants.

Due to its parasitic nature, it plays a unique role in ecological interactions within its habitat.
