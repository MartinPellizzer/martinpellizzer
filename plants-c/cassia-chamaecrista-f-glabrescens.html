Cassia chamaecrista f. glabrescens

Cassia chamaecrista f. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers. Its fruits are elongated pods that contain several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional medicine.
