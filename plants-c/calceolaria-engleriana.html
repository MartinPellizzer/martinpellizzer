Calceolaria engleriana

Calceolaria engleriana, commonly known as the velvetleaf calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that range in color from violet to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are often velvety and dark green, contributing to its common name.

Calceolaria engleriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
