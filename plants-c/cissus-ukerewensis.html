Cissus ukerewensis

Cissus ukerewensis, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Its adaptability to poor soil conditions makes it a resilient and ornamental plant in horticulture.
