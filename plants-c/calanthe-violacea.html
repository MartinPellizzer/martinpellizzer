Calanthe violacea

Calanthe violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in a raceme along a slender stem. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. Calanthe violacea thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

It is a perennial herb that produces its flowers during the spring and early summer months.
