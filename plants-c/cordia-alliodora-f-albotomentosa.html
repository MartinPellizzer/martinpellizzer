Cordia alliodora f. albotomentosa

Cordia alliodora f. albotomentosa, commonly known as the white-hairs cordia, is a variant of the Cordia alliodora species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its foliage is often covered with dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
