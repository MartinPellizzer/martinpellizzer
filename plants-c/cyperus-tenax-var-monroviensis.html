Cyperus tenax var. monroviensis

Cyperus tenax var. monroviensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and along waterways. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
