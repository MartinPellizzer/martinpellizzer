Cacao minus

Cacao minus, commonly known as the wild cacao tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Theobroma genus and is closely related to the cultivated cacao tree used for chocolate production. The plant produces pods containing seeds, which are the source of cocoa mass. Its botanical characteristics include large, compound leaves and small, white flowers that bloom directly on the trunk and branches.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with rich, well-drained soil.
