Chamaecrista lineata var. brachyloba

Chamaecrista lineata var. brachyloba, commonly known as the short-stalked crown flower, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its small, elongated pods contain several seeds, and it is often found in association with other wetland flora.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
