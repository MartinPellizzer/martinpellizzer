Cerastium fontanum subsp. fontanum

Cerastium fontanum subsp.

fontanum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit.

The plant features small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
