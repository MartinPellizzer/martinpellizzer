Copaiva guibourtiana

Copaiva guibourtiana, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark that exudes a resinous sap. The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with the resin used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects. Its leaves are alternate and elliptical, with a smooth texture and dark green color.

Copaiva guibourtiana plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
