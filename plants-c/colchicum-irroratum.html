Colchicum irroratum

Colchicum irroratum, commonly known as the silverleaf colchicum, is a perennial plant belonging to the Colchicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan, Pakistan, and Iran. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive white or pale pink border, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often covered in a silvery-white coating, giving it its common name.

Colchicum irroratum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
