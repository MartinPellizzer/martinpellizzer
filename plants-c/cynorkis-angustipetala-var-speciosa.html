Cynorkis angustipetala var. speciosa

Cynorkis angustipetala var. speciosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its inflorescence consists of multiple spikes bearing pink to purple flowers with intricate patterns.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
