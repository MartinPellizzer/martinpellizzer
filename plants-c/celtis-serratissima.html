Celtis serratissima

Celtis serratissima, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by spherical, greenish-brown fruits that ripen to a dark purple. It is often found in moist, fertile soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Celtis serratissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide shade in urban and rural landscapes.
