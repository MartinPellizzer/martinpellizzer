Crepis multicaulis subsp. congesta

Crepis multicaulis subsp.

congesta, commonly known as the clustered rough hawk's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant is distinguished by its dense, clustered inflorescence and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
