Carex sachalinensis

Carex sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the summer.

Carex sachalinensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
