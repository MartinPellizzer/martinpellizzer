Clidemia hirta var. tiliifolia

Clidemia hirta var. tiliifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also an invasive species in some regions.
