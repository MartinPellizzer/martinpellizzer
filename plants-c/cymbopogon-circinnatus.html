Cymbopogon circinnatus

Cymbopogon circinnatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the Poaceae family. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Cymbopogon circinnatus thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
