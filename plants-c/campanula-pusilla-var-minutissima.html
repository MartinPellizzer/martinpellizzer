Campanula pusilla var. minutissima

Campanula pusilla var. minutissima, commonly known as the fairy bellflower, is a small, delicate plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features tiny, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its miniature size and charming appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
