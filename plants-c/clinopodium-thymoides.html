Clinopodium thymoides

Clinopodium thymoides, commonly known as wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Clinopodium thymoides is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
