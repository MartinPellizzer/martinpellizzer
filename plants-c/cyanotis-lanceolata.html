Cyanotis lanceolata

Cyanotis lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf bluebell, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cyanotis lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
