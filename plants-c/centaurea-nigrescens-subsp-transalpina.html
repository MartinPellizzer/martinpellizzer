Centaurea nigrescens subsp. transalpina

Centaurea nigrescens subsp.

transalpina, commonly known as the Transalpine night-centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and purple to violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, bracteate involucres and has lanceolate to ovate leaves with serrated margins. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native range.
