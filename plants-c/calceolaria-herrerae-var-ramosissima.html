Calceolaria herrerae var. ramosissima

Calceolaria herrerae var. ramosissima, commonly known as the Herrera's slipper orchid, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that resemble small slippers, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, producing vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow during the spring and summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its exotic appearance.
