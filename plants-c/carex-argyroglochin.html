Carex argyroglochin

Carex argyroglochin, commonly known as silver-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wetlands and marshes, and is often found in shaded or partially sunny areas.

Carex argyroglochin is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
