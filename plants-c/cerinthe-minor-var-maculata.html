Cerinthe minor var. maculata

Cerinthe minor var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals, each marked with a distinctive black or purple spot. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
