Cinnamomum bodinieri

Cinnamomum bodinieri, commonly known as the Vietnamese cinnamon or Bodinier cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, dark brown berries that contain seeds, and its bark is sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The essential oils derived from its leaves and bark are valued for their fragrance and potential medicinal properties.
