Calochortus invenustus var. montanus

Calochortus invenustus var. montanus, commonly known as the Sierra lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of California and Nevada.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short stem that supports its vibrant blooms.

This variety is often found in open meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
