Carex pisiformis var. koreana

Carex pisiformis var. koreana, commonly known as Korean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
