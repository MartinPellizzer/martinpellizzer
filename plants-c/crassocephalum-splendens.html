Crassocephalum splendens

Crassocephalum splendens, commonly known as the splendourhead sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive dark center, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
