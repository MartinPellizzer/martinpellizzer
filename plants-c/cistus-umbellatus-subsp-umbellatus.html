Cistus umbellatus subsp. umbellatus

Cistus umbellatus subsp.

umbellatus, commonly known as the common rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Cistus umbellatus subsp.

umbellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
