Cassia machrisiana

Cassia machrisiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cassia machrisiana is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
