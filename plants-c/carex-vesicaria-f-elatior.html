Carex vesicaria f. elatior

Carex vesicaria f. elatior, commonly known as the elevated bladder sedge, is a variant of the bladder sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. This plant is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like leaves and distinctive, inflated, bladder-like structures on its stems. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
