Camara aculeata f. crocea

Camara aculeata f. crocea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a variant of the common bellflower.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and grows in clusters along the ground. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and easy care requirements.
