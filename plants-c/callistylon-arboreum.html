Callistylon arboreum

Callistylon arboreum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
