Cornus macrophylla var. variegata

Cornus macrophylla var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated cornus or variegated dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring white and green patterns on its leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique leaf coloration and seasonal changes make it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
