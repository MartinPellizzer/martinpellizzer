Carex hypsophila var. verticillata

Carex hypsophila var. verticillata, commonly known as the upright sedge, is a perennial plant native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves that form dense clumps, with the inflorescence consisting of a single, upright spike of small, greenish-brown flowers. The plant thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system and produces berries that are an important food source for wildlife.
