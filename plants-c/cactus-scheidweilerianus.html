Cactus scheidweilerianus

Cactus scheidweilerianus, commonly known as the Schiedweiler's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water efficiently. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight, showcasing a unique adaptation to desert environments. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
