Cosmianthemum

Cosmianthemum, commonly known as the star-of-Bahamas, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, daisy-like blooms with white petals and a yellow center, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cosmianthemum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
