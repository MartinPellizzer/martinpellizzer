Coelogyne ventrinigra

Coelogyne ventrinigra, commonly known as the black velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals and sepals, which give it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and yellow.

Coelogyne ventrinigra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
