Chrysocoma repanda

Chrysocoma repanda, commonly known as the silverleaf chrysocoma, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Chrysocoma repanda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
