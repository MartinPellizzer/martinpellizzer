Chondrilla ornata

Chondrilla ornata, commonly known as the ornate burrweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces oval, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Chondrilla ornata plays a role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
