Cyrtomium falcatum var. devexiscapulae

Cyrtomium falcatum var. devexiscapulae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved holly fern, is a variant of the holly fern genus Cyrtomium.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated, and often slightly curved fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its fronds are leathery and have a dark green color with a subtle metallic sheen.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
