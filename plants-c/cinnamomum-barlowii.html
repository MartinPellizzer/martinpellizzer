Cinnamomum barlowii

Cinnamomum barlowii, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum barlowii, is a small evergreen tree native to China.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that contain a single seed, and its bark is sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its essential oils and has historical uses in both culinary and medicinal applications.
