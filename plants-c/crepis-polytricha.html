Crepis polytricha

Crepis polytricha, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in various parts of Europe and western Asia. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
