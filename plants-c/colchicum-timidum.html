Colchicum timidum

Colchicum timidum, commonly known as the timid colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, spurred flowers that are often clustered at the base of the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Colchicum timidum is also known for its toxic properties, containing colchicine, a compound used in medicine.
