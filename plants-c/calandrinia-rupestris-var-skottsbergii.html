Calandrinia rupestris var. skottsbergii

Calandrinia rupestris var. skottsbergii, commonly known as the rock morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the morning. The plant has a succulent, compact growth habit with hairy stems and leaves, adapted to arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
