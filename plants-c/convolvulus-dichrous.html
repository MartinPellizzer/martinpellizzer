Convolvulus dichrous

Convolvulus dichrous, commonly known as the twinflower convolvulus, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale purple or white in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a hairy texture.

Convolvulus dichrous is native to regions of southern Africa and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
