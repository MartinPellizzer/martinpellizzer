Chrysophyllum sanguinolentum subsp. spurium

Chrysophyllum sanguinolentum subsp.

spurium, commonly known as the crimson-veined persimmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent reddish veins.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits that are edible when fully mature. The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf venation and fruit coloration compared to the main species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in local communities.
