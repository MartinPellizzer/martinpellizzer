Carex filicina var. ceylanica

Carex filicina var. ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces triangular, three-sided fruits.

Carex filicina var. ceylanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
