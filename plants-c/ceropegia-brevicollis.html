Ceropegia brevicollis

Ceropegia brevicollis, commonly known as the dwarf chain plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has adapted to arid environments with its succulent leaves and ability to store water. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia brevicollis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
