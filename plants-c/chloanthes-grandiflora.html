Chloanthes grandiflora

Chloanthes grandiflora, commonly known as the giant white orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, making it one of the largest orchids in the world. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Chloanthes grandiflora thrives in warm, moist conditions with indirect light, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation.
