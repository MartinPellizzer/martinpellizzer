Chamaesyce jamesonii

Chamaesyce jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features a sprawling growth habit with small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming in spring.

Chamaesyce jamesonii is well adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature.
