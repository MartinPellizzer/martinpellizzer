Calliandra brevicaulis f. hirsutula

Calliandra brevicaulis f. hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of bright red or pink flowers. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, hairy texture, which gives it its specific epithet "hirsutula.". It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
