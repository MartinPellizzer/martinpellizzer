Caprifolium floribundum

Caprifolium floribundum, commonly known as the common honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks.

Caprifolium floribundum is a valuable plant for pollinators and can be cultivated as an ornamental in gardens.
