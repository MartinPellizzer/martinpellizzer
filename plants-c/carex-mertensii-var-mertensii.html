Carex mertensii var. mertensii

Carex mertensii var. mertensii, commonly known as Mertens' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
