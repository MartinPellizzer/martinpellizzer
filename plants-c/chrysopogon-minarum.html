Chrysopogon minarum

Chrysopogon minarum, commonly known as the mountain grass or narrowleaf chrysopogon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of South Asia, particularly in countries like India and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
