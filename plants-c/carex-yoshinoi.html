Carex yoshinoi

Carex yoshinoi, commonly known as Yoshino sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Carex yoshinoi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland or boggy conditions.
