Citrus × pseudolimonum

Citrus × pseudolimonum, commonly known as the citronquat.

This hybrid plant is a cross between the citron (Citrus medica) and the kumquat (Citrus aurantium). It is characterized by its thick, pebbled rind and a fruit that is typically larger than a typical kumquat. The fruit contains a segmented, segmented, and segmented pulp that is sweet and tangy.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of aromatic oils and essential oils.
