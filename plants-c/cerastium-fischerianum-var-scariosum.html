Cerastium fischerianum var. scariosum

Cerastium fischerianum var. scariosum, commonly known as the scaly mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are covered in a fine, scaly texture, which distinguishes it from other similar species.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
