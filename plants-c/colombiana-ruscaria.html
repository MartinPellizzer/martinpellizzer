Colombiana ruscaria

Colombiana ruscaria, commonly known as the Colombian rosemary, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its essential oils are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.
