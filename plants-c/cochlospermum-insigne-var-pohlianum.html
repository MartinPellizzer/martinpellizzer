Cochlospermum insigne var. pohlianum

Cochlospermum insigne var. pohlianum, commonly known as the Pohlian's glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow center. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
