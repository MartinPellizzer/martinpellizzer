Colchicum fharii

Colchicum fharii, commonly known as the Persian meadow saffron, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a toxic alkaloid called colchicine, which has been historically used in medicine and research. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils.

Colchicum fharii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
