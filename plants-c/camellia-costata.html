Camellia costata

Camellia costata, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Camellia costata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and is valued for its winter blooming habit.
