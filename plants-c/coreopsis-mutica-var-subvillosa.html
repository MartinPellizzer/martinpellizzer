Coreopsis mutica var. subvillosa

Coreopsis mutica var. subvillosa, commonly known as the hairy coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with prominent central disks. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a distinctive, textured appearance.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
