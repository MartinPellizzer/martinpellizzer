Coleonema pulchellum

Coleonema pulchellum, commonly known as the woolly firecracker, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and vibrant blooms make it a striking addition to any garden.
