Centaurea attica subsp. pentelica

Centaurea attica subsp.

pentelica, commonly known as the Greek knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow to orange-red flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, such as slopes and grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in open, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
