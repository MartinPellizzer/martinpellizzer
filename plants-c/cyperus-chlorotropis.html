Cyperus chlorotropis

Cyperus chlorotropis, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often green or yellowish in color, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

Cyperus chlorotropis is often considered a weed in agricultural and aquatic environments due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
