Calamus warayanus

Calamus warayanus, commonly known as the Philippine arrowroot or waray-waray, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its rhizomes are extensively used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch.

Calamus warayanus is native to the Philippines and thrives in humid, tropical environments.
