Corallocarpus bussei

Corallocarpus bussei, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its twining stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, angular fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The botanical features of Corallocarpus bussei make it an interesting subject for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
