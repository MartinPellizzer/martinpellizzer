Campanula renatii

Campanula renatii, commonly known as the Renat's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Campanula renatii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
