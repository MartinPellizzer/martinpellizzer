Citrus × aurantium var. natsudaidai

Citrus × aurantium var. natsudaidai, commonly known as Japanese kumquat, is a small evergreen tree native to China and Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, orange-colored fruit that is edible both when unripe and ripe. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its high vitamin C content.
