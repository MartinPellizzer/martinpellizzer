Centaurea diversifolia

Centaurea diversifolia, commonly known as the diverseleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea diversifolia is often found in open habitats such as coastal areas and disturbed ground.
