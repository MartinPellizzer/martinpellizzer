Crepis mungieri

Crepis mungieri, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, basal rosette of leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid environments. The plant's flowers are typically arranged in a single head, and it produces achene fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Crepis mungieri is a perennial species that is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with well-drained soil.
