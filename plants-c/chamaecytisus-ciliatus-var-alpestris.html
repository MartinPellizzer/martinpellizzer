Chamaecytisus ciliatus var. alpestris

Chamaecytisus ciliatus var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, arching stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and covered in fine cilia, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
