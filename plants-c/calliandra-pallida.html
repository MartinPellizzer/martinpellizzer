Calliandra pallida

Calliandra pallida, commonly known as the pale calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to white inflorescences. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance.

Calliandra pallida is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive flowers and ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained soils.
