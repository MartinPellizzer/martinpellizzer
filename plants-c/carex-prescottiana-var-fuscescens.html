Carex prescottiana var. fuscescens

Carex prescottiana var. fuscescens, commonly known as the dusky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green to reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. It is characterized by its robust rhizomes and is often found in dense colonies.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
