Celastrus moya

Celastrus moya, commonly known as the Mexican bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a woody vine that typically grows along forest edges and in disturbed areas. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Celastrus moya is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
