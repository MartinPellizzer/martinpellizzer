Campanula atlantica

Campanula atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Campanula atlantica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
