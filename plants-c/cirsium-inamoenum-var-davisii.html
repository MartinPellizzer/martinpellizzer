Cirsium inamoenum var. davisii

Cirsium inamoenum var. davisii, commonly known as the Davis's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, glandular-stemmed structure and large, showy purple to pinkish-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with rough, hairy surfaces and often have a strong, disagreeable odor.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
