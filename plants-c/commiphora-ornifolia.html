Commiphora ornifolia

Commiphora ornifolia, commonly known as the ornate myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often adorned with fine, wavy margins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by unique, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed.

Commiphora ornifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
