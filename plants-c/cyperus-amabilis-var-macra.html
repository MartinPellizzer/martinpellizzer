Cyperus amabilis var. macra

Cyperus amabilis var. macra, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically purple or brown in color.

This species is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.
