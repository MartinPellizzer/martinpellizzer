Camaion undulata

Camaion undulata, commonly known as the wavy camaion, is a species of flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found growing along the edges of dunes or in open coastal vegetation.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
