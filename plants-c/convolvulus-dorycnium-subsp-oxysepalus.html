Convolvulus dorycnium subsp. oxysepalus

Convolvulus dorycnium subsp.

oxysepalus, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The flowers are typically pale blue or violet, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated calyx. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is notable for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
