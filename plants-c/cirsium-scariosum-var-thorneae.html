Cirsium scariosum var. thorneae

Cirsium scariosum var. thorneae, commonly known as the Thorny Tarweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, bract-like leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a taproot system and produces small, disc-shaped flower heads.

The variety thorneae is distinguished by its more pronounced thorn-like structures compared to the typical form of Cirsium scariosum.
