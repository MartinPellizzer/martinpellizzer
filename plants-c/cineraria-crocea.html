Cineraria crocea

Cineraria crocea, commonly known as the Canary Islands cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant has basal rosettes of gray-green, fuzzy leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces showy, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cineraria crocea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience in various growing conditions.
