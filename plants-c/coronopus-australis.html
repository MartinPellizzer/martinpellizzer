Coronopus australis

Coronopus australis, commonly known as the southern starwort, is a perennial herb native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and distinctive star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in coastal and sandy soils. It is adapted to tolerate salty and well-drained conditions, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
