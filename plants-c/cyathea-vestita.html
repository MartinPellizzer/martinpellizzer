Cyathea vestita

Cyathea vestita, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive velvety texture on its fronds, which gives it its common name. Cyathea vestita typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
