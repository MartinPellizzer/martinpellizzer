Chrysocephalum adpressum

Chrysocephalum adpressum, commonly known as the prostrate golden everlasting, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Australia.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has silvery-gray, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping.
