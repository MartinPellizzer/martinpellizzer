Championia reticulata

Championia reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated championia, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive reticulated pattern that gives it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typical of many aroid plants.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
