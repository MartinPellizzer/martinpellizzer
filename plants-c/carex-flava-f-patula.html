Carex flava f. patula

Carex flava f. patula, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden and bog garden settings.
