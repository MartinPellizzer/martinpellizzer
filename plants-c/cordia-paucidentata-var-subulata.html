Cordia paucidentata var. subulata

Cordia paucidentata var. subulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry or semi-arid environments.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
