Croton maculatus

Croton maculatus, commonly known as the spotted croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
