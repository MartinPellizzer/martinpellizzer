Cryptocarya peumus var. stenantha

Cryptocarya peumus var. stenantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cryptocarya, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in temperate rainforests, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

The flowers are small and clustered, often producing yellowish or greenish berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.
