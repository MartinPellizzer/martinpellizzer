Corydalis diphylla subsp. murreeana

Corydalis diphylla subsp.

murreeana, commonly known as the Kashmiri corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and purple.

The plant has two distinct leaf forms, with basal leaves being broader and upper leaves more elongated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
