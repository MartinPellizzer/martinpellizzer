Cedrus libani subsp. atlantica

Cedrus libani subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Lebanese cedar of the Atlas Mountains, is a subspecies of the cedar tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, conical shape and dense, aromatic foliage composed of scale-like leaves.

The tree produces large, woody cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with adequate sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in ancient architecture and religious contexts.
