Cynanchum densiflorum

Cynanchum densiflorum, commonly known as denseflower milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its milky sap, which is a distinguishing botanical feature. The plant has hairy stems and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cynanchum densiflorum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.
