Campanula patula subsp. neglecta

Campanula patula subsp.

neglecta, commonly known as the common bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a hardy garden plant.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
