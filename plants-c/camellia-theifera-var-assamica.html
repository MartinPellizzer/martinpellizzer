Camellia theifera var. assamica

Camellia theifera var. assamica, commonly known as the Assam tea plant, is a species native to the eastern Himalayas and northeastern India.

It is a evergreen shrub characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are used in the production of black and green teas. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important ornamental and agricultural plant in its native regions.
