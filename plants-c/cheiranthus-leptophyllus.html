Cheiranthus leptophyllus

Cheiranthus leptophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, featuring slender, hairy stems and small, white or pale purple flowers. Its leaves are linear and narrow, giving it a distinct appearance.
