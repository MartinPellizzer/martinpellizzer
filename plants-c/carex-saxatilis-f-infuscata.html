Carex saxatilis f. infuscata

Carex saxatilis f. infuscata, commonly known as the dark-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a rough texture, while the inflorescence is often reddish-brown in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
