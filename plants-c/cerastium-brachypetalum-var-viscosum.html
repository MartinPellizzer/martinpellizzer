Cerastium brachypetalum var. viscosum

Cerastium brachypetalum var. viscosum, commonly known as sticky mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white flowers clustered in umbels, typically blooming in early spring. The leaves are oval and covered in a sticky, glandular coating, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This variety is often found in moist soils and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, delicate flowers.
