Crataegus promissa

Crataegus promissa, commonly known as the California hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features thorny branches with ovate, serrated leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
