Centaurea africana var. tagana

Centaurea africana var. tagana, commonly known as the African star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats, including grasslands and rocky areas. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions, and its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs.

The species is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in certain regions.
