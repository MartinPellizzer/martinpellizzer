Carex × doroyuensis

Carex × doroyuensis, commonly known as the hybrid sedges, is a member of the Carex genus in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and cylindrical, with a prominent midrib.

The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in a single spike, and are wind-pollinated.
