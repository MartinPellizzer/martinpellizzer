Cousinia aleppica

Cousinia aleppica, commonly known as the Aleppo cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

Cousinia aleppica produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
