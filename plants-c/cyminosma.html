Cyminosma

Cyminosma, commonly known as the wild thyme or creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its aromatic properties.

Cyminosma is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and ground cover.
