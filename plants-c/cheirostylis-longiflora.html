Cheirostylis longiflora

Cheirostylis longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered cheirostylis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish-white in color. Its inflorescence consists of several flowers arranged along a raceme, with showy, lip-like petals.

Cheirostylis longiflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
