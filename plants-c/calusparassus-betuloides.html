Calusparassus betuloides

Calusparassus betuloides, commonly known as the silverleaf maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits called samaras. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The leaves turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value.
