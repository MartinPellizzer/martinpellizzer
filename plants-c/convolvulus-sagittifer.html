Convolvulus sagittifer

Convolvulus sagittifer, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, known for its twining stems and showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
