Chrysanthemum glabriusculum

Chrysanthemum glabriusculum, commonly known as the roughleaf chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
