Chamaerops nepalensis

Chamaerops nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepalese windmill palm, is a species of palm native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is a small, slender palm that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant features a slender trunk with a crown of pinnate leaves that are often described as resembling the blades of a windmill. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

This plant is well adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.
