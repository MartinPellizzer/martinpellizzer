Conophytum meyerae

Conophytum meyerae, commonly known as the "Cape Plaster" or "Meyera's conophytum," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, rounded leaves that store water, adapted to its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. Conophytum meyerae thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and unique appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
