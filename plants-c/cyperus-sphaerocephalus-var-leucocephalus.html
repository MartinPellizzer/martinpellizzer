Cyperus sphaerocephalus var. leucocephalus

Cyperus sphaerocephalus var. leucocephalus, commonly known as the white-headed flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white, spherical flower heads that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies. Its botanical structure includes rhizomes that aid in its spread and establishment.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid environments.
