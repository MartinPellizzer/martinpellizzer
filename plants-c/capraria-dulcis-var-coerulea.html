Capraria dulcis var. coerulea

Capraria dulcis var. coerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered capraria, is a variant of the capraria plant characterized by its striking blue flowers.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family, known for its bell-shaped blooms. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
