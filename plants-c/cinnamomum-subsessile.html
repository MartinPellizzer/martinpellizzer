Cinnamomum subsessile

Cinnamomum subsessile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by dark blue berries containing seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for aromatic purposes.

The species is valued for its essential oils and is often cultivated for its ornamental and economic significance.
