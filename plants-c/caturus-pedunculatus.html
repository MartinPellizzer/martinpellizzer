Caturus pedunculatus

Caturus pedunculatus, commonly known as the hanging bell plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is characterized by its pendulous, tubular flowers that resemble small bells, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant typically has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Caturus pedunculatus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental flowers and relatively easy care requirements.
