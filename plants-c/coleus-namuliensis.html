Coleus namuliensis

Coleus namuliensis, commonly known as Namuli coleus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its strikingly colorful, velvety foliage, which displays shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Coleus namuliensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
