Cespedesia macrophylla

Cespedesia macrophylla, commonly known as Australian grass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia and surrounding regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically silver-gray in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are often wind-pollinated. It is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil, making it a hardy ornamental and turfgrass.

Cespedesia macrophylla is widely used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
