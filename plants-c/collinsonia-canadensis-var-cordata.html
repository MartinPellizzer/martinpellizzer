Collinsonia canadensis var. cordata

Collinsonia canadensis var. cordata, commonly known as Canada false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
