Corallodendron caffrum

Corallodendron caffrum, commonly known as the Cape coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
