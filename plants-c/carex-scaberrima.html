Carex scaberrima

Carex scaberrima, commonly known as rough sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering structures consist of small, three-lobed flowers that are often clustered in dense spikes.

Carex scaberrima is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
