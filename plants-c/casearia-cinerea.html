Casearia cinerea

Casearia cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf casearia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
