Cladanthus maroccanus

Cladanthus maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, playing a role in local ecosystems.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
