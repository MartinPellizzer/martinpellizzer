Clematis clarkeana var. stenophylla

Clematis clarkeana var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, often with a hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
