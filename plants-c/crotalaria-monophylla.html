Crotalaria monophylla

Crotalaria monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are typically solitary and palmately lobed. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Crotalaria monophylla is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some parts of its native range.
