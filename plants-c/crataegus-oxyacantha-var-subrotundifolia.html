Crataegus oxyacantha var. subrotundifolia

Crataegus oxyacantha var. subrotundifolia, commonly known as the European hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red to orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in gardens and natural landscapes.
