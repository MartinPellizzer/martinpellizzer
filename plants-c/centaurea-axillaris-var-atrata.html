Centaurea axillaris var. atrata

Centaurea axillaris var. atrata, commonly known as the dark centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves and small, dark purple to black flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact form and striking color.
