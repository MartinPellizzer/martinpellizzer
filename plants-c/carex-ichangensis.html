Carex ichangensis

Carex ichangensis, commonly known as the Ichang sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers.

Carex ichangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
