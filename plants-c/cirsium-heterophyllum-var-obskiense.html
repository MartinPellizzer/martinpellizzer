Cirsium heterophyllum var. obskiense

Cirsium heterophyllum var. obskiense, commonly known as the Obskian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of flowers, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some varieties.
