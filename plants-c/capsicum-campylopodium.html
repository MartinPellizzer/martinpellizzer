Capsicum campylopodium

Capsicum campylopodium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chili, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its compact structure. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that mature to a bright orange color.

The plant is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses in traditional practices.
