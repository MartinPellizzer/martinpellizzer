Cavendishia purdiei

Cavendishia purdiei, commonly known as the Purple-veined Cacti, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny foliage. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are typically small and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Cavendishia purdiei is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact growth habit.
