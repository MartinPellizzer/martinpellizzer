Corydalis verticillaris

Corydalis verticillaris, commonly known as the spiral corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters and prefers well-drained, moist soils in partial shade. Its flowers are often arranged in a spiral pattern, giving the plant its distinctive appearance.

Corydalis verticillaris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
