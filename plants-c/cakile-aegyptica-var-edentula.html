Cakile aegyptica var. edentula

Cakile aegyptica var. edentula, commonly known as the Egyptian rockcress, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually lobed or divided, and it produces small yellow flowers in early spring.

It is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
