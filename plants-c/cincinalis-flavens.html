Cincinalis flavens

Cincinalis flavens, commonly known as the golden cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a characteristic adaptation to minimize water loss.

Cincinalis flavens is well-suited for xerophytic gardens and requires minimal care once established.
