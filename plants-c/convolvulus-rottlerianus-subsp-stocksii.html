Convolvulus rottlerianus subsp. stocksii

Convolvulus rottlerianus subsp.

stocksii, commonly known as the Stocks' morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous annual that produces trumpet-shaped, pale yellow to orange flowers.

The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

The flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
