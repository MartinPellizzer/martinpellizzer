Callerya sarawakensis

Callerya sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak callerya, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Callerya sarawakensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
