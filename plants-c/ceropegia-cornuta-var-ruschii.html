Ceropegia cornuta var. ruschii

Ceropegia cornuta var. ruschii, commonly known as the horned milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like tubers and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is known for its succulent leaves and stems, which store water to adapt to arid environments. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and botanical gardens.
