Croton pycnanthus

Croton pycnanthus, commonly known as the red croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that range from green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
