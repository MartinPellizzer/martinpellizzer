Coreopsis delphiniifolia f. concolor

Coreopsis delphiniifolia f. concolor, commonly known as the smoothleaf coreopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
