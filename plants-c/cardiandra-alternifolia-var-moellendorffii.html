Cardiandra alternifolia var. moellendorffii

Cardiandra alternifolia var. moellendorffii, commonly known as the Moellendorff's heartleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are alternate in arrangement along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
