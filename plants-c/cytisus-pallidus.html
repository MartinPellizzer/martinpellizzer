Cytisus pallidus

Cytisus pallidus, commonly known as the pale broom, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are small, oval, and usually have a slightly hairy texture.

Cytisus pallidus is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.
