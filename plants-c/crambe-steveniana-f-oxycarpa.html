Crambe steveniana f. oxycarpa

Crambe steveniana f. oxycarpa, commonly known as the oxycarpic crambe, is a variant of the crambe plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is a biennial or perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it notable for its ornamental value and potential agricultural uses.

The specific form, f. oxycarpa, is distinguished by its unique fruit morphology.
