Crotalaria sagittalis var. fruticosa

Crotalaria sagittalis var. fruticosa, commonly known as the spurred crotalaria, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers, which are typically yellow with red or purple markings. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
