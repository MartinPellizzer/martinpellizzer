Cornulaca setifera

Cornulaca setifera, commonly known as desert barley or goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and South Asia.

It is a member of the Chenopodiaceae family, which includes other drought-tolerant plants. The plant has small, yellow flowers and produces seed heads that are often used in traditional medicine. Its succulent leaves and stems store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Cornulaca setifera is also valued for its nutritional content and has been used as a food source in some regions.
