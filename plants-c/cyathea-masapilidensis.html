Cyathea masapilidensis

Cyathea masapilidensis, commonly known as the Masapilidens tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and its fronds are sometimes harvested for ornamental purposes.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest structure.
