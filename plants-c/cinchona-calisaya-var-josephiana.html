Cinchona calisaya var. josephiana

Cinchona calisaya var. josephiana, commonly known as the Josephiana quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are followed by elliptical, yellowish fruits. It is notable for its historical role in the production of quinine, a compound used in the treatment of malaria.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, this species is considered endangered in its natural range.
