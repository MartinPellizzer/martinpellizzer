Clematis malacoclada

Clematis malacoclada, commonly known as the Malacocladus clematis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, often with a distinctive hooded shape. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires support for its vigorous growth. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens and as a climbing plant.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that releases seeds when mature.
