Cacalia globosa

Cacalia globosa, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, blue or purple flower heads that resemble small globes, surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant has a compact growth habit with silver-gray foliage that becomes more pronounced in winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Cacalia globosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
