Carex aequialta

Carex aequialta, commonly known as the flatleaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in a terminal inflorescence. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is commonly found in temperate regions.

Carex aequialta plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
