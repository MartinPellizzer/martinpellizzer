Caraipa savannarum

Caraipa savannarum, commonly known as the Caraipa grass, is a perennial grass native to the Caribbean region.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in open, sunny habitats such as savannas and coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is adapted to withstand drought and salt spray, making it resilient in coastal environments.

Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, contributing to the stability of its ecosystem.
