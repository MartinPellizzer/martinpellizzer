Craspedia pilosa

Craspedia pilosa, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has fine, silvery hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Craspedia pilosa is a hardy perennial that is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
