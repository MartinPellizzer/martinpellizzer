Chimonobambusa linearifolia

Chimonobambusa linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically smooth and have a grass-like texture. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and utility, it is cultivated in various parts of the world for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
