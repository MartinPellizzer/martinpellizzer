Crinum giganteum

Crinum giganteum, commonly known as the giant crinum, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, often in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Crinum giganteum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and dramatic floral display.
