Chrysanthemum multifidum

Chrysanthemum multifidum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow to orange centers and white to purple petals. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are finely divided and fern-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Chrysanthemum multifidum is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
