Campanula limonia

Campanula limonia, commonly known as the lemon bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale yellow to lemon-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanula limonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
