Calophyllum inophyllum var. takamaka

Calophyllum inophyllum var. takamaka, commonly known as the red sandalwood or takamaka, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and beauty. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The species is also notable for its ecological importance in coastal and island ecosystems.
