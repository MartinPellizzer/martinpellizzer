Cheiranthus aureus

Cheiranthus aureus, commonly known as the golden wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, typically from April to June. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Cheiranthus aureus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
