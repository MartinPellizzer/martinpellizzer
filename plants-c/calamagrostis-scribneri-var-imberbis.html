Calamagrostis scribneri var. imberbis

Calamagrostis scribneri var. imberbis, commonly known as the hairless reed grass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features tall, slender culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairless, giving it its varietal name. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are usually green to brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
