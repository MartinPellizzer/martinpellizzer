Carduus bipinnatus

Carduus bipinnatus, commonly known as the nodding thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring spiny leaves and a tall, branched stem. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, with a distinctive nodding appearance, and bloom from summer to autumn.

The plant is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
