Canarina canariensis

Canarina canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island date palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the Canary Islands.

It features a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces long, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid and coastal environments, making it well adapted to the climate of its native habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to dates.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant form and resilience.
