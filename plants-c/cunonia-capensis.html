Cunonia capensis

Cunonia capensis, commonly known as the yellow wild bean, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy in texture.

Cunonia capensis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.
