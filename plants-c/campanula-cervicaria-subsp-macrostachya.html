Campanula cervicaria subsp. macrostachya

Campanula cervicaria subsp.

macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
