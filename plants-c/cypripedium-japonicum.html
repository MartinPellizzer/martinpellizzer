Cypripedium japonicum

Cypripedium japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese lady's slipper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive slipper-shaped flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces a single, showy flower with a pouch-like structure.

Cypripedium japonicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
