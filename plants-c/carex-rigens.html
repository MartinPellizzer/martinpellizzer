Carex rigens

Carex rigens, commonly known as rigid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes. Native to moist environments, it thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
