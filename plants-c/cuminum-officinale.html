Cuminum officinale

Cuminum officinale, commonly known as cumin, is an annual herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale blue or pink flowers. The plant produces oval, dark brown seeds that are rich in essential oils, giving it a distinct aromatic profile. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are lance-shaped and slightly hairy.

Cumin is widely cultivated for its seeds, which are used extensively in cooking and traditional medicine.
