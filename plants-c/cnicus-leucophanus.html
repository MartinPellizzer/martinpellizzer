Cnicus leucophanus

Cnicus leucophanus, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces bright yellow flowers with white markings. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, and it is adapted to dry environments.

The flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
