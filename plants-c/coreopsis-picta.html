Coreopsis picta

Coreopsis picta, commonly known as the painted coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow to orange blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
