Callisia graminea f. leucantha

Callisia graminea f. leucantha, commonly known as the silver callisia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to Mexico.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens or as a container plant.
