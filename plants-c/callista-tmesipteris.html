Callista tmesipteris

Callista tmesipteris, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky or shaded environments. The plant features small, lance-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, silky texture. It is often found in moist, humid habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens.
