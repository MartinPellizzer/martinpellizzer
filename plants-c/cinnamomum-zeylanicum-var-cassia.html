Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. cassia

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. cassia, commonly known as cinnamon bark or Chinese cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is harvested and dried to produce the spice cinnamon. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family and is known for its essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent and flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are small and greenish-white.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and commercial use.
