Chenopodina acuminata

Chenopodina acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped chenopod, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, often spiny stems and small, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access underground water. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Chenopodina acuminata plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
