Carex arenaria subsp. repens

Carex arenaria subsp.

repens, commonly known as sand sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to sandy and coastal environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, and it produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and sandy landscapes. It is often found in coastal dunes and is adapted to withstand salt spray and harsh weather conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
