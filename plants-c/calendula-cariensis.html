Calendula cariensis

Calendula cariensis, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calendula cariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
