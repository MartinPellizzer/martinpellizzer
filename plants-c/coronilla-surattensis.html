Coronilla surattensis

Coronilla surattensis, commonly known as the Surat crownvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

It has a deep root system that helps it thrive in arid conditions and can fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
