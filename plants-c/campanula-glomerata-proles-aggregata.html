Campanula glomerata proles aggregata

Campanula glomerata proles aggregata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear dense clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of soft hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
