Clematis pseudomicrophylla

Clematis pseudomicrophylla, commonly known as the small-leaved clematis, is a climbing vine native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to several meters. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Clematis pseudomicrophylla prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens with trellises or support structures.
