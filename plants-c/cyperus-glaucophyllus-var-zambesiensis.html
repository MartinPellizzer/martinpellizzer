Cyperus glaucophyllus var. zambesiensis

Cyperus glaucophyllus var. zambesiensis, commonly known as the Zambesi flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
