Comanthera dealbata

Comanthera dealbata, commonly known as the white-flowered rock heath.

It is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States, particularly found in rocky and sandy soils. The plant features small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Comanthera dealbata is adapted to full sun and is often found in open woodland or coastal dunes.
