Campylocentrum fasciola

Campylocentrum fasciola, commonly known as the purple-flowered piper, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface and a strong, aromatic scent.

Campylocentrum fasciola is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
