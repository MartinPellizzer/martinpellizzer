Cheirodendron trigynum var. mauiense

Cheirodendron trigynum var. mauiense, commonly known as the Maui flame tree, is a tropical tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
