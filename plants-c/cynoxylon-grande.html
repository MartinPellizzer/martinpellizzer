Cynoxylon grande

Cynoxylon grande, commonly known as the large heartwood tree, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has a tall, straight trunk with a broad, spreading crown, and its leaves are typically simple and alternate. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive fragrance that attracts pollinators.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
