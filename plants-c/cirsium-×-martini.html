Cirsium × martini

Cirsium × martini, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. This plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spherical head, and the plant can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Cirsium × martini is known for its ornamental value but can also be considered invasive in certain areas.
