Chlorocyperus paranaguensis

Chlorocyperus paranaguensis, commonly known as the Paraguay sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
