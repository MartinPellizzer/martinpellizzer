Comesperma ericinum var. latifolium

Comesperma ericinum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to withstand dry conditions.
