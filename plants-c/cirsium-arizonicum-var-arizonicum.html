Cirsium arizonicum var. arizonicum

Cirsium arizonicum var. arizonicum, commonly known as the Arizona thistle, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and tall, purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as desert scrub and grasslands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
