Calophyllum bracteatum

Calophyllum bracteatum, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, purple rind and a sweet, white, segmented pulp. Its ornamental value is notable, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The plant also has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
