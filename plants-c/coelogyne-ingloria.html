Coelogyne ingloria

Coelogyne ingloria, commonly known as the noble orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Coelogyne ingloria is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
