Crinum viviparum var. ensifolium

Crinum viviparum var. ensifolium, commonly known as the swordleaf crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its strap-like, sword-shaped leaves that are typically broad and lanceolate. The plant produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that are often white or pale pink, arranged in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
