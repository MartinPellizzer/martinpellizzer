Capsicum angustifolium

Capsicum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chili or the Carolina pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in the coastal plains of North and South Carolina. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically red or orange when mature, though they can also be yellow or green. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and for the spicy flavor of its fruits, which are used in various culinary applications.
