Capsicum grossum var. pomiforme

Capsicum grossum var. pomiforme, commonly known as the horned pepper or horn pepper, is a unique variety of chili pepper characterized by its distinctive, elongated, horn-like shape.

This plant belongs to the Solanaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. It produces small, yellow to orange fruits that mature to a deep red color, often with a mild to medium heat level. The plant typically grows as an annual, reaching heights of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lobed leaves.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental purposes and in specialty culinary applications.
