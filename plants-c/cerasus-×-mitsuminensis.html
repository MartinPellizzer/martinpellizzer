Cerasus × mitsuminensis

Cerasus × mitsuminensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yaezakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is known for its early blooming pink flowers that appear before the leaves in spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, edible fruits that are typically not cultivated for consumption.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy floral display and is a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
