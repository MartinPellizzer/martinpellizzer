Crypsis stricta

Crypsis stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf cryptogram, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical classification places it within the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Crypsis stricta is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
