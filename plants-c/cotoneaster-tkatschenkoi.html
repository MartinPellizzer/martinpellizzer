Cotoneaster tkatschenkoi

Cotoneaster tkatschenkoi, commonly known as the Katschenko cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features dense, compact growth with small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are a food source for birds.

Cotoneaster tkatschenkoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
