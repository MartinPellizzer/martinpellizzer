Cyperus leptolepis

Cyperus leptolepis, commonly known as narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus leptolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in water gardens or as a decorative plant in humid climates.
