Cyperus tolucensis

Cyperus tolucensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering spikes are usually small and clustered, producing tiny, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus tolucensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
