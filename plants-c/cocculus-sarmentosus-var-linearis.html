Cocculus sarmentosus var. linearis

Cocculus sarmentosus var. linearis, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, bumpy, yellowish fruit that matures in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
