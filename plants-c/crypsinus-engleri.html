Crypsinus engleri

Crypsinus engleri, commonly known as the Madagascar starfish plant, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It features a rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that are often blue-green in color, with a distinctive star-shaped pattern on the surface. The plant produces striking, violet-pink flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a dramatic visual contrast to its foliage. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves to survive dry periods.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species.
