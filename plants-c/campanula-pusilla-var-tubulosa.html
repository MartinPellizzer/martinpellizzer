Campanula pusilla var. tubulosa

Campanula pusilla var. tubulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in moderate climates.
