Cerasus californica

Cerasus californica, commonly known as the California wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that ripen to a dark red or black color in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Cerasus californica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
