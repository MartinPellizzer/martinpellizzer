Cardamine sibirica

Cardamine sibirica, commonly known as Siberian watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a peppery flavor.

The small white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.
