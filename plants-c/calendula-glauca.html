Calendula glauca

Calendula glauca, commonly known as the blueflower or glaucous marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Calendula glauca is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
