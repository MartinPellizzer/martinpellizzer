Calamus mattanensis var. sabut

Calamus mattanensis var. sabut, commonly known as the Sabut calamus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. Its rhizomes are valued for their aromatic properties and are sometimes used in local medicine.

This variety is typically found in wetland areas and along riverbanks in Southeast Asia.
