Cabomba

Cabomba, commonly known as waterweed, is a genus of aquatic plants belonging to the family Cabombaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, floating foliage and intricate, lace-like leaves that often resemble the structure of a fern. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Cabomba species are often used in aquatic gardens for their ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.

They are also popular in aquariums due to their aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
