Crocus constantinopolitanus

Crocus constantinopolitanus, commonly known as the Constantinople crocus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its flowers are tubular and have six petals, which are fused at the base.

The bulb contains starch and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
