Calea fruticosa

Calea fruticosa, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its unique nocturnal blooming habit, with flowers opening at night and closing by morning. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive shape, while the flowers are typically white or pale blue.

Calea fruticosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
