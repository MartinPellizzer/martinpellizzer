Calliandra blakeana

Calliandra blakeana, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
