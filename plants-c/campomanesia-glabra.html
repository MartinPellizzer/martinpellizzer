Campomanesia glabra

Campomanesia glabra, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in tropical ecosystems.
