Citrosma limoniodora

Citrosma limoniodora, commonly known as the lemon-scented citrosma, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, citrus-like fragrance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by oval, leathery fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive, fragrant aroma when crushed.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the essential oils extracted from its leaves and fruits.
