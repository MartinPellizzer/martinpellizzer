Cyphomandra betacea var. velutina

Cyphomandra betacea var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet leaf tomato, is a variant of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and small, orange-red fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green. The plant is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals and are followed by berries that contain several seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruits in certain regions.
