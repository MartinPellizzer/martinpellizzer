Croton pseudoniveus

Croton pseudoniveus, commonly known as the white croton, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that often display a combination of green, yellow, and white hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in tropical climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Croton pseudoniveus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.
