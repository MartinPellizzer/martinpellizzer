Charlwoodia ensata

Charlwoodia ensata, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers with five petals. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and stems that are often covered in fine hairs.

Charlwoodia ensata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
