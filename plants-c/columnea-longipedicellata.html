Columnea longipedicellata

Columnea longipedicellata, commonly known as the long pedicellate flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds, which are attracted to its nectar-rich structure.

Columnea longipedicellata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
