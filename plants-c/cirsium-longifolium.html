Cirsium longifolium

Cirsium longifolium, commonly known as the longleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Cirsium longifolium is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
