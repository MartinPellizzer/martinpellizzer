Calceolaria herbertiana var. parviflora

Calceolaria herbertiana var. parviflora, commonly known as the Herbert's slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color. The plant grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky soils, and thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and slightly hairy, adapted to the harsh mountain climate.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
