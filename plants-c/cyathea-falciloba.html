Cyathea falciloba

Cyathea falciloba, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically broad and arching, with a dark green coloration on both sides.

Cyathea falciloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in horticulture and landscaping.
