Centaurea claryi

Centaurea claryi, commonly known as Clary or Sweet William, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red and white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched.

Centaurea claryi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in herbal medicine.
