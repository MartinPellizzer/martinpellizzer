Corispermum bardunovii

Corispermum bardunovii, commonly known as the Bardunov's bladderwrack, is a rare halophytic plant belonging to the family Corispermaceae.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, greenish-blue leaves that store water, a feature adapted to its saline environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is typically found in coastal areas with high salinity. It is native to the eastern Mediterranean and has limited distribution due to its specific ecological requirements.

Its conservation status is threatened by habitat loss and environmental changes.
