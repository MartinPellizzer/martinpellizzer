Capsella bursa-pastoris sinuatolinearis

Capsella bursa-pastoris sinuatolinearis, commonly known as the heart-shaped weed or shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular or heart-shaped seed pods, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It has small white flowers and hairy stems, and it is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and have a slightly succulent texture.
