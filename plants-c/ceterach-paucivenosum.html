Ceterach paucivenosum

Ceterach paucivenosum, commonly known as the slender sea grass, is a marine flowering plant found in the intertidal zones of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or partially exposed depending on tidal conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences near the base of the stems. Its rhizomes form dense mats that help stabilize coastal sediments.

Ceterach paucivenosum plays an important ecological role in providing habitat for various marine organisms.
