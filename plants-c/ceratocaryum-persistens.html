Ceratocaryum persistens

Ceratocaryum persistens, commonly known as the African silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with bracts resembling tiny leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its striking visual appeal.
