Cynosurus domingensis

Cynosurus domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in three rows, a characteristic feature of many sedges.

Cynosurus domingensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
