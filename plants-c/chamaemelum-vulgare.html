Chamaemelum vulgare

Chamaemelum vulgare, commonly known as yarrow, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its feathery leaves and flat, clustered flower heads that range in color from white to pink. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant has been used for centuries in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its aromatic compounds contribute to its use in herbal remedies and as a natural insect repellent.
