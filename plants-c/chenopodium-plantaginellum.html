Chenopodium plantaginellum

Chenopodium plantaginellum, commonly known as wild spinach, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields. It is known for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and have a slightly bitter taste.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
