Conyza dampieri

Conyza dampieri, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, white seeds dispersed by the wind. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water, featuring deep roots and hairy leaves to reduce water loss.

Conyza dampieri is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
