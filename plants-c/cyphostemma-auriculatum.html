Cyphostemma auriculatum

Cyphostemma auriculatum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Cyphostemma auriculatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
