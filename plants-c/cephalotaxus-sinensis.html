Cephalotaxus sinensis

Cephalotaxus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that form flat, horizontal shoots. The plant produces small, reddish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its medicinal properties.

Cephalotaxus sinensis is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in well-drained soils.
