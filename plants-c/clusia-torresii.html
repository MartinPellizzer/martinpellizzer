Clusia torresii

Clusia torresii, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. Clusia torresii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also known for its resilience to drought and air pollution, making it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
