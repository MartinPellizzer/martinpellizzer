Cestrum neomiersianum

Cestrum neomiersianum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is known for its fragrant, tubular flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with thick, woody stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a strong, sweet scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking nighttime blooms.
