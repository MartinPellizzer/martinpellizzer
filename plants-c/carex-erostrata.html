Carex erostrata

Carex erostrata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are pollinated by wind.

Carex erostrata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
