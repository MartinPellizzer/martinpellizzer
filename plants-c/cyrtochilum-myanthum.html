Cyrtochilum myanthum

Cyrtochilum myanthum, commonly known as the yellow-tipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-green flowers with prominent, showy lips that are often adorned with contrasting markings. Cyrtochilum myanthum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is typically found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its unique floral structure and coloration make it a distinctive and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
