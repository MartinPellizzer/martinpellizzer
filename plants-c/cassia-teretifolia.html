Cassia teretifolia

Cassia teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Cassia teretifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
