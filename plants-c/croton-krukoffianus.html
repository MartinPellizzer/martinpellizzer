Croton krukoffianus

Croton krukoffianus, commonly known as the Krukoff's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from bright yellow to deep red, depending on the variety. The plant features opposite, simple leaves with serrated margins and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Croton krukoffianus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant and long-lasting leaf colors.
