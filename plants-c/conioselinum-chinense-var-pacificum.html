Conioselinum chinense var. pacificum

Conioselinum chinense var. pacificum, commonly known as Pacific Chinese caraway, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific coastal regions of North America.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky areas.

Its seeds are used in traditional herbal medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
