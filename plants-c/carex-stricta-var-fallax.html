Carex stricta var. fallax

Carex stricta var. fallax, commonly known as fallax sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for various wildlife.
