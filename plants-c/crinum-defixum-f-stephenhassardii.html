Crinum defixum f. stephenhassardii

Crinum defixum f. stephenhassardii, commonly known as the Stephen Hassard's Crinum, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are often broad and lance-shaped. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
