Callistemon wimmerensis

Callistemon wimmerensis, commonly known as the Wimmera bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or crimson flower spikes, which are arranged in dense, brush-like clusters. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

Callistemon wimmerensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and gardens.
