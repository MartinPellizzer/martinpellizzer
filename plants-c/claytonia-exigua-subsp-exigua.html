Claytonia exigua subsp. exigua

Claytonia exigua subsp.

exigua, commonly known as the "spring beauty," is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States. It features delicate, clustered leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, often growing in woodland environments. Its foliage is typically bluish-green, and the flowers range from white to pinkish in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in early-season pollination.
