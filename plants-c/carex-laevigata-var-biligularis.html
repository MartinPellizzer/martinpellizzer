Carex laevigata var. biligularis

Carex laevigata var. biligularis, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually smooth and dark green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in hue.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to wetland conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic ecosystems.
