Croton nitidulus var. angustiglans

Croton nitidulus var. angustiglans, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct veining pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
