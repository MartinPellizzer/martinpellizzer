Clinopodium olla

Clinopodium olla, commonly known as mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the small flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems.

Clinopodium olla is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
