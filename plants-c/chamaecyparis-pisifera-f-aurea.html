Chamaecyparis pisifera f. aurea

Chamaecyparis pisifera f. aurea, commonly known as the golden cypress, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer with a dense, pyramidal shape.

Its foliage is characterized by bright golden-yellow needles that remain throughout the year, providing a striking visual contrast to its dark bark. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.

This variety is prized for its unique color and ability to add year-round interest to the garden.
