Capparis stylosa var. velutina

Capparis stylosa var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red or orange berries that are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or sandy soils.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and showy blooms.
