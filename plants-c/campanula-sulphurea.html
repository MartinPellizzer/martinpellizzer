Campanula sulphurea

Campanula sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which are typically bell-shaped and clustered in racemes. The plant has hairy, often glandular leaves that are typically opposite or whorled in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula sulphurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
