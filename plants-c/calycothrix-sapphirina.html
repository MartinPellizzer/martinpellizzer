Calycothrix sapphirina

Calycothrix sapphirina, commonly known as the sapphire leaf orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, sapphire-blue leaves and small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and specialized roots that help it absorb nutrients from the forest floor.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation efforts.
