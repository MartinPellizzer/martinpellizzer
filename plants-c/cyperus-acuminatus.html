Cyperus acuminatus

Cyperus acuminatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the stem. Cyperus acuminatus is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
