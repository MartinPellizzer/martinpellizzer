Clerodendrum trichotomum var. ferrugineum

Clerodendrum trichotomum var. ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty-veined cleerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves with a reddish or rusty-colored venation. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
