Ceropegia areniphila

Ceropegia areniphila, commonly known as the sand ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to arid environments. The plant produces distinctive tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
