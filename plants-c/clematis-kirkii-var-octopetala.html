Clematis kirkii var. octopetala

Clematis kirkii var. octopetala, commonly known as the octopetala clematis, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with eight petals arranged in a distinctive octopus-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
