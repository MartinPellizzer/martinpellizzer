Carex vulpina f. capitulata

Carex vulpina f. capitulata, commonly known as the fox sedge.

It is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America, characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers in the summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is notable for its compact form and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
