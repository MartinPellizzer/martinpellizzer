Claoxylon sanctae-crucis

Claoxylon sanctae-crucis, commonly known as the sacred cross tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Claoxylon sanctae-crucis is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
