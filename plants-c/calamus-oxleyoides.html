Calamus oxleyoides

Calamus oxleyoides, commonly known as the Oxley's canes.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's unique morphology makes it a notable species within the genus Calamus.
