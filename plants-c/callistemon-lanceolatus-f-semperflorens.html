Callistemon lanceolatus f. semperflorens

Callistemon lanceolatus f. semperflorens, commonly known as the common bottlebrush, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, bright red or orange flower spikes that appear throughout the growing season. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs. This variety is known for its continuous flowering habit, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.
