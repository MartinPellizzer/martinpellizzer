Cypripedium × patens

Cypripedium × patens, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces a single flower per stem during the spring.

Cypripedium × patens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
