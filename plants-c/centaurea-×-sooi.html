Centaurea × sooi

Centaurea × sooi, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and tubular disc florets, typical of the genus Centaurea.

Centaurea × sooi is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
