Cyathea imbricata

Cyathea imbricata, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its common name. Cyathea imbricata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various small organisms.
