Cylindropuntia hamiltonii

Cylindropuntia hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in sharp spines and are typically green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits known as "tunas.". Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The plant plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
