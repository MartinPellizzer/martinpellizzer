Colchicum szovitsii var. freynii

Colchicum szovitsii var. freynii, commonly known as the Freyn's autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
