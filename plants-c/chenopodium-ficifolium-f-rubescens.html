Chenopodium ficifolium f. rubescens

Chenopodium ficifolium f. rubescens, commonly known as the red-leaf goosefoot, is a variant of the common goosefoot plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish pigmentation in its leaves, which is more pronounced in cooler temperatures. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a textured surface and a slightly succulent texture.

This cultivar is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
