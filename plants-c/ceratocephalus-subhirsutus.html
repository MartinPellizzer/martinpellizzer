Ceratocephalus subhirsutus

Ceratocephalus subhirsutus, commonly known as the hairy burr grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal. Its leaves are hairy and narrow, often with a rough texture.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
