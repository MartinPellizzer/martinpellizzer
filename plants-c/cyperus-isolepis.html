Cyperus isolepis

Cyperus isolepis, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, a common feature among sedges.

Cyperus isolepis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
