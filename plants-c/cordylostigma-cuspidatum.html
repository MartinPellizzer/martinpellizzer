Cordylostigma cuspidatum

Cordylostigma cuspidatum, commonly known as the spiky bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
