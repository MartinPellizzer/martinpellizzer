Calamus kerrianus

Calamus kerrianus, commonly known as the kerrian's arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their starchy roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Calamus kerrianus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.
