Crepis nemausensis

Crepis nemausensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. The plant has a compact growth habit and is known for its ability to form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
