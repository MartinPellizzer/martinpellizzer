Calligonum connivens

Calligonum connivens, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, fleshy, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Calligonum connivens is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
