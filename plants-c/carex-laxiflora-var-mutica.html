Carex laxiflora var. mutica

Carex laxiflora var. mutica, commonly known as the soft-flowered sedges, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

The variety mutica is distinguished by its softer, more spreading leaves compared to the typical form of C. laxiflora.
