Carex praticola var. subcoriacea

Carex praticola var. subcoriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its rough, scaly leaves that give it a distinctive texture. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown, and it plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
