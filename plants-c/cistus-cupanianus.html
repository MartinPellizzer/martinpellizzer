Cistus cupanianus

Cistus cupanianus, commonly known as the Portuguese rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
