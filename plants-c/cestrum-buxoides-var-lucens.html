Cestrum buxoides var. lucens

Cestrum buxoides var. lucens, commonly known as the shining boxwood nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature, and they contain toxic seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
