Cleome macrantha

Cleome macrantha, commonly known as the swamp cleome, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Cleome macrantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.
