Carex tehuacana

Carex tehuacana, commonly known as the Mexican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex tehuacana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
