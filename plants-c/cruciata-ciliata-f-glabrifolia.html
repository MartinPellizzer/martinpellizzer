Cruciata ciliata f. glabrifolia

Cruciata ciliata f. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf cruciata, is a variant of the cruciata ciliata species native to certain regions of North America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and lacking in pubescence. The flowers are small and clustered in racemes, featuring four petals that are often pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host for certain insect species.
