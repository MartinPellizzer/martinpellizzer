Coelopleurum rupestre

Coelopleurum rupestre, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to rocky and arid environments in Europe and western Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding its survival in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found on rocky slopes or gravelly ground.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
