Cruciata laevipes subsp. chersonensis

Cruciata laevipes subsp.

chersonensis, commonly known as the Chersonese cruciata, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and geographic distribution compared to the main species.
