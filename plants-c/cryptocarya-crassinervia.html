Cryptocarya crassinervia

Cryptocarya crassinervia, commonly known as the broad-veined cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves with prominent, broad veins. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue berries.

Cryptocarya crassinervia is often found in lowland and hill forests, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
