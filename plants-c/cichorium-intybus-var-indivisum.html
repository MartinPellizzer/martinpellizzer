Cichorium intybus var. indivisum

Cichorium intybus var. indivisum, commonly known as the Italian chicory, is a biennial or perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces a large, hollow stem that can grow up to several feet tall, topped with a cluster of blue or purple flowers. Its leaves are often used in salads, while the root is used to make coffee substitutes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
