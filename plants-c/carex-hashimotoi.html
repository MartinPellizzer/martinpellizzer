Carex hashimotoi

Carex hashimotoi, commonly known as Hashimoto's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
