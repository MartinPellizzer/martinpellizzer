Carex × schmidtiana f. superpseudocyperus

Carex × schmidtiana f. superpseudocyperus, commonly known as the giant marsh sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and produces dense clusters of spikelets that are often mistaken for grasses. This plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in temperate regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
