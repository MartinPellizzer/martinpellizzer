Cyperus brunneus

Cyperus brunneus, commonly known as the brown flatsedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, often found in wetlands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that grow close to the ground, with small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ability to thrive in moist environments.

Cyperus brunneus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
