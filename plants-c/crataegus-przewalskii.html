Crataegus przewalskii

Crataegus przewalskii, commonly known as Przewalski's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
