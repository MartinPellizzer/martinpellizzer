Cascarilla rostrata

Cascarilla rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated sassafras, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces fruit that is typically a capsule containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique fragrance and historical use in traditional medicine, it holds cultural and ecological significance in its native habitats.
