Chaetostoma acuminatum var. ramosissimum

Chaetostoma acuminatum var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the hairy-leaf water plantain, is a tropical aquatic plant characterized by its finely serrated, hairy leaves that provide excellent surface area for oxygen exchange.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common sight in aquatic habitats across its native range.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is also cultivated in ornamental ponds and water gardens.
