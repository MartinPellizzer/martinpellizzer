Cynanchum campii

Cynanchum campii, commonly known as Camp's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
