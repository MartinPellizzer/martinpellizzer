Ceanothus spinosus

Ceanothus spinosus, commonly known as the spiny ceanothus, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, spiny stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Ceanothus spinosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
