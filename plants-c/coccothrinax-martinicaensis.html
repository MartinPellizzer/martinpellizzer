Coccothrinax martinicaensis

Coccothrinax martinicaensis, commonly known as the Martinique palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is a small, slender palm native to the island of Martinique in the Caribbean. The plant typically grows to a height of about 5 to 7 meters and features a tall, unbranched trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets that are dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by edible, fibrous fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.
