Cryptangium melanocarpum

Cryptangium melanocarpum, commonly known as the black-fruited cryptangium, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, dark purple to black fruits that remain on the plant for an extended period. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture typical of the Myrtaceae family.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
