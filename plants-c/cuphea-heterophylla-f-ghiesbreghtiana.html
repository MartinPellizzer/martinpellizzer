Cuphea heterophylla f. ghiesbreghtiana

Cuphea heterophylla f. ghiesbreghtiana, commonly known as the Ghiesbreghtiana cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
