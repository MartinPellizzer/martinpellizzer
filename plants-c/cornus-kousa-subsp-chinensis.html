Cornus kousa subsp. chinensis

Cornus kousa subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It features showy, white bracts that surround small yellowish flowers, which appear in early spring.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green on top and hairy underneath. It produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible for birds but not for humans.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal interest.
