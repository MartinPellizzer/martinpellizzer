Calliandra goldmanii

Calliandra goldmanii, commonly known as the golden calliandra, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has compound leaves composed of small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil. It is often used in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display and ability to attract pollinators.

Calliandra goldmanii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, which can benefit soil health in agricultural settings.
