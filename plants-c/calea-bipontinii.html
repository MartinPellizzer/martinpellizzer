Calea bipontinii

Calea bipontinii, commonly known as the sacred flame plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, red-orange inflorescence that resembles a flame. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Calea bipontinii thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
