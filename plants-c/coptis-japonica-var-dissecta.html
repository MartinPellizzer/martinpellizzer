Coptis japonica var. dissecta

Coptis japonica var. dissecta, commonly known as the Japanese goldthread, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are deeply dissected, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a sought-after plant in horticulture and traditional medicine.
