Codiaeum austinianum

Codiaeum austinianum, commonly known as the Austin croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that display vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Croton family (Euphorbiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in both indoor and outdoor settings.
