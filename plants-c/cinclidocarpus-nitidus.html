Cinclidocarpus nitidus

Cinclidocarpus nitidus, commonly known as the glossy boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form neat, formal shapes.

Cinclidocarpus nitidus prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
