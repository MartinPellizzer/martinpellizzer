Calamagrostis californica

Calamagrostis californica, commonly known as California reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 to 3 meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a blue-green hue that becomes more pronounced in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of panicles of spikelets, which are typically pale green to golden in color.

This plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
