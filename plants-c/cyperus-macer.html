Cyperus macer

Cyperus macer, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and disturbed soils. Cyperus macer is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful plant for erosion control and a potential weed in agricultural areas.
