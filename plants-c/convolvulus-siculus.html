Convolvulus siculus

Convolvulus siculus, commonly known as Sicilian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. The plant has creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread aggressively in suitable conditions. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, making it a notable plant in gardens and natural habitats.
