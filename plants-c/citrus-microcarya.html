Citrus microcarya

Citrus microcarya, commonly known as the Chinese dwarf citrus or mountain kumquat, is a small evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.

Citrus microcarya is also valued for its potential in horticulture and as a source of aromatic compounds.
