Cymbidium hookerianum

Cymbidium hookerianum, commonly known as the Hooker's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, long-lasting flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has linear, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Cymbidium hookerianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
