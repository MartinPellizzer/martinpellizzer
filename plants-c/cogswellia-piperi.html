Cogswellia piperi

Cogswellia piperi, commonly known as the desert rose or desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and large, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and may have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Cogswellia piperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
