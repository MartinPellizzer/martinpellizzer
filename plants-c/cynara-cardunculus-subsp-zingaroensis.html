Cynara cardunculus subsp. zingaroensis

Cynara cardunculus subsp.

zingaroensis, commonly known as the Zingaro cardoon, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and tall, spiny stalks that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces yellow flowers in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in cuisine, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
