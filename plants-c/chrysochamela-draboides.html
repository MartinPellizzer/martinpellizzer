Chrysochamela draboides

Chrysochamela draboides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vibrant and showy appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
