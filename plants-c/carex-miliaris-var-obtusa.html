Carex miliaris var. obtusa

Carex miliaris var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with broad, flat leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a loose, cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
