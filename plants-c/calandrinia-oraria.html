Calandrinia oraria

Calandrinia oraria, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is a small, annual herb with erect, branched stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.

Calandrinia oraria is adapted to tolerate salt spray and is an important part of the local flora in its native range.
