Chloris intermedia

Chloris intermedia, commonly known as common grass violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Chloris intermedia is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
