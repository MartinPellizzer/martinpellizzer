Convolvulus coccineus

Convolvulus coccineus, commonly known as the red morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, vibrant red flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Convolvulus coccineus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
