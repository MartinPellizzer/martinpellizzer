Centaurea amadanensis var. gymnoclada

Centaurea amadanensis var. gymnoclada, commonly known as the Amadán thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring upright stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, purple to pink flower heads composed of numerous ray florets surrounded by a dense, spiny involucre. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

This variety is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
