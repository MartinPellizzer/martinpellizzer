Clematis fujisanensis

Clematis fujisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese mountain clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing blue to purple flowers with a distinctive, slightly fragrant aroma. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
