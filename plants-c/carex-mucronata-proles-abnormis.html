Carex mucronata proles abnormis

Carex mucronata proles abnormis, commonly known as the abnormal spike sedge, is a variant of the Carex genus characterized by its unique floral structure.

This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces elongated spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of its stems. The abnormal form of this sedge is distinguished by its irregular or modified inflorescence, which sets it apart from typical Carex species.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
