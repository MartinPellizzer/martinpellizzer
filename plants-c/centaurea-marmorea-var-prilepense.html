Centaurea marmorea var. prilepense

Centaurea marmorea var. prilepense, commonly known as the Prilep stonecrop, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures around its flower heads. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
