Campylocentrum pygmaeum

Campylocentrum pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf star apple, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, star-shaped fruits resembling tiny apples. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it is not widely cultivated for commercial fruit production.
