Cytisus rectipilosus

Cytisus rectipilosus, commonly known as the erect bristly broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow, butterfly-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

Cytisus rectipilosus is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
