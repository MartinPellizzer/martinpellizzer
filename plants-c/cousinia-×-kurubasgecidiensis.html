Cousinia × kurubasgecidiensis

Cousinia × kurubasgecidiensis, commonly known as the hybrid alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Cousinia × kurubasgecidiensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh climates.
