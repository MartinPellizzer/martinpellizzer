Crataegus zagrica

Crataegus zagrica, commonly known as the Zagreb hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
