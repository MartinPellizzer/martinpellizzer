Cuscuta sandwichiana var. mimosae

Cuscuta sandwichiana var. mimosae, commonly known as the mimic dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is native to parts of the southeastern United States and is often found on woody plants such as oaks and hickories. Its rapid growth and ability to spread quickly make it a challenging weed in agricultural and natural settings.

The species is also notable for its ability to mimic the appearance of its host, aiding in its attachment and survival.
