Cytisus pseudoumbellatus

Cytisus pseudoumbellatus, commonly known as the false broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and gravelly soils. Its leaves are pinnate, composed of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus pseudoumbellatus is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in coastal and inland areas with poor soil.
