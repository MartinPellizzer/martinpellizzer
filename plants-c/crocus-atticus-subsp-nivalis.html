Crocus atticus subsp. nivalis

Crocus atticus subsp.

nivalis, commonly known as the alpine crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean. It typically blooms in early spring, producing delicate, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow.

The plant grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a small, rounded bulb.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, high-altitude conditions.
