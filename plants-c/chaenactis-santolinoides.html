Chaenactis santolinoides

Chaenactis santolinoides, commonly known as the Santa Barbara daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing a compact, mat-forming growth habit. Its yellow to white flowers bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to its natural environment.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in arid regions.
