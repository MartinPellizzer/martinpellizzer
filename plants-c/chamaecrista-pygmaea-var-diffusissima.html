Chamaecrista pygmaea var. diffusissima

Chamaecrista pygmaea var. diffusissima, commonly known as the dwarf cassia, is a low-growing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, pinnately compound leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

The species is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
