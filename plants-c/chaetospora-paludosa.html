Chaetospora paludosa

Chaetospora paludosa, commonly known as the marsh beardgrass, is a grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and marshes.

Chaetospora paludosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms in its ecosystem.
