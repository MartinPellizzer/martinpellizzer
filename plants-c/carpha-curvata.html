Carpha curvata

Carpha curvata, commonly known as the curved grass, is a species of flowering plant in the Poaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicles. Carpha curvata thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
