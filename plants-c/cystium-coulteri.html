Cystium coulteri

Cystium coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is typically found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Cystium coulteri is a low-growing perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
