Cassia torosa

Cassia torosa, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, dark brown seeds. Cassia torosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
