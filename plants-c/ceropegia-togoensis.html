Ceropegia togoensis

Ceropegia togoensis, commonly known as the Togo moonvine, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white. The plant has succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Ceropegia togoensis is known for its ability to climb or trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets or shelves.
