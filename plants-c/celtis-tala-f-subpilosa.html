Celtis tala f. subpilosa

Celtis tala f. subpilosa, commonly known as the silky hackberry, is a small to medium-sized tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are usually covered with fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that mature to a dark purple color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
