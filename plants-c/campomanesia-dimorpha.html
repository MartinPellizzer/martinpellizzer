Campomanesia dimorpha

Campomanesia dimorpha, commonly known as the Natal plum or Brazilian plum, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, yellow or orange berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils.
