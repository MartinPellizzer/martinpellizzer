Caleya

Caleya, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its large, white, fragrant blooms that open at night. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers have a unique ability to close during the day, adapting to low-light conditions.

Caleya is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
