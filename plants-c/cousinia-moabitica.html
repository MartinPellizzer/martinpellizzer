Cousinia moabitica

Cousinia moabitica, commonly known as the Moabitic hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry riverbanks. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Cousinia moabitica is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
