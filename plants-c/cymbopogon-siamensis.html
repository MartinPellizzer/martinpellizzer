Cymbopogon siamensis

Cymbopogon siamensis, commonly known as Siamese lemongrass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stalks and aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other lemongrass species. Its essential oils, rich in citral, are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Cymbopogon siamensis thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its fragrance and utility.
