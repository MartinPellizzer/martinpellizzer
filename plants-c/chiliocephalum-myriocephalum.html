Chiliocephalum myriocephalum

Chiliocephalum myriocephalum, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish to orange flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
