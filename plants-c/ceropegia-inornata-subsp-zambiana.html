Ceropegia inornata subsp. zambiana

Ceropegia inornata subsp.

zambiana, commonly known as the Zambian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, tuberous stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers that are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in challenging conditions.
