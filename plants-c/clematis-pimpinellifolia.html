Clematis pimpinellifolia

Clematis pimpinellifolia, commonly known as the lesser clematis, is a flowering vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three heart-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale blue.

Clematis pimpinellifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
