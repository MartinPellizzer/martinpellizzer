Chamerion × prantlii

Chamerion × prantlii, commonly known as the hybrid fireweed, is a perennial plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is a hybrid between Chamerion angustifolium (narrow-leaved fireweed) and Chamerion purpureum (purple fireweed), resulting in a plant with distinctive morphological traits. The species is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and wetlands, and is adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ecological restoration projects.
