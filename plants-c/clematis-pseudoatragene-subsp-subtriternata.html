Clematis pseudoatragene subsp. subtriternata

Clematis pseudoatragene subsp.

subtriternata, commonly known as the slender clematis, is a flowering vine native to parts of East Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late summer.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb using its twining stems.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Ranunculaceae family.
