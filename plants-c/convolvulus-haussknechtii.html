Convolvulus haussknechtii

Convolvulus haussknechtii, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is a trailing or climbing herb characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale purple or white, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Convolvulus haussknechtii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
