Croton irregularis

Croton irregularis, commonly known as the irregular croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often variegated leaves that display a mix of green, yellow, and red hues. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Croton irregularis typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits.
