Capparis srilankensis

Capparis srilankensis, commonly known as the Sri Lankan caper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its distinctive, tangy and spicy fruit, which is used in various culinary applications. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while the stems are often thorny.

Capparis srilankensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
