Cyathea moorei

Cyathea moorei, commonly known as the Moore's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem covered with persistent, spirally arranged leaf bases. The fronds are pinnately compound, with a dark green, leathery texture and a distinctive, elongated shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various organisms.
