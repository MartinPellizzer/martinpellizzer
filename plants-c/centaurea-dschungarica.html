Centaurea dschungarica

Centaurea dschungarica, commonly known as the Central Asian knapweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and linear. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, forming dense, spherical heads that bloom in summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
