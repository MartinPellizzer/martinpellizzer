Cryptochilus carinatus

Cryptochilus carinatus, commonly known as the ridge-leaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves with a distinct ridge. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow.

Cryptochilus carinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
