Cassia burkartiana

Cassia burkartiana, commonly known as the Burkart's cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate and arranged in a feathery pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed or secondary forest areas.

Cassia burkartiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
