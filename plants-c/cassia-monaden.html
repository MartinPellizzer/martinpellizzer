Cassia monaden

Cassia monaden, commonly known as golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, giving the plant a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cassia monaden is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
