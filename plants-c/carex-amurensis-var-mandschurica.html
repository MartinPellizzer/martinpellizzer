Carex amurensis var. mandschurica

Carex amurensis var. mandschurica, commonly known as the Amur sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
