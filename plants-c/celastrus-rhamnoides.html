Celastrus rhamnoides

Celastrus rhamnoides, commonly known as the Indian sativa or the Indian bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
