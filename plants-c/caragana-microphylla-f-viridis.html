Caragana microphylla f. viridis

Caragana microphylla f. viridis, commonly known as the greenleaf shrub, is a cold-hardy, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, multi-branched stems that often grow in dense thickets, and its leaves are narrow, elongated, and typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system and hardy nature.
