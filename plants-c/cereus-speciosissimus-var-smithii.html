Cereus speciosissimus var. smithii

Cereus speciosissimus var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's night bloomer, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

It requires minimal water and full sun exposure to thrive.
