Carex claviformis

Carex claviformis, commonly known as the club-shaped sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
