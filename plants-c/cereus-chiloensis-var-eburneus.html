Cereus chiloensis var. eburneus

Cereus chiloensis var. eburneus, commonly known as the white-flowered night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are typically green and covered with white, woolly hairs. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
