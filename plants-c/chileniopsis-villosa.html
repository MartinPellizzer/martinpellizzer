Chileniopsis villosa

Chileniopsis villosa, commonly known as the Chilean cushion plant, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the Andes of Chile.

It features dense clusters of small, fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and is adapted to withstand strong winds and intense sunlight.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in rock gardens and alpine settings.
