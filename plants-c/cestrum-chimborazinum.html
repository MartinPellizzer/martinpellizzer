Cestrum chimborazinum

Cestrum chimborazinum, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. Its flowers are followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Cestrum chimborazinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
