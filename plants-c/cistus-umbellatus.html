Cistus umbellatus

Cistus umbellatus, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers in rounded clusters called umbels, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

Cistus umbellatus is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
