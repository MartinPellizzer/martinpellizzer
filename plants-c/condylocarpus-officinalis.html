Condylocarpus officinalis

Condylocarpus officinalis, commonly known as the medicinal fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are the primary part used for medicinal purposes, containing compounds with anti-inflammatory properties.

The botanical structure of Condylocarpus officinalis supports its role in herbal remedies and ecological systems.
