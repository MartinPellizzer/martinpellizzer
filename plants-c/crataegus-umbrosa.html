Crataegus umbrosa

Crataegus umbrosa, commonly known as the shade crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red or yellow berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance.

Crataegus umbrosa is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
