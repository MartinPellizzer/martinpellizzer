Ceratocephalus acmella var. uliginosa

Ceratocephalus acmella var. uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp sunflower, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, swampy areas and is adapted to waterlogged soils. It has a short, upright stem and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom during the warmer months.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
