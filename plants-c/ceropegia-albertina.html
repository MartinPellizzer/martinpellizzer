Ceropegia albertina

Ceropegia albertina, commonly known as the Albertina's African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and attractive, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
