Cattleya eldorado

Cattleya eldorado, commonly known as the Eldorado orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers with vibrant orange and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, making it one of the largest orchid blooms in the genus.

Cattleya eldorado is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
