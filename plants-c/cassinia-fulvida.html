Cassinia fulvida

Cassinia fulvida, commonly known as the golden cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while its flowers are arranged in compact clusters.

Cassinia fulvida is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
