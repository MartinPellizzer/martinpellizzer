Calamus calciphilus

Calamus calciphilus, commonly known as the limestone calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves. This plant is adapted to grow in calcareous soils, which are rich in calcium carbonate. Calamus calciphilus is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions with a warm climate.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in suitable environments.
