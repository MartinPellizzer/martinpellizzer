Casparia castrata

Casparia castrata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.

Casparia castrata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
