Crocus artvinensis

Crocus artvinensis, commonly known as the Artvin crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of northeastern Turkey, particularly in the Artvin province. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinct purple or brownish hue at the base. It typically blooms in late winter or early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in its habitat.

The plant grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
