Clitoria glabella

Clitoria glabella, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, butterfly-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a climbing vine with pinnate leaves composed of several small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.

The seeds of Clitoria glabella are encased in a hairy, pod-like structure that splits open when mature.
