Claoxylon angustifolium

Claoxylon angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved claoxylon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody fruits containing smooth, oval seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
