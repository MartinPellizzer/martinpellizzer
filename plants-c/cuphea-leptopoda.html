Cuphea leptopoda

Cuphea leptopoda, commonly known as the slender cuphea, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its foliage is often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cuphea leptopoda is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
