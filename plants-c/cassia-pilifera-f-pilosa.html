Cassia pilifera f. pilosa

Cassia pilifera f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with soft, hairy textures on both surfaces. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. Its fruit is a elongated pod that splits open to release winged seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
