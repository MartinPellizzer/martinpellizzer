Carex gracilenta

Carex gracilenta, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its grass-like leaves are narrow and grass-green, with small, spike-like flowering structures that produce tiny seeds.

Carex gracilenta plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
