Cerastium fischerianum var. beeringianum

Cerastium fischerianum var. beeringianum, commonly known as the Arctic mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy, oval leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early summer. It thrives in nutrient-poor, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in tundra ecosystems.
