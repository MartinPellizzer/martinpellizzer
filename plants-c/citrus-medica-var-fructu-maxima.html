Citrus medica var. fructu-maxima

Citrus medica var. fructu-maxima, commonly known as the pummelo, is a large citrus fruit native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its thick, rough rind and large, segmented fruit. The plant is a vigorous shrub or small tree, often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant aroma, while the flowers are white and fragrant.

The pummelo is known for its high water content and mild, sweet flavor compared to other citrus fruits.
