Calamagrostis heterogluma

Calamagrostis heterogluma, commonly known as the Japanese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silvery-blue foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers in the summer, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability, it is a popular choice for landscaping in regions with high humidity.
