Cassia ornithopodioides

Cassia ornithopodioides, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.

Cassia ornithopodioides is also notable for being the floral emblem of Australia.
