Columbia hastata

Columbia hastata, commonly known as the mountain bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Columbia hastata is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
