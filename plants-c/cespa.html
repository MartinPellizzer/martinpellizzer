Cespa

Cespa, commonly known as grass, is a diverse group of flowering plants that form the basis of many ecosystems.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and the ability to spread through rhizomes or stolons. Grasses are typically found in a variety of habitats, including meadows, lawns, and grasslands. They play a crucial role in soil stabilization and carbon sequestration.

Due to their adaptability, grasses are among the most widespread plant types on Earth.
