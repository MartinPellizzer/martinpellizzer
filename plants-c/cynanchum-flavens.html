Cynanchum flavens

Cynanchum flavens, commonly known as yellow milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its milky sap is a distinguishing feature, often containing toxic compounds.

Cynanchum flavens is typically found in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
