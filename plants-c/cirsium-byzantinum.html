Cirsium byzantinum

Cirsium byzantinum, commonly known as the Byzantine thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Cirsium byzantinum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
