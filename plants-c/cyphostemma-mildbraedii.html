Cyphostemma mildbraedii

Cyphostemma mildbraedii, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thin, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid and semi-arid regions.
