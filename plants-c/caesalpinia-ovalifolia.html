Caesalpinia ovalifolia

Caesalpinia ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf legume, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. The plant features compound leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets and produces bright yellow or orange floral clusters. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical ecosystems.
