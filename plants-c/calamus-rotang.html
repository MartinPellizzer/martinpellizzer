Calamus rotang

Calamus rotang, commonly known as rattan, is a climbing palm native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that are often used in the production of furniture and handicrafts. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its stems are covered in small, spiny projections. Calamus rotang thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

It plays an important role in both ecological systems and traditional industries.
