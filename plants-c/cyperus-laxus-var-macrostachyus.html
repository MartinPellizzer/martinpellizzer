Cyperus laxus var. macrostachyus

Cyperus laxus var. macrostachyus, commonly known as the broad-stalked flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish or reddish in color.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
