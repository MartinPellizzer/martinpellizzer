Carex chlorophila

Carex chlorophila, commonly known as the green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in a cylindrical shape, typical of the sedge family.
