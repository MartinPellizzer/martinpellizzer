Cynanchum anthopotamicum

Cynanchum anthopotamicum, commonly known as the American dogbane, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks.

Its fibrous roots and robust stems allow it to thrive in a variety of soil types.
