Conospermum caeruleum var. spathulatum

Conospermum caeruleum var. spathulatum, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Blueberry Bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking visual element to the landscape. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
