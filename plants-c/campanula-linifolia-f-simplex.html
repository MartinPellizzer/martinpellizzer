Campanula linifolia f. simplex

Campanula linifolia f. simplex, commonly known as the simple-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. Native to southern Europe, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance and ease of care.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
