Curcuma stenochila

Curcuma stenochila, commonly known as the narrow-leafed turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish rhizomes. The plant produces tall, narrow leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.

Curcuma stenochila is often cultivated for both ornamental and traditional medicinal purposes.
