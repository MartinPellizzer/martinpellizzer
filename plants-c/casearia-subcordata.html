Casearia subcordata

Casearia subcordata, commonly known as the red-flowered casearia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its vibrant foliage and unique fruit structure.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
