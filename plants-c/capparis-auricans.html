Capparis auricans

Capparis auricans, commonly known as the golden caper, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often fragrant and attract various pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils.

Capparis auricans is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
