Campanula androsacea

Campanula androsacea, commonly known as the Androsace bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense mat, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Campanula androsacea is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
