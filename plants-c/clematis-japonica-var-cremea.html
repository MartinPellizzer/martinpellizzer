Clematis japonica var. cremea

Clematis japonica var. cremea, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Japan.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a slight pinkish hue. The plant produces compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to climb trellises or walls.
