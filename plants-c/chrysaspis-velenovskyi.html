Chrysaspis velenovskyi

Chrysaspis velenovskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky's chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features showy yellow to orange inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly encountered in its natural wild state.
