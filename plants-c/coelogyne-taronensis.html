Coelogyne taronensis

Coelogyne taronensis, commonly known as the Taronese orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and red. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its roots are fibrous and help anchor it in the soil while absorbing moisture from the environment.

Coelogyne taronensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
