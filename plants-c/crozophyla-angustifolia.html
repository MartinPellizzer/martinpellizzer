Crozophyla angustifolia

Crozophyla angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
