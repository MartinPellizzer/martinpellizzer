Chamaecytisus proliferus var. calderae

Chamaecytisus proliferus var. calderae, commonly known as the Caldera broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in arid climates.
