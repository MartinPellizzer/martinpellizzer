Cassuvium roxburghii

Cassuvium roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its flowers are white or pale green, and it is often cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional properties.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement due to its high antioxidant content.
