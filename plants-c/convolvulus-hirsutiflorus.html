Convolvulus hirsutiflorus

Convolvulus hirsutiflorus, commonly known as the hairy-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often opening in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread in various habitats.
