Carduncellus hispanicus var. araneosus

Carduncellus hispanicus var. araneosus, commonly known as the spider-thorn cardoon, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an ornamental plant and a subject of interest in horticultural studies.
