Calothamnus laevigatus

Calothamnus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved emu bush, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom from late autumn to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Calothamnus laevigatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and vibrant floral display.
