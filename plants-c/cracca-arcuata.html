Cracca arcuata

Cracca arcuata, commonly known as the curled cracca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique, curling leaves and showy blooms make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
