Campanula glomerata var. cervicarioides

Campanula glomerata var. cervicarioides, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. This variety is often found in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
