Caryocar amygdaliferum

Caryocar amygdaliferum, commonly known as the Brazilian almond or piquizeiro, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It produces small, egg-shaped fruits that are covered in a thick, waxy coating. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody shell. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in tropical ecosystems.
