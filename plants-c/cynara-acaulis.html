Cynara acaulis

Cynara acaulis, commonly known as the artichoke thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, silver-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces purple to pink flower heads, which consist of numerous small florets arranged in a spherical shape. Its leaves and flower heads are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cynara acaulis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for Mediterranean climates.
