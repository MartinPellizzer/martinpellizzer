Chrysanthemum baranovii

Chrysanthemum baranovii, commonly known as the Baranov chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to the Far East, particularly found in regions of Russia and China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy flower heads. The plant typically produces daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and as a cut flower.
