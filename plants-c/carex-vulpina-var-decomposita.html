Carex vulpina var. decomposita

Carex vulpina var. decomposita, commonly known as the mossy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest floors. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a rough texture, while its rhizomes help it spread vegetatively.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
