Capparis pringlei

Capparis pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's caper, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and wind exposure.

Due to its unique ecological role and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
