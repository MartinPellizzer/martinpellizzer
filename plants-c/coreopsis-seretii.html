Coreopsis seretii

Coreopsis seretii, commonly known as the purple coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to pink ray flowers and yellow central disk, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

Coreopsis seretii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
