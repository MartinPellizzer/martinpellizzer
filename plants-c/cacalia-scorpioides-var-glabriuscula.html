Cacalia scorpioides var. glabriuscula

Cacalia scorpioides var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smoothleaf thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are smooth and lance-shaped, and it produces achenes as its fruit.

It is often found in the eastern and central United States.
