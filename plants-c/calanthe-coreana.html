Calanthe coreana

Calanthe coreana, commonly known as the Korean calanthe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white or pale pink petals with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in cool, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Calanthe coreana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
