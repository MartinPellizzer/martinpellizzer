Ceiba pubiflora

Ceiba pubiflora, commonly known as the woolly ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its towering height and a distinctive, buttressed trunk that helps it thrive in wet, humid environments. The tree produces large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in soft, fibrous hairs.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for numerous species.
