Critoniopsis suaveolens

Critoniopsis suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of rainforests. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, and it produces small, white or pale pink blossoms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
