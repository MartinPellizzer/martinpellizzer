Calamagrostis epigejos f. fissa

Calamagrostis epigejos f. fissa, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and its ability to form dense, upright clumps. The plant produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that add ornamental value, especially in late summer and autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other waterlogged areas.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
