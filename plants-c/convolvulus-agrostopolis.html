Convolvulus agrostopolis

Convolvulus agrostopolis, commonly known as the Armenian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, tubular flowers typically in shades of pink or purple. The plant has a trailing or climbing growth habit, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.
