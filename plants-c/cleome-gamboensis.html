Cleome gamboensis

Cleome gamboensis, commonly known as the spider flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while the flowers come in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

Cleome gamboensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
