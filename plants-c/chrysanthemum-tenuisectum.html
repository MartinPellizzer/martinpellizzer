Chrysanthemum tenuisectum

Chrysanthemum tenuisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and vibrant, daisy-like flowers that come in a range of colors. This species belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both a decorative and ecologically valuable plant.
