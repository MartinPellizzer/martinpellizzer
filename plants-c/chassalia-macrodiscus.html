Chassalia macrodiscus

Chassalia macrodiscus, commonly known as the large disc chassalia, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Chassalia macrodiscus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
