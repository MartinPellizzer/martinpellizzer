Cyperus diaphanus var. latespicatus

Cyperus diaphanus var. latespicatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like structure with numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the vegetation in aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
