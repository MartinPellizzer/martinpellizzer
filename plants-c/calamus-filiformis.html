Calamus filiformis

Calamus filiformis, commonly known as slender calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant features long, thin rhizomes that can grow up to several meters in length, often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, while its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe.

Calamus filiformis is also valued for its aromatic rhizomes, which are used in perfumery and as a flavoring agent.
