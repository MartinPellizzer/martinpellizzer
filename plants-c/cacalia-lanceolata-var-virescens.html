Cacalia lanceolata var. virescens

Cacalia lanceolata var. virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough texture and a hairy stem. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its botanical name reflects its lanceolate leaf shape and the greenish hue of its foliage.
