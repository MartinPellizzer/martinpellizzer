Carex rupestris subsp. alticola

Carex rupestris subsp.

alticola, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its foliage is typically dense and can form compact clumps, making it a hardy plant suited to harsh environmental conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.
