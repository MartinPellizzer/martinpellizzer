Cyperus iria var. flavescens

Cyperus iria var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is considered an invasive species in some areas, outcompeting native vegetation in wetland ecosystems.
