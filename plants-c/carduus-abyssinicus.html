Carduus abyssinicus

Carduus abyssinicus, commonly known as Ethiopian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaves. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves that are typically hairy and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often blooming in late summer to early autumn.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
