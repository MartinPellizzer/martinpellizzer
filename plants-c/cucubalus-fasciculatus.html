Cucubalus fasciculatus

Cucubalus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered cucumber or wild cucumber, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with slender, hairy stems that often form dense clusters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruit is a long, slender, green or yellowish gourd that matures in late summer.

The species is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
