Centaurea alba subsp. costae

Centaurea alba subsp.

costae, commonly known as the white centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in compact clusters.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are linear to lance-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

It is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
