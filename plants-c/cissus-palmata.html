Cissus palmata

Cissus palmata, commonly known as the heartleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically waxy and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
