Centaurea monticola subsp. orthoacantha

Centaurea monticola subsp.

orthoacantha, commonly known as the mountain centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright stems covered in fine, bristly hairs, and it produces striking blue to purple flower heads that are typically surrounded by pale yellow rays.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
