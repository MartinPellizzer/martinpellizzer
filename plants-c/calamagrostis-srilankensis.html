Calamagrostis srilankensis

Calamagrostis srilankensis, commonly known as the Srilankan reed, is a grass species native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies. It produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Calamagrostis srilankensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
