Chaetocalyx scandens

Chaetocalyx scandens, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its aesthetic appeal.
