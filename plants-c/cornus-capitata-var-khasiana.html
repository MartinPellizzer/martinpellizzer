Cornus capitata var. khasiana

Cornus capitata var. khasiana, commonly known as the Kashmiri dogwood, is a shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at higher elevations.
