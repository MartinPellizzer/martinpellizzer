Combretum mayumbense

Combretum mayumbense, commonly known as Mayumbense flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central and eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its striking red or orange floral bracts that appear during the dry season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually opposite and can reach up to 15 cm in length. It produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.
