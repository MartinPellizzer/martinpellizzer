Crocus reticulatus f. adspersus

Crocus reticulatus f. adspersus, commonly known as the scented crocus, is a botanical variant of the reticulated crocus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
