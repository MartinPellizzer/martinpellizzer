Cereus cochabambensis

Cereus cochabambensis, commonly known as the Cochabamba candlestick cactus, is a species native to Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, unbranched stem that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant produces white flowers that bloom at night, a characteristic feature of many cacti in the Cereus genus. Its spines are typically short and sparse, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
