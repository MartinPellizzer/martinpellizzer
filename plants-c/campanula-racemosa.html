Campanula racemosa

Campanula racemosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile garden plant.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
