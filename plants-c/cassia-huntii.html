Cassia huntii

Cassia huntii, commonly known as golden cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. Cassia huntii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a useful species in reforestation and erosion control efforts.
