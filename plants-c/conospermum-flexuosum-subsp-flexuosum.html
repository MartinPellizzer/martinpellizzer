Conospermum flexuosum subsp. flexuosum

Conospermum flexuosum subsp.

flexuosum, commonly known as the variable flame bush, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red or orange flowers that appear in spring.

The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
