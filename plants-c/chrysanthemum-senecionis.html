Chrysanthemum senecionis

Chrysanthemum senecionis, commonly known as the Seneca snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodlands. It is often found in moist soils and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used historically by indigenous peoples for various health purposes.
