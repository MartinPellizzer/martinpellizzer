Chloris imberbis

Chloris imberbis, commonly known as the hairless grass or slender grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairless leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often hidden among the foliage.

Chloris imberbis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native habitat.
