Cyperus inconspicuus

Cyperus inconspicuus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often subtended by bracts.

Cyperus inconspicuus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
