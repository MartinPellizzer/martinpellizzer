Camelina linicola

Camelina linicola, commonly known as prairie camelina, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, yellow flowers and has pinnately divided leaves.

Its seeds are small and oval, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
