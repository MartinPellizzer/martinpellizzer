Cordiera vinosa

Cordiera vinosa, commonly known as the red-flowered cordiera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cordiera vinosa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
