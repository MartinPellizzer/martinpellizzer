Cerasus bigarella

Cerasus bigarella, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or dwarf cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits that are often used in culinary applications. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red berries in late summer. Its ornamental value is significant due to its delicate blossoms and compact growth habit.

Cerasus bigarella is often cultivated in gardens for both its aesthetic appeal and edible yield.
