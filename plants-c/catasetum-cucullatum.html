Catasetum cucullatum

Catasetum cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a specialized labellum that resembles a hood, which plays a role in pollination by specific wasps. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and display a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red.

Catasetum cucullatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
