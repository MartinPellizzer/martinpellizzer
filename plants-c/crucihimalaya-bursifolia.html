Crucihimalaya bursifolia

Crucihimalaya bursifolia, commonly known as the Himalayan burflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with distinctive cruciform flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Brassicaceae family.
