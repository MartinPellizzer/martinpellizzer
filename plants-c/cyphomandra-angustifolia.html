Cyphomandra angustifolia

Cyphomandra angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. The plant features small, pale green leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits. The fruits are typically edible but contain toxic alkaloids, so caution is advised.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
