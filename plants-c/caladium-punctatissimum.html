Caladium punctatissimum

Caladium punctatissimum, commonly known as the spotted arrowhead, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored with contrasting patterns of white, red, and yellow. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage is the main ornamental feature, making it popular in gardens and as a houseplant.

It produces small, pale green flowers that are less showy compared to its vibrant leaves.
