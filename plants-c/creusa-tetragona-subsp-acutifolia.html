Creusa tetragona subsp. acutifolia

Creusa tetragona subsp.

acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf creusa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with acute tips, giving it its subspecific name. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale green and bloom in late spring.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in association with other wetland flora.
