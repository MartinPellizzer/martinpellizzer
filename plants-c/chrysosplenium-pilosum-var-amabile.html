Chrysosplenium pilosum var. amabile

Chrysosplenium pilosum var. amabile, commonly known as the silky spleenwort, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to parts of Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves with a soft, hairy texture that gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to woodland gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
