Cyanotis cerifolia

Cyanotis cerifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a bluish or purple tinge. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Cyanotis cerifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
