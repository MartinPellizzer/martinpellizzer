Carex × fussii

Carex × fussii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Carex brevicuspis and Carex echinata, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant forms dense clumps of grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. It produces cylindrical spikes of small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Carex × fussii is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
