Cucurbita pepo var. rotunda

Cucurbita pepo var. rotunda, commonly known as the scallop pumpkin, is a widely cultivated member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its round, slightly flattened shape and vibrant orange to yellow-orange skin. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, with the fruit typically reaching weights of up to 10 kilograms. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses, as well as for ornamental purposes in gardens.
