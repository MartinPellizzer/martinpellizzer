Cyperus sensilis

Cyperus sensilis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Cyperus sensilis has small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
