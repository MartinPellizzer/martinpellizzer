Carex keiskei

Carex keiskei, commonly known as the Keiske sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex keiskei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable moisture conditions.
