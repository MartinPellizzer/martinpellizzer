Carex paniculata var. elongata

Carex paniculata var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color. The plant produces spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in water management and wildlife habitat.
