Carex amblyorhyncha

Carex amblyorhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex amblyorhyncha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
