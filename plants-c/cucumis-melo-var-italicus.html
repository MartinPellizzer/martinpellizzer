Cucumis melo var. italicus

Cucumis melo var. italicus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a botanical variety belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine pubescence.

The fruit matures in late summer and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
