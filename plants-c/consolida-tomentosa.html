Consolida tomentosa

Consolida tomentosa, commonly known as the fuzzy poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with a narrow, elongated upper petal and two rounded lower petals.

Consolida tomentosa is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
