Clitoria flagellaris

Clitoria flagellaris, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a striking contrast between the petals and the violet throat. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is well adapted to warm, humid climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
