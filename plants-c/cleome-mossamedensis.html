Cleome mossamedensis

Cleome mossamedensis, commonly known as the African spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its showy, daisy-like flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Cleome mossamedensis is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
