Crataegus lancifolia

Crataegus lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red to orange berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Crataegus lancifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
