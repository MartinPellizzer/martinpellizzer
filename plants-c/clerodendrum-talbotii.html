Clerodendrum talbotii

Clerodendrum talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
