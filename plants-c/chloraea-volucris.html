Chloraea volucris

Chloraea volucris, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive silver-blue foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Chloraea volucris is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
