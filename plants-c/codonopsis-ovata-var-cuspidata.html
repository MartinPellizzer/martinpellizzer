Codonopsis ovata var. cuspidata

Codonopsis ovata var. cuspidata, commonly known as the pointed codonopsis, is a species of flowering plant in the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant has oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive pointed tip, giving it its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
