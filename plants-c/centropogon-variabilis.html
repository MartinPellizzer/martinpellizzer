Centropogon variabilis

Centropogon variabilis, commonly known as the variable centropogon, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that come in various colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in wet or marshy habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, while its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Centropogon variabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
