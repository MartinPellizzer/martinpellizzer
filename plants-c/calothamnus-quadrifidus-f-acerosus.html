Calothamnus quadrifidus f. acerosus

Calothamnus quadrifidus f. acerosus, commonly known as the hard-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped inflorescences. The leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the bright red or pink flowers. This variety is particularly noted for its robust, woody stems and its ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
