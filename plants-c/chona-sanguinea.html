Chona sanguinea

Chona sanguinea, commonly known as the bloodroot, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It features a distinctive red-orange root that is often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring season.

Chona sanguinea is well adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
