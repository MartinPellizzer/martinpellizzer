Calopogonium racemosum

Calopogonium racemosum, commonly known as the racemose cowpea, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being ovate and hairy. The plant produces racemose inflorescences, bearing small, yellowish-white flowers that are attractive to pollinators. Its seeds are typically small and can be used for forage or as a green manure in agricultural systems.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
