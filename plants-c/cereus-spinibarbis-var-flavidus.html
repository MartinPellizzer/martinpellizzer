Cereus spinibarbis var. flavidus

Cereus spinibarbis var. flavidus, commonly known as the yellow-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in sharp, spiny ridges. The plant produces large, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming at night. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to survive.

Due to its striking appearance and unique adaptations, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
