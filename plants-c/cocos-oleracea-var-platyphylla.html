Cocos oleracea var. platyphylla

Cocos oleracea var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf nickerbean, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are rich in protein and essential nutrients.

Due to its adaptability, it is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some regions.
