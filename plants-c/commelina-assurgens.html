Commelina assurgens

Commelina assurgens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features striking blue-violet bracts that surround its small, white flowers, which are clustered in umbels. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and water features.
