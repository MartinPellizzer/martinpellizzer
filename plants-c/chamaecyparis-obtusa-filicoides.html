Chamaecyparis obtusa filicoides

Chamaecyparis obtusa filicoides, commonly known as the Japanese Falsecypress or Filicoides cedar, is a coniferous evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It has a distinctive appearance with scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and silvery white underneath, giving it a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its foliage remains dense and lush throughout the year, contributing to its aesthetic value in landscaping.
