Chaetopappa alsinoides

Chaetopappa alsinoides, commonly known as the hairy pappus plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flower heads that mature into fluffy, white pappi for wind dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
