Castelnavia pendulosa

Castelnavia pendulosa, commonly known as the hanging castelnavia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, elongated stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces white to pale yellow blooms that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, textured surface.

Castelnavia pendulosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
