Caesalpinia bahamensis subsp. rugeliana

Caesalpinia bahamensis subsp.

rugeliana, commonly known as the Bahamian flame tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas. It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom during the dry season.

The plant has compound, bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its pods are elongated and woody. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
