Carex alterniflora var. arimaensis

Carex alterniflora var. arimaensis, commonly known as the Arima sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a clumping growth habit and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The leaves are often dark green and have a distinctive alternate arrangement along the stem.
