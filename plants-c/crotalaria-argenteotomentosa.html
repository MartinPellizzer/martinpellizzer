Crotalaria argenteotomentosa

Crotalaria argenteotomentosa, commonly known as silver-tomentose cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant is distinguished by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers with prominent purple markings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and potential as a forage crop, it is cultivated in some parts of the world.
