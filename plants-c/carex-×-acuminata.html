Carex × acuminata

Carex × acuminata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and triangular stems. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of spikelets, which are arranged in a cylindrical shape.

Carex × acuminata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
