Crataegus comparata

Crataegus comparata, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features thorny branches with opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves. The plant produces white to pink flowers in clusters during spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are usually 3 to 8 centimeters long and have a serrated margin.

Crataegus comparata is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along streams, thriving in a variety of soil types.
