Camellia kucha

Camellia kucha, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blossoms.

Camellia kucha is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
