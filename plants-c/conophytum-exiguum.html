Conophytum exiguum

Conophytum exiguum, commonly known as the dwarf conophytum, is a small, compact succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that are typically green with a bluish or purple tinge, adapted for storing water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
