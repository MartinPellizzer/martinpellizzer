Carduus uncinatus var. gymnocephalus

Carduus uncinatus var. gymnocephalus, commonly known as the nakedhead thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a distinctive, naked head of bracts at the top of the stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
