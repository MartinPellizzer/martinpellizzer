Centaurea janeri subsp. babiana

Centaurea janeri subsp.

babiana, commonly known as the Babian Redhead, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its striking red and purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
