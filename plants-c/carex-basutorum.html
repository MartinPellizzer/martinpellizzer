Carex basutorum

Carex basutorum, commonly known as the Basutoland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes and stream banks.

Carex basutorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
