Cupressus conoidea

Cupressus conoidea, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramid-shaped crown and scale-like leaves that are dark green and closely pressed together. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by cone-like structures that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental tree in landscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
