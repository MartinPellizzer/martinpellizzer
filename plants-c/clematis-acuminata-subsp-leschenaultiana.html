Clematis acuminata subsp. leschenaultiana

Clematis acuminata subsp.

leschenaultiana, commonly known as the Leschenault's Clematis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers.

The plant features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically blue or purple with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
