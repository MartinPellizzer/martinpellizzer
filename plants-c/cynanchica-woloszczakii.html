Cynanchica woloszczakii

Cynanchica woloszczakii, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often grayish stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Cynanchica woloszczakii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
