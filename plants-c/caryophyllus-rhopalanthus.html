Caryophyllus rhopalanthus

Caryophyllus rhopalanthus, commonly known as the crested carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters and features hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Caryophyllus rhopalanthus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and delicate appearance.
