Cirrhopetalum lepidum

Cirrhopetalum lepidum, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety-textured leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts around the world.
