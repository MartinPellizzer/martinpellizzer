Chondrilla tenuifolia

Chondrilla tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf goatweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

Chondrilla tenuifolia is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas.
