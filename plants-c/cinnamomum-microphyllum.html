Cinnamomum microphyllum

Cinnamomum microphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon or small-leaved camphor, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple berries containing winged seeds. Its aromatic bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal remedies and for their essential oils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine and perfumery.
