Cuphea gracilis var. media

Cuphea gracilis var. media, commonly known as the dwarf flame flower, is a compact shrub native to Central America.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
