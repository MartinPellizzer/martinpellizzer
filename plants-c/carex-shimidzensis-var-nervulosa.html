Carex shimidzensis var. nervulosa

Carex shimidzensis var. nervulosa, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized root systems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a distinct veining pattern.

The inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
