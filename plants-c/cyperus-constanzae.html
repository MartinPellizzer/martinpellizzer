Cyperus constanzae

Cyperus constanzae, commonly known as the Constanza sedge, is a flowering plant species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically brown or purple in color. Cyperus constanzae thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
