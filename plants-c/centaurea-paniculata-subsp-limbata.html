Centaurea paniculata subsp. limbata

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

limbata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other Centaurea species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
