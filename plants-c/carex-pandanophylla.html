Carex pandanophylla

Carex pandanophylla, commonly known as the pandanus-like sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

Carex pandanophylla is often used in traditional landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
