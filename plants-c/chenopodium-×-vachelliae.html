Chenopodium × vachelliae

Chenopodium × vachelliae, commonly known as wild spinach, is a hybrid species within the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Chenopodium × vachelliae is valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines.
