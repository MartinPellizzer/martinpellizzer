Caesalpinia delphinensis

Caesalpinia delphinensis, commonly known as the blue crown or blue velvet bean, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and distinctive, bright blue or purple flower clusters that resemble a crown. The plant produces long, slender pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and unique foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Caesalpinia delphinensis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring warm, humid climates.
