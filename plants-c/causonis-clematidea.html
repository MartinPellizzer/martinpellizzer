Causonis clematidea

Causonis clematidea, commonly known as the narrow-leafed clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with five petals and is often found in woodland or hedgerow habitats. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a finely toothed margin.

Causonis clematidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
