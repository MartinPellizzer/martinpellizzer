Capparis ovata var. sicula

Capparis ovata var. sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, greenish-yellow buds that are harvested before they open. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

The Sicilian caper is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
