Comandra

Comandra, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves and produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine. Comandra is adapted to thrive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in harsh environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
