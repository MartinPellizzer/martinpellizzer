Coriaria arborea

Coriaria arborea, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Coriaria arborea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
