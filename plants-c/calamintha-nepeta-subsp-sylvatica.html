Calamintha nepeta subsp. sylvatica

Calamintha nepeta subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, arranged in dense clusters.

The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Calamintha nepeta subsp.

sylvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
