Clematoclethra variabilis

Clematoclethra variabilis, commonly known as the variable clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Clethraceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities. It produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans.

Clematoclethra variabilis is relatively hardy and can thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
