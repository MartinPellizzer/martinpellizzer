Celtis taiyuanensis

Celtis taiyuanensis, commonly known as the Taiyuan hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, round berries that turn dark purple when mature. Celtis taiyuanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
