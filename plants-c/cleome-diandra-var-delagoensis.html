Cleome diandra var. delagoensis

Cleome diandra var. delagoensis, commonly known as the "Spider Flower," is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that are elongated and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cleome diandra var. delagoensis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
