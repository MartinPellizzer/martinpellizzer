Capurodendron pseudoterminalia

Capurodendron pseudoterminalia, commonly known as the red-flowered tamarind tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive red flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include a woody stem, bipinnate leaves, and a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
