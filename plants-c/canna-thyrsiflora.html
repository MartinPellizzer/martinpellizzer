Canna thyrsiflora

Canna thyrsiflora, commonly known as the red canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by brightly colored bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna thyrsiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
