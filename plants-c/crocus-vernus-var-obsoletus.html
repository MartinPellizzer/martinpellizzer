Crocus vernus var. obsoletus

Crocus vernus var. obsoletus, commonly known as the meadow crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and early spring display.
