Crataegus × subsphaerica nothosubsp. serromonogyna

Crataegus × subsphaerica nothosubsp.

serromonogyna, commonly known as the Sierra cherry, is a hybrid shrub native to California. It is characterized by its small, red or yellow berries that ripen in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open woodlands and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a hairy underside.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
