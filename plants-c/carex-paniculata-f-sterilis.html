Carex paniculata f. sterilis

Carex paniculata f. sterilis, commonly known as the sterile panicled sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense, spike-like inflorescence. Unlike its fertile counterpart, this form does not produce seeds, making it a sterile variant often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is typically found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

This plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
