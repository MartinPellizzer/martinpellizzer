Calathea silvicola

Calathea silvicola, commonly known as the forest calathea, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with intricate patterns of green, silver, and pink. The plant thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

Calathea silvicola is also known for its ability to purify the air, adding to its appeal as a decorative and beneficial houseplant.
