Cirsium arizonicum

Cirsium arizonicum, commonly known as Arizona thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
