Cassia regia

Cassia regia, commonly known as the royal flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value. Cassia regia produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
