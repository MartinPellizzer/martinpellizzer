Cryptocarya caesia

Cryptocarya caesia, commonly known as the blue-leafed cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a distinctive bluish tinge when fresh. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native birds.

The plant is often found in coastal and lowland forests, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
