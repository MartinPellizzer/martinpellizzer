Cardamine intermedia f. stylosa

Cardamine intermedia f. stylosa, commonly known as the slender water cress, is a variant of the broad-leaved water cress.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist, temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. Its small, white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in wetland habitats and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
