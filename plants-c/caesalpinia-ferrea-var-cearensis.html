Caesalpinia ferrea var. cearensis

Caesalpinia ferrea var. cearensis, commonly known as the Cearense ironwood, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its dense, hard wood and compound leaves. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences that bloom during the dry season. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and savannas.

Due to its durable wood, it has been historically used for construction and tool-making.
