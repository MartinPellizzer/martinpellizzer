Caladenia serrata

Caladenia serrata, commonly known as the serrated spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. The plant features a single, slender stem that bears a single, slender, greenish-white flower with a distinctive, intricate lip. Its flowering period occurs during the autumn months, and it is pollinated by insects, particularly wasps.

The species is part of the Caladenia genus, which is known for its diverse and often striking floral morphology.
