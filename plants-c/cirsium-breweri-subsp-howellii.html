Cirsium breweri subsp. howellii

Cirsium breweri subsp.

howellii, commonly known as Howell's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as chaparral and sagebrush communities. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, bristly textures, and the flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family.

It is often found in California and parts of Arizona, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
