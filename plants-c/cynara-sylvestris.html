Cynara sylvestris

Cynara sylvestris, commonly known as the wild artichoke, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and purple flower heads. The plant produces edible buds that are harvested before the flowers open. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Cynara sylvestris is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal.
