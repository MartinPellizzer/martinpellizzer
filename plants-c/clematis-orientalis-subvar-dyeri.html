Clematis orientalis subvar. dyeri

Clematis orientalis subvar. dyeri, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive, fragrant scent. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its climbing habit, it is commonly used to cover trellises or walls.
