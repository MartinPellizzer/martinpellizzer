Cartrema megacarpa

Cartrema megacarpa, commonly known as the giant fruit tree, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy fruits that can grow up to several centimeters in diameter. The plant has a thick, woody stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters.

The botanical interest of Cartrema megacarpa lies in its unusual fruit morphology and potential medicinal properties.
