Calea eitenii

Calea eitenii, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a rare and striking plant native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, tomatoes, and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white or violet flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Calea eitenii is also noted for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in local herbal remedies.
