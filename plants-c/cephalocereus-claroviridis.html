Cephalocereus claroviridis

Cephalocereus claroviridis, commonly known as the green torch cactus, is a species native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in dense, pale green spines. The plant produces large, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and gardeners.
