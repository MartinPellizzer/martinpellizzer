Caulanthus crassicaulis var. glaber

Caulanthus crassicaulis var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth stalked silversword, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its sturdy, upright stems and dense clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually basal, linear, and covered in a fine layer of white wax.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems, which set it apart from other varieties within the species.
