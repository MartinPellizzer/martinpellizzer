Caesalpinia insignis

Caesalpinia insignis, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer months. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warmer climates.

The botanical features of Caesalpinia insignis make it both visually striking and ecologically valuable.
