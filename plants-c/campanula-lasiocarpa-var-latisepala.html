Campanula lasiocarpa var. latisepala

Campanula lasiocarpa var. latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, adapted to its alpine habitat. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters at the top of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
