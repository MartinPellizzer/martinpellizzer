Crotalaria nigricans

Crotalaria nigricans, commonly known as black spurred crotalaria, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features distinctive yellow flowers with spurred petals, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. Crotalaria nigricans is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.

It is also noted for containing toxic alkaloids, making it a plant of both ecological and cautionary interest.
