Carex tricostata var. schummelii

Carex tricostata var. schummelii, commonly known as Schummel's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
