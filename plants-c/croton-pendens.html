Croton pendens

Croton pendens, commonly known as the weeping croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous leaves that hang in clusters, giving it a distinctive cascading appearance. The leaves are typically bright green with a velvety texture and often feature prominent veins. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red berries.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
