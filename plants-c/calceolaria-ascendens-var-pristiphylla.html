Calceolaria ascendens var. pristiphylla

Calceolaria ascendens var. pristiphylla, commonly known as the ascending slipper orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive slipper-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant features a hairy stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height, with opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
