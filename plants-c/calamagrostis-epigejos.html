Calamagrostis epigejos

Calamagrostis epigejos, commonly known as the silvergrass or giant Japanese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom. The plant produces panicles of fluffy, silver-gray flowers in late summer, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
