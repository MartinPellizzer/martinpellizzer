Camellia japonica f. macrocarpa

Camellia japonica f. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded Japanese camellia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pale pink, often with a contrasting eye. The plant produces large, elliptical seeds that are edible and have a mild, nutty flavor. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is prized for its winter blooming habit and ornamental value in both landscape design and container gardening.
