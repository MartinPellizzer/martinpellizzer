Cyathea tenerifrons

Cyathea tenerifrons, commonly known as the Canary Island tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in coastal areas or mountainous regions. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its tall stature, while its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters.

Cyathea tenerifrons plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
