Camellia oleosa

Camellia oleosa, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white, and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Camellia oleosa is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
