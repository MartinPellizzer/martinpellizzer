Cyperus reflexus f. evolutus

Cyperus reflexus f. evolutus, commonly known as the broadleaf sedgeland sedge, is a variant of the Cyperus reflexus species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense, grass-like foliage. The leaves are typically broad, flat, and arranged in a fan-like pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.
