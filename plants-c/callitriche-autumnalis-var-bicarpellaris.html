Callitriche autumnalis var. bicarpellaris

Callitriche autumnalis var. bicarpellaris, commonly known as the autumn water-starwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in ponds, ditches, and stream edges. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, opposite leaves that are usually submerged or floating. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale blue, and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
