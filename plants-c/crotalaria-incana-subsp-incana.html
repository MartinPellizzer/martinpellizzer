Crotalaria incana subsp. incana

Crotalaria incana subsp.

incana, commonly known as the slender crownflower, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and alternate leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in racemose inflorescences, which are often surrounded by bracts. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is also grown as an ornamental in gardens.
