Calamagrostis steinbergii

Calamagrostis steinbergii, commonly known as Steinberg's reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually erect and can produce small, spikelet-like flowers.

Calamagrostis steinbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
