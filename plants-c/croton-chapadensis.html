Croton chapadensis

Croton chapadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a deciduous shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant leaf colors.

Croton chapadensis is relatively low-maintenance but requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
