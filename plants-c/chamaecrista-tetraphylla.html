Chamaecrista tetraphylla

Chamaecrista tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leaf clover of the legume family, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with four pairs of leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
