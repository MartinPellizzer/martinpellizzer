Carduus acanthifolius

Carduus acanthifolius, commonly known as the thistleleaf cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its rough texture.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
