Calamus pycnocarpus

Calamus pycnocarpus, commonly known as the dense-fruited rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae and is characterized by its sturdy, woody stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by dark, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its primary economic value lies in its strong, flexible stems used for furniture and handicrafts.

The plant thrives in tropical rainforests, often climbing trees to reach sunlight.
