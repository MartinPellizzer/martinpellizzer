Cousinia gigantolepis

Cousinia gigantolepis, commonly known as the giant leaf cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, basal leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a dense, globular inflorescence, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Cousinia gigantolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
