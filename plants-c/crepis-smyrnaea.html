Crepis smyrnaea

Crepis smyrnaea, commonly known as the Smyrna hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow composite flowers. The plant features basal and alternate leaves that are often hairy and lobed. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into achene fruits.

Crepis smyrnaea is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
