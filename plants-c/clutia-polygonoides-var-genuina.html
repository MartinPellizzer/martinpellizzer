Clutia polygonoides var. genuina

Clutia polygonoides var. genuina, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, white, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
