Crataegus porracea

Crataegus porracea, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in woodland edges and dry soils, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
