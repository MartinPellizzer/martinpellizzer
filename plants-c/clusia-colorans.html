Clusia colorans

Clusia colorans, commonly known as the golden trumpet plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, blooming throughout the year in warm climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Clusia colorans is also known for its air-purifying qualities and resilience to poor soil conditions.
