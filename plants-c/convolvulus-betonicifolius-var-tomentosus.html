Convolvulus betonicifolius var. tomentosus

Convolvulus betonicifolius var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
