Cerastium decalvans subsp. macedonicum

Cerastium decalvans subsp.

macedonicum, commonly known as the Macedonian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and locally endemic species.
