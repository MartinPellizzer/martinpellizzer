Cassia tonduzii

Cassia tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduzii cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cassia tonduzii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
