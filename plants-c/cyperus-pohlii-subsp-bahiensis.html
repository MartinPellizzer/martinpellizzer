Cyperus pohlii subsp. bahiensis

Cyperus pohlii subsp.

bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves.

The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riparian zones, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

This subspecies is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.
