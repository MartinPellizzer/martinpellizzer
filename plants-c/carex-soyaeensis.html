Carex soyaeensis

Carex soyaeensis, commonly known as the Korean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in the summer.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland conservation.
