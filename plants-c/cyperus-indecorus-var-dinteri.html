Cyperus indecorus var. dinteri

Cyperus indecorus var. dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly Namibia and Angola, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
