Climacoptera Lachnophylla

Climacoptera lachnophylla is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes. Known for its unique spiral growth pattern, it forms a dense, shrub-like structure that defies conventional plant morphology. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence that reflects sunlight. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil and ample shade. Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, Climacoptera lachnophylla is considered a conservation concern.
