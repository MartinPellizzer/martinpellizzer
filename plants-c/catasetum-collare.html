Catasetum collare

Catasetum collare, commonly known as the collar orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, including a specialized lip that plays a role in pollination. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive red or purple stripe along the lip.

Catasetum collare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
