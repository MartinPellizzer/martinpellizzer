Calamagrostis sachalinensis var. argyraea

Calamagrostis sachalinensis var. argyraea, commonly known as silver feather reed grass, is a tall, ornamental grass native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, silver-blue blades that give it a striking appearance, especially in the fall when they turn coppery hues. The plant produces feathery, plume-like seed heads that add visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often used in water gardens or as a border plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for landscaping due to its elegant form and seasonal color changes.
