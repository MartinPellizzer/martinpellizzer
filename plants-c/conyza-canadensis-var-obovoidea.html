Conyza canadensis var. obovoidea

Conyza canadensis var. obovoidea, commonly known as Canada fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is an annual herb characterized by its hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its rapid growth.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators but can also be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
