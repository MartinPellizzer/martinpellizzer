Cistus intermedius

Cistus intermedius, commonly known as the Spanish rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid environments. Its leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic uses.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
