Caladenia attingens

Caladenia attingens, commonly known as the Purple Spider-orchid, is an orchid species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and striking purple and green floral structures that resemble spiders, giving it its common name. The plant produces a single, elongated flower with a distinctive, hooded labellum. It thrives in sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as heaths and woodlands.

Caladenia attingens is a protected species due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss.
