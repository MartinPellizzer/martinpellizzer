Coleocephalocereus fluminensis var. fluminensis

Coleocephalocereus fluminensis var. fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian night bloomer, is a cacti species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green and covered with white spines. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and pink. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique blooming habit and striking appearance, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and botanical collections.
