Cinna pendula var. mutica

Cinna pendula var. mutica, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The leaves are compound, with five to nine oval leaflets that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
