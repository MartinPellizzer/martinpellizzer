Cymbopogon goeringii var. viridis

Cymbopogon goeringii var. viridis, commonly known as green citron grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typical of the Poaceae family. It is widely cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.

Its aromatic properties make it a valuable species in both horticulture and commercial applications.
