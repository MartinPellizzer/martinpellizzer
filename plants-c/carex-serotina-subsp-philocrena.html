Carex serotina subsp. philocrena

Carex serotina subsp.

philocrena, commonly known as the late-flowering sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
