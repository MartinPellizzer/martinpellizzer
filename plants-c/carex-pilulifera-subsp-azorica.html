Carex pilulifera subsp. azorica

Carex pilulifera subsp.

azorica, commonly known as the Azorean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Azores. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with greenish-yellow flowers, typical of many sedges.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
