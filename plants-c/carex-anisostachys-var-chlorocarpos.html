Carex anisostachys var. chlorocarpos

Carex anisostachys var. chlorocarpos, commonly known as the green-stem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually green, and the inflorescence is often green or reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

It is a valuable plant in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
