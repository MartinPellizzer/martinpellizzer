Cerasus laurocerasus var. erecta

Cerasus laurocerasus var. erecta, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, cherry-like berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

However, its berries are toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
