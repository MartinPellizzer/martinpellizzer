Cuscuta pedicellata

Cuscuta pedicellata, commonly known as yellow dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a complex life cycle that involves germination, attachment to a host, and the formation of a haustorium to extract water and nutrients. The plant is often found growing on crops like corn and soybeans, making it a significant agricultural pest. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds make it challenging to control.

Cuscuta pedicellata plays an important role in ecological systems, though its parasitic nature can have detrimental effects on its host plants.
