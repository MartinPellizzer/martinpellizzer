Cardamine glacialis proles subcarnosa

Cardamine glacialis proles subcarnosa, commonly known as the alpine water-cress, is a herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, clustered in umbels, and it produces small, round seeds.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine zones.
