Cranichis fertilis

Cranichis fertilis, commonly known as the fertilized cranichis, is a rare orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous or forested areas. Its reproductive cycle is closely tied to specific pollinators, which contribute to its limited distribution.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Cranichis fertilis is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
