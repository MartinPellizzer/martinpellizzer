Cyperus torreyanus

Cyperus torreyanus, commonly known as the Torrey's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Cyperus torreyanus is a common indicator species for wetland habitats and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
