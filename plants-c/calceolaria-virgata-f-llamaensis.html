Calceolaria virgata f. llamaensis

Calceolaria virgata f. llamaensis, commonly known as the Llamaensis slipper orchid, is a striking member of the Calceolaria genus native to the Andean regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, resembling a small cup or pouch. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a velvety texture that aids in moisture retention. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant and unusual blooms.
