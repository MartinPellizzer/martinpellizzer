Ceraia odorata

Ceraia odorata, commonly known as the sweet senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The sweet senna is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
