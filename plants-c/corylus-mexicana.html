Corylus mexicana

Corylus mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and catkin-like flowers. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are an important food source for wildlife. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
