Clusia imbricata

Clusia imbricata, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Clusia imbricata is also known for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile plant for landscaping.
