Ceropegia calcarata

Ceropegia calcarata, commonly known as the rock ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellow, with a distinctive calyx that resembles a shield. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia calcarata is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
