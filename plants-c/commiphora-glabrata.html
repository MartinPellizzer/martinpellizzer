Commiphora glabrata

Commiphora glabrata, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The tree produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
