Cuphea repens

Cuphea repens, commonly known as the prostrate flamebeard, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that produces showy, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and a branched, prostrate stem that often roots at the nodes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cuphea repens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
