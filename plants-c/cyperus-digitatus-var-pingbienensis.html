Cyperus digitatus var. pingbienensis

Cyperus digitatus var. pingbienensis, commonly known as the Pingbien sedge, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist to aquatic conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes over 5,000 species of sedges.
