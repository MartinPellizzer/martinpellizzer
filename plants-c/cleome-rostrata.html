Cleome rostrata

Cleome rostrata, commonly known as the beakcleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated seed pods that resemble a beak, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Cleome rostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
