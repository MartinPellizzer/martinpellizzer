Colladonia syriaca

Colladonia syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Colladonia syriaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
