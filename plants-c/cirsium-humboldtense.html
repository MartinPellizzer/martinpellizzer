Cirsium humboldtense

Cirsium humboldtense, commonly known as Humboldt's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, achene fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Cirsium humboldtense is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
