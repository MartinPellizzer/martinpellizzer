Chamaecrista harmsiana

Chamaecrista harmsiana, commonly known as the yellow crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs.

Chamaecrista harmsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
