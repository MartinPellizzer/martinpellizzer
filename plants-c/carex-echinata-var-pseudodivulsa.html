Carex echinata var. pseudodivulsa

Carex echinata var. pseudodivulsa, commonly known as the spiked sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, three-lobed flowers. This variety is typically found in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
