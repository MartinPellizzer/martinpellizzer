Calophyllum pisiferum

Calophyllum pisiferum, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
