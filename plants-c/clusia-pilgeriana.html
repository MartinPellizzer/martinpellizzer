Clusia pilgeriana

Clusia pilgeriana, commonly known as the waxy leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and glossy foliage.

Clusia pilgeriana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
