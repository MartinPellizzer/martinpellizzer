Catananche lutea subsp. carpholepis

Catananche lutea subsp.

carpholepis, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, erect stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that have hairy surfaces.

The plant produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers with five petals, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds.

It is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
