Cupressus vietnamensis

Cupressus vietnamensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese cypress, is a coniferous tree native to Vietnam.

It belongs to the family Cupressaceae and is characterized by its scale-like leaves and aromatic wood. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species. Its cones are small and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its hardiness and is often used in horticulture and reforestation projects.
