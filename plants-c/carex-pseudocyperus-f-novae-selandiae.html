Carex pseudocyperus f. novae-selandiae

Carex pseudocyperus f. novae-selandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, streambanks, and forest margins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a panicle inflorescence.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
