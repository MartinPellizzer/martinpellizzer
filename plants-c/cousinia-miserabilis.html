Cousinia miserabilis

Cousinia miserabilis, commonly known as the weedy cudweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, harsh environments.

Cousinia miserabilis is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a resilient species in its native habitat.
