Camellia pulchella

Camellia pulchella, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species of flowering plant in the Theaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Camellia pulchella is typically evergreen and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
