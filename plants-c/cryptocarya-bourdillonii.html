Cryptocarya bourdillonii

Cryptocarya bourdillonii, commonly known as the New Zealand pohutukawa, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that are often clustered in umbels, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in coastal and lowland forest environments, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
