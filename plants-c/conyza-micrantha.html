Conyza micrantha

Conyza micrantha, commonly known as smallflower ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It features erect, branching stems covered in fine, white hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fluffy yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Conyza micrantha is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
