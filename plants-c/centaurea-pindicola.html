Centaurea pindicola

Centaurea pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spiny bracts around its flowers.

Centaurea pindicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
