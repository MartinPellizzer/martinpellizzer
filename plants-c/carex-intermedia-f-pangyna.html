Carex intermedia f. pangyna

Carex intermedia f. pangyna, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedgeland sedge, is a variant of the common sedge found in moist to wet environments.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. This plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
