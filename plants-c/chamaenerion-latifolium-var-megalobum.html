Chamaenerion latifolium var. megalobum

Chamaenerion latifolium var. megalobum, commonly known as the broad-leaved fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing showy pink to red flowers in summer. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, often with a velvety texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and sunny locations, often colonizing areas after fires or human disturbance.
