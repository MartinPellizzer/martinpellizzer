Cryptochloa decumbens

Cryptochloa decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate grass, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its creeping growth habit and slender, leafy stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, a common feature among grasses. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cryptochloa decumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
