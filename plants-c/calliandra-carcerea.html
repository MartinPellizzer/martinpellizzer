Calliandra carcerea

Calliandra carcerea, commonly known as the scarlet calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red, feather-like flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Additionally, it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
