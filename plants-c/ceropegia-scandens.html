Ceropegia scandens

Ceropegia scandens, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing perennial that produces small, tubular flowers with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators. The plant has a succulent, fleshy stem and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
