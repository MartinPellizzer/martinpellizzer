Cordia vanhermannii

Cordia vanhermannii, commonly known as the Vanherrmann's cordia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.
