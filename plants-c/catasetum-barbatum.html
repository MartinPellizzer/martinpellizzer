Catasetum barbatum

Catasetum barbatum, commonly known as the beard orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a single, elongated spike bearing multiple flowers. The plant produces showy, often fragrant blooms in shades of yellow, orange, and red, with a distinctive lip that is modified for pollination. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment.

Catasetum barbatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow under the right conditions.
