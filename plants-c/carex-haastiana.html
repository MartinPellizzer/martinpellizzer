Carex haastiana

Carex haastiana, commonly known as Haast's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex haastiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
