Campanula paniculata

Campanula paniculata, commonly known as the panicled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Campanula paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
