Cirsium osseticum subsp. tricholoma

Cirsium osseticum subsp.

tricholoma, commonly known as the Ossetian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, lobed leaves.

The plant produces showy purple to pink flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or steppe habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
