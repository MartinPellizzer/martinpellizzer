Cephalaria microdonta

Cephalaria microdonta, commonly known as the small-flowered cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, yellowish to brownish flowers are clustered in dense spherical heads, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Cephalaria microdonta is often found in dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
