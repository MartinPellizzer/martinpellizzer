Clerodendrum silvanum var. buchholzii

Clerodendrum silvanum var. buchholzii, commonly known as the Buchholz's flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered inflorescences that produce bright red or orange flowers, often resembling flames. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
