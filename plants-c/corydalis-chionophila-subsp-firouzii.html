Corydalis chionophila subsp. firouzii

Corydalis chionophila subsp.

firouzii, commonly known as the Kashmir snowflax, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Himalayas. It features slender, hairy stems that produce clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its frost-resistant nature. It thrives in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a striking addition to rock gardens and alpine collections.
