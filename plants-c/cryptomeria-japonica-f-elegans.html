Cryptomeria japonica f. elegans

Cryptomeria japonica f. elegans, commonly known as the Japanese cedar variegated form, is a cultivar of the Japanese cedar characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features narrow, needle-like leaves that are often a mix of green, gold, and white, creating a striking visual contrast. This form is typically smaller than the standard Japanese cedar, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting good tolerance to urban conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural landscapes.
