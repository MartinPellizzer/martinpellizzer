Conoclinium vindex

Conoclinium vindex, commonly known as the hummingbird vine, is a climbing perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Conoclinium vindex is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in gardens.
