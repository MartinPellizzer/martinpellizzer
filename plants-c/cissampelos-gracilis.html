Cissampelos gracilis

Cissampelos gracilis, commonly known as the slender leaf moonvine, is a climbing perennial vine native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and delicate, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically green when mature, turning red upon ripening. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
