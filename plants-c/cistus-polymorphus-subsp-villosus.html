Cistus polymorphus subsp. villosus

Cistus polymorphus subsp.

villosus, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, gray-green foliage covered in fine, hairy glands.

The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
