Carex jemtlandica var. gotlandiae

Carex jemtlandica var. gotlandiae, commonly known as Gotland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Baltic region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and ponds.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically appearing in late summer.
