Cinnamomum obtusatum

Cinnamomum obtusatum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are ovate with a waxy surface, and the flowers are small and greenish-white.

The bark is often harvested for use in traditional medicine and as a spice.
