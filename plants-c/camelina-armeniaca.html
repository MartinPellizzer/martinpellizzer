Camelina armeniaca

Camelina armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian mustard, is an annual herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It produces small, yellow flowers and has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant's leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Camelina armeniaca is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
