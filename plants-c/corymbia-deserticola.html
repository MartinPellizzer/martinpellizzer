Corymbia deserticola

Corymbia deserticola, commonly known as the desert bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that often appears flaky or peeling. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Corymbia deserticola is also known for its hard, dense timber, which has been used by Indigenous communities for various purposes.
