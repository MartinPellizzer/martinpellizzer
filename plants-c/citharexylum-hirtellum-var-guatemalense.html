Citharexylum hirtellum var. guatemalense

Citharexylum hirtellum var. guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan citharexylum, is a shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and foliage.
