Cyanotis rupestris

Cyanotis rupestris, commonly known as the rock violet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically broad and hairy, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Cyanotis rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
