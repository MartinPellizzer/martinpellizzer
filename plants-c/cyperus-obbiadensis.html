Cyperus obbiadensis

Cyperus obbiadensis, commonly known as the African umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Nigeria and Sudan. The plant is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with dense, umbrella-shaped clusters of spikelets at the top of its stems. Cyperus obbiadensis typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
