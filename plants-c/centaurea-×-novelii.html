Centaurea × novelii

Centaurea × novelii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a cross between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea jacea (red cornflower), resulting in a species with distinctive bi-color flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring lance-shaped leaves and showy, composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in gardens and naturalized areas.

Its ornamental value is high due to its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
