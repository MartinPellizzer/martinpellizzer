Cacalia vespertilio

Cacalia vespertilio, commonly known as the evening bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the late afternoon. It has hairy stems and leaves that are generally lance-shaped.

Cacalia vespertilio is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
