Cereus speciosissimus var. edesii

Cereus speciosissimus var. edesii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and its ability to produce large, showy white flowers at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers open only during the night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

This variety is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and unique blooming behavior.
