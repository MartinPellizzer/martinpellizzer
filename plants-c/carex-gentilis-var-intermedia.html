Carex gentilis var. intermedia

Carex gentilis var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in saturated soils.
