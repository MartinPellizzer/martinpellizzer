Centaurea × aemiliae

Centaurea × aemiliae, commonly known as the Aemilia's cornflower, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Centaurea cyanus and Centaurea micrantha.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
