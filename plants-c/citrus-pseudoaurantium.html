Citrus pseudoaurantium

Citrus pseudoaurantium, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish-orange fruits, which are typically smaller and more acidic than those of the common orange. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and as a rootstock for other citrus species.
