Cissus lombardiana

Cissus lombardiana, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it climb. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Cissus lombardiana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor environments.
