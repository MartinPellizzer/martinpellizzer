Ceratopetalum apetalum var. typicum

Ceratopetalum apetalum var. typicum, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-red flowers in late spring, which are clustered in terminal racemes. Its botanical features include woody stems with a distinctive, rough texture and the ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
