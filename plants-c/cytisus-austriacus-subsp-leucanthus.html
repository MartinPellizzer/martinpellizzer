Cytisus austriacus subsp. leucanthus

Cytisus austriacus subsp.

leucanthus, commonly known as the Austrian broom, is a shrub native to Central Europe. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, often thorned stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
