Cratylia desvauxii

Cratylia desvauxii, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in thick, woody pods that can persist on the plant for extended periods.

Cratylia desvauxii is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
