Carex caudata

Carex caudata, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish or reddish flowers, typically in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
