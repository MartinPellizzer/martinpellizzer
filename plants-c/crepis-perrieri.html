Crepis perrieri

Crepis perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

Crepis perrieri is often found in open habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
