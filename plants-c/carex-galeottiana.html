Carex galeottiana

Carex galeottiana, commonly known as the Galeotti's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to wetland habitats in North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and is often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
