Cyrtopera monotropis

Cyrtopera monotropis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironwood, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and dense, woody structure, which provide habitat for various desert wildlife. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Cyrtopera monotropis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in desert ecosystems.
