Curcuma vamana

Curcuma vamana, commonly known as the lesser turmeric, is a rhizomatous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and cardamom. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers and has aromatic, rhizome-like roots that are used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a compact growth habit.

The rhizomes are often harvested for their medicinal and culinary properties.
