Cyrtandra × oulophylla

Cyrtandra × oulophylla, commonly known as the hybrid beautyberry, is a flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Cyrtandra pedata and Cyrtandra oulophylloides, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its striking clusters of purple to violet berries, which are produced after the flowering season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
