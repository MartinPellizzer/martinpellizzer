Carlina discolor

Carlina discolor, commonly known as the purpledisk aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Carlina discolor has a taproot system and is often found in moist, open habitats like meadows and woodland edges.

Its foliage is generally gray-green with hairy surfaces, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
