Crassula nudicaulis

Crassula nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked stem crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are small, thick, and rounded, arranged in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant.

Crassula nudicaulis is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
