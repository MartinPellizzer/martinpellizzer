Carex melanophora

Carex melanophora, commonly known as black-stem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and streambanks.

Carex melanophora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
