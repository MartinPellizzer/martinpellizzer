Chassalia lukwangulensis

Chassalia lukwangulensis, commonly known as the Lukwanguli chassalia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude grasslands of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh climatic conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
