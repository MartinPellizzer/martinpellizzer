Calla virginica

Calla virginica, commonly known as the swamp pink, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, white spatheae that enclose the small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are arrow-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Calla virginica plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
