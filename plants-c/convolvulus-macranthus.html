Convolvulus macranthus

Convolvulus macranthus, commonly known as the giant convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
