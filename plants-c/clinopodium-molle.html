Clinopodium molle

Clinopodium molle, commonly known as slender woodruff, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a minty scent when crushed.

Clinopodium molle thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.
