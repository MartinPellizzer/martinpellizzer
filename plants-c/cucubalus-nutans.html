Cucubalus nutans

Cucubalus nutans, commonly known as the hanging cucumber or pendulous cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, edible fruits resembling cucumbers. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and tend to trail along the ground or climb supports.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits in warm climates.
