Coreopsis scandens

Coreopsis scandens, commonly known as the climbing coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with radiating petals and a central disk. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often hairy.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
