Coreopsis schlechteri

Coreopsis schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its showy yellow flowers, which resemble daisies, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Coreopsis schlechteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
