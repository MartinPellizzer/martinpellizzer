Carex rubicunda

Carex rubicunda, commonly known as redstem sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually reddish or purplish, adding visual interest in late summer.

Carex rubicunda plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
