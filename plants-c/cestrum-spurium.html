Cestrum spurium

Cestrum spurium, commonly known as the redflower cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly rough texture.

Cestrum spurium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
