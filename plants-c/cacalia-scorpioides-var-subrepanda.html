Cacalia scorpioides var. subrepanda

Cacalia scorpioides var. subrepanda, commonly known as the slender false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
