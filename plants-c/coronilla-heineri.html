Coronilla heineri

Coronilla heineri, commonly known as the Spanish clover, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Coronilla heineri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration.
