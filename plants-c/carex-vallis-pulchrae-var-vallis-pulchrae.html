Carex vallis-pulchrae var. vallis-pulchrae

Carex vallis-pulchrae var. vallis-pulchrae, commonly known as the valley beauty sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. This plant prefers moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy or boggy environments. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
