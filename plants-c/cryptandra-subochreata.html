Cryptandra subochreata

Cryptandra subochreata, commonly known as the golden cryptandra, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
