Cestrum panamense

Cestrum panamense, commonly known as the Panama nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, reddish seeds.

Cestrum panamense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
