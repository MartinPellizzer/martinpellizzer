Cucurbita pepo var. kintogwa

Cucurbita pepo var. kintogwa, commonly known as the West African bitter melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a trailing vine that produces elongated, ribbed fruits with a bitter taste. The plant is native to West Africa and is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in traditional cuisines. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with five petals.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and medicinal properties in local communities.
