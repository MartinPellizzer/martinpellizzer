Citrus grandis var. pyriformis

Citrus grandis var. pyriformis, commonly known as the star-shaped citrus or pyriform citrus, is a variant of the longan tree.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers and elongated, pear-like fruit. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves that provide a dense canopy. Its fruit is typically harvested when it turns from green to yellow or red, depending on the variety.

The botanical features of this plant make it a unique subject for both ornamental and potential agricultural study.
