Conyza megensis

Conyza megensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragweeds, and is known for its composite flower heads.
