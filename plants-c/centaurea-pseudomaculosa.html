Centaurea pseudomaculosa

Centaurea pseudomaculosa, commonly known as the spotted centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
