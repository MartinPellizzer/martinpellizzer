Cerastium pumilum glutinosum

Cerastium pumilum glutinosum, commonly known as sticky mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a sticky, glandular coating, which is a distinguishing botanical trait. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals, and bloom in early spring.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.
