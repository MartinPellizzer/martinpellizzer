Casearia rupestris

Casearia rupestris, commonly known as the rock casearia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by distinctive elliptical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
