Cleome kalachariensis subsp. kalachariensis

Cleome kalachariensis subsp.

kalachariensis, commonly known as the Kalahari spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like inflorescences that consist of numerous small, white to pink flowers.

The plant typically grows as an annual herb with branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
