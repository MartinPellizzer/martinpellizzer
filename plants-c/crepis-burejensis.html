Crepis burejensis

Crepis burejensis, commonly known as the Burej mountain cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Crepis burejensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
