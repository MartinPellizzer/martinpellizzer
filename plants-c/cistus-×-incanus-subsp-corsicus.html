Cistus × incanus subsp. corsicus

Cistus × incanus subsp.

corsicus, commonly known as Corsican rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and southern Sardinia. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas. It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions makes it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
