Cerastium balearicum

Cerastium balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic mouse-ear, is a small flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant features hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Cerastium balearicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
