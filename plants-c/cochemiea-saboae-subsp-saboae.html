Cochemiea saboae subsp. saboae

Cochemiea saboae subsp.

saboae, commonly known as the Saboae cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing, branching plant with cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Its structure makes it well adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.
