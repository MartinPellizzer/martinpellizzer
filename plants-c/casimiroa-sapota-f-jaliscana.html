Casimiroa sapota f. jaliscana

Casimiroa sapota f. jaliscana, commonly known as the Mexican sapote, is a cultivar of the sapote tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, smooth, green fruit with a sweet, creamy white flesh. The plant produces flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by the development of its edible fruit. The tree is evergreen, with broad, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
