Cistus corsicus

Cistus corsicus, commonly known as Corsican rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Cistus corsicus plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
