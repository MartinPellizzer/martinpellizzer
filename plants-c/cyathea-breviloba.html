Cyathea breviloba

Cyathea breviloba, commonly known as the short-stalked tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, frond-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a distinctive crown of pinnate fronds that are often drooping or arching. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cyathea breviloba plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
