Cladrastis scandens

Cladrastis scandens, commonly known as the fragrant vine or sweet pea vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb trellises or other structures. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it sheds its foliage in the autumn.

Cladrastis scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
