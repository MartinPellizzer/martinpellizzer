Cucumis persicus

Cucumis persicus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated fruits that are typically green and have a mild, crisp flavor. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a vine-like growth habit, often climbing or trailing. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinct appearance.

This species is native to regions of Central Asia and is cultivated for both its edible fruits and ornamental value.
