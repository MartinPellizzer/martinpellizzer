Cyathea revoluta

Cyathea revoluta, commonly known as the revolute tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive revolute (rolled under) margin.

Cyathea revoluta plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
