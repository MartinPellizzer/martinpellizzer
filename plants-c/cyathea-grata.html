Cyathea grata

Cyathea grata, commonly known as the pretty tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds. The plant has a distinctive root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bright green and can grow quite large, adding a dramatic visual effect in gardens.

Cyathea grata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in humid climates.
