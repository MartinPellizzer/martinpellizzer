Cyperus ferax var. auriculatus

Cyperus ferax var. auriculatus, commonly known as the auriculate flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic habitats. Its slender, grass-like leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flower spikes.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various water conditions and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
