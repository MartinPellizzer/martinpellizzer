Catachaenia alternifolia

Catachaenia alternifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved catachaenia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, linear leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color, arranged in dense terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
