Cineraria appendiculata

Cineraria appendiculata, commonly known as the blueweed or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
