Cyperus elegans

Cyperus elegans, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and wetlands. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
