Cyathea petiolulata

Cyathea petiolulata, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a robust root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Cyathea petiolulata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
