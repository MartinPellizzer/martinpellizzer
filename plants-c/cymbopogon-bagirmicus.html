Cymbopogon bagirmicus

Cymbopogon bagirmicus, commonly known as the Bagirmi grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in both ecological and agricultural systems in its native region.
