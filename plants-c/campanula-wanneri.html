Campanula wanneri

Campanula wanneri, commonly known as the Wanner’s bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Campanula wanneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
