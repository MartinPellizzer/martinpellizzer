Coprosma rotundifolia

Coprosma rotundifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf coprosma, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, rounded leaves that are a characteristic dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for native birds.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and forested areas, showcasing its adaptability to different environmental conditions.
