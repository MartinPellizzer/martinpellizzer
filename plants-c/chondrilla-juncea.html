Chondrilla juncea

Chondrilla juncea, commonly known as yellow goatweed, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and have a serrated margin, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Chondrilla juncea is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
