Cyathea ocanensis

Cyathea ocanensis, commonly known as the Ocanes fern, is a species of tree fern native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soil. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat.

Cyathea ocanensis plays an important ecological role by providing shelter and food for various forest organisms.
