Campanula rotundifolia subsp. pinifolia

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are rounded and often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
