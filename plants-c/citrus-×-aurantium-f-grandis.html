Citrus × aurantium f. grandis

Citrus × aurantium f. grandis, commonly known as the sweet orange.

It is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its large, round fruits that are typically sweet and less acidic than other citrus varieties. The tree has smooth, dark green leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. The fruit's peel, or zest, is widely used in cooking and perfumery due to its aromatic properties.

This variety is valued for its culinary and commercial applications in the food and beverage industry.
