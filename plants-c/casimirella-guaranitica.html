Casimirella guaranitica

Casimirella guaranitica, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and clustered, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, anchoring itself with its aerial roots. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Casimirella guaranitica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
