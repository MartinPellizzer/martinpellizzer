Cyperus fuscoater

Cyperus fuscoater, commonly known as the dark-stemmed flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically found at the base of the stems. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies.

Cyperus fuscoater plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
