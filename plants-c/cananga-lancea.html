Cananga lancea

Cananga lancea, commonly known as the fragrant lanceleaf champa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of essential oils. Its flowers emit a sweet, spicy aroma, which is highly valued in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Cananga lancea thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
