Catha multiflora

Catha multiflora, commonly known as Khat, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula and East Africa.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple branches. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are elongated, opposite, and have a distinctive serrated edge.

The plant is known for containing psychoactive alkaloids, particularly cathinone and cathine, which contribute to its traditional use in social and cultural contexts.
