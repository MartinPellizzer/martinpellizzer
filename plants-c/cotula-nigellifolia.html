Cotula nigellifolia

Cotula nigellifolia, commonly known as the black cumin or nigella plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy species in suitable climates.

Cotula nigellifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
