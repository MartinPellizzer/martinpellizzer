Cryptocarya mindanaensis

Cryptocarya mindanaensis, commonly known as the Mindanao cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland to montane rainforests, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental potential and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
