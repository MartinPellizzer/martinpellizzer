Cicerbita decipiens var. multifida

Cicerbita decipiens var. multifida, commonly known as the forked bittercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a distinctive lobed or divided appearance.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.
