Capparis bussei

Capparis bussei, commonly known as the Socotra caper, is a flowering plant native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and thick, fleshy leaves adapted to arid environments. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is well-suited to rocky and coastal habitats.
