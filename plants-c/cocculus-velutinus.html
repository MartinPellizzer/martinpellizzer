Cocculus velutinus

Cocculus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet tara, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by hairy, pod-like fruits containing smooth, black seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, though its botanical properties remain under scientific study.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical research.
