Castanopsis carlesii

Castanopsis carlesii, commonly known as the Taiwan chinkapin, is a species of evergreen tree belonging to the Fagaceae family.

It is native to southern China, Taiwan, and parts of Vietnam, typically growing in mountainous regions with high humidity. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its fruit is a spiny acorn enclosed in a cupule, which is characteristic of the Fagaceae family.

Castanopsis carlesii is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also an important component of its native ecosystems.
