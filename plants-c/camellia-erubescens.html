Camellia erubescens

Camellia erubescens, commonly known as the red camellia, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pink and white. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Camellia erubescens is an evergreen shrub that can grow up to 6 feet tall, often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts.
