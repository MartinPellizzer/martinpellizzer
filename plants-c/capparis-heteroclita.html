Capparis heteroclita

Capparis heteroclita, commonly known as the rock caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has woody stems and is often found growing on rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a rough texture and can have a slightly aromatic scent.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
