Crataegus rosei subsp. parryana

Crataegus rosei subsp.

parryana, commonly known as Parry's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring.

The plant produces red, persistent berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun. Crataegus rosei subsp.

parryana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
