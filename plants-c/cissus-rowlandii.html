Cissus rowlandii

Cissus rowlandii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-green leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Cissus rowlandii thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often grown as an ornamental plant.
