Carduus macrocephalus subsp. sporadum

Carduus macrocephalus subsp.

sporadum, commonly known as the common sowthistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a rosette of spiny, hairy leaves that are typically lobed and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a large, spherical inflorescence, which gives it its common name. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
