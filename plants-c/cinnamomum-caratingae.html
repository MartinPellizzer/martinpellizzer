Cinnamomum caratingae

Cinnamomum caratingae, commonly known as the Caribbean cinnamon or Guianese cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant bark and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark purple, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface with a strong, spicy scent.

The bark is used in traditional medicine and for its aromatic properties, similar to other cinnamon species.
