Carex koidzumii

Carex koidzumii, commonly known as the Koidzumi sedge, is a perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that are often a deep green color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.
