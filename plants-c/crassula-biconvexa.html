Crassula biconvexa

Crassula biconvexa, commonly known as the two-convex crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Crassula biconvexa is popular as a low-maintenance houseplant and for rock garden displays.
