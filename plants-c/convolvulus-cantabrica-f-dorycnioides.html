Convolvulus cantabrica f. dorycnioides

Convolvulus cantabrica f. dorycnioides, commonly known as the Cantabrian bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while the stems are hairy and slightly angular. This subspecies is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is adapted to rocky and coastal environments.

It blooms from spring to early summer, producing small, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
