Cordemoya integrifolia

Cordemoya integrifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum integrifolium*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, reddish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Cordemoya integrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in local herbal remedies.
