Cyathea hypocrateriformis

Cyathea hypocrateriformis, commonly known as the Australian tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a long lifespan and can grow up to several meters in height, with a slow growth rate. Its rhizome is thick and woody, supporting the upright structure of the plant.

The fronds are typically dark green and can be used in traditional practices by Indigenous Australians.
