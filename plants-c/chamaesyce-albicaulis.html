Chamaesyce albicaulis

Chamaesyce albicaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing herb with succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a whitish coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow, fleshy, and usually covered in fine hairs.

Chamaesyce albicaulis is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
