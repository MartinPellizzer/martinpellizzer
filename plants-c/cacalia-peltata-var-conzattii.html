Cacalia peltata var. conzattii

Cacalia peltata var. conzattii, commonly known as the Conzatti's peltate thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of large, spoon-shaped, peltate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, purple to pink flowers in late summer, which are arranged in a single, terminal head. Its leaves and stems are typically hairy, and it thrives in dry, rocky soils.

This variety is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
