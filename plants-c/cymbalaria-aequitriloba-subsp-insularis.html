Cymbalaria aequitriloba subsp. insularis

Cymbalaria aequitriloba subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the island leafy weed, is a flowering plant species native to certain Mediterranean islands. It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
