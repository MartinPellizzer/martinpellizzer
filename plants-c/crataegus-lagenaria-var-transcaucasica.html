Crataegus lagenaria var. transcaucasica

Crataegus lagenaria var. transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
