Cyperus persquarrosus

Cyperus persquarrosus, commonly known as the spiked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences with small, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance.

Cyperus persquarrosus is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
