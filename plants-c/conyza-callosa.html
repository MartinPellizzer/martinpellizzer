Conyza callosa

Conyza callosa, commonly known as roughleaf groundsel, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
