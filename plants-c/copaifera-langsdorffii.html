Copaifera langsdorffii

Copaifera langsdorffii, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces yellowish to reddish-brown resin, which is harvested for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hillside areas.

The tree plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to the rainforest's biodiversity.
