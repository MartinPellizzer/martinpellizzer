Cyperus engelmannii

Cyperus engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It is native to North America and is often found in the eastern and central regions of the United States.

Due to its ability to thrive in wet conditions, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
