Cyperus kegelianus

Cyperus kegelianus, commonly known as the Kegelian flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the base of the inflorescence. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus kegelianus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
