Cassia acuruensis

Cassia acuruensis, commonly known as the acuru tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded canopy. The leaves are bipinnate, with small, ovate leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in racemes, while the pods are elongated and contain flat, edible seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
