Crotalaria pechueliana

Crotalaria pechueliana, commonly known as the yellow spurred crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with spurred petals. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and its role in soil nitrogen fixation.
