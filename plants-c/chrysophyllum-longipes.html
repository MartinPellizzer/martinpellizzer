Chrysophyllum longipes

Chrysophyllum longipes, commonly known as the longipes starfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in some parts of the world for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
