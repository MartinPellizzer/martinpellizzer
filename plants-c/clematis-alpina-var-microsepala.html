Clematis alpina var. microsepala

Clematis alpina var. microsepala, commonly known as the alpine clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Clematis alpina var. microsepala is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
