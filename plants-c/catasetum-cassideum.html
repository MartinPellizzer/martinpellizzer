Catasetum cassideum

Catasetum cassideum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and is part of the Catasetum genus, which is known for its unique pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers have a distinctive structure with a specialized lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
