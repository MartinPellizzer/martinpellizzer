Cerastium scaposum subsp. peninsularum

Cerastium scaposum subsp.

peninsularum, commonly known as the Peninsular mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, hairy pubescence.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with a dense layer of fine hairs.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
