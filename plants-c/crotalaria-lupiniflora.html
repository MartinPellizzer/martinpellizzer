Crotalaria lupiniflora

Crotalaria lupiniflora, commonly known as the lupine-flowered crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and compound leaves. Its seed pods are cylindrical and contain several oblong seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria lupiniflora is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
