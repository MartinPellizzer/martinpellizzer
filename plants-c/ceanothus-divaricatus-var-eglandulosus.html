Ceanothus divaricatus var. eglandulosus

Ceanothus divaricatus var. eglandulosus, commonly known as the glandular ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often glandular-dotted, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
