Cupressus pisifera var. filiera

Cupressus pisifera var. filiera, commonly known as the narrowleaf Chinese fir, is a variant of the Chinese fir native to China.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and conical shape. The plant belongs to the Cupressaceae family and is known for its resilience to environmental stress. It produces small, round cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
