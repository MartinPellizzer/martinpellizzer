Colchicum capense subsp. capense

Colchicum capense subsp.

capense, commonly known as the Cape colchicum, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa. It features striking, nodding, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a fibrous root system and produces a single, lance-shaped leaf during its growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic alkaloids.
