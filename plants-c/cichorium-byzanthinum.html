Cichorium byzanthinum

Cichorium byzanthinum, commonly known as the Byzantine chicory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and bright blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and roots, which are used in salads and as a coffee substitute.

Additionally, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
