Crinum biflorum

Crinum biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, with two to three flowers per stem, each composed of six petals. Its leaves are strap-like, broad, and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Crinum biflorum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
