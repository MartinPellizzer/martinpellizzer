Campanula nobilis

Campanula nobilis, commonly known as the noble bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Campanula nobilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
