Cladium fimbristyloides

Cladium fimbristyloides, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or saturated soil. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Cladium fimbristyloides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
