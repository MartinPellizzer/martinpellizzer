Carex varia f. australis

Carex varia f. australis, commonly known as the Australian sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. This variety is native to Australia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is typically clump-forming, making it a suitable choice for water gardens or bog landscapes.

Its adaptability to wet conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
