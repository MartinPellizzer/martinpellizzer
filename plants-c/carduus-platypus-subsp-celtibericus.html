Carduus platypus subsp. celtibericus

Carduus platypus subsp.

celtibericus, commonly known as the Iberian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and North Africa. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

The plant features a robust taproot and upright stems covered in coarse, hairy foliage. Its striking purple to pink flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn, are arranged in a single, terminal head.

The species is notable for its ecological resilience and is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates.
