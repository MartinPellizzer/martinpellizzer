Chamaecrista lineata var. trifoliata

Chamaecrista lineata var. trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf burr legume, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
