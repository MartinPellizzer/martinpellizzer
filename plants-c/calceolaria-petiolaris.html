Calceolaria petiolaris

Calceolaria petiolaris, commonly known as the hanging lantern flower, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, hood-like flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calceolaria petiolaris is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
