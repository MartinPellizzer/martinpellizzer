Crataegus brunetiana

Crataegus brunetiana, commonly known as the Brunet hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees and Alps. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its small, red berries are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

The Brunet hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
