Carex wutuensis

Carex wutuensis, commonly known as the Wutu sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
