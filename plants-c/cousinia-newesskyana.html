Cousinia newesskyana

Cousinia newesskyana, commonly known as the Afghan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cousinia newesskyana is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
