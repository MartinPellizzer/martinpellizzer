Crataegus coreana

Crataegus coreana, commonly known as the Korean hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Korea and parts of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive spring blooms and autumn foliage.
