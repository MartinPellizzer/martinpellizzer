Citrullus colocynthis

Citrullus colocynthis, commonly known as the bitter apple or colocynth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish-green fruits that resemble small watermelons. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a distinctive, rugged appearance with spiny stems. Its fruit contains a thick, bitter pulp that is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in arid environments, making it a hardy species adapted to dry conditions.
