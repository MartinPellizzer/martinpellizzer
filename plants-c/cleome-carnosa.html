Cleome carnosa

Cleome carnosa, commonly known as the woolly cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and alternate leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Cleome carnosa is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular addition to gardens and landscapes.
