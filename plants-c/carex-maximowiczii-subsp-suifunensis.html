Carex maximowiczii subsp. suifunensis

Carex maximowiczii subsp.

suifunensis, commonly known as the Korean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
