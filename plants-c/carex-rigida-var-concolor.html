Carex rigida var. concolor

Carex rigida var. concolor, commonly known as the smooth sedges, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions makes it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
