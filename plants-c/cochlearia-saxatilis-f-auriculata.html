Cochlearia saxatilis f. auriculata

Cochlearia saxatilis f. auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate cochlearia, is a botanical variant of the coastal flowering plant Cochlearia saxatilis.

It is characterized by its distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves, which are often lobed or notched. This plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It produces small, violet or white flowers in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
