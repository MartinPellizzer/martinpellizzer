Carex ligulata var. austrokoreensis

Carex ligulata var. austrokoreensis, commonly known as the Korean narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
