Cyperus natalensis

Cyperus natalensis, commonly known as Natal flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of the stems.

Natal flatsedge is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
