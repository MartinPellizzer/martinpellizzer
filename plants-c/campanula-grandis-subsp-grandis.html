Campanula grandis subsp. grandis

Campanula grandis subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the Great bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
