Cassia nutans

Cassia nutans, commonly known as the weeping cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia nutans thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and seasonal flowering.
