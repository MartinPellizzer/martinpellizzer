Carex ferruginea var. macrostachys

Carex ferruginea var. macrostachys, commonly known as the redstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green with reddish hues, especially at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
