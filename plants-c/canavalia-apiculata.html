Canavalia apiculata

Canavalia apiculata, commonly known as the hairy jackbean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces and small, yellow flowers that develop into pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agroforestry systems.

Canavalia apiculata is also valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein.
