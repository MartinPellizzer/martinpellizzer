Coppensia regentii

Coppensia regentii, commonly known as the regent's orchid, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family found in the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Coppensia regentii is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its ornamental value.

However, it is also considered endangered due to habitat loss and over-collection.
