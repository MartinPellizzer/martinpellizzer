Cinnamomum subaveniopsis

Cinnamomum subaveniopsis, commonly known as the Philippine cinnamon tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The species is also valued for its potential in the production of essential oils and as a source of timber.
