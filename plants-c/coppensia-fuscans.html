Coppensia fuscans

Coppensia fuscans, commonly known as the black sandplant, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Coppensia fuscans is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
