Cyathea eriophora

Cyathea eriophora, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its upright growth.

Cyathea eriophora plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
