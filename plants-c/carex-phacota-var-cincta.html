Carex phacota var. cincta

Carex phacota var. cincta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in temperate regions.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
