Camissonia specicola

Camissonia specicola, commonly known as the narrowleaf camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Camissoniaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to the dry, rocky environments it inhabits.

Camissonia specicola is often found in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
