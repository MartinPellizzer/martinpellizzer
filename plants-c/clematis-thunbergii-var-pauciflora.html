Clematis thunbergii var. pauciflora

Clematis thunbergii var. pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that climb by means of small tendrils. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have three to five lobes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.
