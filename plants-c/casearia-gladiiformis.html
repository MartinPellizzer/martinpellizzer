Casearia gladiiformis

Casearia gladiiformis, commonly known as the broadleaf casearia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences. Its fruits are elongated, woody capsules that split open to release tiny seeds.

Casearia gladiiformis is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
