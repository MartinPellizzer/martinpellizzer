Cleisocentron

Cleisocentron, commonly known as the false holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It consists of about 20 species, most of which are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plants are shrubs or small trees with opposite, simple, and often lance-shaped leaves. They produce small, clustered flowers that are usually white or yellow, and their fruits are follicles containing seeds with tufts of hair.

The genus is characterized by its milky sap and often aromatic foliage.
