Crucihimalaya

Crucihimalaya, commonly known as the Himalayan crucifix, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its delicate, cruciform flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or purple. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to the harsh mountain environment.

Crucihimalaya is often found in rocky and alpine regions, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
