Clematis pterantha

Clematis pterantha, commonly known as the slender clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its twining stems and is often found in woodland or shady environments. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Clematis pterantha requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun for optimal growth.
