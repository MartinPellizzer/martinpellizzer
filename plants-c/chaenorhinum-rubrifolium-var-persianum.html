Chaenorhinum rubrifolium var. persianum

Chaenorhinum rubrifolium var. persianum, commonly known as the Persian redleaf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange foliage, which contrasts with its yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
