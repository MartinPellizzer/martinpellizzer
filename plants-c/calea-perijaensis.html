Calea perijaensis

Calea perijaensis, commonly known as the Perijan nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Calea perijaensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in local herbal remedies.
