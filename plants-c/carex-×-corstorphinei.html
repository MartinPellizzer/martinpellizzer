Carex × corstorphinei

Carex × corstorphinei, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Carex lyngbyei and Carex echinata, characterized by its robust growth and dense clumping habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is cultivated in wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
