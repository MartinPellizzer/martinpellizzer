Cyperus rigens var. weberbaueri

Cyperus rigens var. weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and pond edges, where it thrives in moist soil conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often subtended by bracts.

This variant is distinguished by its specific morphological traits, making it a notable species within the Cyperus genus.
