Culcitium pavonii

Culcitium pavonii, commonly known as the peacock orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble a peacock's tail. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as tropical forests. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and the flowers bloom in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Culcitium pavonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
