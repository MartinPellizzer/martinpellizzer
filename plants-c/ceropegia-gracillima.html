Ceropegia gracillima

Ceropegia gracillima, commonly known as the slender milkweed or spider plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a unique structure that resembles a spider's web. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia gracillima is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
