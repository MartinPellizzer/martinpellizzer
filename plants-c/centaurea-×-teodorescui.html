Centaurea × teodorescui

Centaurea × teodorescui, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea × teodorescui is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in various soil conditions.
