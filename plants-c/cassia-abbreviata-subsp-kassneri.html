Cassia abbreviata subsp. kassneri

Cassia abbreviata subsp.

kassneri, commonly known as the Kassner's cassia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats. Cassia abbreviata subsp.

kassneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
