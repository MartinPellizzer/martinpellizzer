Cyperus flabellaris

Cyperus flabellaris, commonly known as the flatleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

Cyperus flabellaris is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various wetland environments.
