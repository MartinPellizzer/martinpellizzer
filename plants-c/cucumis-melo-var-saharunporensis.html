Cucumis melo var. saharunporensis

Cucumis melo var. saharunporensis, commonly known as the Saharanpore melon, is a variant of the cantaloupe belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is typically grown in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for both local consumption and commercial sale.
