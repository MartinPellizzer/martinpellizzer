Capparis trinervia var. chungiana

Capparis trinervia var. chungiana, commonly known as the Chung's caper, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with three main veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered together, followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique flavor, it is occasionally cultivated for both garden use and culinary purposes.
