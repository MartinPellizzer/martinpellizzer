Columnea tomentulosa var. tulae

Columnea tomentulosa var. tulae, commonly known as the Tulae Shrub, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species in its native ecosystem.
