Cymbidium acuminatum

Cymbidium acuminatum, commonly known as the acute-angled orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae. The plant features upright, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

Cymbidium acuminatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
