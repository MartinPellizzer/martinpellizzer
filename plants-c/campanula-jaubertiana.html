Campanula jaubertiana

Campanula jaubertiana, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Campanula jaubertiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
