Crotalaria meyeriana

Crotalaria meyeriana, commonly known as Meyer's crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are palmately compound, and it produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes.

The seeds are enclosed in hairy, elongated pods and are dispersed by wind or animals.
