Cistanthe amarantoides

Cistanthe amarantoides, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, adding visual interest to its desert habitat. Its leaves are typically fleshy and gray-green, adapted to store water in harsh environments.

Cistanthe amarantoides is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
