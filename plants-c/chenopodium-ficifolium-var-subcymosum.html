Chenopodium ficifolium var. subcymosum

Chenopodium ficifolium var. subcymosum, commonly known as the clustered goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense, compact clusters, which give it its "subcymosum" variety name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
