Caprifolium adenocarpum

Caprifolium adenocarpum, commonly known as the common honeysuckle.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia, characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Caprifolium adenocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
