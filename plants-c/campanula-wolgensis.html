Campanula wolgensis

Campanula wolgensis, commonly known as the Wolgensis bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula wolgensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to rocky or arid environments.
