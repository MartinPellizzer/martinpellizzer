Convolvulus fulgens

Convolvulus fulgens, commonly known as the golden weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 30 centimeters tall, featuring hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
