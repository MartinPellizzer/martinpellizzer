Cynoglossum latifolium

Cynoglossum latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments like woodlands and stream banks.

Its distinctive foliage and delicate flowers make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
