Crataegus dissona var. bellula

Crataegus dissona var. bellula, commonly known as the bellflower hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white or pink flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a glossy green surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
