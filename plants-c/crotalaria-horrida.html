Crotalaria horrida

Crotalaria horrida, commonly known as the spiked crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny seed pods and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Crotalaria horrida is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid conditions.
