Carpodinus nigerina

Carpodinus nigerina, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are typically white or pale green, with five petals and a distinctive tubular shape.
