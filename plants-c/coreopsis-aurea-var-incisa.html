Coreopsis aurea var. incisa

Coreopsis aurea var. incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and deeply lobed, palmately cut leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
