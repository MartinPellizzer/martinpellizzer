Convolvulus tuberosus

Convolvulus tuberosus, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces tuberous roots that store nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, alternate leaves, and it blooms throughout the growing season.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
