Clematis pimpinellifolia var. pseudoscabiosifolia

Clematis pimpinellifolia var. pseudoscabiosifolia, commonly known as the creeping clematis, is a perennial vine native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with finely toothed edges.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for garden borders or as a ground cover.
