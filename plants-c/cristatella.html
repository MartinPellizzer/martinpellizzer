Cristatella

Cristatella, commonly known as the crown fern, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crown-like arrangement of leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically features a short, stout rhizome from which the leaves and flowers emerge. Its flowers are usually small and clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a colorful spathe.

Cristatella species are often found in moist, tropical or subtropical regions and are valued for their ornamental appeal.
