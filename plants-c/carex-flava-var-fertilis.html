Carex flava var. fertilis

Carex flava var. fertilis, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex flava var. fertilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
