Cephalotaxus sinensis var. wilsoniana

Cephalotaxus sinensis var. wilsoniana, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a deciduous conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive, scale-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental planting in gardens.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate urban conditions.
