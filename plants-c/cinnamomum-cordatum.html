Cinnamomum cordatum

Cinnamomum cordatum, commonly known as the heart-shaped cinnamon or the Malabar cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its elliptical, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by red or orange berries containing seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its aromatic properties.
