Cestrum rigidifolium

Cestrum rigidifolium, commonly known as the rigid-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white, yellow, or red. The plant has a woody stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
