Carex glaucescens var. polystachya

Carex glaucescens var. polystachya, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish coating. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
