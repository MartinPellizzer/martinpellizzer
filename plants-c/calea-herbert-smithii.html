Calea herbert-smithii

Calea herbert-smithii, commonly known as the Herbert-Smith's calea, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spiky flowers that range from red to orange, often with a distinctive central tuft of filaments. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a subtle aromatic scent when crushed.

Calea herbert-smithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
