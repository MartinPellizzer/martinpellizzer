Clinopodium cirenianum

Clinopodium cirenianum, commonly known as the Sierra basil or mountain basil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at higher elevations. Its small, white or pinkish flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild aromatic properties.
