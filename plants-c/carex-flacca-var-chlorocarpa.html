Carex flacca var. chlorocarpa

Carex flacca var. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green thread sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly curved. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually clustered at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic and riparian habitats.
