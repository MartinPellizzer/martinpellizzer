Camellia theifera var. macrophylla

Camellia theifera var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf camellia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in winter and early spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.

The leaves are evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in gardens.
