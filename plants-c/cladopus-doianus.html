Cladopus doianus

Cladopus doianus, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting well to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure to its habitat.

Cladopus doianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
