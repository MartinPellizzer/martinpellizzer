Citronella peruviana

Citronella peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian citronella, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive citrus-like scent, which is attributed to the presence of citronella oil.

This species is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and ornamental value.
