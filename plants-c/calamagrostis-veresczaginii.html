Calamagrostis veresczaginii

Calamagrostis veresczaginii, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, ditches, and stream banks. The plant has tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
