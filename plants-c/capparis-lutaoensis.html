Capparis lutaoensis

Capparis lutaoensis, commonly known as the Lutao prickly currant, is a flowering plant native to the island of Lutao in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which includes various shrubs and herbs characterized by their distinctive flowers and often thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and its leaves are simple, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Capparis lutaoensis is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
