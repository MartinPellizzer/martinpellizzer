Chamaecrista densifolia

Chamaecrista densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its dense foliage and is often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Chamaecrista densifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
