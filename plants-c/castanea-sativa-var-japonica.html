Castanea sativa var. japonica

Castanea sativa var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces spiny, spherical fruits called nuts, which enclose edible seeds. These nuts are typically harvested in the fall and are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a food source in its native habitat.
