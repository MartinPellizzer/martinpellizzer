Carex curvicollis

Carex curvicollis, commonly known as narrow-necked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Carex curvicollis has small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikes, and it plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for wetland health.
