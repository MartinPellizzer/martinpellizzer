Cyphia deltoidea

Cyphia deltoidea, commonly known as the delta sponge cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its unique, spongy, and segmented stems. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom during the dry season. It is adapted to survive in harsh, low-water environments with thick, fleshy stems that store moisture.

Cyphia deltoidea is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
