Cacalia penduliflora

Cacalia penduliflora, commonly known as the hangingflower or pendulous bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, wiry stems that often trail or hang from the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, arranged alternately along the stems.

Cacalia penduliflora thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
