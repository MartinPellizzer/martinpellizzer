Convolvulus neei

Convolvulus neei, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
