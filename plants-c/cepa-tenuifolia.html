Cepa tenuifolia

Cepa tenuifolia, commonly known as slender-leaved onion, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible bulbs.

Cepa tenuifolia prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
