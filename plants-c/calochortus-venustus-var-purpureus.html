Calochortus venustus var. purpureus

Calochortus venustus var. purpureus, commonly known as the purple glory-of-the-snow, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to California.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from white to deep purple, often with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats, such as chaparral and coastal scrub. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
