Centaurea bagadensis

Centaurea bagadensis, commonly known as the Bagadensian thistle, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces bracts that form a spiny, leaf-like structure surrounding the flower cluster. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Centaurea bagadensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
