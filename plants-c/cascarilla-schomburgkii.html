Cascarilla schomburgkii

Cascarilla schomburgkii, commonly known as the yellow wood or yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is often cultivated for its timber and essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
