Clematis odora

Clematis odora, commonly known as the sweet clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to seven leaflets, and it typically grows as a vine or shrub. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis odora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.
