Caina erratica

Caina erratica, commonly known as the wandering cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a branching structure with spiny stems that help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces small, vibrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the dry season. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Caina erratica is adapted to thrive in extreme conditions, making it a resilient and fascinating example of desert flora.
