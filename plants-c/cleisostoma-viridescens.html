Cleisostoma viridescens

Cleisostoma viridescens, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. Its leaves are usually ovate and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating efficient water retention.

Cleisostoma viridescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
