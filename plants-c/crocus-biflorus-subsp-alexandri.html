Crocus biflorus subsp. alexandri

Crocus biflorus subsp.

alexandri, commonly known as Alexandrian crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, globe-shaped bulbs that help it spread vegetatively. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
