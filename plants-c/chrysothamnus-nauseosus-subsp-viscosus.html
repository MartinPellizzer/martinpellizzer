Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp. viscosus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus subsp.

viscosus, commonly known as sticky goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and glandular, sticky leaves that give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage contains aromatic compounds that contribute to its distinctive scent.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native pollinators.
