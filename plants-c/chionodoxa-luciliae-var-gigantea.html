Chionodoxa luciliae var. gigantea

Chionodoxa luciliae var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant snowdrops, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Turkey.

It produces delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its robust, thick bulbs that store energy for growth. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in some regions.
