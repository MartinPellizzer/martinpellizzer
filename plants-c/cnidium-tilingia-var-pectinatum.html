Cnidium tilingia var. pectinatum

Cnidium tilingia var. pectinatum, commonly known as the hairy cnidium, is a variant of the cnidium plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, aromatic seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley.
