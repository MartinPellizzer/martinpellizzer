Croton elliotianus

Croton elliotianus, commonly known as Elliot's croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, palmate leaves. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
