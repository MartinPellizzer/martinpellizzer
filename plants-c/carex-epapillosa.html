Carex epapillosa

Carex epapillosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering structures consist of tiny, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense clusters.

Carex epapillosa plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
