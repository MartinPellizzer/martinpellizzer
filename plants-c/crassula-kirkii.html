Crassula kirkii

Crassula kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Crassula kirkii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth and aesthetic appeal.
