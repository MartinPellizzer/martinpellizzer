Cicer floribundum var. amanicola

Cicer floribundum var. amanicola, commonly known as the Armenian vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, narrow pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or arid soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally used in traditional agriculture or as a forage plant.
