Cattleya amabilis

Cattleya amabilis, commonly known as the fragrant cattleya, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often arranged in two rows along the stem.

Cattleya amabilis is prized for its strong fragrance and is a popular choice for both indoor cultivation and botanical gardens.
