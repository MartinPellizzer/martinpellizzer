Chrysoglossum chloranthum

Chrysoglossum chloranthum, commonly known as the golden shield, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, shield-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white waxy coating.

Chrysoglossum chloranthum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
