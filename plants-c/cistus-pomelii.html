Cistus pomelii

Cistus pomelii, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.

Cistus pomelii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
