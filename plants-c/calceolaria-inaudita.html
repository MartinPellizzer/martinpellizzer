Calceolaria inaudita

Calceolaria inaudita, commonly known as the silent bellflower, is a striking alpine plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to violet, often with intricate patterns. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that help it retain moisture in its high-altitude environment. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist conditions.

Calceolaria inaudita is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
