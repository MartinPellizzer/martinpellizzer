Cucubalus wolgensis

Cucubalus wolgensis, commonly known as the Wolgensis cucumber, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces long, slender fruits that are typically harvested when immature for their edible, crisp texture. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is valued both for its culinary use and its ornamental appeal.
