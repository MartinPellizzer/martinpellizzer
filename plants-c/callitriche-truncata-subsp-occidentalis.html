Callitriche truncata subsp. occidentalis

Callitriche truncata subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western water-starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to North America. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and wetlands.

The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is often found in still or slow-moving water, contributing to aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen.
