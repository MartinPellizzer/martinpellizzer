Ceratophyllum muticum

Ceratophyllum muticum, commonly known as the spiked watermilfoil, is a freshwater plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces tiny, floating flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in marshy or aquatic environments.

Ceratophyllum muticum is an important species for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
