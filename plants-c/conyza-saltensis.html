Conyza saltensis

Conyza saltensis, commonly known as saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, white to yellowish flowers and hairy, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant is often found in saline soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions. Its structure includes a basal rosette of leaves with upright flowering stems.

Conyza saltensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
