Cynanchum sauvallei

Cynanchum sauvallei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Cynanchum sauvallei is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It is considered a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
