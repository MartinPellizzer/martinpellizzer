Cereus setaceus var. viridior

Cereus setaceus var. viridior, commonly known as the green cereus, is a cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom at night, typically during the warmer months. Its foliage consists of sharp, spiny leaves that are often shed as the plant matures.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.
