Ceratostylis alberteduardi

Ceratostylis alberteduardi, commonly known as the Albert Eduardo's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its inflorescence consists of a single raceme bearing several minute flowers, which are usually clustered near the base of the stem.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and is of interest to orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
