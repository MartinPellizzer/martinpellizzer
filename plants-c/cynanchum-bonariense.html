Cynanchum bonariense

Cynanchum bonariense, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny seed pods. It is known for its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds, and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for certain insect species.
