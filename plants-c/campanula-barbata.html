Campanula barbata

Campanula barbata, commonly known as the beard bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has hairy stems and leaves. Its scientific name refers to the hairy appearance of its stems, which resemble a beard.

Campanula barbata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
