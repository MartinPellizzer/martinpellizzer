Chlorophytum longiscapum

Chlorophytum longiscapum, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a hardy, evergreen perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or yellow variegation, and it produces long, slender flower stalks that bear small white or greenish flowers. The plant is well adapted to low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it an ideal houseplant.

Its ability to purify indoor air and tolerate neglect further enhances its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
