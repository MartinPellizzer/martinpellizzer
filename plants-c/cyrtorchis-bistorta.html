Cyrtorchis bistorta

Cyrtorchis bistorta, commonly known as the mountain bistort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers in early summer, arranged in dense clusters at the top of its flowering stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in specialized gardens.
