Cissus oblonga

Cissus oblonga, commonly known as the rock fig or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are popular in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus oblonga is also valued for its ability to grow on rocky surfaces, making it a hardy and versatile plant.
