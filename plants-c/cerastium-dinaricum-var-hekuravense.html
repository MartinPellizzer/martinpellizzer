Cerastium dinaricum var. hekuravense

Cerastium dinaricum var. hekuravense, commonly known as the Dinaric mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
