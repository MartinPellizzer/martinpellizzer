Cissus emarginella

Cissus emarginella, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for trellises and vertical gardens.
