Calorophus minor

Calorophus minor, commonly known as the lesser candleplant, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wastelands.

Calorophus minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
