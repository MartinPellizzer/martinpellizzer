Carex pallescens var. subsilvatica

Carex pallescens var. subsilvatica, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist forested areas of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, wet environments such as stream banks and swamps, where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils. Its pale green to silver-gray foliage gives it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
