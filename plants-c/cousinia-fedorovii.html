Cousinia fedorovii

Cousinia fedorovii, commonly known as the Fedorov's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia fedorovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
