Celianella montana

Celianella montana, commonly known as the mountain cranberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and evergreen leaves. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. Its fruit, a small, red, berry-like structure, is edible and has a tart flavor.

Celianella montana plays an important role in stabilizing soil in alpine environments and supports local wildlife.
