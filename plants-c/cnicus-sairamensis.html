Cnicus sairamensis

Cnicus sairamensis, commonly known as the Sairam thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Indian state of Telangana.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a compact head.

Due to its limited distribution and unique ecological role, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
