Cardamine raphanifolia

Cardamine raphanifolia, commonly known as the turnip-rooted watercress, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features delicate, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. Its small white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early summer.

The roots are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to that of watercress.
