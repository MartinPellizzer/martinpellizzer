Crataegus congestiflora

Crataegus congestiflora, commonly known as the denseflower hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red to black berries in the fall, which are a food source for various wildlife species. Crataegus congestiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
