Chlorophytum caudatibracteatum

Chlorophytum caudatibracteatum, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or yellow variegation. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing small, white flowers, which are followed by distinctive bracts. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Its ability to purify indoor air makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
