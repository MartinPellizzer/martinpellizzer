Cynanchica Aristata Subsp. Nestia

Arrowleaf milkweed, scientifically known as Cynanchica aristata subsp. nestia, is a striking native plant found in the arid regions of the southwestern United States. This species is notable for its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It plays a vital role in supporting local pollinators, particularly monarch butterflies, by providing both nectar and a host plant for their larvae. Despite its ecological importance, it is often overlooked due to its modest appearance and limited distribution. Its resilience in harsh environments makes it a fascinating subject for botanists and conservationists alike.
