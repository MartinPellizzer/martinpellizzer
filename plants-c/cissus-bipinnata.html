Cissus bipinnata

Cissus bipinnata, commonly known as the bone vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and compound leaves that are deeply divided into narrow, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that are a striking feature of the species. Cissus bipinnata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
