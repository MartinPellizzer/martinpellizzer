Cremanthodium bomiense

Cremanthodium bomiense, commonly known as the Bomi sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, demonstrating remarkable adaptability to cold and arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in mountainous ecosystems.
