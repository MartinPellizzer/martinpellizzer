Cirsium setschwanicum

Cirsium setschwanicum, commonly known as the Sichuan thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cooler climates. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a rough, serrated edge.

Cirsium setschwanicum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
