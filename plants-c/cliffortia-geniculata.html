Cliffortia geniculata

Cliffortia geniculata, commonly known as the mountain blushing bush, is a South African species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

Cliffortia geniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
