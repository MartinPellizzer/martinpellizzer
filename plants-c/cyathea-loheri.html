Cyathea loheri

Cyathea loheri, commonly known as the Loher's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, providing structural support and enabling it to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Cyathea loheri plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
