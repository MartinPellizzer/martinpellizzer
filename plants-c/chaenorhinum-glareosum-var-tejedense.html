Chaenorhinum glareosum var. tejedense

Chaenorhinum glareosum var. tejedense, commonly known as the Tejedense Cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
