Cirsium pseudobracteosum

Cirsium pseudobracteosum, commonly known as the false bract thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a robust stem covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, waxy texture, while its flowering heads are composed of numerous small florets.

This species is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
