Calceolaria arachnoidea var. lanata

Calceolaria arachnoidea var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly slipper orchid, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers that resemble tiny slippers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features dense, woolly hairs on its stems and leaves, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and delicate beauty, it is a sought-after plant among horticulturists and botanical enthusiasts.
