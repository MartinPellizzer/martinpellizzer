Chenopodium ambrosioides var. oblanceolatum

Chenopodium ambrosioides var. oblanceolatum, commonly known as pigweed, is a flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate to oblanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It is native to the Americas and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

This species is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
