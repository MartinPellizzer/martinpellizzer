Cactus humilis

Cactus humilis, commonly known as the low cholla, is a small, spiny cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh desert conditions. The plant features dense clusters of cylindrical stems covered in white, hair-like spines that provide insulation and protection. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, blooming in the spring, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible for some wildlife.

Cactus humilis plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert animals.
