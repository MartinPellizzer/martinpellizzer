Cyananthus insignis

Cyananthus insignis, commonly known as the blue bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and upright.

Cyananthus insignis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
