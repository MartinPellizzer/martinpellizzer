Chamaeraphis corrugata

Chamaeraphis corrugata, commonly known as the zigzag glassworts, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a halophyte, meaning it thrives in saline environments such as coastal areas and salt marshes. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and have a zigzag appearance. Its small, green flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round seeds.

Chamaeraphis corrugata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
