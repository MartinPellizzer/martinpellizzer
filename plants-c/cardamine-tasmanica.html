Cardamine tasmanica

Cardamine tasmanica, commonly known as Tasmanian watercress, is a small herbaceous plant native to Tasmania and parts of southern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant has trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in a raceme, and it produces elongated, cylindrical fruits.

The plant is valued for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in local cuisine or as a ornamental plant.
