Crataegus leeana

Crataegus leeana, commonly known as the Leeana hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

Crataegus leeana is often found in woodland edges and stream banks, contributing to local biodiversity.
