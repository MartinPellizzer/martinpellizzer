Cheilanthes fragrans subsp. maderensis

Cheilanthes fragrans subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as the fragrant rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and in arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually glabrous and have a faintly fragrant scent when crushed.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
