Cheirodendron trigynum var. fosbergii

Cheirodendron trigynum var. fosbergii, commonly known as the Fosberg's three-lobed evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, trifoliate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its unique leaf structure and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
