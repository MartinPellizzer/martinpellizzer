Campomanesia rufa

Campomanesia rufa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in its native habitat.
