Clematis japonica var. villosula

Clematis japonica var. villosula, commonly known as the Japanese Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets that are hairy, giving it its varietal name "villosula.". The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or lilac. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden trellises or as a backdrop plant.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
