Clematis mae

Clematis mae, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to scale supports. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, lobed leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun.

Clematis mae is relatively hardy and can be a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
