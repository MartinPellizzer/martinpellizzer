Condalia retusa

Condalia retusa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
