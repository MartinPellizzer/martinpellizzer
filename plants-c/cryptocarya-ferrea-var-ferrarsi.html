Cryptocarya ferrea var. ferrarsi

Cryptocarya ferrea var. ferrarsi, commonly known as ironwood or Brazilian ironwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a fruit that is a dark, woody capsule containing several seeds.
