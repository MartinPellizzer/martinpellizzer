Cajanus scarabaeoides var. argyrophyllus

Cajanus scarabaeoides var. argyrophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leaf groundnut, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
