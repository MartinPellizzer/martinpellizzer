Calophyllum lanceola

Calophyllum lanceola, commonly known as the lanceleaf calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces woody, fragrant fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a hard, leathery capsule. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
