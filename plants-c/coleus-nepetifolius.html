Coleus nepetifolius

Coleus nepetifolius, commonly known as the lemon-scented coleus, is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue to purple in color.

Native to Southeast Asia, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.
