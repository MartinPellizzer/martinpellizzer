Catasetum ricii

Catasetum ricii, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like flowers that are often brightly colored and intricately structured. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated by specific species of bees, which are attracted by the plant's nectar and specialized structures.

Catasetum ricii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
