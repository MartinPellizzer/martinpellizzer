Chondrilla mattfeldii

Chondrilla mattfeldii, commonly known as the desert hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its yellow, dandelion-like flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage adapted to withstand harsh desert conditions. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and supporting local wildlife.
