Crataegus reverchonii var. palmeri

Crataegus reverchonii var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a red or orange berry that ripens in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species. This plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
