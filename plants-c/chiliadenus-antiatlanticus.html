Chiliadenus antiatlanticus

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to the arid and saline conditions of its habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically in late spring or early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Chiliadenus antiatlanticus is considered vulnerable and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme environments.
