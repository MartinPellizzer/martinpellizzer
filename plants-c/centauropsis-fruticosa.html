Centauropsis fruticosa

Centauropsis fruticosa, commonly known as the golden bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, branched structure and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
