Calliandra surinamensis

Calliandra surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam calliandra, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Suriname and neighboring regions. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that produce vibrant, papery flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Calliandra surinamensis is often used in ornamental horticulture for its showy blooms and is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, which enrich the soil.
