Cytisus glabrescens

Cytisus glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Cytisus glabrescens is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and scrublands.
