Carum sylvestre

Carum sylvestre, commonly known as wild caraway, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its seeds, which are oval and aromatic, are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Carum sylvestre thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
