Cardamine anemonoides f. suavis

Cardamine anemonoides f. suavis, commonly known as sweet water shield, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize moist soils effectively.

The species is notable for its aromatic foliage, which contributes to its common name.
