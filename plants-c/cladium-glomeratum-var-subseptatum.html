Cladium glomeratum var. subseptatum

Cladium glomeratum var. subseptatum, commonly known as the swamp cordgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically found in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. The plant has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in marshy areas. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, often with a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

Cladium glomeratum var. subseptatum plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
