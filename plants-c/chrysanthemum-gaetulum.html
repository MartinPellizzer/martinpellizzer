Chrysanthemum gaetulum

Chrysanthemum gaetulum, commonly known as the Gaetulan chrysanthemum, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
