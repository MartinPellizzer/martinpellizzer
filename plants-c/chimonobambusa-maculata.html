Chimonobambusa maculata

Chimonobambusa maculata, commonly known as the spotted bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hollow stems with a distinctive spotted or striped appearance on the surface. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, providing a dense foliage that contributes to its ornamental value.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its unique aesthetic and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative element in gardens.
