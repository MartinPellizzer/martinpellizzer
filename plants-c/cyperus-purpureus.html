Cyperus purpureus

Cyperus purpureus, commonly known as purple flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, making it a common sight in marshy environments.

Cyperus purpureus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive purple flower spikes.
