Cirrhopetalum ochraceum

Cirrhopetalum ochraceum, commonly known as the golden petal orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

The flowers are borne in clusters and have a unique, wavy petal structure that distinguishes it from other orchid species.
