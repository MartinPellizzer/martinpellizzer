Cynanchum racemosum var. cyathiforme

Cynanchum racemosum var. cyathiforme, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Chinese milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by follicles that contain the seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
