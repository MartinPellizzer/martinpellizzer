Cestrum virgaurea

Cestrum virgaurea, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its showy blooms attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and full sun conditions.
