Clinopodium pulchellum

Clinopodium pulchellum, commonly known as sweetwood or purple false penny-cress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, purple or pink flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, rounded seeds.

Clinopodium pulchellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
