Crataegus × kupfferi

Crataegus × kupfferi, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but typically used in jams and wines. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in temperate climates.

Crataegus × kupfferi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
