Charybdis undulata

Charybdis undulata, commonly known as the zigzag sea anemone, is a marine cnidarian found in tropical and subtropical waters.

It belongs to the order Actinioidea and is characterized by its distinctive, undulating oral disc. The polyp has a short, flexible body with long, flexible tentacles that are used for capturing prey. Its coloration can vary, often displaying shades of yellow, orange, and brown.

This species plays a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to the biodiversity of coral reef environments.
