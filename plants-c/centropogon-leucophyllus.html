Centropogon leucophyllus

Centropogon leucophyllus, commonly known as the white-leaf centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
