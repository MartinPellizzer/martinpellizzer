Crassula lasiantha

Crassula lasiantha, commonly known as the silver leaf crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hair, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Crassula lasiantha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
