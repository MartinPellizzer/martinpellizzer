Cedrus deodara f. robusta

Cedrus deodara f. robusta, commonly known as the Deodar cedar, is a cultivar of the Himalayan cedar, characterized by its dense, coniferous foliage and tall, pyramidal growth habit.

It is native to the western Himalayas and is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant produces small, blue-green needles that are shed annually, and its cones are typically reddish-brown and contain winged seeds. It is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

This variety is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
