Ceradenia microcystis

Ceradenia microcystis, commonly known as the small-cyst orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, leafy structure and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a faint fragrance, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
