Centropogon vulpinus

Centropogon vulpinus, commonly known as the firecracker flower, is a tropical plant native to regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces striking red-orange flowers that resemble small fireworks, hence its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Centropogon vulpinus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
