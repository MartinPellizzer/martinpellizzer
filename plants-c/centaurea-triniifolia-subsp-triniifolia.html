Centaurea triniifolia subsp. triniifolia

Centaurea triniifolia subsp.

triniifolia, commonly known as the Three-feathered Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
