Cheiranthus × cheiri var. grandiflorus

Cheiranthus × cheiri var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the sweet rocket, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from purple to white, typically appearing in clusters. The plant is native to southern Europe and has been widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its foliage is lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
