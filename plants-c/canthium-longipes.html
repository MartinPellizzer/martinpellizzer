Canthium longipes

Canthium longipes, commonly known as the long-fruited spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
