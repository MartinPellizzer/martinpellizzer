Commelina badamica

Commelina badamica, commonly known as the Indian spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are upright and branched.

Commelina badamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
