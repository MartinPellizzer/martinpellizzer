Crepidium sundaicum

Crepidium sundaicum, commonly known as the Sunda marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is typically found in wetland and swampy habitats. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It has a rhizomatous root system and is adapted to waterlogged soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
