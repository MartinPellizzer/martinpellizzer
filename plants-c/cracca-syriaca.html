Cracca syriaca

Cracca syriaca, commonly known as Syrian cracca, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant has pinnately divided leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and horticultural interest.
