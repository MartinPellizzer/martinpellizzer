Crotalaria nogalensis

Crotalaria nogalensis, commonly known as the Nogal shrub, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod-bearing fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
