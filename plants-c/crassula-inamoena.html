Crassula inamoena

Crassula inamoena, commonly known as the "Coral Bells" or "Lamb's Ear," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the spring or summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Crassula inamoena is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
