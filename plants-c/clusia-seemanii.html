Clusia seemanii

Clusia seemanii, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring thick, waxy leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Clusia seemanii is known for its ability to thrive in coastal areas and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
