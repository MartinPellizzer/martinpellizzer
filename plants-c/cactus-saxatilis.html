Cactus saxatilis

Cactus saxatilis, commonly known as the stone cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, showcasing a unique adaptation to harsh desert environments. The plant features cylindrical stems covered in spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. Its flowers, often yellow or orange, appear in late spring and are an important food source for pollinators.

Overall, Cactus saxatilis exemplifies the resilience and specialized morphology of desert flora.
