Chironia maxima

Chironia maxima, commonly known as the giant water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has broad, waxy leaves that are usually dark green with a bluish tint, and it produces showy purple or pink flowers. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense mats on the water surface. Its rapid growth rate can lead to waterway clogging, making it an invasive species in many areas.

Chironia maxima plays a role in water purification by absorbing nutrients, but its ecological impact is often negative.
