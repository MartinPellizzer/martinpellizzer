Carex divisa var. chaetophylla

Carex divisa var. chaetophylla, commonly known as the hairy-fronded sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

The variety 'chaetophylla' is distinguished by its hairy or pubescent leaf blades, which give it a unique texture and appearance.
