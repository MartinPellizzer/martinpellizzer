Commelina demissa

Commelina demissa, commonly known as Asiatic dayflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a prostrate or ascending stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers with three petals and a distinctive three-lobed spatulate bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Commelina demissa is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
