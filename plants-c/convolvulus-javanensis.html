Convolvulus javanensis

Convolvulus javanensis, commonly known as the Java morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers bloom in the morning, hence its common name, and are pollinated by insects.
