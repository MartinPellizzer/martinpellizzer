Collomia linearis var. picta

Collomia linearis var. picta, commonly known as the painted larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

The variety 'picta' is distinguished by its vivid, contrasting coloration, making it a striking addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
