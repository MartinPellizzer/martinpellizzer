Coriandrum

Coriandrum, commonly known as cilantro, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant produces small white flowers and has feathery, delicate leaves that are often used in cooking. Its seeds, known as coriander, are widely used as a spice in various cuisines around the world.

The botanical features of coriandrum make it a valuable plant for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
