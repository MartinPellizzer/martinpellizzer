Clematitis erecta

Clematitis erecta, commonly known as the upright clematis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are often ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually blue or violet in color, with five petals and prominent stamens.

Clematitis erecta thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
