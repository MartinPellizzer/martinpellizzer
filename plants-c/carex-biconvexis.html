Carex biconvexis

Carex biconvexis, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant features broad, flat leaves that are often arranged in a dense, grassy clump. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wetlands and marshes, and is often found in areas with poor to moderately fertile soils.

Carex biconvexis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
