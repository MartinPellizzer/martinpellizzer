Chaetocyperus obtusatus

Chaetocyperus obtusatus, commonly known as the bluntleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial grass-like plant that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, a characteristic common in many Cyperaceae species.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
