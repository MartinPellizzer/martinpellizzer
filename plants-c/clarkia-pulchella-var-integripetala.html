Clarkia pulchella var. integripetala

Clarkia pulchella var. integripetala, commonly known as the Sierra clarkia, is a flowering plant native to California and parts of Nevada.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is a member of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
