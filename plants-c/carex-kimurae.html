Carex kimurae

Carex kimurae, commonly known as the Kimura's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or shaded forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
