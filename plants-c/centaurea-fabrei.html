Centaurea fabrei

Centaurea fabrei, commonly known as the Fabre's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its bright blue to violet flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Centaurea fabrei is known for its hardy nature and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
