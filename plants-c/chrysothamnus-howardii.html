Chrysothamnus howardii

Chrysothamnus howardii, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has silvery-gray leaves and a sprawling growth habit, making it well adapted to arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert and sagebrush habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
