Campanula sibirica var. minor

Campanula sibirica var. minor, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the colder regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early flowering and ability to attract pollinators.
