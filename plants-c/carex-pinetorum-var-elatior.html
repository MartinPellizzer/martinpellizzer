Carex pinetorum var. elatior

Carex pinetorum var. elatior, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, narrow leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers in the summer. This variety is distinguished by its taller growth habit compared to other Carex species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
