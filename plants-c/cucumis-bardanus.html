Cucumis bardanus

Cucumis bardanus, commonly known as the wild cucumber, is a species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in warm, sunny environments.

Cucumis bardanus is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
