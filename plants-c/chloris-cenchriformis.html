Chloris cenchriformis

Chloris cenchriformis, commonly known as the spear grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils.

Chloris cenchriformis is adapted to withstand drought and is an important component of native ecosystems.
