Chimonocalamus griffithianus

Chimonocalamus griffithianus, commonly known as the Griffith's bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
