Caraipa paraensis var. robusta

Caraipa paraensis var. robusta, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the wet season. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
