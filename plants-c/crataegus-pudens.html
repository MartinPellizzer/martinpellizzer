Crataegus pudens

Crataegus pudens, commonly known as the Downy Hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn bright red in the fall.

Crataegus pudens is often found in moist woodlands and thrives in partial to full sunlight.
