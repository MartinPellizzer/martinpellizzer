Calamagrostis nana subsp. hayachinensis

Calamagrostis nana subsp.

hayachinensis, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf reed, is a compact, clump-forming grass native to Japan. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a fine texture.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate ornamental quality. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
