Commelina nigritana subsp. aggregata

Commelina nigritana subsp.

aggregata, commonly known as the clustered commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and lanceolate leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers with three blue or purple petals, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
