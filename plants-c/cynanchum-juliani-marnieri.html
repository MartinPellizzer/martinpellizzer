Cynanchum juliani-marnieri

Cynanchum juliani-marnieri, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed or the blue milkweed, is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Korean Peninsula. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its milky sap, which is characteristic of the genus, contains toxic compounds and is used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
