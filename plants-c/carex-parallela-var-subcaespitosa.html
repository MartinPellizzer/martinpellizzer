Carex parallela var. subcaespitosa

Carex parallela var. subcaespitosa, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it a useful plant for erosion control in wetland habitats.
