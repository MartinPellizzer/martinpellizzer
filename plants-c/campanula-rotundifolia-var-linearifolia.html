Campanula rotundifolia var. linearifolia

Campanula rotundifolia var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
