Calamus semoi

Calamus semoi, commonly known as the Semoi calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a tall, robust plant that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its long, narrow, and often aromatic leaves. The plant produces a spadix, which is a type of inflorescence typical of the Aroid family, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Calamus semoi is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps, where it thrives in humid conditions.
