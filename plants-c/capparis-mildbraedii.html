Capparis mildbraedii

Capparis mildbraedii, commonly known as the African sour fig, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its pungent, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible but have a strong, sour taste. Its leaves are usually small, oval, and have a rough texture.

Capparis mildbraedii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
