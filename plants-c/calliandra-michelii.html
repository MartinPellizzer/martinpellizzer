Calliandra michelii

Calliandra michelii, commonly known as the Mexican coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking pink or red floral clusters that resemble coral. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant flowers.

Calliandra michelii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
