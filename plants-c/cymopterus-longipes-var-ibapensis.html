Cymopterus longipes var. ibapensis

Cymopterus longipes var. ibapensis, commonly known as the desert lupine, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is distinguished by its unique floral structure and ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
