Cerradicola bullata var. magnifolia

Cerradicola bullata var. magnifolia, commonly known as the magnolia bellflower, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive bell-shaped structure. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage consists of broad, glossy leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its unique appearance and fragrance.
