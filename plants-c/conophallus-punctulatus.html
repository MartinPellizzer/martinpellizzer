Conophallus punctulatus

Conophallus punctulatus, commonly known as the spotted conophallus, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that are often adorned with distinctive spots or markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as tropical rainforests, where it relies on specific symbiotic relationships with fungi. Its leaves are usually broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its humid habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from deforestation and habitat loss.
