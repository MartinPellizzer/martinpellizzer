Carex atrata var. laxa

Carex atrata var. laxa, commonly known as the looseleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
