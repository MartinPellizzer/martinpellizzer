Campanula micrantha

Campanula micrantha, commonly known as the small bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its root system is fibrous and rhizomatous.

Campanula micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
