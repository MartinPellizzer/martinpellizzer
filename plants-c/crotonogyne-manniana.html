Crotonogyne manniana

Crotonogyne manniana, commonly known as the Malayan croton, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves with a distinctive greenish-white coloration. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
