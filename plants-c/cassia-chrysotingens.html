Cassia chrysotingens

Cassia chrysotingens, commonly known as golden cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
