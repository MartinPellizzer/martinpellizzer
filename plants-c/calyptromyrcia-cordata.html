Calyptromyrcia cordata

Calyptromyrcia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf calyptromyrcia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known genera such as Eucalyptus and Myrtus. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
