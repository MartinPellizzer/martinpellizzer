Campanula ledebourii

Campanula ledebourii, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of Namibia and South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky or limestone habitats, where it forms dense clusters.

Campanula ledebourii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens with good drainage and full sun.
