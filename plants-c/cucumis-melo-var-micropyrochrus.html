Cucumis melo var. micropyrochrus

Cucumis melo var. micropyrochrus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the melon genus.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits with a thin, crisp rind and a sweet, mild flavor. The plant typically grows as a vine, producing yellow flowers that develop into the edible fruit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its use in both fresh consumption and culinary applications.
