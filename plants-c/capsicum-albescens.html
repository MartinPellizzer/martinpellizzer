Capsicum albescens

Capsicum albescens, commonly known as the white bell pepper.

It is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae, native to the tropical regions of South America. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Capsicum albescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has limited use in culinary applications compared to other pepper varieties.
