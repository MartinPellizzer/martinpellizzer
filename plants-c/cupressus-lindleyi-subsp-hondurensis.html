Cupressus lindleyi subsp. hondurensis

Cupressus lindleyi subsp.

hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras cypress, is a rare and endangered conifer native to a small region in Central America. It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and dense, scale-like foliage.

The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid, subtropical climate.

Due to habitat loss and limited natural range, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
