Chamaecrista vestita

Chamaecrista vestita, commonly known as the silverleaf crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its seeds are encased in slender, twisted pods that split open when mature.

Chamaecrista vestita is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
