Carex mixta var. gynobasis

Carex mixta var. gynobasis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
