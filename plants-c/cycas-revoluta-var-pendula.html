Cycas revoluta var. pendula

Cycas revoluta var. pendula, commonly known as the pendant cycas, is a unique variant of the cycad species Cycas revoluta.

It is characterized by its drooping, feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant is native to southern China and is often cultivated as an ornamental species due to its striking foliage. It has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-term addition to gardens.

The plant's cycad-like structure includes a stout trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves, reflecting its ancient lineage.
