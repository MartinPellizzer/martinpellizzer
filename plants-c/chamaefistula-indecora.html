Chamaefistula indecora

Chamaefistula indecora, commonly known as the common frangipani, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features opposite, simple, and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most striking feature is its fragrant, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
