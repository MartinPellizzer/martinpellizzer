Cedrus atlantica f. glauca

Cedrus atlantica f. glauca, commonly known as the Atlas cedar glauca, is a compact, slow-growing conifer with a broad, rounded crown.

It features scale-like leaves that are a distinctive blue-green color, giving it a striking appearance in gardens. The plant produces aromatic, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It is native to the Atlas Mountains of North Africa and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
