Centaurea × pouzinii nothovar. vulgaris

Centaurea × pouzinii nothovar. vulgaris, commonly known as the common centaury, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple composite flowers and spiny, bract-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is native to southern Europe and has been introduced to other regions as an ornamental.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
