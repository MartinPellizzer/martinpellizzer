Carex lenticularis var. albimontana

Carex lenticularis var. albimontana, commonly known as the white mountain sedge, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically light green to white in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
