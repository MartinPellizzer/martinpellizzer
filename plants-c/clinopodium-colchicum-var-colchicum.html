Clinopodium colchicum var. colchicum

Clinopodium colchicum var. colchicum, commonly known as purple deadnettle, is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
