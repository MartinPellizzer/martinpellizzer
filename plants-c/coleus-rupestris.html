Coleus rupestris

Coleus rupestris, commonly known as the rock coleus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its attractive leaves and compact growth habit.
