Carex divisa var. setifolia

Carex divisa var. setifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in hue. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
