Carex brachyrhyncha

Carex brachyrhyncha, commonly known as the short-beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

Carex brachyrhyncha is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
