Calceolaria petioalaris var. cuspidata

Calceolaria petioalaris var. cuspidata, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-shaped petals that resemble a small hood, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with partial shade. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the flowers are usually violet or purple in color.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and for its unique floral structure.
