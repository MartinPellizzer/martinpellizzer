Crepis nemorum

Crepis nemorum, commonly known as the wood cranesbill, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single head, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. Crepis nemorum is typically found in moist, shaded woodlands and is known for its ability to thrive in acidic soils.

It is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
