Chenopodium leptophyllum subsp. desiccatum

Chenopodium leptophyllum subsp.

desiccatum, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers.

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through mechanisms such as crassulacean acid metabolism (CAM), which allows it to conserve water. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and the plant can go dormant during extreme drought conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in desert ecosystems.
