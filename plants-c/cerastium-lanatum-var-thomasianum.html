Cerastium lanatum var. thomasianum

Cerastium lanatum var. thomasianum, commonly known as Thomas's hairy mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
