Cirsium andersonii

Cirsium andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and plains. It has a short stature, usually reaching heights of less than one meter.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
