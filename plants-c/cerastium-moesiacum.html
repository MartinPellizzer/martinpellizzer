Cerastium moesiacum

Cerastium moesiacum, commonly known as the Moesia chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, white flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are prostrate, often forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Cerastium moesiacum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
