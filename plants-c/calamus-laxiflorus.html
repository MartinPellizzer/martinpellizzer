Calamus laxiflorus

Calamus laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araucariaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines.

Calamus laxiflorus is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
