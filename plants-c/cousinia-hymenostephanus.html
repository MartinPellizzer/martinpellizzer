Cousinia hymenostephanus

Cousinia hymenostephanus, commonly known as the Kashmir Himalayan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blossoms. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The flowers, which are typically yellow or white, bloom in summer and attract various pollinators.
