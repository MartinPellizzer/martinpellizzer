Chailletia floribunda

Chailletia floribunda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in native and exotic flora.
