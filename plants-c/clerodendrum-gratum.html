Clerodendrum gratum

Clerodendrum gratum, commonly known as the sweet clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Clerodendrum gratum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
