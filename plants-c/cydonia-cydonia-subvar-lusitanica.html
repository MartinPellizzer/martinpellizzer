Cydonia cydonia subvar. lusitanica

Cydonia cydonia subvar. lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese quince, is a cultivar of the quince species native to southern Europe.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in the spring. The fruit is typically oval or pear-shaped, with a tough, russet-colored skin and a sweet, aromatic flesh when ripe. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in traditional dishes.
