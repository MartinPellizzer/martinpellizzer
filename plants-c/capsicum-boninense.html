Capsicum boninense

Capsicum boninense, commonly known as the Bonin chili, is a species of chili pepper native to the Bonin Islands.

It is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its small, elongated fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bilaterally symmetrical, typical of the Solanaceae family.

The fruit is usually red or orange when mature and is used in local cuisines for its mild to medium heat level.
