Cyathea lepidigera

Cyathea lepidigera, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, dark green fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in favorable conditions. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and enabling it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Cyathea lepidigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its unique, elegant appearance.
