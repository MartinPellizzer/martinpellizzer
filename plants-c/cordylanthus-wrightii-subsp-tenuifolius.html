Cordylanthus wrightii subsp. tenuifolius

Cordylanthus wrightii subsp.

tenuifolius, commonly known as the slenderleaf scrophularia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are pink to purple and arranged in dense spikes, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system to access moisture.
