Calliandra houstoniana

Calliandra houstoniana, commonly known as the Houstonia tree or the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like leaves and showy, bright orange or red floral clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is well-suited for tropical and subtropical regions.
