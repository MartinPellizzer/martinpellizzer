Cyperus hypopitys

Cyperus hypopitys, commonly known as the waterwheel plant, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus hypopitys is often found in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Its structure and growth habits make it both ecologically significant and aesthetically pleasing in wetland habitats.
