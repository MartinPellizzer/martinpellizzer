Cucurbita pepo var. fungiformis

Cucurbita pepo var. fungiformis, commonly known as the horned melon, is a tropical vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, green to yellowish fruit with a thick, waxy rind. The fruit contains a sweet, edible pulp covered in a layer of white, powdery coating called bloom. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are followed by the unique horn-shaped fruit.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flavor.
