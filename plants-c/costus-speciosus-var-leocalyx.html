Costus speciosus var. leocalyx

Costus speciosus var. leocalyx, commonly known as the golden costus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
