Cassia caesia

Cassia caesia, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia caesia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various climates.
