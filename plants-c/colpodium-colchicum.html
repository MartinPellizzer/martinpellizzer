Colpodium colchicum

Colpodium colchicum, commonly known as the autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in the fall, typically from September to November. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. Its flowers are composed of three petal-like sepals and three narrow stamens, with a central pistil.

The autumn crocus is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of colchicine, a compound used to treat gout and certain types of cancer.
