Chamira

Chamira, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of water, while its roots grow underwater. Chamira is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and holds cultural significance in various traditions.

Its botanical name, *Nelumbo nucifera*, reflects its importance in ancient botanical classifications.
