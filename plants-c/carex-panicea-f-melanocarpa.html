Carex panicea f. melanocarpa

Carex panicea f. melanocarpa, commonly known as the black-fruited sedges, is a variant of the panicled sedge.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, berries that contrast with its grass-like foliage. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
