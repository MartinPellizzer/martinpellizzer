Combretum apiculatum subsp. apiculatum

Combretum apiculatum subsp.

apiculatum, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the rainy season.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its striking floral display makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
