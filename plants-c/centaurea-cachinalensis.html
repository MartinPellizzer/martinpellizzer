Centaurea cachinalensis

Centaurea cachinalensis, commonly known as the Cachinal Bluet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its blue to violet flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea cachinalensis is often found in open habitats like slopes and coastal areas, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
