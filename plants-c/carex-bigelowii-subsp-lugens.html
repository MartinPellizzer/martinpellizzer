Carex bigelowii subsp. lugens

Carex bigelowii subsp.

lugens, commonly known as Bigelow's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
