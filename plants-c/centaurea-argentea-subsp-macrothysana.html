Centaurea argentea subsp. macrothysana

Centaurea argentea subsp.

macrothysana, commonly known as silver centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and bright yellow to orange flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It produces slender, branching stems with opposite leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
