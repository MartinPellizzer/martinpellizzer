Chaptalia rotundifolia

Chaptalia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that rises above the foliage.

Chaptalia rotundifolia is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to cold, arid climates.
