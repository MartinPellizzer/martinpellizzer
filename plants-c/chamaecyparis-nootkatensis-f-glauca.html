Chamaecyparis nootkatensis f. glauca

Chamaecyparis nootkatensis f. glauca, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a cultivar of the Nootka cypress.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage, which is dense and needle-like. The plant is evergreen, with a conical shape and slow growth rate, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. Its needles are stiff and have a slightly resinous scent, typical of the cypress family.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
