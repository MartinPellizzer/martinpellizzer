Callista fimbriata

Callista fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed bower vine, is a climbing plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
