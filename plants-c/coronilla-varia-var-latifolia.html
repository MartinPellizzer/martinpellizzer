Coronilla varia var. latifolia

Coronilla varia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for gardens and erosion control.
