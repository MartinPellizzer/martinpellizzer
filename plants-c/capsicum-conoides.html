Capsicum conoides

Capsicum conoides, commonly known as the horned pepper, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often horn-like fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and branching.

Capsicum conoides is cultivated for its unique appearance and is sometimes grown as an ornamental plant.
