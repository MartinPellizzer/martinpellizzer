Carex caryophyllea f. vidalii

Carex caryophyllea f. vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
