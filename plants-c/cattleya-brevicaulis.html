Cattleya brevicaulis

Cattleya brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stalked cattleya, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features broad, dark green leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cattleya brevicaulis is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
