Carex arctogena

Carex arctogena, commonly known as the arctic sedge, is a perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cold climates. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in harsh environments.

Carex arctogena plays a vital role in Arctic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various small animals and insects.
