Camellia brevistyla f. rubida

Camellia brevistyla f. rubida, commonly known as the red-stemmed camellia, is a unique variant of the camellia species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and leaves, which contrast with its typical green foliage. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
