Cuphea utriculosa var. panamensis

Cuphea utriculosa var. panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces unique seed capsules that are often inflated, giving it its species name "utriculosa," which refers to its bladder-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
