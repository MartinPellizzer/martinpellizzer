Cistus Heterophyllus Subsp. Carthaginensis

Cistus carthaginensis, scientifically known as Cistus heterophyllus subsp. carthaginensis, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the rocky landscapes of southern Europe and North Africa. This plant is characterized by its fragrant, resinous leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in Mediterranean climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats. Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species within the Cistaceae family.
