Cassia bicapsularis var. pubescens

Cassia bicapsularis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with hairy surfaces, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences, which are typical of the legume family, Fabaceae. Its fruit is a two-valved pod, releasing seeds when mature.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and vibrant flowers.
