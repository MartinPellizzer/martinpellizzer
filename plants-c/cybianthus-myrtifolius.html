Cybianthus myrtifolius

Cybianthus myrtifolius, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved cybianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features aromatic, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and have a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in late spring.

Cybianthus myrtifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual beauty.
