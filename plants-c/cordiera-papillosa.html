Cordiera papillosa

Cordiera papillosa, commonly known as the silky cordiera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate leaves with a soft, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cordiera papillosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
