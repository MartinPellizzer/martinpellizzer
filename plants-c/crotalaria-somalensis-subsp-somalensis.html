Crotalaria somalensis subsp. somalensis

Crotalaria somalensis subsp.

somalensis, commonly known as the Somali crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It has small, hairy pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.
