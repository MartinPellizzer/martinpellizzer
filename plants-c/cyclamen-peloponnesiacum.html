Cyclamen peloponnesiacum

Cyclamen peloponnesiacum, commonly known as the Peloponnesian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Peloponnese in southern Greece.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive wavy margin and produces striking, bowl-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, calcareous soils. It blooms from late autumn to early spring, making it a valuable addition to woodland gardens.

Cyclamen peloponnesiacum is also noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
