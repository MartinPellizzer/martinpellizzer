Cavendishia complectens

Cavendishia complectens, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant features long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length, often climbing or hanging from supports. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Native to regions of South America, it thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light.
