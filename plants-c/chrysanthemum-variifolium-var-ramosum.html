Chrysanthemum variifolium var. ramosum

Chrysanthemum variifolium var. ramosum, commonly known as the branched-leaf chrysanthemum, is a species native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its diverse foliage, with leaves that vary in shape and size, often featuring a serrated edge. The plant produces daisy-like flowers in a range of colors, including yellow, white, and purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in horticulture for both decorative and medicinal purposes.
