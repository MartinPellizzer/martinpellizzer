Camaridium aurantiacum

Camaridium aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
