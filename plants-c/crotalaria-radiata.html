Crotalaria radiata

Crotalaria radiata, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes. Crotalaria radiata is also used as a forage crop for livestock due to its high nutritional value.

However, some species within the genus can produce toxic alkaloids, requiring caution in its use.
