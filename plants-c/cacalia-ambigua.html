Cacalia ambigua

Cacalia ambigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Cacalia ambigua thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.
