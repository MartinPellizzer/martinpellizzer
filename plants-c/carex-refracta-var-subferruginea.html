Carex refracta var. subferruginea

Carex refracta var. subferruginea, commonly known as the rusty-fringed sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its foliage often exhibits a reddish-brown hue, particularly along the edges, giving it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
