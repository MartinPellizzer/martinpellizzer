Crataegus macounii

Crataegus macounii, commonly known as Macoun's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that ripen in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Crataegus macounii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
