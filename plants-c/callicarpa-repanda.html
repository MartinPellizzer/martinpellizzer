Callicarpa repanda

Callicarpa repanda, commonly known as the red beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces striking clusters of bright red berries in the fall, which are a key feature for its ornamental value. These berries are not only visually appealing but also serve as a food source for various birds and wildlife.

Callicarpa repanda thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant seasonal display.
