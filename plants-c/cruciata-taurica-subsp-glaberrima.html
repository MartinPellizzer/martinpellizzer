Cruciata taurica subsp. glaberrima

Cruciata taurica subsp.

glaberrima, commonly known as the Tauric cruciata, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
