Campanula hagielia

Campanula hagielia, commonly known as Hagiel's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Campanula hagielia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
