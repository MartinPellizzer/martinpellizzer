Citharexylum ulei var. obovatum

Citharexylum ulei var. obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed citharexylum, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to obovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its growth habit is typically compact, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
