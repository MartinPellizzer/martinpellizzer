Calophyllum articulatum

Calophyllum articulatum, commonly known as the articulated calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red fruits containing winged seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction.

Additionally, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
