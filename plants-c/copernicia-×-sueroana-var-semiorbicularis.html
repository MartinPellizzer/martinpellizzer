Copernicia × sueroana var. semiorbicularis

Copernicia × sueroana var. semiorbicularis, commonly known as the semiorbicular copernicia, is a hybrid palm species native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive white to yellowish bloom. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, fibrous fruits. Its trunk is typically slender and covered with persistent leaf bases, giving it a unique appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
