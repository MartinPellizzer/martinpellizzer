Campanula mollis f. longicornis

Campanula mollis f. longicornis, commonly known as the long-beaked bellflower, is a cultivar of the Campanula genus native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
