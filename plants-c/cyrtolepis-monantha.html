Cyrtolepis monantha

Cyrtolepis monantha, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture.

Cyrtolepis monantha thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
