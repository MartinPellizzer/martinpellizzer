Crocus lageniflorus var. landerianus

Crocus lageniflorus var. landerianus, commonly known as the Lander's crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, with a distinctive, swollen throat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
