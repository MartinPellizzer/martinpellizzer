Calamintha pantocsekii

Calamintha pantocsekii, commonly known as the Hungarian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calamintha pantocsekii is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its antiseptic and digestive benefits.
