Calamus ingens

Calamus ingens, commonly known as the giant calamus, is a striking member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is native to the wetlands and marshy areas of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, nutrient-rich environments. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizome and large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus ingens is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in aquatic gardens.
