Clematis addisonii

Clematis addisonii, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a flowering vine native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow buds that open into five-petaled flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five leaflets, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

Clematis addisonii prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
