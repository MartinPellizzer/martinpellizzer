Cyperus paluster var. uniglumoides

Cyperus paluster var. uniglumoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 1 inch wide, with a bluish-green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
