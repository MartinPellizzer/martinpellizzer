Croton greveanus var. borealis

Croton greveanus var. borealis, commonly known as the northern croton, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or orange, depending on the season. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage and adaptability, it is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
