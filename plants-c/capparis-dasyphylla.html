Capparis dasyphylla

Capparis dasyphylla, commonly known as the rough-fruited caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, thorny foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The berries are edible when ripe but are typically bitter and require preparation before consumption.
