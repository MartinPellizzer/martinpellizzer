Cymbachne amplectens var. calva

Cymbachne amplectens var. calva, commonly known as the woolly panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow panicle that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its unique texture and resilience.
