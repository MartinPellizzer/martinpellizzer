Caucalis infesta

Caucalis infesta, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Caucalis infesta thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
