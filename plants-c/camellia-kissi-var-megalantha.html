Camellia kissi var. megalantha

Camellia kissi var. megalantha, commonly known as the giant African camellia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
