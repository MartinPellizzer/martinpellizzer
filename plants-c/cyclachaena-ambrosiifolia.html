Cyclachaena ambrosiifolia

Cyclachaena ambrosiifolia, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Cyclachaena ambrosiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
