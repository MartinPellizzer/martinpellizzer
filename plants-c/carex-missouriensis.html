Carex missouriensis

Carex missouriensis, commonly known as Missouri sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. Missouri sedge typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
