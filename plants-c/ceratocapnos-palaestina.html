Ceratocapnos palaestina

Ceratocapnos palaestina, commonly known as the Palestine bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, edible fruits that are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.

Ceratocapnos palaestina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
