Cyperus muricatus

Cyperus muricatus, commonly known as the spiny flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically brown or reddish in color. Cyperus muricatus thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate moist conditions.
