Cyperus polystachyos var. hookerianus

Cyperus polystachyos var. hookerianus, commonly known as the Hooker's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often green or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or moist soils, commonly found in marshy areas, wetlands, and along waterways.

Its botanical structure supports its adaptation to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
