Caprifolium biflorum

Caprifolium biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually oval in shape and have a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Caprifolium biflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
