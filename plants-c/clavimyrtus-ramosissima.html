Clavimyrtus ramosissima

Clavimyrtus ramosissima, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, often attracting birds.

Clavimyrtus ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in coastal conditions.
