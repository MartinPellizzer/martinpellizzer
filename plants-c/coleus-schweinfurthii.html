Coleus schweinfurthii

Coleus schweinfurthii, commonly known as the "Cape Verdi" or "Tropical Coleus," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with deep green, purple, and red hues, often featuring intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add visual interest to shaded or partially sunny areas.
