Campanula albanica subsp. sancta

Campanula albanica subsp.

sancta, commonly known as the Albanian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Albania. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
