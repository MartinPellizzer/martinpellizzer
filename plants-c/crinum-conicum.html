Crinum conicum

Crinum conicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that serve as storage organs and are essential for its propagation. Crinum conicum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long flowering period during the summer months.
