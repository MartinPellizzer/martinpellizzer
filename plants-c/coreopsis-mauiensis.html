Coreopsis mauiensis

Coreopsis mauiensis, commonly known as Maui coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and dryland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Maui coreopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the unique flora of Hawaii.
