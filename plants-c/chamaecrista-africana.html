Chamaecrista africana

Chamaecrista africana, commonly known as African ticktrefoil, is a leguminous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Chamaecrista africana is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
