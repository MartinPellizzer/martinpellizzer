Crassocephalum diversifolium var. crepidioides

Crassocephalum diversifolium var. crepidioides, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom from spring to autumn. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough or hairy texture. This species is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
