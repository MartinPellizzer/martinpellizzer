Caroli-gmelina lancifolia

Caroli-gmelina lancifolia, commonly known as the slender ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and sturdy, dark-colored wood. The plant typically grows in lowland forests and is valued for its durability and resistance to decay. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or yellow in color.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
