Crataegus filipes

Crataegus filipes, commonly known as the slender hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
