Crotalaria pterocaula

Crotalaria pterocaula, commonly known as the winged cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, winged leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. It grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Crotalaria pterocaula is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
