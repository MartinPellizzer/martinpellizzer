Citrus oleocarpa

Citrus oleocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican lemon or kaffir lime, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It produces small, fragrant fruits with a thick, pebbled rind and a high concentration of essential oils. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in cooking, medicine, and essential oil extraction.
