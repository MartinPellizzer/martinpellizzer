Cyananthus macrocalyx subsp. macrocalyx

Cyananthus macrocalyx subsp.

macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered cyananthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
