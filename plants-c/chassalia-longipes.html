Chassalia longipes

Chassalia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed chassalia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its showy yellow flowers are arranged in a discoid head, often surrounded by pale green or white ray florets.

Chassalia longipes is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.
