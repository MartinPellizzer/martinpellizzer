Comocarpa

Comocarpa, commonly known as the Christmas berry, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably *Comocarpa quadrifida*, which is native to Australia and New Zealand. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe, making it visually striking. These berries are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, often used in jams and desserts.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
