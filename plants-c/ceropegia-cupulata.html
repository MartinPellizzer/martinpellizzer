Ceropegia cupulata

Ceropegia cupulata, commonly known as the flame vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of bright orange or red tubular flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, waxy coating. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Ceropegia cupulata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental flowers and easy care requirements.
