Cerinthe major

Cerinthe major, commonly known as the common toadflax, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.

Cerinthe major is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
