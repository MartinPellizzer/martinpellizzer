Chamaecrista harneyi

Chamaecrista harneyi, commonly known as Harney's burr legume, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, angular seeds.

Chamaecrista harneyi is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes many leguminous plants known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities.
