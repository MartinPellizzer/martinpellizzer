Campuloclinium scabrum

Campuloclinium scabrum, commonly known as roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and conical flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its flowers are usually purple to pink in color and bloom in late summer.

It serves as a valuable nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
