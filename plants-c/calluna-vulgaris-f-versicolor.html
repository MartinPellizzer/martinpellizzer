Calluna vulgaris f. versicolor

Calluna vulgaris f. versicolor, commonly known as the variable heather, is a cultivar of the common heather plant.

It is characterized by its ability to produce flowers in a range of colors, from white to pink and even red, depending on environmental conditions. The plant is evergreen, with small, needle-like leaves that form dense, low-growing mats. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various garden settings.
