Chileranthemum

Chileranthemum, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a popular ornamental plant belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is native to South America, specifically Brazil, and is characterized by its segmented stems and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms during the holiday season, producing flowers in shades of red, pink, orange, and white. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with moderate humidity.

Chileranthemum is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
