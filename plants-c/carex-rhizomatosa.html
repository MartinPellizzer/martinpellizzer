Carex rhizomatosa

Carex rhizomatosa, commonly known as the redstem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread via underground stems and form dense clumps. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers.

Carex rhizomatosa is often found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.
