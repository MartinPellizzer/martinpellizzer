Cardamine bracteata

Cardamine bracteata, commonly known as the bracted watercress, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, with five sepals and numerous stamens. Cardamine bracteata is characterized by its distinctive bracts that enclose the developing flowers, giving it its common name.

It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
