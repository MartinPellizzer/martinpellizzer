Cambogia gutta

Cambogia gutta, commonly known as the gutta tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, yellowish fruits. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a fragrant aroma.

The fruit, which contains a high amount of citric acid, is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
