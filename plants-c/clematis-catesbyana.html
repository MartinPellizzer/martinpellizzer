Clematis catesbyana

Clematis catesbyana, commonly known as the white clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Its climbing habit is supported by tendrils that allow it to grow along trellises or structures.

Clematis catesbyana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
