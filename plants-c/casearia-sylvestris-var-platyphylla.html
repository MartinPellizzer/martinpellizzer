Casearia sylvestris var. platyphylla

Casearia sylvestris var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf casearia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, white flowers are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
