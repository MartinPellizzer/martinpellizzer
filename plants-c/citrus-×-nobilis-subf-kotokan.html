Citrus × nobilis subf. kotokan

Citrus × nobilis subf.

kotokan, commonly known as the kumquat, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that turn orange when ripe.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

The fruit is edible, with a thin, sweet peel and a segmented, juicy pulp inside.
