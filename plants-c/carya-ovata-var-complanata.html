Carya ovata var. complanata

Carya ovata var. complanata, commonly known as the flat-leaf hickory, is a variant of the bitternut hickory.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that distinguish it from the typical ovate leaves of the main species. The plant is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States, typically found in moist, well-drained soils. Its fruits are small, spiny nuts that mature in the fall, and the tree produces a strong, aromatic scent when the leaves are crushed.

The flat-leaf hickory is valued for its hard wood and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
