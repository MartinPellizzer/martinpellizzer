Camellia tetracocca

Camellia tetracocca, commonly known as the four-leaf camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is evergreen and thrives in shaded, moist environments. It belongs to the Theaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Camellia tetracocca is also cultivated for its potential medicinal properties.
