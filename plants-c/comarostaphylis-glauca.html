Comarostaphylis glauca

Comarostaphylis glauca, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small, blue-gray clusters. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Comarostaphylis glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
