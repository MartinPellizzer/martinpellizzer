Cucurbita maxima cercuraicensis

Cucurbita maxima cercuraicensis, commonly known as the Mexican ridge gourd, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces elongated, ridged fruits that can vary in color from green to yellow or orange when mature. It is cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various traditional dishes.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight for optimal growth.
