Chrysophyllum myrsinodendron

Chrysophyllum myrsinodendron, commonly known as the African star fruit or Natal star fruit, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that resemble star shapes, hence its common name. The fruit has a tangy, acidic flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
