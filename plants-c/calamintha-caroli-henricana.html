Calamintha caroli-henricana

Calamintha caroli-henricana, commonly known as Carlow's calamint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs such as mint and lavender. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in well-drained, sunny environments.

The aromatic leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
