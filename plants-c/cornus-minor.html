Cornus minor

Cornus minor, commonly known as dwarf cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its small, white flowers and red berries. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1-2 meters and prefers moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Cornus minor is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.
