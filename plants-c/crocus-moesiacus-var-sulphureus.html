Crocus moesiacus var. sulphureus

Crocus moesiacus var. sulphureus, commonly known as the sulfur crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans and is known for its delicate, cup-shaped petals. It typically grows to a height of about 10-15 cm and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season blooms.
