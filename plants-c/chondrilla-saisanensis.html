Chondrilla saisanensis

Chondrilla saisanensis, commonly known as the Sisan Desert Cudweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and yellow flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that allow it to colonize sandy and rocky soils.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic adaptations.
