Coryphopteris pubirachis var. philippinensis

Coryphopteris pubirachis var. philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine holly fern, is a variant of the holly fern genus native to the Philippines.

It is a fern characterized by its large, leathery, compound fronds that are typically dark green with a pubescent underside. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its fronds are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to their ornamental value.

The species is notable for its distinct morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
