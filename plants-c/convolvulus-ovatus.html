Convolvulus ovatus

Convolvulus ovatus, commonly known as the spreading bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to orange flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and somewhat succulent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy habitats.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
