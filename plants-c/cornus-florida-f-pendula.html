Cornus florida f. pendula

Cornus florida f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping flowering dogwood, is a cultivar of the flowering dogwood tree.

It is characterized by its graceful, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The plant produces showy white bracts in the spring, which are often mistaken for flowers. Its leaves are typically oval and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to the blossoms.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
