Cerastium furcatum f. takedae

Cerastium furcatum f. takedae, commonly known as the Takeda's mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, hairy leaves that are often lobed or notched. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals and a prominent central stamen, blooming in early spring. Native to Japan, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground covers in suitable conditions.
