Cytisus rufulus

Cytisus rufulus, commonly known as the red bush crake, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, woody stems that are often reddish in color, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and trifoliate, with a slightly hairy surface.

Cytisus rufulus is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
