Cyphostemma masukuense subsp. nguruense

Cyphostemma masukuense subsp.

nguruense, commonly known as the Nguru candelabra, is a climbing plant native to Central Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
