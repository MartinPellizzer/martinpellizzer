Calendula sinuata

Calendula sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous calendula, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, orange to yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments and has finely divided, serrated leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calendula sinuata is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and easy maintenance.
