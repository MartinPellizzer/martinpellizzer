Crucianella suffruticosa

Crucianella suffruticosa, commonly known as the bladder nut or bladderleaf, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is characterized by its unique, bladder-like fruits that contain a single seed. It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
