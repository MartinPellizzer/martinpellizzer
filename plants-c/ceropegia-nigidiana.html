Ceropegia nigidiana

Ceropegia nigidiana, commonly known as the woolly ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces a unique type of pollination mechanism involving specific insects, such as flies and beetles. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens with suitable conditions.
