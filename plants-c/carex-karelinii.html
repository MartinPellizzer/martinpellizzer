Carex karelinii

Carex karelinii, commonly known as the Karelin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, greenish-brown flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex karelinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
