Cruciata pedemontana var. pedemontana

Cruciata pedemontana var. pedemontana, commonly known as the Pedemontana cruciata, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lanceolate leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers cool and moist conditions.
