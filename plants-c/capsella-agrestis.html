Capsella agrestis

Capsella agrestis, commonly known as mouse-ear or field speedwell, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is known for its rapid growth cycle.

Capsella agrestis is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
