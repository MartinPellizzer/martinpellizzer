Cyperus enervis var. laxus

Cyperus enervis var. laxus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. This variant is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has a rhizomatous root system, which helps it spread and colonize new areas.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
