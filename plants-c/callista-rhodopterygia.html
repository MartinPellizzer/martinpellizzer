Callista rhodopterygia

Callista rhodopterygia, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vivid yellow and orange flowers that resemble flames, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its elegant, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Callista rhodopterygia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
