Cassia kirkii var. velutina

Cassia kirkii var. velutina, commonly known as velvet cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in terminal panicles, which are followed by elongated, flat pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
