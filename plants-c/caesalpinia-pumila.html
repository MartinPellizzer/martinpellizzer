Caesalpinia pumila

Caesalpinia pumila, commonly known as the red sand mat, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces bright red or orange flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Caesalpinia pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize dunes.
