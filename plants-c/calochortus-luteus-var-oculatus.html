Calochortus luteus var. oculatus

Calochortus luteus var. oculatus, commonly known as the yellow mariposa lily, is a striking bulbous perennial native to California.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers with a distinctive yellow coloration and a prominent, often orange or red, central spot. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches, with grass-like leaves that are narrow and strap-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
