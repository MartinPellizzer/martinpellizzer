Cassia retusa var. dietrichiae

Cassia retusa var. dietrichiae, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like inflorescences that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and its bark is often smooth with a reddish or brownish hue. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
