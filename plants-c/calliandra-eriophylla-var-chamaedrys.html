Calliandra eriophylla var. chamaedrys

Calliandra eriophylla var. chamaedrys, commonly known as the shrub legume or chamaedrys calliandra, is a deciduous shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features compound leaves with numerous small, elliptical leaflets and produces bright pink to red inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep taproots and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low water requirements.

Additionally, it has been studied for its potential in soil stabilization and as a source of forage for livestock.
