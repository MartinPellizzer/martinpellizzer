Calopsis peronata

Calopsis peronata, commonly known as the yellow bell heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calopsis peronata is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique type of vegetation endemic to South Africa.
