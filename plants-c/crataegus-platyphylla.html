Crataegus platyphylla

Crataegus platyphylla, commonly known as the flatleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Crataegus platyphylla is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
