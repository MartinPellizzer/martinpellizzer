Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. piligera

Calamagrostis langsdorffii var. piligera, commonly known as the silky blue reed, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems that produce feathery, blue-gray inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, giving them a silky texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
