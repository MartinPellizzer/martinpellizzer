Colchicum cretense

Colchicum cretense, commonly known as the Cretan colchic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, which is surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
