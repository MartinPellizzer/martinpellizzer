Clematis viorna

Clematis viorna, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, yellow or cream-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically twining and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis viorna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
