Crataegus macra

Crataegus macra, commonly known as the large-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and showy white to pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
