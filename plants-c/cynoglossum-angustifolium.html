Cynoglossum angustifolium

Cynoglossum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive blue or purple coloration. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet, blooming from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks.

Cynoglossum angustifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
