Capsicum laeve

Capsicum laeve, commonly known as the smooth pepper or bell pepper, is a species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy surface and typically round or bell-shaped fruits. The plant produces flowers that are yellow or white, which develop into the edible, fleshy fruits. Capsicum laeve is a member of the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and potatoes.

Its fruits are rich in vitamins and are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
