Cirsium lecontei

Cirsium lecontei, commonly known as Leconte's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are spiny and lobed, with a rough, prickly texture.

The showy purple to pink flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
