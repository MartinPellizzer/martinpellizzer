Corydalis verticillaris subsp. verticillaris

Corydalis verticillaris subsp.

verticillaris, commonly known as the spiral poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western China. It is characterized by its striking spiral arrangement of flowers, which bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Corydalis verticillaris subsp.

verticillaris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
