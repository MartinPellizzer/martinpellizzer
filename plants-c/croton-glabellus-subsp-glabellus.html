Croton glabellus subsp. glabellus

Croton glabellus subsp.

glabellus, commonly known as the smooth croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are typically bright green with a smooth texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
