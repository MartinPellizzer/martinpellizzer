Cyperus tomaiophyllus var. alpestris

Cyperus tomaiophyllus var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pale green or brown in color. It is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils, often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
