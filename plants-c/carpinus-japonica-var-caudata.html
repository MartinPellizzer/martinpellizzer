Carpinus japonica var. caudata

Carpinus japonica var. caudata, commonly known as the Japanese hornbeam or Japanese ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by woody, horn-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
