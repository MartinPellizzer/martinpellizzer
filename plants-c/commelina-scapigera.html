Commelina scapigera

Commelina scapigera, commonly known as the zigzagged commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies. Its leaves are broad and sometimes have a waxy coating, which helps in water retention.

The flowers are usually borne on slender stalks and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
