Cardamine minima

Cardamine minima, commonly known as lesser watercress, is a small annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant has slender, branched stems with opposite, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small white or greenish-white flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
