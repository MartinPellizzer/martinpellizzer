Carex cusickii

Carex cusickii, commonly known as Cusick's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, pond edges, and riparian zones. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex cusickii plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
