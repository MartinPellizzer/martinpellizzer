Coleus delpierrei

Coleus delpierrei, commonly known as the Delpierre's coleus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, purple, and white. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often square in cross-section, a common trait among members of the Lamiaceae family.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
