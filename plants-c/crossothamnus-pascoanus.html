Crossothamnus pascoanus

Crossothamnus pascoanus, commonly known as Pasco's wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers in winter. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy soils.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
