Cleisostoma pacificum

Cleisostoma pacificum, commonly known as the Pacific cleistostoma, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical forests. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in shaded, well-drained environments.

The flowers bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking addition to botanical collections.
