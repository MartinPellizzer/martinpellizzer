Cuphea acinifolia

Cuphea acinifolia, commonly known as the flame wheel plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red bracts that surround small, yellow flowers, creating a visually appealing display. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cuphea acinifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
