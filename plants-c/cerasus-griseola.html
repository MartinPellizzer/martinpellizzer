Cerasus griseola

Cerasus griseola, commonly known as the gray cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its grayish bark and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
