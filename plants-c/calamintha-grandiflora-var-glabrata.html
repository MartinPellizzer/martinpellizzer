Calamintha grandiflora var. glabrata

Calamintha grandiflora var. glabrata, commonly known as broadleaf calamintha, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are typically oval to ovate in shape. The plant produces showy, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for both ornamental and ecological landscaping.
