Centaurea jiroftensis

Centaurea jiroftensis, commonly known as the Jiroft thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from blue to purple in color.

Centaurea jiroftensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also studied for its potential ecological and medicinal applications.
