Calliandra parvifolia

Calliandra parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Calliandra parvifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
