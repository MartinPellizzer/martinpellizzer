Crataegus alutacea var. subhispidula

Crataegus alutacea var. subhispidula, commonly known as the smooth thornapple, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red, juicy berries in the fall. Its berries are edible but should be consumed in moderation due to their high acidity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
