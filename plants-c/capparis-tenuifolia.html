Capparis tenuifolia

Capparis tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender coral tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, coral-like flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Capparis tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
