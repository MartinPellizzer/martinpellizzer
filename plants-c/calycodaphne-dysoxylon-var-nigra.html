Calycodaphne dysoxylon var. nigra

Calycodaphne dysoxylon var. nigra, commonly known as black bamboo, is a variant of the genus Calycodaphne within the family Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant typically grows as a clump-forming shrub or small tree, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually evergreen. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing edible fruits that are sometimes consumed by wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique aesthetic.
