Centaurea loscosii

Centaurea loscosii, commonly known as the Loscosii knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flower heads, which are composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species well adapted to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea loscosii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
