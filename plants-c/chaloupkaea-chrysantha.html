Chaloupkaea chrysantha

Chaloupkaea chrysantha, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and its roots are fibrous and adapted to rocky soils. It thrives in well-drained, sunny environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
