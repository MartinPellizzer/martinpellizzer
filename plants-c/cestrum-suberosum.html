Cestrum suberosum

Cestrum suberosum, commonly known as the rough cestrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the evening. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.
