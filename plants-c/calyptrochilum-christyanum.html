Calyptrochilum christyanum

Calyptrochilum christyanum, commonly known as the Christyan's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a hood-like labellum that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests or high-altitude grasslands. Its flowers are usually small and brightly colored, with a complex arrangement of petals and sepals.

Calyptrochilum christyanum is notable for its ornamental value and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
