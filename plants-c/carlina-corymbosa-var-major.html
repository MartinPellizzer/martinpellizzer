Carlina corymbosa var. major

Carlina corymbosa var. major, commonly known as the giant cudweed, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy composite flower heads composed of yellow disk flowers and white ray flowers. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate with serrated margins and hairy surfaces. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
