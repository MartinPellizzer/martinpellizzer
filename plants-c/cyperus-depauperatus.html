Cyperus depauperatus

Cyperus depauperatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Cyperus depauperatus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
