Carex breviculmis var. perciliata

Carex breviculmis var. perciliata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and damp woodlands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in width. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-ranked spikes, with male flowers at the base and female flowers above.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
