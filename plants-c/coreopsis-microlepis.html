Coreopsis microlepis

Coreopsis microlepis, commonly known as the dwarf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open woodlands and sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Coreopsis microlepis is also a host plant for certain butterfly species, making it ecologically significant.
