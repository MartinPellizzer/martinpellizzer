Cyathea zollingeriana

Cyathea zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger's tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a large, evergreen fern that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its massive, trunk-like rhizome and a crown of large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its fronds are typically dark green and can remain on the plant for several years before being replaced.

Cyathea zollingeriana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
