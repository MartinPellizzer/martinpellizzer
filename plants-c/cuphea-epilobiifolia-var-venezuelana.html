Cuphea epilobiifolia var. venezuelana

Cuphea epilobiifolia var. venezuelana, commonly known as the Venezuelan cuphea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique flower structure attracts hummingbirds and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.
