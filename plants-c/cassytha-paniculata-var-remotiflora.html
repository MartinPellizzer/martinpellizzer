Cassytha paniculata var. remotiflora

Cassytha paniculata var. remotiflora, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a parasitic flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its slender, serpentine stems that twine around host plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Due to its parasitic nature, it derives nutrients from its host, often causing partial or complete mortality in the host plant.
