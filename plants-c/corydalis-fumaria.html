Corydalis fumaria

Corydalis fumaria, commonly known as the smoky corydalis, is a flowering plant in the family Fumariaceae.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive smoky or dusty appearance. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Corydalis fumaria is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
