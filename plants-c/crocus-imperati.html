Crocus imperati

Crocus imperati, commonly known as the imperial crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family, Iridaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a single, showy flower with three petals and three sepals, which are often marked with contrasting patterns. Its corms are underground storage organs that allow it to survive harsh winters and regenerate each year.

The imperial crocus is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its historical use in perfumery and medicine.
