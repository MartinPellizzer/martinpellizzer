Cassia mimosoides var. ankaratrensis

Cassia mimosoides var. ankaratrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in dry forest and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
