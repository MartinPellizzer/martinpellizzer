Camellia sasanqua var. angustifolia

Camellia sasanqua var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved camellia, is a species native to southern Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of deep red to pink flowers in autumn. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for ornamental gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
