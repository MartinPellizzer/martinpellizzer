Ceropegia miscella

Ceropegia miscella, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent-like stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ceropegia miscella is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience to drought conditions.
