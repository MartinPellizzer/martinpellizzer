Capparis aculeata

Capparis aculeata, commonly known as the prickly gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, acidic berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to dry, rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species within its native habitats.
