Cyperus geminatus

Cyperus geminatus, commonly known as the twinflower sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It is often found in wetland environments, including marshes and pond margins.

Cyperus geminatus is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
