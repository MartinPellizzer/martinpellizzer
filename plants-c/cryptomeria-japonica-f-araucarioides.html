Cryptomeria japonica f. araucarioides

Cryptomeria japonica f. araucarioides, commonly known as the Japanese cedar variety with a resemblance to the Araucaria genus, is a variant of the Japanese cedar characterized by its distinct foliage and growth habit.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree that exhibits a conical shape with dense, needle-like leaves that are shorter and more clustered than those of the typical Japanese cedar. The foliage is often a deep green, providing a striking visual contrast against the bark, which is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age. This form is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture and its ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.

It is also notable for its slow growth rate, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces.
