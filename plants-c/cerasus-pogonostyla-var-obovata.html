Cerasus pogonostyla var. obovata

Cerasus pogonostyla var. obovata, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or Chinese date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features smooth, grey-barked stems and alternate, ovate to obovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet, and tangy fruits. The fruits are typically ellipsoid or spherical, with a thin, leathery skin.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
