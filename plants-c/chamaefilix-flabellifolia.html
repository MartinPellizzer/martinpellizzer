Chamaefilix flabellifolia

Chamaefilix flabellifolia, commonly known as the fan-leaved rockrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant features elliptical to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Chamaefilix flabellifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
