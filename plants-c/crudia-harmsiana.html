Crudia harmsiana

Crudia harmsiana, commonly known as the white-flowered wood sorrel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
