Crassula × justus

Crassula × justus, commonly known as the justus crassula, is a hybrid succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among succulent enthusiasts and gardeners.
