Chamaeraphis italica var. macrochaeta

Chamaeraphis italica var. macrochaeta, commonly known as the Italian sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bead-like fruits. Its leaves are typically succulent and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
