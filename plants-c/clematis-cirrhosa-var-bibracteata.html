Clematis cirrhosa var. bibracteata

Clematis cirrhosa var. bibracteata, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
