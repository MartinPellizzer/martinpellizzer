Cochlearia alyssoides

Cochlearia alyssoides, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers. Native to mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in rocky and alpine environments. The plant has a short growing season and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
