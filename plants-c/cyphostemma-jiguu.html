Cyphostemma jiguu

Cyphostemma jiguu, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits. Cyphostemma jiguu is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable plant in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
