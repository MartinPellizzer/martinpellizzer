Cyathea rufopannosa

Cyathea rufopannosa, commonly known as the red-veined tree fern, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant features a distinctive reddish-pink coloration on its young fronds, which becomes more green as it matures. Cyathea rufopannosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
