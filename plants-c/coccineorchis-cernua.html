Coccineorchis cernua

Coccineorchis cernua, commonly known as the drooping coral orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purplish tinge.

The orchid's flowers are usually pink or red, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.
