Cyperus planifolius f. gracilis

Cyperus planifolius f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soils. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
