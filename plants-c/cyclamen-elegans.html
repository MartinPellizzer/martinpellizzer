Cyclamen elegans

Cyclamen elegans, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iran.

It features distinctive heart-shaped leaves with a waxy, velvety texture and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soil. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Cyclamen elegans is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking foliage and delicate blossoms.
