Coccinia aostae

Coccinia aostae, commonly known as the Aosta morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some botanical gardens, though it remains relatively rare in the wild.
