Cactus spectabilis

Cactus spectabilis, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

Cactus spectabilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
