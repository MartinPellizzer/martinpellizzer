Camellia tadungensis

Camellia tadungensis, commonly known as the Tadungensis camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant produces fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in winter, making it a valuable ornamental plant. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in forested areas.

Camellia tadungensis is also appreciated for its potential in horticulture and as a source of ornamental foliage.
