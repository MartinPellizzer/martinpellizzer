Cylindropyrum cylindricum subsp. pauciaristatum

Cylindropyrum cylindricum subsp.

pauciaristatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cypress, is a member of the Cupressaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along the branches.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate climates. It produces cones that are usually small and clustered, contributing to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its unique appearance and adaptability to various garden environments.
