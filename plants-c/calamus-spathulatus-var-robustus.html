Calamus spathulatus var. robustus

Calamus spathulatus var. robustus, commonly known as the broad-leaved calamus, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a decorative element. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
