Cinnamomum bhaskarii

Cinnamomum bhaskarii, commonly known as Bhaskari's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white berries that contain aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
