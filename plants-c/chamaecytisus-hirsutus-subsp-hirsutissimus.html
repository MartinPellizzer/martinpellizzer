Chamaecytisus hirsutus subsp. hirsutissimus

Chamaecytisus hirsutus subsp.

hirsutissimus, commonly known as the Hirsute Broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, wiry, hirsute stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is evergreen, with leathery, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
