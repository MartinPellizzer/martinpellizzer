Coreopsis mutica var. leptomera

Coreopsis mutica var. leptomera, commonly known as the slender coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense clumps over time.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
