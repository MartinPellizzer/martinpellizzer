Catasetum planiceps

Catasetum planiceps, commonly known as the flat-headed orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive, flat, and broad flower spike. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Catasetum planiceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
