Cussonia arborea

Cussonia arborea, commonly known as the African nettle or tree nettle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a tall, upright shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive net-like venation. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by red, fleshy berries.

Cussonia arborea is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both moist and dry conditions.
