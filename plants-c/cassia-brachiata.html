Cassia brachiata

Cassia brachiata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain flat, oval seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia brachiata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
