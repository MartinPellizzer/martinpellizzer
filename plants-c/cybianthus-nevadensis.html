Cybianthus nevadensis

Cybianthus nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open meadows at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in the Sierra Nevada mountain range.

Cybianthus nevadensis is adapted to cold, arid environments and is an important part of the local flora.
