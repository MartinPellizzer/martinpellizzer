Calliandra fasciculata

Calliandra fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to red bracteate flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has bipinnate leaves, with each leaflet being small and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Calliandra fasciculata is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
