Commelina cuneata

Commelina cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its erect stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that are clustered in umbels, often with a distinctive white or yellowish spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina cuneata is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a useful pioneer species and a potential weed.
