Clematis udayanii

Clematis udayanii, commonly known as the Udayanii Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Clematis udayanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
