Crataegus basilica var. viridimontana

Crataegus basilica var. viridimontana, commonly known as the Green Mountain hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open woods.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
