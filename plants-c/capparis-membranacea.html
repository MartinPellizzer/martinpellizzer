Capparis membranacea

Capparis membranacea, commonly known as the membranous caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has woody stems and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to arid environments.
