Calamagrostis agrostiiformis

Calamagrostis agrostiiformis, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in moist environments.
