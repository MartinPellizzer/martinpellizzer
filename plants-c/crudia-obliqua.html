Crudia obliqua

Crudia obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rubiaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, including South Africa, and thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stems and small, opposite leaves. It produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by small, round fruits.

Crudia obliqua is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
