Chloris eleusinoides var. vestita

Chloris eleusinoides var. vestita, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray leaves and delicate, panicle-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
