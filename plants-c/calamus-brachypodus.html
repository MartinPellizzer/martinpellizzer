Calamus brachypodus

Calamus brachypodus, commonly known as the short-stemmed arrowroot, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, followed by the development of three-seeded berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its fibrous rhizomes, it has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
