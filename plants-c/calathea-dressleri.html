Calathea dressleri

Calathea dressleri, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, and intricately patterned foliage, which features deep green veins on a lighter green background. The leaves are broad and oval-shaped, often with a waxy texture that enhances their visual appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
