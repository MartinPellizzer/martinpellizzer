Cirsium murdockii

Cirsium murdockii, commonly known as the Murdock's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
