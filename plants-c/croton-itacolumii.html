Croton itacolumii

Croton itacolumii, commonly known as the Khasi croton, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern region of India.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that can vary in color from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
