Cururu brachycarpa

Cururu brachycarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark red, edible berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The fruit is an important food source for various wildlife species in its native habitat.
