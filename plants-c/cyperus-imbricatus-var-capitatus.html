Cyperus imbricatus var. capitatus

Cyperus imbricatus var. capitatus, commonly known as the clustered flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a potential indicator of soil moisture conditions.
