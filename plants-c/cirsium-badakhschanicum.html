Cirsium badakhschanicum

Cirsium badakhschanicum, commonly known as the Badakhshan thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, rough leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its stems are often covered in stiff hairs, and it produces small, spherical seed heads that disperse easily.

Cirsium badakhschanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in its native habitats.
