Conoclinium macrocephalum

Conoclinium macrocephalum, commonly known as the honeyflower or giant horsemint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, tubular, orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Conoclinium macrocephalum is a valuable plant for pollinators, particularly hummingbirds and bees.
