Clematicissus

Clematicissus, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in late spring. Its berries are dark blue to black when mature and are often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Clematicissus is also popular as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to climb walls or trellises.
