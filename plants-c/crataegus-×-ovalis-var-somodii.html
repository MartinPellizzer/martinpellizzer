Crataegus × ovalis var. somodii

Crataegus × ovalis var. somodii, commonly known as the Somodii hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
