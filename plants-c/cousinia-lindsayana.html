Cousinia lindsayana

Cousinia lindsayana, commonly known as Lindsay's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cousinia lindsayana is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
