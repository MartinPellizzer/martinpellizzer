Clinopodium rotundifolium

Clinopodium rotundifolium, commonly known as roundleaf betony, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its rounded, velvety leaves and spikes of blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Clinopodium rotundifolium is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and antispasmodic effects.
