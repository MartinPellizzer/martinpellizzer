Carpinus kenpukwan

Carpinus kenpukwan, commonly known as the Korean hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its hard, dense wood and is used in traditional crafts and construction.
