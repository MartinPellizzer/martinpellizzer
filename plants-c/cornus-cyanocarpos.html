Cornus cyanocarpos

Cornus cyanocarpos, commonly known as the bluebead bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dark blue, bead-like fruits that are clustered in racemes, giving it its common name. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels.

Cornus cyanocarpos is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and visual appeal.
