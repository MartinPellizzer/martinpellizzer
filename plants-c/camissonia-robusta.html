Camissonia robusta

Camissonia robusta, commonly known as the California rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in coastal and inland areas.

Camissonia robusta is adapted to dry climates and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.
