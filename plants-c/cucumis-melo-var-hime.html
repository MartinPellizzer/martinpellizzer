Cucumis melo var. hime

Cucumis melo var. hime, commonly known as the Japanese melon, is a variety of melon characterized by its small, round fruits with a smooth, pale green rind.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to East Asia, particularly Japan. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with palmate leaves and produces yellow flowers. Its fruit is prized for its sweet, aromatic flesh and is often used in desserts and beverages.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
