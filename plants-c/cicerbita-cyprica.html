Cicerbita cyprica

Cicerbita cyprica, commonly known as the Cyprus bittercress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in early spring, and it produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

The leaves are usually oval-shaped and have a slightly bitter taste, which is characteristic of many species in its family.
