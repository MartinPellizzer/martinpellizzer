Cerastium amanum

Cerastium amanum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and clustered at the base of the plant. The flowers are white or pale pink, with five petals, and appear in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

This species is often found in moist, cool habitats and is known for its ability to spread through stolons.
