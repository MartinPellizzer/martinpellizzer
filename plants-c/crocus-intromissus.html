Crocus intromissus

Crocus intromissus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Turkey, as well as parts of the Caucasus and western Iran. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, often fragrant scent. It typically blooms in late winter or early spring, making it one of the first crocus species to flower.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
