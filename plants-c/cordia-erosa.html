Cordia erosa

Cordia erosa, commonly known as the white cordia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
