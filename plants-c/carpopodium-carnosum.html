Carpopodium carnosum

Carpopodium carnosum, commonly known as the red alga, is a species of marine algae belonging to the order Rhodophyta.

It is characterized by its bright red to pink coloration, which results from the presence of phycoerythrin, a pigment that allows it to absorb light for photosynthesis. This organism typically grows in shallow coastal waters, where it attaches to rocky substrates. Its thallus is filamentous and branched, with a gelatinous texture that aids in water retention.

Carpopodium carnosum plays an important role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
