Chenopodium tenerrimum

Chenopodium tenerrimum, commonly known as tumbleweed, is an annual plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and production of dry, papery seeds. The plant's leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are succulent and often reddish in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and other open areas.

Due to its ability to spread rapidly, it is considered a weed in many agricultural settings.
