Callipsyche mexicana

Callipsyche mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican longhorned beetle, is a species of longhorned beetle native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cerambycidae and is characterized by its elongated body and prominent antennae. The plant it is associated with is the Mexican yew, Taxus mexicana, on which it lays its eggs. The larvae feed on the wood of the host plant, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.

This beetle is of interest to entomologists due to its unique morphology and specific habitat requirements.
