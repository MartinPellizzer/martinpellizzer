Cordyline congesta

Cordyline congesta, commonly known as the broad-leafed cabbage tree, is a striking tropical plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant's foliage is typically a deep green, with some varieties exhibiting variegated patterns. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Cordyline congesta is also valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
