Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. hololeucus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. hololeucus, commonly known as the white rabbitbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a woody stem and is often found in areas with well-drained soil.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
