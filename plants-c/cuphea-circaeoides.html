Cuphea circaeoides

Cuphea circaeoides, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Cuphea circaeoides is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
