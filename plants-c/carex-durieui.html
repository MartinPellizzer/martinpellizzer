Carex durieui

Carex durieui, commonly known as the Durieu's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spikes of small, typically green or brownish flowers. Native to certain regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to its ecological significance in wetland ecosystems.
