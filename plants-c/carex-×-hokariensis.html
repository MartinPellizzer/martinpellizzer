Carex × hokariensis

Carex × hokariensis, commonly known as the Hokariensis sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Carex genus in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Native to Japan, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive, compact growth habit.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for water gardens and shaded landscapes.
