Corispermum simplicissimum

Corispermum simplicissimum, commonly known as the simple bladderwrack, is a small annual plant belonging to the family Corispermaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, erect stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its seeds are enclosed in a hard, leathery capsule, which helps in seed dispersal.

It is typically found in sandy or coastal soils, thriving in arid and saline environments.
