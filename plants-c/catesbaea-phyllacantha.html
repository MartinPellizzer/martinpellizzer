Catesbaea phyllacantha

Catesbaea phyllacantha, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The Natal plum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and local cuisine.
