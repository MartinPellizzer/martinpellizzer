Cucumis melo var. minutissimus

Cucumis melo var. minutissimus, commonly known as the mouse melon, is a rare and small variety of melon native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tiny, round fruits that are typically less than 2 inches in diameter. The plant produces yellow or green fruits with a thin, edible rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its unique flavor and ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and specific growing conditions, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive botanical specimen.
