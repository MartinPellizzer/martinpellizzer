Camellia trichoclada f. leucantha

Camellia trichoclada f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered camellia, is a unique horticultural form of the camellia genus native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white petals that contrast with its deep red or maroon buds, creating a striking visual effect. The plant is an evergreen shrub with glossy, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its blooms. It thrives in partially shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
