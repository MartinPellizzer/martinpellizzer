Crotalaria chondrocarpa

Crotalaria chondrocarpa, commonly known as the rock crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive, inflated seed pods. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Crotalaria chondrocarpa is often found in rocky soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.
