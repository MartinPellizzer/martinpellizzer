Crataegus lindmanii var. ronnigeri

Crataegus lindmanii var. ronnigeri, commonly known as the Ronniger's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 7 leaflets, and its showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist woodlands or along stream banks.
