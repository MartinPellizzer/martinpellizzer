Cousinia × paraatripurpurea

Cousinia × paraatripurpurea, commonly known as the purple flaxflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, lavender-pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Cousinia × paraatripurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
