Calamagrostis halleriana var. ambigua

Calamagrostis halleriana var. ambigua, commonly known as the slender marsh reed, is a perennial grass native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet that is often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
