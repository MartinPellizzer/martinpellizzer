Chlamysporum thyrsoideum

Chlamysporum thyrsoideum, commonly known as the thyrsoideum plant, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally grown in gardens and botanical collections.
