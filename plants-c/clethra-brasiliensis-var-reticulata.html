Clethra brasiliensis var. reticulata

Clethra brasiliensis var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate sweet pepper tree, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, reticulate pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. These flowers are followed by small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and contain seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
