Celtis inglisii

Celtis inglisii, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, greenish fruits. Celtis inglisii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
