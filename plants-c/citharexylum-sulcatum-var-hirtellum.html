Citharexylum sulcatum var. hirtellum

Citharexylum sulcatum var. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy citharexylum, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody stems are usually slender and can be somewhat flexible, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

The leaves are evergreen, providing year-round foliage, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild aromatic properties.
