Coptis omeiensis

Coptis omeiensis, commonly known as the Sichuan goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and clustered yellow flowers. The plant is notable for containing berberine, a powerful alkaloid with medicinal properties. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and stout.

Coptis omeiensis is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
