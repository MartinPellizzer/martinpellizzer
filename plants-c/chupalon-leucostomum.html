Chupalon leucostomum

Chupalon leucostomum, commonly known as the white-flowered chupalon, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
