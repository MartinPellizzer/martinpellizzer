Cistus coriaceus

Cistus coriaceus, commonly known as rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are leathery and gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Cistus coriaceus is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
