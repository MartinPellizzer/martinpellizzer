Crocus acutiflorus

Crocus acutiflorus, commonly known as the sharpflower crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with yellow centers, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.

The bulbs can be divided and planted in the fall for propagation.
