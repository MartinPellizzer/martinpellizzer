Covellia racemifera

Covellia racemifera, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Covellia racemifera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
