Celastrus oleoides

Celastrus oleoides, commonly known as the yellow elder, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
