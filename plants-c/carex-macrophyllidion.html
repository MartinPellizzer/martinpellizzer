Carex macrophyllidion

Carex macrophyllidion, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are often broader than those of other sedges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and it thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and pond edges. Carex macrophyllidion is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable environments.
