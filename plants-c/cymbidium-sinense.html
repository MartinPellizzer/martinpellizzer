Cymbidium sinense

Cymbidium sinense, commonly known as the Chinese orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, with petals that range in color from white to pink and yellow.

Cymbidium sinense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is widely cultivated for horticultural purposes.
