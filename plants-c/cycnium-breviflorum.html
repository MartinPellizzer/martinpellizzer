Cycnium breviflorum

Cycnium breviflorum, commonly known as the short-flowered cicada vine, is a herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Cycnium breviflorum thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
