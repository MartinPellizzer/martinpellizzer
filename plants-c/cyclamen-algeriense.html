Cyclamen algeriense

Cyclamen algeriense, commonly known as Algerian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North Africa.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or shaded borders.

Cyclamen algeriense is also valued for its ability to bloom throughout the winter months.
