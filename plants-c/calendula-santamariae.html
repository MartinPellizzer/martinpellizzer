Calendula santamariae

Calendula santamariae, commonly known as the Santa Maria marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, producing numerous ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calendula santamariae is often used in herbal medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its bright colors and medicinal properties.
