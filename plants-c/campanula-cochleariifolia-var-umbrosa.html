Campanula cochleariifolia var. umbrosa

Campanula cochleariifolia var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
