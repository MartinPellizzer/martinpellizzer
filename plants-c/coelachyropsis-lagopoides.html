Coelachyropsis lagopoides

Coelachyropsis lagopoides, commonly known as the woolly star, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that give it its common name. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually white or pale green.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
