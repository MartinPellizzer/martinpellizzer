Cryptolepis apiculata

Cryptolepis apiculata, commonly known as the African bitters or bitterleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, sometimes hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Its botanical significance lies in its chemical composition, which includes various bioactive compounds used in traditional medicine.
