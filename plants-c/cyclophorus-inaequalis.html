Cyclophorus inaequalis

Cyclophorus inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal cyclophorus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairie or woodland habitats. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
