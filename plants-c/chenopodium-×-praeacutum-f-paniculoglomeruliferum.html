Chenopodium × praeacutum f. paniculoglomeruliferum

Chenopodium × praeacutum f. paniculoglomeruliferum, commonly known as the clustered flowering goosefoot, is a hybrid species within the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and dense, panicle-like inflorescences that bear small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically exhibits a robust root system and thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate to rhombic in shape, and covered with a fine layer of white pubescence.

This plant is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological benefits.
