Capparis brevispina

Capparis brevispina, commonly known as the short-stemmed caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round, green berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
