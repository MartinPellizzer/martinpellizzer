Carex antarctogena

Carex antarctogena, commonly known as Antarctic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. Its reproductive structures consist of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex antarctogena plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
