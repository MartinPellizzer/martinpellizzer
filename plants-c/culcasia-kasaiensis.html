Culcasia kasaiensis

Culcasia kasaiensis, commonly known as the Kasai giant arrowhead, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter, making it a prominent feature in wetland environments. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as marshes and ponds, where it can anchor itself with its fibrous roots. Its leaves are typically a deep green, often with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

Culcasia kasaiensis plays an important ecological role by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
