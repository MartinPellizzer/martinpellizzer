Cornus alternifolia var. corallina

Cornus alternifolia var. corallina, commonly known as the coral dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking coral-pink bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, which appear in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn red in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and attractive addition to native plant gardens.
