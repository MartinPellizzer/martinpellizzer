Convolvulus anatolicus

Convolvulus anatolicus, commonly known as Anatolian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers with five petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Convolvulus anatolicus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
