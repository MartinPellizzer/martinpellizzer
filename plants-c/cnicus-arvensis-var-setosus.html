Cnicus arvensis var. setosus

Cnicus arvensis var. setosus, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial flowering plant characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in disturbed soils, often found in fields and along roadsides. The plant has a taproot system and produces small, bracts that surround its flowering heads. It is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and a common weed in agricultural settings.
