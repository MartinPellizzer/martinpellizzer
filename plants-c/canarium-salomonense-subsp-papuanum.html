Canarium salomonense subsp. papuanum

Canarium salomonense subsp.

papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan candlenut, is a tropical tree native to the highlands of Papua and the surrounding regions of Indonesia. It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by large, fleshy fruits that contain a single, oily seed. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil, while the tree is also valued for its timber.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in montane rainforests.
