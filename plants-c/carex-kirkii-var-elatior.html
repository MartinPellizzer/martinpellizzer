Carex kirkii var. elatior

Carex kirkii var. elatior, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown, and it blooms during the warmer months. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
