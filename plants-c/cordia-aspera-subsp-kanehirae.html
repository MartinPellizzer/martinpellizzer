Cordia aspera subsp. kanehirae

Cordia aspera subsp.

kanehirae, commonly known as the Kanhira cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
