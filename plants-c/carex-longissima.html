Carex longissima

Carex longissima, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex longissima prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
