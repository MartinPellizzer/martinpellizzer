Carolinea campestris

Carolinea campestris, commonly known as the field gentian, is a flowering plant belonging to the Gentianaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which typically bloom in late summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Carolinea campestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
