Chrysochlamys pavonii

Chrysochlamys pavonii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dazzling display during the flowering season. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Chrysochlamys pavonii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
