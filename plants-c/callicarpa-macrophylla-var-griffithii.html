Callicarpa macrophylla var. griffithii

Callicarpa macrophylla var. griffithii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to reddish berries that appear in clusters, which are a key feature of its botanical profile. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
