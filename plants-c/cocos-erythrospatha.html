Cocos erythrospatha

Cocos erythrospatha, commonly known as the red-spined coconut, is a unique member of the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces large, fibrous fruits that contain a single, large seed. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is adapted to coastal and saline environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in various tropical gardens and landscapes.
