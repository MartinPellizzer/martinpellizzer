Capsicum solanaceum var. macrophyllum

Capsicum solanaceum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant bell pepper, is a cultivar characterized by its large, thick-walled fruits.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with bushy foliage and stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and funnel-shaped, typical of the Solanaceae family, and it produces fruits that can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters.

This variety is prized for its robust growth and the size of its edible fruits, making it a popular choice for both home gardens and commercial cultivation.
