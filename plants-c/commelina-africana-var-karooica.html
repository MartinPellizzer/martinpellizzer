Commelina africana var. karooica

Commelina africana var. karooica, commonly known as the Karoo Commelina, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers with three petals and a distinctive three-lobed bract beneath them. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

This species is often found in rocky outcrops and open grasslands, where it plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
