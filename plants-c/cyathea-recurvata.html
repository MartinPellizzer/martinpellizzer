Cyathea recurvata

Cyathea recurvata, commonly known as the recurved tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like fronds that are often curved or recurved. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance in forest understories.

Cyathea recurvata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystems.
