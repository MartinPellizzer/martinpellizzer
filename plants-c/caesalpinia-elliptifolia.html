Caesalpinia elliptifolia

Caesalpinia elliptifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that turn from green to brown as they mature.

Caesalpinia elliptifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in various parts of the world.
