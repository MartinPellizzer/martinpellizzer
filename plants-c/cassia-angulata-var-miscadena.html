Cassia angulata var. miscadena

Cassia angulata var. miscadena, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous racemes of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces small, flat, winged pods that contain seeds, and its leaves are typically bipinnate, consisting of many small leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
