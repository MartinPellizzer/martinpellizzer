Chloris pectinata

Chloris pectinata, commonly known as the silver grass or silky grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its fine, silver-tomentose foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Chloris pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its distinctive appearance.
