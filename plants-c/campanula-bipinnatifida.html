Campanula bipinnatifida

Campanula bipinnatifida, commonly known as the double-lobed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are deeply lobed and pinnatifid, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
