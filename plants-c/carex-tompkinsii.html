Carex tompkinsii

Carex tompkinsii, commonly known as Tompkins' sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, grass-green leaves that form dense clumps.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets.
