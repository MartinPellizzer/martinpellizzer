Crepidium aphyllum

Crepidium aphyllum, commonly known as the leafless orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring primarily through its floral bracts. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and moderate humidity.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
