Cestrum calycinum var. tenuiflorum

Cestrum calycinum var. tenuiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that turn from green to red as they mature.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
