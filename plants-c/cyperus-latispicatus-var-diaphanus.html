Cyperus latispicatus var. diaphanus

Cyperus latispicatus var. diaphanus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other similar plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. It typically grows in wet or marshy areas, thriving in moist soil conditions.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.
