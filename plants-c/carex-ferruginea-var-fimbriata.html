Carex ferruginea var. fimbriata

Carex ferruginea var. fimbriata, commonly known as the red-sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variant is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are often hidden among its foliage.
