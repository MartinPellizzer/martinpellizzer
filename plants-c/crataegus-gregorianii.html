Crataegus gregorianii

Crataegus gregorianii, commonly known as the Armenian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountains of Armenia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates, making it a hardy and resilient species.
