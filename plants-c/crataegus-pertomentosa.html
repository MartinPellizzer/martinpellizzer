Crataegus pertomentosa

Crataegus pertomentosa, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northern United States and Canada.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red or yellow berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
