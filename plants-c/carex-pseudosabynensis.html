Carex pseudosabynensis

Carex pseudosabynensis, commonly known as the Florida sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has triangular stems and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, and prefers moist, acidic soils.

Carex pseudosabynensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
