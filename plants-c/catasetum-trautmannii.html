Catasetum trautmannii

Catasetum trautmannii, commonly known as the Trautmann's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with a specialized lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are pollinated by specific bee species, showcasing a fascinating example of co-evolution.

The orchid's botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
