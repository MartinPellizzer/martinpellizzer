Carex glomerata

Carex glomerata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown, and it often grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors.

Carex glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
