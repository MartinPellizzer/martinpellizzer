Calceolaria flabellifolia

Calceolaria flabellifolia, commonly known as the bellwether flower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that resemble small bells, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Calceolaria flabellifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
