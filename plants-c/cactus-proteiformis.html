Cactus proteiformis

Cactus proteiformis, commonly known as the variable cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Cactus proteiformis exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a unique subject for botanical study.
