Clethra incana

Clethra incana, commonly known as gray clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its grayish-white bark and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are hairy on both surfaces. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and streambanks.

Clethra incana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
