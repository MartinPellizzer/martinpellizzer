Centaurea urvillei subsp. nimrodis

Centaurea urvillei subsp.

nimrodis, commonly known as the Nimrod's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. The plant produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native range.
