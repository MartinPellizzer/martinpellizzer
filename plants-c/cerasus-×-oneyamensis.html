Cerasus × oneyamensis

Cerasus × oneyamensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Yamazakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is valued for its ornamental value, particularly its showy spring blossoms. The plant produces small, sweet fruits known as cherries, which are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cerasus × oneyamensis is also cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and public spaces.
