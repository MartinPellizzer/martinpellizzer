Croton scaber var. viridis

Croton scaber var. viridis, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive, thick, velvety leaves.

The leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy texture, and they have a unique appearance due to their serrated edges. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
