Cyphostemma allophylloides

Cyphostemma allophylloides, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by yellow, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
