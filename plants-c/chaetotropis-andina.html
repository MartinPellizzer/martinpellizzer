Chaetotropis andina

Chaetotropis andina, commonly known as the Andean hairroot, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its fibrous root system, which is often harvested for its medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
