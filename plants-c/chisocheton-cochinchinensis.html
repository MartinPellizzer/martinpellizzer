Chisocheton cochinchinensis

Chisocheton cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese beautyberry, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright purple, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
