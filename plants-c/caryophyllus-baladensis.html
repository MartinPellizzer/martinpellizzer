Caryophyllus baladensis

Caryophyllus baladensis, commonly known as the Balad carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

The Balad carnation is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both garden use and traditional medicine.
