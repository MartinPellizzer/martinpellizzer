Crotalaria inhabilis

Crotalaria inhabilis, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and palmately lobed leaves. Its yellow flowers are often accompanied by two small, spurred petals that attract pollinators.

Crotalaria inhabilis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
