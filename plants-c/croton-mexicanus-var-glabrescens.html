Croton mexicanus var. glabrescens

Croton mexicanus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, spiky fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
