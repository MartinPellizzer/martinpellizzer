Cardamine bellidifolia var. protractior

Cardamine bellidifolia var. protractior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watercress, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elliptical, glossy leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland edges. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisine, and it is valued for its peppery flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
