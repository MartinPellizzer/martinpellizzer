Cerastium lineare

Cerastium lineare, commonly known as the linear mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant features small, hairy leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Cerastium lineare is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet conditions.
