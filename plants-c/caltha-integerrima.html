Caltha integerrima

Caltha integerrima, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and has a short, upright stem. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and are surrounded by a ring of sepals.

Caltha integerrima thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.
