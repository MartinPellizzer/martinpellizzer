Cycnoches maculatum

Cycnoches maculatum, commonly known as the spotted cycnoches, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers with a distinctive spotted pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
