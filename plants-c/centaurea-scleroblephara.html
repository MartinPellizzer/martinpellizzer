Centaurea scleroblephara

Centaurea scleroblephara, commonly known as the woolly centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny leaves and vibrant purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its foliage is densely covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Centaurea scleroblephara is often found in open habitats such as coastal cliffs and dry grasslands.
