Crusea allococca

Crusea allococca, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking plant characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells.

Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Crusea allococca is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
