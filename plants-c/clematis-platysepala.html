Clematis platysepala

Clematis platysepala, commonly known as the flat-sepaled clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically woody and twining. Clematis platysepala thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
