Cirsium microcephalum

Cirsium microcephalum, commonly known as the small-headed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other flowering plants.

Cirsium microcephalum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
