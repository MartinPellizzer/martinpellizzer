Cacalia desertorum

Cacalia desertorum, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions through its deep root system. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Cacalia desertorum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
