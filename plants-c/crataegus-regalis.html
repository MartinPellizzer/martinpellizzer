Crataegus regalis

Crataegus regalis, commonly known as the royal hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are typically harvested in the fall. Crataegus regalis is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
