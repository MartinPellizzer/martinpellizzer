Castellia tuberculata

Castellia tuberculata, commonly known as the rough castellia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, tuberculate stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Castellia tuberculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
