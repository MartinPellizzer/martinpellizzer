Conophytum pellucidum var. neohallii

Conophytum pellucidum var. neohallii, commonly known as the translucent conophytum, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and adaptability to dry environments.
