Coelorachis parodiana

Coelorachis parodiana, commonly known as the Parodiana orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant blossoms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Coelorachis parodiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
