Carex subphacota

Carex subphacota, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to North America, it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
