Cheirostylis chinensis var. clibborndyeri

Cheirostylis chinensis var. clibborndyeri, commonly known as the Clibborndyer's orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to specific regions of China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that typically display shades of white and green. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding its survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulbs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
