Campanula polymorpha f. latifolia

Campanula polymorpha f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
