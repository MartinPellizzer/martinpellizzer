Cucurbita citrullus var. alba

Cucurbita citrullus var. alba, commonly known as the white muskmelon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white, sweet, and fragrant fruit with a distinctive netted rind. The plant is a trailing vine that produces both male and female flowers, with the female flowers developing into the edible fruit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its mild flavor and is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
