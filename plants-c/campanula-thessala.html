Campanula thessala

Campanula thessala, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
