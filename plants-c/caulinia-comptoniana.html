Caulinia comptoniana

Caulinia comptoniana, commonly known as the Compton's candelabra, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive candelabra-like growth habit, with multiple stems branching from a single point. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
