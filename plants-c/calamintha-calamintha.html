Calamintha calamintha

Calamintha calamintha, commonly known as caraway mint, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Calamintha calamintha is valued for its fragrant, mint-like aroma and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is also known for its historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
