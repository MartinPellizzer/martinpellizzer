Chrysanthemum hololeucum

Chrysanthemum hololeucum, commonly known as the white chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its large, white to pale yellow flowers and sturdy, upright stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
