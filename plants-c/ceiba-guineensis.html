Ceiba guineensis

Ceiba guineensis, commonly known as the African ceiba, is a large, fast-growing tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its buttressed roots and towering height, often reaching up to 30 meters. The tree produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark is thick and fibrous, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its timber and as a source of fiber.
