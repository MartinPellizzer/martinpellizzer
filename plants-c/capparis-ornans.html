Capparis ornans

Capparis ornans, commonly known as the showy caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually small, oval-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
