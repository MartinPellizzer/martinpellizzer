Cyperus fertilis

Cyperus fertilis, commonly known as the nut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has triangular stems and produces small, three-ranked leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. Its flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it is often found in wetland or disturbed soils.

Cyperus fertilis is an invasive species in many areas, often competing with native vegetation.
