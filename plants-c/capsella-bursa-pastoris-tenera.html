Capsella bursa-pastoris tenera

Capsella bursa-pastoris tenera, commonly known as the common velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and alternate, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into distinctive, inflated pods resembling a shepherd's purse. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various environments, contributing to its widespread distribution.
