Carex trachyantha

Carex trachyantha, commonly known as the rough-flowered sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to North America and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. Its roots help stabilize soil and improve water filtration in wetland ecosystems.

Carex trachyantha is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes used in restoration projects.
