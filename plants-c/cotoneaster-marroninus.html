Cotoneaster marroninus

Cotoneaster marroninus, commonly known as the orange cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange berries that persist through winter, adding visual interest to the landscape. The plant has small, oval leaves that turn red in the fall, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Cotoneaster marroninus is also valued for its ability to attract birds and its hardy nature in temperate climates.
