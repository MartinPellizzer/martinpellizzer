Cyperus mundii

Cyperus mundii, commonly known as the African bulrush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color.

Cyperus mundii is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
