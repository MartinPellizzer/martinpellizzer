Centropogon capitatus var. trichandrus

Centropogon capitatus var. trichandrus, commonly known as the slender panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface, and it has a fibrous root system.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a stabilizing plant in wetland restoration projects.
