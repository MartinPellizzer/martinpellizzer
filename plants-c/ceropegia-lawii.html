Ceropegia lawii

Ceropegia lawii, commonly known as the silver string plant, is a succulent belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, which are adapted for specific pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in hanging baskets.
