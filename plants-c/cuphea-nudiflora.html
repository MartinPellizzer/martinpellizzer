Cuphea nudiflora

Cuphea nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flowered cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually red or orange, with prominent stamens that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
