Cyperus cruciformis

Cyperus cruciformis, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are arranged in a distinctive cross shape, giving it its scientific name.

Cyperus cruciformis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
