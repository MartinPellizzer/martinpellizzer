Cavanilla vogeliana

Cavanilla vogeliana, commonly known as the Vogel's cavanilla, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to several feet in length, often with a glossy green surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, surrounded by a showy, colorful spathe.

Cavanilla vogeliana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for humid, shaded environments.
