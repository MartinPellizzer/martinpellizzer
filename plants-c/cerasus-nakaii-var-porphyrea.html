Cerasus nakaii var. porphyrea

Cerasus nakaii var. porphyrea, commonly known as the Porphyran cherry, is a variant of the Japanese cherry species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking deep red to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and a glossy green surface, providing an attractive backdrop to the showy blossoms.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
