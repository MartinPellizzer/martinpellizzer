Connarus suberosus

Connarus suberosus, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its leaves are typically alternate and bipinnate, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
