Cochlearia danica

Cochlearia danica, commonly known as the Danish cochlearia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to northern Europe and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a single, upright flowering stem. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in a raceme, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Cochlearia danica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
