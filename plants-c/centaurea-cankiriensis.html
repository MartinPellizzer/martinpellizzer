Centaurea cankiriensis

Centaurea cankiriensis, commonly known as the Cankiri thistle, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Centaurea cankiriensis is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
