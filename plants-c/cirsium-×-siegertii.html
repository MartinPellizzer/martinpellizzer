Cirsium × siegertii

Cirsium × siegertii, commonly known as the Siegert's thistle, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that resemble thistle blooms. Native to the United States, particularly in the western regions, it thrives in dry, disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is often found in grasslands, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.

Due to its aggressive growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a noxious weed in many areas.
