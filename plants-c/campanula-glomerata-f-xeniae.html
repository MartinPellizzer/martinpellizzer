Campanula glomerata f. xeniae

Campanula glomerata f. xeniae, commonly known as the xeniae bellflower, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the campanula genus.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of sky-blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant is a perennial herbaceous species native to specific regions in Europe, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine downy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
