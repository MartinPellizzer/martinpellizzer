Collinsia sparsiflora var. solitaria

Collinsia sparsiflora var. solitaria, commonly known as the solitary flaxflower, is a delicate annual plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
