Crocus biflorus subsp. artvinensis

Crocus biflorus subsp.

artvinensis, commonly known as the Artvin crocus, is a rare and endemic species native to the high-altitude regions of Turkey. It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which bloom in early spring, typically between March and April.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces two to three flowers per stem, which are often borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
