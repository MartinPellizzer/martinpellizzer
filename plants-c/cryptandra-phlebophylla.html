Cryptandra phlebophylla

Cryptandra phlebophylla, commonly known as the red-stemmed cryptandra, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale pink to white in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Cryptandra phlebophylla is well adapted to arid conditions and is an important component of local ecosystems.
