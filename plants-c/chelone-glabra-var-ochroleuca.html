Chelone glabra var. ochroleuca

Chelone glabra var. ochroleuca, commonly known as white hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in native plant gardens.
