Carex parryana var. hallii

Carex parryana var. hallii, commonly known as Parry's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its ecological importance, it is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
