Calathea chiriquensis

Calathea chiriquensis, commonly known as the Chiriqui calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, patterned foliage that often features a mix of green, silver, and pink hues. The leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a velvety texture that enhances its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant coloration.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic value and relatively easy care requirements.
