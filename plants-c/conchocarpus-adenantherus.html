Conchocarpus adenantherus

Conchocarpus adenantherus, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsules as its fruit, which contain seeds covered in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its attractive foliage and blooms.
