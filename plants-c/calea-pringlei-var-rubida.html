Calea pringlei var. rubida

Calea pringlei var. rubida, commonly known as the red-flowered calea, is a striking species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that resemble petals, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in native and drought-tolerant plants.
