Cerasus cyclamina

Cerasus cyclamina, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
