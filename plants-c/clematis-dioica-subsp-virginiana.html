Clematis dioica subsp. virginiana

Clematis dioica subsp.

virginiana, commonly known as the Virginian Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant produces small, green berries that ripen to red, containing seeds dispersed by birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
