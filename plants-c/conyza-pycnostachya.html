Conyza pycnostachya

Conyza pycnostachya, commonly known as rough hound's tongue, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a robust taproot and produces a dense cluster of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, contributing to its common name. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.

Conyza pycnostachya is sometimes considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
