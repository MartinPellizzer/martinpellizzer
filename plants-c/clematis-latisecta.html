Clematis latisecta

Clematis latisecta, commonly known as the broadleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis latisecta is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
