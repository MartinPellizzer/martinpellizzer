Cyphostemma cymosum subsp. orientale

Cyphostemma cymosum subsp.

orientale, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in cymes, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
