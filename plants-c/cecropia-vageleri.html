Cecropia vageleri

Cecropia vageleri, commonly known as the giant water willow, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant has a unique symbiotic relationship with ants, which inhabit the hollow stems and provide protection against herbivores. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize riverbanks and prevent erosion.

Cecropia vageleri is also notable for its ability to thrive in wetland environments, making it an important species in riparian ecosystems.
