Cinchona paniculata

Cinchona paniculata, commonly known as the white cinchona, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that contain elliptical seeds. It is notable for its ornamental value and historical significance in the cultivation of quinine.
