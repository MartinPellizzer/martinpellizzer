Citrus × bigaradia var. sinensis

Citrus × bigaradia var. sinensis, commonly known as the mandarin orange, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and segmented fruit with a thin, loose rind. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree or shrub, with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
