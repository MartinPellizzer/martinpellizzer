Cassia obtusata

Cassia obtusata, commonly known as sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, sickle-shaped pods. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. It produces yellow flowers and has bipinnate leaves with small, oval leaflets.

Cassia obtusata is an annual weed that can be invasive in certain regions.
