Cornus capitata var. hypoleuca

Cornus capitata var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white cornel.

This variant is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
