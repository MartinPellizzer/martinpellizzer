Centaurea ledebourii

Centaurea ledebourii, commonly known as the Ledebour's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea ledebourii is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
