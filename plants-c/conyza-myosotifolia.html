Conyza myosotifolia

Conyza myosotifolia, commonly known as blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Conyza myosotifolia is also known for its ability to produce a large number of lightweight seeds that are easily dispersed by the wind.
