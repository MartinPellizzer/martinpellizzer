Cerastium alsinifolium var. eglandulosum

Cerastium alsinifolium var. eglandulosum, commonly known as the sticky mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to gardens and a valuable component of natural ecosystems.
