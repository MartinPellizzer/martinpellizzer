Cyperus amuricus var. pterygorrachis

Cyperus amuricus var. pterygorrachis, commonly known as the Amur flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has slender, triangular stems and small, clustered spikes of inconspicuous flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
