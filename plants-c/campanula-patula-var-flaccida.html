Campanula patula var. flaccida

Campanula patula var. flaccida, commonly known as the drooping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its delicate, cascading growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
