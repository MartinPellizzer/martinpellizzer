Carex kraliana

Carex kraliana, commonly known as the Kral's sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Carex kraliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates.
