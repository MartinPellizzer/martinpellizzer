Clematis microphylla var. leptophylla

Clematis microphylla var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a desirable addition to shrub borders and woodland gardens.
