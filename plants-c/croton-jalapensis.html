Croton jalapensis

Croton jalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
