Croton pigmentarius

Croton pigmentarius, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant leaf coloration and adaptability to various growing conditions.
