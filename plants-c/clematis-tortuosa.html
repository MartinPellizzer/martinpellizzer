Clematis tortuosa

Clematis tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and distinctive, twisting stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Clematis tortuosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and requires support for its climbing habit.
