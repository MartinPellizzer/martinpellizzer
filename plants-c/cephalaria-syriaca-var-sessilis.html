Cephalaria syriaca var. sessilis

Cephalaria syriaca var. sessilis, commonly known as the sessile cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural collections.
