Cleistocactus morawetzianus var. pycnacanthus

Cleistocactus morawetzianus var. pycnacanthus, commonly known as the dense-spined cleistocactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing cactus with a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, sharp spines. The plant produces flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming in the evening. It is well adapted to desert conditions, storing water in its thick stem and having a shallow root system.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
