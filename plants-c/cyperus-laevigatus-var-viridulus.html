Cyperus laevigatus var. viridulus

Cyperus laevigatus var. viridulus, commonly known as the green flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy, with a distinctive appearance that resembles a sedges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist soil conditions and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
