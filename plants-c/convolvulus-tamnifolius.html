Convolvulus tamnifolius

Convolvulus tamnifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple or blue. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, often growing in dry, rocky soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
