Cercodia racemosa

Cercodia racemosa, commonly known as the clustered broomrape, is a parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are often inconspicuous. Its root system consists of haustoria, specialized structures that penetrate the roots of host plants.
