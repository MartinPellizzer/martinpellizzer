Calliandra affinis

Calliandra affinis, commonly known as the white calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are often arranged in spherical clusters. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, rounded leaflets.

Calliandra affinis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.
