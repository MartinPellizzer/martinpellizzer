Carex mairiana

Carex mairiana, commonly known as Mair's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex mairiana is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
