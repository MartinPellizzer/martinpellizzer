Chresta lanceolata

Chresta lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf chresta, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland landscapes.
