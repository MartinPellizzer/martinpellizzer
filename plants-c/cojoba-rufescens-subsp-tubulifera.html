Cojoba rufescens subsp. tubulifera

Cojoba rufescens subsp.

tubulifera, commonly known as the tubular cojoba, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible and often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

The subspecies is notable for its distinctive tubular flower structure, which aids in pollination by specific insect species.
