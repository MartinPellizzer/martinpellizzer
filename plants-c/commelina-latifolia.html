Commelina latifolia

Commelina latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, often accompanied by two white bracts. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina latifolia is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a valuable ornamental and a potential weed.
