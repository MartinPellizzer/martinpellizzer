Carex scirpiformis

Carex scirpiformis, commonly known as the slender flat-sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences of greenish-brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along the edges of ponds.

Carex scirpiformis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
