Cirsium xanthacanthum

Cirsium xanthacanthum, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, purple-tinged leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cirsium xanthacanthum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
