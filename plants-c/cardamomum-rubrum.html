Cardamomum rubrum

Cardamomum rubrum, commonly known as the red cardamom, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink to red in color. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule containing aromatic, dark brown seeds.

The plant is valued for its strong, spicy flavor and is widely used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
