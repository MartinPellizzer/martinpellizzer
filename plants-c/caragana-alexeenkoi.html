Caragana alexeenkoi

Caragana alexeenkoi, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to central Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow flowers in early summer. The plant is adapted to harsh climates and is often found in dry, sandy soils. Its fruit is a legume that contains edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine.

Caragana alexeenkoi is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a source of forage for livestock.
