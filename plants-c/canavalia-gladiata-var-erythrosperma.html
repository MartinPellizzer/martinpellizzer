Canavalia gladiata var. erythrosperma

Canavalia gladiata var. erythrosperma, commonly known as the red-seeded cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, slender, often hairy pods. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers, which are followed by elongated, red-brown pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional seeds and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
