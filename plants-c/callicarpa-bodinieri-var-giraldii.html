Callicarpa bodinieri var. giraldii

Callicarpa bodinieri var. giraldii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant violet to purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife.
