Crataegus hispanica

Crataegus hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters, featuring thorny branches and oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
