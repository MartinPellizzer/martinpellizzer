Cyphostemma bullatum

Cyphostemma bullatum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems with distinctive, warty tubercles and produces small, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry that contains several seeds.

Cyphostemma bullatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
