Cucurbita moschata var. vulgaris

Cucurbita moschata var. vulgaris, commonly known as the pumpkin, is a widely cultivated species within the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, oval-shaped fruits that vary in color from orange to white or green. The plant produces tendrils and lobed leaves, with yellow flowers that are either male or female. Its fruit is rich in nutrients, including beta-carotene, vitamins, and dietary fiber.

The plant thrives in warm climates and is often grown for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
