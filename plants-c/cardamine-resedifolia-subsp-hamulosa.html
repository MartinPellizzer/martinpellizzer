Cardamine resedifolia subsp. hamulosa

Cardamine resedifolia subsp.

hamulosa, commonly known as the hooked watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, branching stems covered with small, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in a single umbel, and it produces small, round seeds.

The species is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and preference for wet, nutrient-rich soils.
