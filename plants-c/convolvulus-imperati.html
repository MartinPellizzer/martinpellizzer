Convolvulus imperati

Convolvulus imperati, commonly known as the imperial bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a nuisance in certain regions.
