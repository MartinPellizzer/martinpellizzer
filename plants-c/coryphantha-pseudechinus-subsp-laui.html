Coryphantha pseudechinus subsp. laui

Coryphantha pseudechinus subsp.

laui, commonly known as the Laui's hedgehog cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features a globular or cylindrical stem that is typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, edible fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
