Cercidium andicola var. petiolatum

Cercidium andicola var. petiolatum, commonly known as the petioled creosote bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically sessile or have short petioles, distinguishing it from other varieties of the species.

This plant is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing desert soils.
