Carissa diffusa

Carissa diffusa, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring dense, thorny branches and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. Its leaves and fruits contain toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.

Carissa diffusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
