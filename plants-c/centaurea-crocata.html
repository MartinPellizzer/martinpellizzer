Centaurea crocata

Centaurea crocata, commonly known as the Spanish redweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. The plant has spiny, bristly stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as rocky soils and coastal areas.

Centaurea crocata is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its hardy, resilient nature.
