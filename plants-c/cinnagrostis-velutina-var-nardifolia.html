Cinnagrostis velutina var. nardifolia

Cinnagrostis velutina var. nardifolia, commonly known as the woolly goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive velvety texture, and its yellow flowers bloom in late summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators and as a component of local ecosystems.
