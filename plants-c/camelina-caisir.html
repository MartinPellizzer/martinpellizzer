Camelina caisir

Camelina caisir, commonly known as false flax, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, sunny environments. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs.

Camelina caisir is often cultivated for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in both culinary and industrial applications.
