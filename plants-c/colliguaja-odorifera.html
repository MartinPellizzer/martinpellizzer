Colliguaja odorifera

Colliguaja odorifera, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

The flowers emit a sweet, spicy aroma, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths.
