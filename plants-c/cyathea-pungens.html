Cyathea pungens

Cyathea pungens, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth rate and long lifespan. Its rhizome system supports the growth of the trunk and roots, while the fronds are responsible for photosynthesis.

Cyathea pungens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
