Capsella alandica

Capsella alandica, commonly known as the alpine catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early summer.

Its leaves are hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it to retain moisture in its harsh environment.
