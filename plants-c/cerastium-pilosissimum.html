Cerastium pilosissimum

Cerastium pilosissimum, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes other flowering plants such as carnations and chickweed. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are densely covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals, and bloom in early spring.

Cerastium pilosissimum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
