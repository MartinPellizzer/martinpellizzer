Caucalis neglecta

Caucalis neglecta, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are usually linear or lance-shaped. Caucalis neglecta is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in stabilizing soil.
