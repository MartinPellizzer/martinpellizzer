Clowesia

Clowesia, commonly known as the "Garden Balm," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It consists of a single species, Clowesia serotina, which is native to the western United States. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Clowesia thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
