Ceropegia barberae

Ceropegia barberae, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and succulent stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia barberae is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
