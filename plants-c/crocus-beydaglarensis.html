Crocus beydaglarensis

Crocus beydaglarensis, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Crocus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late winter or early spring. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
