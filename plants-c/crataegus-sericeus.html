Crataegus sericeus

Crataegus sericeus, commonly known as silver hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Crataegus sericeus typically grows in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and hedgerows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
