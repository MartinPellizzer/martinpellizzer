Capparis pauciflora

Capparis pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered caper, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Capparis pauciflora is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can withstand periods of drought.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
