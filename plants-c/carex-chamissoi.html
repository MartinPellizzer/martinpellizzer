Carex chamissoi

Carex chamissoi, commonly known as Chamisso's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their distinctive three-ranked leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex chamissoi is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity in wetland ecosystems.
