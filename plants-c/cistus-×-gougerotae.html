Cistus × gougerotae

Cistus × gougerotae, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal remedies.
