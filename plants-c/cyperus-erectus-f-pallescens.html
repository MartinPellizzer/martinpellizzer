Cyperus erectus f. pallescens

Cyperus erectus f. pallescens, commonly known as the pale flatsedge, is a variant of the common flatsedge.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, pale green leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually borne on short, leaf-like bracts.

This subspecies is notable for its pale coloration and adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
