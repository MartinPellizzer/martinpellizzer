Carex divulsa var. pairae

Carex divulsa var. pairae, commonly known as the paired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized flowering structures called spikelets. The plant typically grows in dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are usually borne on slender, branching stems.

This variety is distinguished by its paired or closely spaced inflorescences, giving it its specific epithet "pairae.".
