Copaifera guyanensis

Copaifera guyanensis, commonly known as Guianese copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and neighboring regions.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and large, compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces a valuable resin, known as copaiba balsam, which has been used for centuries for its aromatic and medicinal properties. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Copaifera guyanensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
