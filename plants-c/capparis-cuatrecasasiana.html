Capparis cuatrecasasiana

Capparis cuatrecasasiana, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas's caper, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which includes various species known for their distinctive, often spiny structures. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a strong, pungent odor.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
