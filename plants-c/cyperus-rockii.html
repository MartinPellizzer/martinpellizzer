Cyperus rockii

Cyperus rockii, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its appearance is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers.

Cyperus rockii is often used in landscaping for its ability to stabilize soil and its ornamental value.
