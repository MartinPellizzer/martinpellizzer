Carex douglasii var. laxiflora

Carex douglasii var. laxiflora, commonly known as Douglas's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
