Chlamydojatropha kamerunica

Chlamydojatropha kamerunica, commonly known as the Cameroon jatropha, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces yellow flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its fruit is a capsule that contains toxic seeds, which are not suitable for human consumption.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to dry conditions.
