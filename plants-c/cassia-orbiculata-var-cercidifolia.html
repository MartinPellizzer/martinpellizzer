Cassia orbiculata var. cercidifolia

Cassia orbiculata var. cercidifolia, commonly known as the flame tree or flame of the woods, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and often reddish in color. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers that are clustered in racemes, blooming in the summer months. Its seeds are encased in flat, leathery pods that split open when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
