Carex subloliacea

Carex subloliacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that consist of numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex subloliacea is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
