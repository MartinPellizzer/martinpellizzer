Cotoneaster pseudoambiguus

Cotoneaster pseudoambiguus, commonly known as the Chinese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Cotoneaster pseudoambiguus is also valued for its role in wildlife habitats, providing food for birds and small mammals.
