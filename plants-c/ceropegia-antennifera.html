Ceropegia antennifera

Ceropegia antennifera, commonly known as the Cape Violets, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive corona structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia antennifera is popular among succulent enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
