Cirsium paniculatum

Cirsium paniculatum, commonly known as the tall thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, purple to pinkish-blue flowering heads. The plant produces both ray and disk flowers, with the ray flowers typically being the most prominent. Its leaves are usually spiny and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Cirsium paniculatum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.
