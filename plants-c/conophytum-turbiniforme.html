Conophytum turbiniforme

Conophytum turbiniforme, commonly known as the Turbaned Cactus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy stems that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Conophytum turbiniforme is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
