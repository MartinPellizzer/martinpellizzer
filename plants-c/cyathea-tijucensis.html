Cyathea tijucensis

Cyathea tijucensis, commonly known as the Tijuca fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a vital role in the ecosystem as a habitat for various organisms.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species in its native range.
