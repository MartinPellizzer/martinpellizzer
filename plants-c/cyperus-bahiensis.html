Cyperus bahiensis

Cyperus bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to South America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and flooded grasslands. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Cyperus bahiensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
