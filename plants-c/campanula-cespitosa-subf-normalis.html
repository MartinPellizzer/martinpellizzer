Campanula cespitosa subf. normalis

Campanula cespitosa subf.

normalis, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is valued for its attractive blooms and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
