Cleome erosa subsp. breviglandulosa

Cleome erosa subsp.

breviglandulosa, commonly known as the narrow-glandular cleome, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread in suitable habitats.

It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun.
