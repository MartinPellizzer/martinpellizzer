Cirsium aytatchii

Cirsium aytatchii, commonly known as the Aytatchi thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cirsium aytatchii is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
