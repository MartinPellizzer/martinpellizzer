Crataegus roanensis var. heidelbergensis

Crataegus roanensis var. heidelbergensis, commonly known as the Heidelberg hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the European region, particularly found in Germany and surrounding areas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and dense foliage. The plant produces white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or scrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
