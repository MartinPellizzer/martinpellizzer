Cheiranthus pallasii

Cheiranthus pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas's honesty, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant produces small, white flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a dry, segmented pod that splits open to release the seeds.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and slightly hairy, with a distinctive blue-green color.
