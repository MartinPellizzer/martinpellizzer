Culcasia sapinii

Culcasia sapinii, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in diameter, making it a prominent feature in wetland ecosystems. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its broad leaves provide habitat for various aquatic organisms and help in water filtration.

Culcasia sapinii plays an important ecological role in maintaining the balance of wetland environments.
