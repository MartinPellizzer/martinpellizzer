Crotalaria similis

Crotalaria similis, commonly known as the small false morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with two lobes. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional medicinal practices.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural studies.
