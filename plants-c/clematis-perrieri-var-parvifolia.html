Clematis perrieri var. parvifolia

Clematis perrieri var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features slender, twining stems that climb by means of small, hooked tendrils. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with small, ovate leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
