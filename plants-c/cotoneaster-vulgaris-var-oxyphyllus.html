Cotoneaster vulgaris var. oxyphyllus

Cotoneaster vulgaris var. oxyphyllus, commonly known as the red-leaf cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, red-orange berries that appear in the fall. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy, dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly for its vibrant foliage and winter interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
