Crataegus helenae

Crataegus helenae, commonly known as Helen's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges.

Helen's hawthorn is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
