Campanula skanderbegii

Campanula skanderbegii, commonly known as the Skanderbeg bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are sturdy and branched.

Campanula skanderbegii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
