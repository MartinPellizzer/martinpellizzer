Cyphostemma stenolobum

Cyphostemma stenolobum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and pairs of opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cyphostemma stenolobum is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
