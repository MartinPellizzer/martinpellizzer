Chloris texensis

Chloris texensis, commonly known as Texas dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and fine, blue-green foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer, which are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Texas dropseed is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
