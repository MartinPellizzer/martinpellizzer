Chaetocyperus emarginatus

Chaetocyperus emarginatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in saturated soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctively pointed tip, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are followed by triangular, three-angled seeds.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in disturbed habitats and can be an indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
