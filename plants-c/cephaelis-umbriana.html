Cephaelis umbriana

Cephaelis umbriana, commonly known as the darkleaf celandine, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a dark green hue and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its delicate blooms.

Cephaelis umbriana is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
