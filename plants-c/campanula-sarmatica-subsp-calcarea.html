Campanula sarmatica subsp. calcarea

Campanula sarmatica subsp.

calcarea, commonly known as the limestone bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
