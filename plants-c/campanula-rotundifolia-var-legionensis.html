Campanula rotundifolia var. legionensis

Campanula rotundifolia var. legionensis, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its round, broad leaves and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is a perennial species that spreads via rhizomes, forming dense mats in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
