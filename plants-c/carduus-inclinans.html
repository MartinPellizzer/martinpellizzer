Carduus inclinans

Carduus inclinans, commonly known as the leaning thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, slightly zigzagged edge.

The species is often found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in full sun.
