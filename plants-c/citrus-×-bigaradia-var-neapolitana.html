Citrus × bigaradia var. neapolitana

Citrus × bigaradia var. neapolitana, commonly known as the Neapolitan lemon, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from southern Italy.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits with a thick, aromatic rind. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is prized for its high acidity and is often used in culinary applications.

The Neapolitan lemon is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
