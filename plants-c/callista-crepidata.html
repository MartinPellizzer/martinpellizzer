Callista crepidata

Callista crepidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features aromatic, dark green leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Callista crepidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
