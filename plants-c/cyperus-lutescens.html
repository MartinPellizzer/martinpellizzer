Cyperus lutescens

Cyperus lutescens, commonly known as yellow papyrus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in wetlands, ponds, and along waterways.

Cyperus lutescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a water purifier.
