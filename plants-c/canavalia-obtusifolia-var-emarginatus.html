Canavalia obtusifolia var. emarginatus

Canavalia obtusifolia var. emarginatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with lanceolate leaflets and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both food and forage.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable crop in sustainable agricultural systems.
