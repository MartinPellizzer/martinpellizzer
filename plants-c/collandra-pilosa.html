Collandra pilosa

Collandra pilosa, commonly known as the hairy collandra, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, which contribute to its common name.

Collandra pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
