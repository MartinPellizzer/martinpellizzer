Capsicum annuum subsp. abbreviatum

Capsicum annuum subsp.

abbreviatum, commonly known as the dwarf chili pepper, is a compact variety of the common chili plant. It is characterized by its small, rounded fruits that typically range in color from red to yellow.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild to medium heat levels. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant's botanical features include hairy stems and lobed leaves, typical of the Capsicum genus.
