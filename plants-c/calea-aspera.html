Calea aspera

Calea aspera, commonly known as the rough calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Calea aspera is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
