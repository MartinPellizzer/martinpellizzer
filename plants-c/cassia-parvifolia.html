Cassia parvifolia

Cassia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences, which consist of clusters of small, delicate flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia parvifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
