Centaurea bombycina subsp. bombycina

Centaurea bombycina subsp.

bombycina, commonly known as the cotton thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features erect, sparsely branched stems that are typically hairy and covered in fine, white to pale blue hairs.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower heads that are surrounded by a dense, fluffy pappus, giving it a cotton-like appearance. Its leaves are usually lanceolate, with a bluish-gray underside and a serrated margin.

This species is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
