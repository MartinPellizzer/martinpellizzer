Campanula ovacikensis

Campanula ovacikensis, commonly known as the Ovacik bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Campanula ovacikensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
