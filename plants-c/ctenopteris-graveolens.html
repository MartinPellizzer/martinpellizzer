Ctenopteris graveolens

Ctenopteris graveolens, commonly known as the fragrant sea palm, is a species of fern native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is typically found growing near the coast or in shaded, moist areas. Its leaves emit a mild, aromatic scent, which is believed to have therapeutic properties.

Ctenopteris graveolens is valued for its resilience and ability to adapt to saline conditions, making it a unique addition to coastal ecosystems.
