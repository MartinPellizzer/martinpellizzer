Carduus acanthoides subsp. multiflorus

Carduus acanthoides subsp.

multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features a robust, upright stem covered with spiny leaves that are typically blue-gray in color.

The plant produces dense clusters of purple to pink flowers during the summer months, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are composed of many small florets.

This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
