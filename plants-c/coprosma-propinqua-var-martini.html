Coprosma propinqua var. martini

Coprosma propinqua var. martini, commonly known as the Titi Island coprosma, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Oceania.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in coastal and lowland environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
