Clusia portlandiana

Clusia portlandiana, commonly known as the Portland glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to humid, warm climates and thrives in well-drained soil.

Clusia portlandiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and continuous flowering.
