Cucurbita pinnatifida

Cucurbita pinnatifida, commonly known as the Chinese pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and vibrant, orange to yellow fruits. The plant is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit's skin is thick and can be used in traditional Chinese medicine and cooking.
