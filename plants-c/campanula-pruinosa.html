Campanula pruinosa

Campanula pruinosa, commonly known as the powdered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, grayish leaves that give it a powdery appearance, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Campanula pruinosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
