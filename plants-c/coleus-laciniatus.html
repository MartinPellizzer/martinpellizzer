Coleus laciniatus

Coleus laciniatus, commonly known as the shredded leaf coleus, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with deeply lobed, shredded leaves that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, tubular flowers in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vivid leaf colors.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in garden borders and as a houseplant.
