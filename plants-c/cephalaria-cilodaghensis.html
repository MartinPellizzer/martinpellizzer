Cephalaria cilodaghensis

Cephalaria cilodaghensis, commonly known as the Cilodagh heath, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

The flowers, which appear in the summer, are small and yellowish, contributing to its role in local pollination systems.
