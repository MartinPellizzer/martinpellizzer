Capsicum annuum var. subconicum

Capsicum annuum var. subconicum, commonly known as the horn pepper, is a variant of the common chili pepper characterized by its elongated, horn-like shape.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family and is native to Central and South America. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow, and the fruit develops from a single ovary, forming a distinctive elongated pod.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and mild to medium heat level in culinary applications.
