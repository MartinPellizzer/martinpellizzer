Cardamine flexuosoides var. glabricaulis

Cardamine flexuosoides var. glabricaulis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed water cress, is a herbaceous perennial native to moist, temperate regions.

It features slender, upright stems that are glabrous, or hairless, and are often submerged or partially submerged in water. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and have a peppery taste, making it a valued edible plant.

It thrives in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often found along stream banks and in wet meadows.
