Cyathea leucostegia

Cyathea leucostegia, commonly known as the white-stemmed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a smooth, pale, and often whitish or cream-colored rind. The plant produces large, pinnately divided fronds that can reach several meters in length, giving it a dramatic and elegant appearance. Cyathea leucostegia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a striking addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
