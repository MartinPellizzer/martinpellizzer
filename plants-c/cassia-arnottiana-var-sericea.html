Cassia arnottiana var. sericea

Cassia arnottiana var. sericea, commonly known as silver cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage.
