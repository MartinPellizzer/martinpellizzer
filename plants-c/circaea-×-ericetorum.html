Circaea × ericetorum

Circaea × ericetorum, commonly known as the common cranberry shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic soils and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, while its berries are small, red, and edible.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
