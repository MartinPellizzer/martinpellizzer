Cineraria scandens

Cineraria scandens, commonly known as the climbing cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises. Its leaves are typically silver or gray-green, with a fuzzy texture, and it produces showy blue or purple flowers.

Cineraria scandens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
