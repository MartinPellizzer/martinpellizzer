Cylista pycnostachya

Cylista pycnostachya, commonly known as the dense-stamen cylista, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Cylista pycnostachya is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
