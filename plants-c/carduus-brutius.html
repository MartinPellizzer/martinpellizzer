Carduus brutius

Carduus brutius, commonly known as the greater centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a sturdy, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in rough, hairy leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly bitter taste, while its roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Carduus brutius is often found in dry grasslands, rocky soils, and along roadsides.
