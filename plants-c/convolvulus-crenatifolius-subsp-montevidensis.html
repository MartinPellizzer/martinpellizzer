Convolvulus crenatifolius subsp. montevidensis

Convolvulus crenatifolius subsp.

montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and blue.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky soils. It is an annual herb that thrives in warm, sunny conditions.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
