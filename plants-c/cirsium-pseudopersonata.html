Cirsium pseudopersonata

Cirsium pseudopersonata, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has deeply lobed, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
