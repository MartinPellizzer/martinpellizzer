Carduus schimperi

Carduus schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a dense, spherical cluster.

Carduus schimperi is known for its resilience and is often found in regions with limited rainfall.
