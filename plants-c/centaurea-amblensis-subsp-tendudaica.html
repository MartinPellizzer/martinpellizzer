Centaurea amblensis subsp. tendudaica

Centaurea amblensis subsp.

tendudaica, commonly known as the narrowleaf starthistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a taproot system and produces small, bract-like leaves that are narrow and pointed.

Due to its invasive tendencies in some regions, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
