Citrus medica var. digitata

Citrus medica var. digitata, commonly known as the shaddock or Chinese grapefruit, is a citrus species characterized by its elongated, segmented fruit.

The plant is a small to medium-sized tree with thick, rough bark and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the distinctive, segmented fruit. The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and segmented pulp, which can be sweet or sour depending on the variety.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
