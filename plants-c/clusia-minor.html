Clusia minor

Clusia minor, commonly known as the little glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the year. Clusia minor is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It is also noted for its tolerance to urban conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
