Carex laxiflora var. latifolia

Carex laxiflora var. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three ranks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
