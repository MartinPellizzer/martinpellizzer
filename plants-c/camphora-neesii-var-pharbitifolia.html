Camphora neesii var. pharbitifolia

Camphora neesii var. pharbitifolia, commonly known as the Chinese camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark containing camphor. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, red berries.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
