Carthamus plumosus

Carthamus plumosus, commonly known as the silky thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces fluffy, white to grayish seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Carthamus plumosus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and unique foliage.
