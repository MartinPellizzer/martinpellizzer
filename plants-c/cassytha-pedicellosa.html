Cassytha pedicellosa

Cassytha pedicellosa, commonly known as the creeping grapevine, is a tropical vine native to regions of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, serpentine stems that can grow along the ground or climb other vegetation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually oval in shape, with a slightly hairy surface.

Cassytha pedicellosa is also known for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
