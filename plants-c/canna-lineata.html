Canna lineata

Canna lineata, commonly known as the red canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, and its showy, orange-red flower spikes that appear in summer. The plant is a member of the Cannaceae family and thrives in moist, sunny environments. It is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and floral display in gardens and water features.

Canna lineata is relatively low maintenance and can tolerate a variety of soil types once established.
