Capparis rotundifolia

Capparis rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible buds that are often used in culinary applications, similar to other caper species. It thrives in rocky or coastal areas and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Its botanical features include a woody stem and a distinctive, fragrant aroma.
