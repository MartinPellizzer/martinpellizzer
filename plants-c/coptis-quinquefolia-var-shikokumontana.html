Coptis quinquefolia var. shikokumontana

Coptis quinquefolia var. shikokumontana, commonly known as the Japanese goldthread, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features a rhizomatous root system and produces clusters of small, yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with three to five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its alkaloid content.
