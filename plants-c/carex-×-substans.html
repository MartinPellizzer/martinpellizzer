Carex × substans

Carex × substans, commonly known as the common sedge.

It is a hybrid species within the Carex genus, characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats, contributing to ecological diversity in these environments.
