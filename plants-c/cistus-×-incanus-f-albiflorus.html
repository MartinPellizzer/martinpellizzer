Cistus × incanus f. albiflorus

Cistus × incanus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grey-green leaves and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
