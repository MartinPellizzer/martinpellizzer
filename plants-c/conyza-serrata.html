Conyza serrata

Conyza serrata, commonly known as the roughleaf horseweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are serrated and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Conyza serrata produces small white to yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
