Cleome minima

Cleome minima, commonly known as dwarf spiderflower, is a small annual plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a compact addition to gardens. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

Cleome minima thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate floral display.
