Cyperus mossii

Cyperus mossii, commonly known as the African umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or purple in color, arranged in spikelets. Cyperus mossii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in humid climates.
