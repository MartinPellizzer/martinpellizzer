Cattleya walkeriana subsp. princeps

Cattleya walkeriana subsp.

princeps, commonly known as the Prince's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant produces pendulous blooms with a strong, sweet fragrance, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

This subspecies is prized for its beauty and is often used in floral arrangements and as a decorative plant.
