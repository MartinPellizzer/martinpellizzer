Crassula minuta

Crassula minuta, commonly known as the baby crassula or dwarf crassula, is a small, compact succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, round leaves that are typically green with a slight blue or purple tint, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Crassula minuta is popular as a low-maintenance houseplant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
