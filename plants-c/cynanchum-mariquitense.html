Cynanchum mariquitense

Cynanchum mariquitense, commonly known as the Philippine milkweed, is a climbing plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its milky sap contains latex, which is a common feature among milkweeds.

Cynanchum mariquitense is often found in forested areas and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
