Crataegus punctata

Crataegus punctata, commonly known as the dotted hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red-orange berries in the fall. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn. The plant is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along streams.

It has historical uses in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
