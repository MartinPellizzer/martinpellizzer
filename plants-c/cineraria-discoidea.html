Cineraria discoidea

Cineraria discoidea, commonly known as the cushion cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with dense, woolly leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to botanical collections.
