Cryptocarya ferrea var. ferrea

Cryptocarya ferrea var. ferrea, commonly known as ironwood or Brazilian ironwood, is a dense, evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.
