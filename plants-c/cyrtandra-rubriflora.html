Cyrtandra rubriflora

Cyrtandra rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered Cyrtandra, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its striking red petals that contrast with its green foliage. It typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in moist, shaded forest understories.

Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
