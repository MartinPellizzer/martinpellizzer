Carex buxbaumii var. submutica

Carex buxbaumii var. submutica, commonly known as the rough-skinned sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically rough to the touch, with a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
