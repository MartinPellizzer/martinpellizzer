Camellia thea var. stricta

Camellia thea var. stricta, commonly known as the Chinese tea plant, is a species native to eastern Asia.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant is cultivated for its leaves, which are used to produce various types of tea. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and economically significant.
