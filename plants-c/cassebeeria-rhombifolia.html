Cassebeeria rhombifolia

Cassebeeria rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhomboid leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant has elliptic to rhomboid-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
