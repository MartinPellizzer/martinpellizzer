Cucurbita maxima var. turbanula

Cucurbita maxima var. turbanula, commonly known as the turban squash, is a variant of the pumpkin species characterized by its unique, turban-shaped fruit.

The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, known for its climbing habit and large, lobed leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow, with both male and female flowers present on the same plant. The fruit develops from the female flowers and can vary in color from green to orange or red depending on maturity.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
