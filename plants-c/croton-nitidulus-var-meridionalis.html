Croton nitidulus var. meridionalis

Croton nitidulus var. meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern Croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a range of hues including red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
