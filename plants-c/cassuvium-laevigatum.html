Cassuvium laevigatum

Cassuvium laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth-surfaced cassuvium, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor firmly in rocky soils.

Due to its unique adaptations, Cassuvium laevigatum is considered an important species for ecological studies in alpine ecosystems.
