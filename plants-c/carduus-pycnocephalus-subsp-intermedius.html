Carduus pycnocephalus subsp. intermedius

Carduus pycnocephalus subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, upright stem that bears a dense cluster of purple to pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
