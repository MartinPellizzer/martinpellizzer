Calothamnus

Calothamnus, commonly known as the emu bush, is a genus of flowering plants native to Australia.

These shrubs typically feature narrow, needle-like leaves and produce showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The flowers are often clustered in spikes and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Calothamnus species are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
