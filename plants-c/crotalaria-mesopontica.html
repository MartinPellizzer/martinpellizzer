Crotalaria mesopontica

Crotalaria mesopontica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers with a spurred corolla. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several angular, ridged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Crotalaria mesopontica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
