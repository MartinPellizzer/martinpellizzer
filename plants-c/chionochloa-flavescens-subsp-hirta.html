Chionochloa flavescens subsp. hirta

Chionochloa flavescens subsp.

hirta, commonly known as the hairy golden grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spikelet that is often hidden within the foliage.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
