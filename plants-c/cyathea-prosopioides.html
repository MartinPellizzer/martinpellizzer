Cyathea prosopioides

Cyathea prosopioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes produce roots that help anchor the plant and absorb nutrients from the soil.

Cyathea prosopioides plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
