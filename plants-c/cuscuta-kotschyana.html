Cuscuta kotschyana

Cuscuta kotschyana, commonly known as the desert dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a rapid growth pattern, often covering its host with a dense network of tendrils. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions, including parts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through mechanical means, allowing it to colonize new areas effectively.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops and native vegetation.
