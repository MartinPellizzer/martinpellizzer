Cheiridopsis purpurascens var. leipoldtii

Cheiridopsis purpurascens var. leipoldtii, commonly known as the Leipoldt's ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a dense mat, and it produces small, pink to purple flowers during the winter months. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves to survive dry periods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant.
