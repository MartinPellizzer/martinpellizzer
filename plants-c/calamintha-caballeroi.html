Calamintha caballeroi

Calamintha caballeroi, commonly known as Mexican catmint, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their mint-like scent and mild medicinal properties.

Calamintha caballeroi is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a suitable plant for xeriscaping.
