Cnidium apioides subsp. orientale

Cnidium apioides subsp.

orientale, commonly known as oriental hogweed, is a biennial or perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered with coarse hairs.

The plant produces large, umbrella-shaped clusters of small white flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive, strong odor.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and in open woodlands.
