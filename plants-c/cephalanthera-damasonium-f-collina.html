Cephalanthera damasonium f. collina

Cephalanthera damasonium f. collina, commonly known as the alpine lady's tresses, is a rare and beautiful orchid species characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a short, fleshy rhizome and a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a spike of pale green to white flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in cool, moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forest floors.

This subspecies is particularly adapted to high-altitude regions and is often found in the alpine zones of Europe.
