Chondrilla crepidinea

Chondrilla crepidinea, commonly known as the crested goatweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It has a distinctive appearance with a rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions with a deep root system and succulent foliage for water storage.

Chondrilla crepidinea is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
