Cyperus sharonensis

Cyperus sharonensis, commonly known as Sharon sedge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Sharon sedge has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native environment.
