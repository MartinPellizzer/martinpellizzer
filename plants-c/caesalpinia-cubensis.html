Caesalpinia cubensis

Caesalpinia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban candlewood, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bright red bracts that resemble candles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its compound leaves are pinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and it has a woody stem that becomes more prominent with age.

Caesalpinia cubensis is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, well-drained soils.
