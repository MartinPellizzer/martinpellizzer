Coleus apricus

Coleus apricus, commonly known as the golden flame coleus, is a vibrant tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of gold, red, and orange. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its showy, small tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in garden settings.
