Cubitanthus

Cubitanthus, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves and produces showy, yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive cup-like shape. The plant is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its growth habit is usually compact, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Cubitanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
