Chaenorhinum grandiflorum var. formenterae

Chaenorhinum grandiflorum var. formenterae, commonly known as the Formentera bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and coastal landscapes.
