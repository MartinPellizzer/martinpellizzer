Colchicum melanthioides

Colchicum melanthioides, commonly known as the yellow autumn crocus, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves and produces a tall, leafless stem that bears a single, showy flower. It is known for its toxic properties, containing colchicine, which has been used in medical treatments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
