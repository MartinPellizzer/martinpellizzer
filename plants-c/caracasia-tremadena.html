Caracasia tremadena

Caracasia tremadena, commonly known as the trembling caracasia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant features slender, branching stems that often sway in the wind, giving it its common name. Its leaves are finely divided, with a delicate, fern-like appearance, and it produces small white to pale yellow flowers in clusters.

Caracasia tremadena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
