Callicarpa inamoena

Callicarpa inamoena, commonly known as the red beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking, vibrant purple to red berries in the fall, which are a key feature of its ornamental value. These berries are not only visually appealing but also attract birds and other wildlife.

Callicarpa inamoena thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance plants.
