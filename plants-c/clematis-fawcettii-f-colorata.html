Clematis fawcettii f. colorata

Clematis fawcettii f. colorata, commonly known as the White Queen Clematis, is a compact, flowering vine with a distinctive growth habit.

It produces large, showy white flowers with a faint pinkish tinge, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a vigorous growth rate and thrives in full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for trellises or arbors. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

It is relatively disease-resistant and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
