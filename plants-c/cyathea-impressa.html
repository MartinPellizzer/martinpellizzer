Cyathea impressa

Cyathea impressa, commonly known as the ridge-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds have a distinctive ridge-like pattern on their upper surface, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cyathea impressa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
