Clomophyllum

Clomophyllum, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems that appear to float in air, giving it a spectral appearance. The plant lacks traditional leaves, instead featuring small, scale-like structures at the base of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical structure makes it an intriguing subject for study in plant morphology and adaptation.
