Cybele elegans

Cybele elegans, commonly known as the elegant cybele, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique beauty and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
