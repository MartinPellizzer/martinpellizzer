Convolvulus griseus

Convolvulus griseus, commonly known as the gray morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that features slender, trailing stems with hairy foliage. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Convolvulus griseus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
