Chrysanthemum cuneatum

Chrysanthemum cuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chrysanthemum, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
