Carex pamirensis var. angustispicata

Carex pamirensis var. angustispicata, commonly known as the narrow-spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with the inflorescence consisting of a dense cluster of spikelets.

This variety is distinguished by its slender, sharply pointed spikes, which contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
