Carex fernaldiana

Carex fernaldiana, commonly known as Fernald's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowering spikes are usually borne in late spring to early summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex fernaldiana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
