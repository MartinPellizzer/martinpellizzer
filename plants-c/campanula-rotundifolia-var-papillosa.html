Campanula rotundifolia var. papillosa

Campanula rotundifolia var. papillosa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are round and slightly hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
