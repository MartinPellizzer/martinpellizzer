Cirsium culebraense

Cirsium culebraense, commonly known as the Culebra thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

The species is relatively rare and has limited distribution, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
