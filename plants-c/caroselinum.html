Caroselinum

Caroselinum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Caroselinum thrives in moist, sunny environments and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in various historical applications.
