Centrolepis strigosa subsp. rupestris

Centrolepis strigosa subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock centrolepis, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its unique morphology and adaptability to harsh conditions make it a notable species in its native habitat.

It is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky terrain.
