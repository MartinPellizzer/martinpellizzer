Chamaefistula pendula

Chamaefistula pendula, commonly known as the weeping catalpa, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by long, bean-like pods that mature in the summer. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
