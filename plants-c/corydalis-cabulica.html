Corydalis cabulica

Corydalis cabulica, commonly known as the Afghan Corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It is a member of the Fumariaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy and ascending.

Corydalis cabulica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
