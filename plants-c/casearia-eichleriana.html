Casearia eichleriana

Casearia eichleriana, commonly known as the Eichler's casearia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
