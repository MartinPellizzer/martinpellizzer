Chloris megastachya

Chloris megastachya, commonly known as giant flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences composed of clustered spikelets, which are often pale green to reddish in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways.

Chloris megastachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
