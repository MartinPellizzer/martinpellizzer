Crassula rubicunda

Crassula rubicunda, commonly known as the Red Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, Crassula rubicunda is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
