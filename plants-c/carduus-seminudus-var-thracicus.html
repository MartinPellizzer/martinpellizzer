Carduus seminudus var. thracicus

Carduus seminudus var. thracicus, commonly known as the Thrace thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats like rocky soils and open fields.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
