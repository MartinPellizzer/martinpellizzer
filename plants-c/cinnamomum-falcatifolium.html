Cinnamomum falcatifolium

Cinnamomum falcatifolium, commonly known as the Japanese cinnamon tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and have a distinctive yellowish-green hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
