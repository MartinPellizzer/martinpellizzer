Chondrosea caesia

Chondrosea caesia, commonly known as the blue-green algae, is a type of cyanobacteria that thrives in aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its unique ability to perform photosynthesis and produce oxygen. This organism is often found in freshwater lakes and ponds, where it forms dense surface blooms. Chondrosea caesia can be both beneficial and harmful, depending on its concentration in the water.
