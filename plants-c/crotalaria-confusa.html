Crotalaria confusa

Crotalaria confusa, commonly known as the yellow spurred crotalaria, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces vibrant yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Crotalaria confusa is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
