Crataegus superata

Crataegus superata, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

Crataegus superata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential cardiovascular benefits due to its high content of flavonoids and other bioactive compounds.
