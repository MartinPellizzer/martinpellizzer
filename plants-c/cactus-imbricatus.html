Cactus imbricatus

Cactus imbricatus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in clusters of spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and aiding in photosynthesis.

The cactus is well adapted to desert environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
