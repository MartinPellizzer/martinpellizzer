Carex hypolytroides

Carex hypolytroides, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshy or swampy areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers.

Carex hypolytroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
