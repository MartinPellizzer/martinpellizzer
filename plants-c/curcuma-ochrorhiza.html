Curcuma ochrorhiza

Curcuma ochrorhiza, commonly known as the yellow ginger or wild turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Botanically, it is notable for its aromatic compounds and medicinal properties.
