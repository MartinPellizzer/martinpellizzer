Cirsium viride

Cirsium viride, commonly known as green thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features spiny, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in a cluster. Cirsium viride grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
