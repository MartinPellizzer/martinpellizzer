Conyza orientalis

Conyza orientalis, commonly known as the oriental horseweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant features small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Conyza orientalis is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
