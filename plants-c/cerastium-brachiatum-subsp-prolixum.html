Cerastium brachiatum subsp. prolixum

Cerastium brachiatum subsp.

prolixum, commonly known as the long-stemmed mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often branched and hairy. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
