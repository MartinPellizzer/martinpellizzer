Calliandra subnervosa

Calliandra subnervosa, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual appeal to gardens. The plant has compound leaves with small, thread-like leaflets, and its structure is often used in ornamental horticulture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Calliandra subnervosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
