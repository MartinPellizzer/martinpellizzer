Cimicifuga elata var. alpestris

Cimicifuga elata var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine bugbane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, showy white or pale pink flowers arranged in panicle clusters. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, which are typically arranged in an alternate pattern. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its use in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
