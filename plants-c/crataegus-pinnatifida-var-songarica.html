Crataegus pinnatifida var. songarica

Crataegus pinnatifida var. songarica, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in the spring. The plant bears red to orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
