Camelina paphlagonica

Camelina paphlagonica, commonly known as the Paphlagonian camelina, is a flowering plant native to the region of Paphlagonia in Anatolia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, and its leaves are simple and alternate. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Camelina paphlagonica is of interest to botanists due to its historical and potential agricultural significance.
