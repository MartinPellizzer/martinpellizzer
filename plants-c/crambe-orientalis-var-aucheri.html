Crambe orientalis var. aucheri

Crambe orientalis var. aucheri, commonly known as the Persian kale, is a biennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, deeply lobed leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear small, yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, often used in salads or cooked dishes.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and nutritional content.
