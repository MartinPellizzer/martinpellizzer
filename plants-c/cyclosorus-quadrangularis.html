Cyclosorus quadrangularis

Cyclosorus quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-cornered water fern, is a species of aquatic fern found in freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and delicate, lanceolate leaves. The plant typically grows in slow-moving or still water, such as ponds and marshes. Its fronds are usually submerged or floating, contributing to the aquatic ecosystem's biodiversity.

Cyclosorus quadrangularis plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.
