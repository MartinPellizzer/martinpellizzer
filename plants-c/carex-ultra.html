Carex ultra

Carex ultra, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of sedge native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often hidden among the leaves.

Carex ultra plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
