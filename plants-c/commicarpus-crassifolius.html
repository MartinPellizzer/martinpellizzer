Commicarpus crassifolius

Commicarpus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf commicarpus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Commicarpus crassifolius is often used in traditional medicine and is adapted to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor soils.
