Ceropegia tentaculata var. puberula

Ceropegia tentaculata var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine, hairy pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
