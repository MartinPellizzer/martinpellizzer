Callitriche chathamensis

Callitriche chathamensis, commonly known as the Chatham Island water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to the Chatham Islands of New Zealand.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds and streams, where it thrives in still or slow-moving water. The plant has delicate, floating leaves that are usually oval or ovate in shape, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often appearing in clusters, and it reproduces both sexually through seeds and asexually through vegetative propagation.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native region.
