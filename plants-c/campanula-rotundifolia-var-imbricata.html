Campanula rotundifolia var. imbricata

Campanula rotundifolia var. imbricata, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and natural landscapes.
