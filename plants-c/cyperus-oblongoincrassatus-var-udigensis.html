Cyperus oblongoincrassatus var. udigensis

Cyperus oblongoincrassatus var. udigensis, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and flooded areas, and has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive reddish hue. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are often surrounded by bracts.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
