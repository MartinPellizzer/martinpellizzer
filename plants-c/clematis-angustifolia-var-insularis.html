Clematis angustifolia var. insularis

Clematis angustifolia var. insularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length, supporting its climbing habit. The plant produces small, delicate white or pale blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
