Capsicum glandulosum

Capsicum glandulosum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellow or orange fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are yellow with five petals, and it produces capsular fruits that contain several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some use in traditional medicine.
