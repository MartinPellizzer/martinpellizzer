Corydalis bidwelliae

Corydalis bidwelliae, commonly known as Bidwell's larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountains of California.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant produces striking purple to blue flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

Corydalis bidwelliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
