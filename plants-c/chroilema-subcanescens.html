Chroilema subcanescens

Chroilema subcanescens, commonly known as the roughleaf chroilema, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Chroilema subcanescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique leaf texture and seasonal blooms.
