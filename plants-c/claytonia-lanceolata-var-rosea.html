Claytonia lanceolata var. rosea

Claytonia lanceolata var. rosea, commonly known as the pink rock lettuce, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features rosettes of fleshy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a pink or reddish hue. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or woodland soils.

Its striking foliage and delicate flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
