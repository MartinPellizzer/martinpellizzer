Crepis coriacea

Crepis coriacea, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow composite flowers, which are made up of many tiny florets arranged in a single head. It typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate regions.

Crepis coriacea is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.
