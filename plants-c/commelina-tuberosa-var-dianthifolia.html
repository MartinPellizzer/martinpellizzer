Commelina tuberosa var. dianthifolia

Commelina tuberosa var. dianthifolia, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are often adorned with white markings resembling spider-like patterns. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its tuberous roots are an important part of its structure, contributing to its ability to store water and nutrients.
