Centaurea boissieri subsp. pomeliana

Centaurea boissieri subsp.

pomeliana, commonly known as the Pomelina bluemink, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, grayish-blue foliage.

The plant produces striking purple to pink flower heads composed of ray florets and central disc florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native environment.
