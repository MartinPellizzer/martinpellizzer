Cirsium pitcheri f. magenteum

Cirsium pitcheri f. magenteum, commonly known as the Magenta Pitcher's Thistle, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the Great Lakes region of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shores of lakes and coastal dunes. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to magenta flowers, which are adapted for pollination by specific insect species. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to survive.

Its unique floral coloration and ecological niche make it a significant species for studying plant adaptation and biodiversity.
