Cyanus laciniatus

Cyanus laciniatus, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that resemble small bells, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Cyanus laciniatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
