Chesneya tribuloides

Chesneya tribuloides, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
