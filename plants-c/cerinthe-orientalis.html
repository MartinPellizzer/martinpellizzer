Cerinthe orientalis

Cerinthe orientalis, commonly known as the oriental toadflax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces striking blue or purple flowers with two lips, typical of the Plantaginaceae family. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cerinthe orientalis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
