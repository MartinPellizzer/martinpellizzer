Crinum × kircape

Crinum × kircape, commonly known as the hybrid crinum lily, is a striking perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant features a robust rhizomatous root system, which allows it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often evergreen in warmer climates.

Crinum × kircape is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
