Coletia

Coletia, commonly known as the "Brazilian nightshade," is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It includes several species native to South America, particularly Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay. The plants typically have shrub-like growth habits with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Their flowers are usually small and yellow or white, often clustered in inflorescences.

Coletia species are known for their toxic properties, containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
