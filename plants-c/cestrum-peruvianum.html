Cestrum peruvianum

Cestrum peruvianum, commonly known as the night blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical in shape.

Cestrum peruvianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.
