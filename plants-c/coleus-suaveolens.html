Coleus suaveolens

Coleus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented coleus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers and attractive foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink, purple, or white, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are often colored in shades of red, purple, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Coleus suaveolens thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
