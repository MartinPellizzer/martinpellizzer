Campanula valdensis

Campanula valdensis, commonly known as the Valdensis bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

Campanula valdensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
