Cattleya galeottiana

Cattleya galeottiana, commonly known as the Galeotti's orchid, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with broad, grass-like leaves and a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. Its flowers are borne on tall, erect spikes and are known for their elegant, spurred shape.

Cattleya galeottiana is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
