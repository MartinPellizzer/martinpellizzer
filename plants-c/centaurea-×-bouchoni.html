Centaurea × bouchoni

Centaurea × bouchoni, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Centaurea × bouchoni is often used in ornamental gardening for its showy blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
