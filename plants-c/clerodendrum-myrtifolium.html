Clerodendrum myrtifolium

Clerodendrum myrtifolium, commonly known as the rose-of-Jerusalem, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous tiny seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
