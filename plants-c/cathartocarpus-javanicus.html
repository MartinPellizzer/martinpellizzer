Cathartocarpus javanicus

Cathartocarpus javanicus, commonly known as the Java sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
