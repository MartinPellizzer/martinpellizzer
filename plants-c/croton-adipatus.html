Croton adipatus

Croton adipatus, commonly known as the fat croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from yellow to orange and red. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

Croton adipatus has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
