Cytisus supinus var. lasiosemius

Cytisus supinus var. lasiosemius, commonly known as the woolly broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy underside.

This variety is often found in coastal and limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions.
