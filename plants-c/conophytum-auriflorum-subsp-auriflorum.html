Conophytum auriflorum subsp. auriflorum

Conophytum auriflorum subsp.

auriflorum, commonly known as the golden flower conophytum, is a compact, succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, adapted to its arid environment.

The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
