Cerastium viscosum var. eglandulosum

Cerastium viscosum var. eglandulosum, commonly known as sticky chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species adaptable to various soil types.
