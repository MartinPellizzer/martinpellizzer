Croton incrustatus

Croton incrustatus, commonly known as the velvet croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. Its foliage is highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.

Croton incrustatus thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
