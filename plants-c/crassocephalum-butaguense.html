Crassocephalum butaguense

Crassocephalum butaguense, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
