Crocus armeniensis

Crocus armeniensis, commonly known as the Armenian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Crocus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, including parts of Armenia and Georgia. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the petals. It typically blooms in late winter or early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in its habitat.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant prefers well-drained, calcareous soils.
