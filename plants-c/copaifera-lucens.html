Copaifera lucens

Copaifera lucens, commonly known as yellow copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has compound leaves with elongated leaflets. Its bark and resin have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

The species is valued for its ecological role and potential economic importance in sustainable forestry.
