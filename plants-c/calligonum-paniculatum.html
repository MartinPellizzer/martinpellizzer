Calligonum paniculatum

Calligonum paniculatum, commonly known as the silky tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of pink to reddish flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in poor soil conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
