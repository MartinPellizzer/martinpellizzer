Chloroxylon falcatum

Chloroxylon falcatum, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown to black bark and its long, slender leaves that are often curved. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark, round fruits. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
