Centaurea micrantha var. squamomutica

Centaurea micrantha var. squamomutica, commonly known as the squamose centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in compact, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine, scale-like structures.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
