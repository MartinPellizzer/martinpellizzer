Crocus reticulatus subvar. reflexus

Crocus reticulatus subvar. reflexus, commonly known as the reflexed crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive reticulate pattern on the petals. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that appear after flowering.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit and unique flower structure.
