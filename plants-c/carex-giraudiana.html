Carex giraudiana

Carex giraudiana, commonly known as the Giraud's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex giraudiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
