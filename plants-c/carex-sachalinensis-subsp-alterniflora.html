Carex sachalinensis subsp. alterniflora

Carex sachalinensis subsp.

alterniflora, commonly known as the Sachalin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including Japan and the Russian Far East. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
