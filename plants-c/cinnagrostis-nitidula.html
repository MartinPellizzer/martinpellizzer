Cinnagrostis nitidula

Cinnagrostis nitidula, commonly known as the smooth blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cinnagrostis nitidula is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
