Croton brachypus

Croton brachypus, commonly known as the short-stemmed croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that often display vibrant colors, ranging from green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant in warm climates.
