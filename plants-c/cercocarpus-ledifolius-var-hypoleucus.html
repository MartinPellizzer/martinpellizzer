Cercocarpus ledifolius var. hypoleucus

Cercocarpus ledifolius var. hypoleucus, commonly known as the silverleaf curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its unique leaf shape and coloration make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable plant in its native range.
