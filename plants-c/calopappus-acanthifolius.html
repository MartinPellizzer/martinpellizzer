Calopappus acanthifolius

Calopappus acanthifolius, commonly known as the spinyleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage features distinctive spiny margins, which provide a unique botanical trait.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
