Campanula carpatica var. grandiflora

Campanula carpatica var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Bigflower Bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that produce clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

This variety is valued for its attractive blooms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
