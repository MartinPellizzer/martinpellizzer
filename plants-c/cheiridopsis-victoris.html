Cheiridopsis victoris

Cheiridopsis victoris, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is known for its unique adaptations to arid environments. The plant features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that store water. It produces small, white to pink flowers during the winter months, adding ornamental value.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
