Cinnamomum mutabile

Cinnamomum mutabile, commonly known as variable cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, it is less commonly cultivated compared to other cinnamon species due to its limited commercial use.
