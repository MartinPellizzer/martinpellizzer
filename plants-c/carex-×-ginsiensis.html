Carex × ginsiensis

Carex × ginsiensis, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

Carex × ginsiensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
