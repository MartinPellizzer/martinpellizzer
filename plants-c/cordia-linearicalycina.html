Cordia linearicalycina

Cordia linearicalycina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, linear leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Cordia linearicalycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
