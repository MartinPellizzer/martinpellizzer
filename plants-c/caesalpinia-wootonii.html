Caesalpinia wootonii

Caesalpinia wootonii, commonly known as the flame bean or red flame acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red, feathery inflorescences that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods are long, slender, and often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Caesalpinia wootonii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
