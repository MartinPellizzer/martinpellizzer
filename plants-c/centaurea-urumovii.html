Centaurea urumovii

Centaurea urumovii, commonly known as the Urumov's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with distinctive bracts that give it a unique appearance.

Centaurea urumovii is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
