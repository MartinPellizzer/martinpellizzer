Clinopodium × bernhardtianum

Clinopodium × bernhardtianum, commonly known as the hybrid mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Clinopodium vulgare and Clinopodium scoticum, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas across its native range.

Clinopodium × bernhardtianum is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
