Cynanchum mahafalense

Cynanchum mahafalense, commonly known as Mahafal milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers with five lobes, typically in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The milky sap it exudes is a distinguishing feature, often used in traditional medicine by local communities.
