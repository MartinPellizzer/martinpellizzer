Campbellia aurantiaca

Campbellia aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden flame bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, resembling flames. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to the unique climate and soil conditions of Hawaii. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Campbellia aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its eye-catching appearance.
