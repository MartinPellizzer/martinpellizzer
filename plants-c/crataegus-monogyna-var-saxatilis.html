Crataegus monogyna var. saxatilis

Crataegus monogyna var. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, persistent berries. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in exposed or coastal areas. Its leaves are oval to elliptical, with serrated edges, and the bark is usually grey and fissured.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ornamental qualities in horticulture.
