Ceropegia angiensis

Ceropegia angiensis, commonly known as the Angolan moonvine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, berry-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a houseplant.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
