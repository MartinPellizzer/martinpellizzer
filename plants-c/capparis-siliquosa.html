Capparis siliquosa

Capparis siliquosa, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has woody stems and spiny leaves, which help it thrive in coastal and rocky environments. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

Capparis siliquosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
