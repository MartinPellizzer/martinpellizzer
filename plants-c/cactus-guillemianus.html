Cactus guillemianus

Cactus guillemianus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of spines that protect it from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Overall, it is a hardy and visually distinctive plant well adapted to desert conditions.
