Chorigyne ensiformis

Chorigyne ensiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chorigyne, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its habitat.
