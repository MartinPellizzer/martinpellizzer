Carex sakonis

Carex sakonis, commonly known as the Sakon Carex, is a species of sedge native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.

Carex sakonis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
