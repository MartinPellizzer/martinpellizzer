Carex fulvorubescens var. longistipes

Carex fulvorubescens var. longistipes, commonly known as the red-brown sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green with a reddish tinge on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
