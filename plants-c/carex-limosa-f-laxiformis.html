Carex limosa f. laxiformis

Carex limosa f. laxiformis, commonly known as the loose-form bulrush, is a variant of the common bulrush, characterized by its more loosely arranged leaves.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clustered spikes.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life.
