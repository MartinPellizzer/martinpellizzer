Cirsium × erucagineum

Cirsium × erucagineum, commonly known as the lovegrass thistle, is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
