Chytraculia clusiifolia

Chytraculia clusiifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has simple, opposite, and often lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its bark is smooth and may have a reddish or brownish hue, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
