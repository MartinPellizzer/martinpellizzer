Cerastium nigrescens var. nigrescens

Cerastium nigrescens var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black willow herb, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant thrives in moist, shady environments.

It is often found in woodland edges, stream banks, and other damp habitats.
