Crepinia friedlanderiana

Crepinia friedlanderiana, commonly known as the Friedlander's crepe jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that resemble small crepe paper folds, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
