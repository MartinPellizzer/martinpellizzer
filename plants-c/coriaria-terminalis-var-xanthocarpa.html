Coriaria terminalis var. xanthocarpa

Coriaria terminalis var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited coriaria, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, simple leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are edible but have a mild, spicy flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
