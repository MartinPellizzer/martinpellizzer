Chuquiraga spinosa var. parviflora

Chuquiraga spinosa var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered chuquiraga, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
