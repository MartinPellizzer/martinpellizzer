Calycanthus reticulatus

Calycanthus reticulatus, commonly known as the fragrant woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, red-brown flowers that are highly aromatic, often blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture and dark green upper surfaces. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Calycanthus reticulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
