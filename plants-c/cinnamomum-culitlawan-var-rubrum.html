Cinnamomum culitlawan var. rubrum

Cinnamomum culitlawan var. rubrum, commonly known as red cinnamon or red camphor laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by reddish berries, which contain hard, shiny seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties.

The leaves and bark are used in traditional medicine and for making perfumes and flavorings.
