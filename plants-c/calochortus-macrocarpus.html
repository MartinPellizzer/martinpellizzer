Calochortus macrocarpus

Calochortus macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant mariposa lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to California.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 6 inches in diameter, typically displaying shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features a tall, upright stem with narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Calochortus macrocarpus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
