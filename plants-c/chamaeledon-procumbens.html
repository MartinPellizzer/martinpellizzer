Chamaeledon procumbens

Chamaeledon procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate leek, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its fibrous roots are edible and have a mild, leek-like flavor.

Chamaeledon procumbens is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
