Campanula polymorpha subf. umbrosa

Campanula polymorpha subf.

umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture. Campanula polymorpha subf.
