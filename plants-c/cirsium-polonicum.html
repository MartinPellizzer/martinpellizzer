Cirsium polonicum

Cirsium polonicum, commonly known as Polish thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its structure includes a thick, hairy stem and a basal rosette of leaves.

Cirsium polonicum is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
