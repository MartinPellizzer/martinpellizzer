Cola mayumbensis

Cola mayumbensis, commonly known as Mayumbensis cola, is a species of the genus Cola native to Central and West Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive berries that contain stimulant compounds, primarily caffeine and theobromine. It thrives in tropical rainforest environments and is often found in humid, shaded areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique botanical features and potential economic value, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
