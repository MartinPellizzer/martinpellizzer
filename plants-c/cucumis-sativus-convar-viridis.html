Cucumis sativus convar. viridis

Cucumis sativus convar. viridis, commonly known as the green cucumber, is a variant of the common cucumber characterized by its slender, elongated fruit.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to South Asia, where it has been cultivated for centuries. The plant typically grows as an annual vine with hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and the fruit develops from the female flowers.

The green cucumber is valued for its crisp texture and mild flavor, making it a popular choice for fresh consumption.
