Cassia maxonii

Cassia maxonii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia maxonii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
