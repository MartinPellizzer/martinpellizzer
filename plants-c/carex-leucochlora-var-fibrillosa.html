Carex leucochlora var. fibrillosa

Carex leucochlora var. fibrillosa, commonly known as the fibrous white sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces achenes as its reproductive structures.

The varietal form, fibrillosa, is distinguished by its fibrous, hairy appearance on the stems and leaves.
