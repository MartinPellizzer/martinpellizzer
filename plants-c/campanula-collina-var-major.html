Campanula collina var. major

Campanula collina var. major, commonly known as the Great Bluebell, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and can reach up to 30 cm in length.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
