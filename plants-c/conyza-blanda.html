Conyza blanda

Conyza blanda, commonly known as common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, and it produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Conyza blanda is often considered a weed but has some historical use in traditional medicine.
