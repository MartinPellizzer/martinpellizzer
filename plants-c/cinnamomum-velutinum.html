Cinnamomum velutinum

Cinnamomum velutinum, commonly known as the velvet cinnamon tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic bark that exudes a cinnamon-like scent when scraped. The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a glossy upper surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clustered, oval-shaped fruits.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
