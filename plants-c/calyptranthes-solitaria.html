Calyptranthes solitaria

Calyptranthes solitaria, commonly known as the solitary calyptranthes, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This evergreen shrub typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features small, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
