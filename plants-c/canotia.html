Canotia

Canotia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Canotia has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
