Cyrtostylis pectinata

Cyrtostylis pectinata, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, stocky rhizome and produces a single, showy spike of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has a distinctive, velvety texture to its leaves, which are lanceolate and covered in fine, soft hairs. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and is often found in areas with rich, organic soil.

Cyrtostylis pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
