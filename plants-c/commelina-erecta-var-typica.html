Commelina erecta var. typica

Commelina erecta var. typica, commonly known as the upright spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue-violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, typical of the Commelina genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
