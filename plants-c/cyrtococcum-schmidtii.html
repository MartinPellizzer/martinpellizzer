Cyrtococcum schmidtii

Cyrtococcum schmidtii, commonly known as the Schmidt's cryptococcus, is a rare and elusive fungal plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Cryptococcaceae and is characterized by its small, dark, spherical fruiting bodies. This plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing on decaying wood or leaf litter. Its morphology is simple, with a lack of distinct stems or leaves, making it difficult to identify without specialized knowledge.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of ecological health.
