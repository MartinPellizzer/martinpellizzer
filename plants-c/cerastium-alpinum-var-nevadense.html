Cerastium alpinum var. nevadense

Cerastium alpinum var. nevadense, commonly known as the Sierra mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.
