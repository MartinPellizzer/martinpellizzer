Carex sachalinensis var. iwakiana

Carex sachalinensis var. iwakiana, commonly known as the Iwakiana sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, low-maintenance plants.
