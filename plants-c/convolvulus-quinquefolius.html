Convolvulus quinquefolius

Convolvulus quinquefolius, commonly known as wild morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found along roadsides and in disturbed soils, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
