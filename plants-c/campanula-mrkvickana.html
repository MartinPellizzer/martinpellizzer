Campanula mrkvickana

Campanula mrkvickana, commonly known as the Czech bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Campanula mrkvickana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
