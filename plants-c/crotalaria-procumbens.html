Crotalaria procumbens

Crotalaria procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate cowpea or yellow burr medic, is a low-growing annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides.

Crotalaria procumbens is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural systems.
