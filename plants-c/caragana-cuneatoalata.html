Caragana cuneatoalata

Caragana cuneatoalata, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr sage, is a shrub native to the dry regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often thorned stems and small, compound leaves that are typically arranged in a zigzag pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by distinctive, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Caragana cuneatoalata is also valued for its role in preventing soil erosion in degraded landscapes.
