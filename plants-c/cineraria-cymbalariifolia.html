Cineraria cymbalariifolia

Cineraria cymbalariifolia, commonly known as the silver cineraria, is a perennial plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which is covered in dense, fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and purple during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
