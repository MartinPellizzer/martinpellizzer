Centaurea ambigua subsp. laciniata

Centaurea ambigua subsp.

laciniata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically narrow and lobed, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
