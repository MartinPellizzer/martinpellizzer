Calyptranthes langsdorffii var. macrophylla

Calyptranthes langsdorffii var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf calyptranthes, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
