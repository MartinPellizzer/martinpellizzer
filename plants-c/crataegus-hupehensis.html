Crataegus hupehensis

Crataegus hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
