Cirsium Ciliatum Subsp. Szovitsii

Cirsium ciliatum subsp. szovitsii is a rare and lesser-known thistle species native to specific regions of Europe. It is characterized by its finely haired leaves and spiny, purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. This subspecies is often found in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in environments with poor soil conditions. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes. Understanding and protecting this plant is crucial for preserving biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
