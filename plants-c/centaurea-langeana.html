Centaurea langeana

Centaurea langeana, commonly known as Lange's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
