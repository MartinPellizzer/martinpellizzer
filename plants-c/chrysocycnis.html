Chrysocycnis

Chrysocycnis, commonly known as the golden cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and large, feather-like leaves that give it a prehistoric appearance. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
