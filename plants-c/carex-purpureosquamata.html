Carex purpureosquamata

Carex purpureosquamata, commonly known as purple-squamate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often reddish or purple in color. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands.

Carex purpureosquamata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
