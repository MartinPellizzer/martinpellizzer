Calothamnus spathulatus

Calothamnus spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or pink in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Calothamnus spathulatus is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.
