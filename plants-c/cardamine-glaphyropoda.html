Cardamine glaphyropoda

Cardamine glaphyropoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watercress, is a small perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small white or greenish flowers are clustered in umbels and appear in early spring.

The plant is valued for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
