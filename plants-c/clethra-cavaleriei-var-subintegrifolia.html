Clethra cavaleriei var. subintegrifolia

Clethra cavaleriei var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese sweet pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated margins, and it produces small, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
