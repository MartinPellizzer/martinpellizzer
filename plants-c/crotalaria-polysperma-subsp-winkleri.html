Crotalaria polysperma subsp. winkleri

Crotalaria polysperma subsp.

winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's crotalaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has a taproot system and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
