Crocus haussknechtii

Crocus haussknechtii, commonly known as the Haussknechtii crocus, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, recurved petal shape. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering crocuses. Its underground corms are adapted to survive harsh winters and support growth in the following season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring display.
