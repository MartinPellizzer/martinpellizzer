Carex viridula var. pulchella

Carex viridula var. pulchella, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and wet meadows. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, a characteristic feature of many sedges. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its aesthetic qualities in garden settings.
