Cnicus nelsonii

Cnicus nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by white to pale green seeds.

The species is considered a perennial herb and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
