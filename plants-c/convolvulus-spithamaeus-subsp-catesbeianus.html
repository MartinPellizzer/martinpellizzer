Convolvulus spithamaeus subsp. catesbeianus

Convolvulus spithamaeus subsp.

catesbeianus, commonly known as the Catesby's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant has a climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend supports such as fences or trellises. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
