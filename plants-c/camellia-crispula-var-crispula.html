Camellia crispula var. crispula

Camellia crispula var. crispula, commonly known as the wrinkled camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, double-petaled flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a splash of color during the colder months.

Camellia crispula var. crispula is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand mild frosts.
