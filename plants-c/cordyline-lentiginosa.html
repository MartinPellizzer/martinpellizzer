Cordyline lentiginosa

Cordyline lentiginosa, commonly known as the spotted cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its foliage is highly decorative, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.

Cordyline lentiginosa prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in warm, humid climates.
