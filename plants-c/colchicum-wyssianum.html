Colchicum wyssianum

Colchicum wyssianum, commonly known as the Wyssian colchicum, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, which is surrounded by several leaf-like bracts. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
