Capparis galeata

Capparis galeata, commonly known as the rock caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its aromatic foliage.

Capparis galeata is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value in gardens.
