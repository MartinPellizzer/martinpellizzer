Cleome angustifolia var. damarensis

Cleome angustifolia var. damarensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. This variety is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

It also produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its propagation in suitable environments.
