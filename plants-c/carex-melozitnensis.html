Carex melozitnensis

Carex melozitnensis, commonly known as the Melozitnensk Carex, is a species of sedge native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are usually inconspicuous and clustered in spikes.

Due to its habitat preferences, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
