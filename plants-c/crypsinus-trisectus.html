Crypsinus trisectus

Crypsinus trisectus, commonly known as the three-lobed fern, is a rare and delicate species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, trifoliate leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its foliage is covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, Crypsinus trisectus is considered a vulnerable species.
