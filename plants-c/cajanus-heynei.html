Cajanus heynei

Cajanus heynei, commonly known as the wild pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Cajanus heynei is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
