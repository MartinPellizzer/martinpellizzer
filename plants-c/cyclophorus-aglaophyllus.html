Cyclophorus aglaophyllus

Cyclophorus aglaophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaved cyclophorus, is a small shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically broad and leathery, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a landscape accent.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
