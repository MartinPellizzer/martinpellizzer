Cytisus mutabilis

Cytisus mutabilis, commonly known as the variable broom, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is notable for its variable flower color, which can shift from yellow to orange or red depending on environmental conditions.
