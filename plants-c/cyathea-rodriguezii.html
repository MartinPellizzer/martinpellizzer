Cyathea rodriguezii

Cyathea rodriguezii, commonly known as the Rodriguez's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, providing structural support and aiding in nutrient absorption.

Cyathea rodriguezii is often found in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the understory.
