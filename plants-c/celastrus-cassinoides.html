Celastrus cassinoides

Celastrus cassinoides, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that appear in the spring, followed by vibrant purple berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. Its fruit is an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Celastrus cassinoides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and forest edges.
