Carex meridensis

Carex meridensis, commonly known as the southern sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has triangular stems and produces clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers in early summer.

Carex meridensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
