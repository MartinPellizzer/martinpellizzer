Crepis multicaulis subsp. multicaulis

Crepis multicaulis subsp.

multicaulis, commonly known as the common mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils, grasslands, and along roadsides.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically heart-shaped with toothed margins. Its flowers are arranged in loose clusters and are pollinated by insects.

It is an annual or biennial species, depending on the climate, and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
