Chelyella longicolumna

Chelyella longicolumna, commonly known as the long-stemmed chelyella, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant features small, spirally arranged leaves that help it maximize sunlight absorption in its habitat. Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, relying on water currents for seed dispersal.

Chelyella longicolumna is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic life.
