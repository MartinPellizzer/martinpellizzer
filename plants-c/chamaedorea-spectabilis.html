Chamaedorea spectabilis

Chamaedorea spectabilis, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central and South America.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and grows to a height of up to 15 feet. The plant produces feather-like, compound leaves that are bright green and can reach lengths of up to 6 feet. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil with regular watering.

Chamaedorea spectabilis is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and air-purifying qualities.
