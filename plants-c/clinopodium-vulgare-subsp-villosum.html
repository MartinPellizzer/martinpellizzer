Clinopodium vulgare subsp. villosum

Clinopodium vulgare subsp.

villosum, commonly known as woolly wood betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and are usually blue to purple in color.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.
