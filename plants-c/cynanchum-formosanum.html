Cynanchum formosanum

Cynanchum formosanum, commonly known as the Formosan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia, particularly Taiwan and parts of China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release fine, hairy seeds. It thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
