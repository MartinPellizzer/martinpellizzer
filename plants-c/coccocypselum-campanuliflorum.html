Coccocypselum campanuliflorum

Coccocypselum campanuliflorum, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
