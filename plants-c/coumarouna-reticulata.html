Coumarouna reticulata

Coumarouna reticulata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a reticulate venation pattern.

The wood of Coumarouna reticulata is valued for its fine texture and is used in furniture and carving.
