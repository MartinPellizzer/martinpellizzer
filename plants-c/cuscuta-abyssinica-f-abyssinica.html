Cuscuta abyssinica f. abyssinica

Cuscuta abyssinica f. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is native to Africa and is often found growing on various species of shrubs and trees. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops.
