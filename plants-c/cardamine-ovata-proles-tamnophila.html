Cardamine ovata proles tamnophila

Cardamine ovata proles tamnophila, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water crowfoot, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet woodlands. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
