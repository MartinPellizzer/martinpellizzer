Cyperus coercens

Cyperus coercens, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a member of the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often green or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

Cyperus coercens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
