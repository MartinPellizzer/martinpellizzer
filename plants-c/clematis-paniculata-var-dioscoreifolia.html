Clematis paniculata var. dioscoreifolia

Clematis paniculata var. dioscoreifolia, commonly known as the creeping clematis, is a vigorous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets, arranged in a feather-like pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs, and it thrives in partial to full sun with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
