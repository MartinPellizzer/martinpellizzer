Campanula polymorpha f. kladnianioides

Campanula polymorpha f. kladnianioides, commonly known as the Kladnianum bellflower, is a compact, mat-forming perennial with distinctive blue-violet flowers.

It features narrow, slightly hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
