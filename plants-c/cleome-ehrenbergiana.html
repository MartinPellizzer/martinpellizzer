Cleome ehrenbergiana

Cleome ehrenbergiana, commonly known as spider flower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems and showy, clustered flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer.

Cleome ehrenbergiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
