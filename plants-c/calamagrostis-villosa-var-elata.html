Calamagrostis villosa var. elata

Calamagrostis villosa var. elata, commonly known as tall hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and soft, silky foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet to moist soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces fluffy, silver-gray seed heads in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland restoration and naturalized garden settings.
