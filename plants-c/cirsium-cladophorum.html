Cirsium cladophorum

Cirsium cladophorum, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, serrated leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its thick, woolly foliage helps to retain moisture and protect against extreme temperatures.

Cirsium cladophorum is often found in alpine meadows and is an important part of the local flora in its native habitat.
