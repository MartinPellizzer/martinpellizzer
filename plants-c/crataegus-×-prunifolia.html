Crataegus × prunifolia

Crataegus × prunifolia, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife. It is native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Crataegus × prunifolia is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its cardiovascular benefits.
