Cissus macrothyrsa

Cissus macrothyrsa, commonly known as the giant grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into round, fleshy fruits. Its stems are succulent and can store water, which helps it survive in arid conditions.

Cissus macrothyrsa is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to climb trellises or walls.
