Calyptranthes capitellata

Calyptranthes capitellata, commonly known as the white-flowered calyptranthes, is a species of flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within mesic forests. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences at the ends of the branches.

Calyptranthes capitellata is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
