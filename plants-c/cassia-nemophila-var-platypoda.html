Cassia nemophila var. platypoda

Cassia nemophila var. platypoda, commonly known as the mountain cassia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its clusters of bright yellow, fluffy inflorescences. The leaves are typically bipinnate, with fine, thread-like leaflets that give the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, often found in forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
