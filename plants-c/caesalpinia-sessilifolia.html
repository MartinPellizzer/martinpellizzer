Caesalpinia sessilifolia

Caesalpinia sessilifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of small, oval leaflets arranged in two rows. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that turn from green to brown as they mature.

Caesalpinia sessilifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
