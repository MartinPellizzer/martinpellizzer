Cirsium lanceolatum var. latilobum

Cirsium lanceolatum var. latilobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a robust stem with rough, hairy surfaces and large, spiny leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flower heads are usually purple to pink, consisting of both ray and disk flowers.

This variety is characterized by its broad, lobed leaf structure, which distinguishes it from other thistle species.
