Cyperus crispulus

Cyperus crispulus, commonly known as the curly sedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive curling at the tips.

Cyperus crispulus produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, often found in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.
