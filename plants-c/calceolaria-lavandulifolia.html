Calceolaria lavandulifolia

Calceolaria lavandulifolia, commonly known as the lavenderleaf calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that resemble small cups. The plant produces showy, violet to purple blossoms that are often clustered in racemes, adding visual interest to gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-gray underside, giving it a unique appearance.

Calceolaria lavandulifolia prefers cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
