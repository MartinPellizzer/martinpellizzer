Carex simensis var. mauensis

Carex simensis var. mauensis, commonly known as the Mauensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
