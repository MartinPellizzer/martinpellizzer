Carlina radiata

Carlina radiata, commonly known as the rayless thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a tall, upright stem with silvery-gray, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces large, showy flower heads composed of blue to violet ray florets and yellow disk florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or grassy areas.

Carlina radiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
