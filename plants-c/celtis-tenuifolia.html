Celtis tenuifolia

Celtis tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf boxelder, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically 5-9 lobed. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by small, round fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, open woodlands.

Celtis tenuifolia is noted for its rapid growth and adaptability, making it a common urban and suburban tree.
