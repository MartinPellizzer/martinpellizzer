Chamaemelum tetragonospermum

Chamaemelum tetragonospermum, commonly known as the four-seeded chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in herbal remedies, though it is less commercially cultivated than other chamomile species.

The four-seeded chamomile is valued for its aromatic compounds and potential therapeutic applications.
