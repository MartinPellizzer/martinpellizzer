Carinta repens var. americana

Carinta repens var. americana, commonly known as the American trailing ardisia, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green oval leaves that are arranged in a trailing manner, often cascading from hanging baskets or ground covers. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal berries, though it is considered invasive in some regions.
