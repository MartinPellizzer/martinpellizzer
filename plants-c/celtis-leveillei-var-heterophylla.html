Celtis leveillei var. heterophylla

Celtis leveillei var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable hackberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptic to ovate leaves that vary in size and shape, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits that are typically white or yellow when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different light conditions, making it a resilient species.

The leaves and fruits are often used in local ecosystems, providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
