Colubrina buxifolia

Colubrina buxifolia, commonly known as waxleaf colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, dark green leaves that have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Colubrina buxifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
