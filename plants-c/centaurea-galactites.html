Centaurea galactites

Centaurea galactites, commonly known as the milk thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers and spiny, lobed leaves. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. Its most notable feature is the milky sap that exudes from the stems and leaves when damaged.

Centaurea galactites is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
