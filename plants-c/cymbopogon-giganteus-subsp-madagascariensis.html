Cymbopogon giganteus subsp. madagascariensis

Cymbopogon giganteus subsp.

madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar lemongrass, is a perennial grass native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct lemony aroma.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
