Convolvulus demissus var. andinus

Convolvulus demissus var. andinus, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the seeds are enclosed in a spiral-shaped capsule.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
