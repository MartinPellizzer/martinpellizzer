Chamaecyparis lawsoniana filiformis

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana filiformis, commonly known as the Lawson's cypress dwarf form, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer with a unique, twisted, and spiral-shaped foliage.

It features scale-like leaves that are closely pressed to the branches, giving it a dense, mat-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones and has a naturally pyramidal or rounded form. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative accent plant.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to add texture and interest to landscapes.
