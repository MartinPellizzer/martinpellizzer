Cleome wrightii

Cleome wrightii, commonly known as Wright's spiderflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that resemble spider webs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cleome wrightii thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woods.
