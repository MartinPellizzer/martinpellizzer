Carex digitata var. pallens

Carex digitata var. pallens, commonly known as the pale-flowered sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that bear small, pale flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Its foliage and flowering structure make it an attractive addition to wetland gardens and naturalistic landscapes.
