Camomilla

Camomilla, commonly known as chamomile, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North America, and is widely cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic properties. The plant features daisy-like yellow flowers with white petals, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are finely divided and covered in a soft, downy texture.

Camomilla is particularly valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from the flowers and used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.
