Cathissa

Cathissa, commonly known as Indian frankincense or gharapathra, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Olacaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with small, simple leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its bark and resin have been used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Cathissa species are often cultivated for their fragrant resins, which are used in incense and perfumery.
