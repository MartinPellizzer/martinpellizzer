Carex leersii subvar. virescens

Carex leersii subvar. virescens, commonly known as the green-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are generally a vibrant green, with some variation in color depending on environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
