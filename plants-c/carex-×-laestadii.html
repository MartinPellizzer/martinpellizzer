Carex × laestadii

Carex × laestadii, commonly known as the silver hair sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Carex genus, characterized by its distinctive silver-white inflorescence.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often wind-pollinated. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

Carex × laestadii is also noted for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
