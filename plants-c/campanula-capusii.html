Campanula capusii

Campanula capusii, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Campanula capusii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
