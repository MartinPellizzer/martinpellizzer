Chimonanthus

Chimonanthus, commonly known as the winter jasmine, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Thymelaeaceae.

These plants are native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and are characterized by their showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the stems are often woody and branched. Chimonanthus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value, especially in gardens where their early blooming period adds color during the colder months.
