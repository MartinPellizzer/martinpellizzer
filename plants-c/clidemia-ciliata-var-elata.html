Clidemia ciliata var. elata

Clidemia ciliata var. elata, commonly known as the hairy mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are hairy on both surfaces, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain seeds dispersed by birds.

This species thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on the branches of trees.
