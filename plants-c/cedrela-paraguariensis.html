Cedrela paraguariensis

Cedrela paraguariensis, commonly known as *palo de rosa* or *sapea*, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves with alternate arrangement. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which has been traditionally used in furniture and construction.

Additionally, its leaves and seeds have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
