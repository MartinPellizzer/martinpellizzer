Ceratostylis sessilis

Ceratostylis sessilis, commonly known as the sessile horned orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, often hairy stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in a spike, with a distinctive horn-shaped spur.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its unique floral structure and ecological role in its habitat.
