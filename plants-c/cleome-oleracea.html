Cleome oleracea

Cleome oleracea, commonly known as the common cleome, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Cleome oleracea is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
