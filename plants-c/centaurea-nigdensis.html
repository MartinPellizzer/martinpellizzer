Centaurea nigdensis

Centaurea nigdensis, commonly known as the Black-centred Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black central disc flowers, which contrast with its blue to violet outer petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It is a perennial species with slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Centaurea nigdensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
