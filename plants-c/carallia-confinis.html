Carallia confinis

Carallia confinis, commonly known as the Brazilian holly, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Carallia confinis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
