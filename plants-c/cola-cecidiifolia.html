Cola cecidiifolia

Cola cecidiifolia, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a member of the Sterculiaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing hard, brown seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
