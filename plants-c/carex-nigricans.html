Carex nigricans

Carex nigricans, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Native to wetlands and moist soils in North America, it thrives in environments with consistent moisture. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
