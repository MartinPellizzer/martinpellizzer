Crataegus rosiformis subsp. lindmanii

Crataegus rosiformis subsp.

lindmanii, commonly known as the Lindman's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
