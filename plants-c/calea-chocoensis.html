Calea chocoensis

Calea chocoensis, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white, showy flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic leaves and stems. Calea chocoensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its unique flowering behavior and medicinal properties have made it a subject of both botanical and cultural interest.
