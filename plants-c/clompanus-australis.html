Clompanus australis

Clompanus australis, commonly known as the southern clompanus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant has silver-green foliage that is covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal areas.

Clompanus australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
