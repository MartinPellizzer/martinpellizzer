Croton caladiifolius

Croton caladiifolius, commonly known as the flame tree or torch tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red or orange bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically palmate, with five to seven lobes, and are often a vibrant green.

Croton caladiifolius thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
