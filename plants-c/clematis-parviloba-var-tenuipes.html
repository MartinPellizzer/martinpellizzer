Clematis parviloba var. tenuipes

Clematis parviloba var. tenuipes, commonly known as the slenderleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, wiry stems and small, delicate white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval to lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to woodland gardens and shaded landscapes.
