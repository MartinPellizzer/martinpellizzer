Cassuvium cochinchinense

Cassuvium cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese starfruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Oxalidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive five-pointed star-shaped fruit. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves and has a climbing habit, often requiring support for growth. Its fruit is edible, with a tangy, slightly acidic flavor, and is widely cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
