Calliandra viscidula

Calliandra viscidula, commonly known as sticky calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
