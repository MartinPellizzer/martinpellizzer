Cassia italica subsp. micrantha

Cassia italica subsp.

micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered cassia, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellow, fragrant flowers arranged in spikes.

The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes.
