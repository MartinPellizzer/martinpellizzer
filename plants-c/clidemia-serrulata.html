Clidemia serrulata

Clidemia serrulata, commonly known as the hairy miconia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
