Casparya umbellata

Casparya umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, arching fronds that resemble an umbrella, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, stocky rhizome from which the fronds emerge. Its leaves are typically bright green and can grow quite long, reaching up to several feet in length.

Casparya umbellata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
