Cyperus flavus var. australis

Cyperus flavus var. australis, commonly known as the Australian flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale yellow to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus flavus var. australis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
