Cirsium acaulon

Cirsium acaulon, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, spiny stems and clusters of purple or pink flower heads. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs and have a serrated edge. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Cirsium acaulon is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in many areas.
