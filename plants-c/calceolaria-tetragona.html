Calceolaria tetragona

Calceolaria tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored, ranging from yellow to orange. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Calceolaria tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
