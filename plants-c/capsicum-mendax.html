Capsicum mendax

Capsicum mendax, commonly known as the wild chili pepper, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated margins, and the flowers are yellowish-green with five petals. Its fruit is typically a small, elongated chili pepper that can range in color from green to red depending on maturity.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine and cuisine in its native regions.
