Crocodilium creticum

Crocodilium creticum, commonly known as the Cretan crocodile plant, is a rare and unique species native to the island of Crete.

It is characterized by its distinctive, scale-like leaves that resemble the skin of a crocodile, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
