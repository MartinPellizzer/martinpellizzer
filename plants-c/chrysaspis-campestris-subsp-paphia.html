Chrysaspis campestris subsp. paphia

Chrysaspis campestris subsp.

paphia, commonly known as the broadleaf goldenrod, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
