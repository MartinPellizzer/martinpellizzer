Clausena vestita

Clausena vestita, commonly known as the white-leafed tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale-green bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and climates.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
