Capsicum fasciculatum

Capsicum fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered pepper or wild chili, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its small, clustered fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces yellow flowers that are followed by the characteristic capsicum fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
