Castanopsis laotica

Castanopsis laotica, commonly known as the Laotian tallow tree, is a species of evergreen tree belonging to the Fagaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Laos and Vietnam. The plant has a dense, rounded crown and produces small, cupule-enclosed nuts. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and leathery, with a glossy surface.

Castanopsis laotica is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in reforestation efforts due to its ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
