Calocephalus francisii

Calocephalus francisii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis fruit, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange berries that enclose a fleshy, edible pulp. The plant produces clusters of bright red calyces that resemble lanterns, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The fruit is not only ornamental but also used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
