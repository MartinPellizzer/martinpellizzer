Cinnamosma fragrans var. baillonii

Cinnamosma fragrans var. baillonii, commonly known as the Baillon's cinnamon tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by dark purple berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
