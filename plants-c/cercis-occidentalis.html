Cercis occidentalis

Cercis occidentalis, commonly known as the western redbud, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, flat, brown pods that contain several seeds. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters and prefers well-drained soil.

The western redbud is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
