Calamus ruber

Calamus ruber, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia and North America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often aromatic and have a distinct, pleasant scent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in late summer. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Calamus ruber thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near water bodies.
