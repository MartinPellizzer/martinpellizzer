Cistus microphyllus

Cistus microphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and thrives in full sun. Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Cistus microphyllus is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
