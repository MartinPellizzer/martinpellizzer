Crataegus monogyna var. aurea

Crataegus monogyna var. aurea, commonly known as the golden hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn red in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedging and ornamental planting.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
