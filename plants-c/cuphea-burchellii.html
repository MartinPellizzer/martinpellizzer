Cuphea burchellii

Cuphea burchellii, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Cuphea burchellii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
