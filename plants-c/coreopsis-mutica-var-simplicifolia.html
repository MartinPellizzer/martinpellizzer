Coreopsis mutica var. simplicifolia

Coreopsis mutica var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the slender coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and linear, while its flowers are yellow with a prominent central disk.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
