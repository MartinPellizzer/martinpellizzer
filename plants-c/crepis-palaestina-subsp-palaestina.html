Crepis palaestina subsp. palaestina

Crepis palaestina subsp.

palaestina, commonly known as the Palestinian mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous areas.

The plant has small, basal rosettes of hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its inflorescence consists of a single flower per stem, which is characteristic of the genus Crepis.

It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
