Centaurea triumfettii subsp. cana

Centaurea triumfettii subsp.

cana, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellow to reddish-orange flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue. This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
