Cryptocarya obtusifolia

Cryptocarya obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cryptocarya, is a flowering plant species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are borne in clusters.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that develops from the flower and contains a single seed.
