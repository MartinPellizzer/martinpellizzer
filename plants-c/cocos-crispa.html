Cocos crispa

Cocos crispa, commonly known as the hairy coconut, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces large, fibrous fruits that contain a single, hard-shelled seed. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitats.
