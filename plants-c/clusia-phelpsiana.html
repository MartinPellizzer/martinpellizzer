Clusia phelpsiana

Clusia phelpsiana, commonly known as the Phelps's glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family, also known as the guttiferae family, and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are borne in dense inflorescences, and it is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments.

Clusia phelpsiana is also notable for its ability to tolerate shade and its use in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage.
