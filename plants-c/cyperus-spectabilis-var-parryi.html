Cyperus spectabilis var. parryi

Cyperus spectabilis var. parryi, commonly known as the Parry flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
