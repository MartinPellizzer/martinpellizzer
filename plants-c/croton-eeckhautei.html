Croton eeckhautei

Croton eeckhautei, commonly known as the yellow croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often features shades of yellow, red, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
