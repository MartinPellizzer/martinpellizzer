Chloris castilloniana var. pubescens

Chloris castilloniana var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent chloris, is a low-growing perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features fine, hair-covered leaves that give it its varietal name, "pubescens," meaning hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or white florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
