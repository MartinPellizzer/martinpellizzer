Cerasus occidentalis

Cerasus occidentalis, commonly known as the western cherry or Prunus occidentalis, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to western North America, particularly found in regions like California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, dark red cherry that ripens in late summer, and it is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
