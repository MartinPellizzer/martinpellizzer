Cistus × incanus

Cistus × incanus, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in the spring, which are clustered in terminal racemes. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Cistus × incanus is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
