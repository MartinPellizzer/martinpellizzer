Cryptocarya rubiginosa

Cryptocarya rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed cryptocarya, is a member of the Lauraceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and distinctive bark.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia, where it thrives in tropical rainforests. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, dark green on top, and lighter underneath, often with a fragrant scent.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing fruits that are oval and reddish when mature.
