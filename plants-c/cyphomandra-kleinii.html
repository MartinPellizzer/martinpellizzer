Cyphomandra kleinii

Cyphomandra kleinii, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features hairy, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains numerous seeds.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is considered a weed in some regions due to its aggressive growth habit.
