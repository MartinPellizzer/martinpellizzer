Carex stenochlaena

Carex stenochlaena, commonly known as narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typically found in wet or moist environments. Carex stenochlaena is often found in marshy areas, stream banks, and other wetland habitats.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
