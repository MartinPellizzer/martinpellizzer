Campanula collina subsp. collina

Campanula collina subsp.

collina, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine down, giving them a soft texture. Campanula collina subsp.

collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
