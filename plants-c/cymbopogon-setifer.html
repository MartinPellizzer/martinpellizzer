Cymbopogon setifer

Cymbopogon setifer, commonly known as the hairy lemongrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. It is often cultivated for its aromatic essential oils, which are extracted from its leaves and used in perfumes and traditional medicine.

Cymbopogon setifer thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
