Cyperus ascofibrillosus

Cyperus ascofibrillosus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus ascofibrillosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
