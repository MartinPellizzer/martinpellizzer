Castanopsis penangensis

Castanopsis penangensis, commonly known as Penang chestnut, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the island of Penang in Malaysia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its hard, durable wood. The plant produces small, cup-shaped fruits that contain one or two seeds, typical of the genus Castanopsis. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
