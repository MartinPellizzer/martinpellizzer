Crinum asiaticum

Crinum asiaticum, commonly known as the Indian lily, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Crinum asiaticum is also known for its strong, spicy fragrance, which is released when the flowers open.
