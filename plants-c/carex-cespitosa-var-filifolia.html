Carex cespitosa var. filifolia

Carex cespitosa var. filifolia, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often crowded at the base, forming dense clumps. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that bears small, three-ranked spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
