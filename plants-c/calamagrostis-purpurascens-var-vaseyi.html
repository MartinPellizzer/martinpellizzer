Calamagrostis purpurascens var. vaseyi

Calamagrostis purpurascens var. vaseyi, commonly known as the purple reed grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces feathery, plume-like inflorescences that add ornamental value, especially in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
