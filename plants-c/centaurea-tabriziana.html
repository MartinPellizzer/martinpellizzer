Centaurea tabriziana

Centaurea tabriziana, commonly known as Tabriz thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features blue to purple flower heads with yellow centers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Centaurea tabriziana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.
