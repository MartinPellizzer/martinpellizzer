Cucurbita maxima var. rostrata

Cucurbita maxima var. rostrata, commonly known as the Turk's Turban squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, turban-shaped fruit.

The plant produces large, vibrant orange to yellow fruits with a thick, waxy skin that protects the edible flesh inside. Its leaves are broad and lobed, typical of many squash species, while the vines can spread extensively across the ground. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal as well as its culinary uses, offering a sweet, nutty flavor when cooked.

It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
