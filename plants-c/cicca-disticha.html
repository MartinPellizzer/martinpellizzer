Cicca disticha

Cicca disticha, commonly known as the two-branched cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinct, bifurcating stem that splits into two branches near the base. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its reproductive structures include a cone-like inflorescence that bears both male and female flowers.

Native to the tropical regions of South America, it is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.
