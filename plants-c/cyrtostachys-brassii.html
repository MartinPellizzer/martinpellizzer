Cyrtostachys brassii

Cyrtostachys brassii, commonly known as the Malabar thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its woody stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin, while its flowers are typically borne in dense inflorescences.

Cyrtostachys brassii is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
