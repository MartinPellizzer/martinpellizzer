Clematis dilatata

Clematis dilatata, commonly known as the large-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its long, climbing stems can reach lengths of up to 20 feet, making it an excellent choice for trellises or arbors.

Clematis dilatata is known for its ability to bloom multiple times throughout the growing season, adding continuous floral interest to gardens.
