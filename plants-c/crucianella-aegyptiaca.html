Crucianella aegyptiaca

Crucianella aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian crucianella, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting a compact growth habit with small, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by blue-black berries. It is well adapted to saline and drought conditions, making it a hardy species in Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
