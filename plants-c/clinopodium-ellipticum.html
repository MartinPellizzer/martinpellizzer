Clinopodium ellipticum

Clinopodium ellipticum, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped, serrated leaves. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in dense whorls at the ends of the stems.

The foliage emits a strong minty scent when crushed, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Clinopodium.
