Chenopodium tataricum

Chenopodium tataricum, commonly known as Russian thistle or goatweed, is an annual flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit, with slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural and disturbed areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution and ecological adaptability.
