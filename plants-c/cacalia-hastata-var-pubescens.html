Cacalia hastata var. pubescens

Cacalia hastata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

The hairy bluebell is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
