Corylopsis rotundifolia

Corylopsis rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf flame twister, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has oval, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early bloomers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant flowers and attractive foliage.
