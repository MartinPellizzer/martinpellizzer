Carex karafutoana

Carex karafutoana, commonly known as the Sakhalin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Far East of Russia and parts of northern Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex karafutoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
