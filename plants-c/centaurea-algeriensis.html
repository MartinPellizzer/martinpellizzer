Centaurea algeriensis

Centaurea algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Centaurea algeriensis is often found in scrubland and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
