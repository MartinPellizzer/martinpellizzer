Ceropegia neocompta

Ceropegia neocompta, commonly known as the silver leaf ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
