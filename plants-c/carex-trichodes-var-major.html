Carex trichodes var. major

Carex trichodes var. major, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It produces small, clustered flower heads that are often wind-pollinated.

This variety is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
