Callisia palmeri

Callisia palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, three-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are succulent and can branch profusely.

Callisia palmeri is noted for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season, making it a valuable addition to xeriscaped gardens.
