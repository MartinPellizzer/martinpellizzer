Cassia mucronifera

Cassia mucronifera, commonly known as the Indian laburnum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in spring, followed by long, narrow pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. Cassia mucronifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
