Colchicum asteranthum

Colchicum asteranthum, commonly known as the starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Colchicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean, particularly in southern Europe and North Africa. The plant produces striking, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bulbs are toxic and contain colchicine, a compound used in medicine.

The leaves are basal, lanceolate, and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.
