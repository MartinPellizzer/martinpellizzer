Carex × mendica

Carex × mendica, commonly known as the hybrid sedges, is a species within the Carex genus, characterized by its grass-like appearance and clustered growth habit.

It typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters that are often wind-pollinated. This plant is native to regions with moist, temperate climates and thrives in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that aids in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Carex × mendica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate various soil conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
