Christella × kumaunica

Christella × kumaunica, commonly known as the Himalayan snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 2,500 to 4,500 meters. Its leaves are dark green, leathery, and evergreen, providing a striking contrast to the delicate flowers.

Christella × kumaunica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens for its unique floral display.
