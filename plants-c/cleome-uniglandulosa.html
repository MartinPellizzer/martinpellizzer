Cleome uniglandulosa

Cleome uniglandulosa, commonly known as the one-gland cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are characterized by their five petals and numerous stamens, which give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

Cleome uniglandulosa is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
