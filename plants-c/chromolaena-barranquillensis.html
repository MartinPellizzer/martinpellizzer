Chromolaena barranquillensis

Chromolaena barranquillensis, commonly known as the Barranquilla chromolaena, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong, somewhat aromatic odor.

It produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
