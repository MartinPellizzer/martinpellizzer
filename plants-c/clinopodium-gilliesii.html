Clinopodium gilliesii

Clinopodium gilliesii, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often at high elevations. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in summer.

The leaves are fragrant and have been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for medicinal and culinary purposes.
