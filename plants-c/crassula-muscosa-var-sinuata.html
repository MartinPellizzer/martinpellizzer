Crassula muscosa var. sinuata

Crassula muscosa var. sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuous Stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or arid environments.

Its unique leaf texture and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
