Crepis riparia

Crepis riparia, commonly known as the river cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to wetland and riverine habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and marshes, where it thrives in semi-shaded conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant produces small, oblong seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Crepis riparia is often found in disturbed or aquatic environments, showcasing its adaptability to varying moisture levels.
