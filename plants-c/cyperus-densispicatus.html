Cyperus densispicatus

Cyperus densispicatus, commonly known as the dense-hair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it a common component of wetland ecosystems.

Cyperus densispicatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
