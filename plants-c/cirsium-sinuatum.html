Cirsium sinuatum

Cirsium sinuatum, commonly known as the heart-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its spiny leaves and stems serve as a natural defense mechanism against herbivores.
