Cyanthillium conyzoides

Cyanthillium conyzoides, commonly known as the blueweed or blue thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a hairy stem and produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cyanthillium conyzoides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
