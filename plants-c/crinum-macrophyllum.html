Crinum macrophyllum

Crinum macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant crinum, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers with a strong, spicy scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Crinum macrophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow quite tall, reaching up to 6 feet in height.
