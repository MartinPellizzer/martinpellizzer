Cupania pentaphylla

Cupania pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf cupania, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that often have five lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
