Cardamine flexuosa f. umbrosa

Cardamine flexuosa f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving water cress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, shaded environments.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or floating in still or slow-moving water. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. Its rhizomes are frequently used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic properties.

This variant is distinguished by its preference for low-light conditions and its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
