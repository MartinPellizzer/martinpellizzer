Chlorogalum grandiflorum

Chlorogalum grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant whiteball lily, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, fleshy, light green leaves that form a dense, spherical shape. The plant produces showy white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native range.
