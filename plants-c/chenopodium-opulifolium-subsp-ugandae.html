Chenopodium opulifolium subsp. ugandae

Chenopodium opulifolium subsp.

ugandae, commonly known as the Ugandan goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to East Africa. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, greenish-white inflorescences, and it produces small, bead-like fruits.

It is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
