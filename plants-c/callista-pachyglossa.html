Callista pachyglossa

Callista pachyglossa, commonly known as the broad-lipped lily, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Callista pachyglossa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
