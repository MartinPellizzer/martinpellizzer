Calliandra houstoniana var. acapulcensis

Calliandra houstoniana var. acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant flowers.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
