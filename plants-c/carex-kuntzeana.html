Carex kuntzeana

Carex kuntzeana, commonly known as the Kuntze's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Carex kuntzeana is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
