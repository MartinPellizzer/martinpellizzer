Coelachne angolensis

Coelachne angolensis, commonly known as Angola grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Coelachne angolensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in such habitats.
