Cambessedesia hilariana var. bidentata

Cambessedesia hilariana var. bidentata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The species is also noted for its unique bark, which is thick and fissured, contributing to its ecological and economic significance.
