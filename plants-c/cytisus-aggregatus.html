Cytisus aggregatus

Cytisus aggregatus, commonly known as the clustered broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus aggregatus is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
