Chorispermum persicum

Chorispermum persicum, commonly known as the Persian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its seeds are encased in a hard, dry fruit known as a utricle, which aids in seed dispersal.

Chorispermum persicum is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
