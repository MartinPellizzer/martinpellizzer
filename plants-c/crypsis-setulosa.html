Crypsis setulosa

Crypsis setulosa, commonly known as the hairy cryptogram, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a creeping growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. Its scientific name reflects its cryptic appearance and the presence of fine, hairy structures on its stems and leaves.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
