Crataegus viridis var. velutina

Crataegus viridis var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, preferring partial to full shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
