Celastrus liebmannii

Celastrus liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, elliptical seeds.

Celastrus liebmannii is often found in woodland and forest understory habitats.
