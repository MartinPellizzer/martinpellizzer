Cerasus conadenia

Cerasus conadenia, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or conadenia cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Prunus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Cerasus conadenia is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
