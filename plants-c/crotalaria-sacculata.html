Crotalaria sacculata

Crotalaria sacculata, commonly known as the bladderpod crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and pod-like seed capsules. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the seeds are enclosed in inflated, bladder-like pods, which give the species its common name.

Crotalaria sacculata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
