Carex vulgaris var. alpina

Carex vulgaris var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to alpine and subalpine regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species in high-altitude environments.
