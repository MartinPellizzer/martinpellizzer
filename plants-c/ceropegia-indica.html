Ceropegia indica

Ceropegia indica, commonly known as the dwarf chainplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, waxy leaves. The plant produces unique, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.
