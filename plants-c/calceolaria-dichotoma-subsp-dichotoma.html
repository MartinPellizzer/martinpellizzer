Calceolaria dichotoma subsp. dichotoma

Calceolaria dichotoma subsp.

dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive bilaterally symmetrical flowers that resemble small slippers, with a unique pouch-like structure at the base.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
