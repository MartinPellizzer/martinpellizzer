Carex ramenskii var. caudata

Carex ramenskii var. caudata, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in eastern Asia.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and tapering, with a distinctive tufted growth habit at the base. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
