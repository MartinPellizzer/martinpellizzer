Convallaria rosea

Convallaria rosea, commonly known as the sweet William, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Primulaceae.

It is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas or shaded gardens.

Convallaria rosea is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in floral arrangements.
