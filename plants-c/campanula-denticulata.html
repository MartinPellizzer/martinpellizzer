Campanula denticulata

Campanula denticulata, commonly known as the serrated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
