Ceratocephala spicata

Ceratocephala spicata, commonly known as the spicate horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, horn-shaped petals that are typically a vibrant red or orange in color. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ceratocephala spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
