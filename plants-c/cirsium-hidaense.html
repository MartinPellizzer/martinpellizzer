Cirsium hidaense

Cirsium hidaense, commonly known as Hida thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and its flowers are arranged in a spherical cluster.

Cirsium hidaense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
