Chrysophyllum oppositum

Chrysophyllum oppositum, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow bell, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, sweet, and fleshy fruits resembling small cherries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and the nutritional value of its fruits.
