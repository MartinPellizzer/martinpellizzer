Carex brownii var. transversa

Carex brownii var. transversa, commonly known as the transversa sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their ecological importance in wetland habitats. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a distinct midrib, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster.

This variety is characterized by its unique leaf structure and is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
