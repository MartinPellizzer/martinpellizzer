Crassina tenuiflora

Crassina tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered crassina, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive floral display.
