Crotalaria priestleyoides

Crotalaria priestleyoides, commonly known as Priestley's crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Crotalaria. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
