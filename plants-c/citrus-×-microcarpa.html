Citrus × microcarpa

Citrus × microcarpa, commonly known as the calamondin orange, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from the cross between the mandarin and the pomelo.

It is characterized by its small, round, and thick-skinned fruit, which is typically bright orange when ripe. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves and a compact growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible, acidic fruit.

The fruit is commonly used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
