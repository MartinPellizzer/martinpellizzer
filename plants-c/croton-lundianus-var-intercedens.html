Croton lundianus var. intercedens

Croton lundianus var. intercedens, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display a mix of red, yellow, and green hues. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its vibrant foliage.
