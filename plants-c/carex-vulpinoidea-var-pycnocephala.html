Carex vulpinoidea var. pycnocephala

Carex vulpinoidea var. pycnocephala, commonly known as the dense-headed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in areas with poor to moderately fertile soil.

Its dense growth habit makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
