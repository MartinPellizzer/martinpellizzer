Caladenia clarkiae

Caladenia clarkiae, commonly known as Clark's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to western Australia.

It produces a single, slender stem that bears a single, intricately patterned orchid flower. The flower has a narrow, club-shaped labellum and is typically pink to purple in color. It blooms during the autumn season and is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils.

This species is part of the Caladenia genus, which is known for its diverse and often striking floral structures.
