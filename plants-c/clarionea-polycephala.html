Clarionea polycephala

Clarionea polycephala, commonly known as the multi-headed clarion, is a striking plant native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique appearance, featuring multiple heads or shoots emerging from a single stem, giving it a distinctive and often whimsical look. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the warm season. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to thrive in high humidity and partial shade.

Despite its unusual appearance, Clarionea polycephala is relatively low maintenance and can be a fascinating addition to tropical gardens.
