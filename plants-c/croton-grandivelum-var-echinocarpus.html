Croton grandivelum var. echinocarpus

Croton grandivelum var. echinocarpus, commonly known as the spiny fruit croton, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often green with reddish veins. The plant produces distinctive, spiny capsules that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
