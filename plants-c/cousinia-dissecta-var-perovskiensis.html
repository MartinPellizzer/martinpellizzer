Cousinia dissecta var. perovskiensis

Cousinia dissecta var. perovskiensis, commonly known as the Perovskian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage is finely dissected, giving it a delicate appearance, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
