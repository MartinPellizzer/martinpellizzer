Ceratocephalus decumbens

Ceratocephalus decumbens, commonly known as the common sandbur, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and produces small, spiny burrs that enclose its seeds. The plant has hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
