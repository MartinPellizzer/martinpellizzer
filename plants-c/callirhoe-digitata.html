Callirhoe digitata

Callirhoe digitata, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
