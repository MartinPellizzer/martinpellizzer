Cereus eriophorus var. fragrans

Cereus eriophorus var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and its ability to produce large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
