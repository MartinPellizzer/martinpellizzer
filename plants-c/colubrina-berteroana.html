Colubrina berteroana

Colubrina berteroana, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, fleshy berries that are violet or purple in color, which are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along forest edges.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
