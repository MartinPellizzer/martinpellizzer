Cassia × desolata var. involucrata

Cassia × desolata var. involucrata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
