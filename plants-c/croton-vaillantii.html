Croton vaillantii

Croton vaillantii, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, colorful, and often velvety leaves that display a variety of shades, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

Croton vaillantii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
