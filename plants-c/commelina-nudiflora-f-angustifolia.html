Commelina nudiflora f. angustifolia

Commelina nudiflora f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf commelina, is a flowering plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale blue flowers that lack petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical climates.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
