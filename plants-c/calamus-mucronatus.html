Calamus mucronatus

Calamus mucronatus, commonly known as the narrow-tipped calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers.

Calamus mucronatus is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
