Campanula rhomboidalis f. paniculata

Campanula rhomboidalis f. paniculata, commonly known as the panicled bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
