Centaurea polycephala

Centaurea polycephala, commonly known as the many-headed centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, spiky flower heads that range in color from blue to purple. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea polycephala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
