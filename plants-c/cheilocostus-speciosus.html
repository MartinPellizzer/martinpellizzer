Cheilocostus speciosus

Cheilocostus speciosus, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
