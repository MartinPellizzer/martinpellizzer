Chrysanthemum tomentosum

Chrysanthemum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-white hairs covering its stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange, typically blooming in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
