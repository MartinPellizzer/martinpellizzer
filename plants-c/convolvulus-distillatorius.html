Convolvulus distillatorius

Convolvulus distillatorius, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing vines and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue and purple. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Convolvulus distillatorius is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and rapid growth habit.
