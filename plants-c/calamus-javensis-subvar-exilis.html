Calamus javensis subvar. exilis

Calamus javensis subvar. exilis, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, which are followed by elongated, canoe-shaped fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
