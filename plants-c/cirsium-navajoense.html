Cirsium navajoense

Cirsium navajoense, commonly known as Navajo thistle, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as deserts and grasslands. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Cirsium navajoense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
