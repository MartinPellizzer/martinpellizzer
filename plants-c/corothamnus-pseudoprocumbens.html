Corothamnus pseudoprocumbens

Corothamnus pseudoprocumbens, commonly known as the creeping goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically thrives in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
