Cyperus amane

Cyperus amane, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Cyperus amane produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.
