Centaurea × nouelii subsp. duffortii

Centaurea × nouelii subsp.

duffortii, commonly known as the Duffort's cornflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and serrated leaves, which are typical of the Centaurea genus.

This subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to attract pollinators.
