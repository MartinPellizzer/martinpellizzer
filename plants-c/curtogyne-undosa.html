Curtogyne undosa

Curtogyne undosa, commonly known as the wavy leaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that have a distinct wavy or undulating margin. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in the spring. Curtogyne undosa thrives in moist, swampy soils and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
