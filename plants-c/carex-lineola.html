Carex lineola

Carex lineola, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of inconspicuous flowers, which are usually green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex lineola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
