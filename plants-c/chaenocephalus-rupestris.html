Chaenocephalus rupestris

Chaenocephalus rupestris, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by brown, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a hardy and resilient species in its native habitat.
