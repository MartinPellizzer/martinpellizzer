Cyanus vorasanus

Cyanus vorasanus, commonly known as the Vorasan bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Cyanus vorasanus is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native environment.
