Canna peruviana-robusta

Canna peruviana-robusta, commonly known as the giant canna, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, and its showy, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread aggressively, making it both a popular ornamental and a potential weed in some regions.

Canna peruviana-robusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
