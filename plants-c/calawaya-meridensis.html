Calawaya meridensis

Calawaya meridensis, commonly known as the mountain calawaya, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations above 1,500 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Calawaya meridensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine meadows.
