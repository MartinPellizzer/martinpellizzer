Castalia ampla

Castalia ampla, commonly known as the broadleaf castalia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its blooming period occurs throughout the warm seasons, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Castalia ampla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
