Cistus polymorphus f. leiocarpus

Cistus polymorphus f. leiocarpus, commonly known as the smooth-corked rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces distinctive corky buds that give it its common name, and its leaves are typically leathery and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
