Ceropegia aperta

Ceropegia aperta, commonly known as the string of turtles, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and fleshy, tubular leaves that resemble small, rounded structures. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique structure that resembles a lantern. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia aperta is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
