Campanula dracunculifolia

Campanula dracunculifolia, commonly known as the dragon's tongue bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula dracunculifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and easy cultivation.
