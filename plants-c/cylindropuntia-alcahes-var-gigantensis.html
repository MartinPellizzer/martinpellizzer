Cylindropuntia alcahes var. gigantensis

Cylindropuntia alcahes var. gigantensis, commonly known as the giant barrel cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has prominent spines and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer. Its stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

It plays an important role in the desert ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
