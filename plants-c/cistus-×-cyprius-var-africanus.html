Cistus × cyprius var. africanus

Cistus × cyprius var. africanus, commonly known as the African rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
