Cymbidium poilanei

Cymbidium poilanei, commonly known as the Poilane's orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing vibrant, often pink or red, spurred flowers. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
