Centaurea urvillei subsp. armata

Centaurea urvillei subsp.

armata, commonly known as the Armenian knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features erect, sparsely branched stems and produces striking purple to pink flowers in late summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
