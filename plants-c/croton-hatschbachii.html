Croton hatschbachii

Croton hatschbachii, commonly known as the white croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
