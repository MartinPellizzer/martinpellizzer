Chusquea limensis

Chusquea limensis, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, forming dense clumps or stands. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems.

Due to its structural strength and adaptability, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
