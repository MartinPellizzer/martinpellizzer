Cestrum stipulatum

Cestrum stipulatum, commonly known as the red-veined cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, with prominent veins. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a distinctive red or purple midrib.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, dark seeds.
