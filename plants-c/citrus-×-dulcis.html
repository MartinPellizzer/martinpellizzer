Citrus × dulcis

Citrus × dulcis, commonly known as the sweet orange.

It is a hybrid citrus fruit that originated from the cross between the pomelo and mandarin. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, glossy leaves and a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowers are white and fragrant, typically appearing in spring, followed by the development of round, orange-colored fruits.

The fruit contains a segmented pulp that is juicy and rich in vitamin C, making it a popular fruit in culinary and medicinal uses.
