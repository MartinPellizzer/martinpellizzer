Crataegus oxyacantha f. vernicosa

Crataegus oxyacantha f. vernicosa, commonly known as the glossy hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive glossy appearance. This shrub or small tree typically grows in temperate regions and produces small, red berries in the fall. The plant is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils, where it thrives in partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
