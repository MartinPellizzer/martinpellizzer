Carex moniezii

Carex moniezii, commonly known as the Montezuma sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex moniezii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
