Cuphea avigera var. pulcherrima

Cuphea avigera var. pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame flower or whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, whorled clusters. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant colors and unique flower structure.
