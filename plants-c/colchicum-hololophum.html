Colchicum hololophum

Colchicum hololophum, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada larkspur, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Colchicum hololophum is part of the Colchicaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
