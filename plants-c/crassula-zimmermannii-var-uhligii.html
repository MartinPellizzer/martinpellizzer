Crassula zimmermannii var. uhligii

Crassula zimmermannii var. uhligii, commonly known as the Uhlig's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
