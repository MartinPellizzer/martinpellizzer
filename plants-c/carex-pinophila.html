Carex pinophila

Carex pinophila, commonly known as the pine forest sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which is part of the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in pine and mixed forests. Carex pinophila has grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
