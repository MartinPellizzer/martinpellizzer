Carex obovata

Carex obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows, characteristic of many sedges. Its flowering structure consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex obovata is often found in moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
