Chamaecrista fallacina

Chamaecrista fallacina, commonly known as the yellow clusterbean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds used in various culinary applications. It is often grown for its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing capabilities, which benefit soil fertility.

Chamaecrista fallacina thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.
