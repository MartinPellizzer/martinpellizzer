Carum scaberulum

Carum scaberulum, commonly known as rough parsley, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, dill, and parsley. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white or yellowish flowers that appear in umbels. It is often found in moist, calcareous soils along streams and in meadows.

Carum scaberulum is valued for its aromatic qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
