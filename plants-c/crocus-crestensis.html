Crocus crestensis

Crocus crestensis, commonly known as the Alps crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal at the base. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-altitude meadows, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Crocus crestensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
