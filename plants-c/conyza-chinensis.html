Conyza chinensis

Conyza chinensis, commonly known as Chinese cudweed, is an annual flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. It has hairy stems and leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are usually yellowish or white.

The plant is also known for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, which contribute to its widespread distribution.
