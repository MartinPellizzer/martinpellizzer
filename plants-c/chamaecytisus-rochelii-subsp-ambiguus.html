Chamaecytisus rochelii subsp. ambiguus

Chamaecytisus rochelii subsp.

ambiguus, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features slender, woody stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are small, alternate, and often have a slight pubescence.

It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
