Carduus × polyacanthos

Carduus × polyacanthos, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to pink flower heads, which consist of both ray florets and disk florets. The plant has a robust, upright stem with stiff, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
