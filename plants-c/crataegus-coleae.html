Crataegus coleae

Crataegus coleae, commonly known as the Cole's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it typically grows in moist, wooded areas.

Crataegus coleae is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native habitat.
