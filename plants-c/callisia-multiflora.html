Callisia multiflora

Callisia multiflora, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has fibrous roots and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for container gardening. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Callisia multiflora is also known for its ability to climb or trail along surfaces, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellises.
