Cirsium eriophorum subsp. chodatii

Cirsium eriophorum subsp.

chodatii, commonly known as the Chodat's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish purple composite flower heads.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture and serrated edges. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
