Campanula merxmuelleri

Campanula merxmuelleri, commonly known as the Merxmueller's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Campanula merxmuelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
