Crataegus sertata

Crataegus sertata, commonly known as the Japanese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its seasonal interest.
