Capparis rotundifolia var. longispina

Capparis rotundifolia var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features prickly stems and leaves with a rounded shape, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
