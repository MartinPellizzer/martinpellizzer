Ceropegia longidens

Ceropegia longidens, commonly known as the long-stemmed ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thin, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Ceropegia longidens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
