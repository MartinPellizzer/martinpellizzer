Cystium macrolobum

Cystium macrolobum, commonly known as the large-leafed rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in clusters, providing a compact and rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Cystium macrolobum is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, though it may require protection from frost in colder climates.
