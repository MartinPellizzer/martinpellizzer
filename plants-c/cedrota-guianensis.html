Cedrota guianensis

Cedrota guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese cedar, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and smooth, grayish bark. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, while its flowers are small and yellowish, typically appearing in clusters.

Cedrota guianensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
