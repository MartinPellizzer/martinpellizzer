Chamaemelum praecox

Chamaemelum praecox, commonly known as early chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers with white ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a common sight in meadows and grasslands.

Chamaemelum praecox is often used in herbal remedies due to its aromatic properties and mild sedative effects.
