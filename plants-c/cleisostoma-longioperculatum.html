Cleisostoma longioperculatum

Cleisostoma longioperculatum, commonly known as the long-pericarp cleisostoma, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
