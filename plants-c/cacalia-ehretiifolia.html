Cacalia ehretiifolia

Cacalia ehretiifolia, commonly known as the fringed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in a compact, bushy form. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cacalia ehretiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
