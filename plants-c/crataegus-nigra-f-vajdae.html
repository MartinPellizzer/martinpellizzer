Crataegus nigra f. vajdae

Crataegus nigra f. vajdae, commonly known as the blackthorn form vajdae, is a rare horticultural variant of the common blackthorn.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries and dense, thorny branches. The plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by its distinctive fruit.

This form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
