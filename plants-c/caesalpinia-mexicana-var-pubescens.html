Caesalpinia mexicana var. pubescens

Caesalpinia mexicana var. pubescens, commonly known as the Mexican bristley legume, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod fruits. The plant is notable for its hairy or pubescent surfaces, which give it its varietal name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry to semi-arid environments.

Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
