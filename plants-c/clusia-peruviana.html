Clusia peruviana

Clusia peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in coastal regions.

Clusia peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
