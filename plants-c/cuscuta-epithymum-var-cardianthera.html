Cuscuta epithymum var. cardianthera

Cuscuta epithymum var. cardianthera, commonly known as the redstem dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a vine-like structure composed of thin, thread-like stems. The plant is often found growing on various host plants, including grasses and shrubs, in arid and semi-arid environments. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through mechanical means, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Despite its parasitic nature, it plays a role in ecological systems by influencing host plant populations and providing food for certain insect species.
