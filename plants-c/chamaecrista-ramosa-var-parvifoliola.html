Chamaecrista ramosa var. parvifoliola

Chamaecrista ramosa var. parvifoliola, commonly known as the small-leaf sicklepod, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pinnate leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange pea-like flowers that are often clustered in spikes. Its fruits are dry pods that split open to release small, round seeds.

It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
