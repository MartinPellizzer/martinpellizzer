Chamaecrista polystachya

Chamaecrista polystachya, commonly known as the partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its seeds are encased in a hairy pod and are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Chamaecrista polystachya is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
