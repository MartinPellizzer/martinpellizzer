Clematis coccinea var. parviflora

Clematis coccinea var. parviflora, commonly known as the scarlet clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange-red in color, with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are opposite on the stems, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis coccinea var. parviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
