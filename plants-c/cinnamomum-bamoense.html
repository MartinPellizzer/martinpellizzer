Cinnamomum bamoense

Cinnamomum bamoense, commonly known as Bamo cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Myanmar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries containing winged seeds. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are valued for their fragrance and medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
