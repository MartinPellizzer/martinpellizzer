Carpinus betulus subsp. carpinizza

Carpinus betulus subsp.

carpinizza, commonly known as the European hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits called samaras. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in ecological habitats.
