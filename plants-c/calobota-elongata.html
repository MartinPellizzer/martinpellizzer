Calobota elongata

Calobota elongata, commonly known as the elongated calobota, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its elongated, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in whorls, typically displaying shades of pink or purple.

Calobota elongata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
