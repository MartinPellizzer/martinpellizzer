Calytrix verticillata

Calytrix verticillata, commonly known as the clustered calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the eastern and southern regions of the country. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in groups, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and it is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Calytrix verticillata is also known for its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it well-suited for coastal landscapes.
