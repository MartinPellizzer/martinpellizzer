Corydalis pauciflora var. aquilegifolia

Corydalis pauciflora var. aquilegifolia, commonly known as the aquatic corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in shaded woodland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
