Capparis pseudocerasifera

Capparis pseudocerasifera, commonly known as the Mediterranean caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and small, waxy leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by edible, greenish-yellow fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its capers in culinary applications.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
