Coelogyne krauseana

Coelogyne krauseana, commonly known as the Krause's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically orange-red with white and yellow markings. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Coelogyne krauseana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
