Cistus hispidus

Cistus hispidus, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant produces showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance and aiding in water retention.

Cistus hispidus is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
