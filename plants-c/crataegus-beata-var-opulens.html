Crataegus beata var. opulens

Crataegus beata var. opulens, commonly known as the opulent hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the autumn, which are edible but often tart.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its cardiovascular benefits.
