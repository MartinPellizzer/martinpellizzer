Crotalaria nigrescens

Crotalaria nigrescens, commonly known as black crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive black pods that develop after flowering. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are usually yellow with prominent violet or purple markings, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is also known for its toxic properties, containing compounds that can be harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
