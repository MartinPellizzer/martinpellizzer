Cyperus pinguis

Cyperus pinguis, commonly known as the broadleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus pinguis is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
