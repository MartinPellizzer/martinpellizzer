Cyperus junceus

Cyperus junceus, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along waterways. The plant is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to soil stabilization in wetland environments.
