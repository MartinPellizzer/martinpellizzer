Cigarrilla

Cigarrilla, commonly known as the tobacco plant, is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or greenish flowers. The plant is cultivated for its leaves, which contain nicotine and other alkaloids. Cigarrilla has a hairy stem and produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often grown in tropical and subtropical regions.
