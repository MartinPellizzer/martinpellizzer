Calophyllum aureum

Calophyllum aureum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The tree has smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
