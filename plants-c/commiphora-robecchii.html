Commiphora robecchii

Commiphora robecchii, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its leaves are opposite, elliptic, and have a leathery texture, while its bark is thick and fissured.

The tree is valued for its unique chemical composition, including various terpenoids and sesquiterpenes.
