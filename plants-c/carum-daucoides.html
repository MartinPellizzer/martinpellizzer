Carum daucoides

Carum daucoides, commonly known as wild chervil, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley, dill, and carrots, and is characterized by its umbellate inflorescences. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white to greenish flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Carum daucoides is also known for its mild aromatic scent and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
