Convolvulus burmanni

Convolvulus burmanni, commonly known as the Burmann's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its flowers range in color from white to pink and blue.

Convolvulus burmanni is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
