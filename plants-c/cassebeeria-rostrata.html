Cassebeeria rostrata

Cassebeeria rostrata, commonly known as the rostrate cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
