Cheirolophus lanceolatus

Cheirolophus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
