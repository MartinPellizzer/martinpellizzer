Cirsium bulgaricum subsp. armatum

Cirsium bulgaricum subsp.

armatum, commonly known as the Bulgarian thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
