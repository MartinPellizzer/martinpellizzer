Commelina attenuata

Commelina attenuata, commonly known as the slender spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers with three petals and three sepals, typical of the Commelina genus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential weed.
