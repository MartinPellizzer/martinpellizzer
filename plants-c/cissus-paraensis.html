Cissus paraensis

Cissus paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian rock fig, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and ability to adhere to surfaces using tendrils. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, raised dots. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, fleshy berry.

Cissus paraensis is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in humid, shaded environments.
