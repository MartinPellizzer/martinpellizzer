Cerastium fontanum var. glandulosum

Cerastium fontanum var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves with a distinctive glandular surface that gives it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its delicate appearance and rapid growth make it a common sight in temperate regions.
