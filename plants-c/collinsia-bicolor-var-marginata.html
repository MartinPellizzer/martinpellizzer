Collinsia bicolor var. marginata

Collinsia bicolor var. marginata, commonly known as the pink and white fairybells, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range from pale pink to white, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or meadows. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered blooms throughout the spring.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
