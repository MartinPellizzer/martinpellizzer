Cydonia piriformis

Cydonia piriformis, commonly known as the quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces a fragrant, yellowish flower in spring, which is followed by a green, hard fruit that ripens to a golden-orange color. The fruit is characterized by its rough, warty skin and has a strong, aromatic scent when mature. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to apples and pears.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in cooking and traditional medicine.
