Centropogon trianae var. cuspidata

Centropogon trianae var. cuspidata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, while the flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its visual appeal.
