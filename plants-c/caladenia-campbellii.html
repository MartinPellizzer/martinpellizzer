Caladenia campbellii

Caladenia campbellii, commonly known as Campbell's spider orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to Western Australia.

It features a slender, hairy stem with a single, intricate flower that resembles a spider in appearance. The plant typically grows in sandy soils within open habitats, often in heathlands or shrublands. Its flowers are predominantly pink or purple, with a distinctive, narrow labellum that enhances its visual appeal.

The species is known for its short flowering period, usually occurring in late winter to early spring.
