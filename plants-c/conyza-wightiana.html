Conyza wightiana

Conyza wightiana, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fine, hairy leaves and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Conyza wightiana is also known for its ability to spread easily through wind-dispersed seeds.
