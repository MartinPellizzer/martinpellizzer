Croton sibundoyensis

Croton sibundoyensis, commonly known as the Sibundoy croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and striking, colorful flowers that range from yellow to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments at moderate elevations, often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
