Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. iridis

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. iridis, commonly known as the iridescent goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its common name reflects the iridescent sheen of its foliage under sunlight.
