Carex vesiculosa f. pallida

Carex vesiculosa f. pallida, commonly known as the pale bulblet sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, pale green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

This variety is notable for its distinctive pale coloration and is valued in wetland restoration projects.
