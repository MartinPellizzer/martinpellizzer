Crocus versicolor var. crestensis

Crocus versicolor var. crestensis, commonly known as the Crested Crocus, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to the alpine regions of the Alps.

This variety is distinguished by its striking, often bicolor flowers that feature a mix of yellow and purple hues. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped blooms that typically appear in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge after the flowers have faded.
