Calathea leucocephala

Calathea leucocephala, commonly known as the white-headed candle plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or cream-colored margin. The plant features a short, woody stem and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Calathea leucocephala thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
