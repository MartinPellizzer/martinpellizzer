Crataegus brevispina f. subinermis

Crataegus brevispina f. subinermis, commonly known as the smooth-fruited hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the hawthorn genus.

It is characterized by its short, spiny thorns, which are less pronounced than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and attract various wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its foliage consists of ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.
