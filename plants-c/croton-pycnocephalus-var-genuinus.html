Croton pycnocephalus var. genuinus

Croton pycnocephalus var. genuinus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a unique, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are usually thick and leathery, with a deep green hue.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its striking foliage.
