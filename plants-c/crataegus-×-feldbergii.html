Crataegus × feldbergii

Crataegus × feldbergii, commonly known as the Feldberg hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus laevigata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart, and it is native to Europe.

Crataegus × feldbergii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and as a hedge.
