Crataegus monogyna var. polonskiana

Crataegus monogyna var. polonskiana, commonly known as the Polish hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
