Cola quintasii

Cola quintasii, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that develop into hard, woody pods containing elliptical seeds. Cola quintasii is notable for its caffeine content, similar to that of the more well-known cola acuminata.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions of Africa.
