Cassia rusa

Cassia rusa, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Cassia rusa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds contain compounds that have been studied for potential pharmacological applications.
