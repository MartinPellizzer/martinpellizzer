Cycnium verdickii

Cycnium verdickii, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble cups, hence its common name. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Cycnium verdickii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
