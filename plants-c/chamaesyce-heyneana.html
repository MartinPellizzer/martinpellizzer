Chamaesyce heyneana

Chamaesyce heyneana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandwort, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically features small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, usually during the wet season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
