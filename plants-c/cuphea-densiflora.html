Cuphea densiflora

Cuphea densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea densiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
