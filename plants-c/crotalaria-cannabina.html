Crotalaria cannabina

Crotalaria cannabina, commonly known as Indian hemp or devil's weed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant contains various alkaloids, some of which have medicinal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
