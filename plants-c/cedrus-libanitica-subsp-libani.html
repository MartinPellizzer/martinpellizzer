Cedrus libanitica subsp. libani

Cedrus libanitica subsp.

libani, commonly known as the Lebanon cedar, is a tall, coniferous tree native to the eastern Mediterranean. It is characterized by its dark green, scale-like leaves and its distinctive, flattened, cone-like fruits.

The tree produces aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, temperate climate.

This subspecies is a symbol of resilience and has cultural significance in the region.
