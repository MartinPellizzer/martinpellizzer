Cirsium haussknechtii

Cirsium haussknechtii, commonly known as the Hausseckner's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, demonstrating a high degree of adaptability to arid conditions. Its morphology includes a robust stem with basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in stiff hairs.

Cirsium haussknechtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
