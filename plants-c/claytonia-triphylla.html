Claytonia triphylla

Claytonia triphylla, commonly known as the "spring beauty," is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, white or pinkish, and bloom in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest floors and along stream banks.
