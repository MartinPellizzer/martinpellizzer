Croton mazapensis var. obtusifolius

Croton mazapensis var. obtusifolius, commonly known as the Mexican croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
