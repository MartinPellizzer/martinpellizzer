Crocus pallasii subsp. dispathaceus

Crocus pallasii subsp.

dispathaceus, commonly known as the Armenian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare and exotic plants.
