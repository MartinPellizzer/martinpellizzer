Chamaeiris marschalliana

Chamaeiris marschalliana, commonly known as the Marschall's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid climates.
