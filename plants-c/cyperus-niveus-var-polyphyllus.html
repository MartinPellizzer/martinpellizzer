Cyperus niveus var. polyphyllus

Cyperus niveus var. polyphyllus, commonly known as the white flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in spikelets. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a potential indicator of water quality.
