Cytinus ruber subsp. canariensis

Cytinus ruber subsp.

canariensis, commonly known as the Canary carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is a member of the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red, bilaterally symmetrical flowers.

The plant produces a fleshy, club-shaped structure called a "cyst" that houses the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is notable for its unique growth habit and is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptations.
