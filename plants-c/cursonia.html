Cursonia

Cursonia, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn bright orange when mature, containing a single seed. Cursonia is valued for its high vitamin C content and is widely used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
