Cleome pungens

Cleome pungens, commonly known as the spiked cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome pungens is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and upright growth habit.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
