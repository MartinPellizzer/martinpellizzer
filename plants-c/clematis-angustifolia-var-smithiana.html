Clematis angustifolia var. smithiana

Clematis angustifolia var. smithiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, climbing stems with small, finely toothed leaves that are typically dark green in color. The flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive, showy appearance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
