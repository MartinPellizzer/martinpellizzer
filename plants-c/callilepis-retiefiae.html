Callilepis retiefiae

Callilepis retiefiae, commonly known as the Retief's candle bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble candles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Callilepis retiefiae is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
