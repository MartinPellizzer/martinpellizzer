Cerastium hybridum

Cerastium hybridum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to June. The plant has rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks.

Cerastium hybridum is part of the Caryophyllaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
