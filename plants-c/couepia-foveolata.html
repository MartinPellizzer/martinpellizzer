Couepia foveolata

Couepia foveolata, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth habit. Its flowers typically open in the morning, hence its common name, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, round seeds.
