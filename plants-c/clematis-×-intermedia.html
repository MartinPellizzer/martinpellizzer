Clematis × intermedia

Clematis × intermedia, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and can reach lengths of up to 20 feet. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.

Clematis × intermedia prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth and flowering.
