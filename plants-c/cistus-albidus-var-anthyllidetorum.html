Cistus albidus var. anthyllidetorum

Cistus albidus var. anthyllidetorum, commonly known as the white rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
