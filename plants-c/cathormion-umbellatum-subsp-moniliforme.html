Cathormion umbellatum subsp. moniliforme

Cathormion umbellatum subsp.

moniliforme, commonly known as the horned bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color.

The subspecies is notable for its unique inflorescence, which consists of a cluster of flowers arranged in a cylindrical or horn-like shape. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
