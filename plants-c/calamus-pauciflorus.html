Calamus pauciflorus

Calamus pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in wetland and marshy environments. The inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike bearing few small, yellowish flowers.

Calamus pauciflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
