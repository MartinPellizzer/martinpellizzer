Cerastium carniolicum

Cerastium carniolicum, commonly known as alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a dense layer of fine pubescence.

Cerastium carniolicum thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
