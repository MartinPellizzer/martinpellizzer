Crassothonna patula

Crassothonna patula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved kalahari plant, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. Its flowering stems bear small, pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Crassothonna patula is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
