Carex laeta var. major

Carex laeta var. major, commonly known as the Silver Hair Grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
