Cistus halimifolius var. rhiphaeus

Cistus halimifolius var. rhiphaeus, commonly known as the dwarf rock rose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage and produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
