Clinopodium forbesii

Clinopodium forbesii, commonly known as Forbes' wood betony, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, often at high elevations. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or purple and bloom in late summer.

Clinopodium forbesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
