Cyperus virens var. acutangulus

Cyperus virens var. acutangulus, commonly known as the sharp-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering stems produce small, three-lobed flowers, which are often clustered in dense spikes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
