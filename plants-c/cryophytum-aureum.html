Cryophytum aureum

Cryophytum aureum, commonly known as the golden cryptic plant, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
