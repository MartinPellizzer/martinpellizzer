Cerastium constantinopolitanum

Cerastium constantinopolitanum, commonly known as Constantinople mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and forms a mat-like growth habit, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is well adapted to dry and sunny conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its common name reflects its historical association with the city of Constantinople, where it was once cultivated.
