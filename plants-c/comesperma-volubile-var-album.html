Comesperma volubile var. album

Comesperma volubile var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, showy white flowers. The plant produces fluffy, seed-filled capsules that are adapted for wind dispersal. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.
