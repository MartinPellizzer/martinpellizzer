Conophytum tantillum subsp. tantillum

Conophytum tantillum subsp.

tantillum, commonly known as the "Cape tulip," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that store water.

The plant produces striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
