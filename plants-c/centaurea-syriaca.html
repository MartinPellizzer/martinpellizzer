Centaurea syriaca

Centaurea syriaca, commonly known as Syrian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts that surround the central disk. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Centaurea syriaca is often used in herbal medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
