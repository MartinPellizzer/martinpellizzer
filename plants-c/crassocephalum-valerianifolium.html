Crassocephalum valerianifolium

Crassocephalum valerianifolium, commonly known as the violet crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy purple or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often aromatic and have a slightly rough texture, while the stems are hairy and sturdy.

This species is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.
