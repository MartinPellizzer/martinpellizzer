Coincya wrightii

Coincya wrightii, commonly known as Wright's water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has a rosette-like growth pattern with narrow, waxy leaves that help it float on the water's surface. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in early spring.

Coincya wrightii plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
