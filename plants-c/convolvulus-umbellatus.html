Convolvulus umbellatus

Convolvulus umbellatus, commonly known as the umbrella morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive umbellate inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. Native to regions of southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
