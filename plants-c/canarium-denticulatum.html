Canarium denticulatum

Canarium denticulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
