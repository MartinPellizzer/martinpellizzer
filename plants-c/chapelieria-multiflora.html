Chapelieria multiflora

Chapelieria multiflora, commonly known as the multibract fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in compact inflorescences. Its leaves are typically divided into many narrow segments, giving it a lacy appearance.

Chapelieria multiflora is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its elegant foliage.
