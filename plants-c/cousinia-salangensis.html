Cousinia salangensis

Cousinia salangensis, commonly known as Salang hillflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in arid climates.

Cousinia salangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
