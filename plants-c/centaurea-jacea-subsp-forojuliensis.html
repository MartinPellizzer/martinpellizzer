Centaurea jacea subsp. forojuliensis

Centaurea jacea subsp.

forojuliensis, commonly known as the Balkan cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open grasslands. It produces slender, branching stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly smaller flowers and more compact growth form compared to the typical subspecies of Centaurea jacea.
