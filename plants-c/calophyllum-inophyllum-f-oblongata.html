Calophyllum inophyllum f. oblongata

Calophyllum inophyllum f. oblongata, commonly known as the broadleaf calophyllum, is a variant of the tropical tree Calophyllum inophyllum.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically a vibrant green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, orange-colored fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
