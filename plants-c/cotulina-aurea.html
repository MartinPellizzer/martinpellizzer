Cotulina aurea

Cotulina aurea, commonly known as the golden cotula, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, yellow-orange flowers arranged in a flat, disc-like structure, surrounded by pale green bracts. Cotulina aurea typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often used in rock gardens due to its compact size and vibrant color.
