Calea angusta

Calea angusta, commonly known as the narrowleaf calea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Calea angusta is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in folk remedies.
