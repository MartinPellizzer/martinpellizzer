Cynanchum bicampanulatum

Cynanchum bicampanulatum, commonly known as the two-campanulate milkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asclepiadaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.

The species is noted for its toxic sap, which contains cardiac glycosides, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans.
