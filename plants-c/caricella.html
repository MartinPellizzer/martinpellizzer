Caricella

Caricella, commonly known as the "peach-faced love-grope" or "lady's tears," is a flowering plant belonging to the family Violaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that resemble tiny bells. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a sweet, fragrant aroma. Caricella thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and delicate addition to their landscape.
