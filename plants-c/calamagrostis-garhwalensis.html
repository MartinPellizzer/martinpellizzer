Calamagrostis garhwalensis

Calamagrostis garhwalensis, commonly known as the Garhwal reed, is a perennial grass species native to the Himalayan region of India.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces inflorescences that are often panicle-like, with small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
