Cadaba trinervia

Cadaba trinervia, commonly known as the coral tree or red coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking, coral-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters of red or orange flowers. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with three prominent veins, giving it its scientific name *trinervia*. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

Cadaba trinervia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
