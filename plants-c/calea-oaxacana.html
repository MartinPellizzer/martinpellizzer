Calea oaxacana

Calea oaxacana, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that open at night. The plant has a bushy growth habit with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its unique nocturnal blooming pattern makes it a popular choice for night-blooming gardens.

Calea oaxacana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
