Carum pimpinelloides var. trichocarpum

Carum pimpinelloides var. trichocarpum, commonly known as the hairy-fruited caraway, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its hairy, elongated fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
