Chenopodium graveolens var. neomexicanum

Chenopodium graveolens var. neomexicanum, commonly known as New Mexico goosefoot, is a species native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its seeds are small, dry, and three-angled, typical of the Chenopodium genus.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
