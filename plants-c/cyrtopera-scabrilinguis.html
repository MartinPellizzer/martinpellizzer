Cyrtopera scabrilinguis

Cyrtopera scabrilinguis, commonly known as the rough-hairy nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation, are a notable botanical feature.
