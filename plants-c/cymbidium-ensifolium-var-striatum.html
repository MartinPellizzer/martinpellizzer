Cymbidium ensifolium var. striatum

Cymbidium ensifolium var. striatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive green and white stripes. The flowers are typically pale yellow with red or orange markings, and they bloom in late winter to early spring. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

It requires well-draining soil and moderate to high light conditions to thrive.
