Campanula rotundifolia subsp. baumgartenii

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

baumgartenii, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features basal leaves that are round or heart-shaped, and its flowers are bell-shaped, typically blue or violet in color.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, oval berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
