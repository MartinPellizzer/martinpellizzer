Carex brazatana

Carex brazatana, commonly known as the Brazilian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a dense, compact growth habit. Its flowering structure consists of small, clustered spikes that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex brazatana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
