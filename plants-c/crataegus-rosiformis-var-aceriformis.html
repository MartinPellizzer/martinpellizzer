Crataegus rosiformis var. aceriformis

Crataegus rosiformis var. aceriformis, commonly known as the redleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, acorn-shaped fruits and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically red or reddish-purple in autumn, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
