Conopodium denudatum subvar. ramosissimum

Conopodium denudatum subvar. ramosissimum, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a aquatic plant characterized by its small, bladder-like structures used for capturing prey.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often submerged. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and are usually borne on separate plants.

The subvariety ramosissimum is distinguished by its highly branched stems, which contribute to its unique appearance and ecological role.
