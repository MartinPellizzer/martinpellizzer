Carex macloviana var. pachystachya

Carex macloviana var. pachystachya, commonly known as the broad-stemmed sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, grass-like foliage and is native to the Andes region of South America. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
