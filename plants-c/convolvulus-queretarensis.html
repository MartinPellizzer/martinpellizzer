Convolvulus queretarensis

Convolvulus queretarensis, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Convolvulus queretarensis is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.
