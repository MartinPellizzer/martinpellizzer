Calliandra mayana

Calliandra mayana, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking plant. The leaves are bipinnately compound, consisting of multiple small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. It thrives in warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
