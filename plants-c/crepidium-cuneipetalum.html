Crepidium cuneipetalum

Crepidium cuneipetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette, and the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white with a distinct lip.

It is often found in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
