Calamus helferianus

Calamus helferianus, commonly known as the Helfer's arrowroot, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are large, arrow-shaped, and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that are typically green when mature.
