Clematis catesbyana var. plukenetii

Clematis catesbyana var. plukenetii, commonly known as the Catesby's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a feather-like pattern. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, often with a distinctive yellow center. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
