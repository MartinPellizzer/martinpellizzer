Calamus poensis

Calamus poensis, commonly known as the Pōpōnui reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in coastal areas. Calamus poensis plays a significant role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat for various insects and birds.

Its unique morphology and ecological importance make it a notable species in Hawaiian botany.
