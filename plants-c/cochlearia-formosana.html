Cochlearia formosana

Cochlearia formosana, commonly known as the Formosan cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal and rosette-like, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Cochlearia formosana is noted for its unique floral structure, which resembles a spiral or cochlea, giving it its scientific name.
