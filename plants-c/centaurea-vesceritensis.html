Centaurea vesceritensis

Centaurea vesceritensis, commonly known as the Vescerita thistle, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.

Centaurea vesceritensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
