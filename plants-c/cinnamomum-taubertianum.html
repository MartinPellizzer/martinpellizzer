Cinnamomum taubertianum

Cinnamomum taubertianum, commonly known as the Taubert's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the樟树科 (Lauraceae) family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that contain elongated, aromatic seeds. It is valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for conservation efforts.
