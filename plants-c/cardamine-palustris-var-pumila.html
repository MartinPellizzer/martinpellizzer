Cardamine palustris var. pumila

Cardamine palustris var. pumila, commonly known as swamp buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as bogs, swamps, and along the edges of streams. The plant has slender, hairy stems that bear alternate, trifoliate leaves with rounded lobes. Its flowers are yellow and consist of five petals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
