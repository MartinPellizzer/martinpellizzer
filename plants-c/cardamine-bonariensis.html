Cardamine bonariensis

Cardamine bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine watercress, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
