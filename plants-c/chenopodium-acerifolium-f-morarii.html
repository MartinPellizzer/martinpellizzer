Chenopodium acerifolium f. morarii

Chenopodium acerifolium f. morarii, commonly known as the Morari's goosefoot, is a variant of the common goosefoot plant.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its alternate, lanceolate leaves with a rough texture. The leaves are typically red-tinged, especially on the underside, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by triangular, beak-like bracts.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
