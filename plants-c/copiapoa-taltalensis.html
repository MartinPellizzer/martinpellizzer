Copiapoa taltalensis

Copiapoa taltalensis, commonly known as the Taltal cactus, is a species of cactus native to northern Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its columnar stem and dense, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
