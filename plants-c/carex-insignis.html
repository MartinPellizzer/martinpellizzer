Carex insignis

Carex insignis, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex insignis is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
