Capparis asperifolia

Capparis asperifolia, commonly known as the rough caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces round, greenish-yellow fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.
