Caucalis coerulescens

Caucalis coerulescens, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Caucalis coerulescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its delicate appearance.
