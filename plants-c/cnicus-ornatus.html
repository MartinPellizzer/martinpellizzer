Cnicus ornatus

Cnicus ornatus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its spread.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes many other flowering plants with similar composite flower structures.
