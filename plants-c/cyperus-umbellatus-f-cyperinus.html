Cyperus umbellatus f. cyperinus

Cyperus umbellatus f. cyperinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its growth habit makes it a common weed in agricultural and aquatic environments.
