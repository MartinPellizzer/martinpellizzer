Cabucala penduliflora

Cabucala penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging flower or pendulous orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in warm climates.
