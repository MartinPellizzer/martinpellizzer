Cladrastis kentukea f. tomentosa

Cladrastis kentukea f. tomentosa, commonly known as the Kentucky yellowwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a doubly serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable ornamental plant for woodland gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
