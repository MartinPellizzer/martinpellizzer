Cotyledon tomentosa subsp. tomentosa

Cotyledon tomentosa subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs.

The leaves are typically gray-green in color and can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. This species is known for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant.

It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding ornamental value to gardens.
