Chascanum mixtum

Chascanum mixtum, commonly known as the Andean pepper or wild chili, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and eggplants. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into red or orange berries, which are used in traditional Andean cuisine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and woody.

Chascanum mixtum is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its role in traditional medicine.
