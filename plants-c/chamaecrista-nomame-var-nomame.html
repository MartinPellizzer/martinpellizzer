Chamaecrista nomame var. nomame

Chamaecrista nomame var. nomame, commonly known as the velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its high protein content in the seeds.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds, requiring careful processing before consumption.
