Cucumis melo var. chandalak

Cucumis melo var. chandalak, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruit with a pale green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with hairy stems and lobed leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its high water content and distinct flavor, making it a popular choice in both fresh consumption and culinary uses.
