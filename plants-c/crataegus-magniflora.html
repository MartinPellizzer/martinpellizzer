Crataegus magniflora

Crataegus magniflora, commonly known as the showy crabsbark, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its ornamental, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun.

Crataegus magniflora is valued in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
