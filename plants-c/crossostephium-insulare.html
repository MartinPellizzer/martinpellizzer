Crossostephium insulare

Crossostephium insulare, commonly known as the island crossostephium, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Crossostephium insulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
