Chamaecrista desertorum

Chamaecrista desertorum, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branching stems covered in small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers during the spring and summer months. Its seed pods are slender, elongated, and often curl at the tips, containing several small, hard seeds.

Chamaecrista desertorum is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh desert environments.
