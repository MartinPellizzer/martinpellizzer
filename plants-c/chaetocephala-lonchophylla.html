Chaetocephala lonchophylla

Chaetocephala lonchophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline environments. It is often found in dune ecosystems, contributing to coastal stabilization.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
