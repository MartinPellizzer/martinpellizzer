Cyperus flexuosus f. fulvescens

Cyperus flexuosus f. fulvescens, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a variant of the flat sedge species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. Its foliage often exhibits a distinctive golden or reddish hue, particularly in the lower leaves, which contributes to its ornamental value.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ability to thrive in moist conditions and its aesthetic appeal in aquatic gardens.
