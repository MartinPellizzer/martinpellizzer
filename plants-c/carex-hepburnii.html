Carex hepburnii

Carex hepburnii, commonly known as Hepburn's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Carex hepburnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its upright, textured foliage.
