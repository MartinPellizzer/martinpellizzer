Cereus pentlandii var. luteoviolaceus

Cereus pentlandii var. luteoviolaceus, commonly known as the purple-lavender night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are known for their long-lasting bloom and striking appearance.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
