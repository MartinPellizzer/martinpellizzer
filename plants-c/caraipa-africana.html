Caraipa africana

Caraipa africana, commonly known as the African starfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a broad, spreading canopy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into the characteristic star-shaped fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a waxy coating.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns orange, and it is valued for its edible pulp and medicinal properties.
