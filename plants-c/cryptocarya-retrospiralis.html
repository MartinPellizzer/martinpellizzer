Cryptocarya retrospiralis

Cryptocarya retrospiralis, commonly known as the spiral-leaved cryptocarya, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves along the stems. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

It produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
