Capsicum cereolum

Capsicum cereolum, commonly known as the Christmas bell pepper, is a species of chili pepper native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped fruits that are typically red when mature, though they can also be yellow or orange. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by the distinctive, elongated peppers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications, particularly in festive dishes due to its vibrant color and mild flavor.
