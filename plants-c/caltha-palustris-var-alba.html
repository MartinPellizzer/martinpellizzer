Caltha palustris var. alba

Caltha palustris var. alba, commonly known as white marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that are typically surrounded by white bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually submerged or floating in water. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.

The flowers bloom in early spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
