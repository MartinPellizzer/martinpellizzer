Calendula suffruticosa subsp. trialata

Calendula suffruticosa subsp.

trialata, commonly known as the creeping calendula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial shrub that typically grows low to the ground, featuring woody stems and fleshy, lobed leaves.

The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
