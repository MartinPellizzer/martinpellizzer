Cyperus paucispiculatus

Cyperus paucispiculatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands.

Cyperus paucispiculatus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
