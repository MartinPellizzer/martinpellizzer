Clematis aristata subsp. gentianoides

Clematis aristata subsp.

gentianoides, commonly known as the gentian-like clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
