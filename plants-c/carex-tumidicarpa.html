Carex tumidicarpa

Carex tumidicarpa, commonly known as the inflated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces distinctive, inflated spikelets that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually bluish-green and have a rough texture, while the inflorescence consists of two spikelets, one terminal and one lateral.

Carex tumidicarpa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
