Cyathea schizochlamys

Cyathea schizochlamys, commonly known as the Schizochlamys tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that supports its growth in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive appearance that makes it a popular ornamental plant.

Cyathea schizochlamys plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
