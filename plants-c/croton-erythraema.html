Croton erythraema

Croton erythraema, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the family.

Croton erythraema is valued in horticulture for its vibrant leaves and adaptability to various growing conditions.
