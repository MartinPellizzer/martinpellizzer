Chrysophyllum leptocladum

Chrysophyllum leptocladum, commonly known as the slender-leaved star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, sweet, star-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and the nutritional value of its fruit.
