Cerasus corymbosa

Cerasus corymbosa, commonly known as the Chinese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits known as cherries, which are typically red when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic flowers.

The species is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
