Camaridium pendulum

Camaridium pendulum, commonly known as the weeping brookweed, is a delicate aquatic plant characterized by its long, hanging stems that bear small, bright green leaves.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. The plant's leaves are often ribbon-like and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow, which develop into small, round fruits.

Camaridium pendulum is valued for its aesthetic qualities and its role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
