Cyperus nigriceps

Cyperus nigriceps, commonly known as black-headed flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Cyperus nigriceps typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
