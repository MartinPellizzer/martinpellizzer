Carex echinata var. cephalantha

Carex echinata var. cephalantha, commonly known as the spiked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, usually greenish or brownish flowers that are clustered at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting various wildlife species.
