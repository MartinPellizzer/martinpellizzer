Cirsium oblongifolium

Cirsium oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are broad, oblong, and covered with sharp, hooked prickles.

The flowers bloom in late summer and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
