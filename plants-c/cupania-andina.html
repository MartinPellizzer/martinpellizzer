Cupania andina

Cupania andina, commonly known as the Andean cupuaçu, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant produces a fruit similar to a large, elongated cupuaçu, which is rich in nutrients and used in various food products. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique fruit structure.

Cupania andina plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is valued for its economic and nutritional contributions.
