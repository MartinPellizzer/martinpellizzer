Centaurea × pomoensis

Centaurea × pomoensis, commonly known as the apple blossom centaurea, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy blue to purple flower heads that resemble apple blossoms, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture, and it produces seeds in a fluffy, wind-dispersed form.

Centaurea × pomoensis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
