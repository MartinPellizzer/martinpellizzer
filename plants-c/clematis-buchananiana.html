Clematis buchananiana

Clematis buchananiana, commonly known as the Buchanan's clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Due to its beauty and adaptability, it is often cultivated in USDA hardiness zones 4 through 8.
