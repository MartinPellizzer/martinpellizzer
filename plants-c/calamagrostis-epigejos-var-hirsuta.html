Calamagrostis epigejos var. hirsuta

Calamagrostis epigejos var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy calamagrostis, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. It produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
