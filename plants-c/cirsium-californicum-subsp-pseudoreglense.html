Cirsium californicum subsp. pseudoreglense

Cirsium californicum subsp.

pseudoreglense, commonly known as the Santa Barbara thistle, is a flowering plant native to California. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple to pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a noxious weed in some regions.
