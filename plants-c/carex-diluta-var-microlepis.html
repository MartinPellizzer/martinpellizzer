Carex diluta var. microlepis

Carex diluta var. microlepis, commonly known as the small-leaved sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to parts of Asia, this plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are generally shorter and more densely packed compared to other Carex species.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
