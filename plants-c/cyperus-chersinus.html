Cyperus chersinus

Cyperus chersinus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, although it is often classified under the Cyperaceae family due to its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and along water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus chersinus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
