Cassia itabiritoana

Cassia itabiritoana, commonly known as Itabiritoan cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in folk medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and savannas.
