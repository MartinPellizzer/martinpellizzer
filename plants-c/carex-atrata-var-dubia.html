Carex atrata var. dubia

Carex atrata var. dubia, commonly known as the black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, dark brown to blackish spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems.

Carex atrata var. dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
