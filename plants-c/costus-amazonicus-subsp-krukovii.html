Costus amazonicus subsp. krukovii

Costus amazonicus subsp.

krukovii, commonly known as the Amazonian costus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America. It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of red or orange. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
