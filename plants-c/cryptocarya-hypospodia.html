Cryptocarya hypospodia

Cryptocarya hypospodia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple berries that are edible for some bird species.

Cryptocarya hypospodia is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
