Ceanothus oreganus

Ceanothus oreganus, commonly known as the Oregon ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.

Ceanothus oreganus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscaping.
