Clematis viticella f. kermesiana

Clematis viticella f. kermesiana, commonly known as the Kermesian Clematis, is a compact, vigorous vine with distinctive botanical features.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and lilac, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves with deep lobes, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-brown hairs. It is a deciduous species, shedding its leaves in autumn, and thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to climb using its twining stems.
