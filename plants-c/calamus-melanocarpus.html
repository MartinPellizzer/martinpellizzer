Calamus melanocarpus

Calamus melanocarpus, commonly known as black fruit calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

The plant is notable for its dark purple or black berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.
