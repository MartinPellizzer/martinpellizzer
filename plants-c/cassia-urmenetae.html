Cassia urmenetae

Cassia urmenetae, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, clustered flowers that bloom in summer, followed by long, narrow pods. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its bark is often smooth with a reddish or brownish hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Cassia urmenetae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
