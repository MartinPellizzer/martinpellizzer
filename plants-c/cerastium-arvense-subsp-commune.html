Cerastium arvense subsp. commune

Cerastium arvense subsp.

commune, commonly known as common mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has hairy, spreading stems and opposite leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and horticultural settings.
