Capparis cynophallophora subsp. linearifolia

Capparis cynophallophora subsp.

linearifolia, commonly known as the slender caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features woody stems and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant has pinnate leaves with elongated, linear leaflets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in local habitats.
