Crepis hellenica

Crepis hellenica, commonly known as the Greek mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Crepis hellenica is often found in open habitats, including grasslands and coastal areas.
