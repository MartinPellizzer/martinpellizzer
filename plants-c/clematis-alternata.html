Clematis alternata

Clematis alternata, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a serrated edge. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis alternata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
