Crepis hirta

Crepis hirta, commonly known as hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a composite head. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Crepis hirta is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.
