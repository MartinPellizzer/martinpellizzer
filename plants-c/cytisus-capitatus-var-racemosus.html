Cytisus capitatus var. racemosus

Cytisus capitatus var. racemosus, commonly known as the racemose broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that are typically arranged in racemes, giving it its common name. The plant has slender, woody stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sunny soils and is adapted to coastal and rocky environments.

This variety is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and ecological importance in certain habitats.
