Campanula latiloba subsp. rizeensis

Campanula latiloba subsp.

rizeensis, commonly known as the Rize bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous areas. It has hairy, ovate leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
