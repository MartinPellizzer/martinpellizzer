Cerasus semperflorens

Cerasus semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming cherry or compact cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage is dark green and glossy, providing an attractive ornamental appearance.

Cerasus semperflorens is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
