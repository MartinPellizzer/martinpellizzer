Cirsium iraqense

Cirsium iraqense, commonly known as the Iraqi thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Iraq and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are broad, lobed, and covered in stiff, prickly hairs, while its flowers are purple or pink, arranged in a compact head.

Cirsium iraqense is often found in grasslands and along rocky outcrops, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
