Columnea peruviana

Columnea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian flame or red flame plant, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Columnea peruviana is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
