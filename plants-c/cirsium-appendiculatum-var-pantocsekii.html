Cirsium appendiculatum var. pantocsekii

Cirsium appendiculatum var. pantocsekii, commonly known as the Hungarian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is a perennial species belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in grasslands and open woodlands. It has a robust root system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
