Cayratia mollis

Cayratia mollis, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Cayratia mollis is also known for its ability to attract various insects and birds due to its nectar-rich flowers.
