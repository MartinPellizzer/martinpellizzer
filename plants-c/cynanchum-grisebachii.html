Cynanchum grisebachii

Cynanchum grisebachii, commonly known as the gray milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its latex contains toxic compounds, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Cynanchum grisebachii is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
