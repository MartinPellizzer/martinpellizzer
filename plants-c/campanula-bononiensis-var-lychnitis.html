Campanula bononiensis var. lychnitis

Campanula bononiensis var. lychnitis, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue or violet in color. The leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

This variety is valued for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
