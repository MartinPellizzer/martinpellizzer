Cleome consimilis

Cleome consimilis, commonly known as the false pimpernel, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, with lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and usually have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. Cleome consimilis is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
