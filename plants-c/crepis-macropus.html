Crepis macropus

Crepis macropus, commonly known as the large-headed mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster at the top of a slender stem. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Crepis macropus is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
