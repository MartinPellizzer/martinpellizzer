Conostomium hispidulum

Conostomium hispidulum, commonly known as the roughleaf conostomium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that blooms during the spring and summer seasons.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
