Cotoneaster mazandaranicus

Cotoneaster mazandaranicus, commonly known as the Mazandaran cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of Iran.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it flowers in the spring with clusters of white or pink flowers.

Cotoneaster mazandaranicus is well adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
