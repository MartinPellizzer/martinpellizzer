Crepidium punctatum

Crepidium punctatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that are often adorned with distinct purplish or brownish spots. Native to various regions in the Americas, it typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant's roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in its natural habitat.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental features make it a subject of interest in both botanical studies and horticultural practices.
