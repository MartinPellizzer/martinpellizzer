Cacalia radulifolia

Cacalia radulifolia, commonly known as the leafy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cacalia radulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
