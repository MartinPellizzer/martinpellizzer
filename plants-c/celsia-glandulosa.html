Celsia glandulosa

Celsia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular celsia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in glandular structures that secrete oils. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Celsia glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
