Centaurea argyrolepis

Centaurea argyrolepis, commonly known as silverleaf centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its height can range from 30 to 60 centimeters, and it produces small, spiky seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal.

Centaurea argyrolepis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
