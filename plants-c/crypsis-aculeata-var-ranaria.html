Crypsis aculeata var. ranaria

Crypsis aculeata var. ranaria, commonly known as the running thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of spiny, basal leaves that are typically grey-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, terminal clusters during the summer months. Its stems are upright and branched, often becoming woody at the base.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.
