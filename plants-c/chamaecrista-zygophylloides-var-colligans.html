Chamaecrista zygophylloides var. colligans

Chamaecrista zygophylloides var. colligans, commonly known as the yellow sensitiveplant, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in sandy or disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with the ability to reseed itself, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

The species is also noted for its sensitivity to touch, as the leaves curl when touched, a trait that gives it its common name.
