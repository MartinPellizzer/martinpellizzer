Centaurea granatensis var. acaulis

Centaurea granatensis var. acaulis, commonly known as the Granada thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are generally lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The variety acaulis is distinguished by its lack of basal leaves, giving it a more upright appearance compared to other varieties.
