Centaurea cariensis

Centaurea cariensis, commonly known as the Turkish knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flower heads, which are typically composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often lobed or serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Centaurea cariensis is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
