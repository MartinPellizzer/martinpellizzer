Chenopodium strictum var. angustifolium

Chenopodium strictum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and has a sprawling or ascending growth habit. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and are wind-pollinated.

The seeds are small, dry, and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
