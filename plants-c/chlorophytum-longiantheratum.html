Chlorophytum longiantheratum

Chlorophytum longiantheratum, commonly known as the snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or yellow variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers on long, slender stems, which are followed by dark blue or purple berries. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its air-purifying properties, it is popular as both an ornamental and indoor plant.
