Cressa vallicola

Cressa vallicola, commonly known as mountain cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early summer.

The leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.
