Chenopodium incognitum

Chenopodium incognitum, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are encased in a hard, triangular capsule. Chenopodium incognitum is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and has potential as a forage plant for livestock.
