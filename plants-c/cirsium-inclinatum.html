Cirsium inclinatum

Cirsium inclinatum, commonly known as the leaning thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in stiff, bristly hairs, while its flowers are purple to pink in color.

Cirsium inclinatum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
