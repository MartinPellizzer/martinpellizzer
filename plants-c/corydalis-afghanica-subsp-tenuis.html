Corydalis afghanica subsp. tenuis

Corydalis afghanica subsp.

tenuis, commonly known as the slender Afghan corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high elevations with cool, moist conditions.

The plant features slender stems and lanceolate leaves, with clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in spring. Its flowers have two lower petals that are often spurred, a characteristic feature of the Corydalis genus.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
