Calea megacephala var. pachutlana

Calea megacephala var. pachutlana, commonly known as the sacred candle plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, candle-like inflorescence composed of densely packed, white to pale yellow florets. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or limestone-rich environments.

This species holds cultural significance in traditional Mexican practices and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
