Croton gracilis var. genuinus

Croton gracilis var. genuinus, commonly known as the slender croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
