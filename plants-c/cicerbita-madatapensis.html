Cicerbita madatapensis

Cicerbita madatapensis, commonly known as the Madatapensis Cicerbita, is a lesser-known species within the Cichorieae tribe.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lobed, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a loose inflorescence, typically blooming in the summer months. It is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists studying biodiversity in the region.
