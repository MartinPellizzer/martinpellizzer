Callicarpa formosana var. longifolia

Callicarpa formosana var. longifolia, commonly known as the longflower beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for humans and wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native gardens.
