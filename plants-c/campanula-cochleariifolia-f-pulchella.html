Campanula cochleariifolia f. pulchella

Campanula cochleariifolia f. pulchella, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its stems are upright with a tendency to branch.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in a variety of temperate climates.
