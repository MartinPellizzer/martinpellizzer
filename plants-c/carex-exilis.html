Carex exilis

Carex exilis, commonly known as slender sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex exilis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
