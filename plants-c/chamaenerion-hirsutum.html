Chamaenerion hirsutum

Chamaenerion hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy fireweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as along roadsides or in wet meadows. The plant features tall, slender stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, which give it its common name. Its showy pink to red flowers appear in clusters at the tips of the stems during the summer months.

Chamaenerion hirsutum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
