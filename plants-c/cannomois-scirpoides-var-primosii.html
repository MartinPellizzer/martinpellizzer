Cannomois scirpoides var. primosii

Cannomois scirpoides var. primosii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial wetland plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams. The plant has tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish in color. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and providing habitat for aquatic life.
