Cordia polyantha

Cordia polyantha, commonly known as the red-flowered cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often blooming in summer. Its fruit is a small, round, fleshy capsule that turns red when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Cordia polyantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
