Carex quadrifida

Carex quadrifida, commonly known as the four-awn sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex quadrifida is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
