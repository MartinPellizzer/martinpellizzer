Cytisus arcuatus

Cytisus arcuatus, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
