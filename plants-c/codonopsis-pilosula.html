Codonopsis pilosula

Codonopsis pilosula, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng or the "poor man's ginseng," is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine, valued for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it distinct within its family, with a unique combination of morphology and medicinal significance.
