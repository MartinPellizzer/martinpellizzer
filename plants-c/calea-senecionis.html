Calea senecionis

Calea senecionis, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety blue or purple flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its unique floral behavior. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive, somewhat bitter taste.

Calea senecionis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
