Cordia peruviana var. mexicana

Cordia peruviana var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican white cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale to whitish bark and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, spherical capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and adaptability.
