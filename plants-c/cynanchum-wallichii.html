Cynanchum wallichii

Cynanchum wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
