Cereus rhodoleucanthus

Cereus rhodoleucanthus, commonly known as the pink and white cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of striking pink and white flowers that bloom at night. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of succulents.
