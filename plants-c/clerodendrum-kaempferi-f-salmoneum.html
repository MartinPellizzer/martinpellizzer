Clerodendrum kaempferi f. salmoneum

Clerodendrum kaempferi f. salmoneum, commonly known as the salmon-colored clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking salmon-pink bracts that surround small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1-3 meters and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are ovate and have a glossy texture, while the stems are often woody and branched.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
