Campanula dieckii

Campanula dieckii, commonly known as the Siebold's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

Campanula dieckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
