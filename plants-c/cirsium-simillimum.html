Cirsium simillimum

Cirsium simillimum, commonly known as the similar thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium simillimum is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
