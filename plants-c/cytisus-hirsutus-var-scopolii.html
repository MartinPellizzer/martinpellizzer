Cytisus hirsutus var. scopolii

Cytisus hirsutus var. scopolii, commonly known as the Scopolii broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine, soft hairs. This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
