Crassula pubescens subsp. rattrayi

Crassula pubescens subsp.

rattrayi, commonly known as the Rattray's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
