Colladonia tinifolia

Colladonia tinifolia, commonly known as the slender flax or narrow-leaved flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Colladonia tinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
