Centaurea pterocaula subsp. iranica

Centaurea pterocaula subsp.

iranica, commonly known as the Iranian needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny, thistle-like appearance.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its inflorescence consists of purple to pink flowers arranged in a composite head, which is typical of the Asteraceae family.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphology and habitat preferences, making it a notable species in regional flora.
