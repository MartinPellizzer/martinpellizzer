Codonopsis kawakamii

Codonopsis kawakamii, commonly known as the Kawakami's ginseng, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China and Taiwan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
