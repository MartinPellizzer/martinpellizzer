Campomanesia pubescens

Campomanesia pubescens, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and red, edible berries. The plant has hairy, elliptical leaves and produces fruit that is rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of dense forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
