Coleus thyrsoideus

Coleus thyrsoideus, commonly known as the scarlet globe coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble small, colorful flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, clustered in dense inflorescences. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring combinations of green, white, and red, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal rather than its edible properties.
