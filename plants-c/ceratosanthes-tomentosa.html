Ceratosanthes tomentosa

Ceratosanthes tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its grayish-white, hairy foliage and clusters of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a distinctive velvety texture.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its resistance to pests and diseases.
