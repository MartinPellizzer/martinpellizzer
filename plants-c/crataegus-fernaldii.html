Crataegus fernaldii

Crataegus fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and Florida. The plant is characterized by its small, thorned branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in the spring. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
