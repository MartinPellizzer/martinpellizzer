Clitoria pinnata

Clitoria pinnata, commonly known as the winged bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate structures, giving it its scientific name. It produces edible pods containing seeds and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Clitoria pinnata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural systems.
