Cucurbita × scabridifolia

Cucurbita × scabridifolia, commonly known as the scabrous leaf gourd, is a hybrid species within the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough, and textured leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces elongated, pale green fruits that are typically harvested when immature for culinary use. It is native to the southern United States and is often grown as an ornamental or edible plant.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of both Cucurbita maxima and Cucurbita argyrosperma, contributing to its unique morphology and adaptability.
