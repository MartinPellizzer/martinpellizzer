Ceropegia europaea var. judaica

Ceropegia europaea var. judaica, commonly known as the Jewish ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a trailing perennial that produces tubular, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive hooded shape. The plant has fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of care.
