Cerastium banaticum f. glanduliusculum

Cerastium banaticum f. glanduliusculum, commonly known as the glandular mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves and stems are covered with fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
