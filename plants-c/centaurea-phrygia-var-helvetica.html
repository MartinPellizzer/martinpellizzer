Centaurea phrygia var. helvetica

Centaurea phrygia var. helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss cornflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple radial flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
