Coussarea verticillata var. glabra

Coussarea verticillata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth coussarea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and often have a smooth texture, hence the variety name *glabra*.

The flowers are borne in terminal panicles and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
