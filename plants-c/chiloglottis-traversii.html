Chiloglottis traversii

Chiloglottis traversii, commonly known as the yellow spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces a single, erect stem with several leaves and blooms during the autumn season. It is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving certain insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
