Carex ramosa

Carex ramosa, commonly known as the tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in three ranks, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers during the summer months.

Carex ramosa is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
