Carex vesicaria var. compacta

Carex vesicaria var. compacta, commonly known as the compact spikerush, is a sedge species native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often tinged with reddish hues, distinguishing it from other Carex species. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife, it is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
