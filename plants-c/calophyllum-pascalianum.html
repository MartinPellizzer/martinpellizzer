Calophyllum pascalianum

Calophyllum pascalianum, commonly known as the Pascalian tallowwood, is a tropical tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, glossy leaves. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
