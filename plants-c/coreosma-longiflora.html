Coreosma longiflora

Coreosma longiflora, commonly known as the sweet pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trailing stems with heart-shaped, glossy green leaves and produces clusters of showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant is known for its fragrant blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Coreosma longiflora is often used in garden landscapes for its ornamental value and ability to cascade from containers or trellises.
