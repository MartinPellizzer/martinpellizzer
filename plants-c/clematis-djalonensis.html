Clematis djalonensis

Clematis djalonensis, commonly known as the Djalonensis Clematis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, usually white or pink flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often armed with hooked prickles. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific care to flourish.
