Croton cortesianus

Croton cortesianus, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display vibrant shades of red, yellow, and orange. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. Its leaves are typically broad and alternate, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Croton cortesianus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
