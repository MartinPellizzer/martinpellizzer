Campsis grandiflora

Campsis grandiflora, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting birds and insects. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Campsis grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
