Calanticaria greggii

Calanticaria greggii, commonly known as Gregg's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a disk of tubular flowers, typical of the family. It is typically found in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
