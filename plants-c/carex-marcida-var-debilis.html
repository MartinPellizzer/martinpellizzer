Carex marcida var. debilis

Carex marcida var. debilis, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense clumps. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually borne on slender, branching stems.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
