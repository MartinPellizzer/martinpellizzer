Calamagrostis tenuifolia

Calamagrostis tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf giant reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching leaves and tall, slender flowering stems that produce panicles of small, spike-like flowers. The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil, making it valuable for erosion control.

The plant's ornamental value comes from its graceful appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland conditions.
