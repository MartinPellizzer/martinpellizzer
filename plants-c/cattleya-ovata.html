Cattleya ovata

Cattleya ovata, commonly known as the Ovate Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with multiple blooms, making it a popular choice for horticultural displays. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

Cattleya ovata is known for its fragrant flowers and is cultivated for both its ornamental value and its role in orchid hybridization.
