Chaetogastra arthrostemmoides

Chaetogastra arthrostemmoides, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in cloud forests. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, and it has a woody stem that becomes more branched with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
