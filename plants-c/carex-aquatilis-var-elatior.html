Carex aquatilis var. elatior

Carex aquatilis var. elatior, commonly known as the greater marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
