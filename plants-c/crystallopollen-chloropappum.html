Crystallopollen chloropappum

Crystallopollen chloropappum, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the United States.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy, golden-yellow flower heads in the summer. The plant's inflorescence consists of a cluster of small, disk-like flowers surrounded by pale yellow bracts. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
