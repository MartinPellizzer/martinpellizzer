Coccothrinax dussiana

Coccothrinax dussiana, commonly known as the Bahama palm, is a species of palm native to the Bahamas and the Turks and Caicos Islands.

It is a small, slender palm that typically grows to a height of about 5 to 10 meters. The plant has a smooth, grayish trunk with a distinctive ring of leaf scars, and its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, slender leaflets. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, green fruits, which are edible when ripe.

This plant is well adapted to coastal environments and is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline.
