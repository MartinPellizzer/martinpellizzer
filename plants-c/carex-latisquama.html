Carex latisquama

Carex latisquama, commonly known as the broad-squama sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and pond edges. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on tall, slender stems, and the seeds are enclosed in small, three-angled capsules.

Carex latisquama plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
