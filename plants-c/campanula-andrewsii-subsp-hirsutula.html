Campanula andrewsii subsp. hirsutula

Campanula andrewsii subsp.

hirsutula, commonly known as the hirsute bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecific name "hirsutula.". It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats. Campanula andrewsii subsp.

hirsutula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
