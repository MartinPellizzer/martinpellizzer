Cyperus rigens var. selmirae

Cyperus rigens var. selmirae, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus rigens var. selmirae plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
