Cassia gracilis var. erythrocalyx

Cassia gracilis var. erythrocalyx, commonly known as the red calyx cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, with the calyces of the flowers being a striking red color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
