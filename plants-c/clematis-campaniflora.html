Clematis campaniflora

Clematis campaniflora, commonly known as the bellflower clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens.

Clematis campaniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
