Cyathodes straminea

Cyathodes straminea, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically narrow and linear. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Cyathodes straminea is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
