Cleome intermedia

Cleome intermedia, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Cleome intermedia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
