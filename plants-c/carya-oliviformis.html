Carya oliviformis

Carya oliviformis, commonly known as the boxwood hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins and is known for its hard, heavy wood that is resistant to decay.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
