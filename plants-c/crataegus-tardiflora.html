Crataegus tardiflora

Crataegus tardiflora, commonly known as the late-flowering hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom late in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
