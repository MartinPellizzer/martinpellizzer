Croton nitidulus var. spatulatus

Croton nitidulus var. spatulatus, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its striking, colorful foliage.

The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a smooth, waxy surface that reflects light, giving the plant a vibrant appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually yellow or green, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive leaves and ability to tolerate partial shade.
