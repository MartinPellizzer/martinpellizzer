Carex tentaculata

Carex tentaculata, commonly known as the tentacle sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex tentaculata plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
