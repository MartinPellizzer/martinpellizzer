Clusia carinata

Clusia carinata, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.

Clusia carinata is also known for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
