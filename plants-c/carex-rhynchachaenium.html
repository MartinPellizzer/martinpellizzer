Carex rhynchachaenium

Carex rhynchachaenium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers usually occurring on separate spikes.
