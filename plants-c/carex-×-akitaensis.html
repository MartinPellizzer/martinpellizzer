Carex × akitaensis

Carex × akitaensis, commonly known as the Akita sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is native to Japan, particularly the Akita Prefecture. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex × akitaensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
