Colocasia gigantea

Colocasia gigantea, commonly known as the giant elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other familiar plants like taro and calla lilies. Its leaves are usually a deep green with a glossy texture, and it produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences.

Colocasia gigantea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its dramatic foliage and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid climates.
