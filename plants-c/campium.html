Campium

Campium, commonly known as the common campion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in racemes. The plant has erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils.

Campium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
