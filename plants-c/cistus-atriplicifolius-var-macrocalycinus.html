Cistus atriplicifolius var. macrocalycinus

Cistus atriplicifolius var. macrocalycinus, commonly known as the large-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen foliage and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically large, elliptical, and have a rough, leathery texture. This species thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
