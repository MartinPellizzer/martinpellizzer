Carex leporina f. monostachya

Carex leporina f. monostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a single, erect spike, which is a distinguishing feature of this form. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Its foliage provides habitat for various invertebrates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
