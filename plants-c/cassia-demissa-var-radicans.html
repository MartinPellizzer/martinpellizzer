Cassia demissa var. radicans

Cassia demissa var. radicans, commonly known as the spreading cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes, making it a striking ornamental plant. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cassia demissa var. radicans is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a source of natural dye.
