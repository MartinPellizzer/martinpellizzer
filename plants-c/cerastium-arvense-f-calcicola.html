Cerastium arvense f. calcicola

Cerastium arvense f. calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to calcareous soils.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as disturbed soils and rocky areas.

Its botanical traits make it well adapted to alkaline environments.
