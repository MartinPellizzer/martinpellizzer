Carex rostrata f. pendulina

Carex rostrata f. pendulina, commonly known as the drooping sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is characterized by its gracefully drooping inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows.

This ornamental plant is valued for its elegant form and is often used in water garden settings.
