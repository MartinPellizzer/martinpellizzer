Coleochloa microcephala

Coleochloa microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features small, trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
