Chamaecrista ulmea

Chamaecrista ulmea, commonly known as yellow cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are edible and can be used in cooking, while the plant has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Chamaecrista ulmea is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
