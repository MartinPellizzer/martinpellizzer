Carroa

Carroa, commonly known as *Carthamus tinctorius*, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems, yellow composite flowers, and distinctive seed heads that resemble small spheres. The plant produces a bright orange or red dye from its seeds, which has been historically used in textile industries. Carroa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and economically valuable.
