Celastrus multiflorus

Celastrus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered staff-vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cymes, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
