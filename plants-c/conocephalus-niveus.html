Conocephalus niveus

Conocephalus niveus, commonly known as the white marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Conocephalus niveus plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
