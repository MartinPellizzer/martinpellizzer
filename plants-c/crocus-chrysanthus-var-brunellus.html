Crocus chrysanthus var. brunellus

Crocus chrysanthus var. brunellus, commonly known as the Brunell's crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces small, showy flowers that are typically shades of yellow to orange, often with a distinctive darker center. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its early spring blooming habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
