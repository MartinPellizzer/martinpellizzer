Carex disticha f. pangyna

Carex disticha f. pangyna, commonly known as the silver hair sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex disticha.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, pond edges, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a silvery or grayish sheen, contributing to its ornamental value.

The inflorescence consists of multiple small, three-lobed spikelets that are typically green to brown in color.
