Clerodendrum botryoides

Clerodendrum botryoides, commonly known as the clustered flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble clusters of small, flame-like flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, branched panicles. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
