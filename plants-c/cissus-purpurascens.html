Cissus purpurascens

Cissus purpurascens, commonly known as the purple leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by green, three-lobed fruits.

Cissus purpurascens is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
