Carica boliviana

Carica boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian papaya, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is closely related to the common papaya. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow when ripe, though some varieties may have different colors. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it has a short, stout stem.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, edible pulp and is often used in local cuisines.
