Curio archeri

Curio archeri, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, with leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Curio archeri is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
