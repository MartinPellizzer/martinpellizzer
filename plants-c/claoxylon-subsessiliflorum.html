Claoxylon subsessiliflorum

Claoxylon subsessiliflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tamanu tree, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, greyish-brown bark. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic to ovate in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a pale underside. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in traditional practices.
