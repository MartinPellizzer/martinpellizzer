Cnicus chanroenicus

Cnicus chanroenicus, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are usually lobed or toothed, with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, rocky habitats.
