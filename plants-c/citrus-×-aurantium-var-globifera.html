Citrus × aurantium var. globifera

Citrus × aurantium var. globifera, commonly known as the pomelo or golden apple.

It is a citrus fruit characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruit with a sweet and slightly acidic flavor. The plant is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It produces fragrant white flowers and has a dense, evergreen canopy.

The fruit is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications due to its high vitamin C content and aromatic properties.
