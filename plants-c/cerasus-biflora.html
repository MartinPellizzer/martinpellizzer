Cerasus biflora

Cerasus biflora, commonly known as the double-flora cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive flowers, which often have two clusters of petals, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Cerasus biflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
