Calanthe crispifolia

Calanthe crispifolia, commonly known as the crisped calanthe, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, Japan, and the Philippines. This species is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
