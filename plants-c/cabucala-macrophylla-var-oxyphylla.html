Cabucala macrophylla var. oxyphylla

Cabucala macrophylla var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a floating canopy on water surfaces. The plant features showy white flowers that open at night, attracting specific pollinators such as moths. Its roots anchor it in shallow water, while its leaves support a diverse ecosystem of aquatic life.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in wetland habitats.
