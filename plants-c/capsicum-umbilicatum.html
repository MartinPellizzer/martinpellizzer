Capsicum umbilicatum

Capsicum umbilicatum, commonly known as the Mexican wild pepper or mountain chili, is a species native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a branched stem.

The flowers are usually yellow or green, with five petals and a superior ovary.
