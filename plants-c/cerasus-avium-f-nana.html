Cerasus avium f. nana

Cerasus avium f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or miniature cherry, is a compact, ornamental form of the wild cherry tree.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for bonsai and small-space landscaping.
