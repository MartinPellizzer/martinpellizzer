Cerasus affinis

Cerasus affinis, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet-tart berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.

Cerasus affinis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
