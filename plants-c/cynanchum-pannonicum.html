Cynanchum pannonicum

Cynanchum pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian dogbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry grasslands and open woodlands, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually greenish-yellow with a distinct scent.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy and often branched.
