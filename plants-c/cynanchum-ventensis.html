Cynanchum ventensis

Cynanchum ventensis, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is often toxic, containing various alkaloids. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Cynanchum ventensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
