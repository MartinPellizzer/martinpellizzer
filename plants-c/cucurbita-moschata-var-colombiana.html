Cucurbita moschata var. colombiana

Cucurbita moschata var. colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian pumpkin, is a cultivar native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces large, elongated fruits with a pale to dark orange rind and a sweet, nutty-flavored flesh. It is valued for its culinary uses and is often cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.

The variety is also notable for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
