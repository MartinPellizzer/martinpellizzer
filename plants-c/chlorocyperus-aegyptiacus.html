Chlorocyperus aegyptiacus

Chlorocyperus aegyptiacus, commonly known as the Egyptian flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom during the wet season.

Chlorocyperus aegyptiacus is adapted to survive in environments with fluctuating water levels and can tolerate periods of drought.
