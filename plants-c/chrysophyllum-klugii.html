Chrysophyllum klugii

Chrysophyllum klugii, commonly known as the African cherry or klug's starfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to yellow-orange fruits resembling starfruits. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet, acidic flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional remedies.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in diverse tropical environments.
