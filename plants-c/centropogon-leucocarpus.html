Centropogon leucocarpus

Centropogon leucocarpus, commonly known as the white-fruited panic grass, is a perennial grass native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense inflorescences. Its fruiting structures, known as caryopses, are typically white or pale in color, contributing to its common name.

Centropogon leucocarpus is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions.
