Catasetum milleri

Catasetum milleri, commonly known as the orchid with the unique floral structure, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that feature a specialized column for pollination. Its inflorescence typically consists of multiple showy blooms arranged in a raceme.
