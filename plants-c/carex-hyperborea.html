Carex hyperborea

Carex hyperborea, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

Carex hyperborea plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environments.
