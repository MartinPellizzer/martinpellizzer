Carex laxiflora var. striatula

Carex laxiflora var. striatula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and moist soils in temperate regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy, with a distinctive striped pattern on the leaf blades. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in early summer, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in partial to full shade and requires consistent moisture for optimal growth.

Carex laxiflora var. striatula is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
