Campanula lanceolata var. praesignis

Campanula lanceolata var. praesignis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, with a pointed tip and a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
