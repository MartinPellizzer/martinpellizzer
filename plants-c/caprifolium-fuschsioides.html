Caprifolium fuschsioides

Caprifolium fuschsioides, commonly known as the purple honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in late summer.

Caprifolium fuschsioides thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
