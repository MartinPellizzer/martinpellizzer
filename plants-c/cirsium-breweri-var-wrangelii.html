Cirsium breweri var. wrangelii

Cirsium breweri var. wrangelii, commonly known as Wrangel's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in desert or semi-desert environments. It produces achenes as its seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh conditions.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
