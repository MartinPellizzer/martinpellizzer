Cordia trachyphylla

Cordia trachyphylla, commonly known as the rough-leaved cordia, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, rough texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry or semi-arid environments.

Cordia trachyphylla is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional healing practices.
