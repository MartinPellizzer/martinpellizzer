Cyperus subchoristachys

Cyperus subchoristachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it often serves as an indicator species for wetland habitats.

Cyperus subchoristachys plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
