Cyperus hemadrii

Cyperus hemadrii, commonly known as the Madagascar sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae, although it is often classified under the sedge family Cyperaceae. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, sandy soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often green or reddish in color.

Cyperus hemadrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a ground cover in humid climates.
