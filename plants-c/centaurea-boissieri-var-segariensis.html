Centaurea boissieri var. segariensis

Centaurea boissieri var. segariensis, commonly known as the Segariense thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and vibrant purple to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its structure includes a basal rosette of leaves with a tall, branching stem that supports the flower heads.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
