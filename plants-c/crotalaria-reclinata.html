Crotalaria reclinata

Crotalaria reclinata, commonly known as the hanging crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of many legumes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Crotalaria reclinata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
