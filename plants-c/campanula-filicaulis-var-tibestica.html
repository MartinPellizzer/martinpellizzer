Campanula filicaulis var. tibestica

Campanula filicaulis var. tibestica, commonly known as the Tibesti bellflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibesti Mountains in Chad.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, characterized by its bell-shaped flowers and showy blooms. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating. Its flowers, which range in color from blue to violet, bloom in late summer and are adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its unique appearance and limited distribution.
