Calopogonium caeruleum var. glabrescens

Calopogonium caeruleum var. glabrescens, commonly known as blue stylo, is a perennial legume native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in pastures and disturbed areas.

Its seeds are edible and can be used in traditional cuisines.
