Crataegus buckleyi

Crataegus buckleyi, commonly known as Buckley's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Florida and Georgia. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, small white or pink flowers, and red berries that appear in the fall. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.

Buckley's hawthorn is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
