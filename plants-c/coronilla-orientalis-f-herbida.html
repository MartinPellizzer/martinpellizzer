Coronilla orientalis f. herbida

Coronilla orientalis f. herbida, commonly known as the soft coronilla, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in spikes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval to lance-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
