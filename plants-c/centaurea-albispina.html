Centaurea albispina

Centaurea albispina, commonly known as the white thorned centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and bright yellow flowers with white tips. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Centaurea albispina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
