Carex lasiocarpa f. kansana

Carex lasiocarpa f. kansana, commonly known as the Kansas sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
