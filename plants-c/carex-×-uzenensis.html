Carex × uzenensis

Carex × uzenensis, commonly known as the Uzen sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their ecological importance in wetlands and moist environments. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It is often found in marshy areas, along stream banks, and in shaded forest understories.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with moist soil conditions.
