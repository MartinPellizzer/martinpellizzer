Ceratostema nutans

Ceratostema nutans, commonly known as the nodding bellflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that curve downward, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Ceratostema nutans produces small, dark blue to purple berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals.
