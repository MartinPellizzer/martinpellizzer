Carex × albidula

Carex × albidula, commonly known as the white sedge, is a hybrid plant within the Carex genus, belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale green to white in color, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex × albidula is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
