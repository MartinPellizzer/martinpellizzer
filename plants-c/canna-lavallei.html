Canna lavallei

Canna lavallei, commonly known as the Lavalle's canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, ornamental leaves that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length, often with a vibrant green or variegated coloration. The plant produces showy, inflorescences with bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna lavallei is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
