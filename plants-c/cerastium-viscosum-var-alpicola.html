Cerastium viscosum var. alpicola

Cerastium viscosum var. alpicola, commonly known as alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, sticky, ovate leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
