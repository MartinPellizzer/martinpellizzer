Cucumis × hytivus

Cucumis × hytivus, commonly known as the hybrid cucumber, is a botanical hybrid resulting from the cross between Cucumis sativus (common cucumber) and Cucumis melo (musk melon).

It exhibits a combination of traits from both parent species, including fruit size, texture, and flavor. The plant typically grows as a vine with lobed leaves and produces elongated, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The hybrid is valued for its adaptability and potential for improved yield and disease resistance in agricultural settings.
