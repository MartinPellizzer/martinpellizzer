Coronilla orientalis f. condensata

Coronilla orientalis f. condensata, commonly known as the dense-flowered oriental coronilla, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of blue to purple, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage, and its root system is fibrous and well-developed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
