Cerastium alpinum f. lanatum

Cerastium alpinum f. lanatum, commonly known as the downy mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant is distinguished by its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in meadows and mountainous habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.
