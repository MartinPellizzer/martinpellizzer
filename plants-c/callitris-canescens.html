Callitris canescens

Callitris canescens, commonly known as the coastal white cypress, is a hardy evergreen conifer native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and distinctive, cone-like structures that mature to a reddish-brown color. The plant typically grows in coastal regions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its bark is scaly and light gray, providing a rugged appearance.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
