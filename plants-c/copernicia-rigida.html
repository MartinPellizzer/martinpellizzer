Copernicia rigida

Copernicia rigida, commonly known as the rigid copernicia, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish flowers, which are pollinated by insects. Its fruits are small, round, and contain a single seed, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

Copernicia rigida is also valued for its hard, durable leaves, which have been used traditionally for various purposes.
