Cuphea persistens var. formosana

Cuphea persistens var. formosana, commonly known as the Chinese firecracker, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to China.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that resemble tiny firecrackers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a compact shrub, typically reaching heights of 1 to 2 feet, making it suitable for borders or containers.

This species is valued for its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
