Crocus hadriaticus var. peloponnesiacus

Crocus hadriaticus var. peloponnesiacus, commonly known as the Peloponnesian crocus, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a yellow center, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations. Its bulbs are perennial, and the plant is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
