Carduus × puechii

Carduus × puechii, commonly known as the hybrid cardoon, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid between Carduus benedictus and Carduus marianus, resulting in a robust plant with large, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces spiny, thistle-like heads that contain small, dark purple flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in cooking and herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and hardy member of the thistle family.
