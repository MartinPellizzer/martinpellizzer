Cordyline odorata

Cordyline odorata, commonly known as the mountain cabbage tree, is a striking evergreen plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It features a tall, unbranched stem with large, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow streaks. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically pink or white, followed by red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Cordyline odorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
