Cassia fagonioides

Cassia fagonioides, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Cassia fagonioides thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
