Carum macrocarpum

Carum macrocarpum, commonly known as large-fruited caraway, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, and its leaves are finely divided and aromatic. The fruit, or schizocarp, is distinctive for its large, oval shape and is often used in traditional medicine.

It is valued for its essential oils, which have been used for their potential therapeutic properties.
