Cunonia pulchella var. montana

Cunonia pulchella var. montana, commonly known as the mountain cunonia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions. It produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a coastal plant due to its drought tolerance.
