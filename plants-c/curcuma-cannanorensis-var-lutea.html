Curcuma cannanorensis var. lutea

Curcuma cannanorensis var. lutea, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a tropical plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers with a distinctive, fragrant aroma. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

The rhizomes of this species are used in traditional medicine and are valued for their potential health benefits.
