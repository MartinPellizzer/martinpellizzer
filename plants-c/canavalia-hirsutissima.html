Canavalia hirsutissima

Canavalia hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy jackbean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces large, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural systems for its nitrogen-fixing properties.

Canavalia hirsutissima is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in sustainable farming practices.
