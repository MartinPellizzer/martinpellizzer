Cassia wislizeni var. painteri

Cassia wislizeni var. painteri, commonly known as the painter's cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of bright yellow, feather-like flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological value.
