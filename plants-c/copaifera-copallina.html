Copaifera copallina

Copaifera copallina, commonly known as the yellow copaiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish resin that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, lowland tropical forests and is often found in the understory of dense vegetation.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
