Crocus boryanus var. caspius

Crocus boryanus var. caspius, commonly known as the Caspian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Central Asia.

It produces showy, early spring flowers with petals that range from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained conditions. Its bulbs are adapted to withstand harsh winters and bloom before the foliage fully emerges.

The Caspian crocus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
