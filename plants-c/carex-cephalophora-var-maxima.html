Carex cephalophora var. maxima

Carex cephalophora var. maxima, commonly known as the giant sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and large, strap-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. This variety thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers in the summer months.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
