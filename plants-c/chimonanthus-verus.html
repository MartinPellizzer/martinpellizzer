Chimonanthus verus

Chimonanthus verus, commonly known as the winter jasmine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its bark is typically reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age.

Chimonanthus verus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
