Coronilla minima var. hispanica

Coronilla minima var. hispanica, commonly known as Spanish vetch, is a low-growing annual legume native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has a fibrous root system and is well adapted to dry, rocky soils. It is often found in disturbed areas and is used for soil stabilization and forage.

Its flowers attract pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in Mediterranean ecosystems.
