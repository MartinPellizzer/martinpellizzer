Chamaesyce setiloba

Chamaesyce setiloba, commonly known as the slender sandmat, is a low-growing annual herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features a sprawling stem system with numerous small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing food for various insects and serving as a pioneer species in disturbed soils.
