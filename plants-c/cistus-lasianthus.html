Cistus lasianthus

Cistus lasianthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cistus lasianthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
