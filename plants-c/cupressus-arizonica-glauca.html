Cupressus arizonica glauca

Cupressus arizonica glauca, commonly known as the Arizona cypress, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a shallow root system and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The Arizona cypress is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic qualities.
