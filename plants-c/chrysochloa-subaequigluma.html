Chrysochloa subaequigluma

Chrysochloa subaequigluma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved golden grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with golden hues. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping and erosion control.
