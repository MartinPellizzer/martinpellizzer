Cucurbita pepo var. diachrysa

Cucurbita pepo var. diachrysa, commonly known as the Mexican bitter gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, slender, green fruits with a bumpy, warty surface. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a trailing growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is native to Mexico and is often cultivated for its edible, bitter-tasting fruits.
