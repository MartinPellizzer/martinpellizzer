Callitriche truncata proles occidentalis

Callitriche truncata proles occidentalis, commonly known as western waterstar, is a small aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has delicate, floating or submerged leaves that are usually oval or egg-shaped with a truncate or notched base. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
