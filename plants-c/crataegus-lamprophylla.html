Crataegus lamprophylla

Crataegus lamprophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white bloom of fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and riverbanks.

Crataegus lamprophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
