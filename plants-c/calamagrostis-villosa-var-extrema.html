Calamagrostis villosa var. extrema

Calamagrostis villosa var. extrema, commonly known as the Sierra velvetgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and tall, slender culms that often reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in meadows, streambanks, and forest edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of pale green to golden florets.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
