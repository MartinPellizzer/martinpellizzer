Crocanthus croceus

Crocanthus croceus, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and compact, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are grass-like and form a dense rosette at the base.

Crocanthus croceus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.
