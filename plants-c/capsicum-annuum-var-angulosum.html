Capsicum annuum var. angulosum

Capsicum annuum var. angulosum, commonly known as the bird's eye chili, is a variety of Capsicum annuum native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, pointed, and often red or yellow fruits, which are typically used in culinary applications for their heat. The plant is an annual herb with simple, alternate leaves and erect stems. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both home gardening and commercial cultivation.
