Caroxylon denudatum

Caroxylon denudatum, commonly known as the naked carob tree, is a member of the Caesalpiniaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
