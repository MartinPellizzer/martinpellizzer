Clerodendrum subtruncatum f. magnifolium

Clerodendrum subtruncatum f. magnifolium, commonly known as the giant-leaved clerodendrum, is a striking tropical shrub characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom from summer to autumn. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is also valued as an ornamental due to its dramatic appearance.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking bold visual impact.
