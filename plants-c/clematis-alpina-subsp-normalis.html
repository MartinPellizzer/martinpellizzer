Clematis alpina subsp. normalis

Clematis alpina subsp.

normalis, commonly known as the Alpine Clematis, is a perennial vine native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers range in color from white to pale blue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
