Cordyline fraseri

Cordyline fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's cordyline, is a striking evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features tall, upright stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a reddish tinge along the midrib. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that appear in late winter to early spring, followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its popularity in both cultivated and natural settings.
