Cinnamomum scrobiculatum

Cinnamomum scrobiculatum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum reticulatum, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that exudes a fragrant, cinnamon-like scent. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. Its small, fragrant flowers are followed by dark blue berries that contain small, hard seeds.

This species is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice in various cuisines.
