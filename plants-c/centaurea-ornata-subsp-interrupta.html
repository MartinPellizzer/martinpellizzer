Centaurea ornata subsp. interrupta

Centaurea ornata subsp.

interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a branched flowering head that resembles a coneflower. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
