Crotalaria rufocaulis

Crotalaria rufocaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of yellow flowers. Its fruit is a slender pod containing several elliptical seeds.

Crotalaria rufocaulis is often grown as a forage crop and has medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
