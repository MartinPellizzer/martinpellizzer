Cyrtorchis erythraeae

Cyrtorchis erythraeae, commonly known as the Red Sea orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in arid and saline environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the limited soil, allowing it to survive in harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
