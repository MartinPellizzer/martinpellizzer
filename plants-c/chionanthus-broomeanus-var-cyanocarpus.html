Chionanthus broomeanus var. cyanocarpus

Chionanthus broomeanus var. cyanocarpus, commonly known as the blue-fruited chionanthus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture.

The fruit is distinctive for its blue or bluish-black color, which contrasts with the plant's delicate floral display.
