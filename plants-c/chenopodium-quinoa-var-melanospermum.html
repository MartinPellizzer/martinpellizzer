Chenopodium quinoa var. melanospermum

Chenopodium quinoa var. melanospermum, commonly known as black quinoa, is a cultivated variety of the Andean plant quinoa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black seeds that are rich in protein, fiber, and essential amino acids. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure. It is an annual plant that produces small, green leaves and yellow flowers.

Black quinoa is valued for its nutritional profile and is commonly used in various cuisines around the world.
