Carex homoiolepis

Carex homoiolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
