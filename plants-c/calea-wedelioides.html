Calea wedelioides

Calea wedelioides, commonly known as the sacred candle plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescences that range in color from white to pink. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Calea wedelioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in traditional medicine.
