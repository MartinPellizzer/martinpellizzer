Campanula rotundifolia var. farinulenta

Campanula rotundifolia var. farinulenta, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rounded, hairy leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety farinulenta is distinguished by its dense, white to greyish pubescence on the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance.
