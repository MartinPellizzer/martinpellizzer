Centranthus cadevallii

Centranthus cadevallii, commonly known as the Balkan redflower.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the Balkan Peninsula, characterized by its striking red-pink tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Centranthus cadevallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
