Crypsinus engleri var. coriaceus

Crypsinus engleri var. coriaceus, commonly known as the hard-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are thick and leathery, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in humid environments with abundant moisture. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
