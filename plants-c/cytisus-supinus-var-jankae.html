Cytisus supinus var. jankae

Cytisus supinus var. jankae, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
