Croton caudatus var. obovoideus

Croton caudatus var. obovoideus, commonly known as the broad-leaf croton, is a striking tropical shrub characterized by its large, obovate-shaped leaves with a waxy, often variegated appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, followed by distinctive, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its foliage exhibits a wide variety of color patterns, making it a popular choice for garden displays.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
