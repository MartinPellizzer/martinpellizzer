Coleus mitis

Coleus mitis, commonly known as the silverleaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage with a velvety texture. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a prominent central vein. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale purple.

This plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
