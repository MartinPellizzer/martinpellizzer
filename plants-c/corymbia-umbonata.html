Corymbia umbonata

Corymbia umbonata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bloodwood, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with a rugged, fissured bark that is often grey or white in color. The leaves are narrow and leathery, typically measuring between 5 and 15 centimeters in length. It produces small, creamy-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season.

The tree is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in challenging climates.
