Centaurea × impura

Centaurea × impura, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea jacea (woollyhead). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring blue to purple radial flowers with a distinctive yellow center. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Centaurea × impura is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
