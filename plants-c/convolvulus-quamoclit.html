Convolvulus quamoclit

Convolvulus quamoclit, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has hairy, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Convolvulus quamoclit is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along supports.
