Carex cherokeensis

Carex cherokeensis, commonly known as Cherokee sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that contain small, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

Cherokee sedge is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
