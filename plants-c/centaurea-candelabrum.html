Centaurea candelabrum

Centaurea candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, upright growth habit and its distinctive, candle-like flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central cluster of tubular disk florets. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette.

Centaurea candelabrum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
