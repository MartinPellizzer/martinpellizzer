Cassia xinguensis

Cassia xinguensis, commonly known as the Xingu cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its bark and leaves have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

Cassia xinguensis thrives in humid, tropical environments with well-drained soil.
