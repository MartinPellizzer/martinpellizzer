Coleus subspicatus

Coleus subspicatus, commonly known as the spicate coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb with square stems and opposite leaves. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring combinations of green, white, and red, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.
