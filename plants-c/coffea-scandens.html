Coffea scandens

Coffea scandens, commonly known as the West African coffee plant, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of West Africa.

It produces small, red berries that contain the seeds used for coffee production. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. It is closely related to other coffee species but has distinct botanical characteristics.

Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, with a strong aroma when crushed.
