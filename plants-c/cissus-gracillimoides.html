Cissus gracillimoides

Cissus gracillimoides, commonly known as the slender cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in warm climates.

Cissus gracillimoides thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
