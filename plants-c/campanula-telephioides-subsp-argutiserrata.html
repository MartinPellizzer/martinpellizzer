Campanula telephioides subsp. argutiserrata

Campanula telephioides subsp.

argutiserrata, commonly known as the rough-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically serrated and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name. This species thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it plays a role in local biodiversity.
