Cervicina undulata

Cervicina undulata, commonly known as the wavy bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibacillaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and tiny, bladder-like structures called pneumatocysts, which aid in buoyancy. Cervicina undulata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
