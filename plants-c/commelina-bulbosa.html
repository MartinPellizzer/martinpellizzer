Commelina bulbosa

Commelina bulbosa, commonly known as the spikemoss or wild lettuce, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, blue or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a bulbous structure at its base, which serves as a storage organ for nutrients. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina bulbosa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
