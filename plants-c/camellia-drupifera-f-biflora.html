Camellia drupifera f. biflora

Camellia drupifera f. biflora, commonly known as the double-flowered camellia, is a rare and ornamental variant of the camellia genus.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring two sets of petals, which give it a more elaborate and showy appearance compared to typical camellias. This plant is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and red, adding vibrant visual interest to gardens.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its striking, double blooms.
