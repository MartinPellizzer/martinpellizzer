Coccinia abyssinica

Coccinia abyssinica, commonly known as the African eggplant or wild cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other plants like cucumbers and squash. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and edible, orange or red fruits that resemble small eggs. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Coccinia abyssinica is valued for its nutritional content and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
