Cyperus gossweileri

Cyperus gossweileri, commonly known as the African flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along water bodies. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered at the base, and it produces small, triangular spikelets.

Cyperus gossweileri is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding and is often found in areas with saturated soil.
