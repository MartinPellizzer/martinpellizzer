Cynoglossum cheirifolium var. stylosum

Cynoglossum cheirifolium var. stylosum, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are erect and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
