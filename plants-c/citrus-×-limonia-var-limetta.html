Citrus × limonia var. limetta

Citrus × limonia var. limetta, commonly known as the lemon.

This variety is a hybrid citrus plant that originated from the cross between the citron and the mandarin. It is characterized by its small, yellow, oval-shaped fruits with a thin, fragrant peel. The fruit contains a high concentration of citric acid, making it a popular choice for culinary and commercial use.

It is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.
