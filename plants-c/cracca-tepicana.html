Cracca tepicana

Cracca tepicana, commonly known as the Mexican wild lettuce, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open areas and has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months, producing small, elongated fruits.

Cracca tepicana is often used in traditional medicine for its potential sedative and anti-inflammatory properties.
