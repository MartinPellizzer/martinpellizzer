Crassula lycopodioides

Crassula lycopodioides, commonly known as the Cape Harebell, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, typically in spring.

Crassula lycopodioides is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
