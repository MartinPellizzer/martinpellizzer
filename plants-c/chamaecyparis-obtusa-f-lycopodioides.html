Chamaecyparis obtusa f. lycopodioides

Chamaecyparis obtusa f. lycopodioides, commonly known as the Japanese cedar dwarf form, is a compact, evergreen conifer with a distinctive, shrub-like appearance.

It features scale-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a dense, foliage-rich look. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones, typical of the cypress family. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative accent plant.

Its unique form and slow growth rate make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, ornamental evergreen.
