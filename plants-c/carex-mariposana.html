Carex mariposana

Carex mariposana, commonly known as the Mariposa sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, linear leaves that are often dark green in color.

The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer.
