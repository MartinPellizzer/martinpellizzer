Crotalaria grahamiana

Crotalaria grahamiana, commonly known as the Graham's spurred cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and compound leaves. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria grahamiana is also noted for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
