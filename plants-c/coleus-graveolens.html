Coleus graveolens

Coleus graveolens, commonly known as the horse mint or pennyroyal, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves that vary in color from green to red, purple, or bronze. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Coleus graveolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
