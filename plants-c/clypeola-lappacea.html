Clypeola lappacea

Clypeola lappacea, commonly known as the lappaceae or the lappace, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences.

It is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, often found in moist, calcareous soils. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in complex umbels, and it produces small, dry fruits known as schizocarps.

Clypeola lappacea is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its culinary uses.
