Chayota edulis

Chayota edulis, commonly known as the cushaw squash, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It produces large, edible, green, elongated fruits that resemble a cross between a pumpkin and a cucumber. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it climb. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and is often used in soups, stews, and as a vegetable in various cuisines.
