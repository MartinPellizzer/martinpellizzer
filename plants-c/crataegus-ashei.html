Crataegus ashei

Crataegus ashei, commonly known as the Ashe serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in the summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, persistent fruits that are important for wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The Ashe serviceberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
