Carex pycnothyrsos

Carex pycnothyrsos, commonly known as the dense-spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of spikelets that form its flowering structures. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Carex pycnothyrsos is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
