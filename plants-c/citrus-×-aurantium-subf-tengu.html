Citrus × aurantium subf. tengu

Citrus × aurantium subf.

tengu, commonly known as the bitter orange, is a hybrid citrus plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, peely rind and small, sour fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its botanical features include a complex hybrid origin, making it a subject of interest in horticultural and genetic studies. The subsp.

tengu is distinguished by its specific morphological traits and is closely related to other citrus species like the sweet orange and mandarin.
