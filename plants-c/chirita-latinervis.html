Chirita latinervis

Chirita latinervis, commonly known as the narrow-veined chirita, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with prominent, parallel veins. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a distinctive two-lipped structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
