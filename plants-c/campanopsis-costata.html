Campanopsis costata

Campanopsis costata, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Campanopsis costata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
