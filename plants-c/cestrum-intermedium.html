Cestrum intermedium

Cestrum intermedium, commonly known as the night blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and lance-shaped, while its fruits are capsule-like and contain numerous seeds.

Cestrum intermedium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
