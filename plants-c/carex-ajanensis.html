Carex ajanensis

Carex ajanensis, commonly known as the Ajanensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshy areas of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. Carex ajanensis thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and serving as a food source for various wildlife species.
