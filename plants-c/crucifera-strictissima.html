Crucifera strictissima

Crucifera strictissima, commonly known as the rigid crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits called siliques, which contain several seeds. It is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streams.
