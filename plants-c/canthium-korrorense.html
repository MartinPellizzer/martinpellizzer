Canthium korrorense

Canthium korrorense, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a tropical shrub native to coastal regions of the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed, adapted for dispersal by water. The plant has aerial roots, known as pneumatophores, which help it breathe in oxygen-poor, waterlogged soils. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a waxy surface that reduces water loss.

Canthium korrorense plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
