Chrysomelon pomiferum

Chrysomelon pomiferum, commonly known as the sweet apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible, sweet, and juicy fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value, combined with its edible fruit, makes it a popular choice for both gardens and commercial cultivation.
