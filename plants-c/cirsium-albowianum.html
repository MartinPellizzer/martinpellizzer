Cirsium albowianum

Cirsium albowianum, commonly known as the albow's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Cirsium albowianum is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
