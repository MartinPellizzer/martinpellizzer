Crataegus azarolus var. triloba

Crataegus azarolus var. triloba, commonly known as the Mediterranean hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, three-lobed leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
