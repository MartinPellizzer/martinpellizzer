Copaiba

Copaiba, commonly known as Brazilian frankincense, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its aromatic resin, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine. The tree produces a yellowish to reddish sap, known as copaiba balsam, which is harvested from its bark. This resin contains various bioactive compounds, including terpenoids and flavonoids, contributing to its medicinal properties.

Copaiba is also valued for its role in the production of essential oils and its ecological importance in rainforest ecosystems.
