Crepis oporinoides var. prostrata

Crepis oporinoides var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
