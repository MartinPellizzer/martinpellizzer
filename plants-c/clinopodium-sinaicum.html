Clinopodium sinaicum

Clinopodium sinaicum, commonly known as Sinaitic oregano, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's compact growth habit and resilience make it a notable species in its native habitats.
