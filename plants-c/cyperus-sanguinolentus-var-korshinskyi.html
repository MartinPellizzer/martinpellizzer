Cyperus sanguinolentus var. korshinskyi

Cyperus sanguinolentus var. korshinskyi, commonly known as the red-flowered flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers that bloom during the warm seasons. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within its genus, valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
