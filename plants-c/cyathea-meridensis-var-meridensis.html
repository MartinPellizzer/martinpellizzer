Cyathea meridensis var. meridensis

Cyathea meridensis var. meridensis, commonly known as the coastal huperzia, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its tall, upright fronds with a distinctive, segmented structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds.

Cyathea meridensis var. meridensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in maintaining soil stability.
