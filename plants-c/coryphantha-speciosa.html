Coryphantha speciosa

Coryphantha speciosa, commonly known as the showy coryphantha, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
