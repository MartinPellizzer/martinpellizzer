Carex halmaturina

Carex halmaturina, commonly known as the Halmaturina sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or damp environments, such as marshes and stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, clustered flower heads in the summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
