Cuscuta argentinana

Cuscuta argentinana, commonly known as the Argentinian dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a vine-like structure covered in tiny, thread-like stems. The plant is native to South America and is often found growing on grasses and other herbaceous plants. Its life cycle involves attaching to a host with specialized structures called haustoria, which penetrate the host's tissues.

Cuscuta argentinana plays a role in ecological interactions, though it can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
