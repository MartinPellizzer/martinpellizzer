Croton texensis var. utahensis

Croton texensis var. utahensis, commonly known as the Utah croton, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with reddish or purplish veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
