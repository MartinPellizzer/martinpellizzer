Carex intacta

Carex intacta, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically green or brown in color. Carex intacta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
