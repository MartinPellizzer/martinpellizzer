Cerasus jamasakura f. purpureorubra

Cerasus jamasakura f. purpureorubra, commonly known as the deep pink cherry blossom, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its striking, intensely pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and they turn a vibrant red in the autumn.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens and landscapes where its showy blooms add a dramatic visual impact.
