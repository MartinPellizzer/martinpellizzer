Cyathea tripinnata

Cyathea tripinnata, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a robust rhizome system that supports its height and helps anchor it in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are bi-directionally arranged, with a distinctive tri-pinnate structure that gives it its scientific name.

Cyathea tripinnata plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various organisms.
