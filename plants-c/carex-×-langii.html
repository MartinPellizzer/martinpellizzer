Carex × langii

Carex × langii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Carex lyngbyei and Carex acutata, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and roadside ditches. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex × langii is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
