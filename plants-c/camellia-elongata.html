Camellia elongata

Camellia elongata, commonly known as the longleaf camellia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, leathery leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a yellow center. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of Camellia elongata make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
