Coreopsis cardaminifolia

Coreopsis cardaminifolia, commonly known as the creeping coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
