Cotoneaster soongoricus

Cotoneaster soongoricus, commonly known as the Korean cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Cotoneaster soongoricus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and winter interest.
