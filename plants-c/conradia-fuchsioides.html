Conradia fuchsioides

Conradia fuchsioides, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Conradia fuchsioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
