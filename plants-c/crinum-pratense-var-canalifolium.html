Crinum pratense var. canalifolium

Crinum pratense var. canalifolium, commonly known as the canal-leaved swamp lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It features large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, often with a strong, spicy fragrance. The plant has strap-like, broad leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
