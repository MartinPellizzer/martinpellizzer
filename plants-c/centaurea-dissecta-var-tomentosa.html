Centaurea dissecta var. tomentosa

Centaurea dissecta var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
