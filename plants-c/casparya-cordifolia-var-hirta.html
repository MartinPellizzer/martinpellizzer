Casparya cordifolia var. hirta

Casparya cordifolia var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens across southern Europe and parts of North Africa.
