Cissus adenocaulis var. pubescens

Cissus adenocaulis var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvety wood sorrel, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish-brown stem. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical conditions and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
