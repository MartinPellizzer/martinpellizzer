Caralluma incarnata

Caralluma incarnata, commonly known as the red spine cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and protect it from herbivores.

Caralluma incarnata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
