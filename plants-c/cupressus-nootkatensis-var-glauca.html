Cupressus nootkatensis var. glauca

Cupressus nootkatensis var. glauca, commonly known as the Alaska cedar or glaucous cedar, is a variant of the western red cedar.

It is native to the coastal regions of Alaska and western Canada, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, scale-like foliage that gives it a distinctive bluish-gray appearance. Its cones are small and woody, typically maturing within one to two years.

This variety is valued for its aromatic wood and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental qualities.
