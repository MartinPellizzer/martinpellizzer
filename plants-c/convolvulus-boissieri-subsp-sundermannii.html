Convolvulus boissieri subsp. sundermannii

Convolvulus boissieri subsp.

sundermannii, commonly known as the Sudanese bindweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East. It is a trailing or climbing herb characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
