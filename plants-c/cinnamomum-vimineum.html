Cinnamomum vimineum

Cinnamomum vimineum, commonly known as the climbing cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue-black berries containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its aromatic bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine and for flavoring.

The botanical features of Cinnamomum vimineum make it a valuable species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
