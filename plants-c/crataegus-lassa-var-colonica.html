Crataegus lassa var. colonica

Crataegus lassa var. colonica, commonly known as the Blackthorn or Sloe, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are enclosed in a white, waxy coating. The plant has thorny branches and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It typically grows in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

The berries are used in making jams, liqueurs, and traditional medicines.
