Capsella frigida

Capsella frigida, commonly known as the frostweed or winter cress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Capsella frigida is known for its ability to survive in cooler climates and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
