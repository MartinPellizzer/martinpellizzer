Calotis scabiosifolia var. pubescens

Calotis scabiosifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy calotis, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Calotis scabiosifolia var. pubescens is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.
