Capuronia

Capuronia, commonly known as the white balm, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale yellow flowers that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Capuronia has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
