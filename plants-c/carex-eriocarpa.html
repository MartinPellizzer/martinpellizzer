Carex eriocarpa

Carex eriocarpa, commonly known as the soft-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with a fine, soft hair, giving it its common name.

Carex eriocarpa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
