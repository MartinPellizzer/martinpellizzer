Cestrum laurifolium

Cestrum laurifolium, commonly known as the laurel bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its evergreen, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
