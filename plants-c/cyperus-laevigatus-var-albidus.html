Cyperus laevigatus var. albidus

Cyperus laevigatus var. albidus, commonly known as the white flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly waxy texture, and it thrives in shallow water or moist soil.

This variant is distinguished by its pale or white flower color, which sets it apart from other Cyperus species.
