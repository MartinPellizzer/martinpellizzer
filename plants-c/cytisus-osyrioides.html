Cytisus osyrioides

Cytisus osyrioides, commonly known as the Portuguese broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus osyrioides is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
