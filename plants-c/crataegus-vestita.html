Crataegus vestita

Crataegus vestita, commonly known as the hairy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves with hairy surfaces, and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
