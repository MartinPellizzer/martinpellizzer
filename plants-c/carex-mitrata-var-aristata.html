Carex mitrata var. aristata

Carex mitrata var. aristata, commonly known as the aristate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color, and it blooms in the summer months.

Carex mitrata var. aristata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
