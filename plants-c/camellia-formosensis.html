Camellia formosensis

Camellia formosensis, commonly known as the Formosa camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pale pink, often with a contrasting yellow or white base. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Camellia formosensis is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
