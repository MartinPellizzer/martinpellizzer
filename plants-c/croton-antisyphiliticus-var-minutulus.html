Croton antisyphiliticus var. minutulus

Croton antisyphiliticus var. minutulus, commonly known as the miniature croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its small, clustered leaves and vibrant, colorful bracts.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is known for its ornamental value, with its striking foliage and unique floral structures attracting both gardeners and botanists. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
