Cuphea dryadica

Cuphea dryadica, commonly known as the flame flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular red or orange flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds due to their shape and color.

Cuphea dryadica thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
