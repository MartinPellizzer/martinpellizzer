Ctenodon simulans

Ctenodon simulans, commonly known as the false brome, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-brown flowers. Ctenodon simulans typically thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various insect and small animal species.
