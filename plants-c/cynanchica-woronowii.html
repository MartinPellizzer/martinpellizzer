Cynanchica woronowii

Cynanchica woronowii, commonly known as the woody milkvetch, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Cynanchica woronowii is known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
