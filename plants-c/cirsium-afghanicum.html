Cirsium afghanicum

Cirsium afghanicum, commonly known as Afghan thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia, including regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Cirsium afghanicum is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
