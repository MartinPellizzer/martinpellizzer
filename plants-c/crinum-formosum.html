Crinum formosum

Crinum formosum, commonly known as the Chinese crinum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Crinum formosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
