Crotalaria tenella

Crotalaria tenella, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea or slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits containing seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or as a cover crop.
