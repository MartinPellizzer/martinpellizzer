Cuphea anagalloidea var. dumosa

Cuphea anagalloidea var. dumosa, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
