Centaurea limbata subvar. intermedia

Centaurea limbata subvar. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, sparsely hairy stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters called capitula. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.
