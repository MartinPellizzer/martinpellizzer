Chenopodium pallidicaule f. melanospermum

Chenopodium pallidicaule f. melanospermum, commonly known as the black-seeded goosefoot, is a variant of the common goosefoot plant.

It is characterized by its pale green, succulent leaves and distinctive black seeds. This plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The black seeds are a notable feature, contributing to its identification and ecological role in seed dispersal.
