Cirsium macounii

Cirsium macounii, commonly known as Macoun's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Cirsium macounii is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
