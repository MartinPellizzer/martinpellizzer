Capparis myrioneura var. latifolia

Capparis myrioneura var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved caper, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It features woody stems with thorny branches and pinnately compound leaves that are broad and oval in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, spherical berries. Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
