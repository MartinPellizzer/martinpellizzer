Campanula michauxioides var. hirtifolia

Campanula michauxioides var. hirtifolia, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name, hirtifolia. It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
