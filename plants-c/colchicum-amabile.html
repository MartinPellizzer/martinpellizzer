Colchicum amabile

Colchicum amabile, commonly known as the sweet colchicum, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has a distinctive, spurred corolla with a prominent, hood-like structure at the base. Its leaves are basal, broad, and often die back after flowering.

Colchicum amabile is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.
