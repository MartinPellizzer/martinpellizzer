Cinchona parabolica

Cinchona parabolica, commonly known as the Parabolica quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits containing elliptical seeds. It is particularly noted for its bark, which contains quinine, a compound historically used in the treatment of malaria.

Due to its medicinal value, it has been cultivated in various parts of the world for pharmaceutical purposes.
