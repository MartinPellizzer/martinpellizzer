Campanula punctata f. partita

Campanula punctata f. partita, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a striking perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet, with distinctive white or pale yellow markings. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings due to its hardiness and visual interest.
