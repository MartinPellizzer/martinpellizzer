Chamomilla aurea

Chamomilla aurea, commonly known as golden chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring full sun.

The flowers are used in traditional medicine for their calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
