Centaurea mersinensis

Centaurea mersinensis, commonly known as Mersin cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
