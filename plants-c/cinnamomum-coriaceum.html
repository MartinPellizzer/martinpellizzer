Cinnamomum coriaceum

Cinnamomum coriaceum, commonly known as cinnamon bark or Chinese cinnamon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a fragrant, slightly oily texture.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, followed by dark blue to black berries.
