Camellia fulva

Camellia fulva, commonly known as the yellow camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and a woody stem, typical of the Camellia genus. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Camellia fulva is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.
