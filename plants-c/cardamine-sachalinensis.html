Cardamine sachalinensis

Cardamine sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin watercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northeastern Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
