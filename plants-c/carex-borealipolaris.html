Carex borealipolaris

Carex borealipolaris, commonly known as the Arctic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and wetland habitats. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex borealipolaris plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
