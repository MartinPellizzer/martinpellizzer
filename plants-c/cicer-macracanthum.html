Cicer macracanthum

Cicer macracanthum, commonly known as the giant lentil, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, robust leaves and showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds, which are edible and rich in protein. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in ecological restoration projects.
