Caesalpinia hughesii

Caesalpinia hughesii, commonly known as the golden glorybower, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow to orange inflorescences that resemble plumes, which are clustered in dense, branched heads. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Caesalpinia hughesii is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to warm climates.
