Crassula inchangensis

Crassula inchangensis, commonly known as the Inchangensis Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit and ability to form dense clusters make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
