Cyperus maderaspatanus

Cyperus maderaspatanus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along water bodies. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
