Carex surculosa

Carex surculosa, commonly known as the creeping sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive waxy coating. It produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Carex surculosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.
