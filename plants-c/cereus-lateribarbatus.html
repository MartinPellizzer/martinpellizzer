Cereus lateribarbatus

Cereus lateribarbatus, commonly known as the lateribarbatus cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and nocturnal blooming flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil. Its flowers, which open at night, are usually white or pale pink and attract moths.

Cereus lateribarbatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
