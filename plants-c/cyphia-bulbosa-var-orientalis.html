Cyphia bulbosa var. orientalis

Cyphia bulbosa var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental bulbous cyphia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bulbous underground stem and striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings for its aesthetic appeal.
