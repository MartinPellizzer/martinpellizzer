Croton aubrevilecta

Croton aubrevilecta, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and diverse species. The plant features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that vary in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in catkins, while the fruit is a three-lobed capsule.

The plant is adapted to dry environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
