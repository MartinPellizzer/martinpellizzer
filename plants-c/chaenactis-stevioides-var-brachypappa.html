Chaenactis stevioides var. brachypappa

Chaenactis stevioides var. brachypappa, commonly known as the short-stemmed paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a low, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, while its flowers range from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid environments.
