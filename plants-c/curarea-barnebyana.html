Curarea barnebyana

Curarea barnebyana, commonly known as the Amazonian curare plant, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant is notable for containing toxic alkaloids, primarily curarine, which have been historically used by indigenous peoples for arrow poison. Its roots and stems are typically harvested for medicinal and traditional purposes.

Due to its toxic properties, handling the plant requires caution and expertise.
