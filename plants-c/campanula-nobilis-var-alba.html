Campanula nobilis var. alba

Campanula nobilis var. alba, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula nobilis var. alba is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy maintenance.
