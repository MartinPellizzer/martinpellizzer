Caladenia luteola

Caladenia luteola, commonly known as the Yellow Spider Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a single, slender stem that bears one to three flowers, each with a distinctive yellow and green coloration. Its flowers have a spurred lip and are often pollinated by thynnid wasps.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.
