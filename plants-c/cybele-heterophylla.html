Cybele heterophylla

Cybele heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf cybele, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often lobed leaves that vary in shape depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Cybele heterophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
