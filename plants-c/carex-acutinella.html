Carex acutinella

Carex acutinella, commonly known as narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex acutinella produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
