Carex saskatschewana

Carex saskatschewana, commonly known as Saskatoon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
