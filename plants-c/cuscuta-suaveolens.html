Cuscuta suaveolens

Cuscuta suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet smilax, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a vine-like structure that wraps around its host. The plant is native to various regions in the Americas and is often found growing on shrubs and trees. Its seeds are dispersed by animals that consume the berries.

Cuscuta suaveolens plays a role in ecological interactions, though its parasitic nature can negatively impact its host plants.
