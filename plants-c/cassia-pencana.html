Cassia pencana

Cassia pencana, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. Cassia pencana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
