Cardamine lazica

Cardamine lazica, commonly known as the Lazica watercress, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and have a peppery aroma, similar to other members of its family.

The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in a raceme, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
