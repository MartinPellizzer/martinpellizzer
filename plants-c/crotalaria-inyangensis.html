Crotalaria inyangensis

Crotalaria inyangensis, commonly known as the Inyangensis cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive yellow flowers with a spurred petal. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Crotalaria inyangensis is also valued for its role in soil enrichment and as a forage crop in certain agricultural systems.
