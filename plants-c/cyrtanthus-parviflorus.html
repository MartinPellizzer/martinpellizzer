Cyrtanthus parviflorus

Cyrtanthus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered rain lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens with adequate sunlight.

Cyrtanthus parviflorus is also valued for its resilience and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
