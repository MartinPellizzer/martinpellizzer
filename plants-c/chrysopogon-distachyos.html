Chrysopogon distachyos

Chrysopogon distachyos, commonly known as dwarf grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia, including India and China.

It typically grows in grasslands, open woodlands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in pairs, giving it the species name "distachyos.".

Chrysopogon distachyos is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
