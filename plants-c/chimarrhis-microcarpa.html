Chimarrhis microcarpa

Chimarrhis microcarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hop bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, woody fruits. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native landscaping.
