Carex blakei

Carex blakei, commonly known as Blake's sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to North America, it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex blakei plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
