Chascolytrum spicigerum

Chascolytrum spicigerum, commonly known as the Chilean wild onion, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bulbous structures that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
