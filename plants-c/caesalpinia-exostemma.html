Caesalpinia exostemma

Caesalpinia exostemma, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
