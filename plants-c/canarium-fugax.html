Canarium fugax

Canarium fugax, commonly known as the wild candlenut tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy dark green upper surface.

The tree is valued for its oil-rich seeds, which have traditional and commercial uses in various cultures.
