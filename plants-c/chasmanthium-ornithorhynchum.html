Chasmanthium ornithorhynchum

Chasmanthium ornithorhynchum, commonly known as the chicken foot grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It features a distinctive appearance with its dense, feather-like inflorescences that resemble a chicken's foot, hence its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.

Chasmanthium ornithorhynchum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
