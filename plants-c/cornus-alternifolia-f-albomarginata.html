Cornus alternifolia f. albomarginata

Cornus alternifolia f. albomarginata, commonly known as the white-marginred dogwood, is a cultivar of the flowering dogwood tree.

It is characterized by its striking white-edged leaves that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small white flowers in spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic features.
