Callicarpa involucrata

Callicarpa involucrata, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple to violet berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for birds and small mammals.
