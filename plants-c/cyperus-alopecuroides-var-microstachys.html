Cyperus alopecuroides var. microstachys

Cyperus alopecuroides var. microstachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It is native to regions in Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is often found in disturbed or wetland habitats.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
