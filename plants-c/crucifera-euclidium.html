Crucifera euclidium

Crucifera euclidium, commonly known as the Euclidean Mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species of mustard and cabbage.

It is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The plant produces slender, elongated pods containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Crucifera euclidium is native to temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
