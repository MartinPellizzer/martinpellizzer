Crassula expansa subsp. expansa

Crassula expansa subsp.

expansa, commonly known as the "Laceleaf Crassula," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its compact growth habit and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
