Crocus reticulatus var. reflexus

Crocus reticulatus var. reflexus, commonly known as the reflexed crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive reflexed (curled backward) petal shape, which gives it its common name. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short stem that supports its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming habit, often emerging before many other spring flowers.
