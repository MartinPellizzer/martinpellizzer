Commiphora setulifera

Commiphora setulifera, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its flowering and fruiting occur in the rainy season, contributing to its ecological significance in its native range.
