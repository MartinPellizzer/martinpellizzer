Cereus chiloensis var. flavescens

Cereus chiloensis var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow night bloomer, is a succulent cactus native to Chile.

It features a tall, columnar stem with a smooth, pale green surface that often turns yellowish in color. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or yellow. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
