Calanthe discolor

Calanthe discolor, commonly known as the variable calanthe, is a perennial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often variegated leaves and its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant prefers cool, shaded environments and well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white with a distinctive yellowish throat, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Calanthe discolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
