Cistus vulgaris var. crispus

Cistus vulgaris var. crispus, commonly known as the wrinkled rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its distinctive crinkled foliage and resilience to drought make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Additionally, it is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
