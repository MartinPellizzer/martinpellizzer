Clematis maritima var. semiplena

Clematis maritima var. semiplena, commonly known as the partial-flowered sea clematis, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, with partially double petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability to saline environments make it a popular choice for seaside landscaping.
