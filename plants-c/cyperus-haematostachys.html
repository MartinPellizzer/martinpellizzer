Cyperus haematostachys

Cyperus haematostachys, commonly known as redstem flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and pond edges. The plant features slender, erect stems with whorled leaves, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often reddish in color. Its flowering period usually occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus haematostachys is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed wetlands, making it a common indicator species in such environments.
