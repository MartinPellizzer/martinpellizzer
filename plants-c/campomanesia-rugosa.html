Campomanesia rugosa

Campomanesia rugosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round, red drupe that contains a single seed.

The leaves are leathery, elliptical, and have a distinctive rugose texture, contributing to its botanical uniqueness.
