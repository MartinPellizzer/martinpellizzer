Cancrinia karatavica

Cancrinia karatavica, commonly known as the Karatavica thistle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a tall, erect stem that bears purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Cancrinia karatavica is often found in steppes and semi-desert regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small animals.
