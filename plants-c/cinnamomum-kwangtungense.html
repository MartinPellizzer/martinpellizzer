Cinnamomum kwangtungense

Cinnamomum kwangtungense, commonly known as Guangdong cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is highly valued in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, dark green leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves and bark contain compounds like cinnamaldehyde, contributing to its medicinal and culinary uses.
