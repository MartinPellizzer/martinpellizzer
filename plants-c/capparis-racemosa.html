Capparis racemosa

Capparis racemosa, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences that produce fragrant, white to pink flowers. The plant has woody stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and forest margins.

Capparis racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its showy blooms.
