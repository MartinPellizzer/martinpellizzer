Cyperus longus f. lucanus

Cyperus longus f. lucanus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. This plant is often found in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize new areas effectively.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various wetland habitats and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
