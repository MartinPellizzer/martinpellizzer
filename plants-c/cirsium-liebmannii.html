Cirsium liebmannii

Cirsium liebmannii, commonly known as Liebmann's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are lance-shaped, rough to the touch, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single head.

Cirsium liebmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and also has historical uses in traditional medicine.
