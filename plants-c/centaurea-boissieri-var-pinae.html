Centaurea boissieri var. pinae

Centaurea boissieri var. pinae, commonly known as the Pina's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with a distinctive central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
