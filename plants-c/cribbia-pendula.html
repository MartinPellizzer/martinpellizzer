Cribbia pendula

Cribbia pendula, commonly known as the weeping sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial with pale green, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise desolate habitat. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

Cribbia pendula is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
