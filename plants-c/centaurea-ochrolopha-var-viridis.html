Centaurea ochrolopha var. viridis

Centaurea ochrolopha var. viridis, commonly known as the green centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
