Cerasus caproniana var. umbraculifera

Cerasus caproniana var. umbraculifera, commonly known as the Umbrella Cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible, red drupes that ripen in late summer, attracting various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream valleys.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in shaded areas.
