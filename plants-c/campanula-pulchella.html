Campanula pulchella

Campanula pulchella, commonly known as the blue-eyed Mary, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender stems that are often hairy and bear clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
