Centaurea akcadaghensis

Centaurea akcadaghensis, commonly known as the Aksu Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate to alpine climates. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with narrow, grass-like leaves.

Centaurea akcadaghensis is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
