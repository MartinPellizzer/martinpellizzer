Cleisostoma chrysomelas

Cleisostoma chrysomelas, commonly known as the golden wax plant, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, yellow flowers that resemble small lanterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are usually broad and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Cleisostoma chrysomelas is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
