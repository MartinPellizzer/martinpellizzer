Crataegus brainerdii var. cyclophylla

Crataegus brainerdii var. cyclophylla, commonly known as the cyclophylla hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure.
