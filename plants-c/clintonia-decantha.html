Clintonia decantha

Clintonia decantha, commonly known as the white fringed gentian, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. The plant features striking, white, bell-shaped flowers with prominent, fringed petals that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a distinctive waxy texture.

Clintonia decantha is valued in botanical gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance to pollinators.
