Crocus bowlesianus

Crocus bowlesianus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family.

It is characterized by its early blooming habit, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers with a distinctive, spurred petal. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

Crocus bowlesianus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
