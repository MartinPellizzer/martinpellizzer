Crocus pamphylicus

Crocus pamphylicus, commonly known as the Anatolian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It is part of the Iris family and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
