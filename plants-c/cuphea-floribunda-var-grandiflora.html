Cuphea floribunda var. grandiflora

Cuphea floribunda var. grandiflora, commonly known as the flame flower or red hot poker, is a striking plant with vibrant, tubular red bracts that encase its small, yellowish flowers.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, adding to its ornamental appeal. It produces showy inflorescences that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.
