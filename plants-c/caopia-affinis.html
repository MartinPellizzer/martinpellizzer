Caopia affinis

Caopia affinis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, clustered flowers in shades of purple, white, and pink. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to prevent leaf scorching. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its compact size and long blooming period.

Caopia affinis is often cultivated as a houseplant, making it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
