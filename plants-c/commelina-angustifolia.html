Commelina angustifolia

Commelina angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf commelina, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, blue-violet flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Commelina angustifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
