Cestrum ehrenbergii

Cestrum ehrenbergii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking, fragrant white flowers that bloom at night, opening in the evening and closing by morning. These flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Cestrum ehrenbergii is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants, and is characterized by its milky sap and showy floral display.
