Cousinia barbeyi

Cousinia barbeyi, commonly known as the Armenian sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow or brown flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Cousinia barbeyi is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
