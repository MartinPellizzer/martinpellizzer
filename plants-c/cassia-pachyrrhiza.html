Cassia pachyrrhiza

Cassia pachyrrhiza, commonly known as the thick-veined cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It produces pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
