Copaifera langsdorffii var. rupestris

Copaifera langsdorffii var. rupestris, commonly known as the rock copaiba, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rugged, often twisted trunk and dense, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its bark is known for containing copaiba resin, which has been used in traditional medicine.

This species thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, tropical environments.
