Cerastium viscarium

Cerastium viscarium, commonly known as sticky chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that are covered with fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cerastium viscarium is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
