Carex gifuensis var. koreana

Carex gifuensis var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to East Asia, particularly Korea and parts of Japan. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
