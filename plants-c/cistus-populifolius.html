Cistus populifolius

Cistus populifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are broad and lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cistus populifolius is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
