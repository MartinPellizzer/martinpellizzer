Crataegus pomasae

Crataegus pomasae, commonly known as the Pomasae hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America, specifically in regions of Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, small white or pink flowers, and red or orange berries that appear in the fall. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The Pomasae hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
