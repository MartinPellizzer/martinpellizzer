Carex vikingensis

Carex vikingensis, commonly known as the Viking sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in tundra or wetland environments. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section typical of sedges.

Carex vikingensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the genus.
