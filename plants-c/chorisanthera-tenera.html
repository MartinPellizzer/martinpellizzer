Chorisanthera tenera

Chorisanthera tenera, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
