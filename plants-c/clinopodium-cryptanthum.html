Clinopodium cryptanthum

Clinopodium cryptanthum, commonly known as the purple mountain mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are often hairy and have a mint-like fragrance, which is a distinguishing feature.

Clinopodium cryptanthum is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
