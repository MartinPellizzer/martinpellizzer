Cuphea viscosissima var. parietarioides

Cuphea viscosissima var. parietarioides, commonly known as the sticky cuphea or parietarioid cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, sticky texture. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and orange, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a valuable addition to both garden landscapes and container plantings.
