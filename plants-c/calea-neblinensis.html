Calea neblinensis

Calea neblinensis, commonly known as the misty calea, is a flowering plant native to the humid cloud forests of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Calea neblinensis is also noted for its aromatic foliage, which emits a subtle, sweet scent.
