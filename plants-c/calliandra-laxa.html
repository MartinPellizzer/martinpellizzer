Calliandra laxa

Calliandra laxa, commonly known as the silky calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Calliandra laxa is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
