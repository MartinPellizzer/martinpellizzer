Chlorophytum radula

Chlorophytum radula, commonly known as the snake plant, is a hardy, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright leaves that are typically green with white or yellow variegation. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering. It produces small, greenish-white flowers on slender stalks, which appear in the spring.

Chlorophytum radula is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
