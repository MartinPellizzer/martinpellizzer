Cirsium odontolepis var. montenegrinum

Cirsium odontolepis var. montenegrinum, commonly known as the Montenegro thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, achene fruits that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits that set it apart from other members of the Cirsium genus.
