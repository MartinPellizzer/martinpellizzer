Centaurea × vladescui

Centaurea × vladescui, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disc florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea × vladescui is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
