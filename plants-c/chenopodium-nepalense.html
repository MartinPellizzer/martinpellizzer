Chenopodium nepalense

Chenopodium nepalense, commonly known as Nepal goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine areas. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and a bluish-green color. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits containing single seeds.

It is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
