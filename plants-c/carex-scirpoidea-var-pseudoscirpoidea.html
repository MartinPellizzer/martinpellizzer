Carex scirpoidea var. pseudoscirpoidea

Carex scirpoidea var. pseudoscirpoidea, commonly known as the narrowleaf bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of spikelets that form its flowering structure. This variety is typically found in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in wet soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
