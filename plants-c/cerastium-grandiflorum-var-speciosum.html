Cerastium grandiflorum var. speciosum

Cerastium grandiflorum var. speciosum, commonly known as the showy mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable plant in ecological gardens.
