Claoxylon rubescens

Claoxylon rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered claoxylon, is a member of the Clusiaceae family.

It is a tropical evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface with a slightly serrated edge.

The tree is native to Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
