Calceolaria cuneiformis subsp. xerophila

Calceolaria cuneiformis subsp.

xerophila, commonly known as the desert calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-shaped petals, which resemble small cups or pouches.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage is often succulent, helping to store water.

It blooms with vibrant flowers, usually in shades of red, orange, or yellow, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
