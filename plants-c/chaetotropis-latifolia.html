Chaetotropis latifolia

Chaetotropis latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf chaetotropis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, which helps in reducing water loss in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
