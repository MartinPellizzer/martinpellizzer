Cinnamomum blandfordii

Cinnamomum blandfordii, commonly known as the slender cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andaman Islands and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their essential oils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
