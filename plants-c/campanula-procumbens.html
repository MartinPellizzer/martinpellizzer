Campanula procumbens

Campanula procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, spreading stems that often lie close to the ground, bearing clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
