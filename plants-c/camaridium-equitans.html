Camaridium equitans

Camaridium equitans, commonly known as the saddleback orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, saddle-shaped flower that is typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces small, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found growing on tree trunks or in the understory of dense forests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
