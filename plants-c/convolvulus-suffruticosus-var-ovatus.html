Convolvulus suffruticosus var. ovatus

Convolvulus suffruticosus var. ovatus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its trailing stems and hairy, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale purple or white, blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
