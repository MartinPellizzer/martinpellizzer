Cinnamomum dulce

Cinnamomum dulce, commonly known as sweet cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves and bark, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to dark blue berries. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.

Cinnamomum dulce is often cultivated for its aromatic qualities and has a long history of use in herbal remedies.
