Cornus brachypoda var. variegata

Cornus brachypoda var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which displays white and green patterns on its leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact size and seasonal color changes make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
