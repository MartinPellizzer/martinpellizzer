Corymbia ficifolia

Corymbia ficifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed apple or the red gum, is a species of flowering tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, football-shaped fruits.

It is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
