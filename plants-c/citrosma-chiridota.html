Citrosma chiridota

Citrosma chiridota, commonly known as the Indian citron or wild citron, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color when mature. The fruit's peel contains essential oils and is used in traditional medicine for its aromatic and therapeutic properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
