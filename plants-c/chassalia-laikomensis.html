Chassalia laikomensis

Chassalia laikomensis, commonly known as the Laikomensi chassalia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small and clustered in a conical shape, often displaying shades of yellow to orange.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
