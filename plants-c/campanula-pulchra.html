Campanula pulchra

Campanula pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula pulchra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
