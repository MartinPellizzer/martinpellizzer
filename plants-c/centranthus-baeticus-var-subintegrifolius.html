Centranthus baeticus var. subintegrifolius

Centranthus baeticus var. subintegrifolius, commonly known as the Baetican flameweed, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features striking red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
