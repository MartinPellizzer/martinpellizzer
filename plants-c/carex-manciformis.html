Carex manciformis

Carex manciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along streambanks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
