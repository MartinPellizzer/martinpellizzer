Capparis cynophallophora var. acutifolia

Capparis cynophallophora var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and the plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
