Canistrum triangulare

Canistrum triangulare, commonly known as the triangular canister, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are usually triangular in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It is often found in dry to semi-deciduous forests and is adapted to thrive in warm, sunny environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
