Centaurea hanryi

Centaurea hanryi, commonly known as Henry's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Centaurea hanryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
