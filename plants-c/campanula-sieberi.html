Campanula sieberi

Campanula sieberi, commonly known as Sieber's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Campanula sieberi is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
