Canavalia gaudichaudii

Canavalia gaudichaudii, commonly known as the "Gaudichaud's bean," is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Canavalia gaudichaudii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
