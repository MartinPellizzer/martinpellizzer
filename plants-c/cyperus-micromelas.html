Cyperus micromelas

Cyperus micromelas, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes and floodplains, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily, often forming dense mats that can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Cyperus micromelas is also known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a common species in both natural and disturbed habitats.
