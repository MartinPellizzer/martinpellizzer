Cestrum diurnum var. odontospermum

Cestrum diurnum var. odontospermum, commonly known as the evening glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the late afternoon and night. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, spherical berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
