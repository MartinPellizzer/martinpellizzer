Canthium brownii

Canthium brownii, commonly known as the African wolfberry, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
