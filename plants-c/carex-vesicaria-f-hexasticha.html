Carex vesicaria f. hexasticha

Carex vesicaria f. hexasticha, commonly known as the six-awned sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and pond margins. Its inflorescence consists of two spikelets, each with six awns, giving it its distinctive name.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
