Calyptocarpus blepharolepis

Calyptocarpus blepharolepis, commonly known as the white-flowered calyptocarpus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that remains attached to the pedicel, a feature that gives it its scientific name.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate floral display.
