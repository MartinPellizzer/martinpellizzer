Cissus crusei

Cissus crusei, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun exposure.

Cissus crusei is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
