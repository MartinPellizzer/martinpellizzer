Campanula alpini var. setulosa

Campanula alpini var. setulosa, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, bristly hairs, which give it its varietal name "setulosa.". The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered with a fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.
