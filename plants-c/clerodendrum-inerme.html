Clerodendrum inerme

Clerodendrum inerme, commonly known as the white flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
