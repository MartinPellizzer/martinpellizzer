Croton lachnocladus

Croton lachnocladus, commonly known as the poisonwood tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage, which can range from green to red, orange, or even purple. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its latex sap is toxic and can cause skin irritation or more severe reactions in humans.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has also been used in traditional medicine.
