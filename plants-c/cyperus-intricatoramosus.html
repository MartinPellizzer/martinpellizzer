Cyperus intricatoramosus

Cyperus intricatoramosus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often inconspicuous. Its roots help stabilize soil and contribute to wetland ecosystems by filtering water.

Cyperus intricatoramosus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
