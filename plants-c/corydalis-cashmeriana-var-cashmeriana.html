Corydalis cashmeriana var. cashmeriana

Corydalis cashmeriana var. cashmeriana, commonly known as the Kashmir corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It features delicate, slender stems that often grow in clusters, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and alternate along the stem, with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
