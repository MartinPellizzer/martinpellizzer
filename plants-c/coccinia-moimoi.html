Coccinia moimoi

Coccinia moimoi, commonly known as the African butterfruit, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that turn yellow when ripe, often with a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and tend to climb using tendrils.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in some regions.
