Conradia humilis

Conradia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf conradia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has simple, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring. It is often found in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Conradia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
