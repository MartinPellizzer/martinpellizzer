Carex morrowii var. expallida

Carex morrowii var. expallida, commonly known as the white-flowered sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces pale to white spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming habit and can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
