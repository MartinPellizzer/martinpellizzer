Calotis hispidula

Calotis hispidula, commonly known as the hispid calotis, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calotis hispidula is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
