Calyptrostigma oblongifolium

Calyptrostigma oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf calyptrostigma, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct basal rosette structure. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stems. Its fruit is a dry, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Calyptrostigma oblongifolium is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks.
