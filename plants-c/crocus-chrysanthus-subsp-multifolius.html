Crocus chrysanthus subsp. multifolius

Crocus chrysanthus subsp.

multifolius, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the iris family. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a cluster of small, petal-like florets. It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
