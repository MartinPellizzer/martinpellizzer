Chomelia recordii

Chomelia recordii, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Malvaceae, which includes other plants such as cotton and hibiscus.
