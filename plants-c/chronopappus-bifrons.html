Chronopappus bifrons

Chronopappus bifrons, commonly known as the two-lobed sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Chronopappus bifrons is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
