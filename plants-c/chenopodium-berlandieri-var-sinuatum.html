Chenopodium berlandieri var. sinuatum

Chenopodium berlandieri var. sinuatum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its serrated, spiny leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas.
