Carex phylloscirpoides

Carex phylloscirpoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, grass-green leaves that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are often borne on slender, upright stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex phylloscirpoides plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
