Cheirostylis bidentata

Cheirostylis bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped leaves and delicate, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a single raceme bearing several small, fragrant blooms.

Cheirostylis bidentata is noted for its unique floral structure, with sepals and petals that have two distinct teeth, giving it its scientific name.
