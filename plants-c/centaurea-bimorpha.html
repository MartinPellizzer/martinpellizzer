Centaurea bimorpha

Centaurea bimorpha, commonly known as the two-faced cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, typically featuring a purple or blue upper petal and a yellow lower petal. The plant grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Centaurea bimorpha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
