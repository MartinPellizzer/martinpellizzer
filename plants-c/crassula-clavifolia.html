Crassula clavifolia

Crassula clavifolia, commonly known as the spikeleaf crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Crassula clavifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and unique foliage.
