Cicer laetum

Cicer laetum, commonly known as the Persian vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as forage for livestock. Cicer laetum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it valuable for soil enrichment and sustainable agriculture.
