Crypsis minuartioides

Crypsis minuartioides, commonly known as the alpine rock-cress, is a small flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. Its leaves are usually small, linear, and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The flowers are white or pale yellow, blooming in early summer and attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.
