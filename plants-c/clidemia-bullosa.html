Clidemia bullosa

Clidemia bullosa, commonly known as the bubble vine, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, round berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Clidemia bullosa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it a potential invasive species in certain areas.
