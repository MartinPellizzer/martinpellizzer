Celtis spinosa

Celtis spinosa, commonly known as the burr oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and distinctive, spiny fruits that hang in clusters. The leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a serrated margin. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Celtis spinosa plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
