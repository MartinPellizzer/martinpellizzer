Caesalpinia dubia

Caesalpinia dubia, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like inflorescences that appear during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in racemes. Caesalpinia dubia produces hard, woody pods that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
