Cajanus indicus var. maculatus

Cajanus indicus var. maculatus, commonly known as the spotted cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate with adequate rainfall.

Its ornamental features make it suitable for both agricultural and landscaping purposes.
