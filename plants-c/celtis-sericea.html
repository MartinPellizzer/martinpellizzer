Celtis sericea

Celtis sericea, commonly known as silky hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible for some wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Celtis sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
