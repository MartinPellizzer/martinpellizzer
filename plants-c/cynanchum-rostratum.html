Cynanchum rostratum

Cynanchum rostratum, commonly known as the rostrate milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its milkweed sap contains toxic compounds, which deter herbivores.

Cynanchum rostratum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including roadsides and woodland edges.
