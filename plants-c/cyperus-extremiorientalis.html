Cyperus extremiorientalis

Cyperus extremiorientalis, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, triangular stems that are often bright yellow or golden in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in water gardens and as a landscape plant.
