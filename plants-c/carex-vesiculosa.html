Carex vesiculosa

Carex vesiculosa, commonly known as the bladder sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
