Commelina auriculata f. albiflora

Commelina auriculata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The f. albiflora form is distinguished by its notably lighter floral coloration compared to the typical species.
