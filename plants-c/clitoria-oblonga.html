Clitoria oblonga

Clitoria oblonga, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple butterfly-shaped flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural food dye. The plant is a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers are also popular in culinary applications, particularly in beverages and desserts.
