Cyperus decompositus

Cyperus decompositus, commonly known as the decomposed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus decompositus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
