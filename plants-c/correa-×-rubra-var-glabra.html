Correa × rubra var. glabra

Correa × rubra var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth correa, is a hybrid plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
