Carex virginiana

Carex virginiana, commonly known as swamp millet, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. It has rhizomes that help it spread and establish dense stands in moist soils.

Carex virginiana plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
