Convolvulus hadramauticus

Convolvulus hadramauticus, commonly known as the Hadramaut bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a violet or purple hue. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.
