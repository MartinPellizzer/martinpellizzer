Campanula celsii subsp. carystea

Campanula celsii subsp.

carystea, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are hairy beneath.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers and hardy nature.
