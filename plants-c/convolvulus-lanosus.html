Convolvulus lanosus

Convolvulus lanosus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, creeping stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus lanosus is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
