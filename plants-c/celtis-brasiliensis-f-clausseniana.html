Celtis brasiliensis f. clausseniana

Celtis brasiliensis f. clausseniana, commonly known as the Brazilian hackberry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round, fleshy fruits that are typically orange to red in color.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, making it a versatile and ornamental plant in horticulture.
