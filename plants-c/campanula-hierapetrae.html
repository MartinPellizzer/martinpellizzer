Campanula hierapetrae

Campanula hierapetrae, commonly known as the Hierapetra bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Campanula hierapetrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
