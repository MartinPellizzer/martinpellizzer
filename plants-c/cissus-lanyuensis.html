Cissus lanyuensis

Cissus lanyuensis, commonly known as the Lanyu devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and ability to climb using tendrils. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and adaptability to various light conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though its ornamental value is also appreciated.

Cissus lanyuensis thrives in humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
