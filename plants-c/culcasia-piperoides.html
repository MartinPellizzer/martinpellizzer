Culcasia piperoides

Culcasia piperoides, commonly known as the arrowhead vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in dense forests.

Its rapid growth makes it both a valuable ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in non-native regions.
