Campium diversifolium

Campium diversifolium, commonly known as the diverse-leaf campion, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape, giving it its common name. Its showy pink or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
