Cyrtopodium sanguineum

Cyrtopodium sanguineum, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features a pseudobulb and produces striking red or pink flowers with a velvety texture. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Cyrtopodium sanguineum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or warm climates.
