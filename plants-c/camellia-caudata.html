Camellia caudata

Camellia caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed camellia, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a contrasting central band. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. Camellia caudata is an evergreen shrub that thrives in shaded, moist environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
