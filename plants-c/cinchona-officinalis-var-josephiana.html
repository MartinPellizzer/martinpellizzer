Cinchona officinalis var. josephiana

Cinchona officinalis var. josephiana, commonly known as the Josephine cinchona, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the production of quinine. Its bark has been historically used to treat malaria due to the alkaloids it contains.

This variant is often cultivated for both ornamental and pharmacological purposes.
