Chimonocalamus gallatlyi

Chimonocalamus gallatlyi, commonly known as the Galway reed, is a species of grass native to the wetlands of New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, thriving in environments with consistent hydration. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of spikelets, which are often adorned with bristles.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
