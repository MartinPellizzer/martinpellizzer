Calandrinia vernicosa

Calandrinia vernicosa, commonly known as the stickyweed or redstem, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, bright red or purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

The plant's unique appearance and adaptability make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and ecological research.
