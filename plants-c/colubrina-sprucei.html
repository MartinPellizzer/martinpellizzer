Colubrina sprucei

Colubrina sprucei, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

Colubrina sprucei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
