Capsicum indicum microcarpon

Capsicum indicum microcarpon, commonly known as the Indian chili or wild chili, is a species of Capsicum native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with hairy stems and oval, pointed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically less than 1 centimeter in diameter. It is characterized by its high capsaicin content, which gives it a spicy flavor.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
