Coriaria thymifolia

Coriaria thymifolia, commonly known as the sweet germander, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The plant has aromatic, needle-like leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
