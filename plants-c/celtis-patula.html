Celtis patula

Celtis patula, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by yellowish berries that mature in the fall. Celtis patula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, particularly birds and insects.
