Cajanus kerstingii

Cajanus kerstingii, commonly known as Kersting's groundnut, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing several edible seeds, which are rich in protein and oil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its nutritional value, it has potential as a crop for food security in arid regions.
