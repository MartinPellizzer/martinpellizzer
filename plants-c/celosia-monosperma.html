Celosia monosperma

Celosia monosperma, commonly known as the flame flower or fire plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by colorful, papery bracts. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.

Celosia monosperma is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
