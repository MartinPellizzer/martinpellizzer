Cynorkis buchwaldiana subsp. nyassana

Cynorkis buchwaldiana subsp.

nyassana, commonly known as the East African orchid, is a species native to the highlands of East Africa. It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces strikingly colorful flowers.

The plant features a short, erect stem with a single, showy inflorescence that bears several flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
