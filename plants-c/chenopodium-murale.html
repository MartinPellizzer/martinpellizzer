Chenopodium murale

Chenopodium murale, commonly known as goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found growing in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed.
