Chenopodium multifidum f. typicum

Chenopodium multifidum f. typicum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate to triangular leaves that are often covered with fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, typically in late summer. Its succulent stems and leaves make it drought-tolerant and adaptable to various soil types.

It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
