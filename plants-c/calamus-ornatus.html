Calamus ornatus

Calamus ornatus, commonly known as the ornate calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm-like plant that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its gracefully arching leaves. The plant features a robust, slender stem with a fibrous outer covering, and its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a slightly wavy edge. Calamus ornatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly for its elegant foliage.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.
