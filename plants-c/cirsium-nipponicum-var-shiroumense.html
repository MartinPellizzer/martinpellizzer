Cirsium nipponicum var. shiroumense

Cirsium nipponicum var. shiroumense, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats. It produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
