Carlina vulgaris subsp. vulgaris

Carlina vulgaris subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features a rosette of basal leaves with hairy surfaces and produces yellow flowers in composite heads. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flower heads are surrounded by bracts.

It is often found in temperate climates and is adapted to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
