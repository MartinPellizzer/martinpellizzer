Clematis henryi var. leptophylla

Clematis henryi var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets that are often hairy beneath. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its climbing habit and floral display.
