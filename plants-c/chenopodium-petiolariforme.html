Chenopodium petiolariforme

Chenopodium petiolariforme, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields. It is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Chenopodium petiolariforme is also known for its edible leaves, which are used in some culinary traditions.
