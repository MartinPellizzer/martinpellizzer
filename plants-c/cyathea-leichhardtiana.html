Cyathea leichhardtiana

Cyathea leichhardtiana, commonly known as the Leichhardt's tree fern, is a large, evergreen tree fern native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, compound fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a distinctive crown of fronds that form a rosette, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea leichhardtiana plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
