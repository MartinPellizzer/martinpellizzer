Conyza rubra

Conyza rubra, commonly known as red roughhopper, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy leaves and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Conyza rubra is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
