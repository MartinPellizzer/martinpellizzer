Centroglossa nunes-limae

Centroglossa nunes-limae, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small, with a distinctive coloration that varies among individuals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
