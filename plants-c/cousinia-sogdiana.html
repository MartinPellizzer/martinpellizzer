Cousinia sogdiana

Cousinia sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian hawk's-beard, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit with upright stems and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cousinia sogdiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
