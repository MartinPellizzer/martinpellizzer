Chloranthus insignis

Chloranthus insignis, commonly known as the golden star, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble stars. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers bloom in early spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.
