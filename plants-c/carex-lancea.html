Carex lancea

Carex lancea, commonly known as slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinct central vein. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually borne on upright flowering shoots. Carex lancea prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
