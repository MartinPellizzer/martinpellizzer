Cynosurus paniculatus

Cynosurus paniculatus, commonly known as the slender wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, greenish flowers that are often inconspicuous. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense stands.

Cynosurus paniculatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
