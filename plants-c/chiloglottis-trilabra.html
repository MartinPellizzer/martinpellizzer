Chiloglottis trilabra

Chiloglottis trilabra, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-lobed labellum, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are usually pale green to brownish in color, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Chiloglottis trilabra is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
