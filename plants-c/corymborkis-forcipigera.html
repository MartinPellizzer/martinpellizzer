Corymborkis forcipigera

Corymborkis forcipigera, commonly known as the beakless orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its striking, elongated flower spikes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in some areas.
