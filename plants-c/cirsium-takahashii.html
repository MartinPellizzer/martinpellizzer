Cirsium takahashii

Cirsium takahashii, commonly known as the Takahashi thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest margins. It produces small, tubular flowers that are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Cirsium takahashii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
