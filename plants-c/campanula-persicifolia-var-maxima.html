Campanula persicifolia var. maxima

Campanula persicifolia var. maxima, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features sturdy, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supported by fibrous roots. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
