Cyclobalanopsis macrocalyx var. litoralis

Cyclobalanopsis macrocalyx var. litoralis, commonly known as the coastal cyclobalanopsis, is a variant of the cyclobalanopsis genus native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing pale yellow or greenish blooms.

The tree produces woody fruits that contain one or two seeds, which are dispersed by water or wind.
