Chenopodium quinoa var. laciniatum

Chenopodium quinoa var. laciniatum, commonly known as wild quinoa, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces small, round seeds that are typically harvested when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its nutritional value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a traditional crop.
