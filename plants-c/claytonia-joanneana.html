Claytonia joanneana

Claytonia joanneana, commonly known as Joanne's springbeauty, is a herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink or white. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves that are oval to spoon-shaped with a hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Claytonia joanneana is a member of the Portulacaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
