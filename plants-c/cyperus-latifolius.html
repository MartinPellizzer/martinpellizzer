Cyperus latifolius

Cyperus latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, often at the base of the plant. It is commonly found in wetland habitats, such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus latifolius is an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
