Chlorophora alba

Chlorophora alba, commonly known as the white chlorophora, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used in reforestation efforts. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces woody fruits containing seeds.

The tree plays an important ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
