Cousinia antonowi

Cousinia antonowi, commonly known as the Antonow's cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation across dry landscapes.

Cousinia antonowi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
