Caesalpinia pringlei

Caesalpinia pringlei, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. Caesalpinia pringlei produces woody pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
