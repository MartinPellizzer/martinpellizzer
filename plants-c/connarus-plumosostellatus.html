Connarus plumosostellatus

Connarus plumosostellatus, commonly known as the feathered star vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble small stars, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in loose, branching clusters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Connarus plumosostellatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and greenhouses due to its unique appearance.
