Catesbaea sphaerocarpa

Catesbaea sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the sweet olive or Brazilian cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree is known for its edible, red, cherry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
