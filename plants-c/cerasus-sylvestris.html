Cerasus sylvestris

Cerasus sylvestris, commonly known as the wild cherry or sour cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, serrated leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, tart red or black fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Cerasus sylvestris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
