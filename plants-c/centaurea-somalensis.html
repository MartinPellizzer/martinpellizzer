Centaurea somalensis

Centaurea somalensis, commonly known as the Somali knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought conditions. Its vibrant blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea somalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
