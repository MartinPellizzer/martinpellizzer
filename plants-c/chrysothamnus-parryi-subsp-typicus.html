Chrysothamnus parryi subsp. typicus

Chrysothamnus parryi subsp.

typicus, commonly known as Parry's goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and silver-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and produces compact, rounded clusters of yellow blooms.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native range.
