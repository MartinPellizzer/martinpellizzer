Camellia parvicuspidata

Camellia parvicuspidata, commonly known as the small-pointed camellia, is a species native to China.

It is a evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red and bloom in winter or early spring. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms.
