Cassia nelsonii

Cassia nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Hawaii.

It is a small shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia nelsonii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
