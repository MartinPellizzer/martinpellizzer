Calceolaria arachnoidea

Calceolaria arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flowers that resemble spider webs, with a hooded lip and long, slender sepals. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Calceolaria arachnoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unusual appearance.
