Clematis fuertesii

Clematis fuertesii, commonly known as the Mexican clematis, is a flowering vine native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of white or pale blue flowers. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Clematis fuertesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
