Capurodendron nodosum

Capurodendron nodosum, commonly known as the knotty wood or nodose wood, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, woody stems that bear small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are often hidden within cupular structures, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Capurodendron nodosum is native to tropical regions of South America and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
