Cassia nictitans var. multipinnata

Cassia nictitans var. multipinnata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with numerous small leaflets. The plant produces long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers, which are highly fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it can grow up to 10 meters in height under favorable conditions.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
