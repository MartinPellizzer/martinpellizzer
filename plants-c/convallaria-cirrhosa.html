Convallaria cirrhosa

Convallaria cirrhosa, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a contrasting green throat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically evergreen, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to produce berries that contain toxic compounds.
