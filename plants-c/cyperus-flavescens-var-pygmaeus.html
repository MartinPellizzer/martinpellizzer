Cyperus flavescens var. pygmaeus

Cyperus flavescens var. pygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf sedgeland sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or flooded soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.
