Cataria vulgaris

Cataria vulgaris, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small purple or white flowers. Its leaves contain high concentrations of nepetalactone, a compound responsible for its stimulating effects on cats.

Cataria vulgaris is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
