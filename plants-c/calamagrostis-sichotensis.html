Calamagrostis sichotensis

Calamagrostis sichotensis, commonly known as the Siberian reed, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern China.

It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces feathery, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat. Its ability to tolerate frost and waterlogged soils makes it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Calamagrostis sichotensis plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local wildlife.
