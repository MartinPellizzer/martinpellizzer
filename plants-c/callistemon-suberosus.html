Callistemon suberosus

Callistemon suberosus, commonly known as the rough bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or pink cylindrical flowers that resemble bottle brushes. The plant has a woody, aromatic trunk and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.
