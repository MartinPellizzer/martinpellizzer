Crotalaria napaulensis

Crotalaria napaulensis, commonly known as the Indian velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces elongated pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.

Its ornamental qualities make it a popular choice for garden cultivation in warm climates.
