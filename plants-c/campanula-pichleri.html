Campanula pichleri

Campanula pichleri, commonly known as the Pichler's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Campanula pichleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blooms.
