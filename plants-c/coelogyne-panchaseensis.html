Coelogyne panchaseensis

Coelogyne panchaseensis, commonly known as the Panchase orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the high altitudes of Nepal.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescence and vibrant, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in cool, humid environments, showcasing its epiphytic nature. Its flowers, which can range from white to yellow and orange, are often adorned with intricate patterns and are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Coelogyne panchaseensis is considered an important species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
