Calophyllum whitfordii

Calophyllum whitfordii, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and aromatic leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest and woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
