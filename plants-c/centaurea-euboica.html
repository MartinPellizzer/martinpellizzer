Centaurea euboica

Centaurea euboica, commonly known as the Euboean knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Centaurea euboica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
