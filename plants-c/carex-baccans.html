Carex baccans

Carex baccans, commonly known as the redstem sedges, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and pond edges. Its flowering stems are often reddish, giving it its common name.

Carex baccans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
