Carex orbicularis var. petunnikowii

Carex orbicularis var. petunnikowii, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with broad, flat leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to yellowish flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
