Cestrum porphyroneuron

Cestrum porphyroneuron, commonly known as the blood-red cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are often bright red or orange, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds. Its berries are initially green and turn red when mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by animals.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
