Cenchrus echinatus var. pennisetoides

Cenchrus echinatus var. pennisetoides, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability makes it a common ornamental and forage grass in various climates.
