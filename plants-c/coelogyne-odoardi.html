Coelogyne odoardi

Coelogyne odoardi, commonly known as the Odoardi's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often displaying shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and help store water, allowing it to survive in seasonal climates.

The orchid's unique floral structure and aromatic properties make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
