Cicerbita spicata var. integrifolia

Cicerbita spicata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf bittercress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that are entire along their margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This species is notable for its peppery taste and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
