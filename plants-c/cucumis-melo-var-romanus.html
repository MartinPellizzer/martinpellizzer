Cucumis melo var. romanus

Cucumis melo var. romanus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon plant characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a smooth, thin rind.

The fruit typically has a pale green to yellowish color and is known for its sweet, aromatic flesh. It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. This variety is well-suited to warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant produces both male and female flowers, with pollination typically occurring through insect activity.
