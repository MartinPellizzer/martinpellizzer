Centaurium moschatum

Centaurium moschatum, commonly known as sweet centaury, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically clustered in umbels, and it produces small, dark brown seeds enclosed in a capsule.

Centaurium moschatum is often found in dry meadows and rocky soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
