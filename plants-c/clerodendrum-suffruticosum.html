Clerodendrum suffruticosum

Clerodendrum suffruticosum, commonly known as the tropical glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive tubular throat, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
