Cornopteris crenulatoserrulata

Cornopteris crenulatoserrulata, commonly known as the serrulate wood fern, is a species of fern characterized by its intricate, finely toothed fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to the eastern United States, particularly in woodland habitats. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with fertile fronds that produce spores on the underside. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance that contributes to its ornamental value in gardens.

Cornopteris crenulatoserrulata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.
