Campanula daralaghezica

Campanula daralaghezica, commonly known as the Daralaghezica bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Carpathians in Romania.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
