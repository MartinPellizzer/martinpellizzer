Chitonia bubalina

Chitonia bubalina, commonly known as the giant sea snail, is a species of sea snail in the family Tegulidae.

It is characterized by its large, spiral-shaped shell that can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters. The shell is typically reddish-brown in color and has a rough, textured surface. The animal itself has a soft, muscular body with a prominent radula used for feeding on algae and other marine plants.

It is found in tropical and subtropical waters, often inhabiting rocky substrates in shallow marine environments.
