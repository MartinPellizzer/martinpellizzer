Cynanchica lutea subsp. lutea

Cynanchica lutea subsp.

lutea, commonly known as the yellow milkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which is characteristic of the Asclepiadaceae family.
