Calamagrostis glomerata

Calamagrostis glomerata, commonly known as bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like clusters of blue-green leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems produce panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are often tinged with purple or blue.

Calamagrostis glomerata is valued in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and ornamental appeal.
