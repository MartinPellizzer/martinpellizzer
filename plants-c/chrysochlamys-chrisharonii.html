Chrysochlamys chrisharonii

Chrysochlamys chrisharonii, commonly known as the golden chrysochlamys, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green surface.
