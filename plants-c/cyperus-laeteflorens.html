Cyperus laeteflorens

Cyperus laeteflorens, commonly known as the happy flowering sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.

Cyperus laeteflorens is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
