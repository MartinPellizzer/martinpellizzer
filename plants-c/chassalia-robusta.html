Chassalia robusta

Chassalia robusta, commonly known as the robust chassalia, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces feathery, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation across open habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a notable ornamental and ecological plant in its native region.
