Cissus quadrangularis var. quadrangularis

Cissus quadrangularis var. quadrangularis, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular (four-sided) stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, round, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, including anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
