Crepis murmannii

Crepis murmannii, commonly known as the Murmann's hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters.

Crepis murmannii is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient component of polar and mountain ecosystems.
