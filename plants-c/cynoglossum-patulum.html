Cynoglossum patulum

Cynoglossum patulum, commonly known as the broad-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture, and produces showy blue to purple flowers in late summer. The flowers are bell-shaped, with five lobes, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
