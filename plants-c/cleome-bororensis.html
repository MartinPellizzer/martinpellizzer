Cleome bororensis

Cleome bororensis, commonly known as the Brazilian spider flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals and prominent stamens.

Cleome bororensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
