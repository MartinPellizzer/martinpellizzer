Calliphysa polygonea

Calliphysa polygonea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces a cluster of bracts beneath its flowers, giving it a coneflower appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Calliphysa polygonea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
