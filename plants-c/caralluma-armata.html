Caralluma armata

Caralluma armata, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its branching, spine-tipped stems that resemble a backbone. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems.

Caralluma armata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
