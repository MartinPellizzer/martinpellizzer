Chrysopteris billardierei

Chrysopteris billardierei, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in heathlands and open forests. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often mistaken for leaves. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
