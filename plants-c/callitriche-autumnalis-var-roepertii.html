Callitriche autumnalis var. roepertii

Callitriche autumnalis var. roepertii, commonly known as the autumn willow herb, is a species of aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams. The plant features small, delicate, floating or submerged leaves and produces tiny, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to various water conditions.
