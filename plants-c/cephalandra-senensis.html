Cephalandra senensis

Cephalandra senensis, commonly known as the silver fern, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, glossy, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Cephalandra senensis is also valued for its air-purifying properties and is relatively easy to care for in indoor settings.
