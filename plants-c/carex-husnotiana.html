Carex husnotiana

Carex husnotiana, commonly known as the Husnot's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or alpine environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Carex husnotiana is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
