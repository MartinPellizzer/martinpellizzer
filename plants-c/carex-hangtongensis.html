Carex hangtongensis

Carex hangtongensis, commonly known as the Hangtong sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to China and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.

The species is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to form clumps, making it suitable for wetland restoration projects.
