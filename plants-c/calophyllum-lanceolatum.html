Calophyllum lanceolatum

Calophyllum lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces woody, aromatic fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a hard shell. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in various cultures.
