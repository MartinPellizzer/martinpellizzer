Callicarpa japonica f. albibaccata

Callicarpa japonica f. albibaccata, commonly known as the white-berried beautyberry, is a compact shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces striking clusters of white to pale pink berries in the fall, which are a distinguishing feature of this variety. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
