Citrus × aurantium subf.

medioglobosa, commonly known as the common mandarin. This subsp.

is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a thin, easy-to-peel rind. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing to a height of 3–5 meters.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is widely cultivated for its sweet, juicy fruits.
