Cirsium laniflorum

Cirsium laniflorum, commonly known as the downy thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny foliage. The plant produces showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in dense, white hairs, giving it a soft, downy appearance.

Cirsium laniflorum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
