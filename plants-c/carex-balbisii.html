Carex balbisii

Carex balbisii, commonly known as Balbis' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a fibrous root system. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often green to brown in color.

Carex balbisii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland gardens.
