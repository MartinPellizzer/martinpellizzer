Convolvulus chryseides

Convolvulus chryseides, commonly known as the golden morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking yellow-orange flowers with five petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and easy cultivation.
