Caccinia macranthera var. glauca

Caccinia macranthera var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous caccinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.
