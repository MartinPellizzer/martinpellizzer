Calliandra anthoniae

Calliandra anthoniae, commonly known as the yellow calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, yellow inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Calliandra anthoniae is also used in some regions for soil stabilization and as a forage crop.
