Centropogon × lucyanus

Centropogon × lucyanus, commonly known as the hybrid lantana, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Centropogon coccineus and Centropogon corymbosus. The plant is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that range in color from red to orange and yellow. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
