Carex heteroneura var. chalciolepis

Carex heteroneura var. chalciolepis, commonly known as the dark-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green, with a distinctive midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to reddish in color.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
