Cyphostemma laza var. parvifolia

Cyphostemma laza var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cyphostemma, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by elliptical, orange-red berries. It thrives in semi-arid to arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
