Coursetia hintonii

Coursetia hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's coursetia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Coursetia hintonii is often found in rocky soils and is considered a rare and endemic species in its range.
