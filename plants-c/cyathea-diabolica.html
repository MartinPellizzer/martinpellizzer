Cyathea diabolica

Cyathea diabolica, commonly known as the devil's cycad, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cyathea diabolica is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and along stream banks.
