Cerastium banaticum var. ilicii

Cerastium banaticum var. ilicii, commonly known as the creeping snowdrop, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, and the stems are often reddish in color.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
