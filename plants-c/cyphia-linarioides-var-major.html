Cyphia linarioides var. major

Cyphia linarioides var. major, commonly known as the giant cyphia, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that attract various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
