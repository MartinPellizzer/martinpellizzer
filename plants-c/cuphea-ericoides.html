Cuphea ericoides

Cuphea ericoides, commonly known as the heather cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, leathery leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

Cuphea ericoides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
