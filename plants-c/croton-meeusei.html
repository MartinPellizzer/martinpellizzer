Croton meeusei

Croton meeusei, commonly known as the Meeuse's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful foliage. The plant features alternate, simple leaves that can vary in shape from ovate to elliptical. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Croton meeusei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant leaf patterns.
