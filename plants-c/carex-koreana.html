Carex koreana

Carex koreana, commonly known as Korean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which is part of the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually brown or green, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
