Crassula rupestris subsp. marnieriana

Crassula rupestris subsp.

marnieriana, commonly known as the Marnier's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
