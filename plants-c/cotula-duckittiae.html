Cotula duckittiae

Cotula duckittiae, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a mat-forming growth habit. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance.

Cotula duckittiae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
