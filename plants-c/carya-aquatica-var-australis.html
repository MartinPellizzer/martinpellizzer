Carya aquatica var. australis

Carya aquatica var. australis, commonly known as the Australian water hickory, is a deciduous tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elongated, oval leaves that are alternate and serrated. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in wetland environments and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
