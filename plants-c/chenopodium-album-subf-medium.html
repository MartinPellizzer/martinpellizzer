Chenopodium album subf. medium

Chenopodium album subf.

medium, commonly known as common lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family. It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense, branched clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It is a common weed found in agricultural fields and disturbed soils worldwide.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a nuisance and a potential forage species.
