Cuphea linarioides

Cuphea linarioides, commonly known as the Indian whorlflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its unique whorled arrangement of leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and blooms.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and ecological landscapes.
