Commelina weimarckiana

Commelina weimarckiana, commonly known as the weimarckian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
