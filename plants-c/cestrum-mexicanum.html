Cestrum mexicanum

Cestrum mexicanum, commonly known as night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, yellow, or red. Its flowers are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths and bats.

The fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.
