Cytisus blockii

Cytisus blockii, commonly known as the Cook Islands broom, is a shrub native to the Cook Islands and nearby Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, pinnate leaves and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some areas outside its native range.
