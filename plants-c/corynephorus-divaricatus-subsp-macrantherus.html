Corynephorus divaricatus subsp. macrantherus

Corynephorus divaricatus subsp.

macrantherus, commonly known as the broad-leaved hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
