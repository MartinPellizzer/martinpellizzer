Copernicia escarzana

Copernicia escarzana, commonly known as the Escarzana wax palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruits that contain a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of natural wax.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
