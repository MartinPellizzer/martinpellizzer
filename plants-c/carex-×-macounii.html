Carex × macounii

Carex × macounii, commonly known as Macoun's sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the genus Carex, which includes over 2,000 species of sedges. This particular species is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex × macounii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
