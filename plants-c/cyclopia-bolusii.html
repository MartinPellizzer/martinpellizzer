Cyclopia bolusii

Cyclopia bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus's eyeberry, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, spherical seed pods that resemble small eyeballs. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its distinctive seed capsules. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Cyclopia bolusii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
