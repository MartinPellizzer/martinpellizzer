Carex vesicaria var. friesii

Carex vesicaria var. friesii, commonly known as the Fries' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
