Carlina corymbosa var. graeca

Carlina corymbosa var. graeca, commonly known as the Greek carline thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that are covered in dense, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-centered composite flower heads that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens across temperate regions.
