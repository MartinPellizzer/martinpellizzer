Celastrus mexicanus

Celastrus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican staffvetch, is a climbing shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas within forests.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
