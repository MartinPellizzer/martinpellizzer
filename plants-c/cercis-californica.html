Cercis californica

Cercis californica, commonly known as the California redbud, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, flat pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and thrives in well-drained soils.

The California redbud is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban conditions.
