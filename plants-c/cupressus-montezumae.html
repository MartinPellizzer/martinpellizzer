Cupressus montezumae

Cupressus montezumae, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its slender, columnar shape and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and its bark is reddish-brown and fissured. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
