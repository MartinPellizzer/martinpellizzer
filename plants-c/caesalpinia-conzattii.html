Caesalpinia conzattii

Caesalpinia conzattii, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red, fluffy flower clusters that appear in the summer months. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Caesalpinia conzattii make it a popular choice for gardens and public landscapes.
