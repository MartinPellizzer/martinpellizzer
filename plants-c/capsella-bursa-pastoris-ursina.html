Capsella bursa-pastoris ursina

Capsella bursa-pastoris ursina, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It features small, white or pinkish flowers and has alternate, hairy leaves that are often notched or lobed. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed with ecological and historical significance.
