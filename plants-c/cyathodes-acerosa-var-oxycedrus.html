Cyathodes acerosa var. oxycedrus

Cyathodes acerosa var. oxycedrus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cyathodes, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, a characteristic feature of the genus Cyathodes. The leaves are narrow and often have a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
