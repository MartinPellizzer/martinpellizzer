Cistus umbellatus var. erectus

Cistus umbellatus var. erectus, commonly known as the upright rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, woolly grey-green foliage. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or purple flowers in the summer, which are borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy, drought-tolerant plant.
