Cotoneaster niger var. daghestanicus

Cotoneaster niger var. daghestanicus, commonly known as the Black Cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black berries that persist into winter, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a glossy surface, and it produces small, white flowers in early spring.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its winter interest and hardy nature.
