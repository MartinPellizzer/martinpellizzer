Cephalanthera longifolia f. conformis

Cephalanthera longifolia f. conformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its elongated, pale green to yellowish flowers that are clustered in a spike, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical form, giving it its specific epithet "conformis.". It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and requires a specific mycorrhizal association for nutrient uptake.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is often protected and requires careful conservation efforts.
