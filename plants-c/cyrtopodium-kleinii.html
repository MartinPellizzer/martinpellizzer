Cyrtopodium kleinii

Cyrtopodium kleinii, commonly known as the Klein's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, with flowers that are often clustered in racemes. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.

Cyrtopodium kleinii is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
