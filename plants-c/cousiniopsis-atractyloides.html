Cousiniopsis atractyloides

Cousiniopsis atractyloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive silver-green coloration.

The showy purple flowers appear in late summer and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
