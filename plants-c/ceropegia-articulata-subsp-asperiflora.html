Ceropegia articulata subsp. asperiflora

Ceropegia articulata subsp.

asperiflora, commonly known as the asperiflora moonvine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, fleshy, leaf-like structures that are often mistaken for leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a unique, intricate structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in hanging baskets.
