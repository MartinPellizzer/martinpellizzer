Crotalaria indigofera

Crotalaria indigofera, commonly known as the Indian helmet or blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Crotalaria indigofera is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
