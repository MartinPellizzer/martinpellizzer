Calyptrochilum orientale

Calyptrochilum orientale, commonly known as the oriental calyptrochilum, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a spurred labellum and showy, often fragrant flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are usually ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant's flowering period varies by region but often occurs during the rainy season, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
