Cattleya mossiae var. fimbriata

Cattleya mossiae var. fimbriata, commonly known as the Fimbriated Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking floral display.

It features large, showy flowers with a velvety texture and a distinctive fimbriated (frayed) edge on the petals. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture from the air.

Its botanical appeal lies in its vibrant colors, which range from deep purples to soft pinks, making it a favorite among orchid enthusiasts.
