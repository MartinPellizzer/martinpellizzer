Cota brachycentros

Cota brachycentros, commonly known as the narrow-centred cota, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive, narrow central disc.

Cota brachycentros is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
