Cordia furcans

Cordia furcans, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its distinctive red bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Cordia furcans is known for its aromatic wood, which has been historically valued for its use in furniture and carving.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
