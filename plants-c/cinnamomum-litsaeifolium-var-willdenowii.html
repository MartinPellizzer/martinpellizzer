Cinnamomum litsaeifolium var. willdenowii

Cinnamomum litsaeifolium var. willdenowii, commonly known as the Willdenow's cinnamonwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and emit a distinct cinnamon-like scent.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
