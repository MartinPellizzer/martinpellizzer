Cassia gymnothyrsa

Cassia gymnothyrsa, commonly known as the naked-stem cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often glabrous stems. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which are typical of the Cassia genus. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cassia gymnothyrsa is often found in dry, open woodlands and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.
