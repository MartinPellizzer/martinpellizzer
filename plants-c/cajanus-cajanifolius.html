Cajanus cajanifolius

Cajanus cajanifolius, commonly known as the Indian clusterbean, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain small, round seeds used in various culinary applications. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop for sustainable agriculture.

Additionally, it is cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
