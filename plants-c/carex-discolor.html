Carex discolor

Carex discolor, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive pale green coloration that gives it its common name.

The inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
