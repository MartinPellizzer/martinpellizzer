Chassalia lanceolata subsp. latifolia

Chassalia lanceolata subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf chassalia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with soft, hairy foliage.

The leaves are lanceolate in shape, with a broad base and a pointed tip, and are often arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in loose panicles, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
