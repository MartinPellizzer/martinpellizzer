Capsicum cerasiforme

Capsicum cerasiforme, commonly known as the red bell pepper.

It is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae. This plant is native to the Americas and is characterized by its large, fleshy, red fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant produces small yellow flowers.

It is cultivated for its edible fruits, which are used in various culinary applications around the world.
