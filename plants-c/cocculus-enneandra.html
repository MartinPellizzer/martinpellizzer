Cocculus enneandra

Cocculus enneandra, commonly known as the Indian devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and palmately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by bright orange or red berries. Its roots and fruits have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The plant's morphology and chemical composition make it an interesting subject for botanical and pharmacological research.
