Convolvulus mucronifer

Convolvulus mucronifer, commonly known as the narrow-pointed bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, funnel-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and extensive root system make it an effective ground cover but also a potential weed in agricultural settings.
