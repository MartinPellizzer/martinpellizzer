Crepidomanes makinoi var. tosae

Crepidomanes makinoi var. tosae, commonly known as the Japanese rock fern, is a variant of the crepidomanes genus native to East Asia.

It is a small, delicate fern characterized by its intricate fronds and spore-bearing structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, preferring moist and well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive, finely divided appearance.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
