Centaurea × paucispina

Centaurea × paucispina, commonly known as the narrowleaf starthistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, narrow leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that resemble thistles. Native to the western United States, it thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that can persist in the soil for several years.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
