Carex hamata

Carex hamata, commonly known as the bristly sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spiky inflorescences. Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along streambanks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
