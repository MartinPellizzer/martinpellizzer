Coelogyne pectinata

Coelogyne pectinata, commonly known as the Orchid of the Gods, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its fleshy, pseudobulb-like stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Coelogyne pectinata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and unique floral display.
