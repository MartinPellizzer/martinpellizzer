Corylus avellana var. nitens

Corylus avellana var. nitens, commonly known as the shining hazel, is a variety of the common hazel native to Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, shiny leaves that are typically broader and more elongated than those of the typical variety. The plant produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
