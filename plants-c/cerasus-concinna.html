Cerasus concinna

Cerasus concinna, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and vibrant pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, sweet-tart red berries that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blossoms and attractive foliage.
