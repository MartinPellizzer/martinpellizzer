Calonemorchis roei

Calonemorchis roei, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually purple or violet in color, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

Calonemorchis roei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
