Cebatha orbiculata

Cebatha orbiculata, commonly known as the orbiculate cebatha, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its orbicular (rounded) leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a short, herbaceous stem and hairy foliage.

It is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
