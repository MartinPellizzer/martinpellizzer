Campanula rigidipila var. sarmentosa

Campanula rigidipila var. sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features upright stems that produce clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to spread via stolons.
