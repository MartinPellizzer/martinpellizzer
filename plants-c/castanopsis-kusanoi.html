Castanopsis kusanoi

Castanopsis kusanoi, commonly known as the Kusano tara or Japanese tara, is a species of evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its hard, durable wood. The plant produces small, cup-shaped fruits that enclose one or two seeds, typical of the genus Castanopsis. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
