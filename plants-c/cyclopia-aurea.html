Cyclopia aurea

Cyclopia aurea, commonly known as the golden cyclops, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical seed heads that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cyclopia aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
