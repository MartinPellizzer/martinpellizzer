Cyperus roylei

Cyperus roylei, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually erect, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus roylei is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
