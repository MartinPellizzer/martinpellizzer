Cleome griffithiana

Cleome griffithiana, commonly known as the Indian spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems and clusters of small, white or pink flowers. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
