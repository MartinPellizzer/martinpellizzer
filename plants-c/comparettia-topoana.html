Comparettia topoana

Comparettia topoana, commonly known as the Mexican violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Comparettia topoana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.
