Carapa parviflora

Carapa parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes other mahogany species. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits containing several seeds.

Carapa parviflora is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
