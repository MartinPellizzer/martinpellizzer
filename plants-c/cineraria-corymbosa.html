Cineraria corymbosa

Cineraria corymbosa, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silvery-green leaves and clusters of small, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Cineraria corymbosa is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
