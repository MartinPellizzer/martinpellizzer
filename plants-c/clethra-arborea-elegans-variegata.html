Clethra arborea elegans-variegata

Clethra arborea elegans-variegata, commonly known as the variegated sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often variegated with yellow and white patterns. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from both its showy flowers and attractive foliage.
