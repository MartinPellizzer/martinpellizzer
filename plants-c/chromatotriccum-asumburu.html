Chromatotriccum asumburu

Chromatotriccum asumburu, commonly known as the Zambian star grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, star-shaped inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

Chromatotriccum asumburu is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils.
