Clinopodium × padureanum

Clinopodium × padureanum, commonly known as the hybrid mint of the Carpathian region, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest undergrowth in its native habitat.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
