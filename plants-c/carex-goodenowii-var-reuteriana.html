Carex goodenowii var. reuteriana

Carex goodenowii var. reuteriana, commonly known as the Reuter's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
