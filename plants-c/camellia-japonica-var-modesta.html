Camellia japonica var. modesta

Camellia japonica var. modesta, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooms.
