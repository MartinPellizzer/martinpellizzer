Cestrum acutifolium var. glabrius

Cestrum acutifolium var. glabrius, commonly known as the smooth-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
