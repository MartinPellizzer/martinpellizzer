Crataegus subflavida

Crataegus subflavida, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish flowers and red berries, which appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins, and it produces thorned branches.

Crataegus subflavida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
