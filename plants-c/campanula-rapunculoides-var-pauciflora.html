Campanula rapunculoides var. pauciflora

Campanula rapunculoides var. pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
