Cousinia khorasanica

Cousinia khorasanica, commonly known as the Khorasan hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust growth habit. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow to orange disk flowers.

Cousinia khorasanica is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
