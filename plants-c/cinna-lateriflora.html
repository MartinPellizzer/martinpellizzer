Cinna lateriflora

Cinna lateriflora, commonly known as the side-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are usually alternate and have a distinctive five-lobed shape. Its showy yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Cinna lateriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
