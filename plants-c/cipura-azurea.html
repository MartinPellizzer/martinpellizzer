Cipura azurea

Cipura azurea, commonly known as the blue cipura, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue flowers, which are often clustered in inflorescences that resemble small umbrellas. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
