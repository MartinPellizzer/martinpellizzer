Cleomella fremontii

Cleomella fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing both male and female flowers on the same inflorescence. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cleomella fremontii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
