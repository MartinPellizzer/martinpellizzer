Cymbopogon caesius var. gracillimus

Cymbopogon caesius var. gracillimus, commonly known as the slender lemon grass, is a variant of the lemon grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in loose panicles, typical of the Poaceae family. It is valued for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.

Due to its ornamental and practical uses, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
