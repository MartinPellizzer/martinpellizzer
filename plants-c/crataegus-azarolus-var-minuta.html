Crataegus azarolus var. minuta

Crataegus azarolus var. minuta, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a small shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and thorny branches. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and a glossy surface.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
