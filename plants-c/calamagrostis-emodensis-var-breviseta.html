Calamagrostis emodensis var. breviseta

Calamagrostis emodensis var. breviseta, commonly known as the short-bristled reed, is a grass species native to the wetlands of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in marshy areas and along waterways. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and spike-like, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
