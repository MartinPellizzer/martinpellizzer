Clematis obvallata f. incisa

Clematis obvallata f. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clematis, is a cultivar of the clematis genus native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
