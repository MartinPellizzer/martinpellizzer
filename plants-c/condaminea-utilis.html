Condaminea utilis

Condaminea utilis, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

Condaminea utilis is also valued for its ability to climb and can be trained on trellises or fences.
