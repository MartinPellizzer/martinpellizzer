Cirsium rechingeri

Cirsium rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger's thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It has a robust root system and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

The flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
