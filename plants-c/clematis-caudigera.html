Clematis caudigera

Clematis caudigera, commonly known as the long-stemmed clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, recurved form. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis caudigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
