Calliandra schottii subsp. rosei

Calliandra schottii subsp.

rosei, commonly known as the rosewood calliandra, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae. It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, pink to red inflorescences.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil stabilization and can be used in ecological restoration projects.
