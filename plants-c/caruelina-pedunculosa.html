Caruelina pedunculosa

Caruelina pedunculosa, commonly known as the slender stem caruelina, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Caruelina pedunculosa plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
