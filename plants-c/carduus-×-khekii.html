Carduus × khekii

Carduus × khekii, commonly known as the Crimean thistle, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Carduus acanthoides and Carduus crispus.

It is native to the Crimean Peninsula and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant features robust, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically purple to blue, with a prominent central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Carduus × khekii is often found in open habitats such as steppes and along roadsides.
