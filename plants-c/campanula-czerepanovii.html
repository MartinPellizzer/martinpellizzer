Campanula czerepanovii

Campanula czerepanovii, commonly known as the Czerepanov's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Campanula czerepanovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
