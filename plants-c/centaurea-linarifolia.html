Centaurea linarifolia

Centaurea linarifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, giving it its common name.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
