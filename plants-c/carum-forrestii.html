Carum forrestii

Carum forrestii, commonly known as the Tibetan parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and dill. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white or yellowish flowers that bloom in umbels. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
