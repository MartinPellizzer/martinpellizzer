Chrysocome

Chrysocome, commonly known as the golden cup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble small cups, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is native to regions of southern Africa. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Chrysocome is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
