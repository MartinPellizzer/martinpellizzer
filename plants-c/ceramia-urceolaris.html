Ceramia urceolaris

Ceramia urceolaris, commonly known as the bottlebrush plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescences, which resemble a bottlebrush due to their dense, cylindrical clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually red or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

Ceramia urceolaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, sunny climates.
