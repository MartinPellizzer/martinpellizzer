Centaurea gadorensis

Centaurea gadorensis, commonly known as the Gadorensis thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial species characterized by its spiny leaves and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
