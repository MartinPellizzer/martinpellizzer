Caryophyllus armerius

Caryophyllus armerius, commonly known as the Armenian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to white. The plant grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, round fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caryophyllus armerius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
