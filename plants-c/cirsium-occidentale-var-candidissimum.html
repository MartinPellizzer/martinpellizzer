Cirsium occidentale var. candidissimum

Cirsium occidentale var. candidissimum, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, woolly, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has deeply lobed, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

This variety is notable for its showy blooms and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
