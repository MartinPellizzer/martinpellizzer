Cerasus claviculata

Cerasus claviculata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine and beverages. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Cerasus claviculata thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
