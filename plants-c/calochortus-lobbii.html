Calochortus lobbii

Calochortus lobbii, commonly known as the desert mariposa lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette that helps conserve moisture.

Calochortus lobbii is a perennial herb that blooms in spring, adding a burst of color to desert landscapes.
