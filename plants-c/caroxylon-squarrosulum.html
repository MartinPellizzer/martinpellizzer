Caroxylon squarrosulum

Caroxylon squarrosulum, commonly known as the squarrosular acacia, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody structure and distinctive, often branched thorns. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a leathery texture that helps it conserve water.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in arid environments for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
