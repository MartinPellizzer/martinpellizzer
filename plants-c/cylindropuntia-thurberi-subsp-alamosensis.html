Cylindropuntia thurberi subsp. alamosensis

Cylindropuntia thurberi subsp.

alamosensis, commonly known as the Alamos prickly pear, is a cactus native to the Sonoran Desert. It features cylindrical, spiny stems that are typically green and covered in white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
