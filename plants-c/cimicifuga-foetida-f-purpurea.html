Cimicifuga foetida f. purpurea

Cimicifuga foetida f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear large, showy, white to pale pink flowers clustered in paniculate inflorescences. The plant has compound, pinnately arranged leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical significance includes its use in traditional medicine and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
