Cynanchica beckiana

Cynanchica beckiana, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed vine, is a climbing plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, crown-like seed pods. It is known for its latex sap, which is milky and can cause skin irritation.

Cynanchica beckiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
