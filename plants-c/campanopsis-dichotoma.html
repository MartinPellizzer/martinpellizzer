Campanopsis dichotoma

Campanopsis dichotoma, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Campanopsis dichotoma prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
