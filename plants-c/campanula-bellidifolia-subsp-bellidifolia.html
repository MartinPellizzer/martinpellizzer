Campanula bellidifolia subsp. bellidifolia

Campanula bellidifolia subsp.

bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
