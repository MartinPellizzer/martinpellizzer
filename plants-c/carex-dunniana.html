Carex dunniana

Carex dunniana, commonly known as the Dunne's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color.

Carex dunniana produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
