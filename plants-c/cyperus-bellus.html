Cyperus bellus

Cyperus bellus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Cyperus bellus typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it both a pioneer species and a potential weed in aquatic environments.
