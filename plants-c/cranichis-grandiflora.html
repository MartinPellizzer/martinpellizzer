Cranichis grandiflora

Cranichis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy orchid flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant has a single, slender leaf that is often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It blooms in late spring to early summer, and its growth is often associated with moist, shaded woodlands.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
