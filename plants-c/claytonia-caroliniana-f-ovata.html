Claytonia caroliniana f. ovata

Claytonia caroliniana f. ovata, commonly known as the ovate woodland violet, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy violet flowers with a distinctive white throat, often blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
