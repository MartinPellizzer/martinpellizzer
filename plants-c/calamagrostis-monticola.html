Calamagrostis monticola

Calamagrostis monticola, commonly known as the mountain reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-gray in color. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across rocky and alpine habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates, making it a hardy species.

Calamagrostis monticola is often found in meadows, slopes, and along stream banks in its native range.
