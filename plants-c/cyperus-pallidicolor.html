Cyperus pallidicolor

Cyperus pallidicolor, commonly known as the pale-colored flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along the edges of ponds or in flooded soils. Its pale green to bluish foliage gives it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus pallidicolor is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
