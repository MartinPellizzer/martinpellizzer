Conophytum quarziticum

Conophytum quarziticum, commonly known as the quartz ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry conditions make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Conophytum quarziticum is also noted for its slow growth and low maintenance requirements.
