Calamagrostis gusindei

Calamagrostis gusindei, commonly known as the Gusinde reed, is a grass species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver or gray-green in color. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are arranged in panicles.

Calamagrostis gusindei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
