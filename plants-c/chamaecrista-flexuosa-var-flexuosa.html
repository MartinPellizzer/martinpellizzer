Chamaecrista flexuosa var. flexuosa

Chamaecrista flexuosa var. flexuosa, commonly known as the flexible crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats on disturbed soils. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it useful for soil stabilization and ecological restoration projects.
