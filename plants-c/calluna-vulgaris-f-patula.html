Calluna vulgaris f. patula

Calluna vulgaris f. patula, commonly known as the purple heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands. Its leaves are needle-like and remain green throughout the year, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is valued in horticulture for its ability to form thick, mat-like ground cover and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
