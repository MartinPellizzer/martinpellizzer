Calanthe secunda

Calanthe secunda, commonly known as the slender calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchid species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe secunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in horticulture for its beauty and resilience.
