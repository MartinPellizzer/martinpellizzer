Cirsium × nevoleanum

Cirsium × nevoleanum, commonly known as the Neapolitan thistle, is a hybrid plant species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. It is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
