Cyperus meyerianus

Cyperus meyerianus, commonly known as Meyer's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus meyerianus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
