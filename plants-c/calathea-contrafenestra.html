Calathea contrafenestra

Calathea contrafenestra, commonly known as the veined calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate foliage with a unique pattern of veining and contrasting coloration. The leaves are typically oval or heart-shaped, with a waxy texture and a deep green base that often features silver or white veins. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity to maintain its vibrant appearance.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
