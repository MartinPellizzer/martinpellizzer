Cercocarpus ravenscragensis

Cercocarpus ravenscragensis, commonly known as the Ravenscrag curlleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically curled at the edges. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and other arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
