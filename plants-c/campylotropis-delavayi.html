Campylotropis delavayi

Campylotropis delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's bottlebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bottle-shaped inflorescences that bear numerous small, pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
