Columnea sanguinolenta

Columnea sanguinolenta, commonly known as the blood-red coca, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
