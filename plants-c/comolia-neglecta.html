Comolia neglecta

Comolia neglecta, commonly known as the small-flowered comolia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale pink flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Comolia neglecta is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
