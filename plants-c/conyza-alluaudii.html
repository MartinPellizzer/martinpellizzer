Conyza alluaudii

Conyza alluaudii, commonly known as the "white daisy" or "false dandelion," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow composite flowers that resemble dandelions, arranged in a single head. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is an annual herb that produces a large number of seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Conyza alluaudii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
