Ceropegia rangeana subsp. maculata

Ceropegia rangeana subsp.

maculata, commonly known as the spotted-string plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa. It features cascading stems with tubular leaves that are often adorned with small, distinctive spots.

The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for hanging baskets and indoor gardening.
