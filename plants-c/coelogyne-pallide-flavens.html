Coelogyne pallide-flavens

Coelogyne pallide-flavens, commonly known as the pale-yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendant, showy flowers that typically feature a pale yellow or orange hue with intricate patterns. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-shaped, and it produces a fragrant bloom during the rainy season.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
