Cirsium oviforme

Cirsium oviforme, commonly known as the ovate thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, ovate-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and the flowers are arranged in a globular head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
