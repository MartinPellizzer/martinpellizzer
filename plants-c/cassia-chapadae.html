Cassia chapadae

Cassia chapadae, commonly known as chapada de mesa cassia, is a shrub native to the Caatinga biome of northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Cassia chapadae thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
