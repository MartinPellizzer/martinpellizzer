Crataegus tracyi

Crataegus tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Tracy's hawthorn is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
