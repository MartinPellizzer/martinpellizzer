Cornus alba f. spaethii

Cornus alba f. spaethii, commonly known as the variegated dogwood, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, glossy, variegated foliage with white and green patterns, which adds visual interest throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, white, bract-like flowers in early summer, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden landscapes for its unique appearance.
