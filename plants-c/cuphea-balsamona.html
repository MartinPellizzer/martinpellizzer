Cuphea balsamona

Cuphea balsamona, commonly known as the flamebeard plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble flames, which attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has erect, branching stems and hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Cuphea balsamona is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
