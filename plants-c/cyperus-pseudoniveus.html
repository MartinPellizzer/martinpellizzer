Cyperus pseudoniveus

Cyperus pseudoniveus, commonly known as the silver flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
