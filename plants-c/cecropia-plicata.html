Cecropia plicata

Cecropia plicata, commonly known as the giant ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, often whitish or grayish bark. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody fruits containing seeds. It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine by some indigenous communities.
