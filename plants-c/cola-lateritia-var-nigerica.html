Cola lateritia var. nigerica

Cola lateritia var. nigerica, commonly known as the Nigerian cola or African cola, is a species of the Cola genus native to West Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it bears distinctive, swollen, seed-containing pods.

The plant is notable for its potential medicinal uses and is sometimes cultivated for its seeds, which are used in traditional remedies.
