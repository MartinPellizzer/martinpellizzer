Cerinthe gymnandra var. quichiotis

Cerinthe gymnandra var. quichiotis, commonly known as the Quichioti Cerinthe, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its historical use in traditional medicine.
