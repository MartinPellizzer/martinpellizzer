Coronopus niloticus subsp. lepidioides

Coronopus niloticus subsp.

lepidioides, commonly known as the Egyptian daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers with a central brownish disc.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
