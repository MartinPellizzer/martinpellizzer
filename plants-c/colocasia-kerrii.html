Colocasia kerrii

Colocasia kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 60 cm in diameter, often with a deep green color and a waxy texture. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which is known for its unique inflorescence structure called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Colocasia kerrii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
