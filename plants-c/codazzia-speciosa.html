Codazzia speciosa

Codazzia speciosa, commonly known as the showy codazzia, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features large, palmately lobed leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or purple, depending on the variety. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Codazzia speciosa is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
