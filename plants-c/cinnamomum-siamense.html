Cinnamomum siamense

Cinnamomum siamense, commonly known as Siamese cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by oval, dark green leaves. Its essential oils contain compounds like cinnamaldehyde, contributing to its distinctive scent.

This species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its economic importance in the spice trade.
