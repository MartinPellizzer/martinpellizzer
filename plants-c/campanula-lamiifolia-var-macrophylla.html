Campanula lamiifolia var. macrophylla

Campanula lamiifolia var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are larger than those of the typical Campanula lamiifolia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
