Citrosma pyricarpa

Citrosma pyricarpa, commonly known as the pineapple fruit tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, spiny leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green fruits that resemble pineapples in appearance. The plant belongs to the Rutaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties. Its fruits are edible, though typically consumed in their immature stage, and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
