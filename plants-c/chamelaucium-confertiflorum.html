Chamelaucium confertiflorum

Chamelaucium confertiflorum, commonly known as the coastal banksia, is a shrub native to the southwestern region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has leathery, narrow leaves that are adapted to withstand coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It produces nectar-rich flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Chamelaucium confertiflorum plays an important ecological role in coastal ecosystems and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
