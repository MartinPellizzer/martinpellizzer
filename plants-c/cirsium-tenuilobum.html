Cirsium tenuilobum

Cirsium tenuilobum, commonly known as slenderleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flower heads are often surrounded by a series of bracts.

Cirsium tenuilobum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun.
