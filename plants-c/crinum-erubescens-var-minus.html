Crinum erubescens var. minus

Crinum erubescens var. minus, commonly known as the red-flowered crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red throat. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
