Caladenia denticulata subsp. rubella

Caladenia denticulata subsp.

rubella, commonly known as the red-streaked spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to southwestern Western Australia. It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring a single, slender stem that bears a single, showy pink or purple flower.

The plant has a basal leaf that is linear and often covered in fine hairs, while the flower has a narrow, hood-like labellum with intricate markings. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands.

The species is noted for its striking coloration and is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
