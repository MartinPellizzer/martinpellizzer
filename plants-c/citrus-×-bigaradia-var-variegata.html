Citrus × bigaradia var. variegata

Citrus × bigaradia var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated kumquat, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its unique foliage and fruit.

It features variegated leaves with white and green patterns, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that change color from green to orange as they ripen. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

While primarily grown for its aesthetic appeal, the fruit is also edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.
