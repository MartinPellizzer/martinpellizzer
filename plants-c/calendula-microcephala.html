Calendula microcephala

Calendula microcephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer months and are known for their medicinal properties.

Calendula microcephala is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and traditional herbal use.
