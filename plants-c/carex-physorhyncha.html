Carex physorhyncha

Carex physorhyncha, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early to mid-summer.

Carex physorhyncha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
