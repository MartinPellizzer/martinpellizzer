Capsella bursa-pastoris turoniensis

Capsella bursa-pastoris turoniensis, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It features small, white or pink flowers and has hairy, alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides, fields, and gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in many temperate regions.
