Clerodendrum variifolium

Clerodendrum variifolium, commonly known as the variable-leaved clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the warm seasons. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Clerodendrum variifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
