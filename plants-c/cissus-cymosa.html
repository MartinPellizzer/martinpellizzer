Cissus cymosa

Cissus cymosa, commonly known as the coral vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, waxy, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red or orange berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly succulent texture.

Cissus cymosa is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to climb trellises or walls.
