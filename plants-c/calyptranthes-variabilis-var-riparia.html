Calyptranthes variabilis var. riparia

Calyptranthes variabilis var. riparia, commonly known as the river calyptranthes, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows near streams and rocky outcrops, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features simple, opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface and a slightly serrated margin. Its small, white to pale pink flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Calyptranthes variabilis var. riparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
