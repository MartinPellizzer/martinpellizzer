Campanula toletana

Campanula toletana, commonly known as the Toletan bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula toletana is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
