Croton congensis

Croton congensis, commonly known as the Congo croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display shades of red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
