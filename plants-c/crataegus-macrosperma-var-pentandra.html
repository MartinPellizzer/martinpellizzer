Crataegus macrosperma var. pentandra

Crataegus macrosperma var. pentandra, commonly known as the five-lobed hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea, and is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

The fruit is an important food source for birds and other wildlife.
