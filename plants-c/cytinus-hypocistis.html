Cytinus hypocistis

Cytinus hypocistis, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has a short, thick stem that often grows from a swollen base, which is a feature shared with other members of its genus. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytinus hypocistis is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as cliffs and slopes.
