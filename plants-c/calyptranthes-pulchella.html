Calyptranthes pulchella

Calyptranthes pulchella, commonly known as the beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine, silvery hairs. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the showy berries.

The berries are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock.
