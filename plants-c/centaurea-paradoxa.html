Centaurea paradoxa

Centaurea paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradox Centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts that surround the flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Centaurea paradoxa is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens due to its vibrant color and unique foliage.
