Chaetoseris lyriformis

Chaetoseris lyriformis, commonly known as the lyreleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are lyre-shaped, with a distinctive lobed structure, and its flowers are yellow, producing achenes that are dispersed by the wind.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
