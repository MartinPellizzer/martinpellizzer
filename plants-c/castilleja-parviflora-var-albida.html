Castilleja parviflora var. albida

Castilleja parviflora var. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered grass vetch.

This plant is a hemiparasitic species native to the western United States, particularly in California and Oregon. It has small, white to pinkish flowers and is often found growing in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with fungi, which help it absorb nutrients from host plants.
