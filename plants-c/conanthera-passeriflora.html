Conanthera passeriflora

Conanthera passeriflora, commonly known as the bird-of-paradise flower, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescences that resemble birds in flight. The plant features palmate leaves and produces flowers with a distinctive structure, including a spathaceous bract and a spurred corona. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
