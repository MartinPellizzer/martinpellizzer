Cardamine subcarnosa

Cardamine subcarnosa, commonly known as narrowleaf water-crowfoot, is a small perennial herb belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and a flowering stem that bears small, white, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Its leaves and flowers are often covered with a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its wetland habitat.
