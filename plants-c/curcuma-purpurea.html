Curcuma purpurea

Curcuma purpurea, commonly known as purple ginger, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant produces spindle-shaped rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice. Its foliage is broad and lance-shaped, with a distinctive purple hue on the undersides.

Curcuma purpurea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its medicinal properties.
