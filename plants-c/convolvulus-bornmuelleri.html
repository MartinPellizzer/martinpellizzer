Convolvulus bornmuelleri

Convolvulus bornmuelleri, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with trailing stems that produce small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of violet or blue. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and blooms throughout the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
