Clematis alpina var. sibirica

Clematis alpina var. sibirica, commonly known as Siberian Clematis, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, allowing it to climb trees and shrubs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and are arranged alternately along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
