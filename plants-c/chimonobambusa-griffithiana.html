Chimonobambusa griffithiana

Chimonobambusa griffithiana, commonly known as the Griffith's bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

This bamboo is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
