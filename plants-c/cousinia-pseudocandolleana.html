Cousinia pseudocandolleana

Cousinia pseudocandolleana, commonly known as the Kashmiri globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. It has hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss, and its roots are deep, allowing it to access groundwater.

Cousinia pseudocandolleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
