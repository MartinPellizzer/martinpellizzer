Chomelia crassifolia

Chomelia crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf chomelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically broad and fleshy. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
