Centaurea rigescens

Centaurea rigescens, commonly known as the rigid centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its slender, erect stems are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers.

Centaurea rigescens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
