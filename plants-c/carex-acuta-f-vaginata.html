Carex acuta f. vaginata

Carex acuta f. vaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves that are typically arranged in three ranks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated, sheathing leaf bases, which give it its specific epithet "vaginata.".
