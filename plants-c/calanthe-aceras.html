Calanthe aceras

Calanthe aceras, commonly known as the white calanthe, is a species of orchid native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Calanthe aceras is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
