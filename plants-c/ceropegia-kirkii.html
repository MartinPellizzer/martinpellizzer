Ceropegia kirkii

Ceropegia kirkii, commonly known as the African violet or the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has succulent leaves that are often fleshy and thick, adapted to store water in its native arid environment. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Ceropegia kirkii is also noted for its ability to produce aerial roots, which help it climb and anchor itself to surfaces.
