Calceolaria lanceolata var. pusilla

Calceolaria lanceolata var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf calceolaria, is a small flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, with a distinctive hood-like structure. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as a garden ornamental due to its unique appearance.

This variety is particularly valued for its compact size and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
