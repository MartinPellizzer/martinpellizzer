Clethra micrantha

Clethra micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the plant family Clethraceae and is characterized by its clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Clethra micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and wildlife.
