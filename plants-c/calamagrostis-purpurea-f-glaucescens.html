Calamagrostis purpurea f. glaucescens

Calamagrostis purpurea f. glaucescens, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-blue foliage and panicles of purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wet meadows or along streambanks. Its glaucous (bluish-gray) leaves give it a distinctive appearance, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

It is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
