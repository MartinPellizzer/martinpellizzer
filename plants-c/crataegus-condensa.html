Crataegus condensa

Crataegus condensa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorned branches and opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
