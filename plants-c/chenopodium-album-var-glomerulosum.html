Chenopodium album var. glomerulosum

Chenopodium album var. glomerulosum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, triangular leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, greenish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
