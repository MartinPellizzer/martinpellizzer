Celastrus filiformis

Celastrus filiformis, commonly known as the slender bindweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a vine that typically grows along the ground or climbing supports, producing small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Celastrus filiformis is often found in moist, woodland areas and is considered a persistent weed in some regions.
