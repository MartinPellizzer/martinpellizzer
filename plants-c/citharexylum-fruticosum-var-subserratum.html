Citharexylum fruticosum var. subserratum

Citharexylum fruticosum var. subserratum, commonly known as the serrated citharexylum, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically serrated along the edges, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes in warm regions.
