Clethra occidentalis

Clethra occidentalis, commonly known as western sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture.

Clethra occidentalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
