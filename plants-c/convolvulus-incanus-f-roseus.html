Convolvulus incanus f. roseus

Convolvulus incanus f. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered hairy bindweed, is a variant of the common bindweed characterized by its pale pink to rose-colored flowers.

It is a flowering plant in the Convolvulaceae family, native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant features slender, twining stems that often climb or sprawl along the ground. Its leaves are typically hairy and heart-shaped, while the flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive floral display.
