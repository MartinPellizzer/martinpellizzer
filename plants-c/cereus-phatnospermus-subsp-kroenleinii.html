Cereus phatnospermus subsp. kroenleinii

Cereus phatnospermus subsp.

kroenleinii, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to South America. It is characterized by its distinctive dark red or purple stems, which are often covered in spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the night, a trait that aligns with its nocturnal blooming habit. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
