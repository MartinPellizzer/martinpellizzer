Cimicifuga dahurica var. candollei

Cimicifuga dahurica var. candollei, commonly known as Chinese actaea, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by dark red berries. Its leaves are compound, with lance-shaped leaflets that are arranged alternately along the stem.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
