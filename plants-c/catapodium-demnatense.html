Catapodium demnatense

Catapodium demnatense, commonly known as the Saharan feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed and barren soils. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is often found in degraded landscapes.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many other grasses adapted to harsh environments.
