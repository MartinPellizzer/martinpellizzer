Clematis brevicaudata var. malacotricha

Clematis brevicaudata var. malacotricha, commonly known as the Malacotricha Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, alternate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

This variety is also noted for its hardiness and ability to climb trellises or other supports.
