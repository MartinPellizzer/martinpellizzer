Calycorectes psidiiflorus var. legrandii

Calycorectes psidiiflorus var. legrandii, commonly known as the Grand's hibiscus, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
