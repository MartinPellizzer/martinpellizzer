Carex leptosaccus

Carex leptosaccus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets, and it produces minute, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex leptosaccus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
