Carex × serravalensis

Carex × serravalensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a hybrid sedge species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include fibrous roots, cylindrical stems, and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex × serravalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
