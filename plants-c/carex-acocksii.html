Carex acocksii

Carex acocksii, commonly known as the Acocks' sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

Carex acocksii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
