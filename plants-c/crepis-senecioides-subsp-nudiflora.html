Crepis senecioides subsp. nudiflora

Crepis senecioides subsp.

nudiflora, commonly known as the nakedflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that bear small, yellowish-green flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its lack of leafy bracts beneath the flowers, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring temperate climates.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
