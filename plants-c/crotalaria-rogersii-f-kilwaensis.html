Crotalaria rogersii f. kilwaensis

Crotalaria rogersii f. kilwaensis, commonly known as the Kilwa crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or coastal soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, elliptical leaves and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in nitrogen fixation and its potential use in traditional medicine.
