Crataegus spiralis

Crataegus spiralis, commonly known as the spiral hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiraling branches that give it a unique, twisted appearance. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Crataegus spiralis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
