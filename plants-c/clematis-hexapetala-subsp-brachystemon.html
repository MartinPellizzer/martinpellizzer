Clematis hexapetala subsp. brachystemon

Clematis hexapetala subsp.

brachystemon, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue.

The flowers are typically five-petaled, though the subspecies brachystemon has shorter, more compact petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
