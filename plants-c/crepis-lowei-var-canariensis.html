Crepis lowei var. canariensis

Crepis lowei var. canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite inflorescences, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in rocky and coastal habitats, often found in sunny, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
