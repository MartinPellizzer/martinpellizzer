Carex tristis var. pendulina

Carex tristis var. pendulina, commonly known as the weeping sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It is characterized by its drooping, grass-like leaves that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and naturalized landscapes.
