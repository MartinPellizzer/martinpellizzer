Cirsium remotifolium subsp. oregonense

Cirsium remotifolium subsp.

oregonense, commonly known as the Oregon thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has deeply lobed, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing it with a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is known for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
