Curcuma micrantha

Curcuma micrantha, commonly known as the lesser turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric species. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has aromatic, yellow rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include strap-like leaves and a compact growth habit.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its medicinal and culinary properties.
