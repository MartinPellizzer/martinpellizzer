Cirsium montenegrinum

Cirsium montenegrinum, commonly known as the Montenegro thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, showy purple or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical cluster.

Cirsium montenegrinum is often found in mountainous areas and is considered a hardy, resilient species.
