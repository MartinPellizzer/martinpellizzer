Cotula pectinata subsp. willcoxii

Cotula pectinata subsp.

willcoxii, commonly known as the Willcox daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic component of arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
