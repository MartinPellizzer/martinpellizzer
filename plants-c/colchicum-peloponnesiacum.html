Colchicum peloponnesiacum

Colchicum peloponnesiacum, commonly known as the Peloponnesian colchic, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking violet-blue flowers in the spring. The plant belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its underground corms, which store nutrients for the growing season. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
