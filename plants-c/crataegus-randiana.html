Crataegus randiana

Crataegus randiana, commonly known as the Red Osier Dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red stems, which are particularly striking in the winter months. The plant produces small white flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Crataegus randiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
