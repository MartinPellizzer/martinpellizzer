Campanula mauritanica

Campanula mauritanica, commonly known as the Algerian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant. It has lance-shaped leaves and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Campanula mauritanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
