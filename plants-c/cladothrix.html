Cladothrix

Cladothrix, commonly known as a type of cyanobacterium, is a genus of filamentous, aquatic bacteria that form colonies in freshwater environments.

These microorganisms are capable of photosynthesis and often produce a variety of pigments, including phycocyanin and chlorophyll. Cladothrix species are notable for their ability to form biofilms on surfaces such as rocks, soil, and even on the surfaces of other organisms. They play a significant role in nutrient cycling and can contribute to the formation of microbial mats in aquatic ecosystems.

Some species of Cladothrix are also capable of nitrogen fixation, enhancing their ecological importance in nutrient-poor environments.
