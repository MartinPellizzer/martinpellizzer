Capparis zippeliana var. novohibernica

Capparis zippeliana var. novohibernica, commonly known as the New Ireland caper, is a flowering plant native to the island of New Ireland in the Bismarck Archipelago.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in coastal and humid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
