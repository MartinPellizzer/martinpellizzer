Camassia quamash subsp. teapeae

Camassia quamash subsp.

teapeae, commonly known as the western camas, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to purple floral spikes that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its bulbs are edible when properly prepared.

This subspecies is often found in the Pacific Northwest and is valued for both its ornamental beauty and historical use by Indigenous peoples.
