Ceropegia venenosa subsp. hesperidum

Ceropegia venenosa subsp.

hesperidum, commonly known as the "Madagascar star" or "string of hearts," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features trailing stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns.

The plant produces small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting specific pollinators like moths. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
