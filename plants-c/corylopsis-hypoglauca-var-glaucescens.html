Corylopsis hypoglauca var. glaucescens

Corylopsis hypoglauca var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous corylopsis, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and early spring blooms.
