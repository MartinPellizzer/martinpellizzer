Cyperus perrieri

Cyperus perrieri, commonly known as the African umbrella plant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and distinctive umbrella-shaped flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along waterways. Its flowering spikes are often borne on long, slender stems, and the species is known for its ornamental value in aquatic landscapes.

Cyperus perrieri is also sometimes cultivated for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and its aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
