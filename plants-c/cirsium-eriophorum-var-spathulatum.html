Cirsium eriophorum var. spathulatum

Cirsium eriophorum var. spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are broad, spiny, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are purple to pink in color.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
