Clusia scariosepala

Clusia scariosepala, commonly known as the scarlet shieldleaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red or orange. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Clusia scariosepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
