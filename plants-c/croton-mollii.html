Croton mollii

Croton mollii, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. Croton mollii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its foliage is popular in horticulture for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
