Cicerbita conferta

Cicerbita conferta, commonly known as the clustered bittercress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas and has small, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly bitter taste. Its flowers are pale yellow and arranged in clusters, contributing to its common name.

The species is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks, where it thrives in damp conditions.
