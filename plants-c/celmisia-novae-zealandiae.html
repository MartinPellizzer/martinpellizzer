Celmisia novae-zealandiae

Celmisia novae-zealandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand celmisia, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy, yellow or brownish flower heads. The plant typically grows in swampy or boggy areas, where it thrives in the moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a tendency to form dense clumps.

Celmisia novae-zealandiae is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its unique botanical features that reflect its adaptation to its wetland habitat.
