Centaurea subcordata

Centaurea subcordata, commonly known as the subcordate centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bristly seeds in its composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Centaurea subcordata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
