Cattleya pumila var. major

Cattleya pumila var. major, commonly known as the dwarf cattleya, is a compact orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, yellow flowers with purple and green markings. This variety is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.

The plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to ensure healthy growth.
