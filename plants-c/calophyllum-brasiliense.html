Calophyllum brasiliense

Calophyllum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian yellowwood, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous ellipsoidal seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in local medicine.
