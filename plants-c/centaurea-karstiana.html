Centaurea karstiana

Centaurea karstiana, commonly known as Karst's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Karst regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and slender, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Centaurea karstiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
