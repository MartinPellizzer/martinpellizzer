Chamaecrista tenella

Chamaecrista tenella, commonly known as the yellow crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Chamaecrista tenella is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for ecological restoration projects.
