Carex kelloggii var. kelloggii

Carex kelloggii var. kelloggii, commonly known as the Kellogg's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
