Cyamopsis dentata

Cyamopsis dentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr medick, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its flowers are small and usually pink or white, and it produces edible seeds that are used in some traditional cuisines.

Cyamopsis dentata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
