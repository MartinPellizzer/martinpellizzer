Carex chalciolepis var. larimerana

Carex chalciolepis var. larimerana, commonly known as the Larimer's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
