Commelina crispata

Commelina crispata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are small, blue or white, with three petals and a prominent three-lobed bract. It produces small, three-angled berries that contain numerous seeds.

Commelina crispata is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
