Calytrix pulchella

Calytrix pulchella, commonly known as the sweet pepper bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.
