Camarea hirsuta

Camarea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy snake plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is a member of the Agavaceae family, which includes other succulent plants.

Camarea hirsuta is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
