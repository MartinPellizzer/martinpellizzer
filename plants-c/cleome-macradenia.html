Cleome macradenia

Cleome macradenia, commonly known as the "Mexican spider flower," is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring simple, alternate leaves and showy, spindly flowers that resemble spiders. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance ornamental plants.

Cleome macradenia is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
