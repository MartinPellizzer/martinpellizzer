Caladenia osmera

Caladenia osmera, commonly known as the pink spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. The plant produces a single, slender stem bearing a single, strikingly pink or purple flower with a distinctive spider-like appearance. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly wasps, due to their specialized floral structure.

Caladenia osmera is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
