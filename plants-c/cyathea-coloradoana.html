Cyathea coloradoana

Cyathea coloradoana, commonly known as the Colorado tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
