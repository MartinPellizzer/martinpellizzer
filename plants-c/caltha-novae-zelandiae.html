Caltha novae-zelandiae

Caltha novae-zelandiae, commonly known as the New Zealand marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from September to November. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wet, boggy environments and is often found in forest margins or along stream banks.

Caltha novae-zelandiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting pollinators.
