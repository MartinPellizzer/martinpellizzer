Centaurea amblensis

Centaurea amblensis, commonly known as the Amblensian thistle, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, bract-covered flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Centaurea amblensis is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
