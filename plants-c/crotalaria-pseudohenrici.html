Crotalaria pseudohenrici

Crotalaria pseudohenrici, commonly known as the false horse bean, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain several seeds. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
