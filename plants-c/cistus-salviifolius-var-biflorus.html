Cistus salviifolius var. biflorus

Cistus salviifolius var. biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, gray-green foliage and produces showy pink or purple flowers in the spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in arid landscapes.
