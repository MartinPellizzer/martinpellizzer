Critoniopsis canaliculata

Critoniopsis canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems for survival in dry conditions.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique appearance and resilience.
