Carduus personata

Carduus personata, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, often branching near the top. The plant produces yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with numerous small florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and often have a fuzzy texture.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
