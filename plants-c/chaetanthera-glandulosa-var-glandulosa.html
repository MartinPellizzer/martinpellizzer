Chaetanthera glandulosa var. glandulosa

Chaetanthera glandulosa var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular chaetanthera, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its unique morphology includes opposite leaves with prominent veins and a compact growth habit.

It is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks, contributing to local biodiversity.
