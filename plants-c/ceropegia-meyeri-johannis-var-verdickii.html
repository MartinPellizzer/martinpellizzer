Ceropegia meyeri-johannis var. verdickii

Ceropegia meyeri-johannis var. verdickii, commonly known as the velvet leaf ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown as a houseplant.
