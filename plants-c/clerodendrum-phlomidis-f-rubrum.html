Clerodendrum phlomidis f. rubrum

Clerodendrum phlomidis f. rubrum, commonly known as the Red-veined Clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often variegated foliage with red veins. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.

The leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
