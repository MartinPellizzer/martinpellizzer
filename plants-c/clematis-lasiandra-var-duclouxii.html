Clematis lasiandra var. duclouxii

Clematis lasiandra var. duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple center. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
