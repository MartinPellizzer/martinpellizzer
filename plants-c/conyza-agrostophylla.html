Conyza agrostophylla

Conyza agrostophylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf horseweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields. It has small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are narrow and often hairy.

Conyza agrostophylla is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in many regions.
