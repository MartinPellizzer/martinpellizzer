Capsella bursa-pastoris praelonga

Capsella bursa-pastoris praelonga, commonly known as the longleaf shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods that resemble a purse, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and often have a hairy texture.

It produces small white or yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.
