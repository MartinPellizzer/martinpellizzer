Camaridium campanulatum

Camaridium campanulatum, commonly known as the bell-shaped camaridium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Camaridium campanulatum is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
