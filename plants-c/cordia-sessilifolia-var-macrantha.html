Cordia sessilifolia var. macrantha

Cordia sessilifolia var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered cottonwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in summer. Its fruits are woody capsules that split open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
