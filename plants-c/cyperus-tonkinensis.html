Cyperus tonkinensis

Cyperus tonkinensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

Cyperus tonkinensis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic ecosystems.
