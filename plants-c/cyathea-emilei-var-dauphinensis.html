Cyathea emilei var. dauphinensis

Cyathea emilei var. dauphinensis, commonly known as the Dauphin's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is typically found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its fronds are bi-directionally arranged, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
