Caesalpinia andamanica

Caesalpinia andamanica, commonly known as the Andaman flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Andaman Islands.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like inflorescences that appear during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Caesalpinia andamanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
