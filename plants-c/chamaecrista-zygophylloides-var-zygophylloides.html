Chamaecrista zygophylloides var. zygophylloides

Chamaecrista zygophylloides var. zygophylloides, commonly known as the yellow flame acacia, is a shrub native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in dense, racemose inflorescences. The plant has a robust root system that helps it survive in dry, sandy soils. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in degraded landscapes.
