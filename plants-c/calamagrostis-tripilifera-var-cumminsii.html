Calamagrostis tripilifera var. cumminsii

Calamagrostis tripilifera var. cumminsii, commonly known as the Cummins' reedgrass, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in wet meadows and along stream banks. Its flowering stalks produce panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
