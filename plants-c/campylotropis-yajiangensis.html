Campylotropis yajiangensis

Campylotropis yajiangensis, commonly known as the Yajiang climbing bottlebrush, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, bottlebrush-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring pinnately compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
