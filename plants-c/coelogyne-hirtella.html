Coelogyne hirtella

Coelogyne hirtella, commonly known as the hairy coelogyne, is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are often covered with fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Coelogyne hirtella is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.
