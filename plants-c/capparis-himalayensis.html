Capparis himalayensis

Capparis himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan caper, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are also used in local cuisines, and it thrives in rocky, arid environments.

Capparis himalayensis is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal properties.
