Celosia hastata

Celosia hastata, commonly known as the flame flower or crested grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, plume-like inflorescences that resemble flames or crests. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with slender, branching stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are usually bright red or orange, providing ornamental value in gardens.

Celosia hastata is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
