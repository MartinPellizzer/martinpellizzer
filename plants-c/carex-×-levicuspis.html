Carex × levicuspis

Carex × levicuspis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and a distinctive flowering structure called a spikelet. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Carex × levicuspis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
