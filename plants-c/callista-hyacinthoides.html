Callista hyacinthoides

Callista hyacinthoides, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the woodlands of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The bluebell is also known for its fragrant blooms and is a symbol of spring in many regions.
