Carduus taygeteus

Carduus taygeteus, commonly known as the Greek thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is often found in rocky or dry soils. Its foliage is covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, having been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and astringent effects.
