Cistus creticus var. corsicus

Cistus creticus var. corsicus, commonly known as the Corsican rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features woody stems and small, aromatic leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized, with uses in traditional remedies.
