Carex venusta var. oblita

Carex venusta var. oblita, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other water-saturated environments, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for wet garden landscapes.
