Cissus papillosa

Cissus papillosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically consumed raw. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
