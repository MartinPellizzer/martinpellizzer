Cordia uruguaya

Cordia uruguaya, commonly known as the Uruguayan cordia, is a small evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its rough, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.
