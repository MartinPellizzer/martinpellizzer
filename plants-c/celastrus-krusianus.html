Celastrus krusianus

Celastrus krusianus, commonly known as the Krusian Bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges, where it can form dense thickets.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in some areas.
