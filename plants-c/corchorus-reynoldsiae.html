Corchorus reynoldsiae

Corchorus reynoldsiae, commonly known as Reynolds' dock, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-yellow, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Reynolds' dock is known for its ability to tolerate wet conditions, making it a common species in riparian zones.
