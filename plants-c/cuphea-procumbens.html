Cuphea procumbens

Cuphea procumbens, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub that features striking, tubular red or purple flowers. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea procumbens is often used in gardens for its vibrant flowers and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
