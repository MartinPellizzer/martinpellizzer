Calliandra tetraphylla

Calliandra tetraphylla, commonly known as the four-leafed calliandra, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with four leaflets, which contribute to its common name. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Calliandra tetraphylla is also used in some regions for fodder and erosion control.
