Casearia cordillerana

Casearia cordillerana, commonly known as the mountain casearia, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
