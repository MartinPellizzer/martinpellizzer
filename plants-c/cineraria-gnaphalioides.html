Cineraria gnaphalioides

Cineraria gnaphalioides, commonly known as the silverleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pale blue, and violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Cineraria gnaphalioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate mild drought conditions.
