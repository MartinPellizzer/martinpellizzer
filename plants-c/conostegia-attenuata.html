Conostegia attenuata

Conostegia attenuata, commonly known as the slender waxleaf.

It is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Conostegia attenuata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and pleasant fragrance.
