Ceropegia sacculata

Ceropegia sacculata, commonly known as the silver leaf ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xerophytic gardens and rockeries.
