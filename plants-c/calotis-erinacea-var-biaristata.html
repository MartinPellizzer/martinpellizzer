Calotis erinacea var. biaristata

Calotis erinacea var. biaristata, commonly known as the hairy calotis, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its hairy leaves help reduce water loss, making it well-suited for drought conditions.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appearance.
