Chasmanthium nitidum

Chasmanthium nitidum, commonly known as the smooth false brome, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are smooth and often have a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of loose, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers that are often overlooked.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
