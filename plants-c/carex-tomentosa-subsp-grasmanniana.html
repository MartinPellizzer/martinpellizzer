Carex tomentosa subsp. grasmanniana

Carex tomentosa subsp.

grasmanniana, commonly known as the Grasman's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and wetland conservation efforts.
