Carex turgidula

Carex turgidula, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three ranks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex turgidula is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
