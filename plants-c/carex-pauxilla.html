Carex pauxilla

Carex pauxilla, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer.

Carex pauxilla plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
