Campanula lanuginosa

Campanula lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, grayish-green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Campanula lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
