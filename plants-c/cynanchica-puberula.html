Cynanchica puberula

Cynanchica puberula, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh conditions. Its hairy stems and leaves provide protection against water loss.

While it is not widely cultivated, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
