Cardiospermum corindum f. ferrugineum

Cardiospermum corindum f. ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty heartleaf.

This plant is a variant of the heartleaf morning glory, characterized by its distinctive rust-colored veins and foliage. It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with prominent reddish markings. The flowers are trumpet-shaped, usually pale blue or violet, and bloom in the morning.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
