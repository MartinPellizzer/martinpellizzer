Conostegia ombrophila

Conostegia ombrophila, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value is due to its dense foliage and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.

Conostegia ombrophila is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
