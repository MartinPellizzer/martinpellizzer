Chionanthus obtusifolius

Chionanthus obtusifolius, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and seasonal blooming habit.
