Carex flacca var. dinarica

Carex flacca var. dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric sedge, is a variant of the common sedge found in the Balkan region.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, with a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters that produce wind-pollinated flowers.
