Carex laxiflora var. divaricata

Carex laxiflora var. divaricata, commonly known as the slender-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized inflorescence structures. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
