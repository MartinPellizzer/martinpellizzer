Cordia miranda

Cordia miranda, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-colored drupe that contains several seeds.

Cordia miranda is valued for its hard, durable wood and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
