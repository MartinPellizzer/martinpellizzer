Crataegus monogyna f. alba-plena

Crataegus monogyna f. alba-plena, commonly known as the white-flowered hawthorn, is a cultivar of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its abundant white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit, which follows the flowers, is a small red berry that matures in late summer.
