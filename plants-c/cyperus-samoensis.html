Cyperus samoensis

Cyperus samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy areas and has narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually small and clustered, with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
