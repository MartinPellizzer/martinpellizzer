Conyza popayanensis

Conyza popayanensis, commonly known as the Popayan weed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed areas and has small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a bluish-gray hue.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
