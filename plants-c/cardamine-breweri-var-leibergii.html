Cardamine breweri var. leibergii

Cardamine breweri var. leibergii, commonly known as Leiberg's watercress, is a delicate herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. This variety typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor similar to other watercress species.

The plant is notable for its narrow, grass-like foliage and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
