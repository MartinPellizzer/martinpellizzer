Carex filicina var. leptocarpa

Carex filicina var. leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender filica carex, is a variant of the filica carex species within the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats.

This species is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
