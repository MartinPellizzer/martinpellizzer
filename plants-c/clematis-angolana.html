Clematis angolana

Clematis angolana, commonly known as the Angola Clematis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Clematis angolana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
