Cirsium nanhutashanense var. hopingense

Cirsium nanhutashanense var. hopingense, commonly known as the Hope's thistle, is a rare variant of the thistle genus native to China.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its botanical features include a robust stem, lobed foliage, and a compact growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
