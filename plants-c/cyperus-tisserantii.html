Cyperus tisserantii

Cyperus tisserantii, commonly known as the silver grass or slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pale or silver in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure is adapted for efficient water absorption and survival in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
