Caucalis orientalis

Caucalis orientalis, commonly known as the oriental caucalis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments. Its small, white or yellowish flowers are arranged in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

The leaves are usually pinnately divided, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
