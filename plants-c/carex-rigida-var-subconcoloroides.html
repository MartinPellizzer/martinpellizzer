Carex rigida var. subconcoloroides

Carex rigida var. subconcoloroides, commonly known as the smooth sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its stiff, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and smooth to the touch. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting wetland biodiversity.
