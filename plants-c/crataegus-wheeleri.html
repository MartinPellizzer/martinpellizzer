Crataegus wheeleri

Crataegus wheeleri, commonly known as Wheeler's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia and South Carolina. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins, and it produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring.

The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.
