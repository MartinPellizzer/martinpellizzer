Cotyledon arborescens

Cotyledon arborescens, commonly known as the tree cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branching, tree-like structure with fleshy, thick leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered in a powdery coating called farina. It produces vibrant yellow flowers in late spring or early summer.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.
