Crotalaria insignis

Crotalaria insignis, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces distinctive yellow, spurred flowers. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and is often found in grassland and disturbed soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is a host plant for certain insect species.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its wide distribution.
