Caryota rumphiana var. australiensis

Caryota rumphiana var. australiensis, commonly known as the Australian fan palm, is a variant of the native palm species found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a striking visual display. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red berries, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
