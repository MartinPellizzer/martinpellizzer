Crassula archeri

Crassula archeri, commonly known as the Camp Fire Plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features compact, upright growth with fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge under stress. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
