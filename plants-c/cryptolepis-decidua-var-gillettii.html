Cryptolepis decidua var. gillettii

Cryptolepis decidua var. gillettii, commonly known as the African snakeroot, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruits. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimalarial properties.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments.
