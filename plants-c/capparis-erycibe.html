Capparis erycibe

Capparis erycibe, commonly known as the red caper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its succulent, thorny stems and vibrant red or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Capparis erycibe is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
