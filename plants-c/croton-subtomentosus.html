Croton subtomentosus

Croton subtomentosus, commonly known as the velvet croton, is a tropical plant characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and a deep green color. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red capsules containing seeds.

Croton subtomentosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
