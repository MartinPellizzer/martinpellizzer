Cistus anthurus

Cistus anthurus, commonly known as the purple rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to violet in color. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cistus anthurus is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
