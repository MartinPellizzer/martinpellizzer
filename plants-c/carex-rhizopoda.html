Carex rhizopoda

Carex rhizopoda, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Carex rhizopoda is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
