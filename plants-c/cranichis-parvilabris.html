Cranichis parvilabris

Cranichis parvilabris, commonly known as the small-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine woods and stream banks. The plant has a slender, leafless stem that bears a single, showy flower with a distinctive, narrow lip. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

The orchid is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native habitat.
