Cornus tatarica

Cornus tatarica, commonly known as Tatarian dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in late summer, providing food for birds and small mammals.

Cornus tatarica is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
