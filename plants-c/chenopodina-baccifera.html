Chenopodina baccifera

Chenopodina baccifera, commonly known as the red gooseberry, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, red berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. The plant has succulent leaves and stems, adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants.

Chenopodina baccifera is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source by local communities.
