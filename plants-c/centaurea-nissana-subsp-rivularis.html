Centaurea nissana subsp. rivularis

Centaurea nissana subsp.

rivularis, commonly known as the river centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads.

The species typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers consist of a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
