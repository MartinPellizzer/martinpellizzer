Calliandra cumingii

Calliandra cumingii, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red, pea-like flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calliandra cumingii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
