Calceolaria valdiviana subsp. valdiviana

Calceolaria valdiviana subsp.

valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian slipper orchid, is a striking member of the Calceolaria genus native to South America. It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

The plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

This species is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
