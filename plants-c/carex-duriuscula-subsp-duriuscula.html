Carex duriuscula subsp. duriuscula

Carex duriuscula subsp.

duriuscula, commonly known as the hardy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly rough texture. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers in the summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
