Corynopuntia bulbispina subsp. basileocephala

Corynopuntia bulbispina subsp.

basileocephala, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to Mexico. It is a spineless cactus with a globular or cylindrical shape, often covered in small, white or pale green tubercles.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
