Coryphantha durangensis subsp. durangensis

Coryphantha durangensis subsp.

durangensis, commonly known as the Durango coryphantha, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in spines and small, rounded areoles.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
