Capparis ambigua

Capparis ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive, fragrant scent. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with five petals and a tubular throat, and it produces small, red or orange berries.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
