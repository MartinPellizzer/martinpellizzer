Celtis willdenowiana

Celtis willdenowiana, commonly known as the white ash or willdenow's hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
