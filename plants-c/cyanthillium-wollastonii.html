Cyanthillium wollastonii

Cyanthillium wollastonii, commonly known as the Wollaston's bluebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
