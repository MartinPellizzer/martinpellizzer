Campylotropis drummondii

Campylotropis drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soils and during periods of drought.

Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.
