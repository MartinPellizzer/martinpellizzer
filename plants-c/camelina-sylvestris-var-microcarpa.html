Camelina sylvestris var. microcarpa

Camelina sylvestris var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf wild camelina, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as disturbed soils and rocky areas. The plant has small, yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, and its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided. It produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.
