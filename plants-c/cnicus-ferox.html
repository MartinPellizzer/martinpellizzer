Cnicus ferox

Cnicus ferox, commonly known as the devil's-bit scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in sunny, dry habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Cnicus ferox is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
