Carex goodenowii f. subramosa

Carex goodenowii f. subramosa, commonly known as the slender bulrush, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. This subspecies is native to regions of North America, particularly in the southeastern United States. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

The plant plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
