Cnidoscolus egregius

Cnidoscolus egregius, commonly known as the giant arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, stinging hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble small, green apples. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
