Chamaecrista pilicarpa

Chamaecrista pilicarpa, commonly known as the silky senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, opposite leaflets and yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant produces elongated, hairy pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Chamaecrista pilicarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
