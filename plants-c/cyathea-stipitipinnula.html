Cyathea stipitipinnula

Cyathea stipitipinnula, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome that supports its growth and helps in nutrient absorption. Its fronds are typically arranged in a pinnate pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea stipitipinnula thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
