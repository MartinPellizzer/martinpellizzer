Cassia bacillaris

Cassia bacillaris, commonly known as the small-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, elongated seed pods that contain numerous tiny, flat seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Cassia bacillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
