Chrysanthemum zawadzkii var. leiophyllum

Chrysanthemum zawadzkii var. leiophyllum, commonly known as the white-rayed chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disc florets. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
