Cattleya × jacintoana

Cattleya × jacintoana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cattleya labiata and Cattleya mosquensis.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its large, fragrant, and showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. It is a sympodial orchid with pseudobulbs and produces numerous racemes of blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated for horticultural purposes.
