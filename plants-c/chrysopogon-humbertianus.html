Chrysopogon humbertianus

Chrysopogon humbertianus, commonly known as Humbert's grass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered with fine hairs.

Humbert's grass is typically found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
