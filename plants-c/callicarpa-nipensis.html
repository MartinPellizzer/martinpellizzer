Callicarpa nipensis

Callicarpa nipensis, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are not only visually appealing but also serve as a food source for birds and other wildlife.
