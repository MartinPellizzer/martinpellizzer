Conchopetalum brachysepalum

Conchopetalum brachysepalum, commonly known as the short-sepal petaled plant, is a member of the Meliaceae family, characterized by its unique floral structure.

It features small, clustered flowers with short, broad sepals that are often mistaken for petals. The plant typically grows in tropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
