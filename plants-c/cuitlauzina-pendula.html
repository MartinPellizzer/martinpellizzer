Cuitlauzina pendula

Cuitlauzina pendula, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features slender, branching stems that often trail or hang from elevated surfaces. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cuitlauzina pendula thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and mountainous regions.
