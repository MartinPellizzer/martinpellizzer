Chrysopogon elongatus var. filipes

Chrysopogon elongatus var. filipes, commonly known as slender false grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicle inflorescences, which are adapted for wind pollination.

This species is valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
