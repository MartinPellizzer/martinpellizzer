Cyperus lanceus var. palaestinensis

Cyperus lanceus var. palaestinensis, commonly known as the Palestine flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ditches, and along waterways. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are often pale green or brown.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
