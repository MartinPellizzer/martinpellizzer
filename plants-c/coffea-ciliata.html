Coffea ciliata

Coffea ciliata, commonly known as the Brazilian coffee tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits called berries, which contain the seeds used for coffee production. The leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a distinct waxy coating.

It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
