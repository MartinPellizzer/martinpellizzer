Capparis dioica

Capparis dioica, commonly known as the common caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, green berries. These berries are often used in culinary applications, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions.
