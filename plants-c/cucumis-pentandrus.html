Cucumis pentandrus

Cucumis pentandrus, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces distinctive, spiky fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with five sepals.

The fruit's unique appearance and flavor make it a notable species within the genus Cucumis.
