Convallaria cordifolia

Convallaria cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf lily of the valley, is a flowering plant native to the forests of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often heart-shaped at the base, giving it its common name. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Convallaria cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
