Cyrtopodium eburneum

Cyrtopodium eburneum, commonly known as the white orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pendulous, white flowers with a single lip, which are fragrant and attract pollinators. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Cyrtopodium eburneum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
