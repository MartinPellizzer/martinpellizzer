Cucumis figarei

Cucumis figarei, commonly known as the fig-leaf gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and elongated, ribbed fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is edible when mature, often used in culinary applications similar to other gourds.
