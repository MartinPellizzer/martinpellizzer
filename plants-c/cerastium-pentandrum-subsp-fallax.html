Cerastium pentandrum subsp. fallax

Cerastium pentandrum subsp.

fallax, commonly known as the autumn mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces small, white flowers in clusters.

The leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
