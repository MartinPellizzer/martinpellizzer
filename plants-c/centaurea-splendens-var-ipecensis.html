Centaurea splendens var. ipecensis

Centaurea splendens var. ipecensis, commonly known as the Ipecacuanha centaury, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its bright red flowers with yellow centers, which bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open areas and has a bushy habit with hairy stems and leaves. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
