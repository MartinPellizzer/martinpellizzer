Calathea concolor

Calathea concolor, commonly known as the variegated calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns of green, white, and silver. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, often featuring a waxy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
