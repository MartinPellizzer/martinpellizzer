Commelina conspicua

Commelina conspicua, commonly known as the showy spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with white markings. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, three-lobed bracts that surround the inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina conspicua is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
