Ceanothus scandens

Ceanothus scandens, commonly known as coastal ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue-green leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
