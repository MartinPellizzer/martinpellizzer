Cirsium undulatum subsp. helleri

Cirsium undulatum subsp.

helleri, commonly known as the Heller's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
