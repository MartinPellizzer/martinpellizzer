Chenopodium rubrum subsp. botryoides

Chenopodium rubrum subsp.

botryoides, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on branched inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This species is also known for its edible leaves, which can be cooked and used in various culinary applications.
