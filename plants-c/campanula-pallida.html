Campanula pallida

Campanula pallida, commonly known as the pale bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender stems that often grow in clusters, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or white, with five petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Campanula pallida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.
