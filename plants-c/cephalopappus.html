Cephalopappus

Cephalopappus, commonly known as the "head feather" or "cephalopappus," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, where the florets are clustered in a dense, spherical head, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are often narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine or as ornamental plants.
