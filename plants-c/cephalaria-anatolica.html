Cephalaria anatolica

Cephalaria anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought resistance.

Cephalaria anatolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
