Carex laeviculmis

Carex laeviculmis, commonly known as the smooth-surfaced sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically smooth and slightly glossy. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown in color, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Carex laeviculmis is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
