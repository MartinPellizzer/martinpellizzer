Cyperus lanceola

Cyperus lanceola, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. Its root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

Cyperus lanceola is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
