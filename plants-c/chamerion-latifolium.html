Chamerion latifolium

Chamerion latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf fireweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in tundra, meadows, and along roadsides.

Chamerion latifolium is also known for its ability to colonize areas after fire or other disturbances.
