Cestrum laevigatum var. pauperculum

Cestrum laevigatum var. pauperculum, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its nocturnal blooms and fragrance.
