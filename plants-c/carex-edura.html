Carex edura

Carex edura, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Carex edura has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
