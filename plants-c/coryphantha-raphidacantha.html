Coryphantha raphidacantha

Coryphantha raphidacantha, commonly known as the sharp-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in spiny areoles, which are clusters of spines and flowers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
