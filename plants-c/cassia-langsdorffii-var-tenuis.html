Cassia langsdorffii var. tenuis

Cassia langsdorffii var. tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small yellow flowers arranged in spikes. The plant is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and is typically found in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The seeds of this variety are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used as a spice or in culinary applications.
