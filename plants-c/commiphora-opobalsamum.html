Commiphora opobalsamum

Commiphora opobalsamum, commonly known as the myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces a fragrant resin, which is harvested from the bark and used in traditional medicine and incense. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

The resin of Commiphora opobalsamum has been historically valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
