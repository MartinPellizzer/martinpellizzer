Condalia lycioides

Condalia lycioides, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
