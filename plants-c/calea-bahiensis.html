Calea bahiensis

Calea bahiensis, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a perennial herb native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety blue or purple flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.

Calea bahiensis is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
