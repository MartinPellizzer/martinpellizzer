Carex helodes

Carex helodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex helodes is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
