Calceolaria palustris

Calceolaria palustris, commonly known as the marsh calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in wet, boggy environments. The plant features showy, pouch-like flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Calceolaria palustris is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in wetland ecosystems.
