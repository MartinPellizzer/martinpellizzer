Cyperus polystachyos var. pallidus

Cyperus polystachyos var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically pale or whitish in appearance. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions where it outcompetes native vegetation.
