Chondrocarpus elongatus

Chondrocarpus elongatus, commonly known as the slender spike sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, often with a slightly rough texture.

The flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and are usually greenish in color.
