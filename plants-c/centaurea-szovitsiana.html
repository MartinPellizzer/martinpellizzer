Centaurea szovitsiana

Centaurea szovitsiana, commonly known as the Szovits' knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features bright yellow flowers with purple-brown bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea szovitsiana is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
