Cephalopentandra ecirrhosa

Cephalopentandra ecirrhosa, commonly known as the slender-leafed evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical fruits containing smooth, dark seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
