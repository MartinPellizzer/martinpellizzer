Cyperus speciosus var. parvus

Cyperus speciosus var. parvus, commonly known as the dwarf sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often brown or reddish in color. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller size compared to the typical Cyperus speciosus.
