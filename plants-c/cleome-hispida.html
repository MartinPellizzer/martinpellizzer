Cleome hispida

Cleome hispida, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, colorful flowers that range from white to pink or purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cleome hispida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
