Cinnamomum maximum

Cinnamomum maximum, commonly known as wild cinnamon or cinnamon tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, while the bark is thick and reddish-brown.

The plant is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.
