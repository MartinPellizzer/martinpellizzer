Cupressus torulosa var. nana

Cupressus torulosa var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf Arizona cypress, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It has a dense, rounded form with scale-like leaves that are a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers followed by cone-like structures that release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a foundation plant.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact size and year-round foliage.
