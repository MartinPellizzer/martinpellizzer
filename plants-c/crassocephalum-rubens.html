Crassocephalum rubens

Crassocephalum rubens, commonly known as the red-headed grass or red-headed veldtgrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often forming dense stands. Its bright red flowers are prominent and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
