Calamintha montenegrina

Calamintha montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegrin calamint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It is often found in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine and have a mild minty aroma.
