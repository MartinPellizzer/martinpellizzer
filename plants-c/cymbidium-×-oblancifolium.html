Cymbidium × oblancifolium

Cymbidium × oblancifolium, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cymbidium goeringii and Cymbidium lowianum.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant is native to China and thrives in temperate climates with cool winters and warm, humid summers. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
