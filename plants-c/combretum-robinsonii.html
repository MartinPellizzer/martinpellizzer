Combretum robinsonii

Combretum robinsonii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.

The bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stress.
