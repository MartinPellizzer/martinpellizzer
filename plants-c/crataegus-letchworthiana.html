Crataegus letchworthiana

Crataegus letchworthiana, commonly known as Letchworth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
