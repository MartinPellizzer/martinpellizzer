Clusia brittonii

Clusia brittonii, commonly known as the yellow poinsettia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are surrounded by vibrant red bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Clusia brittonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and festive appearance during the holiday season.
