Carex maritima var. erectiuscula

Carex maritima var. erectiuscula, commonly known as salt marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions.

It typically grows in saline or brackish wetlands, thriving in muddy or sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ability to tolerate saltwater makes it a key component of salt marsh ecosystems.
