Cyperus andongensis

Cyperus andongensis, commonly known as the Andong sedge, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Cyperus andongensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
