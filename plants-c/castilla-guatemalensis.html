Castilla guatemalensis

Castilla guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan castilla, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry forests and along riverbanks.
