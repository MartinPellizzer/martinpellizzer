Casearia sylvestris subvar. campestris

Casearia sylvestris subvar. campestris, commonly known as the yellow casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and fruit.
