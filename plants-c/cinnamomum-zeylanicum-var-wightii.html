Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. wightii

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. wightii, commonly known as Ceylon cinnamon, is a variant of the cinnamon tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is harvested and used as a spice. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. Its bark is thin and has a distinct, sweet aroma, making it highly valued in culinary and medicinal applications.

The variety is known for its high quality and is often considered superior to other cinnamon types.
