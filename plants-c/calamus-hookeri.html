Calamus hookeri

Calamus hookeri, commonly known as the hooker's canes, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and arrow-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus hookeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in various cultural practices.
