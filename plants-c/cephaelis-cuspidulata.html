Cephaelis cuspidulata

Cephaelis cuspidulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved celandine, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for woodland gardens and naturalized settings.
