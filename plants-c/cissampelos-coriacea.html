Cissampelos coriacea

Cissampelos coriacea, commonly known as the rough leaf moon vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its thick, corrugated stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
