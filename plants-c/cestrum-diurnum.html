Cestrum diurnum

Cestrum diurnum, commonly known as the day-blooming coffee plant, is a flowering shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that open in the daytime and close at night. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but should be consumed with caution. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slight pubescence.

Cestrum diurnum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its pleasant scent.
