Coris monspeliensis

Coris monspeliensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy blue to violet flowers in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

Coris monspeliensis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
