Crataegus vesca

Crataegus vesca, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. Crataegus vesca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its cardiovascular benefits.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
