Carex aspratilis

Carex aspratilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three vertical rows. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Carex aspratilis is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
