Crotalaria deserticola

Crotalaria deserticola, commonly known as the desert crownflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmate leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
