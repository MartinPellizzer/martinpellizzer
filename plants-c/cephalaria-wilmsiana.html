Cephalaria wilmsiana

Cephalaria wilmsiana, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with dark green, lance-shaped leaves and yellow to orange composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Cephalaria wilmsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
