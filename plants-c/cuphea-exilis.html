Cuphea exilis

Cuphea exilis, commonly known as the slender cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant has opposite, hairy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea exilis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
