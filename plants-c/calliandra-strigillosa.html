Calliandra strigillosa

Calliandra strigillosa, commonly known as the hairy calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that consist of numerous small, bright red or pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, hairy leaflets, giving it a textured appearance. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and attractive foliage.

Calliandra strigillosa is also valued in some areas for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
