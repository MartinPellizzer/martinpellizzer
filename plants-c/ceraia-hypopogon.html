Ceraia hypopogon

Ceraia hypopogon, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features velvety leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ceraia hypopogon is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
