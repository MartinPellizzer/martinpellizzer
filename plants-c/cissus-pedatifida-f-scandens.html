Cissus pedatifida f. scandens

Cissus pedatifida f. scandens, commonly known as the climbing cissus or silver dollar plant, is a vigorous trailing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its rapid growth makes it ideal for vertical gardening or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
