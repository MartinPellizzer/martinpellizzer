Coreocarpus arizonicus var. filiformis

Coreocarpus arizonicus var. filiformis, commonly known as the Arizona coreocarpus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its fruit is a follicle that opens to release seeds, a feature common in the Fabaceae family.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
