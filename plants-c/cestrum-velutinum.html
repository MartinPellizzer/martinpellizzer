Cestrum velutinum

Cestrum velutinum, commonly known as the velvet leaf trumpet flower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves that are velvety to the touch. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Cestrum velutinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
