Convolvulus oxyphyllus subsp. cateniflorus

Convolvulus oxyphyllus subsp.

cateniflorus, commonly known as the cateniflorous bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils.

The plant features slender, trailing stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, making it both ornamental and potentially invasive.
