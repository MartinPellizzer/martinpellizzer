Cerasus × kashioensis

Cerasus × kashioensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or kashio cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is valued for its ornamental value, particularly its showy pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and features alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its fruit, a small, dark purple drupe, is edible but not commonly cultivated for consumption.

The species is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
