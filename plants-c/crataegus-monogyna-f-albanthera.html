Crataegus monogyna f. albanthera

Crataegus monogyna f. albanthera, commonly known as the Albanian hawthorn, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to the Balkans.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. The plant has thorned branches and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This form is noted for its dense growth habit and is sometimes used in hedges or as a specimen plant.
