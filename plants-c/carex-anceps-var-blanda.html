Carex anceps var. blanda

Carex anceps var. blanda, commonly known as the soft bulblet sedge, is a perennial plant native to moist, temperate regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in wet or damp soil and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
