Conospermum taxifolium

Conospermum taxifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Conospermum taxifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
