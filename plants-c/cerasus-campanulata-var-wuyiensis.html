Cerasus campanulata var. wuyiensis

Cerasus campanulata var. wuyiensis, commonly known as the Wuyi cherry, is a variant of the bell cherry native to China.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
