Carex maritima var. setina

Carex maritima var. setina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal and wetland areas.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in saline or brackish environments, making it well adapted to coastal regions. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of salt marsh ecosystems.
