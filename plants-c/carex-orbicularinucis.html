Carex orbicularinucis

Carex orbicularinucis, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex orbicularinucis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
