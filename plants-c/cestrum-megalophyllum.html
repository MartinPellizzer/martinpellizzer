Cestrum megalophyllum

Cestrum megalophyllum, commonly known as the giant bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshade plants, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 15 feet, with opposite, simple, ovate leaves. The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.
