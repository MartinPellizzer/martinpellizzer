Crepis glauca

Crepis glauca, commonly known as the blue hawksbeard, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Crepis glauca is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
