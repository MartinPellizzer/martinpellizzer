Cucurbita citrullus var. flava

Cucurbita citrullus var. flava, commonly known as the yellow watermelon, is a variant of the watermelon species characterized by its vibrant yellow rind and sweet, juicy flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. The plant typically grows as a sprawling vine with large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit is usually harvested when the rind has hardened and the flesh is fully mature.

This variety is valued for its distinct color and flavor, making it a popular choice in both culinary and ornamental settings.
