Chaptalia tomentosa

Chaptalia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a thick layer of fine hairs, which help to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Chaptalia tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
