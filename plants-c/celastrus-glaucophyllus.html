Celastrus glaucophyllus

Celastrus glaucophyllus, commonly known as the silver-leafed staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries. The plant belongs to the family Celastraceae and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, allowing it to climb trees and other structures.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, making it an important plant to avoid in certain environments.
