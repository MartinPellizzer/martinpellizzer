Chrysothamnus oreophilus var. artus

Chrysothamnus oreophilus var. artus, commonly known as the Sierra goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to dry conditions.

Its leaves are often covered in a fine layer of wax, which helps reduce water loss.
