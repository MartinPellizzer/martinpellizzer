Coutoubea spicata var. densiflora

Coutoubea spicata var. densiflora, commonly known as the denseflowered coutoubea, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically glossy and evergreen, providing ornamental value throughout the year. This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and ecological studies.
