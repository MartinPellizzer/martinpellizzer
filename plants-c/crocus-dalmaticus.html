Crocus dalmaticus

Crocus dalmaticus, commonly known as the Dalmatian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, including parts of southern Europe and North Africa. This plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, often fragrant, scent. The bulbs of Crocus dalmaticus are typically used in horticulture for their ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
