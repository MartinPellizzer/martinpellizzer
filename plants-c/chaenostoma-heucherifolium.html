Chaenostoma heucherifolium

Chaenostoma heucherifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf chaenostoma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features palmately divided, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland gardens and stream banks.

Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
