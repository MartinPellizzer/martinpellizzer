Calamagrostis canescens subsp. vilnensis

Calamagrostis canescens subsp.

vilnensis, commonly known as Vilnius reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to eastern Europe. It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant features slender, silver-haired stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces inflorescences of spikelets that are often loosely arranged in a panicle shape.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
