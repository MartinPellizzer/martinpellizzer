Calliandra longipedicellata

Calliandra longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-pedicellate calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy, pink or red inflorescences. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its showy flowers.

Calliandra longipedicellata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
