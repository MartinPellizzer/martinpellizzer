Calanthe discolor var. discolor

Calanthe discolor var. discolor, commonly known as the white-flowered calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and sepals, which contrast with its greenish-yellow lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a lush, evergreen appearance.

The orchid blooms in late spring to early summer, producing delicate flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and moths.
