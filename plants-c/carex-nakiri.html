Carex nakiri

Carex nakiri, commonly known as the Nakiri sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex nakiri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
