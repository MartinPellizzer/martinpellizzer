Cousinia eriophora

Cousinia eriophora, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and has a low, mat-forming growth habit. Its leaves are usually small, grayish-green, and covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Cousinia eriophora produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
