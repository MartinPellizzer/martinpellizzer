Cereus venezuelensis

Cereus venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to Venezuela.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, slender stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming at night. Its spines are relatively short and sparse, and it is adapted to arid environments.

Cereus venezuelensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
