Caralluma europaea var. confusa

Caralluma europaea var. confusa, commonly known as the European devil's backbone, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features spiny, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

It is often found in rocky or limestone areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
