Cyathula prostrata var. pedicellata

Cyathula prostrata var. pedicellata, commonly known as the creeping cyathula, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a prostrate stem that spreads along the ground, often with short, erect branches. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and are usually arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are borne on short pedicels.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
