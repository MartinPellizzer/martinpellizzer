Cuphea llavea

Cuphea llavea, commonly known as the wax plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that resemble wax or candle wax, hence its common name. The plant produces small, colorful bracts that surround the actual flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Cuphea llavea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
