Ceropegia stapeliiformis

Ceropegia stapeliiformis, commonly known as the African violet or the stapelioid ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, leaf-like stems that are often mistaken for leaves, and it produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-white. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
