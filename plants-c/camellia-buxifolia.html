Camellia buxifolia

Camellia buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that resembles the leaves of boxwood, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Camellia buxifolia is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand colder temperatures compared to other camellia species.
