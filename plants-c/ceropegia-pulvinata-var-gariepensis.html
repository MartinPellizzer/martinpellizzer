Ceropegia pulvinata var. gariepensis

Ceropegia pulvinata var. gariepensis, commonly known as the Gariep Ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green. The leaves are often rounded and may have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. This plant is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
