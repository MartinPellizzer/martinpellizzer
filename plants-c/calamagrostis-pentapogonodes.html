Calamagrostis pentapogonodes

Calamagrostis pentapogonodes, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and distinctive, drooping panicles that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. It has a clumping growth habit and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes and wetland restoration projects.
