Carapichea araguariensis

Carapichea araguariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
