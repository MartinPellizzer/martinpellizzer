Corymbia × chillagoensis

Corymbia × chillagoensis, commonly known as the chillago cherry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between *Corymbia citrinella* and *Eucalyptus camaldulensis*.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, often peeling bark that displays a range of colors from grey to red. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy, with a distinctive bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small and pinkish-white, appearing in clustered inflorescences, and it produces edible, sweet-tart berries that are popular in local cuisine.

This plant is native to the coastal regions of southern Australia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
