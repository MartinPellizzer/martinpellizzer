Catharanthus roseus var. roseus

Catharanthus roseus var. roseus, commonly known as the Madagascar periwinkle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub habit and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant is known for its glossy, dark green leaves and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is also notable for containing alkaloids such as vincristine and vinblastine, which have medicinal applications.

This plant is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and in horticultural settings.
