Crataegus × lobata

Crataegus × lobata, commonly known as the Pacific crabapple, is a hybrid plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters. The plant features thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, white or pink flowers in spring. Its fruit is a red or yellow berry that matures in the fall, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

The Pacific crabapple is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
