Cardamine macrophylla var. albiflora

Cardamine macrophylla var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered water buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It features a basal rosette of glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
