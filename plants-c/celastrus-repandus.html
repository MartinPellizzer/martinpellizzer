Celastrus repandus

Celastrus repandus, commonly known as the spreading staff-vine, is a woody climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. Celastrus repandus thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems it inhabits.
