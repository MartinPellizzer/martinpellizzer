Crataegus cuthbertii

Crataegus cuthbertii, commonly known as Cuthbert's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other hawthorn species.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
