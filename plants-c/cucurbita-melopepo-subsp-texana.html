Cucurbita melopepo subsp. texana

Cucurbita melopepo subsp.

texana, commonly known as the Texas prickly gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This plant is characterized by its spiny, trailing vines and large, heart-shaped leaves. The fruit is typically elongated, with a rough, warty surface and a thick, fibrous rind.

It produces yellow-orange flowers and is often cultivated for its edible fruits, though it is more commonly found in wild populations.
