Campanula pumila

Campanula pumila, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy, gray-green leaves. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or mountainous regions.

Campanula pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
