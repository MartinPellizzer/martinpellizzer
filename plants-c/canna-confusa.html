Canna confusa

Canna confusa, commonly known as the confused cannas, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, strap-like leaves and striking, spadix-shaped inflorescences that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is a member of the Cannaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Canna confusa is a perennial herb that prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
