Caesalpinia glauca var. glandulosissima

Caesalpinia glauca var. glandulosissima, commonly known as the glossy senna, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
