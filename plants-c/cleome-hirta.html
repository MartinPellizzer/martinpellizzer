Cleome hirta

Cleome hirta, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers that resemble spider legs. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Cleome hirta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique floral display and drought tolerance.
