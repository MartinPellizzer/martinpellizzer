Chrysopsis mariana var. floridana

Chrysopsis mariana var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils, often found in open habitats such as roadsides and pine flatwoods. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
