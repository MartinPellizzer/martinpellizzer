Cistus × purpureus f. holorhodos

Cistus × purpureus f. holorhodos, commonly known as the purple rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

The plant is well adapted to coastal and mountainous environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
