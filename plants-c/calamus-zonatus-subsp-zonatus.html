Calamus zonatus subsp. zonatus

Calamus zonatus subsp.

zonatus, commonly known as the zoned calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Indonesia and the Philippines.

The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinct zonation patterns. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spadices, typical of the Araceae family.

This species is often found in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
