Cissus cucurbitina

Cissus cucurbitina, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a trailing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus cucurbitina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail along structures.
