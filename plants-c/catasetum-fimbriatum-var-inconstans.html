Catasetum fimbriatum var. inconstans

Catasetum fimbriatum var. inconstans, commonly known as the variable catasetum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful inflorescences that can range from yellow to red or orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are known for their intricate structure, featuring specialized pollination mechanisms that attract specific insect pollinators.

This variety is valued by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental beauty and unique botanical features.
