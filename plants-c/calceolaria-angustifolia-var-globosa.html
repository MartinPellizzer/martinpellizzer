Calceolaria angustifolia var. globosa

Calceolaria angustifolia var. globosa, commonly known as the globular calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has slender, hairy stems and produces small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.

The flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated primarily by insects.
