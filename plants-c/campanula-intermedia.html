Campanula intermedia

Campanula intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Campanula intermedia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
