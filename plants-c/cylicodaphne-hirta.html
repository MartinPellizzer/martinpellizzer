Cylicodaphne hirta

Cylicodaphne hirta, commonly known as the hairy sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its stems are thick and segmented, often covered in a waxy, protective layer.

Cylicodaphne hirta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
