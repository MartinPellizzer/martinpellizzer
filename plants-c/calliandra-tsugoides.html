Calliandra tsugoides

Calliandra tsugoides, commonly known as the Japanese calliandra, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
