Caragana microphylla var. buriatica

Caragana microphylla var. buriatica, commonly known as the Buriat shrub or Buriat caragana, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is well adapted to harsh climates, thriving in poor, sandy soils and tolerating extreme temperature fluctuations. Its wood is hard and durable, making it useful for construction and fuel.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.
