Celastrus napaulensis

Celastrus napaulensis, commonly known as the Indian bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a notable species in ecological studies.
