Carex × ishimaensis

Carex × ishimaensis, commonly known as the Ishima sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves and compact, clustered flower heads. Native to Japan, this species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
