Capparis ovalifolia

Capparis ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous areas. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, spiny leaves, and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Capparis ovalifolia is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and unique appearance.
