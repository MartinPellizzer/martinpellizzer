Chionachne javanica

Chionachne javanica, commonly known as the Javan silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grassy areas. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

Chionachne javanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
