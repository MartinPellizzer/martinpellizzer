Campanula alpini var. asperula

Campanula alpini var. asperula, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in its high-altitude environment. This species thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
