Calamagrostis koibalensis

Calamagrostis koibalensis, commonly known as the Koibalensis reed, is a grass species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or riparian habitats, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. It produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Calamagrostis koibalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
