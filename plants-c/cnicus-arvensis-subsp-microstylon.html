Cnicus arvensis subsp. microstylon

Cnicus arvensis subsp.

microstylon, commonly known as the lesser sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically features prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, hairy foliage.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are usually linear to oblanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
