Carum fortunati

Carum fortunati, commonly known as lucky caraway, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, caraway-like scent.

Carum fortunati is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
