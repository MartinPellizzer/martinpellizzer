Campanula sarmatica var. gracilis

Campanula sarmatica var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
