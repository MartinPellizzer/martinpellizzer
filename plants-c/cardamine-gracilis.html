Cardamine gracilis

Cardamine gracilis, commonly known as slender water cress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It typically grows in shaded, wet environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a peppery aroma, and its small, white flowers are arranged in clusters. It is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.

Cardamine gracilis is often used in culinary applications due to its mild, spicy flavor.
