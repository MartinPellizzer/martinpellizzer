Cistus rorismarinifolius

Cistus rorismarinifolius, commonly known as the coastal cistus, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, coastal conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy environments.

Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
