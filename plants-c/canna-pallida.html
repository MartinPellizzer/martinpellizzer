Canna pallida

Canna pallida, commonly known as the white canna, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, typically in shades of white, yellow, or orange. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna pallida is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
