Campomanesia langsdorffii

Campomanesia langsdorffii, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or pitanga-do-cerrado, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright orange, star-shaped fruits that are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry forests and savannas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
