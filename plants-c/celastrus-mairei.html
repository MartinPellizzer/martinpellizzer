Celastrus mairei

Celastrus mairei, commonly known as the Maire's bindfruit, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Celastrus mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
