Chaerophyllum aureum f. hybridum

Chaerophyllum aureum f. hybridum, commonly known as the golden cow parsley.

It is a hybrid form of the golden groundsel, characterized by its bright yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that bears numerous small, clustered flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
