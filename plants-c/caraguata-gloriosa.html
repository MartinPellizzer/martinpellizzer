Caraguata gloriosa

Caraguata gloriosa, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
