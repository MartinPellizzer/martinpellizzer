Cyperus sparsiflorus

Cyperus sparsiflorus, commonly known as the sparseflower flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in early spring, and it produces small, three-angled seeds.

Cyperus sparsiflorus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
