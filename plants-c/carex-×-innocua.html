Carex × innocua

Carex × innocua, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex × innocua plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
