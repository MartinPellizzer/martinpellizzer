Crepinia cylindrorhyncha

Crepinia cylindrorhyncha, commonly known as the slender-stemmed rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in southern Africa.

It features tall, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically borne on slender, branching inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Crepinia cylindrorhyncha is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
