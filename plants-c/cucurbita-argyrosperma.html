Cucurbita argyrosperma

Cucurbita argyrosperma, commonly known as the silver-seed gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The fruit is typically oval or elongated, with a smooth, pale green skin that turns yellow when mature. The seeds are small, white, and covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving the plant its common name.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
