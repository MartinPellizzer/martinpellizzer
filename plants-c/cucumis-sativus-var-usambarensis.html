Cucumis sativus var. usambarensis

Cucumis sativus var. usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambar cucumber, is a wild variety native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its small, elongated fruits with a thin, pale green skin. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are broad and lobed, while the flowers are yellow and solitary.

This variety is valued for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving cucumber resilience and yield.
