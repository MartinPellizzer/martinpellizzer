Crotalaria imbricata

Crotalaria imbricata, commonly known as the African blue pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its palmate leaves and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that are typically covered in a dense, hairy coating. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
