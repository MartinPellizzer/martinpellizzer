Crataegus oxyacantha var. paulii

Crataegus oxyacantha var. paulii, commonly known as the Paul's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported cardiovascular benefits.
