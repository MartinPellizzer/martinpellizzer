Calamagrostis calamagrostis var. rufescens

Calamagrostis calamagrostis var. rufescens, commonly known as the redstem bulrush, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge along the edges. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
