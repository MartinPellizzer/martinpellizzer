Cymbidium guttatum

Cymbidium guttatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy texture, while the flowers typically have a distinctive spotted or striped pattern on the petals. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
