Cremanthodium spathulifolium

Cremanthodium spathulifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved cremanthodium, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and spoon-shaped, giving it its specific epithet "spathulifolium.". It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This species is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 3,000 meters.
