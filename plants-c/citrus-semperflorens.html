Citrus semperflorens

Citrus semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming lemon.

This citrus species is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom continuously throughout the year. The plant produces small, sour fruits that are often used for culinary and medicinal purposes. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its glossy, dark green leaves and persistent flowering, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
