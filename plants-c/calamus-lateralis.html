Calamus lateralis

Calamus lateralis, commonly known as the lateral calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Calamus lateralis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its rhizomes are also utilized in the production of certain natural products.
