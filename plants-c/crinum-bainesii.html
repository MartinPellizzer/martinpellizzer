Crinum bainesii

Crinum bainesii, commonly known as the Baines' crinum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters at the top of the stem. Crinum bainesii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
