Cyperus crassivaginatus

Cyperus crassivaginatus, commonly known as the broad sheath flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swampy areas and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus crassivaginatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
