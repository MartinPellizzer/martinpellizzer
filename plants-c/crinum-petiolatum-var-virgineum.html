Crinum petiolatum var. virgineum

Crinum petiolatum var. virgineum, commonly known as the Virgin's Crinum, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system, allowing it to spread and form dense clumps. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
