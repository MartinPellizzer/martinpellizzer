Cissus conradsii

Cissus conradsii, commonly known as the climbing fig or silver vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus conradsii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to climb trellises or walls.
