Chloris incompleta

Chloris incompleta, commonly known as the incomplete grass, is a small perennial grass native to Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 cm in length. Its flowering heads are small and spike-like, producing numerous tiny seeds.

It is a hardy species that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
