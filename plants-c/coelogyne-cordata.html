Coelogyne cordata

Coelogyne cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers, which can be white, yellow, or pink, are often adorned with intricate patterns and a pleasant fragrance.

Coelogyne cordata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
