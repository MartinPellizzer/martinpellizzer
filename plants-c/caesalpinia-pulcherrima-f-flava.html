Caesalpinia pulcherrima f. flava

Caesalpinia pulcherrima f. flava, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage.

The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, feather-like leaflets. The plant produces bright red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a dramatic visual impact. It is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, sunny climates.

This ornamental species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
