Chenopodium pallidum

Chenopodium pallidum, commonly known as pigweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium pallidum is known for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
