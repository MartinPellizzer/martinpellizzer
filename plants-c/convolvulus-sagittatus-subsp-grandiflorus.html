Convolvulus sagittatus subsp. grandiflorus

Convolvulus sagittatus subsp.

grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered arrowhead morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit.

The flowers are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
