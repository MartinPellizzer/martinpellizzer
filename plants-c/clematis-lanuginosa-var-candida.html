Clematis lanuginosa var. candida

Clematis lanuginosa var. candida, commonly known as the white-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features delicate, white, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems that climb by means of small tendrils. Its leaves are compound, with three to five oval leaflets, and are covered in a fine, silky pubescence.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
