Crocus versicolor var. morleon

Crocus versicolor var. morleon, commonly known as the Morleon crocus, is a variant of the common crocus characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

This plant belongs to the Iridaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cup-shaped blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
