Campanopsis dicentrifolia

Campanopsis dicentrifolia, commonly known as the two-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanopsis dicentrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
