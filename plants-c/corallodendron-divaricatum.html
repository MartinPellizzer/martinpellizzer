Corallodendron divaricatum

Corallodendron divaricatum, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-like inflorescences, which are dense clusters of small, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
