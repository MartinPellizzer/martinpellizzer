Crotalaria xanthoclada var. stolzii

Crotalaria xanthoclada var. stolzii, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties, allowing the plant to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub or vine, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

The species is native to parts of Africa and is cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
