Cassia quinquangulata var. majus-tomentosa

Cassia quinquangulata var. majus-tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces yellow flowers in raceme clusters, which are typical of the legume family. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
