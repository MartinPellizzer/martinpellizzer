Cyrtanthus speciosus

Cyrtanthus speciosus, commonly known as the showy cyrtanthus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of vibrant, often pink or white, bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Cyrtanthus speciosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its early blooming period.
