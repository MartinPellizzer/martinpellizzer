Carex × parentii

Carex × parentii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces triangular, three-angled fruits.

Carex × parentii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
