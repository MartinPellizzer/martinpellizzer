Cardamine pratensis var. glabra

Cardamine pratensis var. glabra, commonly known as meadow cress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, grassy areas and along stream banks. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the upper leaves are more elongated. Its small white or pink flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions across Europe and Asia.
