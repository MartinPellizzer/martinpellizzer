Centratherum reticulatum

Centratherum reticulatum, commonly known as the nettleleaf centatherum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
