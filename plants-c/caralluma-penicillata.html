Caralluma penicillata

Caralluma penicillata, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments by storing water in its thick stems.
