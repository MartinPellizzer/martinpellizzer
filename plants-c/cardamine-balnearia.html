Cardamine balnearia

Cardamine balnearia, commonly known as the spring celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy flowers and often toxic sap. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and it produces small yellow flowers in early spring.

The species is often found in calcareous soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
