Campanula glomerata var. foliosa

Campanula glomerata var. foliosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base, with some variation in leaf size and shape depending on the growing conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its attractive flowers and foliage.
