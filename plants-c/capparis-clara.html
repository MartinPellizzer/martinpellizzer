Capparis clara

Capparis clara, commonly known as the white caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has woody stems and produces small, edible buds that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly succulent texture.

Capparis clara thrives in coastal and tropical regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
