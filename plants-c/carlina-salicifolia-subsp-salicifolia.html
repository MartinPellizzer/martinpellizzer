Carlina salicifolia subsp. salicifolia

Carlina salicifolia subsp.

salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved cudweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features lanceolate, serrated leaves that resemble those of willow trees, hence its common name. It produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets. Carlina salicifolia subsp.

salicifolia typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and rocky soils, often in temperate climates.
