Cassuvium parvifolium

Cassuvium parvifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a delicate perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a unique and elegant appearance. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it ideal for woodland gardens. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually hidden among the foliage, and it produces fluffy, white seed heads in the autumn.

Cassuvium parvifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
