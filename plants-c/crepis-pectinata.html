Crepis pectinata

Crepis pectinata, commonly known as the pectinate hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, and the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Crepis pectinata is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
