Crotalaria distans subsp. mediocris

Crotalaria distans subsp.

mediocris, commonly known as the narrowleaf crownflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil types, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
