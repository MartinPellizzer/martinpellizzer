Crassocephalum ducis-aprutii

Crassocephalum ducis-aprutii, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that resemble those of the common dandelion. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for various climates.

Its ability to produce a large number of seeds makes it a common weed in disturbed areas.
