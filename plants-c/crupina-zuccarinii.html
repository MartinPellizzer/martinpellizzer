Crupina zuccarinii

Crupina zuccarinii, commonly known as the Italian crupina, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Crupina zuccarinii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in coastal areas due to its salt tolerance.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
