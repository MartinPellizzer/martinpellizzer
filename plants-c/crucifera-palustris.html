Crucifera palustris

Crucifera palustris, commonly known as the marsh cabbage, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which is characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and can vary in shape depending on the growing conditions.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and the plant plays a role in supporting local insect populations.
