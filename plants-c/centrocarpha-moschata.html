Centrocarpha moschata

Centrocarpha moschata, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting fragrance and appearance.
