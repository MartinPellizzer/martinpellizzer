Ceanothus dillenianus

Ceanothus dillenianus, commonly known as the California lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of small, bright blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment. Its leaves are typically gray-green and have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and often reddish-brown.

Ceanothus dillenianus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
