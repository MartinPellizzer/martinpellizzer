Cinnamomum pickellii

Cinnamomum pickellii, commonly known as Pickell's cinnamon, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
