Calliandra alternans

Calliandra alternans, commonly known as the coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, bipinnate leaves that are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Calliandra alternans is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
