Cereus schmollii

Cereus schmollii, commonly known as the Schmolli's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white flowers at night, which are typically fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a deep root system to store water.

Cereus schmollii is adapted to extreme desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
