Claytonia perfoliata

Claytonia perfoliata, commonly known as the spring beauty, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
