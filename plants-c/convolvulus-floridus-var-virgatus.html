Convolvulus floridus var. virgatus

Convolvulus floridus var. virgatus, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a climbing vine with tendrils that allow it to attach to various surfaces. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
