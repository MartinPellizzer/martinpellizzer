Cynara baetica

Cynara baetica, commonly known as the Spanish artichoke, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and purple bracts that enclose the edible flower buds. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are edible and often used in Mediterranean cuisine, while the flower buds are harvested before they fully open.

Cynara baetica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.
