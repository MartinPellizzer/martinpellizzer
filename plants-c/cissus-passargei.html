Cissus passargei

Cissus passargei, commonly known as the African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often silver-green leaves that are opposite and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus passargei is also valued for its ability to climb and trail, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellises.
