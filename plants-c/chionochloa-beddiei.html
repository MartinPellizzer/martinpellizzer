Chionochloa beddiei

Chionochloa beddiei, commonly known as the Antarctic hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold, high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and silver-gray foliage, which helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils and is adapted to extreme climates with low temperatures and strong winds.

Chionochloa beddiei plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine and subantarctic ecosystems.
