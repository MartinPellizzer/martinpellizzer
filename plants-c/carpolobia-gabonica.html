Carpolobia gabonica

Carpolobia gabonica, commonly known as the African horned cucumber, is a climbing plant native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and gourds. The plant produces large, warty, horn-shaped fruits that are green when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and tend to climb using tendrils.

The fruit is edible when young but becomes bitter and fibrous as it matures.
