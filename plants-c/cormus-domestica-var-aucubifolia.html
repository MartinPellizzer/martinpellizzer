Cormus domestica var. aucubifolia

Cormus domestica var. aucubifolia, commonly known as the aucubifolia variety of the common cormus, is a cultivar characterized by its unique foliage.

This plant exhibits broad, palmate leaves that resemble those of the aucuba plant, giving it its common name. It is typically evergreen, with a compact, bushy growth habit. The leaves are usually dark green with a glossy surface, and the plant is often grown for its ornamental value in shaded gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.
