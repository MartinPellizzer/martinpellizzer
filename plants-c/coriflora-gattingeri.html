Coriflora gattingeri

Coriflora gattingeri, commonly known as the Gattenger's coral flower, is a unique and rare plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that resemble delicate, branching structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in clusters along its stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
