Carex hispida f. soleirolii

Carex hispida f. soleirolii, commonly known as the Soleirolii sedge, is a compact, mat-forming perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens or wetland areas.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
