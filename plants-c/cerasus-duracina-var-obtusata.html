Cerasus duracina var. obtusata

Cerasus duracina var. obtusata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its delicate pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated edge, and it prefers well-drained soil in a sunny location.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
