Citrus × nobilis subf. succosa

Citrus × nobilis subf.

succosa, commonly known as the pummelo, is a citrus plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruits that are typically less acidic than other citrus varieties.

The plant has a sprawling growth habit with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications. The subsp.

succosa is distinguished by its elongated, succulent fruit structure and is considered a close relative of the common grapefruit.
