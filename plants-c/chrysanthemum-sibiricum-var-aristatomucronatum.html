Chrysanthemum sibiricum var. aristatomucronatum

Chrysanthemum sibiricum var. aristatomucronatum, commonly known as the Siberian chicory, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers in the autumn, which consist of ray florets surrounding a central disk. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storage and survival in harsh conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both garden use and traditional medicinal purposes.
