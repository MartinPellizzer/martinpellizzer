Castanea vesca var. tristis

Castanea vesca var. tristis, commonly known as the common horse chestnut.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing one or more chestnuts, which are edible after the spines are removed.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
