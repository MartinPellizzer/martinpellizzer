Calanthe ecalcarata

Calanthe ecalcarata, commonly known as the rock calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette around the base.

Calanthe ecalcarata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
