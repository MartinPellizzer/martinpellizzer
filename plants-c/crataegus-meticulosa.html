Crataegus meticulosa

Crataegus meticulosa, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
