Carex × helvola

Carex × helvola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. This plant is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Carex × helvola typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and has a clumping growth habit.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
