Cirsium × csepeliense

Cirsium × csepeliense, commonly known as the Hungarian thistle, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that resemble those of other thistle species. Native to Central Europe, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant's morphology includes a robust stem with rough, hairy surfaces and a central flower cluster composed of both ray and disc florets.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
