Crucianella patula var. squarrosa

Crucianella patula var. squarrosa, commonly known as the hairy sea bean, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is a small shrub characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and coastal environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize dunes.
