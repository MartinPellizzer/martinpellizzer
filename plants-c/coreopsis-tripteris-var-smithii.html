Coreopsis tripteris var. smithii

Coreopsis tripteris var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's golden tickseed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers with a dark center, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and is drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
