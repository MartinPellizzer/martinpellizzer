Crataegus oxyacantha var. kyrtostyla

Crataegus oxyacantha var. kyrtostyla, commonly known as the knotted hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn species native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, knotted or twisted branches, which give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and hedgerows.

Due to its hardy nature and aesthetic features, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
