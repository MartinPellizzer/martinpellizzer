Carex alpigena

Carex alpigena, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, often found in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in high-altitude environments with limited nutrients.

Carex alpigena plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine wildlife.
