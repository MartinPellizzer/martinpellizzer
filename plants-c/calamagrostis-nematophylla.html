Calamagrostis nematophylla

Calamagrostis nematophylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poorly drained soils and its tolerance to periodic flooding.

Calamagrostis nematophylla plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
