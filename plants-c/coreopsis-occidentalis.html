Coreopsis occidentalis

Coreopsis occidentalis, commonly known as the western coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flower heads that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are composed of ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular florets.

Coreopsis occidentalis is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
