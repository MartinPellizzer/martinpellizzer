Coptis minamitaniana

Coptis minamitaniana, commonly known as the Minamitanian goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are palmately compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces small, inconspicuous fruits.

Coptis minamitaniana is valued for its medicinal properties, containing alkaloids similar to those found in other members of the genus.
