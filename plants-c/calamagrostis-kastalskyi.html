Calamagrostis kastalskyi

Calamagrostis kastalskyi, commonly known as the Kastalsky reed, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Calamagrostis kastalskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging mountainous habitats.
