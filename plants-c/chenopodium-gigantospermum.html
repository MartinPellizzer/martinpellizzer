Chenopodium gigantospermum

Chenopodium gigantospermum, commonly known as giant goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are encased in a hard, three-angled capsule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and desert margins.

Due to its resilience, it is considered a pioneer species in arid ecosystems.
