Crepis occidentalis var. gracilis

Crepis occidentalis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender western cock's-comb, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its slender stems and pale yellow to white flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The species is often found in open habitats such as meadows and alpine tundras.
