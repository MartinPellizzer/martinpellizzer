Carex annulata

Carex annulata, commonly known as the ring sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Carex annulata thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in temperate regions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
