Cocos butyrosa

Cocos butyrosa, commonly known as the butternut or butyrospermum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into fruits containing a single, oily seed. The seed is valued for its high oil content, which is used in cosmetics and as a source of nourishment.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
