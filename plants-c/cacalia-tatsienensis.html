Cacalia tatsienensis

Cacalia tatsienensis, commonly known as the Tatsienensis bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and has slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia tatsienensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
