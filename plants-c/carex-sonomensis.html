Carex sonomensis

Carex sonomensis, commonly known as Sonoma sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

Carex sonomensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
