Carex pseudocyperus var. fascicularis

Carex pseudocyperus var. fascicularis, commonly known as the fasciated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a cylindrical cluster, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The varietal form 'fascicularis' is distinguished by its fasciated, or striped, leaf patterns, making it a visually striking species.
