Campanula sparsa subsp. sphaerothrix

Campanula sparsa subsp.

sphaerothrix, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color.

The plant has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy and adapted to cold, rocky environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and alpine tundras.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
