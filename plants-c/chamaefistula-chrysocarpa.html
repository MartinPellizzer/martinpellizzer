Chamaefistula chrysocarpa

Chamaefistula chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden-fruited boxwood, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
