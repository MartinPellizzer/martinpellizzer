Cincinalis tarapacana

Cincinalis tarapacana, commonly known as the Andean lily, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in cool, dry climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cincinalis tarapacana is also valued for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
