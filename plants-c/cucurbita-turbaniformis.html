Cucurbita turbaniformis

Cucurbita turbaniformis, commonly known as the turban squash, is a species native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and gourds. The plant produces large, elongated fruits with a distinctive turban-like shape and a rough, green to yellowish skin. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it is monoecious, meaning it has both male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is edible when mature, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
