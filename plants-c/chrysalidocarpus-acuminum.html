Chrysalidocarpus acuminum

Chrysalidocarpus acuminum, commonly known as the slender palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed enclosed in a fibrous pulp.

This palm is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in humid, warm climates.
