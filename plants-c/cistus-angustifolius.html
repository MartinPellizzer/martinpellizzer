Cistus angustifolius

Cistus angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features grey-green, narrow, and leathery leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cistus angustifolius is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
