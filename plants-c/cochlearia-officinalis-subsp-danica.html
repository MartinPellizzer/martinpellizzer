Cochlearia officinalis subsp. danica

Cochlearia officinalis subsp.

danica, commonly known as Danish cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal and maritime habitats, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, wind-exposed environments.
