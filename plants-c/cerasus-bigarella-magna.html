Cerasus bigarella magna

Cerasus bigarella magna, commonly known as the giant cherry plum, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the emergence of leaves. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically small to medium in size, with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blossoms.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
