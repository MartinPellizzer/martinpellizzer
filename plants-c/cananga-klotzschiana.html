Cananga klotzschiana

Cananga klotzschiana, commonly known as the white orchid or white frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, waxy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in a fan-like arrangement. The plant produces long, narrow leaves and has a woody stem that can grow into a small tree or shrub. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic blooms.

The flowers are used in traditional ceremonies and are also valued for their perfume.
