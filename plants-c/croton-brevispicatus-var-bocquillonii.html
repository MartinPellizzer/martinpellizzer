Croton brevispicatus var. bocquillonii

Croton brevispicatus var. bocquillonii, commonly known as the Bocquillon's croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
