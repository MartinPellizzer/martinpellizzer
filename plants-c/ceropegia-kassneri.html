Ceropegia kassneri

Ceropegia kassneri, commonly known as the Cape Vincas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in hanging baskets.
