Chamaedrys cinerascens

Chamaedrys cinerascens, commonly known as the silver cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and have a woolly texture, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in mild, coastal climates.
