Clematis pseudootophora

Clematis pseudootophora, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscaped areas.
