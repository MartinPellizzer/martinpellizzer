Carum montanum

Carum montanum, commonly known as mountain caraway, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers and has finely divided, feathery leaves. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their aromatic properties.

Carum montanum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and rocky habitats.
