Cucumis melo subsp. spontaneum

Cucumis melo subsp.

spontaneum, commonly known as wild melon, is a wild relative of the common melon. It is native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Asia.

This subspecies is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. Its leaves are typically large, lobed, and have a rough texture.

It produces small, green fruits that are often less succulent compared to cultivated melons.
