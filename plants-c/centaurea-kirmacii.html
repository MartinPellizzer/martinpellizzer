Centaurea kirmacii

Centaurea kirmacii, commonly known as Kirmac's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces seed heads that are often used in traditional medicine.

Centaurea kirmacii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
