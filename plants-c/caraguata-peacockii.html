Caraguata peacockii

Caraguata peacockii, commonly known as the peacock tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its woody stem is often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

The botanical features of Caraguata peacockii make it a unique and valuable species within its ecosystem.
