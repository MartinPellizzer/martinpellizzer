Ceropegia arachnifera

Ceropegia arachnifera, commonly known as the spider plant or moon vine, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It features thin, wiry stems with small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hair-like coating. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white, with a unique, intricate structure. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to climb, it is often used as a trailing or hanging plant.
