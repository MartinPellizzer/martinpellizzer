Crataegus tripartita

Crataegus tripartita, commonly known as the three-part hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
