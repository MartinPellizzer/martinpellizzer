Cistus brevifolius

Cistus brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and flowers, are used in perfumes and traditional medicine.

Cistus brevifolius plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
