Carduus inclinatus

Carduus inclinatus, commonly known as the leaning thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. The plant's leaves are covered in fine hairs, and its stem is typically erect with a reddish hue.

It serves as a host for certain insect species and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
