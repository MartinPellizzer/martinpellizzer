Cyathea aureonitens

Cyathea aureonitens, commonly known as the golden-stippled tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds that are often adorned with golden or yellowish streaks. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forested areas. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

Cyathea aureonitens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
