Campanula lavandulifolia

Campanula lavandulifolia, commonly known as the lavender bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant features aromatic, gray-green leaves that resemble those of lavender, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula lavandulifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
