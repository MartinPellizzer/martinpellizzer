Calliprora ixioides var. lugens

Calliprora ixioides var. lugens, commonly known as the Mexican mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the high elevations of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
