Centaurea angelescui

Centaurea angelescui, commonly known as the Angelescu's cornflower, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by petal-like bracts.

Centaurea angelescui is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
