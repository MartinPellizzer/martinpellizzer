Cissus platanifolia

Cissus platanifolia, commonly known as the "Palm-leaf Creeper," is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that resemble palm fronds, which are typically dark green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by yellow, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Cissus platanifolia thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
