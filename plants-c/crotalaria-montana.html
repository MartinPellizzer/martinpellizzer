Crotalaria montana

Crotalaria montana, commonly known as the mountain crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow or orange flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in sandy or well-drained soils and is adapted to dry conditions.

Crotalaria montana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
