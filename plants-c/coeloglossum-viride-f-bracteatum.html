Coeloglossum viride f. bracteatum

Coeloglossum viride f. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate green hawksbeard, is a variant of the green hawksbeard plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, which are surrounded by bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

This form is distinguished by the presence of prominent bracts that give it its specific epithet.
