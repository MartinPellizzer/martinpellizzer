Cousinia rechingerae

Cousinia rechingerae, commonly known as the Tibetan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. It has hairy, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Cousinia rechingerae is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.
