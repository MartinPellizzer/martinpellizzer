Campanula cespitosa subf. typica

Campanula cespitosa subf.

typica, commonly known as the common bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It features upright, clump-forming stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, with hairy surfaces that help reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
