Clematis grata var. lobulata

Clematis grata var. lobulata, commonly known as the fragrant clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Europe.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly lobed appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its climbing habit and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
