Carex guniuensis

Carex guniuensis, commonly known as the Guniea sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. This plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Carex guniuensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
