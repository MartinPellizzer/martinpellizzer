Chascanum latifolium var. transvaalense

Chascanum latifolium var. transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The Transvaal daisy is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
