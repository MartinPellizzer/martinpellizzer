Carex pyrenaica var. cephalotes

Carex pyrenaica var. cephalotes, commonly known as the Pyrenean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color, and it blooms during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
