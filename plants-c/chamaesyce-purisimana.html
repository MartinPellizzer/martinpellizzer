Chamaesyce purisimana

Chamaesyce purisimana, commonly known as the coastal sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and along shorelines. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with five petals and a distinct central cone.

Chamaesyce purisimana is adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a resilient species in coastal ecosystems.
