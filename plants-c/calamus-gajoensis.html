Calamus gajoensis

Calamus gajoensis, commonly known as the Gajo's rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, flexible stems used for making rattan furniture. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with numerous leaflets, and its inflorescences are borne in clusters. It thrives in humid tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and utility, it is sometimes cultivated for commercial purposes.
