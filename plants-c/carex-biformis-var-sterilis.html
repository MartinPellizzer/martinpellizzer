Carex biformis var. sterilis

Carex biformis var. sterilis, commonly known as the sterile spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that are typically sterile, giving it its common name. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
