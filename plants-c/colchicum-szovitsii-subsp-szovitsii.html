Colchicum szovitsii subsp. szovitsii

Colchicum szovitsii subsp.

szovitsii, commonly known as the Szovits' colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant features basal leaves that are broad and lanceolate, often with a waxy coating. It produces a tall, slender scape that bears a single, large, showy flower.

This species is adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
