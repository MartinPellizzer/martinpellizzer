Carex tumidicarpa subsp. cedercreutzii

Carex tumidicarpa subsp.

cedercreutzii, commonly known as the Cedercreutz sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their distinctive inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are usually broad and flat, with a grass-like appearance, and it produces small, clustered spikelets in the summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
