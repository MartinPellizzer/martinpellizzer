Cordia imparilis

Cordia imparilis, commonly known as the white cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces showy white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The fruit is a fleshy, yellow or orange berry that contains several seeds.
