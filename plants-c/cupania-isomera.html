Cupania isomera

Cupania isomera, commonly known as the Brazilian elderberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, tropical environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cupania isomera is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
