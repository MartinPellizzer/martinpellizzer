Carex collimitanea

Carex collimitanea, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Carex collimitanea plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
