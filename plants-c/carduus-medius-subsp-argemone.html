Carduus medius subsp. argemone

Carduus medius subsp.

argemone, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
