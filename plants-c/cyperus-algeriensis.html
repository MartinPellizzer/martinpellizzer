Cyperus algeriensis

Cyperus algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian flatsedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to North Africa, particularly Algeria, and thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and along water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize moist soils.

Due to its ecological adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
