Chlorocyperus rivularis

Chlorocyperus rivularis, commonly known as the river sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and flooded meadows.

Chlorocyperus rivularis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
