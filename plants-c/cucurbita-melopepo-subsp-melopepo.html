Cucurbita melopepo subsp. melopepo

Cucurbita melopepo subsp.

melopepo, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is characterized by its spiny stems and fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments.

The plant produces vibrant flowers that transition into edible fruits, known as tunas, which are rich in nutrients. Its botanical structure includes a succulent stem system and a unique reproductive cycle involving both sexual and asexual propagation.

This species is native to the Americas and has been cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
