Capsella bursa-pastoris subsp. rubella

Capsella bursa-pastoris subsp.

rubella, commonly known as the red shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods that turn red when mature.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions across Europe and Asia. It has small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The subspecies rubella is distinguished by its reddish coloration of the inflorescence and mature pods, setting it apart from other subspecies of the species.
