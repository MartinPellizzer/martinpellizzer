Carex boelckeiana

Carex boelckeiana, commonly known as the Boelcke's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the genus Carex in the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex boelckeiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
