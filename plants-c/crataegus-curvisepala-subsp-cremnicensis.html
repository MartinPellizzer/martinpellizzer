Crataegus curvisepala subsp. cremnicensis

Crataegus curvisepala subsp.

cremnicensis, commonly known as the Carpathian hawthorn, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the autumn.

The plant has thorny branches and elliptical leaves with a pointed tip. It thrives in rocky or mountainous soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

This subspecies is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its unique geographic distribution and ecological adaptations.
