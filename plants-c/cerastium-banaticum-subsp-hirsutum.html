Cerastium banaticum subsp. hirsutum

Cerastium banaticum subsp.

hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky slopes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy foliage and compact growth form compared to other members of the species.
