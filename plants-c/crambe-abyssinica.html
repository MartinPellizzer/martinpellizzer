Crambe abyssinica

Crambe abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian crambe, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

Crambe abyssinica is also noted for its potential as a source of oil and its use in traditional medicine.
