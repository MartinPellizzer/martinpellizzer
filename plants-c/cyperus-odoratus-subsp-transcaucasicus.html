Cyperus odoratus subsp. transcaucasicus

Cyperus odoratus subsp.

transcaucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as riverbanks and wetlands, and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the broader Cyperus odoratus species.
