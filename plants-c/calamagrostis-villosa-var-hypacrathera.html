Calamagrostis villosa var. hypacrathera

Calamagrostis villosa var. hypacrathera, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia and the United States.

It features slender, upright stems with silky, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that are often used in ornamental landscaping. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
