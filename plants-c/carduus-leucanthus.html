Carduus leucanthus

Carduus leucanthus, commonly known as the white hawk's-bill, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant features a robust stem with rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have serrated edges. Its showy white to pale yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carduus leucanthus is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
