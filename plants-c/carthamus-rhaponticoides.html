Carthamus rhaponticoides

Carthamus rhaponticoides, commonly known as the Russian safflower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features upright stems covered with soft, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the summer months. Its flowers are followed by slender, seed-bearing capsules that release small, brown seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
