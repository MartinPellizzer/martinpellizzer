Caragana brachyantha

Caragana brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bitterbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with closely spaced leaflets, and its bark is typically grayish-brown and fissured. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry climates.

Caragana brachyantha plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.
