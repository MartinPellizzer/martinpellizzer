Crataegus azarolus var. chlorocarpa

Crataegus azarolus var. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-fruited hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in late summer, often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with mild winters.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
