Camellia sinensis var. pubilimba

Camellia sinensis var. pubilimba, commonly known as the Thai or Siamese tea plant, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are slightly pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical features make it well-suited for cultivation in tropical and subtropical climates.

While not widely used for commercial tea production, it holds significance in regional horticulture and botanical studies.
