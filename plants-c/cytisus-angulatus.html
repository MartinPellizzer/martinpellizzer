Cytisus angulatus

Cytisus angulatus, commonly known as angular broom, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features slender, angular stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Cytisus angulatus is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.
