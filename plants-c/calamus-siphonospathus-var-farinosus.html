Calamus siphonospathus var. farinosus

Calamus siphonospathus var. farinosus, commonly known as the farinose bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its inflorescences are terminal and consist of a dense cluster of small, pale yellow flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
