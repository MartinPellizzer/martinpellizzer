Cistus × longifolius

Cistus × longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen foliage and showy, pink to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a leathery texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Cistus × longifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
