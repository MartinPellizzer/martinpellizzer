Cyclopogon graciliscapus

Cyclopogon graciliscapus, commonly known as the slender-tipped cyclopogon, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous areas with high humidity. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanical research.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
