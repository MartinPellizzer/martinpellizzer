Carex flava var. serotina

Carex flava var. serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and pond margins. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom later in the season compared to other Carex species.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
