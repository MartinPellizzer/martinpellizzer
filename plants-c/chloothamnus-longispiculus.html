Chloothamnus longispiculus

Chloothamnus longispiculus, commonly known as the long-spined goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and produces small, showy yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has adapted to harsh, dry environments with its deep root system and water-conserving leaves. It is often found in rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local pollinators.
