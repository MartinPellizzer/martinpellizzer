Crocus violaceus

Crocus violaceus, commonly known as the violet crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive, often fragrant scent. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in rocky or well-drained soils, and it typically blooms in early spring.

The violet crocus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early-season blooms.
