Croton mooriae

Croton mooriae, commonly known as the Mooriae croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that vary in shape and hue. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
