Clethra delavayi var. yuiana

Clethra delavayi var. yuiana, commonly known as the Yunnan clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to western China.

It belongs to the plant family Clethraceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests, and prefers well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin, while its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and attractive foliage.
