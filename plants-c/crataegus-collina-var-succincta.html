Crataegus collina var. succincta

Crataegus collina var. succincta, commonly known as the smooth crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet, featuring dense, thornless branches and oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall.

Its fruit is often used in jams and jellies, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat.
