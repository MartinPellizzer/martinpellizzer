Celastrus phyllacanthus

Celastrus phyllacanthus, commonly known as the Chinese staff vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional purposes.
