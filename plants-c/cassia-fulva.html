Cassia fulva

Cassia fulva, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant produces oval, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Cassia fulva is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
