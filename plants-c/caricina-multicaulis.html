Caricina multicaulis

Caricina multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Caricina multicaulis is adapted to withstand drought and has a slow growth rate.
