Cirsium lanceolatum var. brevilobum

Cirsium lanceolatum var. brevilobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features spiny, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in a spherical cluster. The plant grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions, and its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
