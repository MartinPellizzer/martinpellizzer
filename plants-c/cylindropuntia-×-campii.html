Cylindropuntia × campii

Cylindropuntia × campii, commonly known as the Mexican fire cactus, is a hybrid species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The species is also known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in arid environments.
