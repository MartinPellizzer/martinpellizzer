Cattleya loddigesii subvar. candida

Cattleya loddigesii subvar. candida, commonly known as the White Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid originating from South America.

It is characterized by its large, white flowers with a slight pinkish tinge and a prominent, fragrant lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, storing water to help it survive dry periods.

This variety is highly prized in horticulture for its beauty and fragrance.
