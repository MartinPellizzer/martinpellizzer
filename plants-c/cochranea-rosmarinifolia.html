Cochranea rosmarinifolia

Cochranea rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silvery-gray foliage that is covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its aromatic properties make it both ornamental and potentially useful in herbal applications.
