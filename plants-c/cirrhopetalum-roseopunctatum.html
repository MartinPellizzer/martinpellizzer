Cirrhopetalum roseopunctatum

Cirrhopetalum roseopunctatum, commonly known as the red-spotted orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that display a combination of pink and white petals with distinctive red spots. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it thrives on the forest floor. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
