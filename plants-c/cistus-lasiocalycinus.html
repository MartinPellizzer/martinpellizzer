Cistus lasiocalycinus

Cistus lasiocalycinus, commonly known as the rough cistus, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its essential oils contain various compounds, including flavonoids and terpenes, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Cistus lasiocalycinus is also valued for its role in traditional herbal medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
