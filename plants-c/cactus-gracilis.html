Cactus gracilis

Cactus gracilis, commonly known as the slender cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that store water efficiently, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a delicate touch of color to its otherwise desolate habitat. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Cactus gracilis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ability to withstand extreme temperatures.
