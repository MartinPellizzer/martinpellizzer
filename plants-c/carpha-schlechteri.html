Carpha schlechteri

Carpha schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's reed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typically in shades of green or brown.
