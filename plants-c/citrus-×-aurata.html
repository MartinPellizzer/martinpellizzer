Citrus × aurata

Citrus × aurata, commonly known as the trifoliate orange, is a hybrid citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and oval, glossy green leaves that often occur in sets of three. The plant produces small, sour oranges that are typically not eaten but used for ornamental purposes. It is widely cultivated as a decorative shrub due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.

Citrus × aurata is also valued for its use in perfumery and as a source of citronella oil.
