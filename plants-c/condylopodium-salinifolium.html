Condylopodium salinifolium

Condylopodium salinifolium, commonly known as saltleaf, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, greenish flowers that are often inconspicuous.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is an important species for ecological studies in arid and coastal regions.
