Cinnamomum tazia

Cinnamomum tazia, commonly known as the Tazia cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that contain smooth, pale seeds. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Cinnamomum tazia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional herbal remedies.
