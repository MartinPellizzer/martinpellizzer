Chaetotropis asperigluma

Chaetotropis asperigluma, commonly known as the rough-sheath grass, is a species of grass native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often covered in short, stiff hairs.

The botanical features of Chaetotropis asperigluma make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
