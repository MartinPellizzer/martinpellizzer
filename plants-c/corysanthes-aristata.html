Corysanthes aristata

Corysanthes aristata, commonly known as the golden torch orchid, is a tropical plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, candle-like flower spikes. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching several feet in height. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Corysanthes aristata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for tropical gardens.
