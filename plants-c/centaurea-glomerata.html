Centaurea glomerata

Centaurea glomerata, commonly known as the clustered knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical flower heads that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant grows from a basal rosette of leaves and produces slender, branching stems that bear the compact inflorescences. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.
