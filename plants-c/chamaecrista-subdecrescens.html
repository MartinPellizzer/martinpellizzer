Chamaecrista subdecrescens

Chamaecrista subdecrescens, commonly known as the slender ticktree, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes, while its leaves are pinnately divided with small, pointed leaflets.

The species is notable for its ecological role in nitrogen fixation and as a food source for certain insects.
