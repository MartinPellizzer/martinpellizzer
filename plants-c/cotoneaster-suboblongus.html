Cotoneaster suboblongus

Cotoneaster suboblongus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of white to pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Cotoneaster suboblongus is relatively low maintenance and adaptable to various environmental conditions.
