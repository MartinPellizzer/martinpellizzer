Chamaemelum trichophyllum

Chamaemelum trichophyllum, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the northern regions of Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its leaves and flowers contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional herbal medicine.
