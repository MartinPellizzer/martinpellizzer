Croton melanoleucus

Croton melanoleucus, commonly known as the black-leaved croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green to nearly black leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its unique foliage.
