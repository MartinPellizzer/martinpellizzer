Cassia socotrana

Cassia socotrana, commonly known as Socotra cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula, particularly the island of Socotra.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its distinctive, narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by long, narrow pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and high temperatures.

Cassia socotrana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local cultures.
