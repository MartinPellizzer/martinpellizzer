Croton scandens

Croton scandens, commonly known as the climbing croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from bright yellow to deep red, depending on the variety. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often lobed or serrated, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its stems are woody and can climb or trail, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Croton scandens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
