Chionanthus palembanicus

Chionanthus palembanicus, commonly known as the Palembang Chionanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling a cascading veil. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.
