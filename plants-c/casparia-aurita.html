Casparia aurita

Casparia aurita, commonly known as the golden starflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Casparia aurita is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant, star-shaped blooms.
