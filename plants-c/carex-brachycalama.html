Carex brachycalama

Carex brachycalama, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Carex brachycalama produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are often clustered at the ends of its stems.
