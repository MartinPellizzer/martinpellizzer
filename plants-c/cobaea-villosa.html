Cobaea villosa

Cobaea villosa, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
