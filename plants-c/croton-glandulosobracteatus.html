Croton glandulosobracteatus

Croton glandulosobracteatus, commonly known as the glandular bract croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, glandular-armed bracts. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

The flowers are unisexual and clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.
