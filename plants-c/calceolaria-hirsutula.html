Calceolaria hirsutula

Calceolaria hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it the species name "hirsutula," and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract various pollinators.

Calceolaria hirsutula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
