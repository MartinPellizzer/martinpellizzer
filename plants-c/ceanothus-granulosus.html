Ceanothus granulosus

Ceanothus granulosus, commonly known as the woolly ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats. Its leaves are aromatic and contain compounds that can be used in traditional herbal medicine.

Ceanothus granulosus plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
