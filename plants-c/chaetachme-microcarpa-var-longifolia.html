Chaetachme microcarpa var. longifolia

Chaetachme microcarpa var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved chaetachme, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, with deep root systems that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
