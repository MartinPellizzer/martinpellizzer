Clematis iochanica

Clematis iochanica, commonly known as the Indian clematis, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a deciduous vine that typically grows in moist, shaded areas of forests. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has a woody stem covered in fine hairs.

Clematis iochanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
