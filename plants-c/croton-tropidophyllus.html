Croton tropidophyllus

Croton tropidophyllus, commonly known as the red-leaf croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a distinctive veining pattern that enhances its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
