Croton velame

Croton velame, commonly known as velvet croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a velvety appearance, and its striking, colorful flowers that range from yellow to red. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.

The sap of Croton velame contains toxic compounds, requiring caution when handling the plant.
