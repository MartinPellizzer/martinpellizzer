Cassia chamaecrista f. flavicoma

Cassia chamaecrista f. flavicoma, commonly known as yellow cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
