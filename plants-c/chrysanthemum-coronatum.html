Chrysanthemum coronatum

Chrysanthemum coronatum, commonly known as the Crown Daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which consist of white ray florets surrounding a central yellow disk. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Chrysanthemum coronatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
