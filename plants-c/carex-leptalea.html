Carex leptalea

Carex leptalea, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Carex leptalea plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
