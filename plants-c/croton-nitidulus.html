Croton nitidulus

Croton nitidulus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries containing seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and sometimes toxic species.
