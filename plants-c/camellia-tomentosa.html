Camellia tomentosa

Camellia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly camellia, is a species native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces large, showy flowers with five petals, often in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it its common name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade.

Camellia tomentosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in winter.
