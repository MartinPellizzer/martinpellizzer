Campanula velutina var. maroccana

Campanula velutina var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Morocco.

It features upright stems covered in soft, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive tactile quality. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy layer.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
