Cavendishia amplexa

Cavendishia amplexa, commonly known as the creeping orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle scent. Its leaves are typically elongated and can be either opposite or whorled in arrangement.

Cavendishia amplexa thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
