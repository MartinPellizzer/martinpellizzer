Cyathea atrospinosa

Cyathea atrospinosa, commonly known as the black-spined tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive dark brown to black spines along the frond rachis, which give it its common name. Cyathea atrospinosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
