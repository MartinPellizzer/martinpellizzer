Carex mercarensis

Carex mercarensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flowering structures. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively and colonize wetland areas.

Carex mercarensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
