hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Zingiberaceae. It is characterized by its tall, rhizomatous growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in inflorescences, which are typically borne on the upper portions of the stems. It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of streams.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some parts of the world for both horticultural and traditional uses.
