Cerasus avium var. major

Cerasus avium var. major, commonly known as the wild cherry or Prunus avium, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible cherries that ripen in summer. The plant is native to Europe and western Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. It typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.
