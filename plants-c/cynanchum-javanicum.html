Cynanchum javanicum

Cynanchum javanicum, commonly known as the Java milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds. It is often found in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate to subtropical climates.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
