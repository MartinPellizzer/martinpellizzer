Chusquea pubescens

Chusquea pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Chusquea pubescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
