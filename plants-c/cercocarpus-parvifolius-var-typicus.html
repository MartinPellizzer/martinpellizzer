Cercocarpus parvifolius var. typicus

Cercocarpus parvifolius var. typicus, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of red, berry-like fruits. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round interest, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
