Cyperus corynephorus

Cyperus corynephorus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has a distinctive appearance with its clustered, umbrella-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus corynephorus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
