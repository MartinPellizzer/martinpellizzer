Crocus weldenii

Crocus weldenii, commonly known as the Welden's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically a shade of yellow or orange, with a distinctive violet or purple stripe down the center. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is noted for its early spring blooming habit, often appearing before many other spring flowers.
