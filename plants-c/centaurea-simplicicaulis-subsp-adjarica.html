Centaurea simplicicaulis subsp. adjarica

Centaurea simplicicaulis subsp.

adjarica, commonly known as the Adjara centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers in conical clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for well-drained soils. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
