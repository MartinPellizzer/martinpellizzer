Corsia

Corsia, commonly known as Corsican hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Corsia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
