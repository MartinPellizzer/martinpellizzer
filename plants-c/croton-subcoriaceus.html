Croton subcoriaceus

Croton subcoriaceus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
