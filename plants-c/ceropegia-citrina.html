Ceropegia citrina

Ceropegia citrina, commonly known as the yellow moonvine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive tubular flowers that are often visited by specific insect pollinators. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Ceropegia citrina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
