Crataegus azarolus subsp. aronia

Crataegus azarolus subsp.

aronia, commonly known as the blackthorn or sloe, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dark, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark purple to black berries in late summer. These berries are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
