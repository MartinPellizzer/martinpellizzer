Crataegus keepii

Crataegus keepii, commonly known as the Keep's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
