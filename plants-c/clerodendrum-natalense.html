Clerodendrum natalense

Clerodendrum natalense, commonly known as Natal glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
