Conostomium quadrangulare

Conostomium quadrangulare, commonly known as the four-cornered conostomium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its square or quadrangular stems, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
