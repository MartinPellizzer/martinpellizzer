Cordia hermanniifolia var. calycina

Cordia hermanniifolia var. calycina, commonly known as the golden glory balm, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds enclosed in a gelatinous pulp.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
