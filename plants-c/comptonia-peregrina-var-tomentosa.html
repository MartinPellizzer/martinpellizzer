Comptonia peregrina var. tomentosa

Comptonia peregrina var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly candleplant, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, narrow leaves that are densely covered in white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers in late spring, which are arranged in candle-like spikes. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
