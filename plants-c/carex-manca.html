Carex manca

Carex manca, commonly known as slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces three-sided, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically green to brown in color.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
