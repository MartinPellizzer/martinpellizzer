Centaurea ragusina subsp. ragusina

Centaurea ragusina subsp.

ragusina, commonly known as the Ragusa centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and bright blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and sometimes hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
