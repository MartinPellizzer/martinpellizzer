Clinopodium dalmaticum subsp. bulgaricum

Clinopodium dalmaticum subsp.

bulgaricum, commonly known as Balkan mint, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, tubular flowers are usually blue to purple in color and are arranged in whorled clusters.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
