Cordia anderssonii

Cordia anderssonii, commonly known as the Andes cordia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, tropical climates. The plant features simple, opposite leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cordia anderssonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
