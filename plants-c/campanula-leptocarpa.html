Campanula leptocarpa

Campanula leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula leptocarpa is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
