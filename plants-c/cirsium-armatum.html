Cirsium armatum

Cirsium armatum, commonly known as the Prickly Spear Thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and large, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs and have a reddish or purplish hue. Its flowers are typically surrounded by a series of bracts that form a distinctive, spiky involucre.

Cirsium armatum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
