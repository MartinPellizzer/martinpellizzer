Centaurea albofimbriata

Centaurea albofimbriata, commonly known as the white-edged knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and bright yellow flowers with white-edged petals. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a silvery or grayish underside.

Centaurea albofimbriata is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
