Cissus ruspolii

Cissus ruspolii, commonly known as the rock fig or stone fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Cissus ruspolii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in harsh environments.
