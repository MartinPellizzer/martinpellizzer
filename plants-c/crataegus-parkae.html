Crataegus parkae

Crataegus parkae, commonly known as the Park's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in areas of California and Nevada. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant often grows as a shrub or small tree.

Crataegus parkae is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
