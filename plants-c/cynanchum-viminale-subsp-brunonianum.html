Cynanchum viminale subsp. brunonianum

Cynanchum viminale subsp.

brunonianum, commonly known as the Brunonian milkvetch, is a perennial climbing plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by follicles containing seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
