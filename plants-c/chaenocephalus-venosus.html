Chaenocephalus venosus

Chaenocephalus venosus, commonly known as the veiny-headed cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its structure includes areoles, which are specialized structures that bear spines, flowers, and sometimes branches.

The veiny-headed cactus is an important species in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
