Campanula cespitosa subf. austriaca

Campanula cespitosa subf.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It features upright, clumping growth habit with basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
