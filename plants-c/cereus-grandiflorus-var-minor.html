Cereus grandiflorus var. minor

Cereus grandiflorus var. minor, commonly known as the miniature night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, spiny areoles. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
