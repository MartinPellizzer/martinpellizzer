Calamus corrugatus

Calamus corrugatus, commonly known as the ridged reed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Arecaceae, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature in many palm species. Calamus corrugatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional basketry and as a source of fiber.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
