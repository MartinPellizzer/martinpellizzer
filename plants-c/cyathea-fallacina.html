Cyathea fallacina

Cyathea fallacina, commonly known as the false tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of fertile fronds that produce spores, which are essential for its reproduction. Cyathea fallacina thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its slow growth and unique appearance make it a notable species in New Zealand's botanical landscape.
