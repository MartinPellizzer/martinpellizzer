Cattleya dichotoma

Cattleya dichotoma, commonly known as the forked cattleya, is a tropical orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive bifurcated or forked flower spike, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a yellow or orange throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright indirect light for optimal growth.

Cattleya dichotoma is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relative ease of cultivation.
