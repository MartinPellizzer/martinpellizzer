Cissus lamprophylla

Cissus lamprophylla, commonly known as the silver leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
