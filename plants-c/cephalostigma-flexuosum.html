Cephalostigma flexuosum

Cephalostigma flexuosum, commonly known as the flexible-headed orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often curved floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale pink, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Cephalostigma flexuosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
