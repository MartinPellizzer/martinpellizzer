Crocus albiflorus subsp. heuffelianus

Crocus albiflorus subsp.

heuffelianus, commonly known as the Alpine white crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers with six petals, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is relatively hardy, though it requires well-drained conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
