Cordiofontis laka

Cordiofontis laka, commonly known as the heartleaf laka, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Cordiofontis laka is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
