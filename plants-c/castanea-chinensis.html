Castanea chinensis

Castanea chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Fagaceae and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and serrated leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by spiny, cup-like structures that enclose its edible nuts. The nuts are typically sweet and have a rich, nutty flavor, making them popular in both culinary and ornamental uses.

Castanea chinensis is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
