Carex caryophyllea var. rhizostachya

Carex caryophyllea var. rhizostachya, commonly known as the purple tussock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purple tinge along the midrib. The plant produces dense, cylindrical clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are often reddish-brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
