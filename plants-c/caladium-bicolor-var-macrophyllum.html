Caladium bicolor var. macrophyllum

Caladium bicolor var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant elephant ear, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length.

The leaves are typically a deep red or maroon color with a striking white or cream-colored border. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers partial to full shade and is known for its ability to add dramatic visual impact to landscapes.
