Cassia hilariana

Cassia hilariana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds. Cassia hilariana thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
