Clerodendrum capitatum var. capitatum

Clerodendrum capitatum var. capitatum, commonly known as the red-headed flower or red-headed clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small, pale yellow flowers, creating a visually appealing display. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, while the stems are often woody and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
