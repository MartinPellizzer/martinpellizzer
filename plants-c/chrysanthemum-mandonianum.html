Chrysanthemum mandonianum

Chrysanthemum mandonianum, commonly known as the Mandon's chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is usually gray-green with a slightly hairy texture, adding to its visual appeal.
