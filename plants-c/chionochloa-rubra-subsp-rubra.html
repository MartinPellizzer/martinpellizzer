Chionochloa rubra subsp. rubra

Chionochloa rubra subsp.

rubra, commonly known as the red mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, reddish stems that often display a striking coloration.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
