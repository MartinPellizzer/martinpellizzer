Conyza valdiviana

Conyza valdiviana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy surface that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields.

Conyza valdiviana is known for its rapid growth and ability to become a weed in cultivated areas.
