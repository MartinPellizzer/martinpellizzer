Cicca scandens

Cicca scandens, commonly known as the climbing thornapple, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has spiny stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. Its fruit is a fleshy, berry-like structure that contains several seeds.

Cicca scandens is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in some areas.
