Corylus macrocarpa

Corylus macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded hazel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow-green catkins. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, which mature in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.

Corylus macrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
