Cynanchum contrapetalum

Cynanchum contrapetalum, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruits are follicles that release seeds with silky appendages. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant contains various alkaloids and is used to treat ailments such as inflammation and respiratory conditions.
