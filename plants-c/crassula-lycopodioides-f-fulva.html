Crassula lycopodioides f. fulva

Crassula lycopodioides f. fulva, commonly known as the red-flowered crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The leaves are typically fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is prized for its vibrant foliage and ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor settings.
