Conophytum wiesemannianum

Conophytum wiesemannianum, commonly known as the "Cape fynbos" plant, is a succulent belonging to the Aizoaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features a compact rosette of fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability to arid conditions, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
