Crataegus monogyna f. semperflorens

Crataegus monogyna f. semperflorens, commonly known as the everflowering hawthorn, is a compact shrub native to southern Europe.

It is a form of the common hawthorn, distinguished by its prolonged flowering period, which extends from early spring through late summer. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it bears red berries in the fall.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in gardens for its attractive flowers and hardy nature.
