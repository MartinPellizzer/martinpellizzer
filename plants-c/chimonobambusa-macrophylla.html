Chimonobambusa macrophylla

Chimonobambusa macrophylla, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense clumps. Its culms are often used in traditional crafts and construction due to their strength and flexibility.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
