Combretum acuminatum

Combretum acuminatum, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often glandular and may have a slight pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
