Celtis laevigata subsp. reticulata

Celtis laevigata subsp.

reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are important for wildlife, particularly birds and mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and riparian areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
