Conopodium capillifolium

Conopodium capillifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved bladderpod, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its small, pinnately divided leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that are inflated and often contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Conopodium capillifolium is often found in temperate regions of North America and is adapted to wet or disturbed soils.
