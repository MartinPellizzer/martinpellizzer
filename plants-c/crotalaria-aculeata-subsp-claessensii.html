Crotalaria aculeata subsp. claessensii

Crotalaria aculeata subsp.

claessensii, commonly known as the prickly crownflower, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its distinctive spiny stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid environments.

Due to its prickly structure and medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
