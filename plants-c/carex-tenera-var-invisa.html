Carex tenera var. invisa

Carex tenera var. invisa, commonly known as the velvet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a soft, velvety texture on the leaves. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
