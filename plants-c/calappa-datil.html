Calappa datil

Calappa datil, commonly known as the pink morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
