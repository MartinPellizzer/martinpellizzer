Caragana shuidingensis

Caragana shuidingensis, commonly known as the Shuiding pea shrub, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. It has shallow roots that help prevent soil erosion, making it valuable for ecological restoration.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
