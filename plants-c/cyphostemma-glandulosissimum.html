Cyphostemma glandulosissimum

Cyphostemma glandulosissimum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by pale yellow, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found growing along fences or in rocky areas.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
