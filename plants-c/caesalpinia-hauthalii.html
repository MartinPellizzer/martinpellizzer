Caesalpinia hauthalii

Caesalpinia hauthalii, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual display. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that contains several seeds.

Caesalpinia hauthalii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, sunny climates.
