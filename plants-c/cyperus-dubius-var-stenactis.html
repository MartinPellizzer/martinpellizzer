Cyperus dubius var. stenactis

Cyperus dubius var. stenactis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that are often subtended by bracts.

Cyperus dubius var. stenactis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
