Catasetum caxarariense

Catasetum caxarariense, commonly known as the Caxarari Orchid, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in Peru.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a specialized column that facilitates pollination by specific wasps. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a complex arrangement that includes a labellum and a spurred structure.

Catasetum caxarariense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
