Cleome coeruleorosea

Cleome coeruleorosea, commonly known as the blue rose cleome, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and blue. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cleome coeruleorosea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
