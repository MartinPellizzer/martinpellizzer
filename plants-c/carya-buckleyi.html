Carya buckleyi

Carya buckleyi, commonly known as Buckley's hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by hard-shelled nuts that mature in the fall. Its bark is thick, scaly, and deeply furrowed, providing a distinctive appearance.

Buckley's hickory is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in the region.
