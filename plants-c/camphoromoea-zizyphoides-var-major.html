Camphoromoea zizyphoides var. major

Camphoromoea zizyphoides var. major, commonly known as the Chinese camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature. It is valued for its timber, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The plant is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resistance to pests and diseases.
