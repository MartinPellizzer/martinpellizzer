Ceiba pubiflora f. coaetanea

Ceiba pubiflora f. coaetanea, commonly known as the Coaetanea ceiba, is a variant of the ceiba tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, and often buttressed trunk, which provides structural support in its natural habitat. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in terminal panicles. It produces large, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest communities.
