Chloris inflata

Chloris inflata, commonly known as the inflated grass or balloon grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in dry to moist conditions. Its unique seed structure, which resembles a small balloon, aids in wind dispersal.

Chloris inflata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its visual appeal and ecological benefits.
