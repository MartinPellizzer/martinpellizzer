Coleocephalocereus brevicylindricus var. longispinus

Coleocephalocereus brevicylindricus var. longispinus, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the deserts of North America.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and produces clusters of white flowers during the summer months. The plant is characterized by its long, sharp spines that provide protection against herbivores. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
