Chrysanthemum carinatum var. burridgeanum

Chrysanthemum carinatum var. burridgeanum, commonly known as the Burridge's thorned chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

This variety is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.
