Cyperus trinervis var. typicus

Cyperus trinervis var. typicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet with small, three-ranked spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Cyperus trinervis var. typicus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
