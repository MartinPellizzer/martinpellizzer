Carex harlandii subsp. khoi

Carex harlandii subsp.

khoi, commonly known as the Khoi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and along riverbanks.

The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in water gardens or as a ground cover.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and habitat preferences.
