Carex hirtella

Carex hirtella, commonly known as hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-parted flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex hirtella plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
