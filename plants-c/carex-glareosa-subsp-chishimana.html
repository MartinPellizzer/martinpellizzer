Carex glareosa subsp. chishimana

Carex glareosa subsp.

chishimana, commonly known as the Chishimane sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer months.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
