Cassia longirostrata

Cassia longirostrata, commonly known as the long-beaked cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and spikes of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Cassia longirostrata is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the legume family, Fabaceae.
