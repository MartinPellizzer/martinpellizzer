Coreopsis odora

Coreopsis odora, commonly known as sweet coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The plant is also known for its pleasant, sweet fragrance, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
