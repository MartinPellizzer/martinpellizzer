Ceropegia pulvinata var. vetula

Ceropegia pulvinata var. vetula, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features trailing stems with fleshy, opposite leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, with a unique structure that resembles a bird's beak. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
