Clematis acuminata subsp. sikkimensis

Clematis acuminata subsp.

sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, usually blue or purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely divided, palmately lobed leaves and its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments. It is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
