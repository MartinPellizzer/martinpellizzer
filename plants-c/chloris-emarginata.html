Chloris emarginata

Chloris emarginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chloris emarginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
