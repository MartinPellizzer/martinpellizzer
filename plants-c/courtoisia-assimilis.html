Courtoisia assimilis

Courtoisia assimilis, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Courtoisia assimilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
