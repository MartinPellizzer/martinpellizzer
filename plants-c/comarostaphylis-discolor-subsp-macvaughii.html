Comarostaphylis discolor subsp. macvaughii

Comarostaphylis discolor subsp.

macvaughii, commonly known as the Texas mountain laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves with oval, dark green leaflets and its showy clusters of yellow-green flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces woody pods that contain flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Botanically, it is notable for its aromatic foliage and its role in local ecosystems.
