Carex nudata f. sessiliflora

Carex nudata f. sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered sedge, is a variant of the narrow-leaved sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive white or pale stripe along the midrib.

The inflorescence consists of a single, sessile spike that bears small, wind-pollinated flowers.
