Crataegus pallasii

Crataegus pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
