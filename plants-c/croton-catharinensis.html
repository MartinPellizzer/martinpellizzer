Croton catharinensis

Croton catharinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its colorful, palmate leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
