Cerastium siculum subsp. riaei

Cerastium siculum subsp.

riaei, commonly known as the Sicilian mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features small, rounded leaves arranged in a rosette and produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in alpine and coastal regions due to its tolerance for harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
