Cryptocarya murrayi

Cryptocarya murrayi, commonly known as the Murray's cryptocarya, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, rounded form with dense foliage. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a slight sheen, while the flowers are small and clustered.

Cryptocarya murrayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
