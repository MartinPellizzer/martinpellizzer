Calyptromyrcia eugenioides var. glabra

Calyptromyrcia eugenioides var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved myrtle, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in coastal or humid environments. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental appearance make it a valued plant in horticulture.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
