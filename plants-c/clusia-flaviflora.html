Clusia flaviflora

Clusia flaviflora, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year. These flowers are followed by elliptical, orange-red fruits that contain three to five seeds.

Clusia flaviflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
