Cimicifuga japonica var. acerina

Cimicifuga japonica var. acerina, commonly known as Japanese bugbane, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of white, spire-like flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are compound, with elongated leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
