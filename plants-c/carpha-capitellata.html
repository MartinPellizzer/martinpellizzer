Carpha capitellata

Carpha capitellata, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Carpha capitellata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
