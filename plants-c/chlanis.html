Chlanis

Chlanis, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant has a short flowering period, typically blooming only at night, which adds to its mystique. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to store water in its native environment.

Despite its beauty, Chlanis is rare and requires specific conditions to thrive, making it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
