Centaurea kotschyi subsp. floccosa

Centaurea kotschyi subsp.

floccosa, commonly known as the woolly centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, fluffy appearance, with hairy leaves and bracts that give it a woolly texture. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
