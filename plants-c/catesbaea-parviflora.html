Catesbaea parviflora

Catesbaea parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf satinwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
