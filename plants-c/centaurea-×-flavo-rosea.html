Centaurea × flavo-rosea

Centaurea × flavo-rosea, commonly known as the hybrid gladiolus, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, vibrant flowers that range from pink to orange-red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and tubular disc florets, arranged in a composite head.

Centaurea × flavo-rosea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens due to its showy appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
