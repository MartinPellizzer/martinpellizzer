Cheiranthus dubius

Cheiranthus dubius, commonly known as the winter flowering mustard, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features basal leaves that are elongated and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, yellow, and borne in racemes. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its flowering period typically occurs from late autumn to early spring.
