Chamaecytisus proteus

Chamaecytisus proteus, commonly known as the sweet honey shrub, is a flowering plant native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chamaecytisus proteus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
