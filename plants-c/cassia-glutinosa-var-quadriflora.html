Cassia glutinosa var. quadriflora

Cassia glutinosa var. quadriflora, commonly known as the four-flowered cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves with four to six pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seed pods are long, slender, and contain numerous seeds covered in a sticky substance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
