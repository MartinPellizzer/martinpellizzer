Carduus amplexicaulis

Carduus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves that often form a basal rosette and its tall, branched stems that bear purple to pinkish flowers. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in dry, disturbed soils. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also known for its hardy nature and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative growth.
