Chironia purpurascens subsp. purpurascens

Chironia purpurascens subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purple chironia, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

The species is valued both for its aesthetic qualities and its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
