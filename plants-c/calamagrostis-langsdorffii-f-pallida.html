Calamagrostis langsdorffii f. pallida

Calamagrostis langsdorffii f. pallida, commonly known as the silver bluestem grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage that turns coppery in the fall, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are initially green and later turn golden, adding to its seasonal appeal.

It is valued in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
