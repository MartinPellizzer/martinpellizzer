Cupressus obtusa var. plumosa-argentea

Cupressus obtusa var. plumosa-argentea, commonly known as the silver feather cypress, is a variant of the Japanese cedar.

It is characterized by its feathery, spreading foliage that gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The leaves are scale-like and covered in a silvery-blue hue, which enhances its visual appeal. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for landscaping in temperate climates.

It is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value and ability to provide privacy or structure in landscape designs.
