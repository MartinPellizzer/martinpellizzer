Cardamine flaccida proles laxa

Cardamine flaccida proles laxa, commonly known as the laxa watercress, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its small, yellow flowers appear in early spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits.

The species is notable for its ability to tolerate wet conditions and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
