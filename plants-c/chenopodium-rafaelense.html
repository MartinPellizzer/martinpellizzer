Chenopodium rafaelense

Chenopodium rafaelense, commonly known as Rafael's goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Chenopodium rafaelense is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its unique foliage and resilience.
