Cyperus paolii

Cyperus paolii, commonly known as the Pauli flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries such as Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Cyperus paolii is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
