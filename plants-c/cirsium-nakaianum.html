Cirsium nakaianum

Cirsium nakaianum, commonly known as the Nakaian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream sides. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
