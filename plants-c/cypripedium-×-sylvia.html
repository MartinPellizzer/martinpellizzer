Cypripedium × sylvia

Cypripedium × sylvia, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its flowers are usually pink or white with a yellowish or greenish throat, and it produces a single, elongated stem with a few leaves.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
