Caesalpinia procera

Caesalpinia procera, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa, South America, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like inflorescences that appear during the dry season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are showy and attract birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 20 meters tall.
