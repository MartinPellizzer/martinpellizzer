Campanula garganica subsp. cephallenica

Campanula garganica subsp.

cephallenica, commonly known as the Garganica bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and hairy, providing a distinct texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
