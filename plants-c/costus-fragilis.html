Costus fragilis

Costus fragilis, commonly known as the delicate costus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its ornamental, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
