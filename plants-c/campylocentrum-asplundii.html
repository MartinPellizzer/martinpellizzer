Campylocentrum asplundii

Campylocentrum asplundii, commonly known as the asplundii pomegranate, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are oval-shaped, leathery, and have a glossy appearance.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant fruit and attractive foliage.
