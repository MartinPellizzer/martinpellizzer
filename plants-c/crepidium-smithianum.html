Crepidium smithianum

Crepidium smithianum, commonly known as the Smith's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in clusters, showcasing a range of colors from white to pale pink. The plant has a slender, upright stem and produces lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its native range.
