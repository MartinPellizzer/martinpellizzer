Calceolaria repens

Calceolaria repens, commonly known as the creeping calceolaria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features trailing stems that produce striking, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and hanging baskets.

The plant's unique floral structure, with a pouch-like lip, is characteristic of the Calceolaria genus.
