Centaurea mariolensis

Centaurea mariolensis, commonly known as the Mariolense thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean regions. It has spiny leaves and produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

Centaurea mariolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
