Chionanthus caymanensis var. longipetala

Chionanthus caymanensis var. longipetala, commonly known as the Cayman Islands whiteflower tree, is a unique variant of the chionanthus genus native to the Cayman Islands.

It is characterized by its striking white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

This variant is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance.
