Carex psammogaea

Carex psammogaea, commonly known as the sand sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and sandy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Carex psammogaea plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
