Cyrtopodium plantagineum

Cyrtopodium plantagineum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained soil. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in fluctuating environmental conditions.

Cyrtopodium plantagineum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
