Cyperus picardae

Cyperus picardae, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus picardae is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic ecosystems.
