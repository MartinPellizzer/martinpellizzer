Centaurium minus subsp. austriacum

Centaurium minus subsp.

austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian lesser catchfly, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color.

The plant produces tiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous minute seeds. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
