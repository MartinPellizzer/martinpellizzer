Cactus gladiatus

Cactus gladiatus, commonly known as the sword cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that resemble swords, typically ranging from 2 to 6 feet in height. The plant has spiny areoles and produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months. Its adaptations include thick, fleshy stems for water storage and reduced leaves to minimize water loss.

Cactus gladiatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
