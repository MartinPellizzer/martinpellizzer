Cereaster grandiflorus

Cereaster grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorous cereaster, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
