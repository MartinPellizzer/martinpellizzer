Crataegus buistii

Crataegus buistii, commonly known as the Buru hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically in moist, well-drained soils.

Crataegus buistii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
