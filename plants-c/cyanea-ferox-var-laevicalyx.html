Cyanea ferox var. laevicalyx

Cyanea ferox var. laevicalyx, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree with thick, waxy leaves that are typically smooth and leathery in texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a prominent central stigma. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in Hawaiian tradition.
