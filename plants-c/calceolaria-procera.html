Calceolaria procera

Calceolaria procera, commonly known as the mountain calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that resemble small cups, which are typically violet or purple in color. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Calceolaria procera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
