Coreopsis bituminosa

Coreopsis bituminosa, commonly known as the pitchleaf coreopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and dark green, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
