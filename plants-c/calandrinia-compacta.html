Calandrinia compacta

Calandrinia compacta, commonly known as the compact portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves and produces vibrant pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It has a taproot system and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Calandrinia compacta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
