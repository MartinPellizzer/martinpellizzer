Calandrinia adscendens

Calandrinia adscendens, commonly known as the ascending portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces vibrant pink to red flowers, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves and stems, adapted to thrive in arid environments. It belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
