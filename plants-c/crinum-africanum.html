Crinum africanum

Crinum africanum, commonly known as the African crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, and it produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. Crinum africanum is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some African communities.
