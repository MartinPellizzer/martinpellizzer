Calochortus albus

Calochortus albus, commonly known as the white glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant native to California and southern Oregon.

It belongs to the genus Calochortus, which includes a variety of bulbous plants commonly referred to as "mariposa lilies.". The plant features a white, bell-shaped flower that blooms in early spring, often with a yellowish-green stripe running down the center. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing from a bulbous base that stores nutrients for the plant.

Calochortus albus is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, thriving in full sun.
