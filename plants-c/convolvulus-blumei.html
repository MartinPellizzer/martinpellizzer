Convolvulus blumei

Convolvulus blumei, commonly known as the Blume's morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive spiral-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
