Cephalanthera subaphylla

Cephalanthera subaphylla, commonly known as the lesser twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces pale green to yellowish flowers that are clustered in a spike, with a distinctive hooded structure over the reproductive organs. It is a lithophytic species, often growing on rock surfaces or in crevices, and is found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
