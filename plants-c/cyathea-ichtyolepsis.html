Cyathea ichtyolepsis

Cyathea ichtyolepsis, commonly known as the fish-scale tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The fronds are often covered with small, scale-like structures, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
