Cytisus nigricans subsp. atratus

Cytisus nigricans subsp.

atratus, commonly known as the black thorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and small, yellow pea-like flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
