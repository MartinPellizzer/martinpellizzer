Cineraria bergeriana

Cineraria bergeriana, commonly known as the Canary Island cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to violet, bloom in spring and summer.

Cineraria bergeriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
