Cydonia vulgaris var. constantinopolitana

Cydonia vulgaris var. constantinopolitana, commonly known as the Persian apple or Constantino apple, is a variety of the quince plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in early spring. The fruit is typically oval, with a rough, pale yellow skin that turns orange when ripe. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

It is also valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in perfumery and traditional crafts.
