Cyphomeris crassifolia

Cyphomeris crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved Cyphomeris, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in clusters at the base of the stems. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
