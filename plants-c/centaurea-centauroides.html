Centaurea centauroides

Centaurea centauroides, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Centaurea centauroides is drought-tolerant and has a deep root system, making it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.
