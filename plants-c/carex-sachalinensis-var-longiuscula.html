Carex sachalinensis var. longiuscula

Carex sachalinensis var. longiuscula, commonly known as the long-beaked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, typical of the Carex genus. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and forest floors. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture.

The inflorescence consists of multiple spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
