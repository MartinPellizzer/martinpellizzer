Clerodendrum lindleyi

Clerodendrum lindleyi, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, brown seeds.

Clerodendrum lindleyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
