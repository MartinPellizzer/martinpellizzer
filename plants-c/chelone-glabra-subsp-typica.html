Chelone glabra subsp. typica

Chelone glabra subsp.

typica, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
