Carex × patuensis

Carex × patuensis, commonly known as the New Zealand flat sedge, is a hybrid plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb that grows in wetland and riparian habitats, often forming dense, grass-like clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Due to its ecological significance, it is often cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
