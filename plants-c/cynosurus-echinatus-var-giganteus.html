Cynosurus echinatus var. giganteus

Cynosurus echinatus var. giganteus, commonly known as the giant tussock grass, is a variant of the tussock grass species native to certain regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, stiff leaves that can reach significant heights. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, cylindrical spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in natural habitats.
