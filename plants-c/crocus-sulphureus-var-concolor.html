Crocus sulphureus var. concolor

Crocus sulphureus var. concolor, commonly known as the yellow crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, rounded shape and a dark center. The plant produces small, oval-shaped bulbs that are essential for propagation. Its leaves are basal, narrow, and grass-like, emerging in early spring.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
