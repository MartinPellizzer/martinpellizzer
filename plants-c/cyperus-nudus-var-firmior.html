Cyperus nudus var. firmior

Cyperus nudus var. firmior, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the base of the plant.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to droop slightly at the tips.
