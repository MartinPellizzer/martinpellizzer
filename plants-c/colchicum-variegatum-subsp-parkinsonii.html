Colchicum variegatum subsp. parkinsonii

Colchicum variegatum subsp.

parkinsonii, commonly known as the Parkinson's colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred shape.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
