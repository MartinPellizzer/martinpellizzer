Cayaponia ternata

Cayaponia ternata, commonly known as the Cayaponia tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palm-like appearance, with long, slender leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are consumed by both humans and wildlife. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in warm climates.

However, it can become invasive if not properly managed.
