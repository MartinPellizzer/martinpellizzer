Carex goodenowii f. androgyna

Carex goodenowii f. androgyna, commonly known as the Androgynous Goodenow sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, a feature that gives it its "androgynous" designation. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its unique floral structure and ecological preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying plant morphology and wetland ecosystems.
