Carex infuscata var. gracilenta

Carex infuscata var. gracilenta, commonly known as the slender black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and moist forest environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized inflorescence structures. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It thrives in partially shaded to sunny conditions and requires consistently moist soil.

This variant is distinguished by its slender stems and dark green foliage, making it a valuable addition to wetland restoration projects.
