Cattleya guttata var. russelliana

Cattleya guttata var. russelliana, commonly known as the Russell's spotted orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, and often brightly colored flowers with distinctive spots or markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
