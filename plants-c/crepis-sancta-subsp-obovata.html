Crepis sancta subsp. obovata

Crepis sancta subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, obovate leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Native to Europe and parts of western Asia, this species thrives in grassy habitats and disturbed soils. It is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

The plant is notable for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
