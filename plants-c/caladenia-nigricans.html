Caladenia nigricans

Caladenia nigricans, commonly known as the black-venued spider orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and its unique, spider-like appearance, with a single, narrow flower that is typically dark purple or black. The plant produces a single, erect, lance-shaped leaf and has a short, hairy stem. It blooms during the autumn season, producing a single, intricate flower with a distinctive, elongated labellum.

This species is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats.
