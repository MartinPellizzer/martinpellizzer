Cleyera albopunctata

Cleyera albopunctata, commonly known as the white-spotted holly, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are glossy and have distinct white or pale spots. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cleyera albopunctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
