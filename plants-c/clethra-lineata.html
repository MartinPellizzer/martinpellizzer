Clethra lineata

Clethra lineata, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in cylindrical clusters during the summer months. These flowers are pollinated by insects and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The shrub is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
