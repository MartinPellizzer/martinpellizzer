Crataegus margaretta f. xanthocarpa

Crataegus margaretta f. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the hawthorn genus.

It is characterized by its bright yellow berries, which contrast with the typical red or orange fruits of other hawthorn species. The plant is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of oval, serrated leaves that turn red in the fall.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant fruit display.
