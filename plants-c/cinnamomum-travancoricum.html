Cinnamomum travancoricum

Cinnamomum travancoricum, commonly known as Malabar cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and have a distinct cinnamon-like fragrance.

The bark, which is the primary source of its name, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
