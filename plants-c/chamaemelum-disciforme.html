Chamaemelum disciforme

Chamaemelum disciforme, commonly known as the disc-shaped tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish flowers that resemble small discs. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Chamaemelum disciforme is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
