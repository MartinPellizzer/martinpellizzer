Carex purpureovaginata

Carex purpureovaginata, commonly known as the purple-veined sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves that have purple-tinged veins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands.

Carex purpureovaginata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
