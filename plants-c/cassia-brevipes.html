Cassia brevipes

Cassia brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in spikes. The plant's leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets. Cassia brevipes is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
