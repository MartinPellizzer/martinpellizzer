Cordia brownei

Cordia brownei, commonly known as the red mangrove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. The plant thrives in saline and waterlogged soils, making it an essential component of mangrove ecosystems. Its roots form a complex network that helps stabilize coastal sediments and prevent erosion.

Cordia brownei plays a vital role in providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
