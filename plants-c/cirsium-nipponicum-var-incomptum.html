Cirsium nipponicum var. incomptum

Cirsium nipponicum var. incomptum, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its flowers are composite, consisting of numerous small florets arranged in a rounded head.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
