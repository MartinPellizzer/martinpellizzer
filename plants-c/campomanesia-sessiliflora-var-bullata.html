Campomanesia sessiliflora var. bullata

Campomanesia sessiliflora var. bullata, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when mature, containing a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
