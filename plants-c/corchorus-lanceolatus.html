Corchorus lanceolatus

Corchorus lanceolatus, commonly known as common kochia, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia and North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Its seeds are small and easily dispersed, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.
