Catocoma

Catocoma, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal regions and lowland forests.
