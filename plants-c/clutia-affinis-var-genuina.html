Clutia affinis var. genuina

Clutia affinis var. genuina, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Clutia affinis var. genuina is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
