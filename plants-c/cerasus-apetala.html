Cerasus apetala

Cerasus apetala, commonly known as the white cherry or Japanese white cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically not edible by humans but are used in traditional medicine. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate blossoms and graceful appearance.

The tree has a compact growth habit and is well-suited for urban landscaping and small garden spaces.
