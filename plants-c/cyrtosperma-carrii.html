Cyrtosperma carrii

Cyrtosperma carrii, commonly known as the Carri's sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, round fruits. Its roots contain a starchy substance used in traditional food preparations.

Cyrtosperma carrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
