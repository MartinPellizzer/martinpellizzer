Chrysanthemum macrocarpum

Chrysanthemum macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant has a bushy growth habit with gray-green leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
