Clinopodium ritsaense

Clinopodium ritsaense, commonly known as Ritsa mint, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus, particularly in Georgia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are often fragrant and have a mint-like aroma, which is due to the presence of essential oils.

Clinopodium ritsaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
