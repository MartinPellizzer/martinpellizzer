Chlorophytum limurense

Chlorophytum limurense, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences of white or pink flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in subtropical and tropical regions.
