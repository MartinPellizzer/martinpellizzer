Crataegus floridula

Crataegus floridula, commonly known as the Florida hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and features thorny branches with oval, dark green leaves. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its botanical characteristics include simple, alternate leaves and a woody, often multi-stemmed structure.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.
