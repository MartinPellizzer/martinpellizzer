Centratherum pulchellum

Centratherum pulchellum, commonly known as the purpletop grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple-tinted flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with the inflorescence consisting of a dense cluster of spikelets.

Centratherum pulchellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
