Calamus viridispinus

Calamus viridispinus, commonly known as the green-spined rattan, is a climbing plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, flexible stems and distinctive, serrated leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically green or yellowish in color.

Calamus viridispinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional crafts and as a source of rattan for furniture.
