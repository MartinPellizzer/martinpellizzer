Corytholoma sceptrum

Corytholoma sceptrum, commonly known as the crown cap, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, helmet-like cap formed by the fused bracts of its inflorescence. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in a dense umbel, contributing to its ornamental value.

Corytholoma sceptrum is often found in the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.
