Centaurea pullata var. discolor

Centaurea pullata var. discolor, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive greyish-blue hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs. It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, preferring well-drained environments.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
