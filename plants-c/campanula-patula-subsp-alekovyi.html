Campanula patula subsp. alekovyi

Campanula patula subsp.

alekovyi, commonly known as the Alekov's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and often have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
