Cereus coccineus var. melanacanthus

Cereus coccineus var. melanacanthus, commonly known as the black spined night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often covered in spines, with the variety's name referring to its distinctive black spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pink. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
