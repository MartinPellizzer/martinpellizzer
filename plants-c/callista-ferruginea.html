Callista ferruginea

Callista ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty callista, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, rusty-colored pubescence.

The flowers are borne in clusters and are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds.
