Chamaefilix serrata var. crenulata

Chamaefilix serrata var. crenulata, commonly known as the serrated rockfoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that have a distinctive serrated edge. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
