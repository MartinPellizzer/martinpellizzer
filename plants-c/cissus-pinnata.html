Cissus pinnata

Cissus pinnata, commonly known as the Indian nettle or heartleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor cultivation.
