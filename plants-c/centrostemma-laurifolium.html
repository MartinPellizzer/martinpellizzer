Centrostemma laurifolium

Centrostemma laurifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf centrostemma, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.

Its botanical features include a hairy stem, showy inflorescences, and fruits that are capsule-like, containing numerous tiny seeds.
