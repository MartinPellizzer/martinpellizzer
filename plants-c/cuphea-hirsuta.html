Cuphea hirsuta

Cuphea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, short pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea hirsuta is also known for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators due to its nectar-rich flowers.
