Cymbopogon hamatulus

Cymbopogon hamatulus, commonly known as lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with tall, slender stalks that bear small, pale blue or white flowers. Its rhizomes and lower stems are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The essential oils extracted from the leaves are widely used in aromatherapy and as a flavoring agent.
