Cryptandra eriocephala

Cryptandra eriocephala, commonly known as the woolly cryptandra, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
