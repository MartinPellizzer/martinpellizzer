Cleyera mexicana

Cleyera mexicana, commonly known as Mexican holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae, which includes true hollies. The plant features small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are inconspicuous, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are edible for some bird species.

Cleyera mexicana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
