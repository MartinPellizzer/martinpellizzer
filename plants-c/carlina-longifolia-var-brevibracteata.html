Carlina longifolia var. brevibracteata

Carlina longifolia var. brevibracteata, commonly known as the short-bract edgeworthia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stem and clusters of yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. Its flowers are composed of numerous small florets, with the outer ones being sterile and forming a yellow disk.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
