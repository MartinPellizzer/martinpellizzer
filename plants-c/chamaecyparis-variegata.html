Chamaecyparis variegata

Chamaecyparis variegata, commonly known as the variegated cedar or Japanese white cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are typically a mix of green and white or silver, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, round cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and its bark is reddish-gray and peels in thin, papery layers.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
