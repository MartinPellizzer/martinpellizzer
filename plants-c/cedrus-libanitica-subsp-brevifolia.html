Cedrus libanitica subsp. brevifolia

Cedrus libanitica subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as the Lebanese cedar, is a subspecies of the cedar tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, conical shape and dense, evergreen foliage.

The tree produces small, blue-gray cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against wildfires and harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its durable wood and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.
