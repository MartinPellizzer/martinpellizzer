Carduus × theriotii nothosubsp. cyrneus

Carduus × theriotii nothosubsp.

cyrneus, commonly known as the Cyrene thistle, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, primarily Carduus theriotii and Carduus cyrneus. It is a perennial herb with robust, spiny stems and large, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow composite flower heads that are typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to regions of southern Europe and thrives in dry, rocky, or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
