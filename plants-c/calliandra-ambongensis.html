Calliandra ambongensis

Calliandra ambongensis, commonly known as the Ambongensis calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Calliandra ambongensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
