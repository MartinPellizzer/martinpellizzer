Cereus nitens

Cereus nitens, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and nocturnal blooming habit. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and remain open for only a few hours. Its stems are thick and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Cereus nitens is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and unique flowering behavior.
