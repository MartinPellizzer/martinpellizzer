Chenopodium fasciculosum var. schimperi

Chenopodium fasciculosum var. schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian goosefoot, is a shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and glaucous, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which are typically arranged in clusters. Its seeds are small and oval, with a hard coat that aids in dispersal by wind or animals.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
