Convolvulus goyderi

Convolvulus goyderi, commonly known as Goyder's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, and it has distinctive heart-shaped leaves. It is known for its climbing habit, using tendrils to support itself along fences or shrubs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in dry environments.
