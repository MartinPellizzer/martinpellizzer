Carex vulgaris var. atra

Carex vulgaris var. atra, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark, almost blackish stems and leaves, which give it its distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers.

Carex vulgaris var. atra is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
