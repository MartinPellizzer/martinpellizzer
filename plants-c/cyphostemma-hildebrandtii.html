Cyphostemma hildebrandtii

Cyphostemma hildebrandtii, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, pale green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellow, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, and it is often found in rocky or arid environments. Cyphostemma hildebrandtii is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
