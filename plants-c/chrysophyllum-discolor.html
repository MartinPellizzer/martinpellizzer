Chrysophyllum discolor

Chrysophyllum discolor, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The fruit is a smooth, yellow or reddish-orange berry that turns bright red when ripe, containing a sweet, edible pulp with a single large seed. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible fruit.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
