Capparis sepiaria var. fischeri

Capparis sepiaria var. fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's caper, is a variant of the caper plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its prickly stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible, green, sour-tasting fruits that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a slightly rough texture.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible parts.
