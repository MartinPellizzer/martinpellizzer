Campanula marsupiiflora

Campanula marsupiiflora, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive, soft-textured appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with a similar fuzzy coating.

Campanula marsupiiflora is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
