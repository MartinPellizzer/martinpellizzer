Cominsia

Cominsia, commonly known as the African buttercup, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

These plants are native to various regions in Africa and are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs. Cominsia species are known for their ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and are often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

They play an important role in their ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
