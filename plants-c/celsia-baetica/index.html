Celsia Baetica

Celsia baetica is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is known for its striking blue flowers. This species was first described by the botanist Celsus in the 1st century. Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is now critically endangered. Conservation efforts are underway to protect and preserve this unique botanical gem.
