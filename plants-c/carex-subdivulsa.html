Carex subdivulsa

Carex subdivulsa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green to brown in color.

Carex subdivulsa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
