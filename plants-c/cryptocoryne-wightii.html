Cryptocoryne wightii

Cryptocoryne wightii, commonly known as the Wight's cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a popular choice for freshwater aquariums due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically dark green in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. It is often used as a background plant in aquariums because of its tall, upright growth habit.

Cryptocoryne wightii is also valued for its role in biological filtration and its aesthetic appeal in planted tanks.
