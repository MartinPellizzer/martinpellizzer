Ceanothus floribundus

Ceanothus floribundus, commonly known as the California lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, fragrant clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that remain on the shrub throughout the winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
