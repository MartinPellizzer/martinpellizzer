Croton hadriani

Croton hadriani, commonly known as the golden croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage, which often displays shades of yellow, green, and red. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Its leaves are typically broad and palmately lobed, adding a dramatic visual element to any landscape.

Croton hadriani thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
