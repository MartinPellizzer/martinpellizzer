Crassula virgata

Crassula virgata, commonly known as the "Little Baby Crassula," is a compact, succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that form a low, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Crassula virgata is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive appearance.
