Chamaefistula speciosa

Chamaefistula speciosa, commonly known as the showy boxwood, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking blue-green color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is often used in landscaping for its compact form and seasonal interest.
