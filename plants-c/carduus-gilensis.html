Carduus gilensis

Carduus gilensis, commonly known as Gil's thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Turkey and Iran. The plant is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish composite flower heads. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Gil's thistle has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
