Cactus crassispinus

Cactus crassispinus, commonly known as the thick-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, sharp spines, which help to reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny form. It is a succulent, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in dry environments.

Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for desert habitats.
