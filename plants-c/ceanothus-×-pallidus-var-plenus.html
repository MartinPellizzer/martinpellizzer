Ceanothus × pallidus var. plenus

Ceanothus × pallidus var. plenus, commonly known as the full-flowered white ceanothus, is a hybrid shrub native to California.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its profuse display of white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in arid and Mediterranean climates.
