Campanula heterophylla

Campanula heterophylla, commonly known as the variable bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its varying leaf shapes, which can range from basal rosettes to alternate leaves along the stem. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple, during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula heterophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
