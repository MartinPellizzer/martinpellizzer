Catalpa × erubescens f. purpurea

Catalpa × erubescens f. purpurea, commonly known as the Purpleleaf Catalpa, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish foliage, which adds ornamental value to gardens and landscapes. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. Its heart-shaped leaves are large and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The tree is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
