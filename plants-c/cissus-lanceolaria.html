Cissus lanceolaria

Cissus lanceolaria, commonly known as the arrowhead creeper, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
