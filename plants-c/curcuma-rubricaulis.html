Curcuma rubricaulis

Curcuma rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed turmeric, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking red-orange rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive red calyx.

Curcuma rubricaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential medicinal uses similar to other turmeric species.
