Carex flava subsp. marssonii

Carex flava subsp.

marssonii, commonly known as yellow sedges, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae. It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and light green, with a distinct midrib.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing achenes that are dispersed by wind.
