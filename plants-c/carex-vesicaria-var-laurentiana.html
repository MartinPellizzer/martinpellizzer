Carex vesicaria var. laurentiana

Carex vesicaria var. laurentiana, commonly known as Laurentian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
