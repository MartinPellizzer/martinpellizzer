Cardamine pacensis

Cardamine pacensis, commonly known as Pacific watercress, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. It has small, alternate leaves that are usually kidney-shaped with a hairy texture.

Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
