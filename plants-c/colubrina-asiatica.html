Colubrina asiatica

Colubrina asiatica, commonly known as Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Asia.

It features opposite, simple, and elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, urn-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by vibrant purple to blue berries. These berries are edible for birds but toxic to humans and livestock.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
