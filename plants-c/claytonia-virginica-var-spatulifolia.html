Claytonia virginica var. spatulifolia

Claytonia virginica var. spatulifolia, commonly known as the spoonleaf chickweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its succulent leaves and delicate flowers make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
