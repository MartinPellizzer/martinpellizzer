Centaurea napulifera subsp. tuberosa

Centaurea napulifera subsp.

tuberosa, commonly known as the Tuscan thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stem and striking purple to pink flower heads.

The plant produces a distinctive, tuberous root system that contributes to its resilience in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
