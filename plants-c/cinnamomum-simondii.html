Cinnamomum simondii

Cinnamomum simondii, commonly known as Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum cassia, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that is often used as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct cinnamon scent.

The tree is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its economic importance in the spice trade.
