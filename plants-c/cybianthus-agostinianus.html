Cybianthus agostinianus

Cybianthus agostinianus, commonly known as the Agostinian's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cybianthus agostinianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
