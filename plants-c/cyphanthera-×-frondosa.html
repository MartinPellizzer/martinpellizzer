Cyphanthera × frondosa

Cyphanthera × frondosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved cyphanthera, is a hybrid plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
