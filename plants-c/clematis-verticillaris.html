Clematis verticillaris

Clematis verticillaris, commonly known as the clustered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are opposite or whorled along the stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, blue, or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its climbing habit and ability to thrive in partial shade make it a popular choice for gardeners.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
