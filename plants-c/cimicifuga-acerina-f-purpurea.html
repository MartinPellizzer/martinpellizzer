Cimicifuga acerina f. purpurea

Cimicifuga acerina f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered bugbane, is a striking perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white to pinkish-white flowers. The plant features large, palmately compound leaves that are often deeply lobed, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and naturalized landscapes.
