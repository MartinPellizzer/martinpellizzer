Crataegus × subsphaerica nothosubsp. fallacina

Crataegus × subsphaerica nothosubsp.

fallacina, commonly known as the fallacina hawthorn, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its small, thorned branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but often have a tart flavor. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in well-drained soils.

Botanically, it exhibits traits of its parent species, Crataegus subsphaerica and Crataegus fallacina, making it a notable example of natural hybridization in hawthorns.
