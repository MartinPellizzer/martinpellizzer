Carthamus lucens

Carthamus lucens, commonly known as the Persian saffron crocus, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with yellow stigmas that resemble saffron threads. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in dyeing and medicinal applications.
