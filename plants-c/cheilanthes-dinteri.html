Cheilanthes dinteri

Cheilanthes dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's rock fern, is a species of fern native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and is adapted to survive in harsh, rocky environments. The plant produces small, spore-bearing fronds that are typically found on exposed rock surfaces. It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in low-nutrient, well-drained soils.
