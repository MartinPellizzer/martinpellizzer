Coccinia sessilifolia var. major

Coccinia sessilifolia var. major, commonly known as the giant yellow eggplant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and produces small, yellow, egg-shaped fruits that are edible. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a distinctive, fleshy fruit with a mildly sweet taste.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary use in local cuisines.
