Ceropegia elongata

Ceropegia elongata, commonly known as the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a favored ornamental plant.
