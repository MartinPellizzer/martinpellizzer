Cynoglossum malabaricum

Cynoglossum malabaricum, commonly known as Malabar mouse-ear, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. It has a compact growth habit with a short, stout stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Cynoglossum malabaricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
