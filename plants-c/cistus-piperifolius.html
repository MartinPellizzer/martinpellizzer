Cistus piperifolius

Cistus piperifolius, commonly known as the pepperleaf rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow or white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive peppery scent when crushed.

Cistus piperifolius is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
