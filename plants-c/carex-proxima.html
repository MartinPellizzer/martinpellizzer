Carex proxima

Carex proxima, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves, which are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Carex proxima is often found in wet or moist soils, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
