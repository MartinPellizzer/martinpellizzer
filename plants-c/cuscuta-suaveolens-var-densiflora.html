Cuscuta suaveolens var. densiflora

Cuscuta suaveolens var. densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a dense arrangement of tiny, thread-like stems that wrap around host plants. The plant is typically found in open areas where it can attach to a variety of host species, including grasses and shrubs. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through mechanical means, allowing it to colonize new areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and health of its host plants.
