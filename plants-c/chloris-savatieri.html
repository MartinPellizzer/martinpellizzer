Chloris savatieri

Chloris savatieri, commonly known as the slender grass or blue grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Chloris savatieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy and drought-resistant qualities.
