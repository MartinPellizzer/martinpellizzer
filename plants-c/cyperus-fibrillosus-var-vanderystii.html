Cyperus fibrillosus var. vanderystii

Cyperus fibrillosus var. vanderystii, commonly known as the Vanderyst's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to moist environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-sided spikelets, which are often adorned with fine, thread-like hairs.

This variant is distinguished by its unique foliage and habitat preferences, making it a notable species within the Cyperus genus.
