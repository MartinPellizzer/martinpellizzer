Cajanus elongatus

Cajanus elongatus, commonly known as the longpod groundnut, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated pods that contain small, edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

Cajanus elongatus is valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
