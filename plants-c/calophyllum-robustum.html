Calophyllum robustum

Calophyllum robustum, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The tree produces yellow flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its bark and wood are valued for their rich, aromatic scent and durable qualities.

Calophyllum robustum is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
