Carduus hamulosus subsp. spinigerus

Carduus hamulosus subsp.

spinigerus, commonly known as the prickly sowthistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this species thrives in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant has a taproot system and produces numerous seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
