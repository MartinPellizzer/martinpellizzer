Crambe orientalis f. aculeolata

Crambe orientalis f. aculeolata, commonly known as the spiny crambe, is a variant of the oriental crambe, characterized by its distinctive spines along the stem and leaves.

It is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often have a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
