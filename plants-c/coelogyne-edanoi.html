Coelogyne edanoi

Coelogyne edanoi, commonly known as the Bornean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Coelogyne edanoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
