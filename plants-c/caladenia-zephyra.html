Caladenia zephyra

Caladenia zephyra, commonly known as the Cloudy Spider-Orchid, is an orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to yellowish flowers that resemble spiders. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils within open forests or heathlands. It produces a single, narrow leaf and blooms between September and November.

Caladenia zephyra is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological specificity.
