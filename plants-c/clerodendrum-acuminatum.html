Clerodendrum acuminatum

Clerodendrum acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a pointed tip, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract birds and insects, making it a valuable addition to biodiversity-rich gardens.
