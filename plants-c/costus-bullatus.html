Costus bullatus

Costus bullatus, commonly known as the bullate costus, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a member of the Costaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by colorful, fleshy bracts that can range in color from red to orange.

Costus bullatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
