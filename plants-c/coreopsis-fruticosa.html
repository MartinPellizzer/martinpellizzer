Coreopsis fruticosa

Coreopsis fruticosa, commonly known as the greenhead coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its bright yellow flowers. The blooms, which appear in late spring to early summer, are composed of ray flowers surrounding a central disk, making it visually appealing in gardens.

This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy and low-maintenance addition to native plant landscapes.
