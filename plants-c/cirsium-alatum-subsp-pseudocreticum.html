Cirsium alatum subsp. pseudocreticum

Cirsium alatum subsp.

pseudocreticum, commonly known as the silverleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces purple to pink flower heads composed of many small florets, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
