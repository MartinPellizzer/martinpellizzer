Conanthus densus

Conanthus densus, commonly known as the dense conanthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which helps it thrive in high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs, providing protection against harsh weather conditions.

Conanthus densus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
