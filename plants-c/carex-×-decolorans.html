Carex × decolorans

Carex × decolorans, commonly known as the pink sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink or reddish in color, adding visual interest to its appearance.

Carex × decolorans is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
