Cochlearia cyclocarpa

Cochlearia cyclocarpa, commonly known as the curled-cup saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in a tight, cup-like cluster. Its leaves are typically basal, rosette-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Cochlearia cyclocarpa is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season, blooming in late summer.
