Cheiranthus coccineus

Cheiranthus coccineus, commonly known as the crimson candytuft, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, and the plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
