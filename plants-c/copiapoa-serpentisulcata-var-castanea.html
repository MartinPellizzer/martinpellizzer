Copiapoa serpentisulcata var. castanea

Copiapoa serpentisulcata var. castanea, commonly known as the Castanea Copiapoa, is a rare cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and distinctive, wavy ribs that give it a unique, snake-like appearance. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in the arid environment.

This variety is highly sought after by collectors due to its rarity and striking morphology.
