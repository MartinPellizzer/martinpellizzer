Cirsium × affine

Cirsium × affine, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

Cirsium × affine is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
