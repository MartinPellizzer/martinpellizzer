Carthamus lanatus subsp. creticus

Carthamus lanatus subsp.

creticus, commonly known as the Cretan thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Greece. It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny stems and yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
