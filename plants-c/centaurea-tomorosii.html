Centaurea tomorosii

Centaurea tomorosii, commonly known as the Corsican centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with prominent central disk florets.

Centaurea tomorosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in wildflower gardens.
