Campanula linarioides

Campanula linarioides, commonly known as the lesser bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Campanula linarioides is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
