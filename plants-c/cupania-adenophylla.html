Cupania adenophylla

Cupania adenophylla, commonly known as the hairy cupania, is a woody shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are often subtended by bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cupania adenophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
