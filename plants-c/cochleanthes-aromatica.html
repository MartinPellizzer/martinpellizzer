Cochleanthes aromatica

Cochleanthes aromatica, commonly known as the aromatic cochleanthes, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its unique fragrance and beauty, it is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
