Convolvulus leptocladus

Convolvulus leptocladus, commonly known as the slenderleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus leptocladus is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
