Cystium sesquimetrale

Cystium sesquimetrale, commonly known as the creeping thimbleweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by small, bladder-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Cystium sesquimetrale is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in open woodlands.
