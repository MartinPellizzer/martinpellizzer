Caryophyllus superbus

Caryophyllus superbus, commonly known as the noble carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, brightly colored flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its flowers are known for their strong, spicy fragrance, which is derived from essential oils produced in the petals.

Caryophyllus superbus is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in perfumery and herbal medicine.
