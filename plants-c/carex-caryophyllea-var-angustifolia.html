Carex caryophyllea var. angustifolia

Carex caryophyllea var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas, stream sides, and wet meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
