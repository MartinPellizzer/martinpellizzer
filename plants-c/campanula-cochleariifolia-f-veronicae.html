Campanula cochleariifolia f. veronicae

Campanula cochleariifolia f. veronicae, commonly known as the Veronica's bellflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in a fuzzy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and charming, clustered blooms.
