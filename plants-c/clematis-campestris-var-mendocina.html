Clematis campestris var. mendocina

Clematis campestris var. mendocina, commonly known as the Mendocino clematis, is a deciduous vine native to California.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, recurved form. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often climbing through shrubs or trees in its natural habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
