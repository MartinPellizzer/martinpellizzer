Calyptranthes musciflora var. spathulata

Calyptranthes musciflora var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leaf calyptranthes, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, elliptical to ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the wet season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
