Clianthus magnificus

Clianthus magnificus, commonly known as the New Zealand glory of the valley, is a striking flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant red and yellow floral display. The plant features compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces striking, pendulous flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in open grasslands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and public spaces worldwide.
