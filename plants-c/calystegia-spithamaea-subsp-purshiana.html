Calystegia spithamaea subsp. purshiana

Calystegia spithamaea subsp.

purshiana, commonly known as the western morning glory, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a trailing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in prairies and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a garden favorite and an invasive species in some regions.
