Carex goodenowii var. melaena

Carex goodenowii var. melaena, commonly known as black-stemmed sedges, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, almost black stems that contrast with its grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
