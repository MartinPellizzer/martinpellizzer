Cordia stuhlmannii

Cordia stuhlmannii, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually hairless. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with a subtropical climate.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
