Coniogramme japonica var. gracilis

Coniogramme japonica var. gracilis, commonly known as the Japanese feather fern, is a delicate and ornamental fern species native to Japan.

It features finely divided, lacy fronds that give it a soft, ethereal appearance, making it a popular choice for shady garden settings. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its fronds, which are typically covered by a protective indusium. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both cultivated gardens and natural habitats.
