Coryphantha chihuahuensis

Coryphantha chihuahuensis, commonly known as the Chihuahua prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and typically appears in the fall.

Coryphantha chihuahuensis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
