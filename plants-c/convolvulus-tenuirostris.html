Convolvulus tenuirostris

Convolvulus tenuirostris, commonly known as narrow-beaked morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Africa and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Convolvulus tenuirostris is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
