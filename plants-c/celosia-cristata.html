Celosia cristata

Celosia cristata, commonly known as the cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, plume-like inflorescence that resembles a rooster's comb, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms in shades of red, orange, and pink. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of growth.

Celosia cristata thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
