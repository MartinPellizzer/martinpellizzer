Canna glauca

Canna glauca, commonly known as the blue canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a bluish tint, and its showy flowers come in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant is a member of the Cannaceae family and is characterized by its robust, rhizomatous growth habit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for water gardens and tropical landscapes.

Canna glauca is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
