Carum furcatum

Carum furcatum, commonly known as the forked caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, carroty scent.

The fruit is a dry, two-lobed capsule that contains tiny, aromatic seeds.
