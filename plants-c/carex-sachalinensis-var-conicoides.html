Carex sachalinensis var. conicoides

Carex sachalinensis var. conicoides, commonly known as the conicoides sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
