Chrysocoma punctulata

Chrysocoma punctulata, commonly known as the African snowdrop, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens and botanical collections.
