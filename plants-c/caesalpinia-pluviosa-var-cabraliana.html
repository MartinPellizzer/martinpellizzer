Caesalpinia pluviosa var. cabraliana

Caesalpinia pluviosa var. cabraliana, commonly known as the Cabral's rain tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, bright yellow floral clusters that bloom throughout the rainy season. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid tropical and subtropical regions. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and unique leaf structure, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
