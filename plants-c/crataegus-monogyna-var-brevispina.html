Crataegus monogyna var. brevispina

Crataegus monogyna var. brevispina, commonly known as the common hawthorn or simply hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits called haws in the autumn, which are edible but often used in jams and wines.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
