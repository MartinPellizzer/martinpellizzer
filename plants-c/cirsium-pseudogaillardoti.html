Cirsium pseudogaillardoti

Cirsium pseudogaillardoti, commonly known as the Sierra California thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as chaparral and grasslands. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the flowers are arranged in a dense, rounded cluster.

Cirsium pseudogaillardoti plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or open areas.
