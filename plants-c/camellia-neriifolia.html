Camellia neriifolia

Camellia neriifolia, commonly known as the black leaf camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that turn a striking reddish-brown in the fall. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It prefers partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

Camellia neriifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal foliage and floral display.
