Cynometra bauhiniifolia var. grandiflora

Cynometra bauhiniifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a shade tree in tropical regions.
