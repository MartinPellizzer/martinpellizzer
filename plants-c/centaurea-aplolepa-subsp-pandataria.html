Centaurea aplolepa subsp. pandataria

Centaurea aplolepa subsp.

pandataria, commonly known as the Narrowleaf Cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flower heads.

The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for petals, and its leaves are narrow and linear. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
