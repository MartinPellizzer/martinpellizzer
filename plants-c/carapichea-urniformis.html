Carapichea urniformis

Carapichea urniformis, commonly known as the urn-shaped acanthus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its distinctive, urn-shaped inflorescences. The plant features palmate leaves with a velvety texture and produces showy, spiny flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Carapichea urniformis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
