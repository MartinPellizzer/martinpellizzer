Cimicifuga foetida f. femina

Cimicifuga foetida f. femina, commonly known as the White Actaea, is a striking cultivar of the Actaea genus.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy white flowers that appear in late summer. The plant produces compound, pinnate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
