Campanula valdensis var. scheuchzeri

Campanula valdensis var. scheuchzeri, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Central Europe.

It produces bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in mountain meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Campanula valdensis var. scheuchzeri is also noted for its resilience to cold temperatures and its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments.
