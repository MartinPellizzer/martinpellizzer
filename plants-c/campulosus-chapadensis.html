Campulosus chapadensis

Campulosus chapadensis, commonly known as the Brazilian mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
