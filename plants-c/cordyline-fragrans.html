Cordyline fragrans

Cordyline fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle scent to the air. Its foliage is popular in landscaping for its visual appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Cordyline fragrans is also valued for its cultural significance in traditional Polynesian societies.
