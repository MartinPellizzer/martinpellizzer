Chusquea uruguayensis

Chusquea uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay grass, is a species of bamboo native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tall, slender culms and dense, feathery foliage. The plant thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it an important component of local ecosystems.

Chusquea uruguayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional basket-making in some regions.
