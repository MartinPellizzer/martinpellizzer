Cryptocarya boldus

Cryptocarya boldus, commonly known as boldus tree or boldus wood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are rich in essential oils and have a distinctive camphor-like scent.

Cryptocarya boldus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
