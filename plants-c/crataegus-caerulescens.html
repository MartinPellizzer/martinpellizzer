Crataegus caerulescens

Crataegus caerulescens, commonly known as the blue hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple berries, which are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Crataegus caerulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
