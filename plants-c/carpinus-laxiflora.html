Carpinus laxiflora

Carpinus laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in catkins during the spring, followed by small, spiny fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
