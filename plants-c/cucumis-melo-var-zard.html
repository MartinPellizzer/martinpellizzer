Cucumis melo var. zard

Cucumis melo var. zard, commonly known as Persian melon, is a cultivar of the cantaloupe species characterized by its distinctive netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to Central Asia, with historical cultivation dating back thousands of years. The plant is a climbing vine that produces large, round fruits with a pale green to yellowish exterior. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

The fruit is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary applications due to its high water content and mild flavor.
