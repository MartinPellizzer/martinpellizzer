Carex sibirica

Carex sibirica, commonly known as Siberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of wetland habitats.
