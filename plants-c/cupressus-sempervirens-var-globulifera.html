Cupressus sempervirens var. globulifera

Cupressus sempervirens var. globulifera, commonly known as the globe cypress, is a compact, slow-growing coniferous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical shape, dense foliage, and small, scale-like leaves that give it a lush, evergreen appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique form and resilience make it a favored plant in garden settings.
