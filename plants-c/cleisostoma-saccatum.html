Cleisostoma saccatum

Cleisostoma saccatum, commonly known as the sac-like cleisostoma, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a sac-like pouch that encloses the reproductive organs. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are ovate and arranged in a rosette pattern, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and fragrant, blooming in clusters during the wet season.
