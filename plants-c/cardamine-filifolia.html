Cardamine filifolia

Cardamine filifolia, commonly known as the slender watercress, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes. It produces small, green fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Cardamine filifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
