Carduncellus monspelliensium var. valentinus

Carduncellus monspelliensium var. valentinus, commonly known as the Valentine thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardens, though it requires careful handling due to its prickly foliage.
