Catappa

Catappa, commonly known as Indian almond or Malabar chestnut, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves with alternate arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, round, woody fruits that split open to release seeds. Its leaves are often used in aquariums for their beneficial properties in water treatment.

The tree is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
