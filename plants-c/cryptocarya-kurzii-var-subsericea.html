Cryptocarya kurzii var. subsericea

Cryptocarya kurzii var. subsericea, commonly known as the velvet ash or narrow-leaved cryptocarya, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Australia, particularly found in the eastern regions of Queensland and New South Wales. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. It produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that matures to a dark color, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
