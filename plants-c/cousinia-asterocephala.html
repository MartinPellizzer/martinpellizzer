Cousinia asterocephala

Cousinia asterocephala, commonly known as the star-headed cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a dense, star-like cluster at the top of its stems. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Cousinia asterocephala is often found in steppe and desert ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
