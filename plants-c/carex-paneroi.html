Carex paneroi

Carex paneroi, commonly known as the Panero's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in wetlands and moist soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires consistent moisture.

Due to its ecological importance, it is often used in wetland restoration projects.
