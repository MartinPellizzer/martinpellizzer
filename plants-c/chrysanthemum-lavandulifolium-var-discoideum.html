Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium var. discoideum

Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium var. discoideum, commonly known as the fragrant chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant is known for its strong, lavender-like scent, which is released when the leaves are crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
