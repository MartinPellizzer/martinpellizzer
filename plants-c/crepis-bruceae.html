Crepis bruceae

Crepis bruceae, commonly known as the Bruce's hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Crepis bruceae is known for its resilience to cold climates and is often found in meadows and grasslands at high altitudes.
