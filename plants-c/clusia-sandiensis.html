Clusia sandiensis

Clusia sandiensis, commonly known as the sandia or sandia plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Clusia sandiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
