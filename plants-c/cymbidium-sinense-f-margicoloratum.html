Cymbidium sinense f. margicoloratum

Cymbidium sinense f. margicoloratum, commonly known as the Margicolored Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of orchid-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, with a distinctive coloration pattern on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This hybrid form is prized by horticulturists for its unique floral display and adaptability to specific growing conditions.
