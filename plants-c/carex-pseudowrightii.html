Carex pseudowrightii

Carex pseudowrightii, commonly known as the eastern woodland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often clustered at the top of the plant.

Carex pseudowrightii plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
