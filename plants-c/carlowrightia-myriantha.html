Carlowrightia myriantha

Carlowrightia myriantha, commonly known as the broad-leafed hibiscus, is a striking flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with opposite branching, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
