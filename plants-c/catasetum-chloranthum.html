Catasetum chloranthum

Catasetum chloranthum, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant green petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are notable for their bright green coloration and the presence of specialized structures called "pollinia," which are involved in its pollination mechanism.

Catasetum chloranthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
