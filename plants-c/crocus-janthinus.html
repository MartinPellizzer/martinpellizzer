Crocus janthinus

Crocus janthinus, commonly known as the violet crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to April. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to well-drained soils in full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.

The species is also valued for its historical use in dye production.
