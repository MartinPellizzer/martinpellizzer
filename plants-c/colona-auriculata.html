Colona auriculata

Colona auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate colona, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and woody stems that can grow quite tall.

Colona auriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
