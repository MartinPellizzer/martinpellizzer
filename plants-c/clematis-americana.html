Clematis americana

Clematis americana, commonly known as the American clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained soil. Its vines can grow up to 20 feet long, making it suitable for trellises or arbors.

Clematis americana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its spring blossoms.
