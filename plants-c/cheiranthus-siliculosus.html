Cheiranthus siliculosus

Cheiranthus siliculosus, commonly known as the slender wallflower, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. Native to southern Europe and western Asia, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
