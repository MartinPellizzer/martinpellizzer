Campanula napuligera var. longisepala

Campanula napuligera var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its sepals are notably elongated, giving it its specific variety name.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
