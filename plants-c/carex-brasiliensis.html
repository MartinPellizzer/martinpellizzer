Carex brasiliensis

Carex brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense, grass-like clusters. Its flowering structure consists of small, inconspicuous spikes that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex brasiliensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
