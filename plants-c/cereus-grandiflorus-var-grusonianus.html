Cereus grandiflorus var. grusonianus

Cereus grandiflorus var. grusonianus, commonly known as the Gruson's night bloomer, is a cacti species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent collections.
