Crataegus × robesoniana

Crataegus × robesoniana, commonly known as the Robeson's hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
