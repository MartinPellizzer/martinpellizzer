Carex longiligula

Carex longiligula, commonly known as the long-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in areas with poor to moderately fertile soils.

Carex longiligula plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
