Cosmiza coccinea

Cosmiza coccinea, commonly known as the red cosmos, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, pink, or white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual display to gardens. The plant is an annual herb with slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cosmiza coccinea thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
