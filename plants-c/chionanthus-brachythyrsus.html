Chionanthus brachythyrsus

Chionanthus brachythyrsus, commonly known as the short-thyrse chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a dry, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Chionanthus brachythyrsus is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.
