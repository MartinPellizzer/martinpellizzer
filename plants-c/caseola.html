Caseola

Caseola, commonly known as the mountain bluet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its delicate blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Caseola has small, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It plays an important role in pollination and is a symbol of resilience in harsh environments.
