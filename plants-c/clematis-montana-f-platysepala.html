Clematis montana f. platysepala

Clematis montana f. platysepala, commonly known as the flat-sepaled clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple with showy, flat sepals. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis montana f. platysepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or fences.
