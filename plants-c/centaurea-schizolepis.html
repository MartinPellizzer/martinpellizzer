Centaurea schizolepis

Centaurea schizolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Centaurea schizolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
