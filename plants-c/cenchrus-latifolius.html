Cenchrus latifolius

Cenchrus latifolius, commonly known as broadleaf sandgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its robust, grass-like appearance with broad, flat leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to thrive in poor soil environments. It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its fibrous structure and resilience make it an important component of desert and coastal ecosystems.
