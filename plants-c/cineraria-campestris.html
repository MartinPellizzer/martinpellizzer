Cineraria campestris

Cineraria campestris, commonly known as the field cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and coastal areas. It has hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in the spring.

Cineraria campestris is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
