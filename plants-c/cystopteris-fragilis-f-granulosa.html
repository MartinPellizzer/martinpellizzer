Cystopteris fragilis f. granulosa

Cystopteris fragilis f. granulosa, commonly known as the granular maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is native to regions of eastern Asia, including China and Japan. The plant's fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, finely serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fronds are fragile and easily broken, contributing to its common name.
