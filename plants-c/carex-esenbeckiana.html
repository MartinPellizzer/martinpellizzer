Carex esenbeckiana

Carex esenbeckiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered flower spikes during the summer months.

Carex esenbeckiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
