Commelina congesta

Commelina congesta, commonly known as the clustered spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale blue flowers with three petals and three sepals. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of flowers, which gives it its common name.

Commelina congesta is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and swampy areas.
