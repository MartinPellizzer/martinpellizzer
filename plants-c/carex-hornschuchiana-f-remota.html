Carex hornschuchiana f. remota

Carex hornschuchiana f. remota, commonly known as the remote sedge, is a variant of the hornschuchiana sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects and wetland gardens.
