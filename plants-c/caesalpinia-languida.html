Caesalpinia languida

Caesalpinia languida, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain flat, edible seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits.
