Callicarpa dichotoma

Callicarpa dichotoma, commonly known as the red beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that give way to vibrant red berries in the fall. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its berries are a popular food source for birds and wildlife.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
