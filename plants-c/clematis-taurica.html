Clematis taurica

Clematis taurica, commonly known as the Caucasian Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the Caucasus region.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its climbing habit and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to landscapes.
