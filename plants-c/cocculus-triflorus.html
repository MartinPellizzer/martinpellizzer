Cocculus triflorus

Cocculus triflorus, commonly known as devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted, and often spiny stems that resemble a backbone, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, warty fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Cocculus triflorus is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties, though its use is limited due to its toxic compounds.
