Capsicum indicum var. longum

Capsicum indicum var. longum, commonly known as the bird pepper or long pepper, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruits that range in color from green to red when mature. The plant is an annual herb with simple, alternate leaves and produces small, white flowers. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent flavor.

The botanical features of this plant make it distinct from other Capsicum species, particularly in its fruit morphology and growth habit.
