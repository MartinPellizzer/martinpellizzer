Cassia subtriflora

Cassia subtriflora, commonly known as the three-flowered cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, featuring compound leaves with three to five pairs of leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the genus Cassia. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
