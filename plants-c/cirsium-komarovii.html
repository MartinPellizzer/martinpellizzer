Cirsium komarovii

Cirsium komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
