Cerasus duracina var. mamillaris

Cerasus duracina var. mamillaris, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or miniature cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It features small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red berries. The plant has a dense, bushy growth habit with dark green, glossy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens and as a container plant.
