Combretum ternifolium

Combretum ternifolium, commonly known as the three-leaf combretum, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lythraceae and is characterized by its opposite, trifoliate leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riverine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
