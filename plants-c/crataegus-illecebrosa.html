Crataegus illecebrosa

Crataegus illecebrosa, commonly known as the sweet thimbleberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Crataegus illecebrosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
