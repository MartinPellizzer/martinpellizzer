Cornus mas f. nana

Cornus mas f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf cornel cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has a rounded growth habit with dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its seasonal interest and hardy nature.

This cultivar is valued for its compact size and ornamental value in landscaping.
