Convolvulus glaucescens

Convolvulus glaucescens, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking blue to purple flowers with five petals. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a desirable and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
