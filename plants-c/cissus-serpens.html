Cissus serpens

Cissus serpens, commonly known as the stonecrop fig or snake's head, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and ability to climb using tendrils. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

Cissus serpens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
