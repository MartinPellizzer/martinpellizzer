Campanula rotundifolia var. parviflora

Campanula rotundifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are rounded and hairy, with a distinctive bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
