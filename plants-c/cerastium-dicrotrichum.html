Cerastium dicrotrichum

Cerastium dicrotrichum, commonly known as the two-bristled mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Cerastium dicrotrichum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local insect populations.
