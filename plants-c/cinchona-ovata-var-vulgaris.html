Cinchona ovata var. vulgaris

Cinchona ovata var. vulgaris, commonly known as the Peruvian bark, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the alkaloid quinine, which has been historically used to treat malaria.
