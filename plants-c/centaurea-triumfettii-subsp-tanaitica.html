Centaurea triumfettii subsp. tanaitica

Centaurea triumfettii subsp.

tanaitica, commonly known as the Tanaitic knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous areas.

The plant features slender, branched stems and produces small, bright yellow flowers with purple markings. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is characterized by its adaptability to harsh environments and is of interest in botanical studies due to its limited distribution.
