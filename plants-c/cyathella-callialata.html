Cyathella callialata

Cyathella callialata, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and upright.

Cyathella callialata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
