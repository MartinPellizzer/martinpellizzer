Cattleya alexandrae var. maculata

Cattleya alexandrae var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a white or pale pink base with distinctive red or purple spots. The plant produces pendulous blooms that are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in its natural habitat.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and resilience in cultivation.
