Cassia jamaicensis

Cassia jamaicensis, commonly known as Jamaican cassia, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia jamaicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
