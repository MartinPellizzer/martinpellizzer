Corypha japonica

Corypha japonica, commonly known as the Japanese palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces a unique inflorescence that resembles a spike, with both male and female flowers arranged in separate clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Despite its ornamental value, it is relatively slow-growing and can be challenging to cultivate outside its native range.
