Cichorium divaricatum

Cichorium divaricatum, commonly known as the slender chicory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and slightly lobed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
