Carex acuta f. pedicellata

Carex acuta f. pedicellata, commonly known as the slender spike sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex acuta.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with short, pedicellate (stalked) flowers. It thrives in wet or moist soils, typically found in marshes, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
