Calamagrostis arundinacea subsp. monticola

Calamagrostis arundinacea subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the mountain reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
