Curcuma raktakanta

Curcuma raktakanta, commonly known as the red turmeric or crimson ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is valued for its vibrant orange-red rhizomes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, and its flowers are typically orange or red, depending on the variety. The rhizomes are rich in curcuminoids, which contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
