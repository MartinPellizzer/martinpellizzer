Castellia

Castellia, commonly known as the castellia or castellia glauca, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically blue or violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Castellia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
