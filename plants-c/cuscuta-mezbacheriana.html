Cuscuta mezbacheriana

Cuscuta mezbacheriana, commonly known as the desert dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a unique ability to attach to various host species, including shrubs and trees. The plant's stems are covered in tiny, hair-like structures that help it grip its host. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the growth and survival of its host plants.
