Cercocoma wallichii

Cercocoma wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan date palm, is a small, evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Palmae and is characterized by its clustered, fan-shaped leaves and small, edible fruits. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing at high altitudes. Its fruit, resembling a date, is used both for food and traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
