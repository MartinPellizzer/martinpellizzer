Conophyton

Conophyton, commonly known as the desert stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically blooming in spring. Its stems are often low-growing and can form dense mats, which help reduce soil erosion.

Conophyton is well adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient species in harsh desert conditions.
