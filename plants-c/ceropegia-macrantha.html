Ceropegia macrantha

Ceropegia macrantha, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or mountain arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. The plant features succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that help it store water, adapting it to arid environments. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive, intricate structure, attracting specific pollinators.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
