Castanea crenata var. pulchella

Castanea crenata var. pulchella, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut or sweet chestnut, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces catkin-like male flowers and female flowers in separate clusters, followed by spiny, cup-like husks enclosing edible nuts.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible yield.
