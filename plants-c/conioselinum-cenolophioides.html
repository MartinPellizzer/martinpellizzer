Conioselinum cenolophioides

Conioselinum cenolophioides, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it blooms during the spring and early summer.

Conioselinum cenolophioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
