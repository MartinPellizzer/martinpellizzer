Capraria ericacea

Capraria ericacea, commonly known as the heath capraria, is a flowering plant native to the western and southern regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Capraria ericacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
