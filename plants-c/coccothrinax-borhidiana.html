Coccothrinax borhidiana

Coccothrinax borhidiana, commonly known as the Borhidian palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and pinnate leaves that are often feather-like in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits, which are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

This palm is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
