Celtis biondii

Celtis biondii, commonly known as the Italian hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by round, greenish fruits that turn reddish when mature. Celtis biondii is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

It is often used in landscaping for its attractive form and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
