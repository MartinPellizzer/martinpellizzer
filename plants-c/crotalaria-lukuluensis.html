Crotalaria lukuluensis

Crotalaria lukuluensis, commonly known as the Lukulu crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in grasslands and woodland areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with distinctive spurred petals, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
