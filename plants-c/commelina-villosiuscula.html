Commelina villosiuscula

Commelina villosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on both surfaces. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers that are typically clustered in a whorl at the tip of the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
