Centaurea jacea var. transalpina

Centaurea jacea var. transalpina, commonly known as the Transalpine Blazing Star, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces striking violet-blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, ray-like florets surrounded by a dense cluster of tubular flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and are typically covered in a fine layer of down.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant summer blooms.
