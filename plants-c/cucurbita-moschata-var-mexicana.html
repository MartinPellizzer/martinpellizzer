Cucurbita moschata var. mexicana

Cucurbita moschata var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican pumpkin, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, oval-shaped fruits that can range in color from white to orange or yellow. The plant produces lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is a vigorous vine that thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and is often cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
