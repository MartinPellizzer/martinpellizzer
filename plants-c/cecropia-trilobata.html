Cecropia trilobata

Cecropia trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with three distinct lobes, which help it capture maximum sunlight in dense forest environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects, followed by the development of woody, capsule-like fruits. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

This species is often used in reforestation projects due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
