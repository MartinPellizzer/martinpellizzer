Corispermum navicula

Corispermum navicula, commonly known as the narrow-boatwort, is a small, aquatic flowering plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in wet meadows and marshes.

Corispermum navicula plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
