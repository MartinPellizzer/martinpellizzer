Chamaedorea pulchra

Chamaedorea pulchra, commonly known as the golden bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by dark blue, spherical berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and moderate watering requirements.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both residential and commercial spaces.
