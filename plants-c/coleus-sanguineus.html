Coleus sanguineus

Coleus sanguineus, commonly known as red curl, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red or purple foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
