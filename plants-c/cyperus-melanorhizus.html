Cyperus melanorhizus

Cyperus melanorhizus, commonly known as blackroot sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its root system is notable for its dark, thick rhizomes, which contribute to its common name.

Cyperus melanorhizus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
