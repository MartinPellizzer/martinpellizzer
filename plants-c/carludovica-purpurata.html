Carludovica purpurata

Carludovica purpurata, commonly known as the purple cactus or the red cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-pink spines and fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
