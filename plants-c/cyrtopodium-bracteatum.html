Cyrtopodium bracteatum

Cyrtopodium bracteatum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, velvety flowers. The plant produces showy, often fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, which are typically borne on erect, branched inflorescences. Cyrtopodium bracteatum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal among orchid enthusiasts.
