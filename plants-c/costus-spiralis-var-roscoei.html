Costus spiralis var. roscoei

Costus spiralis var. roscoei, commonly known as the Roscoe's spiral costus, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy, spiral-arranged leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its foliage and floral display make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
