Carex triceps

Carex triceps, commonly known as three-veined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green and have three prominent veins, giving it its common name.

Carex triceps plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
