Chailletia sessiliflora

Chailletia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered chailletia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant features simple, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
