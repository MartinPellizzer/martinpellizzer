Crocus isauricus

Crocus isauricus, commonly known as the Isaurian crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Iris family and is characterized by its delicate, early-blooming flowers that typically appear in late winter or early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces violet or purple-colored blooms with a distinctive, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
