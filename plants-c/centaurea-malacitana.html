Centaurea malacitana

Centaurea malacitana, commonly known as the Malacitana thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically blue or purple in color.

Centaurea malacitana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
