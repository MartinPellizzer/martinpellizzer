Cynophalla saligna

Cynophalla saligna, commonly known as the silky bush or silky couch, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. The plant is recognized for its soft, silvery foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow or white.

Cynophalla saligna is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
