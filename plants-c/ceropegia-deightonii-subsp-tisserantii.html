Ceropegia deightonii subsp. tisserantii

Ceropegia deightonii subsp.

tisserantii, commonly known as the Tisserant's lion's tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow.

The plant produces specialized flowers that attract specific pollinators, such as certain species of flies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.
