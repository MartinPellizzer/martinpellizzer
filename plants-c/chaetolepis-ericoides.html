Chaetolepis ericoides

Chaetolepis ericoides, commonly known as the coastal heath or coastal ericoid, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring.

This plant is well adapted to sandy, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops.
