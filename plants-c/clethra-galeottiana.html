Clethra galeottiana

Clethra galeottiana, commonly known as the Italian clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, branching stems covered in small, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of stipules at the base of the leaves.

Clethra galeottiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
