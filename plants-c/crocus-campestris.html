Crocus campestris

Crocus campestris, commonly known as the meadow crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces showy, early spring flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange with a purple or red center. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Crocus campestris is a hardy species that is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
