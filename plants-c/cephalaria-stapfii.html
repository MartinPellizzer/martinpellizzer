Cephalaria stapfii

Cephalaria stapfii, commonly known as the Stapf's cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Ethiopia and Eritrea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often pubescent. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cephalaria stapfii is typically found in rocky or arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
