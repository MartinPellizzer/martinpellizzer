Cyclamen pseudibericum

Cyclamen pseudibericum, commonly known as the Canary Islands cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a deep green color and striking, spurred flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid environments. It blooms from late winter to early spring, adding a touch of elegance to gardens and greenhouses.

Cyclamen pseudibericum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
