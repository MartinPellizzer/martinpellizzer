Cimicifuga simplex var. tschonoskii

Cimicifuga simplex var. tschonoskii, commonly known as the Siberian bugloss, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats at high elevations.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
