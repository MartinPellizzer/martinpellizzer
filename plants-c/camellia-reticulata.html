Camellia reticulata

Camellia reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are often deeply veined and have a distinctive reticulate pattern. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Camellia reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooming period.
