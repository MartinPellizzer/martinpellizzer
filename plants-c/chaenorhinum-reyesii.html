Chaenorhinum reyesii

Chaenorhinum reyesii, commonly known as the Reyes' horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Chaenorhinum reyesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
