Carronia thyrsiflora

Carronia thyrsiflora, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Carronia thyrsiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
