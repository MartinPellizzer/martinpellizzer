Convolvulus supinus var. melliflorus

Convolvulus supinus var. melliflorus, commonly known as the sweetpea flower or the lesser bindweed, is a flowering plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three, with five petals each. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and sunny environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its flowers emit a mild, sweet fragrance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
