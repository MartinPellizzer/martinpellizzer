Crepis bracteolata

Crepis bracteolata, commonly known as the bracteolate hawk's-beard, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a rough texture, while the flower heads consist of both ray and disk florets.

Crepis bracteolata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
