Chuquiraga straminea

Chuquiraga straminea, commonly known as the yellow chuchundra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions and is well adapted to dry conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Chuquiraga straminea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
