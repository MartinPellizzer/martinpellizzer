Cyrtosperma senegalense

Cyrtosperma senegalense, commonly known as the Senegalese palm, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.
