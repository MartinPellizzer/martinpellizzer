Chrysophyllum glycyphloeum

Chrysophyllum glycyphloeum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, round, yellowish fruit with a sweet, juicy pulp and a single large seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
