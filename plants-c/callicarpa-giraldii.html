Callicarpa giraldii

Callicarpa giraldii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple to violet berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and smooth on top. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological value.
