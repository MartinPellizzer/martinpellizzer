Cinnamomum gigaphyllum

Cinnamomum gigaphyllum, commonly known as the giant cinnamon tree, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive size, reaching heights of up to 50 meters, and its distinctive aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries containing a single seed. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The tree plays an important role in its native ecosystems and is valued for its unique botanical features.
