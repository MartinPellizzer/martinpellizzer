Commelina stefaniniana

Commelina stefaniniana, commonly known as the Stefaninian Dayflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Commelinales order and the Commelinaceae family, which includes many species of dayflowers and spiderworts. The plant is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which bloom in clusters atop slender stalks. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy surface, aiding in water retention in its native habitat.

Commelina stefaniniana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
