Caesalpinia dictamnoides

Caesalpinia dictamnoides, commonly known as the flame tree or the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange inflorescences, which resemble flames, and its bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
