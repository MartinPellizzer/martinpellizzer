Cleome usambarica

Cleome usambarica, commonly known as the Usambara cleome, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, branching stems and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while the flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Cleome usambarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
