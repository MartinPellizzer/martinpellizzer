Crotalaria bodinieri

Crotalaria bodinieri, commonly known as the Bodinier's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive crown-like structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

Crotalaria bodinieri is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
