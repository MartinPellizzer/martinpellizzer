Cheirodendron wabiawense var. populneum

Cheirodendron wabiawense var. populneum, commonly known as the Wabiawense Poplar Tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, which are clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
