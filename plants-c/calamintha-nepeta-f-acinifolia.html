Calamintha nepeta f. acinifolia

Calamintha nepeta f. acinifolia, commonly known as the fragrant germander, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves with a mint-like scent, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its horticultural appeal lies in its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.

It is also valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
