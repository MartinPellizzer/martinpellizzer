Ceropegia connivens f. angustata

Ceropegia connivens f. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
