Carpolobia zenkeri

Carpolobia zenkeri, commonly known as the African orchid or Zenker's orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble orchids. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its native habitat.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological importance in its ecosystem.
