Carex canescens f. subsubulosa

Carex canescens f. subsubulosa, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically silver-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to wet conditions. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, typical of the Carex genus.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
