Camelina rumelica

Camelina rumelica, commonly known as the Balkan false flax, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

Camelina rumelica is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
