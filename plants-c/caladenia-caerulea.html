Caladenia caerulea

Caladenia caerulea, commonly known as the Bluehooded Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple petals and a prominent hood-like structure on its flower. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces a single flower per stem, which is often visited by insects such as flies and beetles.

Caladenia caerulea is a perennial herb that is adapted to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
