Crepis virens var. agrestis

Crepis virens var. agrestis, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single, elongated head.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structures and medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
