Crepis taraxacifolia var. tangerina

Crepis taraxacifolia var. tangerina, commonly known as the orange hawkweed, is a variant of the hairy hawkweed species.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its yellow-orange flower heads and hairy, lobed leaves. Native to Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, herbaceous stems that branch out near the base.

Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters, making it a distinctive and colorful addition to its natural environment.
