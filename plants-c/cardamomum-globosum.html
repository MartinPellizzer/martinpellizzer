Cardamomum globosum

Cardamomum globosum, commonly known as the globular cardamom, is a flowering plant belonging to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, rounded inflorescence that resembles a globe. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often clustered together, giving it its common name. Its leaves are elongated and have a strong, aromatic scent similar to other members of the ginger family.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
