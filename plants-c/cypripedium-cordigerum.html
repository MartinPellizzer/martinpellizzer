Cypripedium cordigerum

Cypripedium cordigerum, commonly known as the spurred lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers with distinctive spurred petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Cypripedium cordigerum is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
