Crocus alexandri

Crocus alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is closely related to other members of the Iris family. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in rocky or well-drained soils, and it is often found in sunny, open habitats.

The Alexandrian crocus is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
