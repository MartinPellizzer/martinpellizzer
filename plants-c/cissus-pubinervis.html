Cissus pubinervis

Cissus pubinervis, commonly known as the hairy-veined devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically glossy and have a distinctive pubescent (hairy) texture on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by red berries, which are edible for birds but toxic to humans.

It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.
