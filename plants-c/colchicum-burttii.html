Colchicum burttii

Colchicum burttii, commonly known as the Burtt's colchicum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Colchicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers. The plant produces large, showy blooms in the spring, often with a distinctive spathaceous bract. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Colchicum burttii is also known for its medicinal properties, containing the alkaloid colchicine, which has been used in medical treatments.
