Cestrum arborescens

Cestrum arborescens, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 feet tall, featuring woody stems and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant is most notable for its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that open at night and emit a strong scent to attract nocturnal pollinators. Its flowers are typically tubular with five petals and can measure up to 3 inches in diameter.

Cestrum arborescens thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
