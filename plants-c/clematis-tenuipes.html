Clematis tenuipes

Clematis tenuipes, commonly known as the slender clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thin, wiry stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided, trifoliate leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Clematis tenuipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care in garden settings.
