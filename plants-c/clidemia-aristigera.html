Clidemia aristigera

Clidemia aristigera, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and typically grows on the branches of trees such as mahogany and ceiba. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silver-white hairs, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Clidemia aristigera plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain birds and insects, though it can also be a potential invasive species in some regions.
