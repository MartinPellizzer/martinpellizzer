Cytisus + sordidus

Cytisus + sordidus, commonly known as the sordid broom, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and thorny stems. The plant has small, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as scrublands and woodland edges.

The species is known for its resilience and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
