Clausena simplicifolia

Clausena simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leafed Clausena, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
