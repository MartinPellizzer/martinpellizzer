Calicotome spinosa var. macrantha

Calicotome spinosa var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is well adapted to drought conditions.

This species is often used for erosion control and as a source of forage for wildlife.
