Cucumis asininus

Cucumis asininus, commonly known as the bitter gourd or karela, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to South and Southeast Asia and is characterized by its long, slender, and often spiny fruits. The plant produces both male and female flowers, with the female flowers typically developing into the edible fruit. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and trailing.

The fruit contains small, edible seeds and is used in various culinary and medicinal applications.
