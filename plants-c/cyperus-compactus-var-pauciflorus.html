Cyperus compactus var. pauciflorus

Cyperus compactus var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to greenish flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats in its native environment.

The species is characterized by its low-growing form and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
