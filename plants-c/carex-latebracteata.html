Carex latebracteata

Carex latebracteata, commonly known as the late bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage consists of grass-like leaves that are often dark green and stiff, with a triangular cross-section typical of sedges.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.
