Citrus × aurantium var. myrtifolia

Citrus × aurantium var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtleleaf citron, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its aromatic, thick-skinned fruit.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is closely related to the common citron (Citrus medica). The leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a glossy, myrtle-like appearance, giving the plant its varietal name. The fruit is typically small, green, and has a high concentration of essential oils and citric acid.

It is valued for its fragrance and is used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
