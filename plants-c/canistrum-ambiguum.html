Canistrum ambiguum

Canistrum ambiguum, commonly known as the variable mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae.

It grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its berries are orange to red in color and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Canistrum ambiguum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
