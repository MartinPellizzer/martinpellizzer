Chrysothamnus teretifolius

Chrysothamnus teretifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
