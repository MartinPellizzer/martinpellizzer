Capsicum eximium

Capsicum eximium, commonly known as the wild bell pepper, is a species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, green, bell-shaped fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and a pubescent surface.

Capsicum eximium is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
