Chaerophyllum daucifolium

Chaerophyllum daucifolium, commonly known as the meadow parsley or wild carrot, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white or yellowish flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a taproot system and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Its aromatic foliage and flowers make it a valuable component of early spring plant communities.
