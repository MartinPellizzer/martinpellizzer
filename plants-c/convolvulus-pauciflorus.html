Convolvulus pauciflorus

Convolvulus pauciflorus, commonly known as the few-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often with a slight pinkish tinge. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.
