Crataegus ignava

Crataegus ignava, commonly known as the Canadian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins, and turn yellow in autumn.

Crataegus ignava is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
