Colocasia indica var. rubra

Colocasia indica var. rubra, commonly known as the red elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The leaves are typically a deep red or purple color, which distinguishes it from other varieties of colocasia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative indoor plant.
