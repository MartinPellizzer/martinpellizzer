Chassalia montana

Chassalia montana, commonly known as the mountain chassalia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, while its flowers are small and clustered in a head.

Chassalia montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
