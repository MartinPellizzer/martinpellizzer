Centaurea rhizocalathium

Centaurea rhizocalathium, commonly known as the purplehead centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and spiny, hairy stems. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in Mediterranean climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bracteate flower heads.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
