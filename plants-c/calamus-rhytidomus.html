Calamus rhytidomus

Calamus rhytidomus, commonly known as the wrinkled reed, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Aroid family, characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and inflorescence. The plant is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and forest floors. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its ornamental value is also appreciated.

The plant's unique wrinkled texture on its leaves makes it easily identifiable.
