Croton grandivelum

Croton grandivelum, commonly known as the red-stemmed croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves that often display vibrant shades of red, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellow in color, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
