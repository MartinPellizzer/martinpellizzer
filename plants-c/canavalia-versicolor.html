Canavalia versicolor

Canavalia versicolor, commonly known as the multicolored morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, colorful flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and has compound pods that contain edible seeds. Its flowers typically range in color from white to purple, and they bloom in the morning, hence its common name.

Canavalia versicolor is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
