Chiliotrichum amelloides var. rosmarinifolium

Chiliotrichum amelloides var. rosmarinifolium, commonly known as the fragrant marsh plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy structures called trichomes, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic quality, reminiscent of rosemary.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
