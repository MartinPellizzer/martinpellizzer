Ceropegia barbata

Ceropegia barbata, commonly known as the barbed ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species that features fleshy, trailing stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, often with a distinctive beard-like structure at the base. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia barbata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
