Crassula basutica

Crassula basutica, commonly known as the Basutu Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit. The plant features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, especially when exposed to sunlight. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the spring.

Crassula basutica is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it an ideal choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
