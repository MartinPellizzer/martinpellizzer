Convolvulus stapfii

Convolvulus stapfii, commonly known as the Stapf's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces vibrant blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing growth patterns.

Convolvulus stapfii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
