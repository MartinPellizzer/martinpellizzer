Costarica hamata

Costarica hamata, commonly known as the Costa Rican spiny rat plant, is a rare and unique species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of dense rainforests. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance.

However, it faces threats from habitat loss and climate change, making its preservation a critical concern.
