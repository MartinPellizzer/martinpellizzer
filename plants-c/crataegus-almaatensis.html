Crataegus almaatensis

Crataegus almaatensis, commonly known as the Almaty hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and southern Siberia. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the autumn and are edible.

Crataegus almaatensis is often used in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
