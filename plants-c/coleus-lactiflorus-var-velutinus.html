Coleus lactiflorus var. velutinus

Coleus lactiflorus var. velutinus, commonly known as velvet coleus, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Velvet coleus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage and floral display.
