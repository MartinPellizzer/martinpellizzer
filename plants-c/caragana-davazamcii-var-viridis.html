Caragana davazamcii var. viridis

Caragana davazamcii var. viridis, commonly known as the greenleaf caragana, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, compound leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees. Its hardy nature allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it adaptable to poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
