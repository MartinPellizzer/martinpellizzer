Carex macrotrichoides

Carex macrotrichoides, commonly known as the long-hair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside, contributing to its common name.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
