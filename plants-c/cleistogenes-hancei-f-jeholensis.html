Cleistogenes hancei f. jeholensis

Cleistogenes hancei f. jeholensis, commonly known as the Jehol grass, is a perennial grass species native to northern China.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland ecosystems.

Due to its ecological significance, it is considered an important component of the regional flora.
