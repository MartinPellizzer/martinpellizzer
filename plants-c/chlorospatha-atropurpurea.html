Chlorospatha atropurpurea

Chlorospatha atropurpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf chlorospatha, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its delicate white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, aiding in moisture retention. Its flowering period typically occurs during the late spring to early summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
