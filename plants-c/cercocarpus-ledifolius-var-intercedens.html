Cercocarpus ledifolius var. intercedens

Cercocarpus ledifolius var. intercedens, commonly known as the intercedent's bitterbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-green foliage and small, red-brown fruits that mature in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
