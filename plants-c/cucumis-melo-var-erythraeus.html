Cucumis melo var. erythraeus

Cucumis melo var. erythraeus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the melon family.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruits with a pale green rind and a sweet, crisp flesh. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, producing yellow flowers and requiring a warm, sunny climate. Its fruit is often used in fresh consumption or as a decorative element in gardens.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and ornamental appeal.
