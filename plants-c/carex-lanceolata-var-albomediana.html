Carex lanceolata var. albomediana

Carex lanceolata var. albomediana, commonly known as the white-veined sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually bright green with distinct white veins. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of pale yellow to green flowers in the summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along streams.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
