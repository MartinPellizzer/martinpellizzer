Centaurea × javorkae

Centaurea × javorkae, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea solstitialis (yellow starthistle). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring lance-shaped leaves and striking blue to purple flowers with a yellow center. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
