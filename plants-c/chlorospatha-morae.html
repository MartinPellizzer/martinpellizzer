Chlorospatha morae

Chlorospatha morae, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, moist soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing soruses, are typically located on the underside of its leaves.

Chlorospatha morae is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional medicine in its native habitats.
