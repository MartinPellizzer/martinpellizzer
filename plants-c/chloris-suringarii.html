Chloris suringarii

Chloris suringarii, commonly known as the Suringar's grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, particularly India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish or pale in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
